Houttuynia cordata f. polypetaloidea

Houttuynia cordata f. polypetaloidea, commonly known as the fragrant swamp onion, is a cultivar of the Houttuynia cordata species.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green or variegated with white and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to purify water in wetland environments.
