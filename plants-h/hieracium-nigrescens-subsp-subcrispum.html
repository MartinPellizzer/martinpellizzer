Hieracium nigrescens subsp. subcrispum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

subcrispum, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow disk flowers.

The subspecies subcrispum is distinguished by its finely toothed, serrated leaf margins. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates.

This plant is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
