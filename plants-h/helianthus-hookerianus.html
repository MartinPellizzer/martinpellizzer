Helianthus hookerianus

Helianthus hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Helianthus. It is often found in dry grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils.

Helianthus hookerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
