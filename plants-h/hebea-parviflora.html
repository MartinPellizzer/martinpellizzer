Hebea parviflora

Hebea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows as a shrub with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Hebea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
