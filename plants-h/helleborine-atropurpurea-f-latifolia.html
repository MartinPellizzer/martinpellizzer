Helleborine atropurpurea f. latifolia

Helleborine atropurpurea f. latifolia, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments. It produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
