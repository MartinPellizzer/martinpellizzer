Hieracium alpinum var. tubulosum

Hieracium alpinum var. tubulosum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats or cushions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is known for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
