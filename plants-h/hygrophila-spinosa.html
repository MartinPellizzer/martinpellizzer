Hygrophila spinosa

Hygrophila spinosa, commonly known as the spiked hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. Hygrophila spinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and can be cultivated in both terrestrial and water gardens.
