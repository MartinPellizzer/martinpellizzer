Hyoscyamus insanus

Hyoscyamus insanus, commonly known as the mad dervish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
