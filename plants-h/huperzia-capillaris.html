Huperzia capillaris

Huperzia capillaris, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and small, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Huperzia capillaris lacks true roots and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its leaf-like structures.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms in its ecosystem.
