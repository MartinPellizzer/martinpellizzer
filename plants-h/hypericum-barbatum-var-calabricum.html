Hypericum barbatum var. calabricum

Hypericum barbatum var. calabricum, commonly known as the California St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features bright yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems covered in short, glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
