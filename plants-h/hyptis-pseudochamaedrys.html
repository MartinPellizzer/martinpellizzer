Hyptis pseudochamaedrys

Hyptis pseudochamaedrys, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, and its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
