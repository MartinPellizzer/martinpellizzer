Hydrocotyle moschata var. parvifolia

Hydrocotyle moschata var. parvifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf or marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features a low-growing rosette of heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, umbellate flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its fragrant leaves and adaptability to wet conditions make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
