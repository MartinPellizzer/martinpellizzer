Hoorebekia chiloensis

Hoorebekia chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Hoorebekia chiloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
