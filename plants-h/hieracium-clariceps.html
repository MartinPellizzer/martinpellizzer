Hieracium clariceps

Hieracium clariceps, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium clariceps is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
