Hosta pycnophylla

Hosta pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaf hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the summer months. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

Its foliage is often prized for its ornamental value in landscaping.
