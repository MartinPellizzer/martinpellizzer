Heuchera woodsiaphila

Heuchera woodsiaphila, commonly known as the Woodsiaphila alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, matted foliage that ranges from deep green to red and purple hues, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in late spring, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Heuchera woodsiaphila prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
