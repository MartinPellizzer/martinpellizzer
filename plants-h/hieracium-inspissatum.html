Hieracium inspissatum

Hieracium inspissatum, commonly known as the thickened hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium inspissatum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and can sometimes become invasive in non-native regions.
