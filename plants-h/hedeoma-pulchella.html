Hedeoma pulchella

Hedeoma pulchella, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.
