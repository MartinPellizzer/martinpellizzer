Hedysarum praticola

Hedysarum praticola, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Hedysarum praticola is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
