Hymenophyllum undulatum

Hymenophyllum undulatum, commonly known as the undulating fern, is a small, delicate moss-like plant belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, undulating fronds that give it a wave-like appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually less than 1 cm in length and are covered with a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures.

Hymenophyllum undulatum plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms.
