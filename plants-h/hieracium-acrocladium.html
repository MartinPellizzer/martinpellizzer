Hieracium acrocladium

Hieracium acrocladium, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually hairy and lobed.

Hieracium acrocladium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
