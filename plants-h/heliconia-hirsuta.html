Heliconia hirsuta

Heliconia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that enclose the actual flowers, which are small and clustered in a spadix. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are broad and often have a hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Heliconia hirsuta thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
