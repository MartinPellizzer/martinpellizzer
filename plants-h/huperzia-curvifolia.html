Huperzia curvifolia

Huperzia curvifolia, commonly known as the curled fern, is a species of clubmoss found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its evergreen, needle-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia curvifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
