Hedyotis galioides

Hedyotis galioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttonweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Hedyotis galioides is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in riparian zones.
