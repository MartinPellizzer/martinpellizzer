Hieracium subcaesium subsp. crispans

Hieracium subcaesium subsp.

crispans, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat head.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in some regions.
