Hieracium murorum subsp. ramselense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

ramselense, commonly known as the Ramsele hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear basal and alternate leaves with rough, serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced from late spring to early autumn.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in calcareous soils.
