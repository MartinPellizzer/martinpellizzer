Hieracium flagellare subsp. bicapitatum

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

bicapitatum, commonly known as the two-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single or two flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its subspecies name, *bicapitatum*, refers to the presence of two distinct flower heads on the stem.

The plant contains compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
