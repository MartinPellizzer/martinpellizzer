Hedysarum humile var. maroccanum

Hedysarum humile var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Hedysarum humile var. maroccanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
