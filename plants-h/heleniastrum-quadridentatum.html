Heleniastrum quadridentatum

Heleniastrum quadridentatum, commonly known as the four-toothed helenea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with four distinct teeth along the margins, giving it its scientific name.

Heleniastrum quadridentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like flowers.
