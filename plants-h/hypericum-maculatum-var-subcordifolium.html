Hypericum maculatum var. subcordifolium

Hypericum maculatum var. subcordifolium, commonly known as the spotted jewelweed, is a variant of the yellow root plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers and distinctive, often spotted stems. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
