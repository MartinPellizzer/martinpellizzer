Herschelia goetzeana

Herschelia goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence composed of ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Herschelia goetzeana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
