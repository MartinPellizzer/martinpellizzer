Hieracium variegatisquamum

Hieracium variegatisquamum, commonly known as the variegated sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves with a distinctive white or pale margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and alpine habitats.
