Hippocrepis univalvis

Hippocrepis univalvis, commonly known as the single-valved rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a dense cluster. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Hippocrepis univalvis thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
