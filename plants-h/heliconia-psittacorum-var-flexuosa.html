Heliconia psittacorum var. flexuosa

Heliconia psittacorum var. flexuosa, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant features long, narrow leaves arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically clustered in a spiral arrangement, with the female flowers at the base and the male flowers above.

This species is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
