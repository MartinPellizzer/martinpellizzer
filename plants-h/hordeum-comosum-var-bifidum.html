Hordeum comosum var. bifidum

Hordeum comosum var. bifidum, commonly known as the twin-awned barley, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces two distinct awns on each lemma, which are thin, bristle-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This variety is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological roles.
