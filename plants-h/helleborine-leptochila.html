Helleborine leptochila

Helleborine leptochila, commonly known as the slender helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which aids in nutrient absorption.

The slender helleborine is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
