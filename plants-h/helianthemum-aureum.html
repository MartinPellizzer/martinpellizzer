Helianthemum aureum

Helianthemum aureum, commonly known as the golden rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, oval-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Helianthemum aureum is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
