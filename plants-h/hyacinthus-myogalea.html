Hyacinthus myogalea

Hyacinthus myogalea, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking clusters of violet-blue flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. Its flowers are composed of six tepals that are tightly packed, giving the inflorescence a dense, globe-like appearance.

Hyacinthus myogalea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
