Helichrysum lucidum var. normalis

Helichrysum lucidum var. normalis, commonly known as the shining immortelle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow flower heads that resemble small suns, which remain intact even when dried, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
