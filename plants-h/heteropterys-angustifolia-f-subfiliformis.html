Heteropterys angustifolia f. subfiliformis

Heteropterys angustifolia f. subfiliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heteropterys, is a variant of the broadleaf heteropterys species.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often described as thread-like in appearance. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant morphology and genetic variation.
