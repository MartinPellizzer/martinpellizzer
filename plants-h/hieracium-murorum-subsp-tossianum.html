Hieracium murorum subsp. tossianum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

tossianum, commonly known as the southern meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and meadows.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrowly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The species is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
