Hugonia buchananii

Hugonia buchananii, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that float on the water surface, with a yellowish-green color and a distinctive white or yellow flower that blooms in summer. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in the mud and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Hugonia buchananii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through its natural filtration processes.
