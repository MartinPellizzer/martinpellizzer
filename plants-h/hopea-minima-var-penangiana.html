Hopea minima var. penangiana

Hopea minima var. penangiana, commonly known as Penang hopea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing single seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
