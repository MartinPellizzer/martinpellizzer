Hottea

Hottea, commonly known as the tea plant, is a flowering plant in the genus Camellia, native to eastern and southern Asia.

It is cultivated for its leaves, which are processed into various types of tea. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a deep green color and a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and they appear in late spring to early summer.
