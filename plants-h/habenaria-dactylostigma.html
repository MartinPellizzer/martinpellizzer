Habenaria dactylostigma

Habenaria dactylostigma, commonly known as the orchid with the distinctive red and white flowers, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are often arranged in a loose cluster and feature intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The orchid's botanical structure reflects adaptations to its ecological niche, making it a notable species in tropical flora.
