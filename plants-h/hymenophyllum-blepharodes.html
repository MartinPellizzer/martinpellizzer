Hymenophyllum blepharodes

Hymenophyllum blepharodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rock surfaces or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.
