Hibiscus amambayensis

Hibiscus amambayensis, commonly known as the Amambay hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Paraguay.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a robust, upright growth habit. Hibiscus amambayensis is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions of South America.
