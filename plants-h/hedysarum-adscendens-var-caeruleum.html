Hedysarum adscendens var. caeruleum

Hedysarum adscendens var. caeruleum, commonly known as blue peavine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to blue or violet. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three ovate leaflets.
