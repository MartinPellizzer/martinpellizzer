Hieracium bifidum subsp. ungeri

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

ungeri, commonly known as the Unger's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
