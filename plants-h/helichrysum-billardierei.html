Helichrysum billardierei

Helichrysum billardierei, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, papery bracts that persist after the flowers have faded, giving it a long-lasting appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Helichrysum billardierei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
