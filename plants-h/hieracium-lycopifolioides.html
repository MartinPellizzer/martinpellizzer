Hieracium lycopifolioides

Hieracium lycopifolioides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in clusters. It thrives in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
