Heliotropium subulatum

Heliotropium subulatum, commonly known as the needleleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in dry environments.

Heliotropium subulatum is also known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a mild, sweet scent.
