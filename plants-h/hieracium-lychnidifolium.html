Hieracium lychnidifolium

Hieracium lychnidifolium, commonly known as the candleleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy blooms. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
