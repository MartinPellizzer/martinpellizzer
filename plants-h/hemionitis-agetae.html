Hemionitis agetae

Hemionitis agetae, commonly known as the small-leaf hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded habitats. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate, while its spores are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Hemionitis agetae is considered a vulnerable species.
