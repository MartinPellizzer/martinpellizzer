Hieracium limbolariceps

Hieracium limbolariceps, commonly known as the limbed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves with a short, upright flowering stem.

It is generally considered a weed but has ornamental value due to its bright yellow blooms.
