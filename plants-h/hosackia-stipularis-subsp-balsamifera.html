Hosackia stipularis subsp. balsamifera

Hosackia stipularis subsp.

balsamifera, commonly known as the balsamroot false poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and a tall, hairy stem that bears yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid environments with limited water availability.
