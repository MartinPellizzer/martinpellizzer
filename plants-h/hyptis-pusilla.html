Hyptis pusilla

Hyptis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf hyptis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring hairy, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Hyptis pusilla thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
