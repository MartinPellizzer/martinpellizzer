Hieracium piloselloides subsp. pseudoflorentinum

Hieracium piloselloides subsp.

pseudoflorentinum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

The plant features basal rosettes of grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, which is surrounded by involucral bracts.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
