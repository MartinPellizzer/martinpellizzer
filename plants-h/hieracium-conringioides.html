Hieracium conringioides

Hieracium conringioides, commonly known as the Conring's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium conringioides is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
