Humboltia bilamellata

Humboltia bilamellata, commonly known as the two-lipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with two distinct lip lobes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark without parasitizing its host. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Humboltia bilamellata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
