Hydrocotyle ranunculoides

Hydrocotyle ranunculoides, commonly known as the marsh mallow or water shield, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features a rosette of rounded, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Hydrocotyle ranunculoides thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
