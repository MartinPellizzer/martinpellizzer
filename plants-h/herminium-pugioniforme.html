Herminium pugioniforme

Herminium pugioniforme, commonly known as the sword-leaved twayblade, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It has a tuberous root system and is part of the Orchidaceae family.

Herminium pugioniforme is a non-photosynthetic plant, relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
