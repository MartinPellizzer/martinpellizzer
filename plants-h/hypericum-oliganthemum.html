Hypericum oliganthemum

Hypericum oliganthemum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Hypericum oliganthemum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.
