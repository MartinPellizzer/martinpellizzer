Hordeum vulgare var. latinudipyramidatum

Hordeum vulgare var. latinudipyramidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barley, is a variant of the common barley species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in temperate regions and is adapted to cooler climates. It has a lower yield compared to other barley types but is valued for its unique morphology.

The plant's structure makes it an important subject in botanical studies and taxonomy.
