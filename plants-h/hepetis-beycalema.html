Hepetis beycalema

Hepetis beycalema, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Hepetis beycalema is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
