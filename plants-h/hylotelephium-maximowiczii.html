Hylotelephium maximowiczii

Hylotelephium maximowiczii, commonly known as the Armenian carpet or Armenian fireweed, is a perennial succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Hylotelephium maximowiczii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
