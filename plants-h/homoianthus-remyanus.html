Homoianthus remyanus

Homoianthus remyanus, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
