Hesperis steveniana subsp. cinerea

Hesperis steveniana subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the gray scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
