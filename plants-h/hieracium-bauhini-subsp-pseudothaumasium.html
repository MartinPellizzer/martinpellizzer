Hieracium bauhini subsp. pseudothaumasium

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

pseudothaumasium, commonly known as the false thomas' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow flowering heads, which are composed of numerous small disc flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish bracts.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, hairy texture and a slightly lobed appearance.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in temperate climates.
