Hieracium tigrinum

Hieracium tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger's eye hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive reddish or brownish center.

Hieracium tigrinum is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
