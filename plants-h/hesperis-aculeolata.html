Hesperis aculeolata

Hesperis aculeolata, commonly known as the spiked candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Hesperis aculeolata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
