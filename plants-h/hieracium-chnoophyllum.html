Hieracium chnoophyllum

Hieracium chnoophyllum, commonly known as the bog hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and fens, where it forms dense colonies. The plant has hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped with rough textures. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium chnoophyllum plays a role in stabilizing peat soils and supporting specialized invertebrate communities.
