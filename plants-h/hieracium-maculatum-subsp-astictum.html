Hieracium maculatum subsp. astictum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

astictum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are often covered with glandular dots, giving it a distinctive spotted appearance.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth habits.
