Hymenopappus nudipes

Hymenopappus nudipes, commonly known as the naked-stemmed burrweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous annual with a distinctive appearance, featuring a slender, hairless stem and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation across open landscapes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in sandy areas.

Due to its adaptability and ecological role, it plays a part in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
