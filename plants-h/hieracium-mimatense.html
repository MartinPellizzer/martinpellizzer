Hieracium mimatense

Hieracium mimatense, commonly known as the Mimatense Hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, grassy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
