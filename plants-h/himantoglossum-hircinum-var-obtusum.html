Himantoglossum hircinum var. obtusum

Himantoglossum hircinum var. obtusum, commonly known as the blunt-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a rounded base, and it produces showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
