Hieracium subtumidum

Hieracium subtumidum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subtumidum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
