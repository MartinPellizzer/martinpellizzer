Hieracium magyaricum subsp. adenocladum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

adenocladum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The subspecies adenocladum is distinguished by its glandular-cushion-like leaves and shorter flowering stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several countries.
