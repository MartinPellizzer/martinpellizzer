Hippeastrum platense var. angustum

Hippeastrum platense var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
