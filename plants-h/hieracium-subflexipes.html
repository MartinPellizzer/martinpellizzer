Hieracium subflexipes

Hieracium subflexipes, commonly known as the bent-stem hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, typical of the dandelion family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium subflexipes is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
