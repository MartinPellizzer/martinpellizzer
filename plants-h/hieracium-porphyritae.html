Hieracium porphyritae

Hieracium porphyritae, commonly known as the porphyry hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils, thriving in harsh, mountainous environments. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often arranged in a single head.

Hieracium porphyritae is known for its resilience to cold and low-nutrient conditions, making it a common sight in alpine meadows.
