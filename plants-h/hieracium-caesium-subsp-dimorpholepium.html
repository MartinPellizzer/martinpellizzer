Hieracium caesium subsp. dimorpholepium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

dimorpholepium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

The plant has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
