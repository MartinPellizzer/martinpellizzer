Hedyotis greenei

Hedyotis greenei, commonly known as the Greene's inchplant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically features small, oval leaves that are arranged alternately along its slender stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. Hedyotis greenei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
