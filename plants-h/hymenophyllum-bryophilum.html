Hymenophyllum bryophilum

Hymenophyllum bryophilum, commonly known as the moss-feeding filmy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its thin, translucent fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like growth, which provides moisture and protection. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This species plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and moisture retention.
