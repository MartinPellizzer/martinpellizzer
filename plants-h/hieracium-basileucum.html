Hieracium basileucum

Hieracium basileucum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green to white leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has finely toothed leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is considered a perennial and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
