Hieracium laevigatum subsp. euconspicuum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

euconspicuum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower and more upright. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk and ray florets, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in sunny, open habitats.
