Heliotropium subcanescens

Heliotropium subcanescens, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
