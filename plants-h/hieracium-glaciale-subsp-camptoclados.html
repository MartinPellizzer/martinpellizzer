Hieracium glaciale subsp. camptoclados

Hieracium glaciale subsp.

camptoclados, commonly known as the Alpine bentleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats or cushions.

The plant has dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often curled or folded, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their medicinal and ecological significance.
