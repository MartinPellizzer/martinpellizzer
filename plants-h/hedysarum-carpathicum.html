Hedysarum carpathicum

Hedysarum carpathicum, commonly known as Carpathian sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy species.

It is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along roadsides, contributing to soil stabilization through nitrogen fixation.
