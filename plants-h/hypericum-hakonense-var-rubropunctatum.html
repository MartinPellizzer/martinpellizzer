Hypericum hakonense var. rubropunctatum

Hypericum hakonense var. rubropunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture. The variety name 'rubropunctatum' refers to the reddish dots that appear on the stems and leaves.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
