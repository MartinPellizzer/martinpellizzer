Hemianemia tripinnata

Hemianemia tripinnata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hemianthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty plant enthusiasts.
