Hedyotis corymbosa var. nana

Hedyotis corymbosa var. nana, commonly known as dwarf blushing groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-care-for plant for gardeners.
