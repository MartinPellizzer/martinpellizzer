Hedyotis strumosa

Hedyotis strumosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hedyotis strumosa is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often found in the understory of forests.
