Hieracium ochrolomum

Hieracium ochrolomum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and the plant produces achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium ochrolomum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
