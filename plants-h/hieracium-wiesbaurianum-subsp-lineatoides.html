Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. lineatoides

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

lineatoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically linear to lanceolate in shape. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely serrated leaf margins and relatively small flower heads.
