Helichrysum puteale

Helichrysum puteale, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange-colored flowers that remain open for a prolonged period, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Helichrysum puteale is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
