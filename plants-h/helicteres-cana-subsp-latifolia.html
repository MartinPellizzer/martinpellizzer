Helicteres cana subsp. latifolia

Helicteres cana subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf helicteres, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland environments.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are clustered in panicles, often with yellow or greenish hues. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
