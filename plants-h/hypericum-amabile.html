Hypericum amabile

Hypericum amabile, commonly known as sweet stitchwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
