Hieracium murorum subsp. junciniforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

junciniforme, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and its yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is typically found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both vegetative means and seeds, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
