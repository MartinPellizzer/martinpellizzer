Hedyotis inamoena

Hedyotis inamoena, commonly known as the small-leaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hedyotis inamoena is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
