Harpagophytum procumbens var. sublobatum

Harpagophytum procumbens var. sublobatum, commonly known as the "devil's claw," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like seed pods and yellow, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, particularly for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
