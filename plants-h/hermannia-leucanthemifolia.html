Hermannia leucanthemifolia

Hermannia leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered hermannia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white flowers with prominent yellow centers, resembling daisies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hermannia leucanthemifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
