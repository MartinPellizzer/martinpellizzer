Hieracium nigrescens subsp. trichoglossoides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

trichoglossoides, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The species is notable for its distinctive foliage and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats.
