Hirtella insignis

Hirtella insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Hirtella insignis is typically found in moist, tropical environments such as rainforests and wetlands.
