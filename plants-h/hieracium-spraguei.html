Hieracium spraguei

Hieracium spraguei, commonly known as Sprague's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
