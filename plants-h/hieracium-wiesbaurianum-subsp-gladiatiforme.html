Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. gladiatiforme

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

gladiatiforme, commonly known as the gladiolus-like hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange flowering heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically produces a single, showy composite flower head at the top of a leafy stem. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.
