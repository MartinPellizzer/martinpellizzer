Hieracium uriense

Hieracium uriense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium uriense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in alpine meadows.
