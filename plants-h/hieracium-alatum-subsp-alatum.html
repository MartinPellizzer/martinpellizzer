Hieracium alatum subsp. alatum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

alatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
