Hosackia confinis

Hosackia confinis, commonly known as the western prairie fringed orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open prairies and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, and it has a fibrous root system.

Hosackia confinis is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its ecological importance as a pollinator attractor.
