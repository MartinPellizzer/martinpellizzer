Hypericum ciliatum var. angustifolium

Hypericum ciliatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has slender, angular stems and narrow, linear leaves with ciliate margins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
