Hyacinthus pycnanthus

Hyacinthus pycnanthus, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
