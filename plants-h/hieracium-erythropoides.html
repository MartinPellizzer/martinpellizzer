Hieracium erythropoides

Hieracium erythropoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive red stem.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
