Helianthemum intermedium var. ambiguum

Helianthemum intermedium var. ambiguum, commonly known as the pink sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink or pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
