Hoya villosa

Hoya villosa, commonly known as the velvety wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and it produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements. Hoya villosa thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts.
