Hedera helix var. sulphurea

Hedera helix var. sulphurea, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its striking yellow-variegated foliage.

This plant is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe, typically found in woodland and shaded areas. The leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture and exhibit a vibrant yellow-green coloration. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires moderate moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
