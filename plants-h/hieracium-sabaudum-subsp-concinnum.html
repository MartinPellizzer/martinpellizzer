Hieracium sabaudum subsp. concinnum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

concinnum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
