Huernia macrocarpa subsp. concinna

Huernia macrocarpa subsp.

concinna, commonly known as the horned African violet, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy stems that are often branched and covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom at night. Its leaves are typically green and somewhat triangular in shape, and it thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
