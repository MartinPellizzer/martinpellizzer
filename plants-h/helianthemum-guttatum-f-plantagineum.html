Helianthemum guttatum f. plantagineum

Helianthemum guttatum f. plantagineum, commonly known as the sticky rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a sticky, glandular coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
