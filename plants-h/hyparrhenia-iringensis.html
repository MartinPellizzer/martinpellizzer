Hyparrhenia iringensis

Hyparrhenia iringensis, commonly known as the African grass or tall flatsedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils. Hyparrhenia iringensis is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various small animals.
