Hieracium inquinatum

Hieracium inquinatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

Hieracium inquinatum is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
