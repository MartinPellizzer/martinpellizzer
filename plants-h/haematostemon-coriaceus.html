Haematostemon coriaceus

Haematostemon coriaceus, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
