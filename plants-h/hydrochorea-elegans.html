Hydrochorea elegans

Hydrochorea elegans, commonly known as the elegant waterwheel, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of delicate, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands.

Its unique appearance and ability to purify water make it a popular choice for aquatic gardens.
