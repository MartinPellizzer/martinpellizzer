Homoianthus triceps

Homoianthus triceps, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth from a node. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure, with three prominent lobes, makes it a distinctive and sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.
