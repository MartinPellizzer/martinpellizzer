Helleborine persica

Helleborine persica, commonly known as the Persian helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, particularly in regions with moist, temperate climates. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in shaded, woodland environments and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

Helleborine persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
