Helianthemum scoparium var. aldersonii

Helianthemum scoparium var. aldersonii, commonly known as the Alderson's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in coastal and inland areas with full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
