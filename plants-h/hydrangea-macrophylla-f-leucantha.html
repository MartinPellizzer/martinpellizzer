Hydrangea macrophylla f. leucantha

Hydrangea macrophylla f. leucantha, commonly known as the "white hydrangea," is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy white flower heads.

This variety is part of the Hydrangea macrophylla species, which is native to Japan and is known for its ability to change flower color based on soil pH. The flowers of f. leucantha typically bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking display of white blooms that can persist for several weeks. The plant thrives in partial shade and requires consistently moist, well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add a soft, elegant touch to landscapes.
