Haplopappus stellatus

Haplopappus stellatus, commonly known as the star pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, yellow to orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a low, spreading growth habit and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
