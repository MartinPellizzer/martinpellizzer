Hieracium barbicaule

Hieracium barbicaule, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium barbicaule is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
