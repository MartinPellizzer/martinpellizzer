Hibiscus drynarioides

Hibiscus drynarioides, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus drynarioides is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
