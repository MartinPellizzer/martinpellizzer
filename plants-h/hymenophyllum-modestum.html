Hymenophyllum modestum

Hymenophyllum modestum, commonly known as the small shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, small, and often intricately veined fronds, which are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and the plant reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Hymenophyllum modestum is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations that allow it to flourish in specific microhabitats.
