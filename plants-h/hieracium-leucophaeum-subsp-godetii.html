Hieracium leucophaeum subsp. godetii

Hieracium leucophaeum subsp.

godetii, commonly known as the Godet's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
