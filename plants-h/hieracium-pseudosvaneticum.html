Hieracium pseudosvaneticum

Hieracium pseudosvaneticum, commonly known as the false sandhill hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium pseudosvaneticum is often found in disturbed or nutrient-poor environments, showing adaptability to various soil conditions.
