Homalium nitens

Homalium nitens, commonly known as the shining woodbine, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
