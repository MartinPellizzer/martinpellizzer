Hieracium pellitum subsp. leuconeuron

Hieracium pellitum subsp.

leuconeuron, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with white centers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
