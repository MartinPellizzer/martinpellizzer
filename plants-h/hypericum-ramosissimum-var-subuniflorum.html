Hypericum ramosissimum var. subuniflorum

Hypericum ramosissimum var. subuniflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
