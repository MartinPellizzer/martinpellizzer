Heliophila seselifolia var. nigellifolia

Heliophila seselifolia var. nigellifolia, commonly known as the blackseed heliophila, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly toothed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are known for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional herbal medicine.

The variety is distinguished by its dark, almost black seeds, which give it its common name.
