Hookera minor var. multiflora

Hookera minor var. multiflora, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are generally basal, lanceolate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Hookera minor var. multiflora is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
