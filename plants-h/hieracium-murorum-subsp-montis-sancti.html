Hieracium murorum subsp. montis-sancti

Hieracium murorum subsp.

montis-sancti, commonly known as the Saint-Mont hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant has basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their medicinal and ecological significance.
