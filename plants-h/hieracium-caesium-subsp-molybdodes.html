Hieracium caesium subsp. molybdodes

Hieracium caesium subsp.

molybdodes, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.
