Helianthus parishii

Helianthus parishii, commonly known as the Parish sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides.

Helianthus parishii is a member of the Asteraceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
