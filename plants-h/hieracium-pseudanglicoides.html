Hieracium pseudanglicoides

Hieracium pseudanglicoides, commonly known as the false English hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
