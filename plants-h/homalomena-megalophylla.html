Homalomena megalophylla

Homalomena megalophylla, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Homalomena megalophylla prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, thriving in humid environments.
