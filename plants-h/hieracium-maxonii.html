Hieracium maxonii

Hieracium maxonii, commonly known as the Maxon's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or alpine environments. Hieracium maxonii has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called cypselae.
