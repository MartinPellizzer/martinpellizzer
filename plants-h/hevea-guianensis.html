Hevea guianensis

Hevea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces a milky latex sap that is harvested for natural rubber. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and protection against pests.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to the forest canopy.
