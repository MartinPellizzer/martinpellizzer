Hyacinthus abortivus

Hyacinthus abortivus, commonly known as the aborted hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

However, it is considered a weed in some regions due to its aggressive spreading nature.
