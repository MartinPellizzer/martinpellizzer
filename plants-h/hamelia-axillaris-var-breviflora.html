Hamelia axillaris var. breviflora

Hamelia axillaris var. breviflora, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
