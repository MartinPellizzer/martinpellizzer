Hypericum × desetangsii nothovar. perforatiforme

Hypericum × desetangsii nothovar. perforatiforme, commonly known as the perforateleaf St. John's wort, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with opposite, simple, and often perforated leaves, which distinguish it from other members of the Hypericaceae family. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the genus Hypericum. It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowering habits make it a notable species in horticultural collections.
