Hieracium querciticum

Hieracium querciticum, commonly known as woodcock hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
