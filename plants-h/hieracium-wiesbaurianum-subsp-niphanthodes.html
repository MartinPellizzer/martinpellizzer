Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. niphanthodes

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

niphanthodes, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
