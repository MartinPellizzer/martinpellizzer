Holcus sorghum var. technicus

Holcus sorghum var. technicus, commonly known as the technical sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces tall, branched stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. It is often cultivated for its drought tolerance and potential use in biofuel production.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important species in agricultural and ecological studies.
