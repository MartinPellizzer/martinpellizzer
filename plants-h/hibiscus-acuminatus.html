Hibiscus acuminatus

Hibiscus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink or red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus acuminatus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
