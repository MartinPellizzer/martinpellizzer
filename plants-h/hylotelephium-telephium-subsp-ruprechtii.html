Hylotelephium telephium subsp. ruprechtii

Hylotelephium telephium subsp.

ruprechtii, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a perennial succulent plant native to the Caucasus region. It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to arid landscapes.
