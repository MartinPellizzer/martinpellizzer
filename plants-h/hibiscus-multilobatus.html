Hibiscus multilobatus

Hibiscus multilobatus, commonly known as the lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with five petals that often have a lobed or notched appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus multilobatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

It blooms throughout the growing season, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
