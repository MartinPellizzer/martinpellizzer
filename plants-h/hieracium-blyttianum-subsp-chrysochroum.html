Hieracium blyttianum subsp. chrysochroum

Hieracium blyttianum subsp.

chrysochroum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed group within the genus Hieracium.
