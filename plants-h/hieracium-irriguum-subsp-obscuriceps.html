Hieracium irriguum subsp. obscuriceps

Hieracium irriguum subsp.

obscuriceps, commonly known as the dark-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stem structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more compact flower heads compared to other members of the species.
