Horsfieldia fulva

Horsfieldia fulva, commonly known as the red orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Horsfieldia fulva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
