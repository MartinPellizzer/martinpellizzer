Hieracium erubescens

Hieracium erubescens, commonly known as the red hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Hieracium erubescens is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
