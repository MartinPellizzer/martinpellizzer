Hypericum japonicum var. stenocarpum

Hypericum japonicum var. stenocarpum, commonly known as narrow-fruit St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, elliptical fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
