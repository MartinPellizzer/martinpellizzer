Hieracium trachycaulon

Hieracium trachycaulon, commonly known as rough-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hieracium trachycaulon is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
