Hieracium perthusianum

Hieracium perthusianum, commonly known as the Perthus hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer.

Hieracium perthusianum is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
