Haloragis ivifolia

Haloragis ivifolia, commonly known as the common rivergrass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and they are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Haloragis ivifolia is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing riverbanks through its extensive root system.
