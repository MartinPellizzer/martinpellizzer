Hieracium pilosum subsp. favrei

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

favrei, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
