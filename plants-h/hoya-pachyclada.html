Hoya pachyclada

Hoya pachyclada, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, dark green leaves that often have a slight variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and coral, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hoya pachyclada is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
