Hieracium pseudograndidens

Hieracium pseudograndidens, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its aggressive growth and has been classified as an invasive species in some regions.
