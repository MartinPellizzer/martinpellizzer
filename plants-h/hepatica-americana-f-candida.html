Hepatica americana f. candida

Hepatica americana f. candida, commonly known as the white hepatica, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. This plant thrives in moist, rich woodlands and is often found in shaded environments.

It is an important early bloomer in forest ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role.
