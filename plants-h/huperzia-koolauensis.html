Huperzia koolauensis

Huperzia koolauensis, commonly known as the Koolau fern, is a species of clubmoss native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive spore-producing structure called a synangium, which is a key feature in its reproductive cycle. Huperzia koolauensis is typically found growing on rocky outcrops or in coastal areas, where it contributes to the unique flora of Hawaii.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
