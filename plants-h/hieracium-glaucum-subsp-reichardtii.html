Hieracium glaucum subsp. reichardtii

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

reichardtii, commonly known as Reichardt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

The plant has bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture. It is characterized by its slender stems and is part of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
