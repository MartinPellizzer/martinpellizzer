Helianthemum × sulphureum f. roseum

Helianthemum × sulphureum f. roseum, commonly known as the sulphur rockrose, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, compact growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal or Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
