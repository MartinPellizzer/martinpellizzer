Hieracium onosmaceum

Hieracium onosmaceum, commonly known as the meadowhawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
