Hordeum vulgare var. hybernum

Hordeum vulgare var. hybernum, commonly known as Scottish barley, is a variant of the common barley species.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often branched inflorescence. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is adapted to cooler, moist environments. Its seeds are small and hardy, making them suitable for cultivation in marginal agricultural lands.

This variety is historically significant in traditional farming practices across parts of Europe.
