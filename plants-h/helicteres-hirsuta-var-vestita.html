Helicteres hirsuta var. vestita

Helicteres hirsuta var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet helicteres, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the fruits are capsule-like structures containing several seeds.

This species is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
