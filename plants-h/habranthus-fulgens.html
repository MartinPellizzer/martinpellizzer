Habranthus fulgens

Habranthus fulgens, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen.

Habranthus fulgens is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its showy blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
