Hesperis nana

Hesperis nana, commonly known as the dwarf sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters, making it a compact and suitable choice for rock gardens or borders. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in early summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis nana is hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, preferring full sun to partial shade.
