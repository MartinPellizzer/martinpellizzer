Hieracium subtenebrosum

Hieracium subtenebrosum, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

Hieracium subtenebrosum is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a unique addition to woodland gardens.
