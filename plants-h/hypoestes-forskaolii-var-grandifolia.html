Hypoestes forskaolii var. grandifolia

Hypoestes forskaolii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the rainbow plant or false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
