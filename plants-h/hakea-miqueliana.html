Hakea miqueliana

Hakea miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's hakea, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant features dense, needle-like leaves and produces striking yellow or orange flower spikes during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
