Hieracium inuloides subsp. durans

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

durans, commonly known as the perennial hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
