Hyssopus officinalis subsp. pilifer

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

pilifer, commonly known as hairy hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
