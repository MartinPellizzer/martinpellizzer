Hedypnois polymorpha subsp. rhagadioloides

Hedypnois polymorpha subsp.

rhagadioloides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats. Hedypnois polymorpha subsp.

rhagadioloides is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
