Hyptis comaroides

Hyptis comaroides, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
