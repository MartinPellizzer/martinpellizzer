Hieracium glaucinum subsp. glaucinum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

glaucinum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-gray coating. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually absent or reduced.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive inflorescence structure.
