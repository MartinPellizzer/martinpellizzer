Hepatica hepatica var. albiflora

Hepatica hepatica var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that emerge early in the spring. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or blue, depending on the variety. It thrives in woodland habitats with moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

Hepatica hepatica var. albiflora is known for its early blooming period and is often found in shady, forested areas.
