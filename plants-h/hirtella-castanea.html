Hirtella castanea

Hirtella castanea, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Hirtella castanea is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
