Heuchera glabra

Heuchera glabra, commonly known as smooth heatherleaf, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green, red, or purple in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring and early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
