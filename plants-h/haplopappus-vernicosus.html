Haplopappus vernicosus

Haplopappus vernicosus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive grayish-green color with prominent veins.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.
