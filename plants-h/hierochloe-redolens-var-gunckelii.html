Hierochloe redolens var. gunckelii

Hierochloe redolens var. gunckelii, commonly known as the fragrant hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, hair-like leaves that emit a subtle, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
