Hibiscus aneuthe

Hibiscus aneuthe, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus aneuthe is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
