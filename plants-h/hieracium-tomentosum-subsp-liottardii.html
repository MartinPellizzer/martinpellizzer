Hieracium tomentosum subsp. liottardii

Hieracium tomentosum subsp.

liottardii, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are covered in dense white or gray pubescence.

The subspecies liottardii is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the species.
