Haitiella munizii

Haitiella munizii, commonly known as the Haitian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Haiti.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous structure.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a critically endangered species.
