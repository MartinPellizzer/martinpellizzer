Homalanthus collinus

Homalanthus collinus, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
