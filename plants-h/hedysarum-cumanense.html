Hedysarum cumanense

Hedysarum cumanense, commonly known as the Cumanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

Hedysarum cumanense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
