Hedysarum boveanum

Hedysarum boveanum, commonly known as the Bovean sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
