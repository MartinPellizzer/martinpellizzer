Heliocarpus occidentalis

Heliocarpus occidentalis, commonly known as the western sun-fruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Heliocarpus occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
