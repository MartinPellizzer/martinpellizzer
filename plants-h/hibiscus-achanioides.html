Hibiscus achanioides

Hibiscus achanioides, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Hibiscus achanioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
