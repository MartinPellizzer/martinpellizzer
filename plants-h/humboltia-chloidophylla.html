Humboltia chloidophylla

Humboltia chloidophylla, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its leaves, which are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting various pollinators. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities for its purported healing properties.

Humboltia chloidophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
