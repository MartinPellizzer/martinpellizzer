Hieracium pilosifurcum

Hieracium pilosifurcum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some habitats.
