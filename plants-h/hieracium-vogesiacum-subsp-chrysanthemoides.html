Hieracium vogesiacum subsp. chrysanthemoides

Hieracium vogesiacum subsp.

chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its yellow flowers, which resemble chrysanthemums, bloom from June to September, and the plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
