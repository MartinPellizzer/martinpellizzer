Helianthemum cinereum subsp. cinereum

Helianthemum cinereum subsp.

cinereum, commonly known as the silver thyme or woolly rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
