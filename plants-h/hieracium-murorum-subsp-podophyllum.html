Hieracium murorum subsp. podophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

podophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain habitats.
