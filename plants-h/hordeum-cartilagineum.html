Hordeum cartilagineum

Hordeum cartilagineum, commonly known as the rock barley, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of hairs.

Hordeum cartilagineum is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats and is considered a pioneer species in certain ecosystems.
