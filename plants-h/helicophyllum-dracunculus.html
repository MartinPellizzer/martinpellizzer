Helicophyllum dracunculus

Helicophyllum dracunculus, commonly known as the dragon's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features a distinctive spiral arrangement of its leaves, which gives it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Helicophyllum dracunculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
