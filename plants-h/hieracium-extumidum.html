Hieracium extumidum

Hieracium extumidum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and rosette-like leaves, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Hieracium extumidum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed soils.
