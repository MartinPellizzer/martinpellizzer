Hieracium pilosella subsp. serpens

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

serpens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant features a sprawling growth habit with creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative propagation.
