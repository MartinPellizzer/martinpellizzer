Hopea pachycarpa

Hopea pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited hopea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and dense, fibrous bark. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by woody, thick-shelled fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and is valued for its timber and ecological role.

Hopea pachycarpa is also important for biodiversity, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
