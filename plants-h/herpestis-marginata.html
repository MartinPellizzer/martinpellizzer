Herpestis marginata

Herpestis marginata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to curl at the edges.

Herpestis marginata thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds or streams.
