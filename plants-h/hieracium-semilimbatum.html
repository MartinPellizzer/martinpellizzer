Hieracium semilimbatum

Hieracium semilimbatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive notched or lobed appearance, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, daisy-like cluster.

Hieracium semilimbatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
