Hieracium sparsiflorum var. multifolium

Hieracium sparsiflorum var. multifolium, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and a rosette of basal leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy.

It is a perennial species that produces seeds through wind dispersal, contributing to its widespread distribution.
