Hieracium adenothales

Hieracium adenothales, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green bracts.

Hieracium adenothales is typically found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
