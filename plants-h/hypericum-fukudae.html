Hypericum fukudae

Hypericum fukudae, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow with prominent yellow stamens, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

Hypericum fukudae is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
