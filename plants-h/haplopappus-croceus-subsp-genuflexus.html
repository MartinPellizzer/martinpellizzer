Haplopappus croceus subsp. genuflexus

Haplopappus croceus subsp.

genuflexus, commonly known as the bent-stemmed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its drooping stems and smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other members of the species.
