Hieracium halsicum

Hieracium halsicum, commonly known as the Halsic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium halsicum is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
