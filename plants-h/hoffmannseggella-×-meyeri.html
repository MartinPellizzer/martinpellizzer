Hoffmannseggella × meyeri

Hoffmannseggella × meyeri, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between *Pyrus* and *Crataegus* species.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent genera, featuring thorny stems and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with serrated edges, while its fruits are small, fleshy pomes.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
