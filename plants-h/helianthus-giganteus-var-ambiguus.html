Helianthus giganteus var. ambiguus

Helianthus giganteus var. ambiguus, commonly known as the giant sunflower, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 8 feet high, supporting its expansive foliage. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators.
