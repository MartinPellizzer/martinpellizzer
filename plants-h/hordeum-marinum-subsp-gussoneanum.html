Hordeum marinum subsp. gussoneanum

Hordeum marinum subsp.

gussoneanum, commonly known as the coastal barley, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in saline or coastal environments, showing tolerance to salt and wind exposure. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, beige to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation to harsh, coastal conditions.
