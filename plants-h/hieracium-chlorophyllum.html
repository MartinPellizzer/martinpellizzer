Hieracium chlorophyllum

Hieracium chlorophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Hieracium chlorophyllum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
