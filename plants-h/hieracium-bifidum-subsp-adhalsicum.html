Hieracium bifidum subsp. adhalsicum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

adhalsicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flower heads.

The leaves are typically narrow and sometimes lobed, and the plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
