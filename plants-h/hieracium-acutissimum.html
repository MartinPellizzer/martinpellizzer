Hieracium acutissimum

Hieracium acutissimum, commonly known as the acuteleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Hieracium acutissimum is often found in temperate regions and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
