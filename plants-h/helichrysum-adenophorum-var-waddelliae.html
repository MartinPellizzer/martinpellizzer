Helichrysum adenophorum var. waddelliae

Helichrysum adenophorum var. waddelliae, commonly known as the Waddell's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden inflorescences that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its long-lasting flowers.
