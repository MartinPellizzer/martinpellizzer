Hydrangea candida

Hydrangea candida, commonly known as the white hydrangea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the pale blossoms.

Hydrangea candida prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
