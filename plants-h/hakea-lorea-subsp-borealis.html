Hakea lorea subsp. borealis

Hakea lorea subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or conical inflorescences that bear small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to coastal environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
