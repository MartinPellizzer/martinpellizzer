Hermas capitata

Hermas capitata, commonly known as the helmet flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Hermas capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
