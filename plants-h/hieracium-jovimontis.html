Hieracium jovimontis

Hieracium jovimontis, commonly known as the Jovimontis hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. Native to the alpine regions of Central Europe, it thrives in rocky and gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant is typically found in mountainous areas, where it adapts to cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Its morphology reflects its ecological niche, with compact growth habits suited to prevent desiccation and herbivory.
