Hieracium triadanum subsp. epinephum

Hieracium triadanum subsp.

epinephum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
