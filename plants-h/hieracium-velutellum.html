Hieracium velutellum

Hieracium velutellum, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and meadows.

Due to its ornamental qualities, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
