Hyptis rivularis

Hyptis rivularis, commonly known as water hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as stream banks and marshy areas. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong, mint-like scent, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Hyptis rivularis is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
