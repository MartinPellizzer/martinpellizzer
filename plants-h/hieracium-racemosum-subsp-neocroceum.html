Hieracium racemosum subsp. neocroceum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

neocroceum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with rough or hairy surfaces. It produces a single flower head at the end of each stem, which is surrounded by phyllaries and consists of both ray and disk flowers.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
