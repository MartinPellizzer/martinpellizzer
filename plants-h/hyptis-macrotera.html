Hyptis macrotera

Hyptis macrotera, commonly known as the large-leaved hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis macrotera is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
