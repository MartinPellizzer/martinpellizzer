Hieracium cantalicum subsp. drazeticum

Hieracium cantalicum subsp.

drazeticum, commonly known as the Drazetic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from other members of the Hieracium genus.
