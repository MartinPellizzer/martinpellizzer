Helichrysum × lamarckii

Helichrysum × lamarckii, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its fluffy, golden-yellow flowers that resemble daisies, which remain intact for a long time after drying. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit.
