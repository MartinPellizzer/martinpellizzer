Hyparrhenia quinqueplex

Hyparrhenia quinqueplex, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Hyparrhenia quinqueplex is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and erosion control.
