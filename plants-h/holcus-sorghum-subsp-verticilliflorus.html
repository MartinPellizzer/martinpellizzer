Holcus sorghum subsp. verticilliflorus

Holcus sorghum subsp.

verticilliflorus, commonly known as the clustered-flower sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of tightly clustered spikelets.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense vegetation in wetlands and along water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and somewhat fibrous, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
