Hordeum juliae

Hordeum juliae, commonly known as the Julian barley, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. Hordeum juliae is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in studies related to plant adaptation and conservation.
