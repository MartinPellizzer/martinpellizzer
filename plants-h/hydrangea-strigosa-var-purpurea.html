Hydrangea strigosa var. purpurea

Hydrangea strigosa var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple serrated hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features large, showy inflorescences that range from white to deep purple, depending on soil pH. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its floral display can be manipulated through soil acidity, allowing gardeners to achieve various color variations.
