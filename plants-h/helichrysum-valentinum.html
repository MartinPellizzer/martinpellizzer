Helichrysum valentinum

Helichrysum valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine's Day flower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum valentinum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
