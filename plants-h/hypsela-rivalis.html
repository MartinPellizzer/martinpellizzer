Hypsela rivalis

Hypsela rivalis, commonly known as the rival's height, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a glossy appearance. Its native habitat includes tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
