Hygrophila rivularis

Hygrophila rivularis, commonly known as the river hygrophila, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as rivers and ponds. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. It produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are borne in clusters near the water's surface.

Hygrophila rivularis is often used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its aesthetic appeal.
