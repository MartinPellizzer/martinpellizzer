Hyptis collina

Hyptis collina, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a minty scent. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis collina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
