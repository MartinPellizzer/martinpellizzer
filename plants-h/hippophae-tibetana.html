Hippophae tibetana

Hippophae tibetana, commonly known as the Tibetan sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in arid and cold environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are small, oval-shaped, and have a dense covering of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential medicinal uses.
