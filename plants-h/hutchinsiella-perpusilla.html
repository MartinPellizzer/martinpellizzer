Hutchinsiella perpusilla

Hutchinsiella perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf hutchinsiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its tiny, clustered leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its compact growth form. It is often found in coastal regions and can thrive in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
