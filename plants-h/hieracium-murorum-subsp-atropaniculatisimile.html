Hieracium murorum subsp. atropaniculatisimile

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atropaniculatisimile, commonly known as the dark-pink hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering head composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of dark pink and yellow, with the outer florets being more prominently colored.

It is a relatively small plant, rarely exceeding 30 cm in height, and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.
