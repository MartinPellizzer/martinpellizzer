Helichrysum rupestre f. latifolium

Helichrysum rupestre f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf helichrysum, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of golden-yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.

The foliage and flowers are also used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.
