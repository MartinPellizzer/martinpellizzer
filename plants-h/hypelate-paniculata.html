Hypelate paniculata

Hypelate paniculata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that produce clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hypelate paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
