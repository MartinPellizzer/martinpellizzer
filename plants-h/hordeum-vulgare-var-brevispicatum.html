Hordeum vulgare var. brevispicatum

Hordeum vulgare var. brevispicatum, commonly known as dwarf barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its shorter and more compact inflorescence.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions and is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in various food and industrial applications. The plant typically grows to a height of around 60 to 100 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that bear the grains. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a panicle-like arrangement of spikelets.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to different soil conditions and its use in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
