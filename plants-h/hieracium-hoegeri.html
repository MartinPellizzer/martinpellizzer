Hieracium hoegeri

Hieracium hoegeri, commonly known as the Hoeger's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
