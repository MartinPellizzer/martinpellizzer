Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. pulmonarioides

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

pulmonarioides, commonly known as the lungwort hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant has basal leaves that are broadly lobed and hairy, with a rosette-like growth habit. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense, terminal clusters, forming a distinctive, rounded inflorescence.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy ray florets.
