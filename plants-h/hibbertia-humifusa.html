Hibbertia humifusa

Hibbertia humifusa, commonly known as the ground-hibbertia, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features woody stems that are often prostrate or decumbent, with small, hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hibbertia humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
