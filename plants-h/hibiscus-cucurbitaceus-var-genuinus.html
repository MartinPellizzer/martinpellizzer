Hibiscus cucurbitaceus var. genuinus

Hibiscus cucurbitaceus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in wetland or marshy environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
