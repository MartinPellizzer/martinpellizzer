Hieracium faurei subsp. limonicum

Hieracium faurei subsp.

limonicum, commonly known as the coastal hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along sea cliffs and dunes, thriving in salty, well-drained environments.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray, making it a resilient coastal plant.
