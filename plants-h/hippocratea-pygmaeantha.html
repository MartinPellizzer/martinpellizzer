Hippocratea pygmaeantha

Hippocratea pygmaeantha, commonly known as the dwarf hippocratea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
