Heliopsis lanceolata var. lanceolata

Heliopsis lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf heliopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
