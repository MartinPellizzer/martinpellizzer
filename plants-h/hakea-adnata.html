Hakea adnata

Hakea adnata, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which appear in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Hakea adnata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
