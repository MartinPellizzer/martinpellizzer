Houttuynia californica

Houttuynia californica, commonly known as wild betony, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia but widely cultivated in the United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Houttuynia californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
