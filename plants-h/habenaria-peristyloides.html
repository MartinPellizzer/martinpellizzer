Habenaria peristyloides

Habenaria peristyloides, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, spiraling inflorescences that bear numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often clustered and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

The species is noted for its intricate floral structure and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
