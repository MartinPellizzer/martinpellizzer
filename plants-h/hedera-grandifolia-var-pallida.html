Hedera grandifolia var. pallida

Hedera grandifolia var. pallida, commonly known as the white-veined ivy, is a variant of the English ivy species.

It is characterized by its pale green leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is a climbing evergreen that thrives in shaded environments, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage remains lush throughout the year, providing visual interest in both indoor and outdoor settings.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate moderate drought conditions contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
