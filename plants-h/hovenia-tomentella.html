Hovenia tomentella

Hovenia tomentella, commonly known as the hairy hovenia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, sour taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Hovenia tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
