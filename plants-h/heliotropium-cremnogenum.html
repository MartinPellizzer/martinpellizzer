Heliotropium cremnogenum

Heliotropium cremnogenum, commonly known as the rock heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a globe. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Heliotropium cremnogenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
