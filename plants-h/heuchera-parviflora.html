Heuchera parviflora

Heuchera parviflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera parviflora is also valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
