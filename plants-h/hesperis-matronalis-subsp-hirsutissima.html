Hesperis matronalis subsp. hirsutissima

Hesperis matronalis subsp.

hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
