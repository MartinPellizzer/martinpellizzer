Hypericum alpestre subsp. polygonifolium

Hypericum alpestre subsp.

polygonifolium, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
