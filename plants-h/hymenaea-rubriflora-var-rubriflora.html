Hymenaea rubriflora var. rubriflora

Hymenaea rubriflora var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and long, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces large, woody pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. Its flowers are bright red and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and artisanal crafts.
