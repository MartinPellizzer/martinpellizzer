Hieracium gracillimum

Hieracium gracillimum, commonly known as slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium gracillimum is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various environments.
