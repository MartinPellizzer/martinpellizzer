Hieracium vulgatum subsp. triviale

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

triviale, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear clusters of small, daisy-like flowers.

It is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
