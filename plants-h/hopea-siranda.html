Hopea siranda

Hopea siranda, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy, and durable wood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and dark, glossy bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture.

Hopea siranda is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
