Hesperis humilis

Hesperis humilis, commonly known as the humble sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming from late spring to early summer. Hesperis humilis is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
