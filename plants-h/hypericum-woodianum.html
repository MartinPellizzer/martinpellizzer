Hypericum woodianum

Hypericum woodianum, commonly known as the yellow St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Hypericum woodianum is a member of the Hypericaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
