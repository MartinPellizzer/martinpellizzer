Hibiscus gangeticus

Hibiscus gangeticus, commonly known as the Indian rose or lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring moist and sunny environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are thick and succulent.

Hibiscus gangeticus plays an important role in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
