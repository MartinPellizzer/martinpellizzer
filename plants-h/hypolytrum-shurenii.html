Hypolytrum shurenii

Hypolytrum shurenii, commonly known as the Shuren's spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like floral structure that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually a vibrant mix of red, orange, and yellow, with long, slender petals and sepals that resemble spider legs.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
