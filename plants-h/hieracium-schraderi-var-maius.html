Hieracium schraderi var. maius

Hieracium schraderi var. maius, commonly known as the greater meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in open, sunny environments.
