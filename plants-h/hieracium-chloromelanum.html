Hieracium chloromelanum

Hieracium chloromelanum, commonly known as the green-black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in meadows and woodland edges across Europe. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive bluish-green hue, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium chloromelanum is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
