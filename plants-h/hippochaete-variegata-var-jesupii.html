Hippochaete variegata var. jesupii

Hippochaete variegata var. jesupii, commonly known as the Jesup's variegated heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to coastal environments with salt spray. It is often found in dunes and other coastal habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil.

The variegated leaves of this species are a notable feature, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
