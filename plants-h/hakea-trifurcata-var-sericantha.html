Hakea trifurcata var. sericantha

Hakea trifurcata var. sericantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces cylindrical, woody fruiting structures called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Hakea trifurcata var. sericantha is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
