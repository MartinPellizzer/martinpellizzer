Hypolytrum viridinux

Hypolytrum viridinux, commonly known as the green false orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes. It is characterized by its unique morphology, which resembles that of true orchids but lacks the typical orchid scent.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
