Hieracium phlomoides subsp. bowlesianum

Hieracium phlomoides subsp.

bowlesianum, commonly known as the Bowles' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
