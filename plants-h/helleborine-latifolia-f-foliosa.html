Helleborine latifolia f. foliosa

Helleborine latifolia f. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a rare orchid species characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking, spurred flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often dark green and waxy, while the flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive, elongated spur.

This orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
