Haenkea villosa

Haenkea villosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed. The plant produces showy blue to purple bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Haenkea villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
