Hieracium gnaphalodes

Hieracium gnaphalodes, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium gnaphalodes is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
