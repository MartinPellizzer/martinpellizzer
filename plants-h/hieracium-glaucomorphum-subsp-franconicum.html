Hieracium glaucomorphum subsp. franconicum

Hieracium glaucomorphum subsp.

franconicum, commonly known as the Franconian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a glandular surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
