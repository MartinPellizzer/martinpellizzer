Homalanthus trivalvis

Homalanthus trivalvis, commonly known as the three-valved bladder nut, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open when mature, releasing the seeds. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in forest understories and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
