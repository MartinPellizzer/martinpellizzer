Helichrysum fulvum

Helichrysum fulvum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to retain color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum fulvum is also valued for its use in floral arrangements and traditional medicine.
