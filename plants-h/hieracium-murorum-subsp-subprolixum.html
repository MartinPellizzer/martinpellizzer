Hieracium murorum subsp. subprolixum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subprolixum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small, tubular florets arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer stem and more elongated leaves compared to other hawkweed species.
