Hieracium dentatum subsp. carinthicola

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

carinthicola, commonly known as the Carinthian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head.

Native to Central Europe, it thrives in grassy habitats and disturbed soils. The subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins and shorter flower stems compared to other members of the species.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the local flora diversity.
