Hybanthus latifolius

Hybanthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the family Hybanthus, characterized by its small, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, clustered in dense inflorescences, and attract various pollinators.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
