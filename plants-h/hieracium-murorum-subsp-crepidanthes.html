Hieracium murorum subsp. crepidanthes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

crepidanthes, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, yellowish to pale green composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
