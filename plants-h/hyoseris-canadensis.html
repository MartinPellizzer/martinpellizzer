Hyoseris canadensis

Hyoseris canadensis, commonly known as Canada ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Canada ironweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
