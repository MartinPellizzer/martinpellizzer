Habenaria gibbsiae

Habenaria gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to aid in nutrient absorption.

The flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.
