Helianthus hypargyreus

Helianthus hypargyreus, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Helianthus hypargyreus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
