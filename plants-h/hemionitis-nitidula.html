Hemionitis nitidula

Hemionitis nitidula, commonly known as the smooth hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that are often covered in a thin, waxy cuticle. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or forested areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis nitidula is considered a botanically significant and ecologically sensitive species.
