Haplopappus prunelloides

Haplopappus prunelloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Haplopappus prunelloides is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
