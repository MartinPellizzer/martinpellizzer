Hylocereus setaceus

Hylocereus setaceus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its nocturnal flowering habit makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in unique plant displays.
