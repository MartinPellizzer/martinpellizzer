Heracleum anatolicum

Heracleum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey and southern Europe. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hollow, ridged stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
