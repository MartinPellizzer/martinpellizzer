Hieracium nigrescens subsp. pseudotransiens

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

pseudotransiens, commonly known as the Transylvanian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
