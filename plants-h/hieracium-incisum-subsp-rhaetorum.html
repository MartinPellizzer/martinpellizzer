Hieracium incisum subsp. rhaetorum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

rhaetorum, commonly known as the Rhaetian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and rocky slopes.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its potential as an invasive species in certain regions.
