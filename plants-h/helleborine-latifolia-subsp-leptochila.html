Helleborine latifolia subsp. leptochila

Helleborine latifolia subsp.

leptochila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers with prominent markings.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the broader leaves of the main species, Helleborine latifolia.
