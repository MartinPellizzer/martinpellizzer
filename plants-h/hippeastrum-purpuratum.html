Hippeastrum purpuratum

Hippeastrum purpuratum, commonly known as the purple hurricane lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers with vibrant purple petals and a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that supports its dramatic floral display. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, emerging from a bulbous base that stores nutrients for future growth.

Hippeastrum purpuratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
