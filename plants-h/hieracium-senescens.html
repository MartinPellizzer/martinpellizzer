Hieracium senescens

Hieracium senescens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium senescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
