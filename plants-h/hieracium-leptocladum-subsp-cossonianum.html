Hieracium leptocladum subsp. cossonianum

Hieracium leptocladum subsp.

cossonianum, commonly known as the Cossonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
