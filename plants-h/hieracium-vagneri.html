Hieracium vagneri

Hieracium vagneri, commonly known as the Vagner's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, this species thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, forest edges, and along riverbanks.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
