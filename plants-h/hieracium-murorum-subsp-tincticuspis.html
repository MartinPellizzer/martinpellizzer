Hieracium murorum subsp. tincticuspis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

tincticuspis, commonly known as the purple-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its dark green to purple leaves that are typically hairy and lobed.

The flowers are yellow and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf coloration and is often found in calcareous soils.
