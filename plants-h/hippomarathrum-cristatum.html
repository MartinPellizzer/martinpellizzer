Hippomarathrum cristatum

Hippomarathrum cristatum, commonly known as the crested tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, scale-like, and arranged in a dense, mat-like structure.

The species is also known for its salt tolerance and is often used in coastal and reclaimed land restoration projects.
