Helianthemum atriplicifolium var. macrocalycinum

Helianthemum atriplicifolium var. macrocalycinum, commonly known as the large-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

The variety macrocalycinum is distinguished by its larger leaves compared to the typical form of Helianthemum atriplicifolium.
