Hippeastrum × gladwynense

Hippeastrum × gladwynense, commonly known as the Gladwyn hybrid amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different species of the Hippeastrum genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically produces multiple blooms per stem, often in late winter or early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This species is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
