Hieracium chondrilloides

Hieracium chondrilloides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
