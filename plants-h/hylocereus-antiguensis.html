Hylocereus antiguensis

Hylocereus antiguensis, commonly known as the Antigua night bloom, is a climbing cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in warm, arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Hylocereus antiguensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering pattern.
