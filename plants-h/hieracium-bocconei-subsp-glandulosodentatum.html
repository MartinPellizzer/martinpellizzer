Hieracium bocconei subsp. glandulosodentatum

Hieracium bocconei subsp.

glandulosodentatum, commonly known as the glandular-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically glandular-dotted and have serrated margins, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in rocky or alpine habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
