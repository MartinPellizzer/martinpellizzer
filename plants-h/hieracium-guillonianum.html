Hieracium guillonianum

Hieracium guillonianum, commonly known as the Guillonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
