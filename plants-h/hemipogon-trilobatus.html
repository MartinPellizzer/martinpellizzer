Hemipogon trilobatus

Hemipogon trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along supports.

The flowers bloom during the day, opening in the morning and closing in the late afternoon.
