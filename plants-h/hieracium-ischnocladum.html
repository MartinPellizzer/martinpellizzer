Hieracium ischnocladum

Hieracium ischnocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
