Hibiscus mustiae

Hibiscus mustiae, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and a prominent, yellow-centered pistil. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Hibiscus mustiae blooms throughout the year, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
