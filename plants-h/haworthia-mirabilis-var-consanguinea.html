Haworthia mirabilis var. consanguinea

Haworthia mirabilis var. consanguinea, commonly known as the "Cape Rhabdo" or "Cape Rhabdophyllum," is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, pale green leaves with prominent white or pale markings. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
