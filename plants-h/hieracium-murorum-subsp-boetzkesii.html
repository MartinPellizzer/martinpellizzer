Hieracium murorum subsp. boetzkesii

Hieracium murorum subsp.

boetzkesii, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
