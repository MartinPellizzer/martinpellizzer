Hieracium catalanoalpinum

Hieracium catalanoalpinum, commonly known as the Catalano-Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

Hieracium catalanoalpinum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
