Hedeoma oblongifolia var. mexicana

Hedeoma oblongifolia var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican narrow-leaf hedeoma, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The leaves are elongated and narrow, often with a slightly serrated margin, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and ecological significance.
