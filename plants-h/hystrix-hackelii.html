Hystrix hackelii

Hystrix hackelii, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hystrix hackelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
