Heracleum caucasicum

Heracleum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and tall, umbrella-shaped inflorescences.

It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a hollow, ridged stem that is often hairy. Its small white or greenish-yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
