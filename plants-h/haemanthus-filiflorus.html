Haemanthus filiflorus

Haemanthus filiflorus, commonly known as the grass-leaved haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated by gardeners and botanists alike.
