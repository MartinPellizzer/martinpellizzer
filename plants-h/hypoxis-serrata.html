Hypoxis serrata

Hypoxis serrata, commonly known as the sawleaf false lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hypoxis serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
