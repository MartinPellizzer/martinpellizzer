Hyptis barbarensis

Hyptis barbarensis, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes.

Hyptis barbarensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
