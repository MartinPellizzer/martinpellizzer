Hyperbaena laurifolia

Hyperbaena laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hyperbaena, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are leathery and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry conditions, with a deep root system to access water.

Hyperbaena laurifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
