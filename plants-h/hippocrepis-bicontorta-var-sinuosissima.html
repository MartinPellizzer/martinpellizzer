Hippocrepis bicontorta var. sinuosissima

Hippocrepis bicontorta var. sinuosissima, commonly known as the sinuous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive two-lobed structure. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
