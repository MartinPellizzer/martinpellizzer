Hieracium umbrosum subsp. abietinum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

abietinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.
