Hookera douglasii

Hookera douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's hooker, is a rare and delicate alpine plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh weather.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
