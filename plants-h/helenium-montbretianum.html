Helenium montbretianum

Helenium montbretianum, commonly known as the Montbretian sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its showy yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

Helenium montbretianum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
