Hieracium aguilari

Hieracium aguilari, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is often found in mountain meadows and slopes at high altitudes.

Its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
