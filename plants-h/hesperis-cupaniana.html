Hesperis cupaniana

Hesperis cupaniana, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis cupaniana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
