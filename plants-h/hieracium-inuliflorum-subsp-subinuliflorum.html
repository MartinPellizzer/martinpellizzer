Hieracium inuliflorum subsp. subinuliflorum

Hieracium inuliflorum subsp.

subinuliflorum, commonly known as the lesser wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow with five petals. It produces small, oblong fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its preference for nutrient-rich soils.
