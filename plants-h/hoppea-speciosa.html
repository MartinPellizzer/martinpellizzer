Hoppea speciosa

Hoppea speciosa, commonly known as the golden hoppea, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
