Hieracium cirrulum

Hieracium cirrulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is an annual or biennial plant, often found in temperate regions.
