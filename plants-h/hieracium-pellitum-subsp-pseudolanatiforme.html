Hieracium pellitum subsp. pseudolanatiforme

Hieracium pellitum subsp.

pseudolanatiforme, commonly known as the pale-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, pale hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
