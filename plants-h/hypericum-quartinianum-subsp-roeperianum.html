Hypericum quartinianum subsp. roeperianum

Hypericum quartinianum subsp.

roeperianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features woody stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
