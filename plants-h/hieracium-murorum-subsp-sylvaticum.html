Hieracium murorum subsp. sylvaticum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sylvaticum, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
