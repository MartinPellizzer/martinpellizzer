Hieracium eurypholis

Hieracium eurypholis, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium eurypholis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
