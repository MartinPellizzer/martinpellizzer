Hypoxis pratensis

Hypoxis pratensis, commonly known as the meadow hypoxis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Hypoxis pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
