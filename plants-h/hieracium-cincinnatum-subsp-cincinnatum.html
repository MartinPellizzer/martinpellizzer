Hieracium cincinnatum subsp. cincinnatum

Hieracium cincinnatum subsp.

cincinnatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, terminal head. Its inflorescence is characterized by a dense cluster of disc flowers surrounded by a single row of ray florets.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
