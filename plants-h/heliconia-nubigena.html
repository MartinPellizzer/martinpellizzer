Heliconia nubigena

Heliconia nubigena, commonly known as the cloud heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a bird's nest. The plant features long, narrow, waxy leaves that form a rosette, providing structural support for its floral clusters. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a distinctive spadix surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Heliconia nubigena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
