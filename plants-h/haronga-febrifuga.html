Haronga febrifuga

Haronga febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported fever-reducing properties. Haronga febrifuga is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which contains compounds believed to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
