Hyssopus latilabiatus

Hyssopus latilabiatus, commonly known as the broad-lipped hyssop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Hyssopus latilabiatus is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
