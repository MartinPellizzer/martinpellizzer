Hyptis multiseta

Hyptis multiseta, commonly known as the hairy hyptis, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Hyptis multiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
