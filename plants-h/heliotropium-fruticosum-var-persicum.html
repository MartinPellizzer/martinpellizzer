Heliotropium fruticosum var. persicum

Heliotropium fruticosum var. persicum, commonly known as Persian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
