Hyptis mocinoana

Hyptis mocinoana, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy blue or purple flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.

Hyptis mocinoana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
