Hyptis caespitosa var. elliptica

Hyptis caespitosa var. elliptica, commonly known as curly top hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, elliptical leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both landscape design and traditional medicine.
