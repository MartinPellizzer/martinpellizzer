Hieracium vulgatum subsp. plurisinuatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

plurisinuatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its deeply lobed leaves and multiple sinuses, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its widespread distribution and adaptability, it is often considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
