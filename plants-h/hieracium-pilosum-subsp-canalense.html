Hieracium pilosum subsp. canalense

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

canalense, commonly known as the canalense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, herbaceous stems.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
