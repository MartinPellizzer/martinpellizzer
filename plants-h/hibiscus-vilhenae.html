Hibiscus vilhenae

Hibiscus vilhenae, commonly known as the Vilhena hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus vilhenae is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical regions.
