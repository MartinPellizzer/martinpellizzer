Hieracium incisum subsp. ciliatifolium

Hieracium incisum subsp.

ciliatifolium, commonly known as the ciliated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive ciliate margin, giving the species its subspecific name. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite inflorescence, which are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
