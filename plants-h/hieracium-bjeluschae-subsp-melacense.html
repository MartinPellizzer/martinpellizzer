Hieracium bjeluschae subsp. melacense

Hieracium bjeluschae subsp.

melacense, commonly known as the blackhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, while its leaves are lanceolate and have a rough texture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darkened flower heads and specific geographic distribution.
