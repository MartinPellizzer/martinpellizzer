Hieracium euryanthes

Hieracium euryanthes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow flowers, which bloom from June to August, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium euryanthes is often found in clusters, with hairy stems and serrated leaves that help it thrive in various environments.
