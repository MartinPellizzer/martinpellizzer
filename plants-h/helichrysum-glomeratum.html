Helichrysum glomeratum

Helichrysum glomeratum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered yellow flowers that resemble small suns, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are also used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.
