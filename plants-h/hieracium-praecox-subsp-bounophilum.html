Hieracium praecox subsp. bounophilum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

bounophilum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
