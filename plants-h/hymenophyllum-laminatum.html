Hymenophyllum laminatum

Hymenophyllum laminatum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are leathery and have a unique, almost metallic appearance due to the reflective surface.

Hymenophyllum laminatum plays an important role in forest ecosystems, contributing to moisture retention and providing habitat for microorganisms.
