Hieracium amphilobum

Hieracium amphilobum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium amphilobum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
