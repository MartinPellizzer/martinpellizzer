Hieracium subvulgatum

Hieracium subvulgatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium subvulgatum is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is considered a weed in some regions.
