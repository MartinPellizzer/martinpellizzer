Holcoglossum junceum

Holcoglossum junceum, commonly known as the golden humphead orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellowish-green pseudobulbs and its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a humpbacked head. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name, while its flowers bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
