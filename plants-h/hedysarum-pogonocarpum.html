Hedysarum pogonocarpum

Hedysarum pogonocarpum, commonly known as the beardtongue vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces long, spiny pods that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Hedysarum pogonocarpum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in certain environments.
