Hypericum virginicum var. tubulosum

Hypericum virginicum var. tubulosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
