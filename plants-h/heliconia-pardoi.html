Heliconia pardoi

Heliconia pardoi, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences composed of brightly colored bracts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Heliconia pardoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
