Haloragis exalata subsp. velutina

Haloragis exalata subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf starflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia. It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions or near freshwater sources. It has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and its leaves are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
