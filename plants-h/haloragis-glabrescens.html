Haloragis glabrescens

Haloragis glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth water star, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of wax. Its flowers are clustered in dense, star-shaped inflorescences, often with four to six petals.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
