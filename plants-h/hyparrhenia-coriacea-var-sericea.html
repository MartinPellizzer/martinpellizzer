Hyparrhenia coriacea var. sericea

Hyparrhenia coriacea var. sericea, commonly known as silky grass or silver grass, is a variant of the Hyparrhenia coriacea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This grass is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
