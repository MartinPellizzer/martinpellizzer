Hakea eucalyptoides

Hakea eucalyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces spikes of small, red or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark.

Hakea eucalyptoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
