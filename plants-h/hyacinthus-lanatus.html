Hyacinthus lanatus

Hyacinthus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white during late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hyacinthus lanatus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
