Hyptis albicoma

Hyptis albicoma, commonly known as white hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hyptis albicoma is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
