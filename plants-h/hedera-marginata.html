Hedera marginata

Hedera marginata, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow margin that contrasts with its dark green center. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a versatile and attractive plant for gardeners.
