Helianthemum rhiphaeum

Helianthemum rhiphaeum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are succulent and covered in fine hairs, which help it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Helianthemum rhiphaeum is often found growing on rocky slopes and in dry, sunny habitats.
