Hieracium calophyllum subsp. hercegovinicum

Hieracium calophyllum subsp.

hercegovinicum, commonly known as the Hercegovina hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas, and it is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
