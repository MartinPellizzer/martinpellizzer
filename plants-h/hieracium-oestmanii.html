Hieracium oestmanii

Hieracium oestmanii, commonly known as the Oestman's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a compact cluster. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
