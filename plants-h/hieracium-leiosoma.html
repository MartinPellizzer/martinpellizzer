Hieracium leiosoma

Hieracium leiosoma, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
