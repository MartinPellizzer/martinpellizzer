Hypochaeris rosengurttii var. pinnatifida

Hypochaeris rosengurttii var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, pinnately lobed leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

This variety is closely related to other species in the genus Hypochaeris, which are known for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
