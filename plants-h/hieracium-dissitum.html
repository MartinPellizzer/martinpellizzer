Hieracium dissitum

Hieracium dissitum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium dissitum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
