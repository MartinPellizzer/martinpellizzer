Heteropterys coriacea f. eglandulosa

Heteropterys coriacea f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandless hardy hibiscus, is a variant of the hardy hibiscus species characterized by its lack of glandular structures on the leaves.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is thick and leathery, providing a textured appearance, while its flowers are typically large and showy, with five to seven petals. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
