Hackelia scaberrima var. angustata

Hackelia scaberrima var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its stems are often hairy, and the flowers are arranged in dense, curling inflorescences.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
