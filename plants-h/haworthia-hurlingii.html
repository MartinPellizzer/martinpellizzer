Haworthia hurlingii

Haworthia hurlingii, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are typically pale green with distinctive white or light-colored markings, which help in reflecting sunlight. It produces small, white flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
