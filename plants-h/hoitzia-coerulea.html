Hoitzia coerulea

Hoitzia coerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Hoitzia coerulea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
