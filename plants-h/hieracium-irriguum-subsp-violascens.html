Hieracium irriguum subsp. violascens

Hieracium irriguum subsp.

violascens, commonly known as the violet leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with a violet or bluish tinge. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are borne on slender, hairy stems.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
