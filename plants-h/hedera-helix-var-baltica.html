Hedera helix var. baltica

Hedera helix var. baltica, commonly known as the Baltic ivy, is a variety of common ivy native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically broader and more elongated compared to other ivy varieties. This plant is a climbing vine that produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The leaves are evergreen, providing year-round foliage in gardens and landscapes.
