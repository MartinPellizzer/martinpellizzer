Hakea amplexicaulis

Hakea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces striking, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically appearing in spring. Hakea amplexicaulis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and arid landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native wildlife.
