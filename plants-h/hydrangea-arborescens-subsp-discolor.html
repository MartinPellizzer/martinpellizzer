Hydrangea arborescens subsp. discolor

Hydrangea arborescens subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can change color depending on soil pH, ranging from white to pink or even red.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broadly ovate and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is commonly used in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
