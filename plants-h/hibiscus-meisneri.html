Hibiscus meisneri

Hibiscus meisneri, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Hibiscus meisneri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
