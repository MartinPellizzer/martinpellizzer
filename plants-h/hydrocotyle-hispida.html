Hydrocotyle hispida

Hydrocotyle hispida, commonly known as the hairy pennywort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, round leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries as fruit.

Hydrocotyle hispida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a ground cover in suitable climates.
