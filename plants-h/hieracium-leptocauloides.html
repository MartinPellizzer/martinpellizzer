Hieracium leptocauloides

Hieracium leptocauloides, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has slender, erect stems and hairy leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
