Hieracium glaucinum var. serratense

Hieracium glaucinum var. serratense, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually serrated, giving it its common name, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
