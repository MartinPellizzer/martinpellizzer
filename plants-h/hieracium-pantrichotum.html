Hieracium pantrichotum

Hieracium pantrichotum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are typically narrow and hairy, with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attracts various pollinators.

Hieracium pantrichotum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
