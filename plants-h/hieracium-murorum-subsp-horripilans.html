Hieracium murorum subsp. horripilans

Hieracium murorum subsp.

horripilans, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes, allowing it to spread readily.

The plant is typically found in meadows, waste grounds, and along roadsides.
