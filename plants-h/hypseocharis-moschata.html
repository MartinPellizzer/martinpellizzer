Hypseocharis moschata

Hypseocharis moschata, commonly known as the musk lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Andes of South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a strong, musky scent. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several flowers clustered at the top. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in high-altitude environments with cold winters and cool summers.

Hypseocharis moschata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
