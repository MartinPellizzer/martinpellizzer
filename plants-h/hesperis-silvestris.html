Hesperis silvestris

Hesperis silvestris, commonly known as the woodland sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the mustard family.

Hesperis silvestris blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
