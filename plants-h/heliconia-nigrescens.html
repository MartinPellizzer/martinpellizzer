Heliconia nigrescens

Heliconia nigrescens, commonly known as the black-tinged heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous inflorescences that resemble a crown, with bright orange bracts and yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia nigrescens is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
