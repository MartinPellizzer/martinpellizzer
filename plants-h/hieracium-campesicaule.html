Hieracium campesicaule

Hieracium campesicaule, commonly known as the field dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its basal leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green color, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring.

The plant is known for its ability to spread via acaulescent stems and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
