Hieracium guldbergense

Hieracium guldbergense, commonly known as the Guldberg's hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a single head, often with a yellow-orange coloration.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
