Hibiscus indicus var. integrilobus

Hibiscus indicus var. integrilobus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a prominent yellow center. The leaves are ovate and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
