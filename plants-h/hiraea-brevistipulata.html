Hiraea brevistipulata

Hiraea brevistipulata, commonly known as the short-stippled hiraea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many acid-loving plants such as heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, leathery leaves. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
