Homaida olivieri

Homaida olivieri, commonly known as the olive tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into drupes, or fruits, which are typically green when unripe and turn purple or black when mature. The fruit contains a single stone with a single seed, and the tree is known for its aromatic leaves and oil-rich fruit.

It is widely cultivated for its oil, which is used in cooking and cosmetics.
