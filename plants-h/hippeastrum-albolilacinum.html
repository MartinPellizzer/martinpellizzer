Hippeastrum albolilacinum

Hippeastrum albolilacinum, commonly known as the white-lilac amaryllis, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a subtle lilac hue, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and pointed, often reaching up to 30 centimeters in length. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with mild climates.
