Habenaria pectinata subsp. ensifolia

Habenaria pectinata subsp.

ensifolia, commonly known as the sword-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that resemble a sword, giving it its common name.

The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinct lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks. It typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
