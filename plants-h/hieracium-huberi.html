Hieracium huberi

Hieracium huberi, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough textures.

Hieracium huberi is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
