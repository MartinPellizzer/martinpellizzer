Hieracium eriosphaerophorum var. castillense

Hieracium eriosphaerophorum var. castillense, commonly known as the Castilian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering heads are surrounded by a dense, woolly layer of bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.
