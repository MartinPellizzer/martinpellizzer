Hybanthus hirtus var. klotzschii

Hybanthus hirtus var. klotzschii, commonly known as the hairy hybanthus, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The leaves are usually hairy, giving the plant its common name, and it often grows in shaded, moist environments. This variant is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, including the dense pubescence on its stems and leaves.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
