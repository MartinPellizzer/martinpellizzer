Hebe hectorii subsp. subsimilis

Hebe hectorii subsp.

subsimilis, commonly known as the Hector's hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which attract native pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
