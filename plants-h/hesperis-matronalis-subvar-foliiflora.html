Hesperis matronalis subvar. foliiflora

Hesperis matronalis subvar. foliiflora, commonly known as the leafy sweet William, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant features basal and stem leaves that are pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming from late spring to early summer.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
