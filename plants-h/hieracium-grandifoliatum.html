Hieracium grandifoliatum

Hieracium grandifoliatum, commonly known as the large-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium grandifoliatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
