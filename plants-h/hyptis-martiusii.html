Hyptis martiusii

Hyptis martiusii, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis martiusii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
