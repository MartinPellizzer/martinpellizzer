Hieracium fuscum subsp. fariniceps

Hieracium fuscum subsp.

fariniceps, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
