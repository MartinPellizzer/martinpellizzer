Hieracium inuloides subsp. subvirentiforme

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

subvirentiforme, commonly known as the green-leafed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
