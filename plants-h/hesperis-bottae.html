Hesperis bottae

Hesperis bottae, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved White Mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Hesperis bottae is known for its early spring blooming period and its role in supporting pollinators.
