Haumaniastrum linearifolium

Haumaniastrum linearifolium, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils and withstand periods of drought. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its linear, grass-like leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Haumaniastrum linearifolium is adapted to extreme heat and low water availability, making it a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
