Herorchis israelitica

Herorchis israelitica, commonly known as the Israeli orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a short, erect stem with a single or few leaves, and its flowers are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
