Hydrangea hortensis var. maculata

Hydrangea hortensis var. maculata, commonly known as the lacecap hydrangea, is a cultivar prized for its unique floral display.

It features large, showy clusters of sterile, lace-like flowers surrounded by smaller, fertile blooms. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is typically green with a reddish or pinkish tinge, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide seasonal color.
