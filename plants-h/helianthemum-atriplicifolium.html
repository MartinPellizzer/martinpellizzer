Helianthemum atriplicifolium

Helianthemum atriplicifolium, commonly known as the rock rose or narrow-leaved rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Helianthemum atriplicifolium is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate drought and salt spray.
