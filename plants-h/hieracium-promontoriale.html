Hieracium promontoriale

Hieracium promontoriale, commonly known as the promontory hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a distinctive yellowish or reddish hue.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
