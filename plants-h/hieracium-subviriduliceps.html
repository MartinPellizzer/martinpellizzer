Hieracium subviriduliceps

Hieracium subviriduliceps, commonly known as the green-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
