Heliopsis laevis var. gracilis

Heliopsis laevis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender heliopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
