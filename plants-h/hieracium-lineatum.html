Hieracium lineatum

Hieracium lineatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Hieracium lineatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense stands in suitable conditions.
