Hedysarum hamiltonii

Hedysarum hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a climbing habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum hamiltonii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
