Hibiscus wilsonii

Hibiscus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus wilsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
