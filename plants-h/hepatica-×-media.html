Hepatica × media

Hepatica × media, commonly known as the hybrid liverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to woodland areas in eastern North America.

It features distinctive trifoliate leaves that are often bluish-green with a waxy coating, and it blooms with delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in partial to full shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers appear in early spring, often before the leaves fully unfurl, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.

Hepatica × media is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in forest ecosystems.
