Hippeastrum bifidum var. spathaceum

Hippeastrum bifidum var. spathaceum, commonly known as the Spathecentrum, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive spatulate shape. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted for storing water. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in warm climates.
