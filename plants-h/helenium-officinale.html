Helenium officinale

Helenium officinale, commonly known as the American sunflower, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray flowers and yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helenium officinale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
