Hieracium magyaricum subsp. submagyaricum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

submagyaricum, commonly known as Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and shorter peduncles compared to other members of the species.
