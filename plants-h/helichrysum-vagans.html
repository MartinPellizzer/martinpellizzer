Helichrysum vagans

Helichrysum vagans, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Helichrysum vagans is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
