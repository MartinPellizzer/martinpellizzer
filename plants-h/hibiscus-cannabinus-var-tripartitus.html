Hibiscus cannabinus var. tripartitus

Hibiscus cannabinus var. tripartitus, commonly known as the three-parted hemp hibiscus, is a variant of the common hemp hibiscus.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three distinct lobes. It produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
