Hippeastrum pictum

Hippeastrum pictum, commonly known as the painted amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, emerging from a bulbous base. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, sturdy stems and can reach up to 3 feet in height.

Hippeastrum pictum is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
