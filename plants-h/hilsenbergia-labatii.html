Hilsenbergia labatii

Hilsenbergia labatii, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hilsenbergia labatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
