Hemigraphis parvifolia

Hemigraphis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved iron plant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or as a border plant.

Hemigraphis parvifolia is also valued for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to various soil types.
