Hippeastrum ambiguum var. tweedieanum

Hippeastrum ambiguum var. tweedieanum, commonly known as the Tweedie's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can be white, pink, or red, often with intricate patterns. The plant has strap-like, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
