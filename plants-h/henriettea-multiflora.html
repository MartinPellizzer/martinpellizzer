Henriettea multiflora

Henriettea multiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
