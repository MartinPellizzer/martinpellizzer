Hakea gordoniana

Hakea gordoniana, commonly known as Gordon's hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea gordoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
