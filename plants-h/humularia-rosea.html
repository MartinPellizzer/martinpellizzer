Humularia rosea

Humularia rosea, commonly known as the roseum bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value in garden ecosystems.
