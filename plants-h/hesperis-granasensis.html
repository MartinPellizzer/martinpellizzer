Hesperis granasensis

Hesperis granasensis, commonly known as the Granada horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with distinctive horn-shaped petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It produces small, round seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Hesperis granasensis is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
