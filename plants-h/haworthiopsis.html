Haworthiopsis

Haworthiopsis, commonly known as the African pine or dwarf ponytail plant, is a genus of succulent plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact, rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The leaves are typically fleshy and may display a variety of colors, including green, blue, or even red, depending on the species. Haworthiopsis species are generally low-maintenance, making them popular houseplants.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and require minimal watering, ideal for indoor cultivation.
