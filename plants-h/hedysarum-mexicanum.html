Hedysarum mexicanum

Hedysarum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum mexicanum is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native range.
