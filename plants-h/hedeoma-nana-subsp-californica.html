Hedeoma nana subsp. californica

Hedeoma nana subsp.

californica, commonly known as California dwarf horsemint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features hairy, aromatic leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a hardy addition to native gardens.
