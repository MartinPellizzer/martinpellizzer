Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp. calocerinthe

Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp.

calocerinthe, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

It is considered a rare and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
