Horsfieldia horrida

Horsfieldia horrida, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid rainforest environments. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Horsfieldia horrida plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native regions.
