Hieracium sommerfeltii var. lachnaeilepium

Hieracium sommerfeltii var. lachnaeilepium, commonly known as the Alpine Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellowish to reddish blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
