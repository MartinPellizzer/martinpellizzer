Helianthus aridus var. citrinus

Helianthus aridus var. citrinus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a variant of the desert sunflower native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring hairy leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. Its bright color and resilience make it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
