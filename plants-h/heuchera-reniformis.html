Heuchera reniformis

Heuchera reniformis, commonly known as the red earshade, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range in color from bronze to deep red. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera reniformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
