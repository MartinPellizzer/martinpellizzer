Heuchera richardsonii var. hispidior

Heuchera richardsonii var. hispidior, commonly known as the hairy leaf alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, basal foliage that ranges from bronze to red, with hairy textures on the leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in upright cymes. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.
