Hibiscus jaliscensis

Hibiscus jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and a prominent central stigma.

Hibiscus jaliscensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
