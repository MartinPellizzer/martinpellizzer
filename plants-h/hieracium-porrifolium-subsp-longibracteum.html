Hieracium porrifolium subsp. longibracteum

Hieracium porrifolium subsp.

longibracteum, commonly known as the long-bract hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are surrounded by several bracts. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

The species is known for its distinctive foliage and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.
