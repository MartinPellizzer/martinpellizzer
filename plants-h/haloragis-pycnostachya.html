Haloragis pycnostachya

Haloragis pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stalked willowherb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils near water bodies. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or red in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal and wetland soils and provides nectar for pollinators.
