Hedyotis parvifolia

Hedyotis parvifolia, commonly known as small-leaved dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
