Horsfieldia canariformis

Horsfieldia canariformis, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Horsfieldia canariformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
