Hyacinthus purpureus

Hyacinthus purpureus, commonly known as the purple hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh winters.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardens and have cultural significance in various traditions.
