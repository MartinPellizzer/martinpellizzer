Hedera canariensis var. latifolia-maculata

Hedera canariensis var. latifolia-maculata, commonly known as the Canary ivy, is a variant of the ivy plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to attach to surfaces and grow vertically. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
