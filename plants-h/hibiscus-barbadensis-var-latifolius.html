Hibiscus barbadensis var. latifolius

Hibiscus barbadensis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the warm season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
