Hieracium formigalense

Hieracium formigalense, commonly known as the Formigal hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
