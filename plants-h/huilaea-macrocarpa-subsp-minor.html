Huilaea macrocarpa subsp. minor

Huilaea macrocarpa subsp.

minor, commonly known as the small-fruited huilaea, is a member of the Polygonaceae family. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered, edible berries. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.
