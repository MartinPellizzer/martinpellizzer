Hieracium arolae subsp. feursteinii

Hieracium arolae subsp.

feursteinii, commonly known as the Feurstein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky and grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
