Helleborus × lemonnierae

Helleborus × lemonnierae, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are typically borne on short, sturdy stems.

Helleborus × lemonnierae is also valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
