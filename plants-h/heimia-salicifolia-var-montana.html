Heimia salicifolia var. montana

Heimia salicifolia var. montana, commonly known as mountain heimia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, somewhat aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
