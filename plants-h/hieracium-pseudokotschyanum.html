Hieracium pseudokotschyanum

Hieracium pseudokotschyanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Hieracium pseudokotschyanum is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
