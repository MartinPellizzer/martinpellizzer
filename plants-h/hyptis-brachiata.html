Hyptis brachiata

Hyptis brachiata, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis brachiata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
