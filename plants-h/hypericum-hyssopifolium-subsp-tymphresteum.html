Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp. tymphresteum

Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp.

tymphresteum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
