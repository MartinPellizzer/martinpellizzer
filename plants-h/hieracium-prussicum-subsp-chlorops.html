Hieracium prussicum subsp. chlorops

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

chlorops, commonly known as the Prussian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green flower heads and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in a single head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale coloration and compact growth habit compared to other Hieracium species.
