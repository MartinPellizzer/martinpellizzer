Hapalochilus xanthophaeus

Hapalochilus xanthophaeus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered daisy, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-centered daisies with white petals, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
