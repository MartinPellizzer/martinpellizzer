Hieracium calophyllum subsp. karaulanum

Hieracium calophyllum subsp.

karaulanum, commonly known as the Karaulan hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellowish to orange-colored flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This subspecies is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in grassy habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to plant biodiversity.
