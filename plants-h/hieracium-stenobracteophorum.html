Hieracium stenobracteophorum

Hieracium stenobracteophorum, commonly known as the narrow-bract hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are surrounded by a single, narrow bract.

It is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
