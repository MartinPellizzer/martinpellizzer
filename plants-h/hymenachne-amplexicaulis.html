Hymenachne amplexicaulis

Hymenachne amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often clasping around the stems, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Hymenachne amplexicaulis is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to flooded conditions.
