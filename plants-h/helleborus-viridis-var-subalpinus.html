Helleborus viridis var. subalpinus

Helleborus viridis var. subalpinus, commonly known as the Alpine green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers with prominent, veined petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or forested soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
