Hymenocallis caribaea var. cinerascens

Hymenocallis caribaea var. cinerascens, commonly known as the Caribbean white ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling orchids. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
