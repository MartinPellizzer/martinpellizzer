Hieracium × incrassatum

Hieracium × incrassatum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–80 cm, featuring rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, often with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium × incrassatum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its aggressive growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
