Hesperis ciliolata

Hesperis ciliolata, commonly known as the pink scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive central white stripe.

Hesperis ciliolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
