Hieracium incisum subsp. murrianum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

murrianum, commonly known as the Murrian's Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in various environments.
