Hieracium rapunculoides subsp. contractum

Hieracium rapunculoides subsp.

contractum, commonly known as the contracted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
