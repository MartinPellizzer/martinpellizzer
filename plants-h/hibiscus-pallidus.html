Hibiscus pallidus

Hibiscus pallidus, commonly known as the pale hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Hibiscus pallidus has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in riparian zones and is an important part of the local ecosystem.
