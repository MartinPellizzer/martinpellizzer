Hieracium caesium subsp. caesium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

caesium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear numerous small, daisy-like flowers.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
