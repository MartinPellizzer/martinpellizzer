Hippodamia

Hippodamia, commonly known as the ladybird beetle, is a type of beetle belonging to the family Coccinellidae.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange body with black spots, which serves as a warning to potential predators. Hippodamia species are often found on plants where they feed on aphids and other small insects, making them beneficial for plant health. The genus includes over 400 species, many of which are important in biological pest control.

Their botanical significance lies in their role as natural enemies of plant pests and their use in integrated pest management strategies.
