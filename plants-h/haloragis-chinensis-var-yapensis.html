Haloragis chinensis var. yapensis

Haloragis chinensis var. yapensis, commonly known as the Yap waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Yap.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments. The plant has elongated, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or pink flowers. It belongs to the family Haloragaceae and is adapted to saline or brackish water conditions.

This species plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
