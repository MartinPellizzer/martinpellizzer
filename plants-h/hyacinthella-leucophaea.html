Hyacinthella leucophaea

Hyacinthella leucophaea, commonly known as the white-flowered hyacinthella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
