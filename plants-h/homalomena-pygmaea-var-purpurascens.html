Homalomena pygmaea var. purpurascens

Homalomena pygmaea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-veined dwarf caladium, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that display vibrant shades of green, red, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, making it a compact and ornamental choice for indoor or shaded garden settings. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
