Hakea geraldtonica

Hakea geraldtonica, commonly known as Geraldton waratah, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red or pink, woody flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dense and provide a distinctive appearance. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Hakea geraldtonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the region's unique flora.
