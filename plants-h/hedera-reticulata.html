Hedera reticulata

Hedera reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined ivy, is a species of climbing plant belonging to the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. Native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments. Hedera reticulata produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
