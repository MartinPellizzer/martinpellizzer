Helleborus orientalis subsp. abschasicus

Helleborus orientalis subsp.

abschasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features dark green, waxy, evergreen leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern.

The plant produces striking, nodding flowers in shades of deep purple, pink, and white, often with intricate markings. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
