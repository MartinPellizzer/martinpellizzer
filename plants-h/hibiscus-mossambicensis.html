Hibiscus mossambicensis

Hibiscus mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus mossambicensis is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in humid climates and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
