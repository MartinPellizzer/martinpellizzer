Hieracium turritifolium subsp. scrofulosum

Hieracium turritifolium subsp.

scrofulosum, commonly known as the scrofulous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often deeply lobed and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and upright.

It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and growth habits.
