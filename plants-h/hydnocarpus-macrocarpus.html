Hydnocarpus macrocarpus

Hydnocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain several seeds surrounded by a fleshy, acidic pulp. It is valued for its timber, which is durable and resistant to termites, as well as for its medicinal properties.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
