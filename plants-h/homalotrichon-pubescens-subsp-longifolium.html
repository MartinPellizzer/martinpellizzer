Homalotrichon pubescens subsp. longifolium

Homalotrichon pubescens subsp.

longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hairgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive foliage. It typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
