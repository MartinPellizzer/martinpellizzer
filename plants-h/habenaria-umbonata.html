Habenaria umbonata

Habenaria umbonata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive hooded lip and vibrant colors, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid's floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
