Hydrolea spinosa var. glabra

Hydrolea spinosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth hydrolea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and smooth, glabrous leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
