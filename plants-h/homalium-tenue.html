Homalium tenue

Homalium tenue, commonly known as the slender homalium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
