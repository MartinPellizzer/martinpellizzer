Hedysarum setigerum

Hedysarum setigerum, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself on other vegetation or structures.

Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
