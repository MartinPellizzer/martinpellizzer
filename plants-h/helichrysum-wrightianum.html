Helichrysum wrightianum

Helichrysum wrightianum, commonly known as the Wright's immortelle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow to golden-orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in spherical or corymb-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum wrightianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
