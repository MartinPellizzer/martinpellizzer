Hypericum tomentosum subsp. pubescens

Hypericum tomentosum subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, typical of the Hypericum genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
