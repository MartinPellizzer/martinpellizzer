Hieracium × marianum

Hieracium × marianum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

It is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
