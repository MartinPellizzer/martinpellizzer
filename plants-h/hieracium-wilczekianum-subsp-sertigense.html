Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. sertigense

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

sertigense, commonly known as the Wilczek's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its habitat.
