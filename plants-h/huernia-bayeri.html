Huernia bayeri

Huernia bayeri, commonly known as the "Bayer's moon cactus," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, ribbed stems that resemble a moon's surface. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Huernia bayeri is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
