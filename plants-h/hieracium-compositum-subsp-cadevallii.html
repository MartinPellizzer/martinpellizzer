Hieracium compositum subsp. cadevallii

Hieracium compositum subsp.

cadevallii, commonly known as the Cadevall's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium, known for its ornamental and ecological significance.
