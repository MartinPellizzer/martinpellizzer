Hieracium schmidtii subsp. cochleariforme

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

cochleariforme, commonly known as the cochlear leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lanceolate to ovate, with a rough texture and a distinctive, sometimes hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in a compact cluster, which are surrounded by pale green or white bracts.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
