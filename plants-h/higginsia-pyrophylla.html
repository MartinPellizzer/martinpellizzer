Higginsia pyrophylla

Higginsia pyrophylla, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family native to South Africa.

This shrub is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating. Higginsia pyrophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native environment.
