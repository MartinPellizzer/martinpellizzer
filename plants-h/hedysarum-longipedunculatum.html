Hedysarum longipedunculatum

Hedysarum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long pedunculate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Hedysarum longipedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
