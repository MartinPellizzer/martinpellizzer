Hadrolaelia × adrienne

Hadrolaelia × adrienne, commonly known as the Adrienne's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Hadrolaelia and another orchid species.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences and showy, fragrant flowers that often display a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions with partial shade. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a rhizomatous root system, which help it adapt to its native habitat.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral characteristics.
