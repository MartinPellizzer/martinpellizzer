Hesperaloe funifera subsp. chiangii

Hesperaloe funifera subsp.

chiangii, commonly known as the Chiang Mai torch lily, is a succulent plant native to southern China. It features striking, red-brown, cylindrical flower spikes that emerge from a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It blooms in late spring or early summer, producing showy, lantern-shaped flowers.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in landscaping.
