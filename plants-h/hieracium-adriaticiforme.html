Hieracium adriaticiforme

Hieracium adriaticiforme, commonly known as the Adriatic hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to parts of Europe, including the Adriatic region. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Hieracium adriaticiforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
