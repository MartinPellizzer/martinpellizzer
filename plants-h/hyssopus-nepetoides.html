Hyssopus nepetoides

Hyssopus nepetoides, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that emit a fresh, lemony scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils are widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming properties.
