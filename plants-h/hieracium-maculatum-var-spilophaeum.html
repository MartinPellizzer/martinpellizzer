Hieracium maculatum var. spilophaeum

Hieracium maculatum var. spilophaeum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, reddish stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and often bear red or brown spots.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
