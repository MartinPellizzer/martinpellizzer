Hieracium machlochaetum

Hieracium machlochaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
