Hedeoma hyssopifolia

Hedeoma hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hedeoma hyssopifolia typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its foliage is aromatic and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
