Hemigyrosa canescens var. trichocarpa

Hemigyrosa canescens var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
