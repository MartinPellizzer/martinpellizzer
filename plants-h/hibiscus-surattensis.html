Hibiscus surattensis

Hibiscus surattensis, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow or orange flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and often have a serrated edge.

Hibiscus surattensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
