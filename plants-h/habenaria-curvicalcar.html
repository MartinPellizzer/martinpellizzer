Habenaria curvicalcar

Habenaria curvicalcar, commonly known as the curved spur orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, particularly the elongated, curved spur that houses the nectar. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and swampy areas. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
