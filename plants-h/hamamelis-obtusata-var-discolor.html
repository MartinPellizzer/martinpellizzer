Hamamelis obtusata var. discolor

Hamamelis obtusata var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features white and green markings on the leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers in late autumn, which are typical of the Hamamelis genus. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue, adding to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest throughout the year.
