Hieracium dichellum

Hieracium dichellum, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often thriving in sunny conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of spiny, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are hairy and often have a reddish tinge.

Hieracium dichellum is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to spread through seeds and vegetative reproduction.
