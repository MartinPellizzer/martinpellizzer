Hieracium lycopifolium subsp. pseudeminens

Hieracium lycopifolium subsp.

pseudeminens, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies pseudeminens is distinguished by its elongated, slender leaves and reduced flowering structure compared to other members of the species. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it adapts to cold and harsh climatic conditions.
