Hemiandra hirsuta

Hemiandra hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hemiandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Hemiandra hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
