Hypericum × dawsonianum

Hypericum × dawsonianum, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Hypericum patulum and Hypericum olympicum.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, often with a reddish hue on the underside.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
