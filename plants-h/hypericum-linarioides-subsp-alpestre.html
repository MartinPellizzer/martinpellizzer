Hypericum linarioides subsp. alpestre

Hypericum linarioides subsp.

alpestre, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
