Hyptis sancti-gabrielii

Hyptis sancti-gabrielii, commonly known as the sacred hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis sancti-gabrielii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
