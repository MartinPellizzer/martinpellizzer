Helwingia japonica var. parviflora

Helwingia japonica var. parviflora, commonly known as the Japanese helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and unique floral display.
