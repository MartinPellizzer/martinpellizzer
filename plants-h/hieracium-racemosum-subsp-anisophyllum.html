Hieracium racemosum subsp. anisophyllum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

anisophyllum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It features a caespitose growth habit with a central stem and multiple flowering stems.

Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a raceme.
