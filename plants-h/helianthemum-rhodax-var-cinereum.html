Helianthemum rhodax var. cinereum

Helianthemum rhodax var. cinereum, commonly known as the silver sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
