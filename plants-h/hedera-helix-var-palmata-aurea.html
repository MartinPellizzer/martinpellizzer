Hedera helix var. palmata-aurea

Hedera helix var. palmata-aurea, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of gold and green, giving it a striking appearance. It is a climbing plant that thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments, making it suitable for indoor or outdoor cultivation. The leaves are typically glossy and can grow up to several inches in size, adding ornamental value to gardens or interiors.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark berries, though it is primarily grown for its foliage rather than its blooms.
