Hieracium thegaleum

Hieracium thegaleum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a rough texture.

Hieracium thegaleum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
