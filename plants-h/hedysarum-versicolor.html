Hedysarum versicolor

Hedysarum versicolor, commonly known as the variable peavine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a trailing habit that makes it suitable for ground cover.

Hedysarum versicolor is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid environments.
