Hieracium murorum subsp. sparsidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sparsidens, commonly known as the sparseleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sparse leaf arrangement and smaller flower heads compared to other members of the species.
