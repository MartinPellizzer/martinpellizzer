Hamelia declinata

Hamelia declinata, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its long-lasting, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Hamelia declinata is also drought-tolerant once established, contributing to its adaptability in various climates.
