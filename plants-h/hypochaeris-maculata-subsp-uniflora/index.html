Hypochaeris Maculata Subsp. Uniflora

Yellow oxeye, scientifically known as Hypochaeris maculata subsp. uniflora, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive dark center, making it easily recognizable in natural habitats. This species often thrives in disturbed soils and open fields, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Despite its common presence, the yellow oxeye remains a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike due to its ecological significance and ornamental value.
