Hochreutinera amplexifolia

Hochreutinera amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping-leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often clasping around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Hochreutinera amplexifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
