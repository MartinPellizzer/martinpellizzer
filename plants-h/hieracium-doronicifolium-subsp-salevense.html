Hieracium doronicifolium subsp. salevense

Hieracium doronicifolium subsp.

salevense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, often with a woolly or hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in alpine meadows, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
