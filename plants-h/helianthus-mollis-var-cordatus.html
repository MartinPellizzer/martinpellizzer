Helianthus mollis var. cordatus

Helianthus mollis var. cordatus, commonly known as the soft sunflower or heartleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive brown center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
