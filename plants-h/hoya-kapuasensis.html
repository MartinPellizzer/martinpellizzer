Hoya kapuasensis

Hoya kapuasensis, commonly known as the Kapuas Woundwort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest undergrowth.

Hoya kapuasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
