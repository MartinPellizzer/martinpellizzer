Halimolobos diffusus

Halimolobos diffusus, commonly known as the creeping lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It forms a dense, carpet-like growth on tree bark, rocks, and soil surfaces. The plant's thallus is typically greenish-gray in color, with a leathery texture that aids in water retention. It contains symbiotic algae that provide nutrients through photosynthesis.

Halimolobos diffusus plays a crucial role in ecological succession by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms.
