Hyssopus officinalis var. cyaneus

Hyssopus officinalis var. cyaneus, commonly known as blue hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Blue hyssop is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional remedies.
