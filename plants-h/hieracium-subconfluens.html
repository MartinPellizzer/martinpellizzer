Hieracium subconfluens

Hieracium subconfluens, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium subconfluens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
