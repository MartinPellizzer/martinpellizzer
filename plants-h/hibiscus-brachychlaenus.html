Hibiscus brachychlaenus

Hibiscus brachychlaenus, commonly known as the narrowleaf hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. Hibiscus brachychlaenus typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
