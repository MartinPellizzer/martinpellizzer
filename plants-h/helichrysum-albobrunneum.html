Helichrysum albobrunneum

Helichrysum albobrunneum, commonly known as the white-brown everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish to white flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Helichrysum albobrunneum is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
