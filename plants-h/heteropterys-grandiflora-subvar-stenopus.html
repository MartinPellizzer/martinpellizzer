Heteropterys grandiflora subvar. stenopus

Heteropterys grandiflora subvar. stenopus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huito, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
