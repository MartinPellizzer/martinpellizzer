Homalolepis suaveolens

Homalolepis suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, aromatic fronds that emit a pleasant fragrance, especially when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and soothing aroma.
