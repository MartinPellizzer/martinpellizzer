Hieracium pseudintegratum

Hieracium pseudintegratum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an invasive species in some regions, often outcompeting native vegetation.
