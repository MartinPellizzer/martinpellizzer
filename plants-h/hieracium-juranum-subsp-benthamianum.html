Hieracium juranum subsp. benthamianum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

benthamianum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its presence in local flora.
