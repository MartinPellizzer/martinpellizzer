Hieracium atratum subsp. scotaiolepis

Hieracium atratum subsp.

scotaiolepis, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a rosette growth habit with basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The subspecies scotaiolepis is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and flowering patterns compared to other members of the species.
