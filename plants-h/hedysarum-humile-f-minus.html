Hedysarum humile f. minus

Hedysarum humile f. minus, commonly known as the dwarf sweet clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in spherical clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Hedysarum humile f. minus is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant with ornamental value in gardens.
