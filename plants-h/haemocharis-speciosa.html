Haemocharis speciosa

Haemocharis speciosa, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or orange throat. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Haemocharis speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
