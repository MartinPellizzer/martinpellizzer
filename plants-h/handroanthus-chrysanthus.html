Handroanthus chrysanthus

Handroanthus chrysanthus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and large, ovate leaves. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
