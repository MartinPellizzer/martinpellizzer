Hypericum phellos subsp. marcescens

Hypericum phellos subsp.

marcescens, commonly known as the yellow flowering St. John's wort, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and medicinal research.
