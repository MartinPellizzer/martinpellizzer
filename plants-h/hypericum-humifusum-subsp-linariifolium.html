Hypericum humifusum subsp. linariifolium

Hypericum humifusum subsp.

linariifolium, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features woody stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or cushions.

The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
