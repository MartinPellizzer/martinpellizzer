Hicorius alba var. albicans

Hicorius alba var. albicans, commonly known as the white lily variant, is a botanical variety characterized by its pale, almost white petals and stems.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is native to specific regions in Europe. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate and fragrant display.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
