Hieracium verlotii

Hieracium verlotii, commonly known as the Verlot's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium verlotii is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
