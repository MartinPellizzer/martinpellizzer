Hypericum mutilum var. minimum

Hypericum mutilum var. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and floral display.
