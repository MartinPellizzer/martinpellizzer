Heptapleurum eriocephalum

Heptapleurum eriocephalum, commonly known as the velvet-leaf heptapleurum, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Aextoxiconaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
