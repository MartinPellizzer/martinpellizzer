Hieracium sparsum subsp. sublubricicaule

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

sublubricicaule, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features erect, hairy stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head.

It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
