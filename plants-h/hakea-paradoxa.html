Hakea paradoxa

Hakea paradoxa, commonly known as the red hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical, red or orange-pink flower spikes that appear in late spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Hakea paradoxa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
