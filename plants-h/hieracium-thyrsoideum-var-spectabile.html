Hieracium thyrsoideum var. spectabile

Hieracium thyrsoideum var. spectabile, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow-orange flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it a notable wildflower in meadows and grasslands.

It is typically found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
