Helicteres cana subsp. cana

Helicteres cana subsp.

cana, commonly known as the redroot false holly, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptic to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white or yellowish, followed by indehiscent fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
