Hieracium velenovskyi subsp. chaetothyrsum

Hieracium velenovskyi subsp.

chaetothyrsum, commonly known as the velvety hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in its native range.
