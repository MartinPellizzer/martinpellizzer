Hibiscus diversifolius

Hibiscus diversifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. Hibiscus diversifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is a valued ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
