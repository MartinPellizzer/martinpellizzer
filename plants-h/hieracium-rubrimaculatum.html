Hieracium rubrimaculatum

Hieracium rubrimaculatum, commonly known as the red-spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with reddish markings.

The species is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction, making it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
