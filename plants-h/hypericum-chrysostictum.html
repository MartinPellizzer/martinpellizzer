Hypericum chrysostictum

Hypericum chrysostictum, commonly known as the golden-streaked St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers with distinctive orange stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces small, yellow-orange berries that are a food source for birds and small mammals.

Hypericum chrysostictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
