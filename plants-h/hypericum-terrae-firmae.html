Hypericum terrae-firmae

Hypericum terrae-firmae, commonly known as the firm land St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to specific regions with rocky, well-drained soils.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its stems are woody and tend to become more branched with age, contributing to its shrub-like structure.

Hypericum terrae-firmae is known for its resilience in harsh environments, making it a hardy addition to rocky gardens.
