Hordeum asperum

Hordeum asperum, commonly known as rough barley, is a wild grass species native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences, with bracts that are often rough or hairy. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are enclosed in a tough, inedible hull. Hordeum asperum is typically found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

It has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
