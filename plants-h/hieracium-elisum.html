Hieracium elisum

Hieracium elisum, commonly known as the Elis golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and form dense, flat clusters, while its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
