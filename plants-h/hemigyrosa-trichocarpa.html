Hemigyrosa trichocarpa

Hemigyrosa trichocarpa, commonly known as the western redcedar, is a coniferous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and flat, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hemigyrosa trichocarpa is valued for its durable, aromatic wood and its ability to thrive in moist, temperate climates.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and stabilizing soil in forest ecosystems.
