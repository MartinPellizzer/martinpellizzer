Hieracium aestivum subsp. arctogeton

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

arctogeton, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
