Hieracium umbellaticeps

Hieracium umbellaticeps, commonly known as the umbrella-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a tall, branched flowering stem that bears numerous yellowish flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers arranged in a loose, umbrella-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium umbellaticeps is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
