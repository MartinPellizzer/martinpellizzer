Hackelia brevicula

Hackelia brevicula, commonly known as the dwarf fairybells, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Hackelia brevicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
