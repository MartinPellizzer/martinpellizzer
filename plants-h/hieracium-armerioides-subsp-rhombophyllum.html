Hieracium armerioides subsp. rhombophyllum

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

rhombophyllum, commonly known as the rhomboid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate to rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
