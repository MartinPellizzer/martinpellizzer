Hyphaene guineensis

Hyphaene guineensis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates. Its roots form a dense network that helps prevent soil erosion.

Hyphaene guineensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
