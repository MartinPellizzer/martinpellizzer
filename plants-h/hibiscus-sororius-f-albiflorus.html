Hibiscus sororius f. albiflorus

Hibiscus sororius f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and a prominent yellow stamen, which make it visually striking. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting well to aquatic environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Hibiscus sororius f. albiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
