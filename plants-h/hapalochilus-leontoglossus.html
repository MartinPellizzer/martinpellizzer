Hapalochilus leontoglossus

Hapalochilus leontoglossus, commonly known as the lion's tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that resemble a lion's tongue, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Hapalochilus leontoglossus is noted for its unique morphology and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
