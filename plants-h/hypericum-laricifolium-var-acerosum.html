Hypericum laricifolium var. acerosum

Hypericum laricifolium var. acerosum, commonly known as the hardleaf St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, stiff, and often hairy leaves that resemble those of a fir tree. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coniferous forests.

This variety is distinguished by its more rigid foliage compared to other Hypericum species.
