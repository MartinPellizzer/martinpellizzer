Hibiscus tridactylites

Hibiscus tridactylites, commonly known as the three-toed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hibiscus tridactylites plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
