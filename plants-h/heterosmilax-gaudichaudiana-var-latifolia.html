Heterosmilax gaudichaudiana var. latifolia

Heterosmilax gaudichaudiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved smilacaceous vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces yellow, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure includes tendrils for climbing and a robust root system for nutrient absorption.
