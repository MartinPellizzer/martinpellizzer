Hieracium subedentatum

Hieracium subedentatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
