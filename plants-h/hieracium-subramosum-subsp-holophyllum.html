Hieracium subramosum subsp. holophyllum

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

holophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems that often form a low, bushy mound.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust and hairy leaves compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
