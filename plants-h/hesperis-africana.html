Hesperis africana

Hesperis africana, commonly known as the African whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and hairy, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Hesperis africana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
