Hibiscus mauritianus

Hibiscus mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus mauritianus thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and forests.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in its native region.
