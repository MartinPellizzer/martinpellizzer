Hydrangea arborescens var. deamii

Hydrangea arborescens var. deamii, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round, white or pink flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it ideal for woodland gardens. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
