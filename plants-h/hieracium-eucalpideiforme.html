Hieracium eucalpideiforme

Hieracium eucalpideiforme, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has rough, hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it can pose a challenge in ecological management.
