Hieracium intermarginatum

Hieracium intermarginatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered with fine hairs, while its bright yellow flowers bloom from June to August.

Hieracium intermarginatum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
