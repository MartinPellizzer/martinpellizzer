Hieracium araeochaetum

Hieracium araeochaetum, commonly known as the rough-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is often found in temperate regions and can be an indicator of nutrient-rich soils.
