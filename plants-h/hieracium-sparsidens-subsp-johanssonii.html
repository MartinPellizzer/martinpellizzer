Hieracium sparsidens subsp. johanssonii

Hieracium sparsidens subsp.

johanssonii, commonly known as the Swedish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges.

The plant features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it is characterized by its haploid chromosome number and is part of the Asteraceae family.
