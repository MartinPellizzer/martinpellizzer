Hippocrepis glandulosa

Hippocrepis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
