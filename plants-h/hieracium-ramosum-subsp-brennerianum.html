Hieracium ramosum subsp. brennerianum

Hieracium ramosum subsp.

brennerianum, commonly known as the Brenner's Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, dandelion-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
