Hieracium schlosseri

Hieracium schlosseri, commonly known as the Schlosser's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium schlosseri is often found in sunny, open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
