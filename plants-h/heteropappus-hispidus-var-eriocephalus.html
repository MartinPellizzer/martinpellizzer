Heteropappus hispidus var. eriocephalus

Heteropappus hispidus var. eriocephalus, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its fuzzy, grayish foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers make it visually distinctive.

It produces small, bristly pappus structures that aid in wind pollination and seed dispersal.
