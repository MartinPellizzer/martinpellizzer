Hypericum pulchrum var. filicaule

Hypericum pulchrum var. filicaule, commonly known as the fern-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
