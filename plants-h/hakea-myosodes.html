Hakea myosodes

Hakea myosodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, bottle-shaped red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its root system is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments.

Hakea myosodes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
