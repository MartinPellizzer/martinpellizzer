Hypericum nambuense

Hypericum nambuense, commonly known as the Nambu St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, orange-red berries that contain seeds, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological study.
