Hieracium caesium subsp. basifolium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

basifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, oblanceolate to ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are arranged in a compact inflorescence.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
