Hieracium austrobavaricum

Hieracium austrobavaricum, commonly known as the southern European hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
