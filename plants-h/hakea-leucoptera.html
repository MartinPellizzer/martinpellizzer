Hakea leucoptera

Hakea leucoptera, commonly known as the white-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea leucoptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
