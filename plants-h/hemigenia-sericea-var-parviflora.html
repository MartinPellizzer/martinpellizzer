Hemigenia sericea var. parviflora

Hemigenia sericea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sand dunes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
