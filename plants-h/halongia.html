Halongia

Halongia, commonly known as the mountain pepper or wild pepper, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Halongia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
