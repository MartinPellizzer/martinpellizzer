Helichrysum pamphylicum

Helichrysum pamphylicum, commonly known as the Pamphylian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in compact, rounded inflorescences, and the plant is known for its long-lasting, dried flowers that retain their color.

Helichrysum pamphylicum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
