Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. chavannense

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

chavannense, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and small, daisy-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its translucent, waxy leaves and reduced flower heads compared to other hawkweed species. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many regions.
