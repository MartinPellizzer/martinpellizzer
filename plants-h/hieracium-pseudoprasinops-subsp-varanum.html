Hieracium pseudoprasinops subsp. varanum

Hieracium pseudoprasinops subsp.

varanum, commonly known as the Varanum hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, narrow ligulate corolla.

This subspecies is known for its relatively small stature and is often found in low nutrient soils.
