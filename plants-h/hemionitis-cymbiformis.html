Hemionitis cymbiformis

Hemionitis cymbiformis, commonly known as the fan moss, is a small, delicate fern-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or near water sources. It has a unique reproductive structure known as a synangium, which contains spore-producing structures.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
