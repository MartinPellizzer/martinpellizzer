Hieracium albosignatum

Hieracium albosignatum, commonly known as the white-marked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a white-veined center.

Hieracium albosignatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
