Helleborine latifolia subsp. viridiflora

Helleborine latifolia subsp.

viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip, which is a common feature in orchids.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
