Hyptis rugosa var. villosissima

Hyptis rugosa var. villosissima, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and as a medicinal plant.
