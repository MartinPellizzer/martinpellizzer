Heliconia vellerigera

Heliconia vellerigera, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, and its flowers are clustered in a spiral arrangement. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
