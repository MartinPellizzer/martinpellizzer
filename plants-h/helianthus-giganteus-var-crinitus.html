Helianthus giganteus var. crinitus

Helianthus giganteus var. crinitus, commonly known as the giant sunflower with curly leaves, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and distinctive, curly, fern-like leaves. The plant can grow up to 6 feet tall, with a robust stem that supports its massive flower heads. Its botanical features include pinnately divided leaves and composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun with well-drained soil.
