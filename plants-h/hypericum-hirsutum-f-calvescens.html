Hypericum hirsutum f. calvescens

Hypericum hirsutum f. calvescens, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a botanical variant of the St. John's wort species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. It produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are often used in herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
