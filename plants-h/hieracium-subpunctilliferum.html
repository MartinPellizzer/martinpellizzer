Hieracium subpunctilliferum

Hieracium subpunctilliferum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subpunctilliferum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential invasive species in some regions.
