Haplopappus remyanus

Haplopappus remyanus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures, aiding in wind pollination. It is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Haplopappus remyanus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small insects in its native environment.
