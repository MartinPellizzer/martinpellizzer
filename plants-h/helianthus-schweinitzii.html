Helianthus schweinitzii

Helianthus schweinitzii, commonly known as Schweinitz's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schweinitz's sunflower is often found in open habitats like prairies and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
