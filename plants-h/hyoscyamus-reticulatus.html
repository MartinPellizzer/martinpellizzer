Hyoscyamus reticulatus

Hyoscyamus reticulatus, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, ridged stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
