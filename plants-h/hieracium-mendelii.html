Hieracium mendelii

Hieracium mendelii, commonly known as Mendel's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a perennial life cycle and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
