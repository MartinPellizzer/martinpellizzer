Hedyotis butterwickiae

Hedyotis butterwickiae, commonly known as the New Zealand silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy, silver-green appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Hedyotis butterwickiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
