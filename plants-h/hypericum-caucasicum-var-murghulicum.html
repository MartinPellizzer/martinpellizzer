Hypericum caucasicum var. murghulicum

Hypericum caucasicum var. murghulicum, commonly known as the Caucasian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and elliptical, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Hypericaceae family.
