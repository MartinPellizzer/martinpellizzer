Hybanthus longifolius

Hybanthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
