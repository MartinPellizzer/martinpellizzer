Hypolytrum pynaertii

Hypolytrum pynaertii, commonly known as the Pynaert's false orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
