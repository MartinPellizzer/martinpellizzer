Hygrophila ringens var. longihirsuta

Hygrophila ringens var. longihirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, pale yellow flowers. The variety longihirsuta is distinguished by its long, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

This plant is valued in aquascaping for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in aquatic settings.
