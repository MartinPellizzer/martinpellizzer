Hieracium caesium subsp. zabelianum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

zabelianum, commonly known as the Zabel's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and often endangered species in many regions.
