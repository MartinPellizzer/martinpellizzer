Helianthemum nummularium subsp. pyrenaicum

Helianthemum nummularium subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean sunflower. It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Pyrenees region, characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, rounded, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow, occurring in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sunny habitats, showing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
