Hieracium inuloides subsp. fistulosum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

fistulosum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
