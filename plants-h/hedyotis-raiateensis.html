Hedyotis raiateensis

Hedyotis raiateensis, commonly known as the Tahitian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands of French Polynesia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that grows in coastal and lowland areas, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season.

Hedyotis raiateensis is adapted to tropical climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
