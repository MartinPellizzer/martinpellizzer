Hieracium caesiogenum subsp. caesiogenum

Hieracium caesiogenum subsp.

caesiogenum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and flowering heads that produce pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
