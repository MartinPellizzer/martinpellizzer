Hoya samoensis

Hoya samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Hoya samoensis thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
