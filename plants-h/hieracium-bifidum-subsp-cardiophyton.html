Hieracium bifidum subsp. cardiophyton

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

cardiophyton, commonly known as the heartleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually lanceolate or ovate, with a rough texture and often have a distinct heart-shaped base in some subspecies.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
