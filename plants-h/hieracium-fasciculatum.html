Hieracium fasciculatum

Hieracium fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to August.

Hieracium fasciculatum is often found in sunny, open habitats and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
