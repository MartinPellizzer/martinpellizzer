Hypoestes involucrata

Hypoestes involucrata, commonly known as the "Magic Bean Plant," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble small flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Hypoestes involucrata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
