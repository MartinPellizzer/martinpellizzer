Heliconia indica var. dennisiana

Heliconia indica var. dennisiana, commonly known as the Dennisian heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that enclose the small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming clumps with broad, waxy leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
