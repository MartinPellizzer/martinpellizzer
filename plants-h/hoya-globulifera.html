Hoya globulifera

Hoya globulifera, commonly known as the globular waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Hoya globulifera is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
