Hyparrhenia variabilis

Hyparrhenia variabilis, commonly known as the variable blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a bladed appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Hyparrhenia variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
