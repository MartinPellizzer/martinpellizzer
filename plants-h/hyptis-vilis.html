Hyptis vilis

Hyptis vilis, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

Hyptis vilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
