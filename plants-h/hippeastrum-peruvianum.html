Hippeastrum peruvianum

Hippeastrum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, or pink. The plant produces strap-like, linear leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of cool dormancy to initiate flowering.

This species is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
