Hypericum tetrapterum var. boeticum

Hypericum tetrapterum var. boeticum, commonly known as the Scottish St John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with orange stamens and bright red berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
