Hieracium phaeolepioides

Hieracium phaeolepioides, commonly known as the pale-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant has serrated, alternate leaves that are usually pale green, with a rough texture. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium phaeolepioides is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its aggressive spreading habit.
