Heliotropium angustifolium

Heliotropium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Heliotropium angustifolium is also known for its attractive, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
