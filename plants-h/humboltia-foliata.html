Humboltia foliata

Humboltia foliata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves that are opposite and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by small, round berries. Humboltia foliata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
