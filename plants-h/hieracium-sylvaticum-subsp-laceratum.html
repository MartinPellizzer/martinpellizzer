Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. laceratum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

laceratum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and shaded areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and lobed, with a distinctive hairy texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is often found in association with other members of the Hieracium genus, sharing similar ecological preferences and floral structures.
