Hutchinsia pectinata var. densiflora

Hutchinsia pectinata var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered hutchinsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense arrangement of small, yellowish to orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
