Hypogynium spathiflorum

Hypogynium spathiflorum, commonly known as the spathiflorous hypogynium, is a unique botanical structure found in certain flowering plants.

It refers to the specialized region at the base of the pistil where the ovary is attached. This structure plays a crucial role in the reproductive process by facilitating pollination and seed development. The hypogynium is often surrounded by modified floral parts, such as bracts or nectar glands, which aid in attracting pollinators.

Its morphology can vary significantly among species, reflecting adaptations to different ecological niches.
