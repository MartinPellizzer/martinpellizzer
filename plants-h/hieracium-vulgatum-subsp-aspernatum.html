Hieracium vulgatum subsp. aspernatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

aspernatum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its subspecies name, *aspernatum*, refers to the rough texture of its leaves and stems.
