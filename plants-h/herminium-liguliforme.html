Herminium liguliforme

Herminium liguliforme, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and slender stems. The plant produces small, greenish or white flowers with a narrow lip, which is a distinguishing feature. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Herminium liguliforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
