Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. epiphaeum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

epiphaeum, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The plant is relatively small, with a height ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters.
