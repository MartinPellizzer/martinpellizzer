Hoarea nivea

Hoarea nivea, commonly known as the snowflake plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, white, star-shaped flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for snow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Hoarea nivea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
