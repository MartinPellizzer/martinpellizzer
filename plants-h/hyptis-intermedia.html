Hyptis intermedia

Hyptis intermedia, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
