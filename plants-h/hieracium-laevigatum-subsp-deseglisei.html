Hieracium laevigatum subsp. deseglisei

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

deseglisei, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
