Helicostylis affinis

Helicostylis affinis, commonly known as the red-flowered helicostylis, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces striking red flowers that attract pollinators such as beetles and flies.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
