Hydrocotyle novae-zeelandiae var. involucrata

Hydrocotyle novae-zeelandiae var. involucrata, commonly known as the New Zealand water shield, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant features small, floating or submerged leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
