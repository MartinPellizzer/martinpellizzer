Hutera cheiranthos

Hutera cheiranthos, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive five-petaled structure. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or forested habitats. Its leaves are usually broad and serrated, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hutera cheiranthos is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
