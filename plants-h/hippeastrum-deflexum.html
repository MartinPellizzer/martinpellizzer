Hippeastrum deflexum

Hippeastrum deflexum, commonly known as the flexed amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, requiring well-drained soil and moderate sunlight.

Hippeastrum deflexum is popular in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
