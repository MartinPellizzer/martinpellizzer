Hylophila orientalis

Hylophila orientalis, commonly known as the oriental water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that float on the water's surface. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients.

Hylophila orientalis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
