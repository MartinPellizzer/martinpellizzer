Hoheria populnea subsp. lanceolata

Hoheria populnea subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland areas. Its nectar-rich flowers attract native birds, particularly the tui and bellbird.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
