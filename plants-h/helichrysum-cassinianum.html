Helichrysum cassinianum

Helichrysum cassinianum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that remain open for an extended period, giving it a golden appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Helichrysum cassinianum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
