Heracleum courtallense

Heracleum courtallense, commonly known as the hill buttercup, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems.

It is native to parts of South and Southeast Asia, including India and Sri Lanka, where it thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences. Its roots contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities, requiring caution in its use.
