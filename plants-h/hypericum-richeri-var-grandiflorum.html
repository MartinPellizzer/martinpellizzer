Hypericum richeri var. grandiflorum

Hypericum richeri var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are often clustered in terminal inflorescences, adding a striking visual display.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
