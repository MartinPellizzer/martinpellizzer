Hibiscus physaloides var. genuinus

Hibiscus physaloides var. genuinus, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
