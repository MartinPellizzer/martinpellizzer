Hieracium phrixophyton

Hieracium phrixophyton, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium phrixophyton is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
