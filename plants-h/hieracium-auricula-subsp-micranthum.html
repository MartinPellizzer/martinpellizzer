Hieracium auricula subsp. micranthum

Hieracium auricula subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow to orange composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other hawkweed species. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
