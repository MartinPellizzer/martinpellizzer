Helianthemum marifolium subsp. andalusicum

Helianthemum marifolium subsp.

andalusicum, commonly known as the Andalusian rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Spain and northern Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
