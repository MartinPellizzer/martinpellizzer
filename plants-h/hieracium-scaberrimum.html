Hieracium scaberrimum

Hieracium scaberrimum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium scaberrimum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
