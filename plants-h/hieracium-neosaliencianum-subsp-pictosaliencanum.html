Hieracium neosaliencianum subsp. pictosaliencanum

Hieracium neosaliencianum subsp.

pictosaliencanum, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or reddish tinge.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
