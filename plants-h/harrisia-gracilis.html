Harrisia gracilis

Harrisia gracilis, commonly known as the slender harrisia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Harrisia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
