Haageocereus longiareolatus

Haageocereus longiareolatus, commonly known as the long-spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and clusters of small, nocturnal flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and they emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
