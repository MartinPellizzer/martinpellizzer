Helenium autumnale var. pumilum

Helenium autumnale var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces vibrant orange to red-orange ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, resembling small sunflowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
