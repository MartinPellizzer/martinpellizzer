Hieracium juranum subsp. olivaceum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

olivaceum, commonly known as the olive-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
