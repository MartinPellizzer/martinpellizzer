Heliconia berguidoi

Heliconia berguidoi, commonly known as the Yellow Heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that form a rosette around the central stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract.

Heliconia berguidoi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
