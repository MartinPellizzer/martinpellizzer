Hydrostachys imbricata var. thuarsiana

Hydrostachys imbricata var. thuarsiana, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in shallow waters or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
