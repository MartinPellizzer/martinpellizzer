Heliconia aristeguietae

Heliconia aristeguietae, commonly known as the Aristeguieta's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy, spiral-shaped inflorescences, which are often mistaken for actual flowers. Its leaves are broad and elongated, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its native environment.

Heliconia aristeguietae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing nectar for pollinators.
