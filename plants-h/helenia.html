Helenia

Helenia, commonly known as the "false hellebore," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or subshrubs with rough, often hairy stems. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a distinctive veining pattern. Helenia produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

These plants are native to regions of Europe and Asia and are often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
