Helichrysum vermiculatum

Helichrysum vermiculatum, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum vermiculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
