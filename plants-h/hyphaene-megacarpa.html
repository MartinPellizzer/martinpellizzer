Hyphaene megacarpa

Hyphaene megacarpa, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination. Its fruit is a large, woody capsule that contains a single seed, which is dispersed by animals.

The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access underground water sources.
