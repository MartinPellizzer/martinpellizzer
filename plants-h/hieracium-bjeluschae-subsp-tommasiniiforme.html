Hieracium bjeluschae subsp. tommasiniiforme

Hieracium bjeluschae subsp.

tommasiniiforme, commonly known as the Croatian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in forested or open habitats. It has lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, disc-like flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
