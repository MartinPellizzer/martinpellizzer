Hieracium maurocybe

Hieracium maurocybe, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish-purple ray florets. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium maurocybe thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
