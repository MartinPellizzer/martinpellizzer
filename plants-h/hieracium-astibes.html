Hieracium astibes

Hieracium astibes, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its showy inflorescences make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
