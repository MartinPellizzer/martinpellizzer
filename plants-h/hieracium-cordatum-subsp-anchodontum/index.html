Hieracium Cordatum Subsp. Anchodontum

Hieracium cordatum subsp. anchodontum is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its unique leaf shapes and delicate flowers. This subspecies is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in grassy meadows and rocky habitats. Its distinguishing features include heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish blooms that appear in late spring. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered plant. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this fascinating botanical specimen.
