Hibiscus punctatus

Hibiscus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus punctatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
