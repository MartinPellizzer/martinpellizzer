Haya

Haya, commonly known as the white cedar or *Toona sinensis*, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. Haya is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

It also has cultural and medicinal significance in traditional practices.
