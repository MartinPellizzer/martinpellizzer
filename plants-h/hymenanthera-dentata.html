Hymenanthera dentata

Hymenanthera dentata, commonly known as the serrated honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hymenanthera dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
