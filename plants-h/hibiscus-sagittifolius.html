Hibiscus sagittifolius

Hibiscus sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.

Hibiscus sagittifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
