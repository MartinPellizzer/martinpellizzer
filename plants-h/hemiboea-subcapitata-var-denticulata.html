Hemiboea subcapitata var. denticulata

Hemiboea subcapitata var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed hemiboea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins, giving it its specific epithet "denticulata.". Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
