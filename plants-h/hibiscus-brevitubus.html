Hibiscus brevitubus

Hibiscus brevitubus, commonly known as the short-stemmed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are short and often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus brevitubus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water sources.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
