Heliconia chrysocraspeda

Heliconia chrysocraspeda, commonly known as the golden-crimson heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within the vibrant bracts. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting, showy appearance.
