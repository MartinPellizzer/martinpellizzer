Hypoxis curculigoides var. schlechteri

Hypoxis curculigoides var. schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
