Hackelia nipponica

Hackelia nipponica, commonly known as Japanese fairybells, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hackelia nipponica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
