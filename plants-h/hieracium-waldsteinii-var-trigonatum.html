Hieracium waldsteinii var. trigonatum

Hieracium waldsteinii var. trigonatum, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the inflorescence consists of ray flowers surrounded by disk flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
