Hieracium murorum subsp. rhabdoides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

rhabdoides, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its stems are often reddish and covered with stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms.
