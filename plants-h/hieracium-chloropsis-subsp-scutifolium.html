Hieracium chloropsis subsp. scutifolium

Hieracium chloropsis subsp.

scutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat head.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, tolerating a range of soil conditions.
