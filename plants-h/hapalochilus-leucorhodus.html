Hapalochilus leucorhodus

Hapalochilus leucorhodus, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that display a unique combination of white and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

The orchid's flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
