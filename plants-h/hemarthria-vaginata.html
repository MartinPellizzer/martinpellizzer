Hemarthria vaginata

Hemarthria vaginata, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Hemarthria vaginata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ability to thrive in moist environments makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
