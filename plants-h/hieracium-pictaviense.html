Hieracium pictaviense

Hieracium pictaviense, commonly known as the Picardy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
