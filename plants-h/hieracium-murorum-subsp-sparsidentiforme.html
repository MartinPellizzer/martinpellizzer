Hieracium murorum subsp. sparsidentiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sparsidentiforme, commonly known as the sparse-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sparse, toothed leaf margins and shorter flower heads compared to other members of the species. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
