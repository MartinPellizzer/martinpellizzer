Haloragis racemosa var. baeuerlenii

Haloragis racemosa var. baeuerlenii, commonly known as the Baeuerlen's willowherb, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Haloragaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, such as along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other members of the species.
