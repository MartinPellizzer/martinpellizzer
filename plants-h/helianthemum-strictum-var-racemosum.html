Helianthemum strictum var. racemosum

Helianthemum strictum var. racemosum, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in xeriscaping.
