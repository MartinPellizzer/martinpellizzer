Hieracium maculatum subsp. arrectarium

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

arrectarium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
