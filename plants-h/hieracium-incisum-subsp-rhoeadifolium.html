Hieracium incisum subsp. rhoeadifolium

Hieracium incisum subsp.

rhoeadifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, with a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Its subspecific name, *rhoeadifolium*, refers to its leaf structure resembling that of the genus *Rhoeadium*.
