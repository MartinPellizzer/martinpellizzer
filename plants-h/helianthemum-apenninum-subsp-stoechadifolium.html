Helianthemum apenninum subsp. stoechadifolium

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

stoechadifolium, commonly known as the Scottish heath rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Scottish Highlands and parts of northern England. It features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
