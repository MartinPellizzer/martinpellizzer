Hedera poetarum var. taurica

Hedera poetarum var. taurica, commonly known as the Taurian ivy, is a variant of the common ivy species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found growing along rocky outcrops or in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Araliaceae family.
