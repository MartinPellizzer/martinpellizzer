Hieracium schmidtii subsp. marchettii

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

marchettii, commonly known as Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are erect and bear numerous small, daisy-like flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
