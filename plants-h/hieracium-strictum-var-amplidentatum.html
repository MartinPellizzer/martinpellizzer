Hieracium strictum var. amplidentatum

Hieracium strictum var. amplidentatum, commonly known as the broad-toothed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a robust stem that is usually unbranched and covered with stiff hairs. Its basal leaves are broadly lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
