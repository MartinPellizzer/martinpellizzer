Hymenophyllum junghuhnii

Hymenophyllum junghuhnii, commonly known as the Junghuhn's filmy fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in small, clustered structures called sorus.

Hymenophyllum junghuhnii plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
