Hoarea ciliata

Hoarea ciliata, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Hoarea ciliata is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native habitat.
