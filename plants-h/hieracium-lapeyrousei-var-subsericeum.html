Hieracium lapeyrousei var. subsericeum

Hieracium lapeyrousei var. subsericeum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are alternate, broadly ovate, and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
