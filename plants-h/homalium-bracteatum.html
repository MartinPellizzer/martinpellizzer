Homalium bracteatum

Homalium bracteatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
