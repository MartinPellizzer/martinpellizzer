Hexadesmia crurigera

Hexadesmia crurigera, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic flora. Its vines can climb or trail along the ground, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Hexadesmia crurigera plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small aquatic organisms.
