Hibiscus ricinifolius

Hibiscus ricinifolius, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink and orange. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. Hibiscus ricinifolius is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and tropical appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
