Hesperis jasa

Hesperis jasa, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Hesperis, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with flowers that are usually pink or violet in color. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of four-petaled flowers.

Hesperis jasa is native to the mountainous areas of Central Asia and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
