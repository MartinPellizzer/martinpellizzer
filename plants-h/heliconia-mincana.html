Heliconia mincana

Heliconia mincana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that consist of brightly colored bracts surrounding a cluster of small, true flowers. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are typically short and stout.

Heliconia mincana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
