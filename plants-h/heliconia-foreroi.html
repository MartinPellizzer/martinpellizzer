Heliconia foreroi

Heliconia foreroi, commonly known as the Forero's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces long, segmented inflorescences that attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention in its native habitat.

Heliconia foreroi is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in tropical gardens around the world.
