Homalachne caespitosa

Homalachne caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted homalachne, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant features small, clustered inflorescences with delicate, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense tufts at the base of the plant.

Homalachne caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
