Havardia acatlensis

Havardia acatlensis, commonly known as the Mexican trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a slender, often multi-branched trunk and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
