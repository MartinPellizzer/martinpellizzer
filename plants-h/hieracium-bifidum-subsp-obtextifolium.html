Hieracium bifidum subsp. obtextifolium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

obtextifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, arranged in a single terminal head. Its leaves are typically rough to the touch and have a distinctive, slightly lobed appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.
