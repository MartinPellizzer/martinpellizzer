Henckelia connata

Henckelia connata, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. Henckelia connata is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
