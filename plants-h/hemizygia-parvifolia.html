Hemizygia parvifolia

Hemizygia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hemizygia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments, such as the Atacama Desert. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are densely packed along its stems, reducing water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Hemizygia parvifolia is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
