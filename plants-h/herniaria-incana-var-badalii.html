Herniaria incana var. badalii

Herniaria incana var. badalii, commonly known as the white sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in arid and coastal environments.
