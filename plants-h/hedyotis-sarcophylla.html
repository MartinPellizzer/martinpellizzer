Hedyotis sarcophylla

Hedyotis sarcophylla, commonly known as the rock parsley, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Hedyotis sarcophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
