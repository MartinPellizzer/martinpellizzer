Hebe catarractae

Hebe catarractae, commonly known as the rock hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hebe catarractae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in crevices.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
