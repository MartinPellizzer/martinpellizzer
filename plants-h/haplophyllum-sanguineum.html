Haplophyllum sanguineum

Haplophyllum sanguineum, commonly known as the red-stemmed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Haplophyllum sanguineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens.
