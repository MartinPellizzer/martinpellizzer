Hieracium garganum

Hieracium garganum, commonly known as the Garganum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium garganum is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained soils.
