Hyacinthus pallasianus

Hyacinthus pallasianus, commonly known as the Pallasian hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Hyacinthus pallasianus is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
