Hedysarum humicubum

Hedysarum humicubum, commonly known as the black locust or sweet pea vine, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Hedysarum humicubum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
