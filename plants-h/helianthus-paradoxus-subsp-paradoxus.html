Helianthus paradoxus subsp. paradoxus

Helianthus paradoxus subsp.

paradoxus, commonly known as the paradox sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to moist environments.
