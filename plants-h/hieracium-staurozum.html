Hieracium staurozum

Hieracium staurozum, commonly known as the starry hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive star-like pattern.

Hieracium staurozum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
