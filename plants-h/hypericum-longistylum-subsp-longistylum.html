Hypericum longistylum subsp. longistylum

Hypericum longistylum subsp.

longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
