Hibiscus apodus

Hibiscus apodus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus apodus is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
