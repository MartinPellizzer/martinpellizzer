Heracleum transcaucasicum

Heracleum transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian cowparsnip, is a tall biennial or perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemistry.
