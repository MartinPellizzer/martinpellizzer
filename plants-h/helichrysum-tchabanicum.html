Helichrysum tchabanicum

Helichrysum tchabanicum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its golden-yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Helichrysum tchabanicum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
