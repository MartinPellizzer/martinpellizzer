Hieracium sparsum subsp. werneri

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

werneri, commonly known as the Werneri's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and serrated edges, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
