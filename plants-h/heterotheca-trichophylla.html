Heterotheca trichophylla

Heterotheca trichophylla, commonly known as the hairyleaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive conical shape. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Heterotheca trichophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
