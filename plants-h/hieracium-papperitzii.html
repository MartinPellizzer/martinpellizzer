Hieracium papperitzii

Hieracium papperitzii, commonly known as the Papperitz's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer, are arranged in a dense cluster and are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves.

Hieracium papperitzii is often found in mountain meadows and is a notable species within the genus Hieracium, which includes many other hawkweed species.
