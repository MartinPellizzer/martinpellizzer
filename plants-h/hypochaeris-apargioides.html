Hypochaeris apargioides

Hypochaeris apargioides, commonly known as the common false dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and other open areas. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is native to Europe but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
