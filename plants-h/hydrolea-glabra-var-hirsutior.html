Hydrolea glabra var. hirsutior

Hydrolea glabra var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy hydrolea, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
