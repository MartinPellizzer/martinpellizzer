Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudoplaceriforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudoplaceriforme, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a compact inflorescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated flower heads compared to other members of the species.
