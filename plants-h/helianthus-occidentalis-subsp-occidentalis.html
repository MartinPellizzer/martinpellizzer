Helianthus occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Helianthus occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features sturdy, upright stems with rough, hairy foliage that is typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces large, showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of smaller, tubular flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes and native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
