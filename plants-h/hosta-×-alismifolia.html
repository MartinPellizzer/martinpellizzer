Hosta × alismifolia

Hosta × alismifolia, commonly known as the waterleaf hosta, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Hosta plantaginea and Hosta sieboldii.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and yellow margins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is native to Japan and is cultivated for its attractive, textured leaves.
