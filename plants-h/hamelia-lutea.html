Hamelia lutea

Hamelia lutea, commonly known as the yellow flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hamelia lutea is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
