Hibiscus ambovombensis

Hibiscus ambovombensis, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton.

Hibiscus ambovombensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
