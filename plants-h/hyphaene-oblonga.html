Hyphaene oblonga

Hyphaene oblonga, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa and Namibia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important pioneer species in coastal ecosystems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits.
