Hieracium glaucum var. subglaucum

Hieracium glaucum var. subglaucum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems that are hairy and often reddish in color, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, and the plant produces small, dry fruits called cypselae.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
