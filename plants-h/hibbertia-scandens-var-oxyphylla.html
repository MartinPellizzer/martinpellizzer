Hibbertia scandens var. oxyphylla

Hibbertia scandens var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the red-stemmed hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its slender, often red-tinged stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive yellowish-green color with a rough texture. It produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
