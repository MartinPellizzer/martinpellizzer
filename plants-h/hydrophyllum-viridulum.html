Hydrophyllum viridulum

Hydrophyllum viridulum, commonly known as greenleaf hydrophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish to white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Hydrophyllum viridulum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
