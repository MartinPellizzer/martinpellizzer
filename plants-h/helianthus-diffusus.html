Helianthus diffusus

Helianthus diffusus, commonly known as the spreading sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Helianthus diffusus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
