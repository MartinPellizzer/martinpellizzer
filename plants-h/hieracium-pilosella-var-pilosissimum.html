Hieracium pilosella var. pilosissimum

Hieracium pilosella var. pilosissimum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
