Huperzia parvifolia

Huperzia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, and the spores are released from the upper surface of these structures.

Huperzia parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
