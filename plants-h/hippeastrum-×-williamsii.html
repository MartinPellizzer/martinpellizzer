Hippeastrum × williamsii

Hippeastrum × williamsii, commonly known as the Amazon lily or red hurricane, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that emerge from a bulb-like structure called a corm. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape during the cooler months.
