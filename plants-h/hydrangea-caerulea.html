Hydrangea caerulea

Hydrangea caerulea, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces sterile flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. Hydrangea caerulea prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates and its ornamental appeal throughout the growing season.
