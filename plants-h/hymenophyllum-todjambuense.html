Hymenophyllum todjambuense

Hymenophyllum todjambuense, commonly known as the Todjambu fern, is a species of moss-like fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or tree trunks in tropical regions. Its reproductive structures, called soridia, are borne on the underside of the leaves and are responsible for spore production.

Hymenophyllum todjambuense plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various invertebrates.
