Haberlea rhodopensis var. ferdinandi-coburgii

Haberlea rhodopensis var. ferdinandi-coburgii, commonly known as the Coburg's rockrose, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
