Hieracium longifolium subsp. briquetianum

Hieracium longifolium subsp.

briquetianum, commonly known as the Briquet's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange-pink flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and relatively small, clustered flower heads. It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, where it plays a role in local ecological systems.
