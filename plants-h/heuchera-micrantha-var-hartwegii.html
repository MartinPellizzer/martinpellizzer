Heuchera micrantha var. hartwegii

Heuchera micrantha var. hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy texture and a distinctive purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and native plant landscapes.
