Hedysarum albiflorum

Hedysarum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and rocky areas. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three leaflets, and it has slender, climbing stems.

Hedysarum albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
