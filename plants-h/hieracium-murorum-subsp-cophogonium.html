Hieracium murorum subsp. cophogonium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

cophogonium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in association with other early successional plant species.
