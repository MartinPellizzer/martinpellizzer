Hexastylis speciosa

Hexastylis speciosa, commonly known as the showy hooded tonguefern, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hooded spathes that enclose the inflorescence. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, green to reddish-brown spores in its sporangia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
