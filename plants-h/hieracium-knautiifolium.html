Hieracium knautiifolium

Hieracium knautiifolium, commonly known as Knautia's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or brown center.

It is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
