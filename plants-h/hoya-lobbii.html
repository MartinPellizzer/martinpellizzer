Hoya lobbii

Hoya lobbii, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Porcelain Flower," is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the summer months. Hoya lobbii is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored choice for indoor gardeners.
