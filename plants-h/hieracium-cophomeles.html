Hieracium cophomeles

Hieracium cophomeles, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium cophomeles is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
