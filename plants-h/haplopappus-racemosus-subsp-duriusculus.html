Haplopappus racemosus subsp. duriusculus

Haplopappus racemosus subsp.

duriusculus, commonly known as the rough pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme. Its most distinctive botanical feature is the rough, bristly pappus that surrounds its seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
