Hieracium norvegicum var. apoclitum

Hieracium norvegicum var. apoclitum, commonly known as the Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It features erect, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc.

The leaves are usually alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine pubescence.
