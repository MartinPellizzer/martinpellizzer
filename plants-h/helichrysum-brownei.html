Helichrysum brownei

Helichrysum brownei, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that retain their color even after drying. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum brownei is also valued for its ornamental use and has been used in traditional medicine.
