Heuchera sanguinea f. hirtella

Heuchera sanguinea f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy coral bells, is a decorative perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, velvety, dark red to purple foliage that is densely hairy, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces upright spikes of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, providing year-round visual interest.
