Hieracium murorum subsp. haegerstroemii

Hieracium murorum subsp.

haegerstroemii, commonly known as the Haegerstroem's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is often found in specific regions of Europe.
