Hieracium lanifolium

Hieracium lanifolium, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant is perennial and has a taproot system, allowing it to survive in various soil types.

Its hairy foliage and clustered inflorescence make it distinctive within the genus Hieracium.
