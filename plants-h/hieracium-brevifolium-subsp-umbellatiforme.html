Hieracium brevifolium subsp. umbellatiforme

Hieracium brevifolium subsp.

umbellatiforme, commonly known as the umbellate hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

The subspecies is distinguished by its umbellate arrangement of flower heads, which gives it its common name.
