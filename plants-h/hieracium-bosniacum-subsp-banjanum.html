Hieracium bosniacum subsp. banjanum

Hieracium bosniacum subsp.

banjanum, commonly known as the Bosnian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

It is considered a minor weed but plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
