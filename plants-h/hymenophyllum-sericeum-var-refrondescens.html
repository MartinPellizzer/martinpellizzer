Hymenophyllum sericeum var. refrondescens

Hymenophyllum sericeum var. refrondescens, commonly known as the silky polypore, is a fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. This variety is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in the study of epiphytic and lithophytic ferns.
