Hieracium leptolaephum

Hieracium leptolaephum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing, herbaceous habit. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
