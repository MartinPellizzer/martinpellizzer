Hieracium magyaricum subsp. amnoon

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

amnoon, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract various pollinators.

The subspecies amnoon is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and flowering time compared to other members of the species.
