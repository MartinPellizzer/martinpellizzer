Hesperis scabrida

Hesperis scabrida, commonly known as the rough-hesperis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar characteristics. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are covered in a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Hesperis scabrida is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
