Hieracium bifidum subsp. pourriacense

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

pourriacense, commonly known as the Corsican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
