Hydrangea mollis

Hydrangea mollis, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy clusters of flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces opposite, simple, and serrated leaves that are typically medium to dark green. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hydrangea mollis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide seasonal interest.
