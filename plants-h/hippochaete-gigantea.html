Hippochaete gigantea

Hippochaete gigantea, commonly known as the giant woolly plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, woolly, and often colorful inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a fluffy appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, spiky structure.

Hippochaete gigantea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique visual appeal in botanical gardens.
