Hieracium murorum subsp. punctidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

punctidens, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with basal leaves that are broadly ovate and toothed.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green bracts. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some regions.
