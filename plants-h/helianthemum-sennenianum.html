Helianthemum sennenianum

Helianthemum sennenianum, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has fleshy, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
