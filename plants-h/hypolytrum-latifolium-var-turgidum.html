Hypolytrum latifolium var. turgidum

Hypolytrum latifolium var. turgidum, commonly known as the broadleaf hypolytrum, is a variant of the hypolytrum genus native to specific regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
