Hieracium polybracteatum

Hieracium polybracteatum, commonly known as the many-bracted hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
