Heptapleurum tomentosum

Heptapleurum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly heptapleurum, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a velvety, grayish-white underside due to the presence of fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense, attractive foliage.
