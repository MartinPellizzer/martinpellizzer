Helianthemum thymiphyllum

Helianthemum thymiphyllum, commonly known as the sweet thyme shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic, resembling those of thyme, and are often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
