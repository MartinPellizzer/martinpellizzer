Heliciopsis incisa

Heliciopsis incisa, commonly known as the serrated evergreen hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Heliciopsis incisa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
