Hieracium tephranthelum

Hieracium tephranthelum, commonly known as the tephra hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, particularly in areas with volcanic soil, which is reflected in its common name. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium tephranthelum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in environments with low nutrient levels.
