Hippocratea ellipticarpa

Hippocratea ellipticarpa, commonly known as the ellipticarpa hippocratea, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits.

Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of forests. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical in shape, contributing to its common name. The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.
