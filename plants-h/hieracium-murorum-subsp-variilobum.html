Hieracium murorum subsp. variilobum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

variilobum, commonly known as the variable leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from lanceolate to oblanceolate. It typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats.

The plant has been studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in phytotherapy.
