Hieracium illyricum subsp. feliciense

Hieracium illyricum subsp.

feliciense, commonly known as the Illyrian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, often with a central disk.

It is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
