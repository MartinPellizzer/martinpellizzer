Hieracium staui

Hieracium staui, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium staui is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
