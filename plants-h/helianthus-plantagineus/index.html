Helianthus Plantagineus

Yellow sunflower, scientifically known as Helianthus plantagineus, is a vibrant and hardy member of the Asteraceae family. Native to the southeastern United States, this plant is celebrated for its striking golden blooms and resilience in various growing conditions. With its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, it is a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers alike. The plant's cheerful appearance and symbolic association with warmth and positivity make it a beloved addition to both wild and cultivated spaces. Its historical and cultural significance further enriches its appeal as a plant of both beauty and meaning.
