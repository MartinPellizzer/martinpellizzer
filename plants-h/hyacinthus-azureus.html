Hyacinthus azureus

Hyacinthus azureus, commonly known as the blue hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. Its botanical classification places it in the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants.

Hyacinthus azureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
