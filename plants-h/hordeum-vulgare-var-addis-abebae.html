Hordeum vulgare var. addis-abebae

Hordeum vulgare var. addis-abebae, commonly known as Ethiopian barley, is a variant of barley native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and resistance to drought and poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, hard grains that are typically used in local food preparations. It has a high genetic diversity, making it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving cereal crops.

Its botanical features reflect adaptations to the highland ecosystems of Ethiopia.
