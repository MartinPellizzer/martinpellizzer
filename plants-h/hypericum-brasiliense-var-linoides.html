Hypericum brasiliense var. linoides

Hypericum brasiliense var. linoides, commonly known as the Brazilian St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant has elliptical to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
