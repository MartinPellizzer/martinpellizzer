Haageocereus clavatus

Haageocereus clavatus, commonly known as the clustered night bloom cactus, is a species native to southern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical stems. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
