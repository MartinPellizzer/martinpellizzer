Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. prasinophyton

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

prasinophyton, commonly known as the green-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive ray floret structure. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and specialized reproductive structures.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker green foliage compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
