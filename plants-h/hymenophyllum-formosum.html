Hymenophyllum formosum

Hymenophyllum formosum, commonly known as the Formosan haircap moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely textured leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered with a layer of tiny, hair-like structures called rhizoids, which aid in water absorption.

Hymenophyllum formosum plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
