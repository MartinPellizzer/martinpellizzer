Huperzia lindeneri

Huperzia lindeneri, commonly known as the Lindner's clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form dense, moss-like clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can absorb sufficient humidity. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are borne on the upper part of the plant and produce spores for propagation.

Huperzia lindeneri is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
