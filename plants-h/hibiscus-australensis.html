Hibiscus australensis

Hibiscus australensis, commonly known as the New Zealand hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its flowers are followed by distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Hibiscus australensis is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
