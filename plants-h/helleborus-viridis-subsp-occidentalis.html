Helleborus viridis subsp. occidentalis

Helleborus viridis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western regions of the United States. It features dark green, leathery leaves that are usually heart-shaped and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow markings, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add early color to woodland gardens.
