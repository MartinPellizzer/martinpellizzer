Hedyotis globosa

Hedyotis globosa, commonly known as the globular geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features small, bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Hedyotis globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
