Habranthus splendens

Habranthus splendens, commonly known as the splendour bellflower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.

Habranthus splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
