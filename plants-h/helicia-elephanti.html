Helicia elephanti

Helicia elephanti, commonly known as the elephant's tusk tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

Helicia elephanti is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
