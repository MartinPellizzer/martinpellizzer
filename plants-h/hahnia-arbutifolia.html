Hahnia arbutifolia

Hahnia arbutifolia, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.

Hahnia arbutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid landscapes.
