Halanthium rarifolium var. belangeri

Halanthium rarifolium var. belangeri, commonly known as the Belanger's water clover, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, submerged leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, resembling the appearance of a clover. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually submerged, contributing to its limited visibility.
