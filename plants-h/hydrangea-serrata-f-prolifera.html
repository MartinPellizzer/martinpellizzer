Hydrangea serrata f. prolifera

Hydrangea serrata f. prolifera, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in late summer. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
