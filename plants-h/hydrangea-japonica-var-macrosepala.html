Hydrangea japonica var. macrosepala

Hydrangea japonica var. macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in partial shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
