Habenaria verdickii var. lindblomii

Habenaria verdickii var. lindblomii, commonly known as the Lindblom's marsh helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized mycorrhizal relationships with fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
