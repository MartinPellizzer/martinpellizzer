Helianthemum sanguineum subvar. erectipes

Helianthemum sanguineum subvar. erectipes, commonly known as the upright thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The subvariety erectipes is distinguished by its more upright growth habit compared to other varieties of Helianthemum sanguineum.
