Hedera helix var. marmorata

Hedera helix var. marmorata, commonly known as the marble evergreen ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which feature a mix of green, white, and cream colors, creating a marbled appearance. This plant thrives in partial shade to full shade and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and indoor settings.
