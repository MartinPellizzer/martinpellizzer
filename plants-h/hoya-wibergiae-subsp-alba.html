Hoya wibergiae subsp. alba

Hoya wibergiae subsp.

alba, commonly known as the White Wiberg Hoya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and oval, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight sheen.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive appearance. Hoya wibergiae subsp.

alba thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.
