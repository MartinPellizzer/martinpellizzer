Hymenophyllum flexile

Hymenophyllum flexile, commonly known as the flexile fern, is a small, delicate epiphytic fern native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It features intricate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety layer of trichomes. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves and are protected by a membranous indusium. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
