Hieracium sulphureum subsp. glogaviense

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

glogaviense, commonly known as the Glogau yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive orange-tinged center, and are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
