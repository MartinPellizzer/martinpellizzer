Haplophyllum dauricum

Haplophyllum dauricum, commonly known as Daurian haplophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or white, clustered in umbels, and bloom in late summer.
