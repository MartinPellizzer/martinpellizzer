Helichrysum cassiope

Helichrysum cassiope, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers are long-lasting and are used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and durability.
