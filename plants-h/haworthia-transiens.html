Haworthia transiens

Haworthia transiens, commonly known as the wandering haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
