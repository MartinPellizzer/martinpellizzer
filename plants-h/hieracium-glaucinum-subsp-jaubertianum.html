Hieracium glaucinum subsp. jaubertianum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

jaubertianum, commonly known as the Jaubert's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat diversity.
