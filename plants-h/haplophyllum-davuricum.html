Haplophyllum davuricum

Haplophyllum davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Haplophyllum davuricum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
