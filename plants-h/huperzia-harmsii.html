Huperzia harmsii

Huperzia harmsii, commonly known as the giant huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect, and often branched stems covered with dense, scale-like leaves. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its sporophylls are arranged in whorls and produce spores that are dispersed by wind.

Huperzia harmsii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
