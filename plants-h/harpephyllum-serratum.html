Harpephyllum serratum

Harpephyllum serratum, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found along the coastlines.

Harpephyllum serratum is also known for its rapid growth and ability to withstand salt spray, making it a hardy and resilient species.
