Heterotropa maxima

Heterotropa maxima, commonly known as the giant false flax, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to several meters in height, featuring large, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Heterotropa maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
