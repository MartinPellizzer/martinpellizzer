Hieracium sagittatum subsp. polyspilum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

polyspilum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lanceolate leaves that often have a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow, forming a single head with ray florets and a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its numerous small, spiky bracts beneath the flower head, giving it a distinctive appearance.
