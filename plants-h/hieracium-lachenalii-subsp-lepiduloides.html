Hieracium lachenalii subsp. lepiduloides

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

lepiduloides, commonly known as the Lachenal's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
