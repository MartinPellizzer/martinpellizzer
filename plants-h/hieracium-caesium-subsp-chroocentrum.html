Hieracium caesium subsp. chroocentrum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

chroocentrum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has basal leaves that are hairy and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed edges.

The subspecies chroocentrum is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure and coloration compared to other Hieracium species.
