Hemicarpha micrantha var. humboldtii

Hemicarpha micrantha var. humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native insects.
