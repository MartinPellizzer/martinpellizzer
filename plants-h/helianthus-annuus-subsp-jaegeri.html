Helianthus annuus subsp. jaegeri

Helianthus annuus subsp.

jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's sunflower, is a botanical variant of the common sunflower. It is native to central and eastern North America, where it thrives in a variety of soil types.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are sturdy and erect.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional practices.
