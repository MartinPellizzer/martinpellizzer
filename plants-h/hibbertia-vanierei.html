Hibbertia vanierei

Hibbertia vanierei, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. Hibbertia vanierei is part of the Dilleniaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
