Hieracium umbricola var. pinetorum

Hieracium umbricola var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to forested areas of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in pine and mixed woodlands. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are typically shorter than the disk florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
