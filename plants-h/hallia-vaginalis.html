Hallia vaginalis

Hallia vaginalis, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. Hallia vaginalis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
