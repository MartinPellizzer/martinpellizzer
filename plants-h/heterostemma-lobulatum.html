Heterostemma lobulatum

Heterostemma lobulatum, commonly known as the clustered globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a branched stem and capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Heterostemma lobulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
