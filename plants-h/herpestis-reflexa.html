Herpestis reflexa

Herpestis reflexa, commonly known as the trailing arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Herpestis reflexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
