Hemionitis germanotta

Hemionitis germanotta, commonly known as the Germanotta's hemionitis, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other primitive ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
