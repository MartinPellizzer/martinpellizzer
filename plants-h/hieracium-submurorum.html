Hieracium submurorum

Hieracium submurorum, commonly known as the wall groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found along walls and in dry habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

It is part of the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.
