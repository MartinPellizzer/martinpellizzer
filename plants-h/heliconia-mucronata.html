Heliconia mucronata

Heliconia mucronata, commonly known as the spiky heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, bract-like inflorescence that resembles a spike or a crown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements due to its vibrant appearance.
