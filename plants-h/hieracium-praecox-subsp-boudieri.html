Hieracium praecox subsp. boudieri

Hieracium praecox subsp.

boudieri, commonly known as the Boudier's early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions across the continent.
