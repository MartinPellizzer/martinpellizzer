Hieracium villosiceps subsp. faucillense

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

faucillense, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and as a component of diverse plant communities.
