Helianthus tuberosus var. subcanescens

Helianthus tuberosus var. subcanescens, commonly known as the curlytop sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in the summer. The plant is herbaceous, with a robust root system that can grow quite deep, allowing it to thrive in various soil conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible tubers.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
