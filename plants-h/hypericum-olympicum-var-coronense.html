Hypericum olympicum var. coronense

Hypericum olympicum var. coronense, commonly known as the Olympic St. John's Wort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant features opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
