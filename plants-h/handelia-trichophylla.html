Handelia trichophylla

Handelia trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
