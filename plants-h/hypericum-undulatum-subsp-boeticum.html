Hypericum undulatum subsp. boeticum

Hypericum undulatum subsp.

boeticum, commonly known as the wrinkled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often wrinkled or undulating, giving it its specific epithet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Its flowering period makes it a notable plant in Mediterranean flora.
