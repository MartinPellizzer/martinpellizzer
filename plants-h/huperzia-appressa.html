Huperzia appressa

Huperzia appressa, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a compact, low-growing structure with whorled leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Huperzia appressa reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its habitat.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
