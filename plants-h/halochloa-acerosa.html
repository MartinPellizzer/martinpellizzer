Halochloa acerosa

Halochloa acerosa, commonly known as the sharpleaf saltgrass, is a halophytic grass species adapted to saline environments.

It is native to coastal areas of Australia and thrives in soils with high salt content. The plant has narrow, rigid leaves with a prominent midrib, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Halochloa acerosa plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.
