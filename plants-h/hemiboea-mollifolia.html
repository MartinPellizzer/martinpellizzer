Hemiboea mollifolia

Hemiboea mollifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Hemiboea mollifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
