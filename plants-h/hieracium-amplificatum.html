Hieracium amplificatum

Hieracium amplificatum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a robust stem and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium amplificatum is known for its aggressive growth and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
