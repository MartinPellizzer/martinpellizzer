Hieracium juranum subsp. fontanalbae

Hieracium juranum subsp.

fontanalbae, commonly known as the Fontanalba hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the Western Mediterranean. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas with cool, moist conditions.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lanceolate in shape. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in subalpine and alpine meadows.
