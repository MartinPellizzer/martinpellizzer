Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. kalmutinum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

kalmutinum, commonly known as the Kalmutin hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils.

Its subspecific classification reflects its distinct geographical distribution and morphological traits compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
