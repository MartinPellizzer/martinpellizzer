Hieracium pinicola subsp. pandatum

Hieracium pinicola subsp.

pandatum, commonly known as the pine hill hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its subspecies is characterized by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
