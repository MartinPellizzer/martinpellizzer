Hohenbergia terminalis

Hohenbergia terminalis, commonly known as the silver stripe bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, typically in shades of red or orange. Its foliage is succulent, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Hohenbergia terminalis thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance.
