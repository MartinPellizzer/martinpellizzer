Hieracium triangulare subsp. subtriangulare

Hieracium triangulare subsp.

subtriangulare, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescence compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
