Hyssopus officinalis subsp. angustifolius

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
