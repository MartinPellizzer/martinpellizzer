Hieracium murorum subsp. argyropodum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

argyropodum, commonly known as the silverpod hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
