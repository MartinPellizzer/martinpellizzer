Helianthus tetragonus

Helianthus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angle sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive four-angled stems. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers with radiating ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Helianthus tetragonus is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
