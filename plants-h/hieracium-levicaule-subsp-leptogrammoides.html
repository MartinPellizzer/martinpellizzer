Hieracium levicaule subsp. leptogrammoides

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

leptogrammoides, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by pale green or white ray florets.

The species is characterized by its slender stem and distinctive leaf morphology, making it identifiable within the genus Hieracium.
