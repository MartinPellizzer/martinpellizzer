Hydnophytum petiolatum var. argentatum

Hydnophytum petiolatum var. argentatum, commonly known as the silver-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland rainforests.
