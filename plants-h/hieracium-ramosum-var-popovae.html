Hieracium ramosum var. popovae

Hieracium ramosum var. popovae, commonly known as the Popov's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family, and bloom in early summer.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
