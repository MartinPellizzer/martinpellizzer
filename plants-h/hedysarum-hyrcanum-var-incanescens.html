Hedysarum hyrcanum var. incanescens

Hedysarum hyrcanum var. incanescens, commonly known as the white bursa, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

This variety is characterized by its pale, almost white flowers and hairy stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.
