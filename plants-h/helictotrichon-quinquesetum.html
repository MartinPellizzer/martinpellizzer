Helictotrichon quinquesetum

Helictotrichon quinquesetum, commonly known as blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Helictotrichon quinquesetum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
