Hugonia trigyna

Hugonia trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed huckleberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Heiracium genus within the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in clusters. Hugonia trigyna produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
