Hemigenia pungens f. pedicellosa

Hemigenia pungens f. pedicellosa, commonly known as the pedicellate hemigenia, is a variant of the hemigenia pungens species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pedicellate (stemmed) flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The foliage is aromatic and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
