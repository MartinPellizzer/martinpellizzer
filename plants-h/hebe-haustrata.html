Hebe haustrata

Hebe haustrata, commonly known as the native hebe or mountain hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in the South Island. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Hebe haustrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
