Huperzia nesselii

Huperzia nesselii, commonly known as the Nessel's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its erect, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are found on the underside of its leaves.

Huperzia nesselii plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
