Hymenophyllum secundum

Hymenophyllum secundum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance and is typically found growing on shaded, moist surfaces such as tree trunks and rocks. The plant has small, overlapping leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Hymenophyllum secundum plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
