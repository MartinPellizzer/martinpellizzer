Hypericum caprifoliatum

Hypericum caprifoliatum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are prostrate, spreading along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hypericum caprifoliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
