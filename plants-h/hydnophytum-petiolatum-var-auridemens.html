Hydnophytum petiolatum var. auridemens

Hydnophytum petiolatum var. auridemens, commonly known as the golden leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, golden-yellow hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species in horticulture.

However, it is less commonly cultivated due to its specific care requirements and limited availability.
