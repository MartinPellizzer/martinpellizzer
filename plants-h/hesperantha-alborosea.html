Hesperantha alborosea

Hesperantha alborosea, commonly known as the white-rose lily, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features elegant, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive rose-like appearance. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperantha alborosea is well-suited for rock gardens and shady borders due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
