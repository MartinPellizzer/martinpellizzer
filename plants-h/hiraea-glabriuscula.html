Hiraea glabriuscula

Hiraea glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth hiraea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Hiraea glabriuscula is adapted to coastal environments, showing resistance to salt spray and strong winds.
