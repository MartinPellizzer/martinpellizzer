Hieracium dasyclados

Hieracium dasyclados, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
