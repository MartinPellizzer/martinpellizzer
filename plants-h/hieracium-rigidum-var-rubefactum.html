Hieracium rigidum var. rubefactum

Hieracium rigidum var. rubefactum, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are upright, rough, and have a distinct reddish tinge, especially along the veins.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
