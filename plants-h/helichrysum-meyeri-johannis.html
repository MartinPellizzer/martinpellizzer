Helichrysum meyeri-johannis

Helichrysum meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, papery bracts that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy habit with narrow, silver-green leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are long-lasting and often used in dried floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and texture.
