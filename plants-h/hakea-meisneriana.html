Hakea meisneriana

Hakea meisneriana, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green and covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers during the winter months, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Hakea meisneriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
