Hieracium subalbocinctum

Hieracium subalbocinctum, commonly known as the white-banded hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a white band near the base.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
