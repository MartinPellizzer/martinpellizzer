Hieracium calodes

Hieracium calodes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. The plant typically has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in meadows and grassy areas, often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Hieracium calodes is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
