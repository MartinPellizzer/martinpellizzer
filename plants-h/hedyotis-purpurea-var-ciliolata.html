Hedyotis purpurea var. ciliolata

Hedyotis purpurea var. ciliolata, commonly known as purple-backed bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with opposite, ovate leaves that have ciliate margins. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
