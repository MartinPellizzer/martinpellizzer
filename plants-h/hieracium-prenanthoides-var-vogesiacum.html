Hieracium prenanthoides var. vogesiacum

Hieracium prenanthoides var. vogesiacum, commonly known as the Voges hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in early summer. Its flowering heads are composed of many small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

It is a member of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure.
