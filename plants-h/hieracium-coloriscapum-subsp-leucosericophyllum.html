Hieracium coloriscapum subsp. leucosericophyllum

Hieracium coloriscapum subsp.

leucosericophyllum, commonly known as the white-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its subspecies name, *leucosericophyllum*, refers to its white, hairy appearance, which is a key identifying feature.
