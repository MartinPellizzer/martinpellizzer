Helichrysum sessile

Helichrysum sessile, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce small, yellowish-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is known for its long-lasting, dried flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum sessile is also valued for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance.
