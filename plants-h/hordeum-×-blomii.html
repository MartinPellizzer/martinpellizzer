Hordeum × blomii

Hordeum × blomii, commonly known as the hybrid barley, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Hordeum spontaneum and Hordeum vulgare.

It is a grass species characterized by its tall, slender stems and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hordeum × blomii is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
