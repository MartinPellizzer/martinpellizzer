Hyptis fasciculata

Hyptis fasciculata, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are square, a common feature in the Lamiaceae family.

Hyptis fasciculata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
