Hoya apoensis subsp. apoensis

Hoya apoensis subsp.

apoensis, commonly known as the Apo Hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant is a succulent, capable of storing water in its leaves and stems, which allows it to thrive in arid conditions. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
