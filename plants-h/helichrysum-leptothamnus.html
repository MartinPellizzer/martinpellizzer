Helichrysum leptothamnus

Helichrysum leptothamnus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage is typically gray-green, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and long-lasting flowers.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
