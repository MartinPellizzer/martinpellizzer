Hosta intermedia

Hosta intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant typically features clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Hosta intermedia prefers shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is particularly notable for its vibrant coloration, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns.
