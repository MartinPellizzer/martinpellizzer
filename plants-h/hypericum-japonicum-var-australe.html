Hypericum japonicum var. australe

Hypericum japonicum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
