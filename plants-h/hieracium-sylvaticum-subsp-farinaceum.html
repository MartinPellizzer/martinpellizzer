Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. farinaceum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

farinaceum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
