Hieracium murorum subsp. asaroides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

asaroides, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish to greenish composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped or kidney-shaped.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and can be an indicator species for certain soil conditions.
