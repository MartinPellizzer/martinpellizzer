Hylotelephium notarjanni

Hylotelephium notarjanni, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a perennial succulent native to the Caucasus region.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers in summer, arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
