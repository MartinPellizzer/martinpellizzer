Homalomena crassinervia

Homalomena crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of starch.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
