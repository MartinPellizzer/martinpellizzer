Habenaria ortiziana

Habenaria ortiziana, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals, often in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
