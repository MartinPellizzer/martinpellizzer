Hebonga mollis

Hebonga mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
