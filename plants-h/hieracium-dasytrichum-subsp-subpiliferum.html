Hieracium dasytrichum subsp. subpiliferum

Hieracium dasytrichum subsp.

subpiliferum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and numerous yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

The leaves are typically hairy and alternate, with a distinctive serrated margin. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
