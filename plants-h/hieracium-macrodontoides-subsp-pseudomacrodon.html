Hieracium macrodontoides subsp. pseudomacrodon

Hieracium macrodontoides subsp.

pseudomacrodon, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, with a serrated margin, and the flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
