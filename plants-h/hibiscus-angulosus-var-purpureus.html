Hibiscus angulosus var. purpureus

Hibiscus angulosus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purpleleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is adapted to well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hibiscus angulosus var. purpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
