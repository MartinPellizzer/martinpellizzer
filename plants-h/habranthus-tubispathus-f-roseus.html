Habranthus tubispathus f. roseus

Habranthus tubispathus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered habranthus, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
