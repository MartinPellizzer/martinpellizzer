Hibiscus syriacus

Hibiscus syriacus, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, or purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Hibiscus syriacus is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and its resilience to urban conditions.
