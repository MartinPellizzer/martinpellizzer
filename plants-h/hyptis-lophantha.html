Hyptis lophantha

Hyptis lophantha, commonly known as the mountain hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Hyptis lophantha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
