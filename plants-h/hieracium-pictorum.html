Hieracium pictorum

Hieracium pictorum, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of small, tubular florets. Its leaves are usually alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium pictorum thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.
