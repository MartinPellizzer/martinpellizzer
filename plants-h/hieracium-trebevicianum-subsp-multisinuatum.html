Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. multisinuatum

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

multisinuatum, commonly known as the multisinuate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, and it blooms from June to August.

It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
