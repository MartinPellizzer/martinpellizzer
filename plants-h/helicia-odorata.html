Helicia odorata

Helicia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant helicia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Helicia odorata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
