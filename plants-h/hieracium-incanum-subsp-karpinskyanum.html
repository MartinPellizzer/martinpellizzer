Hieracium incanum subsp. karpinskyanum

Hieracium incanum subsp.

karpinskyanum, commonly known as the Karpenko's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

Native to Central Asia, this subspecies thrives in rocky and arid environments. It has a creeping growth habit with fibrous roots, allowing it to establish itself in challenging soils.

The plant is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
