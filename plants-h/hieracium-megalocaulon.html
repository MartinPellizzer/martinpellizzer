Hieracium megalocaulon

Hieracium megalocaulon, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Native to Europe, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
