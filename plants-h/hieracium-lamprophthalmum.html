Hieracium lamprophthalmum

Hieracium lamprophthalmum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a whitish, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow with a central disk, and the plant produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
