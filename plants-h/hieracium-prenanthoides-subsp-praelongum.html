Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. praelongum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

praelongum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is notable for its elongated stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
