Hyptis septentrionalis

Hyptis septentrionalis, commonly known as northern hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically clustered in whorls and bloom in shades of purple or white.

Hyptis septentrionalis is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
