Hylotelephium boehmeri

Hylotelephium boehmeri, commonly known as the Armenian carpet cushion, is a perennial succulent native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, rosette-forming leaves that are fleshy and often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
