Hieracium murorum subsp. mecozoum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

mecozoum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite heads.

This subspecies is typically found in moist meadows and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The plant is also a source of nectar for various pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
