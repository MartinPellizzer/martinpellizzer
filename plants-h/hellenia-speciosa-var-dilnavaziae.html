Hellenia speciosa var. dilnavaziae

Hellenia speciosa var. dilnavaziae, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Iran, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
