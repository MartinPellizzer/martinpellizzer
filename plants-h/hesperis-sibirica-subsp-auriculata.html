Hesperis sibirica subsp. auriculata

Hesperis sibirica subsp.

auriculata, commonly known as the Siberian honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
