Hieracium pulchellum subsp. besseanum

Hieracium pulchellum subsp.

besseanum, commonly known as the Bessean Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and geographic distribution compared to other members of the *Hieracium pulchellum* complex.
