Hesperis kitiana

Hesperis kitiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis kitiana is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
