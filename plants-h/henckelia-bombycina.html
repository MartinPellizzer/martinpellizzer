Henckelia bombycina

Henckelia bombycina, commonly known as the woolly henckelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are characterized by their hairy or woolly texture, which gives the plant its common name.

Henckelia bombycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
