Heliconia villosa var. pittieri

Heliconia villosa var. pittieri, commonly known as the Pittieri's Heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and vibrant, clustered inflorescences that resemble a bird's beak. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a distinctive three-lobed structure.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
