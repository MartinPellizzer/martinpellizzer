Hierochloe orientalis

Hierochloe orientalis, commonly known as the oriental hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of fine, silky foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Hierochloe orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
