Helianthus latifolius

Helianthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sunflower, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, broad leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus latifolius is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
