Hippocratea glaga

Hippocratea glaga, commonly known as the rock heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a compact, bushy form.

Hippocratea glaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
