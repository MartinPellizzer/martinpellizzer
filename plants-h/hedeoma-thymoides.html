Hedeoma thymoides

Hedeoma thymoides, commonly known as narrowleaf hedeoma, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

Hedeoma thymoides is known for its aromatic foliage, which has been used in traditional herbal practices.
