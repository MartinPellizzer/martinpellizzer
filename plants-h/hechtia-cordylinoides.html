Hechtia cordylinoides

Hechtia cordylinoides, commonly known as the Mexican hechtia, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Hechtia cordylinoides thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
