Hibiscus ficifolius

Hibiscus ficifolius, commonly known as the forest hibiscus or Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Hibiscus ficifolius thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
