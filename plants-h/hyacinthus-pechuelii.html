Hyacinthus pechuelii

Hyacinthus pechuelii, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dense, spike-like clusters of flowers, each with six petals and prominent stamens. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Hyacinthus pechuelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
