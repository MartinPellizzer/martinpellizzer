Hamiltonia

Hamiltonia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It comprises several species native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. The plants are herbaceous perennials with showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. They typically grow in woodland or rocky habitats and are valued for their ornamental appeal.

Hamiltonia species are often cultivated in gardens for their delicate beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
