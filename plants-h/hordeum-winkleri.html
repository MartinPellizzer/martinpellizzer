Hordeum winkleri

Hordeum winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's barley, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Hordeum winkleri is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
