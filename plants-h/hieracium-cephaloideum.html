Hieracium cephaloideum

Hieracium cephaloideum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
