Hemigraphis sumatrensis var. sumatrensis

Hemigraphis sumatrensis var. sumatrensis, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with striking, velvety leaves that often display a silvery or metallic sheen. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape, and covered in fine, dense hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.
