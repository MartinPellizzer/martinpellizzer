Hibiscus sciadiolepidus

Hibiscus sciadiolepidus, commonly known as the African sand hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus sciadiolepidus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
