Hieracium pallescens subsp. spathodontum

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

spathodontum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrowly lanceolate bracts that give it its specific epithet.
