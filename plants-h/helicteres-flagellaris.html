Helicteres flagellaris

Helicteres flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
