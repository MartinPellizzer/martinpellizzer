Hieracium belonolepis

Hieracium belonolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
