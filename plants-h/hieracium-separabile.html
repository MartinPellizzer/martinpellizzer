Hieracium separabile

Hieracium separabile, commonly known as the divided hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and usually have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Hieracium separabile is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
