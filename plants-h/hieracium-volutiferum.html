Hieracium volutiferum

Hieracium volutiferum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has hairy, rough stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
