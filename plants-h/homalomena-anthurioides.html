Homalomena anthurioides

Homalomena anthurioides, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically arrowhead-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.

Homalomena anthurioides is relatively low-maintenance and can be propagated through division or spores.
