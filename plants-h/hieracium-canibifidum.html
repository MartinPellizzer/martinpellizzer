Hieracium canibifidum

Hieracium canibifidum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium canibifidum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
