Harpephora

Harpephora, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates. Harpephora species are characterized by their small, often brightly colored flowers and their ability to thrive in shaded, high-altitude environments. The plants typically have rhizomatous growth and produce pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.
