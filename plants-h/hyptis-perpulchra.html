Hyptis perpulchra

Hyptis perpulchra, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis perpulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
