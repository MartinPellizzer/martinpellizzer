Hieracium laevigatum subsp. macrodontophyllum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

macrodontophyllum, commonly known as the large-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with each flower having five petals and numerous stamens.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
