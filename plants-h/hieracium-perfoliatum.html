Hieracium perfoliatum

Hieracium perfoliatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its stems are often leafy and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Hieracium perfoliatum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions.
