Hevea brasiliensis f. randiana

Hevea brasiliensis f. randiana, commonly known as the Randiana rubber tree, is a variant of the rubber tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology, featuring larger, more elongated leaves compared to the typical species. The plant is known for its latex production, which is harvested for natural rubber. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils.

This form is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
