Hypericum cerastoides

Hypericum cerastoides, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, woodlands or along stream banks. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a reddish or bronze hue.

Hypericum cerastoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
