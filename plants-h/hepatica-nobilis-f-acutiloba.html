Hepatica nobilis f. acutiloba

Hepatica nobilis f. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a waxy bloom. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, blue, or violet, typically appearing in early spring. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies in shaded, moist environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
