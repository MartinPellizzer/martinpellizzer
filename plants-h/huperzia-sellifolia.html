Huperzia sellifolia

Huperzia sellifolia, commonly known as the rock huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing leaves and is typically found growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a unique morphology with a short, creeping rhizome and lanceolate, often dimorphic leaves. It is native to regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Huperzia sellifolia is also of interest in botanical research due to its evolutionary significance and potential medicinal properties.
