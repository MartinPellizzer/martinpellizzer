Hoodia bainii

Hoodia bainii, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tubular stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Hoodia bainii is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in traditional medicine.

Its unique adaptations make it an important species in ecological and botanical studies.
