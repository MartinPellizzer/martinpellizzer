Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. longistipes

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

longistipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a low-growing plant, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
