Hyacinthus pieridis

Hyacinthus pieridis, commonly known as the alpine hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of blue, violet, and white. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
