Hedyotis depressa

Hedyotis depressa, commonly known as the prostrate wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to various regions of Asia.

It typically features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hedyotis depressa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
