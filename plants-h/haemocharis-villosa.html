Haemocharis villosa

Haemocharis villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf arum, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy, purple spathe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers partial to full shade.

Haemocharis villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
