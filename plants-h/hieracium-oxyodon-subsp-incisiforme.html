Hieracium oxyodon subsp. incisiforme

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

incisiforme, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated margins, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its rapid spread and is considered a weed in some regions due to its competitive nature.
