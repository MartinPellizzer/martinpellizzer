Hieracium pseudoflexuosum

Hieracium pseudoflexuosum, commonly known as the false wandering jew, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with a woolly texture and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
