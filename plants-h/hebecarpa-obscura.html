Hebecarpa obscura

Hebecarpa obscura, commonly known as the dark fruit tree, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark-colored fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance.

The botanical features of Hebecarpa obscura make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological research.
