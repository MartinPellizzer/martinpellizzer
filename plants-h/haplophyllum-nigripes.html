Haplophyllum nigripes

Haplophyllum nigripes, commonly known as the black-stemmed haplophyllum, is a member of the family Polygonaceae, characterized by its succulent, herbaceous growth habit.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky soils and dry conditions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive dark green to blackish stem, contributing to its striking appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish or reddish, and it produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
