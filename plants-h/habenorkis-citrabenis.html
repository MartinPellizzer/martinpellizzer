Habenorkis citrabenis

Habenorkis citrabenis, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
