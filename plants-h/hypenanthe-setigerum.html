Hypenanthe setigerum

Hypenanthe setigerum, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by flies and beetles.
