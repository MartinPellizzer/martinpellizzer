Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. mossambicense

Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. mossambicense, commonly known as the African spikemoss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered with a dense layer of rhizomes, which help anchor it to substrates and absorb moisture.

This variety is notable for its intricate leaf structure and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.
