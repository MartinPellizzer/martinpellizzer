Helenium amarum

Helenium amarum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a single, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its rhizomatous root system is distinctive and often used in traditional medicine.

Helenium amarum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
