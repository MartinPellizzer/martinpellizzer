Hesperis matronalis var. hirsutissima

Hesperis matronalis var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy white campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, pink, or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and ornamental gardening highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
