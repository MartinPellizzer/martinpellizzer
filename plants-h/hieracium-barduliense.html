Hieracium barduliense

Hieracium barduliense, commonly known as the Barduliens hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium barduliense is noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions, making it a notable species in high-altitude flora.
