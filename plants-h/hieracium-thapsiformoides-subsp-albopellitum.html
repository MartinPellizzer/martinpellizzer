Hieracium thapsiformoides subsp. albopellitum

Hieracium thapsiformoides subsp.

albopellitum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is characterized by its subshrub growth habit and is part of the Asteraceae family.
