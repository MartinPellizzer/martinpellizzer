Hymenachne patens

Hymenachne patens, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Hymenachne patens plays a role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and sediment control.
