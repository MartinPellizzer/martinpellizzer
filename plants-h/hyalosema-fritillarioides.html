Hyalosema fritillarioides

Hyalosema fritillarioides, commonly known as the golden rain lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, orange-red flowers that resemble those of the genus Fritillaria, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stems and bloom during the rainy season.

Hyalosema fritillarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
