Habenaria quinqueseta var. macroceratitis

Habenaria quinqueseta var. macroceratitis, commonly known as the broad-fruited marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

The species is noted for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in local ecosystems.
