Hosackia platycarpa

Hosackia platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, broad, inflated pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Hosackia platycarpa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
