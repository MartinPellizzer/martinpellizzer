Hostmannia

Hostmannia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow or orange. The genus includes several species, with Hostmannia monanthica being the most well-known, native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. These plants are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

They are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking appearance and unique foliage.
