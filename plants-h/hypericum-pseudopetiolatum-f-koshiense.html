Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. koshiense

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. koshiense, commonly known as the Koshi St. John's Wort, is a variant of the St. John's wort genus native to the Himalayan region.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers and narrow, elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at higher elevations. Its botanical features include glandular dots on the stems and leaves, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique habitat and potential medicinal properties.
