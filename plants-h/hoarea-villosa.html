Hoarea villosa

Hoarea villosa, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hoarea villosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
