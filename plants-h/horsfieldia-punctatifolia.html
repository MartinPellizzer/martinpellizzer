Horsfieldia punctatifolia

Horsfieldia punctatifolia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature distinctive spots or markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Horsfieldia punctatifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
