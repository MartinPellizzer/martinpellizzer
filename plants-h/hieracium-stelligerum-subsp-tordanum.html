Hieracium stelligerum subsp. tordanum

Hieracium stelligerum subsp.

tordanum, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in open, rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval achenes as its seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
