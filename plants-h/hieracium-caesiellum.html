Hieracium caesiellum

Hieracium caesiellum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite inflorescence, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadside areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural interest.
