Heeria insignis

Heeria insignis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Heeria insignis is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and low maintenance requirements.
