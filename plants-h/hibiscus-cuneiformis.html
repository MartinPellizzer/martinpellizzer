Hibiscus cuneiformis

Hibiscus cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
