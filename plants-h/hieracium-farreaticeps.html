Hieracium farreaticeps

Hieracium farreaticeps, commonly known as the spear-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more narrow. Its flowering stems are often reddish or purplish in color, adding to its visual distinctiveness.

Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
