Hemiglochidion hylodendron

Hemiglochidion hylodendron, commonly known as the goldenleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a waxy surface that aids in moisture retention. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
