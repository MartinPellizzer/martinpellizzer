Hieracium magyaricum subsp. ingricum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

ingricum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies ingricum is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other members of the species.
