Hovea longifolia var. purpurea

Hovea longifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
