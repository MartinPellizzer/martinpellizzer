Hypericum boreale f. callitrichoides

Hypericum boreale f. callitrichoides, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in dense, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting a compact growth form and high tolerance to cold and poor soil conditions.
