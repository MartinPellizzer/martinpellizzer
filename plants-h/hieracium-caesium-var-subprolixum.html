Hieracium caesium var. subprolixum

Hieracium caesium var. subprolixum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
