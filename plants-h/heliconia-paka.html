Heliconia paka

Heliconia paka, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant features large, elongated bracts that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are broad and waxy, often with a reddish or greenish hue.

Heliconia paka is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
