Houstonia angustifolia var. rigidiuscula

Houstonia angustifolia var. rigidiuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers with blue to violet petals. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its rigid, compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Houstonia.
