Heuchera inconstans

Heuchera inconstans, commonly known as the variable alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that range in color from green to red, purple, and bronze, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and adaptability contribute to its value in native plant landscapes.
