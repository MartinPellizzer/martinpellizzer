Huperzia xiphophylla

Huperzia xiphophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and linear, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, sporangia-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
