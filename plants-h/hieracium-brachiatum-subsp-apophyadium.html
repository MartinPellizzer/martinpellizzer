Hieracium brachiatum subsp. apophyadium

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

apophyadium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval seeds.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
