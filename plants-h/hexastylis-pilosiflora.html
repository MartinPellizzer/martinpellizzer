Hexastylis pilosiflora

Hexastylis pilosiflora, commonly known as the hairy-stalked hooded fern, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a dense covering of fine hairs that give it a fuzzy appearance.
