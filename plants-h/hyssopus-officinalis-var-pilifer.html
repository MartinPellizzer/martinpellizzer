Hyssopus officinalis var. pilifer

Hyssopus officinalis var. pilifer, commonly known as hairy hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and historical use in herbal medicine.

The variety 'pilifer' is distinguished by its dense pubescence, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance.
