Hieracium caesium subsp. innsbruckense

Hieracium caesium subsp.

innsbruckense, commonly known as the Innsbruck hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
