Hydrolea spinosa var. megapotamica

Hydrolea spinosa var. megapotamica, commonly known as the Amazonian spurge, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its hardy nature, it has been introduced to other tropical regions as an ornamental plant.
