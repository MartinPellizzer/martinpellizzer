Heptapleurum oxyphyllum var. siamense

Heptapleurum oxyphyllum var. siamense, commonly known as Siam holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy dark green surface and prominent veins. It produces white flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are an ornamental feature.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
