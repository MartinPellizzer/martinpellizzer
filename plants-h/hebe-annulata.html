Hebe annulata

Hebe annulata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Hebe annulata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen nature and ability to attract pollinators.
