Hieracium × spodiocephalum

Hieracium × spodiocephalum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, spiky flower heads composed of densely packed yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
