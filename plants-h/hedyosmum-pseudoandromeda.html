Hedyosmum pseudoandromeda

Hedyosmum pseudoandromeda, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

The tree is often cultivated for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
