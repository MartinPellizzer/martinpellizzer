Hieracium nigrescens var. obliteratum

Hieracium nigrescens var. obliteratum, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a creeping growth habit with rhizomes. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, and it produces achenes as its seed type.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a resilient yet often invasive weed.
