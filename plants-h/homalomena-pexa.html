Homalomena pexa

Homalomena pexa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant features large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage in suitable conditions.
