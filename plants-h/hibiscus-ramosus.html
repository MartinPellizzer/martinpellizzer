Hibiscus ramosus

Hibiscus ramosus, commonly known as the bush hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and often branched. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
