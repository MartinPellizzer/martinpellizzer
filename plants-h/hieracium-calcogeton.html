Hieracium calcogeton

Hieracium calcogeton, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that rise to produce clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Hieracium calcogeton is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in sunny, open habitats.
