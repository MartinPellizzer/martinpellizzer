Hieracium plantaginifrons

Hieracium plantaginifrons, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
