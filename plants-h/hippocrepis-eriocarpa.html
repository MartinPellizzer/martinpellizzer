Hippocrepis eriocarpa

Hippocrepis eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly rock-caper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish layer, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in racemes, producing a subtle fragrance.

Hippocrepis eriocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
