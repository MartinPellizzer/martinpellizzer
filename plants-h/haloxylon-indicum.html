Haloxylon indicum

Haloxylon indicum, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in saline soils.

Haloxylon indicum plays an important role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and providing food for wildlife.
