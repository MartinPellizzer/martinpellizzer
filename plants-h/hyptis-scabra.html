Hyptis scabra

Hyptis scabra, commonly known as rough hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis scabra is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
