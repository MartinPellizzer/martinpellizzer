Hieracium rhodanopum

Hieracium rhodanopum, commonly known as the red-centred hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red center.

Hieracium rhodanopum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
