Hieracium cordatum subsp. heteradenum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

heteradenum, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are cordate (heart-shaped) and are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the disk florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
