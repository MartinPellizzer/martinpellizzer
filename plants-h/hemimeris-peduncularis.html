Hemimeris peduncularis

Hemimeris peduncularis, commonly known as the red-flowered candelabra, is a unique flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling candles on a candelabra. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture.

Hemimeris peduncularis is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
