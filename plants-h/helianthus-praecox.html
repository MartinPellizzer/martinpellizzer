Helianthus praecox

Helianthus praecox, commonly known as early sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Helianthus praecox is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides.
