Hymenophyllum melanocheilos

Hymenophyllum melanocheilos, commonly known as the black-chinned moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, delicate plant that typically grows on moist, shaded rock surfaces or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has intricate, feathery leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length, with a distinctive dark green to blackish coloration on the underside. Its reproductive structures, called sorocarps, are embedded in the leaf axils and contain spores.

Hymenophyllum melanocheilos is often found in humid environments and is an indicator of high air quality and stable microclimates.
