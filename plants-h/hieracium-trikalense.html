Hieracium trikalense

Hieracium trikalense, commonly known as the Trikala hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
