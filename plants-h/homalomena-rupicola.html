Homalomena rupicola

Homalomena rupicola, commonly known as the rock ginger, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Homalomena rupicola is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
