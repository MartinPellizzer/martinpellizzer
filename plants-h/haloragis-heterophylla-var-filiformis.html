Haloragis heterophylla var. filiformis

Haloragis heterophylla var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender-leaved water starwort, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Australia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and wetlands. The plant has thin, thread-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is typically found in shallow water, where its roots anchor it to the substrate.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable aquatic plant.
