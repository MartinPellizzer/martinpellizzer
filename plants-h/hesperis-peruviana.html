Hesperis peruviana

Hesperis peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing nutrients.

Hesperis peruviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
