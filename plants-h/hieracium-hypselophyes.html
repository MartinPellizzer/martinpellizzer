Hieracium hypselophyes

Hieracium hypselophyes, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

Hieracium hypselophyes is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread readily through its rhizomes.
