Helania radialipetala

Helania radialipetala, commonly known as the radiate petal helania, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, radially symmetric floral structure, with petals arranged in a circular pattern around the central reproductive organs. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Helania radialipetala is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
