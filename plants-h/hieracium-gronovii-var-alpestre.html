Hieracium gronovii var. alpestre

Hieracium gronovii var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
