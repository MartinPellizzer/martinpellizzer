Hieracium capsiriense

Hieracium capsiriense, commonly known as the Sicilian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium capsiriense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
