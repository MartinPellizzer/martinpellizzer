Heteropterys argyrophaea

Heteropterys argyrophaea, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
