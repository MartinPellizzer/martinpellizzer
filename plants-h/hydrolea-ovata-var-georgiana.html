Hydrolea ovata var. georgiana

Hydrolea ovata var. georgiana, commonly known as the Georgian hydrolea, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hydrolea ovata var. georgiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
