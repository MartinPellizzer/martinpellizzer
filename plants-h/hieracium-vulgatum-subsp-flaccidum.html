Hieracium vulgatum subsp. flaccidum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

flaccidum, commonly known as the drooping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear basal and alternate leaves with toothed margins. Its flowers are yellow, clustered in dense heads, and are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

It is characterized by its relatively short stature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
