Hieracium nigripilum

Hieracium nigripilum, commonly known as the black-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. Hieracium nigripilum has small, bract-like leaves that are densely covered with stiff hairs, giving it its common name.

It is a flowering plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often seen in wildflower gardens.
