Hieracium magyaricum subsp. pseudomegalomastix

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

pseudomegalomastix, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
