Helianthemum medium

Helianthemum medium, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
