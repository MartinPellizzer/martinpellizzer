Hernandia obovata

Hernandia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed hernandia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Hernandia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
