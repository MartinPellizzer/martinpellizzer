Hieracium florentinum subsp. spathophyllopsis

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

spathophyllopsis, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and serrated.

Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.
