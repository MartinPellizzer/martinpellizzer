Hypericum aviculariifolium subsp. byzantinum

Hypericum aviculariifolium subsp.

byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
