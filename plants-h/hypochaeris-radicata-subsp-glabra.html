Hypochaeris radicata subsp. glabra

Hypochaeris radicata subsp.

glabra, commonly known as common sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and leaves that are smooth and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Hypochaeris radicata subsp.

glabra is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
