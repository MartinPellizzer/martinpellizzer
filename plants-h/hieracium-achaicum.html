Hieracium achaicum

Hieracium achaicum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
