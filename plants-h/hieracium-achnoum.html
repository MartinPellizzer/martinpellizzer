Hieracium achnoum

Hieracium achnoum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its widespread distribution and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
