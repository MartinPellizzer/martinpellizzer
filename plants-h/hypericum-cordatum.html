Hypericum cordatum

Hypericum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in terminal cymes. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodlands and open areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
