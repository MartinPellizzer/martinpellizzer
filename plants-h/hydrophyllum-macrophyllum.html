Hydrophyllum macrophyllum

Hydrophyllum macrophyllum, commonly known as the largeleaf waterleaf, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters at the base of the plant.

Hydrophyllum macrophyllum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
