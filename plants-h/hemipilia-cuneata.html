Hemipilia cuneata

Hemipilia cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hemipilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Hemipilia cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
