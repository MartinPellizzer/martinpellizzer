Hybanthus parviflorus var. chamaedrifolius

Hybanthus parviflorus var. chamaedrifolius, commonly known as the creeping hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage that forms a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
