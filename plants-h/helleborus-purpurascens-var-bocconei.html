Helleborus purpurascens var. bocconei

Helleborus purpurascens var. bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It features dark green, velvety leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
