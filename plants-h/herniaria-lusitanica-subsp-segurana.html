Herniaria lusitanica subsp. segurana

Herniaria lusitanica subsp.

segurana, commonly known as Seguranas rockweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, fleshy, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
