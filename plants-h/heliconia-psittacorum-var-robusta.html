Heliconia psittacorum var. robusta

Heliconia psittacorum var. robusta, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces long, cylindrical inflorescences with numerous small, yellowish flowers clustered within the bracts. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and often have a distinctive red or purple vein pattern.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
