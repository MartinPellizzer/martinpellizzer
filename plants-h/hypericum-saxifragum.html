Hypericum saxifragum

Hypericum saxifragum, commonly known as stonecrop St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in small, yellowish-green leaves. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

Hypericum saxifragum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
