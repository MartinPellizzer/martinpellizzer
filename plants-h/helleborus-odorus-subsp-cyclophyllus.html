Helleborus odorus subsp. cyclophyllus

Helleborus odorus subsp.

cyclophyllus, commonly known as the cyclophyllus hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often mottled with white or pale veins.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of deep purple and violet, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers cool, moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
