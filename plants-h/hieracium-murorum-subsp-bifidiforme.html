Hieracium murorum subsp. bifidiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

bifidiforme, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
