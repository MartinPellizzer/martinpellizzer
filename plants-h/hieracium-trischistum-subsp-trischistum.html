Hieracium trischistum subsp. trischistum

Hieracium trischistum subsp.

trischistum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy meadows, rocky slopes, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the stem's apex. Its leaves and stems are covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
