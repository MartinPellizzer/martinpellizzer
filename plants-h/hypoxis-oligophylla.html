Hypoxis oligophylla

Hypoxis oligophylla, commonly known as the starry false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Hypoxis oligophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.

It is also known for its ability to bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions.
