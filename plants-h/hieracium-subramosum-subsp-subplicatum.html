Hieracium subramosum subsp. subplicatum

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

subplicatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single, terminal capitulum, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.
