Helipterum incanum var. purpureoalbum

Helipterum incanum var. purpureoalbum, commonly known as the purple-flowered sandbur, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender stems and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are characteristic of the genus. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, with five petals, and it blooms during the cooler months.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
