Heterotheca mayoensis

Heterotheca mayoensis, commonly known as the Mayo's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca mayoensis is often found in disturbed sites and is adapted to arid environments with its deep root system and drought-tolerant properties.
