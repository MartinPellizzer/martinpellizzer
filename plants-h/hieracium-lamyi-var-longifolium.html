Hieracium lamyi var. longifolium

Hieracium lamyi var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features elongated, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow and form dense clusters, often resembling a dandelion, and it thrives in open, disturbed habitats.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, where it can spread readily through its rhizomatous growth.
