Hieracium conglutinans

Hieracium conglutinans, commonly known as the sticky hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its sticky, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
