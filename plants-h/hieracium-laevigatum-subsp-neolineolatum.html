Hieracium laevigatum subsp. neolineolatum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

neolineolatum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically from June to August, and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
