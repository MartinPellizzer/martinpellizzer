Hieracium vadledalense

Hieracium vadledalense, commonly known as the Vadledal hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
