Heliotropium gardneri

Heliotropium gardneri, commonly known as the velvetleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and clearings.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
