Holoschoenus tuberculatus

Holoschoenus tuberculatus, commonly known as the woolly sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive hairy texture on the stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in a cylindrical shape, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Holoschoenus tuberculatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
