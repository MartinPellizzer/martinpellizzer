Houstonia mucronata

Houstonia mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in fine hairs.

Houstonia mucronata thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and shaded areas.
