Hirtella megacarpa

Hirtella megacarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
