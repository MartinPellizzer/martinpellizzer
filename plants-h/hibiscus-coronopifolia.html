Hibiscus coronopifolia

Hibiscus coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody and somewhat branched. Hibiscus coronopifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils near water sources.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its use in traditional medicine.
