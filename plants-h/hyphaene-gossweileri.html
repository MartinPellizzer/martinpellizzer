Hyphaene gossweileri

Hyphaene gossweileri, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical Africa.

It features a single, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, palmate crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
