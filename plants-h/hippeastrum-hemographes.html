Hippeastrum hemographes

Hippeastrum hemographes, commonly known as the red-veined amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers with deep red petals and prominent, contrasting veins. The leaves are broad, strap-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This plant is popular for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
