Hieracium falcidens

Hieracium falcidens, commonly known as the sickleleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium falcidens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
