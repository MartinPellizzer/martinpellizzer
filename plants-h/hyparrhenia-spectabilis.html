Hyparrhenia spectabilis

Hyparrhenia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features tall, slender culms with narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Hyparrhenia spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a source of thatching material.
