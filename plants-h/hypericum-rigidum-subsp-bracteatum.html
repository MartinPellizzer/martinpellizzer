Hypericum rigidum subsp. bracteatum

Hypericum rigidum subsp.

bracteatum, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features woody stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
