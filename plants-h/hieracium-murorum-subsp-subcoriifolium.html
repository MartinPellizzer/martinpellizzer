Hieracium murorum subsp. subcoriifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subcoriifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a distinctive coriaceous texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
