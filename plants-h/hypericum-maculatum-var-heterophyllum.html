Hypericum maculatum var. heterophyllum

Hypericum maculatum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the spotted dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and have a slightly hairy texture, while the varietal name "heterophyllum" refers to its varying leaf shapes.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
