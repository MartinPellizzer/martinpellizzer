Hieracium murorum subsp. pleuroleucum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pleuroleucum, commonly known as the pale-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of pale green leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy stems.

This subspecies is part of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses in traditional herbal practices.
