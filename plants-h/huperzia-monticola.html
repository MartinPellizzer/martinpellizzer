Huperzia monticola

Huperzia monticola, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to regions in East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its sporangia are clustered on the underside of the leaves, a feature typical of many ferns.

Huperzia monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
