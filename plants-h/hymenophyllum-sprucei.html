Hymenophyllum sprucei

Hymenophyllum sprucei, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of silky, white trichomes, giving it a silvery appearance. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum sprucei plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil moisture retention and providing habitat for microorganisms.
