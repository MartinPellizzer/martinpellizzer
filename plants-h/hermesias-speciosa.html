Hermesias speciosa

Hermesias speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and sturdy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hermesias speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
