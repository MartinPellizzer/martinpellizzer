Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. mucescens

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

mucescens, commonly known as the sticky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, blooming from June to August.

It is characterized by its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
