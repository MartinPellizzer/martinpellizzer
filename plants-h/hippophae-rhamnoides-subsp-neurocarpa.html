Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. neurocarpa

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

neurocarpa, commonly known as the Siberian sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia and parts of Siberia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture. The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the more widely cultivated H. rhamnoides subsp.

rhamnoides.
