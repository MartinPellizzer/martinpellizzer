Hieracium austericeps

Hieracium austericeps, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Hieracium austericeps is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
