Homoianthus multiflorus

Homoianthus multiflorus, commonly known as the multibloom shrub, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
