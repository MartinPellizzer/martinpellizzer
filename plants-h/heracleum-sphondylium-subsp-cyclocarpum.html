Heracleum sphondylium subsp. cyclocarpum

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

cyclocarpum, commonly known as the round-seeded cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The fruit is a small, round schizocarp with two mericarps, distinguishing it from other subspecies.

This plant is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks in temperate regions.
