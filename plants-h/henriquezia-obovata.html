Henriquezia obovata

Henriquezia obovata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen ash, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
