Hamosa succumbens

Hamosa succumbens, commonly known as the desert hamosa, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Hamosa succumbens is well adapted to high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
