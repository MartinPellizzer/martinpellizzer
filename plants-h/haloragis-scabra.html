Haloragis scabra

Haloragis scabra, commonly known as the slender waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Haloragaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas or freshwater habitats.

Haloragis scabra is typically herbaceous, with a creeping growth habit that allows it to spread along the ground.
