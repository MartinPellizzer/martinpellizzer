Hyptis veronicifolia

Hyptis veronicifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Hyptis veronicifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
