Hermesias cauliflora

Hermesias cauliflora, commonly known as the tropical orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly from the stems rather than the branches. The plant produces striking, brightly colored blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, tropical environment.

Hermesias cauliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
