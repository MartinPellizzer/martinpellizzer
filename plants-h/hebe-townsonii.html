Hebe townsonii

Hebe townsonii, commonly known as the silver hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or blue during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe townsonii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
