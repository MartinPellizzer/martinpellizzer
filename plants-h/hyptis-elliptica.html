Hyptis elliptica

Hyptis elliptica, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Hyptis elliptica thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
