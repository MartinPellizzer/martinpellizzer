Hieracium florentinum subsp. lancifolium

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

lancifolium, commonly known as the lance-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
