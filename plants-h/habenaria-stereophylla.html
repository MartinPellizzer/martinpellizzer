Habenaria stereophylla

Habenaria stereophylla, commonly known as the purple-tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
