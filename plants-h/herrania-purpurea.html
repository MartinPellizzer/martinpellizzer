Herrania purpurea

Herrania purpurea, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
