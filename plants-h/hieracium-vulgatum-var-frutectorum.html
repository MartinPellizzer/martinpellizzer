Hieracium vulgatum var. frutectorum

Hieracium vulgatum var. frutectorum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is an annual or biennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
