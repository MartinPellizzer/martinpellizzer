Hedysarum elongatum lusus leiocarpum

Hedysarum elongatum lusus leiocarpum, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Hedysarum elongatum lusus leiocarpum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
