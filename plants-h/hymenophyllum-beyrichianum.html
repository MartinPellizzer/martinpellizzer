Hymenophyllum beyrichianum

Hymenophyllum beyrichianum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in a silvery, powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. Its unique morphology includes tiny, scale-like structures called trichomes that help reduce water loss.

Hymenophyllum beyrichianum is often found growing on trees or rocks in moist, tropical forests.
