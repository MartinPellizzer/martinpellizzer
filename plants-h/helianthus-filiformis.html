Helianthus filiformis

Helianthus filiformis, commonly known as the slender sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, thread-like leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and blooms from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
