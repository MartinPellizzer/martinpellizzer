Hieracium atratum subsp. personatiforme

Hieracium atratum subsp.

personatiforme, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has a rosette growth habit with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

The subspecies personatiforme is distinguished by its more elongated leaves and slightly different flower structure compared to other forms of Hieracium atratum.
