Hieracium murorum subsp. seripodum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

seripodum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its widespread distribution and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
