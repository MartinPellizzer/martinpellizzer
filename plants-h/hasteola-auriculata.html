Hasteola auriculata

Hasteola auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its scientific name refers to the ear-like structures on its leaves, which are a distinctive botanical feature.

Hasteola auriculata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
