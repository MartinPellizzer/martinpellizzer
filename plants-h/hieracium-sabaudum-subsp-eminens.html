Hieracium sabaudum subsp. eminens

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

eminens, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the end of its stems.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly taller stature and more elongated leaves compared to other Hieracium species.
