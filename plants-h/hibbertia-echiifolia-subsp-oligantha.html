Hibbertia echiifolia subsp. oligantha

Hibbertia echiifolia subsp.

oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibbertia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern.

The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent, petal-like structures, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
