Helictochloa bromoides subsp. pauneroae

Helictochloa bromoides subsp.

pauneroae, commonly known as the Panhandle needlegrass, is a grass species native to the central United States. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
