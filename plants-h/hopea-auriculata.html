Hopea auriculata

Hopea auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that often have a distinctive ear-like shape at the base. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in catkins, which are typically borne in clusters. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Hopea auriculata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
