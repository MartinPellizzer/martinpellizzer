Hieracium austerulum

Hieracium austerulum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish or reddish coloration on the underside.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
