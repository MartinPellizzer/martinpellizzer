Hymenochilus sinuatus

Hymenochilus sinuatus, commonly known as the wavy lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features a slender, leafless stem that bears a cluster of showy, lip-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns and a distinctive wavy edge on the lip. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
