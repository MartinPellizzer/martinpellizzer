Helianthus hirsutus var. trachyphyllus

Helianthus hirsutus var. trachyphyllus, commonly known as roughleaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
