Hebe calcicola

Hebe calcicola, commonly known as the limestone hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained conditions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in challenging environments.

Hebe calcicola is also appreciated for its role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
