Hypericum irazuense

Hypericum irazuense, commonly known as the Irazu hypericum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are woody and branched.

Hypericum irazuense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
