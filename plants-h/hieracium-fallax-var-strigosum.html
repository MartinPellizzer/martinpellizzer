Hieracium fallax var. strigosum

Hieracium fallax var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a hairy stem and basal leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
