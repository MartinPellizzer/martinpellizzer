Hieracium auriculoides subsp. teberdifontis

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

teberdifontis, commonly known as the Teberdifontis Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
