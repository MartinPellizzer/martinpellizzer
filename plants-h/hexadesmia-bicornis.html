Hexadesmia bicornis

Hexadesmia bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned hexadesmia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive two horn-like structures on its calyx. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.
