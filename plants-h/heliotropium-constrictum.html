Heliotropium constrictum

Heliotropium constrictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Heliotropium constrictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
