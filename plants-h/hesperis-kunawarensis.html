Hesperis kunawarensis

Hesperis kunawarensis, commonly known as the Japanese sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis kunawarensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
