Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. grisebachii

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its pale coloration and compact growth habit compared to other hawkweed species.
