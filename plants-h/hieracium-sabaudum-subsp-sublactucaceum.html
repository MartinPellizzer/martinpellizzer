Hieracium sabaudum subsp. sublactucaceum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

sublactucaceum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and shorter flowering stems compared to other Hieracium species.
