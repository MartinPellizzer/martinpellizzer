Hippia frutescens

Hippia frutescens, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hippia frutescens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
