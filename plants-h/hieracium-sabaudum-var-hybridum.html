Hieracium sabaudum var. hybridum

Hieracium sabaudum var. hybridum, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The species is known for its variability, leading to the recognition of several subspecies and hybrids.
