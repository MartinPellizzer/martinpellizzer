Hieracium linguiferum

Hieracium linguiferum, commonly known as the tongue grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium linguiferum is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
