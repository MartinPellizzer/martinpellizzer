Hypericum marahuacanum subsp. marahuacanum

Hypericum marahuacanum subsp.

marahuacanum, commonly known as the Mexican St. John's wort, is a shrub native to Central America. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and forest edges.

The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are simple and opposite. It has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its roots are fibrous.

This species is part of the Hippocastanaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
