Halerpestes tricuspis var. variifolia

Halerpestes tricuspis var. variifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf halerpestes, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, three-lobed leaves. The leaves of this plant exhibit considerable variation in shape and size, giving rise to its varietal name "variifolia.". It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
