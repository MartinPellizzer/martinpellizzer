Hieracium praecurrens subsp. zlatuianum

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

zlatuianum, commonly known as the Zlatuian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, thriving in sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
