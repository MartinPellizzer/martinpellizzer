Hieracium maculatum subsp. asperatum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

asperatum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "asperatum.".

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
