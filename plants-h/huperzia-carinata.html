Huperzia carinata

Huperzia carinata, commonly known as the ridge fern, is a species of clubmoss that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit and its distinctive, segmented, and often ridged stems. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Huperzia carinata is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
