Hieracium glaucum subsp. subporrifolium

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

subporrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its subspecies status reflects subtle morphological differences from the main species, particularly in leaf structure.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
