Hieracium transiens subsp. pollichiotropium

Hieracium transiens subsp.

pollichiotropium, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is known for its variability in leaf shape and flower size, which contributes to its common name. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant has a creeping growth habit and produces numerous small, inconspicuous fruits that aid in its spread.
