Hydrangea aspera var. strigosior

Hydrangea aspera var. strigosior, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH. The plant features rough, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, often found in forested areas.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators and adding ornamental value to gardens.
