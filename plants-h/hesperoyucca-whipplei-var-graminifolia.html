Hesperoyucca whipplei var. graminifolia

Hesperoyucca whipplei var. graminifolia, commonly known as the desert grass yucca, is a variant of the desert yucca native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, which consists of narrow, grass-green leaves that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, typical of the Agavaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xeriscaped landscapes.
