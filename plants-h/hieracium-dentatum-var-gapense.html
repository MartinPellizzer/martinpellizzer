Hieracium dentatum var. gapense

Hieracium dentatum var. gapense, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and serrated, with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
