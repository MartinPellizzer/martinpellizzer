Hieracium subcinerellum

Hieracium subcinerellum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a clustered inflorescence. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
