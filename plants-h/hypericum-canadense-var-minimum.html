Hypericum canadense var. minimum

Hypericum canadense var. minimum, commonly known as dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces tiny, bead-like fruits that are often retained on the plant throughout the winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is prized for its compact size and ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
