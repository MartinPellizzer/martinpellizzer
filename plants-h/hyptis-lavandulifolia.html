Hyptis lavandulifolia

Hyptis lavandulifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
