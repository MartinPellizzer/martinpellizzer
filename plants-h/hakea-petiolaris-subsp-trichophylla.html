Hakea petiolaris subsp. trichophylla

Hakea petiolaris subsp.

trichophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces cylindrical, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water storage.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
