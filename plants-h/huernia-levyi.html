Huernia levyi

Huernia levyi, commonly known as the leopard's ear cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and have a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces pale green or yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are broad and oval, often resembling the shape of a leopard's ear, giving it its common name.

Huernia levyi is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
