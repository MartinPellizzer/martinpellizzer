Haplopappus uncinatus

Haplopappus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by a pappus of hooked bristles, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.

The species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
