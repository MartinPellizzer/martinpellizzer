Helichrysum adolfi-friderici

Helichrysum adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the golden marguerite or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or orange flowers that resemble daisies, which are typically borne on wiry stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum adolfi-friderici is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
