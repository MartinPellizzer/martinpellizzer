Hordeum defectoides

Hordeum defectoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barley, is a grass species native to various regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Hordeum defectoides is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and agricultural studies.
