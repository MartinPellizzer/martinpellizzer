Homalium microphyllum

Homalium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved evergreen tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
