Hovea impressinerva

Hovea impressinerva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hovea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its bean-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves with a distinctive impressed midrib, giving it its specific epithet. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flattened, leathery pods.

Hovea impressinerva is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, adapting well to sandy soils and coastal conditions.
