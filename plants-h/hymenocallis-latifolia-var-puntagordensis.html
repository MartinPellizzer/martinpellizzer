Hymenocallis latifolia var. puntagordensis

Hymenocallis latifolia var. puntagordensis, commonly known as the Puntagorda yucca lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often blue-green in color and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. Its flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This variety is notable for its resilience to salt spray and its ability to grow in sandy or rocky soils.
