Habenaria giriensis

Habenaria giriensis, commonly known as the Giriens Hill Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces a single spike of flowers, each with three sepals and three petals. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
