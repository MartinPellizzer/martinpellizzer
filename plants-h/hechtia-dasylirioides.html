Hechtia dasylirioides

Hechtia dasylirioides, commonly known as the Mexican hechtia, is a striking bromeliad native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy, often displaying vibrant blue or silver hues. The plant produces an inflorescence of showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
