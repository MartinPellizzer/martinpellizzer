Hyacinthus font-queri

Hyacinthus font-queri, commonly known as the Fontqueri hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically in late winter to early spring. It has strap-like, narrow leaves that are often evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
