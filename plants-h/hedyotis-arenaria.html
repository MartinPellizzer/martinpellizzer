Hedyotis arenaria

Hedyotis arenaria, commonly known as coastal knotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shores and dunes, exhibiting a robust ability to withstand salt spray and wind. The plant features small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Hedyotis arenaria plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
