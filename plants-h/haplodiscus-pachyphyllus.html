Haplodiscus pachyphyllus

Haplodiscus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leafed haps, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Haplodiscus pachyphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
