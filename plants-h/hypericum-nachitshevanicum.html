Hypericum nachitshevanicum

Hypericum nachitshevanicum, commonly known as the Armenian St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Hypericum nachitshevanicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
