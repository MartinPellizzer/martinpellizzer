Heuchera curtisii var. crenata

Heuchera curtisii var. crenata, commonly known as the curly-leaved alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, wavy-edged leaves that range in color from deep purple to silver-green, depending on the variety and light conditions. The plant produces upright, cone-shaped clusters of small, white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, providing ornamental value in both garden and natural settings.
