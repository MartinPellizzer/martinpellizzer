Hypoxis apiculata

Hypoxis apiculata, commonly known as the white star ginger, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
