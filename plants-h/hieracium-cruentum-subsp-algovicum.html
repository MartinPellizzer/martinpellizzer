Hieracium cruentum subsp. algovicum

Hieracium cruentum subsp.

algovicum, commonly known as the Bloody Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, and its flowering period usually occurs from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower color and habitat preferences compared to other members of the species.
