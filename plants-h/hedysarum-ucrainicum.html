Hedysarum ucrainicum

Hedysarum ucrainicum, commonly known as the Crimean vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Hedysarum ucrainicum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in agriculture.
