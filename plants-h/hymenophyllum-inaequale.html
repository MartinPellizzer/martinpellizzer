Hymenophyllum inaequale

Hymenophyllum inaequale, commonly known as the unequal moss, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, delicate plant characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds and a unique reproductive structure called a sorus. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of rhizomes, which help anchor it to rocky surfaces or tree bark.

Hymenophyllum inaequale is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
