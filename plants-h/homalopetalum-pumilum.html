Homalopetalum pumilum

Homalopetalum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer with vibrant yellow or orange petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
