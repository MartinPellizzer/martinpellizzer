Hydrangea cuspidata

Hydrangea cuspidata, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a dark green color.

Hydrangea cuspidata thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
