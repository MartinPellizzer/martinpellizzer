Hermannia argillicola

Hermannia argillicola, commonly known as the clay-loving geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, clay soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Hermannia argillicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
