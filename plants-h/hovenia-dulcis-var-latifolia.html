Hovenia dulcis var. latifolia

Hovenia dulcis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Chinese raisin tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed raw or processed into beverages. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
