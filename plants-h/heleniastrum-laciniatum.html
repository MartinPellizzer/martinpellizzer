Heleniastrum laciniatum

Heleniastrum laciniatum, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated or fringed appearance, giving it its common name.

Heleniastrum laciniatum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
