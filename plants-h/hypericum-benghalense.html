Hypericum benghalense

Hypericum benghalense, commonly known as the Indian St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and pseudohypericin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine contribute to its significance in botanical studies.
