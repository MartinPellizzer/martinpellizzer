Haemanthus albiflos var. brachyphyllus

Haemanthus albiflos var. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces umbels of white flowers in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic display to the landscape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking exotic plants.
