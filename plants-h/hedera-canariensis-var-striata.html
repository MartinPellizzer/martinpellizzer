Hedera canariensis var. striata

Hedera canariensis var. striata, commonly known as the Canary ivy, is a variant of the common ivy that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is a climbing evergreen vine characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with a distinctive white or pale green midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange berries, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant cultivation.
