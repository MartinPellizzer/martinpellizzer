Harveya purpurea subsp. purpurea

Harveya purpurea subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple harveya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, temperate climate. Harveya purpurea subsp.

purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
