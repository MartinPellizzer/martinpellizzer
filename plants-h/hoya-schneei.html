Hoya schneei

Hoya schneei, commonly known as the Snowy Wisteria, is a unique species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy white flowers that resemble clusters of small bells, often blooming in late winter or early spring. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green color. Hoya schneei is a climbing vine that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
