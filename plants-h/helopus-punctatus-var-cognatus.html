Helopus punctatus var. cognatus

Helopus punctatus var. cognatus, commonly known as the spotted helopus, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often marked with intricate patterns of dark spots and streaks. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
