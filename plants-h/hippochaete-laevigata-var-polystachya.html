Hippochaete laevigata var. polystachya

Hippochaete laevigata var. polystachya, commonly known as the silky hairvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces oblong, ribbed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
