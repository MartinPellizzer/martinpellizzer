Heisteria uleana

Heisteria uleana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows in a compact, low-growing habit. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heisteria uleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
