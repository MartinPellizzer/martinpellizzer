Hieracium balbisianum subsp. kuemmerlei

Hieracium balbisianum subsp.

kuemmerlei, commonly known as the Balbisian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its specific chromosome number and floral morphology.
