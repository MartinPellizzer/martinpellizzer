Hieracium nigrescens subsp. cyathodes

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

cyathodes, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a dark center.

The subspecies cyathodes is distinguished by its unique flower structure and darker coloration compared to other members of the species.
