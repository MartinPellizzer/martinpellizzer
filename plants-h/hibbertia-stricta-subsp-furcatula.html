Hibbertia stricta subsp. furcatula

Hibbertia stricta subsp.

furcatula, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, ascending stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent, petal-like structures that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands. Hibbertia stricta subsp.

furcatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
