Hemizonia corymbosa var. barclayi

Hemizonia corymbosa var. barclayi, commonly known as the Barclay's hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers ranging in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.
