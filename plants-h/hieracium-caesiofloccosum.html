Hieracium caesiofloccosum

Hieracium caesiofloccosum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive floccose (fluffy) appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
