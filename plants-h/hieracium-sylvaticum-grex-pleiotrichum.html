Hieracium sylvaticum grex pleiotrichum

Hieracium sylvaticum grex pleiotrichum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in association with other woodland flora.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
