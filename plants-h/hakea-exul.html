Hakea exul

Hakea exul, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a shrub native to south-western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive cylindrical or spiny flower spikes. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that are typically borne in dense, terminal clusters. Hakea exul is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
