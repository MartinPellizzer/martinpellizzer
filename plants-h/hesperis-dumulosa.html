Hesperis dumulosa

Hesperis dumulosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Hesperis dumulosa is often found in meadows and rocky areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
