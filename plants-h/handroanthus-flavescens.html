Handroanthus flavescens

Handroanthus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant is a deciduous tree, shedding its leaves during periods of drought. Its flowers have a trumpet-like shape and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
