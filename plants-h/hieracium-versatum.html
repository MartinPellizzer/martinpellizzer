Hieracium versatum

Hieracium versatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Hieracium versatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
