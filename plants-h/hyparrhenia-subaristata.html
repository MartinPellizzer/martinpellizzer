Hyparrhenia subaristata

Hyparrhenia subaristata, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble small plumes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a clumping growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in the grass family (Poaceae).
