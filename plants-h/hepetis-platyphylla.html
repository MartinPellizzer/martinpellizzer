Hepetis platyphylla

Hepetis platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance.

Hepetis platyphylla is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
