Handroanthus chrysotrichus var. obtusata

Handroanthus chrysotrichus var. obtusata, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree or yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with a straight trunk and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
