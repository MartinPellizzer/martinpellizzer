Hieracium paleoscense

Hieracium paleoscense, commonly known as the pale yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium paleoscense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
