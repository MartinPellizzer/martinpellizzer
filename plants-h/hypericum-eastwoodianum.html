Hypericum eastwoodianum

Hypericum eastwoodianum, commonly known as Eastwood's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its botanical features include yellow-orange floral glands and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Hypericum eastwoodianum is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
