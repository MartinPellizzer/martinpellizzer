Hovea longifolia subvar. subacuta

Hovea longifolia subvar. subacuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hovea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, with a distinctive greyish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
