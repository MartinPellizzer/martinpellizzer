Hibiscus cerradoensis

Hibiscus cerradoensis, commonly known as the Cerrado hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil's Cerrado region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Hibiscus cerradoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and horticulture.
