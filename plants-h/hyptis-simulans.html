Hyptis simulans

Hyptis simulans, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Hyptis simulans is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
