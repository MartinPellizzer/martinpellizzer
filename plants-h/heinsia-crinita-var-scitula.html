Heinsia crinita var. scitula

Heinsia crinita var. scitula, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in dry, rocky soils, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silky hair. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and ornamental species in xeriscaped gardens.
