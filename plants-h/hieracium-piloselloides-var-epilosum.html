Hieracium piloselloides var. epilosum

Hieracium piloselloides var. epilosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features showy yellow flowers arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a serrated margin, contributing to its identification.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses in some traditional practices.
