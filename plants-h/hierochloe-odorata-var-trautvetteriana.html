Hierochloe odorata var. trautvetteriana

Hierochloe odorata var. trautvetteriana, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, and aromatic leaves that emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It produces small, greenish-brown inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonial practices.

Due to its unique fragrance and ecological role, it is valued in both ecological restoration and cultural contexts.
