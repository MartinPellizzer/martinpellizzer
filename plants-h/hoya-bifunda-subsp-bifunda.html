Hoya bifunda subsp. bifunda

Hoya bifunda subsp.

bifunda, commonly known as the "Snowy Hoya," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the summer months. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Hoya bifunda subsp.

bifunda is also known for its slow growth rate and ability to develop aerial roots when grown in hanging baskets.
