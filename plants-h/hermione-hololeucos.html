Hermione hololeucos

Hermione hololeucos, commonly known as the white-leafed fern, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are predominantly white or pale green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Despite its aesthetic appeal, it is relatively rare and requires careful cultivation to maintain its health.
