Heliotropium arborescens var. grisellum

Heliotropium arborescens var. grisellum, commonly known as the grayleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its branching structure and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
