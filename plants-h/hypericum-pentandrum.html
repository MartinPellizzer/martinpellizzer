Hypericum pentandrum

Hypericum pentandrum, commonly known as the five-thread St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically oval with a slightly hairy texture. The plant is characterized by its five stamens in each flower, giving it the species name "pentandrum.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Hypericum pentandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
