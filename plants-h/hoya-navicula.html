Hoya navicula

Hoya navicula, commonly known as the sailblades hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of waxy, heart-shaped leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in late summer or early autumn. Hoya navicula is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

It is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ornamental appeal.
