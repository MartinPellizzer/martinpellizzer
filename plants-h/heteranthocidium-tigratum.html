Heteranthocidium tigratum

Heteranthocidium tigratum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns resembling a tiger's stripes. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without being parasitic.

The botanical features of Heteranthocidium tigratum make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
