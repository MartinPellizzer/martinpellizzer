Hieracium denudatum

Hieracium denudatum, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
