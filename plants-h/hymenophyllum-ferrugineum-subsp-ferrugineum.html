Hymenophyllum ferrugineum subsp. ferrugineum

Hymenophyllum ferrugineum subsp.

ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty toothed fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere. It typically grows on tree trunks and rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive rusty brown coloration on the underside. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered along the margins of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
