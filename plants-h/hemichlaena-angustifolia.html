Hemichlaena angustifolia

Hemichlaena angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hemichlaena, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hemichlaena angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
