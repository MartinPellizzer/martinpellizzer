Hydrangea involucrata f. variegata

Hydrangea involucrata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated panicle hydrangea, is a cultivar of the panicle hydrangea species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant produces cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental shrub is valued for its year-round interest, with attractive foliage in both summer and winter.
