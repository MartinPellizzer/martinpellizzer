Halerpestes tricuspis var. heterophylla

Halerpestes tricuspis var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable three-lobed acanthus, is a variant of the acanthus plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves with a waxy texture and a deep green coloration. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences with violet to purple flowers. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical traits make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
