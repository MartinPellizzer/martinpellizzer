Hydrilla roxburghii

Hydrilla roxburghii, commonly known as water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often submerged, followed by dark, spherical fruits. Hydrilla roxburghii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and canals, where it can form dense underwater vegetation.
