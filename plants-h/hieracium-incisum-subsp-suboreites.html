Hieracium incisum subsp. suboreites

Hieracium incisum subsp.

suboreites, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
