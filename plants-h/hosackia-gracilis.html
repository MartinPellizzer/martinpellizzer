Hosackia gracilis

Hosackia gracilis, commonly known as slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hosackia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
