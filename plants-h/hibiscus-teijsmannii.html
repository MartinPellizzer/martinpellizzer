Hibiscus teijsmannii

Hibiscus teijsmannii, commonly known as the Java hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. Hibiscus teijsmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
