Hyacinthus nervosus

Hyacinthus nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hyacinth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and often hairy, with a distinctive, fragrant flower cluster that blooms in early spring. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with six petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
