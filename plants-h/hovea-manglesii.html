Hovea manglesii

Hovea manglesii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, forming dense clusters along the stems. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Hovea manglesii is a key species in the ecological makeup of its native habitat.
