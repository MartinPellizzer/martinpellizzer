Hedera canariensis aurea-maculata

Hedera canariensis aurea-maculata, commonly known as the Canary Island ivy with golden spots, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Canary Islands.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive golden-yellow spots that add visual interest. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy but are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded gardens or as a climbing plant on walls and trellises.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
