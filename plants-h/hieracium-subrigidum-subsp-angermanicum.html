Hieracium subrigidum subsp. angermanicum

Hieracium subrigidum subsp.

angermanicum, commonly known as the Angerman's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It is a relatively rare species, often found in specific microhabitats across its native range.
