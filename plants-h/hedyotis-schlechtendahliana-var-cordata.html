Hedyotis schlechtendahliana var. cordata

Hedyotis schlechtendahliana var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf blushing tibetan geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. The leaves are heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
