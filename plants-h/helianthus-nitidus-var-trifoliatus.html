Helianthus nitidus var. trifoliatus

Helianthus nitidus var. trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaf sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower species.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
