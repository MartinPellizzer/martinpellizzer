Hibiscus divaricatus var. luteus

Hibiscus divaricatus var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow wood hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has a woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
