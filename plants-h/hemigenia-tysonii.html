Hemigenia tysonii

Hemigenia tysonii, commonly known as the Tasmanian honey mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Tasmania.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive honey-scented aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in heathlands and along stream banks.

Hemigenia tysonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
