Hieracium fendleri var. ostreophyllum

Hieracium fendleri var. ostreophyllum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

The yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
