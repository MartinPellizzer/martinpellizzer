Hieracium murorum subsp. elongaticeps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

elongaticeps, commonly known as the long-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and numerous yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated flower heads and is often found in disturbed soils.
