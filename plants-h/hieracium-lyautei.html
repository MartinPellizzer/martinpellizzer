Hieracium lyautei

Hieracium lyautei, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to purple, depending on the variety.

Hieracium lyautei is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a valuable source of nectar for pollinators.
