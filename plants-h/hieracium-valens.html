Hieracium valens

Hieracium valens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium valens is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
