Hopea exalata

Hopea exalata, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

Hopea exalata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
