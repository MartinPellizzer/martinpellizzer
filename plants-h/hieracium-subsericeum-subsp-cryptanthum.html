Hieracium subsericeum subsp. cryptanthum

Hieracium subsericeum subsp.

cryptanthum, commonly known as the cryptanthum hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. Its small, yellowish to reddish flowers appear in late summer, and it produces seeds in a schizocarp that splits into two parts.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
