Habenaria hollandiae

Habenaria hollandiae, commonly known as the Dutch lady's tresses, is an orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive inflorescence of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Habenaria hollandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
