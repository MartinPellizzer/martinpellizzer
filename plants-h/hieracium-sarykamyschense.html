Hieracium sarykamyschense

Hieracium sarykamyschense, commonly known as the Sarykamysh hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and semi-deserts, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like structure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
