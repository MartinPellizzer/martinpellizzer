Hymenophyllum praetervisum

Hymenophyllum praetervisum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically covered in a fine, silvery layer of trichomes. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a distinct midrib and a dense arrangement of cells.

Hymenophyllum praetervisum plays an important ecological role in forest understories by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
