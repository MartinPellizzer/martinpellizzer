Hieracium ovikense

Hieracium ovikense, commonly known as the Alpine Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer.

Hieracium ovikense is known for its ability to colonize disturbed sites and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
