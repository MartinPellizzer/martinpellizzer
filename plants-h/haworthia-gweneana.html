Haworthia gweneana

Haworthia gweneana, commonly known as the Zulu moonstone, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and often have a waxy coating. The leaves are typically green with white or pale markings, and the plant produces small, white flowers during the growing season.

It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive periods of drought.
