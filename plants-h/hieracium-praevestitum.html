Hieracium praevestitum

Hieracium praevestitum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium praevestitum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
