Hieracium irmae

Hieracium irmae, commonly known as the Irme's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
