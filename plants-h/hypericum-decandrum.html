Hypericum decandrum

Hypericum decandrum, commonly known as the nine-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with five to nine petals, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves and produces small, red-orange fruits. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or rocky areas.

Hypericum decandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild medicinal properties.
