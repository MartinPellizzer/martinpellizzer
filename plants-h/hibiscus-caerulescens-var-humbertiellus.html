Hibiscus caerulescens var. humbertiellus

Hibiscus caerulescens var. humbertiellus, commonly known as the Humbert's blue hibiscus, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with a white or pale pink throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its stems are often woody and covered in a white, powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
