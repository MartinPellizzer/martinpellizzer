Huernia urceolata

Huernia urceolata, commonly known as the "cup-shaped" or "honeycomb" plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, ribbed stems that are often green or tinged with red, and it produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is known for its unique, honeycomb-like patterns on its stems, which are formed by the arrangement of its areoles. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Huernia urceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.
