Hieracium grossidentatum

Hieracium grossidentatum, commonly known as the broad-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
