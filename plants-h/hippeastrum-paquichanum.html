Hippeastrum paquichanum

Hippeastrum paquichanum, commonly known as the Paquichan amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and indoor spaces.
