Hieracium squalidiceps

Hieracium squalidiceps, commonly known as the squalid-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disc florets.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
