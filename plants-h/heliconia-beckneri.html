Heliconia beckneri

Heliconia beckneri, commonly known as the Beckner's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces elongated, tubular inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance. Its leaves are broad and palmately lobed, providing a lush, tropical aesthetic.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
