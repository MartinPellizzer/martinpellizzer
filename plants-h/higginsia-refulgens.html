Higginsia refulgens

Higginsia refulgens, commonly known as the glittering higginsia, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has a unique adaptation to its harsh environment, featuring a succulent structure that helps it store water. Its blooming period typically occurs during the cooler months, adding a fleeting but beautiful display to the landscape.

Higginsia refulgens is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and limited distribution.
