Hieracium repandifrons

Hieracium repandifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
