Hymenophyllum fendlerianum

Hymenophyllum fendlerianum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures, giving it a silvery appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are short and often form dense colonies, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important species in the study of epiphytic flora.
