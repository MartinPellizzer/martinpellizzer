Hieracium tephropogon subsp. tephropogon

Hieracium tephropogon subsp.

tephropogon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in various soil conditions.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
