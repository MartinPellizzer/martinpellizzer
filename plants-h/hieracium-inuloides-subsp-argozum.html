Hieracium inuloides subsp. argozum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

argozum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
