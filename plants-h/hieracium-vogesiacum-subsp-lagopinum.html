Hieracium vogesiacum subsp. lagopinum

Hieracium vogesiacum subsp.

lagopinum, commonly known as the Lagopine Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky slopes.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with both ray and disk florets. Its leaves are basal and alternate, often with a rough or hairy texture.

It is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
