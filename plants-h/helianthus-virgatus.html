Helianthus virgatus

Helianthus virgatus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a height that can reach up to 2 meters.

Helianthus virgatus is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in marshy areas.
