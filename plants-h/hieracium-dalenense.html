Hieracium dalenense

Hieracium dalenense, commonly known as the Dutch hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium dalenense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
