Hyptis simplex

Hyptis simplex, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis simplex thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.
