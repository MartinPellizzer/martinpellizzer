Hymenophyllum gracilius

Hymenophyllum gracilius, commonly known as the slender filmy fern, is a member of the Hymenophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It is a epiphytic plant, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are usually narrow, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in small, clustered structures called sorus.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures.
