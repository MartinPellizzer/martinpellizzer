Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. congenitum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

congenitum, commonly known as the wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is a common species in the genus Hieracium, which includes many other hawkweeds with similar ecological and botanical traits.
