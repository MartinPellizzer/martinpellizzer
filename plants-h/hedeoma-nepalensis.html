Hedeoma nepalensis

Hedeoma nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal hedeoma, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, opposite leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Hedeoma nepalensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
