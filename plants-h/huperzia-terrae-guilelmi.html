Huperzia terrae-guilelmi

Huperzia terrae-guilelmi, commonly known as the Guilelmii spikemoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves and a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread along the forest floor. Native to the humid temperate regions of Europe, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. The plant's sporophytes produce spores on the underside of its leaves, which are dispersed by wind to grow into new plants.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
