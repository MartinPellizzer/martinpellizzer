Huperzia formosana

Huperzia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features whorled leaves that are lanceolate in shape and often have a waxy coating. Huperzia formosana is characterized by its unique reproductive structures, including strobila that produce spores.

This species is native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
