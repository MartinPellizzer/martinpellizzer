Hemesteum parvulum

Hemesteum parvulum, commonly known as the small hemesteum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Hemesteum parvulum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add subtle beauty to shaded garden spaces.
