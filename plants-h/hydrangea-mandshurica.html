Hydrangea mandshurica

Hydrangea mandshurica, commonly known as the Korean hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is characterized by its serrated, dark green leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are composed of both sterile and fertile florets.

Hydrangea mandshurica thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
