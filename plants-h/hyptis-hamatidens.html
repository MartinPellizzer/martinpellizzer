Hyptis hamatidens

Hyptis hamatidens, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to violet, attracting various pollinators.

Hyptis hamatidens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
