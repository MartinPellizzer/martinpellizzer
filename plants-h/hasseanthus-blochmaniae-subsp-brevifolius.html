Hasseanthus blochmaniae subsp. brevifolius

Hasseanthus blochmaniae subsp.

brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved star cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardens.
