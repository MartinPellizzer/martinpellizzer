Hyptis muelleri

Hyptis muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
