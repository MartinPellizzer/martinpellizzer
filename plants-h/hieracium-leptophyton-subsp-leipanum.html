Hieracium leptophyton subsp. leipanum

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

leipanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It produces small, dark brown achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal potential in traditional herbal use.
