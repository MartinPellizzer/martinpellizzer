Hieracium latidens

Hieracium latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, where it can form dense colonies.
