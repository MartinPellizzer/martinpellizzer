Hoya neoebudica

Hoya neoebudica, commonly known as the "Raspberry Hoya," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often tinged with pink or red hues. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya neoebudica is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
