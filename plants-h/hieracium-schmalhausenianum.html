Hieracium schmalhausenianum

Hieracium schmalhausenianum, commonly known as the Schmalhausen's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, often arranged in a single head.

It is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
