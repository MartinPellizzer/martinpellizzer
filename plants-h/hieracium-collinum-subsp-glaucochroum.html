Hieracium collinum subsp. glaucochroum

Hieracium collinum subsp.

glaucochroum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with a distinctive bluish-green hue on the upper surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
