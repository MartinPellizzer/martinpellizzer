Hesperolaburnum platycarpum

Hesperolaburnum platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited laburnum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
