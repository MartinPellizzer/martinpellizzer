Hieracium maculatum var. aurelianense

Hieracium maculatum var. aurelianense, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
