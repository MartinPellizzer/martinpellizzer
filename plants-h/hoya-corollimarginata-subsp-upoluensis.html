Hoya corollimarginata subsp. upoluensis

Hoya corollimarginata subsp.

upoluensis, commonly known as the Upolu Hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is popular among succulent and foliage enthusiasts due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
