Heteranthera liebmannii

Heteranthera liebmannii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in wetland environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it thrives in moist, sunny habitats.

Heteranthera liebmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
