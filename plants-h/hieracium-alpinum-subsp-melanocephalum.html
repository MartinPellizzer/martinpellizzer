Hieracium alpinum subsp. melanocephalum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

melanocephalum, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are surrounded by a distinctive dark brown or black involucral bracts. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
