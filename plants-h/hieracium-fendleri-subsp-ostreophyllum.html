Hieracium fendleri subsp. ostreophyllum

Hieracium fendleri subsp.

ostreophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head made up of numerous small florets.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to arid environments.
