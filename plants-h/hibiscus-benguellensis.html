Hibiscus benguellensis

Hibiscus benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguela hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Hibiscus benguellensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
