Hexaplectris bicolor

Hexaplectris bicolor, commonly known as the pink spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Hexaplectris bicolor plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
