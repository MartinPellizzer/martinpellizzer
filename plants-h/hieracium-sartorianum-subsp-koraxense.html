Hieracium sartorianum subsp. koraxense

Hieracium sartorianum subsp.

koraxense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
