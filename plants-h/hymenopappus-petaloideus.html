Hymenopappus petaloideus

Hymenopappus petaloideus, commonly known as the showy thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that transition to pink or red as they mature, followed by fluffy, white pappus hairs that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Hymenopappus petaloideus is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
