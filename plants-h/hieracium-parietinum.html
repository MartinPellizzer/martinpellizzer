Hieracium parietinum

Hieracium parietinum, commonly known as the wall groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found on walls, banks, and roadside areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn, and its leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Hieracium parietinum is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
