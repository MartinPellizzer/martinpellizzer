Habenaria unifoliata

Habenaria unifoliata, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its single, lance-shaped leaf and produces a raceme of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, with the lip being prominently marked.

Habenaria unifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
