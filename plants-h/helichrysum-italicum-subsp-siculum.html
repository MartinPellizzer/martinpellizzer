Helichrysum italicum subsp. siculum

Helichrysum italicum subsp.

siculum, commonly known as *Italian everlasting* or *scented everlasting*, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce numerous small, bright yellow to orange flowers that retain their color even after drying.

The plant has aromatic foliage with a distinctive, citrus-like scent, which is often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its resilience and ornamental value have contributed to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
