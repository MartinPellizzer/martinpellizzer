Hieracium eriophorum subsp. eriophorum

Hieracium eriophorum subsp.

eriophorum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and other damp habitats.

Its subspecies name, eriophorum, refers to the hairy appearance of the plant, which is a distinctive botanical feature.
