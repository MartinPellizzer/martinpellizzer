Herissantia dressleri

Herissantia dressleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a rich texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Herissantia dressleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
