Hieracium lapeyrousei

Hieracium lapeyrousei, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
