Hieracium haemophaeum

Hieracium haemophaeum, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often reddish in color.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
