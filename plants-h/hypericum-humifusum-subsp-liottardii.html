Hypericum humifusum subsp. liottardii

Hypericum humifusum subsp.

liottardii, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
