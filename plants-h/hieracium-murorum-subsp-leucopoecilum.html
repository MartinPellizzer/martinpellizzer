Hieracium murorum subsp. leucopoecilum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

leucopoecilum, commonly known as the white-spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its flowers are surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with low nutrient levels.
