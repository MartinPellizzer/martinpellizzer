Hypericum myrtifolium

Hypericum myrtifolium, commonly known as rosehip or red currant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Hypericum myrtifolium thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
