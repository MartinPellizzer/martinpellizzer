Hypericum barbatum subsp. calabricum

Hypericum barbatum subsp.

calabricum, commonly known as the Calabrian St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features opposite, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are typically reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
