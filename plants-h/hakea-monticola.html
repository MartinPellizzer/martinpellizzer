Hakea monticola

Hakea monticola, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes, typically ranging in color from yellow to orange. Hakea monticola is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
