Helena × gracilis

Helena × gracilis, commonly known as the slender helena, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Helena tetracantha and Helena fragrans.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Helena × gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
