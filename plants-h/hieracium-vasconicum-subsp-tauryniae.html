Hieracium vasconicum subsp. tauryniae

Hieracium vasconicum subsp.

tauryniae, commonly known as the Taurynian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky soils and slopes.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are usually lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on slender peduncles.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.
