Habzelia obtusifolia

Habzelia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved habzelia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
