Hymenophyllum silveirae

Hymenophyllum silveirae, commonly known as the silver moss, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are thin and papery, with a silvery or grayish hue due to the dense covering of tiny, scale-like structures.

Hymenophyllum silveirae plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
