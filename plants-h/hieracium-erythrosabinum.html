Hieracium erythrosabinum

Hieracium erythrosabinum, commonly known as the red-berried hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
