Hippocrepis ambigua

Hippocrepis ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are basal, linear to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which are usually pale blue or purple, bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
