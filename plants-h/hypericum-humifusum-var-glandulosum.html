Hypericum humifusum var. glandulosum

Hypericum humifusum var. glandulosum, commonly known as sticky St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a sticky texture, which is a distinguishing characteristic. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
