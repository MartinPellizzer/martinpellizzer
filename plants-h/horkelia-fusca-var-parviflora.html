Horkelia fusca var. parviflora

Horkelia fusca var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered horkelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers.
