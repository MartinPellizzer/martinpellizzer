Hibiscus micranthus var. clandestinus

Hibiscus micranthus var. clandestinus, commonly known as the clandestine hibiscus, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the hibiscus genus.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to specific regions in Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
