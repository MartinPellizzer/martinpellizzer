Habenaria singularis

Habenaria singularis, commonly known as the singular orchid, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and relatively rare occurrence in the wild.
