Hieracium prassivae

Hieracium prassivae, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its clustered, yellowish-green flowers and hairy, basal leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant has a creeping rootstock and can form dense mats, which helps it spread easily.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
