Hippeastrum bukasovii

Hippeastrum bukasovii, commonly known as the Bukasov's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can be red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are typically broad and smooth, emerging from a fleshy underground bulb. Its blooming period usually occurs in the summer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
