Hydrophyllum albifrons

Hydrophyllum albifrons, commonly known as white-fringed sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with white-fringed petals, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

Hydrophyllum albifrons plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems and is a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
