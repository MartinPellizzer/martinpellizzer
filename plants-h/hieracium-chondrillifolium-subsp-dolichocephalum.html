Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. dolichocephalum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

dolichocephalum, commonly known as the long-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually hairy as well. This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus, with specific adaptations to its ecological niche.
