Hookera uniflora

Hookera uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower hooker, is a rare alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces a single, showy blue or violet flower that blooms in late summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Hookera uniflora is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
