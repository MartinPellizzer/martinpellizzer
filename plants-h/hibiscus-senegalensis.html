Hibiscus senegalensis

Hibiscus senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Senegal, Mali, and Sudan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring thick, rough bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the dry season. Hibiscus senegalensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local cultures.
