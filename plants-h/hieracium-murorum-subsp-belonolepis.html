Hieracium murorum subsp. belonolepis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

belonolepis, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant has basal rosettes of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
