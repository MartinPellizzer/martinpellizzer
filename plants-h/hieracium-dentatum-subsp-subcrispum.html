Hieracium dentatum subsp. subcrispum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

subcrispum, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically serrated and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaves and slightly curled leaf margins.
