Hedysarum marilandicum

Hedysarum marilandicum, commonly known as Maryland briers, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It produces distinctive, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
