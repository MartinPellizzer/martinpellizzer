Hypochaeris spinuligera

Hypochaeris spinuligera, commonly known as the prickly weed or spiny hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and sometimes branched.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, making it a common sight in various habitats across its native range.
