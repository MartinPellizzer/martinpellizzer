Hypopitys fimbriata

Hypopitys fimbriata, commonly known as the fringe-leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like fronds that are often adorned with fine, fringe-like margins. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, typical of cycads, with male and female cones appearing on separate plants. Its leaves are evergreen and can grow quite large, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts for this rare botanical specimen.
