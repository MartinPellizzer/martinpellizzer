Helianthemum scariosum

Helianthemum scariosum, commonly known as the scaly rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has leathery, scaly leaves that are adapted to withstand dry, rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
