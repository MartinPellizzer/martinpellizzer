Hymenorebutia rebutioides

Hymenorebutia rebutioides, commonly known as the Rebutia-like Hymenorebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Hymenorebutia rebutioides is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.
