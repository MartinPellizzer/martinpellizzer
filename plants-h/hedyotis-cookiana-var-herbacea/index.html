Hedyotis Cookiana Var. Herbacea

Herbaceous hedyotis, scientifically known as Hedyotis cookiana var. herbacea, is a delicate flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia. This low-growing perennial is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike. Despite its beauty, the plant remains relatively obscure and is rarely cultivated outside its natural habitat.
