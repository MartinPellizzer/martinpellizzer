Hieracium durisetum

Hieracium durisetum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
