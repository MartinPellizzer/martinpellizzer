Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. apertorum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

apertorum, commonly known as the open-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is upright and often branched. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
