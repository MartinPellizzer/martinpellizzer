Hexasepalum scandens

Hexasepalum scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that allow it to climb and spread over other vegetation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
