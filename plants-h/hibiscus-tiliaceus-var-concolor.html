Hibiscus tiliaceus var. concolor

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. concolor, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in coastal areas, mangroves, and sandy soils, thriving in humid, saline environments. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to coastal conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
