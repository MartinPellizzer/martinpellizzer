Hibiscus involucellatus

Hibiscus involucellatus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus involucellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
