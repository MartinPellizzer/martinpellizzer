Helianthus petiolaris subsp. petiolaris

Helianthus petiolaris subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the slender sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, strap-shaped leaves and produces small, bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to local biodiversity.
