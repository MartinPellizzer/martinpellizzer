Helianthus peruvianus

Helianthus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian sunflower, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Helianthus peruvianus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
