Hippomarathrum cristatum var. pauciradiatum

Hippomarathrum cristatum var. pauciradiatum, commonly known as the spiny cactus or desert cactus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to the desert landscape. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The species is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a hardy example of desert flora.
