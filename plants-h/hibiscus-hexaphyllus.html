Hibiscus hexaphyllus

Hibiscus hexaphyllus, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus hexaphyllus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
