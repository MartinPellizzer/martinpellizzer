Hieracium grandiceps

Hieracium grandiceps, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium grandiceps is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
