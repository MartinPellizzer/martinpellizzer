Hedysarum humile var. speciosum

Hedysarum humile var. speciosum, commonly known as the small-flowered vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces slender, serpentine pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
