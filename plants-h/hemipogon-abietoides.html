Hemipogon abietoides

Hemipogon abietoides, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a small evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows at high elevations, often in cool and moist environments, and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals around the stems. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
