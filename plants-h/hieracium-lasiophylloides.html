Hieracium lasiophylloides

Hieracium lasiophylloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.
