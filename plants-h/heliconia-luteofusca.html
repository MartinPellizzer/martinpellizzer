Heliconia luteofusca

Heliconia luteofusca, commonly known as the orange heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a long, tubular spathe. Its leaves are broad, pointed, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia luteofusca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
