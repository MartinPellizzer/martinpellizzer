Hibiscus ankaramyensis var. grandistipulatus

Hibiscus ankaramyensis var. grandistipulatus, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant features palmate leaves with a broad, lobed shape, and its stems are covered in soft, hairy textures. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
