Hedyotis capitata

Hedyotis capitata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Hedyotis capitata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
