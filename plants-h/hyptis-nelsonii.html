Hyptis nelsonii

Hyptis nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically clustered in whorls along the stem, producing a delicate, fragrant display.

Hyptis nelsonii is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
