Hevea janeirensis

Hevea janeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant is notable for its latex-producing properties, which are harvested for natural rubber. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it plays a significant role in both biodiversity and natural resource management.
