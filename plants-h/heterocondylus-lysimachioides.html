Heterocondylus lysimachioides

Heterocondylus lysimachioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
