Hibiscus asperifolius

Hibiscus asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Hibiscus asperifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
