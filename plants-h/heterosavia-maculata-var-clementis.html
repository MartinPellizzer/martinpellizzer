Heterosavia maculata var. clementis

Heterosavia maculata var. clementis, commonly known as the Clement's spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
