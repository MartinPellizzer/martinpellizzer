Helianthus devernii

Helianthus devernii, commonly known as the Desert Sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features a robust stem with rough, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helianthus devernii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in harsh climates.
