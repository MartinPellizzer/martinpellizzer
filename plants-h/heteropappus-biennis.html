Heteropappus biennis

Heteropappus biennis, commonly known as the common fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers with a central disk and surrounding ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Heteropappus biennis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and prairies.
