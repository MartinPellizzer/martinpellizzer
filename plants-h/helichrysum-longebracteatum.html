Helichrysum longebracteatum

Helichrysum longebracteatum, commonly known as the long bract helichrysum, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow to golden-orange inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small flowers arranged in a compact head. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
