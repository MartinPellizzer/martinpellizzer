Hieracium triangulare subsp. asterocalathium

Hieracium triangulare subsp.

asterocalathium, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are usually triangular in shape, which is reflected in its scientific name, and it can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The species is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
