Hovea villosa

Hovea villosa, commonly known as the hairy hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, hairy leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Hovea villosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
