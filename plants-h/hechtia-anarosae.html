Hechtia anarosae

Hechtia anarosae, commonly known as the Anarosa hechtia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for hydration. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
