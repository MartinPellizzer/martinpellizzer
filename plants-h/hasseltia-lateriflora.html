Hasseltia lateriflora

Hasseltia lateriflora, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single cluster at the end of each stem.

Hasseltia lateriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
