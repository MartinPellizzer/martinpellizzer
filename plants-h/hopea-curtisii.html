Hopea curtisii

Hopea curtisii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Hopea curtisii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
