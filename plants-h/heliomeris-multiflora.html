Heliomeris multiflora

Heliomeris multiflora, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Heliomeris multiflora is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
