Helichrysum rufescens

Helichrysum rufescens, commonly known as the red-haired everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, often with a reddish tinge, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain colorful even when dried, adding to its ornamental value.
