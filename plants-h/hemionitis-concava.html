Hemionitis concava

Hemionitis concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap air and help it float. The plant has delicate, thread-like leaves that are often submerged, while its stems can be partially exposed. Hemionitis concava is native to regions such as North America and parts of Asia.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
