Horminum lyratum

Horminum lyratum, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, lantern-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
