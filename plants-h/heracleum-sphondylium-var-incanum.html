Heracleum sphondylium var. incanum

Heracleum sphondylium var. incanum, commonly known as the hoary cowparsley, is a variant of the common cowparsley.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It grows in open habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and woodland edges.

Its hairy foliage and distinctive appearance make it easily identifiable in its native range.
