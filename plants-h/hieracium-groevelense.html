Hieracium groevelense

Hieracium groevelense, commonly known as the Groevel's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of numerous yellow ray florets. Native to Europe, this species thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a perennial herb that produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist to moderately dry habitats.
