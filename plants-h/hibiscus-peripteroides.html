Hibiscus peripteroides

Hibiscus peripteroides, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Hibiscus peripteroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and regular watering.
