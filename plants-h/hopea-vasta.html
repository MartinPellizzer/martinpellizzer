Hopea vasta

Hopea vasta, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellowish-green, and it produces woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

Hopea vasta plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
