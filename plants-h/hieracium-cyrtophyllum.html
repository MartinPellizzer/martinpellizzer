Hieracium cyrtophyllum

Hieracium cyrtophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Due to its ecological role, it can be an important plant in supporting local insect populations.
