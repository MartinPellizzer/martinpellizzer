Habenaria triphylla

Habenaria triphylla, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with three petals and a distinctive lip, typically in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
