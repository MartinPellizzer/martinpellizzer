Hedyotis longipetala

Hedyotis longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Hedyotis longipetala is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
