Hieracium candelabrum

Hieracium candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium candelabrum is known for its ornamental value but can also be invasive in certain regions.
