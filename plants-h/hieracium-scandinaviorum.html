Hieracium scandinaviorum

Hieracium scandinaviorum, commonly known as the Scandinavian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
