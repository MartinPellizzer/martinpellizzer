Huperzia serrata var. intermedia

Huperzia serrata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate toothed huperzia, is a variant of the Huperzia serrata species, a type of clubmoss.

It is characterized by its distinct serrated leaf margins and a compact, low-growing habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a glossy green surface and a prominent midrib.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
