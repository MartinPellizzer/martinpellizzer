Hasseltia psittacarum

Hasseltia psittacarum, commonly known as the golden bell heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
