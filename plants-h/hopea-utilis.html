Hopea utilis

Hopea utilis, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic in shape with a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

Hopea utilis plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
