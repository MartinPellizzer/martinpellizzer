Hieracium sub-gelmianum

Hieracium sub-gelmianum, commonly known as the lesser spear thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright, spiny stems and basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
