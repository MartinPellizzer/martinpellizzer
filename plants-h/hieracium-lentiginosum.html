Hieracium lentiginosum

Hieracium lentiginosum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium lentiginosum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
