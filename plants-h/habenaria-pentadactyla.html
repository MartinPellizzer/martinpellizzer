Habenaria pentadactyla

Habenaria pentadactyla, commonly known as the five-finger orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces racemes of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
