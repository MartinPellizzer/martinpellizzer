Hieracium lachenalii subsp. aspernatulum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

aspernatulum, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous disc florets surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf pubescence and floral structure, making it a notable species within the Hieracium genus.
