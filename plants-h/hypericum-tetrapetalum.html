Hypericum tetrapetalum

Hypericum tetrapetalum, commonly known as the four-petaled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
