Hieracium florentinum subsp. porettanum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

porettanum, commonly known as the Porettan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
