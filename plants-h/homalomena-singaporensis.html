Homalomena singaporensis

Homalomena singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore arrowroot or Singapore caladium, is a tropical evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with variegation. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage, making it suitable for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
