Hieracium atratum subsp. polymelanum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

polymelanum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies polymelanum is distinguished by its darker foliage and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
