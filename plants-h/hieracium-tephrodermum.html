Hieracium tephrodermum

Hieracium tephrodermum, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in certain regions.
