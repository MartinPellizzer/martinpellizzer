Haemanthus pubescens subsp. pubescens

Haemanthus pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the Natal grass lily, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers during the summer months, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
