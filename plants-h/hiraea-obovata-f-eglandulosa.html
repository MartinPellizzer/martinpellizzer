Hiraea obovata f. eglandulosa

Hiraea obovata f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandless hiraea, is a variant of the hiraea plant characterized by its absence of glandular structures on the leaves.

This plant typically exhibits broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive subject for study in plant morphology and taxonomy.
