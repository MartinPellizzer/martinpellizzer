Hieracium caesium var. decolor

Hieracium caesium var. decolor, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish and hairy.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
