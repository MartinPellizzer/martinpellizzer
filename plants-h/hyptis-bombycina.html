Hyptis bombycina

Hyptis bombycina, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of purple or white.

Hyptis bombycina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
