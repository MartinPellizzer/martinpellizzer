Hakea hilliana

Hakea hilliana, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or club-shaped flowers, which are typically pink or red in color. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Hakea hilliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh coastal climates.
