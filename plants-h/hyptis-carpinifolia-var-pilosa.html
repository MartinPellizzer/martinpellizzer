Hyptis carpinifolia var. pilosa

Hyptis carpinifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the warm regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
