Hibiscus syriacus var. elegantissimus

Hibiscus syriacus var. elegantissimus, commonly known as the Persian silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, velvety, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
