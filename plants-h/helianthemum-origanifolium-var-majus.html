Helianthemum origanifolium var. majus

Helianthemum origanifolium var. majus, commonly known as the greater rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in dense clusters. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are similar in appearance to oregano, giving it its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
