Hieracium pisaturense subsp. eufiedleri

Hieracium pisaturense subsp.

eufiedleri, commonly known as the Eufiedler's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological traits and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
