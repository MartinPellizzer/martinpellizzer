Herniaria ciliolata

Herniaria ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy herniaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically forms a dense mat of prostrate stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with soft, silky hairs.

Herniaria ciliolata thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.
