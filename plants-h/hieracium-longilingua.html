Hieracium longilingua

Hieracium longilingua, commonly known as the longleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hieracium longilingua is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
