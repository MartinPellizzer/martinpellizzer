Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. bupleuroidiforme

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

bupleuroidiforme, commonly known as the purple-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive purple-tinged central disc.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and it is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
