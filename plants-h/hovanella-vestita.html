Hovanella vestita

Hovanella vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Hovanella vestita is also known for its ability to grow rapidly, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.
