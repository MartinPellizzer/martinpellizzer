Hypericum chilense var. muscoides

Hypericum chilense var. muscoides, commonly known as the Chilean St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, and it has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
