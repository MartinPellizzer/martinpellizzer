Hieracium auriculoides subsp. pannonicum

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a single head.

The subspecies pannonicum is distinguished by its specific morphology and habitat preferences compared to other Hieracium species.
