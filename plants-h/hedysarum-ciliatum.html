Hedysarum ciliatum

Hedysarum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky bartsia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has slender, creeping stems and is characterized by its finely toothed leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and rocky slopes.

Hedysarum ciliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
