Huperzia leishanensis

Huperzia leishanensis, commonly known as the Leishan clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan and Guizhou provinces. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobili. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

Huperzia leishanensis is of interest to botanists due to its ancient lineage and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
