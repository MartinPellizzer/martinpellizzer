Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. strictissimum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

strictissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, upright leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust growth habit and medicinal potential.
