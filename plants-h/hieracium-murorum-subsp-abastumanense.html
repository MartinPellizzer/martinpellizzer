Hieracium murorum subsp. abastumanense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

abastumanense, commonly known as the Abastumani hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
