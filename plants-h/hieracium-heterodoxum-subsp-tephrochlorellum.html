Hieracium heterodoxum subsp. tephrochlorellum

Hieracium heterodoxum subsp.

tephrochlorellum, commonly known as the tephrochlorellum hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, oblanceolate, and covered with fine hairs. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive floral morphology and ecological adaptability.
