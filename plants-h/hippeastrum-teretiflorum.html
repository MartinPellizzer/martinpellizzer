Hippeastrum teretiflorum

Hippeastrum teretiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, requiring well-drained soil and a period of dormancy for optimal flowering.

Hippeastrum teretiflorum is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
