Hypericum acutum subsp. boeticum

Hypericum acutum subsp.

boeticum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
