Hieracium caesium var. cambricum

Hieracium caesium var. cambricum, commonly known as the Welsh hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowering heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The species is native to western Europe, including parts of the United Kingdom and Ireland.
