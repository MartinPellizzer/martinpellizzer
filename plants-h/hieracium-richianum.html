Hieracium richianum

Hieracium richianum, commonly known as the richian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium richianum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
