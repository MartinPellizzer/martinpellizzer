Herniaria cyrenaica

Herniaria cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyprus sandwort, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of tiny white flowers. Its unique morphology allows it to survive in harsh, arid environments.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar adaptations to drought and salt.
