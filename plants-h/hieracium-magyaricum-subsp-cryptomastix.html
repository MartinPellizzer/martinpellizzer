Hieracium magyaricum subsp. cryptomastix

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

cryptomastix, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in its native range.
