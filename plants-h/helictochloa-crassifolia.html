Helictochloa crassifolia

Helictochloa crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit. The plant has flat, narrow leaves with a rough texture and is often found in open, sunny areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Helictochloa crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
