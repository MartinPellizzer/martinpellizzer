Hoya pandurata subsp. angustifolia

Hoya pandurata subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental value. Hoya pandurata subsp.

angustifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
