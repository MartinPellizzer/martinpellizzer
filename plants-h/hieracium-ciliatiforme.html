Hieracium ciliatiforme

Hieracium ciliatiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It is typically found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
