Hieracium floribundum subsp. regiomontanum

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

regiomontanum, commonly known as the Mexican oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a hairy stem and basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
