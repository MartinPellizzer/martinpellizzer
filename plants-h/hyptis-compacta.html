Hyptis compacta

Hyptis compacta, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant, minty scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis compacta is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
