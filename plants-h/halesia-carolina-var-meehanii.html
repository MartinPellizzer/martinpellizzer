Halesia carolina var. meehanii

Halesia carolina var. meehanii, commonly known as the Meehan's flowering dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, clustered flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The species is valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and insects.
