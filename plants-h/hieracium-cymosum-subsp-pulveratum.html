Hieracium cymosum subsp. pulveratum

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

pulveratum, commonly known as the powdery hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive powdery coating, which gives it its common name.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
