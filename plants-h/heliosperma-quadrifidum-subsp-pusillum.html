Heliosperma quadrifidum subsp. pusillum

Heliosperma quadrifidum subsp.

pusillum, commonly known as the small-flowered heliosperma, is a small, perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
