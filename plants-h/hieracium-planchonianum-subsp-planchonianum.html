Hieracium planchonianum subsp. planchonianum

Hieracium planchonianum subsp.

planchonianum, commonly known as the French dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green color.

The plant produces a single, tall, yellow flower head that blooms in the summer months. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
