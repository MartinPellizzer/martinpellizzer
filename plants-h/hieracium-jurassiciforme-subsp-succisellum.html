Hieracium jurassiciforme subsp. succisellum

Hieracium jurassiciforme subsp.

succisellum, commonly known as the Jurassic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow composite flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy inflorescence.

The species is adapted to calcareous soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
