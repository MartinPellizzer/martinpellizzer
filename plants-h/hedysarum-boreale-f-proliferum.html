Hedysarum boreale f. proliferum

Hedysarum boreale f. proliferum, commonly known as the Arctic peavine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in open, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped.

Hedysarum boreale f. proliferum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes.
