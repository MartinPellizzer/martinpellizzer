Honckenya parva

Honckenya parva, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in elongated spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Honckenya parva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
