Hopea jucunda

Hopea jucunda, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Hopea jucunda plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
