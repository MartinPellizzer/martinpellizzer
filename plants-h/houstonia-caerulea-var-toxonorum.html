Houstonia caerulea var. toxonorum

Houstonia caerulea var. toxonorum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a low, spreading habit. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
