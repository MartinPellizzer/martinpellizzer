Hieracium biforme

Hieracium biforme, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium biforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
