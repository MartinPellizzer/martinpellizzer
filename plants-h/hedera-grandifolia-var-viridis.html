Hedera grandifolia var. viridis

Hedera grandifolia var. viridis, commonly known as the giant ivy, is a variant of the common ivy species.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broader and more elongated than those of the standard Hedera species. This plant is a climbing vine that can grow quite vigorously, often reaching lengths of several meters. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The varietal name 'viridis' refers to its vibrant green coloration, making it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and as a houseplant.
