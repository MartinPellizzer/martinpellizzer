Hieracium pseudosericeum

Hieracium pseudosericeum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, whitish down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium pseudosericeum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
