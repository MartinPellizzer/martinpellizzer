Hieracium pannonicum subsp. asperrimum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

asperrimum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
