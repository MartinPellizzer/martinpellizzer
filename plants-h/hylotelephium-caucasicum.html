Hylotelephium caucasicum

Hylotelephium caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among gardeners.
