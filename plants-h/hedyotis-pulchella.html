Hedyotis pulchella

Hedyotis pulchella, commonly known as the pretty false pennywort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are white or pale green, arranged in clusters and often mistaken for those of similar species.

Hedyotis pulchella is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
