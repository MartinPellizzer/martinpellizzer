Helicophyllum crassifolium

Helicophyllum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf helicophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette growth form and large, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense terminal cluster, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Helicophyllum crassifolium is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
