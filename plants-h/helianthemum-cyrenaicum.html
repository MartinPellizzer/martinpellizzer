Helianthemum cyrenaicum

Helianthemum cyrenaicum, commonly known as the Crete sandthrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months, adding a bright splash of color to rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Helianthemum cyrenaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
