Hydnocarpus pendulus

Hydnocarpus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Saraceniaceae and is characterized by its drooping, pendulous fruits that resemble small nuts. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are encased in a hard, woody shell and contain a single seed.
