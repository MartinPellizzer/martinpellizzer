Hyalocalyx setifer

Hyalocalyx setifer, commonly known as the hairy calyx orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called setae. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors depending on the species.

Hyalocalyx setifer plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
