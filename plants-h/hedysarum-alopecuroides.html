Hedysarum alopecuroides

Hedysarum alopecuroides, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hedysarum alopecuroides is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in herbal medicine.
