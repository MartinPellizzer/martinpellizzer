Hoya radicalis

Hoya radicalis, commonly known as the "Climbing Wanda," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly glossy texture. Hoya radicalis thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
