Hygrophila prunelloides

Hygrophila prunelloides, commonly known as the purple hygrophila, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Hygrophila prunelloides is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
