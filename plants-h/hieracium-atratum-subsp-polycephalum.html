Hieracium atratum subsp. polycephalum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

polycephalum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its spreading growth habit, it can form dense stands, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed in certain environments.
