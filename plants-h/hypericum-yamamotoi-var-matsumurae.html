Hypericum yamamotoi var. matsumurae

Hypericum yamamotoi var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly rough texture and produces small, red-orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique flower structure.
