Hieracium cataethalum

Hieracium cataethalum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots form a fibrous network.

Hieracium cataethalum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
