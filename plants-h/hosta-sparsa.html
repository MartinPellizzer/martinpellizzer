Hosta sparsa

Hosta sparsa, commonly known as the sparseleaf hosta, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often lighter in color compared to other hosta varieties. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue during the summer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hosta sparsa is valued for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice for woodland gardens.
