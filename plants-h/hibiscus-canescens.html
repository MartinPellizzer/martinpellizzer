Hibiscus canescens

Hibiscus canescens, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white to pale pink flowers with a prominent central red stigma. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and grows as an erect shrub or small tree. Hibiscus canescens thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found near coastal areas.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also have traditional uses in herbal medicine.
