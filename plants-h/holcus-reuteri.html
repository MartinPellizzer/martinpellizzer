Holcus reuteri

Holcus reuteri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-ranked flowers.

Holcus reuteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
