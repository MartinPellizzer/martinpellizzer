Hieracium glaciale subsp. pseudoglaciale

Hieracium glaciale subsp.

pseudoglaciale, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, displaying resilience to cold and strong winds.
