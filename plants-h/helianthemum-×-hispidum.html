Helianthemum × hispidum

Helianthemum × hispidum, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sunny habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
