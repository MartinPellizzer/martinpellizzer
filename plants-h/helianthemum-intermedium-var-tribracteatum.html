Helianthemum intermedium var. tribracteatum

Helianthemum intermedium var. tribracteatum, commonly known as the three-bract rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is distinguished by its unique three-bract structure at the base of each flower cluster.
