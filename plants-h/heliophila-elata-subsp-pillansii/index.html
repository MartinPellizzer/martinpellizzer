Heliophila Elata Subsp. Pillansii

Pillans' heliophila, scientifically known as Heliophila elata subsp. pillansii, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. This subspecies is distinguished by its vibrant blue and purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to the otherwise barren landscape. It thrives in rocky soils and full sunlight, making it a resilient plant adapted to harsh environments. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable component of local biodiversity. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique species from habitat loss and overharvesting.
