Haplopappus venetus var. hartwegii

Haplopappus venetus var. hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's hairyleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery pappus. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and dry conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
