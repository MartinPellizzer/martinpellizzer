Hyptidendron conspersum

Hyptidendron conspersum, commonly known as the rough hyptidendron, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Hyptidendron conspersum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
