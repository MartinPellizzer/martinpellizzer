Herpestis laxiflora var. scabra

Herpestis laxiflora var. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf false holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in terminal spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
