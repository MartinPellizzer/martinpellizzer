Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. pachylum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

pachylum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat head.

It is notable for its translucent, waxy coating on the leaves, which gives it its common name.
