Hemizonia congesta subsp. clevelandii

Hemizonia congesta subsp.

clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
