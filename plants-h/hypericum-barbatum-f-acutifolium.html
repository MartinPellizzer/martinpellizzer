Hypericum barbatum f. acutifolium

Hypericum barbatum f. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to Europe and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
