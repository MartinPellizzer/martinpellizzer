Hieracium bifidum subsp. gracilicaule

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

gracilicaule, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
