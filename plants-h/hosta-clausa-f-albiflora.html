Hosta clausa f. albiflora

Hosta clausa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered enclosure hosta, is a cultivar of the hosta plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, dense foliage that forms a tight clump, giving it its name "clausa," which means "closed" or "enclosed.". The plant produces pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to the landscape. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in shady, moist environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in shaded garden beds.
