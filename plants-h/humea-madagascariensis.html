Humea madagascariensis

Humea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the island's rainforests. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Humea madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
