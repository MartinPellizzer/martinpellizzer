Heliconia tarumaensis

Heliconia tarumaensis, commonly known as the Tarumaensis heliconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Heliconiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are used to attract pollinators. Its inflorescence is a striking, spiral-shaped structure that houses the small, true flowers.

Heliconia tarumaensis is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
