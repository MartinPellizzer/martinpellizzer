Hieracium xestocarenum

Hieracium xestocarenum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
