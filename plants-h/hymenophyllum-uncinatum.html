Hymenophyllum uncinatum

It is a small, acrocarpous moss that typically grows on moist, shaded substrates such as rocks and tree trunks. Hymenophyllum uncinatum is characterized by its spore-producing structures called sporophytes, which are typically short and club-shaped.
