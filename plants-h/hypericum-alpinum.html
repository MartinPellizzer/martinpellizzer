Hypericum alpinum

Hypericum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments. It is characterized by its woody stems and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's botanical name reflects its association with the genus Hypericum, which includes many species with similar medicinal properties.
