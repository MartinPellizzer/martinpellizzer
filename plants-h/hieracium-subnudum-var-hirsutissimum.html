Hieracium subnudum var. hirsutissimum

Hieracium subnudum var. hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
