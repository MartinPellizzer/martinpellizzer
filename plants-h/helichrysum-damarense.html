Helichrysum damarense

Helichrysum damarense, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flowers that resemble miniature suns. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Helichrysum damarense is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
