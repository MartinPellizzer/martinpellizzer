Hypericum phellos

Hypericum phellos, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces orange or red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Hypericum phellos thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
