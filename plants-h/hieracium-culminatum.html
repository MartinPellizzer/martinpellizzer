Hieracium culminatum

Hieracium culminatum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium culminatum is characterized by its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in alpine ecosystems.
