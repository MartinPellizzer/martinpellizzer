Hyptis vitifolia

Hyptis vitifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. Hyptis vitifolia produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
