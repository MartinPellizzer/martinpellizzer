Haageocereus peniculatus

Haageocereus peniculatus, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
