Hyptis elegans var. campicola

Hyptis elegans var. campicola, commonly known as the mountain hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
