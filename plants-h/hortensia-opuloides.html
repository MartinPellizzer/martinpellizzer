Hortensia opuloides

Hortensia opuloides, commonly known as the giant hortensia, is a striking perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and often colorful inflorescences that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Hortensia opuloides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
