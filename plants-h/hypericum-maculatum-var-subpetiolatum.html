Hypericum maculatum var. subpetiolatum

Hypericum maculatum var. subpetiolatum, commonly known as the spotted yellow hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
