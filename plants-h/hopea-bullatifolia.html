Hopea bullatifolia

Hopea bullatifolia, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins.

Hopea bullatifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
