Hemionitis myriophylla

Hemionitis myriophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed hemionitis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which bloom during the short growing season. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture.
