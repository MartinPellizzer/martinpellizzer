Hymenophyllum batuense

Hymenophyllum batuense, commonly known as the Batu Hymenophyll, is a rare and delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with intricate, lace-like structures on its leaves, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like trichomes. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hymenophyllum batuense is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
