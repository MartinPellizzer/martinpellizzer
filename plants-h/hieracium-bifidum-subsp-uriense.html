Hieracium bifidum subsp. uriense

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

uriense, commonly known as the Uri hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
