Hoffmannseggia glauca

Hoffmannseggia glauca, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spiny bracts around its flower heads.

Hoffmannseggia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
