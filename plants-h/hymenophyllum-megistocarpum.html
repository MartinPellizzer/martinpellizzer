Hymenophyllum megistocarpum

Hymenophyllum megistocarpum, commonly known as the giant fernlet, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by botanists and enthusiasts of rare plants.
