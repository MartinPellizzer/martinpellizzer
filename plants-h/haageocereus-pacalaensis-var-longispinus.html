Haageocereus pacalaensis var. longispinus

Haageocereus pacalaensis var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This cactus is often cultivated for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
