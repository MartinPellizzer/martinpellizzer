Helenium elegans var. amphibolum

Helenium elegans var. amphibolum, commonly known as the amphibole helenium, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flower heads, which are composed of ray florets and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and adding color to wetland landscapes.
