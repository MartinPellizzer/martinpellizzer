Heuchera squamosa

Heuchera squamosa, commonly known as the silver queen heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white to pale green foliage, which is often adorned with red or purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera squamosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
