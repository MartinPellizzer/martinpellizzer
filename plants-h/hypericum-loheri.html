Hypericum loheri

Hypericum loheri, commonly known as the Loher's St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces red-orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
