Hermannia testacea

Hermannia testacea, commonly known as the red-eyed cycad, is a unique plant with distinctive botanical features.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Africa. The plant has a short, stout trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer that resembles a testacean structure, hence its scientific name. Its leaves are large, pinnate, and often have a reddish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Hermannia testacea is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
