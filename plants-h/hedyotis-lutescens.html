Hedyotis lutescens

Hedyotis lutescens, commonly known as the golden shieldplant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hedyotis lutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
