Helichrysum × ambiguum

Helichrysum × ambiguum, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to produce long-lasting, golden-yellow flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is aromatic and slightly hairy, adding a tactile element to its appearance.

Helichrysum × ambiguum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
