Hordeum maritimum f. hirtellum

Hordeum maritimum f. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy wild barley, is a botanical variant of the wild barley species.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, which distinguish it from other forms of Hordeum maritimum. This plant is typically found in coastal and saline environments, adapting to harsh conditions. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to regions such as southern Europe and western Asia.

Its morphology includes compact growth habits and small, spikelet-based inflorescences.
