Hibiscus aethiopicus var. angustifolius

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and serrated, with a striking appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
