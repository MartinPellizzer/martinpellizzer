Hieracium ericeticola

Hieracium ericeticola, commonly known as the heath hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often found in heathlands and moorlands. The plant features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Hieracium ericeticola is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
