Hypericum mutilum var. parviflorum

Hypericum mutilum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
