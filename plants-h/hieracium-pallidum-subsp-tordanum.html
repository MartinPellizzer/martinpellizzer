Hieracium pallidum subsp. tordanum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

tordanum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant has yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and the presence of glandular dots on its stems and leaves.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
