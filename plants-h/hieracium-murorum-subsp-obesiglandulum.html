Hieracium murorum subsp. obesiglandulum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

obesiglandulum, commonly known as the fat-gland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its enlarged, glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands.

This plant is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
