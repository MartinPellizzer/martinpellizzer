Hieracium × tricolor

Hieracium × tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color hawkweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
