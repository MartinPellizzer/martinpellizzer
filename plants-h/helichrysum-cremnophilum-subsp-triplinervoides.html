Helichrysum cremnophilum subsp. triplinervoides

Helichrysum cremnophilum subsp.

triplinervoides, commonly known as the three-veined helichrysum, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are typical of the genus Helichrysum. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
