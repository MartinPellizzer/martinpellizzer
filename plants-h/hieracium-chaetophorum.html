Hieracium chaetophorum

Hieracium chaetophorum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in temperate regions and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
