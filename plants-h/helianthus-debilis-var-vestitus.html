Helianthus debilis var. vestitus

Helianthus debilis var. vestitus, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features soft, silvery-white hairs covering its leaves, giving it a distinctive, frosty appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
