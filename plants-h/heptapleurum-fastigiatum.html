Heptapleurum fastigiatum

Heptapleurum fastigiatum, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for hedges and garden landscapes.
