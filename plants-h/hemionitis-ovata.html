Hemionitis ovata

Hemionitis ovata, commonly known as the water fern, is a small aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Asia.

It has delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in marshy areas. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores being the primary method of propagation.

Hemionitis ovata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
