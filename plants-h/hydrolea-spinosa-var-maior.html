Hydrolea spinosa var. maior

Hydrolea spinosa var. maior, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, wiry stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on cliffs or among boulders. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a popular subject for botanists and gardeners alike.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
