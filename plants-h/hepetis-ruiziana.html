Hepetis ruiziana

Hepetis ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully unfold. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
