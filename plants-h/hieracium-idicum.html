Hieracium idicum

Hieracium idicum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium idicum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
