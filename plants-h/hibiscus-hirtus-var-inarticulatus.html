Hibiscus hirtus var. inarticulatus

Hibiscus hirtus var. inarticulatus, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often glandular and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to warm climates.
