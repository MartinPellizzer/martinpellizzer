Hyoseris virginica

Hyoseris virginica, commonly known as Virginia ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and pond edges. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer.

Virginia ironweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
