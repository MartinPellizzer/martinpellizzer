Hardenbergia alba

Hardenbergia alba, commonly known as the white kudzu, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by seed pods that mature in summer.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
