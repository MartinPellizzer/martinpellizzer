Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. romanorum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

romanorum, commonly known as the Roman hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces achenes as its reproductive structures.

It is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
