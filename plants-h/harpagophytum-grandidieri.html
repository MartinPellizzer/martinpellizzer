Harpagophytum grandidieri

Harpagophytum grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar pencil cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical stems that are covered in a layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, spiny fruits. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also considered a symbol of Madagascar's biodiversity.
