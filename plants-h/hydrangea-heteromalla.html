Hydrangea heteromalla

Hydrangea heteromalla, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of serrated, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall.

Hydrangea heteromalla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
