Helinus lanceolatus

Helinus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helinus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Helinus lanceolatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
