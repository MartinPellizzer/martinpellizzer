Hyptis densiflora var. dolichodon

Hyptis densiflora var. dolichodon, commonly known as the long-flowered hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its flowers, which are usually pink to purple in color, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
