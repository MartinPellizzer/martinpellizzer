Hariota sarmentacea

Hariota sarmentacea, commonly known as the creeping hariota, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside.

Hariota sarmentacea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
