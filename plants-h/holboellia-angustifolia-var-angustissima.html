Holboellia angustifolia var. angustissima

Holboellia angustifolia var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf holboellia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
