Hesperis septulata

Hesperis septulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Hesperis septulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
