Homalanthus crinitus

Homalanthus crinitus, commonly known as the curly-top grass or giant curly-top grass, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and distinctive, curly leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
