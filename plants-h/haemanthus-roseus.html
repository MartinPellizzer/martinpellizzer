Haemanthus roseus

Haemanthus roseus, commonly known as the red-veined oxalis, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features bright green, arrow-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom reliably under proper care.
