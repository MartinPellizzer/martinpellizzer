Hoya browniana

Hoya browniana, commonly known as the Brown's Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Hoya browniana is a popular houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying properties.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored choice among indoor gardeners.
