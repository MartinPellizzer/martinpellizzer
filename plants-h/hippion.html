Hippion

Hippion, commonly known as the wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and aromatic applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Hippion thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been recognized for centuries, particularly for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
