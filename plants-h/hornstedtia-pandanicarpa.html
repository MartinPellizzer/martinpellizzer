Hornstedtia pandanicarpa

Hornstedtia pandanicarpa, commonly known as the pandan fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with a slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that resemble pineapples, which are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
