Hieracium × latisquamum subsp. pseudomacranthum

Hieracium × latisquamum subsp.

pseudomacranthum, commonly known as the broad-scale hawkweed, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive, often hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
