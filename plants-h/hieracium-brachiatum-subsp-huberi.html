Hieracium brachiatum subsp. huberi

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

huberi, commonly known as the Huber's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. It is a perennial herb that produces numerous small, herbaceous stems bearing clusters of tiny florets.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
