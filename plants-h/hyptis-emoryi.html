Hyptis emoryi

Hyptis emoryi, commonly known as Emory's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Hyptis emoryi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
