Heliocarpus microcarpus

Heliocarpus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood and small, round fruits that are often covered in a thick, woody husk. The plant typically grows in moist, lowland forests and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

The species is valued for its durable timber and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
