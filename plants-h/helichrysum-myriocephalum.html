Helichrysum myriocephalum

Helichrysum myriocephalum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, yellow to orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical seed heads that persist on the plant for a long time, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Helichrysum myriocephalum is often used in ornamental gardening and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
