Haplophragma serratum

Haplophragma serratum, commonly known as the serrated fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant. The species belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers. Haplophragma serratum is a perennial herb that thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.
