Heliconia schumanniana var. acreana

Heliconia schumanniana var. acreana, commonly known as the Acrean heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are typically hidden within the leaf-like bracts. Its inflorescence is pendulous, hanging from the plant like a curtain, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Heliconia.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
