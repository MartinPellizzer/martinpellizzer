Hypoestes gracilis

Hypoestes gracilis, commonly known as the "blue mist shrub," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that resemble mist, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoestes gracilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
