Hieracium zizianum subsp. cymellanthum

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

cymellanthum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem with yellow disk flowers.

The plant has a taproot system and produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
