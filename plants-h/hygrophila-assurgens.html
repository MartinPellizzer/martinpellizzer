Hygrophila assurgens

Hygrophila assurgens, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant blue or violet flowers. This species thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Hygrophila assurgens is often used in ornamental gardening and can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic insects.
