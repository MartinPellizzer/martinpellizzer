Hieracium subramosum subsp. scotocranum

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

scotocranum, commonly known as the Scottish benthead hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles. It typically grows in grassy areas, heaths, and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly zigzagged appearance.

It is part of the dandelion family (Compositae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
