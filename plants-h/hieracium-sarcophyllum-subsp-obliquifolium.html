Hieracium sarcophyllum subsp. obliquifolium

Hieracium sarcophyllum subsp.

obliquifolium, commonly known as the oblique-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive oblique base, giving it its subspecies name.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.
