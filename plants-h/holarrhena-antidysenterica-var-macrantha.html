Holarrhena antidysenterica var. macrantha

Holarrhena antidysenterica var. macrantha, commonly known as the Indian cockroach tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine, due to the presence of alkaloids in its bark and leaves.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
