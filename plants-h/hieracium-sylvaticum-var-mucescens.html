Hieracium sylvaticum var. mucescens

Hieracium sylvaticum var. mucescens, commonly known as the mossy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a moss-like appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
