Huperzia tenuicaulis

Huperzia tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores on the underside of its fronds, which are released during the dry season.

Huperzia tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
