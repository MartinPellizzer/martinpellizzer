Hyptis lappacea

Hyptis lappacea, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to blue. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value.

Hyptis lappacea is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
