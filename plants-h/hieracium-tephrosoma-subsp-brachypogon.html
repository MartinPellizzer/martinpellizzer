Hieracium tephrosoma subsp. brachypogon

Hieracium tephrosoma subsp.

brachypogon, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.

It is characterized by its compact growth form and is part of the Asteraceae family.
