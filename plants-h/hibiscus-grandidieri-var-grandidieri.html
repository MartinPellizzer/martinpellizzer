Hibiscus grandidieri var. grandidieri

Hibiscus grandidieri var. grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough, hairy texture, while the stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

This species is well adapted to the arid and semi-arid conditions of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
