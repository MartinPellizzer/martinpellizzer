Hieracium crassulum

Hieracium crassulum, commonly known as the thick-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its resilience to various soil conditions.
