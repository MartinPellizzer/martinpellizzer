Hemizygia cinerea

Hemizygia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf hemizygia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in spring. Hemizygia cinerea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along limestone outcrops.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
