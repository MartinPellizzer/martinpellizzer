Hieracium juranum

Hieracium juranum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium juranum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in alpine meadows.
