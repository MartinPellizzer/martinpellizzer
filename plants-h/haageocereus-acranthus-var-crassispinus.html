Haageocereus acranthus var. crassispinus

Haageocereus acranthus var. crassispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are densely covered with sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
