Habenaria nuda var. pygmaea

Habenaria nuda var. pygmaea, commonly known as the pygmy marsh orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to wetlands and marshy areas in southeastern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, slender spike bearing several small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal regions.

It is a flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae, characterized by its specialized pollination mechanisms and rhizomatous growth habit.
