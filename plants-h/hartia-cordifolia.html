Hartia cordifolia

Hartia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Hartia cordifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for indoor gardening and horticultural collections.
