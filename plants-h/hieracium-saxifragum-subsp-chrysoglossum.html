Hieracium saxifragum subsp. chrysoglossum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

chrysoglossum, commonly known as the yellow-edged hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers with orange centers. Its flowers are usually arranged in a single head, and the plant is noted for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
