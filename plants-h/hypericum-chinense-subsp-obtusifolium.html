Hypericum chinense subsp. obtusifolium

Hypericum chinense subsp.

obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to China. It features woody stems and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
