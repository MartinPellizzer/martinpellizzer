Hackelia brachytuba

Hackelia brachytuba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, elliptic fruits that mature into dry capsules.

Hackelia brachytuba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
