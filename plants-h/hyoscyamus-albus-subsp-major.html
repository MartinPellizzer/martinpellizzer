Hyoscyamus albus subsp. major

Hyoscyamus albus subsp.

major, commonly known as the greater white henbane, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lobed, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
