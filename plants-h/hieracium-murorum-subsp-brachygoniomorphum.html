Hieracium murorum subsp. brachygoniomorphum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

brachygoniomorphum, commonly known as the short-stalked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems.

The plant features small, yellowish to reddish flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, and its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
