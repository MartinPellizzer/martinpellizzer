Hieracium gymnodermum

Hieracium gymnodermum, commonly known as the naked skin hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium gymnodermum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
