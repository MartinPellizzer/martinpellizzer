Hemionitis nuda

Hemionitis nuda, commonly known as the naked hemionitis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Hemionitidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Hemionitis nuda is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in conditions of low moisture and high sunlight.
