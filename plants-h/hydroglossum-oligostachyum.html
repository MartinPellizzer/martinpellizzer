Hydroglossum oligostachyum

Hydroglossum oligostachyum, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Hydroglossum, is a member of the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the sediment while its stems support the foliage.

The flowers are usually pink or white, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
