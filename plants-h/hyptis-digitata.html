Hyptis digitata

Hyptis digitata, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Hyptis digitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
