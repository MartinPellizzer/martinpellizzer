Hedysarum brachypterum

Hedysarum brachypterum, commonly known as the narrow-winged vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Hedysarum brachypterum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
