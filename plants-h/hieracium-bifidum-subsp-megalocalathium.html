Hieracium bifidum subsp. megalocalathium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

megalocalathium, commonly known as the giant meadowhawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its robust growth and is often found in disturbed soils.
