Hakea muelleriana

Hakea muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's hakea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal regions. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting birds and insects. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Hakea muelleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
