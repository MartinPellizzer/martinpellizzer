Hemionitis sichouensis

Hemionitis sichouensis, commonly known as the Sichuan hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to China.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds with a unique arrangement of sori. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures are clustered along the undersides of the fronds, a feature common in many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
