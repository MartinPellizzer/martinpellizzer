Hesperis coulteri

Hesperis coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's honesty, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, round seed pods that split open when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
