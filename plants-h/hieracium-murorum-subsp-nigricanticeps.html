Hieracium murorum subsp. nigricanticeps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

nigricanticeps, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange-red flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple or black flower heads, which contrast with its greenish leaves. It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is often found in open habitats with moderate sunlight.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
