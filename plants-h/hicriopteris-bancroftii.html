Hicriopteris bancroftii

Hicriopteris bancroftii, commonly known as the Bancroft's holly fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and often grows in clusters on rocky or humus-rich soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
