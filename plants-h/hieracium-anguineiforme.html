Hieracium anguineiforme

Hieracium anguineiforme, commonly known as the red-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is known for its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
