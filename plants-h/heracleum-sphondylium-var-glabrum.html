Heracleum sphondylium var. glabrum

Heracleum sphondylium var. glabrum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features a tall, hollow stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces umbrella-shaped clusters of small white or greenish-yellow flowers, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, though the variety glabrum is hairless.

The plant is known for its strong, often unpleasant odor and can cause skin irritation upon contact.
