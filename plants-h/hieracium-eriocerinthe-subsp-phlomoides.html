Hieracium eriocerinthe subsp. phlomoides

Hieracium eriocerinthe subsp.

phlomoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
