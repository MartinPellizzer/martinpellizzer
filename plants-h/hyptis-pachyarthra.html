Hyptis pachyarthra

Hyptis pachyarthra, commonly known as the thick-armed hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming during the warmer months.

Hyptis pachyarthra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
