Hieracium serpyllifolium var. sardoum

Hieracium serpyllifolium var. sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in grassy areas, rocky soils, and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive disc shape and ray florets, contributing to its visual appeal.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats across southern Europe.
