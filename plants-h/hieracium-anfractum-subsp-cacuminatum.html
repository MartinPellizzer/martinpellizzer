Hieracium anfractum subsp. cacuminatum

Hieracium anfractum subsp.

cacuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in composite flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disk pattern. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
