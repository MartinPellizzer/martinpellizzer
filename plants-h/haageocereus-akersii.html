Haageocereus akersii

Haageocereus akersii, commonly known as the desert moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
