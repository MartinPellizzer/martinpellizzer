Hibiscus biseptus

Hibiscus biseptus, commonly known as the seven-sepaled hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus biseptus thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swamps or near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
