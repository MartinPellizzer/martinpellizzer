Hypericum lygophyllum

Hypericum lygophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum lygophyllum is part of the Hippocastanaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
