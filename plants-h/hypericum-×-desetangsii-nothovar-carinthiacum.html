Hypericum × desetangsii nothovar. carinthiacum

Hypericum × desetangsii nothovar. carinthiacum, commonly known as the Carinthian St. John's wort, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes numerous species known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to regions of Europe, particularly the Carpathian Basin. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers throughout the summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and resilience in garden settings.
