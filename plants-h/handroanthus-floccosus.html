Handroanthus floccosus

Handroanthus floccosus, commonly known as the white trumpet tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

The flowers emit a sweet, fragrant scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
