Hydrangea heteromalla var. mollis

Hydrangea heteromalla var. mollis, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, sterile flowers surrounded by large, showy bracts. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage is typically dark green and turns red in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the garden.
