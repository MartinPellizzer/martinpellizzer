Hypochaeris andina

Hypochaeris andina, commonly known as Andean sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Hypochaeris andina is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
