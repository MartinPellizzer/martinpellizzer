Hesperis muglensis

Hesperis muglensis, commonly known as the Turkish candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes plants such as cabbage and mustard. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis muglensis is typically found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
