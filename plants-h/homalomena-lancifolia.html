Homalomena lancifolia

Homalomena lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caladium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped, or lanceolate leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, and white. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a feature common in the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its foliage is prized for its aesthetic appeal in both indoor and outdoor gardening settings.
