Hippocratea velutina var. apiculata

Hippocratea velutina var. apiculata, commonly known as the velvet hippocratea, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial shrub, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, dense hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, making it an important plant in its ecosystem.
