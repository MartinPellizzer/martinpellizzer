Hieracium fominianum

Hieracium fominianum, commonly known as the Fominian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium fominianum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
