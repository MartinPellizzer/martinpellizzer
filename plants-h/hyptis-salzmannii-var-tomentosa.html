Hyptis salzmannii var. tomentosa

Hyptis salzmannii var. tomentosa, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
