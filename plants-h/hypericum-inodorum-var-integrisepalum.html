Hypericum inodorum var. integrisepalum

Hypericum inodorum var. integrisepalum, commonly known as the smooth-sepal goldenseal, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, elongated sepals and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
