Hieracium murorum subsp. luteobrunneum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

luteobrunneum, commonly known as the yellow-brown hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to brownish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with numerous flowering stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences.

This subspecies is distinguished by its coloration and is often found in open habitats across temperate regions.
