Hieracium aranicum

Hieracium aranicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in high-altitude habitats.

Hieracium aranicum is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
