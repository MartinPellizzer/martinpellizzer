Hoya subquintuplinervis

Hoya subquintuplinervis, commonly known as the five-veined wax plant, is a species of epiphytic flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves that are often marked with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Hoya subquintuplinervis is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
