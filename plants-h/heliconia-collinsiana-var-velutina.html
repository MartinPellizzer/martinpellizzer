Heliconia collinsiana var. velutina

Heliconia collinsiana var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that consists of brightly colored bracts resembling petals. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered around the central axis, which is often covered in a dense, velvety texture. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
