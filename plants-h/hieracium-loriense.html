Hieracium loriense

Hieracium loriense, commonly known as Lori's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium loriense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
