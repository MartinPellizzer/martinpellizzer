Hedysarum villosissimum

Hedysarum villosissimum, commonly known as the silky vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Hedysarum villosissimum plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
