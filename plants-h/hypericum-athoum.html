Hypericum athoum

Hypericum athoum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features woody stems that are often yellow or reddish in color, which are characteristic of the Hypericaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum athoum is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
