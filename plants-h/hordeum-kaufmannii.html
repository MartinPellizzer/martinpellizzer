Hordeum kaufmannii

Hordeum kaufmannii, commonly known as Kaufmann's barley, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Hordeum kaufmannii is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh landscapes.
