Hibiscus elliottiae

Hibiscus elliottiae, commonly known as Elliott's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. Its blooms appear from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus elliottiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
