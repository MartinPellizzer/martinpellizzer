Homalanthus moerenhoutianus

Homalanthus moerenhoutianus, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some regions.
