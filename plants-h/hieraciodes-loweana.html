Hieraciodes loweana

Hieraciodes loweana, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain daisy, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in open grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
