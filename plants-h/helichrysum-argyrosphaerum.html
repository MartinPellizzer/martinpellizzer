Helichrysum argyrosphaerum

Helichrysum argyrosphaerum, commonly known as silver globe everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small globes, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are long-lasting and often used in floral arrangements due to their preserved color and shape.
