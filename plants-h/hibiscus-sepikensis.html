Hibiscus sepikensis

Hibiscus sepikensis, commonly known as the Sepik hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus sepikensis is often found in wetland and swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species for horticultural purposes in tropical gardens.
