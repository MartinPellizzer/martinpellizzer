Hydrangea subintegra

Hydrangea subintegra, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often white or pink flower heads that can change color based on soil pH. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy inflorescences in the summer.

Hydrangea subintegra is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
