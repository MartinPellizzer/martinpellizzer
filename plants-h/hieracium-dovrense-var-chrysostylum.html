Hieracium dovrense var. chrysostylum

Hieracium dovrense var. chrysostylum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
