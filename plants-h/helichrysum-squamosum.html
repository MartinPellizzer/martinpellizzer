Helichrysum squamosum

Helichrysum squamosum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or Cape marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum squamosum is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
