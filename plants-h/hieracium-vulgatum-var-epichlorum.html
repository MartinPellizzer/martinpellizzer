Hieracium vulgatum var. epichlorum

Hieracium vulgatum var. epichlorum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which typically bloom from June to September, and its basal rosette of leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges.

This variant is distinguished by its chlorotic (yellowish) leaves, which give it its specific epithet "epichlorum.".
