Hieracium oxypleurum

Hieracium oxypleurum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium oxypleurum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
