Hieracium carpathicum subsp. obtusissimum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

obtusissimum, commonly known as the Carpathian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in meadows, forest edges, and rocky soils, preferring temperate climates.

The plant features basal and upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a typical composite structure consisting of ray florets and disk florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
