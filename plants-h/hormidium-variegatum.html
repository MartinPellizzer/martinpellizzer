Hormidium variegatum

Hormidium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated hormidium, is a member of the family Cactaceae, native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a common adaptation in desert environments to minimize transpiration.

Hormidium variegatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
