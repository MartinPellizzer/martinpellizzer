Helichrysum duvigneaudii

Helichrysum duvigneaudii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden inflorescences that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in spherical or cylindrical shapes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
