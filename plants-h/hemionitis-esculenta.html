Hemionitis esculenta

Hemionitis esculenta, commonly known as the edible bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread across the substrate. Its leaves are typically submerged, while its specialized bladders are often found near the water's surface.

Hemionitis esculenta is also notable for its ability to produce edible fruits, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
