Hyptis brunnescens var. canescens

Hyptis brunnescens var. canescens, commonly known as rough hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
