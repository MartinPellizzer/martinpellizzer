Hypochaeris glabra subsp. salzmanniana

Hypochaeris glabra subsp.

salzmanniana, commonly known as Salzmann's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with prominent central cones. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
