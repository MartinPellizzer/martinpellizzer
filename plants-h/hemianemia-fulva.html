Hemianemia fulva

Hemianemia fulva, commonly known as the red hemianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Hemianemia fulva is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
