Hedyotis borrerioides

Hedyotis borrerioides, commonly known as the Mexican bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hedyotis borrerioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
