Herpestis glechomoides

Herpestis glechomoides, commonly known as false hogweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a stout, hairy stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with a reddish tinge. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
