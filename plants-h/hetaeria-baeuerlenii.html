Hetaeria baeuerlenii

Hetaeria baeuerlenii, commonly known as the Baeuerlen's hetaeria, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricately patterned flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract specific pollinators. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
