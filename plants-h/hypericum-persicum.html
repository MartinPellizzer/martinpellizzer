Hypericum persicum

Hypericum persicum, commonly known as Persian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The genus Hypericum is known for its medicinal properties, and Hypericum persicum is no exception, containing compounds with potential therapeutic uses.
