Hieracium dasythrix

Hieracium dasythrix, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium dasythrix is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
