Hieracium bracteatum

Hieracium bracteatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in sunny, open habitats. It features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy, with a flowering stem that bears yellow flowers in a disc-like arrangement.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
