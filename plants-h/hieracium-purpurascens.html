Hieracium purpurascens

Hieracium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, surrounded by a ring of pale green to reddish bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium purpurascens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
