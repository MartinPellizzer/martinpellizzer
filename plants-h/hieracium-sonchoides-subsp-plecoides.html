Hieracium sonchoides subsp. plecoides

Hieracium sonchoides subsp.

plecoides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in areas with moderate to high light exposure.
