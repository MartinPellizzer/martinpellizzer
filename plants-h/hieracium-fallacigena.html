Hieracium fallacigena

Hieracium fallacigena, commonly known as the deceptive hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow with dark centers, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium fallacigena is known for its ability to colonize areas quickly, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
