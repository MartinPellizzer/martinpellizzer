Humboltia mandonii

Humboltia mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's hummingbird flower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, adapted to attract hummingbirds for pollination. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Humboltia mandonii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
