Hieracium prussicum subsp. mohrungenense

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

mohrungenense, commonly known as the Mohrungen hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
