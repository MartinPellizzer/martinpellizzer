Holothrix aphylla

Holothrix aphylla, commonly known as the leafless holothrix, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its adaptive features to arid environments. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
