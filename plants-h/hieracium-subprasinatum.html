Hieracium subprasinatum

Hieracium subprasinatum, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium subprasinatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some habitats.
