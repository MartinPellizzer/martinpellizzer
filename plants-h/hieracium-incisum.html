Hieracium incisum

Hieracium incisum, commonly known as the cutleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium incisum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
