Helianthemon fumanum

Helianthemon fumanum, commonly known as the smokeflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, fluffy, pale yellow to white inflorescence that resembles smoke, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
