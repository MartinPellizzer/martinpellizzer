Hydrangea longipes var. longipes

Hydrangea longipes var. longipes, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as a shrub with stout, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to change flower color, offering a dynamic visual display throughout the growing season.
