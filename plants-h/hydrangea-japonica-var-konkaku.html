Hydrangea japonica var. konkaku

Hydrangea japonica var. konkaku, commonly known as the Konkaku hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant produces broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green during the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to change flower color, adding visual interest throughout the summer months.
