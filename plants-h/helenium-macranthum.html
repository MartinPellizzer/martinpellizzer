Helenium macranthum

Helenium macranthum, commonly known as the giant helenium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, supporting large, orange to red-orange ray flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Helenium macranthum thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.
