Hieracium abietogenum

Hieracium abietogenum, commonly known as the subalpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. It features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
