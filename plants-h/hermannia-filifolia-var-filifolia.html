Hermannia filifolia var. filifolia

Hermannia filifolia var. filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf hermannia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive, grass-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
