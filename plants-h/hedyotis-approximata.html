Hedyotis approximata

Hedyotis approximata, commonly known as the small-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
