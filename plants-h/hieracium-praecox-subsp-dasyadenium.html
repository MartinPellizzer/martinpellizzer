Hieracium praecox subsp. dasyadenium

Hieracium praecox subsp.

dasyadenium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its robust growth and distinctive floral morphology.
