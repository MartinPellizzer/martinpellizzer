Hieracium auricula subsp. beverianum

Hieracium auricula subsp.

beverianum, commonly known as the Beverian's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
