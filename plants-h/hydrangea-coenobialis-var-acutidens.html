Hydrangea coenobialis var. acutidens

Hydrangea coenobialis var. acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-toothed hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are typically covered in stinging hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
