Hoya montana

Hoya montana, commonly known as the wax plant or the sweetheart plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late spring or early summer. Hoya montana is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is well-known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is often grown as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
