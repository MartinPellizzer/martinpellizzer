Habenaria ochyrae

Habenaria ochyrae, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, violet to purple flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or in the undergrowth. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

The species is relatively rare and is often found in specific habitats within its native range.
