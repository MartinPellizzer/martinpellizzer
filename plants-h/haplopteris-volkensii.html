Haplopteris volkensii

Haplopteris volkensii, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often divided into small, lanceolate segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Haplopteris volkensii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various microorganisms and small animals.
