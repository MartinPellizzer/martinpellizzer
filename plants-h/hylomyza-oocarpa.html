Hylomyza oocarpa

Hylomyza oocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruit hylomyza, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red berries that are edible. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces a fragrant aroma.

Hylomyza oocarpa is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
