Hiraea mutisiana

Hiraea mutisiana, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Hiraea mutisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
