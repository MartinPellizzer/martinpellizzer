Hieracium ascendentidens

Hieracium ascendentidens, commonly known as the ascending hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, hairy leaves and produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
