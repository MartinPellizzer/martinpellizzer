Hyptis laniflora var. insularis

Hyptis laniflora var. insularis, commonly known as the West Indian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean.

It features slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers clustered in dense spikes, typically in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded habitats.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
