Helictotrichon sedenense var. gervaisii

Helictotrichon sedenense var. gervaisii, commonly known as the Gervais' sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in disturbed or riparian areas.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
