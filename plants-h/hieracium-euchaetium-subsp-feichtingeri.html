Hieracium euchaetium subsp. feichtingeri

Hieracium euchaetium subsp.

feichtingeri, commonly known as the Feichtinger's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring moist, rocky soils. It is native to Central Europe and is often found in mountainous areas with high elevation.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
