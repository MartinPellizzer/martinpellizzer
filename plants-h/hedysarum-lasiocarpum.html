Hedysarum lasiocarpum

Hedysarum lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Hedysarum lasiocarpum is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
