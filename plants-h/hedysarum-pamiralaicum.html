Hedysarum pamiralaicum

Hedysarum pamiralaicum, commonly known as the Pamir milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods contain edible seeds that can be used as a food source in certain regions.
