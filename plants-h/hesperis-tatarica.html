Hesperis tatarica

Hesperis tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces white or pale purple blooms. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Hesperis tatarica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
