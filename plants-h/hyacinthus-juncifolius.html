Hyacinthus juncifolius

Hyacinthus juncifolius, commonly known as the marsh hyacinth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.

Hyacinthus juncifolius is also known for its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
