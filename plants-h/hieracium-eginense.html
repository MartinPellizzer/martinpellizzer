Hieracium eginense

Hieracium eginense, commonly known as the Eginense hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
