Helicteres spicata var. lanigera

Helicteres spicata var. lanigera, commonly known as the hairy helicteres, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
