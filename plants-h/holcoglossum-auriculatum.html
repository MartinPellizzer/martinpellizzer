Holcoglossum auriculatum

Holcoglossum auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers with dark red or violet petals and a yellow lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Holcoglossum auriculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
