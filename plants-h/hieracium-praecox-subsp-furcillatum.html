Hieracium praecox subsp. furcillatum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

furcillatum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it both a common wildflower and a potential weed.
