Hypocalyptus

Hypocalyptus, commonly known as the white sand lichen, is a unique type of lichen that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is characterized by its symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. The plant forms a crust-like structure on rocks and soil, helping it to retain moisture in harsh conditions. Its slow growth rate makes it an indicator species for stable, undisturbed ecosystems.

Hypocalyptus plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
