Hyssopus schleicheri

Hyssopus schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, hairy seeds in its fruit capsules.

Hyssopus schleicheri is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
