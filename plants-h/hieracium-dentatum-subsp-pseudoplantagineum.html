Hieracium dentatum subsp. pseudoplantagineum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

pseudoplantagineum, commonly known as the false plantain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
