Holostigma spirale

Holostigma spirale, commonly known as the spiral liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the family Pterygopariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped thallus that forms a dense, carpet-like growth on moist, shaded surfaces. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, and instead consists of a leafy structure that absorbs water and nutrients directly from the environment. Holostigma spirale thrives in humid environments, often found in shaded rock crevices or on decaying wood.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
