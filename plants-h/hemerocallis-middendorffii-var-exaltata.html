Hemerocallis middendorffii var. exaltata

Hemerocallis middendorffii var. exaltata, commonly known as the tall yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with numerous florets, each having three petals and three sepals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
