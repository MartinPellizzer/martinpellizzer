Hypericum meridionale

Hypericum meridionale, commonly known as the southern St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its medicinal properties are attributed to the presence of hypericin and other bioactive compounds.
