Hieracium gotoburgense

Hieracium gotoburgense, commonly known as Gottingen hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces seeds in a composite structure typical of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium gotoburgense is often considered a weed but has ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
