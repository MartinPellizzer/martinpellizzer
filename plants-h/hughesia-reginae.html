Hughesia reginae

Hughesia reginae, commonly known as the queen's hughesia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

This perennial shrub is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique form, Hughesia reginae is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.
