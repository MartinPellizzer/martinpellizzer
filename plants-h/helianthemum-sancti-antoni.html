Helianthemum sancti-antoni

Helianthemum sancti-antoni, commonly known as the Saint Anthony's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

The flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.
