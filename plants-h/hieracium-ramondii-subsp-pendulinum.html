Hieracium ramondii subsp. pendulinum

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

pendulinum, commonly known as the hanging hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellowish-orange flowers that attract various pollinators.

The subspecies pendulinum is distinguished by its drooping flower heads, which give it its common name.
