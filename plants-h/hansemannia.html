Hansemannia

Hansemannia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Hansemannia has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
