Hierochloe odorata var. sachalinensis

Hierochloe odorata var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and its distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. Its slender, cylindrical inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in floral arrangements and cultural practices.
