Hieracium camkorijense subsp. boreograecum

Hieracium camkorijense subsp.

boreograecum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its yellow composite flowers consist of both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is adapted to temperate climates and exhibits a degree of frost tolerance.
