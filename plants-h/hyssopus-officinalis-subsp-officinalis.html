Hyssopus officinalis subsp. officinalis

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine for their expectorant and antiseptic properties.

Hyssop is also valued in traditional gardening for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
