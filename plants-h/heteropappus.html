Heteropappus

Heteropappus, commonly known as the "broadleaf groundsel," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

These plants are typically annual or perennial herbs with hairy stems and alternate leaves. The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite heads, and are often accompanied by bracts. Heteropappus species are native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America and are adapted to dry conditions.

They are often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some areas.
