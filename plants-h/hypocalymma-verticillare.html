Hypocalymma verticillare

Hypocalymma verticillare, commonly known as the clustered hypocalymma, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in heathlands and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Hypocalymma verticillare is an important component of the local flora and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
