Heteropogon stipoides

Heteropogon stipoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Heteropogon stipoides is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in well-drained soils.
