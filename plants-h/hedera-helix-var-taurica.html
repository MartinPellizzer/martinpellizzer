Hedera helix var. taurica

Hedera helix var. taurica, commonly known as the Taurica ivy, is a variant of the common ivy that is native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly variegated leaves that often feature a striking yellow or white margin. This plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to attach to surfaces and grow vertically. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The Taurica ivy is valued for its hardiness and ability to flourish in a variety of soil types.
