Hypericum barbatum var. macedonicum

Hypericum barbatum var. macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features woody stems covered in short, stiff hairs, and its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and slightly hairy. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
