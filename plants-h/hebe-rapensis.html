Hebe rapensis

Hebe rapensis, commonly known as the New Zealand hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hebe rapensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
