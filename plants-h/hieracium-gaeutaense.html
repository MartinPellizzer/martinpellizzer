Hieracium gaeutaense

Hieracium gaeutaense, commonly known as the Gaeuta hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
