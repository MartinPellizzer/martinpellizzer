Hieracium pallidum subsp. pallidiglaucinum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

pallidiglaucinum, commonly known as the pale glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered with a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and borne in a single head, often with pale green or yellowish ray florets.

The species is characterized by its pale greenish-blue coloration on the leaves and stems, which gives it its common name.
