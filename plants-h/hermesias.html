Hermesias

Hermesias, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that resemble small raindrops. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The seeds of Hermesias are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind, aiding in its natural propagation.
