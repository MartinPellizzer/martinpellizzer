Hedyotis auricularia var. dispersa

Hedyotis auricularia var. dispersa, commonly known as the scattered earleaf, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive ear-like shape. The plant produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
