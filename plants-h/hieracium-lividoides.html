Hieracium lividoides

Hieracium lividoides, commonly known as the bluish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green to bluish bracts, giving it its common name. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Hieracium lividoides is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
