Hieracium bicurvum

Hieracium bicurvum, commonly known as the two-curved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium bicurvum is often found in sunny areas and can sometimes form dense colonies.
