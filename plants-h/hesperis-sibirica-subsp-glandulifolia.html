Hesperis sibirica subsp. glandulifolia

Hesperis sibirica subsp.

glandulifolia, commonly known as the Siberian wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of linear, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers in early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular-dotted leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
