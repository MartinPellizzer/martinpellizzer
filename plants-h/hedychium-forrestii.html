Hedychium forrestii

Hedychium forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange center, which bloom in clusters. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Hedychium forrestii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its aromatic properties.
