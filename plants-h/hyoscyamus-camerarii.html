Hyoscyamus camerarii

Hyoscyamus camerarii, commonly known as the African jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain toxic seeds, which can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
