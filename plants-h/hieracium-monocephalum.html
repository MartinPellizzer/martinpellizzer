Hieracium monocephalum

Hieracium monocephalum, commonly known as the single-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head at the end of each stem.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
