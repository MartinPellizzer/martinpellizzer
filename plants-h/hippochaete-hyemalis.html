Hippochaete hyemalis

Hippochaete hyemalis, commonly known as the winter heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Hippochaete hyemalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
