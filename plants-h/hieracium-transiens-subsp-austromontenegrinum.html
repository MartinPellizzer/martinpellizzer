Hieracium transiens subsp. austromontenegrinum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

austromontenegrinum, commonly known as the Austro-Montenegro hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in parts of southeastern Europe.
