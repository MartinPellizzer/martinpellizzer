Helianthella grandiflora

Helianthella grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered helianthella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent central disks. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
