Helianthus australis

Helianthus australis, commonly known as the Australian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads consist of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Helianthus australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.
