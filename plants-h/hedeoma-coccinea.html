Hedeoma coccinea

Hedeoma coccinea, commonly known as scarlet beakvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Hedeoma coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
