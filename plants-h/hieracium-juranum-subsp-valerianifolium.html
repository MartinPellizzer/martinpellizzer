Hieracium juranum subsp. valerianifolium

Hieracium juranum subsp.

valerianifolium, commonly known as the Valerian-leaved Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive disc of tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from June to August.

The subspecies is characterized by its valerian-like foliage, which gives it its common name.
