Hibiscus corrugatus

Hibiscus corrugatus, commonly known as the corrugated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive corrugated or ridged leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Hibiscus corrugatus typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
