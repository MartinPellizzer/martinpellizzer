Halianthus peploides var. major

Halianthus peploides var. major, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, adapted to drought conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, woolly leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its seed capsules are hairy and contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
