Haplophyllum spathulatum

Haplophyllum spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly rough texture, and the plant is known for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.

Haplophyllum spathulatum is often used in rock gardens and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
