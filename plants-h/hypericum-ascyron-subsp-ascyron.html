Hypericum ascyron subsp. ascyron

Hypericum ascyron subsp.

ascyron, commonly known as yellow hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, and its lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
