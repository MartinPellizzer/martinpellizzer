Hieracium nigrosilvarum

Hieracium nigrosilvarum, commonly known as the blackwood hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive dark central disc.

Hieracium nigrosilvarum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in forested areas.
