Hypochaeris erectus

Hypochaeris erectus, commonly known as the upright sow thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are spoon-shaped with toothed edges. Its yellow flowers, which appear in clusters, bloom from early spring to late summer.

Hypochaeris erectus is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
