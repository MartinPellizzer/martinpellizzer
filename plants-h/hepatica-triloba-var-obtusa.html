Hepatica triloba var. obtusa

Hepatica triloba var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf hepatica, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its palmate, broad leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of white, blue, or violet, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils under partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in woodland ecosystems.
