Hamatocactus setispinus f. orcuttii

Hamatocactus setispinus f. orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or cylindrical shape, often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it a hardy yet sensitive species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
