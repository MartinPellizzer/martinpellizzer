Halenia parviflora var. latifolia

Halenia parviflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved halenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features lanceolate, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale blue and are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
