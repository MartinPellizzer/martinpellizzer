Horsfieldia squamulosa

Horsfieldia squamulosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and clustered inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Horsfieldia squamulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
