Habenaria lutaria

Habenaria lutaria, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive red-tipped lip, which is a key feature for attracting pollinators. It typically blooms during the rainy season, showcasing its adaptability to moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
