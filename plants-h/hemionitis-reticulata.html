Hemionitis reticulata

Hemionitis reticulata, commonly known as the nettle fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Hemionitis reticulata is also notable for its ability to tolerate moderate levels of pollution, making it an indicator species for water quality.
