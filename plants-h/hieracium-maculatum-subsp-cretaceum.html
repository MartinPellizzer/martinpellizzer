Hieracium maculatum subsp. cretaceum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

cretaceum, commonly known as the Cretaceous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single inflorescence at the end of each stem. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and the presence of a white or pale green central disc.
