Helichrysum phaceloma

Helichrysum phaceloma, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is known for its ability to retain its color when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.

Helichrysum phaceloma thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.
