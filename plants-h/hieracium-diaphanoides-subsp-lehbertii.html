Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. lehbertii

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

lehbertii, commonly known as the Lehbert's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, and its stems are erect with alternate leaves.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and reduced leaf morphology compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
