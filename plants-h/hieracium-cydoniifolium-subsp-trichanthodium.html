Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. trichanthodium

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

trichanthodium, commonly known as the thorned hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a compact inflorescence, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its subspecies name, *trichanthodium*, refers to the hairy appearance of its stems and flowers.
