Hymenophyllum subdemissum

Hymenophyllum subdemissum, commonly known as the slender hymenophyllum, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in humid forests. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This fern is notable for its ability to retain moisture and its unique, lace-like appearance.
