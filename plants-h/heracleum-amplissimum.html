Heracleum amplissimum

Heracleum amplissimum, commonly known as giant hogweed, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It can grow up to 5 meters in height, with large, umbrella-like compound leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant contains phototoxic chemicals in its sap, which can cause severe skin irritation when exposed to sunlight. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
