Heteropsis salicifolia var. riedeliana

Heteropsis salicifolia var. riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's willow-leaf plant, is a variant of the willow-leaf heteropsis.

It is a small, deciduous shrub characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland understories. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
