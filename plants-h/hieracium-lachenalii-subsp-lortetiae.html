Hieracium lachenalii subsp. lortetiae

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

lortetiae, commonly known as the Lortet's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
