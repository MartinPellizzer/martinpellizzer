Heliotropium griffithii

Heliotropium griffithii, commonly known as the pink heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, often shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to violet, often with a distinct central blue or purple spot. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Heliotropium griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
