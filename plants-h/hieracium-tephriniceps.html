Hieracium tephriniceps

Hieracium tephriniceps, commonly known as the tephritic hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has hairy stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
