Hedysarum pallens

Hedysarum pallens, commonly known as the white vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Hedysarum pallens has a creeping or ascending growth habit, often forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Its seeds are small and oval, with a hard coat that allows for long-term seed bank persistence in the soil.
