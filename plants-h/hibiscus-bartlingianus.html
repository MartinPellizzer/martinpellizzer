Hibiscus bartlingianus

Hibiscus bartlingianus, commonly known as the crimson hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in summer.

Hibiscus bartlingianus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
