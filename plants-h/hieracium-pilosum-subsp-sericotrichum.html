Hieracium pilosum subsp. sericotrichum

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

sericotrichum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow daisy-like flowers.

The subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
