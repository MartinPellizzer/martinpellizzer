Hieracium pseudorhaeticum

Hieracium pseudorhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species in many areas.
