Hieracium oreades var. subglabratum

Hieracium oreades var. subglabratum, commonly known as the glabrous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions.
