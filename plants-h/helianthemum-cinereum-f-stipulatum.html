Helianthemum cinereum f. stipulatum

Helianthemum cinereum f. stipulatum, commonly known as the silver-stemmed sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. The plant has succulent, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
