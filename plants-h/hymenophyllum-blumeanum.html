Hymenophyllum blumeanum

Hymenophyllum blumeanum, commonly known as the Blume's filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a specialized covering.

Hymenophyllum blumeanum is known for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and scientific value.
