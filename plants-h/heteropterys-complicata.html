Heteropterys complicata

Heteropterys complicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are typically followed by elliptical, winged fruits. Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas, particularly in the tropics.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
