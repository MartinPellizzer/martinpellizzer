Hieracium corymbosum subsp. umbellatiforme

Hieracium corymbosum subsp.

umbellatiforme, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces a tall, branching stem with numerous small, yellowish flowers arranged in a corymb or umbellate form. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
