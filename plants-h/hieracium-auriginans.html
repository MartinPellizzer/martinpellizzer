Hieracium auriginans

Hieracium auriginans, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with a basal rosette structure.

Hieracium auriginans is often found in open habitats and can spread rapidly through its rhizomes.
