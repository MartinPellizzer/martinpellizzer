Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. grandifolioides

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

grandifolioides, commonly known as the large-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of yellow disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more elongated leaves compared to other members of the species.
