Hieracium retyezatense subsp. macilentoides

Hieracium retyezatense subsp.

macilentoides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and localized species.
