Hyphaene multiformis subsp. moshiensis

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

moshiensis, commonly known as the Moshi palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers followed by clustered, fibrous fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for both horticultural and environmental purposes.
