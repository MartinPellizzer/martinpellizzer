Hieracium hyperboreum subsp. subhyperboreum

Hieracium hyperboreum subsp.

subhyperboreum, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America. It typically grows in open, rocky or sandy habitats, often in high-altitude environments.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the stems are often reddish in color.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a characteristic species of polar and subarctic vegetation.
