Horsfieldia batjanica

Horsfieldia batjanica, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Horsfieldia batjanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
