Howardia geminiflora

Howardia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered candelabra, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are green with a slight blueish hue, often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is valued in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
