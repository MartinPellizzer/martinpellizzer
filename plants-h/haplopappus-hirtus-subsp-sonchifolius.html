Haplopappus hirtus subsp. sonchifolius

Haplopappus hirtus subsp.

sonchifolius, commonly known as the hairyleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, linear leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and it produces small, pappus-equipped seeds for wind dispersal. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environments, though it may pose challenges in ecological management.
