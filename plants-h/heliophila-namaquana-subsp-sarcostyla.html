Heliophila namaquana subsp. sarcostyla

Heliophila namaquana subsp.

sarcostyla, commonly known as the Namaqua sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

The subspecies sarcostyla is distinguished by its robust growth and unique floral structure compared to other subspecies of Heliophila.
