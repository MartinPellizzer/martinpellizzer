Hypericinea dentata

Hypericinea dentata, commonly known as the toothed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats.

Hypericinea dentata is known for its medicinal properties, with some species in the genus Hypericum being used in traditional herbal remedies.
