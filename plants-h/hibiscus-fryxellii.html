Hibiscus fryxellii

Hibiscus fryxellii, commonly known as the Fryxell's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Hibiscus fryxellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
