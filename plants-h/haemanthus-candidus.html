Haemanthus candidus

Haemanthus candidus, commonly known as the rain lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice among gardeners.
