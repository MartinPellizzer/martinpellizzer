Hyparrhenia smithiana var. smithiana

Hyparrhenia smithiana var. smithiana, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a common ornamental and functional plant in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread use in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
