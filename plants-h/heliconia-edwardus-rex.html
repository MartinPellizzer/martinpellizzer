Heliconia edwardus-rex

Heliconia edwardus-rex, commonly known as the Edward's red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, red-orange inflorescences that resemble a crown of flowers. The plant features broad, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often a deep green with prominent veins. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds and bats, which are attracted to its nectar-rich bracts.

Heliconia edwardus-rex is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
