Hoffmannia cercidifolia

Hoffmannia cercidifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed hoffmannia, is a striking shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red leaves, which contrast sharply with its grey-green stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Hoffmannia cercidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
