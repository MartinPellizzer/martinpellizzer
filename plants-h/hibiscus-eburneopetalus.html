Hibiscus eburneopetalus

Hibiscus eburneopetalus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a striking flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that are often tinged with pale pink or lavender at the base. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Hibiscus eburneopetalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
