Hieracium divisum

Hieracium divisum, commonly known as the divided hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
