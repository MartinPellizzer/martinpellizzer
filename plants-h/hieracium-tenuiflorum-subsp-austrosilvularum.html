Hieracium tenuiflorum subsp. austrosilvularum

Hieracium tenuiflorum subsp.

austrosilvularum, commonly known as the slender-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along forest edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to spread through rhizomes.
