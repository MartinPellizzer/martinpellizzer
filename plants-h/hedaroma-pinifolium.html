Hedaroma pinifolium

Hedaroma pinifolium, commonly known as the sweet pineapple mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pine-scented foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, citrus-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique scent and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
