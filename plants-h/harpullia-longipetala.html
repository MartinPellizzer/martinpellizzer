Harpullia longipetala

Harpullia longipetala, commonly known as the long-petioled harpullia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Harpullia longipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a notable species in the Sapindaceae family.
