Hamelia verticillata

Hamelia verticillata, commonly known as flamebeard, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Hamelia verticillata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
