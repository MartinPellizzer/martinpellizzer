Hopea malibato

Hopea malibato, commonly known as the Philippine hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellowish-green in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Hopea malibato is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
