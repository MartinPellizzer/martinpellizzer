Helichrysum polioides

Helichrysum polioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are tightly packed in dense, spherical clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle, spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
