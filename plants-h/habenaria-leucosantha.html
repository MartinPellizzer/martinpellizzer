Habenaria leucosantha

Habenaria leucosantha, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers with white petals and green sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The orchid's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitats.
