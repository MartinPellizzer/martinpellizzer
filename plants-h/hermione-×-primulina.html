Hermione × primulina

Hermione × primulina, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a cross between two species of the Primula genus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors including yellow, orange, and white. The plant has hairy stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
