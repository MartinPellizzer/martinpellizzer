Hieracium goderiense

Hieracium goderiense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium goderiense is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
