Hibiscus ellipticifolius

Hibiscus ellipticifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming during the wet season. Hibiscus ellipticifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses.
