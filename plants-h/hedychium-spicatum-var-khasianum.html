Hedychium spicatum var. khasianum

Hedychium spicatum var. khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi ginger or Himalayan flame.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern Himalayas, characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant features compound leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinct, spicy aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
