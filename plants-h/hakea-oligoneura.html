Hakea oligoneura

Hakea oligoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or conical inflorescences that bear small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access groundwater.

Hakea oligoneura is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
