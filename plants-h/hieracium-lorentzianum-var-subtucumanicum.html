Hieracium lorentzianum var. subtucumanicum

Hieracium lorentzianum var. subtucumanicum, commonly known as the Tucuman hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and semi-arid environments.
