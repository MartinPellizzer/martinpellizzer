Hoya poolei

Hoya poolei, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Hoya of Poole," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with cream and green hues. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Hoya poolei is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low light conditions.

It is also known for its slow growth and ability to develop aerial roots when grown in hanging baskets.
