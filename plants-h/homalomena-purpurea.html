Homalomena purpurea

Homalomena purpurea, commonly known as the purple umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a prominent purple veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces strikingly colorful spathes that range from deep red to purple, often with a contrasting white or yellow spatula. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Homalomena purpurea is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
