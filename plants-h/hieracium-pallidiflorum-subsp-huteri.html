Hieracium pallidiflorum subsp. huteri

Hieracium pallidiflorum subsp.

huteri, commonly known as the paleflower hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with petals that are narrow and pointed.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in local ecosystems.
