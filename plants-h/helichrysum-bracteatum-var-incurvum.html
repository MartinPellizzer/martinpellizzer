Helichrysum bracteatum var. incurvum

Helichrysum bracteatum var. incurvum, commonly known as the curly everlasting or golden daisy, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in dense, compact clusters, giving it a striking, ornamental appearance. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often slightly curled, contributing to its distinctive look. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The plant is also valued for its ability to retain its color when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.
