Hieracium glaucinum subsp. similatum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

similatum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has small, glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
