Hibiscus glandulifer

Hibiscus glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Hibiscus glandulifer produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
