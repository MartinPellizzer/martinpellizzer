Hordeum ithaburense var. ischnatherum

Hordeum ithaburense var. ischnatherum, commonly known as desert barley, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often root at the base. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale green to brown glumes and lemma-like structures. It has adapted to survive in harsh environments through deep root systems and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
