Helichrysum buddlejoides

Helichrysum buddlejoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden-orange inflorescences that resemble small dandelion balls. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, rounded heads. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Helichrysum buddlejoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
