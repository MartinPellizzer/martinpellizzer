Hookera sessilis

Hookera sessilis, commonly known as the mossy cushion plant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It forms dense, cushion-like clusters of foliage, which help it conserve moisture and withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Hookera sessilis is adapted to high altitudes, with specialized structures that allow it to thrive in cold, nutrient-poor soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
