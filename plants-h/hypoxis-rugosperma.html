Hypoxis rugosperma

Hypoxis rugosperma, commonly known as the rough-leaved starflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with six petals arranged in a star-like pattern, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Hypoxis rugosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
