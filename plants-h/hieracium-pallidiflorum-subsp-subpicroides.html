Hieracium pallidiflorum subsp. subpicroides

Hieracium pallidiflorum subsp.

subpicroides, commonly known as the paleflower hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and grasslands.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually unbranched and somewhat hairy. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are often pale yellow.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
