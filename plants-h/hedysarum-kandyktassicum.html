Hedysarum kandyktassicum

Hedysarum kandyktassicum, commonly known as the Kandyktassic honeyvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish-pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native environment.
