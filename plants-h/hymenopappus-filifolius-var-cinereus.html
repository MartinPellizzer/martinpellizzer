Hymenopappus filifolius var. cinereus

Hymenopappus filifolius var. cinereus, commonly known as the silverleaf hymenopappus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by feathery bracts that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environments.
