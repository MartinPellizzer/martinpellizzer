Hydrilla verticillata var. roxburghii

Hydrilla verticillata var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian hydrilla, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of freshwater environments. The plant has whorled leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of small, elongated fruits.

This species is known for its rapid growth and can form dense mats that impact water quality and aquatic ecosystems.
