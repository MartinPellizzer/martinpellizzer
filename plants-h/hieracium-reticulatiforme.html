Hieracium reticulatiforme

Hieracium reticulatiforme, commonly known as the net-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and erect, branched stems that bear yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and prominent veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in temperate climates.
