Hypericum spragueanum

Hypericum spragueanum, commonly known as the Sprague's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
