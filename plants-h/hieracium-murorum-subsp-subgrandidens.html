Hieracium murorum subsp. subgrandidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subgrandidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
