Hieracium pilosella subsp. varium

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

varium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
