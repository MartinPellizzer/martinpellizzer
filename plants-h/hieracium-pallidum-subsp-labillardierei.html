Hieracium pallidum subsp. labillardierei

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

labillardierei, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

The plant features a basal rosette of broadly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head and are surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
