Hieracium tersiflorum

Hieracium tersiflorum, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are white to pale yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium tersiflorum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
