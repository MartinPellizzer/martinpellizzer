Hicorius ovalis var. acuta

Hicorius ovalis var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leafed hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
