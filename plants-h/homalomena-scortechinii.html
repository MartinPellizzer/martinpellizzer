Homalomena scortechinii

Homalomena scortechinii, commonly known as the purple-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves with striking purple veins. The plant produces small, spadix-like flowers that are typically hidden within a leafy bract. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The rhizomes of this plant can be used as a starchy food source in some regions.
