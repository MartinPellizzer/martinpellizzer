Hyptis mollissima

Hyptis mollissima, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis mollissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
