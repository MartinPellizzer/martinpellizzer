Hieracium suvwittrockianum

Hieracium suvwittrockianum, commonly known as the Suwak rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its native habitat.
