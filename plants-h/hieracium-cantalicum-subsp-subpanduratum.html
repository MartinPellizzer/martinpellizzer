Hieracium cantalicum subsp. subpanduratum

Hieracium cantalicum subsp.

subpanduratum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower and more upright. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive ray floret and a central disc, and it blooms from June to August.

The species is characterized by its creeping growth habit and the presence of a yellow-orange rootstock.
