Hylotelephium verticillatum

Hylotelephium verticillatum, commonly known as the Autumn Joy Sedum, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers in the fall. The plant features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
