Heliconia farinosa f. magna

Heliconia farinosa f. magna, commonly known as the giant heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush, tropical appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates due to its dramatic visual impact.
