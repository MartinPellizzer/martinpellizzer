Heteropogon villosus subvar. leianthus

Heteropogon villosus subvar. leianthus, commonly known as the silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, hair-covered stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
