Hosackia grandiflora

Hosackia grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hosackia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
