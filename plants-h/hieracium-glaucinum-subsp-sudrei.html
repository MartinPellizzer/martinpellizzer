Hieracium glaucinum subsp. sudrei

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

sudrei, commonly known as the Sudetic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
