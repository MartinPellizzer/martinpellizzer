Hesperis sibirica var. alba

Hesperis sibirica var. alba, commonly known as the Siberian white candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
