Hysterionica dianthifolia var. pulvinata

Hysterionica dianthifolia var. pulvinata, commonly known as the cushion bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
