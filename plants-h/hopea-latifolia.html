Hopea latifolia

Hopea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing one or two seeds. Its timber is valued for its strength and durability, making it important in local forestry.

The plant also plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
