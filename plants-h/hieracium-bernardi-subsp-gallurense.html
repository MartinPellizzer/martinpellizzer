Hieracium bernardi subsp. gallurense

Hieracium bernardi subsp.

gallurense, commonly known as the Gallican hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, producing a yellow coloration that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
