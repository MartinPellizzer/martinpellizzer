Helenium rivulare

Helenium rivulare, commonly known as the river helenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its striking orange to red flowers, which resemble sunflowers, bloom in late summer to early fall.

Helenium rivulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
