Hakea ferricola

Hakea ferricola, commonly known as the ironbark hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and distinctive foliage composed of narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Hakea ferricola is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
