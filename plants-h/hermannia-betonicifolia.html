Hermannia betonicifolia

Hermannia betonicifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters, often resembling a misty cloud. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Hermannia betonicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
