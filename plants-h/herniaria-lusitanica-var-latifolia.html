Herniaria lusitanica var. latifolia

Herniaria lusitanica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved herniaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces tiny white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
