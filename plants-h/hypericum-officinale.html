Hypericum officinale

Hypericum officinale, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in midsummer, and its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times for treating various ailments, including depression and inflammation.
