Helicia hypoglauca

Helicia hypoglauca, commonly known as the white-flowered helicia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
