Hibiscus vohipahensis

Hibiscus vohipahensis, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus vohipahensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the local ecosystem of Madagascar.
