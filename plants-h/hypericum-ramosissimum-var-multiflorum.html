Hypericum ramosissimum var. multiflorum

Hypericum ramosissimum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered St. John's wort, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features erect, branched stems covered in small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
