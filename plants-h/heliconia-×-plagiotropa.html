Heliconia × plagiotropa

Heliconia × plagiotropa, commonly known as the red ginger heliconia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and vibrant, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves used to attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Heliconia × plagiotropa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to warm climates.
