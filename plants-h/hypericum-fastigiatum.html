Hypericum fastigiatum

Hypericum fastigiatum, commonly known as the upright St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright, woody stems covered in small, glandular hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are bright yellow and consist of four petals with prominent stamens. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain three seeds.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
