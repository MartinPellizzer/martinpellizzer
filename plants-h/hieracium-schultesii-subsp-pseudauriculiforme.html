Hieracium schultesii subsp. pseudauriculiforme

Hieracium schultesii subsp.

pseudauriculiforme, commonly known as the false auriculate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and often have a distinct auriculate base, giving it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant is generally short-lived and can be challenging to control due to its aggressive growth habits.
