Hieracium pseudopilosella subsp. plantaginoides

Hieracium pseudopilosella subsp.

plantaginoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more elongated leaves compared to other hawkweed species.
