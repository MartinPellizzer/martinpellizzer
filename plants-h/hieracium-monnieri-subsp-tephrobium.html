Hieracium monnieri subsp. tephrobium

Hieracium monnieri subsp.

tephrobium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow with a central disc of smaller florets.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
