Hieracium cydoniifolium

Hieracium cydoniifolium, commonly known as the Cydonia-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
