Hypericum porphyrandrum

Hypericum porphyrandrum, commonly known as the fireweed or common St. John's wort, is a perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, and its distinctive red, woody stems that become more prominent as the plant matures. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hypericum porphyrandrum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste ground.
