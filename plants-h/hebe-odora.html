Hebe odora

Hebe odora, commonly known as the sweet hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Hebe odora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
