Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. euparyphum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

euparyphum, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically alternate, with a rough, serrated texture. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in moist or calcareous soils.

This subspecies is noted for its distinct leaf morphology and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and taxonomic significance.
