Hedysarum lazkovii

Hedysarum lazkovii, commonly known as the Balkan milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Hedysarum lazkovii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
