Hieracium majellense

Hieracium majellense, commonly known as the Majella hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like cluster.

Hieracium majellense is often found in mountainous areas, contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
