Habenaria tridactylites

Habenaria tridactylites, commonly known as the three-fingered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, three-lobed lip that resembles fingers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and shrublands. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, often with a faint fragrance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
