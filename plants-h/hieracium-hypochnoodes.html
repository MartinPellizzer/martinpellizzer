Hieracium hypochnoodes

Hieracium hypochnoodes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
