Hieracium floribundum subsp. islandicum

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

islandicum, commonly known as the Icelandic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Iceland. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as grasslands and tundra. It has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
