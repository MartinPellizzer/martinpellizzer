Hyoseris mauritanica

Hyoseris mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyoseris mauritanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
