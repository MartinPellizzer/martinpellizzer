Hieracium odontomeles

Hieracium odontomeles, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-orange composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically has hairy, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
