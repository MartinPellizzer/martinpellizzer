Hydrangea thunbergii

Hydrangea thunbergii, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant produces sterile flowers that form rounded clusters, while its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge. Hydrangea thunbergii thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
