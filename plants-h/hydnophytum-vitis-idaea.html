Hydnophytum vitis-idaea

Hydnophytum vitis-idaea, commonly known as the common crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Hydnophytum vitis-idaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil stabilization.
