Hordeum distichon var. zeocriton

Hordeum distichon var. zeocriton, commonly known as the two-row barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its two-row arrangement of grains on the spike.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike with two rows of spikelets, each containing three to five bracts.

The grain is small and usually yellow or brown, and it is used in various agricultural and brewing applications.
