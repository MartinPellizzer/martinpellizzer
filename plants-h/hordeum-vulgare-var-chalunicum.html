Hordeum vulgare var. chalunicum

Hordeum vulgare var. chalunicum, commonly known as the chalunic wild barley, is a wild grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience to environmental stress.
