Hieracium achalense

Hieracium achalense, commonly known as the Achalense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium achalense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
