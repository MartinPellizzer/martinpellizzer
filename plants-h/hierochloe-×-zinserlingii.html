Hierochloe × zinserlingii

Hierochloe × zinserlingii, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Hierochloe × zinserlingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual texture and drought tolerance.
