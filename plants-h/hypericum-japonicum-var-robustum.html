Hypericum japonicum var. robustum

Hypericum japonicum var. robustum, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in reddish-brown to orange-yellow glands, which give it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
