Hordeum flexuosum

Hordeum flexuosum, commonly known as creeping barley, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, blue-green leaves and small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides or in meadows. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Hordeum flexuosum plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat for insects and stabilizing soil in certain environments.
