Hieracium bifidum subsp. merianum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

merianum, commonly known as the Merian's Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family.
