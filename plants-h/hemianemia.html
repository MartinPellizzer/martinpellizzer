Hemianemia

Hemianemia, commonly known as a type of anemia characterized by a reduction in hemoglobin levels, is primarily a botanical concern in certain plant species.

It is often observed in plants that are deficient in iron, leading to chlorosis and stunted growth. The condition can be exacerbated by poor soil drainage, which limits root access to essential nutrients. Botanists identify hemianemia through visible leaf discoloration and overall plant weakness.

Proper soil management and nutrient supplementation are key to preventing and treating this condition in botanical settings.
