Hieracium irkutense

Hieracium irkutense, commonly known as the Irkutsk hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Mongolia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Hieracium irkutense is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
