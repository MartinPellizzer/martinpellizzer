Haloragis cartilaginea

Haloragis cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilage sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its fleshy, cartilage-like stems, which give it its common name.

Haloragis cartilaginea is often found in shallow water or damp soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
