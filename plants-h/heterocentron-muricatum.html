Heterocentron muricatum

Heterocentron muricatum, commonly known as the roughleaf watercress, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as streams and ponds. The plant has elongated, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, prickly structures called trichomes. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
