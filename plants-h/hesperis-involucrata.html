Hesperis involucrata

Hesperis involucrata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Hesperis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis involucrata is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
