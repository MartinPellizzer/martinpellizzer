Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp. helianthemoides

Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp.

helianthemoides, commonly known as the yellow St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
