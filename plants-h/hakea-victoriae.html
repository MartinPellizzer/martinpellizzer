Hakea victoriae

Hakea victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical or conical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds covered in a thick, fibrous coating. Hakea victoriae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
