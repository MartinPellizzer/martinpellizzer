Hebe hectorii subsp. subulata

Hebe hectorii subsp.

subulata, commonly known as the Hector's hebe, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand. It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardy nature.
