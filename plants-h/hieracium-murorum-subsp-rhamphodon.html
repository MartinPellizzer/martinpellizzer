Hieracium murorum subsp. rhamphodon

Hieracium murorum subsp.

rhamphodon, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter flower stalks and more compact growth habit compared to other hawkweed species.
