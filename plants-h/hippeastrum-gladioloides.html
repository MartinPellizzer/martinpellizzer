Hippeastrum gladioloides

Hippeastrum gladioloides, commonly known as the gladioloid amaryllis, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Hippeastrum gladioloides produces a tall, erect stem that bears a cluster of flowers at the top, often blooming in the summer months.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
