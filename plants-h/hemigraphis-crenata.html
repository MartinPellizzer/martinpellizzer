Hemigraphis crenata

Hemigraphis crenata, commonly known as Mexican native plant or shining geranium, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and red. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Hemigraphis crenata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
