Helichrysum buschii

Helichrysum buschii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, brightly colored yellow flowers that remain intact for a long period. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, tubular florets that form dense, compact flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum buschii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
