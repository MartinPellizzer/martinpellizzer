Hieracium bakurianense

Hieracium bakurianense, commonly known as the Bakurian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
