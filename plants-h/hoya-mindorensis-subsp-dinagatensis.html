Hoya mindorensis subsp. dinagatensis

Hoya mindorensis subsp.

dinagatensis, commonly known as the Dinagat Waxplant, is a rare epiphytic species native to the Philippines. It features thick, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
