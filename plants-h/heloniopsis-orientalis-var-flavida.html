Heloniopsis orientalis var. flavida

Heloniopsis orientalis var. flavida, commonly known as the golden heloniopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with ray florets that are typically bright yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
