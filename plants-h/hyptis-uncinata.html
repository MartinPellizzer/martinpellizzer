Hyptis uncinata

Hyptis uncinata, commonly known as black sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its gray-green leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Hyptis uncinata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
