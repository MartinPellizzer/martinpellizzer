Hieracium liebmannii

Hieracium liebmannii, commonly known as Liebmann's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium liebmannii is generally herbaceous, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy.
