Hieracium vagae

Hieracium vagae, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a discoid head.

Hieracium vagae is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
