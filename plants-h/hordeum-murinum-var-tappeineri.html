Hordeum murinum var. tappeineri

Hordeum murinum var. tappeineri, commonly known as the Tappeiner's wild barley, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in rocky or disturbed soils. It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
