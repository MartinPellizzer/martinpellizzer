Hibiscus glaber

Hibiscus glaber, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. Hibiscus glaber is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
