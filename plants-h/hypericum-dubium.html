Hypericum dubium

Hypericum dubium, commonly known as yellow woodbine, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in small groups at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Hypericum dubium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
