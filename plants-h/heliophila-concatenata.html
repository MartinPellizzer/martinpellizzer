Heliophila concatenata

Heliophila concatenata, commonly known as the water heliopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating on the water's surface.

Heliophila concatenata is known for its ability to thrive in moist conditions, making it a common sight in aquatic habitats.
