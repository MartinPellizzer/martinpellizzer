Hieracium borgundense

Hieracium borgundense, commonly known as the Borgund yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
