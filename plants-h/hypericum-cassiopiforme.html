Hypericum cassiopiforme

Hypericum cassiopiforme, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along streambanks and wooded areas. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are distinctly yellowish-green.

Hypericum cassiopiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
