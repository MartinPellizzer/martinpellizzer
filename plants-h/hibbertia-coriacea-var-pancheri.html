Hibbertia coriacea var. pancheri

Hibbertia coriacea var. pancheri, commonly known as the Pancheri sandblush, is a low-growing shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features narrow, ribbed, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with prominent, showy stamens, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
