Hieracium mattiroloanum subsp. martellianum

Hieracium mattiroloanum subsp.

martellianum, commonly known as the Martell's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
