Hedysarum alpinum var. philoscia

Hedysarum alpinum var. philoscia, commonly known as the alpine sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Hedysarum alpinum var. philoscia is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
