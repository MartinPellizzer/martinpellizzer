Hesperonix

Hesperonix, commonly known as the moonflower, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ability to open its flowers only during the night. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperonix is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its association with nocturnal beauty.
