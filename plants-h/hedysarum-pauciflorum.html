Hedysarum pauciflorum

Hedysarum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum pauciflorum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
