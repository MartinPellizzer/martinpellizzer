Hieracium pseudalpinum subsp. cochlearioides

Hieracium pseudalpinum subsp.

cochlearioides, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
