Hibiscus minimifolius

Hibiscus minimifolius, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates and well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus minimifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
