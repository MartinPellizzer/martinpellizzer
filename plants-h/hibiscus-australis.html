Hibiscus australis

Hibiscus australis, commonly known as the Australian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom throughout the year in some regions.

Hibiscus australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
