Helichrysum ambiguum subsp. vinaceum

Helichrysum ambiguum subsp.

vinaceum, commonly known as the red everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, bright orange to red flowers that are densely packed in clusters.

The plant is known for its long-lasting, dried flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
