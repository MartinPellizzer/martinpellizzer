Hymenachne cordata

Hymenachne cordata, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicles, which produce small, two-lobed flowers.

Hymenachne cordata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
