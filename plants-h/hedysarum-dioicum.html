Hedysarum dioicum

Hedysarum dioicum, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It has a taproot system and produces small, bean-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum dioicum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
