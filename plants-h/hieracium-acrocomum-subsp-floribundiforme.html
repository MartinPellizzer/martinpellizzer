Hieracium acrocomum subsp. floribundiforme

Hieracium acrocomum subsp.

floribundiforme, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
