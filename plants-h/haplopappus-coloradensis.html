Haplopappus coloradensis

Haplopappus coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces a fluffy, white pappus that aids in seed dispersal.

Haplopappus coloradensis is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed habitats.
