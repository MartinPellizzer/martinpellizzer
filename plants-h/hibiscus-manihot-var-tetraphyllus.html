Hibiscus manihot var. tetraphyllus

Hibiscus manihot var. tetraphyllus, commonly known as the four-leaf hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The four-leaf hibiscus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
