Hieracium austroslavicum subsp. austroslavicum

Hieracium austroslavicum subsp.

austroslavicum, commonly known as the Austro-Slavic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

Native to Central and Eastern Europe, this species thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

The plant is typically herbaceous, with a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.
