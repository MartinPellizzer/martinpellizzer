Haenianthus obovatus

Haenianthus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, yellow flowers in early spring.

Haenianthus obovatus is often found in crevices and on cliffs, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh, coastal conditions.
