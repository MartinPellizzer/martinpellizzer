Hyptis gratiolifolia

Hyptis gratiolifolia, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, narrow leaves and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis gratiolifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for its medicinal properties.
