Hieracium bifidum subsp. prasiolaetum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

prasiolaetum, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
