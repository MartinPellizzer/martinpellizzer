Hypericum patulum subsp. hookerianum

Hypericum patulum subsp.

hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's St. John's Wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features woody stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
