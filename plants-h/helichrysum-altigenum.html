Helichrysum altigenum

Helichrysum altigenum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking golden-yellow flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its flowers are composed of small tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, giving it a composite flower appearance.

Helichrysum altigenum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
