Helleborus viridis subsp. liguricus

Helleborus viridis subsp.

liguricus, commonly known as the Ligurian green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dark green, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers with showy, petal-like bracts during late winter to early spring. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique foliage and early blooming habit.
