Hieracium picotianum

Hieracium picotianum, commonly known as the Picotian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium picotianum is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
