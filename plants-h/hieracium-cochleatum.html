Hieracium cochleatum

Hieracium cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a spiral arrangement. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Hieracium cochleatum is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain regions.
