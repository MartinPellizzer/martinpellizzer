Hakea cycloptera

Hakea cycloptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces dense clusters of red or pink flowers, which are typically arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. Hakea cycloptera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features such as succulent stems and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for various pollinators.
