Heliopsis helianthoides var. helianthoides

Heliopsis helianthoides var. helianthoides, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with prominent central disks. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging wetland environments.
