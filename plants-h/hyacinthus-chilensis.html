Hyacinthus chilensis

Hyacinthus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes with their elegant and colorful display.
