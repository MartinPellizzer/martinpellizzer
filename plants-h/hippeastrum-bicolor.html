Hippeastrum bicolor

Hippeastrum bicolor, commonly known as the red and white amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, white, and pink. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb.

Hippeastrum bicolor is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
