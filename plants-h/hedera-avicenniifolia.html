Hedera avicenniifolia

Hedera avicenniifolia, commonly known as the Indian ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by black, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hedera avicenniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
