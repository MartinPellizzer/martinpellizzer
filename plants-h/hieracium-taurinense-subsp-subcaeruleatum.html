Hieracium taurinense subsp. subcaeruleatum

Hieracium taurinense subsp.

subcaeruleatum, commonly known as the Taurine Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically features upright stems with hairy leaves and produces blue or purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
