Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. cassianum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

cassianum, commonly known as the Cassian's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial herb that produces seeds through a process called wind pollination.
