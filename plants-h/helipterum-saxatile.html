Helipterum saxatile

Helipterum saxatile, commonly known as the rock helipterum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Helipterum saxatile is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
