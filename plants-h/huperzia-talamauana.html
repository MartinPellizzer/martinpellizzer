Huperzia talamauana

Huperzia talamauana, commonly known as the Talamauan clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and the presence of specialized structures called sporangia, which produce and release spores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its morphology includes a short, stout stem with whorled leaves that are often thick and leathery.

Huperzia talamauana is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and its potential medicinal properties.
