Hyparrhenia fastigiata

Hyparrhenia fastigiata, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its dense growth, it can form dense vegetation that supports various wildlife species.
