Hieracium levicaule subsp. subglaucicoloratum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

subglaucicoloratum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which blooms from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive glaucous coloration and specific geographic distribution.
