Hymenachne striata

Hymenachne striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant has linear, narrow leaves with a distinctive striated pattern along the midrib, which gives it its scientific name. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation.

Hymenachne striata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
