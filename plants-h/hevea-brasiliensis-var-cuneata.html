Hevea brasiliensis var. cuneata

Hevea brasiliensis var. cuneata, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces latex, which is harvested for natural rubber production. The plant typically grows as a tree with a straight trunk and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

This variety is valued for its latex yield and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
