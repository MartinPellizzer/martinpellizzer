Hieracium acamptum

Hieracium acamptum, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium acamptum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
