Hieracium venifloreum

Hieracium venifloreum, commonly known as the stinging weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

The plant is known for its irritant hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
