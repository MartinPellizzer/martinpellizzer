Herpestis nigrescens

Herpestis nigrescens, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The flowers are star-shaped with five petals and bloom in the summer months.
