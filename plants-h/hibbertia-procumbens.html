Hibbertia procumbens

Hibbertia procumbens, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

Hibbertia procumbens is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.
