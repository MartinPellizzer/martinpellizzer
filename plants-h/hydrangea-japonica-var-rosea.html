Hydrangea japonica var. rosea

Hydrangea japonica var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from pink to blue depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of sterile flowers, which are often mistaken for petals, and these blooms are surrounded by small, fertile flowers. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns red or orange in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
