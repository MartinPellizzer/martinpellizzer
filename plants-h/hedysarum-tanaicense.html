Hedysarum tanaicense

Hedysarum tanaicense, commonly known as Tanaic mountain vetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
