Hakea laevipes subsp. laevipes

Hakea laevipes subsp.

laevipes, commonly known as the smooth-barked hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and dense, cylindrical spikes of bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
