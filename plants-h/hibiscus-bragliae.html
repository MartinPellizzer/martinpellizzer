Hibiscus bragliae

Hibiscus bragliae, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white, and a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus bragliae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Hawaiian culture.
