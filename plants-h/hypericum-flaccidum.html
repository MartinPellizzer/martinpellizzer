Hypericum flaccidum

Hypericum flaccidum, commonly known as the drooping St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum flaccidum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
