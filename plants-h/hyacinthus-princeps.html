Hyacinthus princeps

Hyacinthus princeps, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Netherlands.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces oval, white to yellow bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Hyacinthus princeps is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
