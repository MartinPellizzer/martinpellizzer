Hesperomecon luteola

Hesperomecon luteola, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Hesperomecon luteola plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
