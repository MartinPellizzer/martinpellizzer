Hieracium viscosoides

Hieracium viscosoides, commonly known as sticky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant is notable for its sticky, glandular secretions on its leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped.

Hieracium viscosoides is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
