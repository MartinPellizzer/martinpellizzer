Handelia

Handelia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plants are native to regions in South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. They thrive in temperate climates and are often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Handelia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
