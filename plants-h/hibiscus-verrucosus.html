Hibiscus verrucosus

Hibiscus verrucosus, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus verrucosus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
