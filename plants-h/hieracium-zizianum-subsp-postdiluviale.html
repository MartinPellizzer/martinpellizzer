Hieracium zizianum subsp. postdiluviale

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

postdiluviale, commonly known as the postdiluvial hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, blooming from June to August.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated reproductive system.
