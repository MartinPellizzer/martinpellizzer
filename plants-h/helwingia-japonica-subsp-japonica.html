Helwingia japonica subsp. japonica

Helwingia japonica subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Japanese helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive red or orange inflorescence that appears in late spring.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, winged fruits that disperse via wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture for its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
