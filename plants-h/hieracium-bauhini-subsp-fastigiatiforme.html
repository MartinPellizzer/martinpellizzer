Hieracium bauhini subsp. fastigiatiforme

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

fastigiatiforme, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe, this subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is relatively low maintenance in garden settings.
