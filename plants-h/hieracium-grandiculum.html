Hieracium grandiculum

Hieracium grandiculum, commonly known as the large spearhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is often considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
