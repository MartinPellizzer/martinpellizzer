Hermannia fasciculata

Hermannia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered hermannia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hermannia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
