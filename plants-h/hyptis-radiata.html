Hyptis radiata

Hyptis radiata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis radiata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
