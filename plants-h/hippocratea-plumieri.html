Hippocratea plumieri

Hippocratea plumieri, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elongated and grass-like, providing a lush green backdrop to its colorful blooms.

Hippocratea plumieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
