Hieracium oxyodon subsp. tomense

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

tomense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and harsh environmental conditions. It produces numerous small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaves and specific floral morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
