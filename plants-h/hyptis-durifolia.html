Hyptis durifolia

Hyptis durifolia, commonly known as roughleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Hyptis durifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
