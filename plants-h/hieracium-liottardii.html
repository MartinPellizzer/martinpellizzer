Hieracium liottardii

Hieracium liottardii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered with dense, glandular hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by narrow, petal-like bracts.
