Hydrocotyle quinqueloba

Hydrocotyle quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed water pennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, with five distinct lobes on each leaf. Its small, green flowers are usually borne on slender stalks and bloom in the summer months.

Hydrocotyle quinqueloba plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
