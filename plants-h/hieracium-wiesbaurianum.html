Hieracium wiesbaurianum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum, commonly known as Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its yellow flowers bloom from June to August, and the leaves are alternate, hairy, and often lobed.

It is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
