Hieracium biscutellifolium

Hieracium biscutellifolium, commonly known as the biscutellifolium hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a minor weed in some agricultural areas.
