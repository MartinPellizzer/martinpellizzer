Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. silvicola

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

silvicola, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems with basal leaves that are broadly ovate and have hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
