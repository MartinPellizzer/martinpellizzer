Hemerocallis citrina var. citrina

Hemerocallis citrina var. citrina, commonly known as the lemon yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is noted for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers typically last only one day, hence the genus name "Hemerocallis," meaning "day" and "beauty.".
