Heracleum sphondylium subsp. setosum

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

setosum, commonly known as hairy cowparsnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces umbel-like inflorescences with small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are large, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive, aromatic odor.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found near waterways and in meadows.
