Hordeum kentii

Hordeum kentii, commonly known as Kent's barley, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets per spikelet. Its seeds are typically small and hard, adapted for dispersal by wind.

Hordeum kentii is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
