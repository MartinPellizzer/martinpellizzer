Hieracium brachiatum subsp. matrense

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

matrense, commonly known as the Matrenskaya hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, often reddish leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
