Hieracium longipubens

Hieracium longipubens, commonly known as the long-haired hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium longipubens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
