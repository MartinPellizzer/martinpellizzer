Hordeum parodii var. araucanum

Hordeum parodii var. araucanum, commonly known as the Araucanian barley, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
