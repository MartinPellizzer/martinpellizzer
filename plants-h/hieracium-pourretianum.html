Hieracium pourretianum

Hieracium pourretianum, commonly known as the Pourret's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous florets arranged in a composite head.

It is often found in disturbed or nutrient-poor environments, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological conditions.
