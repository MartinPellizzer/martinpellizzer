Hibiscus albovariegata

Hibiscus albovariegata, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a striking variegation pattern with white and green hues. This plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
