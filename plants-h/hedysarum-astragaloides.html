Hedysarum astragaloides

Hedysarum astragaloides, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Hedysarum astragaloides is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
