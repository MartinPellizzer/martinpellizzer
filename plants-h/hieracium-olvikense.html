Hieracium olvikense

Hieracium olvikense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal and lanceolate, often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium olvikense is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
