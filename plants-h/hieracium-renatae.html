Hieracium renatae

Hieracium renatae, commonly known as Renata's hawkweed, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Hieracium renatae is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
