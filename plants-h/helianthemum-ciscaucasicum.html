Helianthemum ciscaucasicum

Helianthemum ciscaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian sunflower.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the local flora diversity.
