Hyptis spicata var. micrantha

Hyptis spicata var. micrantha, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Additionally, it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
