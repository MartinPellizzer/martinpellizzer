Hieracium levicaule subsp. subfasciculare

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

subfasciculare, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems that are hairy and often branching, with basal leaves that are lanceolate and slightly hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender habit and the presence of sub-fasciculate phyllaries, which are a key taxonomic feature.
