Habenaria suaveolens

Habenaria suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing multiple stems that bear leaves and flowers. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow lip, often arranged in racemes. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, typical of many orchid species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
