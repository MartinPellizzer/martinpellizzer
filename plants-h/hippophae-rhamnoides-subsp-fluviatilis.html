Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. fluviatilis

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

fluviatilis, commonly known as the river sandthorn, is a shrub native to the river valleys of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along riverbanks and has a sprawling, multi-stemmed growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is well adapted to wet and saline environments, making it a hardy plant in challenging ecological conditions.
