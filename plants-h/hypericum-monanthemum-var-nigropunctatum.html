Hypericum monanthemum var. nigropunctatum

Hypericum monanthemum var. nigropunctatum, commonly known as black-spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with black or dark purple spots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue, while the plant is known for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
