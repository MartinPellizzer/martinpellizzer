Hieracium alpinum subsp. furvellum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

furvellum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a hairy stem and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
