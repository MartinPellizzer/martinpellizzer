Hannoa schweinfurthii

Hannoa schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
