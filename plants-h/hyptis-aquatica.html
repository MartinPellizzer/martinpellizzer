Hyptis aquatica

Hyptis aquatica, commonly known as water hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Hyptis aquatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
