Helichrysum arbuscula

Helichrysum arbuscula, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological settings.
