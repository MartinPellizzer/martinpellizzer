Hydrangea anomala subsp. petiolaris

Hydrangea anomala subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the climbing hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub that produces large, showy flower clusters. It is characterized by its ability to climb using aerial roots, making it suitable for trellises or walls.

The plant's leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers, which range from white to pink, are produced in panicle inflorescences.

The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
