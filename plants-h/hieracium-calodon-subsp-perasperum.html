Hieracium calodon subsp. perasperum

Hieracium calodon subsp.

perasperum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the stems are usually erect with a reddish tinge.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
