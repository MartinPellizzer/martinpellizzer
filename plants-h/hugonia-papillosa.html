Hugonia papillosa

Hugonia papillosa, commonly known as the silky waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and marshes, where it can thrive in muddy or sandy substrates. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters, while its seeds are dispersed by water currents.

Hugonia papillosa plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
