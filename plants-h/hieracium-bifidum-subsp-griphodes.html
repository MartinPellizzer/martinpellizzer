Hieracium bifidum subsp. griphodes

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

griphodes, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers with surrounding ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its widespread distribution and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
