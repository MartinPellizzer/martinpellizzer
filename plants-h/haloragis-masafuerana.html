Haloragis masafuerana

Haloragis masafuerana, commonly known as the Masafuera fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

Haloragis masafuerana plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
