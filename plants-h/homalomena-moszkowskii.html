Homalomena moszkowskii

Homalomena moszkowskii, commonly known as the Moskowsky's arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Homalomena moszkowskii is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
