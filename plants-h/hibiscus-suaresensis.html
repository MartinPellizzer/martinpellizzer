Hibiscus suaresensis

Hibiscus suaresensis, commonly known as the Suarese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to the arid environment of its native habitat. Hibiscus suaresensis is known for its striking red or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

It is also noted for its unique seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
