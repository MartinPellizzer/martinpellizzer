Hieracium sylvaticum var. crassum

Hieracium sylvaticum var. crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, preferring rich, loamy soils. Its flowering heads consist of numerous small yellow florets arranged in a composite flower head, typical of the daisy family.

The species is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
