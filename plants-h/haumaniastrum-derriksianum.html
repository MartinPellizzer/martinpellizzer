Haumaniastrum derriksianum

Haumaniastrum derriksianum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
