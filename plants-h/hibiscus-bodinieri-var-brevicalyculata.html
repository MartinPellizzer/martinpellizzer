Hibiscus bodinieri var. brevicalyculata

Hibiscus bodinieri var. brevicalyculata, commonly known as the short-stalked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
