Holopleura carioides

Holopleura carioides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in globular inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and its stems are often woody. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Holopleura carioides is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
