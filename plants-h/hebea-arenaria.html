Hebea arenaria

Hebea arenaria, commonly known as the sand hebe, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure. Hebea arenaria is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.
