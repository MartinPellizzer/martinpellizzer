Hesperis berlandieri

Hesperis berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis berlandieri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
