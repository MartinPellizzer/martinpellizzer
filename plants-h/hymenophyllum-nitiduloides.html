Hymenophyllum nitiduloides

Hymenophyllum nitiduloides, commonly known as the smooth hound's-tongue, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a sorus, which contains the spore-producing organs. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinct, glossy surface.

Hymenophyllum nitiduloides is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.
