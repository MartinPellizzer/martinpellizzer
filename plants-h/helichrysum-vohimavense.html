Helichrysum vohimavense

Helichrysum vohimavense, commonly known as the Vohimava everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange composite flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across its native habitat. It typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its striking appearance and resilience make it a notable species in the study of endemic flora.
