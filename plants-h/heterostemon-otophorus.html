Heterostemon otophorus

Heterostemon otophorus, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, mist-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
