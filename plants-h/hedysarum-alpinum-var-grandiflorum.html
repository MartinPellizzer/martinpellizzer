Hedysarum alpinum var. grandiflorum

Hedysarum alpinum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the alpine sweet clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Hedysarum alpinum var. grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
