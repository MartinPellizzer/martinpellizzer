Howardia bilobata

Howardia bilobata, commonly known as the African violet or the violet geranium, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Howardia bilobata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
