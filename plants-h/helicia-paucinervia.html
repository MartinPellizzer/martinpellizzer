Helicia paucinervia

Helicia paucinervia, commonly known as the Chinese holly or holly tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Helicia paucinervia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
