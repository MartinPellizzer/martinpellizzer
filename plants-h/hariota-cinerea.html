Hariota cinerea

Hariota cinerea, commonly known as the grayleaf harebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its pale blue to violet flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 1,500 meters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Hariota cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
