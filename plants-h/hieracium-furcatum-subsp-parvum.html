Hieracium furcatum subsp. parvum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

parvum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
