Hoffmannseggia drepanocarpa

Hoffmannseggia drepanocarpa, commonly known as the hooked-leaf hoffmannseggia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Hoffmannseggia drepanocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
