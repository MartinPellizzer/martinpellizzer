Heterotheca aspera

Heterotheca aspera, commonly known as roughleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Heterotheca aspera is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
