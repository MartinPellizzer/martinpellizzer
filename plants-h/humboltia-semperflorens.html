Humboltia semperflorens

Humboltia semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Humboltia semperflorens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
