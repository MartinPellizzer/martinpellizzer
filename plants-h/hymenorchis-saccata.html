Hymenorchis saccata

Hymenorchis saccata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, sac-like structure on the base of its leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a slender, upright stem. Its flowers are small and usually greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often more prominent than the other petals.

Hymenorchis saccata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
