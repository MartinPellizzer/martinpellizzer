Hosackia maritima

Hosackia maritima, commonly known as sea blush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hosackia maritima plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
