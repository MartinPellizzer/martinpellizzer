Hieracium tephrocephalum subsp. christoglossum

Hieracium tephrocephalum subsp.

christoglossum, commonly known as the pale-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like structure.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
