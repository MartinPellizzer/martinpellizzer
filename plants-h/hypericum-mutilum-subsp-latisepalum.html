Hypericum mutilum subsp. latisepalum

Hypericum mutilum subsp.

latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers with broad, rounded sepals that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodsy areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, orange-red fruits.

It is often found in the understory of forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
