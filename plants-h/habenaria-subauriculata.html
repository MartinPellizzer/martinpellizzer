Habenaria subauriculata

Habenaria subauriculata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces showy, yellowish-green to golden flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
