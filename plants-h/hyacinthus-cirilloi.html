Hyacinthus cirilloi

Hyacinthus cirilloi, commonly known as the Sicilian hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, showcasing its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are strap-like and grassy, emerging from a basal rosette of bulbs.

Hyacinthus cirilloi is valued not only for its beauty but also for its historical and cultural significance in Mediterranean regions.
