Harpullia aeruginosa

Harpullia aeruginosa, commonly known as the blue harpullia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, bluish-gray bark and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing bright red seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
