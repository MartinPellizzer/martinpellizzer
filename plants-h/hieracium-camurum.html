Hieracium camurum

Hieracium camurum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread rapidly.
