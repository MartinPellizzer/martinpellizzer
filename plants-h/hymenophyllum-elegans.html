Hymenophyllum elegans

Hymenophyllum elegans, commonly known as the silver hair-fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, hair-like fronds that give it a silvery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum elegans is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded conditions.
