Hippeastrum solandriflorum var. conspicuum

Hippeastrum solandriflorum var. conspicuum, commonly known as the showy amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and glossy. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is typically grown as a decorative indoor plant.

Its blooming period is usually in the spring or early summer, making it a popular choice for seasonal floral displays.
