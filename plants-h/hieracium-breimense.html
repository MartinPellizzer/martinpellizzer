Hieracium breimense

Hieracium breimense, commonly known as the Breimense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Hieracium breimense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
