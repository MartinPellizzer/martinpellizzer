Hieracium caesium subsp. arenariifolium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

arenariifolium, commonly known as the sandleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disk.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and ecological adaptability.
