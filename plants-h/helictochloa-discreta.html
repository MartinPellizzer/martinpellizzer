Helictochloa discreta

Helictochloa discreta, commonly known as the scattered wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Helictochloa discreta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
