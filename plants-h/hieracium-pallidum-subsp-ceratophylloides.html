Hieracium pallidum subsp. ceratophylloides

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

ceratophylloides, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
