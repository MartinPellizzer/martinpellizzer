Hieracium cochlearioides subsp. pseudoglanduliferum

Hieracium cochlearioides subsp.

pseudoglanduliferum, commonly known as the false glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its leaves are often glandular-pubescent, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
