Hypochaeris crepioides

Hypochaeris crepioides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a yellow disk.

Hypochaeris crepioides is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
