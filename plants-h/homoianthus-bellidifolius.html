Homoianthus bellidifolius

Homoianthus bellidifolius, commonly known as the bellflower-like shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, hardy plants.
