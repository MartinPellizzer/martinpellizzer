Heliotropium corymbosum

Heliotropium corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Heliotropium corymbosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
