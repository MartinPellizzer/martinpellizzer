Haworthia margaritifera subvar. polyphylla

Haworthia margaritifera subvar. polyphylla, commonly known as the "pearl plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in translucent, waxy tubercles. The leaves are often pale green with white or blue markings, giving the plant a striking, almost gem-like appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

This variety is prized for its unique aesthetics and low maintenance requirements.
