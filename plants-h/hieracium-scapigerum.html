Hieracium scapigerum

Hieracium scapigerum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

Hieracium scapigerum produces yellow flowers from June to August, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
