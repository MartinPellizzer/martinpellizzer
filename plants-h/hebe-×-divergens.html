Hebe × divergens

Hebe × divergens, commonly known as the broadleaf hebe, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of violet or blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
