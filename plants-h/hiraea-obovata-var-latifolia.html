Hiraea obovata var. latifolia

Hiraea obovata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hiraea, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is characterized by its robust growth habit and adaptability to moist, shaded environments.
