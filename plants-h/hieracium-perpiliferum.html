Hieracium perpiliferum

Hieracium perpiliferum, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
