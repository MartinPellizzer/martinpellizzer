Hippophae gyantsensis subsp. gyantsensis

Hippophae gyantsensis subsp.

gyantsensis, commonly known as the Tibetan sandthrift, is a shrub native to high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous terrain. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to harsh climatic conditions.
