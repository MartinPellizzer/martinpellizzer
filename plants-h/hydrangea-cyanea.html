Hydrangea cyanea

Hydrangea cyanea, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green.

Hydrangea cyanea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
