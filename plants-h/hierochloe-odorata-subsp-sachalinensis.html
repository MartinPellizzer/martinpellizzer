Hierochloe odorata subsp. sachalinensis

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to northeastern Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silver-green blades and a distinctive, sweet, aromatic scent when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
