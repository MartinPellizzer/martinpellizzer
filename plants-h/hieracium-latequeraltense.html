Hieracium latequeraltense

Hieracium latequeraltense, commonly known as the late Queeraltense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
