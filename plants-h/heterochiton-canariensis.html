Heterochiton canariensis

Heterochiton canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands rock lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in the unique microclimates of the Canary Islands.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungal component and a photosynthetic alga or cyanobacterium. This lichen is typically found growing on rocky substrates, where it plays a role in soil formation and nutrient cycling. Its structure consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to the surface.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species within the archipelago.
