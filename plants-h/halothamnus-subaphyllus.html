Halothamnus subaphyllus

Halothamnus subaphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed halothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting succulence and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in rocky and sandy soils.
