Hedysarum bupleurifolium

Hedysarum bupleurifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Hedysarum bupleurifolium plays a role in soil stabilization and is occasionally cultivated for its ornamental value.
