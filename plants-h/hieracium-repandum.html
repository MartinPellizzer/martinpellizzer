Hieracium repandum

Hieracium repandum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are usually reddish and hairy.

Hieracium repandum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
