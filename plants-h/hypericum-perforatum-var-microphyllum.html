Hypericum perforatum var. microphyllum

Hypericum perforatum var. microphyllum, commonly known as St. John's Wort with tiny leaves, is a variant of the common St. John's Wort.

It is characterized by its small, densely packed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its leaves are perforated, giving it a distinctive appearance and contributing to its traditional medicinal use.
