Helia ramosissima

Helia ramosissima, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological settings.
