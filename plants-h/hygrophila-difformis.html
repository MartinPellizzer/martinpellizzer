Hygrophila difformis

Hygrophila difformis, commonly known as the water wisteria, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, delicate white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. This plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize large areas in water gardens or ponds.

Hygrophila difformis is also valued for its role in water purification and as a source of oxygen in aquatic ecosystems.
