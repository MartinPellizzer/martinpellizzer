Heteropterys ciliata

Heteropterys ciliata, commonly known as the silky leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
