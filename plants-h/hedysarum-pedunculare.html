Hedysarum pedunculare

Hedysarum pedunculare, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Hedysarum pedunculare has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
