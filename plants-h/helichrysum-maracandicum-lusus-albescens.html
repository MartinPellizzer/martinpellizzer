Helichrysum maracandicum lusus albescens

Helichrysum maracandicum lusus albescens, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small, white clouds. The plant produces tiny, yellowish to white flowers that are often mistaken for cotton due to their soft, fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
