Hypochaeris hispida

Hypochaeris hispida, commonly known as hispid groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hypochaeris hispida is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
