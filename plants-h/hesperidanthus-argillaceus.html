Hesperidanthus argillaceus

Hesperidanthus argillaceus, commonly known as the clayroot or argillaceous gesneriad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or clay-rich soils and prefers shaded, moist environments. It has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain water in its natural habitat.

Hesperidanthus argillaceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
