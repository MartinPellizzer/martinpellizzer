Hieracium subquercetorum

Hieracium subquercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and tall, branched stems that bear yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies, contributing to its ecological significance.
