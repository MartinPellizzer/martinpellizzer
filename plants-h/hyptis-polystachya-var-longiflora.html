Hyptis polystachya var. longiflora

Hyptis polystachya var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered hyptis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces copious amounts of nectar, attracting various pollinators.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
