Helleborus odorus var. violascens

Helleborus odorus var. violascens, commonly known as the violet-scented hellebore, is a perennial evergreen plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking, nodding flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet, violet-like fragrance, which is particularly noticeable in its flowers. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

This variety is also noted for its winter blooming habit, making it a unique addition to gardens.
