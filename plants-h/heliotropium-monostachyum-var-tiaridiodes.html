Heliotropium monostachyum var. tiaridiodes

Heliotropium monostachyum var. tiaridiodes, commonly known as the golden heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The flowers emit a sweet scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
