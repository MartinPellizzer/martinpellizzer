Hyacinthus dubius subsp. hackelii

Hyacinthus dubius subsp.

hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other hyacinth species but is distinguished by its unique floral characteristics and growth habits.
