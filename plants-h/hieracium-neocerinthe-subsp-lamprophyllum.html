Hieracium neocerinthe subsp. lamprophyllum

Hieracium neocerinthe subsp.

lamprophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The subspecies lamprophyllum is distinguished by its particularly dense, silvery pubescence on the leaves, which gives it its common name.
