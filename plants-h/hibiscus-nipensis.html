Hibiscus nipensis

Hibiscus nipensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring large, showy flowers with five petals. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a serrated margin. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom from summer to fall.

Hibiscus nipensis is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
