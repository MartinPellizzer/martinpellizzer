Hydrangea coacta

Hydrangea coacta, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is dark green and dense, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Hydrangea coacta is valued for its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
