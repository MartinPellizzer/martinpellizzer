Hieracium vasconicum subsp. stenoprionatum

Hieracium vasconicum subsp.

stenoprionatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
