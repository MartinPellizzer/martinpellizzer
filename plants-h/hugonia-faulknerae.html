Hugonia faulknerae

Hugonia faulknerae, commonly known as the Falkner's hugonia, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is of significant interest in conservation and botanical research.
