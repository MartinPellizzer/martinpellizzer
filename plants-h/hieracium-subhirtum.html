Hieracium subhirtum

Hieracium subhirtum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the dandelion family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium subhirtum is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some environments.
