Hoya vitellina

Hoya vitellina, commonly known as the golden hoya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Hoya vitellina is known for its slow growth and ability to climb using its aerial roots.
