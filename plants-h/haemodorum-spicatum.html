Haemodorum spicatum

Haemodorum spicatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting pepper, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes several species known for their striking floral displays. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
