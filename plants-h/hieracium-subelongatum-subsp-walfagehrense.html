Hieracium subelongatum subsp. walfagehrense

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

walfagehrense, commonly known as the Walfagehren hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with inflorescences consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. Its flowers bloom from June to August, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.

It is considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
