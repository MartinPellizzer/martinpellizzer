Hemionitis otonis

Hemionitis otonis, commonly known as the mountain feather, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are divided into narrow, linear segments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters during the spring.

Hemionitis otonis is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions and is often found in association with other moisture-loving flora.
