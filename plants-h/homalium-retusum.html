Homalium retusum

Homalium retusum, commonly known as the rough homalium, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
