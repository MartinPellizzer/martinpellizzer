Hibiscus diversifolius var. subdiversifolius

Hibiscus diversifolius var. subdiversifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its botanical features include hairy stems and five-lobed petals that bloom in late summer.

Hibiscus diversifolius var. subdiversifolius is often found in the understory of hardwood forests, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
