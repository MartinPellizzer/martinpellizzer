Helianthus × tricuspis

Helianthus × tricuspis, commonly known as the tri-color sunflower, is a hybrid species within the genus Helianthus, native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive tricolored flower heads, featuring yellow, orange, and red pigments in the central disk and surrounding ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing abundant seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The botanical features of Helianthus × tricuspis make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and a subject of study in horticultural and ecological research.
