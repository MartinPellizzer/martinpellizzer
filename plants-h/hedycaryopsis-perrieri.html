Hedycaryopsis perrieri

Hedycaryopsis perrieri, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant wood. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark, woody fruits containing oval seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.
