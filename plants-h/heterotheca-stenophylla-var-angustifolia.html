Heterotheca stenophylla var. angustifolia

Heterotheca stenophylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
