Hieracium aethalodes

Hieracium aethalodes, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium aethalodes is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
