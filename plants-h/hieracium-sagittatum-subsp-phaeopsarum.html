Hieracium sagittatum subsp. phaeopsarum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

phaeopsarum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green to reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its subspecific name, *phaeopsarum*, refers to its dark-colored leaves and its position in the plant's growth structure.
