Helichrysum longiramum

Helichrysum longiramum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-filled heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its name comes from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chrysum" meaning gold, reflecting its radiant appearance.
