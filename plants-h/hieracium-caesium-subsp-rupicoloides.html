Hieracium caesium subsp. rupicoloides

Hieracium caesium subsp.

rupicoloides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its inflorescence consists of ray flowers surrounded by disk flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
