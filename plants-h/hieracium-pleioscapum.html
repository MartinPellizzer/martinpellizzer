Hieracium pleioscapum

Hieracium pleioscapum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or dissected. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium pleioscapum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
