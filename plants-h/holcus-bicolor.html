Holcus bicolor

Holcus bicolor, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white inflorescence, which contrasts with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture.

Holcus bicolor plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
