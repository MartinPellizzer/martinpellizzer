Holostemma pictum

Holostemma pictum, commonly known as the painted holostemma, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by follicles containing seeds with tufts of hair. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Holostemma pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
