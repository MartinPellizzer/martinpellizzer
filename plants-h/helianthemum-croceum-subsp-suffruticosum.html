Helianthemum croceum subsp. suffruticosum

Helianthemum croceum subsp.

suffruticosum, commonly known as the yellow rockrose, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and full sun, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
