Heterolaena intermedia

Heterolaena intermedia, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
