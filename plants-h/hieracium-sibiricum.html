Hieracium sibiricum

Hieracium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Siberian hawkweed is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
