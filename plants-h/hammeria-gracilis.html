Hammeria gracilis

Hammeria gracilis, commonly known as the common hammer plant, is a flowering plant native to western and central Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hammeria gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded garden conditions.
