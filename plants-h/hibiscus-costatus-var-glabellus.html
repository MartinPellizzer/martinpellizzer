Hibiscus costatus var. glabellus

Hibiscus costatus var. glabellus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are slender and often glabrous. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
