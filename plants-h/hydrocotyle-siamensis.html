Hydrocotyle siamensis

Hydrocotyle siamensis, commonly known as Siam shield-leaf, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, shield-like leaves that are typically light green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Hydrocotyle siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
