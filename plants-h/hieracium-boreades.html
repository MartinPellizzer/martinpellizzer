Hieracium boreades

Hieracium boreades, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually hairy and linear, adapting to cold and windy conditions.

Hieracium boreades produces small yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to the alpine flora.
