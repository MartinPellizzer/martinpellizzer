Hedera helix f. maculata

Hedera helix f. maculata, commonly known as the variegated ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy that features strikingly patterned leaves with white and yellow markings.

This plant is a climbing vine with dark green stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with variegation. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Hedera helix f. maculata is widely used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb walls or trellises.

Its foliage provides a decorative touch in gardens and indoor spaces.
