Hakea acicularis var. smithii

Hakea acicularis var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's needle-hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces cylindrical, red or yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
