Hirtella racemosa

Hirtella racemosa, commonly known as the clustered hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

Hirtella racemosa produces white to pale pink flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.
