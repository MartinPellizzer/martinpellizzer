Helia coerulescens

Helia coerulescens, commonly known as the blue helia or blue heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Helia coerulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
