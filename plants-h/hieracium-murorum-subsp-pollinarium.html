Hieracium murorum subsp. pollinarium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pollinarium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations due to its nectar-rich flowers.
