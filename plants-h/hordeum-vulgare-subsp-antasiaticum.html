Hordeum vulgare subsp. antasiaticum

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

antasiaticum, commonly known as the antasian wild barley, is a wild grass species native to central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas. It plays a role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in studies related to plant genetics and adaptation.
