Hieracium fumatipes

Hieracium fumatipes, commonly known as the smoky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive smoky or purple tinge.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
