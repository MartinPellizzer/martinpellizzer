Hieracium lachenalii subsp. substipatiforme

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

substipatiforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.
