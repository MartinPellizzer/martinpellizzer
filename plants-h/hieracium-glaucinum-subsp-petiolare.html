Hieracium glaucinum subsp. petiolare

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

petiolare, commonly known as the petioled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes.

The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a petiole, giving it its subspecies name. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

This species is often found in meadows and grassy areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
