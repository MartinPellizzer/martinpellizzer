Hemitelia multiflora var. parkeri

Hemitelia multiflora var. parkeri, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a woody stem with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
