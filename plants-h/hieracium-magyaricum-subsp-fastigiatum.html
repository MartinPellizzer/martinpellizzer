Hieracium magyaricum subsp. fastigiatum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

fastigiatum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, grassy habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is known for its upright growth habit and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated flower heads and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and taxonomic significance.
