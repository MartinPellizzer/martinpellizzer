Hieracium catamarcense

Hieracium catamarcense, commonly known as the Catamarcense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, producing yellow daisy-like flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
