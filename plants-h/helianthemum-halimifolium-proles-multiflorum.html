Helianthemum halimifolium proles multiflorum

Helianthemum halimifolium proles multiflorum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
