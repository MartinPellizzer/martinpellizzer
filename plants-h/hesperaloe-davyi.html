Hesperaloe davyi

Hesperaloe davyi, commonly known as the desert spear, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, cylindrical stems covered in sharp, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. Its thick, fleshy leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in low-water environments.

Hesperaloe davyi is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
