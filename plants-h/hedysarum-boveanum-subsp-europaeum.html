Hedysarum boveanum subsp. europaeum

Hedysarum boveanum subsp.

europaeum, commonly known as the European sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces pods containing small, brown seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Hedysarum boveanum subsp.

europaeum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
