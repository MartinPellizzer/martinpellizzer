Hieracium juranum subsp. perfoliatiforme

Hieracium juranum subsp.

perfoliatiforme, commonly known as the perforate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are often lobed and have a distinctive perforated or notched appearance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
