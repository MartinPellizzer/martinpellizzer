Hemionitis pantanalensis

Hemionitis pantanalensis, commonly known as the Pantanal hemionitis, is a rare and unique plant native to the Pantanal wetlands of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant thrives in seasonally flooded environments, adapting to the fluctuating water levels of its habitat. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is crucial for its survival in open, wetland areas.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Hemionitis pantanalensis is considered vulnerable, making conservation efforts essential for its preservation.
