Hyptis crenata

Hyptis crenata, commonly known as the roughleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis crenata is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
