Hieracium rhacodes

Hieracium rhacodes, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium rhacodes is often found in moist to dry soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
