Hieracium subhumile

Hieracium subhumile, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically features upright stems with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and serrated. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Hieracium subhumile is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of wet grasslands.
