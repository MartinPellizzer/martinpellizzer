Hypericum rupestre var. rotundifolium

Hypericum rupestre var. rotundifolium, commonly known as round-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are rounded and often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. This plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
