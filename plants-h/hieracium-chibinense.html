Hieracium chibinense

Hieracium chibinense, commonly known as the Chibin hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium chibinense is known for its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed or nutrient-poor soils.
