Hyptis villosa

Hyptis villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Hyptis villosa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
