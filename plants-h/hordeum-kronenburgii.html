Hordeum kronenburgii

Hordeum kronenburgii, commonly known as the Kronenburg barley, is a rare and ancient species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and has adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its seeds are small and hardy, often surviving in cold climates and poor soil conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Hordeum kronenburgii is considered a valuable subject for botanical and genetic research.
