Hippomarathrum pelviforme

Hippomarathrum pelviforme, commonly known as the cup-shaped burr plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-like structure formed by its bracts, which enclose the seed-producing structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to its arid environment.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
