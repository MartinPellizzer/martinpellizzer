Hemionitis reticulata var. brasiliensis

Hemionitis reticulata var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its distinctive, net-like venation on its leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.
