Hyacinthus pusillus

Hyacinthus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf hyacinth, is a small, bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It produces clusters of fragrant, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
