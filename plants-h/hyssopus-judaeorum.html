Hyssopus judaeorum

Hyssopus judaeorum, commonly known as Jewish hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Hyssopus judaeorum is known for its aromatic foliage and has been historically used in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
