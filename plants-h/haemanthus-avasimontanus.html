Haemanthus avasimontanus

Haemanthus avasimontanus, commonly known as the Natal grassbladder, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is often found in coastal regions. It has strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.

Haemanthus avasimontanus is a perennial herb that is relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soils.
