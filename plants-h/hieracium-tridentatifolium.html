Hieracium tridentatifolium

Hieracium tridentatifolium, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Hieracium tridentatifolium is a perennial plant that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain areas.
