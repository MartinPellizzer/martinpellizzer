Hymenotheca acaciiformis

Hymenotheca acaciiformis, commonly known as the acacia-like hymenotheca, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a feathery pattern, and its small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
