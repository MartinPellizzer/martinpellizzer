Hieracium aleuroclinium

Hieracium aleuroclinium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a rosette growth habit. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium aleuroclinium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is considered a common weed in some regions.
