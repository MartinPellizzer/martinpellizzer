Herniaria hirsuta subsp. cinerea

Herniaria hirsuta subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the grey hernia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It belongs to the stonecress family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, grey-green foliage.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, round capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive component of alpine and tundra ecosystems.
