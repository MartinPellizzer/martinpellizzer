Hymenocallis heliantha

Hymenocallis heliantha, commonly known as the sunflower amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It features large, showy white flowers with yellow centers that resemble sunflowers, hence its common name. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and slightly wavy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hymenocallis heliantha is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with its bulbs capable of producing multiple stems and flowers in a single growing season.
