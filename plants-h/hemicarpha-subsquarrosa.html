Hemicarpha subsquarrosa

Hemicarpha subsquarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose hemicarpha, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. Hemicarpha subsquarrosa typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
