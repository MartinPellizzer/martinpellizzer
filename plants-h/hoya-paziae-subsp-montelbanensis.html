Hoya paziae subsp. montelbanensis

Hoya paziae subsp.

montelbanensis, commonly known as the Montelbanes Hoya, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia. It features thick, fleshy stems and produces clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

This species is popular among succulent and foliage enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
