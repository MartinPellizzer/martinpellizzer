Hieracium laevigatum subsp. pizzulicum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

pizzulicum, commonly known as the Pizzulic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of broadly lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
