Hebe × balfouriana

Hebe × balfouriana, commonly known as the Canterbury bells, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Hebe × balfouriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
