Homopappus paniculatus

Homopappus paniculatus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, papery bracts that surround the flower heads, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to drought conditions with deep roots and a waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
