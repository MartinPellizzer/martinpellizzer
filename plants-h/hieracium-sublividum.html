Hieracium sublividum

Hieracium sublividum, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is recognized for its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Hieracium sublividum produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
