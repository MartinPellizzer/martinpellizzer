Hyptis arguta

Hyptis arguta, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in spikes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in open woods or along stream banks.

Hyptis arguta is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
