Hieracium murorum subsp. diminuens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

diminuens, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
