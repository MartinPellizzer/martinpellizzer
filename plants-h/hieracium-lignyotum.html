Hieracium lignyotum

Hieracium lignyotum, commonly known as the wood hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in woodlands and grassy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium lignyotum is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
