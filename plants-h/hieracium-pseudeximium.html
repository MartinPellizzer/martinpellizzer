Hieracium pseudeximium

Hieracium pseudeximium, commonly known as the false oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets, creating a striking visual appearance.

Hieracium pseudeximium is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
