Hydrobryum koribanum

Hydrobryum koribanum, commonly known as the Koribana water moss, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it anchors itself to submerged surfaces. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width, allowing it to absorb nutrients efficiently from the water. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on water currents for spore dispersal.
