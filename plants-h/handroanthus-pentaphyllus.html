Handroanthus pentaphyllus

Handroanthus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the yellow bell or golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
