Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. austromontenegrinum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

austromontenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegro red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its fruits are distinctive red, fleshy capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

It is characterized by its compact growth form and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.
