Heracleum austriacum subsp. siifolium

Heracleum austriacum subsp.

siifolium, commonly known as the Austrian cowparsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges across Central Europe.

The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, and its stems are often hollow and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it produces oval, brownish fruits that are ridged and hairy.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
