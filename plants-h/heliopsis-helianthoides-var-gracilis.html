Heliopsis helianthoides var. gracilis

Heliopsis helianthoides var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and radiating ray florets, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
