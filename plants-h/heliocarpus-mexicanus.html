Heliocarpus mexicanus

Heliocarpus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican bulletwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research.

Heliocarpus mexicanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
