Hemionitis acutiloba

Hemionitis acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hemionitis, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
