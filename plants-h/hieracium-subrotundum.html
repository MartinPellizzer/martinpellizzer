Hieracium subrotundum

Hieracium subrotundum, commonly known as the round-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are oval to round in shape, with a rough, hairy texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium subrotundum is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
