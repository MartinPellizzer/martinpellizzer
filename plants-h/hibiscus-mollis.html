Hibiscus mollis

Hibiscus mollis, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
