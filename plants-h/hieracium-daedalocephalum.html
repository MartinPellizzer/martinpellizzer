Hieracium daedalocephalum

Hieracium daedalocephalum, commonly known as the "Daealus-headed hawkweed," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, daisy-like composite flowers that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Hieracium daedalocephalum is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.
