Hieracium biebersteinii subsp. hypopogon

Hieracium biebersteinii subsp.

hypopogon, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows.

The plant features a caespitose growth habit with a dense rosette of basal leaves and elongated stems bearing yellow ray florets. Its flowers are usually arranged in a single head, with the outer rays being purple or pink in color.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact stature and is often found in high-altitude environments where it thrives in cold, dry conditions.
