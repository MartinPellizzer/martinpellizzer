Hirpicium gazanioides

Hirpicium gazanioides, commonly known as the gazania, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety, and are often used in floral arrangements.
