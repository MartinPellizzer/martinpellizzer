Hieracium guilielmi

Hieracium guilielmi, commonly known as the William's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread.

It is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habits.
