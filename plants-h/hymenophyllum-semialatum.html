Hymenophyllum semialatum

Hymenophyllum semialatum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
