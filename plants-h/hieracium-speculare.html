Hieracium speculare

Hieracium speculare, commonly known as the silver leaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hieracium speculare is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
