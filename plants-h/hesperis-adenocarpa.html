Hesperis adenocarpa

Hesperis adenocarpa, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis adenocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
