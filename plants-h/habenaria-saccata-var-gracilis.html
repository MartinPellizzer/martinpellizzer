Habenaria saccata var. gracilis

Habenaria saccata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender pouch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant typically features elongated, narrow leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
