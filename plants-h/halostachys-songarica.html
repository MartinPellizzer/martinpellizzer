Halostachys songarica

Halostachys songarica, commonly known as the Songarica halostachys, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and upright growth habit. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in dense clusters and are typically pollinated by insects.

Halostachys songarica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
