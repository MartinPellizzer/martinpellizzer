Hosta rectifolia

Hosta rectifolia, commonly known as the straight-leaf hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow during the summer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hosta rectifolia is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
