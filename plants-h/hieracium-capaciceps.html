Hieracium capaciceps

Hieracium capaciceps, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

Hieracium capaciceps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
