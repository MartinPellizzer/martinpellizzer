Helichrysum parviflorum

Helichrysum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered immortelle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are known to retain their color when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.

Helichrysum parviflorum thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
