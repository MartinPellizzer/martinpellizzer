Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. cappadocicum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp.

cappadocicum, commonly known as the Cappadocian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can vary in color from green to reddish.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.
