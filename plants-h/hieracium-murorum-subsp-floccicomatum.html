Hieracium murorum subsp. floccicomatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

floccicomatum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, flocculent (fluffy) leaf pubescence, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
