Hieracium schmidtii var. superbum

Hieracium schmidtii var. superbum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowering heads composed of numerous small disk flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil.

Its floral structure and growth habit make it a notable species in ecological studies of high-altitude flora.
