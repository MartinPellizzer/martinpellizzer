Hieracium subelongatum subsp. matajurense

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

matajurense, commonly known as the Matajuren hill hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience in disturbed habitats.
