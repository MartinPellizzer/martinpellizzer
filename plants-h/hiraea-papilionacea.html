Hiraea papilionacea

Hiraea papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant is recognized for its striking blue or purple flowers, which resemble butterflies, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
