Homalomena stenophylla

Homalomena stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caladium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, brightly colored leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and white, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Homalomena stenophylla is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
