Humata carolinensis

Humata carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
