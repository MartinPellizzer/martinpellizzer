Hydrangea macrophylla f. mariesii

Hydrangea macrophylla f. mariesii, commonly known as the Marieii hydrangea, is a cultivar prized for its large, showy flower clusters.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers, which can range from blue to pink depending on soil pH, are composed of sterile flowers (pistillate) and fertile flowers (staminate).

This variety is commonly used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant, colorful blooms.
