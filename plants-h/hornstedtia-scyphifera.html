Hornstedtia scyphifera

Hornstedtia scyphifera, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne on short, sturdy stalks, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Hornstedtia scyphifera is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
