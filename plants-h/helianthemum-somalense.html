Helianthemum somalense

Helianthemum somalense, commonly known as the Somali sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Somalia.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically solitary and have five petals, while the leaves are small and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to withstand drought conditions.
