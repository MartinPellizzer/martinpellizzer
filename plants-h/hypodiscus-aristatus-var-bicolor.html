Hypodiscus aristatus var. bicolor

Hypodiscus aristatus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a contrasting white or yellow base, giving it its varietal name. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
