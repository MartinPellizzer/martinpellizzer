Heterotis rupicola

Heterotis rupicola, commonly known as the rock fig, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
