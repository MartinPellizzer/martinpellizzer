Hebokia japonica

Hebokia japonica, commonly known as Japanese false indigo, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Hebokia japonica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
