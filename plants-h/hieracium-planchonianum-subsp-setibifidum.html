Hieracium planchonianum subsp. setibifidum

Hieracium planchonianum subsp.

setibifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive, flat-topped inflorescence. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is notable for its slender stems and relatively small, inconspicuous flowers compared to other hawkweed species.
