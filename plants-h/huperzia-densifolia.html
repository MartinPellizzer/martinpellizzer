Huperzia densifolia

Huperzia densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the sporangia for spore production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Huperzia densifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
