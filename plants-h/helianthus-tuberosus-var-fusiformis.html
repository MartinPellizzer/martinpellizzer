Helianthus tuberosus var. fusiformis

Helianthus tuberosus var. fusiformis, commonly known as the fusiform sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species characterized by its unique tuberous roots.

This plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a dark center. The plant is known for its edible tubers, which are rich in inulin and can be used as a starch source.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
