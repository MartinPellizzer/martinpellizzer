Hydrangea acuminata subsp. yakushimensis

Hydrangea acuminata subsp.

yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimense Hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH.

The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
