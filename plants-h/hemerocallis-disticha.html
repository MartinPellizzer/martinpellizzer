Hemerocallis disticha

Hemerocallis disticha, commonly known as the zigzag daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Hemerocallis disticha is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
