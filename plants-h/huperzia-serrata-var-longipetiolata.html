Huperzia serrata var. longipetiolata

Huperzia serrata var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled clubmoss, is a variant of the Huperzia serrata species, characterized by its elongated petioles.

It is a perennial evergreen fern-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the leaves.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
