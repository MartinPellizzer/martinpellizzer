Hedeoma pusilla

Hedeoma pusilla, commonly known as the small hedeoma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has tiny, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Hedeoma pusilla is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
