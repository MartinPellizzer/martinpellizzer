Hieracium ischnodes

Hieracium ischnodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium ischnodes is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
