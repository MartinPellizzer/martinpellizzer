Hierochloe jacutica

Hierochloe jacutica, commonly known as the jacutian bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted culms and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
