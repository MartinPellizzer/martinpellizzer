Hieracium sermenikense subsp. malevonicum

Hieracium sermenikense subsp.

malevonicum, commonly known as the Malevonic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
