Hieracium alatum subsp. thlaspidifolium

Hieracium alatum subsp.

thlaspidifolium, commonly known as the winged hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
