Homocolleticon praetermissa var. zuluensis

Homocolleticon praetermissa var. zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
