Hieracium trichophyton

Hieracium trichophyton, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium trichophyton is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
