Helianthus niveus

Helianthus niveus, commonly known as the white sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white to light yellow flower heads. The plant produces ray florets that resemble petals and are surrounded by a disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Helianthus niveus typically blooms in the summer months and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
