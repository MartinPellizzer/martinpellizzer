Heuchera richardsonii

Heuchera richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features attractive, palmate leaves that are typically green with red or purple veins, providing ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, offering seasonal interest in both form and color.
