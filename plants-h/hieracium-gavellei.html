Hieracium gavellei

Hieracium gavellei, commonly known as the Gavel's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces yellow-orange flowers from June to August.

Hieracium gavellei is often found in open habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological zones.
