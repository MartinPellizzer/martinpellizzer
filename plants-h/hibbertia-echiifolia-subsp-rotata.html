Hibbertia echiifolia subsp. rotata

Hibbertia echiifolia subsp.

rotata, commonly known as the spiral hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves along the stem, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
