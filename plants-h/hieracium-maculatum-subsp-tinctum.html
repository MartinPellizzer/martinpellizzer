Hieracium maculatum subsp. tinctum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

tinctum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed in certain regions.
