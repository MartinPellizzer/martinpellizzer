Hexadesmia

Hexadesmia, commonly known as the "starfruit" or "carambola," is a tropical plant belonging to the family Carambola.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces a distinctive, five-lobed fruit that turns yellow when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

Hexadesmia is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and has a unique, tangy flavor.
