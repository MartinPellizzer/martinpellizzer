Hepatica nobilis f. rhodantha

Hepatica nobilis f. rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered hepatica, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming period, typically from March to May. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down, and its flowers are notable for their vibrant red or pink hues. It thrives in woodland environments with moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
