Hieracium salicifolium var. eriocaule

Hieracium salicifolium var. eriocaule, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

The varietal name 'eriocaule' refers to the soft, woolly texture of its stems and leaves.
