Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. paniculatum

Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. paniculatum, commonly known as the Magellanic hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
