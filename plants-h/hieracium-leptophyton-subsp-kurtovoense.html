Hieracium leptophyton subsp. kurtovoense

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

kurtovoense, commonly known as the Kurtovoense Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
