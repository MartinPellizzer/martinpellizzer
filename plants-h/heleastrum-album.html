Heleastrum album

Heleastrum album, commonly known as white water flag, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and large, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically floating or partially submerged, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Heleastrum album is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
