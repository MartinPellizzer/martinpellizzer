Hieracium sparsipilum

Hieracium sparsipilum, commonly known as the sparse-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has basal rosettes of leaves that are hairy and lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters at the end of leafy stems.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to produce a large number of seeds.
