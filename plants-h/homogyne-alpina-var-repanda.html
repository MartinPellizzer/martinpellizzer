Homogyne alpina var. repanda

Homogyne alpina var. repanda, commonly known as the alpine homogyne, is a variant of the homogyne alpina species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variant is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments, exhibiting frost resistance and efficient water conservation mechanisms.
