Hypericum scabrum var. asperum

Hypericum scabrum var. asperum, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and has small, opposite leaves. It is part of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its scientific name reflects its distinctive physical features and geographic origin.
