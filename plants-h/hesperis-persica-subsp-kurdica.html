Hesperis persica subsp. kurdica

Hesperis persica subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

The plant produces showy, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained environments.
