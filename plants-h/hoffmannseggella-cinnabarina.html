Hoffmannseggella cinnabarina

Hoffmannseggella cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, often clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
