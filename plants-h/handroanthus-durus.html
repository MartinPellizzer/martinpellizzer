Handroanthus durus

Handroanthus durus, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
