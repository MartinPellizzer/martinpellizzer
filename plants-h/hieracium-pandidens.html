Hieracium pandidens

Hieracium pandidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium pandidens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
