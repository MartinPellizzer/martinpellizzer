Hibbertia platyphylla

Hibbertia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy yellow flowers with prominent red stamens, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Hibbertia platyphylla is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

It is often found in coastal and heathland environments, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
