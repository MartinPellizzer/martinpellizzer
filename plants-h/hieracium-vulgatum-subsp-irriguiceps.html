Hieracium vulgatum subsp. irriguiceps

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

irriguiceps, commonly known as the water hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structure and medicinal properties in some species.
