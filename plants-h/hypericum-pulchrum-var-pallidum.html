Hypericum pulchrum var. pallidum

Hypericum pulchrum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically woody and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
