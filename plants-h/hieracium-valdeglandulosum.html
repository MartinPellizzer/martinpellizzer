Hieracium valdeglandulosum

Hieracium valdeglandulosum, commonly known as the Valdese glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The species is notable for its glandular structures on the leaves, which may play a role in its ecological interactions.
