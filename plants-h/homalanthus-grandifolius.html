Homalanthus grandifolius

Homalanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
