Helichrysum saweri

Helichrysum saweri, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Helichrysum saweri is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
