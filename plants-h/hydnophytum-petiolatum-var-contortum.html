Hydnophytum petiolatum var. contortum

Hydnophytum petiolatum var. contortum, commonly known as the spiral-stemmed bladder fern, is a tropical climbing fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted stems that allow it to climb and entangle other vegetation. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces sporangia in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and adaptation.
