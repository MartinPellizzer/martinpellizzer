Hieracium christianbernardii var. subsarretoides

Hieracium christianbernardii var. subsarretoides, commonly known as the Christian Bernard's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has finely divided leaves and produces numerous small, composite flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is notable for its specific distribution and is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited range.
