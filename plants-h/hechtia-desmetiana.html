Hechtia desmetiana

Hechtia desmetiana, commonly known as the Desert Hechtia, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and often covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces striking orange or red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal.
