Hieracium floccimarginatum

Hieracium floccimarginatum, commonly known as the woolly margin hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
