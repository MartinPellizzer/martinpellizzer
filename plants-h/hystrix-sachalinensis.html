Hystrix sachalinensis

Hystrix sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. The tree grows in cold climates and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
