Hieracium gracile var. alaskanum

Hieracium gracile var. alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaska hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers are adaptations to its harsh environment.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in its native range.
