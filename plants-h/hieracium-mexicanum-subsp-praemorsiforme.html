Hieracium mexicanum subsp. praemorsiforme

Hieracium mexicanum subsp.

praemorsiforme, commonly known as Mexican hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
