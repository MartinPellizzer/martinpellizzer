Hieracium prasiolaetum

Hieracium prasiolaetum, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
