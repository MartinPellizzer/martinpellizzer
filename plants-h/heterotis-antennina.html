Heterotis antennina

Heterotis antennina, commonly known as the African water spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its edible, leafy stems and leaves that are often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for cultivation in wetland areas.

Heterotis antennina is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic ecosystems.
