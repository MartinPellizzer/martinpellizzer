Heterosamara carrissoana

Heterosamara carrissoana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a gelatinous coating. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Heterosamara carrissoana is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
