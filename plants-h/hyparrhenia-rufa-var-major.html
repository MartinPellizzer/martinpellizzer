Hyparrhenia rufa var. major

Hyparrhenia rufa var. major, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental and forage plant in various climates.
