Hibiscus fritzscheae

Hibiscus fritzscheae, commonly known as the Fritzsche's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are woody and somewhat branched. Hibiscus fritzscheae thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
