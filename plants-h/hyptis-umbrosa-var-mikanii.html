Hyptis umbrosa var. mikanii

Hyptis umbrosa var. mikanii, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover or as a border plant in shaded areas.
