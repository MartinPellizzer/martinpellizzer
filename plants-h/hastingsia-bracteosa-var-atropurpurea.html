Hastingsia bracteosa var. atropurpurea

Hastingsia bracteosa var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its striking purple to reddish foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
