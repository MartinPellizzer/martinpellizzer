Hypericum sarothra

Hypericum sarothra, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Hypericum sarothra is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
