Helianthemum apenninum

Helianthemum apenninum, commonly known as the Sicilian rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
