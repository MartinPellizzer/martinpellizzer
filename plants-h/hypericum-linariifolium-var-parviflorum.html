Hypericum linariifolium var. parviflorum

Hypericum linariifolium var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
