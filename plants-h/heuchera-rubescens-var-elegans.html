Heuchera rubescens var. elegans

Heuchera rubescens var. elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, palmate leaves that are typically deep red to maroon in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces upright, spikelike clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and moderate growth habit make it a versatile addition to shade gardens.
