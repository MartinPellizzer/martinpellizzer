Hypochaeris acaulis

Hypochaeris acaulis, commonly known as the small yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it is often found in disturbed soils. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer.

Hypochaeris acaulis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
