Hieracium sabaudum subsp. obliquum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

obliquum, commonly known as the bentleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and lanceolate leaves.

The subspecies obliquum is distinguished by its slightly curved leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to other forms of Hieracium. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
