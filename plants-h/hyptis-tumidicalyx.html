Hyptis tumidicalyx

Hyptis tumidicalyx, commonly known as the woolly hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis tumidicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
