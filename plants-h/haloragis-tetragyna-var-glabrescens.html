Haloragis tetragyna var. glabrescens

Haloragis tetragyna var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, often saline environments such as coastal areas and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, smooth pubescence. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, and they bloom during the spring and summer months.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in coastal or brackish conditions.
