Hieracium manocola

Hieracium manocola, commonly known as the Manocola hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, daisy-like cluster.

Hieracium manocola is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
