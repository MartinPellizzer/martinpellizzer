Hieracium pauculidens

Hieracium pauculidens, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

It is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.
