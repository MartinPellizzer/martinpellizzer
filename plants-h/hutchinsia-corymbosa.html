Hutchinsia corymbosa

Hutchinsia corymbosa, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Hutchinsia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
