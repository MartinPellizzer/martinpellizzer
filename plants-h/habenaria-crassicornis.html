Habenaria crassicornis

Habenaria crassicornis, commonly known as the broad-horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy rhizomes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a complex structure, featuring three sepals and three petals, with the latter often modified into a lip. It typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and swampy areas.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in humid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
