Hieracium pichleri subsp. adamovicii

Hieracium pichleri subsp.

adamovicii, commonly known as the Adamovici's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It is distinguished by its finely divided leaves and the presence of glandular hairs on its foliage.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
