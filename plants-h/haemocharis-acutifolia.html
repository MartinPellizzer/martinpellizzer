Haemocharis acutifolia

Haemocharis acutifolia, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Haemocharis acutifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, long-lasting foliage and unique floral display.
