Hieracium tephropogon subsp. pseudotridentinum

Hieracium tephropogon subsp.

pseudotridentinum, commonly known as the false threepoint hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, composite flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in open habitats across the continent.
