Habenaria × andrewsii

Habenaria × andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that often display a mix of white, purple, and pink hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding substrate, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
