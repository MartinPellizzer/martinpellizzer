Hieracium argutissimum

Hieracium argutissimum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
