Hermione virginea

Hermione virginea, commonly known as the silver moonflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, releasing a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a robust, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals that curl slightly at the edges.

Hermione virginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
