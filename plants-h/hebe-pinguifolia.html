Hebe pinguifolia

Hebe pinguifolia, commonly known as the silver hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe pinguifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
