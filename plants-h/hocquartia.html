Hocquartia

Hocquartia, commonly known as the white star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in clusters with each stem producing new growth from the base. Hocquartia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
