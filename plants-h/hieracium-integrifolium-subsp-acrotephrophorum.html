Hieracium integrifolium subsp. acrotephrophorum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

acrotephrophorum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that support its basal rosette of leaves.

The leaves are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with smooth or slightly toothed margins. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
