Heliconia schumanniana var. apicirubra

Heliconia schumanniana var. apicirubra, commonly known as the Crimson-tipped Heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lobster-claw-shaped inflorescences, which are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its leaves are large, broad, and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are pollinated by birds and insects.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
