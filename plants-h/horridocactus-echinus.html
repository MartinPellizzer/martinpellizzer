Horridocactus echinus

Horridocactus echinus, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a distinctive member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
