Hippeastrum leptandrum

Hippeastrum leptandrum, commonly known as the slender-flowered amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, strap-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hippeastrum leptandrum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
