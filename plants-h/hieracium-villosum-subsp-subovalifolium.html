Hieracium villosum subsp. subovalifolium

Hieracium villosum subsp.

subovalifolium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The leaves are typically ovate to suboval in shape, with a soft, woolly texture, and they are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by bracts, and it is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

It is a relatively low-growing plant, rarely exceeding 30 cm in height.
