Hieracium zizianum subsp. pachyphyes

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

pachyphyes, commonly known as the thick-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a disk.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
