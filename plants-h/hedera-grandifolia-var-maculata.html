Hedera grandifolia var. maculata

Hedera grandifolia var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted ivy, is a variant of the common ivy species.

It is a climbing evergreen vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western and southern China. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its foliage provides a striking contrast against darker backgrounds, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
