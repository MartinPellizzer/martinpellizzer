Hornemannia racemosa

Hornemannia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red flowers that bloom in racemes, giving it a showy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Hornemannia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
