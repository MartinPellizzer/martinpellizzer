Hyparrhenia glauca

Hyparrhenia glauca, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Hyparrhenia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
