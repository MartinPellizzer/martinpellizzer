Hyptis dilatata

Hyptis dilatata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often fragrant and can be used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Hyptis dilatata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
