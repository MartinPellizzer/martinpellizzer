Hieracium turbinicephalum

Hieracium turbinicephalum, commonly known as the turban-shaped hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features yellowish to brownish ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough or hairy surfaces, and it produces achenes as its seed dispersal mechanism.
