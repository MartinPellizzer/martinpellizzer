Hippocrepis comosa var. scorpioides

Hippocrepis comosa var. scorpioides, commonly known as the Scorpioid Heath, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, making it an important plant for supporting local pollinator populations.
