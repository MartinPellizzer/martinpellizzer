Hieracium keldii

Hieracium keldii, commonly known as Keld's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive yellowish or reddish coloration.

Hieracium keldii is known for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its high-altitude environment.
