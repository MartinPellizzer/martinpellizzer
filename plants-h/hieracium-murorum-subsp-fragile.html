Hieracium murorum subsp. fragile

Hieracium murorum subsp.

fragile, commonly known as the fragrant meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and sunny conditions.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

The subspecies is characterized by its delicate, fragile stems and is often found in open, low-nutrient environments.
