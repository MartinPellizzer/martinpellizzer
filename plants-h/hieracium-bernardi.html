Hieracium bernardi

Hieracium bernardi, commonly known as the Bernhard's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium bernardi is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climates and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
