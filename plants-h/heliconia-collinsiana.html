Heliconia collinsiana

Heliconia collinsiana, commonly known as the Collins' heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia collinsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
