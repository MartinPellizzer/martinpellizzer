Halimium umbellatum var. verticillatum

Halimium umbellatum var. verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems, giving it its varietal name "verticillatum.". The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand dry and rocky environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
