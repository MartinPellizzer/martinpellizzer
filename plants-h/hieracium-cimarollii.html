Hieracium cimarollii

Hieracium cimarollii, commonly known as the Cimaroli's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium cimarollii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
