Hypoestes verticillaris

Hypoestes verticillaris, commonly known as the "Whitewash Plant," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to red. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
