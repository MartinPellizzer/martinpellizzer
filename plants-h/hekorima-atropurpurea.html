Hekorima atropurpurea

Hekorima atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf hekorima, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Hekorima atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
