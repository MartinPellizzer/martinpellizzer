Hibiscus tetraphyllus

Hibiscus tetraphyllus, commonly known as the four-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus tetraphyllus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
