Homeria collina var. ochroleuca

Homeria collina var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered homeria, is a variant of the Homeria collina species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a herbaceous stem that is often hairy and a compact growth habit.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique floral coloration and ecological adaptations.
