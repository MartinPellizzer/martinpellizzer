Hymenophyllum thunbergii

Hymenophyllum thunbergii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a silvery, powdery coating. This coating, known as a trichome layer, helps the plant retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitats. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

The plant's intricate morphology and unique appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
