Hieracium lacerellum

Hieracium lacerellum, commonly known as the broken-hair hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
