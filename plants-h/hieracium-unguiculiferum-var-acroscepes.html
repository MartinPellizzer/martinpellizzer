Hieracium unguiculiferum var. acroscepes

Hieracium unguiculiferum var. acroscepes, commonly known as the acroscepes hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has alternate, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish to reddish flowers.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
