Hibiscus denisonii

Hibiscus denisonii, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and heat resistance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Hibiscus denisonii is well adapted to rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
