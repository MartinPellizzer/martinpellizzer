Heteropterys cordifolia f. eglandulosa

Heteropterys cordifolia f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-leafed heartleaf, is a variant of the species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often glandular-dotted, giving the plant its specific epithet. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive aromatic scent that attracts pollinators.
