Hieracium subtardans subsp. amphipolium

Hieracium subtardans subsp.

amphipolium, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a single flower head that blooms in late summer.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
