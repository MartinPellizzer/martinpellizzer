Hypericum kalmianum var. elongatum

Hypericum kalmianum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its longer stems compared to the typical form of Hypericum kalmianum.
