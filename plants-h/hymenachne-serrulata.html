Hymenachne serrulata

Hymenachne serrulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and floodplains, where it forms dense, rhizomatous stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated margins, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Hymenachne serrulata is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its tolerance of nutrient-rich and contaminated environments.
