Helichrysum patulifolium

Helichrysum patulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, disk-like flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Helichrysum patulifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
