Hymenocallis occidentalis

Hymenocallis occidentalis, commonly known as the western spider lily, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spider-like appearance. The plant features a bulbous root system and strap-like, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual element to the landscape.
