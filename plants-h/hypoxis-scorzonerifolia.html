Hypoxis scorzonerifolia

Hypoxis scorzonerifolia, commonly known as the star lily or false arum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, tubular, white or pale yellow flower that resembles a star, surrounded by a showy, leaf-like bract. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

It is commonly found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
