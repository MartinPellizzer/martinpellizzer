Helichrysum paralium

Helichrysum paralium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces compact, bushy growth with hairy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers are also used in herbal remedies and traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
