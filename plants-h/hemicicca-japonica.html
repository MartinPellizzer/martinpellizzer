Hemicicca japonica

Hemicicca japonica, commonly known as the Japanese sun rose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers, blooming in late summer. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem and leaves, adapted to thrive in arid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant appearance.

Hemicicca japonica is also known for its ability to store water in its tissues, making it drought-tolerant.
