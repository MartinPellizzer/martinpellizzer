Hugonia belli

Hugonia belli, commonly known as the bell-shaped honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds.

Hugonia belli is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
