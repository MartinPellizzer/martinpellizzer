Hebe × dartonii

Hebe × dartonii, commonly known as the Canterbury bells, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It features compact, evergreen foliage that is typically a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying variegated patterns. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, Hebe × dartonii is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
