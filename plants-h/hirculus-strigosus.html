Hirculus strigosus

Hirculus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy hirculus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
