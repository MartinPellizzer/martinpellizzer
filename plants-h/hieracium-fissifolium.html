Hieracium fissifolium

Hieracium fissifolium, commonly known as the fissile leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive, fissured appearance, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
