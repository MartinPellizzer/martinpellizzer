Hemerocallis dumortieri

Hemerocallis dumortieri, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow. The plant blooms for only one day, typically in the morning, before wilting by evening. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hemerocallis dumortieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in garden settings.
