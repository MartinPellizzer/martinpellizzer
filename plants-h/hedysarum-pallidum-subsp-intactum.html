Hedysarum pallidum subsp. intactum

Hedysarum pallidum subsp.

intactum, commonly known as the pale vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, rounded seeds. Hedysarum pallidum subsp.

intactum is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
