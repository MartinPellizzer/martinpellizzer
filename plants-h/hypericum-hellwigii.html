Hypericum hellwigii

Hypericum hellwigii, commonly known as the hellwig's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has woody stems covered in short, glandular hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum hellwigii is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
