Hemipilia takasago-montana

Hemipilia takasago-montana, commonly known as the Takasago mountain orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spurred lip and intricate petal arrangements. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are often pale pink or white, adding a subtle elegance to its natural habitat.
