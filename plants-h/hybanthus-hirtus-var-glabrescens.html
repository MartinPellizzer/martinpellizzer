Hybanthus hirtus var. glabrescens

Hybanthus hirtus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded areas within forests.
