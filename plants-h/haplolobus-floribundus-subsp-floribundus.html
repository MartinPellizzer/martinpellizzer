Haplolobus floribundus subsp. floribundus

Haplolobus floribundus subsp.

floribundus, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowering period extends from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens during the warmer months.
