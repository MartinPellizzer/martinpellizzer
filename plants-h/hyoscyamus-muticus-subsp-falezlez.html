Hyoscyamus muticus subsp. falezlez

Hyoscyamus muticus subsp.

falezlez, commonly known as the Mediterranean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
