Hieracium nigrescens subsp. repandum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

repandum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a sprawling growth habit with a central stem that produces numerous flowering stems.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with five petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
