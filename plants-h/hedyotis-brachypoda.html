Hedyotis brachypoda

Hedyotis brachypoda, commonly known as the small-leaved bladdernut, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal panicles, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
