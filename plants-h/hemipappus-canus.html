Hemipappus canus

Hemipappus canus, commonly known as the white burweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and along creek banks.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
