Hortia superba

Hortia superba, commonly known as the noble hortia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often displaying a deep green to bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Hortia superba is known for its resilience, thriving in humid environments with well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
