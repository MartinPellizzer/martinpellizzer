Hymenidium apiolens

Hymenidium apiolens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornapple, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to greenish flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the family.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts when mature.
