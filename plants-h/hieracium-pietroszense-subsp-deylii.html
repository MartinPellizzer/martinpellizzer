Hieracium pietroszense subsp. deylii

Hieracium pietroszense subsp.

deylii, commonly known as the Deylii hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower structure and wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
