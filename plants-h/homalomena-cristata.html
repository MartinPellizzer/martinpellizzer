Homalomena cristata

Homalomena cristata, commonly known as the Crowned Arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a starchy food source.
