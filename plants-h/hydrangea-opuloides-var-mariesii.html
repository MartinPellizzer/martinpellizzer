Hydrangea opuloides var. mariesii

Hydrangea opuloides var. mariesii, commonly known as the Mariei hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
