Haplopappus stenophyllus

Haplopappus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical herbaceous growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Haplopappus stenophyllus is often found in arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
