Hyptis tetragona

Hyptis tetragona, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and landscape designers.
