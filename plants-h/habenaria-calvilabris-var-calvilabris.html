Habenaria calvilabris var. calvilabris

Habenaria calvilabris var. calvilabris, commonly known as the hairy-veined marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features elongated, grass-like leaves and produces intricate, pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive hairy texture on the petals and sepals. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

The species is characterized by its unique floral morphology and is often found in association with specific wetland ecosystems.
