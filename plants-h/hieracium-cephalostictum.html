Hieracium cephalostictum

Hieracium cephalostictum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, basal leaves and produces yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
