Hexaptera famatinensis var. sphaerocarpa

Hexaptera famatinensis var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican star vine, is a climbing shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing food for various wildlife species.

The variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
