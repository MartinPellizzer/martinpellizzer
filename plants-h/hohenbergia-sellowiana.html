Hohenbergia sellowiana

Hohenbergia sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's hohenbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces striking inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette, featuring showy, spathaceous bracts. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hohenbergia sellowiana is a popular choice for indoor gardens due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
