Heptapleurum pseudobrassaium

Heptapleurum pseudobrassaium, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
