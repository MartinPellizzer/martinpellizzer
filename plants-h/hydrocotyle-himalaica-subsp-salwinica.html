Hydrocotyle himalaica subsp. salwinica

Hydrocotyle himalaica subsp.

salwinica, commonly known as the Salwin water shield, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its floating leaves that are usually divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as streams and ponds. Its small white flowers bloom in clusters and are often accompanied by a sweet, aromatic scent.

The species is noted for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
