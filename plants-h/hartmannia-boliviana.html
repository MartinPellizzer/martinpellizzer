Hartmannia boliviana

Hartmannia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with a cluster of narrow, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and are arranged in a dense spike.

Hartmannia boliviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
