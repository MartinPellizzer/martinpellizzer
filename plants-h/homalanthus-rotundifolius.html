Homalanthus rotundifolius

Homalanthus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has rounded, waxy leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Homalanthus rotundifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
