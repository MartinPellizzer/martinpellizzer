Heracleum antasiaticum

Heracleum antasiaticum, commonly known as the Siberian cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves.

It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the carrot family. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including Siberia and parts of China, and thrives in moist, open habitats. Its leaves and stems contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
