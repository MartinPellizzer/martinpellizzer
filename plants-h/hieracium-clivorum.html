Hieracium clivorum

Hieracium clivorum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium clivorum is often found in alpine meadows and slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
