Habenaria macroceratitis

Habenaria macroceratitis, commonly known as the large-fruited marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, shaded habitats such as marshes and forest floors. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store nutrients and water. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

The species is characterized by its elongated, showy flowers and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
