Hibiscus tiliaceus f. variegatus

Hibiscus tiliaceus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus tiliaceus, a species native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow or cream patterns. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Its foliage also provides habitat for various small insects and birds.
