Hypericum quadrangulum subsp. dubium

Hypericum quadrangulum subsp.

dubium, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
