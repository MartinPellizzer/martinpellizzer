Hoya rintzii

Hoya rintzii, commonly known as the "Moonlight Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of white, pink, and green. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the summer months. Hoya rintzii is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

It is also noted for its slow growth and ability to develop aerial roots, making it a unique addition to any collection.
