Hedypnois pendula var. pinnatifida

Hedypnois pendula var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the lace fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features long, pendulous fronds that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
