Hymenophyllum elatius

Hymenophyllum elatius, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small, epiphytic moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety appearance due to the presence of a thick layer of rhizoids and a dense mat of leaf-like structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Hymenophyllum elatius plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
