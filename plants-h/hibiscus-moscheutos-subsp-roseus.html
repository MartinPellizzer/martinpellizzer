Hibiscus moscheutos subsp. roseus

Hibiscus moscheutos subsp.

roseus, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus or roseate hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, with a prominent central stigma.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy surface, and the stems are usually hairy. Hibiscus moscheutos subsp.

roseus is a perennial herb that blooms from summer to fall, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
