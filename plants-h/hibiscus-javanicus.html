Hibiscus javanicus

Hibiscus javanicus, commonly known as the Java hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus javanicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
