Hera hebe

Hera hebe, commonly known as New Zealand forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Hera hebe is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
