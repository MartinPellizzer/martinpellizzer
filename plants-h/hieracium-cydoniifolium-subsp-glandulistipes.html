Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. glandulistipes

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

glandulistipes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features basal rosettes of broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are surrounded by involucral bracts. It is typically found in moist meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
