Helictotrichon mannii

Helictotrichon mannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its leaves are often narrow and have a distinctive blue-gray coloration due to a waxy coating.
