Hosta okamotoi

Hosta okamotoi, commonly known as the Okamoto hosta, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which remains attractive throughout the growing season.
