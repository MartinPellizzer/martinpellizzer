Hieracium distanticeps

Hieracium distanticeps, commonly known as the distant-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium distanticeps is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
