Hieracium soyerifolium

Hieracium soyerifolium, commonly known as the Soyer's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium soyerifolium is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
