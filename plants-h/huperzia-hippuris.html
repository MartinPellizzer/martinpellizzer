Huperzia hippuris

Huperzia hippuris, commonly known as the saddleback huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often segmented fronds that resemble the shape of a saddle. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, rounded clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia hippuris is known for its slow growth and is often found in temperate and subtropical regions.
