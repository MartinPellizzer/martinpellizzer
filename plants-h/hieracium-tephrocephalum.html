Hieracium tephrocephalum

Hieracium tephrocephalum, commonly known as the tephrocephalous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
