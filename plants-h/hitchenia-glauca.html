Hitchenia glauca

Hitchenia glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous hitchenia, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit and small, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Hitchenia glauca is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rocky or sandy landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
