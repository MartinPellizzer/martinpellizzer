Hedysarum varium subsp. syriacum

Hedysarum varium subsp.

syriacum, commonly known as Syrian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has a climbing habit and produces long, slender pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization.
