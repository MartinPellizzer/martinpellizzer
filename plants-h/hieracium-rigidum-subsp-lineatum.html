Hieracium rigidum subsp. lineatum

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

lineatum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is characterized by its rigid, upright growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
