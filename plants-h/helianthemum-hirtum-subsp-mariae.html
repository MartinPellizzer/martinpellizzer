Helianthemum hirtum subsp. mariae

Helianthemum hirtum subsp.

mariae, commonly known as the Marie's stonecress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand dry and rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky crevices or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
