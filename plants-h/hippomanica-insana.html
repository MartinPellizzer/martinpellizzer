Hippomanica insana

Hippomanica insana, commonly known as the mad man's thorn, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, low-water environments through its deep root system and thick, waxy leaves. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

Despite its beauty, it is considered toxic to humans and animals, with its sap containing irritants that can cause skin reactions.
