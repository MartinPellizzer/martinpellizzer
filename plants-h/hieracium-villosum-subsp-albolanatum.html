Hieracium villosum subsp. albolanatum

Hieracium villosum subsp.

albolanatum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and dense, white or pale green pubescence on its stems and leaves.
