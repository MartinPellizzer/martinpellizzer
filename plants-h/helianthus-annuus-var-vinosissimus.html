Helianthus annuus var. vinosissimus

Helianthus annuus var. vinosissimus, commonly known as the vinous sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its deep purple to almost black flower heads.

This cultivar features elongated, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stem is typically sturdy and upright. The flowers are composed of tightly packed, dark-colored ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular florets. It is a herbaceous annual plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its striking coloration makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and pollinator habitats.
