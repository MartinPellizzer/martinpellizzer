Hesperis matronalis

Hesperis matronalis, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Hesperis matronalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
