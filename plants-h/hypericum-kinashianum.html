Hypericum kinashianum

Hypericum kinashianum, commonly known as the Armenian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hypericum kinashianum is part of the genus Hypericum, which is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
