Hieracium transiens subsp. variegatisquamum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

variegatisquamum, commonly known as the variegated sandweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white.

The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium, which includes many common dandelion relatives.
