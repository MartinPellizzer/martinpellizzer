Houttea pardina var. calcarata

Houttea pardina var. calcarata, commonly known as the rock houttea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
