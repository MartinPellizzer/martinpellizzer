Hypericum denticulatum var. typicum

Hypericum denticulatum var. typicum, commonly known as the toothed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically bright yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
