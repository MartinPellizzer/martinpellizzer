Houstonia purpurea f. pubescens

Houstonia purpurea f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with slender stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to woodland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its pubescent appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
