Hortonia ovalifolia

Hortonia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved hortonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Hortonia ovalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
