Helianthus parishii f. oliveri

Helianthus parishii f. oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's swamp sunflower, is a variant of the swamp sunflower native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
