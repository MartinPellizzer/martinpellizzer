Hibiscus hispidus

Hibiscus hispidus, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus hispidus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
