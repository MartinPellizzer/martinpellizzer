Hieracium nigrescens subsp. poecilostictum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

poecilostictum, commonly known as the dark-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
