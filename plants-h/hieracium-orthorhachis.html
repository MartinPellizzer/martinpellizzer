Hieracium orthorhachis

Hieracium orthorhachis, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium orthorhachis is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it an aggressive weed in some regions.
