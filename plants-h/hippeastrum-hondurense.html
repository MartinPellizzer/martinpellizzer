Hippeastrum hondurense

Hippeastrum hondurense, commonly known as the Honduran amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange with a white or yellow throat. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are often striped with green and white. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hippeastrum hondurense is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other popular bulbous plants.
