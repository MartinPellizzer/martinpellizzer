Hypochaeris sichorioides

Hypochaeris sichorioides, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.

The leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape that aids in its identification.
