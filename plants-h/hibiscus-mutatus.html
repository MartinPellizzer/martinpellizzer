Hibiscus mutatus

Hibiscus mutatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in summer and are known for their fragrance, while the leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

Hibiscus mutatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
