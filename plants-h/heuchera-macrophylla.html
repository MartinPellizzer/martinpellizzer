Heuchera macrophylla

Heuchera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant variegation of red, orange, and green. The plant produces upright spikes of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, providing visual interest even when not in bloom.
