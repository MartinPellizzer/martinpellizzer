Hieracium chenocaule

Hieracium chenocaule, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, which is typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium chenocaule is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
