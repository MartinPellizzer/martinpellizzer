Hyptis densifolia f. pohliana

Hyptis densifolia f. pohliana, commonly known as the denseleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, often in shades of white, purple, or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
