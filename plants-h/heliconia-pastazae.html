Heliconia pastazae

Heliconia pastazae, commonly known as the Pastaza heliconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is native to the lowland rainforests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that surround its inflorescence, which resembles a bird's nest. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Heliconia pastazae plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various small animals.
