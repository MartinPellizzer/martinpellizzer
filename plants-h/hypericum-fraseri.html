Hypericum fraseri

Hypericum fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and medicinal properties.
