Hypochaeris pelivanovicii

Hypochaeris pelivanovicii, commonly known as the Pelivanovicii sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape that aids in its identification.
