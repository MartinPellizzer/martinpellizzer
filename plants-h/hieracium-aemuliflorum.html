Hieracium aemuliflorum

Hieracium aemuliflorum, commonly known as the mimic hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the dandelion, which helps it attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium aemuliflorum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habits.
