Hieracium nigrescens subsp. inlingulatum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

inlingulatum, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
