Helictochloa hookeri subsp. schelliana

Helictochloa hookeri subsp.

schelliana, commonly known as Schell's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a fibrous root system.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna in its native environment.
