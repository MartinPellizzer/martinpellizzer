Helichrysum versicolor

Helichrysum versicolor, commonly known as the variable everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that retain their color even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Helichrysum versicolor is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
