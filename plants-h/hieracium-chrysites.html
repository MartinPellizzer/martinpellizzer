Hieracium chrysites

Hieracium chrysites, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow-orange flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium chrysites is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
