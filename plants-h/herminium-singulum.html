Herminium singulum

Herminium singulum, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which are usually greenish-white or pale yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Herminium singulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
