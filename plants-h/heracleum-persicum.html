Heracleum persicum

Heracleum persicum, commonly known as Persian cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound leaves.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a hollow, ridged stem and a taproot. The plant produces umbrella-shaped inflorescences, known as umbels, that bear small white or greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a rough texture and a strong, spicy odor.

The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
