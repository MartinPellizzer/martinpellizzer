Hieracium canadense var. subintegrum

Hieracium canadense var. subintegrum, commonly known as the Canadian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the plant produces numerous small, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

This variant is distinguished by its more rounded leaf margins compared to other forms of Hieracium canadense.
