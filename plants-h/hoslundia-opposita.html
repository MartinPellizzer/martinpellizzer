Hoslundia opposita

Hoslundia opposita, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the distinctive purple or red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed and is known for its aromatic, slightly sweet flavor.

Hoslundia opposita is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its edible fruit, which is often used in traditional medicine.
