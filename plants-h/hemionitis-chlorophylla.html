Hemionitis chlorophylla

Hemionitis chlorophylla, commonly known as the green hemionite, is a unique aquatic plant found in specific freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant has a simple morphology with no true roots, relying instead on rhizomes for anchorage. Its chlorophyll-rich leaves contribute to its green coloration and photosynthetic efficiency.

Hemionitis chlorophylla plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
