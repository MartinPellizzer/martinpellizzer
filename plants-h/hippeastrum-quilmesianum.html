Hippeastrum quilmesianum

Hippeastrum quilmesianum, commonly known as the Quilmesian amaryllis, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The bulb is relatively hardy and can be propagated through division or seed, though it prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.
