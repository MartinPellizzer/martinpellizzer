Homeria ramosissima

Homeria ramosissima, commonly known as the silky-haired homeria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, waxy leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping.
