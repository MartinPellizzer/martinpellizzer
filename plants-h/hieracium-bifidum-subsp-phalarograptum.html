Hieracium bifidum subsp. phalarograptum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

phalarograptum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with two distinct lobes, and its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers. It produces small, oval-shaped achenes with pappus hairs, facilitating wind dispersal.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
