Helianthemum piliferum var. leiogynum

Helianthemum piliferum var. leiogynum, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandverbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are generally smooth and elliptical, with a grayish-green hue, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
