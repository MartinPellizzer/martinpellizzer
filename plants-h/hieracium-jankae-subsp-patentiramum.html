Hieracium jankae subsp. patentiramum

Hieracium jankae subsp.

patentiramum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and a compact inflorescence. It typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
