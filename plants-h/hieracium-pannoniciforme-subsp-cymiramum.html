Hieracium pannoniciforme subsp. cymiramum

Hieracium pannoniciforme subsp.

cymiramum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographic distribution within its native range.
