Helleborus siculus

Helleborus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in late winter or early spring. Helleborus siculus is adapted to grow in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
