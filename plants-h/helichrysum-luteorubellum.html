Helichrysum luteorubellum

Helichrysum luteorubellum, commonly known as the red-orange immortelle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Helichrysum luteorubellum is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
