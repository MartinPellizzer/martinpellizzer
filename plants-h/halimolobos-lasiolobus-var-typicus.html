Halimolobos lasiolobus var. typicus

Halimolobos lasiolobus var. typicus, commonly known as the rough-leaved halimolobos, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native flora.
