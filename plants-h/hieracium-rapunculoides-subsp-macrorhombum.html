Hieracium rapunculoides subsp. macrorhombum

Hieracium rapunculoides subsp.

macrorhombum, commonly known as the large-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with serrated margins, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
