Hydrangea macrophylla f. pulchella

Hydrangea macrophylla f. pulchella, commonly known as the lacecap hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a dense, flat cluster of small, sterile flowers at the center, surrounded by larger, showy sterile bracts that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant is known for its ability to change flower color based on soil pH, with blue hues in acidic conditions and pink in alkaline soils. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
