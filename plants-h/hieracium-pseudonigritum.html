Hieracium pseudonigritum

Hieracium pseudonigritum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium pseudonigritum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
