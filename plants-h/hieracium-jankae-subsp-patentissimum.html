Hieracium jankae subsp. patentissimum

Hieracium jankae subsp.

patentissimum, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more prominent leaves compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
