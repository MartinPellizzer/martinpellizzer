Herniaria hirsuta var. parviflora

Herniaria hirsuta var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, white or pink flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky habitats.

Its compact growth form and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
