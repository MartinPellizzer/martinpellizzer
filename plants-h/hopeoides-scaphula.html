Hopeoides scaphula

Hopeoides scaphula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
