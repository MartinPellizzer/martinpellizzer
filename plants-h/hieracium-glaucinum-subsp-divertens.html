Hieracium glaucinum subsp. divertens

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

divertens, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
