Heteranthelium piliferum

Heteranthelium piliferum, commonly known as the hairy thread moss, is a small, delicate moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its wiry, leafy stems covered in fine, hairy structures, which give it its common name. The leaves are linear and often have a distinct midrib, contributing to its intricate appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for studying moss morphology and ecological adaptations.
