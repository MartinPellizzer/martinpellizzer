Hippomarathrum bocconei var. denticulatum

Hippomarathrum bocconei var. denticulatum, commonly known as the denticulate leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
