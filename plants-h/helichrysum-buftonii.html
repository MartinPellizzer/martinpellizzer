Helichrysum buftonii

Helichrysum buftonii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its golden-yellow, sun-like flower heads. The plant features wiry, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are typically short-lived but are often retained on the plant for an extended period, giving it a decorative appearance.
