Hieracium sparsum subsp. naegelianiforme

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

naegelianiforme, commonly known as the Naegeli's hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clustered yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, leaf-like bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun.
