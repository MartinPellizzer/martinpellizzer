Hydrocotyle sarmentosa

Hydrocotyle sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping marshweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its stems are often prostrate, allowing it to spread across the surface of wet soils.

Hydrocotyle sarmentosa is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
