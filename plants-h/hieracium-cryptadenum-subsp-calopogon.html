Hieracium cryptadenum subsp. calopogon

Hieracium cryptadenum subsp.

calopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters.
