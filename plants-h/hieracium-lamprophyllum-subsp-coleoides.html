Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp. coleoides

Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp.

coleoides, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.
