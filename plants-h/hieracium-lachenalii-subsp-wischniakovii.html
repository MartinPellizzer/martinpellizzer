Hieracium lachenalii subsp. wischniakovii

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

wischniakovii, commonly known as the Wischniakovii hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and a basal rosette structure, which helps it conserve water.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations and distribution patterns.
