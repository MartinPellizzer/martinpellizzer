Hieracium nudum

Hieracium nudum, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has slender, erect stems and lacks leaf hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium nudum is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
