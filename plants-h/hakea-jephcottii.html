Hakea jephcottii

Hakea jephcottii, commonly known as the Jephcott's hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or greyish tint. The plant produces cylindrical, woody flower spikes that bloom in spring, featuring small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Hakea jephcottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
