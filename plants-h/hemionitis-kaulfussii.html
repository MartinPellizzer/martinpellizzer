Hemionitis kaulfussii

Hemionitis kaulfussii, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a small, compact fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping fronds that form a rosette-like structure, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces spore-bearing leaves that are typically pale green or silvery in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a resilient plant that can survive in extreme desert conditions.
