Huperzia friburgensis

Huperzia friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburg moss fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to regions in South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru.

Huperzia friburgensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
