Hesperis glauca

Hesperis glauca, commonly known as the blue scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Hesperis glauca has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as cabbages and mustards.
