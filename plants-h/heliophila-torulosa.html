Heliophila torulosa

Heliophila torulosa, commonly known as the yellow water plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating or submerged depending on water levels.

Heliophila torulosa plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and supporting biodiversity.
