Helichrysum corymbiforme

Helichrysum corymbiforme, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its long-lasting flowers make it an attractive addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
