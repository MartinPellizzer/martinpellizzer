Hydrostachys maximus

Hydrostachys maximus, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and are arranged in spikes.

Hydrostachys maximus is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
