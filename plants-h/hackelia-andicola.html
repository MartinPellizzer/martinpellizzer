Hackelia andicola

Hackelia andicola, commonly known as the Andean bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually erect and branched.

Hackelia andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
