Henryastrum silvestrii

Henryastrum silvestrii, commonly known as the forest starflower, is a strikingly beautiful aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant has long, slender stems that float on the water's surface, with leaves that are narrow and slightly pointed. Its flowers emit a faint fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Henryastrum silvestrii is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
