Hippophae sibirica

Hippophae sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian sandthrift, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and ecological restoration.
