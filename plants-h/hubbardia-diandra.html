Hubbardia diandra

Hubbardia diandra, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a single, terminal inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
