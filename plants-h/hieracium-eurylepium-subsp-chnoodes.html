Hieracium eurylepium subsp. chnoodes

Hieracium eurylepium subsp.

chnoodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
