Huperzia delbrueckii

Huperzia delbrueckii, commonly known as the Delbrueck's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to the substrate, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
