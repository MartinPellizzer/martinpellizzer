Hieracium sabaudum subsp. propinquum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

propinquum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is known for its invasive potential in certain regions, often forming dense mats that can outcompete native vegetation.
