Hieracium caesium subsp. petrocharis

Hieracium caesium subsp.

petrocharis, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. It has basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces numerous small, showy florets arranged in a disk-like inflorescence.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in isolated populations in mountainous areas.
