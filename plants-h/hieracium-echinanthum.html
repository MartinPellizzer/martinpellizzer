Hieracium echinanthum

Hieracium echinanthum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellowish-green, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Hieracium echinanthum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
