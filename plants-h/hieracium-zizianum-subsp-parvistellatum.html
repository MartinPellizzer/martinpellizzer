Hieracium zizianum subsp. parvistellatum

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

parvistellatum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This subspecies is notable for its small, compact inflorescences and is often found in dry, rocky environments.
