Hippia integrifolia

Hippia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically smooth and entire, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
