Hierochloe tongo

Hierochloe tongo, commonly known as tongo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, particularly found in regions of Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hierochloe tongo is often used in traditional practices and has ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity.
