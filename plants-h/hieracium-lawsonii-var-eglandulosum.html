Hieracium lawsonii var. eglandulosum

Hieracium lawsonii var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The variety eglandulosum is distinguished by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique botanical features.
