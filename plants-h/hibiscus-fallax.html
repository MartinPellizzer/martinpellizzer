Hibiscus fallax

Hibiscus fallax, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Hibiscus fallax is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
