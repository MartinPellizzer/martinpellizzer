Hybanthus ilicifolius

Hybanthus ilicifolius, commonly known as the holly-leaf hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often described as resembling holly leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Hybanthus ilicifolius make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
