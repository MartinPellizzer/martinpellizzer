Hieracium pyrenaicum var. pilosum

Hieracium pyrenaicum var. pilosum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with dense, white or grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental value in natural landscapes.
