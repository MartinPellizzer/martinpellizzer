Helianthus debilis subsp. runyonii

Helianthus debilis subsp.

runyonii, commonly known as the Florida blanketflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 inches and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
