Hieracium adusticeps subsp. subadusticeps

Hieracium adusticeps subsp.

subadusticeps, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a single, flat-topped inflorescence.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf coloration and is often found in sunny, well-drained environments.
