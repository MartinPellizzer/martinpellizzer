Humboltia fockei

Humboltia fockei, commonly known as the Focke's humboldtia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and is often found in areas with high salt exposure.

Humboltia fockei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
