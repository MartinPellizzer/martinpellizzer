Helichrysum flexiramum

Helichrysum flexiramum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small flowers. The plant is shrub-like, with narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
