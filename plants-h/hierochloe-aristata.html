Hierochloe aristata

Hierochloe aristata, commonly known as bluegrasses, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Hierochloe aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
