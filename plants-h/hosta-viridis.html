Hosta viridis

Hosta viridis, commonly known as the Green Hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a solid green color, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months. Hosta viridis thrives in shady, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
