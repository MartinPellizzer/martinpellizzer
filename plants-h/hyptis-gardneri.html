Hyptis gardneri

Hyptis gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants, and it typically features gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Hyptis gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
