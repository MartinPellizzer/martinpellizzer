Hibiscus unilateralis

Hibiscus unilateralis, commonly known as the one-sided hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a single, large, showy flower that opens in a spiral pattern, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Hibiscus unilateralis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
