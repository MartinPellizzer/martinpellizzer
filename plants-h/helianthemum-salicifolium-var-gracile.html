Helianthemum salicifolium var. gracile

Helianthemum salicifolium var. gracile, commonly known as the slender rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
