Helichrysum macrocephalum

Helichrysum macrocephalum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow-orange flowers that remain vibrant even after drying, making it popular in dried floral arrangements. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring Mediterranean climates.

Helichrysum macrocephalum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
