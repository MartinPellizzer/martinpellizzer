Hasseltia micrantha

Hasseltia micrantha, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
