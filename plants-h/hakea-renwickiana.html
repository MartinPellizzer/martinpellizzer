Hakea renwickiana

Hakea renwickiana, commonly known as the Renwick's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces vibrant red or orange cylindrical flower spikes during spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Hakea renwickiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
