Helichrysum cunninghamii

Helichrysum cunninghamii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow flower heads that resemble small, compact daisies. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting nature and vibrant color.

Helichrysum cunninghamii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
