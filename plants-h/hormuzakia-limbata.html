Hormuzakia limbata

Hormuzakia limbata, commonly known as the Persian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Hormuzakia limbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
