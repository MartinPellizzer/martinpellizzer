Hieracium bauhini subsp. kerneri

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
