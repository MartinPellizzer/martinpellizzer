Heinsia pulchella

Heinsia pulchella, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant has silvery-gray leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant pink or white blooms. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Heinsia pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
