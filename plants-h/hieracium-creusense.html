Hieracium creusense

Hieracium creusense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a dark green color, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium creusense is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
