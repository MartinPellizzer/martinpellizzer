Hordeum vulgare subsp. agriocrithon

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

agriocrithon, commonly known as barley grass, is a subspecies of barley characterized by its vigorous growth and high nutritional value. It is a cool-season grass that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain the seeds. It is valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a forage crop due to its rich content of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
