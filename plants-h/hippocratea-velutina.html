Hippocratea velutina

Hippocratea velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Hippocratea velutina thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
