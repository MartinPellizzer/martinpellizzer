Hibiscus sharpei

Hibiscus sharpei, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus sharpei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
