Hieracium reuteri

Hieracium reuteri, commonly known as the Reuter's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a mat-forming growth habit and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Hieracium reuteri is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
