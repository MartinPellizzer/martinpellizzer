Hieracium caesium subsp. davallianum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

davallianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable component of its native ecosystems.
