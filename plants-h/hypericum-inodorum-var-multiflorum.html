Hypericum inodorum var. multiflorum

Hypericum inodorum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the sweet woodruff.

This perennial plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes various species known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is widely used in herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
