Hymenidium pachycaule

Hymenidium pachycaule, commonly known as the giant dayflower, is a striking member of the Commelinaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its large, showy flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers, which open during the day, are typically pink or red and attract various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
