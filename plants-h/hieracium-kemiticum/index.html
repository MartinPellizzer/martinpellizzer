Hieracium Kemiticum

Hieracium kemiticum is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of Egypt. It belongs to the dandelion family and is known for its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. This species thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing in isolated pockets of vegetation. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces significant conservation challenges. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
