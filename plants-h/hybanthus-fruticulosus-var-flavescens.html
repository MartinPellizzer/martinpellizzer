Hybanthus fruticulosus var. flavescens

Hybanthus fruticulosus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Morning Glory family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, exotic blooms.
