Hieracium bracteifolium

Hieracium bracteifolium, commonly known as the bracteate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium bracteifolium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
