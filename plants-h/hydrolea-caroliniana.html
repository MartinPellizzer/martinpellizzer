Hydrolea caroliniana

Hydrolea caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina hydrolea, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Hydrolea caroliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
