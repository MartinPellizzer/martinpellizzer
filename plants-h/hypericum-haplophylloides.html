Hypericum haplophylloides

Hypericum haplophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its compact growth and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
