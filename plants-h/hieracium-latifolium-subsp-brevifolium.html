Hieracium latifolium subsp. brevifolium

Hieracium latifolium subsp.

brevifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The subspecies brevifolium is distinguished by its shorter, more compact leaves compared to other varieties within the species.
