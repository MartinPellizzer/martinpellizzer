Hydrangea macrophylla var. megacarpa

Hydrangea macrophylla var. megacarpa, commonly known as the giant hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces both sterile and fertile flowers, with the former contributing to the vibrant blue or pink hues depending on soil pH. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
