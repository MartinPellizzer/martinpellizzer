Hepatica acutiloba f. rosea

Hepatica acutiloba f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered liverleaf, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and early spring blooms. The flowers are typically pink or rose-colored, with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in woodland habitats with moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting early pollinators.
