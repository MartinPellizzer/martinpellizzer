Hyparrhenia andongensis

Hyparrhenia andongensis, commonly known as the Andong grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Hyparrhenia andongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
