Humiria procera

Humiria procera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in disturbed or open habitats.
