Hieracium leptophyton subsp. eurhostum

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

eurhostum, commonly known as the European lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, dandelion-like flowers.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
