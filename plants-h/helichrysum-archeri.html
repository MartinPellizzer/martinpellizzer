Helichrysum archeri

Helichrysum archeri, commonly known as the Cape gold or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small sunflowers, which retain their color and form even after drying. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, hairy leaves that emit a subtle, sweet scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its resilience and ornamental value have made it a sought-after plant in horticulture worldwide.
