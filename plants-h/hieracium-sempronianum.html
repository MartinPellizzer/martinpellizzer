Hieracium sempronianum

Hieracium sempronianum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in glandular dots.

Hieracium sempronianum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in various regions.
