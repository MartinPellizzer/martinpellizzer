Homeria pallida

Homeria pallida, commonly known as the pale homeria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
