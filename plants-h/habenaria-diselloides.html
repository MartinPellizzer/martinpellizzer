Habenaria diselloides

Habenaria diselloides, commonly known as the Diselloide Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale green or white with a distinctive, intricate pattern of markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
