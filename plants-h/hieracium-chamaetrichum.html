Hieracium chamaetrichum

Hieracium chamaetrichum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are sprawling and often root at the nodes.

It is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its runners.
