Helianthus occidentalis var. illinoensis

Helianthus occidentalis var. illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for various habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Illinois goldenrod plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the autumn season.
