Helichrysum hyphocephalum

Helichrysum hyphocephalum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces numerous small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is notable for its aromatic foliage, which emits a strong, spicy scent when crushed. Its flowers are typically long-lasting when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.

Helichrysum hyphocephalum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.
