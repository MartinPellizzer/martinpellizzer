Hypoxis goetzei

Hypoxis goetzei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis goetzei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
