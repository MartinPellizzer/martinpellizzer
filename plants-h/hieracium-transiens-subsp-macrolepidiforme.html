Hieracium transiens subsp. macrolepidiforme

Hieracium transiens subsp.

macrolepidiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed or toothed margin.
