Hypocistis sanguinea

Hypocistis sanguinea, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish itself in the understory of forests. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific species of bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
