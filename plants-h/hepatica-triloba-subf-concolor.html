Hepatica triloba subf. concolor

Hepatica triloba subf.

concolor, commonly known as the smooth hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooms of distinctive blue to purple flowers.

The plant features trifoliate leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, woodsy habitats and is often found in shaded, rich soils.

The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in woodland ecosystems.
