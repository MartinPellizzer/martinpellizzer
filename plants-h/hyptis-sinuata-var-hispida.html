Hyptis sinuata var. hispida

Hyptis sinuata var. hispida, commonly known as roughleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The hairy texture of its leaves gives it the common name "roughleaf.".
