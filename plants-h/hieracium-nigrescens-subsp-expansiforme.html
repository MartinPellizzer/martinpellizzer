Hieracium nigrescens subsp. expansiforme

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

expansiforme, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, daisy-like cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions and is often found in temperate regions.
