Hieraciodes setosa

Hieraciodes setosa, commonly known as the hairy hieracium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in grassy areas, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

Hieraciodes setosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of light conditions.
