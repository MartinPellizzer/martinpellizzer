Hieracium hypeuryum subsp. hanhartii

Hieracium hypeuryum subsp.

hanhartii, commonly known as the Hanhart's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in mountainous regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and a more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
