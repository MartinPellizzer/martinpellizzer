Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. distinctum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

distinctum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
