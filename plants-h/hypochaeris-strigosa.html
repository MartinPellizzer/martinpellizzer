Hypochaeris strigosa

Hypochaeris strigosa, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Hypochaeris strigosa is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain pollinators.
