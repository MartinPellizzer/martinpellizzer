Horaninovia capitata

Horaninovia capitata, commonly known as the golden crown plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller flowers. Native to the arid regions of South America, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its unique structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
