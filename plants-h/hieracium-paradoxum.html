Hieracium paradoxum

Hieracium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem. Hieracium paradoxum is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

It is also known for its invasive tendencies in some regions, making it a concern in ecological management.
