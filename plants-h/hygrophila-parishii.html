Hygrophila parishii

Hygrophila parishii, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny white or pale yellow flowers. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Hygrophila parishii is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in ponds due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
