Hieracium tommasinianum subsp. castelli-novi

Hieracium tommasinianum subsp.

castelli-novi, commonly known as the Castelli-Novian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological adaptation to high-altitude habitats.
