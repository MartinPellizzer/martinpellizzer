Hieracium integrifolium subsp. elegantissimum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

elegantissimum, commonly known as the elegant hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a serrated or lobed edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe, this subspecies is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a species of conservation interest in some regions.
