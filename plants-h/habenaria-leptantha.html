Habenaria leptantha

Habenaria leptantha, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive orchid shape. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
