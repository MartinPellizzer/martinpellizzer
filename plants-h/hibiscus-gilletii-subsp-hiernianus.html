Hibiscus gilletii subsp. hiernianus

Hibiscus gilletii subsp.

hiernianus, commonly known as the Sierra Madre hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a rough texture. Hibiscus gilletii subsp.

hiernianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
