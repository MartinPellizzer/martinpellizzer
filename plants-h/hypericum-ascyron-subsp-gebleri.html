Hypericum ascyron subsp. gebleri

Hypericum ascyron subsp.

gebleri, commonly known as the yellow hypericum or golden St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It produces small, orange-red berries that are attractive to birds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
