Hieracium rubellum subsp. rubrisabinum

Hieracium rubellum subsp.

rubrisabinum, commonly known as the red-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine, reddish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
