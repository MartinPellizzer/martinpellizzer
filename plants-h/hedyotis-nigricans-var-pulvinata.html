Hedyotis nigricans var. pulvinata

Hedyotis nigricans var. pulvinata, commonly known as the blackstem bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
