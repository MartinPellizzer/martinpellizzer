Hydrocotyle crinita

Hydrocotyle crinita, commonly known as the hairy water penny.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States. The plant features small, rounded leaves with hairy undersides, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hydrocotyle crinita is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.
