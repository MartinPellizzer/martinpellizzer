Hieracium ramolainzii

Hieracium ramolainzii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium ramolainzii is often found in open meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
