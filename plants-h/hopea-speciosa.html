Hopea speciosa

Hopea speciosa, commonly known as the white ironwood or yellow meranti, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, winged fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a leathery texture.

Hopea speciosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in providing habitat for various species.
