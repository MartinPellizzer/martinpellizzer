Hippocratea ovata var. crassifolia

Hippocratea ovata var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved hippocratea, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate, and thickly textured leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
