Heliconia straminea

Heliconia straminea, commonly known as the banana heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble banana leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers clustered around a central axis. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Heliconia straminea is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
