Hieracium murorum subsp. legnograptum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

legnograptum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of many small florets.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf venation and the presence of glandular dots on the foliage.
