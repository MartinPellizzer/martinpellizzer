Hypericum saturejifolium

Hypericum saturejifolium, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in yellowish or golden hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum saturejifolium is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
