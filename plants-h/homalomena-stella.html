Homalomena stella

Homalomena stella, commonly known as the star caladium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display vibrant patterns of red, white, and green. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by bright red berries.

Homalomena stella thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers.
