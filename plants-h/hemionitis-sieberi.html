Hemionitis sieberi

Hemionitis sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often spirally arranged fronds that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis sieberi is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
