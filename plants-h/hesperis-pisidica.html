Hesperis pisidica

Hesperis pisidica, commonly known as the Crimean poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny conditions. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces slender, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
