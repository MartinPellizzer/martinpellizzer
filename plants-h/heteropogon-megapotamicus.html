Heteropogon megapotamicus

Heteropogon megapotamicus, commonly known as the Andean feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Heteropogon megapotamicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
