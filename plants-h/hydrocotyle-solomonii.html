Hydrocotyle solomonii

Hydrocotyle solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rosette of heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to wetland gardens.
