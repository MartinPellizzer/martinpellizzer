Hypoxis argentea var. flaccida

Hypoxis argentea var. flaccida, commonly known as silver star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, silver-green leaves that reflect light, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, which bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
