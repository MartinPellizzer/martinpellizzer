Hieracium vittatulum

Hieracium vittatulum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Hieracium vittatulum has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
