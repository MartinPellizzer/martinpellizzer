Hyptis cuneata

Hyptis cuneata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hyptis cuneata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
