Hieracium bifidum subsp. solanum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

solanum, commonly known as the Solanum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
