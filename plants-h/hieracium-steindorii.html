Hieracium steindorii

Hieracium steindorii, commonly known as the Steindorff's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium steindorii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
