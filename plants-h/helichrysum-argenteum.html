Helichrysum argenteum

Helichrysum argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting nature.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
