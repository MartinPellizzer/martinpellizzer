Hyptis leucophylla

Hyptis leucophylla, commonly known as whiteleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open wooded areas or disturbed soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Hyptis leucophylla is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions.
