Heracleum sphondylium subsp. latifolium

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, branched flowering stems. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and roadsides.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinct, often unpleasant odor when crushed.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a notable presence in its native range.
