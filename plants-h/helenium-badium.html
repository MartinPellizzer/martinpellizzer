Helenium badium

Helenium badium, commonly known as the yellow garden hedgerow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a disk of smaller, tubular flowers. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Helenium badium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
