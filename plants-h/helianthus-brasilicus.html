Helianthus brasilicus

Helianthus brasilicus, commonly known as Brazilian sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other sunflowers. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Helianthus. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
