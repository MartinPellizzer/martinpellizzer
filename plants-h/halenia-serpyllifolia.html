Halenia serpyllifolia

Halenia serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common halenia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Halenia serpyllifolia is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture for its delicate appearance.
