Hibiscus lambertianus var. lobatus

Hibiscus lambertianus var. lobatus, commonly known as the lobate hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a lobed appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
