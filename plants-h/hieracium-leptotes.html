Hieracium leptotes

Hieracium leptotes, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium leptotes is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
