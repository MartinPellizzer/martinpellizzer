Hibiscus cuspidatus

Hibiscus cuspidatus, commonly known as the prairie hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
