Hypericum afropalustre

Hypericum afropalustre, commonly known as the African St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often reddish underneath. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Hypericum afropalustre is adapted to wet, swampy habitats and is often found in marshy areas along rivers and lakes.
