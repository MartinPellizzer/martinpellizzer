Hieracium nigrescens subsp. decipiens

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. Its leaves are basal and lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
