Hirtella racemosa var. hispida

Hirtella racemosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in humid tropical climates. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
