Hieracium pseudillyricum

Hieracium pseudillyricum, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
