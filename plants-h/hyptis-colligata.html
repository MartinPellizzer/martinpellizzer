Hyptis colligata

Hyptis colligata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Hyptis colligata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
