Hieracium murorum subsp. polycampylum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

polycampylum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are hairy, lanceolate, and often have a distinctive basal rosette structure.
