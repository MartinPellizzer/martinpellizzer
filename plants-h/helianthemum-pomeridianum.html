Helianthemum pomeridianum

Helianthemum pomeridianum, commonly known as the afternoon sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the afternoon. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
