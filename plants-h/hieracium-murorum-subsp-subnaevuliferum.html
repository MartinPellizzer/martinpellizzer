Hieracium murorum subsp. subnaevuliferum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subnaevuliferum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its subspecies name, subnaevuliferum, refers to the presence of small, dark spots on the leaves.
