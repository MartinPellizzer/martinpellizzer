Hieracium nuoliense var. lancinatum

Hieracium nuoliense var. lancinatum, commonly known as the Lancinate Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
