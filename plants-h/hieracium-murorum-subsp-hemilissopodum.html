Hieracium murorum subsp. hemilissopodum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

hemilissopodum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy surface, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a common weed in agricultural areas and can form dense colonies in the absence of competition.
