Hieracium igoschinae

Hieracium igoschinae, commonly known as the Igoschina hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract various pollinators.

Hieracium igoschinae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
