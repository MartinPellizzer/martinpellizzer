Hyptis capitata f. pilosa

Hyptis capitata f. pilosa, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are densely covered with fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of white to pale blue flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
