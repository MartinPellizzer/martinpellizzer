Hydrangea involucrata var. multiplex

Hydrangea involucrata var. multiplex, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This hydrangea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom reliably in woodland gardens.
