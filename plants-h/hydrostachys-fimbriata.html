Hydrostachys fimbriata

Hydrostachys fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed stachys, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hydrostachys fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
