Helichrysum ruderale

Helichrysum ruderale, commonly known as the field everlasting or common helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in traditional medicine.
