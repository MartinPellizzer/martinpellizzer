Hibiscus holstii

Hibiscus holstii, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with a contrasting darker center. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Hibiscus holstii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
