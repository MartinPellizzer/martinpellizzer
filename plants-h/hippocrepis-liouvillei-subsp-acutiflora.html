Hippocrepis liouvillei subsp. acutiflora

Hippocrepis liouvillei subsp.

acutiflora, commonly known as the acute-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are pale blue to white, with a distinctive spurred lip, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
