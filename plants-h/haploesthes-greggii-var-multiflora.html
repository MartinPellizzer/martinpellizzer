Haploesthes greggii var. multiflora

Haploesthes greggii var. multiflora, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in desert landscapes.

The flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
