Hieracium bifidum subsp. gardetanum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

gardetanum, commonly known as the garden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single, flat-topped head of small yellow flowers surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
