Hibiscus phoeniceus var. rubriflorus

Hibiscus phoeniceus var. rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, with a prominent central yellow stigma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Hibiscus phoeniceus var. rubriflorus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
