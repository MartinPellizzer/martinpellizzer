Hieracium varianifolium

Hieracium varianifolium, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has alternate, hairy leaves that vary in shape, contributing to its common name.

The bright yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
