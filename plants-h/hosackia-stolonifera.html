Hosackia stolonifera

Hosackia stolonifera, commonly known as the prairie vervain, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that help it spread across the ground.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.
