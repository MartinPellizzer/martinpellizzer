Hippochaete ramosissima

Hippochaete ramosissima, commonly known as the bushy cypress, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
