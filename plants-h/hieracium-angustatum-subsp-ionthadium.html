Hieracium angustatum subsp. ionthadium

Hieracium angustatum subsp.

ionthadium, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow, with a single row of ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to produce a large number of seeds.
