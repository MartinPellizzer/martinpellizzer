Holcus annuus

Holcus annuus, commonly known as annual quackgrass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is an annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as those found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale green to blue-gray flowers.

Holcus annuus is known for its rapid growth and ability to compete with other plants, making it both a weed and a useful forage crop in some regions.
