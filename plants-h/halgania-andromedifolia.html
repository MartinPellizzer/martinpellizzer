Halgania andromedifolia

Halgania andromedifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf halgania, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences and bloom in shades of white and pink.

Halgania andromedifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
