Hieracium carroceranum

Hieracium carroceranum, commonly known as the carribean hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

Hieracium carroceranum is often found in open habitats and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its native region.
