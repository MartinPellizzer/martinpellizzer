Hieracium glaucum subsp. glaucum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are usually gray-green due to a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are typically visited by various pollinators.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive blue-green coloration and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
