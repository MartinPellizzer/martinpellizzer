Hieracium cirritum subsp. pseudocochleare

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

pseudocochleare, commonly known as the false coilhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It is native to Europe and is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in some regions.
