Hibbertia huegelii var. subvillosa

Hibbertia huegelii var. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
