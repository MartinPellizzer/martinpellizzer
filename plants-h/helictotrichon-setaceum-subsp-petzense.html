Helictotrichon setaceum subsp. petzense

Helictotrichon setaceum subsp.

petzense, commonly known as the eastern bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves with a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
