Hoffmannia robusta

Hoffmannia robusta, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorching.

Hoffmannia robusta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
