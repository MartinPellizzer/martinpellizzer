Hopea nervosa

Hopea nervosa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and dark bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a prominent venation pattern, contributing to its scientific name "nervosa.". It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by winged fruits, which aid in seed dispersal.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
