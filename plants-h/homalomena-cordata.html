Homalomena cordata

Homalomena cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The roots of Homalomena cordata are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
