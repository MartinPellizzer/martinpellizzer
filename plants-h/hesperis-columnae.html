Hesperis columnae

Hesperis columnae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Hesperis columnae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
