Hieracium salicarium

Hieracium salicarium, commonly known as the willow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium salicarium is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
