Helia pumila

Helia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf heliotrope, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

Helia pumila is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
