Hieracium vulgatum subsp. frondosiforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

frondosiforme, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette structure.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat head, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
