Hedysarum varians

Hedysarum varians, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. Hedysarum varians is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
