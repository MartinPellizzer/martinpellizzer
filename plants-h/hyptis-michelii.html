Hyptis michelii

Hyptis michelii, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis michelii is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
