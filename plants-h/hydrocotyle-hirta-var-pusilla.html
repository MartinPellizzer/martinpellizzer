Hydrocotyle hirta var. pusilla

Hydrocotyle hirta var. pusilla, commonly known as the small hairy water pennywort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, and it is often used in ornamental water gardens.

This species is valued for its ability to help stabilize water ecosystems and provide habitat for aquatic life.
