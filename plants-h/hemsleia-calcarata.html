Hemsleia calcarata

Hemsleia calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for partial shade to full sun.

Hemsleia calcarata is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
