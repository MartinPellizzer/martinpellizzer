Hortonia

Hortonia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. Hortonia belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
