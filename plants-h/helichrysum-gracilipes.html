Helichrysum gracilipes

Helichrysum gracilipes, commonly known as the slender everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces numerous tiny, papery bracts that give it a fluffy appearance, which is typical of the genus Helichrysum. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
