Halocnemum cruciatum

Halocnemum cruciatum, commonly known as sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that help store water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a cross shape, which is characteristic of the Caryophyllaceae family. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

Its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations makes it an important component of salt-tolerant ecosystems.
