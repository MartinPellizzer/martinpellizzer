Hieracium nigrescens subsp. cochleare

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

cochleare, commonly known as the purple meadowhawk, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with purple to reddish petals and yellow centers.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
