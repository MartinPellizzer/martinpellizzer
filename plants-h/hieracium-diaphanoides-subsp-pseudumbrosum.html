Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. pseudumbrosum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

pseudumbrosum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
