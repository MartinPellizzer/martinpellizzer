Hibiscus ovatus var. angustifolius

Hibiscus ovatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hibiscus ovatus var. angustifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
