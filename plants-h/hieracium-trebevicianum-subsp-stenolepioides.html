Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. stenolepioides

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

stenolepioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
