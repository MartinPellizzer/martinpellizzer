Hieracium bucovinense

Hieracium bucovinense, commonly known as the Bucovina hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and lanceolate in shape. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a head.

Hieracium bucovinense is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to moderate environmental conditions.
