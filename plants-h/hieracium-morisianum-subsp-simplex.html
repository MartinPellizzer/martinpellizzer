Hieracium morisianum subsp. simplex

Hieracium morisianum subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the Morisian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native range.
