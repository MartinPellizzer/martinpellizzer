Helichrysum mangorense

Helichrysum mangorense, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that remain open for a long period, giving it the common name. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Helichrysum mangorense is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
