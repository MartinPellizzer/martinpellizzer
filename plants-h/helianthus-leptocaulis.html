Helianthus leptocaulis

Helianthus leptocaulis, commonly known as the slenderstem sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the genus Helianthus.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
