Hieracium paramecocephalum

Hieracium paramecocephalum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, elongated central disk.

It is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
