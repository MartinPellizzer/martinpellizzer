Habenaria pygmaea

Habenaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf lady's tresses, is a small orchid species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, contributing to its compact appearance.

Habenaria pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
