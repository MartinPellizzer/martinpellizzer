Haplopappus croceus var. genuflexus

Haplopappus croceus var. genuflexus, commonly known as the benthead yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and a distinctive, bent capitulum that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and produces small, pappus-equipped seeds for wind dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
