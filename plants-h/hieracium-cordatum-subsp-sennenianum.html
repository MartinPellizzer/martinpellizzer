Hieracium cordatum subsp. sennenianum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

sennenianum, commonly known as the Sennen's heartleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive coloration. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its subspecies-specific leaf morphology and floral structure, making it distinguishable from other hawkweed species.
