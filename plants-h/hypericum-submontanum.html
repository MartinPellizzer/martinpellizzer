Hypericum submontanum

Hypericum submontanum, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its botanical features include small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hypericum submontanum is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
