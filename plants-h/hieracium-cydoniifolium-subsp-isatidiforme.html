Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. isatidiforme

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

isatidiforme, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a rough, hairy texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often found in sandy or calcareous soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
