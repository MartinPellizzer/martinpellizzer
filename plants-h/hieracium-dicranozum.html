Hieracium dicranozum

Hieracium dicranozum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
