Hieracium bauhini subsp. adenocymigeriforme

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

adenocymigeriforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flower heads that resemble dandelions.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and leaf morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
