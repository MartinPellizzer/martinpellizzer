Hieracium chacoense

Hieracium chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. It features basal leaves that are usually hairy and narrow, with yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
