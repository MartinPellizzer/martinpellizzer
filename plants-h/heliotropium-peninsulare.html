Heliotropium peninsulare

Heliotropium peninsulare, commonly known as the Florida heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, often near water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Heliotropium peninsulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
