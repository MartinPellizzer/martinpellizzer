Hieracium Levicaule Subsp. Sejunctum

Hieracium levicaule subsp. sejunctum is a rare and lesser-known member of the dandelion family. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems that help it thrive in specific habitats. Found primarily in Europe, this subspecies is often overlooked due to its modest appearance. Its ecological role is significant, supporting various pollinators and contributing to soil health. Understanding and conserving this plant is essential for maintaining biodiversity in its native regions.
