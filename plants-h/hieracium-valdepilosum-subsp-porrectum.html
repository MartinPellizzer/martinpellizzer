Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. porrectum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

porrectum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in dry or sunny conditions.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
