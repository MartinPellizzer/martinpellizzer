Hirtella adderleyi

Hirtella adderleyi, commonly known as Adderley's hirtella, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a hirsute appearance.

Hirtella adderleyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
