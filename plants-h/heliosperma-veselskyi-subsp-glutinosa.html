Heliosperma veselskyi subsp. glutinosa

Heliosperma veselskyi subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky heliosperma, is a member of the Aizoaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a small, perennial succulent that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features a short, thick stem with fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and borne in clusters, often appearing in late summer.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
