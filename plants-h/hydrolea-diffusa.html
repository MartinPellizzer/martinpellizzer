Hydrolea diffusa

Hydrolea diffusa, commonly known as the blue hydrolea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue and purple tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Hydrolea diffusa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
