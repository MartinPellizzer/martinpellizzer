Hieracium valdeplicatum

Hieracium valdeplicatum, commonly known as the valley fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces yellow to orange ray flowers surrounded by tubular disc flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hieracium valdeplicatum is often found in moist or calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
