Hieracium dentatum subsp. rufobrunnescens

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

rufobrunnescens, commonly known as the rusty brown-hair hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, rusty-brown hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
