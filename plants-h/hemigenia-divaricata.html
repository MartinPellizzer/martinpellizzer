Hemigenia divaricata

Hemigenia divaricata, commonly known as the variable honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. These flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and attractive foliage.
