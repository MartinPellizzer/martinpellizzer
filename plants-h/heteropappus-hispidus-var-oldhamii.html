Heteropappus hispidus var. oldhamii

Heteropappus hispidus var. oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are often hairy and gray-green, while the flower heads are yellow with a central disk.

It serves as a nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
