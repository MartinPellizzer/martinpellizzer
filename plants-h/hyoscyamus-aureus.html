Hyoscyamus aureus

Hyoscyamus aureus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic alkaloids.
