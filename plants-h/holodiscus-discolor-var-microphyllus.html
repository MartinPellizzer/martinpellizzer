Holodiscus discolor var. microphyllus

Holodiscus discolor var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved evergreen holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and year-round foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping.
