Haageocereus versicolor f. lasiacanthus

Haageocereus versicolor f. lasiacanthus, commonly known as the Lasia canthus cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems that bear clusters of white to pink flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a unique morphology, with a more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Haageocereus versicolor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
