Hieracium dichorropum

Hieracium dichorropum, commonly known as the two-seed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium dichorropum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
