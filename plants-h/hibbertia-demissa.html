Hibbertia demissa

Hibbertia demissa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and dry inland areas.

Hibbertia demissa is a hardy shrub that plays an important role in local ecosystems.
