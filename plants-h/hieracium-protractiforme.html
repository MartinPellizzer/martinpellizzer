Hieracium protractiforme

Hieracium protractiforme, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are typically hairy and lance-shaped. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium protractiforme is a common weed in agricultural areas and can spread rapidly due to its extensive root system.
