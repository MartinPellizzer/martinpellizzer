Hemionitis leptolepis

Hemionitis leptolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hemionite, is a unique fern species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, typical of many ferns.

Hemionitis leptolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations.
