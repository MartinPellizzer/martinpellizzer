Hieracium carlsonii

Hieracium carlsonii, commonly known as the Carson's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium carlsonii is generally a short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
