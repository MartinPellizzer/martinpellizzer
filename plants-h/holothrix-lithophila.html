Holothrix lithophila

Holothrix lithophila, commonly known as the rock-holothrix, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red and purple. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries in Mediterranean climates.
