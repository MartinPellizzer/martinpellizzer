Hypericum frondosum

Hypericum frondosum, commonly known as the spreading St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, woody stems that often spread horizontally, forming dense mats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

Hypericum frondosum is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
