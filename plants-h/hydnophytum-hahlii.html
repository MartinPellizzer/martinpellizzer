Hydnophytum hahlii

Hydnophytum hahlii, commonly known as the Hahli's pitcher plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its modified leaves that form tubular, pitcher-like structures used to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its specialized morphology and ecological adaptations in nutrient-poor soils.
