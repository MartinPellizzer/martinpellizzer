Hieracium caesium subsp. suntaliense

Hieracium caesium subsp.

suntaliense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, yellow disk flowers.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
