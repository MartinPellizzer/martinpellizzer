Hieracium vassendliense

Hieracium vassendliense, commonly known as the Vassendliense hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a cluster at the end of each stem. Native to Europe, this species thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in moist or calcareous soils. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems, though it can also be invasive in some regions.
