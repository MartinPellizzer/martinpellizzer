Hieracium vasconicum subsp. laureolum

Hieracium vasconicum subsp.

laureolum, commonly known as the laurel hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense rosette of hairy, dark green leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
