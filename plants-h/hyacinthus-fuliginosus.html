Hyacinthus fuliginosus

Hyacinthus fuliginosus, commonly known as the sooty hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many bulbous plants. Its flowers are arranged in a dense spike and are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and scent.

Hyacinthus fuliginosus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
