Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. incanatum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

incanatum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, woolly texture, and the stems are often reddish and covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
