Haageocereus acranthus var. fortalezensis

Haageocereus acranthus var. fortalezensis, commonly known as the red-tipped columnar cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem that is often covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
