Heracleum sphondylium var. nipponicum

Heracleum sphondylium var. nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese cow parsnip, is a biennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences with small white to greenish flowers. Its leaves are broadly lobed, with a coarse texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows.
