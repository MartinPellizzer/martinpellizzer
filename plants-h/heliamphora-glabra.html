Heliamphora glabra

Heliamphora glabra, commonly known as the smooth pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular leaves that form pitfall traps to capture insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and secrete a digestive fluid to break down prey. It thrives in wet, acidic environments and requires high humidity and moderate light conditions.

Heliamphora glabra is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
