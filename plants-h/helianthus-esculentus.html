Helianthus esculentus

Helianthus esculentus, commonly known as the edible sunflower, is a species native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, yellow flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces large, oval-shaped seeds that are rich in oil and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus esculentus is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
