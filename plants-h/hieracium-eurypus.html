Hieracium eurypus

Hieracium eurypus, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Hieracium eurypus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
