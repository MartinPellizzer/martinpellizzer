Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. ornatiforme

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

ornatiforme, commonly known as the ornate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
