Hoarea nervifolia

Hoarea nervifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the upper surface. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Boraginaceae family.

Hoarea nervifolia is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
