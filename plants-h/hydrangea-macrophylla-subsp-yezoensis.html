Hydrangea macrophylla subsp. yezoensis

Hydrangea macrophylla subsp.

yezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that can range in color from pink to blue, depending on soil pH.

The plant produces bracteate inflorescences composed of sterile flowers surrounded by fertile ones. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a glossy texture.

This species is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
