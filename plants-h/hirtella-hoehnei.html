Hirtella hoehnei

Hirtella hoehnei, commonly known as the Brazilian hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hirtella hoehnei is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Its foliage and floral structure contribute to its ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
