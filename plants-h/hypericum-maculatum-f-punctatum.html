Hypericum maculatum f. punctatum

Hypericum maculatum f. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort.

This variant is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and bright yellow flowers. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
