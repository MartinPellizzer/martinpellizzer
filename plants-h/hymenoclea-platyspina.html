Hymenoclea platyspina

Hymenoclea platyspina, commonly known as the broadspine hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It typically grows in moist, tropical environments and features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. The plant has rough, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a waxy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Hymenoclea platyspina is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils.
