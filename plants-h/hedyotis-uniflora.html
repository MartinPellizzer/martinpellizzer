Hedyotis uniflora

Hedyotis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bedstraw, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Hedyotis uniflora is often found in association with other wetland flora and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
