Hydnocarpus sumatranus var. pentagynus

Hydnocarpus sumatranus var. pentagynus, commonly known as the Sumatran ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, five-lobed fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
