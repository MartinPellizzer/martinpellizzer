Hieracium taedum

Hieracium taedum, commonly known as the field hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces yellow or orange composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it can grow up to 60 cm in height.

Hieracium taedum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
