Hemionitis bicolor

Hemionitis bicolor, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a unique fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are covered in a fine layer of white, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Hemionitis bicolor is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a rare and specialized species.
