Hieracium sagittatum subsp. leucophaeatum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

leucophaeatum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a white or pale underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.
