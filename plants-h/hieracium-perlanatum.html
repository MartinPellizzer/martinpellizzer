Hieracium perlanatum

Hieracium perlanatum, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy textures.

Hieracium perlanatum is often found in disturbed habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
