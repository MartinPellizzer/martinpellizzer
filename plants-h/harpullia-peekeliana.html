Harpullia peekeliana

Harpullia peekeliana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in dense, pendulous clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Harpullia peekeliana is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
