Hesperantha lutea var. luculenta

Hesperantha lutea var. luculenta, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive, recurved lip. The plant produces upright, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
