Hosta longipes f. alba

Hosta longipes f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered giant hosta, is a cultivar of the Hosta genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery margin. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its striking foliage and elegant, showy blooms.
