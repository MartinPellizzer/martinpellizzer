Helianthemum aureum var. teretifolium

Helianthemum aureum var. teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower of the rock rose family, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Its compact form and showy flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
