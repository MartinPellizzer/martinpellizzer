Hieracium murorum subsp. incrassans

Hieracium murorum subsp.

incrassans, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
