Hevea benthamiana

Hevea benthamiana, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, alternate leaves and milky sap. The plant is most notable for its ability to produce natural latex, which is harvested from its bark. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its economic importance, it is widely cultivated for rubber production.
