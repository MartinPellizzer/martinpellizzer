Hieracium macedonicum

Hieracium macedonicum, commonly known as Macedonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
