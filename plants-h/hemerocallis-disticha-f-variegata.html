Hemerocallis disticha f. variegata

Hemerocallis disticha f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated spider lily, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its unique foliage and flowers.

It is a cultivar of the spider lily species, featuring leaves with distinct white and green variegation that add visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

This plant is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread, creating a lush, colorful display over time.
