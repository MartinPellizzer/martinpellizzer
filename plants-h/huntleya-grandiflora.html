Huntleya grandiflora

Huntleya grandiflora, commonly known as the giant rock rose, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Huntleya grandiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
