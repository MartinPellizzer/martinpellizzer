Helicteres angustifolia

Helicteres angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets arranged in pairs along a central axis. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Helicteres angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
