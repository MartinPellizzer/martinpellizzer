Helichrysum kraussii

Helichrysum kraussii, commonly known as the Cape goldball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny balls. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage is aromatic, with a slight citrus scent, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Helichrysum kraussii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
