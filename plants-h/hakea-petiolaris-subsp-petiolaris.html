Hakea petiolaris subsp. petiolaris

Hakea petiolaris subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces woody, spine-tipped seed pods. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
