Homalium grandiflorum

Homalium grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered holly, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a striking component of tropical forests.
