Hyptis tenuifolia

Hyptis tenuifolia, commonly known as Brazilian peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, hairy leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Hyptis tenuifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
