Hieracium virgultorum

Hieracium virgultorum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium virgultorum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
