Hymenopappus filifolius var. luteus

Hymenopappus filifolius var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, sprawling stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
