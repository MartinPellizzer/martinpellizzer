Hieracium caucasiense subsp. gandogeri

Hieracium caucasiense subsp.

gandogeri, commonly known as the Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in mountainous areas and has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and has a preference for well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is part of the larger Hieracium genus, which includes many other species of hawkweeds.
