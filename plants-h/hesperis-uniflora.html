Hesperis uniflora

Hesperis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces a single, showy white or pale purple flower per stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hesperis uniflora is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
