Hieracium leptophyton subsp. bauhinifloriforme

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

bauhinifloriforme, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
