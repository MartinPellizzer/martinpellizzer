Hieracium schmidtii subsp. didymum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

didymum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with flowering stems that bear several capitula. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
