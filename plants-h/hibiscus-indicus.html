Hibiscus indicus

Hibiscus indicus, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus indicus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
