Hieracium alatavicum

Hieracium alatavicum, commonly known as the Alatau hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
