Hemionitis drakeana

Hemionitis drakeana, commonly known as the Drake's hemionitis, is a rare and unusual fern native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, creeping growth habit and small, clustered fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited water availability. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis drakeana is considered a vulnerable species and is of particular interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to harsh environments.
