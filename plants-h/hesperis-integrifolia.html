Hesperis integrifolia

Hesperis integrifolia, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a peppery scent when crushed.

Hesperis integrifolia is typically found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun.
