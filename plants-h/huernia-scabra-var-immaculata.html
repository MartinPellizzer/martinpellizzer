Huernia scabra var. immaculata

Huernia scabra var. immaculata, commonly known as the naked stem huernia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that is often bare of leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stem and requiring minimal watering.

This species is popular among cactus and succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
