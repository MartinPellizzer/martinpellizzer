Hesperostipa neomexicana

Hesperostipa neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers.

Hesperostipa neomexicana plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
