Hieracium mammidens

Hieracium mammidens, commonly known as the meadowhawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence, featuring yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium mammidens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
