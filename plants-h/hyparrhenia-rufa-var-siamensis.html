Hyparrhenia rufa var. siamensis

Hyparrhenia rufa var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves that are usually green, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in erosion control.
