Hordeum eriwanense

Hordeum eriwanense, commonly known as Eriwan barley, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with lemma and palea structures typical of the Poaceae family. Its seeds are encased in a tough, protective hull, which aids in seed dispersal and survival in arid conditions.

Hordeum eriwanense is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitats.
