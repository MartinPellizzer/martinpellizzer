Hebe bidwillii

Hebe bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Hebe bidwillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
