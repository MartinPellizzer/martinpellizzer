Hieracium xerampelinum

Hieracium xerampelinum, commonly known as the purple meadowhawk, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually purple to pink, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like leaves.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
