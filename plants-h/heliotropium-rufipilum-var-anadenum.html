Heliotropium rufipilum var. anadenum

Heliotropium rufipilum var. anadenum, commonly known as the desert heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and can withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies of desert flora.
