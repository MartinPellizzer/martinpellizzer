Hibiscus flaviflorus

Hibiscus flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus flaviflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
