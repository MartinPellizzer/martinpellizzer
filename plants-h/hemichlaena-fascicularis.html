Hemichlaena fascicularis

Hemichlaena fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered hemichlaena, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features slender, often clustered stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinct reddish or purplish hue.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in native gardens.
