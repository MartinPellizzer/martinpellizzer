Hieracium anfractum subsp. oinopolepis

Hieracium anfractum subsp.

oinopolepis, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically has basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
