Hedysarum latisiliquum

Hedysarum latisiliquum, commonly known as the broad-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a climbing habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Hedysarum latisiliquum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
