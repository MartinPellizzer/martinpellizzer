Homonoma

Homonoma, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces seed heads that resemble small cones, which give it its common name. Homonoma thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
