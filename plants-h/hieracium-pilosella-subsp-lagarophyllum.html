Hieracium pilosella subsp. lagarophyllum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

lagarophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its yellow flowers are surrounded by a ring of pale green or white ray florets.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some regions.
