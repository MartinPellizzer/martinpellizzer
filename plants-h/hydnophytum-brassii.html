Hydnophytum brassii

Hydnophytum brassii, commonly known as the sticky root vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphological adaptations, including its sticky roots that allow it to adhere to host trees. The plant has a slender stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces fruit that is a dry capsule containing seeds.

Hydnophytum brassii is primarily found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
