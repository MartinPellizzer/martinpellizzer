Hartogia pubigera

Hartogia pubigera, commonly known as the hairy Hartog's grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color and appear in clusters.

Hartogia pubigera is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
