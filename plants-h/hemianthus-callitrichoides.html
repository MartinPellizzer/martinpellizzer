Hemianthus callitrichoides

Hemianthus callitrichoides, commonly known as the dwarf iron plant, is a species of aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bright green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Hemianthus callitrichoides is popular in aquariums due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
