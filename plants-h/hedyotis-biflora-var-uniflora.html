Hedyotis biflora var. uniflora

Hedyotis biflora var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered inchplant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
