Huperzia linifolia var. linifolia

Huperzia linifolia var. linifolia, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen fern-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, scale-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cones at the base of the leaf clusters. It is characterized by its slow growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
