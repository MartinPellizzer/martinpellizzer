Hemizonia multiglandulosa var. cephalotes

Hemizonia multiglandulosa var. cephalotes, commonly known as the hairy leaf hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its morphology includes a low-growing habit with branched stems and small, serrated leaves.

The flowers are arranged in dense clusters, making it a distinctive and ornamental species.
