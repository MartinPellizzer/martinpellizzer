Homeria spiralis

Homeria spiralis, commonly known as the spiral homeria, is a unique plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiraling growth pattern, which gives it a distinctive and often spiral-shaped form. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a helical manner around its stem. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Homeria spiralis is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
