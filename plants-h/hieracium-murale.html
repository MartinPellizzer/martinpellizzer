Hieracium murale

Hieracium murale, commonly known as the wall groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found along walls and in dry, sunny habitats. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Hieracium murale is a perennial species that produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.
