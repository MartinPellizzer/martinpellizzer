Helichrysum gilesii

Helichrysum gilesii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small, compact flowers. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Helichrysum gilesii is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and for its traditional medicinal applications.
