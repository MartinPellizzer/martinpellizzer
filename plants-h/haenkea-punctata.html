Haenkea punctata

Haenkea punctata, commonly known as the dotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive dotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Haenkea punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
