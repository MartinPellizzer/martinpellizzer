Hieracium cernaeglavae

Hieracium cernaeglavae, commonly known as the Montenegro hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.
