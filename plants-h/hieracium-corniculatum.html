Hieracium corniculatum

Hieracium corniculatum, commonly known as the horned hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
