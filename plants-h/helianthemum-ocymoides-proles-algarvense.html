Helianthemum ocymoides proles algarvense

Helianthemum ocymoides proles algarvense, commonly known as the Algarve sand thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the southern Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is drought-tolerant once established.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
