Hibiscus subtrilobatus

Hibiscus subtrilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The leaves are palmately lobed, giving the plant its common name, and they are often hairy beneath. It thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
