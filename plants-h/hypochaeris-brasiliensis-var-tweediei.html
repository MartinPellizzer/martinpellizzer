Hypochaeris brasiliensis var. tweediei

Hypochaeris brasiliensis var. tweediei, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it has hairy, lobed leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

The species is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through seed dispersal.
