Hedwigia tussaccii

Hedwigia tussaccii, commonly known as the Tussac plant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its botanical features include narrow, elongated leaves and a rhizomatous growth habit.

Hedwigia tussaccii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
