Hippion macrophyllum

Hippion macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant has a robust root system that anchors it in the muddy substrates of slow-moving water bodies. Its flowers, which are typically white or pink, bloom at night and emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators.

Hippion macrophyllum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
