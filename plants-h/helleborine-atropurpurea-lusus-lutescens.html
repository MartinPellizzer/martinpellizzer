Helleborine atropurpurea lusus lutescens

Helleborine atropurpurea lusus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-tongued helleborine, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that are often tinged with yellow or purple, and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with purple markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological niche.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
