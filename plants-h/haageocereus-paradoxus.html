Haageocereus paradoxus

Haageocereus paradoxus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and is known for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces large, white, star-shaped blooms that emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique flowering habit and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
