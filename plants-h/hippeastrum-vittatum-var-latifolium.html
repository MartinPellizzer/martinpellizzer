Hippeastrum vittatum var. latifolium

Hippeastrum vittatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
