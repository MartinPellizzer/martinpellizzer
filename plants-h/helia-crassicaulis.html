Helia crassicaulis

Helia crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed helia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Helia crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
