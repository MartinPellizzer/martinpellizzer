Hallieracantha frutescens

Hallieracantha frutescens, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually orange or yellow in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and inland gardens.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various climates.
