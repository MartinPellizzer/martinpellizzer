Himalayacalamus planatus

Himalayacalamus planatus, commonly known as the flat-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass found in the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, flat leaves that give it its common name. This plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Himalayacalamus planatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native range.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a distinctive member of the grass family, Poaceae.
