Hyptis salzmannii

Hyptis salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Hyptis salzmannii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
