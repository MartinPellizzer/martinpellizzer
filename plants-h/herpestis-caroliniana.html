Herpestis caroliniana

Herpestis caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in spiny capsules, which aid in seed dispersal.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
