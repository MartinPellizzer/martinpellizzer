Hippeastrum equestre var. plena

Hippeastrum equestre var. plena, commonly known as the Double-flowered Amazonian Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are double-petaled, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting inner color. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This variety is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its dramatic floral display and relatively easy care requirements.
