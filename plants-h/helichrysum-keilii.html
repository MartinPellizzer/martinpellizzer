Helichrysum keilii

Helichrysum keilii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small suns. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its foliage is typically silver or gray-green, with small, narrow leaves.

Helichrysum keilii is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to retain its color when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.
