Hieracium schnyderi

Hieracium schnyderi, commonly known as the Schnyder's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are yellow and resemble dandelions, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Hieracium schnyderi is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
