Hibiscus vrieseanus

Hibiscus vrieseanus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. Hibiscus vrieseanus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
