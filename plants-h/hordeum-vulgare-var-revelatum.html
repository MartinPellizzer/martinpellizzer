Hordeum vulgare var. revelatum

Hordeum vulgare var. revelatum, commonly known as the red barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its distinctive reddish grain color.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, leafy stems and compact spikelets that bear the edible grains. Its botanical features include hollow, cylindrical stems and a complex inflorescence structure typical of the Hordeum genus.

This variety is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in both food and industrial applications.
