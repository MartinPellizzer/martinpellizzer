Hieracium venosum

Hieracium venosum, commonly known as the veiny hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium venosum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
