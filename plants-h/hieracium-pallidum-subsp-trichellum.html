Hieracium pallidum subsp. trichellum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

trichellum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and roadside areas.

The plant has basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the stem is usually hairless and somewhat hairy at the base. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and are accompanied by bracts.

The species is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and its ability to form dense colonies, making it a notable plant in its native range.
