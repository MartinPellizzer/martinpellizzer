Herniaria nigrimontium var. degenii

Herniaria nigrimontium var. degenii, commonly known as the dwarf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to rocky and sandy soils in mountainous regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant is well-adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting succulence to store water and resist drought. It typically thrives in full sun and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its limited distribution and specialized habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species.
