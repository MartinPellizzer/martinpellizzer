Hymenoxys acaulis

Hymenoxys acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow flowers. The plant is distinguished by its leafless, hairless stem that rises directly from the ground. Its basal leaves are alternate, elongated, and covered in fine hairs.

Hymenoxys acaulis typically blooms in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
