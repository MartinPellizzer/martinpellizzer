Hibiscus pacificus

Hibiscus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus pacificus is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife in its native habitat.
