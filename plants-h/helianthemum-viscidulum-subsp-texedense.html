Helianthemum viscidulum subsp. texedense

Helianthemum viscidulum subsp.

texedense, commonly known as the Texas sandverbena, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico. It features small, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
