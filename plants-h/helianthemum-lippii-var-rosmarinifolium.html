Helianthemum lippii var. rosmarinifolium

Helianthemum lippii var. rosmarinifolium, commonly known as rosemary rockrose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant leaves and hardy nature.
