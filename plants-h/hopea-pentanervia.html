Hopea pentanervia

Hopea pentanervia, commonly known as the five-veined hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with five distinct veins. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Hopea pentanervia plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
