Hedyotis glomerulata

Hedyotis glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered dotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a reddish hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often prostrate.

Hedyotis glomerulata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
