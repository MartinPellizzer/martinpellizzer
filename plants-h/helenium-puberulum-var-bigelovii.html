Helenium puberulum var. bigelovii

Helenium puberulum var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's sneezeweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces bright orange to yellow ray flowers with a central disk. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
