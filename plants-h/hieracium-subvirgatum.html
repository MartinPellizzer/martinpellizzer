Hieracium subvirgatum

Hieracium subvirgatum, commonly known as the lesser yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Native to Europe, it is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators but can also become invasive in certain environments.
