Hibiscus caesius

Hibiscus caesius, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a dark violet throat. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus caesius thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands, along riverbanks, or in disturbed soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
