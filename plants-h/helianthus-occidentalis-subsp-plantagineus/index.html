Helianthus Occidentalis Subsp. Plantagineus

Helianthus occidentalis subsp. plantagineus is a lesser-known member of the sunflower family native to the western united states. it thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. this subspecies is distinguished by its large, showy flowers and velvety foliage. while not as popular as its more famous relatives, it holds significant ecological value. its unique appearance makes it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
