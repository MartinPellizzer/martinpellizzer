Hieracium dentatum var. expallens

Hieracium dentatum var. expallens, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It features yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The hairy hawkweed is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
