Hieracium subtransiens

Hieracium subtransiens, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by pale green or white ray florets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Hieracium subtransiens is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its extensive root system.
