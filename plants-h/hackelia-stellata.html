Hackelia stellata

Hackelia stellata, commonly known as starflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
