Hyoseris cretica subsp. rhagadioloides

Hyoseris cretica subsp.

rhagadioloides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its resilience in harsh environments.
