Hyparrhenia newtonii var. macra

Hyparrhenia newtonii var. macra, commonly known as the giant grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush, with dense, spike-like clusters of florets. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is noted for its hardiness and adaptability, making it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
