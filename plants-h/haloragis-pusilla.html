Haloragis pusilla

Haloragis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf willow or mossy willow, is a small, mat-forming plant native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Haloragis pusilla is well adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing its resilience in arid and coastal habitats.
