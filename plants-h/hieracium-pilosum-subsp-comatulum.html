Hieracium pilosum subsp. comatulum

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

comatulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc of tubular florets.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in regions with suitable conditions.
