Hosta lancifolia var. longifolia

Hosta lancifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hosta, is a variety of the hosta plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in shaded, moist environments. The leaves are typically a deep green with a glossy texture and can grow up to 30 inches in length. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, which are followed by blue berries.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to garden settings.
