Hieracium caesium var. insulare

Hieracium caesium var. insulare, commonly known as the island hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadside areas.

This variant is noted for its adaptability and is often found in coastal and maritime environments.
