Hylocereus undatus

Hylocereus undatus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

The fruit, known as a dragon fruit, is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor.
