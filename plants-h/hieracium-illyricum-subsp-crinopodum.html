Hieracium illyricum subsp. crinopodum

Hieracium illyricum subsp.

crinopodum, commonly known as the curlyhead hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Turkey. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and locally distributed species.
