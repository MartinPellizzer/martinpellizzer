Hemionitis reinwardtiana

Hemionitis reinwardtiana, commonly known as the reinwardt's hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of the fern family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
