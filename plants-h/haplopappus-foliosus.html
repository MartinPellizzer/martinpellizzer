Haplopappus foliosus

Haplopappus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Haplopappus foliosus is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
