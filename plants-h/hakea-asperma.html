Hakea asperma

Hakea asperma, commonly known as the rough hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in small, stiff hairs.

Hakea asperma is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
