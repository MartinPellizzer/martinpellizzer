Hyptis pringlei

Hyptis pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
