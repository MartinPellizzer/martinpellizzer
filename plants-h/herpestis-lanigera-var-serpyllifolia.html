Herpestis lanigera var. serpyllifolia

Herpestis lanigera var. serpyllifolia, commonly known as the woolly false spurge, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its hairy foliage and distinctive floral structure make it a notable species in its native habitat.
