Haemanthus sinuatus

Haemanthus sinuatus, commonly known as the Zulu bulbous lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bulbous stem and its clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
