Hippia pilosa

Hippia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy hippia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from poor soils.

Hippia pilosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
