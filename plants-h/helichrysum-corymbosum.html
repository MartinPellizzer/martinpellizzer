Helichrysum corymbosum

Helichrysum corymbosum, commonly known as the golden crown or Cape gold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers, which are often arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum corymbosum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
