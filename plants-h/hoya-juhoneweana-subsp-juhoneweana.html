Hoya juhoneweana subsp. juhoneweana

Hoya juhoneweana subsp.

juhoneweana, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of waxy, coral-pink flowers.

The leaves are thick, succulent, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
