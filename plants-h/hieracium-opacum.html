Hieracium opacum

Hieracium opacum, commonly known as the gloomy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a dark green color, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium opacum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in areas with moderate sunlight.
