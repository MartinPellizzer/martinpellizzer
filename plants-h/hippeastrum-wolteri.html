Hippeastrum wolteri

Hippeastrum wolteri, commonly known as the Wolter's amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting stripe. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and typically arranged in a fan shape.

Hippeastrum wolteri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
