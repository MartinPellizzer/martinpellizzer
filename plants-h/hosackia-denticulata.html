Hosackia denticulata

Hosackia denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and along stream banks.

Hosackia denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
