Hieracium beauverdianum subsp. pseudexilentum

Hieracium beauverdianum subsp.

pseudexilentum, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like cluster.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
