Helianthemum × finestratense

Helianthemum × finestratense, commonly known as the rock rose or wallflower, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or limestone areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Helianthemum × finestratense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
