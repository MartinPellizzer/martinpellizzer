Hippomarathrum libanotis var. crispatum

Hippomarathrum libanotis var. crispatum, commonly known as the crinkled Lebanon senna, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often crinkled or wavy along the edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry climates.
