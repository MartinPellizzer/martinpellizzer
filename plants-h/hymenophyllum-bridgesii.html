Hymenophyllum bridgesii

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of rhizoids, which help anchor it to moist substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called capsules, are borne on short stalks and are typically clustered in dense groups.

Hymenophyllum bridgesii plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitat.
