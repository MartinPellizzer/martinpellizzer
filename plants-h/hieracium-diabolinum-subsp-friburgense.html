Hieracium diabolinum subsp. friburgense

Hieracium diabolinum subsp.

friburgense, commonly known as the Friburg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
