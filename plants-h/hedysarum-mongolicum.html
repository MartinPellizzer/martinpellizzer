Hedysarum mongolicum

Hedysarum mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the steppes of Mongolia and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Hedysarum mongolicum plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
