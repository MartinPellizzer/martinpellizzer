Holcus saccharatus

Holcus saccharatus, commonly known as sugar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, along riverbanks, and in woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
