Hibiscus riparius

Hibiscus riparius, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus riparius is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters tall, with a spreading habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
