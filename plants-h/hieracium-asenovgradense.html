Hieracium asenovgradense

Hieracium asenovgradense, commonly known as the Asenovgrad hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually basal, rough, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium asenovgradense is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with moderate to low moisture.
