Hieracium cerinthoides var. vogesiacum

Hieracium cerinthoides var. vogesiacum, commonly known as the Voges hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
