Hibiscus ficalhoanus

Hibiscus ficalhoanus, commonly known as the Cape flossflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Hibiscus ficalhoanus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
