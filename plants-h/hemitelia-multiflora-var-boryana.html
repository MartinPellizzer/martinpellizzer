Hemitelia multiflora var. boryana

Hemitelia multiflora var. boryana, commonly known as the Boryana hemitelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in its arid habitat. This variant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid plant ecology.
