Hieracium murorum subsp. pulchridens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pulchridens, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has alternate, rough-hairy leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
