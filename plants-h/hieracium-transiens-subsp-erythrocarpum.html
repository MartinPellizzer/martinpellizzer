Hieracium transiens subsp. erythrocarpum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its distinctive feature is the bright red, elongated fruits that form after flowering, which aid in seed dispersal.

The subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
