Hieracium sagittatum subsp. segevoldense

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

segevoldense, commonly known as the Segevold bentleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several countries.
