Hydrocharis parvula

Hydrocharis parvula, commonly known as the small water shield, is a small aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has floating leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, white, and fragrant. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hydrocharis parvula plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
