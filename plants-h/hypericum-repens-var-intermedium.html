Hypericum repens var. intermedium

Hypericum repens var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are prostrate, often rooting at the nodes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
