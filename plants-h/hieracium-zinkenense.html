Hieracium zinkenense

Hieracium zinkenense, commonly known as the Zinken's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow flowers that bloom from June to August.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
