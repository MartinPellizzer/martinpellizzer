Henckelia quinquevulnera

Henckelia quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound henckelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
