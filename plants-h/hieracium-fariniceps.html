Hieracium fariniceps

Hieracium fariniceps, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, stemless leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense patches in its native habitat.
