Homocodon pedicellatus

Homocodon pedicellatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
