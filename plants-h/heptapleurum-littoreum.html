Heptapleurum littoreum

Heptapleurum littoreum, commonly known as coastal heptapleurum, is a shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
