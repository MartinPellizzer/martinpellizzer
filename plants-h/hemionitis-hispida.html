Hemionitis hispida

Hemionitis hispida, commonly known as the hairy hemionitis, is a small, perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas with limited moisture.

Due to its unique adaptations, Hemionitis hispida is considered an important species for studying plant resilience in harsh environments.
