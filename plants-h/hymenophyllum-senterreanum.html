Hymenophyllum senterreanum

Hymenophyllum senterreanum, commonly known as the silver mist fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a silvery, powdery coating. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often have a translucent appearance, which helps in reducing water loss.

The plant produces spores in tiny, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.
