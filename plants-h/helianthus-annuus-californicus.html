Helianthus annuus californicus

Helianthus annuus californicus, commonly known as the California sunflower, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular florets. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.
