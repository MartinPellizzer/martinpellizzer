Hieracium calcareum subsp. holleri

Hieracium calcareum subsp.

holleri, commonly known as the limestone hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to alkaline environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
