Hieracium caesielloides

Hieracium caesielloides, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
