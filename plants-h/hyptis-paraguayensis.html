Hyptis paraguayensis

Hyptis paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, where it thrives in full sun.
