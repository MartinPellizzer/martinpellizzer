Hevea guianensis subsp. typica

Hevea guianensis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Guianese rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to South America. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and milky sap.

The plant is known for its latex production, which has historically been used in the rubber industry. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, it is also of interest for conservation and sustainable cultivation practices.
