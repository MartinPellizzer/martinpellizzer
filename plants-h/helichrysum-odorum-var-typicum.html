Helichrysum odorum var. typicum

Helichrysum odorum var. typicum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or sweet everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that are tightly packed and resemble tiny golden stars. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
