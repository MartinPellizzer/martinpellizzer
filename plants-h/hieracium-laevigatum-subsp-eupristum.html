Hieracium laevigatum subsp. eupristum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

eupristum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers with ray florets.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
