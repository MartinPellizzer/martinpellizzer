Hieracium zeyheri

Hieracium zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flower heads that are composed of many small florets. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Hieracium zeyheri is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
