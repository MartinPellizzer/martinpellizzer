Hieracium effusum

Hieracium effusum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, dark green leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium effusum is noted for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is considered a weed in some regions.
