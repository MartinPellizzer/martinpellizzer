Hyptis pseudosinuata

Hyptis pseudosinuata, commonly known as the false hibiscus or Mexican hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis pseudosinuata is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
