Halothamnus cinerascens subsp. cinerascens

Halothamnus cinerascens subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
