Herorchis papilionacea subsp. messenica

Herorchis papilionacea subsp.

messenica, commonly known as the Messenian orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in the region of Messenia in Greece. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip that resembles a butterfly wing.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate, pale pink to white petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
