Helictotrichon argaeum

Helictotrichon argaeum, commonly known as the Argentinian bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Helictotrichon argaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
