Hippophaes

Hippophaes, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a genus of shrubs native to Asia, Europe, and North America.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in harsh environments, including arid and saline soils, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Hippophaes has been used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ecological benefits in soil stabilization.
