Hyptis silvestris

Hyptis silvestris, commonly known as wild hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis silvestris is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
