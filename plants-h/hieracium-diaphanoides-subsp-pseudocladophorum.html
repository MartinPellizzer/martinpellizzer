Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. pseudocladophorum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

pseudocladophorum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is distinguished by its translucent, waxy leaf surfaces and its ability to form dense colonies.
