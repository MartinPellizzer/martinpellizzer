Heuchera micrantha var. typica

Heuchera micrantha var. typica, commonly known as the smoothleaf alumroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, rounded clumps of heart-shaped leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on the variety and seasonal changes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and delicate blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
