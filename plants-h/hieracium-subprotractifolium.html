Hieracium subprotractifolium

Hieracium subprotractifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often forming dense colonies due to its aggressive spreading habit.
