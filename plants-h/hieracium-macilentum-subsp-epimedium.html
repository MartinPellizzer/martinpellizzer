Hieracium macilentum subsp. epimedium

Hieracium macilentum subsp.

epimedium, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus, with a preference for well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
