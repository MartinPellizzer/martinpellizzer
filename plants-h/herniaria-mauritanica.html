Herniaria mauritanica

Herniaria mauritanica, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It thrives in rocky, arid, and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its succulent nature helps it retain moisture in dry climates.

Herniaria mauritanica is often found in coastal dunes and gravelly soils, contributing to stabilizing these fragile ecosystems.
