Hebe tumida

Hebe tumida, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral displays. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. Hebe tumida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
