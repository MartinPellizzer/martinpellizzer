Holoschoenus romanus f. filiformis

Holoschoenus romanus f. filiformis, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, thread-like leaves and elongated inflorescences that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include small, spike-like clusters of flowers and a fibrous root system.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
