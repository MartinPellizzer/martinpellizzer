Hippeastrum paradisiacum

Hippeastrum paradisiacum, commonly known as the Paradise amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant has strap-like, broad leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
