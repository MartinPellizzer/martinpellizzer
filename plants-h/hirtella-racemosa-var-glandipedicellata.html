Hirtella racemosa var. glandipedicellata

Hirtella racemosa var. glandipedicellata, commonly known as the glandular-stemmed hirtella, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical with a rough texture, and the inflorescences are racemose, bearing white or pale yellow blooms. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
