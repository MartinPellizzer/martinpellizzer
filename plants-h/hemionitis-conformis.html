Hemionitis conformis

Hemionitis conformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a small aquatic plant native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Hemionitis conformis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
