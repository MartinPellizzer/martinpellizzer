Hypochaeris brasiliensis var. sulfurea

Hypochaeris brasiliensis var. sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically ray florets with a distinctive sulfur-yellow hue. The plant has basal leaves that are oblanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually unbranched and hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and open forests.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
