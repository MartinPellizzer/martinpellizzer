Hieracium olivaceum subsp. pirolifolium

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

pirolifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a cluster at the end of its stem. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.
