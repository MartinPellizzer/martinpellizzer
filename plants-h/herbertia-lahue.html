Herbertia lahue

Herbertia lahue, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, arching fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in woodland areas. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

Herbertia lahue is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in shaded garden landscapes.
