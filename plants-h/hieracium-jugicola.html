Hieracium jugicola

Hieracium jugicola, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium jugicola is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
