Halesia parviflora

Halesia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, often peeling in thin, papery layers.

Halesia parviflora prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
