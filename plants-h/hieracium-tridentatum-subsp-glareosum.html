Hieracium tridentatum subsp. glareosum

Hieracium tridentatum subsp.

glareosum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges and often have a bluish-green hue.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
