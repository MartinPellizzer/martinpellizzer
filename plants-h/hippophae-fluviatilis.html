Hippophae fluviatilis

Hippophae fluviatilis, commonly known as the river sandthorn, is a shrub native to riverbanks and wetlands in Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in riparian zones. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ecological importance for pollinators.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
