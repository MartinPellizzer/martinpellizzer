Hieracium germanicum subsp. alsaticum

Hieracium germanicum subsp.

alsaticum, commonly known as the Alsatic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant typically features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with flowering stems that bear a cluster of small, composite flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some regions.
