Heliconia ramonensis var. ramonensis

Heliconia ramonensis var. ramonensis, commonly known as the Ramon's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bright red or orange bracts, which serve to attract pollinators.

The leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
