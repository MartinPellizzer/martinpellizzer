Hypochaeris angustifolia f. nuda

Hypochaeris angustifolia f. nuda, commonly known as the narrowleaf sow-thistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it both a weed and a potential indicator of soil disturbance.
