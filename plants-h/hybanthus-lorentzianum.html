Hybanthus lorentzianum

Hybanthus lorentzianum, commonly known as the Lorentzian hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Hybanthusaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale blue with five petals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
