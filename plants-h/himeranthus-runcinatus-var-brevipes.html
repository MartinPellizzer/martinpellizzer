Himeranthus runcinatus var. brevipes

Himeranthus runcinatus var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
