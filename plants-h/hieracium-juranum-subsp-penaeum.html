Hieracium juranum subsp. penaeum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

penaeum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
