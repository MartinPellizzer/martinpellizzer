Hieracium phaeodermum

Hieracium phaeodermum, commonly known as the pale-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a grayish or whitish underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
