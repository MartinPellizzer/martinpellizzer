Hoya pachypus

Hoya pachypus, commonly known as the giant waxy hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that can grow quite large. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Hoya pachypus is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
