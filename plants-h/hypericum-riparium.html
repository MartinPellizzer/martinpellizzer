Hypericum riparium

Hypericum riparium, commonly known as water St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

Hypericum riparium thrives in moist soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.
