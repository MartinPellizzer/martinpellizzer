Hornstedtia rumphii

Hornstedtia rumphii, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and vibrant orange sepals. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, often as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees and wasps.

Hornstedtia rumphii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
