Hieracium murorum var. sanguineum

Hieracium murorum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the blood hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
