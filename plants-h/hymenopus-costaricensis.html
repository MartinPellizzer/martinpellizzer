Hymenopus costaricensis

Hymenopus costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican lily, is a striking plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with tall, strap-like leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive greenish tint. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
