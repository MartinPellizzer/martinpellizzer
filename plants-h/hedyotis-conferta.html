Hedyotis conferta

Hedyotis conferta, commonly known as the small redweed, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as understorey of forests or along stream banks. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, bright red, and clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.

Hedyotis conferta is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions, thriving in sandy or loamy soils.
