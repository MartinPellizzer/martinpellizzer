Hugonia reticulata

Hugonia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Hugonia reticulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
