Hieracium murorum subsp. subcrassum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subcrassum, commonly known as the rough mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its subspecies is distinguished by its coarsely toothed leaves and robust, hairy stems.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
