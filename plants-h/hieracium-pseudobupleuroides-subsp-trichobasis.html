Hieracium pseudobupleuroides subsp. trichobasis

Hieracium pseudobupleuroides subsp.

trichobasis, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies trichobasis is distinguished by its dense, soft pubescence, particularly on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

This plant is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
