Hesperaster chrysanthus

Hesperaster chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Hesperaster chrysanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in desert gardens.
