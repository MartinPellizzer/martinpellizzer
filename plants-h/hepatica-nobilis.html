Hepatica nobilis

Hepatica nobilis, commonly known as the noble liverwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that often display a striking color contrast. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, blue, or purple, typically appearing in early spring. Hepatica nobilis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
