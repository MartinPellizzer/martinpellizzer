Hieracium concoloriforme

Hieracium concoloriforme, commonly known as the pale yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium concoloriforme is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain regions.
