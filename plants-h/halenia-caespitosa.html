Halenia caespitosa

Halenia caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered halenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Halenia caespitosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
