Hermione littoralis

Hermione littoralis, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the sandy shores of the Atlantic coast.

It thrives in saline and well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptation to coastal environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Hermione littoralis plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
