Hieracium pseudatopum

Hieracium pseudatopum, commonly known as the false oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant features a central yellow disk surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

Hieracium pseudatopum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
