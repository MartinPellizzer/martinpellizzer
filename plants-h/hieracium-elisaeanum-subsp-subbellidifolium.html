Hieracium elisaeanum subsp. subbellidifolium

Hieracium elisaeanum subsp.

subbellidifolium, commonly known as the Elisaean Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It prefers open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
