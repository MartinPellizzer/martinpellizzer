Hyacinthus orientalis var. albulus

Hyacinthus orientalis var. albulus, commonly known as the white hyacinth, is a cultivar of the garden hyacinth characterized by its pale, almost white flowers.

This variety belongs to the Asparagales order and the Hyacinthaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other hyacinth species. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, conical clusters and bloom in early spring. The plant is known for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
