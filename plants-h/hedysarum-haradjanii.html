Hedysarum haradjanii

Hedysarum haradjanii, commonly known as the Haradjanii milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hedysarum haradjanii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
