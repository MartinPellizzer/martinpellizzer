Hippeastrum depauperatum

Hippeastrum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its relatively small size compared to other hippeastrum species. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

Hippeastrum depauperatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
