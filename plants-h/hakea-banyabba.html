Hakea banyabba

Hakea banyabba, commonly known as the Banyabba hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or spire-like flower spikes, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has needle-like leaves that are densely packed, providing a dense, shrubby appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Hakea banyabba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
