Haemastegia foliosa

Haemastegia foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed legume, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Haemastegia foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its foliage and floral characteristics.
