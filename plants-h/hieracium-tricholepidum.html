Hieracium tricholepidum

Hieracium tricholepidum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is considered a perennial species, often forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
