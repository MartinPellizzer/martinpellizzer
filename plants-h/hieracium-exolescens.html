Hieracium exolescens

Hieracium exolescens, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a woolly appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.
