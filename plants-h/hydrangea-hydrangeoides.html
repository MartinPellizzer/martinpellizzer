Hydrangea hydrangeoides

Hydrangea hydrangeoides, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hydrangea hydrangeoides is relatively low maintenance and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
