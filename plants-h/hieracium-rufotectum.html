Hieracium rufotectum

Hieracium rufotectum, commonly known as the red-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
