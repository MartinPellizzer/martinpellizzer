Huperzia nuda

Huperzia nuda, commonly known as the naked huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia on its stems. Huperzia nuda is characterized by its lack of true roots, relying instead on rhizomes for nutrient absorption.

It is a primitive vascular plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
