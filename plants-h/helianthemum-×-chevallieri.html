Helianthemum × chevallieri

Helianthemum × chevallieri, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a natural hybrid between Helianthemum nummularium and Helianthemum italicum, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters, often resembling miniature sunflowers, and bloom from spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
