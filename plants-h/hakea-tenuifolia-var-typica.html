Hakea tenuifolia var. typica

Hakea tenuifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces cylindrical, woody flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Hakea tenuifolia var. typica is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
