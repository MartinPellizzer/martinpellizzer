Heterotheca villosa var. horrida

Heterotheca villosa var. horrida, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of purple to pinkish-white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
