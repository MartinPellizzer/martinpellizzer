Hieracium waldsteinii subvar. paschtrikanum

Hieracium waldsteinii subvar. paschtrikanum, commonly known as the Paschtrikan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a single flowering stem with several flower heads.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed group, with specific adaptations to its native habitat.
