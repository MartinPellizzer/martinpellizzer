Hydrangea amplifolia

Hydrangea amplifolia, commonly known as the giant hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of sterile flowers, which are typically blue or pink, depending on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Hydrangea amplifolia thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
