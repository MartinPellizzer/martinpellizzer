Hypericum laricifolium

Hypericum laricifolium, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum laricifolium is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
