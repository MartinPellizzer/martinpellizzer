Hypericum petiolulatum var. subcordatum

Hypericum petiolulatum var. subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
