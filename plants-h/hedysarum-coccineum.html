Hedysarum coccineum

Hedysarum coccineum, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring trifoliate leaves and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a well-developed root system that hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hedysarum coccineum is also valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
