Hypolytrum ohwianum

Hypolytrum ohwianum, commonly known as the Kwazulu-Natal grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Hypolytrum ohwianum is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
