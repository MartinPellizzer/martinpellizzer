Heliotropium lineare

Heliotropium lineare, commonly known as the linear heliotrope, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, often with a distinct violet hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Heliotropium lineare is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
