Hedypnois cretica var. gracilior

Hedypnois cretica var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is closely related to other Hedypnois species and is characterized by its delicate appearance and fragrance.
