Heteropterys caerulea var. diversifolia

Heteropterys caerulea var. diversifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
