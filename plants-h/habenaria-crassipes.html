Habenaria crassipes

Habenaria crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous root system and erect, often branched stems. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The orchid is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist habitats and is often found in forest understories.
