Homalomena bancana

Homalomena bancana, commonly known as the Philippine ginger plant, is a tropical evergreen perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Homalomena bancana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
