Heteromeles fremontiana

Heteromeles fremontiana, commonly known as the fremont's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
