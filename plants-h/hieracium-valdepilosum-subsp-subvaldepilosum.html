Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. subvaldepilosum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

subvaldepilosum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, upright leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.
