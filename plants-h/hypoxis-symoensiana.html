Hypoxis symoensiana

Hypoxis symoensiana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces striking, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis symoensiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
