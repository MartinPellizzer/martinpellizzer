Hieracium levicaule subsp. subuliceps

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

subuliceps, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and it is known for its rapid spread through vegetative reproduction.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender stem and sharply pointed leaves.
