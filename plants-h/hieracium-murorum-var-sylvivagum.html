Hieracium murorum var. sylvivagum

Hieracium murorum var. sylvivagum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions where it thrives in partial to full shade.
