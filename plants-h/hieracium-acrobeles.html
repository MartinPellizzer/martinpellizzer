Hieracium acrobeles

Hieracium acrobeles, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium acrobeles is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some regions.
