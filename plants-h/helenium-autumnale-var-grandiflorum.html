Helenium autumnale var. grandiflorum

Helenium autumnale var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the swamp blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with red centers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
