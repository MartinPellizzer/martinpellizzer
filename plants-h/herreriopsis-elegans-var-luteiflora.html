Herreriopsis elegans var. luteiflora

Herreriopsis elegans var. luteiflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered herreriopsis, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a compact growth habit. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is popular in tropical gardens.
