Hosta rectifolia f. albiflora

Hosta rectifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered straight-leaved hosta, is a cultivar of the hosta plant characterized by its striking white flowers and upright, broad leaves.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is native to Asia, specifically Japan. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 40 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are a deep green. Its flowers are borne in loose clusters and bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
