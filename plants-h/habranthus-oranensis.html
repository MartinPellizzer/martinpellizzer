Habranthus oranensis

Habranthus oranensis, commonly known as the Oran's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
