Helemonium canescens

Helemonium canescens, commonly known as the hoary gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Helemonium canescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
