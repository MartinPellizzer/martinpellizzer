Hieracium sagittatum subsp. heterolepis

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

heterolepis, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long, slender pedicels.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae.
