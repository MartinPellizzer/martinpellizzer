Hedyotis capitellata var. pedicellata

Hedyotis capitellata var. pedicellata, commonly known as the slender bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense terminal spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its unique, bladder-like structures on its stems, which are a distinguishing botanical feature.
