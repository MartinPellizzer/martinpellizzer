Huperzia selago var. serrata

Huperzia selago var. serrata, commonly known as the serrate clubmoss, is a species of fern ally belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its compact growth habit and scale-like leaves. Native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's rhizome is segmented and produces upright, branched shoots.

Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in dense, cylindrical formations along the leaf margins.
