Hellenia melanocarpa

Hellenia melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited helene, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branching.

Hellenia melanocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
