Hieracium vollmannii

Hieracium vollmannii, commonly known as Vollmann's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in the central and western United States, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
