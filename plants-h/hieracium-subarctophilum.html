Hieracium subarctophilum

Hieracium subarctophilum, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to retain heat and moisture.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
