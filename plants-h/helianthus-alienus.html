Helianthus alienus

Helianthus alienus, commonly known as the alien sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and produces numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus alienus is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
