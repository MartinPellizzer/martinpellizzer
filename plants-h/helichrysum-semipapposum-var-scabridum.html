Helichrysum semipapposum var. scabridum

Helichrysum semipapposum var. scabridum, commonly known as the rough helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features narrow, grey-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that are often arranged in dense clusters. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to retain color and form even after flowering.
