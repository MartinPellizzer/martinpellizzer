Hieracium trelodianum

Hieracium trelodianum, commonly known as the Trelodian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium trelodianum is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
