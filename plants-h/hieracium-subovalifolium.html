Hieracium subovalifolium

Hieracium subovalifolium, commonly known as the suboval-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are broadly ovate to suboval in shape, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
