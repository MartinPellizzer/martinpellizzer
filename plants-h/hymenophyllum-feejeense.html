Hymenophyllum feejeense

Hymenophyllum feejeense, commonly known as the Feejeen's filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in the Hymenophyllaceae family.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Hymenophyllum feejeense is considered an endangered species in its native range.
