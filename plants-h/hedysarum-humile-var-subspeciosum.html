Hedysarum humile var. subspeciosum

Hedysarum humile var. subspeciosum, commonly known as the dwarf sweet clover, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
