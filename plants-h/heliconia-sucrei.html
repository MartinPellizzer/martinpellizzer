Heliconia sucrei

Heliconia sucrei, commonly known as the Sucre's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Heliconia sucrei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests and rainforests.
