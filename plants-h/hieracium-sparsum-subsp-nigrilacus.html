Hieracium sparsum subsp. nigrilacus

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

nigrilacus, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
