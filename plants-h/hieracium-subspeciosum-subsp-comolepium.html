Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. comolepium

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

comolepium, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
