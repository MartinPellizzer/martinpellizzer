Haemodorum capitatum

Haemodorum capitatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed everlasting or redroot, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as the "red root" family. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
