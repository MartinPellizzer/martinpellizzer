Hieracium atrogilvum

Hieracium atrogilvum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its dark green leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium atrogilvum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
