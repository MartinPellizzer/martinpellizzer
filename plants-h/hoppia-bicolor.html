Hoppia bicolor

Hoppia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive two-toned petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pink and purple.

Hoppia bicolor is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
