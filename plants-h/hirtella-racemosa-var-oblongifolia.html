Hirtella racemosa var. oblongifolia

Hirtella racemosa var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaved hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded habitats within lowland forests.
