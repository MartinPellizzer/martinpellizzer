Hedysarum aparines

Hedysarum aparines, commonly known as the redstem locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Hedysarum aparines is also known for its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful to livestock.
