Hippocratea grisebachii var. parvifolia

Hippocratea grisebachii var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hippocratea, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its bark is smooth and greyish, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
