Hippophae × goniocarpa

Hippophae × goniocarpa, commonly known as the sea buckthorn hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, showing high adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and wildlife habitat support.
