Hugonia batesii

Hugonia batesii, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy surface that helps repel water, while its roots anchor it in the substrate.

Hugonia batesii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
