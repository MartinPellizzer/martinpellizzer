Hypericum aucheri var. macranthum

Hypericum aucheri var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
