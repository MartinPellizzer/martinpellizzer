Hydrolea azurea

Hydrolea azurea, commonly known as the Azure Hydrolea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
