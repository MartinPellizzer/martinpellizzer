Hyalosperma strictum

Hyalosperma strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hyalosperma, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland margins.

Hyalosperma strictum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
