Hemigramma latifolia

Hemigramma latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a slender, creeping rhizome. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually green and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions.

Hemigramma latifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in controlled environments.
