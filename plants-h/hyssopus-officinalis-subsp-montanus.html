Hyssopus officinalis subsp. montanus

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

montanus, commonly known as mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic.

The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
