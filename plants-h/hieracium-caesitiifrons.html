Hieracium caesitiifrons

Hieracium caesitiifrons, commonly known as the sky-blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
