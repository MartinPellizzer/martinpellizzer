Hebe dilatata

Hebe dilatata, commonly known as the variable hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe dilatata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
