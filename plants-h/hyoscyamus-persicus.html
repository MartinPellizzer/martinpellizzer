Hyoscyamus persicus

Hyoscyamus persicus, commonly known as Persian henbane, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces toxic, bell-shaped fruits containing seeds, and its leaves are usually hairy and have a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
