Hyacinthus sulphureus

Hyacinthus sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur hyacinth, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blossoms. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

The sulphur hyacinth is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful appearance.
