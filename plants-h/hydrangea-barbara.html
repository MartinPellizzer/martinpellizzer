Hydrangea barbara

Hydrangea barbara, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Hydrangea barbara is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
