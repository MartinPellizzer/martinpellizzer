Homogyne alpina

Homogyne alpina, commonly known as the Alpine homogyne, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Homogyne alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
