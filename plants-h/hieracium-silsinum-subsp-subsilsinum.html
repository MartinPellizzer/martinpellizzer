Hieracium silsinum subsp. subsilsinum

Hieracium silsinum subsp.

subsilsinum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. It has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
