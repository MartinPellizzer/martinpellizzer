Hieracium murorum subsp. eubalium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

eubalium, commonly known as the European mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural environments.
