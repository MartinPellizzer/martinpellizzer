Helopus laevis

Helopus laevis, commonly known as the smooth helopus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish-white in color. Helopus laevis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
