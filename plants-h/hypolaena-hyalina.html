Hypolaena hyalina, commonly known as the glassy hypolaena, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America. The plant is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and translucent, waxy leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with vibrant colors that attract pollinators.

Hypolaena hyalina thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation to mimic its natural habitat.
