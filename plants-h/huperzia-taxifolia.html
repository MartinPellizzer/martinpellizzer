Huperzia taxifolia

Huperzia taxifolia, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are typically located at the base of the leaves. Huperzia taxifolia is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
