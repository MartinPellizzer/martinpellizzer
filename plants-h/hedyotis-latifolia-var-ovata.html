Hedyotis latifolia var. ovata

Hedyotis latifolia var. ovata, commonly known as broadleaf inchplant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
