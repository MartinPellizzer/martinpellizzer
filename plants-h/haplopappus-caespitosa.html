Haplopappus caespitosa

Haplopappus caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a short-lived perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its inflorescence consists of a single, short flowering stem with several yellow disk flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric ecosystems.
