Haageocereus australis var. acinacispinus

Haageocereus australis var. acinacispinus, commonly known as the Acacia-spined Cereus, is a cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a columnar growth habit with ribbed stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. Its spines are modified leaves, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
