Helianthus × laetiflorus

Helianthus × laetiflorus, commonly known as the showy sunflower, is a hybrid species within the sunflower genus.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller, darker florets. This plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 feet, with sturdy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
