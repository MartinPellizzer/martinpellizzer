Heliopsis helianthoides var. scabra

Heliopsis helianthoides var. scabra, commonly known as rough helianthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are rough to the touch, giving it its scientific name "scabra," which means "rough" in Latin.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
