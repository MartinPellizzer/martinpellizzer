Hymenodium crassifolium

Hymenodium crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Hymenodium crassifolium is adapted to saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
