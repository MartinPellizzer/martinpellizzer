Hieracium tectorum

Hieracium tectorum, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a central stem that bears yellow flowers in a composite head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

The plant is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environments.
