Haplopappus nelsonii

Haplopappus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's hairyfruit, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.
