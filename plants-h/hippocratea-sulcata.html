Hippocratea sulcata

Hippocratea sulcata, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, wrinkled leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often grown as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
