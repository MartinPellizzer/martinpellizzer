Halesia tetraptera

Halesia tetraptera, commonly known as the Chinese halesia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round fruits. Halesia tetraptera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
