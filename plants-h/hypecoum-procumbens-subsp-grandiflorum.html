Hypecoum procumbens subsp. grandiflorum

Hypecoum procumbens subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflowered Glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
