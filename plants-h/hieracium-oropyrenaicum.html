Hieracium oropyrenaicum

Hieracium oropyrenaicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
