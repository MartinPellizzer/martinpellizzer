Hypopogon

Hypopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and small, alternate leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. Hypopogon is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is considered an ornamental plant in some regions due to its unique foliage and resilience.
