Helianthus annuus

Helianthus annuus, commonly known as the sunflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by green bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows up to 3 meters tall, with a robust stem and broad, rough leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

Helianthus annuus is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
