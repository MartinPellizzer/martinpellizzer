Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. pullatum

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

pullatum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly purple-tinged flowers and is often found in moist or calcareous habitats.
