Hydrangea sachalinensis

Hydrangea sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sargent hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink, red, or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the garden.
