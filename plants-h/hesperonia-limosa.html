Hesperonia limosa

Hesperonia limosa, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Hesperonia limosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
