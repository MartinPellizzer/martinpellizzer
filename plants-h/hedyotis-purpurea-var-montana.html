Hedyotis purpurea var. montana

Hedyotis purpurea var. montana, commonly known as mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has elliptical to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
