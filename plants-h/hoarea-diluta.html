Hoarea diluta

Hoarea diluta, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, glossy, silver-green leaves that are oval-shaped and slightly textured. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

Hoarea diluta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
