Hymenandra rosea

Hymenandra rosea, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms.

Hymenandra rosea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
