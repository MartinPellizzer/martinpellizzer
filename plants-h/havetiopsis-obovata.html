Havetiopsis obovata

Havetiopsis obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Havetiopsis obovata plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
