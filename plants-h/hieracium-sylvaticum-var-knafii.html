Hieracium sylvaticum var. knafii

Hieracium sylvaticum var. knafii, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in association with other meadow and woodland flora.
