Hieracium racemosum subsp. subhirsutum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

subhirsutum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, with leaves that are hairy and often lobed. Its subspecies characteristics include a more hairy stem and leaves compared to the typical form.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
