Haloragis acutangula f. occidentalis

Haloragis acutangula f. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western sharp-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
