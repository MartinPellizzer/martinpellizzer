Hayata sherriffii

Hayata sherriffii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and meadows. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Hayata sherriffii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
