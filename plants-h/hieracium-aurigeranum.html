Hieracium aurigeranum

Hieracium aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its basal leaves are often lobed.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
