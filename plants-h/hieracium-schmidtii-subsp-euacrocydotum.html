Hieracium schmidtii subsp. euacrocydotum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

euacrocydotum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.
