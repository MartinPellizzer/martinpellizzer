Halimodendron halodendron

Halimodendron halodendron, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Halimodendron halodendron plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
