Hieracium odorans

Hieracium odorans, commonly known as sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Hieracium odorans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
