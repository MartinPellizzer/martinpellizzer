Hieracium murorum subsp. pruinale

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pruinale, commonly known as the Prunel-like hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are yellow, with a single ray floret and numerous disk florets, arranged in a compact inflorescence.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy foliage and distinctive floral structure.
