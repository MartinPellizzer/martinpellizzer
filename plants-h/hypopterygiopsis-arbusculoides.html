Hypopterygiopsis arbusculoides, commonly known as the small tree fern, is a unique member of the Dicksoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, stocky trunk and a dense crown of feathery fronds that resemble those of a tree fern. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes form a network that supports the growth of the plant and helps it anchor in the soil.

The fronds are bioluminescent under certain conditions, making it a visually striking and ecologically significant species.
