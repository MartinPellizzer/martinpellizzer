Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. ferdinandi-coburgii

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

ferdinandi-coburgii, commonly known as the Coburg hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers with numerous small florets, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is native to Central Europe and is considered a rare and protected plant in some regions.
