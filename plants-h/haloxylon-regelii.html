Haloxylon regelii

Haloxylon regelii, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss and a deep root system for accessing groundwater. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody pods containing seeds. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing forage for livestock.

Haloxylon regelii is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in extreme desert conditions.
