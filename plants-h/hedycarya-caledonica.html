Hedycarya caledonica

Hedycarya caledonica, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedycarya caledonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
