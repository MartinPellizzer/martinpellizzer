Hieracium grammophyllum

Hieracium grammophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow disk flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a hairy stem and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Hieracium grammophyllum is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
