Habenaria hondurensis

Habenaria hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
