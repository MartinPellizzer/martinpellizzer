Hemionitis eriophora

Hemionitis eriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant has delicate, feathery fronds that are adapted for efficient water absorption and gas exchange. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on water currents for spore dispersal.

Hemionitis eriophora plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
