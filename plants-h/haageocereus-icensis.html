Haageocereus icensis

Haageocereus icensis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinct appearance, featuring a brightly colored upper part known as the "skirt" and a green, stem-like base. The plant is a hybrid between two different cactus species, which contributes to its unique coloration. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant hues.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
