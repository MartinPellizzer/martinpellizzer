Hibiscus tridentatus

Hibiscus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, giving it its common name. Hibiscus tridentatus typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in humid and tropical environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
