Hibiscus pulverulentus

Hibiscus pulverulentus, commonly known as the dusty hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with a distinctive, velvety texture that gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus pulverulentus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
