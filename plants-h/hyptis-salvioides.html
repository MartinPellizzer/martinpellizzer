Hyptis salvioides

Hyptis salvioides, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis salvioides is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
