Hyptis leiocephala

Hyptis leiocephala, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or disturbed areas.

Hyptis leiocephala is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
