Haemanthus puniceus var. magnificus

Haemanthus puniceus var. magnificus, commonly known as the fireplant, is a striking evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
