Hyptis dumetorum

Hyptis dumetorum, commonly known as Brazilian pepper or false pepper, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
