Hieracium atricollum

Hieracium atricollum, commonly known as the black-necked hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have five petals and are pollinated by insects.

Hieracium atricollum is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
