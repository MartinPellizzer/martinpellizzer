Horsfieldia atjehensis

Horsfieldia atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjeh orchid, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary forest areas. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, Horsfieldia atjehensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
