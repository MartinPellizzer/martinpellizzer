Hieracium murorum subsp. steenhoffii

Hieracium murorum subsp.

steenhoffii, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Hieracium. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
