Hedyotis acuminata f. hirtella

Hedyotis acuminata f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy blushing root, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
