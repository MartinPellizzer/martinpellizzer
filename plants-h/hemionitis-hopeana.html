Hemionitis hopeana

Hemionitis hopeana, commonly known as the rock fern, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Hemionitidaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a tight spiral, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Hemionitis hopeana is often found growing on rocky substrates and is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on desert plant adaptations.
