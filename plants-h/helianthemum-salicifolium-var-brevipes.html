Helianthemum salicifolium var. brevipes

Helianthemum salicifolium var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, woody stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a silvery-green color and are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

This plant thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
