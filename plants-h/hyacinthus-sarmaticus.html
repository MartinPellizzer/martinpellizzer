Hyacinthus sarmaticus

Hyacinthus sarmaticus, commonly known as the Sarmatian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.

Hyacinthus sarmaticus is also valued for its ability to naturalize and return year after year.
