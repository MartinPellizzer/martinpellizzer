Hieracium verbascifolium subsp. capreifolium

Hieracium verbascifolium subsp.

capreifolium, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

This species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
