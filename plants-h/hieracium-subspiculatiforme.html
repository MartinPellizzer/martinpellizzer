Hieracium subspiculatiforme

Hieracium subspiculatiforme, commonly known as the spiky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
