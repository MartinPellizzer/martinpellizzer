Haworthia mirabilis subsp. mundula

Haworthia mirabilis subsp.

mundula, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa. It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves with white or pale green markings.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers on slender stems during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
