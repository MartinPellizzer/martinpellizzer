Hieracium oxyodon subsp. subcanescens

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

subcanescens, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flower heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish bracts.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
