Heterotheca pilosa

Heterotheca pilosa, commonly known as the roughleaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive brown central disk. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Heterotheca pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
