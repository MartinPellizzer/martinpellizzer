Hyptis rhomboidea

Hyptis rhomboidea, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
