Hedyotis galeottii

Hedyotis galeottii, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a small herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hedyotis galeottii is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
