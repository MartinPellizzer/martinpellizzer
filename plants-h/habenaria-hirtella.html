Habenaria hirtella

Habenaria hirtella, commonly known as the hairy helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped flower with a hairy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Habenaria hirtella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
