Hackelia difformis

Hackelia difformis, commonly known as the variable hackelia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hackelia difformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
