Heteropholis annua

Heteropholis annua, commonly known as the annual heteropholis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening in regions with warm climates.
