Hieracium nyaradyanum

Hieracium nyaradyanum, commonly known as the Nyaradya hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Nyaradya Mountains in central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool temperatures. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hieracium nyaradyanum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
