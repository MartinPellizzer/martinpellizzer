Hypericum davisii

Hypericum davisii, commonly known as Davis' St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum davisii is often found in wetlands and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
