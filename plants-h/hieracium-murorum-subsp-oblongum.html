Hieracium murorum subsp. oblongum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

oblongum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat creeping.

It is a common weed in agricultural areas and is known for its rapid spread through rhizomes and seeds.
