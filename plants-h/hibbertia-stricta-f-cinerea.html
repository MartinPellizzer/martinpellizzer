Hibbertia stricta f. cinerea

Hibbertia stricta f. cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf hibbertia, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Hibbertia stricta f. cinerea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
