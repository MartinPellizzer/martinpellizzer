Hieracium magyaricum subsp. volhynicum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

volhynicum, commonly known as the Volhynian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and is native to parts of Eastern Europe.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
