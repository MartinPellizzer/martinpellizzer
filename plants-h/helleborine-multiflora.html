Helleborine multiflora

Helleborine multiflora, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by ants, a process known as myrmecochory.

Helleborine multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect pollinators.
