Hieracium infuscatum

Hieracium infuscatum, commonly known as the cloudy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or reddish, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium infuscatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
