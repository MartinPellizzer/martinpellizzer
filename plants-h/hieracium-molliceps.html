Hieracium molliceps

Hieracium molliceps, commonly known as the soft-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium molliceps is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
