Hydrangea nivea var. conformis

Hydrangea nivea var. conformis, commonly known as the snow hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces showy, spherical flower heads that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Hydrangea nivea var. conformis prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
