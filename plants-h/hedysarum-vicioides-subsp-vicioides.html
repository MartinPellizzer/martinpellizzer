Hedysarum vicioides subsp. vicioides

Hedysarum vicioides subsp.

vicioides, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or disturbed areas. Hedysarum vicioides subsp.

vicioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
