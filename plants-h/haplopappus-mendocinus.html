Haplopappus mendocinus

Haplopappus mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino pappus, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. It features small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat, disk-like structure.
