Hieracium fuscescens

Hieracium fuscescens, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium fuscescens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
