Hieracium seridis

Hieracium seridis, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hieracium seridis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and provides nectar for pollinators.
