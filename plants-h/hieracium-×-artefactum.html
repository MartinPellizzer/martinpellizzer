Hieracium × artefactum

Hieracium × artefactum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered invasive in some regions, outcompeting native flora.
