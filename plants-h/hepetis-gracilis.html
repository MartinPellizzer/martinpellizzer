Hepetis gracilis

Hepetis gracilis, commonly known as slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Hepetis gracilis is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
