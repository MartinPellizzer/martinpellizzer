Helleborine palustris f. parvifolia

Helleborine palustris f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved marsh helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and pale greenish-white flowers with prominent purple veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as bogs and marshes. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
