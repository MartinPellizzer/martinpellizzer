Hibiscus purpurascens

Hibiscus purpurascens, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is typically found in wetland or marshy habitats. Hibiscus purpurascens is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters in height.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
