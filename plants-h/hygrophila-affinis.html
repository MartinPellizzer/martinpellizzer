Hygrophila affinis

Hygrophila affinis, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It thrives in wet environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rivers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, pink or purple flowers in spikes. Hygrophila affinis is known for its ability to tolerate both fresh and brackish water, making it adaptable to a variety of aquatic habitats.

Its rapid growth rate and ornamental value make it a popular choice for water gardens and pond landscaping.
