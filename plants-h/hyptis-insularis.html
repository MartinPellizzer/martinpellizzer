Hyptis insularis

Hyptis insularis, commonly known as the island hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with opposite leaves that are often hairy and fragrant.

Hyptis insularis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
