Hedera capitata

Hedera capitata, commonly known as the clustered ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera capitata is also valued for its ability to climb and cover walls or trellises effectively.
