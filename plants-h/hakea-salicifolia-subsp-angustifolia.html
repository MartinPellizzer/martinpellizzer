Hakea salicifolia subsp. angustifolia

Hakea salicifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers in dense spikes, typically during the autumn season. It is adapted to arid and sandy soils, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal or dry garden environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
