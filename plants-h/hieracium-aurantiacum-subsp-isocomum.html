Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. isocomum

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

isocomum, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
