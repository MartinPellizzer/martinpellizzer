Hiraea cinerea

Hiraea cinerea, commonly known as the pale hiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems that are often covered in a fine, grayish bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, contributing to its subtle appearance.
