Hypericum adenotrichum var. myriotrichum

Hypericum adenotrichum var. myriotrichum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and moist woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
