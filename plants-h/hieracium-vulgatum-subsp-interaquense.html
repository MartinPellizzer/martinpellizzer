Hieracium vulgatum subsp. interaquense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

interaquense, commonly known as the water hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in supporting local insect populations.
