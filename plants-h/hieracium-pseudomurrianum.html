Hieracium pseudomurrianum

Hieracium pseudomurrianum, commonly known as the false mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are usually basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
