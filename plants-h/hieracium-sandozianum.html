Hieracium sandozianum

Hieracium sandozianum, commonly known as the Sandoz's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
