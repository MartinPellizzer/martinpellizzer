Hieracium subincisum subsp. psammogenes

Hieracium subincisum subsp.

psammogenes, commonly known as the sand dune hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to sandy and coastal environments. It features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, windy conditions.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other hawkweed species.
