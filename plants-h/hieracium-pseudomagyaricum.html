Hieracium pseudomagyaricum

Hieracium pseudomagyaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
