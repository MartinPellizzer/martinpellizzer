Hieracium murorum subsp. neosparsum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

neosparsum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by a ring of leaf-like bracts.

The species is characterized by its slender growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
