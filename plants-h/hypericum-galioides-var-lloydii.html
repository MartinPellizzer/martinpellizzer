Hypericum galioides var. lloydii

Hypericum galioides var. lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native garden landscapes.
