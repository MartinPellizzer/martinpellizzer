Hexachlamys itararensis

Hexachlamys itararensis, commonly known as the Itarare sand-violet, is a rare flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a humid, warm climate.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
