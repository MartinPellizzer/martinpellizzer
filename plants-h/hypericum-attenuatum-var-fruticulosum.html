Hypericum attenuatum var. fruticulosum

Hypericum attenuatum var. fruticulosum, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces orange-red berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
