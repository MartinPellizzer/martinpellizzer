Hieracium racemosum subsp. crinitum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
