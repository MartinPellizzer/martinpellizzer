Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. lineatiforme

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

lineatiforme, commonly known as the linear-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with distinct venation, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender stems and linear leaves, distinguishing it from other hawkweed species.
