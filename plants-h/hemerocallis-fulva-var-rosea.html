Hemerocallis fulva var. rosea

Hemerocallis fulva var. rosea, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a variant of the daylily species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically lasting only one day. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are bright green and often have a slight blue-gray hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to naturalize easily in suitable climates.
