Heliotropium fruticosum

Heliotropium fruticosum, commonly known as the coastal heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune environments.

Heliotropium fruticosum is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.
