Hydrangea hedyotidea

Hydrangea hedyotidea, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features showy, inflorescent clusters of white or pink flowers that change color depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hydrangea hedyotidea is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
