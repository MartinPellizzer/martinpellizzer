Hieracium lactucella subsp. tricheilema

Hieracium lactucella subsp.

tricheilema, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
