Hieracium murorum subsp. eumeces

Hieracium murorum subsp.

eumeces, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and its stems are upright and often branched.

The yellow hawkweed is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
