Haumaniastrum semilignosum

Haumaniastrum semilignosum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a woody base, which gives it its semi-lignified appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to desert ecosystems.
