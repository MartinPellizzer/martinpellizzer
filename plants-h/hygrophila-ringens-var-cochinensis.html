Hygrophila ringens var. cochinensis

Hygrophila ringens var. cochinensis, commonly known as the Cochin water hyssop, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
