Heuchera eastwoodiae

Heuchera eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that range in color from deep purple to red and green, depending on the season and light conditions. The plant produces upright spikes of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera eastwoodiae is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice in shade gardens.
