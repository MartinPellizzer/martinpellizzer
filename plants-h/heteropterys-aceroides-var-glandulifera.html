Heteropterys aceroides var. glandulifera

Heteropterys aceroides var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-leafed hibiscus, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that often have glandular dots on their surface. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
