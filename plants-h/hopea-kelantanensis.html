Hopea kelantanensis

Hopea kelantanensis, commonly known as the Kelantan hopea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing winged seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
