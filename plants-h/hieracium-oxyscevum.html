Hieracium oxyscevum

Hieracium oxyscevum, commonly known as the sharpleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with sharp margins, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium oxyscevum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
