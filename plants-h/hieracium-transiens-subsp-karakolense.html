Hieracium transiens subsp. karakolense

Hieracium transiens subsp.

karakolense, commonly known as the Karakolense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded locations.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, glandular leaves, which are adaptations to its harsh, high-altitude habitat.

This subspecies is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
