Hieracium magyaricum subsp. heothinum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

heothinum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching a height of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stem is often reddish in color.

It is a relatively rare subspecies, often found in specific habitats across Hungary and parts of Austria.
