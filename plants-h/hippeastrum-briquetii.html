Hippeastrum briquetii

Hippeastrum briquetii, commonly known as the Briquet's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
