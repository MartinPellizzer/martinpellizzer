Hieracium australe subsp. australe

Hieracium australe subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Australia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it features a rosette of basal leaves with a woolly texture.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from spring to autumn. It has fibrous roots and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
