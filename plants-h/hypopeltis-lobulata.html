Hypopeltis lobulata

Hypopeltis lobulata, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem and produces heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Hypopeltis lobulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
