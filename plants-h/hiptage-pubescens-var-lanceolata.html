Hiptage pubescens var. lanceolata

Hiptage pubescens var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate hiptage, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
