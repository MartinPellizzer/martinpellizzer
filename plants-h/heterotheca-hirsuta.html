Heterotheca hirsuta

Heterotheca hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with purple centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Heterotheca hirsuta is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
