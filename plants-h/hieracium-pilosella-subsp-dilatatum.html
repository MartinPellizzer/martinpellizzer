Hieracium pilosella subsp. dilatatum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is an annual or biennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
