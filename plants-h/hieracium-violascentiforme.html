Hieracium violascentiforme

Hieracium violascentiforme, commonly known as the violet-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium violascentiforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
