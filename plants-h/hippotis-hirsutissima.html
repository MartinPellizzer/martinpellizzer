Hippotis hirsutissima

Hippotis hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is a member of the Lentibulariaceae family and is known for its ability to trap small aquatic organisms. It thrives in nutrient-rich, shallow waters and is often found in temperate regions.
