Hieracium lissoglaucum

Hieracium lissoglaucum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Hieracium lissoglaucum is commonly found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is considered a characteristic species of the Carpathian region.
