Hypericum legrandii

Hypericum legrandii, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and woody, branched stems that are often covered in small, sticky glands. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum legrandii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
