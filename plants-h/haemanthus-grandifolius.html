Haemanthus grandifolius

Haemanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that features tall, upright stems covered in dense clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant's foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
