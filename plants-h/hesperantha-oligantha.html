Hesperantha oligantha

Hesperantha oligantha, commonly known as the slender bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, producing intricate, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

Hesperantha oligantha prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
