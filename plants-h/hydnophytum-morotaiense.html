Hydnophytum morotaiense

Hydnophytum morotaiense, commonly known as the Morotai mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees in the rainforests of Indonesia.

It has a distinctive appearance with smooth, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. It produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
