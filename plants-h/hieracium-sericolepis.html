Hieracium sericolepis

Hieracium sericolepis, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes. The plant features slender, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.
