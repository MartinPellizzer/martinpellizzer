Hieracium sabaudum var. linearifolium

Hieracium sabaudum var. linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, linear leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
