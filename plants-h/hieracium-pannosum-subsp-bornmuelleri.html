Hieracium pannosum subsp. bornmuelleri

Hieracium pannosum subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves and tall, branched stems that bear yellow disk flowers. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
