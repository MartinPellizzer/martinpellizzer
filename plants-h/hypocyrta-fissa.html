Hypocyrta fissa

Hypocyrta fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers. Its succulent stems and leaves store moisture, allowing it to endure long periods of drought.

Hypocyrta fissa is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
