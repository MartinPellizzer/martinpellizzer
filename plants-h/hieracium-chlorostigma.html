Hieracium chlorostigma

Hieracium chlorostigma, commonly known as the green-stigma hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which is typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Hieracium chlorostigma is a non-native species in many regions and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
