Hieracium murorum subsp. lugubre

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lugubre, commonly known as the dismal hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive appearance and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
