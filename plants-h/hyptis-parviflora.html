Hyptis parviflora

Hyptis parviflora, commonly known as narrowleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hyptis parviflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
