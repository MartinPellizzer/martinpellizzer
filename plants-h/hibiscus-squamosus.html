Hibiscus squamosus

Hibiscus squamosus, commonly known as the scaly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has palmate leaves with a hairy or scaly texture, which gives it its common name. Hibiscus squamosus thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
