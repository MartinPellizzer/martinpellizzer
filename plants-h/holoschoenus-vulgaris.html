Holoschoenus vulgaris

Holoschoenus vulgaris, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features cylindrical, grass-like stems that are typically green or reddish in color, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Holoschoenus vulgaris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
