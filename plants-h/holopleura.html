Holopleura

Holopleura, commonly known as the sea hare, is a type of marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

It is characterized by its soft, often colorful body and the presence of a muscular foot used for locomotion. The holopleura lacks a shell and instead has a gelatinous mantle that covers its body. These creatures are typically found in shallow coastal waters and are known for their ability to secrete a variety of bioactive compounds.

They play an important role in marine ecosystems as both herbivores and detritivores.
