Hieracium epimanum

Hieracium epimanum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium epimanum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a common weed in many regions.
