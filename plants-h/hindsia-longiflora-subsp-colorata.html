Hindsia longiflora subsp. colorata

Hindsia longiflora subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the colorful hindsia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America. It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
