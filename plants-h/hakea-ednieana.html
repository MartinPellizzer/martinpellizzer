Hakea ednieana

Hakea ednieana, commonly known as the Ednieana hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and clusters of needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its root system is adapted to survive in arid conditions, making it resilient to drought.

Hakea ednieana is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
