Hieracium glareicola

Hieracium glareicola, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium glareicola is adapted to high-altitude environments, demonstrating resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
