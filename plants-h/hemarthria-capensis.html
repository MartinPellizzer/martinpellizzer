Hemarthria capensis

Hemarthria capensis, commonly known as Natal grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Hemarthria capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth and deep root system.
