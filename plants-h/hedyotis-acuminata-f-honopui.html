Hedyotis acuminata f. honopui

Hedyotis acuminata f. honopui, commonly known as the Honopui trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features slender, creeping stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and bloom from late winter to early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
