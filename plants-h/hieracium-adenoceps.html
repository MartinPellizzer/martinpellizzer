Hieracium adenoceps

Hieracium adenoceps, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. Hieracium adenoceps is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it has a tendency to spread through rhizomes.

Its name refers to the glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which secrete sticky substances.
