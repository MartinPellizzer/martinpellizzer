Hermione monspeliensis

Hermione monspeliensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
