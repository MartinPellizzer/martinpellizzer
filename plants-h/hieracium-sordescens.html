Hieracium sordescens

Hieracium sordescens, commonly known as the sordid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive pale green center.

Hieracium sordescens is known for its ability to thrive in challenging environments and is considered a common weed in many regions.
