Hieracium subumbellatum

Hieracium subumbellatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
