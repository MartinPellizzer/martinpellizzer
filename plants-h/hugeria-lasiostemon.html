Hugeria lasiostemon

Hugeria lasiostemon, commonly known as the rough-stemmed hugeria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
