Hieracium laxilimbatum

Hieracium laxilimbatum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a soft, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
