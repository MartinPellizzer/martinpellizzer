Helianthemum croceum subsp. cantabricum

Helianthemum croceum subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian helianthemum, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features compact, evergreen foliage with small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
