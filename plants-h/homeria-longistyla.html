Homeria longistyla

Homeria longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled homeria, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated styles that extend beyond the petals, giving it its common name. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Homeria longistyla produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
