Hieracium praecox var. basalticum

Hieracium praecox var. basalticum, commonly known as the basalt hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils, often on slopes or in open habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

It is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
