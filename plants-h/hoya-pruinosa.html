Hoya pruinosa

Hoya pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet honeysuckle, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, waxy white flowers that bloom in summer. Hoya pruinosa is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its leaves and stems.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
