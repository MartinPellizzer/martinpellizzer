Hylotelephium maximum subsp. maximum

Hylotelephium maximum subsp.

maximum, commonly known as the Armenian carpet, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a low-growing perennial with dense, fleshy leaves that form a rosette structure.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.
