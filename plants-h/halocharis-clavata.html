Halocharis clavata

Halocharis clavata, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow, globular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Halocharis clavata thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
