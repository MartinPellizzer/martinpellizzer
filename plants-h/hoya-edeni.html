Hoya edeni

Hoya edeni, commonly known as the Wax Plant or the Rose of Sharon, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of star-shaped, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Hoya edeni is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardeners.
