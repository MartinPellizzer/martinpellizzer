Heteranthus dubius

Heteranthus dubius, commonly known as the variable flower plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring distinct stamen and pistil arrangements. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with colors ranging from white to pale yellow.

Botanically, it is noted for its variability in morphology, which makes it a subject of interest in taxonomic studies.
