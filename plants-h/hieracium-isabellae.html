Hieracium isabellae

Hieracium isabellae, commonly known as Isabella's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
