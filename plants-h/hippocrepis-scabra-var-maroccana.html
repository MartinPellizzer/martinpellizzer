Hippocrepis scabra var. maroccana

Hippocrepis scabra var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
