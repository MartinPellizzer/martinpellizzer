Helianthemum majus

Helianthemum majus, commonly known as the Canary Island carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and Madeira.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub with succulent leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Helianthemum majus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
