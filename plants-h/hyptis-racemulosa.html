Hyptis racemulosa

Hyptis racemulosa, commonly known as the clustered hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, minty scent. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyptis racemulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
