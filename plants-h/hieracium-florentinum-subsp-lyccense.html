Hieracium florentinum subsp. lyccense

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

lyccense, commonly known as the Lyccense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
