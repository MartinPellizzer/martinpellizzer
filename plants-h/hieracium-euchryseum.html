Hieracium euchryseum

Hieracium euchryseum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium euchryseum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies in suitable conditions.
