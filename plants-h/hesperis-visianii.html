Hesperis visianii

Hesperis visianii, commonly known as the Visianii horned poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, papery petals that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to alpine meadows.

Hesperis visianii is also notable for its unique, horn-like seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
