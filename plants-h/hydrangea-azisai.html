Hydrangea azisai

Hydrangea azisai, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or even red, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are often covered in a thick, waxy coating.

Hydrangea azisai thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
