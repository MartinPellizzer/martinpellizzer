Hyptis excelsa

Hyptis excelsa, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is known for its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis excelsa thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its visual appeal.
