Helleborine lingua

Helleborine lingua, commonly known as the tongue of the hellebore, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, tongue-like flower structure that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are usually green or purple in color.

Helleborine lingua is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
