Hallia trifoliata

Hallia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hallia trifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
