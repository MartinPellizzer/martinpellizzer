Hesperantha radiata subsp. radiata

Hesperantha radiata subsp.

radiata, commonly known as the radiate hesperantha, is a species native to South Africa. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
