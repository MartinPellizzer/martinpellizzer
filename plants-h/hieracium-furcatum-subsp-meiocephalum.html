Hieracium furcatum subsp. meiocephalum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

meiocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other members of the species. It prefers disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and has been studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
