Hydrangea arborescens var. sterilis

Hydrangea arborescens var. sterilis, commonly known as the panicled hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of sterile flower heads surrounded by fertile flowers. The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
