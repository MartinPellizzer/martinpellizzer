Heracantha lanata

Heracantha lanata, commonly known as the woolly heraclina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Heracantha lanata is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
