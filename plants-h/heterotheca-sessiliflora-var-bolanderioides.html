Heterotheca sessiliflora var. bolanderioides

Heterotheca sessiliflora var. bolanderioides, commonly known as the Bolander's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals. The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
