Hoffmannseggia stricta var. demissa

Hoffmannseggia stricta var. demissa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a hairy stem and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It has adapted to survive in harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves.
