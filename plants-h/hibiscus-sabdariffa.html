Hibiscus sabdariffa

Hibiscus sabdariffa, commonly known as roselle, is a flowering plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers and distinctive calyces that are used in food and traditional medicine. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers, which bloom in summer and are followed by the edible calyces. Hibiscus sabdariffa is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and functional uses.
