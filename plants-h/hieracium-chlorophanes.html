Hieracium chlorophanes

Hieracium chlorophanes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium chlorophanes is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in sunny, well-drained soils.
