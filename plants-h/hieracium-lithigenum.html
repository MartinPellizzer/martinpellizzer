Hieracium lithigenum

Hieracium lithigenum, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils on limestone or rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a serrated margin, while its flowers are yellowish to reddish in color.

Hieracium lithigenum is often found in open habitats such as meadows, cliffs, and mountain slopes.
