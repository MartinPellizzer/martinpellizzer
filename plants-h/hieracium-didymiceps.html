Hieracium didymiceps

Hieracium didymiceps, commonly known as the double-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers consist of two distinct types of florets—ray florets and disk florets—giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium didymiceps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
