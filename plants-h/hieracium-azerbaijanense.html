Hieracium azerbaijanense

Hieracium azerbaijanense, commonly known as Azerbaijani hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly Azerbaijan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features yellow or orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

Hieracium azerbaijanense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive, daisy-like appearance.
