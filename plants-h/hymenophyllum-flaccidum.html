Hymenophyllum flaccidum

Hymenophyllum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid moss, is a delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of hair, giving it a soft, almost velvety appearance. This epiphytic plant typically grows on the bark of trees in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on the underside of its fronds and are clustered in small, circular groups known as sori.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat requirements make it an important component of forest understory ecosystems.
