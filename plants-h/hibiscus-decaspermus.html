Hibiscus decaspermus

Hibiscus decaspermus, commonly known as the Cape hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring a rough, fissured bark. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, with a yellow throat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Hibiscus decaspermus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
