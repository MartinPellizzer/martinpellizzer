Hesperis versicolor

Hesperis versicolor, commonly known as the variable hawksbeard, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow or white flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, with pinnate leaves that have serrated edges. It is characterized by its variable flower color, which can range from pale yellow to deep orange.

Hesperis versicolor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
