Hieracium ampliatum var. gracilius

Hieracium ampliatum var. gracilius, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
