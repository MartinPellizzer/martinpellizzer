Hieracium magyaricum subsp. parvistolonum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

parvistolonum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces achenes as its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
