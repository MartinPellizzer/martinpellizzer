Helichrysum brassii

Helichrysum brassii, commonly known as the golden marguerite or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or orange flower heads, which resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Helichrysum brassii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
