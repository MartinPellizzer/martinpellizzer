Hiraea cornifolia

Hiraea cornifolia, commonly known as the horned hiraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like structures that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
