Hieracium arpillense

Hieracium arpillense, commonly known as the Arpillas hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
