Hakea ilicifolia var. minor

Hakea ilicifolia var. minor, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
