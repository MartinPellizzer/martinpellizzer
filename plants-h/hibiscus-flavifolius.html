Hibiscus flavifolius

Hibiscus flavifolius, commonly known as the yellow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually bright green and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus flavifolius thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
