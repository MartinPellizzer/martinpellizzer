Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. subaurantiacum

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

subaurantiacum, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features bright yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
