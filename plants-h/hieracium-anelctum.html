Hieracium anelctum

Hieracium anelctum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium anelctum is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
