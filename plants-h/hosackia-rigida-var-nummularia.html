Hosackia rigida var. nummularia

Hosackia rigida var. nummularia, commonly known as the roundleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats. Its compound leaves are typically arranged in pairs, and the stems are erect with a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
