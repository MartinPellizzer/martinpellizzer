Hypericum pringlei

Hypericum pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its scientific name honors the botanist John Pringle, who made significant contributions to the study of South American flora.

Hypericum pringlei is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
