Haplopappus stolonifer var. glabratus

Haplopappus stolonifer var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth stoloniferous haplopappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in dry conditions, and its stems are often covered with fine, hairless structures. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
