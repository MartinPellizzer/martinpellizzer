Hyacinthus tenuiflorus

Hyacinthus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hyacinthus tenuiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
