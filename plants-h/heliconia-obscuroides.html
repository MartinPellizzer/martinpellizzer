Heliconia obscuroides

Heliconia obscuroides, commonly known as the dark red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, bract-like inflorescences that are typically deep red or orange in color. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds and bats, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.

Heliconia obscuroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and is often used in floral arrangements.
