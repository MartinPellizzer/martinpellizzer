Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudoblongum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudoblongum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of fuzzy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flower heads are surrounded by bracts that resemble small leaves.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and wind-pollinated blooms.
