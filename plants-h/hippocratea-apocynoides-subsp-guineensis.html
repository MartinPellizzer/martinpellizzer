Hippocratea apocynoides subsp. guineensis

Hippocratea apocynoides subsp.

guineensis, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa. It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its fruit is a spherical, greenish-yellow berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
