Hebanthe grandiflora

Hebanthe grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered hebanthe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.

Hebanthe grandiflora is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
