Hemionitis falcata

Hemionitis falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the early spring.

Hemionitis falcata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
