Hyssopus officinalis var. seravschanicus

Hyssopus officinalis var. seravschanicus, commonly known as the Seravshan hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
