Hieracium episcotum

Hieracium episcotum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Hieracium episcotum is an annual or biennial plant that can spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
