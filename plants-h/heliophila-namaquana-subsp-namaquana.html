Heliophila namaquana subsp. namaquana

Heliophila namaquana subsp.

namaquana, commonly known as the Namibian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Namibia and South Africa.
