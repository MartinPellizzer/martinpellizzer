Hieracium vulgatum subsp. divisum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

divisum, commonly known as the divided hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, divided leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom from June to September.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
