Hyptis lutescens

Hyptis lutescens, commonly known as yellow hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening as well.
