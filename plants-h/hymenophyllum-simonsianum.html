Hymenophyllum simonsianum

Hymenophyllum simonsianum, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance and finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on the bark of trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Hymenophyllum simonsianum plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
