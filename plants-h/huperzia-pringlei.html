Huperzia pringlei

Huperzia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered growth habit. The plant typically features spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in clusters on the underside of its leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Huperzia pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
