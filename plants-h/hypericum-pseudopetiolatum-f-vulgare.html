Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. vulgare

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. vulgare, commonly known as the common St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers with prominent stamens and its woody, shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
