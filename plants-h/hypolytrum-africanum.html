Hypolytrum africanum

Hypolytrum africanum, commonly known as African marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, delicate flowers.

Hypolytrum africanum plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
