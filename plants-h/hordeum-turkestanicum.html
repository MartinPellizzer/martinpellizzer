Hordeum turkestanicum

Hordeum turkestanicum, commonly known as Turkestan barley, is a species of wild barley native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous two-rowed grains. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Hordeum turkestanicum is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to harsh climates.
