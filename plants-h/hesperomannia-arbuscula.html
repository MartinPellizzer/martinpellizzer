Hesperomannia arbuscula

Hesperomannia arbuscula, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive globular shape, featuring a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Hesperomannia arbuscula is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
