Hieracium pannosum subsp. epiglossophyllum

Hieracium pannosum subsp.

epiglossophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow disk flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are hairy and broadly lance-shaped, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
