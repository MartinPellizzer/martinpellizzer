Howardia richardii

Howardia richardii, commonly known as the orange ghost orchid, is a rare and unique plant native to the rainforests of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant has a short, stocky stem with a single, showy inflorescence that blooms in clusters of bright orange or red flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
