Homalocarpus narcissiflorus

Homalocarpus narcissiflorus, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a dense, evergreen canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
