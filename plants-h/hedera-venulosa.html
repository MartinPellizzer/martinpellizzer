Hedera venulosa

Hedera venulosa, commonly known as the Italian ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, shiny leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded areas and is often found in woodlands and along rocky outcrops.

Hedera venulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb walls and trees.
