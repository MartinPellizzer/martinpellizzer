Hieracium praecox subsp. conjugatum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

conjugatum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
