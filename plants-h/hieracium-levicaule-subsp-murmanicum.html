Hieracium levicaule subsp. murmanicum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

murmanicum, commonly known as the Murman hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it is often found in alpine and subarctic regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in local ecosystems.
