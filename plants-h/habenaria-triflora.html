Habenaria triflora

Habenaria triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces three to five flowers per stem, each with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, shaded environments where it grows.

Habenaria triflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
