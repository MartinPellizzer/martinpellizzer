Hypericum kamtschaticum var. paramushirense

Hypericum kamtschaticum var. paramushirense, commonly known as the Paramushir hypericum, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
