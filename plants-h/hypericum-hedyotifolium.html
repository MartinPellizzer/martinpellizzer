Hypericum hedyotifolium

Hypericum hedyotifolium, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Nepal.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often reddish in color.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with parts of it traditionally used in herbal remedies.
