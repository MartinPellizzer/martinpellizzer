Helenium suaveolens

Helenium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented helenium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow ray florets and dark central disk. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Helenium suaveolens is valued in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
