Hieracium sabaudum subsp. salticola

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

salticola, commonly known as the saltgrass hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, saline soils, often found in coastal areas and arid regions.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations, making it an important species in saline environments.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to harsh, coastal conditions.
