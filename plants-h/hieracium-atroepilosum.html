Hieracium atroepilosum

Hieracium atroepilosum, commonly known as black-haired hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a dark green color, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium atroepilosum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
