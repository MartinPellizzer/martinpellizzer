Halimodendron argenteum

Halimodendron argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf halimodendron, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Halimodendron argenteum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
