Hosackia neomexicana

Hosackia neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
