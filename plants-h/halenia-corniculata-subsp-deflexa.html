Halenia corniculata subsp. deflexa

Halenia corniculata subsp.

deflexa, commonly known as the flexible halenia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Korean Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink in color.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
