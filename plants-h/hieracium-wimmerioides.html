Hieracium wimmerioides

Hieracium wimmerioides, commonly known as the Wimmer's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to reddish-orange flowers and hairy, basal leaves. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, this species is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, bract-like leaves along its stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
