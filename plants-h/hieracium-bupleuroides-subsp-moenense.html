Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. moenense

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

moenense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
