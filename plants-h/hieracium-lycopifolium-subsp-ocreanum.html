Hieracium lycopifolium subsp. ocreanum

Hieracium lycopifolium subsp.

ocreanum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes.

The subspecies ocreanum is distinguished by its distinctive, inflated, ovoid fruiting structures.
