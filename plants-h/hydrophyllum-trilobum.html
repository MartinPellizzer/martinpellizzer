Hydrophyllum trilobum

Hydrophyllum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are palmately lobed, often with three distinct lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually white or pale green in color.
