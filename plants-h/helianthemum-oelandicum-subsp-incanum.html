Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. incanum

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

incanum, commonly known as the grey thyme or rock thyme, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
