Helianthus herbaceus

Helianthus herbaceus, commonly known as the stiff sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, supporting large, daisy-like yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
