Hyptis velutina var. origanoides

Hyptis velutina var. origanoides, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
