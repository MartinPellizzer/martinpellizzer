Hakea erythroclada

Hakea erythroclada, commonly known as the red-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It produces woody, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Hakea erythroclada is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
