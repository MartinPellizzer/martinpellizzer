Hebeclinium tetragonum

Hebeclinium tetragonum, commonly known as the four-sided hebeclinium, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
