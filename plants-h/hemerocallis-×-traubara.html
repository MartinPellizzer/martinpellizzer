Hemerocallis × traubara

Hemerocallis × traubara, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Hemerocallis genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers that bloom in late summer and last for only one day, typical of the Hemerocallis family. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners. It has strap-like, grassy foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
