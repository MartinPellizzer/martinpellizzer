Helichrysum griffithii

Helichrysum griffithii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces numerous tiny, papery bracts that give it a fluffy appearance, often mistaken for actual flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
