Hieracium aurelianum

Hieracium aurelianum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium aurelianum is an annual or biennial plant that plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
