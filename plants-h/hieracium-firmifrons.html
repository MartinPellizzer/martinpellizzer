Hieracium firmifrons

Hieracium firmifrons, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium firmifrons is known for its resilience and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
