Hybanthus floribundus var. minutifolius

Hybanthus floribundus var. minutifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved hybanthus, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and hairy stems, which help it adapt to humid and shaded environments.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
