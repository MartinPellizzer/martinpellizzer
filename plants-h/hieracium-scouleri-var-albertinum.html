Hieracium scouleri var. albertinum

Hieracium scouleri var. albertinum, commonly known as the Albertine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the stems are usually unbranched.

It is considered a weedy species that can spread aggressively in disturbed soils.
