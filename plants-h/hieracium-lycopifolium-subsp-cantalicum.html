Hieracium lycopifolium subsp. cantalicum

Hieracium lycopifolium subsp.

cantalicum, commonly known as the Cantalicum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, yellowish flower heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky and grassy habitats at high altitudes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
