Hedysarum latibracteatum

Hedysarum latibracteatum, commonly known as the broad-bract pea or the red broad-bract pea, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with stipules that are often broader than the leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils.

Hedysarum latibracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
