Hymenophyllum caulopteron

Hymenophyllum caulopteron, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves, a typical feature of ferns.

The plant's intricate morphology and specialized adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
