Hieracium sagittatum subsp. paganicum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

paganicum, commonly known as the Pagans' dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions, though it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
