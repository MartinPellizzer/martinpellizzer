Helleborine muelleri

Helleborine muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's helleborine, is an evergreen orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive purple spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The flowers bloom from late winter to early spring, adding a unique ornamental value to its natural habitat.
