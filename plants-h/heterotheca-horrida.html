Heterotheca horrida

Heterotheca horrida, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Heterotheca horrida is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
