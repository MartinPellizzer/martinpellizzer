Heliotropium minutiflorum var. rudbaricum

Heliotropium minutiflorum var. rudbaricum, commonly known as the Persian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
