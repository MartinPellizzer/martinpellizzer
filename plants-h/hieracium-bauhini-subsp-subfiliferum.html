Hieracium bauhini subsp. subfiliferum

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

subfiliferum, commonly known as the slender leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
