Helianthus platycephalus

Helianthus platycephalus, commonly known as the flathead sunflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, flat-topped yellow flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus platycephalus is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
