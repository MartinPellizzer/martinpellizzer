Hieracium boreale var. umbrosum

Hieracium boreale var. umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers in a dense cluster at the top of the stem, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
