Hibiscus sturtii var. grandiflorus

Hibiscus sturtii var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Sturt's desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, with a striking contrast between the petals and the hairy, green bracts. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it well-suited for dry environments. Its leaves are typically broad and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
