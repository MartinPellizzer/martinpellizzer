Hugeria erythrocarpa

Hugeria erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tree, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Hugeria erythrocarpa is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Its unique botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
