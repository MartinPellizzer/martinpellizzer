Hieracium pilosella subsp. pachyanthoides

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

pachyanthoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, dark green leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are surrounded by pale green bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence and the shape of its flower heads.
