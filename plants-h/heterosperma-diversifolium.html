Heterosperma diversifolium

Heterosperma diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf water clover, is a aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves that are typically divided into lobes.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. Its leaves are usually green and vary in shape, contributing to its common name.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
