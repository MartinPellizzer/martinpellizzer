Hebecladus ventricosus

Hebecladus ventricosus, commonly known as the rock hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found growing on rocky substrates. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Hebecladus ventricosus is well adapted to harsh environments and is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
