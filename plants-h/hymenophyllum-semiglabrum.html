Hymenophyllum semiglabrum

Hymenophyllum semiglabrum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically covered with a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important component of epiphytic and lithophytic ecosystems.

The species is notable for its ability to retain moisture, which is essential for its survival in humid, low-light conditions.
