Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. hodzae

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

hodzae, commonly known as the Hodza's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while its flowers are arranged in a compact cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
