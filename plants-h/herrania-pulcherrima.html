Herrania pulcherrima

Herrania pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are borne in terminal clusters and are known for their long-lasting display, making it a popular ornamental plant.
