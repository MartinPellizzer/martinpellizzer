Hibiscus malvaviscus

Hibiscus malvaviscus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hollyhocks, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen.

Hibiscus malvaviscus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
