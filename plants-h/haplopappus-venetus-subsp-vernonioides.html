Haplopappus venetus subsp. vernonioides

Haplopappus venetus subsp.

vernonioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas.

The plant features slender, branching stems with small, alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in the western United States and parts of Canada.
