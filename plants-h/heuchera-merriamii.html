Heuchera merriamii

Heuchera merriamii, commonly known as the Merriam's alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically dark green with red or purple veins, and its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white. The plant produces red or orange berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
