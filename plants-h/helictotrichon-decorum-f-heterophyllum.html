Helictotrichon decorum f. heterophyllum

Helictotrichon decorum f. heterophyllum, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple florets arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, often in dry to moist soils.
