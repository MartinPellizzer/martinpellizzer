Hedyotis nigricans var. austrotexana

Hedyotis nigricans var. austrotexana, commonly known as the Australian yellow myrtle, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, dark blue or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
