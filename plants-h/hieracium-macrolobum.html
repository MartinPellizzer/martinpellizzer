Hieracium macrolobum

Hieracium macrolobum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it a rugged appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow and occur in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth habits.
