Hyacinthus battandieri

Hyacinthus battandieri, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring, typically blooming in March or April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its compact growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The Persian hyacinth is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements.
