Hieracium carinthiostiriacum

Hieracium carinthiostiriacum, commonly known as the Carinthian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium carinthiostiriacum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
