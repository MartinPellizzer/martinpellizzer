Hieracium subcaesium subsp. ratisbonense

Hieracium subcaesium subsp.

ratisbonense, commonly known as the Ratisbon hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
