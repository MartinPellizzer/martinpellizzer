Hieracium turbinellum subsp. pseudobracteolatum

Hieracium turbinellum subsp.

pseudobracteolatum, commonly known as the false bracteled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that are usually hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in height.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with each flower having five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are basal and alternate, typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
