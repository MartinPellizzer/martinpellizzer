Hieracium subgermaniciforme

Hieracium subgermaniciforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subgermaniciforme is known for its aggressive growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
