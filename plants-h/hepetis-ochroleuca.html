Hepetis ochroleuca

Hepetis ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, though some varieties may have yellow or white blooms. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
