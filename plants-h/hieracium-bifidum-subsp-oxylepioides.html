Hieracium bifidum subsp. oxylepioides

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

oxylepioides, commonly known as the oxylepioid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
