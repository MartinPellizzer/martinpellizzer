Hypericum hirsutum var. pseudomontanum

Hypericum hirsutum var. pseudomontanum, commonly known as the Alpine St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticulture.
