Hypericum perfoliatum subsp. phitosianum

Hypericum perfoliatum subsp.

phitosianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, bead-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
