Hieracium magyaricum subsp. transgressum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

transgressum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the stems are upright and sometimes branched.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivorous insects.
