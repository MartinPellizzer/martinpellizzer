Hedychium roxburghii var. roxburghii

Hedychium roxburghii var. roxburghii, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Hedychium roxburghii var. roxburghii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
