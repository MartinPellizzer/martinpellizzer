Hieracium lachenalii subsp. pseudacuminatum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

pseudacuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer.

The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and locally distributed species.
