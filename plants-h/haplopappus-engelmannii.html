Haplopappus engelmannii

Haplopappus engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's hairpuff, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
