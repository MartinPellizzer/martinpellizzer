Helianthemum brasiliense f. latifolium

Helianthemum brasiliense f. latifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian rock rose, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features small, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are broader than the typical form, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
