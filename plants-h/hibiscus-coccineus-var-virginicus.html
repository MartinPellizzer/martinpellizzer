Hibiscus coccineus var. virginicus

Hibiscus coccineus var. virginicus, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright red flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun and moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to native gardens and wetland ecosystems.
