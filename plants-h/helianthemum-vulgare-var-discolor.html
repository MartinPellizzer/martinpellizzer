Helianthemum vulgare var. discolor

Helianthemum vulgare var. discolor, commonly known as the variable rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive discoloration in the center. The plant has grey-green foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
