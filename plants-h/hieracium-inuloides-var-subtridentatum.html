Hieracium inuloides var. subtridentatum

Hieracium inuloides var. subtridentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
