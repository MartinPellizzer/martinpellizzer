Hymenoxys depressa

Hymenoxys depressa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features showy orange to red ray flowers that surround a central disk, resembling the blooms of traditional sunflowers. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.

Hymenoxys depressa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
