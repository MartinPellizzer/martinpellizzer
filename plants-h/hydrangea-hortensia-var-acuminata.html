Hydrangea hortensia var. acuminata

Hydrangea hortensia var. acuminata, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy clusters of sterile flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces elongated, cone-shaped inflorescences that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
