Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. serinense

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

serinense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of ray flowers surrounded by disk flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native range.
