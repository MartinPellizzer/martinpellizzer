Hammada negevensis

Hammada negevensis, commonly known as the Negev dwarf aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Negev Desert in Israel.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on tall, slender stems.

This plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
