Halosarcia pergranulata subsp. elongata

Halosarcia pergranulata subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its small, fleshy, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline soils and is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and inland shrubland ecosystems.
