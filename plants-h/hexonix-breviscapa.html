Hexonix breviscapa

Hexonix breviscapa, commonly known as the short-horned bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer. Hexonix breviscapa is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
