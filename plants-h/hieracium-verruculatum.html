Hieracium verruculatum

Hieracium verruculatum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright stems covered in rough, hairy textures, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and the plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium verruculatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
