Hieracium valbusanum

Hieracium valbusanum, commonly known as the Valbusan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium valbusanum is known for its aggressive spreading habit, often forming dense mats that can outcompete other vegetation.
