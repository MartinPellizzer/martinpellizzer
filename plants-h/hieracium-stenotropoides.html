Hieracium stenotropoides

Hieracium stenotropoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium stenotropoides is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an aggressive colonizer in certain environments.
