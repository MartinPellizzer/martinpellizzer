Hieracium onosmoides subsp. cadyense

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

cadyense, commonly known as the Cady's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is often found in specific ecological zones within its native range.
