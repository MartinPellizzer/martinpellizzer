Hippion alpinum

Hippion alpinum, commonly known as the alpine thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Hippion alpinum is often found in alpine meadows and is a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
