Hevea guianensis var. lutea

Hevea guianensis var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow rubber tree, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive yellowish latex sap, which is produced in the milky latex ducts within its stems and leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many plants known for their milky sap.
