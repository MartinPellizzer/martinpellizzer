Hieracium argyrocomum subsp. subulatissimum

Hieracium argyrocomum subsp.

subulatissimum, commonly known as the silver-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, lanceolate leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in summer, typically arranged in a single head on a slender stem. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
