Hieracium glevense

Hieracium glevense, commonly known as the Gleven’s hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm and blooms from June to August.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
