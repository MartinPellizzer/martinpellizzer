Hippeophyllum seidenfadii

Hippeophyllum seidenfadii, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood and smooth, grayish-green foliage. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in inflorescences, while its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
