Helleborus viridis var. atrorubens

Helleborus viridis var. atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are often adorned with red or purple markings. The plant produces bell-shaped, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive purple tinge, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
