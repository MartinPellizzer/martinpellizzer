Humiriastrum villosum

Humiriastrum villosum, commonly known as the silky broomweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators.

Humiriastrum villosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
