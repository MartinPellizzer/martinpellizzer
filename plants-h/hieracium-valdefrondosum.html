Hieracium valdefrondosum

Hieracium valdefrondosum, commonly known as the Valdefrondos hawkweed, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically features yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in areas with well-drained soil.

Hieracium valdefrondosum is known for its low-growing habit and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
