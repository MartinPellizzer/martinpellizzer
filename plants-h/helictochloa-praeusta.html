Helictochloa praeusta

Helictochloa praeusta, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the grass family.
