Hieracium bellidifolium

Hieracium bellidifolium, commonly known as the bellidifolium hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head that produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. It has a hairy stem and leaves with a serrated margin.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
