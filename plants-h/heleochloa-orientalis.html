Heleochloa orientalis

Heleochloa orientalis, commonly known as the oriental hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its fine, soft foliage. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are often clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
