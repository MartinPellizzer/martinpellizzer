Hovea magnibractea

Hovea magnibractea, commonly known as the broad-bract hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, large bracts that enclose the flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Hovea magnibractea is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments.
