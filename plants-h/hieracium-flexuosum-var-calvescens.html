Hieracium flexuosum var. calvescens

Hieracium flexuosum var. calvescens, commonly known as the bentleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are elongated and often curved, giving it the common name "bentleaf.".

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
