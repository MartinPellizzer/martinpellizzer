Hymenophyllum karstenianum

Hymenophyllum karstenianum, commonly known as the Karsten's shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
