Hakea salicifolia subsp. salicifolia

Hakea salicifolia subsp.

salicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish flower spikes that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its woody structure and adaptability to various soil types make it a resilient species in arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
