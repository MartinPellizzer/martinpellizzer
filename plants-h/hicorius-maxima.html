Hicorius maxima

Hicorius maxima, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, featuring vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hicorius maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
