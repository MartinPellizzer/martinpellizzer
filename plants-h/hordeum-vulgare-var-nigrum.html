Hordeum vulgare var. nigrum

Hordeum vulgare var. nigrum, commonly known as black barley, is a variety of the cereal grass species Hordeum vulgare.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grains, which range from black to dark purple, and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in traditional foods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are rich in fiber, protein, and various micronutrients, making it a valuable crop in both agricultural and medicinal contexts.

Black barley is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, contributing to its adaptability and widespread cultivation.
