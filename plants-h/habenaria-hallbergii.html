Habenaria hallbergii

Habenaria hallbergii, commonly known as the Hallberg's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a raceme, which is a type of inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
