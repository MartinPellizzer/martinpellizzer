Helianthemum fasciculatum

Helianthemum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
