Hibiscus malacospermus

Hibiscus malacospermus, commonly known as the narrowleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus malacospermus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
