Heterotheca villosa var. nana

Heterotheca villosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf hairy goldenweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a desirable plant for native gardens.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
