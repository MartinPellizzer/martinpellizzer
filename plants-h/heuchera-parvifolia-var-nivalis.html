Heuchera parvifolia var. nivalis

Heuchera parvifolia var. nivalis, commonly known as the snow heuchera, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage that ranges from silver-white to deep red, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and its ability to add color and texture to woodland landscapes.
