Hymenophyllum splendidum

Hymenophyllum splendidum, commonly known as the shining hymenophyllum, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually less than 10 centimeters long and exhibit a strikingly glossy appearance.

Hymenophyllum splendidum is notable for its intricate morphology and is often used in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
