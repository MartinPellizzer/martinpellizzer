Hieracium gymnocephalum subsp. laxipellitum

Hieracium gymnocephalum subsp.

laxipellitum, commonly known as the loose-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common component of temperate grassland ecosystems.
