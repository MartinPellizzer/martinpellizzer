Hygrophila illinoiensis

Hygrophila illinoiensis, commonly known as Illinois waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the United States.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 inch long. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters near the water's surface.

Hygrophila illinoiensis plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
