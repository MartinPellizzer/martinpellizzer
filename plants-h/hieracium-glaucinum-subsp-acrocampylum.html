Hieracium glaucinum subsp. acrocampylum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

acrocampylum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides.

The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is often found in moist to moderately dry soils.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive blue-green coloration and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
