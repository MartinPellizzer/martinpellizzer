Humboltia convoluta

Humboltia convoluta, commonly known as the twisted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or red throat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Humboltia convoluta is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in areas with high salinity and strong winds.
