Hypolytrum tepuianum

Hypolytrum tepuianum, commonly known as the tepuian marsh orchid, is a rare and endemic flowering plant found in the tepuis of Venezuela.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences with brightly colored flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in rocky or clay soils. It has a short, stocky rhizome and produces small, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
