Hieracium obscuratum subsp. sampeyrense

Hieracium obscuratum subsp.

sampeyrense, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, rough-textured leaves and yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation across suitable environments.
