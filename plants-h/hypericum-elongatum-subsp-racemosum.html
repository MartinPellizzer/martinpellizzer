Hypericum elongatum subsp. racemosum

Hypericum elongatum subsp.

racemosum, commonly known as the erect St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
