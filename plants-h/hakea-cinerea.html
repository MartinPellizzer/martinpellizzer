Hakea cinerea

Hakea cinerea, commonly known as the silver hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking spikes of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its unique adaptations, such as its ability to store water in its woody stems, make it well-suited to arid environments.

Hakea cinerea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
