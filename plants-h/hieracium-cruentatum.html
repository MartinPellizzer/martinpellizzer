Hieracium cruentatum

Hieracium cruentatum, commonly known as the bloody hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and sometimes reddish in color.

Hieracium cruentatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
