Heliotropium europaeum subsp. tenuiflorum

Heliotropium europaeum subsp.

tenuiflorum, commonly known as slenderflower heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a low-growing herb with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, with a distinctive scent reminiscent of vanilla. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
