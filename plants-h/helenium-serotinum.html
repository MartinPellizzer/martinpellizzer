Helenium serotinum

Helenium serotinum, commonly known as late helenium, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red-orange ray flowers and yellow disk flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Helenium serotinum is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
