Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. megalophyllum

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

megalophyllum, commonly known as the giant-eared hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive auriculate base.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its subspecies status highlights its unique morphological features compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
