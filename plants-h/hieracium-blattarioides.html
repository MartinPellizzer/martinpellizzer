Hieracium blattarioides

Hieracium blattarioides, commonly known as the blattarian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and elongated, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs.

Hieracium blattarioides is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
