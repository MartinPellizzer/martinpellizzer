Hibiscus chrysanthus

Hibiscus chrysanthus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus chrysanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
