Hygrophila angustifolia var. phlomoides

Hygrophila angustifolia var. phlomoides, commonly known as narrowleaf hygrophila, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing light green to yellowish blooms.

It is valued in aquatic gardening for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
