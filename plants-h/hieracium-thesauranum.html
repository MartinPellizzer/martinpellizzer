Hieracium thesauranum

Hieracium thesauranum, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow ray florets and green disk florets attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium thesauranum is often found in moist environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
