Hibiscus domingensis var. striatus

Hibiscus domingensis var. striatus, commonly known as the striated hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a rough texture and prominent veins. This plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
