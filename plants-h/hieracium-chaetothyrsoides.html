Hieracium chaetothyrsoides

Hieracium chaetothyrsoides, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves, which help protect it from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitats.
