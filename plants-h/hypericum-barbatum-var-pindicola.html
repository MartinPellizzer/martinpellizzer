Hypericum barbatum var. pindicola

Hypericum barbatum var. pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to the Pindus Mountains in Greece.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and traditional herbal uses.
