Hieracium barrioluciense

Hieracium barrioluciense, commonly known as the Barriolucian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and has basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
