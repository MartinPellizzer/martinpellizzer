Hakea suaveolens

Hakea suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in winter. The flowers are typically pale yellow to cream in color and emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and long-lasting blooms.
