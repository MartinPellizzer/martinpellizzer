Hypericum inodorum var. intermedium

Hypericum inodorum var. intermedium, commonly known as the Middle Eastern St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it distinct from other Hypericum species, particularly in its floral structure and growth habit.
