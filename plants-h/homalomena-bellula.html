Homalomena bellula

Homalomena bellula, commonly known as the golden bamboo fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its elegant, feather-like fronds that resemble bamboo. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with numerous roots and produces small, greenish-white spadices. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a shade-loving plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
