Hieracium magyaricum subsp. branae

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

branae, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.
