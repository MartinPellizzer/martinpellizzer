Hieracium salarduense

Hieracium salarduense, commonly known as the Salardu hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium salarduense is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh habitats.
