Hemizonia pungens subsp. septentrionalis

Hemizonia pungens subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern prickly honeysuckle, is a species native to the northern regions of North America. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, spiny stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas with partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
