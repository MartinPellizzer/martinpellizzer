Hippeastrum arechavaletae

Hippeastrum arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, specifically Ecuador.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that emerge from a bulb-like structure and are often used in horticultural displays. It is relatively easy to cultivate, requiring well-drained soil and moderate watering.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular among gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
