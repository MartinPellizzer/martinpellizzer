Hieracium leptophyton subsp. pseudofiliferum

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

pseudofiliferum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and often bear a dense covering of fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by a series of bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.
