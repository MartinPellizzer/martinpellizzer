Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudosilvularum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudosilvularum, commonly known as the false wood hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and growth habit.
