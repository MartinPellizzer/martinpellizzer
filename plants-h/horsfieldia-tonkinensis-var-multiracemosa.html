Horsfieldia tonkinensis var. multiracemosa

Horsfieldia tonkinensis var. multiracemosa, commonly known as the multiracemose orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered inflorescence, which produces multiple racemes of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
