Hieracium vogesiacum subsp. attractum

Hieracium vogesiacum subsp.

attractum, commonly known as the attractive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, including parts of Germany and the Czech Republic.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
