Haematocarpus incusus

Haematocarpus incusus, commonly known as the hairy burr grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces distinctive, hairy, spiky seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

The species is also recognized for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
