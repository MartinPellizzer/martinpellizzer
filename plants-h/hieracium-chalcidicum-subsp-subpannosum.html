Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. subpannosum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp.

subpannosum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

It is characterized by its compact growth form and is often found in high-altitude environments.
