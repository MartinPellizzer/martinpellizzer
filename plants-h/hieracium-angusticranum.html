Hieracium angusticranum

Hieracium angusticranum, commonly known as narrow-crown hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium angusticranum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
