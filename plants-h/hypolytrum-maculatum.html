Hypolytrum maculatum

Hypolytrum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted lily or spotted grass-of-Parnassus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its flowers are often adorned with reddish or purple markings, giving it its common name.

Hypolytrum maculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
