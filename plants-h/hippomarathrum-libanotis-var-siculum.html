Hippomarathrum libanotis var. siculum

Hippomarathrum libanotis var. siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a distinctive greyish-blue hue, and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
