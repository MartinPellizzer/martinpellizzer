Helichrysum stenopterum

Helichrysum stenopterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its name derives from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chryos" meaning gold, referring to its sunny appearance and golden flowers.
