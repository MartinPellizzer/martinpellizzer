Hymenophyllum suprapaleaceum

Hymenophyllum suprapaleaceum, commonly known as the broad-leafed spikemoss, is a member of the Hymenophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage.

It is a cryptogamic plant, meaning it reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, overlapping leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, with a distinctive midrib. It is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species in the study of bryophyte ecology.
