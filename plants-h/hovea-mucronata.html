Hovea mucronata

Hovea mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hovea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive narrow shape.

Hovea mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
