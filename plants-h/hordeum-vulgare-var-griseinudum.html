Hordeum vulgare var. griseinudum

Hordeum vulgare var. griseinudum, commonly known as gray beard barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its distinctive grayish pubescence on the lemma and palea.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improved yield and stress tolerance.
