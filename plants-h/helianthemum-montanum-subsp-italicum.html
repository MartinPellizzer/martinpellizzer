Helianthemum montanum subsp. italicum

Helianthemum montanum subsp.

italicum, commonly known as the Italian rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
