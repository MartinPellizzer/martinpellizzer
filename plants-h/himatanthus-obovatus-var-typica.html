Himatanthus obovatus var. typica

Himatanthus obovatus var. typica, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, obovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
