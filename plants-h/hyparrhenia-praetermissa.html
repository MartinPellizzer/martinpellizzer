Hyparrhenia praetermissa

Hyparrhenia praetermissa, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia praetermissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
