Hieracium viburgense

Hieracium viburgense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

The species is often found in open habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
