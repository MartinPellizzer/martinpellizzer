Hoffmannia fimbrianthera

Hoffmannia fimbrianthera, commonly known as the lace-veined orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, fimbriated (fringed) anthers, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a complex structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Hoffmannia fimbrianthera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
