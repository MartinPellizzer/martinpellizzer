Hieracium × amphigenoides

Hieracium × amphigenoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the genus Hieracium, which belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically bloom in clusters and are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant is perennial and often forms dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped, and its leaves are often glandular, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

This species is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, which contributes to its widespread distribution.
