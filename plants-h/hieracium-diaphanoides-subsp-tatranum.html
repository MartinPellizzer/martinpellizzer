Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. tatranum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

tatranum, commonly known as the Tatra hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Tatra Mountains in Central Europe. It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
