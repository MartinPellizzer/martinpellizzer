Hieracium saxorum

Hieracium saxorum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
