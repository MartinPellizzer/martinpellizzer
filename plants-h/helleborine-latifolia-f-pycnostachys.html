Helleborine latifolia f. pycnostachys

Helleborine latifolia f. pycnostachys, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
