Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. kiderense

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

kiderense, commonly known as the Kideren hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
