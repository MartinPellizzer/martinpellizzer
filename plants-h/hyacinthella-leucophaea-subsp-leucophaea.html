Hyacinthella leucophaea subsp. leucophaea

Hyacinthella leucophaea subsp.

leucophaea, commonly known as the white hyacinthella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette. This species is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, typically found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, making it a notable plant in its native habitat.
