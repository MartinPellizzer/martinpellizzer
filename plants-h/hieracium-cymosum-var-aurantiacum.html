Hieracium cymosum var. aurantiacum

Hieracium cymosum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, often with a reddish or orange tinge.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
