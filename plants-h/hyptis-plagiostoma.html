Hyptis plagiostoma

Hyptis plagiostoma, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its foliage and flowers are often utilized in herbal teas and as a source of essential oils.
