Hieracium dentatum subsp. gymnoglaucum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

gymnoglaucum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its most distinctive characteristic is the dense, white or pale blue downy covering on the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
