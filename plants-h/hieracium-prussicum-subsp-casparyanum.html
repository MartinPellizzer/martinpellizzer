Hieracium prussicum subsp. casparyanum

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

casparyanum, commonly known as the Casparyan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from June to August.

The plant has basal and cauline leaves that are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
