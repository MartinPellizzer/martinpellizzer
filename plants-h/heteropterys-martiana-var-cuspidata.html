Heteropterys martiana var. cuspidata

Heteropterys martiana var. cuspidata, commonly known as the cuspidate heteropterys, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
