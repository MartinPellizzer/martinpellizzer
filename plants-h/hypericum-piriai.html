Hypericum piriai

Hypericum piriai, commonly known as the yellow St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in small, glandular dots, a characteristic feature of the Hypericum genus.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
