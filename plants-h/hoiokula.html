Hoiokula

Hoiokula, commonly known as the Hawaiian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Hawaii.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that are edible but often bitter. Hoiokula thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest habitats.

Its unique blooming pattern and cultural significance make it an important part of Hawaiian botanical heritage.
