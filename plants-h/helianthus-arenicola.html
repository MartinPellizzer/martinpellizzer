Helianthus arenicola

Helianthus arenicola, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

Helianthus arenicola is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
