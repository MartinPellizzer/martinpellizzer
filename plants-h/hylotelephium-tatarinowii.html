Hylotelephium tatarinowii

Hylotelephium tatarinowii, commonly known as the Armenian carpet, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

Hylotelephium tatarinowii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
