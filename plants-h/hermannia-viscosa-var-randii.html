Hermannia viscosa var. randii

Hermannia viscosa var. randii, commonly known as the sticky hermannia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a sticky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
