Hymenophyllum mathewsii

Hymenophyllum mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are often less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a thin, membranous cover.

The species is noted for its intricate leaf venation and is often used in botanical studies for its unique morphological features.
