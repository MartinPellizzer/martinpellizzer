Hieracium sulgeri

Hieracium sulgeri, commonly known as the Sulger's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in the form of composite heads. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed habitats.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
