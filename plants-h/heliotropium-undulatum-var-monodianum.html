Heliotropium undulatum var. monodianum

Heliotropium undulatum var. monodianum, commonly known as the one-flowered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
