Hieracium pernobile

Hieracium pernobile, commonly known as the greater hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Hieracium pernobile is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
