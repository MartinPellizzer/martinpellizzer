Hieracium fridtzii

Hieracium fridtzii, commonly known as the Fritz's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow ray florets and tubular disk florets. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is typically found in regions with a temperate climate and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
