Haplopappus gramineus

Haplopappus gramineus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces achenes with pappus hairs for wind dispersal.

Haplopappus gramineus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its ecological importance in native plant communities.
