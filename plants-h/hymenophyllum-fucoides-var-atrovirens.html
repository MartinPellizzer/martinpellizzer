Hymenophyllum fucoides var. atrovirens

Hymenophyllum fucoides var. atrovirens, commonly known as the black moss, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance and intricate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually dark green with a glossy surface and possess a unique structure that allows for efficient water retention.

The spore-producing structures, called soridia, are borne on the undersides of the fronds and contribute to its reproductive strategy.
