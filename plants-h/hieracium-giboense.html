Hieracium giboense

Hieracium giboense, commonly known as the Giboense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and produce small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.
