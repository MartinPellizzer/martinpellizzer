Hypericum humbertii

Hypericum humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Hypericum humbertii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
