Hieracium brachycomum subsp. fissum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

fissum, commonly known as the fissured hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a distinctive, often fissured or lobed base.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
