Hieracium neoplatyphyllum subsp. trimontanum

Hieracium neoplatyphyllum subsp.

trimontanum, commonly known as the Three-lobed Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has finely divided, lanceolate leaves and produces numerous small, disc-like flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
