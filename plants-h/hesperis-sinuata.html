Hesperis sinuata

Hesperis sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, making it a valuable addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
