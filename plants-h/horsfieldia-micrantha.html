Horsfieldia micrantha

Horsfieldia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often white or pale yellow, and bloom during the rainy season.

Horsfieldia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
