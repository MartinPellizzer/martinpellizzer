Hyptis glutinosa

Hyptis glutinosa, commonly known as sticky hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis glutinosa is known for its sticky secretions, which help protect it from herbivores and promote moisture retention.
