Hylotelephium argutum subsp. carpaticum

Hylotelephium argutum subsp.

carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in rocky, arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
