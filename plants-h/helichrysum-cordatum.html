Helichrysum cordatum

Helichrysum cordatum, commonly known as the heart's-ease or golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Helichrysum cordatum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
