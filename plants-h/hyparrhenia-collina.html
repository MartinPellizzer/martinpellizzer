Hyparrhenia collina

Hyparrhenia collina, commonly known as the African grass or narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
