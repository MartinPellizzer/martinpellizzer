Handroanthus chrysanthus subsp. meridionalis

Handroanthus chrysanthus subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in the spring and attract a variety of pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
