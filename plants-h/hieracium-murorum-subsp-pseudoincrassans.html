Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudoincrassans

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudoincrassans, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish or brownish coloration on the underside.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
