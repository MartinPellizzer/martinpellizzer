Hieracium laurentinum

Hieracium laurentinum, commonly known as the Laurentian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a cluster of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

It is considered a perennial weed due to its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
