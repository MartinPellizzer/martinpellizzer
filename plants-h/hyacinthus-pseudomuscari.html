Hyacinthus pseudomuscari

Hyacinthus pseudomuscari, commonly known as the false muscari, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing shades of blue, violet, and white. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Hyacinthus pseudomuscari is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
