Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. jacquiniiforme

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

jacquiniiforme, commonly known as the Jacquiniiforme Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
