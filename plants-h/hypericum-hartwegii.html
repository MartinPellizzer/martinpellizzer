Hypericum hartwegii

Hypericum hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces red or orange seed pods that split open to release seeds.

It is part of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
