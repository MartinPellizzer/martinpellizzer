Herpestis matourea

Herpestis matourea, commonly known as the Matourea dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks true leaves and roots.

It relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, attaching itself through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops if left unchecked.
