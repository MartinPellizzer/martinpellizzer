Hoya bifunda subsp. integra

Hoya bifunda subsp.

integra, commonly known as the Wandering Glory, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically ovate to heart-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among houseplant enthusiasts due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
