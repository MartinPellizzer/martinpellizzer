Hieracium aureiceps

Hieracium aureiceps, commonly known as the golden-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of many small florets, with the central disc being particularly vivid.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
