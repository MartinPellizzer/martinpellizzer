Hyptis laurifolia

Hyptis laurifolia, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
