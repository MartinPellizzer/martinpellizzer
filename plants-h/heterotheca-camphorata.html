Heterotheca camphorata

Heterotheca camphorata, commonly known as camphorweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Camphorweed is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains compounds similar to camphor.
