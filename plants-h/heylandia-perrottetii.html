Heylandia perrottetii

Heylandia perrottetii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble bells, blooming in late autumn to early winter. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to the vibrant floral display.

Heylandia perrottetii is well-suited to coastal climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
