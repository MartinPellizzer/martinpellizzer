Hieracium leiosoma subsp. velanum

Hieracium leiosoma subsp.

velanum, commonly known as the velvety hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in a single head, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
