Hyptis apertiflora

Hyptis apertiflora, commonly known as the open-flowered hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hyptis apertiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
