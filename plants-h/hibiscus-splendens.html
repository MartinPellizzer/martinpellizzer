Hibiscus splendens

Hibiscus splendens, commonly known as the scarlet rosemallow, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus splendens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
