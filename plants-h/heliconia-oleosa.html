Heliconia oleosa

Heliconia oleosa, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and has a robust, upright stem. Its inflorescence consists of a spiral of brightly colored, modified leaves that enclose the actual flowers.

Heliconia oleosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
