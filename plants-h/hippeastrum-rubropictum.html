Hippeastrum rubropictum

Hippeastrum rubropictum, commonly known as the Red Painted Glory Lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in shades of red and orange. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum rubropictum is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical climate.
