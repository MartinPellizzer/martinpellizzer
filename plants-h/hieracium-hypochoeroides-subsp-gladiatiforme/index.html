Hieracium Hypochoeroides Subsp. Gladiatiforme

Field hawkweed, scientifically known as Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. gladiatiforme, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow daisy-like flowers. This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched. Despite its beauty, it is considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
