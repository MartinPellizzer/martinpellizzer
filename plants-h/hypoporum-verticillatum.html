Hypoporum verticillatum

Hypoporum verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Hypoporum verticillatum is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
