Homalanthus megaphyllus

Homalanthus megaphyllus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky, red or orange fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
