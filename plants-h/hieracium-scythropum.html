Hieracium scythropum

Hieracium scythropum, commonly known as the Scythian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium scythropum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
