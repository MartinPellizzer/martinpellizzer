Holothrix praecox

Holothrix praecox, commonly known as the early holothrix, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Holothrix praecox is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
