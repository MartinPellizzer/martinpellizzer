Hieracium pilosella subsp. bruennense

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

bruennense, commonly known as the Bruennens hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a single, terminal capitulum.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely pubescent surfaces and distinctive floral structure.
