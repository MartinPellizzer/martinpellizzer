Hanceola suffruticosa

Hanceola suffruticosa, commonly known as the rock nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hanceola suffruticosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils, growing in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
