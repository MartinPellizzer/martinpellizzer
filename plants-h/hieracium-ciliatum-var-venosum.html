Hieracium ciliatum var. venosum

Hieracium ciliatum var. venosum, commonly known as the veiny hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread rapidly through its rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are often covered with fine, vein-like hairs, giving it its common name.

It is an annual or biennial species that produces numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
