Hydrochorea pedicellaris

Hydrochorea pedicellaris, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's leaves are thin and ribbon-like, allowing it to float or remain submerged depending on water conditions. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and fish.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, enhancing its ability to spread in wetland habitats.
