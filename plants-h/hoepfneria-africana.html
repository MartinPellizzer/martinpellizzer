Hoepfneria africana

Hoepfneria africana, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Hoepfneria africana thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.
