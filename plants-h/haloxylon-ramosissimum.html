Haloxylon ramosissimum

Haloxylon ramosissimum, commonly known as the desert date or tamarisk tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East, North Africa, and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in saline soils.

Haloxylon ramosissimum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
