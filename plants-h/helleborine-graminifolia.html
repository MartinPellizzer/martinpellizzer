Helleborine graminifolia

Helleborine graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
