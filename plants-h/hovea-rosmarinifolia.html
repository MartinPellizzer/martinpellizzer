Hovea rosmarinifolia

Hovea rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Hovea rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fragrance.
