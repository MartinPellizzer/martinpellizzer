Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. stoechadum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

stoechadum, commonly known as the Scottish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and form a single head. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in various habitats.
