Hyptis emoryi var. amplifolia

Hyptis emoryi var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable resource for pollinators.
