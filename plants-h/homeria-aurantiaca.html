Homeria aurantiaca

Homeria aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm and prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
