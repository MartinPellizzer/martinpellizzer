Habenaria affinis

Habenaria affinis, commonly known as the common spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to various regions in Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces a dense cluster of small, oval-shaped pseudobulbs.

The flowers are characterized by their intricate, spider-like appearance, which gives the species its common name.
