Helichrysum rupestre subsp. pendulum

Helichrysum rupestre subsp.

pendulum, commonly known as the hanging goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color even when dried.
