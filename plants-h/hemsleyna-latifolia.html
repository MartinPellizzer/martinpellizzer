Hemsleyna latifolia

Hemsleyna latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hemsleyna, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Native to temperate regions of Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The plant's growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact and bushy form.
