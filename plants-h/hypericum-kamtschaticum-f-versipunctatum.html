Hypericum kamtschaticum f. versipunctatum

Hypericum kamtschaticum f. versipunctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a botanical variant of the St. John's wort species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with distinctive black spots, which are more pronounced in this form. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, while its stems are woody and somewhat branched.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
