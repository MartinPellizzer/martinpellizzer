Hippeastrum proctorii

Hippeastrum proctorii, commonly known as the Proctor's amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears several flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, showy blooms.
