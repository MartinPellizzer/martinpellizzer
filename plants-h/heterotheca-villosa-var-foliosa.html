Heterotheca villosa var. foliosa

Heterotheca villosa var. foliosa, commonly known as the hairy golden aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, often with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the fall, which are clustered in composite heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant for pollinators.
