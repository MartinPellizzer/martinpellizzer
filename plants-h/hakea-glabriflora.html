Hakea glabriflora

Hakea glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and distinctive, bottle-shaped flower spikes. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Hakea glabriflora is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
