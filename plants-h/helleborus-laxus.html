Helleborus laxus

Helleborus laxus, commonly known as the loose hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often notched or lobed. The plant produces clusters of dark purple to black flowers with a distinctive, hooded shape. It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is known for its winter blooming habit.

Helleborus laxus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
