Hieracium grovesianum subsp. varellanum

Hieracium grovesianum subsp.

varellanum, commonly known as the Varellanum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It produces yellow or reddish-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and slightly hairy leaves compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
