Hartogia fastigiata

Hartogia fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hartogia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Hartogia fastigiata is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
