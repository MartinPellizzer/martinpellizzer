Haronga revoluta

Haronga revoluta, commonly known as the red-stemmed haronga, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Haronga revoluta is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, where it can tolerate salt spray.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
