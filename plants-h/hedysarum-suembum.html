Hedysarum suembum

Hedysarum suembum, commonly known as the Swedish milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Hedysarum suembum is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
