Hypochaeris spathulata

Hypochaeris spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Hypochaeris spathulata is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
