Hieracium susendalicum

Hieracium susendalicum, commonly known as the Southern European hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
