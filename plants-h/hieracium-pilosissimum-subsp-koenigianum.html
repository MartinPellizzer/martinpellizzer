Hieracium pilosissimum subsp. koenigianum

Hieracium pilosissimum subsp.

koenigianum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
