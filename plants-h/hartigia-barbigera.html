Hartigia barbigera

Hartigia barbigera, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with hairy foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hartigia barbigera thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
