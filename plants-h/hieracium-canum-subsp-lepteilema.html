Hieracium canum subsp. lepteilema

Hieracium canum subsp.

lepteilema, commonly known as the slenderleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are followed by small, oval seeds.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
