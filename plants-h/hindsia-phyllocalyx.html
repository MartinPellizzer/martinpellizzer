Hindsia phyllocalyx

Hindsia phyllocalyx, commonly known as the red-veined hindsia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
