Hieracium bifidum subsp. kemense

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

kemense, commonly known as the Kemenskaya hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its subspecific name, *kemense*, refers to its geographic origin in the Kemin region of Kazakhstan.
