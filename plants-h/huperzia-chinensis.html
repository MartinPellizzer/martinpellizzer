Huperzia chinensis

Huperzia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen fern-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and spore-bearing structures called strobila. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Huperzia chinensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
