Hariota grandiflora

Hariota grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered hariota, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
