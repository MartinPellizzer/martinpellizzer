Helichrysum mechowianum

Helichrysum mechowianum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a long-lasting appearance, making it popular in dried floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
