Hodgsonia macrocarpa var. capniocarpa

Hodgsonia macrocarpa var. capniocarpa, commonly known as the Himalayan rosebay, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a glossy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
