Hieracium obscuricapitatum subsp. obscuricapitatum

Hieracium obscuricapitatum subsp.

obscuricapitatum, commonly known as the dark-capped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the flowers are yellow with a dark center, giving it its common name.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
