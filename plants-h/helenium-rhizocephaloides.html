Helenium rhizocephaloides

Helenium rhizocephaloides, commonly known as the redroot goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Helenium rhizocephaloides is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to support local pollinators.
