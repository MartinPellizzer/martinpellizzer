Hieracium ignatianum

Hieracium ignatianum, commonly known as the Ignatius' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as seeds.

Hieracium ignatianum is often found in disturbed habitats and is relatively hardy, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
