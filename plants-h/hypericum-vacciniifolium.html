Hypericum vacciniifolium

Hypericum vacciniifolium, commonly known as the redroot St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and alternate, oval to elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. Its roots are reddish in color, giving it its common name, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

It is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
