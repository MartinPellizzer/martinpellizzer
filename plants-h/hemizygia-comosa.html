Hemizygia comosa

Hemizygia comosa, commonly known as the woolly hemizygia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Hemizygia comosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
