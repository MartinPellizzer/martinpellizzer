Hippocastanum asiaticum

Hippocastanum asiaticum, commonly known as the Persian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, woody capsules that contain glossy, horse chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with prominent lenticels, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various parts of the world for its aesthetic qualities.
