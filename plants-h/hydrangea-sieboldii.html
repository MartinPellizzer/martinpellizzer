Hydrangea sieboldii

Hydrangea sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns red in the fall.

Hydrangea sieboldii prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
