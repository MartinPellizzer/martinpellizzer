Halogeton tibeticus

Halogeton tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan saltbush, is a halophytic plant native to the arid regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Halogeton tibeticus plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
