Heliconia ramonensis var. lanuginosa

Heliconia ramonensis var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in a soft, velvety texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
