Habrothamnus tomentosus

Habrothamnus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly broom.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional herbal practices.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
