Hieracium spathaceum

Hieracium spathaceum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium spathaceum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
