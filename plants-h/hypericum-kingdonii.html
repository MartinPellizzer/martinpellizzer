Hypericum kingdonii

Hypericum kingdonii, commonly known as the Kingdon's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Hypericum kingdonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
