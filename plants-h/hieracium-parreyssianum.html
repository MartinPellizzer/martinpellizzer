Hieracium parreyssianum

Hieracium parreyssianum, commonly known as the Parreyssian Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
