Harveya vestita

Harveya vestita, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the Andean region of South America, this plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically thick and covered in a fine, soft pubescence, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Harveya vestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
