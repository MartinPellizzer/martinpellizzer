Hyacinthus orientalis subsp. orientalis

Hyacinthus orientalis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the common hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors including purple, blue, white, and pink.

The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves and has a short stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The bulbs are perennial, allowing the plant to return year after year in suitable climates.
