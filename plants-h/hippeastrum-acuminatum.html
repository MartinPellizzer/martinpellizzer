Hippeastrum acuminatum

Hippeastrum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears clusters of blooms, often in late spring or early summer. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements contribute to its popularity in both cultivated and natural settings.
