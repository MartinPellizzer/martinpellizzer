Hieracium spinalense

Hieracium spinalense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium spinalense is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
