Hibbertia australis

Hibbertia australis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive texture. Hibbertia australis is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

It is an important species in the ecological makeup of its native habitats.
