Helichrysum semipapposum

Helichrysum semipapposum, commonly known as the Cape goldflame, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small flames. The plant produces small, tubular flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum semipapposum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
