Hieracium subsimile

Hieracium subsimile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often thriving in disturbed soils and meadows.

Hieracium subsimile is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
