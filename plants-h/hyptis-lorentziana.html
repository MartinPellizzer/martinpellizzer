Hyptis lorentziana

Hyptis lorentziana, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Hyptis lorentziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
