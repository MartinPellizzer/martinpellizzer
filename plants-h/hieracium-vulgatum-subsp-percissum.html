Hieracium vulgatum subsp. percissum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

percissum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disc shape.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy surface, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive weed in various regions.
