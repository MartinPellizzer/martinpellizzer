Hieracium bifidum subsp. perlepidiforme

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

perlepidiforme, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
