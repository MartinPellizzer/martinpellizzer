Hieracium praecox subsp. runcinatolobatum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

runcinatolobatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by bristly, oval-shaped seed heads. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its subspecies name, *runcinatolobatum*, refers to the lobed and often serrated shape of its leaves.
