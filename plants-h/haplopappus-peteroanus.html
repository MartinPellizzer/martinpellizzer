Haplopappus peteroanus

Haplopappus peteroanus, commonly known as the narrow-pappus sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use mechanisms.
