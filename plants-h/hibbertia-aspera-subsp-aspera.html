Hibbertia aspera subsp. aspera

Hibbertia aspera subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough hibbertia, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The plant features wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in spring, are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Hibbertia aspera subsp.

aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
