Hieracium tenuiflorum subsp. sebini

Hieracium tenuiflorum subsp.

sebini, commonly known as the narrow-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
