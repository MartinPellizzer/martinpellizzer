Hieracium umbellatum subsp. eriophorum

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

eriophorum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The subspecies eriophorum is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other subspecies of Hieracium umbellatum.
