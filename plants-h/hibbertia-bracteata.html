Hibbertia bracteata

Hibbertia bracteata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Hibbertia bracteata thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often found in coastal and sandy areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
