Hypocalymma magnificum

Hypocalymma magnificum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble small wattles. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The golden wattle is also the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural beauty and resilience.
