Hieracium murorum subsp. streptobasis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

streptobasis, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The plant is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its ability to thrive in various conditions.
