Hoplophytum nudicaule

Hoplophytum nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hoplophytum nudicaule is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
