Hieracium bifidum subsp. maurolepium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

maurolepium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red composite flowers and basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions of Europe and parts of North America.
