Hedysarum echinocarpum

Hedysarum echinocarpum, commonly known as the spiked caraway or hedge garlic, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Hedysarum echinocarpum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
