Hedysarum varium subsp. pluriphylla

Hedysarum varium subsp.

pluriphylla, commonly known as the many-leafed sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats. Hedysarum varium subsp.

pluriphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
