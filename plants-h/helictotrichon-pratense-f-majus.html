Helictotrichon pratense f. majus

Helictotrichon pratense f. majus, commonly known as the greater bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
