Hieracium ptilopogon

Hieracium ptilopogon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium ptilopogon is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
