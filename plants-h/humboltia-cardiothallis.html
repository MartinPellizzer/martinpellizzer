Humboltia cardiothallis

Humboltia cardiothallis, commonly known as the heartleaf hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming throughout the warm seasons. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Humboltia cardiothallis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
