Haplopappus hirtellus var. hirsutus

Haplopappus hirtellus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and linear, and the inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white bracts.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
