Helianthus littoralis

Helianthus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along shorelines. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets that resemble the sunflower genus. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus littoralis is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
