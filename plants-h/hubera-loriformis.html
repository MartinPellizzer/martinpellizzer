Hubera loriformis

Hubera loriformis, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle purple tinge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hubera loriformis is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
