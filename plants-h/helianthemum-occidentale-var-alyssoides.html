Helianthemum occidentale var. alyssoides

Helianthemum occidentale var. alyssoides, commonly known as the western rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hair. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Crassulaceae family, sharing similar morphological and ecological traits.
