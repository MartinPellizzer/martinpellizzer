Hieracium coloriscapum subsp. coloriscapum

Hieracium coloriscapum subsp.

coloriscapum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from white to purple.

The subspecies is distinguished by its relatively compact inflorescence and the presence of glandular dots on the leaves.
