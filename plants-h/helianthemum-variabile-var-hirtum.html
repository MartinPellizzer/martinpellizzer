Helianthemum variabile var. hirtum

Helianthemum variabile var. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, gray-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
