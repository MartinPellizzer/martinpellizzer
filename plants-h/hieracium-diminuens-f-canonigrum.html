Hieracium diminuens f. canonigrum

Hieracium diminuens f. canonigrum, commonly known as the Canary Islands hawkweed, is a botanical variant of the hawkweed genus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its low-growing habit and yellow flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to the specific environmental conditions of its native habitat.
