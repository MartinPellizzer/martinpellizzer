Hemistylus velutina

Hemistylus velutina, commonly known as the velvet-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has velvety, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pale pink blossoms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hemistylus velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
