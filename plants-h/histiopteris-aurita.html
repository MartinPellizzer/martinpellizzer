Histiopteris aurita

Histiopteris aurita, commonly known as the golden holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces spores in clusters on the underside of its leaves, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
