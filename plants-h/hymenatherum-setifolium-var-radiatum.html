Hymenatherum setifolium var. radiatum

Hymenatherum setifolium var. radiatum, commonly known as the Radiate Scented Glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are known for their subtle, sweet fragrance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
