Helianthus truxillensis

Helianthus truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillensis sunflower, is a species native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba.

It is a herbaceous plant with robust, upright stems and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. Its flowers typically bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus truxillensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
