Hymenocallis kimballiae

Hymenocallis kimballiae, commonly known as the Kimball's spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the tepals, resembling spider webs. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and ephemeral blooming cycle.
