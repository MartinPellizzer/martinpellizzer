Hopea multiflora

Hopea multiflora, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hopea multiflora plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native regions, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
