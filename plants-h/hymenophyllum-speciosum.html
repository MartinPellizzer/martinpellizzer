Hymenophyllum speciosum

Hymenophyllum speciosum, commonly known as the fairy moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, translucent scales. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Hymenophyllum speciosum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
