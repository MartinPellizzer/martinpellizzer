Hyssopus officinalis var. stenothix

Hyssopus officinalis var. stenothix, commonly known as narrow-leaved hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
