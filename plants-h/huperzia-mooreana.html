Huperzia mooreana

Huperzia mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has whorled leaves that are lanceolate in shape and often have a waxy coating. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

Huperzia mooreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
