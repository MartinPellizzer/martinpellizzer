Hemitelia grandifolia var. normalis

Hemitelia grandifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the large-leaved hemitelia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
