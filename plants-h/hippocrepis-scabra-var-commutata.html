Hippocrepis scabra var. commutata

Hippocrepis scabra var. commutata, commonly known as the rough helmet orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
