Hippeastrum kermesinum

Hippeastrum kermesinum, commonly known as the Kermesina amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum kermesinum is typically propagated through corms and is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
