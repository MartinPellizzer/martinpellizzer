Helianthus niveus subsp. canescens

Helianthus niveus subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the white sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States. It is characterized by its white to pale yellow ray flowers and green, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
