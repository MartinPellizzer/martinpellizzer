Hieracium farinatum

Hieracium farinatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium farinatum is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
