Hedysarum krylovii var. caulescens

Hedysarum krylovii var. caulescens, commonly known as the Krylov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Hedysarum krylovii var. caulescens is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
