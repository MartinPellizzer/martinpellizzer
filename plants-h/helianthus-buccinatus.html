Helianthus buccinatus

Helianthus buccinatus, commonly known as the horned sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has hairy, ribbed stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus buccinatus is a perennial plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
