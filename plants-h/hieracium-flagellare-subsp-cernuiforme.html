Hieracium flagellare subsp. cernuiforme

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

cernuiforme, commonly known as the drooping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features slender, often hairy stems that may droop, with basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and hairy. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a cluster, and the plant produces small, oval achenes as its seeds.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
