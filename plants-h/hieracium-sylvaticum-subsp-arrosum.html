Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. arrosum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

arrosum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small, bright yellow flowers in the summer.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
