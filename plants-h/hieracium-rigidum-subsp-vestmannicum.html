Hieracium rigidum subsp. vestmannicum

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

vestmannicum, commonly known as the Northern Field Dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and coastal areas.

The plant features upright, sparsely hairy stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are yellow, with a composite structure typical of the Asteraceae family, and are followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
