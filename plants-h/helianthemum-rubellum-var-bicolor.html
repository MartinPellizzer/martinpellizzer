Helianthemum rubellum var. bicolor

Helianthemum rubellum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with a contrasting white or pale pink center. The plant has compact, succulent-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky slopes or in dry gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
