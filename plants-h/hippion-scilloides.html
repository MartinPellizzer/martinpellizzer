Hippion scilloides

Hippion scilloides, commonly known as the slender swamp lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hippion scilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
