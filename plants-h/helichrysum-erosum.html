Helichrysum erosum

Helichrysum erosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that turn golden with age. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
