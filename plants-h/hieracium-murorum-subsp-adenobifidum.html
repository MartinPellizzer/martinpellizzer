Hieracium murorum subsp. adenobifidum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

adenobifidum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, featuring two lobes at the base of the leaf blade. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.
