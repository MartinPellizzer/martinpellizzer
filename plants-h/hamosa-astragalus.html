Hamosa astragalus

Hamosa astragalus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems that allow it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across desert landscapes.

Hamosa astragalus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
