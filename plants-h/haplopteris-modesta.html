Haplopteris modesta

Haplopteris modesta, commonly known as the common rock fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that typically grow on rocky substrates or in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a distinctive morphology with a short, creeping rhizome and pinnately divided leaves. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Haplopteris modesta is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
