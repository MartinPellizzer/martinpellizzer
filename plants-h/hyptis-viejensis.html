Hyptis viejensis

Hyptis viejensis, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in clusters during the warmer months.

Hyptis viejensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
