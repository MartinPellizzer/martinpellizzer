Hieracium candicans var. winkleri

Hieracium candicans var. winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
