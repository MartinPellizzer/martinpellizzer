Humata subvestita

Humata subvestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a tropical plant native to Central and South America, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. Humata subvestita is known for its rapid growth and is often considered an invasive species in regions where it is introduced.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
