Hyperanthera dioica

Hyperanthera dioica, commonly known as the dioecious hyperanthera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its separate male and female plants, a feature known as dioecy, which is common in many plant species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical interest in Hyperanthera dioica lies in its unique reproductive structure and its potential ecological role in its native habitats.
