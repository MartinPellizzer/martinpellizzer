Hypericum aegypticum subsp. aegypticum

Hypericum aegypticum subsp.

aegypticum, commonly known as the Egyptian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
