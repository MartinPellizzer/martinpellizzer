Hemerocallis japonica

Hemerocallis japonica, commonly known as Japanese daylily, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, though some varieties may have red or pink hues. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is hardy in USDA zones 4 through 8.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil conditions.
