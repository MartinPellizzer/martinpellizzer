Hedyotis quinquinervia

Hedyotis quinquinervia, commonly known as the five-veined shrub, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hedyotis quinquinervia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
