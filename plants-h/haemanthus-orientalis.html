Haemanthus orientalis

Haemanthus orientalis, commonly known as the red-veined oxheart, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, red-veined leaves that form a rosette shape, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is widely cultivated in subtropical and temperate regions.
