Hakea obtusifolia

Hakea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape. The plant produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain numerous small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea obtusifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
