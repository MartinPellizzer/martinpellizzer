Haemanthus magnificus

Haemanthus magnificus, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that are borne on long, slender stalks, often reaching up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The Natal grassblume is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
