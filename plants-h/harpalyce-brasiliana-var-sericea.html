Harpalyce brasiliana var. sericea

Harpalyce brasiliana var. sericea, commonly known as silky harpalyce, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Harpalyce brasiliana var. sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
