Helichrysum bolusianum

Helichrysum bolusianum, commonly known as the Bolus's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or orange inflorescences that resemble dandelion flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Helichrysum bolusianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
