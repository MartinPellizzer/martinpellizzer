Hieracium lepidum

Hieracium lepidum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by pale green or white bracts. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Hieracium lepidum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
