Hibbertia glomerata subsp. glomerata

Hibbertia glomerata subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered hibbertia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. Hibbertia glomerata subsp.

glomerata is a hardy plant that is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native region.
