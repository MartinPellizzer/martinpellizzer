Hemisphaerocarya suffruticosa var. setosa

Hemisphaerocarya suffruticosa var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy hemisphaerocarya, is a shrub-like plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a rough texture and a distinct pubescent surface. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
