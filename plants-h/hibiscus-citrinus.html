Hibiscus citrinus

Hibiscus citrinus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus citrinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
