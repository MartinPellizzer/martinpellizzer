Hoarea pinnata

Hoarea pinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Hoarea pinnata thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
