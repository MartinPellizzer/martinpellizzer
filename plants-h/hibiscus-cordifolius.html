Hibiscus cordifolius

Hibiscus cordifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus cordifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mangrove forests.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
