Hiptage benghalensis var. macroptera

Hiptage benghalensis var. macroptera, commonly known as the Bengal hiptage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
