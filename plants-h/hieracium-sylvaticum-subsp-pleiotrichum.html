Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. pleiotrichum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

pleiotrichum, commonly known as the wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is known for its variability in leaf shape and flowering time, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
