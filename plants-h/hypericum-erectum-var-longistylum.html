Hypericum erectum var. longistylum

Hypericum erectum var. longistylum, commonly known as the erect St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, containing hypericin and other compounds with potential antidepressant effects.
