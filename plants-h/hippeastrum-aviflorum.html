Hippeastrum aviflorum

Hippeastrum aviflorum, commonly known as the golden bird-of-paradise, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a bird in flight, with bright yellow petals and a distinctive orange-red center. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hippeastrum aviflorum is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, sharing similarities with other amaryllis species.
