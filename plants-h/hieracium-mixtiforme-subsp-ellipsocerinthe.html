Hieracium mixtiforme subsp. ellipsocerinthe

Hieracium mixtiforme subsp.

ellipsocerinthe, commonly known as the elliptic-crested hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellowish to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elliptical, hairy pappus and the shape of its involucral bracts. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
