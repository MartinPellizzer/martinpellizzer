Hyptis adpressa

Hyptis adpressa, commonly known as creeping hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, prostrate growth habit and small, hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
