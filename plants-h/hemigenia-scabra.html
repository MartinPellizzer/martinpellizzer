Hemigenia scabra

Hemigenia scabra, commonly known as the rough hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Hemigenia scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.
