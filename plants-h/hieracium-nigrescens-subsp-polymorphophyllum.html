Hieracium nigrescens subsp. polymorphophyllum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

polymorphophyllum, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has a taproot system and produces a variety of leaf shapes, which contribute to its common name.

Its flowers are usually arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it is known for its variable morphology across different populations.
