Hieracium laevigatum subsp. puschlachtae

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

puschlachtae, commonly known as the Puschlach's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal rosettes of narrowly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal inflorescence. Its flowers are composed of 7 to 11 ray florets surrounded by a cluster of disk florets.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence.
