Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. peyerimhoffii

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

peyerimhoffii, commonly known as the Peyerimhoff's yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early to mid-summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
