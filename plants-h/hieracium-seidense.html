Hieracium seidense

Hieracium seidense, commonly known as the Seidense Goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium seidense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic value.
