Hesperantha baurii subsp. formosa

Hesperantha baurii subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive red or purple throat.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
