Hymenophyllum flexuosum

Hymenophyllum flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant's spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves and are protected by a specialized covering known as a indusium. Hymenophyllum flexuosum thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its intricate frond structure and unique reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
