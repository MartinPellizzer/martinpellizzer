Hippocrepis conradiae

Hippocrepis conradiae, commonly known as the Conrad's helmet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that resembles a helmet, giving it its common name.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
