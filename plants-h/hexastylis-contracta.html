Hexastylis contracta

Hexastylis contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a distinctive, intricate pattern that resembles a false shamrock.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
