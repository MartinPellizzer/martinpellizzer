Heterotropa kooyana

Heterotropa kooyana, commonly known as the Kooyana thistle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features spiny leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its arid environment, with reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
