Haplopappus tortifolius

Haplopappus tortifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually rough and hairy, providing a distinctive texture.

The small, yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, white pappus structures that aid in wind pollination.
