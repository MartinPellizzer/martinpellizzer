Hypericum floribundum

Hypericum floribundum, commonly known as the common St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from June to August. Its botanical characteristics include the presence of yellow-orange sap, which contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
