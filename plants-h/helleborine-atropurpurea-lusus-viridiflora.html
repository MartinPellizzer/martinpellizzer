Helleborine atropurpurea lusus viridiflora

Helleborine atropurpurea lusus viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish leaves and striking greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers.

This species is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
