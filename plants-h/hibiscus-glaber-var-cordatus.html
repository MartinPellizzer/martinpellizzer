Hibiscus glaber var. cordatus

Hibiscus glaber var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
