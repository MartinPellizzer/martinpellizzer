Hieracium bifidum subsp. neothuringiacum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

neothuringiacum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct bifurcated base, while its flowers consist of ray florets surrounded by disk florets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
