Hecaste spathacea

Hecaste spathacea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hecaste spathacea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
