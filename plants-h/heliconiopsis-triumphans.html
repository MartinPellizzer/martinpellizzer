Heliconiopsis triumphans

Heliconiopsis triumphans, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, boat-shaped inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features large, elongated bracts that enclose its small, clustered flowers, which are pollinated by birds and insects. Its foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental value.

Heliconiopsis triumphans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
