Hyptis atrorubens var. africana

Hyptis atrorubens var. africana, commonly known as African hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
