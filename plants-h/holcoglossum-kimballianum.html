Holcoglossum kimballianum

Holcoglossum kimballianum, commonly known as the Kimball's holcoglossum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of southern China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescence and large, showy flowers with vivid purple and white markings. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic habit. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
