Hieracium sabaudum subsp. virescens

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, bright green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish composite flower heads, composed of numerous small florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.
