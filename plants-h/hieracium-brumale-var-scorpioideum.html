Hieracium brumale var. scorpioideum

Hieracium brumale var. scorpioideum, commonly known as the winter hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are serrated and hairy, with a rough texture, and the flowers are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
