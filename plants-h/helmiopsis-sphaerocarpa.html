Helmiopsis sphaerocarpa

Helmiopsis sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe fruit or round fruit, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy fruits that resemble small citrus fruits in appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruiting structure.
