Hieracium asteridiophyllum

Hieracium asteridiophyllum, commonly known as the starflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
