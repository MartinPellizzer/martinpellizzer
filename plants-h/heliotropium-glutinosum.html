Heliotropium glutinosum

Heliotropium glutinosum, commonly known as sticky heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
