Hieracium solocinum

Hieracium solocinum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium solocinum is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
