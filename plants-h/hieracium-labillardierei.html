Hieracium labillardierei

Hieracium labillardierei, commonly known as the Labillardiere's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough or hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
