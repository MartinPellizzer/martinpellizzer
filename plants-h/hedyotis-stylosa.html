Hedyotis stylosa

Hedyotis stylosa, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Hedyotis stylosa is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
