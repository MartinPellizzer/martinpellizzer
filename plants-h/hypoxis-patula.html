Hypoxis patula

Hypoxis patula, commonly known as the yellow star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single umbel, and the species is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

Hypoxis patula is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
