Hibiscus platychlamys

Hibiscus platychlamys, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Hibiscus platychlamys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
