Helianthus debilis var. cucumerifolius

Helianthus debilis var. cucumerifolius, commonly known as the cucumberleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinct cucumber-like scent when crushed. The plant produces yellow flowers with radiating petals and a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
