Hieracium laetemarginatum

Hieracium laetemarginatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive yellow margin.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
