Hieracium flagellare subsp. anacraspedum

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

anacraspedum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its slender stem and is often found in dry, open habitats.
