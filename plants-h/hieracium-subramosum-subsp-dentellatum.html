Hieracium subramosum subsp. dentellatum

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

dentellatum, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a common weed that can spread readily through vegetative reproduction.
