Hibiscus aridicola

Hibiscus aridicola, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water availability. Hibiscus aridicola typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
