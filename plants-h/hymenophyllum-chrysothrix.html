Hymenophyllum chrysothrix

Hymenophyllum chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden haircap moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, golden-green coating. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its small, sporophytes are borne on short stalks and produce spores in capsules.

The plant's intricate structure and vibrant coloration make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological surveys.
