Hieracium sparsiramum subsp. halense

Hieracium sparsiramum subsp.

halense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single capitulum surrounded by bracts, and it is known for its rapid spread through rhizomes and seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in calcareous soils.
