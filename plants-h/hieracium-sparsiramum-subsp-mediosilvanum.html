Hieracium sparsiramum subsp. mediosilvanum

Hieracium sparsiramum subsp.

mediosilvanum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in woodland edges and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
