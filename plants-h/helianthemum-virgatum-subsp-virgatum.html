Helianthemum virgatum subsp. virgatum

Helianthemum virgatum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as the Scottish heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Scottish Highlands. It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in heathland and moorland environments.

This subspecies is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
