Hypericum gramineum var. ericifolium

Hypericum gramineum var. ericifolium, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
