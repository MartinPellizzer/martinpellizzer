Helenium nudiflorum f. homochroma

Helenium nudiflorum f. homochroma, commonly known as the naked-flowered helenium, is a compact perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, brightly colored, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, ray florets that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
