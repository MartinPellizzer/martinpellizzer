Hydrocotyle palmata

Hydrocotyle palmata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically light to dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its attractive appearance and durability.
