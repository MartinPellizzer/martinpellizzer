Hakea pimeleoides

Hakea pimeleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, cylindrical clusters of small, bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent stems and reduced leaves.

Hakea pimeleoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
