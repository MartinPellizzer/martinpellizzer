Heckeria peltata

Heckeria peltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or yellowish in color, and are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Heckeria peltata is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
