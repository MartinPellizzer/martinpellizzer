Hypericum patulum subsp. leschenaultii

Hypericum patulum subsp.

leschenaultii, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
