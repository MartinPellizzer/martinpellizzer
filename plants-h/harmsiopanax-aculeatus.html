Harmsiopanax aculeatus

Harmsiopanax aculeatus, commonly known as the spiny sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
