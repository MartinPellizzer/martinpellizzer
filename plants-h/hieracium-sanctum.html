Hieracium sanctum

Hieracium sanctum, commonly known as the sacred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium sanctum is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
