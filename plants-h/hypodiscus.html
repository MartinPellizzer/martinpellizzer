Hypodiscus

Hypodiscus, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and consists of several species, including Hypodiscus angolensis and Hypodiscus tenuifolius. The plants are characterized by their bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the legume family. Hypodiscus species are often found in grasslands and woodlands, where they contribute to the ecological diversity.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some African cultures.
