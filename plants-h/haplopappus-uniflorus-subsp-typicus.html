Haplopappus uniflorus subsp. typicus

Haplopappus uniflorus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the single-flowered pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically small, narrow leaves.

The plant produces a single flower per stem, which is surrounded by a pappus of bristles that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
