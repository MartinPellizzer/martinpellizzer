Helianthemum heterophyllum var. candidum

Helianthemum heterophyllum var. candidum, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky or coastal landscapes. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for horticultural use in dry, sunny conditions.
