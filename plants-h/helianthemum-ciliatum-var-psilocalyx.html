Helianthemum ciliatum var. psilocalyx

Helianthemum ciliatum var. psilocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandverbena, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has grey-green, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
