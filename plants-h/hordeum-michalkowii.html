Hordeum michalkowii

Hordeum michalkowii, commonly known as the Michalkowii barley, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough, hairy texture.

Hordeum michalkowii is of interest to botanists and researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and ecological resilience.
