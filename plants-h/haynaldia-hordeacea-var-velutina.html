Haynaldia hordeacea var. velutina

Haynaldia hordeacea var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
