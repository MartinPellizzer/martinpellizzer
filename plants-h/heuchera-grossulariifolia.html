Heuchera grossulariifolia

Heuchera grossulariifolia, commonly known as the "Cranberry Beauty," is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, wavy-edged foliage that ranges from deep burgundy to vibrant red and purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera grossulariifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to add color to shaded landscapes.
