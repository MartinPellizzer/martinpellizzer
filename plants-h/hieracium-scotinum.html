Hieracium scotinum

Hieracium scotinum, commonly known as the Scottish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stem is upright and often branched.

Hieracium scotinum is a hardy species that thrives in cool, moist environments.
