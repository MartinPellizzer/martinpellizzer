Hieracium armerioides var. nigrellum

Hieracium armerioides var. nigrellum, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish to orange composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The variety nigrellum is distinguished by its dark green stems and foliage, which set it apart from other varieties of Hieracium.
