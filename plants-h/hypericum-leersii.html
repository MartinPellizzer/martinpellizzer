Hypericum leersii

Hypericum leersii, commonly known as the redroot St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hypericum leersii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
