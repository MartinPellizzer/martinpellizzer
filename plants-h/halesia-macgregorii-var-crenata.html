Halesia macgregorii var. crenata

Halesia macgregorii var. crenata, commonly known as the crested halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often multi-lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and alternate leaf arrangement.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
