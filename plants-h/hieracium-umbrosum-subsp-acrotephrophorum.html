Hieracium umbrosum subsp. acrotephrophorum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

acrotephrophorum, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in areas with limited sunlight and high humidity.
