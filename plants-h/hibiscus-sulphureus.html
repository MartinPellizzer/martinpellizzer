Hibiscus sulphureus

Hibiscus sulphureus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which are typically 5 to 7 inches in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Hibiscus sulphureus is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 6 feet tall, producing showy blooms throughout the growing season.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
