Hygrophila longifolia

Hygrophila longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved hygrophila, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Hygrophila longifolia is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
