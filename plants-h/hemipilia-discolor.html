Hemipilia discolor

Hemipilia discolor, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Indonesia. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically with white petals and a distinctive yellow or greenish throat.

Hemipilia discolor thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
