Hedera helix var. digitata-nova

Hedera helix var. digitata-nova, commonly known as the Nova Variegated Ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow, creating an attractive visual contrast. This plant is evergreen and thrives in partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or indoor environments. It has a climbing habit and can be trained to grow on walls, trellises, or as a hanging plant.

The leaves are serrated and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
