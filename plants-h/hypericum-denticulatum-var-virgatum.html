Hypericum denticulatum var. virgatum

Hypericum denticulatum var. virgatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
