Haworthia maraisii var. meiringii

Haworthia maraisii var. meiringii, commonly known as the Meiring's Haworthia, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with distinct white or pinkish markings. The leaves are adorned with translucent windows, a characteristic feature of many Haworthia species, which allow light to penetrate for photosynthesis. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers on slender stalks during the warmer months.

This variety is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
