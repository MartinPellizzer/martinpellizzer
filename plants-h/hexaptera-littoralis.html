Hexaptera littoralis

Hexaptera littoralis, commonly known as the coastal hexaptera, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in saline or brackish environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators.

Hexaptera littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial organisms.
