Hyacinthus atchleyi

Hyacinthus atchleyi, commonly known as the Atchley's hyacinth, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
