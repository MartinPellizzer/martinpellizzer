Habenaria unellezii

Habenaria unellezii, commonly known as the Trinidad orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, hairy stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
