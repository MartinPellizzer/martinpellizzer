Hacquetia epipactis

Hacquetia epipactis, commonly known as the yellow broomweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features erect, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months.

Hacquetia epipactis is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
