Hibiscus betulinus

Hibiscus betulinus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fibrous, woody stems. Hibiscus betulinus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
