Hieracium bifidum subsp. cardiobasis

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

cardiobasis, commonly known as the heartleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, often with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its heart-shaped basal leaves, which give it its common name.
