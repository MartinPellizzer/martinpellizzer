Hua parvifolia

Hua parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are small and lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

Hua parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
