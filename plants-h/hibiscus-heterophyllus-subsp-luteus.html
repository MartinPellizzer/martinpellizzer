Hibiscus heterophyllus subsp. luteus

Hibiscus heterophyllus subsp.

luteus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
