Hakea wirregaensis

Hakea wirregaensis, commonly known as the Wirrega hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers, which appear in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Hakea wirregaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
