Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. reductum

Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. reductum, commonly known as the reduced hymenophyllum, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds with a unique, finely textured surface. The plant's spores are produced in small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, a feature typical of the Hymenophyllaceae family. It is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its compact size and intricate leaf structure make it a notable species in the study of epiphytic flora.
