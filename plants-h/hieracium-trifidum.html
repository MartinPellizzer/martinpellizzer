Hieracium trifidum

Hieracium trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow disk flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lobed, with three to five deep divisions, giving the plant its specific epithet "trifidum.". It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
