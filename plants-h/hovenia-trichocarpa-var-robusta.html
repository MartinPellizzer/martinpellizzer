Hovenia trichocarpa var. robusta

Hovenia trichocarpa var. robusta, commonly known as the Chinese wineberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine and as a source of wine. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates, contributing to its widespread cultivation.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
