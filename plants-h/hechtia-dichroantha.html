Hechtia dichroantha

Hechtia dichroantha, commonly known as the two-colored hechtia, is a striking bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a white or silver midrib. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are often red or orange, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Hechtia dichroantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
