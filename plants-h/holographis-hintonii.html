Holographis hintonii

Holographis hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghostly white petals and sepals that resemble translucent, ethereal structures. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees rather than in soil, and relies on specific humidity and light conditions for survival. Its flowers emit a faint, sweet fragrance, attracting specific pollinators such as moths and beetles.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Holographis hintonii is considered critically endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
