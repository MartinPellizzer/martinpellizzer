Haloragis caprae

Haloragis caprae, commonly known as mountain hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to harsh environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
