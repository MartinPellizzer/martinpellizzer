Hieracium bipes

Hieracium bipes, commonly known as the two-footed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of up to 1.5 meters, with hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium bipes is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
