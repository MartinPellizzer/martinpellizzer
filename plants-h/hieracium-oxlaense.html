Hieracium oxlaense

Hieracium oxlaense, commonly known as the Oxlaense Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium oxlaense is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
