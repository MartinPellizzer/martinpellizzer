Hieracium pallidum subsp. tossicum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

tossicum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in composite inflorescences.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
