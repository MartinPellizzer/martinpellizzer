Harperocallis paniculata

Harperocallis paniculata, commonly known as the panicled amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and lance-shaped.

Harperocallis paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
