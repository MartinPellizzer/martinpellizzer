Hippocratea anthodon

Hippocratea anthodon, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small suns. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators. Hippocratea anthodon thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
