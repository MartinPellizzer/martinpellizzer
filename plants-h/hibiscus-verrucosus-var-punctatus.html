Hibiscus verrucosus var. punctatus

Hibiscus verrucosus var. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on the underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
