Helianthemum perplexans

Helianthemum perplexans, commonly known as the perplexing rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
