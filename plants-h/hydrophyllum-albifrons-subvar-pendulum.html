Hydrophyllum albifrons subvar. pendulum

Hydrophyllum albifrons subvar. pendulum, commonly known as the hanging leaf hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often trail or hang from rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, tubular, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subvariety is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
