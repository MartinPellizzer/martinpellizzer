Hesperantha sufflava

Hesperantha sufflava, commonly known as the orange-flowered bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals and a distinctive, bell-shaped flower that resembles a small lantern. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hesperantha sufflava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
