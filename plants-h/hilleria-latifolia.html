Hilleria latifolia

Hilleria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hilleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal inflorescences. Hilleria latifolia is a woody shrub or small tree that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
