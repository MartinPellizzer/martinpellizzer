Hieracium semipercome

Hieracium semipercome, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium semipercome is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various environments.
