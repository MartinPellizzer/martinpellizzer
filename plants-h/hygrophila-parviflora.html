Hygrophila parviflora

Hygrophila parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hygrophila, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters or moist soil, exhibiting a rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often used in aquatic gardens and as a decorative plant in ponds.

Hygrophila parviflora is also valued for its ability to help improve water quality by absorbing nutrients.
