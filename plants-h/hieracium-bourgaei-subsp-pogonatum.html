Hieracium bourgaei subsp. pogonatum

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

pogonatum, commonly known as the long-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
