Humboltia florulenta

Humboltia florulenta, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Humboltia florulenta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
