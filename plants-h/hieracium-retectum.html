Hieracium retectum

Hieracium retectum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers have yellow ray florets and yellow disk florets.

Hieracium retectum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some regions.
