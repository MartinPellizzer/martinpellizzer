Hieracium sericophyllum subsp. buxbaumii

Hieracium sericophyllum subsp.

buxbaumii, commonly known as the silver-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
