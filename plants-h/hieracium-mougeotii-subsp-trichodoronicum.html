Hieracium mougeotii subsp. trichodoronicum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

trichodoronicum, commonly known as the trichodoronic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are visited by various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage and specific geographic distribution.
