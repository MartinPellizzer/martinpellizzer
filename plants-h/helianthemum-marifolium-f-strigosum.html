Helianthemum marifolium f. strigosum

Helianthemum marifolium f. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
