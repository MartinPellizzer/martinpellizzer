Heliciopsis lobata var. microcarpa

Heliciopsis lobata var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf hill pine, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by clustered, winged fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
