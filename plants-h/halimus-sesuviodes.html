Halimus sesuviodes

Halimus sesuviodes, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It has a dense root system that helps stabilize coastal dunes and prevent erosion.

Halimus sesuviodes is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including high salinity and strong winds.
