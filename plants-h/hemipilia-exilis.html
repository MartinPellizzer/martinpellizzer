Hemipilia exilis

Hemipilia exilis, commonly known as the slender hemipilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Hemipilia exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
