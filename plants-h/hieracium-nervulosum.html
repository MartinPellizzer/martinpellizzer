Hieracium nervulosum

Hieracium nervulosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in moist environments and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
