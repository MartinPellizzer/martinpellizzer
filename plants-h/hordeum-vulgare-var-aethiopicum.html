Hordeum vulgare var. aethiopicum

Hordeum vulgare var. aethiopicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It is an annual grass with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that contain both sterile and fertile florets. Its seeds are typically small, with a hard, amber-colored hull.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important crop in regions with limited water availability.
