Hieracium palleucozum

Hieracium palleucozum, commonly known as the pale yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the end of its flowering stems. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
