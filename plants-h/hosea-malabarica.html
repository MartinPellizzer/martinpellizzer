Hosea malabarica

Hosea malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
