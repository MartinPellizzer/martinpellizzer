Helleborus dumetorum

Helleborus dumetorum, commonly known as the European hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are usually unlobed and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped, greenish-white flowers in late winter to early spring, before the leaves emerge. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky soils.

Helleborus dumetorum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids.
