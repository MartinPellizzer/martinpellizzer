Heliconia willisiana

Heliconia willisiana, commonly known as the pink heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers inside the bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and have a waxy surface, typical of many heliconias.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
