Hieracium bastrerianum

Hieracium bastrerianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium bastrerianum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
