Hieracium pseudopilosella subsp. albarracinum

Hieracium pseudopilosella subsp.

albarracinum, commonly known as the Albarracin's hawkweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, composite flower heads.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is noted for its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
