Hibiscus appendiculatus

Hibiscus appendiculatus, commonly known as the appendiculate hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or pink, with a prominent yellow center, and bloom during the dry season. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
