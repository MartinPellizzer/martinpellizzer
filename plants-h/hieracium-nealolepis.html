Hieracium nealolepis

Hieracium nealolepis, commonly known as the coastal hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and serrated.

Hieracium nealolepis is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
