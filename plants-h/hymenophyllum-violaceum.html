Hymenophyllum violaceum

Hymenophyllum violaceum, commonly known as the violet fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and rocks without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
