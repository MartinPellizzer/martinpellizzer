Hypericum wilsonii

Hypericum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
