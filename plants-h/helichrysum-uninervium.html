Helichrysum uninervium

Helichrysum uninervium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, sun-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in floral arrangements.

Helichrysum uninervium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
