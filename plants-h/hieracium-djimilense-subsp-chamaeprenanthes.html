Hieracium djimilense subsp. chamaeprenanthes

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

chamaeprenanthes, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
