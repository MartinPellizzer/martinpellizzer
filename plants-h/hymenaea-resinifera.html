Hymenaea resinifera

Hymenaea resinifera, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Hymenaea resinifera plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
