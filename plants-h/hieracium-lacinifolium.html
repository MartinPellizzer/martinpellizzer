Hieracium lacinifolium

Hieracium lacinifolium, commonly known as the lace-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble lace, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
