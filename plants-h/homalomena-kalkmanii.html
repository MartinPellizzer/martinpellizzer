Homalomena kalkmanii

Homalomena kalkmanii, commonly known as the Kalkman's arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
