Hybanthus parviflorus var. argentinensis

Hybanthus parviflorus var. argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine hybanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and borne in clusters at the base of the stems.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
