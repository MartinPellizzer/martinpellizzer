Hieracium nobile subsp. bartlingii

Hieracium nobile subsp.

bartlingii, commonly known as the noble hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
