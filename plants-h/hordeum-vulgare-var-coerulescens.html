Hordeum vulgare var. coerulescens

Hordeum vulgare var. coerulescens, commonly known as blue barley, is a variety of barley characterized by its distinctive blue or purple hues in the grains.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are spike-like, bearing small, three-lobed glumes that enclose the palea and lemma.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
