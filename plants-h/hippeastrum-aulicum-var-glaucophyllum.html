Hippeastrum aulicum var. glaucophyllum

Hippeastrum aulicum var. glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep blue, often with a violet or purple hue. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to 1 meter in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
