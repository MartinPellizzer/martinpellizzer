Hutchinsia procumbens f. sommieri

Hutchinsia procumbens f. sommieri, commonly known as the Sommier's prostrate hutchinsia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often hairy and adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is valued in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
