Hieracium calvum

Hieracium calvum, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, while the flowers are borne in compact clusters.

Hieracium calvum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many environments.
