Hakea sulcata var. gilbertii

Hakea sulcata var. gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features woody stems with distinctive furrows and a dense, spiny structure. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in local ecosystems.
