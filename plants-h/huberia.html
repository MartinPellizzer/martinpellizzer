Huberia

Huberia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in a wide range of colors. Huberia species are typically perennial and thrive in humid, well-drained soil conditions. They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.

The flowers are often pollinated by insects, and the plants can be propagated through leaf cuttings or seeds.
