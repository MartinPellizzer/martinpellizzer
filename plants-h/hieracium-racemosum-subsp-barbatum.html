Hieracium racemosum subsp. barbatum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

barbatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies barbatum is distinguished by its dense, bristly hairs on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This plant is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
