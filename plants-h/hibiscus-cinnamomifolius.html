Hibiscus cinnamomifolius

Hibiscus cinnamomifolius, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that have a cinnamon-like scent when crushed. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires plenty of sunlight to flourish.
