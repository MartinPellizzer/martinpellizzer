Hyptis spinulosa

Hyptis spinulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper plant, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a strong, minty scent.

Hyptis spinulosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
