Hieracium isatifolium

Hieracium isatifolium, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
