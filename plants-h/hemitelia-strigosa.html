Hemitelia strigosa

Hemitelia strigosa, commonly known as the rough hemitelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences.

Hemitelia strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
