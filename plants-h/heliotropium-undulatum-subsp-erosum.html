Heliotropium undulatum subsp. erosum

Heliotropium undulatum subsp.

erosum, commonly known as the wavy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with wavy margins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
