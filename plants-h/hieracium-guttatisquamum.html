Hieracium guttatisquamum

Hieracium guttatisquamum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which is a defining feature of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive spotted or glandular appearance, contributing to its common name.

Hieracium guttatisquamum is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
