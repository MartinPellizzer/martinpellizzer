Horsfieldia merrillii

Horsfieldia merrillii, commonly known as the Borneo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Horsfieldia merrillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
