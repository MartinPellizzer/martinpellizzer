Hieracium compositum subsp. lychnitis

Hieracium compositum subsp.

lychnitis, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with a fibrous root system.
