Heliophila pusilla subsp. pusilla

Heliophila pusilla subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as narrow-leaved heliophila, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and wetlands.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic compounds and often edible foliage.
