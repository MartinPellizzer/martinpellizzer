Hieracium inuloides subsp. teberdense

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

teberdense, commonly known as the Teberdian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-shaded habitats, showcasing adaptations to harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite inflorescence.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native environment.
