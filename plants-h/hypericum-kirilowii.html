Hypericum kirilowii

Hypericum kirilowii, commonly known as Kirilow's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Hypericum kirilowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
