Hieracium ophioglossoides

Hieracium ophioglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in a compact cluster. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
