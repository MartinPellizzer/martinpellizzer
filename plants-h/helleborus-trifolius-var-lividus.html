Helleborus trifolius var. lividus

Helleborus trifolius var. lividus, commonly known as the livid hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers with prominent, recurved petals, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and early blooming habit.
