Humata botrychioides

Humata botrychioides, commonly known as the clustered feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
