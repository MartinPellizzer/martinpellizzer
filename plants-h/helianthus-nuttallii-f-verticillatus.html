Helianthus nuttallii f. verticillatus

Helianthus nuttallii f. verticillatus, commonly known as the verticillate sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a variant of the common sunflower, characterized by its unique branching pattern and smaller, more clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow ray flowers surrounded by brown disk flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
