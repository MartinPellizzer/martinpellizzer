Hedysarum tanguticum

Hedysarum tanguticum, commonly known as the Tangut milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Hedysarum tanguticum is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
