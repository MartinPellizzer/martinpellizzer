Hyacinthus hispidus var. platyphyllus

Hyacinthus hispidus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features lance-shaped, broad leaves that are often hairy, giving it its scientific name "hispidus.". The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period.
