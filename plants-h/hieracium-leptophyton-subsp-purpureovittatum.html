Hieracium leptophyton subsp. purpureovittatum

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

purpureovittatum, commonly known as the purple-striped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are often covered with fine, white hairs, and the flowers have five petals with distinctive purple markings.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
