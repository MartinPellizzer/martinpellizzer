Hieracium norvegicum grex calocymum

Hieracium norvegicum grex calocymum, commonly known as the Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
