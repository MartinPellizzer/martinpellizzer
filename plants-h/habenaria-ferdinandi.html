Habenaria ferdinandi

Habenaria ferdinandi, commonly known as the Ferdinand's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It prefers shaded, moist habitats and is often found in tropical forests.

Habenaria ferdinandi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
