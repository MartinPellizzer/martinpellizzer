Hiraea chrysophylla var. argentata

Hiraea chrysophylla var. argentata, commonly known as the silver-leafed hiraea, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, reflective hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in panicles, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
