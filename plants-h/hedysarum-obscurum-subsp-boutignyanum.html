Hedysarum obscurum subsp. boutignyanum

Hedysarum obscurum subsp.

boutignyanum, commonly known as the dark honeysuckle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets that are palmately arranged.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
