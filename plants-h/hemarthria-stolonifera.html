Hemarthria stolonifera

Hemarthria stolonifera, commonly known as silver blade, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and creeping growth habit, which involves stolons that root at the nodes. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies due to its preference for moist soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Hemarthria stolonifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
