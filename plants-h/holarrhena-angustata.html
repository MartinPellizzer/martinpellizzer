Holarrhena angustata

Holarrhena angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching trunk and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with its bark and roots used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
