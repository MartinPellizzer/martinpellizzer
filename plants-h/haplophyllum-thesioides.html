Haplophyllum thesioides

Haplophyllum thesioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. Its leaves are linear to narrowly elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Haplophyllum thesioides is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in arid and coastal climates.
