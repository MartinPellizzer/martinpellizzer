Hedypnois crepidiformis

Hedypnois crepidiformis, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hedypnois crepidiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
