Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. hugeliense

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

hugeliense, commonly known as the Hugel's wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

It is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
