Heliocereus schrankii var. helenae

Heliocereus schrankii var. helenae, commonly known as the Helen's night blooming cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Mexico, where it thrives in hot, dry climates. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems and nocturnal flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with prominent yellow stamens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior, making it a sought-after plant among cactus enthusiasts.
