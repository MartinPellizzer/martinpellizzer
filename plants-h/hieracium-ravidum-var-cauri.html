Hieracium ravidum var. cauri

Hieracium ravidum var. cauri, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
