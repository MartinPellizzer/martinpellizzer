Herrea porcina

Herrea porcina, commonly known as the pig's ear plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Herrea porcina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
