Helicia philippinensis

Helicia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine helenia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
