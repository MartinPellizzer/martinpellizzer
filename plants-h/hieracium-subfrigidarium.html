Hieracium subfrigidarium

Hieracium subfrigidarium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subfrigidarium is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
