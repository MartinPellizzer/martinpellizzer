Huperzia bampsiana

Huperzia bampsiana, commonly known as the Bamps' huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its diminutive, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Huperzia bampsiana is notable for its primitive reproductive structures and its role in ecological systems where it contributes to soil stability and biodiversity.
