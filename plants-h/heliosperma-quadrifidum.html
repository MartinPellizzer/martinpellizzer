Heliosperma quadrifidum

Heliosperma quadrifidum, commonly known as the four-lobed sunspur, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and vibrant, star-shaped flowers. The plant has four lobes on its leaves, which give it its scientific name, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
