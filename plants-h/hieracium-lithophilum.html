Hieracium lithophilum

Hieracium lithophilum, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils on cliffs, slopes, and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium lithophilum is adapted to harsh conditions, demonstrating resilience to cold temperatures and limited nutrients.
