Hypoxis exilis

Hypoxis exilis, commonly known as the slender star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pale blue or white berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hypoxis exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
