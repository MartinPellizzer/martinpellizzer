Hieracium phrixoclonum

Hieracium phrixoclonum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
