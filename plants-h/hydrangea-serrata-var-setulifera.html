Hydrangea serrata var. setulifera

Hydrangea serrata var. setulifera, commonly known as the Japanese forest hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. Its foliage provides seasonal interest with vibrant autumn colors, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
