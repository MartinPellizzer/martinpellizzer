Hieracium racemosum subsp. virgaurea

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

virgaurea, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe, it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator plant for certain ecological conditions.
