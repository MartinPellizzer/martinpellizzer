Helictotrichon petzense

Helictotrichon petzense, commonly known as the Petzendorf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a distinct midvein.

Helictotrichon petzense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
