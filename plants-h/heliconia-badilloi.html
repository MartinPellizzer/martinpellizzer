Heliconia badilloi

Heliconia badilloi, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that resemble a torch. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with a unique structure that attracts birds and insects for pollination.

Heliconia badilloi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a component in floral arrangements.
