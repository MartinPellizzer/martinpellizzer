Hypoxis sobolifera var. pannosa

Hypoxis sobolifera var. pannosa, commonly known as the panicle hypoxis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

The varietal form pannosa is distinguished by its branched inflorescence, which sets it apart from other varieties of Hypoxis.
