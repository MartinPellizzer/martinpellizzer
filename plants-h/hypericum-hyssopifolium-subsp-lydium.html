Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp. lydium

Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp.

lydium, commonly known as the Lydian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, branched stems covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include small, four-lobed petals and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.
