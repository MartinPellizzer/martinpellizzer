Hyptis coerulea

Hyptis coerulea, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

Hyptis coerulea is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
