Hyptis balansae

Hyptis balansae, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Hyptis balansae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
