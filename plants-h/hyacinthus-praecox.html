Hyacinthus praecox

Hyacinthus praecox, commonly known as the garden hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically grown for its ornamental value. Its bulbs are hardy and can be divided to propagate the plant.

Hyacinths are often cultivated in gardens and as indoor plants due to their vibrant colors and pleasant aroma.
