Hieracium capitatum

Hieracium capitatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
