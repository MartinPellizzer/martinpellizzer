Hieracium substrigosum

Hieracium substrigosum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a dark green color, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium substrigosum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
