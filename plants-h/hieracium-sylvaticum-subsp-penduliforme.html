Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. penduliforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

penduliforme, commonly known as the hanging wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies penduliforme is distinguished by its drooping, pendulous flower heads, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
