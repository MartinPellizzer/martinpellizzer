Hypericum silenoides var. mexicanum

Hypericum silenoides var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant properties.
