Hesperis hedgei

Hesperis hedgei, commonly known as the Heath Hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit with grayish bark and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Hesperis hedgei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
