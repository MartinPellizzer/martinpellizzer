Hieracium incanum

Hieracium incanum, commonly known as the hoary hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance, and its flowers are yellow with a pale center.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
