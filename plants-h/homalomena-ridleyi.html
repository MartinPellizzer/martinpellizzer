Homalomena ridleyi

Homalomena ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's arrowleaf, is a tropical evergreen herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Homalomena ridleyi is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
