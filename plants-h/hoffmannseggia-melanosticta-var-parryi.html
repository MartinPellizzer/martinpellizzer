Hoffmannseggia melanosticta var. parryi

Hoffmannseggia melanosticta var. parryi, commonly known as the Parry's sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to the arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, pale green to bluish-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring or early summer, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
