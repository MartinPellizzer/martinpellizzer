Hemipilia cruciata

Hemipilia cruciata, commonly known as the Cruciate Hemipilia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Hemipilia cruciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
