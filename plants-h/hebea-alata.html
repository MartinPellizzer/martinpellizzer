Hebea alata

Hebea alata, commonly known as the silverleaf hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Hebea alata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
