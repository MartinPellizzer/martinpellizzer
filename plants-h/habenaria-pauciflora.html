Habenaria pauciflora

Habenaria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually clustered in a raceme and have three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being showy.

Habenaria pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
