Hordeum imrinum

Hordeum imrinum, commonly known as the lesser barley, is a wild grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Hordeum imrinum is adapted to thrive in environments with low moisture and can tolerate poor soil conditions.
