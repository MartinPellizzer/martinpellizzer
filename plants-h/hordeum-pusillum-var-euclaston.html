Hordeum pusillum var. euclaston

Hordeum pusillum var. euclaston, commonly known as the dwarf barley, is a small annual grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats. Its seeds are small and hardy, allowing it to persist in various environmental conditions.

Due to its compact size and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
