Hieracium rierae

Hieracium rierae, commonly known as the Riera's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence, consisting of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium rierae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
