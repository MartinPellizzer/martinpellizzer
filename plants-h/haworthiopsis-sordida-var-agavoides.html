Haworthiopsis sordida var. agavoides

Haworthiopsis sordida var. agavoides, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red berries. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
