Hieracium murorum subsp. mundulum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

mundulum, commonly known as the common meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

The plant has basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters. Its flowers are surrounded by leaf-like bracts, and it is characterized by its finely divided, hairy foliage.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
