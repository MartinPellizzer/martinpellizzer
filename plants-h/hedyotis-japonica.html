Hedyotis japonica

Hedyotis japonica, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis japonica is known for its rapid growth and has become an invasive species in many regions outside its native habitat.
