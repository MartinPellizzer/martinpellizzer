Hedeoma thymoides var. oblongifolia

Hedeoma thymoides var. oblongifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often aromatic when crushed, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and aromatic properties.
