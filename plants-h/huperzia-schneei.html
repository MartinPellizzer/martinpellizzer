Huperzia schneei

Huperzia schneei, commonly known as the Schneei's clubmoss, is a species of primitive, non-vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and the presence of sporangia on the underside of its leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a short, unbranched stem with whorled leaves that form a dense, rosette-like appearance.

Huperzia schneei is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various microorganisms.
