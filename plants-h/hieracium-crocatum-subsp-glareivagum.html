Hieracium crocatum subsp. glareivagum

Hieracium crocatum subsp.

glareivagum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
