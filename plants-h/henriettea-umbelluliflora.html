Henriettea umbelluliflora

Henriettea umbelluliflora, commonly known as the umbrella-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a central yellow disk surrounded by white to pale pink ray flowers.

Henriettea umbelluliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
