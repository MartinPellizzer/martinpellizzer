Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. supracalvum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

supracalvum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or divided.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
