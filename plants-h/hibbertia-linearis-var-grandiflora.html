Hibbertia linearis var. grandiflora

Hibbertia linearis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered hibbertia, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, ascending stems and alternate, linear leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent orange anthers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
