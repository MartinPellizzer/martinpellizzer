Hemionitis pellaea

Hemionitis pellaea, commonly known as the rock spikerush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Hemionitis pellaea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
