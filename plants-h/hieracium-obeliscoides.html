Hieracium obeliscoides

Hieracium obeliscoides, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with erect, hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium obeliscoides is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense colonies due to its extensive root system.
