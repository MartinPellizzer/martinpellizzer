Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. subelegans

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

subelegans, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in grassy habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features.
