Hyptis nudicaulis

Hyptis nudicaulis, commonly known as white hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical regions and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
