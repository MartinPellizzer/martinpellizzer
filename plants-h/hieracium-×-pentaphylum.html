Hieracium × pentaphylum

Hieracium × pentaphylum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in some regions.
