Homalomena ensiformis

Homalomena ensiformis, commonly known as the arrow leaf caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including green, red, and variegated patterns. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Homalomena ensiformis requires well-draining soil and partial to full shade for optimal growth.
