Hieracium ravaudii

Hieracium ravaudii, commonly known as the Ravaud's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, smooth seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
