Hoya pseudobicolensis

Hoya pseudobicolensis, commonly known as the variegated wax plant, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, waxy, ovate leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya pseudobicolensis is popular among plant enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
