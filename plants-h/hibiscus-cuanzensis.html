Hibiscus cuanzensis

Hibiscus cuanzensis, commonly known as the Cuanza hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus cuanzensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture and botanical gardens.
