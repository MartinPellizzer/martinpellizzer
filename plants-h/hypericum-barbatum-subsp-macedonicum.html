Hypericum barbatum subsp. macedonicum

Hypericum barbatum subsp.

macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
