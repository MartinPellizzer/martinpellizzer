Hymendocarpum odoratum

Hymendocarpum odoratum, commonly known as the sweet-scented hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
