Helichrysum galpinii

Helichrysum galpinii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Helichrysum galpinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
