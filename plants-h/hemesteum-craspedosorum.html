Hemesteum craspedosorum

Hemesteum craspedosorum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and harsh environmental conditions.

Hemesteum craspedosorum is primarily grown as an ornamental plant in rock gardens due to its unique appearance and resilience.
