Hieracium bifidum subsp. glaucochlorophyes

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

glaucochlorophyes, commonly known as the glaucous leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, glaucous leaves and yellow ray flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a rosette of basal leaves with a distinct central stem bearing flowering heads.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Hieracium genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other hawkweed species.
