Hypericum filicaule

Hypericum filicaule, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom from June to August. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
