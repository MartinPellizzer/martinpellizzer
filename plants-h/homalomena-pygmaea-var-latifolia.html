Homalomena pygmaea var. latifolia

Homalomena pygmaea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowhead plant, is a tropical evergreen herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its foliage is prized for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
