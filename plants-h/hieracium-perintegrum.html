Hieracium perintegrum

Hieracium perintegrum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium perintegrum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread through rhizomes and seeds.
