Haageocereus winterianus

Haageocereus winterianus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers typically bloom only once a year, usually during the summer months.

The plant is popular in horticulture for its striking appearance and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
