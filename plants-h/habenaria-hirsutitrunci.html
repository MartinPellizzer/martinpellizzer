Habenaria hirsutitrunci

Habenaria hirsutitrunci, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
