Hopea eglandulosa

Hopea eglandulosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or oblong in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

Hopea eglandulosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
