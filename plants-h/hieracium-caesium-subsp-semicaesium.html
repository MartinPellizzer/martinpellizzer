Hieracium caesium subsp. semicaesium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

semicaesium, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is often found in temperate climates and is relatively hardy, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
