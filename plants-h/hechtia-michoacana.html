Hechtia michoacana

Hechtia michoacana, commonly known as the Michoacan hechtia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
