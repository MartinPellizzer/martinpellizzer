Hyptis alutacea

Hyptis alutacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters, and they bloom in shades of purple or blue.

Hyptis alutacea is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
