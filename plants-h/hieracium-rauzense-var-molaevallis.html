Hieracium rauzense var. molaevallis

Hieracium rauzense var. molaevallis, commonly known as the Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosettes of leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky and mountainous habitats, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
