Hieracium subserratosinuatum

Hieracium subserratosinuatum, commonly known as the serrate-crenated hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated and crenated edges, giving it its scientific name. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium subserratosinuatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
