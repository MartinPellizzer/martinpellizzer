Hieracium vulgatum subsp. exutum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

exutum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom from June to August, surrounded by pale green leafy bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge along the edges.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
