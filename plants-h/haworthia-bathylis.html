Haworthia bathylis

Haworthia bathylis, commonly known as the deep-rooted Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a layer of white wax. The leaves are often pale green to bluish-gray, with white or light-colored markings that provide a striking visual contrast. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
