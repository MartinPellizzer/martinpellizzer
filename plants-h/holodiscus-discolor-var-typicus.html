Holodiscus discolor var. typicus

Holodiscus discolor var. typicus, commonly known as the variable discolor holly.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
