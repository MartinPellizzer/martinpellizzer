Hieracium murorum subsp. aracsense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

aracsense, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, often arranged in clusters.

It is known for its ability to colonize open habitats and is sometimes considered a weed due to its aggressive growth.
