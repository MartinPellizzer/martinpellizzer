Hieracium transvaalense

Hieracium transvaalense, commonly known as Transvaal groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are usually hairy and have a somewhat lobed appearance, contributing to its distinctive botanical profile.
