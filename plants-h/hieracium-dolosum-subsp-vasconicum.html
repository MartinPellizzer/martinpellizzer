Hieracium dolosum subsp. vasconicum

Hieracium dolosum subsp.

vasconicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc and surrounding ray florets, making it visually distinct from other hawkweed species.
