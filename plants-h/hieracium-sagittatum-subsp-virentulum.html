Hieracium sagittatum subsp. virentulum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

virentulum, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and usually have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader and more vibrant green leaves compared to other hawkweed species.
