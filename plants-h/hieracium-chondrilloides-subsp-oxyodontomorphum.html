Hieracium chondrilloides subsp. oxyodontomorphum

Hieracium chondrilloides subsp.

oxyodontomorphum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in mountainous areas with limited soil coverage.
