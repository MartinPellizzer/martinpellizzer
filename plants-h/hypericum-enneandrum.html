Hypericum enneandrum

Hypericum enneandrum, commonly known as the nine-golden or nine-leaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it produces small, red, berry-like fruits.

Hypericum enneandrum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
