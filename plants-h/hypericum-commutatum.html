Hypericum commutatum

Hypericum commutatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Hypericum commutatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
