Hieracium thyraicum

Hieracium thyraicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to Europe, it is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
