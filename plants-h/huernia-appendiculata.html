Huernia appendiculata

Huernia appendiculata, commonly known as the horned ice plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem covered in fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. Its unique appearance, with leaf-like structures resembling horns, gives it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
