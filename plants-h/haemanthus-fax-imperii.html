Haemanthus fax-imperii

Haemanthus fax-imperii, commonly known as the King's Blood or Emperor's Faxe, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red and white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to its arid environment. Its flowers are tubular and are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
