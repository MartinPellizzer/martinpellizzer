Haplopappus spinulosus subsp. incisifolius

Haplopappus spinulosus subsp.

incisifolius, commonly known as the incisifolius hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single terminal head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its structure and growth form make it well adapted to withstand drought and grazing.
