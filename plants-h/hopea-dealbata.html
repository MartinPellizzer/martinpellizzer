Hopea dealbata

Hopea dealbata, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Hopea dealbata also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
