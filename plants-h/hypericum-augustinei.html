Hypericum augustinei

Hypericum augustinei, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems that are often covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum augustinei is typically found in open, sunny habitats like woodlands and roadsides.
