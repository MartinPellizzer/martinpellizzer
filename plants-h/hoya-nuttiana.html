Hoya nuttiana

Hoya nuttiana, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Porcelain Flower," is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Hoya nuttiana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental beauty.
