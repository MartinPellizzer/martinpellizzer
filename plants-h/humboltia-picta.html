Humboltia picta

Humboltia picta, commonly known as the painted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Humboltia picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
