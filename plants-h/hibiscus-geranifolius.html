Hibiscus geranifolius

Hibiscus geranifolius, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.

Hibiscus geranifolius thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
