Hypoestes cinerascens

Hypoestes cinerascens, commonly known as the Blue Mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Hypoestes cinerascens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
