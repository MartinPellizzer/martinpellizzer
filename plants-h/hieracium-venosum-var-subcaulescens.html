Hieracium venosum var. subcaulescens

Hieracium venosum var. subcaulescens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
