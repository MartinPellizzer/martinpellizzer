Hieracium laevigatum subsp. vendeanum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

vendeanum, commonly known as the Vendean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are usually clustered in a single head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
