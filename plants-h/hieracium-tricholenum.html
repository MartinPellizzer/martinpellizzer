Hieracium tricholenum

Hieracium tricholenum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and produces achenes as its seeds.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, where it can form dense colonies.
