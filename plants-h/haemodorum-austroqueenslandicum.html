Haemodorum austroqueenslandicum

Haemodorum austroqueenslandicum, commonly known as the southern Queensland glory flower, is a striking herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes species known for their vibrant, flame-like flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red-orange or yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for native gardens and conservation efforts.
