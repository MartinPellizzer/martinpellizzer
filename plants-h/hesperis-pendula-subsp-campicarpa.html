Hesperis pendula subsp. campicarpa

Hesperis pendula subsp.

campicarpa, commonly known as the alpine sweet rocket, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands at elevations above 1,500 meters.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
