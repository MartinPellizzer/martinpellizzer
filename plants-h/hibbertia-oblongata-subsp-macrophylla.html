Hibbertia oblongata subsp. macrophylla

Hibbertia oblongata subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf hibbertia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to sunny, dry environments. Hibbertia oblongata subsp.

macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
