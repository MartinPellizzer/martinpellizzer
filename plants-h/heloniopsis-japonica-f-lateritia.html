Heloniopsis japonica f. lateritia

Heloniopsis japonica f. lateritia, commonly known as the Japanese rock bellflower, is a unique variant of the rock bellflower species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan, characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings due to its low maintenance requirements and drought tolerance.
