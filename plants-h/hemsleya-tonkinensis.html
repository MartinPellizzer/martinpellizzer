Hemsleya tonkinensis

Hemsleya tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin hemerocallis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in a single day. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Hemsleya tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
