Hieracium cirritum subsp. elisum

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

elisum, commonly known as the Elisum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets surrounding a central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and fibrous root system.
