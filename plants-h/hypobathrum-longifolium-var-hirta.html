Hypobathrum longifolium var. hirta

Hypobathrum longifolium var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy long-leaved hypobathrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a hairy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It typically grows in well-drained soils in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
