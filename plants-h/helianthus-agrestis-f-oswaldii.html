Helianthus agrestis f. oswaldii

Helianthus agrestis f. oswaldii, commonly known as the Ozark sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, pale yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
