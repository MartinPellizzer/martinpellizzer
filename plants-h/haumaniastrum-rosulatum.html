Haumaniastrum rosulatum

Haumaniastrum rosulatum, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
