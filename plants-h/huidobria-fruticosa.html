Huidobria fruticosa

Huidobria fruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Huidobria fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
