Haplopappus nanus

Haplopappus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf pappus, is a small, annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical leaf structure. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, dry achenes with a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Haplopappus nanus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
