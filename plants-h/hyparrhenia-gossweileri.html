Hyparrhenia gossweileri

Hyparrhenia gossweileri, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental elements. Hyparrhenia gossweileri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
