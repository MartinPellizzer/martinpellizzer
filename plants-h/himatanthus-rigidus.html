Himatanthus rigidus

Himatanthus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
