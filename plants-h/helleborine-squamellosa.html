Helleborine squamellosa

Helleborine squamellosa, commonly known as the squamellose helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features a pseudobulb and produces dark green, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and they bloom in early spring.

Helleborine squamellosa is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
