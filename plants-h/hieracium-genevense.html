Hieracium genevense

Hieracium genevense, commonly known as the Geneva hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally hairy and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Hieracium genevense is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
