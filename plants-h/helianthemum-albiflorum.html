Helianthemum albiflorum

Helianthemum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
