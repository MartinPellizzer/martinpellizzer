Helianthemum thymifolium var. barrelieri

Helianthemum thymifolium var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are aromatic and resemble those of thyme, giving it its species name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for Mediterranean-style landscaping.
