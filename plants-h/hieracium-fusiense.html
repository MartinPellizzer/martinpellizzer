Hieracium fusiense

Hieracium fusiense, commonly known as the fusiform hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium fusiense is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
