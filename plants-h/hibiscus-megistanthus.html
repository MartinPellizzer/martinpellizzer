Hibiscus megistanthus

Hibiscus megistanthus, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are usually pink or red in color.

Hibiscus megistanthus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
