Hicorius cordiformis var. latifolia

Hicorius cordiformis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf heartleaf, is a distinctive plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy.

The leaves are typically a vibrant green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink. The plant is native to regions with warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
