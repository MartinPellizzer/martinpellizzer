Hypericum lloydii

Hypericum lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum lloydii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
