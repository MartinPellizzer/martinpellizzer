Hippocrepis emerus subsp. emeroides

Hippocrepis emerus subsp.

emeroides, commonly known as the Emmeroides bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue or violet, clustered in racemes, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
