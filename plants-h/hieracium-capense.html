Hieracium capense

Hieracium capense, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features basal leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped, with a taproot system.

Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
