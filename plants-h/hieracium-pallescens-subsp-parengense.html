Hieracium pallescens subsp. parengense

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

parengense, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in sandy or calcareous soils and is considered a low-growing, spreading plant with a mat-like growth habit.
