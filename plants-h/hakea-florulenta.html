Hakea florulenta

Hakea florulenta, commonly known as the pink hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical foliage and striking pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its specialized root system and water-storing tissues. Its flowers are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Hakea florulenta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
