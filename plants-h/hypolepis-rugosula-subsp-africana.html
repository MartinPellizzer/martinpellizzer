Hypolepis rugosula subsp. africana

Hypolepis rugosula subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
