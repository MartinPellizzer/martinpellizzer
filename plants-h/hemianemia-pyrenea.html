Hemianemia pyrenea

Hemianemia pyrenea, commonly known as the fire blight of hemlock, is a fungal disease caused by the pathogen *Pseudomonas syringae* pv.

*hemlyca*. It primarily affects the foliage and branches of hemlock trees, leading to discoloration, wilting, and eventual dieback.

The disease is characterized by the appearance of blackened, necrotic lesions on the bark and foliage, often resembling a scorched or burned appearance. It spreads through rain splashes and contaminated tools, making it a significant threat to hemlock populations in forested areas.

Management strategies include pruning infected branches, applying bactericides, and planting resistant species to mitigate its impact.
