Hieracium marmoratum

Hieracium marmoratum, commonly known as the marble hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive appearance.
