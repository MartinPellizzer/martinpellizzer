Hieracium racemosum subsp. spinidentatum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

spinidentatum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically borne on elongated stalks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the subspecies is distinguished by its spiny bracts.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
