Hedeoma crenata

Hedeoma crenata, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and they contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Hedeoma crenata is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in disturbed areas.
