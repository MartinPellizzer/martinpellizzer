Hieracium nigrescens var. hirtei

Hieracium nigrescens var. hirtei, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This variant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which set it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus.
