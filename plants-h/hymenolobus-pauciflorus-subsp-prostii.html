Hymenolobus pauciflorus subsp. prostii

Hymenolobus pauciflorus subsp.

prostii, commonly known as the Cape fynbos hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to Mediterranean climates and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
