Hedyotis rotundifolia

Hedyotis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf blushing grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has rounded, succulent-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
