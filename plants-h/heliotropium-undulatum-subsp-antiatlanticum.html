Heliotropium undulatum subsp. antiatlanticum

Heliotropium undulatum subsp.

antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Atlantic coast. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, glandular stems.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its salt tolerance, it is well adapted to harsh coastal conditions.
