Hyptis personata

Hyptis personata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are square in cross-section, a common feature in the Lamiaceae family.

Hyptis personata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
