Helianthus angustifolius subsp. ciliaris

Helianthus angustifolius subsp.

ciliaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have hairy margins.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
