Heracleum testiculatum

Heracleum testiculatum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers. The plant has broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

The plant is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is known for its distinctive, aromatic foliage.
