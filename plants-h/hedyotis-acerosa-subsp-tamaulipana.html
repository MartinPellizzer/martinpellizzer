Hedyotis acerosa subsp. tamaulipana

Hedyotis acerosa subsp.

tamaulipana, commonly known as Tamaulipan bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, often found in areas with moist, sandy soils.

The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, and its small, white flowers are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
