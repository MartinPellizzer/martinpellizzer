Hieracium glaucinum subsp. cispadanum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

cispadanum, commonly known as the Cispadanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its pale greenish-blue coloration and is often found in the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.
