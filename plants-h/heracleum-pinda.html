Heracleum pinda

Heracleum pinda, commonly known as the Kashmiri hogweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and edible plants. The plant features large, compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive white underside. It produces yellowish-green flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the carrot family.

The roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
