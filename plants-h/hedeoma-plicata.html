Hedeoma plicata

Hedeoma plicata, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by triangular, nut-like fruits. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is closely related to other leafy greens.
