Hieracium mattfeldianum subsp. mattfeldianum

Hieracium mattfeldianum subsp.

mattfeldianum, commonly known as the Mattfeld's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, composite flowers that are surrounded by bracts, and it thrives in grassy areas and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
