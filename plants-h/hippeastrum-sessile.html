Hippeastrum sessile

Hippeastrum sessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrower than those of other amaryllis species, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Hippeastrum sessile is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
