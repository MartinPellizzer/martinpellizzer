Hieracium pilosella subsp. zagrabiense

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

zagrabiense, commonly known as the Zagreb hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
