Hieracium ciliosum

Hieracium ciliosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium ciliosum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
