Helichrysum trinervatum

Helichrysum trinervatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is commonly used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers.

Helichrysum trinervatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
