Hieracium scheelei

Hieracium scheelei, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium scheelei is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
