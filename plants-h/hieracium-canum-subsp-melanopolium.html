Hieracium canum subsp. melanopolium

Hieracium canum subsp.

melanopolium, commonly known as the black-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a dark green to almost black coloration, which gives the plant its common name. It produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, and its flowering period usually occurs in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
