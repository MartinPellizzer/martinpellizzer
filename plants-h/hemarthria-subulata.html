Hemarthria subulata

Hemarthria subulata, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Hemarthria subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
