Herreria verticillata

Herreria verticillata, commonly known as the clustered herreria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Herreria verticillata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
