Hibiscus thespesianus var. integrovatus

Hibiscus thespesianus var. integrovatus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically with a prominent central yellow stamen. The leaves are ovate, glossy, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
