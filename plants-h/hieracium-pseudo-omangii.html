Hieracium pseudo-omangii

Hieracium pseudo-omangii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
