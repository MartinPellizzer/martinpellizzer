Hakea kirkalocka

Hakea kirkalocka, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

Hakea kirkalocka is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
