Hutchinsia procumbens var. simplicissimum

Hutchinsia procumbens var. simplicissimum, commonly known as the simpleleaf hutchinsia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the warmer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.
