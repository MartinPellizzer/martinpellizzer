Hibbertia coriacea subsp. angustifolia

Hibbertia coriacea subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a rough, sandpapery texture.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with prominent, recurved petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
