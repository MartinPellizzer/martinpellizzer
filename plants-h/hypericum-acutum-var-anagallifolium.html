Hypericum acutum var. anagallifolium

Hypericum acutum var. anagallifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinct appearance compared to other Hypericum species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
