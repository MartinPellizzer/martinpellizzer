Herniaria hirsuta var. hamata

Herniaria hirsuta var. hamata, commonly known as the hairy herniaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a ground cover in xeriscaping.
