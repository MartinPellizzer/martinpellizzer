Hieracium brachiatum subsp. bavaricum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

bavaricum, commonly known as the Bavarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually pale yellow.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
