Hieracium pyrenaicum proles chevallieri

Hieracium pyrenaicum proles chevallieri, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces numerous flowering stems that bear composite flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in certain regions.
