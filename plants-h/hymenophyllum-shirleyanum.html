Hymenophyllum shirleyanum

Hymenophyllum shirleyanum, commonly known as Shirley's filmy fern, is a delicate and rare species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, translucent fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or on rock surfaces. Its spores are released in small, cup-like structures called sporangia, which are clustered in groups known as sori.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Hymenophyllum shirleyanum is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health and is often found in areas with high biodiversity.
