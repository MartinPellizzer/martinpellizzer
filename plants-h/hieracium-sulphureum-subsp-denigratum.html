Hieracium sulphureum subsp. denigratum

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

denigratum, commonly known as the sulphur hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a head. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

The species is characterized by its distinctive coloration and is part of the Asteraceae family.
