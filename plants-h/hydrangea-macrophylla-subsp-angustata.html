Hydrangea macrophylla subsp. angustata

Hydrangea macrophylla subsp.

angustata, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a species native to Japan. It is characterized by its cone-shaped flower clusters, which range in color from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH.

The plant produces large, broad leaves that are dark green and often have a rough texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
