Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. sparsicrinum

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

sparsicrinum, commonly known as the sparse-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sparse flowering and finely hairy leaves. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
