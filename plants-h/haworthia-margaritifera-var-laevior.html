Haworthia margaritifera var. laevior

Haworthia margaritifera var. laevior, commonly known as the "Pearl Plant," is a compact, evergreen succulent native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with white tubercles that resemble small pearls, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped white flowers on slender stems during the spring or summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
