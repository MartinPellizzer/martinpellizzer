Hemionitis membranacea

Hemionitis membranacea, commonly known as the bladder fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble bladders, which help it float on the water's surface. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome that anchors it in shallow water or moist soil. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture and limited light.
