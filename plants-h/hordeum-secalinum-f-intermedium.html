Hordeum secalinum f. intermedium

Hordeum secalinum f. intermedium, commonly known as intermediate barley, is a botanical variant of barley characterized by its intermediate traits between wild and cultivated forms.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale to reddish flowers. It is notable for its ability to thrive in marginal environments due to its adaptability and resilience.

Its genetic diversity makes it a subject of interest in plant breeding and ecological studies.
