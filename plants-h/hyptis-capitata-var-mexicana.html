Hyptis capitata var. mexicana

Hyptis capitata var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
