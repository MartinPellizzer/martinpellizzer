Hieracium amplidens

Hieracium amplidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium amplidens is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
