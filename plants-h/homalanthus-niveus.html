Homalanthus niveus

Homalanthus niveus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
