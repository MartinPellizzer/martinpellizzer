Hertia kuntzei

Hertia kuntzei, commonly known as the rock ivy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to China.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soil and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Hertia kuntzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
