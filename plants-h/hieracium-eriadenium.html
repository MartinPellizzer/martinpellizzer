Hieracium eriadenium

Hieracium eriadenium, commonly known as the meadowhawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium eriadenium is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
