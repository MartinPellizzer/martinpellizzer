Homalomena subemarginata

Homalomena subemarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow margin, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that are typically pink or red, though they are less showy than the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
