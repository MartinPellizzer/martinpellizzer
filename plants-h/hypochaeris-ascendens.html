Hypochaeris ascendens

Hypochaeris ascendens, commonly known as the ascending sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from spring to early summer, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hypochaeris ascendens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
