Hieracium dentatum subsp. expallens

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

expallens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually broad, lance-shaped, and have toothed margins.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
