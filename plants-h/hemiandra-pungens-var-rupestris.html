Hemiandra pungens var. rupestris

Hemiandra pungens var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock hemiandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often growing on rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
