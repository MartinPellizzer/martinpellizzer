Hieracium neostenophyllum

Hieracium neostenophyllum, commonly known as the eastern yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium neostenophyllum is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
