Habenaria hawkesiana

Habenaria hawkesiana, commonly known as the Hawkes' orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
