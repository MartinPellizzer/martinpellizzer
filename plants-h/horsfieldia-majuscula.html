Horsfieldia majuscula

Horsfieldia majuscula, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Horsfieldia majuscula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
