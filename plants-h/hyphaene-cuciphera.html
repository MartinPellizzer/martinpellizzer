Hyphaene cuciphera

Hyphaene cuciphera, commonly known as the double-tract palm, is a tall, slender palm tree native to the arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and a tall, unbranched stem that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are essential for its reproduction. Its fruits are fibrous and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

The double-tract palm is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
