Hamamelis mexicana

Hamamelis mexicana, commonly known as Mexican witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has rough, bark-like stems and oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The botanical features of Hamamelis mexicana make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
