Hieracium spinatum

Hieracium spinatum, commonly known as the prickly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a somewhat bitter taste.

Hieracium spinatum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
