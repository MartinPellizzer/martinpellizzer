Heterogonium austrosinense

Heterogonium austrosinense, commonly known as the southern sinensis heterogonium, is a rare aquatic plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and small, translucent leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces unique reproductive structures called heterogonia, which are specialized for sexual reproduction. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with high light exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality.
