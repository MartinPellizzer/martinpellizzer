Hydrangea bangii

Hydrangea bangii, commonly known as the pineapple hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a pineapple, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that can change color depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hydrangea bangii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
