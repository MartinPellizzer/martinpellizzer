Hortensia serrata f. plenidecuris

Hortensia serrata f. plenidecuris, commonly known as the laceleaf hydrangea, is a cultivar of the hydrangea plant characterized by its dense, serrated foliage.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant produces large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and have a textured, almost lace-like appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
