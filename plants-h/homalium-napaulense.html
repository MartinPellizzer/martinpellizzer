Homalium napaulense

Homalium napaulense, commonly known as the Napaul's holly.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant features simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in terminal panicles.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
