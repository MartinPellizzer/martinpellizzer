Hydrangea serrata f. alba

Hydrangea serrata f. alba, commonly known as the white-leaf hydrangea, is a cultivar of the hydrangea plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range from white to pale pink, depending on soil pH. The plant features serrated, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding ornamental value beyond flowering.
