Hydrangea vinicolor

Hydrangea vinicolor, commonly known as the pink-leafed hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy inflorescences that range from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. Hydrangea vinicolor thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ability to change flower color with environmental conditions makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
