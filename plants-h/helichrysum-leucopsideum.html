Helichrysum leucopsideum

Helichrysum leucopsideum, commonly known as the white-haired everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, yellow to golden-orange flowers. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a fluffy appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum leucopsideum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dried flower arrangements.
