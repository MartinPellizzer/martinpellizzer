Hypericum bellum subsp. latisepalum

Hypericum bellum subsp.

latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal yellowstar, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America. It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five broad, showy sepals that resemble petals. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
