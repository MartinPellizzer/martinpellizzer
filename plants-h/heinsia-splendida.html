Heinsia splendida

Heinsia splendida, commonly known as the Splendid Heinsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, daisy-like blooms that are typically yellow with a central disk, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Heinsia splendida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
