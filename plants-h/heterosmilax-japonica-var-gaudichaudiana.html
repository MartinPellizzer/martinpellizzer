Heterosmilax japonica var. gaudichaudiana

Heterosmilax japonica var. gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or Gaudichaud's arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used as a starch source. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, giving it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
