Heteropappus hispidus var. pinetorum

Heteropappus hispidus var. pinetorum, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically grouped in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
