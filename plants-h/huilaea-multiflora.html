Huilaea multiflora

Huilaea multiflora, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Huilaea multiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
