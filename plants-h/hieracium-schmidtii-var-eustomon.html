Hieracium schmidtii var. eustomon

Hieracium schmidtii var. eustomon, commonly known as Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to tolerate cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
