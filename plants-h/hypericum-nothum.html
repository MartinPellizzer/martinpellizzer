Hypericum nothum

Hypericum nothum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a reddish hue when mature.

Hypericum nothum is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
