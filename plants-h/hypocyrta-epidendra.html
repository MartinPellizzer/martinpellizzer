Hypocyrta epidendra

Hypocyrta epidendra, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often clustering at the base of the plant.

The flowering period of Hypocyrta epidendra occurs during the wet season, producing clusters of fragrant, colorful blooms that attract various pollinators.
