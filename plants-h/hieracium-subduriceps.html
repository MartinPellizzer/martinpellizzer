Hieracium subduriceps

Hieracium subduriceps, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium subduriceps is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
