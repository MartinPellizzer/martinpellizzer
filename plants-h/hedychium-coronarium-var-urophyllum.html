Hedychium coronarium var. urophyllum

Hedychium coronarium var. urophyllum, commonly known as the golden ginger or yellow ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The varietal name 'urophyllum' refers to its distinctive yellow floral bracts.
