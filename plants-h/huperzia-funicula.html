Huperzia funicula

Huperzia funicula, commonly known as the funiculate huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, circular formations on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia funicula is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
