Helictotrichon pubescens subsp. longifolium

Helictotrichon pubescens subsp.

longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States. It features slender, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
