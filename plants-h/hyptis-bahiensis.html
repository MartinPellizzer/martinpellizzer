Hyptis bahiensis

Hyptis bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
