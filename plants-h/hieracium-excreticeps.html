Hieracium excreticeps

Hieracium excreticeps, commonly known as the excreting hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a dense cluster at the stem's apex. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The species is also notable for its unique secretion of substances from its leaves, which may play a role in its ecological interactions.
