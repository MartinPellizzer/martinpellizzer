Hieracium strigosum

Hieracium strigosum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are covered with stiff, bristly hairs. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Hieracium strigosum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
