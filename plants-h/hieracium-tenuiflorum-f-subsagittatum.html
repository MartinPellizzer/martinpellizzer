Hieracium tenuiflorum f. subsagittatum

Hieracium tenuiflorum f. subsagittatum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered hawkweed, is a botanical variant of the hawkweed genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is a perennial species with a low-growing habit, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
