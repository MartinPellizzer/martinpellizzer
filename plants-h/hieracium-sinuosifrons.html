Hieracium sinuosifrons

Hieracium sinuosifrons, commonly known as the sinuous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically has hairy, alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
