Holarrhena procumbens

Holarrhena procumbens, commonly known as the African angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Holarrhena procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
