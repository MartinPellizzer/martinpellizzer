Helleborus purpurascens f. tuzsonii

Helleborus purpurascens f. tuzsonii, commonly known as the Tuzson's Christmas rose, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a hybrid form of Helleborus purpurascens, characterized by its distinctive, waxy, purple-tinged leaves and striking white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a unique addition to gardens with its early flowering habit.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a valuable and delicate species in botanical collections.
