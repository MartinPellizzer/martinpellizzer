Hypericum loxense

Hypericum loxense, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Hypericum loxense is part of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
