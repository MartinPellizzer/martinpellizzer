Hypericum tetrapterum var. rotundifolium

Hypericum tetrapterum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The leaves are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a rounded base, and are arranged oppositely along the stems. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
