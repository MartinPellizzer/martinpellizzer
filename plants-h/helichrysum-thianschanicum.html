Helichrysum thianschanicum

Helichrysum thianschanicum, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bracteate inflorescences, which are clusters of tiny flowers surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
