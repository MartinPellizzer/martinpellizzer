Hewittia cespitosa

Hewittia cespitosa, commonly known as the silver leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellow composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hewittia cespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
