Heterozeuxine

Heterozeuxine, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in saline soils and can tolerate high levels of salt.

Heterozeuxine plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
