Hymenophyllum polyodon

Hymenophyllum polyodon, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, soft, and velvety fronds. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are thin and have a unique structure with small, scale-like appendages.

Hymenophyllum polyodon is noted for its ability to retain moisture, which is essential for its survival in humid climates.
