Halerpestes lancifolia var. variifolia

Halerpestes lancifolia var. variifolia, commonly known as the variable flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by bright red or orange seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
