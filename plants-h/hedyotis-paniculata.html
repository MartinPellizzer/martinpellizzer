Hedyotis paniculata

Hedyotis paniculata, commonly known as the Indian cranberry or false sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Hedyotis paniculata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
