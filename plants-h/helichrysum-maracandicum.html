Helichrysum maracandicum

Helichrysum maracandicum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or maracanda goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Helichrysum maracandicum is often used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
