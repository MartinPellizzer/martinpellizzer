Hieracium petiolatum subsp. austurgilense

Hieracium petiolatum subsp.

austurgilense, commonly known as the Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a central stem that bears yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually yellow and tubular florets that are shorter.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
