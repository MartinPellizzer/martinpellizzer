Hilairella neglecta

Hilairella neglecta, commonly known as the common redweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It blooms from spring to early summer and is often found in coastal areas.

Hilairella neglecta is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
