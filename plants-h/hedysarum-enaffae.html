Hedysarum enaffae

Hedysarum enaffae, commonly known as the alpine sweetclover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum enaffae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh mountainous terrains.
