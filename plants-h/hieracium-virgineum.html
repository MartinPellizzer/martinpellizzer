Hieracium virgineum

Hieracium virgineum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium virgineum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
