Hemitelia speciosa

Hemitelia speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Hemitelia speciosa is often found in humid forests and can sometimes become invasive in certain ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential agricultural weed.
