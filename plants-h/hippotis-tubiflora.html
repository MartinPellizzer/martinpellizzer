Hippotis tubiflora

Hippotis tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.
