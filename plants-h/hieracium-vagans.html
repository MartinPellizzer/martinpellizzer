Hieracium vagans

Hieracium vagans, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disk.

Hieracium vagans is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
