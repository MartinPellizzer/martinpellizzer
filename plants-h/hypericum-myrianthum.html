Hypericum myrianthum

Hypericum myrianthum, commonly known as the many-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are green on top and lighter underneath. Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
