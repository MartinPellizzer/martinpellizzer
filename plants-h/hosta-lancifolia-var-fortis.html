Hosta lancifolia var. fortis

Hosta lancifolia var. fortis, commonly known as the hardy giant arrowleaf hosta, is a striking perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a prominent white margin. The plant produces tall, upright stems bearing fragrant, white to pale lavender flowers in late summer. It thrives in shady to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its robust growth and is a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
