Helicostylis turbinata

Helicostylis turbinata, commonly known as the turban orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Helicostylis turbinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
