Hibiscus dictyocarpus

Hibiscus dictyocarpus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus dictyocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
