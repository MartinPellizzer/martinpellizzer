Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp. ucenicum

Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp.

ucenicum, commonly known as the Ucenic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sandy or loamy conditions. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated leaves and reduced flower head structure compared to other members of the species.
