Hylotelephium spectabile var. spectabile

Hylotelephium spectabile var. spectabile, commonly known as the Armenian Carpet, is a perennial succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with fleshy, upright stems and bright, showy flowers that range from pink to red in color. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal maintenance.
