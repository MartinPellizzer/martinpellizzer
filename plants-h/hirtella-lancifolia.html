Hirtella lancifolia

Hirtella lancifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian hirtella, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hirtella lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
