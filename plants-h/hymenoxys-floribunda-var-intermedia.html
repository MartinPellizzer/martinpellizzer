Hymenoxys floribunda var. intermedia

Hymenoxys floribunda var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
