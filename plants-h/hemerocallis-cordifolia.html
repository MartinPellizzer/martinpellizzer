Hemerocallis cordifolia

Hemerocallis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Hemerocallidaceae family and is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.

The flowers, which last only one day, are often bright yellow or orange, making it a popular ornamental plant.
