Hysterobaeckea ochropetala subsp. cometes

Hysterobaeckea ochropetala subsp.

cometes, commonly known as the comet's ghost or ghost flower, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its small, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in spring, often appearing in dense clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
