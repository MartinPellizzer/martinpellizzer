Holcus albus

Holcus albus, commonly known as white grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale green flowers that appear in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Holcus albus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
