Hypericum perforatum var. albiflorum

Hypericum perforatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered St. John's wort, is a variant of the common St. John's wort.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features yellow flowers with white petals, distinguishing it from the typical orange-red blooms of the standard variety. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and have small, translucent oil dots.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
