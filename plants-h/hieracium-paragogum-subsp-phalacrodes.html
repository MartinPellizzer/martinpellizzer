Hieracium paragogum subsp. phalacrodes

Hieracium paragogum subsp.

phalacrodes, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is noted for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
