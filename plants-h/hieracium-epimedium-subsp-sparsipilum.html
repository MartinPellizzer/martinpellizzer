Hieracium epimedium subsp. sparsipilum

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

sparsipilum, commonly known as the sparse-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
