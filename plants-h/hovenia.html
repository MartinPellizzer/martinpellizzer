Hovenia

Hovenia, commonly known as the Chinese date or Hovenia dulcis, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. Hovenia is often cultivated for its fruit and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
