Helichrysum subumbellatum

Helichrysum subumbellatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or yellow helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Helichrysum subumbellatum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
