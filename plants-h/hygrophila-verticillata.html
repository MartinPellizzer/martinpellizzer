Hygrophila verticillata

Hygrophila verticillata, commonly known as water wisteria, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, feather-like leaves that are often used in aquariums for their aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life. The plant thrives in shallow waters and can grow both submerged and emergent, depending on environmental conditions. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Hygrophila verticillata is also valued for its role in water filtration and as a natural aquarium decoration.
