Helicteres integerrima

Helicteres integerrima, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf senna, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a velvety coating. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
