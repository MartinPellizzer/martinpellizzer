Hakea pugioniformis var. sericea

Hakea pugioniformis var. sericea, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in spikes, which bloom from late summer to autumn. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
