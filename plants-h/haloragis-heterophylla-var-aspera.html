Haloragis heterophylla var. aspera

Haloragis heterophylla var. aspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be either linear or lobed depending on the environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in spring.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is often found in coastal or inland wetlands.
