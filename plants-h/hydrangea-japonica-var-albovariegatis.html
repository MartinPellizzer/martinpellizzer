Hydrangea japonica var. albovariegatis

Hydrangea japonica var. albovariegatis, commonly known as the white-veined hydrangea, is a cultivar prized for its striking foliage.

Its leaves are predominantly green with distinctive white or cream veins that create a dramatic visual contrast. The plant produces large, showy flower clusters that range from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
