Heterolophus sumensis

Heterolophus sumensis, commonly known as the Sumatran bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite in arrangement, with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
