Habenaria quartiniana

Habenaria quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
