Hieracium lawsonii subsp. elvae

Hieracium lawsonii subsp.

elvae, commonly known as the Elva's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial herb that produces seeds through a process called wind pollination.
