Helenium setigerum

Helenium setigerum, commonly known as the stiff-hair compassplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, stiff, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
