Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. polyphaeum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

polyphaeum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles.

It is characterized by its ability to form dense colonies, making it a notable component of its native ecosystems.
