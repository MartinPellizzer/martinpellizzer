Henriettella umbelluliflora

Henriettella umbelluliflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Henriettella umbelluliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
