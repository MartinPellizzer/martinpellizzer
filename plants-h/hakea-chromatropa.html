Hakea chromatropa

Hakea chromatropa, commonly known as the red-stemmed hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-branching stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and needle-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Hakea chromatropa is well adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
