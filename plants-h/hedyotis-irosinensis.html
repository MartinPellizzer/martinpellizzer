Hedyotis irosinensis

Hedyotis irosinensis, commonly known as the Philippine knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Hedyotis irosinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
