Hieracium laeticolor var. perangustum

Hieracium laeticolor var. perangustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside.

It is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its sensitivity to environmental changes.
