Heteropappus villosus

Heteropappus villosus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Heteropappus villosus is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.
