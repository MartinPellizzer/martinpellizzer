Hieracium pannulosum

Hieracium pannulosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
