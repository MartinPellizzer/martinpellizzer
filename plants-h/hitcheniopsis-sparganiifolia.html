Hitcheniopsis sparganiifolia

Hitcheniopsis sparganiifolia, commonly known as the spiky swamp orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of dense clusters of spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has elongated, strap-shaped leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water, adapted to its aquatic or semi-aquatic environment. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinct, intricate pattern of veins, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat destruction, Hitcheniopsis sparganiifolia is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
