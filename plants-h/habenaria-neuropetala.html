Habenaria neuropetala

Habenaria neuropetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to various regions in Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
