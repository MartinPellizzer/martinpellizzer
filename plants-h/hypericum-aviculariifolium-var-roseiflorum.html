Hypericum aviculariifolium var. roseiflorum

Hypericum aviculariifolium var. roseiflorum, commonly known as the rose-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with distinctive red stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable species for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
