Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. pleiophylloides

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

pleiophylloides, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces numerous small flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Its botanical features make it distinctive within the Hieracium genus, though it is often confused with other similar species.
