Hakea shuttleworthiana

Hakea shuttleworthiana, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Hakea shuttleworthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
