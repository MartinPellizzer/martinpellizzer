Hieracium ruprechtii subsp. tuscheticum

Hieracium ruprechtii subsp.

tuscheticum, commonly known as the Tutschet's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is part of the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and medicinal potential.
