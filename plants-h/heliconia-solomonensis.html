Heliconia solomonensis

Heliconia solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific region, particularly the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has long, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Heliconia solomonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
