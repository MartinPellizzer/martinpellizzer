Houstonia minima

Houstonia minima, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. It is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

Houstonia minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
