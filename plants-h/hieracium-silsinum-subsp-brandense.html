Hieracium silsinum subsp. brandense

Hieracium silsinum subsp.

brandense, commonly known as Brandenburg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
