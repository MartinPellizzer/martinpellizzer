Hemionitis poeppigiana

Hemionitis poeppigiana, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of white or pale green, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hemionitis poeppigiana is well-suited to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant for alpine gardens.
