Hieracium grandidentiforme

Hieracium grandidentiforme, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring alternate, rough, and hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive shape and are arranged in a single head at the end of each stem.

Hieracium grandidentiforme is known for its aggressive growth and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
