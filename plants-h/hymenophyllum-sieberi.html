Hymenophyllum sieberi

Hymenophyllum sieberi, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance and is often found growing on shaded, moist rock surfaces or tree trunks. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery coating, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, cool environments and is often associated with temperate rainforests.

Hymenophyllum sieberi plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms.
