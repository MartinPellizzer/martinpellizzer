Hieracium vulgatum subsp. murmanicum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

murmanicum, commonly known as the Murman hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically green with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and a flowering stem that is usually unbranched.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and is often found in regions with a temperate to subarctic climate.
