Hieracium macranthelum subsp. meganthelum

Hieracium macranthelum subsp.

meganthelum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically woolly and covered in fine hairs. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
