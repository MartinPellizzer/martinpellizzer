Helianthus mollis f. flavidus

Helianthus mollis f. flavidus, commonly known as the yellow stiffleaf sunflower, is a variant of the stiffleaf sunflower native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include a sturdy stem, lance-shaped leaves, and composite flower heads with yellow ray florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
