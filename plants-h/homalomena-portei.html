Homalomena portei

Homalomena portei, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, it can become invasive in certain regions.
