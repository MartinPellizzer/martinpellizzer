Hieracium callimorphoides subsp. pseudoleptophyton

Hieracium callimorphoides subsp.

pseudoleptophyton, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purple hue, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
