Hieracium schultesii subsp. tricoronaemontis

Hieracium schultesii subsp.

tricoronaemontis, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which are typically arranged in a discoid structure. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to cold and nutrient-poor environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of alpine flora.
