Hieracium talvikense

Hieracium talvikense, commonly known as the Talvik hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium talvikense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
