Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. flavoplenus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. flavoplenus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive five-lobed shape. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
