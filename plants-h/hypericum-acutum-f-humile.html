Hypericum acutum f. humile

Hypericum acutum f. humile, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
