Haworthiopsis glauca var. herrei

Haworthiopsis glauca var. herrei, commonly known as the Blue Haworthia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense rosettes of narrow, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers on slender stems during the spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
