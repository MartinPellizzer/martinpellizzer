Hedeoma martirensis

Hedeoma martirensis, commonly known as the Mexican hedeoma, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
