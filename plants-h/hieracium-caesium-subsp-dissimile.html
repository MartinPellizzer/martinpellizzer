Hieracium caesium subsp. dissimile

Hieracium caesium subsp.

dissimile, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
