Hypericum punctulatum

Hypericum punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Its botanical name reflects the distinctive black dots on its stems, which are characteristic of the species.
