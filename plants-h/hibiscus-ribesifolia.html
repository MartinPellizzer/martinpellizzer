Hibiscus ribesifolia

Hibiscus ribesifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Hawaii.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found growing along coastal areas and in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus ribesifolia is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton plants.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
