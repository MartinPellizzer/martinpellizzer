Hieracium grecescui subsp. grecescui

Hieracium grecescui subsp.

grecescui, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
