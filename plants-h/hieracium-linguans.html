Hieracium linguans

Hieracium linguans, commonly known as the tongue-shaped hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium linguans is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
