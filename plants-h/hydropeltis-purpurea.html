Hydropeltis purpurea

Hydropeltis purpurea, commonly known as the purple hydropeltis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
