Hieracium ragognae

Hieracium ragognae, commonly known as the Ragogna hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and arranged in a rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
