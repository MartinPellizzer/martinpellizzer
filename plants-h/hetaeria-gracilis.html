Hetaeria gracilis

Hetaeria gracilis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces slender, often fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with six petals and a distinct central column. Hetaeria gracilis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden borders or woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical studies.
