Hieracium vulgatum subsp. lupimontis

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

lupimontis, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by a dense ring of phyllaries.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific adaptations to high-altitude environments.
