Hieracium lychneum

Hieracium lychneum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by greenish-brown bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in meadows and disturbed soils.

Hieracium lychneum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
