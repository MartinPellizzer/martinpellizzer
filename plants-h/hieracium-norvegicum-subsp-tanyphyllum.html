Hieracium norvegicum subsp. tanyphyllum

Hieracium norvegicum subsp.

tanyphyllum, commonly known as the Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered with glandular dots, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is a common weed in disturbed areas and has a widespread distribution across its native range.
