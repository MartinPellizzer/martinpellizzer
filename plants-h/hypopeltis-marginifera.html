Hypopeltis marginifera

Hypopeltis marginifera, commonly known as the marginate grasshopper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with prominent margins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Hypopeltis marginifera is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
