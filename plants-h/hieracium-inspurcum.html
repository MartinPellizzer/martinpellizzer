Hieracium inspurcum

Hieracium inspurcum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium inspurcum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
