Holcus lanatus subsp. argenteus

Holcus lanatus subsp.

argenteus, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, two-lipped florets.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
