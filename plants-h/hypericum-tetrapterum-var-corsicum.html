Hypericum tetrapterum var. corsicum

Hypericum tetrapterum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage of opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and often branched, and it is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
