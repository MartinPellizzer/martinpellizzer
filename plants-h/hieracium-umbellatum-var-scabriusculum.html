Hieracium umbellatum var. scabriusculum

Hieracium umbellatum var. scabriusculum, commonly known as the rough-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
