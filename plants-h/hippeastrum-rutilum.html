Hippeastrum rutilum

Hippeastrum rutilum, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. Hippeastrum rutilum is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with its underground bulb serving as the primary storage organ.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
