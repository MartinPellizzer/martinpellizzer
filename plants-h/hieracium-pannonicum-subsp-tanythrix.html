Hieracium pannonicum subsp. tanythrix

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

tanythrix, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal head, and the leaves are lanceolate with hairy surfaces.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in regions with moderate climates.
