Hedera rhomboidea

Hedera rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to southern China.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera rhomboidea is a member of the Araliaceae family and is closely related to other ivy species.
