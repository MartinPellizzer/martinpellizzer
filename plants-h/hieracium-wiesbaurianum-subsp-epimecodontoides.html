Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. epimecodontoides

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

epimecodontoides, commonly known as the Wiesbauer's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and exhibits a compact growth form.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and floral structure, which contribute to its ecological niche.
