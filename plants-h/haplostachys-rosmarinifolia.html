Haplostachys rosmarinifolia

Haplostachys rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late spring or early summer.
