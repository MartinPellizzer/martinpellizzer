Hemionitis densa

Hemionitis densa, commonly known as the dense hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and dark green, leathery fronds that are densely packed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spores rather than flowers, which is typical of ferns. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis densa is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental stability.
