Hyssopus officinalis proles aristatus

Hyssopus officinalis proles aristatus, commonly known as the aristate hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and the Middle East.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
