Hedysarum schischkinii

Hedysarum schischkinii, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to grow in harsh, arctic or alpine environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Hedysarum schischkinii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in extreme climates.
