Homalomena rubra

Homalomena rubra, commonly known as the red ginger or red flame plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces berries that range in color from red to black.

Homalomena rubra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
