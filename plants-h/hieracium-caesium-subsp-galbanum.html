Hieracium caesium subsp. galbanum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

galbanum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is notable for its chemical composition, containing compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal and ecological significance.
