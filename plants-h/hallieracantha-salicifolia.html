Hallieracantha salicifolia

Hallieracantha salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. Native to Southeast Asia, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous root system, which helps it anchor in loose, organic soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance.
