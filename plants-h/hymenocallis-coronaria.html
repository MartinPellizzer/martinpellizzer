Hymenocallis coronaria

Hymenocallis coronaria, commonly known as the Crowned Spider Orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure at the base. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Hymenocallis coronaria is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
