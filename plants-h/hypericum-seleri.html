Hypericum seleri

Hypericum seleri, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Hypericum seleri is part of the Hypericaceae family, which includes other species of St. John's wort.

It is often used in traditional medicine, though its medicinal properties are less well-documented compared to other species in the genus.
