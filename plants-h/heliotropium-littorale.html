Heliotropium littorale

Heliotropium littorale, commonly known as sea heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along shorelines. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with a distinctive central yellowish mark. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a hairy texture, adapted to withstand salty winds and tidal spray.

Heliotropium littorale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
