Hypericum quadrialatum

Hypericum quadrialatum, commonly known as the four-angled St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, four-angled stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
