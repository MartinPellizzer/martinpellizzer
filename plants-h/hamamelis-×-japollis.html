Hamamelis × japollis

Hamamelis × japollis, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Hamamelis vernalis and Hamamelis mollis.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Hamamelis × japollis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
