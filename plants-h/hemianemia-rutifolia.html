Hemianemia rutifolia

Hemianemia rutifolia, commonly known as black haw, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its berries are dark purple to black when ripe and are often used in traditional medicine.

Hemianemia rutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
