Hemionitis speciosa

Hemionitis speciosa, commonly known as the sacred fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or floating in still or slow-moving water. The plant has a specialized root system that helps it anchor in muddy substrates and absorb nutrients. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized leaf sheaths, which protect the spores until they are released.

Hemionitis speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
