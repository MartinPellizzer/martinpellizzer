Heliconia lacletteana

Heliconia lacletteana, commonly known as the Laclette's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe, which attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

The leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, providing a dense canopy that helps shade the plant's roots.
