Halocharis farsistanica

Halocharis farsistanica, commonly known as the Persian halocharis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically arranged in clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

Halocharis farsistanica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
