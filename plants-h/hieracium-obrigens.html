Hieracium obrigens

Hieracium obrigens, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium obrigens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
