Hedysarum tetraphyllum

Hedysarum tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leaf clover of the genus Hedysarum, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, and its leaves are compound, typically with four leaflets. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant's ornamental value and ecological significance make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
