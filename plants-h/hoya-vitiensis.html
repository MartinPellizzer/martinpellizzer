Hoya vitiensis

Hoya vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensian waxplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya vitiensis is a climber, often found growing on trees or shrubs in its natural habitat.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
