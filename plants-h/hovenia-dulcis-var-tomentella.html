Hovenia dulcis var. tomentella

Hovenia dulcis var. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy grape or Chinese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
