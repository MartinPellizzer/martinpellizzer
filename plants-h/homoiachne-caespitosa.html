Homoiachne caespitosa

Homoiachne caespitosa, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and prairies.

Blue grama is an important forage plant for livestock and wildlife.
