Hevea spruceana var. similis

Hevea spruceana var. similis, commonly known as the Spruce's rubber tree, is a rare and less-studied variant of the hevea genus.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree is known for its latex-producing capabilities, though it is not as commercially significant as other hevea species.

Its botanical study is of interest due to its potential genetic diversity and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
