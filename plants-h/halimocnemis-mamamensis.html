Halimocnemis mamamensis

Halimocnemis mamamensis, commonly known as the Andean cholla, is a species of cactus native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, branching stems and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for harsh, dry climates.

Halimocnemis mamamensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides shelter for various desert animals.
