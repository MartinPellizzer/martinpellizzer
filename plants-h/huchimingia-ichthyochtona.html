Huchimingia ichthyochtona

Huchimingia ichthyochtona, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the western Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and bracts that resemble sea urchins, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky coastal soils and is often found in tidal zones. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Huchimingia ichthyochtona is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
