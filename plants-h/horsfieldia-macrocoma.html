Horsfieldia macrocoma

Horsfieldia macrocoma, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its floral structure includes a prominent lip and a spurred ovary, which are typical features of orchids.

Horsfieldia macrocoma is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting various insect pollinators.
