Hornstedtia punicea

Hornstedtia punicea, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert and rocky habitats.

Hornstedtia punicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
