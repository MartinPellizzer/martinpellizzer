Hieracium sylvaticum var. asymmetricum

Hieracium sylvaticum var. asymmetricum, commonly known as the asymmetrical wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a flat, composite head. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with a distinctive asymmetrical pattern on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
