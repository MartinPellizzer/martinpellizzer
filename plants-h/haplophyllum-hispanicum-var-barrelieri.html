Haplophyllum hispanicum var. barrelieri

Haplophyllum hispanicum var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's rockrose, is a succulent shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it tolerates high sunlight and limited water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
