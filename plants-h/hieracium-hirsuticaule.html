Hieracium hirsuticaule

Hieracium hirsuticaule, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium hirsuticaule is often considered a weed but can be found in various ecological settings.
