Helia elegans

Helia elegans, commonly known as the elegant helia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its botanical name reflects its graceful appearance, with "elegans" meaning elegant in Latin.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
