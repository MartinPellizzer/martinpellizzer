Hedysarum krylovii var. humile

Hedysarum krylovii var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf Siberian peashrub, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and has a deep root system for moisture retention. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in dryland ecosystems.
