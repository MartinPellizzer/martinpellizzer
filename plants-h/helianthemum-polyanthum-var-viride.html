Helianthemum polyanthum var. viride

Helianthemum polyanthum var. viride, commonly known as the green-flowered rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, upright stems covered in dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in Mediterranean climates.
