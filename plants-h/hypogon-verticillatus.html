Hypogon verticillatus

Hypogon verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are usually borne on short pedicels, and the plant is known for its trifoliate leaves.

Hypogon verticillatus is a member of the Oxalidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
