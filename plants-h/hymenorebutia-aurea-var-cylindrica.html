Hymenorebutia aurea var. cylindrica

Hymenorebutia aurea var. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical golden crown cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of cylindrical, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which bloom at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

It requires minimal water and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
