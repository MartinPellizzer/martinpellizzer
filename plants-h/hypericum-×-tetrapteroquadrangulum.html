Hypericum × tetrapteroquadrangulum

Hypericum × tetrapteroquadrangulum, commonly known as the four-leafed St. John's wort, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from several Hypericum species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces four-leafed flowers, which is a rare and distinctive feature in the genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually appealing plant.
