Heliotropium cornutum

Heliotropium cornutum, commonly known as the horned heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the afternoon, giving it its common name. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has a short life cycle and is adapted to warm, sunny environments.
