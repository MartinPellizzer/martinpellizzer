Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. haslibergense

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

haslibergense, commonly known as the Hasliberg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows at high altitudes. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
