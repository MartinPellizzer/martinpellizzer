Hydrocotyle paratyensis

Hydrocotyle paratyensis, commonly known as the Paraty siltplant, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth pattern, with leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or muddy soils near the shoreline, often in areas with high salinity.

Hydrocotyle paratyensis is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
