Helwingia japonica var. hypoleuca

Helwingia japonica var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a finely toothed margin. This plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its delicate floral display.
