Helichrysum butaguense

Helichrysum butaguense, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that retain their color even when dried. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum butaguense is valued for its ornamental use and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
