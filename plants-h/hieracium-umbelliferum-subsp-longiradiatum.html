Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. longiradiatum

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

longiradiatum, commonly known as the long-rayed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with elongated rays.

It is often found in open habitats and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
