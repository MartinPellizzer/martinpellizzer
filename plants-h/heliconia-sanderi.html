Heliconia sanderi

Heliconia sanderi, commonly known as the red ginger or flame heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers inside its colorful inflorescence. Heliconia sanderi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
