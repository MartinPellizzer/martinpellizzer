Hebe × lewisii

Hebe × lewisii, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its foliage is evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Hebe × lewisii is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal areas due to its tolerance of poor soils and salt spray.
