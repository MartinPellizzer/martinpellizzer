Hieracium murorum subsp. chlorocorium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

chlorocorium, commonly known as the green-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has alternate, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish hue. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its greenish central disk and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.
