Haplopappus villosus

Haplopappus villosus, commonly known as the hairy pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, and the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
