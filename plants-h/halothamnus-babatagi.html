Halothamnus babatagi

Halothamnus babatagi, commonly known as the Babatagi thornbush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. Halothamnus babatagi is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
