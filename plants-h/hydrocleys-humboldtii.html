Hydrocleys humboldtii

Hydrocleys humboldtii, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a tropical aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are usually a vibrant green, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

Hydrocleys humboldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
