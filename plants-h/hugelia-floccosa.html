Hugelia floccosa

Hugelia floccosa, commonly known as the wooly hugelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hugelia floccosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
