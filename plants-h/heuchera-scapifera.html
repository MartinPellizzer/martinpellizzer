Heuchera scapifera

Heuchera scapifera, commonly known as the scarlet false spurge, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green, red, or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Heuchera scapifera is valued for its vibrant foliage and early spring blooms.
