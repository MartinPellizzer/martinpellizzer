Heliconia signa-hispanica

Heliconia signa-hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces long, segmented flower spikes that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. Its leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia signa-hispanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
