Helichrysum biafranum

Helichrysum biafranum, commonly known as the African immortelle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sunflower-like blooms. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for petals. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Helichrysum biafranum is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
