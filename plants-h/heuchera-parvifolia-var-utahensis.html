Heuchera parvifolia var. utahensis

Heuchera parvifolia var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah alumroot, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a striking red or purple underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and adaptability contribute to its horticultural value.
