Hieracium murorum subsp. hypofloccosum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

hypofloccosum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves and produces yellow disc flowers with numerous tubular florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
