Hieracium moldavicum

Hieracium moldavicum, commonly known as Moldavian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Hieracium moldavicum is often found in regions with temperate climates and is relatively hardy in cooler environments.
