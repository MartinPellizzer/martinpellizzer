Homalium racemosum

Homalium racemosum, commonly known as the clustered flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
