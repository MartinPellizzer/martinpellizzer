Holcus lanatus var. purpurascens

Holcus lanatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple hairgrass, is a variant of the common hairgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its dense tufts of fine, silver-green foliage with a distinctive purple tinge on the inflorescence. Its inflorescence is a loose, open panicle with pale green to purple spikelets.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
