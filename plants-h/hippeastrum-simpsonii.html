Hippeastrum simpsonii

Hippeastrum simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

Hippeastrum simpsonii is often grown as a houseplant or in gardens, requiring well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.
