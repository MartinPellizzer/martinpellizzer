Habenaria lehiae

Habenaria lehiae, commonly known as the Leh's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in forested areas with high humidity and partial shade. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.

Habenaria lehiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
