Hieracium guglerianum subsp. lengyelii

Hieracium guglerianum subsp.

lengyelii, commonly known as the Lengyel's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, including the shape and size of its leaves and flower heads.
