Hibiscus liliastrum

Hibiscus liliastrum, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, wetland environments and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Hibiscus liliastrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
