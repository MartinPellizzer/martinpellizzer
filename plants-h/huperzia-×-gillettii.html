Huperzia × gillettii

Huperzia × gillettii, commonly known as the hybrid rock huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Huperzia selago and Huperzia tenuifolia, characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it is of particular interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
