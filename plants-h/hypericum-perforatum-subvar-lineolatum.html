Hypericum perforatum subvar. lineolatum

Hypericum perforatum subvar. lineolatum, commonly known as the dotted St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive leaves with translucent dots, which are visible under sunlight. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
