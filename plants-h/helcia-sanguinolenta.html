Helcia sanguinolenta

Helcia sanguinolenta, commonly known as the bloodroot plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive red-orange sap that leaks from its rhizomes, giving it its common name. The plant has a single, smooth, dark green leaf that is typically heart-shaped. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a umbel, blooming in early spring.

Helcia sanguinolenta thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soil.
