Haplopappus uniflorus subsp. linearis

Haplopappus uniflorus subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the linearleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It produces achenes with pappus bristles, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds.
