Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. chloroides

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

chloroides, commonly known as the greenleaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a compact cluster.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
