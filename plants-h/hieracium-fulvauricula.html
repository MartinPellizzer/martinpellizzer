Hieracium fulvauricula

Hieracium fulvauricula, commonly known as the yellow-hair hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive reddish-brown center.

Hieracium fulvauricula is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with moderate moisture.
