Hieracium vulgatum subsp. mertiniifolium

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

mertiniifolium, commonly known as the Mertiniifolium hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces and serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more elongated leaves compared to other Hieracium species.
