Helicteres lanceolata var. gagnepainiana

Helicteres lanceolata var. gagnepainiana, commonly known as the Gagnepain's urticaria plant, is a variant of the urticaria plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its botanical features include stinging hairs on the leaves, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
