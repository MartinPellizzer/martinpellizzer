Hieracium pichleri subsp. cernyi

Hieracium pichleri subsp.

cernyi, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often hairy and slightly curled, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low stature and adapted morphology to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
