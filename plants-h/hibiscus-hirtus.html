Hibiscus hirtus

Hibiscus hirtus, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its flowers bloom from summer to fall and are often used in traditional medicine.

Hibiscus hirtus is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
