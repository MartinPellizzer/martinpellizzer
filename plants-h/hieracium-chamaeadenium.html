Hieracium chamaeadenium

Hieracium chamaeadenium, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can form dense mats due to its rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, broadly ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
