Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. paphlagonicum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp.

paphlagonicum, commonly known as the Paphlagonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads and hairy stems, typical of many hawkweed species.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, disc-like flowers that are often visited by various pollinators.
