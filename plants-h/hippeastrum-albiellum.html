Hippeastrum albiellum

Hippeastrum albiellum, commonly known as the white-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish hue, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ability to store water in its bulb allows it to survive dry periods, enhancing its adaptability to various climates.
