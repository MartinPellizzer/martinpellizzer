Helleborus purpurascens var. baumgartenii

Helleborus purpurascens var. baumgartenii, commonly known as the Baumann's hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with a waxy texture and distinctive, upward-facing flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant produces globose, spiny seed capsules that release small, black seeds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Helleborus purpurascens var. baumgartenii is also known for its toxic sap, which can be harmful if ingested.
