Hieracium hoppeanum var. submultisetum

Hieracium hoppeanum var. submultisetum, commonly known as the Hoppe's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer.

This variety is distinguished by its densely hairy stems and foliage, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
