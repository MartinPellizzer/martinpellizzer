Heywoodia

Heywoodia, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. The plants are typically low-growing, mat-forming shrubs with fleshy, succulent leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color.

Heywoodia is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
