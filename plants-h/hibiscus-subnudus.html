Hibiscus subnudus

Hibiscus subnudus, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus subnudus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
