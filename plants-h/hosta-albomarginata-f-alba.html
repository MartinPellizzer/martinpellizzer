Hosta albomarginata f. alba

Hosta albomarginata f. alba, commonly known as the White-edged Hosta, is a cultivar characterized by its striking white margins on its heart-shaped leaves.

This variety belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is native to East Asia. The leaves are typically a deep green with a broad, creamy-white border that can vary in width. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, though the foliage is the main ornamental feature.

This plant thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soil.
