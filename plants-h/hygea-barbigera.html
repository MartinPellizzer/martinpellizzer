Hygea barbigera

Hygea barbigera, commonly known as the hairy hygea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Hygea barbigera is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
