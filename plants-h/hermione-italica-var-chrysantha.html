Hermione italica var. chrysantha

Hermione italica var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
