Hieracium florentinum subsp. genuense

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

genuense, commonly known as the Florence hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a flowering stem that bears yellow flowers in a clustered inflorescence. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a single head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its ornamental value in gardens.
