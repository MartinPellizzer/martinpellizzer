Hakea amplexicaulis var. angustifolia

Hakea amplexicaulis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are often clasping around the stems. The plant produces spikes of small, bright red or pink flowers during the winter months. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
