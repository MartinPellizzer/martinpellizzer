Huperzia mildbraedii

Huperzia mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and Southeast Asia. Huperzia mildbraedii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or rocky crevices.

Its spore-producing structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
