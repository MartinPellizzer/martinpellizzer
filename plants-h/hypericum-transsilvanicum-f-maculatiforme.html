Hypericum transsilvanicum f. maculatiforme

Hypericum transsilvanicum f. maculatiforme, commonly known as the spotted form of the Transylvanian St. John's wort, is a compact shrub native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typical of the Hypericaceae family. The leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on the underside. This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.
