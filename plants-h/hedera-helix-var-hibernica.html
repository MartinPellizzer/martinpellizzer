Hedera helix var. hibernica

Hedera helix var. hibernica, commonly known as the Irish ivy, is a hardy evergreen climbing plant native to western Europe.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls or ground surfaces.
