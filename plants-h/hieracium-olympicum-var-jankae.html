Hieracium olympicum var. jankae

Hieracium olympicum var. jankae, commonly known as the Olympic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
