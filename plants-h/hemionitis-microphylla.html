Hemionitis microphylla

Hemionitis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved hemionitis, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and often grows in dense mats on the water's surface or submerged in shallow waters. Its reproductive structures are simple, with small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind.

Hemionitis microphylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
