Hieracium souliei subsp. cezycola

Hieracium souliei subsp.

cezycola, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
