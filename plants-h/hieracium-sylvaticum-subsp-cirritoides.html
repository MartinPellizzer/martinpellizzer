Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. cirritoides

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

cirritoides, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and the shape of its involucral bracts.
