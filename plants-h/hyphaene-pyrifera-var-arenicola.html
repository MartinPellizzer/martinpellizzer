Hyphaene pyrifera var. arenicola

Hyphaene pyrifera var. arenicola, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a variant of the pyrifera species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree with a thick, buttressed trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The plant produces large, spiny pods that contain edible seeds, which are a traditional food source in many regions. Its roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to provide shade and resources in desert ecosystems.
