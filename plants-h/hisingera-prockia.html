Hisingera prockia

Hisingera prockia, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and a delicate, intricate floral structure typical of orchids. The plant produces small, green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hisingera prockia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
