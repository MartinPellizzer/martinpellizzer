Hedyotis wulsinii

Hedyotis wulsinii, commonly known as the small yellow toadflax, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
