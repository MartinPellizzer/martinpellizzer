Halimium atriplicifolium

Halimium atriplicifolium, commonly known as the woolly sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
