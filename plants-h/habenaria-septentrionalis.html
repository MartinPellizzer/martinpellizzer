Habenaria septentrionalis

Habenaria septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and swampy woodlands. The plant features a single, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, hooded flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom from June to August.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insects for reproduction.
