Hemionitis villosa

Hemionitis villosa, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Hemionitis villosa is also known for its ability to survive extreme temperatures and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
