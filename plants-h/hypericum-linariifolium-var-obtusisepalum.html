Hypericum linariifolium var. obtusisepalum

Hypericum linariifolium var. obtusisepalum, commonly known as the blunt-sepal St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and somewhat branched, with a reddish-brown color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
