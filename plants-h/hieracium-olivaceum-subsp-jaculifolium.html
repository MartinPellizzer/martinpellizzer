Hieracium olivaceum subsp. jaculifolium

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

jaculifolium, commonly known as the spear-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant features lanceolate to spear-shaped leaves that are often glandular and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are surrounded by bracts.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
