Helichrysum triplinerve

Helichrysum triplinerve, commonly known as the triple-veined everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive tripartite venation pattern, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
