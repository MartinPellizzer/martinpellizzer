Hieracium saxifragum subsp. tolosanum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

tolosanum, commonly known as the Tolosan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
