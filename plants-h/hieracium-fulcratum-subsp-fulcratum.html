Hieracium fulcratum subsp. fulcratum

Hieracium fulcratum subsp.

fulcratum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It has alternate, heart-shaped to ovate leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its erect stem and the absence of a basal rosette.
