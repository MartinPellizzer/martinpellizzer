Hieracium rigidisetum

Hieracium rigidisetum, commonly known as the rigid-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are generally rough and hairy, with a distinct basal rosette formation.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
