Hieracium nigrescens subsp. handoelense

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

handoelense, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker flower heads and specific geographic distribution.
