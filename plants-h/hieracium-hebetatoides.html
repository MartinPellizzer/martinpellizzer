Hieracium hebetatoides

Hieracium hebetatoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features a basal rosette of fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, and the plant produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium hebetatoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
