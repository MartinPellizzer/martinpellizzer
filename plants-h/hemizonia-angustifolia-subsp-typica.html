Hemizonia angustifolia subsp. typica

Hemizonia angustifolia subsp.

typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is known for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
