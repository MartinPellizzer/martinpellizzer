Hieracium acrothetum

Hieracium acrothetum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, flat inflorescence.

Hieracium acrothetum is adapted to cold climates and is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
