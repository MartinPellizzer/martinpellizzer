Hieracium pavichii

Hieracium pavichii, commonly known as the Pavich's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium pavichii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
