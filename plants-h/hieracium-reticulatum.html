Hieracium reticulatum

Hieracium reticulatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish and hairy.

The flowers bloom in summer, producing yellow to orange petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
