Hamamelis japonica f. incarnata

Hamamelis japonica f. incarnata, commonly known as the Japanese Witch Hazel.

This plant is a compact shrub characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The flowers, typically yellow or orange, are borne in clusters and are unique in their structure, with four to six petals. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture, turning yellow in the fall.

It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
