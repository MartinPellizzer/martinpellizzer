Heracleum henryi

Heracleum henryi, commonly known as Chinese cowparade, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound leaves.

It is native to China and grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant features a tall, hollow stem with a distinct ridged texture and produces small, white to greenish flowers in umbels. Its leaves are broadly triangular with a serrated margin, and it is known for its strong, often spicy scent.

Heracleum henryi is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
