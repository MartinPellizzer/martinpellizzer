Hieracium jailanum

Hieracium jailanum, commonly known as the Kashmir hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium jailanum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
