Heracleum sphondylium subsp. algeriense

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

algeriense, commonly known as Algerian cowparsnip, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided lobes, and the flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbrella-like structures.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp, typical of the carrot family.
