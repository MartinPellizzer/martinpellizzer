Hieracium gemelliforme

Hieracium gemelliforme, commonly known as the twinleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets that resemble petals, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium gemelliforme is often found in open habitats and can sometimes form dense colonies.
