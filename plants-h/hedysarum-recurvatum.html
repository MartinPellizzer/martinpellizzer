Hedysarum recurvatum

Hedysarum recurvatum, commonly known as the curlleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Hedysarum recurvatum plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in restoration projects due to its hardiness.
