Hesperis pilosa

Hesperis pilosa, commonly known as the hoary stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant features hairy, grayish-green foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Hesperis pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
