Herminium tanguticum

Herminium tanguticum, commonly known as the Tangut orchid, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pale green or yellow, are adapted for pollination by insects.

Herminium tanguticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
