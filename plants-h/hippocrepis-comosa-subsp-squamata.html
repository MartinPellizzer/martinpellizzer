Hippocrepis comosa subsp. squamata

Hippocrepis comosa subsp.

squamata, commonly known as the squamate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its subspecific name, *squamata*, refers to the scaly or hairy texture of its leaves.

It is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.
