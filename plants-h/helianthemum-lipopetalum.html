Helianthemum lipopetalum

Helianthemum lipopetalum, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Helianthemum lipopetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
