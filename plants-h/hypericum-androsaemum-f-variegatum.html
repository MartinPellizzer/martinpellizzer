Hypericum androsaemum f. variegatum

Hypericum androsaemum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated St. John's wort, is a hybrid form of the genus Hypericum, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant typically features leaves with a striking variegation of green and white or yellow, enhancing its ornamental value. It is a shrub that produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
