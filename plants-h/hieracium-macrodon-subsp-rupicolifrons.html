Hieracium macrodon subsp. rupicolifrons

Hieracium macrodon subsp.

rupicolifrons, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of numerous small florets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
