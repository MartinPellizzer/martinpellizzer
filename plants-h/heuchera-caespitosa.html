Heuchera caespitosa

Heuchera caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a distinctive red or purple margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage and delicate floral display.
