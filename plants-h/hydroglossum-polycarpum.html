Hydroglossum polycarpum

Hydroglossum polycarpum, commonly known as the many-seeded water-plantain, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its roots help stabilize the sediment, contributing to water clarity and ecosystem health.

Hydroglossum polycarpum is valued for its role in wetland habitats and as a food source for aquatic invertebrates.
