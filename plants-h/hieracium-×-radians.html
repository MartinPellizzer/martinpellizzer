Hieracium × radians

Hieracium × radians, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered flowers.

Hieracium × radians is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth habits.
