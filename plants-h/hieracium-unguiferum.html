Hieracium unguiferum

Hieracium unguiferum, commonly known as the sticky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more linear. Its flowers are yellow and form dense clusters, often surrounded by bracts.

Hieracium unguiferum is known for its sticky secretions on the leaves and stems, which can adhere to animals and clothing.
