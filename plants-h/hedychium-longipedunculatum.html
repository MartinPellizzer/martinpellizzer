Hedychium longipedunculatum

Hedychium longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long pedunculate ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Hedychium longipedunculatum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its aromatic properties.
