Hieracium praecox subsp. subpinicola

Hieracium praecox subsp.

subpinicola, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, ribbed achenes as fruits.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems, glandular leaves, and distinctive floral morphology.
