Helichrysum harveyanum

Helichrysum harveyanum, commonly known as the Harvey's everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that dry into papery, golden bracts, giving it a long-lasting appearance. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum harveyanum is often used in floral arrangements due to its durability and aesthetic appeal after drying.
