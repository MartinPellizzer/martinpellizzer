Hieracium incisum subsp. laceridens

Hieracium incisum subsp.

laceridens, commonly known as the ragged hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant features deeply lobed, often ragged leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
