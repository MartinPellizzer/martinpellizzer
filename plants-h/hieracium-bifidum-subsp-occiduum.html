Hieracium bifidum subsp. occiduum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

occiduum, commonly known as the western lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its subspecific classification reflects its distinct geographical distribution and slight morphological differences from the nominate subspecies.
