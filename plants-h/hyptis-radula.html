Hyptis radula

Hyptis radula, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyptis radula is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
