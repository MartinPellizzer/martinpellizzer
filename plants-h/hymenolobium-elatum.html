Hymenolobium elatum

Hymenolobium elatum, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, iron-like wood, which is highly valued for its durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
