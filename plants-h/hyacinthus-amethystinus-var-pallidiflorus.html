Hyacinthus amethystinus var. pallidiflorus

Hyacinthus amethystinus var. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers of this variety are a pale lilac or almost white, distinguishing it from the more vibrant hues of the standard hyacinth. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its elegant appearance and its ability to thrive in a range of climatic conditions.
