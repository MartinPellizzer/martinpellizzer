Hymenoxys cooperi var. argyrea

Hymenoxys cooperi var. argyrea, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant produces showy, globe-shaped inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
