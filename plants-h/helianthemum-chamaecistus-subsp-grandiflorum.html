Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp. grandiflorum

Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant.
