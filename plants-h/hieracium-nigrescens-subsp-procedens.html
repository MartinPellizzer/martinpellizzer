Hieracium nigrescens subsp. procedens

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

procedens, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black central disk florets surrounded by yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
