Hypericum maculatum subf. lucidum

Hypericum maculatum subf.

lucidum, commonly known as the glossy St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often reddish or purple-hued, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.
