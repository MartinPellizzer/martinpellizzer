Hylotelephium jullianum

Hylotelephium jullianum, commonly known as the Julian's stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late summer, which are borne on branching inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its use in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
