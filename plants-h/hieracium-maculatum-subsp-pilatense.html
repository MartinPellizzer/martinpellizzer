Hieracium maculatum subsp. pilatense

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

pilatense, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
