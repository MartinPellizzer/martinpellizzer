Heteropogon pubescens

Heteropogon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy grass or hairy bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy blades that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

Heteropogon pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and delicate appearance.
