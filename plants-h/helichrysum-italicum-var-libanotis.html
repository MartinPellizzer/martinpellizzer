Helichrysum italicum var. libanotis

Helichrysum italicum var. libanotis, commonly known as the Lebanese everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, branched stems and small, yellowish to golden-orange flowers that are highly aromatic. The plant produces compact, fluffy seed heads that remain intact for a long time, giving it its common name. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy due to its essential oils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
