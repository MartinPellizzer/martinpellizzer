Hieracium nigrescens subsp. heterocalathium

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

heterocalathium, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple or black central disc florets, which contrast with the lighter outer petals.
