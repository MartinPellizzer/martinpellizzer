Hemiandra pungens var. linearis

Hemiandra pungens var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hemiandra, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline environments. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
