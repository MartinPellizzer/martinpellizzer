Hypericum uniflorum

Hypericum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered St. John's wort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Hypericum uniflorum is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
