Huperzia lockyeri

Huperzia lockyeri, commonly known as the Lockyer's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, upright, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Huperzia lockyeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and its role in ecological studies due to its unique reproductive structures.
