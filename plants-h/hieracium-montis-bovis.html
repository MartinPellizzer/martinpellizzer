Hieracium montis-bovis

Hieracium montis-bovis, commonly known as the mountain cow's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold environments. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and often appear in clusters.

Hieracium montis-bovis is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability in mountainous habitats.
