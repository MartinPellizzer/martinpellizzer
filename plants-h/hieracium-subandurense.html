Hieracium subandurense

Hieracium subandurense, commonly known as the Andean hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium subandurense is known for its hardy nature and is often found in mountainous regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.
