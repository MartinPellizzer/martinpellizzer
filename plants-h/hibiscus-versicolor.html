Hibiscus versicolor

Hibiscus versicolor, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that can range from white to pink, red, and even purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with dark green, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant hues.

Hibiscus versicolor prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
