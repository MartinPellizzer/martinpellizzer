Hibiscus riceae

Hibiscus riceae, commonly known as the Rice's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a prominent central throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hibiscus riceae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
