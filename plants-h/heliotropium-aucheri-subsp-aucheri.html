Heliotropium aucheri subsp. aucheri

Heliotropium aucheri subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the Persian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and long blooming period.
