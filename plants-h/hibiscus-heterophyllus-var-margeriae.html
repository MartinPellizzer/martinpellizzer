Hibiscus heterophyllus var. margeriae

Hibiscus heterophyllus var. margeriae, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant features palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus heterophyllus var. margeriae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
