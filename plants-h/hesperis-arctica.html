Hesperis arctica

Hesperis arctica, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in late summer.

Hesperis arctica is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
