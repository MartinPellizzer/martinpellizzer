Hieracium subulicuspis

Hieracium subulicuspis, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in diverse ecological communities.
