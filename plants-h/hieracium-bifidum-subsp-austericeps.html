Hieracium bifidum subsp. austericeps

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

austericeps, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are deeply lobed or divided, with a rough, hairy texture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single, elongated head, and the plant produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
