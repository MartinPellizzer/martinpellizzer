Hoodia pilifera

Hoodia pilifera, commonly known as the desert mouse ear, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in spring or early summer. Hoodia pilifera is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

It is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, thriving in regions with low precipitation.
