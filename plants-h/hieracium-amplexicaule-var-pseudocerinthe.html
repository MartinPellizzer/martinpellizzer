Hieracium amplexicaule var. pseudocerinthe

Hieracium amplexicaule var. pseudocerinthe, commonly known as the False Yellow Wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream sides. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
