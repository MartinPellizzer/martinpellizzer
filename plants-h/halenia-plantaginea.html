Halenia plantaginea

Halenia plantaginea, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Halenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are slender and upright.

Halenia plantaginea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
