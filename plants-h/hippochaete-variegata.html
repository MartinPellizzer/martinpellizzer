Hippochaete variegata

Hippochaete variegata, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hippochaete variegata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
