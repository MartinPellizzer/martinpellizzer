Hieracium incisiforme

Hieracium incisiforme, commonly known as the cut-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed or cut, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its growth habit is typically herbaceous.

Hieracium incisiforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
