Helictotrichon australe

Helictotrichon australe, commonly known as the Australian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
