Holigarna longifolia

Holigarna longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved holigarna, is a unique plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Holigarna longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its visual interest.
