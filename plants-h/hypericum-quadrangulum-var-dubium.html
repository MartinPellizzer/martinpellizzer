Hypericum quadrangulum var. dubium

Hypericum quadrangulum var. dubium, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
