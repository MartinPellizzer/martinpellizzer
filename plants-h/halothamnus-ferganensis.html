Halothamnus ferganensis

Halothamnus ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana halothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is adapted to saline and alkaline soils. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Halothamnus ferganensis is often found in rocky and steppe habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native regions.
