Hexalectris fallax

Hexalectris fallax, commonly known as the false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble those of the orchid genus Epidendrum. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, violet-colored blooms from late spring to early summer.
