Hermannia humilis

Hermannia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf hermannia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, clustered leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
