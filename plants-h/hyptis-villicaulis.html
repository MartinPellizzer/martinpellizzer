Hyptis villicaulis

Hyptis villicaulis, commonly known as the velvetleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis villicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
