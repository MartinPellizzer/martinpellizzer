Hierochloe monstruosa

Hierochloe monstruosa, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with delicate, grass-like flowers. Hierochloe monstruosa thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the extreme conditions of its mountainous habitat.
