Haemodorum polycephalum

Haemodorum polycephalum, commonly known as the black hill bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which is also known as the bloodroot family. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it often grows in dense clusters.

Haemodorum polycephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
