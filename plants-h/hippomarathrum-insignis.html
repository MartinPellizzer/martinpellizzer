Hippomarathrum insignis

Hippomarathrum insignis, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with a deep root system and thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Hippomarathrum insignis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar and habitat for various desert wildlife.
