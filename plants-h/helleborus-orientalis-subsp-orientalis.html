Helleborus orientalis subsp. orientalis

Helleborus orientalis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental hellebore, is a perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its evergreen, palmate leaves and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full shade.

The plant produces nodding, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often used in woodland gardens.

This subspecies is notable for its toxicity, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful if ingested.
