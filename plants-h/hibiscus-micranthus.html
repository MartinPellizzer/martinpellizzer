Hibiscus micranthus

Hibiscus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered hibiscus, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Hibiscus micranthus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
