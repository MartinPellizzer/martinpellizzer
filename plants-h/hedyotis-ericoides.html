Hedyotis ericoides

Hedyotis ericoides, commonly known as the heather-like geranium, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features dense clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

Hedyotis ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
