Hibiscus denudatus var. genuinus

Hibiscus denudatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the naked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a prominent yellow center. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests or near water sources.

Hibiscus denudatus var. genuinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
