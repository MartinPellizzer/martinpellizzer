Hieracium jurassicum subsp. lobeliifolium

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

lobeliifolium, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The subspecies lobeliifolium is distinguished by its lobed, lobeliifolium-like leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
