Hieracium haraldi

Hieracium haraldi, commonly known as the harald's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in grassy habitats and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, where it contributes to the floral diversity of these ecosystems.
