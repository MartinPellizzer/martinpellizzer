Helianthus tuberosus var. nebrascensis

Helianthus tuberosus var. nebrascensis, commonly known as the Nebraska sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its robust stems and large, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible tubers beneath the soil, which are similar in appearance to potatoes and are sometimes cultivated for food. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for beneficial insects.
