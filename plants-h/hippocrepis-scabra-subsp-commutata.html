Hippocrepis scabra subsp. commutata

Hippocrepis scabra subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has serrated, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
