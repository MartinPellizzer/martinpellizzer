Hedysarum fruticosum f. albiflorum

Hedysarum fruticosum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered brier vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

Hedysarum fruticosum f. albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
