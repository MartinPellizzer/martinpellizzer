Hyparrhenia barteri var. calvescens

Hyparrhenia barteri var. calvescens, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia barteri var. calvescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
