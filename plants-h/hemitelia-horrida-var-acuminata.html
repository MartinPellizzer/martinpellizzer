Hemitelia horrida var. acuminata

Hemitelia horrida var. acuminata, commonly known as the spiked hemitelia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and distinctive, spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
