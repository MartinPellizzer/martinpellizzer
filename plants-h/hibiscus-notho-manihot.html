Hibiscus notho-manihot

Hibiscus notho-manihot, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Hibiscus manihot and Hibiscus notho.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, including large, showy flowers and a robust growth habit. The plant is native to regions where its parent species naturally occur, typically in tropical and subtropical climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often deeply lobed, adding visual interest to landscapes.
