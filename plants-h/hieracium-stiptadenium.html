Hieracium stiptadenium

Hieracium stiptadenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium stiptadenium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
