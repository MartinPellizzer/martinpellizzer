Hieracium mattfeldianum

Hieracium mattfeldianum, commonly known as the Mattfeld's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.

Hieracium mattfeldianum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
