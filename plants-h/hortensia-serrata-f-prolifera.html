Hortensia serrata f. prolifera

Hortensia serrata f. prolifera, commonly known as the serrated hortensia or the Japanese hortensia, is a cultivar of the hydrangea plant known for its large, showy flower clusters.

It features broad, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a lush backdrop to its blooms. The plant produces inflorescences of white, blue, or pink flowers, depending on soil pH, which can be manipulated for color variation. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is noted for its vigorous growth and ability to produce multiple shoots, contributing to its ornamental value.
