Hieracium monregalense

Hieracium monregalense, commonly known as the Monregal hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Hieracium monregalense is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
