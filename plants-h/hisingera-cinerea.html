Hisingera cinerea

Hisingera cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, velvety leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Hisingera cinerea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
