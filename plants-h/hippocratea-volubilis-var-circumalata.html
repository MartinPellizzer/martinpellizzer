Hippocratea volubilis var. circumalata

Hippocratea volubilis var. circumalata, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces fleshy, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
