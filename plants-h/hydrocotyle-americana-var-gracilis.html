Hydrocotyle americana var. gracilis

Hydrocotyle americana var. gracilis, commonly known as slender water pennywort, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, with fine, silky hairs on the surface. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
