Hypericum japonicum var. maximowiczii

Hypericum japonicum var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
