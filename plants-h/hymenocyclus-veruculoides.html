Hymenocyclus veruculoides

Hymenocyclus veruculoides, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, globular shape with thick, ribbed stems that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Hymenocyclus veruculoides is well-suited for cultivation in rocky or sandy soils with minimal water requirements.
