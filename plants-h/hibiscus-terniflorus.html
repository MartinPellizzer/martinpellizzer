Hibiscus terniflorus

Hibiscus terniflorus, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in warm, sunny conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Hibiscus terniflorus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to withstand coastal environments.
