Hieracium austroamericanum

Hieracium austroamericanum, commonly known as the American hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium austroamericanum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
