Hieracium murorum subsp. osmundaceum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

osmundaceum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

The subspecies osmundaceum is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
