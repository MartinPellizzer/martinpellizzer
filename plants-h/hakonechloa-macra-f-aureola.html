Hakonechloa macra f. aureola

Hakonechloa macra f. aureola, commonly known as the golden hakonechloa, is a compact, low-growing evergreen grass that forms dense, mat-like clumps.

Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a striking golden-yellow coloration, especially along the edges and tips. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
