Helianthus diversifolius

Helianthus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its flowers are followed by distinctive, spiny seed heads that disperse its seeds by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
