Hydrangea peruviana

Hydrangea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage consists of serrated, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Hydrangea peruviana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
