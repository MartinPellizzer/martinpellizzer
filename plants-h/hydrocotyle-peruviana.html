Hydrocotyle peruviana

Hydrocotyle peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian water arum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often floating on the surface of water. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in shallow freshwater habitats.

Hydrocotyle peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
