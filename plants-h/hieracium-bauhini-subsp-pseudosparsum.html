Hieracium bauhini subsp. pseudosparsum

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

pseudosparsum, commonly known as the southern spearthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are longer than the disk florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, spiny morphology and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
