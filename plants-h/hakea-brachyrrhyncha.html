Hakea brachyrrhyncha

Hakea brachyrrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive bottle-shaped or urn-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. These flowers are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

Hakea brachyrrhyncha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.
