Hyptis melochioides

Hyptis melochioides, commonly known as Mexican hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis melochioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
