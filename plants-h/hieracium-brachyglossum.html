Hieracium brachyglossum

Hieracium brachyglossum, commonly known as the short-lipped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
