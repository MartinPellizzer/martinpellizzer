Heliotropium rogersii

Heliotropium rogersii, commonly known as the desert heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central tube. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
