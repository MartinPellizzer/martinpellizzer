Haworthia depauperata

Haworthia depauperata, commonly known as the Zebra Cactus, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its short, fleshy stems and distinctive, pale green leaves with white or yellowish markings that resemble a zebra's stripes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
