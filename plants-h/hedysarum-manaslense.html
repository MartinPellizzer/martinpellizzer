Hedysarum manaslense

Hedysarum manaslense, commonly known as the Manaslu vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some botanical gardens.
