Hypericum angustifolium

Hypericum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly serrated. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its medicinal properties, containing hypericin and other compounds with potential therapeutic uses.

Hypericum angustifolium thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
