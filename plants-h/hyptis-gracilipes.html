Hyptis gracilipes

Hyptis gracilipes, commonly known as slender hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and it produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

Hyptis gracilipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
