Horsfieldia longipedunculata

Horsfieldia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its roots for moisture and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Horsfieldia longipedunculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
