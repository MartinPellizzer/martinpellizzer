Helianthus praecox subsp. praecox

Helianthus praecox subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features upright, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller tubular florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It blooms in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in open woods, fields, and along streambanks, preferring moist to mesic soils.
