Hemipogon peruvianus

Hemipogon peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Hemipogon peruvianus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.
