Helmia pilosiuscula

Helmia pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy helmia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland gardens and stream banks.
