Helianthus nigrescens

Helianthus nigrescens, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically features yellow ray florets surrounding a dark, disk-like center, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Helianthus nigrescens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
