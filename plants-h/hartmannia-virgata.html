Hartmannia virgata

Hartmannia virgata, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinct red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Hartmannia virgata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
