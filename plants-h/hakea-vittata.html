Hakea vittata

Hakea vittata, commonly known as the yellow hakea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Hakea vittata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
