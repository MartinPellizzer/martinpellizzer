Hakea eryngioides

Hakea eryngioides, commonly known as the needle-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea eryngioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
