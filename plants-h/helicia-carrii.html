Helicia carrii

Helicia carrii, commonly known as the African holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. Helicia carrii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a desirable plant in both horticultural and conservation contexts.
