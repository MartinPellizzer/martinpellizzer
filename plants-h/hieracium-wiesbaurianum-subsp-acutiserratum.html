Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. acutiserratum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

acutiserratum, commonly known as the sharp-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
