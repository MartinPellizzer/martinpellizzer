Hemionitis paradoxa

Hemionitis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox plant, is a unique aquatic fern native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it can form dense mats on the water surface. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the leaves.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of water quality.
