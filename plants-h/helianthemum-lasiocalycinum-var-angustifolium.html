Helianthemum lasiocalycinum var. angustifolium

Helianthemum lasiocalycinum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the hot, dry conditions of its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
