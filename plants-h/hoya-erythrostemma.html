Hoya erythrostemma

Hoya erythrostemma, commonly known as the red-veined hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and waxy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers with prominent red veins, which bloom in summer. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental value.

Hoya erythrostemma thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.
