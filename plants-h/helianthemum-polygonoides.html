Helianthemum polygonoides

Helianthemum polygonoides, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant ideal for xeriscaping.
