Haloragis paucidentata

Haloragis paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers, which are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of lakes, where it can tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Haloragis paucidentata is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
