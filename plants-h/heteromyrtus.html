Heteromyrtus

Heteromyrtus, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plants typically have aromatic leaves and are shrubs or small trees, with some species reaching heights of up to 5 meters. They are adapted to a variety of soil types but generally prefer well-drained conditions.

Heteromyrtus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique floral display.
