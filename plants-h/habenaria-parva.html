Habenaria parva

Habenaria parva, commonly known as the small-lobed orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it produces a single spike of flowers with three to five blooms.

The orchid is noted for its intricate floral structure and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
