Hordeum secalinum var. chilense

Hordeum secalinum var. chilense, commonly known as Chilean barley, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its potential use in forage and conservation efforts due to its adaptability and resilience.
