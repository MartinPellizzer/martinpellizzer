Habenaria galeandriformis

Habenaria galeandriformis, commonly known as the orchid with the unique floral structure, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, often showy flowers that feature a distinctive lip and column. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it can receive the necessary light for photosynthesis. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and botanical interest.
