Hieracium glaucifolium

Hieracium glaucifolium, commonly known as the blue leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by pale green to blue-green leaves. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often colonizing disturbed soils.

Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive bluish-gray hue, contributing to its common name.
