Habenaria elatior

Habenaria elatior, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded forest environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers, with each flower having three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently modified into a lip.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and thrives in humid, warm climates.
