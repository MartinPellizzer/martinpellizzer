Hordeum transcaucasicum

Hordeum transcaucasicum, commonly known as Transcaucasian barley, is a wild grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
