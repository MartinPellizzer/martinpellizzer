Hibiscus lepidotus

Hibiscus lepidotus, commonly known as the African sandalwood or yellow hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming throughout the warmer months. The plant has rough, bark-like stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Hibiscus lepidotus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
