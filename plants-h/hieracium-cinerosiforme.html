Hieracium cinerosiforme

Hieracium cinerosiforme, commonly known as the sooty hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a sooty appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
