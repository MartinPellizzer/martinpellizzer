Hieracium involutiforme

Hieracium involutiforme, commonly known as the curledleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive rolled or curled appearance, contributing to its common name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.
