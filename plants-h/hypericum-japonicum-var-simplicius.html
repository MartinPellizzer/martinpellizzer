Hypericum japonicum var. simplicius

Hypericum japonicum var. simplicius, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
