Heteropterys macrocarpa

Heteropterys macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited pittosporum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by large, showy orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its attractive foliage and fruit.
