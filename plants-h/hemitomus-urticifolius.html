Hemitomus urticifolius

Hemitomus urticifolius, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Hemitomus urticifolius is a perennial plant that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with a fibrous root system that helps it survive in various environmental conditions.
