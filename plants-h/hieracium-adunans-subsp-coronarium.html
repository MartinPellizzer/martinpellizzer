Hieracium adunans subsp. coronarium

Hieracium adunans subsp.

coronarium, commonly known as the crown hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
