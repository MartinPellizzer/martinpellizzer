Hieracium praetenerum subsp. persimile

Hieracium praetenerum subsp.

persimile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The subspecies persimile is distinguished by its slightly smaller flower heads and broader leaves compared to other members of the species.
