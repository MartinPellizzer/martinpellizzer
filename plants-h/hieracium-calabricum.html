Hieracium calabricum

Hieracium calabricum, commonly known as the Calabrian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray florets surrounded by a cluster of tubular disc florets.

Hieracium calabricum is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
