Hiraea tomentosa

Hiraea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hiraea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts.
