Hieracium ochroleuciforme

Hieracium ochroleuciforme, commonly known as the yellow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Hieracium ochroleuciforme is typically perennial and thrives in temperate climates.
