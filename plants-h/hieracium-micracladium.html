Hieracium micracladium

Hieracium micracladium, commonly known as the small clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sunny conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowering stems are usually short and often lie close to the ground.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.
