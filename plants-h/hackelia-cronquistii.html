Hackelia cronquistii

Hackelia cronquistii, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, spherical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
