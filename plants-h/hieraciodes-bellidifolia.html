Hieraciodes bellidifolia

Hieraciodes bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its yellow flowers develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
