Hydrangea involucrata f. plenissima

Hydrangea involucrata f. plenissima, commonly known as the snowball hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan. The plant thrives in partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
