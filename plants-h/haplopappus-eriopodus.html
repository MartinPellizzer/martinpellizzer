Haplopappus eriopodus

Haplopappus eriopodus, commonly known as the roughleaf horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally hairy, giving it a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
