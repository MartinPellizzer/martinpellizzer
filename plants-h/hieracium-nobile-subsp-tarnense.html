Hieracium nobile subsp. tarnense

Hieracium nobile subsp.

tarnense, commonly known as the noble hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ornamental value in garden settings.
