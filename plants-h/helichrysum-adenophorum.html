Helichrysum adenophorum

Helichrysum adenophorum, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum adenophorum is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
