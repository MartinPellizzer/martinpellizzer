Haplopappus rigidifolius

Haplopappus rigidifolius, commonly known as the rigidleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are rigid and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and early summer.
