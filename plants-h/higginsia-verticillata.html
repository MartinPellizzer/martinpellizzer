Higginsia verticillata

Higginsia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Higginsia verticillata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
