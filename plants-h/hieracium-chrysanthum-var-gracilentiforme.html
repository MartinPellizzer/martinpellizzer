Hieracium chrysanthum var. gracilentiforme

Hieracium chrysanthum var. gracilentiforme, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
