Huperzia callitrichifolia

Huperzia callitrichifolia, commonly known as the creeping huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette-like pattern. It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is typically found in forest understories.

Huperzia callitrichifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
