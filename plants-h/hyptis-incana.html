Hyptis incana

Hyptis incana, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of white or purple.

Hyptis incana is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.
