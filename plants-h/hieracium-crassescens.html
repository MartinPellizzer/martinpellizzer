Hieracium crassescens

Hieracium crassescens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium crassescens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to moderate grazing.
