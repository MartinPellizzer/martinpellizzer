Hieracium coloriscapum subsp. parunicum

Hieracium coloriscapum subsp.

parunicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has linear, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, with seeds dispersed by light, feathery appendages.
