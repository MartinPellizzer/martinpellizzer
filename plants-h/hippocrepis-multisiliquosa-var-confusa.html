Hippocrepis multisiliquosa var. confusa

Hippocrepis multisiliquosa var. confusa, commonly known as the Scottish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, often in calcareous soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the stems are erect and branched.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
