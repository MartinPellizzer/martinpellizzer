Hieracioides andryaloides

Hieracioides andryaloides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer.

The species is often found in crevices and on slopes, where it contributes to stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
