Heliotropium anchusifolium f. medium

Heliotropium anchusifolium f. medium, commonly known as the medium-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, violet or purple flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
