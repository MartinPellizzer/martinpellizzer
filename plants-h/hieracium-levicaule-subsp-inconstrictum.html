Hieracium levicaule subsp. inconstrictum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

inconstrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its flowers are yellow and form dense, flat clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
