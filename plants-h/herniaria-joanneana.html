Herniaria joanneana

Herniaria joanneana, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in rocky, coastal, or sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Herniaria joanneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry, sunny areas.
