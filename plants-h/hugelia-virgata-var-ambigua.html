Hugelia virgata var. ambigua

Hugelia virgata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous hugelia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to warm climates.
