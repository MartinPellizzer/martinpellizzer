Hieracium cymaterum

Hieracium cymaterum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure.
