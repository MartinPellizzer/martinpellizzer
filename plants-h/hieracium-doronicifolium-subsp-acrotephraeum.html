Hieracium doronicifolium subsp. acrotephraeum

Hieracium doronicifolium subsp.

acrotephraeum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in alpine ecosystems.
