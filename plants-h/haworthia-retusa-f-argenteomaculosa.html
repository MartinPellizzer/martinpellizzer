Haworthia retusa f. argenteomaculosa

Haworthia retusa f. argenteomaculosa, commonly known as the silver-spotted Haworthia, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically a mix of green and silver, with distinctive white or pale spots. The leaves are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires minimal watering, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.
