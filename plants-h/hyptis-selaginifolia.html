Hyptis selaginifolia

Hyptis selaginifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis selaginifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
