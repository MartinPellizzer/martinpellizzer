Holodiscus argenteus var. matudae

Holodiscus argenteus var. matudae, commonly known as the silverleaf holodiscus, is a rare plant species native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered together. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
