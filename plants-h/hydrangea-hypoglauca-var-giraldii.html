Hydrangea hypoglauca var. giraldii

Hydrangea hypoglauca var. giraldii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre hydrangea, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves with a glossy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
