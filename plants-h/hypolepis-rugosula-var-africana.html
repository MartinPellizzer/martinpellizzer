Hypolepis rugosula var. africana

Hypolepis rugosula var. africana, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a perennial fern native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
