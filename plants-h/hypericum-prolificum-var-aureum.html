Hypericum prolificum var. aureum

Hypericum prolificum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden St. John's wort, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens. The plant has narrow, upright stems covered in small, glandular dots, which are characteristic of the Hypericum genus. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a yellowish-green hue.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value.
