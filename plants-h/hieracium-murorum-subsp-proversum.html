Hieracium murorum subsp. proversum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

proversum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets.
