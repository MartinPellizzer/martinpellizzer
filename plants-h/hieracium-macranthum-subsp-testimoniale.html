Hieracium macranthum subsp. testimoniale

Hieracium macranthum subsp.

testimoniale, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
