Hieracium amplum

Hieracium amplum, commonly known as the greater hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, flat head.

Hieracium amplum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
