Hippeastrum fluviale

Hippeastrum fluviale, commonly known as the river amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can bloom multiple times a year under proper care.

The flowers have a strong, spicy fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
