Huernia recondita

Huernia recondita, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, ribbed stems that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

Huernia recondita is popular among cactus and succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
