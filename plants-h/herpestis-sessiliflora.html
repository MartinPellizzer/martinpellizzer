Herpestis sessiliflora

Herpestis sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered herb, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
