Helianthemum apenninum subsp. estevei

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

estevei, commonly known as the Apennine sandverbena, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has aromatic leaves with a distinctive scent, often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
