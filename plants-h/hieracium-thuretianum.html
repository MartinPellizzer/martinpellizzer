Hieracium thuretianum

Hieracium thuretianum, commonly known as the Thuret's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is considered a minor weed in some agricultural settings.
