Helianthus invenustus

Helianthus invenustus, commonly known as the showy sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. Its botanical features include ray florets that are typically orange to yellow in color.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
