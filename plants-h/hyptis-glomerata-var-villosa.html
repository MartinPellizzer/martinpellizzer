Hyptis glomerata var. villosa

Hyptis glomerata var. villosa, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers, which are often arranged in compact inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis glomerata var. villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
