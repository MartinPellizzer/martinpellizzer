Hieracium glaucoprasinum

Hieracium glaucoprasinum, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a greenish tinge.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
