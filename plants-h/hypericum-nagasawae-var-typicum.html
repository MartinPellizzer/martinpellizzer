Hypericum nagasawae var. typicum

Hypericum nagasawae var. typicum, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are bright yellow with orange-red glands and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
