Hieracium murorum subsp. perviride

Hieracium murorum subsp.

perviride, commonly known as the green field hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is upright and branched. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
