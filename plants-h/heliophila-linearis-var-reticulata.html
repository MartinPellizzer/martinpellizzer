Heliophila linearis var. reticulata

Heliophila linearis var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette and have a distinct net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in aquatic environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic insects and supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
