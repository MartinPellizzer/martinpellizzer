Helianthus trilobatus

Helianthus trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous plant with a robust stem and large, yellow-orange flower heads. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It produces composite flowers that consist of a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
