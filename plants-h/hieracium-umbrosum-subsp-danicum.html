Hieracium umbrosum subsp. danicum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

danicum, commonly known as the Danube hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and wet grasslands, preferring temperate climates.

The plant features basal rosettes of broadly lanceolate leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with numerous small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats.
