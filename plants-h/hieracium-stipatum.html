Hieracium stipatum

Hieracium stipatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium stipatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
