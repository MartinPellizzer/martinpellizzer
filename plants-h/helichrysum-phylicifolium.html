Helichrysum phylicifolium

Helichrysum phylicifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting or sweet everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that are tightly packed in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum phylicifolium is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
