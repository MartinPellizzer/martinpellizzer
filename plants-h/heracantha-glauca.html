Heracantha glauca

Heracantha glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Heracantha, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a waxy surface that reflects light, giving the plant a distinct appearance.

Heracantha glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
