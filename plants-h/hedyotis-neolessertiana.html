Hedyotis neolessertiana

Hedyotis neolessertiana, commonly known as the lesser flameplant, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bright orange to red flowers clustered in spikes. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that is often hairy.

Hedyotis neolessertiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
