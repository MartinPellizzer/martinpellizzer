Hieracium solitarium

Hieracium solitarium, commonly known as the solitary hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant produces achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium solitarium is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
