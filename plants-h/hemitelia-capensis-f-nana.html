Hemitelia capensis f. nana

Hemitelia capensis f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf hemitelia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant, ornamental plants.
