Hieracium auriculoides subsp. huljakii

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

huljakii, commonly known as the Huljak's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellowish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and produces small, disk-shaped flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
