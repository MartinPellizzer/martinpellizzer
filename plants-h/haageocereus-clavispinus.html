Haageocereus clavispinus

Haageocereus clavispinus, commonly known as the spiked night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or yellowish spines. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and botanical collections.
