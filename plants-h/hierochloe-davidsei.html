Hierochloe davidsei

Hierochloe davidsei, commonly known as David's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant has fine, slender leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in alpine ecosystems.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
