Hyparrhenia diplandra

Hyparrhenia diplandra, commonly known as African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including poor and sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silvery or white bristles, giving it its common name. Hyparrhenia diplandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Additionally, it plays a role in traditional practices and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
