Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. bridelianum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

bridelianum, commonly known as the bridelian hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it has a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
