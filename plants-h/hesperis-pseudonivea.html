Hesperis pseudonivea

Hesperis pseudonivea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hesperis pseudonivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
