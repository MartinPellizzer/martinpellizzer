Hyacinthus pallidiflorus

Hyacinthus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered hyacinth, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms on slender stems, which are often used in floral arrangements. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hyacinthus pallidiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
