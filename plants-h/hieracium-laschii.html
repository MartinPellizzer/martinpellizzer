Hieracium laschii

Hieracium laschii, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia, including Siberia and the Far East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

Hieracium laschii thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in tundra and meadow habitats.
