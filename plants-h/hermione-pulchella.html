Hermione pulchella

Hermione pulchella, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale blue. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
