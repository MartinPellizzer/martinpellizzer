Helictochloa praetutiana subsp. rigida

Helictochloa praetutiana subsp.

rigida, commonly known as rigid hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetland or marshy habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single, compact spikelet that is often covered in short, rigid hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
