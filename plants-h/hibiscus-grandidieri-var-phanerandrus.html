Hibiscus grandidieri var. phanerandrus

Hibiscus grandidieri var. phanerandrus, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a unique species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in a thick, white layer.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native habitat.
