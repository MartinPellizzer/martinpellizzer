Hexacentris mysorensis

Hexacentris mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinct lip, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
