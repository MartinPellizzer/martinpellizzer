Hieracium glaucellum

Hieracium glaucellum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium glaucellum thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadside areas.
