Hypericum maculatum f. luteum

Hypericum maculatum f. luteum, commonly known as the spotted yellow St. John's wort, is a variant of the common St. John's wort.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has small, elliptical leaves and produces yellow-orange berries in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and medicinal properties.
