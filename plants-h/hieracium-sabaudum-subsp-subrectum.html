Hieracium sabaudum subsp. subrectum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

subrectum, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
