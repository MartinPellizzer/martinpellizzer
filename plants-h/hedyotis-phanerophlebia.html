Hedyotis phanerophlebia

Hedyotis phanerophlebia, commonly known as the showy inchplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
