Hibiscus tetralocularis

Hibiscus tetralocularis, commonly known as the four-chambered hibiscus, is a species native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.
