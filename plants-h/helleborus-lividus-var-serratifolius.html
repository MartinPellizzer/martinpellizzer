Helleborus lividus var. serratifolius

Helleborus lividus var. serratifolius, commonly known as the serratedleaf hellebore, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is often found in calcareous soils.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is a sought-after plant in horticulture.
