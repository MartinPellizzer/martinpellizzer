Helictotrichon pratense f. sericeum

Helictotrichon pratense f. sericeum, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, blue-green spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
