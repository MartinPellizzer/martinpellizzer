Hieracium murorum subsp. longifrons

Hieracium murorum subsp.

longifrons, commonly known as the long-fringed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow flower heads and longer bracts compared to other hawkweed species.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
