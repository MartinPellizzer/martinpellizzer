Hemerocallis exaltata

Hemerocallis exaltata, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features sword-shaped, dark green leaves that are stiff and upright. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, which bloom for a single day before wilting. It is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Hemerocallis exaltata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
