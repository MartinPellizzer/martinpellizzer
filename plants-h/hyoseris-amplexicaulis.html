Hyoseris amplexicaulis

Hyoseris amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers. The plant features alternate, clasping leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyoseris amplexicaulis is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowers.
