Hoodia currorii subsp. lugardii

Hoodia currorii subsp.

lugardii, commonly known as the desert apple or hoodia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system to access underground moisture.

Hoodia lugardii is known for its unique chemical composition, including compounds that have been studied for potential appetite-suppressing properties.
