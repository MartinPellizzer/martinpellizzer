Heisteria parviflora

Heisteria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered heister, is a member of the Primulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heisteria parviflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
