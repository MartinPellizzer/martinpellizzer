Hieracium epodum

Hieracium epodum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy as well.

Hieracium epodum is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
