Hieracium pseudodiminuens

Hieracium pseudodiminuens, commonly known as the diminutive hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
