Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. acrocymum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

acrocymum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered stems and yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its distinctive feature is the bright red, elongated fruits that mature in late summer.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
