Helichrysum fastigiatum

Helichrysum fastigiatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for actual flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
