Hymenopus reticulatus

Hymenopus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined fern, is a species of fern characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Hymenophylleaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with delicate, bipinnate leaves that have a distinctive reticulated venation pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
