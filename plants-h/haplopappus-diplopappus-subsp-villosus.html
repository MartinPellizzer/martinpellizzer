Haplopappus diplopappus subsp. villosus

Haplopappus diplopappus subsp.

villosus, commonly known as the hairy pappus plant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of yellow ray florets surrounded by a pappus of bristles, aiding in wind pollination.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
