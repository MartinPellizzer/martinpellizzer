Hypericum sampsonii

Hypericum sampsonii, commonly known as the Sampson's St. John's Wort, is a member of the Hypericaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
