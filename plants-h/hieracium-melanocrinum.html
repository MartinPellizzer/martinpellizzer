Hieracium melanocrinum

Hieracium melanocrinum, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive dark center.

Hieracium melanocrinum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some regions.
