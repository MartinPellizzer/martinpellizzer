Hebe pauciramosa var. masoniae

Hebe pauciramosa var. masoniae, commonly known as the Mason's hebe, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
