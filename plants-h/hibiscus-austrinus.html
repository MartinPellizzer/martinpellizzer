Hibiscus austrinus

Hibiscus austrinus, commonly known as the Australian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus austrinus thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found near water sources such as rivers and coastal areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
