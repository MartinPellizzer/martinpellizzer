Harpalyce angustiflora

Harpalyce angustiflora, commonly known as narrow-flowered harpalyce, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters.

Harpalyce angustiflora is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
