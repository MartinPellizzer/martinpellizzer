Homalium ciliatum

Homalium ciliatum, commonly known as the silky tree or silky wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves with fine, silky hairs along the veins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
