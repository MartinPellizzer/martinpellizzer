Hariota triquetra

Hariota triquetra, commonly known as the three-cornered leek, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, succulent leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hariota triquetra is valued for its nutritional content and has been used historically for its purported health benefits.
