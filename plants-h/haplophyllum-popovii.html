Haplophyllum popovii

Haplophyllum popovii, commonly known as the Mongolian rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with woody stems and small, often hairy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor, rocky soils.

Haplophyllum popovii is well-suited to harsh environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
