Holcus muticus

Holcus muticus, commonly known as meadow brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.
