Hydrangea arborescens var. cordata

Hydrangea arborescens var. cordata, commonly known as the snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and easy-to-care-for ornamental plant.
