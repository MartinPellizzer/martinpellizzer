Hieracium pseuderiopodum

Hieracium pseuderiopodum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
