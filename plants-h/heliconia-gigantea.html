Heliconia gigantea

Heliconia gigantea, commonly known as the giant heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful, bract-like flowers that form a spiral structure. The plant's leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Heliconia gigantea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by birds and bats, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
