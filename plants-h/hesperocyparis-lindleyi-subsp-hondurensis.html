Hesperocyparis lindleyi subsp. hondurensis

Hesperocyparis lindleyi subsp.

hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran incense cedar, is a member of the cypress family (Cupressaceae). It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in the humid lowlands of Central America.

The plant is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, rounded cones. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic wood and is used in traditional crafts and medicine.
