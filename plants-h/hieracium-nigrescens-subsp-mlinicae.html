Hieracium nigrescens subsp. mlinicae

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

mlinicae, commonly known as the Mlinica hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate, with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in horticultural settings.
