Hieracium lachenalii subsp. subirriguum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

subirriguum, commonly known as the Alaskan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of ray flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to cool, temperate climates and its role in local ecosystems.
