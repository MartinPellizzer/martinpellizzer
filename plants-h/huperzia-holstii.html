Huperzia holstii

Huperzia holstii, commonly known as the Holst's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its compact stature and dense, spore-bearing cones. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Huperzia holstii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
