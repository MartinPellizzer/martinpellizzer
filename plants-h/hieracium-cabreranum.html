Hieracium cabreranum

Hieracium cabreranum, commonly known as the Cabrera's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium cabreranum is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.
