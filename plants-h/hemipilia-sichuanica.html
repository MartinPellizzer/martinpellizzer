Hemipilia sichuanica

Hemipilia sichuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan hemipilia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several small, white to pale purple flowers.

Hemipilia sichuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
