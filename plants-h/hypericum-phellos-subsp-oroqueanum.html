Hypericum phellos subsp. oroqueanum

Hypericum phellos subsp.

oroqueanum, commonly known as the Oroquean St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
