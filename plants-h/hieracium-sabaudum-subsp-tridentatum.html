Hieracium sabaudum subsp. tridentatum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

tridentatum, commonly known as the Trident-leaved Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a tridentate (three-toothed) appearance, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, hairy achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
