Helianthus petiolaris var. patens

Helianthus petiolaris var. patens, commonly known as the prairie sunflower, is a native North American plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, yellow, daisy-like flowers.

It typically grows in open prairies and dry soils, showcasing a robust root system that helps it thrive in harsh conditions. The plant produces showy blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
