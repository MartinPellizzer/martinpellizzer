Haarera

Haarera, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a coastal plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Haarera has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and conservation efforts.
