Hordeum vulgare var. viride

Hordeum vulgare var. viride, commonly known as green barley, is a variant of the common barley species.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish hue, which is more pronounced than in typical barley varieties. This plant is often cultivated for its young shoots, which are used in various culinary and nutritional applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of green barley include narrow leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
