Hieracium furcatum

Hieracium furcatum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium furcatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
