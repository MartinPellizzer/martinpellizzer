Heuchera chlorantha

Heuchera chlorantha, commonly known as the greenleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering ornamental value both in bloom and when dormant.
