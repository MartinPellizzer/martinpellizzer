Hippomarathrum fedtschenkoi

Hippomarathrum fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, fleshy leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and water-storing tissues. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to colonize new areas efficiently.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
