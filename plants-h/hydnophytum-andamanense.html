Hydnophytum andamanense

Hydnophytum andamanense, commonly known as the Andaman climbing bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant exhibits a semi-epiphytic growth habit, often growing on the surfaces of submerged or floating vegetation. Its leaves are typically thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient water flow and light absorption.

Hydnophytum andamanense plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling in freshwater ecosystems.
