Hieracium praelongum

Hieracium praelongum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
