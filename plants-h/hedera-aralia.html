Hedera aralia

Hedera aralia, commonly known as American ivy, is a climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dark green, lobed leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
