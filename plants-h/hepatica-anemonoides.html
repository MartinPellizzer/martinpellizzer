Hepatica anemonoides

Hepatica anemonoides, commonly known as the blue hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers, which range in color from blue to white and pink, typically appear before the leaves fully expand. This plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable component of forest understory ecosystems.
