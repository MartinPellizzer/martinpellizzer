Hieracium murorum subsp. tanyglochin

Hieracium murorum subsp.

tanyglochin, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, typically growing in meadows and grasslands.

The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow disc flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
