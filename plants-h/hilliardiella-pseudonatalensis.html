Hilliardiella pseudonatalensis

Hilliardiella pseudonatalensis, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
