Hieracium cochlearifolium subsp. pseudoglanduliferum

Hieracium cochlearifolium subsp.

pseudoglanduliferum, commonly known as the false glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, often with glandular dots, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.
