Haplostachys grayana var. angustifolia

Haplostachys grayana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers in racemate clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a desirable choice for native plant gardens.
