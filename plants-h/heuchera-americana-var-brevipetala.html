Heuchera americana var. brevipetala

Heuchera americana var. brevipetala, commonly known as the American alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves that are often adorned with red or purple margins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and early bloom make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
