Hibiscus barbadensis deciduus

Hibiscus barbadensis deciduus, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to deep red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces fibrous, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Hibiscus barbadensis deciduus thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
