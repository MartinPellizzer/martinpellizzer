Hemipilia luteola

Hemipilia luteola, commonly known as the yellow hemipilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Hemipilia luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
