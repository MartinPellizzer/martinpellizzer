Hieracium calcareum subsp. papperitzii

Hieracium calcareum subsp.

papperitzii, commonly known as the limestone hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in calcareous soils, such as limestone-rich meadows and grasslands, and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually hairy and lanceolate in shape. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are arranged in a single capitulum with many small florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its ability to tolerate nutrient-poor conditions.
