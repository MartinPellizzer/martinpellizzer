Hieracium hercegovinum

Hieracium hercegovinum, commonly known as the Herzegovina hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Hieracium hercegovinum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like blooms.
