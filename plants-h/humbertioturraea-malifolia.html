Humbertioturraea malifolia

Humbertioturraea malifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are sweet and aromatic. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
