Hieracium murorum subsp. separatidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

separatidens, commonly known as the separated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a basal rosette of leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces yellow flowers in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, which includes a distinct separation of the leaf margins.
