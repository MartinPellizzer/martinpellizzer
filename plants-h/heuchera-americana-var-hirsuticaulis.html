Heuchera americana var. hirsuticaulis

Heuchera americana var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, distinguishing it from other heuchera varieties. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, heart-shaped, and vary in color from green to reddish or purple.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
