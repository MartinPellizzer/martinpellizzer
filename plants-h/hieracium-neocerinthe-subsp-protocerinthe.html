Hieracium neocerinthe subsp. protocerinthe

Hieracium neocerinthe subsp.

protocerinthe, commonly known as the lesser yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has basal and upright stems, with leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and is often found in moist or calcareous habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Hieracium genus, which includes many similar yellow-flowered species.
