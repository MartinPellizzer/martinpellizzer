Hieracium eusciadium

Hieracium eusciadium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium eusciadium is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
