Haplopappus scopulorum var. hirtellus

Haplopappus scopulorum var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy cliff-foxtail, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy texture and adaptation to harsh, dry conditions.
