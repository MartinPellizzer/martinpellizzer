Hemionitis incana

Hemionitis incana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hemionitis incana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
