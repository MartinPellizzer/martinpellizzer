Hieracium pannonicum subsp. thracicum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

thracicum, commonly known as the Thrace hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and lanceolate leaves, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
