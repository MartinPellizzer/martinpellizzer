Hordeum microcladum

Hordeum microcladum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a small annual grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, compact growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hordeum microcladum is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
