Hosta capitata f. albiflora

Hosta capitata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the clustered hosta, is a cultivar characterized by its striking white blooms.

It is a variant of Hosta capitata, which is native to Japan and belongs to the Asparagaceae family. The plant features broad, dark green leaves that form a dense, clumping foliage. Its flowers are borne in loose clusters and are typically fragrant, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This cultivar is well-suited for shaded areas and is prized for its unique color contrast.
