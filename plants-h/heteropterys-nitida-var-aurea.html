Heteropterys nitida var. aurea

Heteropterys nitida var. aurea, commonly known as the golden leaf huito, is a striking plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that display a vibrant golden-green hue, especially under direct sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and dense foliage.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, valued for their potential therapeutic properties.
