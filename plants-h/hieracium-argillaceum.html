Hieracium argillaceum

Hieracium argillaceum, commonly known as the clay cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, often lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and stems that rise from the center.

Hieracium argillaceum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
