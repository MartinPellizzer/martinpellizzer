Hieracium pellaeum

Hieracium pellaeum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with toothed edges, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium pellaeum is typically found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
