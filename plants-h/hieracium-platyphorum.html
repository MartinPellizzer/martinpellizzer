Hieracium platyphorum

Hieracium platyphorum, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and usually branched. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium platyphorum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
