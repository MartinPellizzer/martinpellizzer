Hicorius ovalis var. megacarpa

Hicorius ovalis var. megacarpa, commonly known as the giant-seeded hibiscus, is a distinctive variant of the hibiscus family characterized by its large, fleshy fruits.

The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth, waxy texture. The flowers are showy, with vibrant colors ranging from red to orange, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit structure, which can reach significant sizes compared to other hibiscus species.
