Hieracium piliscapum

Hieracium piliscapum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Hieracium piliscapum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
