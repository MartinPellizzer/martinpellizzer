Habenaria ernesti-ulei

Habenaria ernesti-ulei, commonly known as the Ule's beard orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and decaying organic matter.

The orchid's unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
