Helichrysum linifolium

Helichrysum linifolium, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant is known for its ability to retain its color and form when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its leaf structure, with "linifolium" referring to its linen-like leaves.
