Hypericum humifusum var. montanum

Hypericum humifusum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It features woody stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, yellowish hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a slightly rough texture and a reddish or purplish hue on the underside.

This variety is often found in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
