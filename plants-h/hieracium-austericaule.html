Hieracium austericaule

Hieracium austericaule, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that are typically hairy and often branching. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which is typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium austericaule is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.
