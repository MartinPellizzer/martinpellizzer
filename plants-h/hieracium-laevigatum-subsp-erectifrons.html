Hieracium laevigatum subsp. erectifrons

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

erectifrons, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its upright growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
