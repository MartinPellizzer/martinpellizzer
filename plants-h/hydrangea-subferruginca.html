Hydrangea subferruginca

Hydrangea subferruginca, commonly known as the rusty hydrangea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or even red, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and features broad, ovate leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hydrangea subferruginca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
