Hyssopus angustifolius

Hyssopus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported antiseptic and expectorant properties.

The leaves and flowers are harvested during the flowering season for culinary and medicinal purposes.
