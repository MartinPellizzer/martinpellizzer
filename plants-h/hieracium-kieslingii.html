Hieracium kieslingii

Hieracium kieslingii, commonly known as the Kiesling's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium kieslingii is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
