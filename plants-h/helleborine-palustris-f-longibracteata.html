Helleborine palustris f. longibracteata

Helleborine palustris f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to wetland and marshy habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent bracts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in peat bogs or along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical features include a slender rhizome and a single, erect flowering stem.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptation to wetland conditions.
