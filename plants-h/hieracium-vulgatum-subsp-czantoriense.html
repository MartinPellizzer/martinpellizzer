Hieracium vulgatum subsp. czantoriense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

czantoriense, commonly known as the Czantori bent grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained to slightly wet soils.

The plant has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
