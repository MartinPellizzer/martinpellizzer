Harpullia pendula

Harpullia pendula, commonly known as the weeping harpullia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in pendulous racemes.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
