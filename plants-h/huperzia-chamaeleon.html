Huperzia chamaeleon

Huperzia chamaeleon, commonly known as the creeping huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered growth habit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on the underside of its leaves.

Huperzia chamaeleon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
