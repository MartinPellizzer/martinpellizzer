Hibiscus trionum

Hibiscus trionum, commonly known as the common yellow woodflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a prominent dark throat. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Hibiscus trionum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
