Hibiscus fluminis-idenburgii

Hibiscus fluminis-idenburgii, commonly known as the Idenburg's hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while the stems are woody and covered in fine pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
