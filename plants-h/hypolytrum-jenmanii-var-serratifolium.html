Hypolytrum jenmanii var. serratifolium

Hypolytrum jenmanii var. serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated-leaf hypolytrum, is a flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy inflorescences. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators.
