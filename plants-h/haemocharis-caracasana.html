Haemocharis caracasana

Haemocharis caracasana, commonly known as the flame flower or Caribbean lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red, often with a contrasting purple throat. The plant features broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Haemocharis caracasana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
