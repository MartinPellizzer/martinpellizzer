Hieracium transiens subsp. excentricum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

excentricum, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.
