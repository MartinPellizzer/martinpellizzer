Hierochloe brunonis

Hierochloe brunonis, commonly known as purple hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence, which adds ornamental value to its appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Hierochloe brunonis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
