Hieracium murorum subsp. atratum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atratum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker coloration and more robust growth compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

The plant is considered a weed in agricultural settings but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
