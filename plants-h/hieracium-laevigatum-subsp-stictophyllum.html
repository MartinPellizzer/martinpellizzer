Hieracium laevigatum subsp. stictophyllum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

stictophyllum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower. Its flowers are small, yellowish to white, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
