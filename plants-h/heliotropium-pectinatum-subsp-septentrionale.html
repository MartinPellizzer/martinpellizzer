Heliotropium pectinatum subsp. septentrionale

Heliotropium pectinatum subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as the northern heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive scent. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
