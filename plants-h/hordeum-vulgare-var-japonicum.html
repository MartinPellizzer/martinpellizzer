Hordeum vulgare var. japonicum

Hordeum vulgare var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese barley, is a variant of barley that is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller grains compared to common barley. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that bear the small, pale grains.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
