Heritiera tinctorum

Heritiera tinctorum, commonly known as the Indian dye plant, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its woody shrub structure with alternate, ovate leaves. The plant is notable for its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forested areas.
