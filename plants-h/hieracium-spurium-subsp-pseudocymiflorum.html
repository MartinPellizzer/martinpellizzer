Hieracium spurium subsp. pseudocymiflorum

Hieracium spurium subsp.

pseudocymiflorum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that produces seeds through wind dispersal, contributing to its widespread distribution.
