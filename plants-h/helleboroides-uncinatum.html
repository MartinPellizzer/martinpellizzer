Helleboroides uncinatum

Helleboroides uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and striking, hooded flowers that bloom in late spring. The flowers typically range in color from pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
