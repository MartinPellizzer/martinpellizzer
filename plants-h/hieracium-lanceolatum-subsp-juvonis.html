Hieracium lanceolatum subsp. juvonis

Hieracium lanceolatum subsp.

juvonis, commonly known as the lance-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
