Hugeria randaiensis

Hugeria randaiensis, commonly known as the Southern Chilean fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly found in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.
