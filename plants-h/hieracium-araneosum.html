Hieracium araneosum

Hieracium araneosum, commonly known as the spider hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium araneosum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
