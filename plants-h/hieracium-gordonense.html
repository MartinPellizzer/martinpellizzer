Hieracium gordonense

Hieracium gordonense, commonly known as Gordon's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
