Hyptis subverticillata

Hyptis subverticillata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Hyptis subverticillata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
