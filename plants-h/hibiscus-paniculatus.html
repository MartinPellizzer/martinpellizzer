Hibiscus paniculatus

Hibiscus paniculatus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.

Hibiscus paniculatus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
