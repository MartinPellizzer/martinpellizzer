Hyptis kuntzeana

Hyptis kuntzeana, commonly known as wild basil or Mexican hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis kuntzeana is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
