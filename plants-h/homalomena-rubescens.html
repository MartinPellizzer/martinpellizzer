Homalomena rubescens

Homalomena rubescens, commonly known as the red-veined swamp taro, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins that contrast vividly with its dark green foliage. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and wetlands. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

Homalomena rubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens and pond landscapes.
