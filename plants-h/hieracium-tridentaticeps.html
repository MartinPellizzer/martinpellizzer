Hieracium tridentaticeps

Hieracium tridentaticeps, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
