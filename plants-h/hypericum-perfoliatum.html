Hypericum perfoliatum

Hypericum perfoliatum, commonly known as the common St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that often appear to be clasping the stem. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Hypericum perfoliatum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
