Hydrangea anomala var. sericea

Hydrangea anomala var. sericea, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its showy blooms appear in late summer and early fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The leaves are typically serrated and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal.
