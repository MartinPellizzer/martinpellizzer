Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. livadicae

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

livadicae, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has basal and upright stems, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
