Hieracium spathulatum subsp. ilergabonum

Hieracium spathulatum subsp.

ilergabonum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
