Hieracium murorum subsp. lobophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lobophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The subspecies lobophyllum is distinguished by its elongated, lobed leaf bases and slightly hairy stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

This plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is often found in temperate regions.
