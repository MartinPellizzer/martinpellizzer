Hibiscus pulvinulifer

Hibiscus pulvinulifer, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, elongated fruit known as a capsule. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to grow optimally.

Hibiscus pulvinulifer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
