Halocnemum fruticosum

Halocnemum fruticosum, commonly known as sea spurge, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and arid regions with high salt content in the soil. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it retain water in harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is part of the Euphorbiaceae family.
