Hieracium subfusciviride

Hieracium subfusciviride, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
