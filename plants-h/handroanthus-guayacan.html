Handroanthus guayacan

Handroanthus guayacan, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The wood of Handroanthus guayacan is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
