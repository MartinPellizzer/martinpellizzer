Hieracium sparsum subsp. nikolovii

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

nikolovii, commonly known as the Balkan meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of southeastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Hieracium genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other hawkweeds.
