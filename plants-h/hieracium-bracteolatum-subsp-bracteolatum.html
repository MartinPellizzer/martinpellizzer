Hieracium bracteolatum subsp. bracteolatum

Hieracium bracteolatum subsp.

bracteolatum, commonly known as the bracted hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
