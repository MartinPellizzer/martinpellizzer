Hieracium inconspicuissimum

Hieracium inconspicuissimum, commonly known as the least conspicuous hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, yellowish-green flowers that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds in small, composite flower heads.

Due to its inconspicuous nature, it is often mistaken for other similar species within the Hieracium genus.
