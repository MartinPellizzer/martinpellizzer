Hibiscus domingensis

Hibiscus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Hibiscus domingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
