Hieracium dentatum subsp. waldense

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

waldense, commonly known as the Waldense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
