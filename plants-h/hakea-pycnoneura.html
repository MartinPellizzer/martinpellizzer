Hakea pycnoneura

Hakea pycnoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of woody fruits that remain on the plant for many years. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late winter to early spring.

Hakea pycnoneura is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
