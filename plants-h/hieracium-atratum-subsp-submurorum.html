Hieracium atratum subsp. submurorum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

submurorum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Its subspecific name, *submurorum*, refers to its association with walls and rocky habitats.
