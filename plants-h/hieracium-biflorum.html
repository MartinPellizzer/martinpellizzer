Hieracium biflorum

Hieracium biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, short flowering stem that bears two yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Hieracium biflorum is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
