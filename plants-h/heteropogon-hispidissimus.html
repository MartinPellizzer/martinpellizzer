Heteropogon hispidissimus

Heteropogon hispidissimus, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Heteropogon hispidissimus is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
