Hieracium oxyodon subsp. acromelanum

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

acromelanum, commonly known as the black-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, often hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its distinctive dark pigmentation in the leaves, which gives it its common name.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
