Helicia grandifolia

Helicia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Helicia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
