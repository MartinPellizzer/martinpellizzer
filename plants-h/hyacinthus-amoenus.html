Hyacinthus amoenus

Hyacinthus amoenus, commonly known as the garden hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, blue, white, and pink. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grassy leaves. Hyacinths thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular garden plants.

Their blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens during the transition from winter to summer.
