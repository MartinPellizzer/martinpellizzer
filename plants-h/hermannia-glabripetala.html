Hermannia glabripetala

Hermannia glabripetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf or glabrous petal hermannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hermannia glabripetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
