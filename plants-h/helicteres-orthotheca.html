Helicteres orthotheca

Helicteres orthotheca, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
