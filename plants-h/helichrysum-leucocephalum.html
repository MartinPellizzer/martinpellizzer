Helichrysum leucocephalum

Helichrysum leucocephalum, commonly known as the white-headed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and white disc florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in full sun. Its small, fluffy seeds are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
