Hedyotis athroantha

Hedyotis athroantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Hedyotis athroantha is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, temperate climates.
