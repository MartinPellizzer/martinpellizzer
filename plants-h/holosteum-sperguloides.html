Holosteum sperguloides

Holosteum sperguloides, commonly known as the common waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as riverbanks and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Holosteum sperguloides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
