Hoffmannseggia falcaria

Hoffmannseggia falcaria, commonly known as the desert hoffmannseggia, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hoffmannseggia falcaria plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local pollinators.
