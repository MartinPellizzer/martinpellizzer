Helichrysum bracteatum var. borrusorum-rex

Helichrysum bracteatum var. borrusorum-rex, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
