Hieracium atratum subsp. dolichaetum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

dolichaetum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in a dense cluster.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and may have a distinctive scent. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
