Hemiboea subcapitata

Hemiboea subcapitata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a short, erect stem with a single, showy flower that is usually pink or purple in color. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive hood-like structure formed by the sepals and petals.

Hemiboea subcapitata is adapted to arid environments and blooms during the warmer months.
