Hieracium adenocladum

Hieracium adenocladum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a glandular surface, which contributes to its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
