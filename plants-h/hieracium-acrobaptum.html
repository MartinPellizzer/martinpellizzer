Hieracium acrobaptum

Hieracium acrobaptum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-orange.

Hieracium acrobaptum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
