Hieracium integrifolium subsp. pseudohemiplecum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

pseudohemiplecum, commonly known as the smoothleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of broadly ovate, toothed leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
