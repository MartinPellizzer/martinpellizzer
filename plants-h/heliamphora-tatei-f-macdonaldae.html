Heliamphora tatei f. macdonaldae

Heliamphora tatei f. macdonaldae, commonly known as the Macdonald's sun pitcher, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the tepuis of Venezuela.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical pitchers that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant's leaves form these specialized traps, which secrete a sticky substance to lure and trap insects. It thrives in high-altitude, nutrient-poor environments, relying on captured prey for essential nutrients.

Its distinctive morphology and habitat make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
