Hieracium adducens

Hieracium adducens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium adducens is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
