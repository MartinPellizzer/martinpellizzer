Hieracium vulgatum subsp. ausserdorferi

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

ausserdorferi, commonly known as the Austrian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
