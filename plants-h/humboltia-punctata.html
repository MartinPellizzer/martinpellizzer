Humboltia punctata

Humboltia punctata, commonly known as the California buckeye, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically five to seven lobes, and its distinctive yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a critical food source for certain wildlife species. Humboltia punctata is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
