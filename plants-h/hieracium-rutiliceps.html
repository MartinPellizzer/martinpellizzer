Hieracium rutiliceps

Hieracium rutiliceps, commonly known as the golden-hair hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
