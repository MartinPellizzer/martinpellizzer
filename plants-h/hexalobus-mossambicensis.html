Hexalobus mossambicensis

Hexalobus mossambicensis, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African communities.
