Hedycarya saligna

Hedycarya saligna, commonly known as the silky gum or silky ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits.

Hedycarya saligna is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
