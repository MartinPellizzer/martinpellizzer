Helichrysum formosissimum

Helichrysum formosissimum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces papery, evergreen leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to retain color when dried makes it a favored plant for floral arrangements.
