Hebe hulkeana

Hebe hulkeana, commonly known as the Hulke's hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and attractive foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in spring and summer. Hebe hulkeana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners in temperate climates.
