Hyptis eximia

Hyptis eximia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis eximia is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
