Holubiella lunarioides

Holubiella lunarioides, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers open at night and close during the day, making it a unique addition to gardens.

Holubiella lunarioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to nocturnal pollinators.
