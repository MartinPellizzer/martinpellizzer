Helichrysum cochinchinense

Helichrysum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese immortelle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellowish-orange inflorescences that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Helichrysum cochinchinense is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
