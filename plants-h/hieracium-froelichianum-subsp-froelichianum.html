Hieracium froelichianum subsp. froelichianum

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

froelichianum, commonly known as the Froelich's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The species is notable for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
