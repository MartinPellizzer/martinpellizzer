Hypobathrum sampitense

Hypobathrum sampitense, commonly known as the Sampit sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique sand-dwelling growth habit. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
