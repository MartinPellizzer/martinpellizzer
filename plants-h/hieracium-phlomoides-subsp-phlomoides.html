Hieracium phlomoides subsp. phlomoides

Hieracium phlomoides subsp.

phlomoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a hardy species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
