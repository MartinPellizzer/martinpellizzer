Hieracium attractum subsp. coriaceum

Hieracium attractum subsp.

coriaceum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, roadsides, and streambanks. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

The subspecies coriaceum is distinguished by its thick, leathery leaves, which help it retain moisture in drier conditions.
