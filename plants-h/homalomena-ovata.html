Homalomena ovata

Homalomena ovata, commonly known as the velvet leaf or red spider lily, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and velvety to the touch. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the year under optimal conditions.
