Hieracium albovelutinum

Hieracium albovelutinum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like appearance. The plant features a hairy stem that is often covered in white or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.
