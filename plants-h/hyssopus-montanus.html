Hyssopus montanus

Hyssopus montanus, commonly known as mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Hyssopus montanus is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
