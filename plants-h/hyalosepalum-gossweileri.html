Hyalosepalum gossweileri

Hyalosepalum gossweileri, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and striking, white, waxy flowers. The plant lacks true leaves, relying instead on its green, photosynthetic stems for nourishment. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing epiphytically on trees or rocks.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
