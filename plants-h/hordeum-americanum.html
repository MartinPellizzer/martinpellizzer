Hordeum americanum

Hordeum americanum, commonly known as wild barley, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and oval in shape.

Hordeum americanum plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
