Hypericum pseudoerectum

Hypericum pseudoerectum, commonly known as the upright St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its botanical features include yellow-orange stamens and a hairy calyx, contributing to its ornamental value.

Hypericum pseudoerectum is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
