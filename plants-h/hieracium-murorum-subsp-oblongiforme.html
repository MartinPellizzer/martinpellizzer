Hieracium murorum subsp. oblongiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

oblongiforme, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the family, and has hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in leaf shape and other morphological traits compared to other members of the species.
