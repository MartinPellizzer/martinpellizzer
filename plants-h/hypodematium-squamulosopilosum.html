Hypodematium squamulosopilosum

Hypodematium squamulosopilosum, commonly known as the silky scurf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive appearance with a pale to dark green or brown thallus.

It is characterized by a dense, woolly covering of tiny, branched structures called pseudopores, which give it a silky texture. This lichen typically grows on bark, wood, or stone in moist, shaded environments. It is found in temperate regions and is often associated with decaying trees.

The species is of interest to mycologists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
