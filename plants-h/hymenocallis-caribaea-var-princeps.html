Hymenocallis caribaea var. princeps

Hymenocallis caribaea var. princeps, commonly known as the Jamaican spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It features large, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow center, often resembling a spider's appearance. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that grow from a bulbous base. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
