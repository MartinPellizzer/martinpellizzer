Hieracium denticulosifolium

Hieracium denticulosifolium, commonly known as the toothed-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive toothed edge, giving it its specific epithet.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
