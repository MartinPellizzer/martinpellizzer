Heliconia bihai

Heliconia bihai, commonly known as the Blood Banana, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble bananas. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a unique structure that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds.

Heliconia bihai is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates.
