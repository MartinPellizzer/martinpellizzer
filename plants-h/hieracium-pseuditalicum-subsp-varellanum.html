Hieracium pseuditalicum subsp. varellanum

Hieracium pseuditalicum subsp.

varellanum, commonly known as the Varellanum hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a minor weed in some agricultural settings.
