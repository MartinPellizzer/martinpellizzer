Hedysarum polybotrys var. latifolium

Hedysarum polybotrys var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and clusters of pea-like, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Hedysarum polybotrys var. latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in ecological restoration projects.
