Haplopappus lehmannii

Haplopappus lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's spinyweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in environments with low moisture. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
