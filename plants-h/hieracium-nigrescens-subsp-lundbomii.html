Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lundbomii

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lundbomii, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the plant produces achenes as its seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker central disc florets compared to other hawkweed species.
