Hieracium leptoprenanthes subsp. sublongissimum

Hieracium leptoprenanthes subsp.

sublongissimum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with rough, hairy surfaces, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
