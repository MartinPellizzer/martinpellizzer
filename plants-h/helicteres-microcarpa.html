Helicteres microcarpa

Helicteres microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited hedge bean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain several seeds. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are accompanied by small, hairy bracts.

Helicteres microcarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
