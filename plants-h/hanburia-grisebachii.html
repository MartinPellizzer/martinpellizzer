Hanburia grisebachii

Hanburia grisebachii, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its pale green, leaf-like stems and its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a challenging plant to cultivate.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
