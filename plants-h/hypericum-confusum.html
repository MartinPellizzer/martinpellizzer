Hypericum confusum

Hypericum confusum, commonly known as the confused St. John's wort, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum confusum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
