Hesperomeles lanuginosa

Hesperomeles lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly hawthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
