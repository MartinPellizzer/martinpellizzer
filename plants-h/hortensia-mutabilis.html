Hortensia mutabilis

Hortensia mutabilis, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, showy flowers that range from white to pink and even red, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features fern-like, bipinnate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
