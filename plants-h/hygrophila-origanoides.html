Hygrophila origanoides

Hygrophila origanoides, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in clusters.

Hygrophila origanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
