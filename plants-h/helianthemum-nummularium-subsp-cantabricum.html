Helianthemum nummularium subsp. cantabricum

Helianthemum nummularium subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, rounded, silver-green leaves that are densely packed, giving it a mat-like appearance.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically borne on short stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
