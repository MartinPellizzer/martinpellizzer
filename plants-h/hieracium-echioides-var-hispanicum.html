Hieracium echioides var. hispanicum

Hieracium echioides var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a caulescent stem and a flowering head composed of numerous small florets.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
