Hieracium megalotrachelum

Hieracium megalotrachelum, commonly known as the giant-horned hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
