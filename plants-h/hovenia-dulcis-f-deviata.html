Hovenia dulcis f. deviata

Hovenia dulcis f. deviata, commonly known as the Japanese date tree or sweet date tree, is a variant of the Hovenia dulcis species native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually 10–20 cm long, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, resembling a date, is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in traditional Asian cuisine.
