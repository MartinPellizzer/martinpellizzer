Helichrysum chrysosphaerum

Helichrysum chrysosphaerum, commonly known as the golden ball helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small balls. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum chrysosphaerum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance.
