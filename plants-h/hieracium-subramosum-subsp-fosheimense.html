Hieracium subramosum subsp. fosheimense

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

fosheimense, commonly known as the Fosheim's hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a head.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
