Hygrophila seretii

Hygrophila seretii, commonly known as the African water hyacinth, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Africa.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots hang submerged in the water. Hygrophila seretii is often used in aquatic gardens and as a decorative plant in water features.

It plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
