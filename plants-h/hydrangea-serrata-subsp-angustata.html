Hydrangea serrata subsp. angustata

Hydrangea serrata subsp.

angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
