Helianthemum pilosum var. carneum

Helianthemum pilosum var. carneum, commonly known as the pink sandverbena, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
