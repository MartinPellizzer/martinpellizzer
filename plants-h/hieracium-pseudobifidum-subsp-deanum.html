Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. deanum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

deanum, commonly known as the Dean's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
