Hieracium urolepium

Hieracium urolepium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium urolepium is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and can become invasive in certain environments.
