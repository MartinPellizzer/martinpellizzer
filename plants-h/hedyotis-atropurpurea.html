Hedyotis atropurpurea

Hedyotis atropurpurea, commonly known as purple bluets, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of five-petaled flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hedyotis atropurpurea thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.
