Haworthia cooperi var. venusta

Haworthia cooperi var. venusta, commonly known as the "Cork-screw Haworthia," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings. The leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.

This species is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
