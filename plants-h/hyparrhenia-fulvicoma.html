Hyparrhenia fulvicoma

Hyparrhenia fulvicoma, commonly known as the yellow-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, pale brown spikelets that are clustered in panicles, which are often found at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia fulvicoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
