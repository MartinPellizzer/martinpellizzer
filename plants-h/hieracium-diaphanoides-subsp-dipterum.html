Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. dipterum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

dipterum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrowly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head at the end of each stem. Its inflorescence is characterized by a single flower head, which is surrounded by a series of bracts.

The species is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.
