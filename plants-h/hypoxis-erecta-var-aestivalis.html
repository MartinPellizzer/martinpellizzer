Hypoxis erecta var. aestivalis

Hypoxis erecta var. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer hypoxis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are typically clustered in umbels and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hypoxis erecta var. aestivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.
