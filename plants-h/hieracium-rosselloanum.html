Hieracium rosselloanum

Hieracium rosselloanum, commonly known as Rossello's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
