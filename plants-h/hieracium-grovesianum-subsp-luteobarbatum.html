Hieracium grovesianum subsp. luteobarbatum

Hieracium grovesianum subsp.

luteobarbatum, commonly known as the yellow beard hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive yellowish bracts and the hairy, barbate appearance of its inflorescence.
