Hordeum patagonicum subsp. santacrucense

Hordeum patagonicum subsp.

santacrucense, commonly known as Patagonian barley, is a wild grass species native to southern South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while the seeds are small and hardy.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration and as a model for studying plant resilience in extreme climates.
