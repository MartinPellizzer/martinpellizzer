Humboltia bicarinata

Humboltia bicarinata, commonly known as the California wild quinine or the coast mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. Humboltia bicarinata thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and coastal areas.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in riparian habitats.
