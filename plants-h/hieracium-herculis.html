Hieracium herculis

Hieracium herculis, commonly known as Hercules' dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a single, tall stem that bears a large, yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It produces a large number of seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
