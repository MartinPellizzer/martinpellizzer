Hyparrhenia takaensis

Hyparrhenia takaensis, commonly known as the African grass or Takaensis grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodland areas and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hyparrhenia takaensis is often used in traditional landscaping and has some applications in soil stabilization due to its dense root system.
