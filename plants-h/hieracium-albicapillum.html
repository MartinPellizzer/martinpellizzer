Hieracium albicapillum

Hieracium albicapillum, commonly known as the white-capped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like appearance. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with the outer florets being sterile and white, giving it its common name.

Hieracium albicapillum is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
