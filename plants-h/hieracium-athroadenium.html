Hieracium athroadenium

Hieracium athroadenium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Hieracium athroadenium is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
