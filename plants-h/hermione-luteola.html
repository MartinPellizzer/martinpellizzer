Hermione luteola

Hermione luteola, commonly known as the yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Hermione luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its cheerful appearance.
