Hypericum cardiophyllum

Hypericum cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heart-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than other St. John's wort species.

Its unique leaf shape and ornamental value make it a subject of botanical interest.
