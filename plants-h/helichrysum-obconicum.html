Helichrysum obconicum

Helichrysum obconicum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its densely packed, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a distinctive appearance.
