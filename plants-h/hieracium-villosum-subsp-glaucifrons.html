Hieracium villosum subsp. glaucifrons

Hieracium villosum subsp.

glaucifrons, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish or whitish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
