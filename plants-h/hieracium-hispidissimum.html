Hieracium hispidissimum

Hieracium hispidissimum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium hispidissimum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
