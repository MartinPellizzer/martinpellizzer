Hemionitis muelleri

Hemionitis muelleri, commonly known as the rock fern, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Hemionitis muelleri is often found in coastal and inland sand dunes, contributing to the ecological stability of these environments.
