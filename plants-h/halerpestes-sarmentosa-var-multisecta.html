Halerpestes sarmentosa var. multisecta

Halerpestes sarmentosa var. multisecta, commonly known as the multifurcate balsa, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves that are deeply divided into multiple lobes. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
