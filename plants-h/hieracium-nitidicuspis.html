Hieracium nitidicuspis

Hieracium nitidicuspis, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and pointed tips, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium nitidicuspis is often found in temperate climates and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
