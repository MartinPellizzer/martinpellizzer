Huperzia amentacea

Huperzia amentacea, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant features whorled leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape, with a glossy green coloration. Huperzia amentacea is notable for its spore-producing structures, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical formations called strobila.

This species is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
