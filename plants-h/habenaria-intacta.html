Habenaria intacta

Habenaria intacta, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by flies and bees, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
