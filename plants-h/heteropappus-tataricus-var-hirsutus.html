Heteropappus tataricus var. hirsutus

Heteropappus tataricus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy tatarian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
