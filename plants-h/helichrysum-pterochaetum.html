Helichrysum pterochaetum

Helichrysum pterochaetum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small, compact flowers. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by papery bracts, giving it a unique, almost feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum pterochaetum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
