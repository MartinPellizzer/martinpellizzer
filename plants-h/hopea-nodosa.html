Hopea nodosa

Hopea nodosa, commonly known as the knobwood or nodose hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive knobby bark and dense, aromatic wood. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Hopea nodosa is valued for its hard, durable timber used in construction and furniture making.
