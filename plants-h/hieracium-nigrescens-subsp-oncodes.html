Hieracium nigrescens subsp. oncodes

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

oncodes, commonly known as the black-tinged hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a distinctive yellowish or reddish hue.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some regions.
