Hyssopus ruber

Hyssopus ruber, commonly known as red hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their antiseptic and expectorant properties.

Hyssopus ruber thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
