Hedychium macrorrhizum

Hedychium macrorrhizum, commonly known as the giant ginger or Himalayan torch ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 1 meter in height. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hedychium macrorrhizum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
