Hermannia quercifolia

Hermannia quercifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hermannia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that resemble the shape of oak leaves, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and floral display.
