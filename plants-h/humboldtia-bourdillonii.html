Humboldtia bourdillonii

Humboldtia bourdillonii, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Humboldtia bourdillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
