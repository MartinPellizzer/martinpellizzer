Hieracium umbrosum

Hieracium umbrosum, commonly known as the shadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium umbrosum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
