Hibiscus chrysochaetus

Hibiscus chrysochaetus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central pistil and five prominent stamens. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Hibiscus chrysochaetus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
