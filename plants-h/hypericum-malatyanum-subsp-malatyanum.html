Hypericum malatyanum subsp. malatyanum

Hypericum malatyanum subsp.

malatyanum, commonly known as the Malatya St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky and forested areas, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
