Hyalolepis revoluta

Hyalolepis revoluta, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
