Hydrangea opuloides var. otaksa

Hydrangea opuloides var. otaksa, commonly known as the Otaksa hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the flower colors.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
