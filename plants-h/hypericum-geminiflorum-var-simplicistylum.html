Hypericum geminiflorum var. simplicistylum

Hypericum geminiflorum var. simplicistylum, commonly known as the simple-styled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
