Hieracium magyaricum subsp. nigrisetum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

nigrisetum, commonly known as Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark brown to black bracts, which give it its specific epithet *nigrisetum*. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is relatively short-lived and has a limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
