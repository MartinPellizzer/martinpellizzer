Hemigraphis hispida

Hemigraphis hispida, commonly known as velvetleaf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its foliage rather than its blooms.
