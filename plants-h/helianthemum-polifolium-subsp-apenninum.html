Helianthemum polifolium subsp. apenninum

Helianthemum polifolium subsp.

apenninum, commonly known as the Apennine rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically on stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
