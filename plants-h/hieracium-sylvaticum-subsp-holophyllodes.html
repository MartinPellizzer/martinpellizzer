Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. holophyllodes

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

holophyllodes, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

Its subspecies distinction is based on morphological differences, particularly in leaf and flower structure compared to other Hieracium species.
