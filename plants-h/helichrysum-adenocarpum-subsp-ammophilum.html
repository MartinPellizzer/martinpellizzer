Helichrysum adenocarpum subsp. ammophilum

Helichrysum adenocarpum subsp.

ammophilum, commonly known as the sand everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
