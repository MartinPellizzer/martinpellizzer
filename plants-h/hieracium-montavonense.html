Hieracium montavonense

Hieracium montavonense, commonly known as the Montavon's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate, rough to the touch, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium montavonense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
