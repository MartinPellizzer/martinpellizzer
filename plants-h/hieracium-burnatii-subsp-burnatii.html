Hieracium burnatii subsp. burnatii

Hieracium burnatii subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as the Burnat's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellowish to orange composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head made up of numerous small florets.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth form and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
