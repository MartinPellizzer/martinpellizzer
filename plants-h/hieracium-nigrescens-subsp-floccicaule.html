Hieracium nigrescens subsp. floccicaule

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

floccicaule, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of fuzzy, gray-green leaves and stems that produce clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its distinctive, fluffy appearance.

It is often found in sandy or calcareous soils and is considered a characteristic species of dry grasslands.
