Hieracium parallelisquameum

Hieracium parallelisquameum, commonly known as the parallel-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive parallel venation pattern, giving it its specific epithet.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
