Hieracium liasaetense

Hieracium liasaetense, commonly known as the Liasaetense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a hairy surface, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium liasaetense is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
