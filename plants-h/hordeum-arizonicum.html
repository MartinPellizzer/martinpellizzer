Hordeum arizonicum

Hordeum arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona wild barley, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both sterile and fertile florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hordeum arizonicum plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local insect and bird populations in its native habitat.
