Hieracium laevigatum subsp. macrolygodes

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

macrolygodes, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broadly ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and often hairy surfaces.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some regions.
