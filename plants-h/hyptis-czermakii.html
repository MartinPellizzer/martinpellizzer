Hyptis czermakii

Hyptis czermakii, commonly known as the Czermak's hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically clustered in spikes and range in color from white to purple.
