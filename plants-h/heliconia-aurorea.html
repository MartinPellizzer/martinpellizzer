Heliconia aurorea

Heliconia aurorea, commonly known as the golden heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a crown of flowers. The plant features broad, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often green with a reddish or orange tint on the underside. Its flowers are typically orange or yellow, with a distinctive red or purple spathe that encloses the actual flowers.

Heliconia aurorea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
