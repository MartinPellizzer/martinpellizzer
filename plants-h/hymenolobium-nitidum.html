Hymenolobium nitidum

Hymenolobium nitidum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, straight stem and compound leaves that are finely divided and covered in a silky, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are enclosed in flattened, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Hymenolobium nitidum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
