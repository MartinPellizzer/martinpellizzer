Hamelia patens var. axillaroides

Hamelia patens var. axillaroides, commonly known as the flamebeard bush, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
