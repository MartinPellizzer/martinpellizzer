Helleborus trifoliatus

Helleborus trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pale green to white flowers. The plant is known for its toxic properties, containing various cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Helleborus trifoliatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
