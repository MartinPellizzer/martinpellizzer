Helicia cauliflora

Helicia cauliflora, commonly known as the tropical evergreen tree, is a member of the Theaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Helicia cauliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
