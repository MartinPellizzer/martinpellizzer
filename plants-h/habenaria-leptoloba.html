Habenaria leptoloba

Habenaria leptoloba, commonly known as the slender-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and usually pale green or white, with three sepals and three petals, forming a distinctive orchid shape.

It is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
