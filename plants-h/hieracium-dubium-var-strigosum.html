Hieracium dubium var. strigosum

Hieracium dubium var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, rough stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its yellowish to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
