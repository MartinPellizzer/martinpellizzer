Hamelia brachystemon

Hamelia brachystemon, commonly known as the flame anther orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a smooth, glossy surface.

The orchid's unique floral structure includes specialized anthers that release pollen in a controlled manner, aiding in its pollination process.
