Huntleya fimbriata

Huntleya fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, hair-like structures, aiding in water retention.

Huntleya fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
