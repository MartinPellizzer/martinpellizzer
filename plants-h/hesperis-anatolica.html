Hesperis anatolica

Hesperis anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers, which range from white to pale purple, bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

Hesperis anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
