Huperzia hildebrandtii

Huperzia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's clubmoss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Huperzia hildebrandtii is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resistance to desiccation.

Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered at the tips of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
