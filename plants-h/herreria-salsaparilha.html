Herreria salsaparilha

Herreria salsaparilha, commonly known as Brazilian sarsaparilla, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries.

Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
