Hemionitis trichophylla

Hemionitis trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy hemionitis, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette growth form and hairy, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions.

Hemionitis trichophylla is often found in rocky or semi-desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
