Hieracium sordidum var. subglandulosum

Hieracium sordidum var. subglandulosum, commonly known as the gloomy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, such as meadows and roadsides, and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are generally hairy and glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
