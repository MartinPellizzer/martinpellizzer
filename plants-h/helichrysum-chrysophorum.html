Helichrysum chrysophorum

Helichrysum chrysophorum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellowish-orange flower heads that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, spherical inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
