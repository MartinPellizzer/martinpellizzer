Hieracium laevigatum subsp. achalzichiense

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

achalzichiense, commonly known as the Achalzichi hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America. It typically grows in grassy areas and open habitats, often in regions with temperate climates.

The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, and its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other flowering plants in its native range.
