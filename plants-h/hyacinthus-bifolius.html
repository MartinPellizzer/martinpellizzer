Hyacinthus bifolius

Hyacinthus bifolius, commonly known as the two-leaf hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-leaf structure during the vegetative stage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hyacinthus bifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
