Hedysarum ildefonsianum

Hedysarum ildefonsianum, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or dry environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
