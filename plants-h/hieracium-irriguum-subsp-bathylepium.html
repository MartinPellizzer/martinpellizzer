Hieracium irriguum subsp. bathylepium

Hieracium irriguum subsp.

bathylepium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a flowering stem that is often hairy and glandular.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
