Heliconia hirsuta var. villosula

Heliconia hirsuta var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within the leaf-like bracts. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant and exotic look.
