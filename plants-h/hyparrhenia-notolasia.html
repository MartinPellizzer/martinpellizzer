Hyparrhenia notolasia

Hyparrhenia notolasia, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used for thatching or as a forage crop.

The plant's adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a significant component of its native habitats.
