Hyptis ovata

Hyptis ovata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
