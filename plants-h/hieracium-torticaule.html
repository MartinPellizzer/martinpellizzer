Hieracium torticaule

Hieracium torticaule, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, basal leaves that curve inward, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

Hieracium torticaule is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
