Helichrysum microphyllum

Helichrysum microphyllum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, densely packed yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns, which remain intact even when dried. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ability to retain color when dried makes it popular in floral arrangements and as a decorative plant.
