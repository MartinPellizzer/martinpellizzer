Hesperaloe

Hesperaloe, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a genus of succulent plants native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of white or yellow flowers, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Hesperaloe species are often used in xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Their unique appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make them popular among gardeners in dry climates.
