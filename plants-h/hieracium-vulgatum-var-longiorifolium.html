Hieracium vulgatum var. longiorifolium

Hieracium vulgatum var. longiorifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are longer and more slender than those of the typical *H. vulgatum*. The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, surrounded by a ring of pale green to white bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
