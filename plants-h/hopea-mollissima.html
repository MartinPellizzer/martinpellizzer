Hopea mollissima

Hopea mollissima, commonly known as the silky hopea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The tree produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife.
