Haplopteris mediosora

Haplopteris mediosora, commonly known as the medium sorus huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered sporangia, which are typically found on the underside of its leaves. The plant has a distinctive morphology with narrow, linear leaves that are often recurved at the edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in temperate regions.

Haplopteris mediosora is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest understories.
