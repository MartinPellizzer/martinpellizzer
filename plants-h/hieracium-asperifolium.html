Hieracium asperifolium

Hieracium asperifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features dark green, rough-textured leaves with prominent veins and a hairy stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium asperifolium is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
