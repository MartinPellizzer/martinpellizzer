Heliconia allenii

Heliconia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful, spiral-shaped inflorescences. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a distinctive, elongated structure that attracts birds and insects.

Heliconia allenii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience in warm, humid climates.
