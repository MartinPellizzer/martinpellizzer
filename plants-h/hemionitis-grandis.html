Hemionitis grandis

Hemionitis grandis, commonly known as the broad-leafed hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme environmental conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Hemionitis grandis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
