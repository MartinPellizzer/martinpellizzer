Heliotropium persicariifolium

Heliotropium persicariifolium, commonly known as the Persian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
