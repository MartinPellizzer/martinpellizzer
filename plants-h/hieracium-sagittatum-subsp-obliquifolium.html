Hieracium sagittatum subsp. obliquifolium

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

obliquifolium, commonly known as the bent-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive downward-curving tip. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, composed of small, daisy-like flowers.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
