Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. variegatisquamum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

variegatisquamum, commonly known as the variegated squirreltail hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variegated leaf scaly patterns, which give it its common name. It typically grows in grassy habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is relatively small, with leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, woolly hairs.
