Hypericum coriaceum

Hypericum coriaceum, commonly known as rough St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow, elliptical, and covered with a rough, textured surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.
