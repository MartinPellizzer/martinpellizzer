Hordeum vulgare var. palestinicum

Hordeum vulgare var. palestinicum, commonly known as the Palestinian barley, is a cultivated variant of barley native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and small, oval-shaped grains. This variety is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional agriculture and can be found in historical and archaeological contexts.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other barley varieties, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.
