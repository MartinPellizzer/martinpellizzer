Helichrysum apiculatum

Helichrysum apiculatum, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum apiculatum is also known for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
