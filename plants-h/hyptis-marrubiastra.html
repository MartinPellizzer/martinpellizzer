Hyptis marrubiastra

Hyptis marrubiastra, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis marrubiastra is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
