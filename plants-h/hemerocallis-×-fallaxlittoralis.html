Hemerocallis × fallaxlittoralis

Hemerocallis × fallaxlittoralis, commonly known as the coastal daylily, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Hemerocallis fulva and Hemerocallis lutea.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in coastal regions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is broad and grass-like, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Hemerocallis × fallaxlittoralis is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to coastal environments.
