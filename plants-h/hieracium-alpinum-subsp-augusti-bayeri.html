Hieracium alpinum subsp. augusti-bayeri

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

augusti-bayeri, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems and is often found in association with other alpine flora.
