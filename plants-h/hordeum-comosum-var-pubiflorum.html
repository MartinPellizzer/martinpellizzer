Hordeum comosum var. pubiflorum

Hordeum comosum var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the pubiflorous barley, is a variant of the barley species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It is a perennial grass native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features slender, tufted culms with dense, spike-like inflorescences covered in soft, pubescent hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variant is often studied for its ecological adaptations and potential uses in sustainable agriculture.
