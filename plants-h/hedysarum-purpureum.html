Hedysarum purpureum

Hedysarum purpureum, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It typically grows in grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed sites, often forming dense stands.

Its seeds are small and dry, and it is sometimes cultivated as a forage plant or for erosion control.
