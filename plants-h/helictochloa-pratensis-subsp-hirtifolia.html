Helictochloa pratensis subsp. hirtifolia

Helictochloa pratensis subsp.

hirtifolia, commonly known as hairy meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its subspecific name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing two to three florets.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
