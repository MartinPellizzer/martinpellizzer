Hieracium nigroglandulosum

Hieracium nigroglandulosum, commonly known as the black glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically features upright, hairy stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that have distinctive glandular dots. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium nigroglandulosum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
