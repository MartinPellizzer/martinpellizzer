Hieracium lomatolepis

Hieracium lomatolepis, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, woolly foliage and yellow to orange ray florets. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Hieracium lomatolepis is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
