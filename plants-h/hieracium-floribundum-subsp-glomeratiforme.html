Hieracium floribundum subsp. glomeratiforme

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

glomeratiforme, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact inflorescence and is often found in moist or damp habitats.
