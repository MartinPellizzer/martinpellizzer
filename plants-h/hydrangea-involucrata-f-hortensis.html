Hydrangea involucrata f. hortensis

Hydrangea involucrata f. hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features large, showy flower clusters that range from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and dense, providing an attractive backdrop to the flowers.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
