Hieracium glaucum subsp. arvetii

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

arvetii, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of broadly lanceolate, glaucous leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of flowering stems. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in moist or sandy soils and is part of the Asteraceae family.
