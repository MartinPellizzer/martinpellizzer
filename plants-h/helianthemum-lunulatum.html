Helianthemum lunulatum

Helianthemum lunulatum, commonly known as the moonflower rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow, typically during late spring to early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
