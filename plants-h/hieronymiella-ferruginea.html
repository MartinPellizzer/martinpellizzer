Hieronymiella ferruginea

Hieronymiella ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf gall midge, is a small insect that induces galls on the leaves of certain plants.

The plant's botanical features include its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It has a smooth, waxy surface and a pale green to yellowish coloration. The leaves are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas and stream sides.
