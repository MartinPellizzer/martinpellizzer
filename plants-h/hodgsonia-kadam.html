Hodgsonia kadam

Hodgsonia kadam, commonly known as the Himalayan kadam, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in moist, temperate forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Hodgsonia kadam is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
