Hopea santosiana

Hopea santosiana, commonly known as the Santosian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Hopea santosiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
