Hydrangea paniculata var. intermedia

Hydrangea paniculata var. intermedia, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy inflorescences that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its visual appeal.

It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
