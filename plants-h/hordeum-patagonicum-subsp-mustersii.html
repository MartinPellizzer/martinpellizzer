Hordeum patagonicum subsp. mustersii

Hordeum patagonicum subsp.

mustersii, commonly known as Patagonian barley, is a wild grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential in sustainable agriculture and ecological restoration.
