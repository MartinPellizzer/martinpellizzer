Hemigenia sieberi

Hemigenia sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Hemigenia sieberi is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rockeries.
