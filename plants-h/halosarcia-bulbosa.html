Halosarcia bulbosa

Halosarcia bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still, nutrient-poor water and uses its specialized structures to obtain nutrients. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems may float on the water's surface.
