Hypericum lagarocladum subsp. angustifolium

Hypericum lagarocladum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
