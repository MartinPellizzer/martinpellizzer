Hieracium tephrosoma subsp. trichothecum

Hieracium tephrosoma subsp.

trichothecum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
