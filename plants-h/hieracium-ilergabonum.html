Hieracium ilergabonum

Hieracium ilergabonum, commonly known as the African oxeye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces a distinctive seed head that disperses its seeds by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
