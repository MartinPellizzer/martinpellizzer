Hypericum quadrangulum subsp. maculatum

Hypericum quadrangulum subsp.

maculatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its four-angled stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge, especially in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland areas.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
