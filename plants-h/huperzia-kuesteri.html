Huperzia kuesteri

Huperzia kuesteri, commonly known as the Kuester's clubmoss, is a species of moss plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, compact foliage and upright, club-like stems. Its spore-producing structures, called sporophytes, are located at the tips of the stems.

Huperzia kuesteri plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
