Hyparrhenia rudis

Hyparrhenia rudis, commonly known as the rough-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Hyparrhenia rudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
