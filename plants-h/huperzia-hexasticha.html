Huperzia hexasticha

Huperzia hexasticha, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of clubmoss found in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its erect, unbranched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces spores in capsules that are borne on short, stalked structures called sporophylls. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Huperzia hexasticha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
