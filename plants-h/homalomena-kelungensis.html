Homalomena kelungensis

Homalomena kelungensis, commonly known as the Kelung ginger-fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
