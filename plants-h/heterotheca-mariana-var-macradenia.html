Heterotheca mariana var. macradenia

Heterotheca mariana var. macradenia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, dark purple to blue flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other varieties of Heterotheca.
