Hieracium platymeles

Hieracium platymeles, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium platymeles is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
