Herniaria hybernonis

Herniaria hybernonis, commonly known as the kidney vetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and hairy. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.

Herniaria hybernonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
