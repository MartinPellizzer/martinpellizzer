Hibiscus populifolius

Hibiscus populifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Hibiscus populifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
