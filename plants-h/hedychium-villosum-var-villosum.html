Hedychium villosum var. villosum

Hedychium villosum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with prominent red or orange spathes, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hedychium villosum var. villosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
