Hydrocotyle biflora

Hydrocotyle biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered water pennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes. The plant has rounded, floating leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in diameter. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in pairs, which gives it its common name.

Hydrocotyle biflora is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
