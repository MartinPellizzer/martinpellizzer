Hermodactylus repens

Hermodactylus repens, commonly known as the creeping hermodactyl, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Hermodactylus repens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
