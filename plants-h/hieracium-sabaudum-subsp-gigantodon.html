Hieracium sabaudum subsp. gigantodon

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

gigantodon, commonly known as the giant toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, toothed leaves that are often hairy and lobed.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
