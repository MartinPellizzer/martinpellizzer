Hieracium callichlorum

Hieracium callichlorum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, and it produces small, ribbed achenes as seeds.

Hieracium callichlorum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
