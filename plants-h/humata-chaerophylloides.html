Humata chaerophylloides

Humata chaerophylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
