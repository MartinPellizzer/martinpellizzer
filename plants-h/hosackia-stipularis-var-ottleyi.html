Hosackia stipularis var. ottleyi

Hosackia stipularis var. ottleyi, commonly known as the Ottley's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are typically arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is notable for its distinctive stipules and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
