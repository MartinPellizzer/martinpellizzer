Hieracium avilae

Hieracium avilae, commonly known as the Avil's Hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

Hieracium avilae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
