Hibiscus rupicola

Hibiscus rupicola, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom during the warmer months. Hibiscus rupicola is adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
