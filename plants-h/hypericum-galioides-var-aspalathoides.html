Hypericum galioides var. aspalathoides

Hypericum galioides var. aspalathoides, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive yellow floral display.
