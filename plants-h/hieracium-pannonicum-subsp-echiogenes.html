Hieracium pannonicum subsp. echiogenes

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

echiogenes, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is often found in the Pannonian region and is adapted to temperate climates.
