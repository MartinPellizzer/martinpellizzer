Hieracium tephrocladum

Hieracium tephrocladum, commonly known as the snowflake hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium tephrocladum is adapted to cold climates and is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
