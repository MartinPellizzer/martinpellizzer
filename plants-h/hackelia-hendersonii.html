Hackelia hendersonii

Hackelia hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hackelia hendersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
