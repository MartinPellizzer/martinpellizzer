Hieracium macrodon subsp. macrodontoides

Hieracium macrodon subsp.

macrodontoides, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies macrodontoides is distinguished by its larger flower heads and more robust growth compared to other subspecies of Hieracium macrodon.
