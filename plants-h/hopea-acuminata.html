Hopea acuminata

Hopea acuminata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape with a smooth margin. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits.

Hopea acuminata is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.
