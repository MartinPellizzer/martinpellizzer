Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. laxifurcans

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

laxifurcans, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its loosely branched inflorescence and reddish-orange fruits. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
