Haageocereus versicolor var. aureispinus

Haageocereus versicolor var. aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spine cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a columnar stem with ribbed, spiny segments that display a range of colors from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, often blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique spination, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
