Hyphaene luandensis

Hyphaene luandensis, commonly known as the Luanda palm, is a species of palm native to central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals. Its fibrous leaves are also used for thatching and construction in local communities.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
