Helichrysum coactum

Helichrysum coactum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, shrub-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, rounded heads.

Helichrysum coactum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting color and ornamental value.
