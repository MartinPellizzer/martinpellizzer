Helichrysum calocephalum

Helichrysum calocephalum, commonly known as the goldenhead everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow to orange-colored flower heads that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its long-lasting blooms make it a favorite in floral arrangements and horticultural displays.
