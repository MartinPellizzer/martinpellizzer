Hieracium lemovicense

Hieracium lemovicense, commonly known as the Ljubljana hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
