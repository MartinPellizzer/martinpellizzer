Hemicrambe

Hemicrambe, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to coastal areas of Europe and Asia, hemicrambe thrives in sandy or salty soils, often found in maritime environments. The plant has a short growing season and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
