Halgania anagalloides

Halgania anagalloides, commonly known as the woolly false nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive bluish-green color. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is often found in the understory of tropical and subtropical forests.
