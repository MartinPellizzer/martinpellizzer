Hieracium lycopifolium subsp. sabaudifolium

Hieracium lycopifolium subsp.

sabaudifolium, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface. It produces yellow ray florets and tubular disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and nutrient-poor soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.
