Hieraciodes decumbens

Hieraciodes decumbens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from May to June. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.
