Hieracium lobelianum

Hieracium lobelianum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and tall, branched stems that bear numerous yellow flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Hieracium lobelianum is native to Europe but has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.
