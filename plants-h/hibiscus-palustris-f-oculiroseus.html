Hibiscus palustris f. oculiroseus

Hibiscus palustris f. oculiroseus, commonly known as the showy swamp hibiscus, is a variant of the swamp hibiscus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stems are covered in fine hairs.

This form is notable for its enhanced floral display, making it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
