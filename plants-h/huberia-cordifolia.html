Huberia cordifolia

Huberia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
