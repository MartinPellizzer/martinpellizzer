Hypericum fimbriatum var. androsaemifolium

Hypericum fimbriatum var. androsaemifolium, commonly known as the Androsaemifolium St. John's Wort, is a variant of the St. John's Wort genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, giving it a unique appearance compared to other Hypericum species. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
