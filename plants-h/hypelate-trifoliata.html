Hypelate trifoliata

Hypelate trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leafed vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are fleshy berries that change color as they mature.

Hypelate trifoliata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
