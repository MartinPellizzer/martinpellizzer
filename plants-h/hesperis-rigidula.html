Hesperis rigidula

Hesperis rigidula, commonly known as the rigid hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat rigid structure, giving it its common name.

Hesperis rigidula is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
