Hepetis burchellii

Hepetis burchellii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate, mist-like appearance and long blooming period.
