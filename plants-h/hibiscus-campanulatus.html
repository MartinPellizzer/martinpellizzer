Hibiscus campanulatus

Hibiscus campanulatus, commonly known as the bell hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hibiscus campanulatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
