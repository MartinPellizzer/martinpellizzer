Heterotheca monarchensis

Heterotheca monarchensis, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and rocky soils.

Heterotheca monarchensis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
