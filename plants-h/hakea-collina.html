Hakea collina

Hakea collina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water retention. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Hakea collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
