Hoita strobilina

Hoita strobilina, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hoita strobilina produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.
