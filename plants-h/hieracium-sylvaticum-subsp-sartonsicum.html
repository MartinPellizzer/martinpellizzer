Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. sartonsicum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

sartonsicum, commonly known as the Sartons' hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and shorter peduncles compared to other Hieracium species.
