Hieracium cuspidatoides

Hieracium cuspidatoides, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
