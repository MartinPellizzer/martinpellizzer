Helleborine athensis

Helleborine athensis, commonly known as the Greek helleborine, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of pale green to white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The flowers often bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle yet elegant presence to its natural habitat.
