Hippocratea buchananii

Hippocratea buchananii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces legume pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
