Holcus zizanioides

Holcus zizanioides, commonly known as yellow nutsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular, smooth, and often yellowish stems that grow from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, triangular, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Yellow nutsedge is an invasive weed that thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and wetland areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to reproduce through both seeds and underground tubers make it a challenging weed to control.
