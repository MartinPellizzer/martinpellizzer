Hesperevax acaulis var. robustior

Hesperevax acaulis var. robustior, commonly known as the robust desert cactus, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

This variety is notable for its resilience to extreme temperatures and poor soil conditions.
