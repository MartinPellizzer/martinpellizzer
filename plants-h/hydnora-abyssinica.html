Hydnora abyssinica

Hydnora abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian hydnora, is a parasitic plant native to the arid regions of Ethiopia and Somalia.

It lacks true leaves, stems, and roots, relying entirely on its host plant for nutrients and water. The plant produces a unique, fleshy, reddish structure that resembles a flower, which serves as an attractant for specific pollinators. Its reproductive structures are adapted for sexual reproduction through insect pollination.

Due to its specialized biology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
