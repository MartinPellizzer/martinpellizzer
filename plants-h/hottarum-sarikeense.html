Hottarum sarikeense

Hottarum sarikeense, commonly known as the Sarikeense Broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and derives its nutrients from host plants through specialized root structures called haustoria. This species is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on the roots of grasses and shrubs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale yellow or greenish in color.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host plant growth and diversity.
