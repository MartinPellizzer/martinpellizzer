Hydrophyllum spiraeifolium

Hydrophyllum spiraeifolium, commonly known as bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush habitats.

Hydrophyllum spiraeifolium is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
