Hakea ceratophylla var. subintegrifolia

Hakea ceratophylla var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry gardens.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
