Hosackia argyraea

Hosackia argyraea, commonly known as the silverleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces showy blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hosackia argyraea is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
