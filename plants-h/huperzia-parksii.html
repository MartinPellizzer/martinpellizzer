Huperzia parksii

Huperzia parksii, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves and a distinctive, compact growth habit. Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is often found in shaded, rocky habitats and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
