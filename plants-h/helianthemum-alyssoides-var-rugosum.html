Helianthemum alyssoides var. rugosum

Helianthemum alyssoides var. rugosum, commonly known as the rough rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
