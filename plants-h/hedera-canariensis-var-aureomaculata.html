Hedera canariensis var. aureomaculata

Hedera canariensis var. aureomaculata, commonly known as the Canary ivy, is a variant of the common ivy species.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which feature a yellowish or cream-colored pattern against a dark green background. The plant is a climbing evergreen vine that produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
