Hymenophyllum herzogii

Hymenophyllum herzogii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on the bark of trees. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which release spores for reproduction.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to forest biodiversity and providing microhabitats for small organisms.
