Helleborus tinctus

Helleborus tinctus, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland habitats. Its flowers have a distinctive appearance, with a central rosette of petals and a unique, almost translucent quality.

Helleborus tinctus is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
