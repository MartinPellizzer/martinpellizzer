Helichrysum striatum var. subflavescens

Helichrysum striatum var. subflavescens, commonly known as the golden everlasting or narrow-leaved everlasting, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers.
