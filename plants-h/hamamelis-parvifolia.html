Hamamelis parvifolia

Hamamelis parvifolia, commonly known as the Chinese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and medicinal properties.
