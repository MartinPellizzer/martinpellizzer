Hieracium laurinum subsp. amygdalinum

Hieracium laurinum subsp.

amygdalinum, commonly known as the almond-hued hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disk shape. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
