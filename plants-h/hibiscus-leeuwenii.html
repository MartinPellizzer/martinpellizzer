Hibiscus leeuwenii

Hibiscus leeuwenii, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent red throat, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Hibiscus leeuwenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and tropical landscapes.
