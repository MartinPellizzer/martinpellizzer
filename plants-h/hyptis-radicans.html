Hyptis radicans

Hyptis radicans, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, mint-like fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white to pale purple and arranged in whorls along the stems.

Hyptis radicans is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
