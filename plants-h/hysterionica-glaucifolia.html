Hysterionica glaucifolia

Hysterionica glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of glaucous, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
