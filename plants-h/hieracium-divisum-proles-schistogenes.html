Hieracium divisum proles schistogenes

Hieracium divisum proles schistogenes, commonly known as the schistogenous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in specific ecological niches and its role in local biodiversity.
