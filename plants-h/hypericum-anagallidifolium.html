Hypericum anagallidifolium

Hypericum anagallidifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hypericum anagallidifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
