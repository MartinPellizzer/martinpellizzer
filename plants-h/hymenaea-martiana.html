Hymenaea martiana

Hymenaea martiana, commonly known as the yellow cedar or Brazilian cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, slender leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces hard, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and workability. Its seeds are enclosed in a thick, woody pod and are dispersed by animals.

Hymenaea martiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
