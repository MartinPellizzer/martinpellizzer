Handroanthus dentatus

Handroanthus dentatus, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces elongated, tube-like flowers that open to reveal five petals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
