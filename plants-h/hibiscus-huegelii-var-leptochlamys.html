Hibiscus huegelii var. leptochlamys

Hibiscus huegelii var. leptochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Hibiscus huegelii var. leptochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
