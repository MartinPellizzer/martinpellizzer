Haplophyllum acutifolium

Haplophyllum acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved haplophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Haplophyllum acutifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
