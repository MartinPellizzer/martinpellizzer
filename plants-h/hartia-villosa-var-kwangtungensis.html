Hartia villosa var. kwangtungensis

Hartia villosa var. kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or clay soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
