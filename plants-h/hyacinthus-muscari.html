Hyacinthus muscari

Hyacinthus muscari, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its early spring blooming habit. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The grape hyacinth is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
