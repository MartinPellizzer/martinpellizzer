Hypericum mexicanum var. intermedium

Hypericum mexicanum var. intermedium, commonly known as Mexican St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrub habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
