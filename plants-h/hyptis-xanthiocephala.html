Hyptis xanthiocephala

Hyptis xanthiocephala, commonly known as the yellow-headed hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
