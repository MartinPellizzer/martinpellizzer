Hieracium flocculiferum

Hieracium flocculiferum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium flocculiferum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
