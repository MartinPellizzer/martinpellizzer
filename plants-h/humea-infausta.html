Humea infausta

Humea infausta, commonly known as the devil's orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often dark-colored flowers and unique, elongated petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests. Its botanical features include a slender stem and lanceolate leaves with a waxy texture.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Humea infausta is considered a conservation concern.
