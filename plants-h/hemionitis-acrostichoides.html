Hemionitis acrostichoides

Hemionitis acrostichoides, commonly known as the broadleaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are typically divided into numerous segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are evergreen, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves.

Hemionitis acrostichoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
