Hieracium distichanthelum

Hieracium distichanthelum, commonly known as the two-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
