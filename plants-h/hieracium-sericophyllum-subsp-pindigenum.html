Hieracium sericophyllum subsp. pindigenum

Hieracium sericophyllum subsp.

pindigenum, commonly known as the velvet hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its flowers are usually yellow to orange, arranged in a single head, and bloom from late spring to early summer.
