Hakea humifusa

Hakea humifusa, commonly known as the low hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Hakea humifusa is adapted to drought conditions and is an important component of local ecosystems.
