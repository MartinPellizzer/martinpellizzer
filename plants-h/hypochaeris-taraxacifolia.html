Hypochaeris taraxacifolia

Hypochaeris taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelion-like hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble those of dandelions. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to various parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces achenes with pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

It is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
