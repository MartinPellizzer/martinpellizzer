Helianthus occidentalis

Helianthus occidentalis, commonly known as the western sunflower, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a straight, sturdy stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are large, bright yellow, and surrounded by green bracts, making it a striking and easily identifiable plant.
