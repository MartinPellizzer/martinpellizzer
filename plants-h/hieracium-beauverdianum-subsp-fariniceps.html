Hieracium beauverdianum subsp. fariniceps

Hieracium beauverdianum subsp.

fariniceps, commonly known as the fariniceps hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a creeping growth habit and produces numerous small, herbaceous stems.

It is considered a minor weed but can be persistent in certain environments.
