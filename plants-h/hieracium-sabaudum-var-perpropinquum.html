Hieracium sabaudum var. perpropinquum

Hieracium sabaudum var. perpropinquum, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
