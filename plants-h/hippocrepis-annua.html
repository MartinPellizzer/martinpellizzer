Hippocrepis annua

Hippocrepis annua, commonly known as the annual gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that are tubular in shape, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Hippocrepis annua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
