Hieracium bealii

Hieracium bealii, commonly known as Beal's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Hieracium bealii is considered a characteristic species of wetland ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
