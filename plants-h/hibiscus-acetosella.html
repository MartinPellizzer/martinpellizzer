Hibiscus acetosella

Hibiscus acetosella, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist or swampy environments, often near water bodies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red. Hibiscus acetosella is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
