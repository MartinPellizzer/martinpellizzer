Hieracium saxifragum subsp. austrovallesiacum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

austrovallesiacum, commonly known as the southern valley hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually covered with fine, glandular hairs.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
