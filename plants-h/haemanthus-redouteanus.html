Haemanthus redouteanus

Haemanthus redouteanus, commonly known as the Redoute's haemanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences. The plant features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. Its flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring, come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
