Heterochaeta hispida

Heterochaeta hispida, commonly known as the rough hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Heterochaeta hispida is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant.
