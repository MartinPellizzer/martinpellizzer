Hedeoma acinoides

Hedeoma acinoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and pond edges. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a soft, hairy texture.
