Hieracium stenstroemii var. subincrassans

Hieracium stenstroemii var. subincrassans, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
