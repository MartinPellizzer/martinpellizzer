Hypericum pauciflorum subsp. involutum

Hypericum pauciflorum subsp.

involutum, commonly known as the curled-stamen St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features small, yellow flowers with prominent stamens that curl inward, a distinctive characteristic of the species.

The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
