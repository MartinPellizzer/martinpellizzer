Homalomena davidiana

Homalomena davidiana, commonly known as the David's elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in diameter. The plant produces striking spadix flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the arum family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
