Hieracium luteolum

Hieracium luteolum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers, which appear in clusters, are accompanied by leafy bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium luteolum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, often forming dense colonies that can outcompete native vegetation.
