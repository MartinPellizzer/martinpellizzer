Hesperis flava

Hesperis flava, commonly known as the yellow horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hesperis flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
