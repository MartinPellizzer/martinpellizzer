Hieracium swaneticiforme

Hieracium swaneticiforme, commonly known as the Swedish hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium swaneticiforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
