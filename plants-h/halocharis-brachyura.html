Halocharis brachyura

Halocharis brachyura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved halocharis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture.

Halocharis brachyura is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
