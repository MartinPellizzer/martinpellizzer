Helinus integrifolius

Helinus integrifolius, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth-edged. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Helinus integrifolius is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun.
