Hosta tardiflora

Hosta tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering hosta, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, which are borne on upright, slender stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hosta tardiflora is valued for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice for woodland gardens.
