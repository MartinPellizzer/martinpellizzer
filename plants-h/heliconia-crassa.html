Heliconia crassa

Heliconia crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Heliconia crassa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
