Hieracium terenodes

Hieracium terenodes, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium terenodes is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
