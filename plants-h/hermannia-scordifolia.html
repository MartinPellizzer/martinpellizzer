Hermannia scordifolia

Hermannia scordifolia, commonly known as the sour grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Hermannia scordifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
