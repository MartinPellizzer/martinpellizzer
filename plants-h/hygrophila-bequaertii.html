Hygrophila bequaertii

Hygrophila bequaertii, commonly known as the African waterweed, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

Hygrophila bequaertii is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide oxygen to the water.
