Hilairanthus nitidus

Hilairanthus nitidus, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hilairanthus nitidus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
