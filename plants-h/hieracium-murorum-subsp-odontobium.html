Hieracium murorum subsp. odontobium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

odontobium, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and usually unbranched. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk.

The subspecies is distinguished by its toothed leaf margins and is part of the Asteraceae family.
