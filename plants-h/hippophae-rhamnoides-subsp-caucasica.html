Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. caucasica

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and its use in traditional medicine and nutrition.
