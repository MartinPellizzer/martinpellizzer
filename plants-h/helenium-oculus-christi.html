Helenium oculus-christi

Helenium oculus-christi, commonly known as the Christ's eye, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features showy, orange-red ray flowers surrounding a central yellow disk, creating a distinctive "eye" appearance. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and blooms from late summer into early fall.

Helenium oculus-christi is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
