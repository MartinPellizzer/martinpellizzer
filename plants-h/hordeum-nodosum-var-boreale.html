Hordeum nodosum var. boreale

Hordeum nodosum var. boreale, commonly known as the northern nodding barley, is a perennial grass species native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often nodding inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
