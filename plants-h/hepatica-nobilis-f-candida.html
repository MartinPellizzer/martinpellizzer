Hepatica nobilis f. candida

Hepatica nobilis f. candida, commonly known as the white hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooms of white or pale pink flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped outline. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is often found in calcareous soils.

Hepatica nobilis f. candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
