Hedysarum lindleyi

Hedysarum lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its pods are slender, hairy, and contain several seeds.

Hedysarum lindleyi is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
