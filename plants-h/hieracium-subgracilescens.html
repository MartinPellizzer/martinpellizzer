Hieracium subgracilescens

Hieracium subgracilescens, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
