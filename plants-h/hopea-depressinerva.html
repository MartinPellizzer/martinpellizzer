Hopea depressinerva

Hopea depressinerva, commonly known as the lowland dipterocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing winged seeds. It plays an important ecological role in lowland rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.

Due to its value in timber and ecological significance, it is also a subject of conservation interest.
