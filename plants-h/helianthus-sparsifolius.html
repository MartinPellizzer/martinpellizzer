Helianthus sparsifolius

Helianthus sparsifolius, commonly known as the sparseleaf sunflower, is a species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems with few, widely spaced leaves, giving it its characteristic sparse foliage. Its yellow composite flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helianthus sparsifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
