Hedysarum atropatanum

Hedysarum atropatanum, commonly known as the blackroot sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Hedysarum atropatanum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
