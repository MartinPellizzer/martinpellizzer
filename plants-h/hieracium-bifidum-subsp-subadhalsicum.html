Hieracium bifidum subsp. subadhalsicum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subadhalsicum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
