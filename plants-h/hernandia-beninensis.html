Hernandia beninensis

Hernandia beninensis, commonly known as the Benin hernandia, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers, which are followed by orange to red fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
