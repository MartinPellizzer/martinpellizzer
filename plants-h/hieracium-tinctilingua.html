Hieracium tinctilingua

Hieracium tinctilingua, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy, purple to pinkish yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Hieracium tinctilingua is noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities.
