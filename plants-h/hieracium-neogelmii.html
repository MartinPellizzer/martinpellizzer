Hieracium neogelmii

Hieracium neogelmii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high altitudes where conditions are harsh. It has a hairy stem and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

Hieracium neogelmii is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
