Hypochaeris palustris

Hypochaeris palustris, commonly known as swamp buttercup, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk and surrounding petals. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and may have a hairy texture.

Hypochaeris palustris is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small insects.
