Huperzia foliacea

Huperzia foliacea, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, linear, and often wiry leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Huperzia foliacea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
