Helianthemum multiflorum

Helianthemum multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters, featuring dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it survive in dry, rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Helianthemum multiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
