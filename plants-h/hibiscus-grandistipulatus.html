Hibiscus grandistipulatus

Hibiscus grandistipulatus, commonly known as the large-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

Hibiscus grandistipulatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
