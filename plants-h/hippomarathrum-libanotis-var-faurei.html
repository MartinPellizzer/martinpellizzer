Hippomarathrum libanotis var. faurei

Hippomarathrum libanotis var. faurei, commonly known as the Lebanese thistle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in local flora and ecological studies.
