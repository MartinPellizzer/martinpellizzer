Huttonella compacta

Huttonella compacta, commonly known as the compact huttonella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, often blooming during the cooler months. Huttonella compacta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
