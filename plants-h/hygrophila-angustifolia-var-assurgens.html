Hygrophila angustifolia var. assurgens

Hygrophila angustifolia var. assurgens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water hyssop, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in spikes during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens or as a decorative element in aquariums.
