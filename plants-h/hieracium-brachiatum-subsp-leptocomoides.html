Hieracium brachiatum subsp. leptocomoides

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

leptocomoides, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its relatively small size and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
