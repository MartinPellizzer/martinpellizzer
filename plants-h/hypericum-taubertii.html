Hypericum taubertii

Hypericum taubertii, commonly known as the Taubert's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant has woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties, though less studied than other Hypericum species, have been explored for potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
