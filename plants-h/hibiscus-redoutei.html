Hibiscus redoutei

Hibiscus redoutei, commonly known as the redoute's hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the summer months. Hibiscus redoutei is characterized by its hibiscus-type flowers, which have five petals and a central yellow stigma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
