Habenaria miersiana

Habenaria miersiana, commonly known as the Miers' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with purple or brown markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
