Holopetalum pumilum

Holopetalum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, making it ideal for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Holopetalum pumilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
