Hakea pallarupensis

Hakea pallarupensis, commonly known as the Western Australian hakea, is a shrub native to the southwestern region of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-branching form and produces distinctive red or orange flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and is drought-tolerant.

Hakea pallarupensis is also known for its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
