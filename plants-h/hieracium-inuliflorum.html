Hieracium inuliflorum

Hieracium inuliflorum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
