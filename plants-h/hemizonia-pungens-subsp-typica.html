Hemizonia pungens subsp. typica

Hemizonia pungens subsp.

typica, commonly known as the spiny honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
