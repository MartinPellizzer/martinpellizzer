Hieracium × bucuranum

Hieracium × bucuranum, commonly known as the Bucur's hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
