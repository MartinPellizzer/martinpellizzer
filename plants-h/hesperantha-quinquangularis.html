Hesperantha quinquangularis

Hesperantha quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled hesperantha, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

This perennial herb features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive dark purple or black basal stripe. The plant has a short, stocky stem and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its unique, angular seed pods are a notable botanical feature, splitting open to release seeds.

Hesperantha quinquangularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
