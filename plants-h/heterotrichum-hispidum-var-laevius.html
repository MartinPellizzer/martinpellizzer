Heterotrichum hispidum var. laevius

Heterotrichum hispidum var. laevius, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed feather moss, is a variant of the heterotrichum genus found in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth and dark green, elongated leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green capsules for spore dispersal, which are often clustered at the tips of the shoots. It thrives in acidic soils and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

This moss is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a component of diverse woodland ecosystems.
