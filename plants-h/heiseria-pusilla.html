Heiseria pusilla

Heiseria pusilla, commonly known as dwarf goldenweed, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers and low-growing habit, typically reaching only a few inches in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blooms in the summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Heiseria pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
