Hieracium sylvaticum var. subcyaneum

Hieracium sylvaticum var. subcyaneum, commonly known as the blue devil's-bit, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are arranged in a composite head, with both ray and disk florets contributing to its ornamental appearance.
