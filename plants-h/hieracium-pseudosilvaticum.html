Hieracium pseudosilvaticum

Hieracium pseudosilvaticum, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hieracium pseudosilvaticum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
