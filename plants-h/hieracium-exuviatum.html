Hieracium exuviatum

Hieracium exuviatum, commonly known as the velvet hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are basal and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in various conditions.
