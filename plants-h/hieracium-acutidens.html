Hieracium acutidens

Hieracium acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head, which is surrounded by a series of bracts. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Hieracium acutidens is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
