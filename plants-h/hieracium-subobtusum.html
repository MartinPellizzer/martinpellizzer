Hieracium subobtusum

Hieracium subobtusum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has hairy stems and is often found in temperate climates.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and flies, contributing to its ecological significance.
