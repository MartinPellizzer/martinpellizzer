Helianthus carnosus

Helianthus carnosus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus carnosus is a perennial plant that is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
