Hevea spruceana var. discolor

Hevea spruceana var. discolor, commonly known as the pink-stemmed rubber tree, is a variant of the Hevea spruceana species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or reddish coloration in its stems and young leaves, which sets it apart from other members of the genus. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 15 meters. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique coloration makes it a sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
