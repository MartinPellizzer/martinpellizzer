Hibbertia coriacea var. lanceolata

Hibbertia coriacea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaf hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Hibbertia coriacea var. lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
