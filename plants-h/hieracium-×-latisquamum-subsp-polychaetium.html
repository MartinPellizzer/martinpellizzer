Hieracium × latisquamum subsp. polychaetium

Hieracium × latisquamum subsp.

polychaetium, commonly known as the broad-scale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the family. Its leaves are usually lanceolate to ovate, with a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is known for its variability in leaf and flower morphology, which contributes to its adaptability in different habitats.
