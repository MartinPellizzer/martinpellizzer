Hibbertia coriacea subsp. coriacea

Hibbertia coriacea subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the rough hibbertia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its woody stems and serrated, rough-textured leaves.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments. Hibbertia coriacea subsp.

coriacea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
