Haldina cordifolia

Haldina cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf haldina, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant features elliptical to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have five petals.

Haldina cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
