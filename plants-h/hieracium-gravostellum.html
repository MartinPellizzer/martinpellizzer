Hieracium gravostellum

Hieracium gravostellum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium gravostellum is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
