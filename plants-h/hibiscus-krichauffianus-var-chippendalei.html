Hibiscus krichauffianus var. chippendalei

Hibiscus krichauffianus var. chippendalei, commonly known as the Chippendale hibiscus, is a striking tropical shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, with a prominent yellow throat. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
