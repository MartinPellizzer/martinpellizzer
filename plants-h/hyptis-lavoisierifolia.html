Hyptis lavoisierifolia

Hyptis lavoisierifolia, commonly known as the Lavoisier's hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis lavoisierifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
