Hibiscus coddii subsp. coddii

Hibiscus coddii subsp.

coddii, commonly known as the Codd's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Hibiscus coddii subsp.

coddii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
