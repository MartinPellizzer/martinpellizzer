Hedysarum boveanum subsp. boveanum

Hedysarum boveanum subsp.

boveanum, commonly known as the Bovean sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its resilience in challenging climates.
