Hydrangea luteovenosa var. yakushimana

Hydrangea luteovenosa var. yakushimana, commonly known as the Yakushimana hydrangea, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in acidic soil conditions and prefers partial to full shade, making it well-suited for woodland gardens. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a vibrant green color that contrasts beautifully with the blooms.

The plant is valued for its unique floral display and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
