Hyparrhenia pilgeriana

Hyparrhenia pilgeriana, commonly known as the African grass or pilger's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia pilgeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
