Hakea loranthifolia

Hakea loranthifolia, commonly known as the misty hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage and distinctive, lantern-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has woody stems and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Hakea loranthifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
