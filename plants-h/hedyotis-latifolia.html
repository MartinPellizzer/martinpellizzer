Hedyotis latifolia

Hedyotis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hedyotis latifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
