Hieracium bifidum subsp. subconfluens

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subconfluens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy surface, and produces yellow flowers in dense clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like blooms.
