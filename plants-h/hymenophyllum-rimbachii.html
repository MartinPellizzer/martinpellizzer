Hymenophyllum rimbachii

Hymenophyllum rimbachii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, scale-like fronds that give it a silvery appearance. This epiphytic plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thin and papery, with a unique structure that allows it to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Hymenophyllum rimbachii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light.
