Hieraciodes pterothecodes

Hieraciodes pterothecodes, commonly known as the golden tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
