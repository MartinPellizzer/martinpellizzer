Hibiscus marmoratus

Hibiscus marmoratus, commonly known as the marble curl hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, curling leaves that resemble marbled patterns, and its vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Hibiscus marmoratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
