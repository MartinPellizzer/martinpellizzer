Hopea garangbuaya

Hopea garangbuaya, commonly known as the Borneo ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellow or green, and it produces woody fruits containing one to three seeds.

Hopea garangbuaya plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
