Helicteres tiliifolia

Helicteres tiliifolia, commonly known as the silky tamarillo, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible and has a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.

Helicteres tiliifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
