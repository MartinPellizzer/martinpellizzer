Humbertiella decaryi var. sakarahensis

Humbertiella decaryi var. sakarahensis, commonly known as the Sakaraha orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within specific forest habitats. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a short rhizome system.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
