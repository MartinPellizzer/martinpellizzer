Halothamnus bamianicus

Halothamnus bamianicus, commonly known as the Bamian Himalayan tamarisk, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soils in harsh, high-altitude ecosystems.
