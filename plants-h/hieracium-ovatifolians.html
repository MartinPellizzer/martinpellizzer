Hieracium ovatifolians

Hieracium ovatifolians, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features ovate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, with a smooth, often reddish stem. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, forming a showy inflorescence that attracts pollinators.

Hieracium ovatifolians is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seed dispersal.
