Hieracium lanatissimum

Hieracium lanatissimum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
