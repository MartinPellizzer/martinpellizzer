Hieracium serratifrons subsp. crassiceps

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

crassiceps, commonly known as the broad-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with numerous small florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often forming dense colonies in moist, well-drained soils.
