Haemadictyon ovatum

Haemadictyon ovatum, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and forest floors. Its small, white to greenish flowers are arranged in a spike and bloom in the spring.

The leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
