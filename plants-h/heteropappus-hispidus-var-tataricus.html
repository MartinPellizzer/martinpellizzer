Heteropappus hispidus var. tataricus

Heteropappus hispidus var. tataricus, commonly known as the Tatarian eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white down. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicle-like inflorescences, and are typically pink to purple in color.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
