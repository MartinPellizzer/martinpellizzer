Heracleum microcarpum var. subbipinnatum

Heracleum microcarpum var. subbipinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that splits into two parts when mature.
