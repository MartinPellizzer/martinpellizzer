Handroanthus barbatus

Handroanthus barbatus, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
