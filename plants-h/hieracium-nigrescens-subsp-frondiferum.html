Hieracium nigrescens subsp. frondiferum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

frondiferum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies frondiferum is distinguished by its more robust foliage and larger flower heads compared to other members of the species.
