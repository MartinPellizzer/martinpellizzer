Habenaria citrina

Habenaria citrina, commonly known as the yellow lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hair-like inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, greenish-brown capsules as fruit.

Habenaria citrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
