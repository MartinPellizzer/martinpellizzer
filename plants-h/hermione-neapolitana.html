Hermione neapolitana

Hermione neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy green. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in both residential and public landscapes.
