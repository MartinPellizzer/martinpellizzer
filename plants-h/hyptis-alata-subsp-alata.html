Hyptis alata subsp. alata

Hyptis alata subsp.

alata, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or purple, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or disturbed areas. Hyptis alata subsp.

alata is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
