Hieracium commersonii var. megapotamicum

Hieracium commersonii var. megapotamicum, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
