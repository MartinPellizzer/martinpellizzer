Heloniopsis japonica var. breviscapa

Heloniopsis japonica var. breviscapa, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
