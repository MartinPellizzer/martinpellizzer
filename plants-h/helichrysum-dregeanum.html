Helichrysum dregeanum

Helichrysum dregeanum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of golden-yellow, papery flowers that remain open for an extended period. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum dregeanum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
