Hydrangea glandulosa

Hydrangea glandulosa, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in small glands. Its flowers are produced in clusters called corymbs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Hydrangea glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
