Hibiscus moscheutos f. peckii

Hibiscus moscheutos f. peckii, commonly known as the Peck's swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, large, rose-like flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
