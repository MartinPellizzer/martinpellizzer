Hieracium caesium subsp. farinisquamum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

farinisquamum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically rough and covered in fine hairs, which give the plant its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
