Hieracium acrocomum subsp. floribundoides

Hieracium acrocomum subsp.

floribundoides, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

It is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies in suitable conditions.
