Hesiodia montana subsp. comosa

Hesiodia montana subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the mountain hesiodia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
