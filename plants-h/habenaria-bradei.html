Habenaria bradei

Habenaria bradei, commonly known as the Brade's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically features a pseudobulb and possesses a complex root system adapted to its moist, shaded habitats. Its floral structure includes three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more showy.

Habenaria bradei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
