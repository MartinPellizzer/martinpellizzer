Hohenbergia utriculosa

Hohenbergia utriculosa, commonly known as the bladder bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, overlapping leaves that form a water-storing cup, a feature typical of bromeliads. The leaves are usually dark green with a waxy coating, and they can develop a reddish or purple hue under certain conditions. The plant produces a tall flower spike bearing small, white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

Hohenbergia utriculosa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and ability to thrive in humid environments.
