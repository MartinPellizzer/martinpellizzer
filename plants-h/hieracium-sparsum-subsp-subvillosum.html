Hieracium sparsum subsp. subvillosum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

subvillosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves.

The subspecies subvillosum is distinguished by its more pronounced pubescence, particularly on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

The plant is often found in open habitats where it can thrive with adequate sunlight and moderate moisture.
