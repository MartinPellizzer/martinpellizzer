Hymenopyramis brachiata

Hymenopyramis brachiata, commonly known as the branched hymenopyramis, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and the ecological niche it occupies in its natural habitat.
