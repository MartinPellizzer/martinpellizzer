Hieracium lacistophylloides

Hieracium lacistophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are narrowly lanceolate, and the flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
