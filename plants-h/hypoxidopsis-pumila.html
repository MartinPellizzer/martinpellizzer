Hypoxidopsis pumila

Hypoxidopsis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf hypoxidopsis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular plant among gardeners interested in succulents and flowering plants.
