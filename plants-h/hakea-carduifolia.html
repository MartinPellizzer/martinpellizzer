Hakea carduifolia

Hakea carduifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea carduifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
