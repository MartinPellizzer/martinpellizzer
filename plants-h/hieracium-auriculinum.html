Hieracium auriculinum

Hieracium auriculinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from June to September, and the leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures.

Hieracium auriculinum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
