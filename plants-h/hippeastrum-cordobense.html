Hippeastrum cordobense

Hippeastrum cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant produces strikingly colorful blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting dark center. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Hippeastrum cordobense is a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
