Hormathophylla cochleata subsp. cochleata

Hormathophylla cochleata subsp.

cochleata, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which form a dense, cylindrical structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
