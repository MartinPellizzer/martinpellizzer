Heuchera americana var. caroliniana

Heuchera americana var. caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, colorful leaves that range from green to red, purple, and bronze. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
