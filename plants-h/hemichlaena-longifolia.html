Hemichlaena longifolia

Hemichlaena longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hemichlaena, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Hemichlaena longifolia thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of swamps.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
