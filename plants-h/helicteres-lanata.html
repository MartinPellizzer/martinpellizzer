Helicteres lanata

Helicteres lanata, commonly known as the hairy helicteres, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bracts that surround clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
