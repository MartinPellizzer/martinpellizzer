Hypericum erectum f. perforatum

Hypericum erectum f. perforatum, commonly known as the perforate St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive perforation along the margins. This variety is closely related to other Hypericum species and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for garden cultivation.
