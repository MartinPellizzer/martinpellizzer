Helianthus annuus var. flavobasis

Helianthus annuus var. flavobasis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its distinctive yellow center and vibrant orange petals.

It is native to North America and thrives in well-drained soils with full sunlight. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 3 meters in height. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
