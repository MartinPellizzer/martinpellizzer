Hieracium scabriceps

Hieracium scabriceps, commonly known as the rough-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are rough to the touch, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are usually yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium scabriceps is often found in sunny, open habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.
