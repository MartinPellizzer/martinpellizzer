Hura crepitans var. senegalensis

Hura crepitans var. senegalensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and open woodlands.

The tree is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to withstand drought conditions.
