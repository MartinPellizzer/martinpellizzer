Heterosperma achaetum

Heterosperma achaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along its stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three sepals and three petals.

Heterosperma achaetum plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
