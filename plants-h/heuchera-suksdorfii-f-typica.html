Heuchera suksdorfii f. typica

Heuchera suksdorfii f. typica, commonly known as the Saksdorf's coral bells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The foliage is typically variegated, with dark green leaves edged in rust or red, adding ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes throughout the growing season.
