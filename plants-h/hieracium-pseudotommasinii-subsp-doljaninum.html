Hieracium pseudotommasinii subsp. doljaninum

Hieracium pseudotommasinii subsp.

doljaninum, commonly known as the Doljanin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
