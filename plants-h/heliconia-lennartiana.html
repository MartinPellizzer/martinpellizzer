Heliconia lennartiana

Heliconia lennartiana, commonly known as the Lennart's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is part of the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spiral arrangement of brightly colored, leaf-like bracts that enclose the actual flowers.

Heliconia lennartiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
