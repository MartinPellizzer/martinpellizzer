Hieracium cuspidatum

Hieracium cuspidatum, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, daisy-like cluster.

Hieracium cuspidatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
