Haplopappus punctatus

Haplopappus punctatus, commonly known as the punctate pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as a low, mat-forming perennial with narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish to white flowers. Its seed heads are covered in fine, bristly hairs, which aid in wind dispersal.

Haplopappus punctatus is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system and drought-tolerant features.
