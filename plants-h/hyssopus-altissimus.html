Hyssopus altissimus

Hyssopus altissimus, commonly known as tall hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens.

Hyssopus altissimus is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
