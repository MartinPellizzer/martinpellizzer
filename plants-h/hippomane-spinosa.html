Hippomane spinosa

Hippomane spinosa, commonly known as the arrowwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody structure, simple leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
