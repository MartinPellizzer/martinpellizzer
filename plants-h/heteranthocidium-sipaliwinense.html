Heteranthocidium sipaliwinense

Heteranthocidium sipaliwinense, commonly known as the Sipaliwin Mountains cactus, is a rare flowering plant native to the Sipaliwin Mountains in Suriname.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from environmental degradation and climate change.
