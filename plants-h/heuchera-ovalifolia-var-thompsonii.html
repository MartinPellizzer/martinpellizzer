Heuchera ovalifolia var. thompsonii

Heuchera ovalifolia var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and purple, providing ornamental value in shaded gardens. The plant produces upright, spikelike clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a versatile ground cover.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, preferring cool, humid conditions.
