Hymenophyllum pilosissimum

Hymenophyllum pilosissimum, commonly known as the silky maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like fronds covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, scale-like leaves are arranged in a tight rosette, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

Hymenophyllum pilosissimum is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, where it can access the high humidity it requires for survival.
