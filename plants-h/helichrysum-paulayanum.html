Helichrysum paulayanum

Helichrysum paulayanum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a long-lasting appearance, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
