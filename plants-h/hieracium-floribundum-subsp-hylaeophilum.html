Hieracium floribundum subsp. hylaeophilum

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

hylaeophilum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
