Hieracium atramentosum

Hieracium atramentosum, commonly known as the black-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and often have a dark green to reddish hue, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
