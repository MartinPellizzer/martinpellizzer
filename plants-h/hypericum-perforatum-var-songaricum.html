Hypericum perforatum var. songaricum

Hypericum perforatum var. songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric St. John's Wort, is a variant of the common St. John's Wort.

It is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive leaf morphology. The leaves are often perforated, a trait shared with the species, but this variant exhibits more pronounced leaf holes. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
