Hypericum armenum

Hypericum armenum, commonly known as Armenian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, and its woody stems covered in small, glandular dots. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

The leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.
