Hippia vahliana

Hippia vahliana, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, saline environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Hippia vahliana is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and ornamental value.
