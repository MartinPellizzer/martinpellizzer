Hieracium hyoseriophyllum

Hieracium hyoseriophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and the leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

It is known for its rapid spread and is often considered an invasive species in certain regions.
