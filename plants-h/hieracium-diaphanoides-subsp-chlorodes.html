Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. chlorodes

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

chlorodes, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale green to translucent leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
