Hybophrynium braunianum var. violaceum

Hybophrynium braunianum var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple-flowered hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to violet, adding a dramatic visual element to any garden. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
