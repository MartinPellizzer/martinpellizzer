Hippeastrum puniceum f. barbatum

Hippeastrum puniceum f. barbatum, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from deep red to orange-red in color. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms that typically appear in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and waxy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This hybrid variety is valued for its dramatic floral display and is often cultivated for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
