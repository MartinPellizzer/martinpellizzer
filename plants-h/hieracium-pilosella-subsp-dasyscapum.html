Hieracium pilosella subsp. dasyscapum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

dasyscapum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
