Hypericum lungusum

Hypericum lungusum, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves with a slightly rough texture and a faint aromatic scent. Its stems are woody and often branched, with a reddish-brown color.

Hypericum lungusum is typically found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
