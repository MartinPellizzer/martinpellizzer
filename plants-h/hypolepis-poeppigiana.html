Hypolepis poeppigiana

Hypolepis poeppigiana, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a unique ability to thrive in aquatic environments, often found in slow-moving rivers and lakes. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

The fronds are typically divided into small, delicate pinnae, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
