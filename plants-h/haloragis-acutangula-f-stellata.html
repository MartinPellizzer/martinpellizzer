Haloragis acutangula f. stellata

Haloragis acutangula f. stellata, commonly known as the star-leaved willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Haloragaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in coastal areas.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
