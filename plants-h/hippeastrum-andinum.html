Hippeastrum andinum

Hippeastrum andinum, commonly known as the Andean amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. Hippeastrum andinum is a bulbous plant, with a fleshy underground stem that supports its growth and flowering cycle.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking blooms and ease of care.
