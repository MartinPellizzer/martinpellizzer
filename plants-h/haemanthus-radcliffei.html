Haemanthus radcliffei

Haemanthus radcliffei, commonly known as the Red Paintbrush, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, brush-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. Its botanical structure includes a bulbous base that stores nutrients, supporting its growth in arid conditions.

Haemanthus radcliffei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
