Hydrangea glabra

Hydrangea glabra, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, bark-like stems and large, rounded clusters of white or pink flowers that often change color depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and serrated edges.

Hydrangea glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various garden conditions.
