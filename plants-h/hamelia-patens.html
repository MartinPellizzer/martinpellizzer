Hamelia patens

Hamelia patens, commonly known as flamebeau or red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant, tubular red flowers in the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hamelia patens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
