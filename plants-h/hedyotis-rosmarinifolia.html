Hedyotis rosmarinifolia

Hedyotis rosmarinifolia, commonly known as rosemaryleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a fragrant, slightly minty aroma, reminiscent of rosemary. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Hedyotis rosmarinifolia is often found in woodland edges and moist, shaded areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
