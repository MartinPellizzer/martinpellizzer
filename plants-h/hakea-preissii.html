Hakea preissii

Hakea preissii, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in winter. The plant produces woody, cone-like structures that contain numerous small, white seeds. It is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Hakea preissii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
