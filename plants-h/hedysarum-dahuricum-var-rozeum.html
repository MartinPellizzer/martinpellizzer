Hedysarum dahuricum var. rozeum

Hedysarum dahuricum var. rozeum, commonly known as the pink-flowered field vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with trifoliate leaves and small, pink to violet flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, colorful blooms that attract pollinators.
