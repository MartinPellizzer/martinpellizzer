Hypoxis dregei var. biflora

Hypoxis dregei var. biflora, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking white to pale yellow flowers at the end of its stems. The plant is characterized by its unique, star-shaped blooms that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
