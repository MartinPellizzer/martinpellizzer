Hedysarum mackenziei var. fraseri

Hedysarum mackenziei var. fraseri, commonly known as Mackenzie's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its unique floral structure and ecological significance make it an important species in its native range.
