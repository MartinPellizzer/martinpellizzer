Hieracium tersundagense

Hieracium tersundagense, commonly known as the Tersundagense Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow, often with a white or greenish base.

Hieracium tersundagense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
