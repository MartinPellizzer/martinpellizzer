Hibiscus cucurbitinus

Hibiscus cucurbitinus, commonly known as the pumpkin hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces fruit that resembles a small pumpkin, hence its common name. Hibiscus cucurbitinus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
