Hieracium maculatum subsp. laevifrons

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

laevifrons, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and often bear characteristic spots or markings.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
