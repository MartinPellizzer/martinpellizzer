Helia splendens

Helia splendens, commonly known as the golden heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture. Helia splendens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
