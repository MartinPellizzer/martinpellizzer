Hysterionica pinnatiloba

Hysterionica pinnatiloba, commonly known as the fern-like plant of the Acanthaceae family, is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that give it a lacy appearance.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a prominent midrib and finely serrated margins. It produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are typically borne in clusters at the leaf axils. Hysterionica pinnatiloba thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural displays.
