Hordeum sativum var. trifurcatum

Hordeum sativum var. trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-awned barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its distinctive three-branched awns on the lemma and palea.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a fibrous root system, which contribute to its adaptability in various environments.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for forage or as a cover crop due to its hardiness and rapid growth.
