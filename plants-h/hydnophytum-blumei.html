Hydnophytum blumei

Hydnophytum blumei, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and reliance on its roots for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Hydnophytum blumei is often found growing on tree branches in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
