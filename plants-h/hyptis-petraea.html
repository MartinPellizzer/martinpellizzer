Hyptis petraea

Hyptis petraea, commonly known as rock hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Hyptis petraea is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
