Hapalochilus geniculifer

Hapalochilus geniculifer, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. This species is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
