Hieracium florentinum subsp. canipedunculatum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

canipedunculatum, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
