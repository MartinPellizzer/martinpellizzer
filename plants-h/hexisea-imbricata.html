Hexisea imbricata

Hexisea imbricata, commonly known as the overlapping leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, overlapping leaf structure that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
