Heliotropium cuspidatum

Heliotropium cuspidatum, commonly known as the spurred heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features distinctive spurred petals, which are a characteristic trait of the heliotrope genus. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

The flowers emit a mild, sweet fragrance, especially in the early morning.
