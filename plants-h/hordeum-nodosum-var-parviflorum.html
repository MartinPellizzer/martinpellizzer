Hordeum nodosum var. parviflorum

Hordeum nodosum var. parviflorum, commonly known as dwarf nodding barley, is a lesser-known variant of the barley genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, nodding inflorescences. The plant typically produces slender spikes with reduced florets, distinguishing it from other Hordeum species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it of interest to botanists and researchers studying plant morphology and adaptation.
