Heptapleurum bipalmatifolium

Heptapleurum bipalmatifolium, commonly known as the double-palmate heptapleurum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are palmately divided into two pairs of lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
