Hartogia melaleucoides

Hartogia melaleucoides, commonly known as the white melaleuca, is a small evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Hartogia melaleucoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salty environments.
