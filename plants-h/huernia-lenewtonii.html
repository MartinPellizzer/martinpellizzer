Huernia lenewtonii

Huernia lenewtonii, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail" cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a unique appearance with thick, ribbed stems and dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage.

Huernia lenewtonii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
