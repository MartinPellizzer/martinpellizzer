Huperzia regnellii

Huperzia regnellii, commonly known as the regnell's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and needle-like leaves. Native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its leaves.

Huperzia regnellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
