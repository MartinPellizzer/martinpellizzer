Helichrysum ciliatum

Helichrysum ciliatum, commonly known as the silver leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum ciliatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers.
