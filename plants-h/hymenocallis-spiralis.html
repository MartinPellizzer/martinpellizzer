Hymenocallis spiralis

Hymenocallis spiralis, commonly known as the spiral ginger lily, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence that consists of multiple layers of waxy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, trumpet-like blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange, which are often fragrant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hymenocallis spiralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
