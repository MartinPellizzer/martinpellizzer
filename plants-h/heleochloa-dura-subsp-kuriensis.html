Heleochloa dura subsp. kuriensis

Heleochloa dura subsp.

kuriensis, commonly known as the Kuriensis needlegrass, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Central America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, where it forms dense mats. It is adapted to cool, moist conditions and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
