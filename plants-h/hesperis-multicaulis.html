Hesperis multicaulis

Hesperis multicaulis, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features upright stems covered in lance-shaped, aromatic leaves. Its flowers are bright purple or pink, arranged in racemes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hesperis multicaulis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
