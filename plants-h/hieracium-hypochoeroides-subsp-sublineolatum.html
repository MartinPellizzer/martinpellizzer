Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. sublineolatum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

sublineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish underside. Its flowers are yellow and form dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its finely veined leaves and is part of the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
