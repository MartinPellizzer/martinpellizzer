Hieracium glorioanum

Hieracium glorioanum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, showy inflorescence at the top of the stem. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium glorioanum is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
