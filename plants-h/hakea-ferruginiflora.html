Hakea ferruginiflora

Hakea ferruginiflora, commonly known as the rusty-flowered hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

Hakea ferruginiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
