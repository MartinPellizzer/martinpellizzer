Hieracium stelligerum var. albogilvum

Hieracium stelligerum var. albogilvum, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with prominent white veins. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This variety is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers with distinct white markings, making it visually distinct from other hawkweed species.
