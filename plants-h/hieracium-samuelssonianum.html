Hieracium samuelssonianum

Hieracium samuelssonianum, commonly known as Samuelsson's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Scandinavia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow, forming a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
