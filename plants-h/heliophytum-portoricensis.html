Heliophytum portoricensis

Heliophytum portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican sunplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant is adapted to full sun exposure and well-drained soils, making it a typical member of dry forest ecosystems. It plays a role in local pollination and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, it is considered rare and is under threat due to habitat loss and invasive species.
