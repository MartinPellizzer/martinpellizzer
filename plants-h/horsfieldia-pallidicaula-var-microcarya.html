Horsfieldia pallidicaula var. microcarya

Horsfieldia pallidicaula var. microcarya, commonly known as the small-fruited horsfieldia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
