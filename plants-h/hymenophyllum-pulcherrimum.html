Hymenophyllum pulcherrimum

Hymenophyllum pulcherrimum, commonly known as the fairy moss, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, often hair-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures that help retain moisture.

The plant is notable for its intricate, lace-like appearance and is often found in rainforests.
