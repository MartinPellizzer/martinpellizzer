Hereroa wilmaniae

Hereroa wilmaniae, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in extreme drought conditions. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers during the brief rainy season. It is a perennial herb that grows close to the ground, often forming dense clusters.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to the harsh, arid environment of the Namib Desert.
