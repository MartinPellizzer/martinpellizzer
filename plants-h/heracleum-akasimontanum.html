Heracleum akasimontanum

Heracleum akasimontanum, commonly known as the Akasimon mountain hogweed, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
