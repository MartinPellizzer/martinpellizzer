Hieracium sabaudum var. ambiguum

Hieracium sabaudum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the Ambiguous Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers.

This variety is known for its variability in leaf shape and flower color, contributing to its common name.
