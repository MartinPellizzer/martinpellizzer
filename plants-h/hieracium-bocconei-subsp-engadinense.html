Hieracium bocconei subsp. engadinense

Hieracium bocconei subsp.

engadinense, commonly known as the Engadine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky and alpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many parts of its range.
