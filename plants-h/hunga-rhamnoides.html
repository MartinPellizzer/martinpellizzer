Hunga rhamnoides

Hunga rhamnoides, commonly known as the smooth buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark, leathery fruits.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
