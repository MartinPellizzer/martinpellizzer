Hieracium tommasinii subsp. castelli-novi

Hieracium tommasinii subsp.

castelli-novi, commonly known as the Castelli-Novian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Alps. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features upright stems that are hairy and bear basal leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from June to August.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude regions of Italy and Switzerland.
