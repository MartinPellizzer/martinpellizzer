Hyperbaena phanerophlebia

Hyperbaena phanerophlebia, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody structure and small, yellowish-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Hyperbaena phanerophlebia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
