Hortsmania myrtifolia

Hortsmania myrtifolia, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly aromatic scent.

Hortsmania myrtifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
