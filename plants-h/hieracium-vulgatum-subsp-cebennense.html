Hieracium vulgatum subsp. cebennense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

cebennense, commonly known as the Cebennense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
