Heliconia zebrina

Heliconia zebrina, commonly known as the zebra heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, segmented inflorescences with numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia zebrina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant appearance.
