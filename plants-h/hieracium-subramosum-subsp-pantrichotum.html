Hieracium subramosum subsp. pantrichotum

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

pantrichotum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
