Hordeum dagestanicum

Hordeum dagestanicum, commonly known as Dagestan barley, is a species of wild barley native to the Caucasus region.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hordeum dagestanicum is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience in challenging climates.
