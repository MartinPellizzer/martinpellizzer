Hornschuchia mediterranea

Hornschuchia mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean horned bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Hornschuchia mediterranea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
