Hypericum majus

Hypericum majus, commonly known as the greater St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hypericum majus is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
