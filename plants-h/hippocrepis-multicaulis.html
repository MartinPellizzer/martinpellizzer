Hippocrepis multicaulis

Hippocrepis multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, violet-colored blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
