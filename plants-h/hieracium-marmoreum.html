Hieracium marmoreum

Hieracium marmoreum, commonly known as the marbleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
