Hibiscus petherickii

Hibiscus petherickii, commonly known as the Petherick's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and grows as an upright shrub or small tree. Hibiscus petherickii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
