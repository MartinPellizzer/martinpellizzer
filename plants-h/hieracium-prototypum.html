Hieracium prototypum

Hieracium prototypum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium prototypum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
