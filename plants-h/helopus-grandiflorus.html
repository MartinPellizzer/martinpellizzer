Helopus grandiflorus

Helopus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous helopus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
