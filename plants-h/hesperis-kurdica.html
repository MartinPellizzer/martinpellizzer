Hesperis kurdica

Hesperis kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces white or pale purple flowers in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a slightly peppery scent when crushed.

Hesperis kurdica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
