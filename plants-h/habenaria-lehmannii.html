Habenaria lehmannii

Habenaria lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
