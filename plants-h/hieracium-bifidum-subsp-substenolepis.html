Hieracium bifidum subsp. substenolepis

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

substenolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

The species is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
