Hieracium caesium subsp. involutum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

involutum, commonly known as the curled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and is often found in temperate climates.
