Hippeastrum roseoalbum

Hippeastrum roseoalbum, commonly known as the white and pink amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a unique combination of white and pink hues, often with a contrasting pink throat. The plant produces strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are broad and succulent, adapted to store moisture. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring or early summer, depending on the climate.

Hippeastrum roseoalbum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
