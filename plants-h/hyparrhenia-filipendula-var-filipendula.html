Hyparrhenia filipendula var. filipendula

Hyparrhenia filipendula var. filipendula, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive reddish coloration in the inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
