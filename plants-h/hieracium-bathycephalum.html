Hieracium bathycephalum

Hieracium bathycephalum, commonly known as the deep-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium bathycephalum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
