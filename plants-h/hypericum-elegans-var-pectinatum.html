Hypericum elegans var. pectinatum

Hypericum elegans var. pectinatum, commonly known as the silky St. John's wort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in native plant landscapes.
