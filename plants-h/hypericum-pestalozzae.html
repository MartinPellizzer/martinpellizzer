Hypericum pestalozzae

Hypericum pestalozzae, commonly known as the Italian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, typically growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum pestalozzae is a member of the Hypericaceae family, which includes other species of St. John's Wort known for their medicinal properties.
