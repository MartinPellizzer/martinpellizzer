Hymenophyllum sororium

Hymenophyllum sororium, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silky-hairy fronds that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. This epiphytic plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a unique structure that allows it to thrive in high humidity.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
