Heptapleurum cambodianum

Heptapleurum cambodianum, commonly known as Cambodian stonecrop, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is gaining popularity among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
