Hedyotis capensis

Hedyotis capensis, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Hedyotis capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
