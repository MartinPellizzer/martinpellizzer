Heteropogon contortus var. allionii

Heteropogon contortus var. allionii, commonly known as the twisted grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often curl and twist, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique, twisting appearance.
