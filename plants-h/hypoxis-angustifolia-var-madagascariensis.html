Hypoxis angustifolia var. madagascariensis

Hypoxis angustifolia var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan star grass, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
