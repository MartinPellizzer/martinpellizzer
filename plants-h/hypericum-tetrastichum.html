Hypericum tetrastichum

Hypericum tetrastichum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
