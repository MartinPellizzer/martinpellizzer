Heliotropium curassavicum var. parviflorum

Heliotropium curassavicum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central gland. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
