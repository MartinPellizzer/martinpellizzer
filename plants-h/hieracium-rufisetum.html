Hieracium rufisetum

Hieracium rufisetum, commonly known as the red-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
