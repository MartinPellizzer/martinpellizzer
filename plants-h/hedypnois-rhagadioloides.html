Hedypnois rhagadioloides

Hedypnois rhagadioloides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a climbing habit and produces showy, tubular flowers with a strong scent. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a rough texture.

Hedypnois rhagadioloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming behavior.
