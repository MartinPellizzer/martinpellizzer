Hordeum distichon var. laxum

Hordeum distichon var. laxum, commonly known as the looseawn barley, is a grass species native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, two-rowed spikelets and loosely arranged awns, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact inflorescence.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its potential in agricultural or ecological settings.
