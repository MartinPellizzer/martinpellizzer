Hosta glauca

Hosta glauca, commonly known as the blue hosta, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Hosta glauca thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
