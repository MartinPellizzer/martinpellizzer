Hesperis longifolia

Hesperis longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperis longifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
