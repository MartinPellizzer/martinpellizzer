Hieracium oppressatum

Hieracium oppressatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium oppressatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
