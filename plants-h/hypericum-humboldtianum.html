Hypericum humboldtianum

Hypericum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt goldenstem, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to coastal conditions.
