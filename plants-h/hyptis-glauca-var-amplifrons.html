Hyptis glauca var. amplifrons

Hyptis glauca var. amplifrons, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-gray underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in moist, open habitats.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
