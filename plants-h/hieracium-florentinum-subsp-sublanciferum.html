Hieracium florentinum subsp. sublanciferum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

sublanciferum, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
