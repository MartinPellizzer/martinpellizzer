Hemionitis harrisoniae

Hemionitis harrisoniae, commonly known as the Harrison's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricately veined fronds that are typically small in size. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-desert environments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are positioned along the margins of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis harrisoniae is considered a species of conservation concern.
