Hybanthus atropurpureus

Hybanthus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Hybanthus atropurpureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
