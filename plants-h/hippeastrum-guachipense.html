Hippeastrum guachipense

Hippeastrum guachipense, commonly known as the Guachipense amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a trumpet-shaped corona. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are often striped with green and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Hippeastrum guachipense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
