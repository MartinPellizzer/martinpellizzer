Helianthemum apenninum var. caraltii

Helianthemum apenninum var. caraltii, commonly known as the Caralt's rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
