Hypericum ascyron var. umbellatum

Hypericum ascyron var. umbellatum, commonly known as the yellow hypericum or the golden St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is part of the Hypericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
