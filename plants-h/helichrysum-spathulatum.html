Helichrysum spathulatum

Helichrysum spathulatum, commonly known as the spoonleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that remain open for a long period, giving it the common name "everlasting.". The plant has spoon-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
