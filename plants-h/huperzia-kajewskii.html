Huperzia kajewskii

Huperzia kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewski's clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen morphology with erect, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores in capsules that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems.

Huperzia kajewskii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
