Hedysarum johartchii

Hedysarum johartchii, commonly known as the Johart's sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Hedysarum johartchii is also valued for its role in soil fixation due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
