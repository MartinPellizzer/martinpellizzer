Hieracium pictiforme subsp. pseudorionii

Hieracium pictiforme subsp.

pseudorionii, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
