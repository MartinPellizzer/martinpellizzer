Hieracium lachenalii subsp. aurulentum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

aurulentum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and finely divided leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native range.
