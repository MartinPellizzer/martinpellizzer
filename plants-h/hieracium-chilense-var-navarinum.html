Hieracium chilense var. navarinum

Hieracium chilense var. navarinum, commonly known as the Navarino hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
