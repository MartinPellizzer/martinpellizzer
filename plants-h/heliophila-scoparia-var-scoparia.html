Heliophila scoparia var. scoparia

Heliophila scoparia var. scoparia, commonly known as narrow-leaved heliophila, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Heliophila scoparia var. scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
