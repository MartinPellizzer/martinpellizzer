Hypochaeris intertexta

Hypochaeris intertexta, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 30 cm tall, with smooth, hairy, or glandular stems. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste.

Hypochaeris intertexta is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, and it can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
