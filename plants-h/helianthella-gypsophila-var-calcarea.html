Helianthella gypsophila var. calcarea

Helianthella gypsophila var. calcarea, commonly known as the gypsum sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage adapted to arid environments. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found in gypsum or limestone areas. Its small, upright growth habit makes it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
