Hieracium prussicum subsp. chloropoides

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

chloropoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
