Hymenocallis speciosa var. longipetiolata

Hymenocallis speciosa var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled spider lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often tinged with green or blue hues. Its stems are robust and capable of supporting the heavy flower clusters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
