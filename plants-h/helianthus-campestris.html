Helianthus campestris

Helianthus campestris, commonly known as the prairie sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped, rough leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in prairies and open fields.

Helianthus campestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
