Hieracium vulgatum subsp. incurrens

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

incurrens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.
