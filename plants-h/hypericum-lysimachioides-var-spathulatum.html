Hypericum lysimachioides var. spathulatum

Hypericum lysimachioides var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in summer. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden borders or rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
