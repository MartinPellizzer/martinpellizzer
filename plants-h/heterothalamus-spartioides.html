Heterothalamus spartioides

Heterothalamus spartioides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in dry, sandy soils, and its leaves are typically reduced to scales or spines. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Heterothalamus spartioides is adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
