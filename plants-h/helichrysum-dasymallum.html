Helichrysum dasymallum

Helichrysum dasymallum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, and it produces fluffy, seed-filled structures that aid in wind pollination.

Helichrysum dasymallum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
