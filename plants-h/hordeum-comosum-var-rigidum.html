Hordeum comosum var. rigidum

Hordeum comosum var. rigidum, commonly known as rigid wild barley, is a wild grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, stiff stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its adaptability and historical significance in early agricultural practices.
