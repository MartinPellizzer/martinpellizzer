Hypolepis forzzae

Hypolepis forzzae, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the water's surface or through shallow water. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Hypolepis forzzae plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling in freshwater habitats.
