Hydrangea hortensia var. maculata

Hydrangea hortensia var. maculata, commonly known as the lacecap hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of two types of flowers: the sterile, showy outer blooms and the fertile, smaller inner flowers. The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slight variegation, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

The color of its flowers can change based on soil pH, ranging from pink to blue, offering a dynamic display in gardens.
