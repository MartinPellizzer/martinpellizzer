Hosta coerulea

Hosta coerulea, commonly known as Blue Moon, is a striking perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, waxy, dark green leaves that turn a striking blue-gray hue in full sun. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers in late summer. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

Hosta coerulea is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
