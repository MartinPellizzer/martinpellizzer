Hieracium blockii

Hieracium blockii, commonly known as the Block's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
