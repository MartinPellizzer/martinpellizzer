Hedysarum guianense

Hedysarum guianense, commonly known as the Guianan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in humid climates. It has a hairy stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum guianense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
