Hyptis tomentosa

Hyptis tomentosa, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Hyptis tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
