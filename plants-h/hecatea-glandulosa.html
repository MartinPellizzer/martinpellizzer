Hecatea glandulosa

Hecatea glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf hecatea, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its leaves, giving them a sticky texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, with a prominent venation pattern.

Hecatea glandulosa produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellow in color.
