Hieracium lanarium

Hieracium lanarium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Hieracium lanarium is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
