Hedycarya arborea

Hedycarya arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Hedycarya arborea is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
