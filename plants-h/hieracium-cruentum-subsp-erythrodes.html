Hieracium cruentum subsp. erythrodes

Hieracium cruentum subsp.

erythrodes, commonly known as the red-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red centre.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
