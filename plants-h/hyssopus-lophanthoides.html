Hyssopus lophanthoides

Hyssopus lophanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Hyssopus lophanthoides is valued for its aromatic leaves and historical use in traditional medicine.
