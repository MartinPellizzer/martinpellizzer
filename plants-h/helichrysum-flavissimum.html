Helichrysum flavissimum

Helichrysum flavissimum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble small sunbursts, which remain intact even when dried. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum flavissimum is widely used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, vibrant appearance.
