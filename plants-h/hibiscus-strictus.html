Hibiscus strictus

Hibiscus strictus, commonly known as the slender hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features upright, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, yellow, and white, often with a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and typically have a rough texture.

Hibiscus strictus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
