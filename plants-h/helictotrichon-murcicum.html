Helictotrichon murcicum

Helictotrichon murcicum, commonly known as the Murcielago grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
