Hieracium miccylotocum

Hieracium miccylotocum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium miccylotocum is known for its resilience to harsh climates and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
