Hibiscus cannabinus

Hibiscus cannabinus, commonly known as cannabis hemp, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for producing fibers from its stalks, which have been used historically for textiles and rope-making. Its flowers are typically pink to white, and it blooms during the summer months.

Hibiscus cannabinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both practical and decorative purposes.
