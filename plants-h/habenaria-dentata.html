Habenaria dentata

Habenaria dentata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces tall, erect stems with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a loose spike.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
