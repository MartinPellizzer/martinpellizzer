Hirtella fruticulosa

Hirtella fruticulosa, commonly known as the rough hirtella, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and contain a single seed.

Hirtella fruticulosa is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
