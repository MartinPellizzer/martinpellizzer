Hieracium incisum subsp. parengense

Hieracium incisum subsp.

parengense, commonly known as the New Zealand hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers.

The plant has deeply lobed, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
