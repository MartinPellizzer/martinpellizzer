Hieracium naegelianum var. andreae

Hieracium naegelianum var. andreae, commonly known as the Andree's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
