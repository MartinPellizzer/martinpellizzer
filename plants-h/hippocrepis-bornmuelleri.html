Hippocrepis bornmuelleri

Hippocrepis bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Balkan orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in calcareous soils, often in open woodlands or rocky areas. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded shape, which is characteristic of the genus Hippocrepis. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some regions and is protected in several countries.
