Heteropappus chejuensis

Heteropappus chejuensis, commonly known as the Jeju heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, herbaceous structure with grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow with white rays, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds in its pappus.

This species is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
