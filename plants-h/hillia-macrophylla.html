Hillia macrophylla

Hillia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf hillia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in the understory of humid forests. The plant is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Hillia macrophylla thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
