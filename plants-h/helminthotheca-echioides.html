Helminthotheca echioides

Helminthotheca echioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround its yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
