Homogyne alpina subsp. cantabrica

Homogyne alpina subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh, alpine environment.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
