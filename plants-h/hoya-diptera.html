Hoya diptera

Hoya diptera, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
