Hieracium urticaceum subsp. delphinale

Hieracium urticaceum subsp.

delphinale, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
