Hieracium hinterhuberi

Hieracium hinterhuberi, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium hinterhuberi is known for its resilience to harsh alpine conditions and is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
