Hemarthria compressa var. fasciculata

Hemarthria compressa var. fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features fine, grass-like leaves with a compressed, cylindrical stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Its dense growth habit also makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
