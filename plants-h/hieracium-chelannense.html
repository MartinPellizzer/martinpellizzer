Hieracium chelannense

Hieracium chelannense, commonly known as the Chelannense hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium chelannense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
