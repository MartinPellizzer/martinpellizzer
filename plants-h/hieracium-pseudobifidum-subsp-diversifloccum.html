Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. diversifloccum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

diversifloccum, commonly known as the diverse flocculent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, lobed appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its variable flower clusters, which can be either dense or loosely arranged, contributing to its common name.
