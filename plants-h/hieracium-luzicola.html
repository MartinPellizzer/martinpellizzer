Hieracium luzicola

Hieracium luzicola, commonly known as the Luzicola hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny locations. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium luzicola is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
