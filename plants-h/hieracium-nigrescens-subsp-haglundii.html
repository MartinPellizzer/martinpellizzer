Hieracium nigrescens subsp. haglundii

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

haglundii, commonly known as the Haglund's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
