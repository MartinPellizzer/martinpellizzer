Heliotropium pullulans

Heliotropium pullulans, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
