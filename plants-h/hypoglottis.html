Hypoglottis

Hypoglottis, commonly known as the "whispering plant," is a unique species characterized by its specialized leaf structures.

Its leaves are modified into narrow, tube-like shapes that facilitate the passage of air, enabling the plant to produce a soft, whisper-like sound when wind passes through. This adaptation is primarily for attracting specific pollinators, such as certain species of moths. The plant's botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that supports its upright growth.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
