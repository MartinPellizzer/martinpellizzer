Hieracium teligerum

Hieracium teligerum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Hieracium teligerum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
