Hieracium mougeotii subsp. doronicoides

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

doronicoides, commonly known as the Doronicoides hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
