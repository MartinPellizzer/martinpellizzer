Habenaria hancockii

Habenaria hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its floral structure includes a lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns, aiding in pollination by specific insect species.

The orchid's botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
