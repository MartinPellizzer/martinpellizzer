Hirculus subamplexicaulis

Hirculus subamplexicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leafed hirculus, is a unique species within the hirculus genus, characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in regions with low annual rainfall. Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods. The flowers are small and clustered, usually producing a pale yellow or greenish hue.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations to harsh climates.
