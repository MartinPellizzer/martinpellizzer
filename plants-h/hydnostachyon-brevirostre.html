Hydnostachyon brevirostre

Hydnostachyon brevirostre, commonly known as the short-snouted hydnostachy, is a rare and unusual member of the Hydnaceae family, characterized by its unique morphology.

The fruiting bodies are small, with a distinctive, narrow, club-shaped stipe and a short, rounded cap. Its spores are released through a network of fine, thread-like structures called hyphal threads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is of interest to mycologists and conservationists alike.
