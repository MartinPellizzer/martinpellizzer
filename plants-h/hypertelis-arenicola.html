Hypertelis arenicola

Hypertelis arenicola, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or desert habitats.

Hypertelis arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
