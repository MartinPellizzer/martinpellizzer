Habenaria parishii

Habenaria parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
