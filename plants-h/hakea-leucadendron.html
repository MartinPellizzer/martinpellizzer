Hakea leucadendron

Hakea leucadendron, commonly known as the white candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stiff, candle-like yellow or white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Hakea leucadendron is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
