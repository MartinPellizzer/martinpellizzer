Hedeoma hispida var. simplex

Hedeoma hispida var. simplex, commonly known as rough hedeoma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments.
