Hedysarum splendens

Hedysarum splendens, commonly known as the golden peavine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume that produces bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and a climbing habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hedysarum splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
