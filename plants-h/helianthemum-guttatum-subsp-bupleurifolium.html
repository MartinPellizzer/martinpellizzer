Helianthemum guttatum subsp. bupleurifolium

Helianthemum guttatum subsp.

bupleurifolium, commonly known as the bupleurifolium rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May.

The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
