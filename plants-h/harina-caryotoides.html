Harina caryotoides

Harina caryotoides, commonly known as the breadnut or tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The plant is valued for its seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary and medicinal applications. Harina caryotoides thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its economic and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and modern agricultural systems.
