Hieracium tricolorans

Hieracium tricolorans, commonly known as the three-colored hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium tricolorans is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
