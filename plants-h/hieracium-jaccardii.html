Hieracium jaccardii

Hieracium jaccardii, commonly known as the Jaccard's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium jaccardii is often found in disturbed areas and can spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
