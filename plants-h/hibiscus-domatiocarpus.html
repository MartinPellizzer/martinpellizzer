Hibiscus domatiocarpus

Hibiscus domatiocarpus, commonly known as the Sierra Madre hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Hibiscus domatiocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
