Hieracium crissaloense

Hieracium crissaloense, commonly known as the Crissaloense Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by petal-like structures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
