Hydrangea serratifolia

Hydrangea serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features serrated, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn red in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hydrangea serratifolia is also noted for its ability to bloom twice a year under optimal conditions.
