Hesperis sibirica subsp. sibirica

Hesperis sibirica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, silver-green leaves and produces clusters of white, four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valued plant in both horticulture and natural habitats.
