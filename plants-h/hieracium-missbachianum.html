Hieracium missbachianum

Hieracium missbachianum, commonly known as the Missbach's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and open woodlands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
