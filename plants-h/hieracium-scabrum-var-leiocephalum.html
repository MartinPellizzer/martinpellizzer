Hieracium scabrum var. leiocephalum

Hieracium scabrum var. leiocephalum, commonly known as the smoothhead hawkweed, is a variant of the hawkweed genus characterized by its distinctive morphology.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are smooth and covered with short, stiff hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in heads, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disc florets.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
