Hydastylus translucens

Hydastylus translucens, commonly known as the translucent water fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that float on the water's surface.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's translucent appearance is due to its thin, papery leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it a glass-like texture. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
