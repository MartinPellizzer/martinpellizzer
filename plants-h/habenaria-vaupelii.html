Habenaria vaupelii

Habenaria vaupelii, commonly known as the Vaupel's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the understory with high humidity. The plant features a pseudobulb and linear leaves, with flowering spikes that bear small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous, and it is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
