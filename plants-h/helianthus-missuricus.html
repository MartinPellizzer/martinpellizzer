Helianthus missuricus

Helianthus missuricus, commonly known as the Missouri sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features a robust stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and prairies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
