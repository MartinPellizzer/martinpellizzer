Heloniopsis japonica var. grandiflora

Heloniopsis japonica var. grandiflora, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
