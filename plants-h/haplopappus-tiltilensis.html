Haplopappus tiltilensis

Haplopappus tiltilensis, commonly known as the Tiltill's pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant produces a fluffy pappus that aids in seed dispersal.

Haplopappus tiltilensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in its native habitat.
