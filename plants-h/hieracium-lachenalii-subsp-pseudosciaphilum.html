Hieracium lachenalii subsp. pseudosciaphilum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

pseudosciaphilum, commonly known as the false shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous florets.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
