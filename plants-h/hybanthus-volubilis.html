Hybanthus volubilis

Hybanthus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vine that produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
