Hosta albomarginata f. mediopicta

Hosta albomarginata f. mediopicta, commonly known as the White-edged Hosta with Mid-green Variegation, is a cultivar of the Hosta plant known for its striking foliage.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a mix of white edges and mid-green variegation, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant is native to Asia and thrives in shady, moist environments, making it ideal for woodland gardens. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture and resist pests.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its unique leaf patterns.
