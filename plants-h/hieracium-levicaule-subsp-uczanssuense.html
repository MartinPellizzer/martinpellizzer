Hieracium levicaule subsp. uczanssuense

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

uczanssuense, commonly known as the Polish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
