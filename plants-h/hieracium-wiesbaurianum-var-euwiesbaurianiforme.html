Hieracium wiesbaurianum var. euwiesbaurianiforme

Hieracium wiesbaurianum var. euwiesbaurianiforme, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often forming dense colonies.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly altered leaf morphology compared to the typical form of H. wiesbaurianum.
