Hypoxis cuspidata

Hypoxis cuspidata, commonly known as the spear-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, pointed leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the stem tips. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in grassy areas and rocky soils. It is adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

Hypoxis cuspidata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
