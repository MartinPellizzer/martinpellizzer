Hymenopappus carolinensis

Hymenopappus carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is characterized by its hairy, linear leaves. Its fruit is a small, wind-dispersed achene, which is surrounded by silky bristles.

The botanical features of Hymenopappus carolinensis make it well adapted to its wetland environment and aid in its reproductive success.
