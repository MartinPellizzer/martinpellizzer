Hydnophytum forbesii

Hydnophytum forbesii, commonly known as the pitcher plant or the tropical pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-rich soil. Its specialized structures secrete digestive enzymes to break down prey, supplementing its nutrient intake.

Hydnophytum forbesii is an important species in the study of plant adaptation and nutrient acquisition in low-nutrient ecosystems.
