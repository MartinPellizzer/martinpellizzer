Hieracium caucasiense subsp. caucasiense

Hieracium caucasiense subsp.

caucasiense, commonly known as Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and forest edges.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
