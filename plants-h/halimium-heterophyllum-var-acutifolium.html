Halimium heterophyllum var. acutifolium

Halimium heterophyllum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawksbeard, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of linear, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, giving it its specific epithet *acutifolium*.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
