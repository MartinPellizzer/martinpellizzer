Horsfieldia sucosa subsp. sucosa

Horsfieldia sucosa subsp.

sucosa, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its creeping rhizomes.

The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
