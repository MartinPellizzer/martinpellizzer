Habenaria lauterbachii

Habenaria lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. It has a short rhizome and produces a single spike of small, fragrant blossoms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
