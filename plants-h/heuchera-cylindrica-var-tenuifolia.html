Heuchera cylindrica var. tenuifolia

Heuchera cylindrica var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated in color, ranging from silver to red and purple. The plant produces upright, cylindrical flower spikes in late spring to early summer, bearing small, white to pinkish flowers. Its foliage is evergreen in mild climates, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
