Hieracium borbasii

Hieracium borbasii, commonly known as the Borbas's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium borbasii is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and is often found in areas with moderate to low moisture.
