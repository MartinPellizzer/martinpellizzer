Hieracium larophyllum

Hieracium larophyllum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, though it has been introduced to other regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
