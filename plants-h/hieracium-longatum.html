Hieracium longatum

Hieracium longatum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows up to 60 cm tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom from June to September, and the plant is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium longatum is known for its ornamental value but can also be considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
