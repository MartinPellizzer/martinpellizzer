Hieracium irriguiceps

Hieracium irriguiceps, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
