Hoodia officinalis

Hoodia officinalis, commonly known as the desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant stores water in its thick leaves, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments. Hoodia officinalis is also noted for its unique ability to produce a compound called P57, which has been studied for its potential appetite-suppressing properties.

Its resilience and adaptability make it a significant species in both ecological and scientific contexts.
