Hakea pauciflora

Hakea pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikes of small, urn-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. It produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering.

Hakea pauciflora is well-suited for coastal and sandy soils, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
