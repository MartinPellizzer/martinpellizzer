Hosta japonica

Hosta japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hosta, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with white or yellow patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained, moist soil, making it a popular choice for shady garden areas.

Hosta japonica is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
