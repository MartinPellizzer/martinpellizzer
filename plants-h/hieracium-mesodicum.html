Hieracium mesodicum

Hieracium mesodicum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
