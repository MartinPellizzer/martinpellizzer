Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. rhamnoides

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

rhamnoides, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It thrives in coastal and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or saline soils.

Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
