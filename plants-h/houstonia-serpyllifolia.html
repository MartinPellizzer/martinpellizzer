Houstonia serpyllifolia

Houstonia serpyllifolia, commonly known as carpet bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Houstonia serpyllifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.
