Hippeastrum gayanum

Hippeastrum gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to Sri Lanka.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a waxy texture and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears a cluster of blooms, which can last for several weeks. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Hippeastrum gayanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
