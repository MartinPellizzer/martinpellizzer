Hieracium phereclades

Hieracium phereclades, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium phereclades is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered invasive in some regions.
