Hieracium ravidifolium

Hieracium ravidifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are rough and hairy, giving it its common name. Its flowering stems bear yellowish-orange composite flowers, which are composed of many small florets.

Hieracium ravidifolium is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its characteristic composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
