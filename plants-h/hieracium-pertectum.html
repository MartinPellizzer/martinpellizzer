Hieracium pertectum

Hieracium pertectum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and arranged in a rosette at the base, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium pertectum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
