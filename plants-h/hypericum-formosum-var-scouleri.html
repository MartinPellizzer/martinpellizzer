Hypericum formosum var. scouleri

Hypericum formosum var. scouleri, commonly known as the Vancouver hypericum, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
