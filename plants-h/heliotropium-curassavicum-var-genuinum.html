Heliotropium curassavicum var. genuinum

Heliotropium curassavicum var. genuinum, commonly known as the purple heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its showy blooms.
