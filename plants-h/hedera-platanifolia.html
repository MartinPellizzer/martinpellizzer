Hedera platanifolia

Hedera platanifolia, commonly known as the ivy palm or Persian ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are broadly ovate with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
