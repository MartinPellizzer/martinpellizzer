Hieracium Murorum Subsp. Panaeolum

Hieracium murorum subsp. panaeolum is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its delicate yellow flowers and hairy stems. This subspecies is often found in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance. Its unique characteristics make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. Understanding this plant helps highlight the diversity within the Hieracium genus.
