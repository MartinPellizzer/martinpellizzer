Hieracium psilopogon

Hieracium psilopogon, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium psilopogon is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
