Haplolobus monophyllus

Haplolobus monophyllus, commonly known as the single-leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a short stem and a single, thick, fleshy leaf that is often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on a short inflorescence. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections of succulents.
