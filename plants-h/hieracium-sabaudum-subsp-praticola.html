Hieracium sabaudum subsp. praticola

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

praticola, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head with ray florets.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in low-nutrient environments.
