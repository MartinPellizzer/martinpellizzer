Hedychium spicatum var. acuminatum

Hedychium spicatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a tall, branched stem with numerous small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The varietal form 'acuminatum' distinguishes it from other Hedychium species by its more pointed leaf tips and unique floral structure.
