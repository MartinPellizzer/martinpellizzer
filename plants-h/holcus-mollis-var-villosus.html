Holcus mollis var. villosus

Holcus mollis var. villosus, commonly known as hairy meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy texture on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are usually green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
