Hypocalyptus glaucus

Hypocalyptus glaucus, commonly known as the bluebush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
