Hippeastrum regium

Hippeastrum regium, commonly known as the imperial giant amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, or pink. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum regium is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
