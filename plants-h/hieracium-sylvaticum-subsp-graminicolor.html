Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. graminicolor

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

graminicolor, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinct greenish hue, which gives the plant its common name. It produces yellow flowers arranged in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family.

This subspecies is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
