Helianthemum halimifolium var. inmaculatum

Helianthemum halimifolium var. inmaculatum, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to rocky or coastal landscapes. The plant has aromatic, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean gardens.
