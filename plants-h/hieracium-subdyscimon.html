Hieracium subdyscimon

Hieracium subdyscimon, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides. It has hairy, rough stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
