Hydrolea cryptantha

Hydrolea cryptantha, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
