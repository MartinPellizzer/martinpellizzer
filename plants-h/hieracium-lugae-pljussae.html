Hieracium lugae-pljussae

Hieracium lugae-pljussae, commonly known as the Pliocene hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and often endangered species.
