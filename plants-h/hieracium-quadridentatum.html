Hieracium quadridentatum

Hieracium quadridentatum, commonly known as the four-toothed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a low-growing habit with upright stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium quadridentatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
