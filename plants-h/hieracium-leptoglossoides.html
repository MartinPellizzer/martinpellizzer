Hieracium leptoglossoides

Hieracium leptoglossoides, commonly known as the slender-tongued hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
