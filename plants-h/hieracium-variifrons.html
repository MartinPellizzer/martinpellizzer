Hieracium variifrons

Hieracium variifrons, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant has hairy stems and produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with variations in shape and size across different individuals.

Hieracium variifrons is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
