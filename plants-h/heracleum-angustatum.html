Heracleum angustatum

Heracleum angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf cowparn.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and umbellate inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along stream banks. Its stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
