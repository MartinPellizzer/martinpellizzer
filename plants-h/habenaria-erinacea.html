Habenaria erinacea

Habenaria erinacea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, pinkish-white floral spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, often growing in humus-rich soil.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
