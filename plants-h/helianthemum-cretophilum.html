Helianthemum cretophilum

Helianthemum cretophilum, commonly known as the cretaceous rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest, while its compact size makes it ideal for small spaces.

Helianthemum cretophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
