Hieracium nepiocratum

Hieracium nepiocratum, commonly known as the Neapolitan hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Native to southern Europe, particularly Italy, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant is perennial and often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
