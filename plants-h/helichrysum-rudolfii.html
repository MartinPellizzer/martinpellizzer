Helichrysum rudolfii

Helichrysum rudolfii, commonly known as the Golden Oregano, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum rudolfii is also valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine.
