Hibiscus longifolius var. tuberosus

Hibiscus longifolius var. tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant produces fleshy, tuberous roots that store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Hibiscus longifolius var. tuberosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
