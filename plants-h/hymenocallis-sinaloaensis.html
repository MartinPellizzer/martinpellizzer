Hymenocallis sinaloaensis

Hymenocallis sinaloaensis, commonly known as the Sinaloa spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of western Mexico.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces elegant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spidery appearance. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, adding a dramatic touch to gardens and natural landscapes.

Hymenocallis sinaloaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
