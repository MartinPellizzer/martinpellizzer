Hedera ovata

Hedera ovata, commonly known as the Japanese ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in gardens.

Hedera ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
