Hyphydra amplexicaulis

Hyphydra amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping hyphydra, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its ribbon-like leaves that wrap around its stems.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant's structure allows it to anchor itself in soft substrates while maximizing light absorption for photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Hyphydra amplexicaulis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
