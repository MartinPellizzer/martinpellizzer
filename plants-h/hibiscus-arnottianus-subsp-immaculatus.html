Hibiscus arnottianus subsp. immaculatus

Hibiscus arnottianus subsp.

immaculatus, commonly known as the immaculate hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests. Hibiscus arnottianus subsp.

immaculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
