Heteronoma diversifolium var. rubijinosa

Heteronoma diversifolium var. rubijinosa, commonly known as the red-leafed fern, is a variant of the heteronoma diversifolium species, characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The leaves are usually broad and lobed, with a vibrant red or maroon coloration that becomes more pronounced in cooler temperatures. This variety is noted for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity levels.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to the fern family, showcasing both ecological and aesthetic significance.
