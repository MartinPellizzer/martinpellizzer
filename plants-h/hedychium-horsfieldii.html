Hedychium horsfieldii

Hedychium horsfieldii, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a sweet scent. The plant features large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are edible and have a flavor similar to ginger.

Hedychium horsfieldii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
