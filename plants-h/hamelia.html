Hamelia

Hamelia, commonly known as flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lythraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Hamelia patens, which is native to the southeastern United States. The plants are shrubs characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red. They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and dry habitats.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
