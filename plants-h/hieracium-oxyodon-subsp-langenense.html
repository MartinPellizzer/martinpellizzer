Hieracium oxyodon subsp. langenense

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

langenense, commonly known as the Langer's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head.

The plant is native to southern Europe and is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphological features, including the structure of its leaves and flower heads.
