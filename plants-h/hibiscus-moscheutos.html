Hibiscus moscheutos

Hibiscus moscheutos, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features large, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can reach up to 15 centimeters in length.

Hibiscus moscheutos thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
