Haplopappus suffruticosus

Haplopappus suffruticosus, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as prairies and open woodlands. The plant features a sprawling, woody stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are surrounded by a single layer of bracts.

Haplopappus suffruticosus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
