Hypericum corsicum

Hypericum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum corsicum is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
