Hymenocallis glauca

Hymenocallis glauca, commonly known as the blue hurricane lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. Its bulbous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid conditions.

Hymenocallis glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
