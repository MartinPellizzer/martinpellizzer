Hieracium lactucella subsp. amaureilema

Hieracium lactucella subsp.

amaureilema, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which help it withstand harsh mountain conditions.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a common sight in alpine meadows and slopes.
