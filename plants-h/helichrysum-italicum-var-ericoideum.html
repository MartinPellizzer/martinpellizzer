Helichrysum italicum var. ericoideum

Helichrysum italicum var. ericoideum, commonly known as Everlasting or Immortelle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that are densely packed on wiry stems, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant has aromatic, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its name derives from the Greek words "helios" (sun) and "chrysos" (gold), reflecting its sunny disposition and colorful blooms.
