Hamamelis japonica var. discolor

Hamamelis japonica var. discolor, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan, characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that appear in late autumn. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a serrated margin, turning reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
