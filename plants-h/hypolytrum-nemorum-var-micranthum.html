Hypolytrum nemorum var. micranthum

Hypolytrum nemorum var. micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered bog sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland and swampy habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The species is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, pale green to brownish flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
