Hieracium umbellascens

Hieracium umbellascens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces yellowish to reddish flowers in clusters.

Hieracium umbellascens is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
