Helichrysum spodiophyllum

Helichrysum spodiophyllum, commonly known as the yellow everlasting or golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
