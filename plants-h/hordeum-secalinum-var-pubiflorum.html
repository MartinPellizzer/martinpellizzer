Hordeum secalinum var. pubiflorum

Hordeum secalinum var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the pubiflorous barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its distinctive pubescent inflorescence.

This plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bracts covered in soft hairs, which give it its common name.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
