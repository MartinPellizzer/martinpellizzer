Hordeum distichon var. spontaneum

Hordeum distichon var. spontaneum, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and two-rowed spikelets, which are typical features of the Hordeum genus. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers, and its seeds are enclosed in spikelets that are often wind-dispersed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is important in agricultural research due to its genetic diversity and potential for improving cultivated barley varieties.
