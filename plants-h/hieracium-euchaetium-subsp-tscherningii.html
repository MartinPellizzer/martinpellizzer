Hieracium euchaetium subsp. tscherningii

Hieracium euchaetium subsp.

tscherningii, commonly known as the Tscherning's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange-yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough or hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head with both ray and disc florets.

This subspecies is often found in regions with temperate climates and is relatively uncommon in cultivation.
