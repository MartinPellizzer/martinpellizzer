Homalium densiflorum

Homalium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflowered holly.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
