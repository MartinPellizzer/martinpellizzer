Hieracium murorum subsp. heterogynium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

heterogynium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

This subspecies is notable for its variability in flower size and leaf morphology, contributing to its adaptability in different habitats.
