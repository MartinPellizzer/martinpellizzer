Hierochloe odorata f. eamesii

Hierochloe odorata f. eamesii, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, fragrant leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use by Indigenous peoples for ceremonial and practical purposes.
