Hieracium pratense subsp. sudetorum

Hieracium pratense subsp.

sudetorum, commonly known as the Sudetic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flower heads and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It has hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated edges.

Its flowers are usually arranged in a single head, and it produces small, elongated achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.
