Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. subcanens

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

subcanens, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowering stems are usually unbranched. This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological and genetic distinctiveness.
