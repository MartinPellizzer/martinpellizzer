Herminium goodyeroides

Herminium goodyeroides, commonly known as the fairy slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and its distinctive, slipper-shaped flower with a violet or purple hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland or alpine areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its subtle, sweet fragrance.

Herminium goodyeroides is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
