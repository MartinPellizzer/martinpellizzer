Heliconia robertoi

Heliconia robertoi, commonly known as the Robert's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a spiral or torch. The plant has large, waxy, strap-like leaves that form a rosette pattern, providing a dense canopy. Its flowers are typically orange-red or yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed structure that attracts birds and insects.

Heliconia robertoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
