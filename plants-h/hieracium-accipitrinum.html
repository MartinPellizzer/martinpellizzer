Hieracium accipitrinum

Hieracium accipitrinum, commonly known as the hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy as well. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium accipitrinum is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
