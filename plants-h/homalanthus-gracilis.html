Homalanthus gracilis

Homalanthus gracilis, commonly known as slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain ecosystems.
