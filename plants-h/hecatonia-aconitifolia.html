Hecatonia aconitifolia

Hecatonia aconitifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that resemble mist. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Hecatonia aconitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
