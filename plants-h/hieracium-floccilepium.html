Hieracium floccilepium

Hieracium floccilepium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is recognized for its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium floccilepium produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
