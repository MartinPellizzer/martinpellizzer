Helleborus caucasicus var. guttatus

Helleborus caucasicus var. guttatus, commonly known as the spotted hellebore, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with distinctive white or pale spots. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during late winter to early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking winter interest.
