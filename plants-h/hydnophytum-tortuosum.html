Hydnophytum tortuosum

Hydnophytum tortuosum, commonly known as the spiral pitcher plant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pitcher-like structures that trap and digest insects, contributing to its nutrient acquisition in nutrient-poor environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing its ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions.

Hydnophytum tortuosum is also notable for its ability to retain water in its leaves, aiding in survival during dry periods.
