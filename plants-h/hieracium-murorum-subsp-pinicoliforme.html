Hieracium murorum subsp. pinicoliforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pinicoliforme, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its subspecific form, pinicoliforme, is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and specific habitat preferences.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, contributing to the diversity of flowering plants in alpine ecosystems.
