Hutchinsonia barbata

Hutchinsonia barbata, commonly known as the barbed hutchinsonia, is a flowering plant species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
