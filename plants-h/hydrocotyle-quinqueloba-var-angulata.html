Hydrocotyle quinqueloba var. angulata

Hydrocotyle quinqueloba var. angulata, commonly known as the angular water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant produces tiny white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands.

Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.
