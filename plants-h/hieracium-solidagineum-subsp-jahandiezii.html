Hieracium solidagineum subsp. jahandiezii

Hieracium solidagineum subsp.

jahandiezii, commonly known as the Persian hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

Native to Iran, this subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is considered a rare and endemic species.
