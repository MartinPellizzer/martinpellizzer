Hieracium exadsimilans

Hieracium exadsimilans, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a flat, disc-like structure. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branching.

Hieracium exadsimilans is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
