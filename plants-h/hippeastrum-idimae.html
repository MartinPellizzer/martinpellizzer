Hippeastrum idimae

Hippeastrum idimae, commonly known as the Idimae amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several blooms, each with six petals and a prominent, spurred tepal. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.

Hippeastrum idimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
