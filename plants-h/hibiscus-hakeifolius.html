Hibiscus hakeifolius

Hibiscus hakeifolius, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Hibiscus hakeifolius plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators like bees and butterflies.
