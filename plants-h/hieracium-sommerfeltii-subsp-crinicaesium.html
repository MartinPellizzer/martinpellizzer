Hieracium sommerfeltii subsp. crinicaesium

Hieracium sommerfeltii subsp.

crinicaesium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
