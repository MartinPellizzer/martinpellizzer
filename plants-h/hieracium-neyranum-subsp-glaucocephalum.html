Hieracium neyranum subsp. glaucocephalum

Hieracium neyranum subsp.

glaucocephalum, commonly known as the blue-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with a distinctive blue or purplish involucre.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
