Hyptis oppositiflora

Hyptis oppositiflora, commonly known as the Mexican mint or purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is known for its strong, mint-like fragrance and is often used in traditional medicine.

Hyptis oppositiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
