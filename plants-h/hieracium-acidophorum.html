Hieracium acidophorum

Hieracium acidophorum, commonly known as the acid-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils, often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Hieracium acidophorum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it a characteristic species of acidic habitats.
