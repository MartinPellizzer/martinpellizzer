Hieracium caesium subsp. bucovinae

Hieracium caesium subsp.

bucovinae, commonly known as the Bucovina hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in temperate climates. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions.
