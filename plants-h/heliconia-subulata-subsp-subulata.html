Heliconia subulata subsp. subulata

Heliconia subulata subsp.

subulata, commonly known as the needle heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its long, narrow, spikelike inflorescence that bears bright orange to red bracts.

The plant's leaves are elongated and pointed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds and other nectar-feeding birds.
