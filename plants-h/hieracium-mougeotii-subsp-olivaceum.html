Hieracium mougeotii subsp. olivaceum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

olivaceum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are yellow with a central yellow disk.

The subspecies olivaceum is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and coloration compared to other subspecies of Hieracium mougeotii.
