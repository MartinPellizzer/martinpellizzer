Helicia sumatrana

Helicia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran helenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to high humidity and warm temperatures. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, are followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds.

Helicia sumatrana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
