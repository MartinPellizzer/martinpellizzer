Hoodia officinalis subsp. delaetiana

Hoodia officinalis subsp.

delaetiana, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces small, pale fruits that contain seeds.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
