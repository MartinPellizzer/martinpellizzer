Hieracium acrophyllum

Hieracium acrophyllum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough textures.

Hieracium acrophyllum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some regions.
