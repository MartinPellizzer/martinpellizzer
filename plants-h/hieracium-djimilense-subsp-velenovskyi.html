Hieracium djimilense subsp. velenovskyi

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated margin, while the stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
