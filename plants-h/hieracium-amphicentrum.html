Hieracium amphicentrum

Hieracium amphicentrum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and hairy, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
