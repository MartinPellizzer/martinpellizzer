Hibiscus sinensis grandiflorus

Hibiscus sinensis grandiflorus, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring thick, woody stems and large, showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge, and the flowers are usually bright red, orange, or yellow, with a prominent central pistil. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
