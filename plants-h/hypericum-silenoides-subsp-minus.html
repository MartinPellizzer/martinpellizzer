Hypericum silenoides subsp. minus

Hypericum silenoides subsp.

minus, commonly known as lesser St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its lack of glandular dots on the stems and leaves.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant properties.
