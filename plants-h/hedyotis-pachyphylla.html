Hedyotis pachyphylla

Hedyotis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved blushing geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hedyotis pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
