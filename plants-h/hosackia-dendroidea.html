Hosackia dendroidea

Hosackia dendroidea, commonly known as the tree vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Hosackia dendroidea has adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed sites.
