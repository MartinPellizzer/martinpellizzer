Helianthemum pilosum subsp. lineare

Helianthemum pilosum subsp.

lineare, commonly known as the linear sandverbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
