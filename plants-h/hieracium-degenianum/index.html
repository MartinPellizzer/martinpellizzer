Hieracium Degenianum

Common hawkweed, scientifically known as Hieracium degenianum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is recognized for its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, making it a common sight in many natural habitats. Despite its beauty, it is considered an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native plants for resources. Its resilience and adaptability have made it a subject of both ecological study and concern.
