Hieracium pratorum-tivi

Hieracium pratorum-tivi, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single, inconspicuous flower head. The flower head consists of numerous small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by a crown of white, petal-like structures called ray florets. It is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of habitats, including lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
