Hopea gracilis

Hopea gracilis, commonly known as the slender hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a distinct midrib.

Hopea gracilis is valued for its timber, which is lightweight and durable, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
