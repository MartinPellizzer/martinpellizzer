Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudotorticeps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudotorticeps, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its tightly curled, often bent leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
