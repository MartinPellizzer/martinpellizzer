Henckelia speciosa

Henckelia speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic soil.

Henckelia speciosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
