Hibiscus scaber

Hibiscus scaber, commonly known as rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Hibiscus scaber thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
