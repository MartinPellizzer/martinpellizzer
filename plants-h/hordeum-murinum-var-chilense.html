Hordeum murinum var. chilense

Hordeum murinum var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean mouse barley, is a wild grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in coastal and sandy environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for saline and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
