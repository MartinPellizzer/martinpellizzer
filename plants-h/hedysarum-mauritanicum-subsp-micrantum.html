Hedysarum mauritanicum subsp. micrantum

Hedysarum mauritanicum subsp.

micrantum, commonly known as the Mauritania sweetvetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East. It is a perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. Hedysarum mauritanicum subsp.

micrantum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland conditions.
