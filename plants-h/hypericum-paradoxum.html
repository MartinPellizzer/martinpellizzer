Hypericum paradoxum

Hypericum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features yellow flowers with prominent yellow stamens and a distinctive red or orange corolla. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can interact with certain medications.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
