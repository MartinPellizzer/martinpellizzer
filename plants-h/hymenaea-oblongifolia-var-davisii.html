Hymenaea oblongifolia var. davisii

Hymenaea oblongifolia var. davisii, commonly known as the pink seringa, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual display to its canopy. The tree has a straight trunk and produces large, flat, green leaves that are compound in structure. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
