Hieracium expallens

Hieracium expallens, commonly known as the spreading hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium expallens is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
