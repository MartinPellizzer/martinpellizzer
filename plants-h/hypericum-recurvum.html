Hypericum recurvum

Hypericum recurvum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate or semi-upright stems that often grow along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum recurvum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
