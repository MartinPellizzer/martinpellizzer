Hoplophytum caerulescens

Hoplophytum caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Hoplophytum caerulescens thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
