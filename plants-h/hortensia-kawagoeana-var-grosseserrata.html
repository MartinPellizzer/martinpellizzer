Hortensia kawagoeana var. grosseserrata

Hortensia kawagoeana var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the Kawagoe hortensia, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its foliage is often serrated, giving it the variety name *grosseserrata*, which refers to its coarse teeth.
