Hieracium murorum subsp. integratifrons

Hieracium murorum subsp.

integratifrons, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
