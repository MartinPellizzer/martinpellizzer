Hibiscus aethiopicus var. aethiopicus

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. aethiopicus, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by five stamens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.
