Hemionitis pothifolia

Hemionitis pothifolia, commonly known as the sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has a distinctive rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive and adapted for nutrient absorption from sandy soils.

Hemionitis pothifolia is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
