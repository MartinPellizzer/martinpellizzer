Hydrangea petiolaris var. cordifolia

Hydrangea petiolaris var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a valuable ornamental plant.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, preferring acidic conditions for optimal flower color.
