Helichrysum dracaenifolium

Helichrysum dracaenifolium, commonly known as the golden marguerite or evergreen helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its dense clusters of yellow or golden-orange flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slight waxy coating. This plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
