Hieracium calcareum subsp. carnicum

Hieracium calcareum subsp.

carnicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has basal and stem leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

The flowers are arranged in a composite head, with both ray and disk florets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
