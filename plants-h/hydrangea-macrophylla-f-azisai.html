Hydrangea macrophylla f. azisai

Hydrangea macrophylla f. azisai, commonly known as the Azisai hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers, which can range from pink to blue depending on soil pH, are composed of sterile flowers (pistillate) and fertile flowers (staminate).

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
