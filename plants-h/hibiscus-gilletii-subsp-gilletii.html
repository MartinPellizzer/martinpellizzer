Hibiscus gilletii subsp. gilletii

Hibiscus gilletii subsp.

gilletii, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink or red.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall. Hibiscus gilletii subsp.

gilletii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh desert conditions.
