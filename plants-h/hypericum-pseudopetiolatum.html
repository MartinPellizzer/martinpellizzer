Hypericum pseudopetiolatum

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
