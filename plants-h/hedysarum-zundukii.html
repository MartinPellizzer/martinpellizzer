Hedysarum zundukii

Hedysarum zundukii, commonly known as the Zundukii sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Hedysarum zundukii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
