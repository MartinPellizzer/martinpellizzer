Helichrysum alpinum

Helichrysum alpinum, commonly known as alpine immortelle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suited to alpine and rocky environments.

Helichrysum alpinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
