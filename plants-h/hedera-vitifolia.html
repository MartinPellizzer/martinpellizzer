Hedera vitifolia

Hedera vitifolia, commonly known as the silver ivy or variegated ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with white and yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are not showy but are followed by dark blue berries. Hedera vitifolia thrives in shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
