Hydrangea paniculata var. praecox

Hydrangea paniculata var. praecox, commonly known as the early panicled hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy, panicle inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
