Hieracium mendiolanum

Hieracium mendiolanum, commonly known as the Mendola hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces yellow to orange flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are usually rough and hairy.

Hieracium mendiolanum is often found in regions with temperate climates and well-drained soils.
