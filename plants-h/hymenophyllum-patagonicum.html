Hymenophyllum patagonicum

Hymenophyllum patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian fern, is a small, delicate moss-like plant belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are clustered in dense, cushion-like formations on the underside of its leaves.

Hymenophyllum patagonicum plays a significant role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
