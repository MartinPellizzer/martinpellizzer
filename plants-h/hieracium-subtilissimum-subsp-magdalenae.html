Hieracium subtilissimum subsp. magdalenae

Hieracium subtilissimum subsp.

magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena's slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is distinguished by its slender stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
