Hieracium monnieri

Hieracium monnieri, commonly known as mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Hieracium monnieri is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes or seeds.
