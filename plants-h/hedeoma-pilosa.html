Hedeoma pilosa

Hedeoma pilosa, commonly known as rough hedeoma, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Hedeoma pilosa is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
