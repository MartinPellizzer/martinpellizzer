Hieracium lachenalii subsp. subpunctillatiforme

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

subpunctillatiforme, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are yellow and form a flat, composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in dry meadows and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
