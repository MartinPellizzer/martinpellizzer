Hedera iberica

Hedera iberica, commonly known as the Iberian ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas. Hedera iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover or as a climber.

It has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
