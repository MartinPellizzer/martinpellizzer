Hieracium pulveratum

Hieracium pulveratum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions and is considered invasive in some areas. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its life cycle includes a rosette stage followed by flowering stems, and it reproduces primarily through seeds.
