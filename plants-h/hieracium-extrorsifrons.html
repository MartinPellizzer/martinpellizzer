Hieracium extrorsifrons

Hieracium extrorsifrons, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
