Hyptis crenata var. microphylla

Hyptis crenata var. microphylla, commonly known as green hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial.
