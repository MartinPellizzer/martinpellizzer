Hieracium depilatiforme

Hieracium depilatiforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows and grassy areas. It has a rosette growth pattern with basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is typically perennial and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
