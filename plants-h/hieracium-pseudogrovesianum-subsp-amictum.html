Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp. amictum

Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp.

amictum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
