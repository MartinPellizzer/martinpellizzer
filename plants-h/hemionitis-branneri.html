Hemionitis branneri

Hemionitis branneri, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique fern species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and its ability to thrive in extreme desert conditions. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss, making it well-adapted to its harsh environment. It produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of its leaves.

Hemionitis branneri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
