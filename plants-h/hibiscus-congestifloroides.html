Hibiscus congestifloroides

Hibiscus congestifloroides, commonly known as the Malayan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Hibiscus congestifloroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
