Hieracium anfractifolium

Hieracium anfractifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
