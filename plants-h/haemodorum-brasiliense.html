Haemodorum brasiliense

Haemodorum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes plants known for their striking, often red or orange-colored flowers. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Haemodorum brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
