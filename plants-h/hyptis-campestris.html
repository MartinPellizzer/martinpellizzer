Hyptis campestris

Hyptis campestris, commonly known as broadleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woods and disturbed soils.

Hyptis campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
