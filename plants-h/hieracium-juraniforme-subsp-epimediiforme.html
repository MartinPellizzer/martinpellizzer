Hieracium juraniforme subsp. epimediiforme

Hieracium juraniforme subsp.

epimediiforme, commonly known as the Jura-like hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, often glandular surface.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
