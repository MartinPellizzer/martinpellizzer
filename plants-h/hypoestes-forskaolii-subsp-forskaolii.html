Hypoestes forskaolii subsp. forskaolii

Hypoestes forskaolii subsp.

forskaolii, commonly known as the "Magic Flower," is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the year under optimal conditions.
