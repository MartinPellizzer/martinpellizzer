Hemiperis globulosa

Hemiperis globulosa, commonly known as the globular hemlock, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its distinctive globe-like clusters of white flowers appear in late spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
