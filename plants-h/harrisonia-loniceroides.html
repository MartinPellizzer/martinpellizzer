Harrisonia loniceroides

Harrisonia loniceroides, commonly known as the creeping spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its latex sap is milky and can cause skin irritation, making it a plant to handle with care.
