Huernia hislopii subsp. cashelensis

Huernia hislopii subsp.

cashelensis, commonly known as Cashel's horned vulture, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny, tubular leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions.

This subspecies is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
