Hieracium urticaceum subsp. bertschianum

Hieracium urticaceum subsp.

bertschianum, commonly known as the Bertschian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and erect, hairy stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
