Hibiscus primulinus

Hibiscus primulinus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent red or orange center. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus primulinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
