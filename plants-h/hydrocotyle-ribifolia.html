Hydrocotyle ribifolia

Hydrocotyle ribifolia, commonly known as the coastal arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy areas, often near the shoreline. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. Its small white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Hydrocotyle ribifolia is adapted to saline environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.
