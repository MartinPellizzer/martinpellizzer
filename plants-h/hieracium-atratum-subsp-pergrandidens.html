Hieracium atratum subsp. pergrandidens

Hieracium atratum subsp.

pergrandidens, commonly known as the large-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
