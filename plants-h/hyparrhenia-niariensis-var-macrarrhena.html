Hyparrhenia niariensis var. macrarrhena

Hyparrhenia niariensis var. macrarrhena, commonly known as the long-awned muhly, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with long, slender awns at the base of its inflorescence, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
