Hieracium praecox subsp. cinerascens

Hieracium praecox subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and stems that bear yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
