Hermannia pedunculata

Hermannia pedunculata, commonly known as the purple-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Hermannia pedunculata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
