Hieracium jeanbernatii

Hieracium jeanbernatii, commonly known as the Jeanbernat's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Hieracium jeanbernatii is often found in open habitats with limited competition, making it a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
