Hieracium gilvocaniceps

Hieracium gilvocaniceps, commonly known as the yellow-hair hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads and hairy stems, which give it its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
