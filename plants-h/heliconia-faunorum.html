Heliconia faunorum

Heliconia faunorum, commonly known as the Faunum heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves and clustered, spadix-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, ranging from red to orange, and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
