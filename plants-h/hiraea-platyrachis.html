Hiraea platyrachis

Hiraea platyrachis, commonly known as the broad-leaved hiraea, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters, making it visually distinctive.

Hiraea platyrachis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
