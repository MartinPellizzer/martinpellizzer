Helichrysum plicatum subsp. isauricum

Helichrysum plicatum subsp.

isauricum, commonly known as the Isaurian everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers.

The plant produces small, papery bracts that persist after flowering, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in horticulture.
