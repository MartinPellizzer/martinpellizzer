Helianthemum chamaecistus var. hirsutum

Helianthemum chamaecistus var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
