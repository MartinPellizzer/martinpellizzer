Hilaria hintonii

Hilaria hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as sandhill prairies and dry woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to poor soils and drought conditions, making it a hardy species.

Hilaria hintonii plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitat.
