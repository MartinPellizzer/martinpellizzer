Hibiscus sinensis

Hibiscus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that resembles a capsule, containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus sinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
