Heteropogon leptocladus var. pilosus

Heteropogon leptocladus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, two-lobed flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
