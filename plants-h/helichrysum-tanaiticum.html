Helichrysum tanaiticum

Helichrysum tanaiticum, commonly known as the Ethiopian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange-colored flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is perennial and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers are known for their long-lasting nature, making it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
