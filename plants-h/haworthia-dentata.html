Haworthia dentata

Haworthia dentata, commonly known as the saw-toothed haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves. The leaves are typically green with white or pale green markings and have small teeth along the edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
