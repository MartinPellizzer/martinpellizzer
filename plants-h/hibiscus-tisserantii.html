Hibiscus tisserantii

Hibiscus tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's hibiscus, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, pleasant fragrance. Hibiscus tisserantii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
