Hedyotis leschenaultiana

Hedyotis leschenaultiana, commonly known as the golden star or small redroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides.

Hedyotis leschenaultiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
