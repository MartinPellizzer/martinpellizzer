Hymenocallis paludosa

Hymenocallis paludosa, commonly known as the marsh spider lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features striking white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved spatulate shape, often adorned with a red or purple stripe at the base. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Hymenocallis paludosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
