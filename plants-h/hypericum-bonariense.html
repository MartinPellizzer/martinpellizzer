Hypericum bonariense

Hypericum bonariense, commonly known as the everlasting bush or yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hypericum bonariense is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
