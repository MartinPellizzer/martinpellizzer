Hibiscus saxatilis

Hibiscus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or red center. Hibiscus saxatilis thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is a perennial shrub that is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray.
