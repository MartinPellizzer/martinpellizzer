Hieracium caucasiense subsp. hypopogon

Hieracium caucasiense subsp.

hypopogon, commonly known as the Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptations to arid environments. It produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, typical of the genus Hieracium.

Its subspecies hypopogon is distinguished by its smaller stature and specific habitat preferences compared to other members of the species.
