Hieracium crocatum subsp. coniciforme

Hieracium crocatum subsp.

coniciforme, commonly known as the conical hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with rough or hairy surfaces. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, disk-like flowers surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun conditions.
