Hieracium frigidum

Hieracium frigidum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium frigidum is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
