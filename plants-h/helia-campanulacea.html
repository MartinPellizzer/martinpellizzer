Helia campanulacea

Helia campanulacea, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, woodland environments. It produces small, oval berries that contain the seeds.

Helia campanulacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
