Heliconia angusta

Heliconia angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that enclose its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its inflorescence, known as a spadix, is surrounded by a large, showy bract that varies in color from red to orange.

Heliconia angusta is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
