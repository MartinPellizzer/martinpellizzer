Helianthemum origanifolium f. majus

Helianthemum origanifolium f. majus, commonly known as the large-leaved rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that resemble those of oregano, giving it its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
