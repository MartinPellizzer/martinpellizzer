Hypolepis sellowiana

Hypolepis sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a species native to South America.

It is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting pollinators.

Hypolepis sellowiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
