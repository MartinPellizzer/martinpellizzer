Hieracium vulgatum subsp. smaragdinum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

smaragdinum, commonly known as the Emerald Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear clusters of small, showy flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its vivid greenish-yellow flowers and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
