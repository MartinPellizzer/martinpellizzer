Hypericum macedonicum

Hypericum macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers. It produces orange-red berries that contain seeds, and it is often found in mountainous areas.

Hypericum macedonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
