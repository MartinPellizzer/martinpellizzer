Helleborus × ashwoodensis

Helleborus × ashwoodensis, commonly known as the Ashwood hellebore, is a hybrid evergreen perennial plant native to the UK.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking, nodding flowers that range from deep purple to white. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in the hellebore genus. It prefers partial to full shade and well-drained, fertile soil, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
