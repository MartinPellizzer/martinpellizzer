Helianthemum glaucum var. erectum

Helianthemum glaucum var. erectum, commonly known as the upright sandthrift, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
