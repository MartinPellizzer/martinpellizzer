Hydrogeton fenestralis

Hydrogeton fenestralis, commonly known as the fenestrated hydrogeton, is a rare aquatic plant characterized by its unique fenestrated leaves.

These leaves have small openings or holes, which help in gas exchange and reduce water resistance. It typically grows in shallow, still waters with abundant sunlight. The plant's root system is adapted for anchoring in soft sediment and nutrient uptake.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often blooming in late summer.
