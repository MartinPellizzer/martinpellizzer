Hordeum apertum

Hordeum apertum, commonly known as wild barley, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces two-rowed spikelets with lemmas that are typically hairy and have a distinct apiculate tip. Hordeum apertum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Its seeds are small and oval, with a tough, protective pericarp that aids in seed dispersal.
