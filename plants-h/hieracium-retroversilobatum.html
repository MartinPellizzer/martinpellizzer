Hieracium retroversilobatum

Hieracium retroversilobatum, commonly known as the reverse-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching a height of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with distinct lobes, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
