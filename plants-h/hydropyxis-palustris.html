Hydropyxis palustris

Hydropyxis palustris, commonly known as the marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to thrive in waterlogged conditions.

Hydropyxis palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
