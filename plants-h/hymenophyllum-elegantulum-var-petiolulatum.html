Hymenophyllum elegantulum var. petiolulatum

Hymenophyllum elegantulum var. petiolulatum, commonly known as the slender-stalked lady's shoe, is a fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate, and often translucent fronds that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its unique morphology includes petiolules, which are short stalks connecting the fronds to the main stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in humid, low-light settings.
