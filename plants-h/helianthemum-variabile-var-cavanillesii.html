Helianthemum variabile var. cavanillesii

Helianthemum variabile var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the variable carnation flower, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring compact, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This species is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and arid environments.
