Helichrysum picardii var. virescens

Helichrysum picardii var. virescens, commonly known as the green everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
