Haageocereus crassiareolatus

Haageocereus crassiareolatus, commonly known as the thick-netted cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for storing water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

The plant's unique net-like structures on its surface are a distinguishing feature in the Cactaceae family.
