Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. yunnanensis

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan sandthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and essential fatty acids.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often on rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
