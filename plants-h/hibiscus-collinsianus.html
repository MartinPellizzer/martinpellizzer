Hibiscus collinsianus

Hibiscus collinsianus, commonly known as the Collins' hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a prominent yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Hibiscus collinsianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
