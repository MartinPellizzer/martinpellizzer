Helianthemum guttatum subsp. villosissimum

Helianthemum guttatum subsp.

villosissimum, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
