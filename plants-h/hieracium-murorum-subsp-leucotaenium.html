Hieracium murorum subsp. leucotaenium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

leucotaenium, commonly known as the white-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellowish to orange flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale, often whitish stem and leaves, which are typically hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
