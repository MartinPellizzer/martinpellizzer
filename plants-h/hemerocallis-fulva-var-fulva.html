Hemerocallis fulva var. fulva

Hemerocallis fulva var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features sword-shaped, bright green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It blooms profusely from late spring to early summer, with each flower lasting only one day.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and resistance to pests and diseases.
