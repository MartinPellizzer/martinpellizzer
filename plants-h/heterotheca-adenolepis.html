Heterotheca adenolepis

Heterotheca adenolepis, commonly known as the blue coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and yellow central disk, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Heterotheca adenolepis is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
