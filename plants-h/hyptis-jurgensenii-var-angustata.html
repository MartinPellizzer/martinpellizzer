Hyptis jurgensenii var. angustata

Hyptis jurgensenii var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in native habitats.
