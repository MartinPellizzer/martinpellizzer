Hypericum maculatum var. perforatum

Hypericum maculatum var. perforatum, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish or reddish spot on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
