Habrothamnus corymbosus

Habrothamnus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
