Hesperis elliptica

Hesperis elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, fragrant blooms and long blooming period.
