Hordeum vulgare var. subpyramidatum

Hordeum vulgare var. subpyramidatum, commonly known as the spikeless barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique morphology.

It features a compact, cylindrical inflorescence that lacks the typical elongated spikelets found in standard barley. The plant is typically shorter and more robust, with reduced awns and a dense arrangement of florets. Its botanical traits make it distinct from other barley varieties, often used in specialized agricultural or horticultural contexts.

This variant is of interest for its potential in breeding programs and ecological studies.
