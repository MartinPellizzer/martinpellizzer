Haageocereus acranthus var. olowinskianus

Haageocereus acranthus var. olowinskianus, commonly known as the Olowinskianus Cereus, is a cacti species native to southern Africa.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, unbranched stem and spines that are often pale or whitish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
