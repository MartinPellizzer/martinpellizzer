Huperzia kamaensis

Huperzia kamaensis, commonly known as the Kama fern, is a species of clubmoss native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophyte is perennial, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Huperzia kamaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
