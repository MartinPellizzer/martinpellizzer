Hieracium ramondii subsp. origanocerinthe

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

origanocerinthe, commonly known as the Corsican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry, nutrient-poor soils.
