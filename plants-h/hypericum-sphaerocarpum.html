Hypericum sphaerocarpum

Hypericum sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globe-shaped, woody stems that are covered in small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum sphaerocarpum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
