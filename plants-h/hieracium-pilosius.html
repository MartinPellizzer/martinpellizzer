Hieracium pilosius

Hieracium pilosius, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
