Heracleum dichotomum

Heracleum dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences that contain small white to greenish flowers. Its leaves are large, deeply lobed, and can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.
