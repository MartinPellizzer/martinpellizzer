Hieracium badense

Hieracium badense, commonly known as the Baden hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Europe and is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces achenes (small, dry fruits) that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium badense is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its invasive potential.
