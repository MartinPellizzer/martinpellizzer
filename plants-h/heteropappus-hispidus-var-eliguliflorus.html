Heteropappus hispidus var. eliguliflorus

Heteropappus hispidus var. eliguliflorus, commonly known as the silky dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its wind-dispersed seeds. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The variety eliguliflorus is distinguished by its more elongated flower heads compared to other varieties of Heteropappus hispidus.
