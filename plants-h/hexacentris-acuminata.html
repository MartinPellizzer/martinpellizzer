Hexacentris acuminata

Hexacentris acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including Hawaii, where it thrives in coastal and wetland habitats. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
