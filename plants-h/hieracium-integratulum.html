Hieracium integratulum

Hieracium integratulum, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Hieracium integratulum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
