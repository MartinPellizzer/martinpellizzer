Hedyotis leschenaultii var. wynaadensis

Hedyotis leschenaultii var. wynaadensis, commonly known as the Wynaad bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. Its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules that release minute seeds when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes.
