Hymenophyllum chiloense

Hymenophyllum chiloense, commonly known as the Chilean filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are embedded in the undersides of its leaves, a feature typical of the Hymenophyllaceae family.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat requirements make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
