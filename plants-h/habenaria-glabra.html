Habenaria glabra

Habenaria glabra, commonly known as the smooth helleborine, is an orchid species native to various regions in Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces pale green to yellowish flowers with three petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands.

Habenaria glabra is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.
