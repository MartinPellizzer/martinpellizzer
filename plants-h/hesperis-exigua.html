Hesperis exigua

Hesperis exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. Hesperis exigua is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
