Hierochloe odorata subsp. hirta

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

hirta, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silky leaves that emit a sweet scent when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. Hierochloe odorata subsp.

hirta is often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies due to its fragrant properties and ornamental value.
