Hieracium brevicaule

Hieracium brevicaule, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium brevicaule is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
