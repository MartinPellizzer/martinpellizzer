Helichrysum porrectum

Helichrysum porrectum, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small crowns, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum porrectum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
