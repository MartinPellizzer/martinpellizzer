Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. ceahlavicum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

ceahlavicum, commonly known as the Ceahlavicum Hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped. It produces small, elongated fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient component of mountainous ecosystems.
