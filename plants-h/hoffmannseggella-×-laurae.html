Hoffmannseggella × laurae

Hoffmannseggella × laurae, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Crataegus monogyna* and *Crataegus laevigata*.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is native to southern England and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
