Hieracium fulvipes

Hieracium fulvipes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and the leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

It is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting pollinators.
