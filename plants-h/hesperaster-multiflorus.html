Hesperaster multiflorus

Hesperaster multiflorus, commonly known as the multibract sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a circular pattern, forming a distinctive, sun-like appearance.

Hesperaster multiflorus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
