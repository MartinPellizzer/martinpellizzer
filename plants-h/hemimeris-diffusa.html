Hemimeris diffusa

Hemimeris diffusa, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Hemimeris diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
