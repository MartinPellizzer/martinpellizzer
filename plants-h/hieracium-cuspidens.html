Hieracium cuspidens

Hieracium cuspidens, commonly known as the spearhead hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a central stem that bears yellow flower heads composed of many small florets. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
