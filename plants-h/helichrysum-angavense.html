Helichrysum angavense

Helichrysum angavense, commonly known as the Angavo everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers. The plant produces numerous ray florets that give it a fluffy, dandelion-like appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Helichrysum angavense is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
