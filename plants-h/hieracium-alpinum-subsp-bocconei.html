Hieracium alpinum subsp. bocconei

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

bocconei, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and specific geographic distribution compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
