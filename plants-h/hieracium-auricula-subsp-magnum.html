Hieracium auricula subsp. magnum

Hieracium auricula subsp.

magnum, commonly known as the giant mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features rosettes of basal, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
