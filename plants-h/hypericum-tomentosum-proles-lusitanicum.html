Hypericum tomentosum proles lusitanicum

Hypericum tomentosum proles lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian hairy St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
