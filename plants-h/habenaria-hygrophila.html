Habenaria hygrophila

Habenaria hygrophila, commonly known as the swamp orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
