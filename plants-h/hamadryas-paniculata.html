Hamadryas paniculata

Hamadryas paniculata, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-pink inflorescences that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and seasonal blooms.

The tree plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
