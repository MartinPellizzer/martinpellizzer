Homeria mossii

Homeria mossii, commonly known as the Texas prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments, allowing it to survive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Homeria mossii plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
