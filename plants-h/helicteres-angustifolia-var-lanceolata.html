Helicteres angustifolia var. lanceolata

Helicteres angustifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus family native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
