Hordeum vulgare var. leioheterolepis

Hordeum vulgare var. leioheterolepis, commonly known as the slender barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, blade-like leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in spikelets, which are arranged in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and potential agricultural applications.
