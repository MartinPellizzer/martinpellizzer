Helichrysum divaricatum

Helichrysum divaricatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces papery, dry flower heads that persist through the winter, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
