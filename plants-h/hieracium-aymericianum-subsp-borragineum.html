Hieracium aymericianum subsp. borragineum

Hieracium aymericianum subsp.

borragineum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are bright yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
