Helianthemum fumana var. procumbens

Helianthemum fumana var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically from April to June. The plant has succulent, hairy leaves that are adapted to dry, sandy soils. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
