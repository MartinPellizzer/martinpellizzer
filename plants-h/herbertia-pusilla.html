Herbertia pusilla

Herbertia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion plant, is a small, flowering perennial native to South America.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces delicate, spiky inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking ornamental plants with a compact form.
