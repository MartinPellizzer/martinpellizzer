Hyacinthus curvulus

Hyacinthus curvulus, commonly known as the nodding hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which hang downward on slender stems. Its leaves are strap-like and form a dense rosette at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hyacinthus curvulus is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
