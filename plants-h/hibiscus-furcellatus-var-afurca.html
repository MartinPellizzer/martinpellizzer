Hibiscus furcellatus var. afurca

Hibiscus furcellatus var. afurca, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking blooms and adaptability.

The species is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient choice for gardeners.
