Hypericum tomentosum var. suberosum

Hypericum tomentosum var. suberosum, commonly known as the woolly St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are typically clustered in groups of three. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in a thick layer of white to grayish woolly hairs.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique texture and color.
