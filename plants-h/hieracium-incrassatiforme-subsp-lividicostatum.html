Hieracium incrassatiforme subsp. lividicostatum

Hieracium incrassatiforme subsp.

lividicostatum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale green to purplish stems and its unique floral morphology.
