Hieracium sommerfeltii subsp. senex

Hieracium sommerfeltii subsp.

senex, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its long, drooping, yellowish-green flower heads that resemble a beard.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are visited by various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
