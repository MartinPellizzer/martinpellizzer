Heracleum moellendorffii var. subbipinnatum

Heracleum moellendorffii var. subbipinnatum, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a large herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a tall, hollow stem that is often marked with purple spots and coarse hairs. The plant has large, compound leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, with a bipinnate structure. Its flowers are white to pale green, arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences called umbels.

This species is known for its toxic sap, which can cause severe skin irritation and photodermatitis when exposed to sunlight.
