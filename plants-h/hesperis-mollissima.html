Hesperis mollissima

Hesperis mollissima, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its clustered, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are lobed and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hesperis mollissima typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
