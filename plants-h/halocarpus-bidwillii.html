Halocarpus bidwillii

Halocarpus bidwillii, commonly known as the kōwhai kākāriki, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Halocarpus bidwillii is also notable for its ability to withstand salt spray and wind, making it well adapted to its natural habitat.
