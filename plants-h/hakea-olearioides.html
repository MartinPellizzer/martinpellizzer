Hakea olearioides

Hakea olearioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, woody fruiting structures called follicles. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring a deep root system to access water. It is often found in heathlands and rocky areas, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its attractive foliage and unique flowers.
