Hymenophyllum cheesemanii

Hymenophyllum cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's shield fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, overlapping, scale-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as rock crevices and forest floors, where it thrives in high humidity. Its leaves are covered with a fine layer of trichomes, which help reduce water loss and protect against desiccation.

Hymenophyllum cheesemanii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
