Hermione discreta

Hermione discreta, commonly known as the shy violet, is a delicate flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, pale purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its blooming season occurs in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its modest appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
