Hibiscus shirensis var. perrierellus

Hibiscus shirensis var. perrierellus, commonly known as the Perrierell's hibiscus, is a rare and distinctive variant of the hibiscus genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with a prominent yellow throat. The plant features palmate leaves that are often lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
