Hyptis blanchetii

Hyptis blanchetii, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
