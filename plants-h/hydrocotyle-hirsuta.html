Hydrocotyle hirsuta

Hydrocotyle hirsuta, commonly known as hairy groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
