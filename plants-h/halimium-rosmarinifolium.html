Halimium rosmarinifolium

Halimium rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Halimium rosmarinifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
