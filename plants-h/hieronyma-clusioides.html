Hieronyma clusioides

Hieronyma clusioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cereja do Brasil*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive.
