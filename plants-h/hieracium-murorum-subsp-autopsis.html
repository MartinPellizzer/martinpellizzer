Hieracium murorum subsp. autopsis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

autopsis, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is often found in disturbed areas and can form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.
