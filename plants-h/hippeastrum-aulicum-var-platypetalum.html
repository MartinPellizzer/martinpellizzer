Hippeastrum aulicum var. platypetalum

Hippeastrum aulicum var. platypetalum, commonly known as the flat-petaled amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring a vibrant range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid conditions. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
