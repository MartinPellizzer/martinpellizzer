Hedyotis nigricans var. floridana

Hedyotis nigricans var. floridana, commonly known as Florida bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, often prostrate stems that produce small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it typically blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
