Hieracium dasycranum

Hieracium dasycranum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, making it easily recognizable in the wild.
