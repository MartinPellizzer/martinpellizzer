Hicorius magnifloridiana

Hicorius magnifloridiana, commonly known as the golden sunflower of the tropics, is a striking plant characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central disk.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the humid lowland regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
