Hieracium salassorum

Hieracium salassorum, commonly known as the Salassian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its basal leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowering stems bear clusters of yellow flowers.

Hieracium salassorum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
