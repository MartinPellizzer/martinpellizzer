Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. acariaeum

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

acariaeum, commonly known as the acarine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its specific epithet.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
