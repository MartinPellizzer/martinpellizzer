Hieracium ciliatidens

Hieracium ciliatidens, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium ciliatidens is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
