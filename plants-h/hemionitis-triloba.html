Hemionitis triloba

Hemionitis triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features small, delicate leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found near the edges of ponds and wetlands. It has a simple structure with no true roots, relying instead on rhizomes for anchorage and nutrient absorption.

Hemionitis triloba plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
