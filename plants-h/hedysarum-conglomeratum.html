Hedysarum conglomeratum

Hedysarum conglomeratum, commonly known as the clustered vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Hedysarum conglomeratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
