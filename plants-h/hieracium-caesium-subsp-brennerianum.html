Hieracium caesium subsp. brennerianum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

brennerianum, commonly known as the Brenner's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

It is considered a minor weed but has ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
