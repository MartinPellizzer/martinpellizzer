Hieracium scotodes

Hieracium scotodes, commonly known as the Scotch hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Hieracium scotodes is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
