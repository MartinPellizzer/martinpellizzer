Hymenocarpos radiatus

Hymenocarpos radiatus, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, star-shaped fruit that turns yellow when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor and a crunchy texture, making it a popular fruit in many cuisines.
