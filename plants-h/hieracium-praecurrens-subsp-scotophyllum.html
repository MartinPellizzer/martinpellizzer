Hieracium praecurrens subsp. scotophyllum

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

scotophyllum, commonly known as the shade-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its leaves are hairy and have a serrated edge, while the stems are upright and often branched.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
