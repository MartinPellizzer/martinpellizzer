Handroanthus uleanus

Handroanthus uleanus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in humid, lowland areas and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
