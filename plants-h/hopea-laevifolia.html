Hopea laevifolia

Hopea laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Hopea laevifolia plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
