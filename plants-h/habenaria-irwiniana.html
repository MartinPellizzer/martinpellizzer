Habenaria irwiniana

Habenaria irwiniana, commonly known as the Irwin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, while its flowers are arranged in a spike with a distinctive, spurred shape.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
