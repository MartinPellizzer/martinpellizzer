Haemocharis haematoxylum

Haemocharis haematoxylum, commonly known as the red-tipped plant.

This tropical plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The plant's foliage consists of broad, glossy leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
