Helosciadium laciniatum var. humile

Helosciadium laciniatum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf cutleaf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, finely cut appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its delicate flowers and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
