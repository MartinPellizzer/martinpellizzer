Helosciadium × longipedunculatum

Helosciadium × longipedunculatum, commonly known as the hybrid marsh woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely divided, thread-like lobes, and it produces small, clustered fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing food for various invertebrates.
