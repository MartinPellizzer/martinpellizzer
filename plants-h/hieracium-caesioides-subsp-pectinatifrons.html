Hieracium caesioides subsp. pectinatifrons

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

pectinatifrons, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and somewhat branched. This subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and a distinctive, pectinate (toothed) margin on the leaves.

It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in moist or semi-shaded environments.
