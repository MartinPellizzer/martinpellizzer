Hyoscyamus pseudophysaloides

Hyoscyamus pseudophysaloides, commonly known as the false balloonflower, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its striking, inflated, balloon-like seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is known for its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

It contains alkaloids, making it potentially toxic to livestock and humans if ingested.
