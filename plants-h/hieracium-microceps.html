Hieracium microceps

Hieracium microceps, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium microceps is often found in moist environments and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
