Hapalochilus quadricaudatus

Hapalochilus quadricaudatus, commonly known as the four-tailed spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring four elongated, thread-like sepals that resemble tails, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect pollinators for reproduction.
