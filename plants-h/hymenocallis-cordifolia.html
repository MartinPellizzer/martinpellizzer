Hymenocallis cordifolia

Hymenocallis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf ginger lily, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hymenocallis cordifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
