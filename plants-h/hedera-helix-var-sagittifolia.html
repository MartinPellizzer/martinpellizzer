Hedera helix var. sagittifolia

Hedera helix var. sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

This evergreen climbing plant features glossy, dark green foliage with a slightly textured surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It is often used in horticulture for ground cover or as a decorative climbing plant.
