Hieracium subobscuriceps

Hieracium subobscuriceps, commonly known as the dark-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
