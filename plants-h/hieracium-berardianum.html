Hieracium berardianum

Hieracium berardianum, commonly known as the Berard's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and numerous yellow flowering heads that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the daisy family.

Hieracium berardianum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in various regions.
