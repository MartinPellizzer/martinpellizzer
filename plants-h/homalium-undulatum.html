Homalium undulatum

Homalium undulatum, commonly known as the undulating hollies, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often waxy and undulating in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by greenish or red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
