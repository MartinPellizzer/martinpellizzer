Hieracium concinniflorum

Hieracium concinniflorum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with a hairy stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single capitulum with tubular florets surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Hieracium concinniflorum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
