Hieracium murorum subsp. skarsinense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

skarsinense, commonly known as the Skarsin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth form compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus. It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces numerous small, yellow florets arranged in a composite head.
