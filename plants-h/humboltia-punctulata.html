Humboltia punctulata

Humboltia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a distinct, waxy coating.

Humboltia punctulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
