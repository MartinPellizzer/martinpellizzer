Haageocereus olowinskianus

Haageocereus olowinskianus, commonly known as the Olowinsk's cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a ribbed stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces white, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in arid gardens for its ornamental value.
