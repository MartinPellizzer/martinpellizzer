Hibiscus integrifolius

Hibiscus integrifolius, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with smooth edges, which are typically light green in color. Hibiscus integrifolius thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

It is a hardy species that plays an important role in coastal ecosystems.
