Hydrocleys grosourdyana

Hydrocleys grosourdyana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater wetlands and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist soils. Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.

Hydrocleys grosourdyana is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological value.
