Hydrangea × ytiensis

Hydrangea × ytiensis, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Hydrangea × ytiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
