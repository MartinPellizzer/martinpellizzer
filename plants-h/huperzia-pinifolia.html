Huperzia pinifolia

Huperzia pinifolia, commonly known as the pine needle huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. Huperzia pinifolia is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila at the tips of the branches.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
