Hebecarpa rivinifolia

Hebecarpa rivinifolia, commonly known as the river ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
