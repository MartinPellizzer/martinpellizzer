Hypericum richeri subsp. richeri

Hypericum richeri subsp.

richeri, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, and its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is often found in the fynbos biome, where it contributes to the region's biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
