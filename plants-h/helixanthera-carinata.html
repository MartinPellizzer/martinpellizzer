Helixanthera carinata

Helixanthera carinata, commonly known as the zigzag bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Helixanthera carinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
