Henningia persica

Henningia persica, commonly known as Persian rose or Persian honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of white or pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, though it is not commonly found in the wild.
