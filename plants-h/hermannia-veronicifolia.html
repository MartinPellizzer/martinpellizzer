Hermannia veronicifolia

Hermannia veronicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Hermannia veronicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
