Helianthemum variabile subvar. viscosopuberulum

Helianthemum variabile subvar. viscosopuberulum, commonly known as the sticky-hairy rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit with dense, hairy foliage that is often coated in a sticky substance. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
