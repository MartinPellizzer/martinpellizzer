Hieracium mlinicae

Hieracium mlinicae, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium mlinicae is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
