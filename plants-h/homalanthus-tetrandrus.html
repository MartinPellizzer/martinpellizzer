Homalanthus tetrandrus

Homalanthus tetrandrus, commonly known as the Hawaiian false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as mesic forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers with four stamens. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in Hawaiian ecosystems and its role in traditional cultural practices.
