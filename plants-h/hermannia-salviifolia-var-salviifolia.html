Hermannia salviifolia var. salviifolia

Hermannia salviifolia var. salviifolia, commonly known as the red-stemmed sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that often display a reddish hue, especially when grown in full sun. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hermannia salviifolia var. salviifolia thrives in well-drained soil and requires regular watering, making it a suitable choice for humid, tropical gardens.
