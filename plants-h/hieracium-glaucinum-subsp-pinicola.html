Hieracium glaucinum subsp. pinicola

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

pinicola, commonly known as the pine mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and forest edges.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

This subspecies is characterized by its adaptability to disturbed soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
