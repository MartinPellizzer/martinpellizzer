Hemigraphis suborbicularis

Hemigraphis suborbicularis, commonly known as the silver mitten plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Hemigraphis suborbicularis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
