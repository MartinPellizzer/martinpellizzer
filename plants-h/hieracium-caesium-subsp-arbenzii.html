Hieracium caesium subsp. arbenzii

Hieracium caesium subsp.

arbenzii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain pastures.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
