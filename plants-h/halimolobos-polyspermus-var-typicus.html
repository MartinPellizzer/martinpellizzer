Halimolobos polyspermus var. typicus

Halimolobos polyspermus var. typicus, commonly known as the coastal beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
