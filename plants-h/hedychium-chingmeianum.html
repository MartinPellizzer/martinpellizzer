Hedychium chingmeianum

Hedychium chingmeianum, commonly known as the Chingmei ginger flower, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hedychium chingmeianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
