Hieracium subamoeniflorum

Hieracium subamoeniflorum, commonly known as the subamoeniflorum hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, narrow ligule.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.
