Hypericum lalandii var. madagascariense

Hypericum lalandii var. madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascan St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and elliptical leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal collections.
