Hypericum brachyphyllum

Hypericum brachyphyllum, commonly known as the short-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with four petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the summer months and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Hypericum brachyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
