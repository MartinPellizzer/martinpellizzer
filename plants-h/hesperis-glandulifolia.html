Hesperis glandulifolia

Hesperis glandulifolia, commonly known as the glandular hoary vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southwestern Europe. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with four petals arranged in a cross shape. Its leaves are hairy and have a glandular texture, which is a distinguishing characteristic.

Hesperis glandulifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
