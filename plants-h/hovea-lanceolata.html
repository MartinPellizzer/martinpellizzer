Hovea lanceolata

Hovea lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or greenish tint. Hovea lanceolata is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
