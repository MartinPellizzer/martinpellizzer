Hieracium luxurians

Hieracium luxurians, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish and hairy.

Hieracium luxurians thrives in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.
