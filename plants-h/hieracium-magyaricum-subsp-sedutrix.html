Hieracium magyaricum subsp. sedutrix

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

sedutrix, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to other hawkweed species.
