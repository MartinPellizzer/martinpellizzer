Hyoseris radiata var. baetica

Hyoseris radiata var. baetica, commonly known as the Iberian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It features a slender, branching stem with narrow, hairy leaves.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
