Hypoestes verticillaris var. latifolia

Hypoestes verticillaris var. latifolia, commonly known as the "Snowflake" or "Whorled Hypoestes," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, showy flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are typically opposite or whorled, with a broad, lance-shaped form that gives the plant its varietal name, *latifolia*. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
