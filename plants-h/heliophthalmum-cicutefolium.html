Heliophthalmum cicutefolium

Heliophthalmum cicutefolium, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
