Heterotheca subaxillaris subsp. latifolia

Heterotheca subaxillaris subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its silver-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
