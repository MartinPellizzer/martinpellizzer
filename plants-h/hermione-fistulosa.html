Hermione fistulosa

Hermione fistulosa, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
