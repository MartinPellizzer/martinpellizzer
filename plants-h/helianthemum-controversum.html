Helianthemum controversum

Helianthemum controversum, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
