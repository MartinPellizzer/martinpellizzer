Hibiscus lebelei

Hibiscus lebelei, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hibiscus lebelei is adapted to thrive in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
