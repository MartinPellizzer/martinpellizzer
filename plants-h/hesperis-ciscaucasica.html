Hesperis ciscaucasica

Hesperis ciscaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sweet rocket, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis ciscaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
