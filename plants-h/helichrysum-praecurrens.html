Helichrysum praecurrens

Helichrysum praecurrens, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ability to retain color when dried makes it a favorite in floral arrangements.
