Horridocactus eriosyzoides

Horridocactus eriosyzoides, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a low, mat-like growth. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its common name reflects its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
