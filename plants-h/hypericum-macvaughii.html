Hypericum macvaughii

Hypericum macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange glands, which are characteristic of the Hypericaceae family. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Hypericum macvaughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
