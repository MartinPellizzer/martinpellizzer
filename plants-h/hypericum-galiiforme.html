Hypericum galiiforme

Hypericum galiiforme, commonly known as the swamp St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and has opposite, linear to ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. It is part of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

The plant's flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
