Hedera cordata

Hedera cordata, commonly known as the ivy-leaved ivy, is a species of flowering plant in the Araliaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. Hedera cordata is often grown as a climbing or trailing plant in gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
