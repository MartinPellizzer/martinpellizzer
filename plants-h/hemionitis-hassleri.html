Hemionitis hassleri

Hemionitis hassleri, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique plant species native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a small, low-growing fern that thrives in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. The plant has a compact rosette structure with closely spaced, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss. Its fronds are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating that provides additional protection against desiccation.

Hemionitis hassleri is adapted to extreme conditions, making it an important indicator of arid ecosystems.
