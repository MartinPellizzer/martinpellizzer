Hemionitis pilifera

Hemionitis pilifera, commonly known as the silky hemionite, is a rare and unique fern-like plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost woolly appearance. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures rather than flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis pilifera is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
