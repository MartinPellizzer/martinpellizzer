Hymenonema graecum

Hymenonema graecum, commonly known as the Greek honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a strong, sweet scent attracting pollinators like bees and moths.

Hymenonema graecum is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
