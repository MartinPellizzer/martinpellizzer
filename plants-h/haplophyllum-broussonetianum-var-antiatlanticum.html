Haplophyllum broussonetianum var. antiatlanticum

Haplophyllum broussonetianum var. antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic rockrose, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of West Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its bright floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging maritime conditions.
