Hieracium thuringorum

Hieracium thuringorum, commonly known as the Thuringian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows and along stream banks. It is native to Central Europe and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its flowering period extends from June to September, making it a notable presence in early successional habitats.
