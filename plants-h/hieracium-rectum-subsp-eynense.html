Hieracium rectum subsp. eynense

Hieracium rectum subsp.

eynense, commonly known as the Eyne hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, terminal head. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
