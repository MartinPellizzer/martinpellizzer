Horsfieldia moluccana

Horsfieldia moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system that allows it to anchor itself to trees in its natural habitat. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in humid, warm environments with high light exposure but can tolerate moderate shade.

Horsfieldia moluccana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
