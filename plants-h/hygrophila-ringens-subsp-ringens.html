Hygrophila ringens subsp. ringens

Hygrophila ringens subsp.

ringens, commonly known as the slender hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Its ability to thrive in moist conditions makes it a popular choice for water gardeners and wetland restoration projects.
