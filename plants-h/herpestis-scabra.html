Herpestis scabra

Herpestis scabra, commonly known as the rough hound's-tongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Herpestis scabra thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
