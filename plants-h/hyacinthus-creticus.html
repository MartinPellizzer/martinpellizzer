Hyacinthus creticus

Hyacinthus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the island of Crete.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky, arid environments. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Hyacinthus creticus is also noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in full sun.
