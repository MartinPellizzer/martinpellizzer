Hoodia pilifera subsp. pillansii

Hoodia pilifera subsp.

pillansii, commonly known as the "desert cucumber," is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. Its unique morphology includes spiny tubercles and a creeping growth habit that helps it anchor in sandy soils.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and has gained attention for its potential nutritional properties.
