Hyptis stachyodes

Hyptis stachyodes, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in moist, open habitats.

Hyptis stachyodes is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
