Halostachys patagonica

Halostachys patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for reclaiming degraded lands. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.

Halostachys patagonica plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecosystem balance in its native habitat.
