Hippophae sinensis

Hippophae sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are small, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

The species is valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and erosion control.
