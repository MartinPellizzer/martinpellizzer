Hibiscus trilineatus

Hibiscus trilineatus, commonly known as the three-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five deeply lobed petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Hibiscus trilineatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks.
