Hornstedtia villosa

Hornstedtia villosa, commonly known as the silky beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Hornstedtia villosa is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
