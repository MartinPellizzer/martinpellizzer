Helianthemum rotundifolium subsp. supracanum

Helianthemum rotundifolium subsp.

supracanum, commonly known as the coastal sunflower or rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
