Hymenopappus integrifolius

Hymenopappus integrifolius, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its spread across dry, sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
