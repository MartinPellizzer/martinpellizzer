Hypericum penticosia

Hypericum penticosia, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and produces red-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
