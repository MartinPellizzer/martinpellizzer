Hedyotis crassifolia var. micrantha

Hedyotis crassifolia var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
