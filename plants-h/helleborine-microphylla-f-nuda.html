Helleborine microphylla f. nuda

Helleborine microphylla f. nuda, commonly known as the naked helleborine, is a rare and intriguing orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that often lack petals, giving it its "naked" appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a unique ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
