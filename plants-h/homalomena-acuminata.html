Homalomena acuminata

Homalomena acuminata, commonly known as the arrowleaf caladium, is a tropical evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that come in a variety of vibrant colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on spathaceous bracts. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Homalomena acuminata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage.
