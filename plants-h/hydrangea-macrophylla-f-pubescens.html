Hydrangea macrophylla f. pubescens

Hydrangea macrophylla f. pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy leaf hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH.

This variety is notable for its hairy or pubescent foliage, which gives it a distinctive, textured appearance. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and produces cone-like fruit capsules that contain small seeds.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
