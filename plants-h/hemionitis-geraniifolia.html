Hemionitis geraniifolia, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a small, perennial herb native to the western United States.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, elongated inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
