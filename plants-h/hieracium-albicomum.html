Hieracium albicomum

Hieracium albicomum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

It is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
