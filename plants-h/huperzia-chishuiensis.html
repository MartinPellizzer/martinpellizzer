Huperzia chishuiensis

Huperzia chishuiensis, commonly known as the Chishui huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to China.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically located at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Huperzia chishuiensis is of interest to botanists due to its primitive features and potential ecological significance.
