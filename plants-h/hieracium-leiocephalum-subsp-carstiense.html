Hieracium leiocephalum subsp. carstiense

Hieracium leiocephalum subsp.

carstiense, commonly known as Carstian's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions.
