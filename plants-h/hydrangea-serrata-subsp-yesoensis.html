Hydrangea serrata subsp. yesoensis

Hydrangea serrata subsp.

yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH.

The plant features serrated leaves that are dark green in summer and turn red or orange in autumn. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
