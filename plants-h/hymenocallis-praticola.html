Hymenocallis praticola

Hymenocallis praticola, commonly known as the prairie spider lily, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers have a subtle, sweet fragrance.

Hymenocallis praticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
