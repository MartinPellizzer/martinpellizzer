Hariota

Hariota, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the genus Schlumbergera and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that store water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, typically blooming in winter. Hariota thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
