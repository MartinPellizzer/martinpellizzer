Hieracium murorum subsp. sublepistoides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sublepistoides, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions.
