Hieracium sylvicola

Hieracium sylvicola, commonly known as the wood harebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland and forested areas, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Hieracium sylvicola is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
