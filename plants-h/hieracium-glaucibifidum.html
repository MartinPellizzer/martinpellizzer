Hieracium glaucibifidum

Hieracium glaucibifidum, commonly known as the glaucous-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
