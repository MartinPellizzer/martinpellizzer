Haplopappus carthamoides var. rigidus

Haplopappus carthamoides var. rigidus, commonly known as rigid horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and roadsides. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces achenes with pappus bristles for wind dispersal.

It is considered a winter annual, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
