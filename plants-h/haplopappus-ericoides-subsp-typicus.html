Haplopappus ericoides subsp. typicus

Haplopappus ericoides subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the typical ericoid haplopappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense heads and attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
