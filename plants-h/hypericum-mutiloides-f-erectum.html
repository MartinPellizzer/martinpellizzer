Hypericum mutiloides f. erectum

Hypericum mutiloides f. erectum, commonly known as the upright form of the common St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
