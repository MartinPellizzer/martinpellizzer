Hydrangea radiata var. canescens

Hydrangea radiata var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, rounded flower heads that range in color from white to pink depending on soil pH. The leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a silvery-white bloom of fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to provide seasonal interest throughout the growing season.
