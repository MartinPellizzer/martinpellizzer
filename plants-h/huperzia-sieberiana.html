Huperzia sieberiana

Huperzia sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and needle-like leaves that form dense, mat-like structures. The plant is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, stalked clusters on the underside of its leaves.

Huperzia sieberiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
