Hieracium cordatum subsp. vayredanum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

vayredanum, commonly known as the Vayredan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosettes of heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
