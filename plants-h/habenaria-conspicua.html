Habenaria conspicua

Habenaria conspicua, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features long, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure, including a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Habenaria conspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
