Hieracium pratense subsp. redenicense

Hieracium pratense subsp.

redenicense, commonly known as the Reddenice hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
