Hieracium tephrellum

Hieracium tephrellum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
