Hedysarum varium

Hedysarum varium, commonly known as variable vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are small and oblong, often dispersed by wind or animals, aiding in its spread across different habitats.
