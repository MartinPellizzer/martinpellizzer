Hypericum quadrangulum var. neapolitanum

Hypericum quadrangulum var. neapolitanum, commonly known as the Naples St. John's Wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has four-sided stems and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact form and vibrant floral display.
