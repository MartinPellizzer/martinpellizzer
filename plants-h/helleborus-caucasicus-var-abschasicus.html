Helleborus caucasicus var. abschasicus

Helleborus caucasicus var. abschasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with a waxy texture and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale green to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its winter blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter or early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The plant contains toxic compounds, so it should be handled with care.
