Haemanthus humilis subsp. humilis

Haemanthus humilis subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the Natal grasslily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of bright red flowers in late winter to early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
