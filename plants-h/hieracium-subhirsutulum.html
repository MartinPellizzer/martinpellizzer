Hieracium subhirsutulum

Hieracium subhirsutulum, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette, while its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The species is often found in moist habitats and is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
