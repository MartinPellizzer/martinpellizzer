Hieracium taurinense subsp. sarracenicum

Hieracium taurinense subsp.

sarracenicum, commonly known as the Sarracenic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
