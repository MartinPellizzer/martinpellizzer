Hypericum atlanticum

Hypericum atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypericum atlanticum is often found in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind.
