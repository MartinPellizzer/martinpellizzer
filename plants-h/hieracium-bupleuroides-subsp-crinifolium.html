Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. crinifolium

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

crinifolium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The leaves are typically lobed and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
