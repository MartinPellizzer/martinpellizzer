Haplopappus greenei subsp. typicus

Haplopappus greenei subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and specialized structures called phyllaries.

The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow and occur in clusters, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
