Helictotrichon gervaisii

Helictotrichon gervaisii, commonly known as Gervais' bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers.

Helictotrichon gervaisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
