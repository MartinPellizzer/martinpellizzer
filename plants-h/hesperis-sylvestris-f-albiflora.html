Hesperis sylvestris f. albiflora

Hesperis sylvestris f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a hybrid form of Hesperis sylvestris, characterized by its pale to white flowers instead of the typical pink or purple hues. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
