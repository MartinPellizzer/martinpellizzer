Hesperis dahurica

Hesperis dahurica, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, with four petals arranged in a cross shape. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis dahurica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
