Haplopappus acradenius

Haplopappus acradenius, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it produces achenes with pappus structures for wind dispersal.

The species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
