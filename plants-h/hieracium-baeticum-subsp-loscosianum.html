Hieracium baeticum subsp. loscosianum

Hieracium baeticum subsp.

loscosianum, commonly known as the Loscosian Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and open woodlands. It has small, linear to lanceolate leaves and produces numerous seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
