Hieracium sagittatum subsp. leucofarreatum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

leucofarreatum, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers with white veins.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in glandular dots. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist or sandy soils.
