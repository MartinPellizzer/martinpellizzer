Hieracium laniferum subsp. spathulatum

Hieracium laniferum subsp.

spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow disk flowers with numerous small florets. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its growth habit and floral morphology make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
