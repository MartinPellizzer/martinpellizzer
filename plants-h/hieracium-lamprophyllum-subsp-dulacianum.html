Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp. dulacianum

Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp.

dulacianum, commonly known as the Dulacian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions.
