Helicia artocarpoides

Helicia artocarpoides, commonly known as the Philippine hibiscus or hibiscus tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble those of the hibiscus. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces fruits that are aggregate drupes, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
