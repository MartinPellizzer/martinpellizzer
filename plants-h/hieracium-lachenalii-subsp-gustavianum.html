Hieracium lachenalii subsp. gustavianum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

gustavianum, commonly known as the Gustav's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Hieracium genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other hawkweeds.
