Helichrysum hilliardiae

Helichrysum hilliardiae, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
