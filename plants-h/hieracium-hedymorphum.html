Hieracium hedymorphum

Hieracium hedymorphum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like rays, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
