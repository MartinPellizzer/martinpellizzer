Hieracium aestivum subsp. valdefrondosum

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

valdefrondosum, commonly known as the summer hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal flower head with ray florets.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological adaptations to Mediterranean climates.
