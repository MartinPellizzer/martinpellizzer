Hydrangea glabripes

Hydrangea glabripes, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy inflorescences that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

Hydrangea glabripes thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
