Heliconia albicosta

Heliconia albicosta, commonly known as the white-veined heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that surround its inflorescence. The plant's leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are clustered in a spiral pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia albicosta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
