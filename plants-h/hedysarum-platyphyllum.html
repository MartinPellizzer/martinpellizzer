Hedysarum platyphyllum

Hedysarum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flatleaf sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Hedysarum platyphyllum is valued for its forage qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for agricultural purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
