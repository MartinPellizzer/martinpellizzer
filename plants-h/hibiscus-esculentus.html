Hibiscus esculentus

Hibiscus esculentus, commonly known as okra, is a flowering plant native to Africa and cultivated in warm climates worldwide.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces edible capsules that are widely used in cooking. Its botanical features include a robust stem, hairy foliage, and a short growing season.

Hibiscus esculentus is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal.
