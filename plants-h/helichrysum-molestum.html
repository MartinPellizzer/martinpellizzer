Helichrysum molestum

Helichrysum molestum, commonly known as the sticky helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its yellow-orange flowers and glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Helichrysum molestum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
