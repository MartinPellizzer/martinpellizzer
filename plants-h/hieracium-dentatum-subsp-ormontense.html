Hieracium dentatum subsp. ormontense

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

ormontense, commonly known as the Ormonia Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry meadows, rocky soils, and disturbed habitats, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and can be found in various conservation areas.
