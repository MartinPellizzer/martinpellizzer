Hieracium auricula proles kralikii

Hieracium auricula proles kralikii, commonly known as the Kralik's hairy hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in dry meadows and rocky soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
