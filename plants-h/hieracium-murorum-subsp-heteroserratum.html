Hieracium murorum subsp. heteroserratum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

heteroserratum, commonly known as the rough spear-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has a robust, upright stem that is often spiny, and its leaves are lance-shaped with irregularly serrated edges. The flowers are yellow and clustered in a loose head, and the plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.
