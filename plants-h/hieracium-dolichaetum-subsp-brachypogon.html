Hieracium dolichaetum subsp. brachypogon

Hieracium dolichaetum subsp.

brachypogon, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is characterized by its shorter stems and smaller flower heads compared to other members of the species.
