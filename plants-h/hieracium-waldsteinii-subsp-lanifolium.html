Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. lanifolium

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

lanifolium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, typically arranged in a single head. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
