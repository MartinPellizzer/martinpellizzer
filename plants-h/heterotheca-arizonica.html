Heterotheca arizonica

Heterotheca arizonica, commonly known as Arizona tansy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca arizonica is adapted to arid conditions and has small, hairy leaves to reduce water loss.
