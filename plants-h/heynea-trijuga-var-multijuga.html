Heynea trijuga var. multijuga

Heynea trijuga var. multijuga, commonly known as the three-angled balsam.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often arranged in a triangular pattern. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
