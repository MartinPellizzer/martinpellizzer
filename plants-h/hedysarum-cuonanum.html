Hedysarum cuonanum

Hedysarum cuonanum, commonly known as the Cuonan sweetvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Hedysarum cuonanum is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native environment.
