Helichrysum rubicundum

Helichrysum rubicundum, commonly known as the red-stemmed everlasting flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and reddish-brown stems, which give it its common name. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy.

Helichrysum rubicundum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
