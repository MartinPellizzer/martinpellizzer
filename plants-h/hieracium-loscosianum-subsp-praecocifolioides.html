Hieracium loscosianum subsp. praecocifolioides

Hieracium loscosianum subsp.

praecocifolioides, commonly known as the early-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath.

Its flowering period occurs in early summer, making it an early bloomer in its habitat.
