Hieracium stuebelii var. consacae

Hieracium stuebelii var. consacae, commonly known as the sacred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has small, basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
