Hypochaeris infesta

Hypochaeris infesta, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, and it produces yellow flowers with ray florets. Its seed heads are characterized by a crown of bracts and are often used in herbal remedies.

Hypochaeris infesta is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
