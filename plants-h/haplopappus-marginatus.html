Haplopappus marginatus

Haplopappus marginatus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry environments. It has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its structure and growth habits make it a hardy species often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
