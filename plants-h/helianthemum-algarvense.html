Helianthemum algarvense

Helianthemum algarvense, commonly known as the Algarve sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.

Helianthemum algarvense is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
