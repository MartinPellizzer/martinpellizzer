Hieracium leptoclonum

Hieracium leptoclonum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium leptoclonum spreads through rhizomes and can form dense colonies, making it both a common wildflower and a potential weed in certain environments.
