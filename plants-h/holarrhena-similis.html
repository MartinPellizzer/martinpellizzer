Holarrhena similis

Holarrhena similis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.

The species is also appreciated for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
