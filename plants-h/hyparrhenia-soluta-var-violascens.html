Hyparrhenia soluta var. violascens

Hyparrhenia soluta var. violascens, commonly known as the purple false grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale green to purplish leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for erosion control in degraded landscapes.
