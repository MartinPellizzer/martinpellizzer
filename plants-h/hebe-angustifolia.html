Hebe angustifolia

Hebe angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe angustifolia is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
