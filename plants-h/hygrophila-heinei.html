Hygrophila heinei

Hygrophila heinei, commonly known as the slender water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along riverbanks.

Hygrophila heinei is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in water gardens.
