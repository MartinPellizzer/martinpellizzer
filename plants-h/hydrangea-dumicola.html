Hydrangea dumicola

Hydrangea dumicola, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Hydrangea dumicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
