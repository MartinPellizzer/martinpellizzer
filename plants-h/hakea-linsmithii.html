Hakea linsmithii

Hakea linsmithii, commonly known as the Linsmith's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to sandy soils.

Hakea linsmithii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
