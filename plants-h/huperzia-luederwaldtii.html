Huperzia luederwaldtii

Huperzia luederwaldtii, commonly known as the Luederwaldt's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, cushion-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky outcrops and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are borne on the underside of the leaves, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Huperzia luederwaldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
