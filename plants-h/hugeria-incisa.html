Hugeria incisa

Hugeria incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf huarango, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. Hugeria incisa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert species.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
