Hypechusa tricolor

Hypechusa tricolor, commonly known as the three-color bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking three-colored petals, typically featuring a combination of white, blue, and violet hues. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hypechusa tricolor is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
