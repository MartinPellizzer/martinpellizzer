Helichrysum angustifrondeum

Helichrysum angustifrondeum, commonly known as narrow-leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to golden-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a long-lasting appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
