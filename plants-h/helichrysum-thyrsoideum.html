Helichrysum thyrsoideum

Helichrysum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden-orange flower heads, which are composed of many small, daisy-like florets. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
