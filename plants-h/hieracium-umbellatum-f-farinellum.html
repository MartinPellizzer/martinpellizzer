Hieracium umbellatum f. farinellum

Hieracium umbellatum f. farinellum, commonly known as the farinellum hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. This subspecies is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has a low-growing habit with a central stem that branches near the base.

It is a perennial plant that produces seeds through its composite flower heads.
