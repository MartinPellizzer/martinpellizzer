Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. praetutiense

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

praetutiense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
