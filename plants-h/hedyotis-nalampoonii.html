Hedyotis nalampoonii

Hedyotis nalampoonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has slender, often hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Hedyotis nalampoonii is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
