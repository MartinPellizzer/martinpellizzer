Hedera minor-marmorata

Hedera minor-marmorata, commonly known as the marble ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or silver marbling patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
