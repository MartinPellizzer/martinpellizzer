Haloragis teucrioides var. lanceolata

Haloragis teucrioides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes. It is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it a common species in riparian zones.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
