Hyptis melissoides

Hyptis melissoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The leaves are frequently used in teas and aromatherapy due to their calming properties.
