Hosackia glabra

Hosackia glabra, commonly known as the plains false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Hosackia glabra is often found in dry prairies and open woods, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
