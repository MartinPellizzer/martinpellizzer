Habenaria lancipetala

Habenaria lancipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying environmental conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
