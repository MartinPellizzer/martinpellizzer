Hieracium stygium

Hieracium stygium, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers bloom in yellow or orange hues.

Hieracium stygium is often found in meadows and grasslands, playing a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
