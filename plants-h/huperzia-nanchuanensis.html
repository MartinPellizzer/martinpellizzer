Huperzia nanchuanensis

Huperzia nanchuanensis, commonly known as the Nanchuan clubmoss, is a rare and endangered species native to China.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia in clusters at the base of its leaves, which are responsible for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
