Hypericum wardianum

Hypericum wardianum, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the genus Hypericum, which includes other species of st. john's wort.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
