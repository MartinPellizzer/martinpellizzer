Habenaria gourlieana

Habenaria gourlieana, commonly known as the Gourlie's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
