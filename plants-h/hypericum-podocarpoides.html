Hypericum podocarpoides

Hypericum podocarpoides, commonly known as the African St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Hypericum podocarpoides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
