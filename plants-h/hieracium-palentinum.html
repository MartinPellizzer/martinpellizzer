Hieracium palentinum

Hieracium palentinum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-orange.

Hieracium palentinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
