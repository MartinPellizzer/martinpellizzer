Helleborus olympicus

Helleborus olympicus, commonly known as the Greek hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is known for its long blooming period, often from late winter to early spring, making it a notable early-flowering plant.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
