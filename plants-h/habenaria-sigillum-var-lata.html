Habenaria sigillum var. lata

Habenaria sigillum var. lata, commonly known as the broad-seal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and often hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique lip that resembles a seal. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
