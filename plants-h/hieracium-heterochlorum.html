Hieracium heterochlorum

Hieracium heterochlorum, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive color variation, ranging from green to reddish or purplish.

Hieracium heterochlorum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
