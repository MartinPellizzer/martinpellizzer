Heteropogon contortus subvar. secundus

Heteropogon contortus subvar. secundus, commonly known as the hooked grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often twisted or curled. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local grassland ecosystems.
