Hieracium muricellum subsp. gandogeri

Hieracium muricellum subsp.

gandogeri, commonly known as the Gandogeri hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
