Hippocratea inundata

Hippocratea inundata, commonly known as the water hippocratea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
