Herniaria incana var. baetica

Herniaria incana var. baetica, commonly known as the Iberian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, succulent stems and narrow, fleshy leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
