Hindsia longiflora subsp. longiflora

Hindsia longiflora subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered hindsia, is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its woody structure and tropical origin. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
