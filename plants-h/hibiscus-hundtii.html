Hibiscus hundtii

Hibiscus hundtii, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green in color. Hibiscus hundtii thrives in tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
