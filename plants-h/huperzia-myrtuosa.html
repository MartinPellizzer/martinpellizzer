Huperzia myrtuosa

Huperzia myrtuosa, commonly known as the myrtle clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Huperzia myrtuosa is a perennial plant that reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche, contributing to its survival in diverse environments.
