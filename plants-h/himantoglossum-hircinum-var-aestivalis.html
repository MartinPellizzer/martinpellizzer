Himantoglossum hircinum var. aestivalis

Himantoglossum hircinum var. aestivalis, commonly known as the early marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wet meadows and marshes in Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in habitats with high water tables.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
