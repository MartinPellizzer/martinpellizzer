Hieracium alpinum var. pusillum

Hieracium alpinum var. pusillum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it tolerates cold temperatures and strong winds. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in a single terminal head, which is characteristic of the genus Hieracium.

It is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous environments.
