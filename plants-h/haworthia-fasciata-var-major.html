Haworthia fasciata var. major

Haworthia fasciata var. major, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with white or light-colored markings resembling a zebra's stripes. The plant typically grows in compact rosettes and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water. It prefers bright, indirect light and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
