Holarrhena africana

Holarrhena africana, commonly known as the African torchwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by elongated pods containing flat, dark seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
