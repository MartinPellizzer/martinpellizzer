Hypericum montanum f. minus

Hypericum montanum f. minus, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ecological and aesthetic value.
