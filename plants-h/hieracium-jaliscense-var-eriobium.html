Hieracium jaliscense var. eriobium

Hieracium jaliscense var. eriobium, commonly known as the Jalisco yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-orange flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in composite heads.

It is often found in the state of Jalisco, from which it derives its name.
