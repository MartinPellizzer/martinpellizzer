Hieracium parvifactum

Hieracium parvifactum, commonly known as the small-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish hue along the veins.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
