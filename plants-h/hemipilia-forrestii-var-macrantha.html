Hemipilia forrestii var. macrantha

Hemipilia forrestii var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered hemipilia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
