Hieracium floribundum subsp. baenitzii

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

baenitzii, commonly known as the Baenitz's common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Its flowers bloom in early summer and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies.
