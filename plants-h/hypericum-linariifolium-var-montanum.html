Hypericum linariifolium var. montanum

Hypericum linariifolium var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Hypericum species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
