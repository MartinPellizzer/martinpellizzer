Hedypnois cretica f. persica

Hedypnois cretica f. persica, commonly known as the Persian fig-leaf tree, is a rare and unique cultivar of the Hedypnois cretica species.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, dry climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value.

Despite its beauty, it is a slow-growing plant that requires careful cultivation to flourish.
