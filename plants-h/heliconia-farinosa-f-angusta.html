Heliconia farinosa f. angusta

Heliconia farinosa f. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow bracts that form a distinctive spiral shape. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Heliconia farinosa f. angusta is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
