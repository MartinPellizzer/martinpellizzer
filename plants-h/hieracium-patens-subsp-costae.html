Hieracium patens subsp. costae

Hieracium patens subsp.

costae, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy stems that often bear reddish or purple spots.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy or toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.
