Hoya danumensis subsp. amarii

Hoya danumensis subsp.

amarii, commonly known as the Sweetheart Hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. Hoya danumensis subsp.

amarii is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
