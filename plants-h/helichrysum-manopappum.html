Helichrysum manopappum

Helichrysum manopappum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble small suns, which remain intact even after drying. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
