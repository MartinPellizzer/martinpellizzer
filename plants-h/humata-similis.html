Humata similis

Humata similis, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, orchid-like flowers that closely resemble those of other species, a trait that aids in its pollination by misleading pollinators. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that appear in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, Humata similis is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
