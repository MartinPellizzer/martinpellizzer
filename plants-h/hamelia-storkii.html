Hamelia storkii

Hamelia storkii, commonly known as the scarlet flame or stork's bill, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant red tubular flowers during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Hamelia storkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.
