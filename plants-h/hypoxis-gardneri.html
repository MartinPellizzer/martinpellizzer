Hypoxis gardneri

Hypoxis gardneri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
