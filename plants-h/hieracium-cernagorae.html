Hieracium cernagorae

Hieracium cernagorae, commonly known as the Cernagora hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium cernagorae is a relatively rare species, with limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
