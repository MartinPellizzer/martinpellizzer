Hypericum lanuriense

Hypericum lanuriense, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
