Hypechusa lutea var. vestita

Hypechusa lutea var. vestita, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
