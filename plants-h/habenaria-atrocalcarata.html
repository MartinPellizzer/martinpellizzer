Habenaria atrocalcarata

Habenaria atrocalcarata, commonly known as the black-spurred orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring dark purple or black spurs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with three sepals and three petals, and the labellum is often adorned with intricate markings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
