Habenaria bosseriana

Habenaria bosseriana, commonly known as the Bosser's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, particularly Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are borne on tall, erect stems.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
