Hippeastrum reticulatum

Hippeastrum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum reticulatum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
