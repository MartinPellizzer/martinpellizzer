Hieracium cruentum

Hieracium cruentum, commonly known as bloody hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive red or brown center.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
