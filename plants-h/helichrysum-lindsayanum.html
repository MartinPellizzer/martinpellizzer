Helichrysum lindsayanum

Helichrysum lindsayanum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is generally gray-green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous leaves.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and ornamental appeal.
