Helichrysum crassinerve

Helichrysum crassinerve, commonly known as the broad-veined everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden-orange inflorescences that resemble small daisies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum crassinerve is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
