Hieracium murorum subsp. paramauron

Hieracium murorum subsp.

paramauron, commonly known as the coastal hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along sea cliffs and dunes.

The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers, which are arranged in a single head and are visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, often with a reddish or purplish tinge.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh coastal environments and is considered a characteristic plant of maritime habitats.
