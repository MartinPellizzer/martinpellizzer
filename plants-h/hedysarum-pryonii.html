Hedysarum pryonii

Hedysarum pryonii, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range from pale yellow to reddish-purple, typically blooming in spring. Hedysarum pryonii has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
