Horsfieldia subtilis var. schlechteri

Horsfieldia subtilis var. schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid forests. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
