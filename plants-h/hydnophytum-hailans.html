Hydnophytum hailans

Hydnophytum hailans, commonly known as the sticky vine or aerial root plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection. The plant has specialized aerial roots that allow it to attach to trees and other structures. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Hydnophytum hailans is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
