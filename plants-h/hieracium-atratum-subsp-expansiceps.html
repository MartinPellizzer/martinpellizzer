Hieracium atratum subsp. expansiceps

Hieracium atratum subsp.

expansiceps, commonly known as the broad-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal rosettes of leaves and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Its subspecific name, *expansiceps*, refers to the broad and spreading nature of its flower heads.
