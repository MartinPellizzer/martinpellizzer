Halosarcia undulata

Halosarcia undulata, commonly known as the undulating burr cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

Halosarcia undulata plays a role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
