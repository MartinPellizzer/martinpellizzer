Himantoglossum caprinum subsp. robustissimum

Himantoglossum caprinum subsp.

robustissimum, commonly known as the robust alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth compared to other members of the species, making it a resilient plant in harsh mountain environments.
