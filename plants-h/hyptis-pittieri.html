Hyptis pittieri

Hyptis pittieri, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Hyptis pittieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
