Hippeastrum argilagae

Hippeastrum argilagae, commonly known as the Argilagae amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Hippeastrum argilagae is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
