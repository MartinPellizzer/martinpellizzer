Hieracium cerinthoides proles longifolium

Hieracium cerinthoides proles longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
