Hypericum mysurense

Hypericum mysurense, commonly known as the Siberian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in small, compact inflorescences. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

Hypericum mysurense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
