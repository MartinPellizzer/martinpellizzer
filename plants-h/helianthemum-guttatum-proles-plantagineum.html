Helianthemum guttatum proles plantagineum

Helianthemum guttatum proles plantagineum, commonly known as the sticky rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with grey-green, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ability to retain moisture in its leaves helps it survive in dry conditions.
