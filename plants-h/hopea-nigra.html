Hopea nigra

Hopea nigra, commonly known as black hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy of elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellow or green, and it produces woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

Hopea nigra plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
