Hieracium saxifragum subsp. lepidolytes

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

lepidolytes, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, which help in reducing water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and invertebrates.
