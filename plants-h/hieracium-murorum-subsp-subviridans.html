Hieracium murorum subsp. subviridans

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subviridans, commonly known as the green mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features dark green, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
