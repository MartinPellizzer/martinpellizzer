Hieracium plantaginifolium

Hieracium plantaginifolium, commonly known as the common meadowhawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
