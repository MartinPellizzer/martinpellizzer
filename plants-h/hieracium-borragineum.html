Hieracium borragineum

Hieracium borragineum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces numerous yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Hieracium borragineum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
