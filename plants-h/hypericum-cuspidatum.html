Hypericum cuspidatum

Hypericum cuspidatum, commonly known as prickly St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its stems are woody and armed with short, sharp spines.

Hypericum cuspidatum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
