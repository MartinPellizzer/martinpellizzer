Hibiscus palustris

Hibiscus palustris, commonly known as swamp rosemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom from late summer to early fall. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet or moist soils, typically found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Hibiscus palustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
