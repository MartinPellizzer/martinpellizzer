Hemarthria japonica

Hemarthria japonica, commonly known as Japanese flat sedge, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Hemarthria japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
