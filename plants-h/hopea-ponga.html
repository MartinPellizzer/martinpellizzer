Hopea ponga

Hopea ponga, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

Hopea ponga plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
