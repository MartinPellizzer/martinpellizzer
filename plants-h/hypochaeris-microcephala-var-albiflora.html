Hypochaeris microcephala var. albiflora

Hypochaeris microcephala var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
