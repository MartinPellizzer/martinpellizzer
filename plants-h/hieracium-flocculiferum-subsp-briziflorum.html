Hieracium flocculiferum subsp. briziflorum

Hieracium flocculiferum subsp.

briziflorum, commonly known as the bristleflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features dark green, broadly lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange-red flowers. Its flowers are surrounded by soft, hairy bracts, which give it its common name.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
