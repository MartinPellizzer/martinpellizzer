Hibbertia calycina

Hibbertia calycina, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has narrow, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibbertia calycina is a woody shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils.
