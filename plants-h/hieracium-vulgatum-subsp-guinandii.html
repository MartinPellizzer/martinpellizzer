Hieracium vulgatum subsp. guinandii

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

guinandii, commonly known as the Guinand's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.
