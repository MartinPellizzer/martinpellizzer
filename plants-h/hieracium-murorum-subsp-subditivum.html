Hieracium murorum subsp. subditivum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subditivum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas. It is characterized by its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to colonizing open, sunny habitats.
