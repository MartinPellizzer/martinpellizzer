Hypericum maculatum var. epunctatum

Hypericum maculatum var. epunctatum, commonly known as the spotted dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves with distinctive purple or red markings. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
