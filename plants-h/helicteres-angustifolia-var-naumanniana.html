Helicteres angustifolia var. naumanniana

Helicteres angustifolia var. naumanniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf hedge bean, is a variant of the hedge bean plant native to parts of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems, serrated leaflets, and a distinct floral structure with yellowish-green blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
