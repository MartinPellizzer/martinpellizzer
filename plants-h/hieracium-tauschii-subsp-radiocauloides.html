Hieracium tauschii subsp. radiocauloides

Hieracium tauschii subsp.

radiocauloides, commonly known as the radiocauloides hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth form with basal leaves that are often lobed or dissected.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
