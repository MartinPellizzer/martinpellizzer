Hypericum revolutum

Hypericum revolutum, commonly known as the rolled-stem St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
