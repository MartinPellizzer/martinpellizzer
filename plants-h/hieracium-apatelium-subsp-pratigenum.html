Hieracium apatelium subsp. pratigenum

Hieracium apatelium subsp.

pratigenum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, often forming dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
