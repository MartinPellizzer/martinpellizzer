Habenaria bifolia

Habenaria bifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, erect stem and two distinct, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are often fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
