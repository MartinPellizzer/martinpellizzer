Hibiscus micranthus f. macranthus

Hibiscus micranthus f. macranthus, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a cultivated form of the common hibiscus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with thick, woody stems. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and the flowers are typically pink to red in color.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in floral arrangements.
