Hesperantha spicata subsp. fistulosa

Hesperantha spicata subsp.

fistulosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa. It features tall, spiky flower clusters that range in color from red to orange, giving it its common name.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a standout in both garden and horticultural settings.
