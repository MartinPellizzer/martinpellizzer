Helwingia japonica subsp. formosana

Helwingia japonica subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan. It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and elliptic leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
