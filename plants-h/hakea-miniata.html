Hakea miniata

Hakea miniata, commonly known as the dwarf hakea, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It features dense, cylindrical clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Hakea miniata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
