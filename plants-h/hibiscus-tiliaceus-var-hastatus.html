Hibiscus tiliaceus var. hastatus

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. hastatus, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows along coastal areas and in wetlands. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Its flowers are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
