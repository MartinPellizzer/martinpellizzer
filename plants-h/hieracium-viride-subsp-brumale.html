Hieracium viride subsp. brumale

Hieracium viride subsp.

brumale, commonly known as the winter hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

It is known for its early flowering habit, often blooming in late winter or early spring.
