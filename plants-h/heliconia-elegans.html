Heliconia elegans

Heliconia elegans, commonly known as the elegant heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, banana-like inflorescence composed of brightly colored bracts that enclose numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The flowers are typically pollinated by hummingbirds and bats, making it an important plant in tropical ecosystems.
