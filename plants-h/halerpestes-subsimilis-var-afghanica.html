Halerpestes subsimilis var. afghanica

Halerpestes subsimilis var. afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan date palm, is a variety of the date palm native to regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, fan-shaped leaves that are typically green and robust. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible dates, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its agricultural significance.
