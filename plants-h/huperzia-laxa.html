Huperzia laxa

Huperzia laxa, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette-like pattern. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Huperzia laxa is a perennial plant that reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important component of certain forest ecosystems.
