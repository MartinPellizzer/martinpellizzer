Hymenophyllum elegans f. minus

Hymenophyllum elegans f. minus, commonly known as the small-leaf filmy fern, is a rare and delicate fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, translucent fronds that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in dense forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a specialized covering.

This fern is notable for its unique morphology and is often sought after by botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
