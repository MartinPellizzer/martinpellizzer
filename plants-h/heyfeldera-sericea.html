Heyfeldera sericea

Heyfeldera sericea, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
