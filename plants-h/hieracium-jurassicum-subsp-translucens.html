Hieracium jurassicum subsp. translucens

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

translucens, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The subspecies translucens is distinguished by its translucent, waxy leaves and yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
