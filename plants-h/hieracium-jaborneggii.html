Hieracium jaborneggii

Hieracium jaborneggii, commonly known as the Austrian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, basal leaves that are often lobed, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium jaborneggii is a perennial species that is relatively uncommon in the wild.
