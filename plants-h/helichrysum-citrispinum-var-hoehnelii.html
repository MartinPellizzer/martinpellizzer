Helichrysum citrispinum var. hoehnelii

Helichrysum citrispinum var. hoehnelii, commonly known as the Hoehnel's golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small suns, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
