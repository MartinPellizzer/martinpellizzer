Habranthus nemoralis

Habranthus nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in woodland habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
