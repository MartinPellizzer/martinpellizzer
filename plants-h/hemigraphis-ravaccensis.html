Hemigraphis ravaccensis

Hemigraphis ravaccensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf sorrel, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hemigraphis ravaccensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
