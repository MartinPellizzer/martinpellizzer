Humboltia glossopogon

Humboltia glossopogon, commonly known as the California goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers. Humboltia glossopogon has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, dandelion-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
