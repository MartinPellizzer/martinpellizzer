Hieracium sophiae

Hieracium sophiae, commonly known as the sacred hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium sophiae is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
