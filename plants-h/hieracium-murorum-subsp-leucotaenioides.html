Hieracium murorum subsp. leucotaenioides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

leucotaenioides, commonly known as the white-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive white band around the base.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
