Hippeastrum chacoense

Hippeastrum chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco tiger flower, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Hippeastrum chacoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
