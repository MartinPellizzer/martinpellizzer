Hypericum papillosum

Hypericum papillosum, commonly known as the yellow archangel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum papillosum is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
