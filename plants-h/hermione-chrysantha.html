Hermione chrysantha

Hermione chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hair, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and relatively low maintenance requirements.
