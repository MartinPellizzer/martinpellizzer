Hedysarum longifolium

Hedysarum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often used in forage and can be an important food source for certain wildlife species.
