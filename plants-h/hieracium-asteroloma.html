Hieracium asteroloma

Hieracium asteroloma, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, while its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium asteroloma is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
