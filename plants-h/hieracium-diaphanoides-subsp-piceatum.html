Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. piceatum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

piceatum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its leaves are characterized by their thick, leathery texture and dark coloration.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
