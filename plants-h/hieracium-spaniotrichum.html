Hieracium spaniotrichum

Hieracium spaniotrichum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial plant that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, making it a notable presence in its natural habitat.
