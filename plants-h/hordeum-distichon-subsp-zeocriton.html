Hordeum distichon subsp. zeocriton

Hordeum distichon subsp.

zeocriton, commonly known as the slender wild barley, is a species of grass native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, two-rowed spikelets.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural use.
