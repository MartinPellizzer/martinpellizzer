Huperzia talpiphila

Huperzia talpiphila, commonly known as the talpiphila clubmoss, is a species of fern ally native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its leaves, typical of the Huperzia genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on ancient plant lineages and ecological adaptations.
