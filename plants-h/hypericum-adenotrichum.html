Hypericum adenotrichum

Hypericum adenotrichum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Hypericum adenotrichum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
