Hugonia macrocarpa

Hugonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hugonia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are a key feature of its botanical profile. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in swampy areas.

Hugonia macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
