Hypoxis probata

Hypoxis probata, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae, which is closely related to the iris family.

Hypoxis probata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
