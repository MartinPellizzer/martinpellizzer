Hieracium nigrescens subsp. amaurocomum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

amaurocomum, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its dark green leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in late summer.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological preferences.
