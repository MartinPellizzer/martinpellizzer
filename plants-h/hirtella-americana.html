Hirtella americana

Hirtella americana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or *Carambola*, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, five-lobed leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, which resembles a star when sliced, is edible and has a tart, sweet flavor.

Hirtella americana is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
