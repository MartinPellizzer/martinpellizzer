Hieracium bicapitatum

Hieracium bicapitatum, commonly known as the two-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellowish-green flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Hieracium bicapitatum is often found in temperate climates and can spread through rhizomes or seeds.
