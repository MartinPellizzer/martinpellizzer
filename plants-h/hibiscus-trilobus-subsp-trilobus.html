Hibiscus trilobus subsp. trilobus

Hibiscus trilobus subsp.

trilobus, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink to red in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. Hibiscus trilobus subsp.

trilobus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
