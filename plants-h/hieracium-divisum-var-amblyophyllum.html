Hieracium divisum var. amblyophyllum

Hieracium divisum var. amblyophyllum, commonly known as the blunt-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and divided leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its foliage is often covered with fine hairs, and it produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers throughout the summer.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
