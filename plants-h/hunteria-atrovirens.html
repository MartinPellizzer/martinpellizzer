Hunteria atrovirens

Hunteria atrovirens, commonly known as the black-green hunteria, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is also valued in conservation efforts.
