Hieracium berninae

Hieracium berninae, commonly known as the Bernina hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium berninae is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold and harsh mountain conditions.
