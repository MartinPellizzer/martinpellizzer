Helianthus sodiroi

Helianthus sodiroi, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, showy yellow-orange flower heads with dark centers, surrounded by broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Helianthus sodiroi is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
