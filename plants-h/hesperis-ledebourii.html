Hesperis ledebourii

Hesperis ledebourii, commonly known as the Alpine honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, range in color from white to pale pink, and the plant produces small, shiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hesperis ledebourii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
