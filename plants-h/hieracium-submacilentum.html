Hieracium submacilentum

Hieracium submacilentum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disc florets.

Hieracium submacilentum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
