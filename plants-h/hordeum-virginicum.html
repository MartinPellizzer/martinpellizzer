Hordeum virginicum

Hordeum virginicum, commonly known as wild barley, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture.

Hordeum virginicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
