Hieracium ariglaucum

Hieracium ariglaucum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium ariglaucum is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be invasive in non-native habitats.
