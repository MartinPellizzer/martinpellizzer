Hyptis mollis var. vulgaris

Hyptis mollis var. vulgaris, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The leaves are typically harvested for use in teas or as a spice in cooking.
