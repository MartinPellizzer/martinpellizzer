Heliconia pulverulenta

Heliconia pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences with small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad and often have a rough, textured surface.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
