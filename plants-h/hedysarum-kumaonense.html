Hedysarum kumaonense

Hedysarum kumaonense, commonly known as Kumaon peacock pea, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum kumaonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
