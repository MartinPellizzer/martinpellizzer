Hypericum uralum var. elongatum

Hypericum uralum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
