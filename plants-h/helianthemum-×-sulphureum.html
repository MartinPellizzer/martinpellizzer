Helianthemum × sulphureum

Helianthemum × sulphureum, commonly known as the sulphur cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has a compact growth habit with woody stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
