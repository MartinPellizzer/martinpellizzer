Hegemone chartosepala

Hegemone chartosepala, commonly known as the silky leaf hegemone, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, smooth, and often velvety leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental species.
