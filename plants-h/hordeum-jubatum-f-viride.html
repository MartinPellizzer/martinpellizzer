Hordeum jubatum f. viride

Hordeum jubatum f. viride, commonly known as green wild barley, is a variant of the wild barley species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive green foliage, which sets it apart from the typical golden hue of the standard variety. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. It has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This form is often studied for its ecological role and potential in agricultural research.
