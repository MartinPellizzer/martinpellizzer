Hieracium murorum subsp. sagittiferum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sagittiferum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
