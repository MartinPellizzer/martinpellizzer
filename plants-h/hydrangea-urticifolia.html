Hydrangea urticifolia

Hydrangea urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that can cause a stinging sensation when touched, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Hydrangea urticifolia is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
