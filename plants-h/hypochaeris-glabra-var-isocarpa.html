Hypochaeris glabra var. isocarpa

Hypochaeris glabra var. isocarpa, commonly known as the smooth sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed dispersal.
