Hieracium baltarganum

Hieracium baltarganum, commonly known as the Baltic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

Hieracium baltarganum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
