Hieracium sartonsicum

Hieracium sartonsicum, commonly known as Sartors' hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Sartors' hawkweed is often found in moist environments and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
