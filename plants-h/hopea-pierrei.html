Hopea pierrei

Hopea pierrei, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hopea pierrei plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
