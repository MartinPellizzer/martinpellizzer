Hieracium pocuticum subsp. pocuticum

Hieracium pocuticum subsp.

pocuticum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to orange-yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It produces numerous small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies pocuticum is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to other Hieracium species.
