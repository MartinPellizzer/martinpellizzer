Hypericum fragile

Hypericum fragile, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Hypericaceae family, known for its medicinal properties.

Due to its fragility, it is often cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
