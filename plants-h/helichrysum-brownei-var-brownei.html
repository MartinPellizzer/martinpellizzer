Helichrysum brownei var. brownei

Helichrysum brownei var. brownei, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
