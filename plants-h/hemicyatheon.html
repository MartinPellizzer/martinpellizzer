Hemicyatheon

Hemicyatheon, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow fronds that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome system is well-developed, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Hemicyatheon is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
