Hieracium nigrescens subsp. seitaniemense

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

seitaniemense, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in regions with a temperate climate.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
