Hibiscus mutabilis var. roseoplenus

Hibiscus mutabilis var. roseoplenus, commonly known as the roseleaf hibiscus, is a variant of the common hibiscus characterized by its distinctive rose-pink foliage.

This plant features large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the stage of bloom. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a soft, velvety texture with a striking rose-pink hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hibiscus mutabilis var. roseoplenus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
