Hymenopogon oligocarpus

Hymenopogon oligocarpus, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Hymenopogon oligocarpus is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
