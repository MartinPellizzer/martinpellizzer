Hypericum albens

Hypericum albens, commonly known as white St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Hypericum albens is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported healing benefits.
