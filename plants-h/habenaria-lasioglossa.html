Habenaria lasioglossa

Habenaria lasioglossa, commonly known as the hairy-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces tall, erect stems with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is hairy, giving the species its scientific name.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in low-light conditions.
