Hibiscus hockii

Hibiscus hockii, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom during the summer months. Hibiscus hockii is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
