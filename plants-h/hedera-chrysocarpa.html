Hedera chrysocarpa

Hedera chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and is often used as a decorative climbing plant in gardens.

Hedera chrysocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or walls.
