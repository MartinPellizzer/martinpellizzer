Herminium quinquelobum

Herminium quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Herminium quinquelobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
