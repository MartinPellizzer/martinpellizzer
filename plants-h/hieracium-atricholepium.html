Hieracium atricholepium

Hieracium atricholepium, commonly known as the blackleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a dark center.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
