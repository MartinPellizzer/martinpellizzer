Hypericum mutilum var. boreale

Hypericum mutilum var. boreale, commonly known as the northern St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments. The plant features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh, cold climates.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
