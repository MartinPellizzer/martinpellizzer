Hedycarya dentata var. australasica

Hedycarya dentata var. australasica, commonly known as the Australian silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
