Hieracium laetevirens

Hieracium laetevirens, commonly known as the cheerful yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
