Heterotheca inuloides var. leptoglossa

Heterotheca inuloides var. leptoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and blue to violet, with narrow, tubular petals.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in arid gardens for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
