Hieracium magyaricum subsp. graecum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

graecum, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has finely divided leaves and produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
