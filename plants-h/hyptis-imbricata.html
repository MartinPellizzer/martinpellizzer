Hyptis imbricata

Hyptis imbricata, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and riverbanks.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for herbal remedies and ornamental gardening.
