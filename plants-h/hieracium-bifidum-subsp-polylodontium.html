Hieracium bifidum subsp. polylodontium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

polylodontium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy, with inflorescences of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to reddish ray florets. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it produces seeds in a schizocarp that splits into two parts upon maturity.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in moist or semi-shaded environments.
