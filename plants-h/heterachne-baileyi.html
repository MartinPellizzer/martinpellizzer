Heterachne baileyi

Heterachne baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's heterachne, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
