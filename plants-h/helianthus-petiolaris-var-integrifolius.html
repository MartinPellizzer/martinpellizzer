Helianthus petiolaris var. integrifolius

Helianthus petiolaris var. integrifolius, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often emerging from muddy or aquatic environments. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are broadly ovate with smooth, entire margins, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist soil and full sun, making it a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
