Hieracium inuloides subsp. striatum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

striatum, commonly known as the striped hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive striated pattern, giving it its common name.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
