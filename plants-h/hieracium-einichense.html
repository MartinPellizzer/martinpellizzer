Hieracium einichense

Hieracium einichense, commonly known as the Einichense hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
