Hermione subcrenata

Hermione subcrenata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hollyhock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that have a slightly wavy edge. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
