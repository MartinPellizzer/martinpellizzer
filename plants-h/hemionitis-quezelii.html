Hemionitis quezelii

Hemionitis quezelii, commonly known as the Quezel's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors, where it can access adequate moisture. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis quezelii is considered a species of conservation concern.
