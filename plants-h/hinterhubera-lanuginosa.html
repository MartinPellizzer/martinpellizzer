Hinterhubera lanuginosa

Hinterhubera lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Hinterhubera lanuginosa is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
