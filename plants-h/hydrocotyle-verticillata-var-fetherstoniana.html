Hydrocotyle verticillata var. fetherstoniana

Hydrocotyle verticillata var. fetherstoniana, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves.

Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy texture and often have a golden or yellowish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
