Hieracium dovrense subsp. atrichocephalum

Hieracium dovrense subsp.

atrichocephalum, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense, dark brown disk floret cluster, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and coloration, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
