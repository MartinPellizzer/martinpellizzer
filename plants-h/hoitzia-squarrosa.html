Hoitzia squarrosa

Hoitzia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hoitzia squarrosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
