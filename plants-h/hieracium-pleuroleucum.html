Hieracium pleuroleucum

Hieracium pleuroleucum, commonly known as the white-rayed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks in temperate regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium pleuroleucum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
