Hieracium cepenthum

Hieracium cepenthum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. It produces seeds through its composite flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.

Hieracium cepenthum is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
