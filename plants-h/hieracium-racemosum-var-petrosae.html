Hieracium racemosum var. petrosae

Hieracium racemosum var. petrosae, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a raceme, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
