Hieracium scabrum var. bipartitum

Hieracium scabrum var. bipartitum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
