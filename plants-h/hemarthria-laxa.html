Hemarthria laxa

Hemarthria laxa, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It is often found in riparian zones and can be an indicator species for wetland health.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
