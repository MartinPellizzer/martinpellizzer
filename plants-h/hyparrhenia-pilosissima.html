Hyparrhenia pilosissima

Hyparrhenia pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Hyparrhenia pilosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
