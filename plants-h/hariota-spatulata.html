Hariota spatulata

Hariota spatulata, commonly known as the broadleaf hariota, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Hariota spatulata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
