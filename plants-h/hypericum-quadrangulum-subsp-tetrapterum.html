Hypericum quadrangulum subsp. tetrapterum

Hypericum quadrangulum subsp.

tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-angled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its quadrangular, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include glandular dots on the stems and leaves, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.
