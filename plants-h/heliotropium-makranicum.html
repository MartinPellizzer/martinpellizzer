Heliotropium makranicum

Heliotropium makranicum, commonly known as the Makran heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Arabian Peninsula and parts of South Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and often have a pink to purple hue.

It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.
