Herminium oxysepalum

Herminium oxysepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose raceme and are notable for their elongated sepals.

Herminium oxysepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
