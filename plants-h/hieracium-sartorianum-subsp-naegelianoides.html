Hieracium sartorianum subsp. naegelianoides

Hieracium sartorianum subsp.

naegelianoides, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appearance.
