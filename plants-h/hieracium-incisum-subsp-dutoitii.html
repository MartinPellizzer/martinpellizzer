Hieracium incisum subsp. dutoitii

Hieracium incisum subsp.

dutoitii, commonly known as the Dutoit's Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head composed of ray florets, and it produces seeds through a specialized structure called a pappus.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in its native habitats.
