Helianthemum cinereum var. fasciculatum

Helianthemum cinereum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered sandverbena, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has grayish-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, adapted to withstand dry and sunny conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
