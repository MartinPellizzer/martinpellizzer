Hygrophila lindaviana

Hygrophila lindaviana, commonly known as the slender water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appeal and ability to oxygenate water.

Hygrophila lindaviana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
