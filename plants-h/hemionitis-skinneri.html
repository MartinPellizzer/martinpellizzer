Hemionitis skinneri

Hemionitis skinneri, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique plant native to arid regions of China and the Middle East.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and leathery, dark green fronds. The plant has a distinctive, shield-shaped rhizome that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. It produces tiny, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Hemionitis skinneri is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient and fascinating species of the botanical world.
