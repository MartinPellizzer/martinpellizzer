Haplostichanthus submontanus subsp. sessiliflorus

Haplostichanthus submontanus subsp.

sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flower bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
