Hieracium magyaricum subsp. chaunocymum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

chaunocymum, commonly known as Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
