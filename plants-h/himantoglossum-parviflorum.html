Himantoglossum parviflorum

Himantoglossum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a single, pale green or white flower with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in nutrient-rich soils. It produces a slender, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower with a three-lobed lip.

Himantoglossum parviflorum is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
