Hieracium bifidum subsp. submaculosum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

submaculosum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lobed and often have a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value but can also be invasive in certain regions.
