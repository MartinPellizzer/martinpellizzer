Hedysarum muricatum

Hedysarum muricatum, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and its distinctive, spiny stems and leaves. The plant is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hedysarum muricatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some regions for its unique appearance.
