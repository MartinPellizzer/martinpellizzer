Hieracium asperum

Hieracium asperum, commonly known as rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium asperum is known for its aggressive growth and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
