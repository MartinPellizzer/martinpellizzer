Hieracium lerodes

Hieracium lerodes, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Hieracium lerodes is typically herbaceous, with a short, fibrous root system.
