Hypericum lycopodioides

Hypericum lycopodioides, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
