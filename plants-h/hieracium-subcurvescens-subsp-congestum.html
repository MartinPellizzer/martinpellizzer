Hieracium subcurvescens subsp. congestum

Hieracium subcurvescens subsp.

congestum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and dense clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, composite flower heads that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
