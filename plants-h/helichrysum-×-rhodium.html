Helichrysum × rhodium

Helichrysum × rhodium, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its densely packed, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both landscape design and horticultural collections.
