Hieracium parvuliceps

Hieracium parvuliceps, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a single head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this perennial plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
