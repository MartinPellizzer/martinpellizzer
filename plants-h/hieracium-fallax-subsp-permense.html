Hieracium fallax subsp. permense

Hieracium fallax subsp.

permense, commonly known as the Permanent Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It has basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that bear yellow flowers.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
