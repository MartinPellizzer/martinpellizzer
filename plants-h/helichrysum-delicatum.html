Helichrysum delicatum

Helichrysum delicatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Helichrysum delicatum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
