Habenaria micheliana

Habenaria micheliana, commonly known as the Michel's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
