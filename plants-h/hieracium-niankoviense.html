Hieracium niankoviense

Hieracium niankoviense, commonly known as the Niankoli hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium niankoviense is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its potential medicinal uses in traditional practices.
