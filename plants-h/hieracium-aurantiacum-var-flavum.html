Hieracium aurantiacum var. flavum

Hieracium aurantiacum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers, which bloom from June to August, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
