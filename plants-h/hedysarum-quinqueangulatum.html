Hedysarum quinqueangulatum

Hedysarum quinqueangulatum, commonly known as the five-angled vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with five-angled leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its seed pods are linear and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Hedysarum quinqueangulatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability in ecological restoration.
