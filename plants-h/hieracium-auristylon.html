Hieracium auristylon

Hieracium auristylon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
