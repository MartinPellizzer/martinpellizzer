Hieracium subtenue

Hieracium subtenue, commonly known as slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium subtenue is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
