Hieracium fuernrohrii

Hieracium fuernrohrii, commonly known as the Fuernrohr hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow daisy-like flowers. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions due to habitat loss and fragmentation.
