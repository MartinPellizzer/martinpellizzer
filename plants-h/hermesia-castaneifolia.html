Hermesia castaneifolia

Hermesia castaneifolia, commonly known as the chestnut-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native habitat.
