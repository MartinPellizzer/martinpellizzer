Hieracium nigritellum

Hieracium nigritellum, commonly known as black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a dark central disc.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
