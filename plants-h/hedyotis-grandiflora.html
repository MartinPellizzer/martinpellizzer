Hedyotis grandiflora

Hedyotis grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower bluemink, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hedyotis grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
