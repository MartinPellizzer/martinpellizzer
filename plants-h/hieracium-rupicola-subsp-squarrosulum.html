Hieracium rupicola subsp. squarrosulum

Hieracium rupicola subsp.

squarrosulum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in limestone or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
