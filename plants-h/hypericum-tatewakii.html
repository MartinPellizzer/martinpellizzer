Hypericum tatewakii

Hypericum tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hypericum tatewakii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for garden cultivation.
