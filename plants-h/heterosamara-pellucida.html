Heterosamara pellucida

Heterosamara pellucida, commonly known as the glassy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, translucent bladders that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still, nutrient-poor waters, using its specialized structures to obtain essential nutrients. Its leaves are typically thin and branched, with the bladders attached to the stems.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling microorganism populations.
