Hieracium vulgatum subsp. maculatiforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

maculatiforme, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish-green composite flowers.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive pattern of dark green spots, which give the plant its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.
