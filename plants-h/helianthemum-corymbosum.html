Helianthemum corymbosum

Helianthemum corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
