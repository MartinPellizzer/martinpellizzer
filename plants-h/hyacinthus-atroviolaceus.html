Hyacinthus atroviolaceus

Hyacinthus atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and unique coloration.
