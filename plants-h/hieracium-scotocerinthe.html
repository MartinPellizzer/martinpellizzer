Hieracium scotocerinthe

Hieracium scotocerinthe, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a dark central disc.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in temperate climates.
