Hackelia stricta

Hackelia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the upper surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Hackelia stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
