Hazunta membranacea f. pilifera

Hazunta membranacea f. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy leafed hazunta, is a variant of the hazunta plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. Its leaves are broad and have a velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow and green.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
