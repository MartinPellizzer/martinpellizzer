Hypericum humifusum var. radicans

Hypericum humifusum var. radicans, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
