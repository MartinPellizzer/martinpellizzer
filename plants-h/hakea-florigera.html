Hakea florigera

Hakea florigera, commonly known as the Christmas hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce vibrant red flowers, which bloom during the winter months. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, providing a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Hakea florigera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
