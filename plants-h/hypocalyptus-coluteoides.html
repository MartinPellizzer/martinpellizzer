Hypocalyptus coluteoides

Hypocalyptus coluteoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into slender pods. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to dry environments.

Hypocalyptus coluteoides is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some parts of its range.
