Hieracium alatum subsp. tectosagum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

tectosagum, commonly known as the winged hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features a rosette of basal, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its stems are often winged, giving it the common name "winged hawkweed.".

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
