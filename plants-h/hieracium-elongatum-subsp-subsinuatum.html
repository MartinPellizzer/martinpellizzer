Hieracium elongatum subsp. subsinuatum

Hieracium elongatum subsp.

subsinuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often recurved, and its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive disc of tubular florets. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely serrated leaf margins and a more slender stem compared to other hawkweed species.
