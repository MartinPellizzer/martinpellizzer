Hymenorebutia chrysantha

Hymenorebutia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
