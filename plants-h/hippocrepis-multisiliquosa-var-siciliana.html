Hippocrepis multisiliquosa var. siciliana

Hippocrepis multisiliquosa var. siciliana, commonly known as the Sicilian Harebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Italy.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
