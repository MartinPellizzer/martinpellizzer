Hieracium integrifolium subsp. subvulsum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

subvulsum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long, slender peduncles.

It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.
