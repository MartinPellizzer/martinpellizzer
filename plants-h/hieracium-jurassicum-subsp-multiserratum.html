Hieracium jurassicum subsp. multiserratum

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

multiserratum, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
