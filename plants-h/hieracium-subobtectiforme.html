Hieracium subobtectiforme

Hieracium subobtectiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
