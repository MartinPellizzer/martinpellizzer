Hasseltiopsis albomicans

Hasseltiopsis albomicans, commonly known as the white-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense, white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical forest environments.
