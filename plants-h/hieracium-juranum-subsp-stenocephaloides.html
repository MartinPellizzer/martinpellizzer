Hieracium juranum subsp. stenocephaloides

Hieracium juranum subsp.

stenocephaloides, commonly known as the narrow-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
