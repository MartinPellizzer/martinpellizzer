Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. cynanchoides

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

cynanchoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are usually clustered in a single inflorescence at the top of the plant.

This subspecies is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
