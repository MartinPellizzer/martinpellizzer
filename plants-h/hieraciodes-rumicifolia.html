Hieraciodes rumicifolia

Hieraciodes rumicifolia, commonly known as the rock holly or rock lettuce, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique texture and visual appeal.
