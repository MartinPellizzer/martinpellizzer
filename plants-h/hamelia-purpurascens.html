Hamelia purpurascens

Hamelia purpurascens, commonly known as the firebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in late summer and persist into autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries.

Hamelia purpurascens is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
