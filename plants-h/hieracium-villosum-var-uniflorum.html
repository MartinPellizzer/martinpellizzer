Hieracium villosum var. uniflorum

Hieracium villosum var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. The plant features a hairy stem and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the single, showy yellow flower that appears at the end of the stem.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.
