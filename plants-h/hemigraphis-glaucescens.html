Hemigraphis glaucescens

Hemigraphis glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in partial shade to bright indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Hemigraphis glaucescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
