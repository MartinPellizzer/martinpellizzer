Hamelia manglillo

Hamelia manglillo, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hamelia manglillo is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm climates.
