Homalium erianthum

Homalium erianthum, commonly known as the velvetleaf holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
