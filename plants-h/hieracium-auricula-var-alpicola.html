Hieracium auricula var. alpicola

Hieracium auricula var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base, which gives it its species name.

This variety is often found at high altitudes and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
