Hyptis brevipes var. robusta

Hyptis brevipes var. robusta, commonly known as the rough hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is valued both for its ecological role and its potential in herbal applications.
