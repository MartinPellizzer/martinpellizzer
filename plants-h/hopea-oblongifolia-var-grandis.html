Hopea oblongifolia var. grandis

Hopea oblongifolia var. grandis, commonly known as the large-leaf hopea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also of ecological importance in its native habitats.
