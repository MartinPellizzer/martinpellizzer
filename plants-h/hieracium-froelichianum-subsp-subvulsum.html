Hieracium froelichianum subsp. subvulsum

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

subvulsum, commonly known as the lesser common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in open habitats and can spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.
