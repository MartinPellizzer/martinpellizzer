Helictonia

Helictonia, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its intricate, curling leaves that resemble a spiral pattern.

It belongs to the orchid family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Helictonia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its exotic appearance.
