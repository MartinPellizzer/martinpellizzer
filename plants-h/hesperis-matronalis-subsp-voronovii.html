Hesperis matronalis subsp. voronovii

Hesperis matronalis subsp.

voronovii, commonly known as the Voronov's sweet rocket, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
