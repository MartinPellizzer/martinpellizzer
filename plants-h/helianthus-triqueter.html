Helianthus triqueter

Helianthus triqueter, commonly known as the three-angled sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces small, three-angled fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus triqueter is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
