Haplophyllum virgatum

Haplophyllum virgatum, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that has a distinctive silver-gray hue on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that attract wildlife. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture.

Haplophyllum virgatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in partially shaded environments.
