Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides var. excurrens

Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides var. excurrens, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that resemble small chrysanthemums, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens and xeriscapes.
