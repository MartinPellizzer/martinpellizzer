Hieracium thapsiformoides subsp. pseudorieni

Hieracium thapsiformoides subsp.

pseudorieni, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering period compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
