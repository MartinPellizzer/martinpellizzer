Homalomena puncticulosa

Homalomena puncticulosa, commonly known as the spotted arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces bright green, heart-shaped leaves that are often speckled with small, dark spots, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

The flowers, which are typically yellow or green, are enclosed within a spathe and are followed by bright red berries.
