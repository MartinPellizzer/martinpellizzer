Hieracium ljapinense

Hieracium ljapinense, commonly known as the Ljapin's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow ray florets that surround a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in meadows and open areas, often in temperate regions of Europe.

Hieracium ljapinense is noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
