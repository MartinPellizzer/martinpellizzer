Hypericum wightianum

Hypericum wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include the presence of yellow-orange sap, which contains various bioactive compounds.

Hypericum wightianum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
