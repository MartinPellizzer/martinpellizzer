Hieracium subcanescens subsp. langenense

Hieracium subcanescens subsp.

langenense, commonly known as the Lagenense Hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head, with phyllaries that are often glandular and hairy. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
