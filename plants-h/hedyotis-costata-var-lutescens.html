Hedyotis costata var. lutescens

Hedyotis costata var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow garden bell, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
