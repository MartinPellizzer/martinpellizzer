Halimium alyssoides var. incanum

Halimium alyssoides var. incanum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
