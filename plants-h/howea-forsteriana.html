Howea forsteriana

Howea forsteriana, commonly known as the palm tree, is a species native to the island of Tonga in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red fruits containing edible seeds. Howea forsteriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm, humid regions.
