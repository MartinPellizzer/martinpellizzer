Hieracium brevifolium subsp. virgaureiforme

Hieracium brevifolium subsp.

virgaureiforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower morphology compared to other members of the species.
