Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. cinereiceps

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

cinereiceps, commonly known as the silver-hair hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-tinged stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
