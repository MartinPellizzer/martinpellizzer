Hieracium pseudalpinum

Hieracium pseudalpinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets and is often found in open, sunny environments.

Hieracium pseudalpinum is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
