Hesperothamnus purpusii

Hesperothamnus purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus' spiny shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Hesperothamnus purpusii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
