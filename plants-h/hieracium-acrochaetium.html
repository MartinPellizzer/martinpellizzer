Hieracium acrochaetium

Hieracium acrochaetium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
