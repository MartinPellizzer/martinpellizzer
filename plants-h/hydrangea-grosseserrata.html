Hydrangea grosseserrata

Hydrangea grosseserrata, commonly known as the roughleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features serrated, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hydrangea grosseserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
