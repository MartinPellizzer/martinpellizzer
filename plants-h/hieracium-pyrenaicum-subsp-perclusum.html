Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp. perclusum

Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp.

perclusum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in high-altitude environments where competition from other plants is minimal.
