Haplopappus lucidulus

Haplopappus lucidulus, commonly known as the smooth pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by papery bracts that aid in seed dispersal.
