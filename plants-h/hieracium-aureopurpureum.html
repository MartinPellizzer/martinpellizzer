Hieracium aureopurpureum

Hieracium aureopurpureum, commonly known as the golden-purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, often growing in meadows and grassy areas. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with some varieties displaying purple hues, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Hieracium aureopurpureum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
