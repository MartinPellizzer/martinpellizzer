Hieracium sylvaticum grex heteroschistum

Hieracium sylvaticum grex heteroschistum, commonly known as the forest dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and meadows, thriving in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are often arranged in a basal rosette and have a distinctive, rough texture.

The species is noted for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.
