Holocarpha virgata subsp. virgata

Holocarpha virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
