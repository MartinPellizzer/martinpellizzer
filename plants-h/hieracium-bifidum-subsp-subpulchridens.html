Hieracium bifidum subsp. subpulchridens

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subpulchridens, commonly known as the beautiful hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc of tubular florets.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it can be invasive in some regions.
