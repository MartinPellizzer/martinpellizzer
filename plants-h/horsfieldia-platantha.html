Horsfieldia platantha

Horsfieldia platantha, commonly known as the white orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. This species is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include long, slender leaves and a unique floral structure that aids in pollination by insects.
