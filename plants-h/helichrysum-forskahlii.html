Helichrysum forskahlii

Helichrysum forskahlii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden-orange blooms that resemble small spheres. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers make it a favored plant in both ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.
