Hubera multistamina

Hubera multistamina, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stem and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hubera multistamina is also known for its drought tolerance and ease of maintenance in gardens.
