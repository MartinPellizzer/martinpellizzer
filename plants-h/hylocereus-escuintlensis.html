Hylocereus escuintlensis

Hylocereus escuintlensis, commonly known as the Escuintla night blooming cactus, is a species native to Central America.

It is a climbing cactus with fleshy, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

The fruit is a small, spiny berry that contains sweet, edible pulp.
