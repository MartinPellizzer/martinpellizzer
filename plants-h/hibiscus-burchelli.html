Hibiscus burchelli

Hibiscus burchelli, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a rough texture with a serrated edge.

Hibiscus burchelli thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverine habitats.
