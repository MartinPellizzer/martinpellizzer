Hordeum vulgare var. glabrideficiens

Hordeum vulgare var. glabrideficiens, commonly known as the smooth barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its reduced glume hair.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, cylindrical stems and spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are small, sessile, and clustered in panicles.

This variety is notable for its agricultural significance and is often used in breeding programs for improved yield and adaptability.
