Hypericum maculatum var. imperforatum

Hypericum maculatum var. imperforatum, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive purple or red discoloration. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in native gardens.
