Hibiscus denudatus

Hibiscus denudatus, commonly known as the naked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. Hibiscus denudatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
