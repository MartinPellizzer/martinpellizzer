Hollandaea riparia

Hollandaea riparia, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Hollandaea riparia is a perennial vine that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
