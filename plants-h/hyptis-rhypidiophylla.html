Hyptis rhypidiophylla

Hyptis rhypidiophylla, commonly known as the blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and fragrant qualities.

Hyptis rhypidiophylla is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
