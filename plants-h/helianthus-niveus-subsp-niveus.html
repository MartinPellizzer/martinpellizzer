Helianthus niveus subsp. niveus

Helianthus niveus subsp.

niveus, commonly known as the white-gold sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by white ray florets, which give it its distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
