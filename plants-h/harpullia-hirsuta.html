Harpullia hirsuta

Harpullia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent yellow center.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
