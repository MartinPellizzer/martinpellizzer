Hypolytrum imeriense

Hypolytrum imeriense, commonly known as the Imeriense grass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Imeri in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
