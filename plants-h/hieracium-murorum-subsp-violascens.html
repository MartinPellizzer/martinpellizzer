Hieracium murorum subsp. violascens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

violascens, commonly known as the violet-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, appressed hairs, giving them a velvety appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
