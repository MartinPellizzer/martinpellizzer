Helianthemum serpillifolium

Helianthemum serpillifolium, commonly known as the rock rose or thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Helianthemum serpillifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
