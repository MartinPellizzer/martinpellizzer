Helianthemum oelandicum var. thessalum

Helianthemum oelandicum var. thessalum, commonly known as the Thessalian sand thyme, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
