Hieracium jankolympicum

Hieracium jankolympicum, commonly known as the Jankolympic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It features yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are generally basal with a rough texture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
