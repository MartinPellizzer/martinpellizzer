Hieracium cerussatum

Hieracium cerussatum, commonly known as the leadplant or leadweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium cerussatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
