Helopus villosus

Helopus villosus, commonly known as the hairy helopus, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Helopus villosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
