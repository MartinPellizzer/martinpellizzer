Hieracium rectum subsp. myriophyllum

Hieracium rectum subsp.

myriophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange flowers.

The subspecies myriophyllum is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and clustered flower heads. It typically thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
