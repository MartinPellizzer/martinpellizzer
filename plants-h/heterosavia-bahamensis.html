Heterosavia bahamensis

Heterosavia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsules that release seeds when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
