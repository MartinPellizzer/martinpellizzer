Hieracium magyaricum subsp. pseudobauhini

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

pseudobauhini, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and geographic distribution within its native range.
