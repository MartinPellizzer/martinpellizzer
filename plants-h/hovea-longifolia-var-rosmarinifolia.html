Hovea longifolia var. rosmarinifolia

Hovea longifolia var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
