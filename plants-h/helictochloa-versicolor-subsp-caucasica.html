Helictochloa versicolor subsp. caucasica

Helictochloa versicolor subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves exhibit a range of colors, from green to bluish-gray, depending on environmental conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its morphological variability and ecological adaptability.
