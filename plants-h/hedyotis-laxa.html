Hedyotis laxa

Hedyotis laxa, commonly known as the slender dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense racemes and are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals.

Hedyotis laxa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
