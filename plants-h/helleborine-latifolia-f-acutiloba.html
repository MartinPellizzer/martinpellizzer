Helleborine latifolia f. acutiloba

Helleborine latifolia f. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves that are narrow and pointed, giving it its specific epithet "acutiloba.". The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands and undergrowth.

This orchid is known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations, which aid in nutrient uptake from the soil.
