Huperzia sieboldii var. christenseniana

Huperzia sieboldii var. christenseniana, commonly known as the Christensen's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in two ranks. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called sporangium clusters, which are clustered on the undersides of the leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories across its native range in East Asia.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique leaf morphology compared to other members of the species.
