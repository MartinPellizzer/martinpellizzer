Heliconia cristata

Heliconia cristata, commonly known as the crown heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with bright yellow or orange flowers surrounded by long, strap-like leaves. Its leaves are broad and waxy, often with a slight curl, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Heliconia cristata thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly found in rainforests and coastal areas.
