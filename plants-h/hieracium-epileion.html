Hieracium epileion

Hieracium epileion, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.
