Hyparrhenia subplumosa

Hyparrhenia subplumosa, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silky-textured leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the leaf blades. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Hyparrhenia subplumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
