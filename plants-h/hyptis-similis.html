Hyptis similis

Hyptis similis, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
