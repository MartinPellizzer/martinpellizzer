Hepatica asiatica f. variegata

Hepatica asiatica f. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Asian Hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with a striking variegation pattern of white, green, and sometimes pink. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique foliage and early flowering habit, making it a valued addition to botanical gardens and woodland landscapes.
