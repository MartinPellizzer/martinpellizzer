Hiraea obovata

Hiraea obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate hiraea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It is native to certain regions of Africa and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hiraea obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
