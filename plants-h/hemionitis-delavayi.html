Hemionitis delavayi

Hemionitis delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's hemionitis, is a rare and unusual fern species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a creeping rhizome and small, clustered fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its bright green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and ecologically significant species.
