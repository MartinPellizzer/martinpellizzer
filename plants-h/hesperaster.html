Hesperaster

Hesperaster, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its striking appearance. Hesperaster has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
