Hakea pulvinifera

Hakea pulvinifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of whitish hair. The plant produces striking cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea pulvinifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral displays.
