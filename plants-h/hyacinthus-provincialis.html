Hyacinthus provincialis

Hyacinthus provincialis, commonly known as the Cape hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant purple or white flowers that attract pollinators. Its bulbs are adapted to survive dry periods, making it a hardy species suited to Mediterranean climates.

The leaves are strap-like and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
