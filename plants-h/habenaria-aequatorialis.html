Habenaria aequatorialis

Habenaria aequatorialis, commonly known as the Equatorial Habenaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more prominent and adorned with intricate markings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
