Hydrangea albostellata

Hydrangea albostellata, commonly known as the white star hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of sterile white flowers surrounded by smaller, showy pink or white petals. The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage is typically green, turning red or orange in the fall.

Hydrangea albostellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
