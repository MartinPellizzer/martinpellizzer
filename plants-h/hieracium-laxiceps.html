Hieracium laxiceps

Hieracium laxiceps, commonly known as the loosehead hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and bloom from June to September, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.
