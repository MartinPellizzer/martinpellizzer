Hookera synandra

Hookera synandra, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Hookera synandra has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in high-altitude ecosystems.
