Hieracium prenanthoides var. parvifolium

Hieracium prenanthoides var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate regions.

The variety parvifolium is distinguished by its smaller leaves compared to other varieties within the species.
