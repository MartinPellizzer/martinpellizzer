Hieracium racemosum subsp. subartvinense

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

subartvinense, commonly known as the Subartvin hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small yellow flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
