Hoodia rosea

Hoodia rosea, commonly known as the red hoodia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically small and sparse, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Hoodia rosea is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
