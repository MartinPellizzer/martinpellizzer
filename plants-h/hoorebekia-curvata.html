Hoorebekia curvata

Hoorebekia curvata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hoorebekia curvata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
