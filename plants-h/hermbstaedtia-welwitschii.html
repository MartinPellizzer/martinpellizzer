Hermbstaedtia welwitschii

Hermbstaedtia welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia plant, is a unique succulent native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a woody stem that can reach several meters in height, often forming a bushy structure. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season.

Hermbstaedtia welwitschii is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stem to survive prolonged droughts.
