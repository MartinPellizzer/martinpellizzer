Hedysarum kemulariae

Hedysarum kemulariae, commonly known as the Kemularia milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, round seeds.

Hedysarum kemulariae plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
