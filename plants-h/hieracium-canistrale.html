Hieracium canistrale

Hieracium canistrale, commonly known as the Canary Island hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has slender, erect stems with hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium canistrale is known for its ability to thrive in coastal environments and is often found in dry, nutrient-poor soils.
