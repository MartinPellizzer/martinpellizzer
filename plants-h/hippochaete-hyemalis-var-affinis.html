Hippochaete hyemalis var. affinis

Hippochaete hyemalis var. affinis, commonly known as the winter chamaedorea, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
