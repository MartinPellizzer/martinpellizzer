Huernia keniensis

Huernia keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan moon cactus, is a succulent native to Kenya.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its unique, swollen stem segments that resemble a cactus. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Huernia keniensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
