Hieracium brevifolium subsp. brevifrons

Hieracium brevifolium subsp.

brevifrons, commonly known as the short-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

The plant has small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads and a variety of leaf shapes.

This subspecies is distinguished by its short, broad leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
