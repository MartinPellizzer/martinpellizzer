Helianthus × cinereus

Helianthus × cinereus, commonly known as the grayhead sunflower, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring a sturdy stem and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus × cinereus is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
