Hyalis gracilis

Hyalis gracilis, commonly known as the slender hyalis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Hyalis gracilis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
