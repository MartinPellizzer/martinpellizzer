Helichrysum algarbiensis

Helichrysum algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algerian immortelle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small sunflowers, which are often used in dried flower arrangements. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Helichrysum algarbiensis is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
