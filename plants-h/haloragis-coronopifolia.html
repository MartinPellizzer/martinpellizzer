Haloragis coronopifolia

Haloragis coronopifolia, commonly known as the crowned glassworts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. It has a rosette growth form, with leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Haloragis coronopifolia is also known for its ornamental value, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
