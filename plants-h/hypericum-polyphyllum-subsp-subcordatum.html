Hypericum polyphyllum subsp. subcordatum

Hypericum polyphyllum subsp.

subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its narrow, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
