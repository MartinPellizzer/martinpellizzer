Heterophragma chelonoides

Heterophragma chelonoides, commonly known as the helmet fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.
