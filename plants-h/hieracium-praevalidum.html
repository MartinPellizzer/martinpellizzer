Hieracium praevalidum

Hieracium praevalidum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium praevalidum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
