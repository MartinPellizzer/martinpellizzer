Heuchera cusickii

Heuchera cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves that range in color from deep purple to green and bronze. The plant produces upright spikes of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Heuchera cusickii is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
