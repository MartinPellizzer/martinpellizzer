Hemianthus adenander

Hemianthus adenander, commonly known as the African dwarf grass, is a small, mat-forming plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its fine texture.

Hemianthus adenander is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.
