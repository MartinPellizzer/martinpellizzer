Helianthus tenellus

Helianthus tenellus, commonly known as the shortleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Helianthus tenellus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
