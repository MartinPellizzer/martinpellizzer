Hibiscus articulatus

Hibiscus articulatus, commonly known as the articulated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically have five to seven petals and are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a rough or hairy texture.

Hibiscus articulatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.
