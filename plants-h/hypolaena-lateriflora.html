Hypolaena lateriflora

Hypolaena lateriflora, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, with lanceolate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are often pink or white. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hypolaena lateriflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
