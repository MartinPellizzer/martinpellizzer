Hyptis frondosa

Hyptis frondosa, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it a valuable component of its native ecosystems.
