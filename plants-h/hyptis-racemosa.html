Hyptis racemosa

Hyptis racemosa, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Hyptis racemosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
