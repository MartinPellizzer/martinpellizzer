Hyptis mattogrossensis

Hyptis mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazon hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the wet season.

Hyptis mattogrossensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and riverbanks.
