Hieracium pallidum subsp. brachetianum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

brachetianum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions, though it can be invasive in some areas.
