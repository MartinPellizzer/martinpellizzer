Hedeoma sancta

Hedeoma sancta, commonly known as sacred hedeoma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Hedeoma sancta is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported healing benefits.
