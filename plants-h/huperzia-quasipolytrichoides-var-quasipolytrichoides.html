Huperzia quasipolytrichoides var. quasipolytrichoides

Huperzia quasipolytrichoides var. quasipolytrichoides, commonly known as the Quasipolytrichoides huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and lanceolate, often serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores in clusters, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
