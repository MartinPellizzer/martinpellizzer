Hoheria populnea subsp. obtusifolia

Hoheria populnea subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hollyhock, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-grey pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and inland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
