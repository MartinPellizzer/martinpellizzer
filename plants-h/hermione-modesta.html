Hermione modesta

Hermione modesta, commonly known as the modest orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
