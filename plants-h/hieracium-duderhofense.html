Hieracium duderhofense

Hieracium duderhofense, commonly known as the Duderhof's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
