Harpochloa pseudoharpechloa

Harpochloa pseudoharpechloa, commonly known as the false harpechloa, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a C4 photosynthetic pathway, which allows it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is of interest to botanists studying grassland ecosystems.
