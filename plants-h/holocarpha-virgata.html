Holocarpha virgata

Holocarpha virgata, commonly known as desert star or silverleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. The plant features silver-gray foliage and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spiny, spherical capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Holocarpha virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
