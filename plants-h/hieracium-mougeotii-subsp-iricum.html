Hieracium mougeotii subsp. iricum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

iricum, commonly known as the Irkutsk hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves and produces numerous ray flowers that are often shorter than the central disk flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its geographic distribution and ecological adaptations.
