Henckelia monophylla

Henckelia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf tree, is a unique flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its singular, large, palmate leaf that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in high-altitude, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.
