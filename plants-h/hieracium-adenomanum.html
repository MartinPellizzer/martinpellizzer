Hieracium adenomanum

Hieracium adenomanum, commonly known as the sticky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
