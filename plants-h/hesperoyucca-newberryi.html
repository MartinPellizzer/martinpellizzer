Hesperoyucca newberryi

Hesperoyucca newberryi, commonly known as Newberry's yucca, is a distinctive plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features tall, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its striking white flower clusters appear in late spring or early summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Hesperoyucca newberryi is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
