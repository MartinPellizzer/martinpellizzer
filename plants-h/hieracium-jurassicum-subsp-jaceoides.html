Hieracium jurassicum subsp. jaceoides

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

jaceoides, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
