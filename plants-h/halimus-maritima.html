Halimus maritima

Halimus maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its salt-tolerant nature. The plant features silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces striking blue or purple flowers. It has small, spiny fruit capsules that contain seeds.

Halimus maritima is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
