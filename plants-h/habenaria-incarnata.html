Habenaria incarnata

Habenaria incarnata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green to reddish-brown stems and its long, spiraling inflorescence of pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves.

Habenaria incarnata is often found in association with other orchids and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
