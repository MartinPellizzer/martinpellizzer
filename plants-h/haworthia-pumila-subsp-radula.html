Haworthia pumila subsp. radula

Haworthia pumila subsp.

radula, commonly known as the dwarf Haworthia or the mouse-eared cactus, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa. It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale markings, forming a rosette shape.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
