Hypericum coris

Hypericum coris, commonly known as the yellow wood or St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hypericum coris is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
