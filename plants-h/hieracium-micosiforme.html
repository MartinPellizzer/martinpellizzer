Hieracium micosiforme

Hieracium micosiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
