Hieracium radicaule

Hieracium radicaule, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attracts various pollinators.

Hieracium radicaule is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
