Hieracium sciadophorum subsp. violaceipes

Hieracium sciadophorum subsp.

violaceipes, commonly known as the violet-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a violet or purple hue, distinguishing it from other hawkweed species.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.
