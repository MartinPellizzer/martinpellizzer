Hieracium subramosum subsp. spurcaticeps

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

spurcaticeps, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
