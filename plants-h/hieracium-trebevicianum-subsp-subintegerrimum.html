Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. subintegerrimum

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

subintegerrimum, commonly known as the Terebinth-leaved Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a single terminal head, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the disk florets.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in association with other members of the Asteraceae family.
