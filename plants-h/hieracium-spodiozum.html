Hieracium spodiozum

Hieracium spodiozum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, branched flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium spodiozum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
