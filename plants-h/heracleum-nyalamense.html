Heracleum nyalamense

Heracleum nyalamense, commonly known as Nyalam cowparnial, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India, thriving in temperate and alpine environments. The plant features tall, hollow stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are large, pinnately compound with serrated margins. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters.

Heracleum nyalamense is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
