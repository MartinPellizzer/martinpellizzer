Hibiscus sagittifolius var. quinquelobus

Hibiscus sagittifolius var. quinquelobus, commonly known as the five-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have five distinct lobes, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
