Hieracium alatum subsp. nouletii

Hieracium alatum subsp.

nouletii, commonly known as the winged hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
