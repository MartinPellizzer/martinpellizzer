Hermesias racemosa

Hermesias racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Hermesias racemosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
