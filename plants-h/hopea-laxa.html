Hopea laxa

Hopea laxa, commonly known as the slender talaud.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Hopea laxa is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also studied for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
