Humularia magnistipulata

Humularia magnistipulata, commonly known as the giant-stippled fern, is a unique and striking plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are intricately divided into numerous small, finger-like segments. The plant's fronds are often covered in a distinctive stippling pattern, which gives it its common name. Humularia magnistipulata thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
