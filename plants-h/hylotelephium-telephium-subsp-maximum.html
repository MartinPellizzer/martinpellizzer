Hylotelephium telephium subsp. maximum

Hylotelephium telephium subsp.

maximum, commonly known as the Armenian carpet or tall stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to the Caucasus region. It features fleshy, gray-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain water in arid environments.

The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
