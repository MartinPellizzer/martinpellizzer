Hieracium alatum subsp. foucaudianum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

foucaudianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive winged appearance on the upper surface.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, contributing to its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
