Hirtella pilosissima

Hirtella pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, often found in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink and are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.
