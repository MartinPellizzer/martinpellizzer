Humata pulcherrima

Humata pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in suitable environments.
