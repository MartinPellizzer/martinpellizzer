Hydrangea buergeri

Hydrangea buergeri, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is dark green and turns red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Hydrangea buergeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
