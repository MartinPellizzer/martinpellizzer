Helianthemum origanifolium subvar. dichotomum

Helianthemum origanifolium subvar. dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with a distinctive, aromatic scent, and its leaves are often divided into two parts, giving it its subspecies name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
