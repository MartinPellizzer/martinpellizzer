Hedysarum sangilense

Hedysarum sangilense, commonly known as the Sangil sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Hedysarum sangilense is valued both for its ecological role and its ornamental appeal.
