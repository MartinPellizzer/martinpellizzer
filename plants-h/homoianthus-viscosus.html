Homoianthus viscosus

Homoianthus viscosus, commonly known as the sticky milkbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sticky, resinous sap, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Homoianthus viscosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
