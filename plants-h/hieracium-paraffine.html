Hieracium paraffine

Hieracium paraffine, commonly known as the paraffin hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange composite flowers and hairy, rough stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium paraffine is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
