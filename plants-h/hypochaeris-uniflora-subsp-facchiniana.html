Hypochaeris uniflora subsp. facchiniana

Hypochaeris uniflora subsp.

facchiniana, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers with numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
