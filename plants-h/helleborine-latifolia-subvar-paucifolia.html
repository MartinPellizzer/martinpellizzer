Helleborine latifolia subvar. paucifolia

Helleborine latifolia subvar. paucifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers with a distinct lip, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is often found growing on the ground or on rocks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
