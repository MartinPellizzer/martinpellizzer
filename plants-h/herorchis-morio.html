Herorchis morio

Herorchis morio, commonly known as the yellow flag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. The plant has a tall, erect stem with several whorls of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated spike of blossoms.

The species is notable for its striking color and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
