Hesperis rigida

Hesperis rigida, commonly known as rigid honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Hesperis rigida is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
