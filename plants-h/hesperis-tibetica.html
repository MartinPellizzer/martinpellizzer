Hesperis tibetica

Hesperis tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan Hesperis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar garden plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in cool, dry climates and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.

Hesperis tibetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in the unique flora of high-altitude ecosystems.
