Hookera hyacinthina var. lactea

Hookera hyacinthina var. lactea, commonly known as the white-flowered hookera, is a striking alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce showy, white to pale blue flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
