Hibiscus lindleyi

Hibiscus lindleyi, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus or the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Hibiscus lindleyi thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
