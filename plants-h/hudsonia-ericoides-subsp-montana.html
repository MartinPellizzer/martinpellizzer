Hudsonia ericoides subsp. montana

Hudsonia ericoides subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the spring.

It is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often used in native plant landscaping.
