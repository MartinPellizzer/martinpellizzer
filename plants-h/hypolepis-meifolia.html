Hypolepis meifolia

Hypolepis meifolia, commonly known as the golden sword fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and dark green fronds with a yellowish or golden hue on the underside. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new shoots and roots. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with closely spaced, lance-shaped pinnae.

Hypolepis meifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
