Heliconia burleana

Heliconia burleana, commonly known as the Burleana heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and the plant is well adapted to humid, shaded environments.
