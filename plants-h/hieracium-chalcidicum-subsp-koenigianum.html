Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. koenigianum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp.

koenigianum, commonly known as the Koenig's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowering heads.

The plant typically features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more narrow. It produces small, dandelion-like flowers that are often clustered in a single head, and it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
