Hydrangea opuloides var. stellata

Hydrangea opuloides var. stellata, commonly known as the star hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive cone-shaped flower clusters, which are typically white or pink in color. The plant produces large, showy bracts that surround small, true flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the garden.
