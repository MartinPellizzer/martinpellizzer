Hamamelis virginiana var. virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana var. virginiana, commonly known as the common witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. Its woody stems are often covered in a thick, corky layer that provides structural support.

Witch-hazel is also known for its medicinal properties, with extracts used in skincare and healing products.
