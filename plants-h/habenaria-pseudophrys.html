Habenaria pseudophrys

Habenaria pseudophrys, commonly known as the showy marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers. The plant has a single, erect stem that bears several flowers arranged in a raceme. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
