Hypericum caucasicum var. wittmannianum

Hypericum caucasicum var. wittmannianum, commonly known as the Caucasian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is less commonly used today compared to other hypericum species.
