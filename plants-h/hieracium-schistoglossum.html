Hieracium schistoglossum

Hieracium schistoglossum, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained and sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, often forming dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Hieracium schistoglossum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging alpine conditions.
