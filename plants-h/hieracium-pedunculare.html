Hieracium pedunculare

Hieracium pedunculare, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small seeds in its flower heads.

Hieracium pedunculare is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
