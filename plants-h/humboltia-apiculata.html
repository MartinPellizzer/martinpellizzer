Humboltia apiculata

Humboltia apiculata, commonly known as the California thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thimble-shaped fruits that resemble miniature berries. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are white and clustered in cymes.

Humboltia apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
