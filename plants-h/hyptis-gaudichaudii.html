Hyptis gaudichaudii

Hyptis gaudichaudii, commonly known as the blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis gaudichaudii is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
