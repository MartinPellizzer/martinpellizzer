Hieracium transylvanicum

Hieracium transylvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers appear in late summer, and it is often found in areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Hieracium transylvanicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
