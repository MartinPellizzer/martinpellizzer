Hieracium laevigatum subsp. macrotonoides

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

macrotonoides, commonly known as the large-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, flat head.

It is a common weed in agricultural fields and is known for its rapid spread through rhizomes and seed dispersal.
