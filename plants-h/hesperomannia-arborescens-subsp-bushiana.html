Hesperomannia arborescens subsp. bushiana

Hesperomannia arborescens subsp.

bushiana, commonly known as the Bush's tree heath, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and coastal scrub. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to the Mediterranean climate of its habitat.
