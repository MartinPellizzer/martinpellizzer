Helianthemum croceum subsp. croceum

Helianthemum croceum subsp.

croceum, commonly known as the yellow rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species well adapted to Mediterranean climates.
