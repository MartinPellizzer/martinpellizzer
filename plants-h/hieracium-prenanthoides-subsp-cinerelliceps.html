Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. cinerelliceps

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

cinerelliceps, commonly known as the cinerella hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
