Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp. subsvaneticum

Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp.

subsvaneticum, commonly known as the subsvanetic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sunny habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flower heads are surrounded by pale green bracts.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
