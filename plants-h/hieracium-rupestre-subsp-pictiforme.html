Hieracium rupestre subsp. pictiforme

Hieracium rupestre subsp.

pictiforme, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its subspecific name, *pictiforme*, refers to the distinctive, almost painted appearance of its leaves.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
