Heliotropium strigosum var. bicolor

Heliotropium strigosum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a contrasting white or yellow base. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with thin, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
