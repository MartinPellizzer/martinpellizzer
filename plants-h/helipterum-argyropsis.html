Helipterum argyropsis

Helipterum argyropsis, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Helipterum argyropsis is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
