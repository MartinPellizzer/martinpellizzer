Holstianthus barbigularis

Holstianthus barbigularis, commonly known as the bearded hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers have prominent stamens with long, hairy filaments, giving it the "bearded" appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
