Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. potamogetifolium

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

potamogetifolium, commonly known as waterleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and wet grasslands, thriving in damp environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower head surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

The species is often found in calcareous soils and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
