Hebe rigidula var. sulcata

Hebe rigidula var. sulcata, commonly known as the Canterbury knapweed, is a small shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, upright stems and small, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
