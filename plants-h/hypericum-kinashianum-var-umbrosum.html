Hypericum kinashianum var. umbrosum

Hypericum kinashianum var. umbrosum, commonly known as the shadow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, glossy foliage that thrives in shaded or semi-shaded conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural habitat. It is often found in rocky or forested areas where it forms dense mats or low shrubs.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
