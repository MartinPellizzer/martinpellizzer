Hieracium beauverdianum subsp. laricicola

Hieracium beauverdianum subsp.

laricicola, commonly known as the alderleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in forest edges and meadows.

The plant has yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a rough texture. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its association with alder trees, contributing to its ecological niche in riparian zones.
