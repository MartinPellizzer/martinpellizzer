Helictotrichon pubescens

Helictotrichon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower spikes in the summer, which are arranged in a loose panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Helictotrichon pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
