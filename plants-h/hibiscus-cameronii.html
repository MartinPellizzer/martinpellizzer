Hibiscus cameronii

Hibiscus cameronii, commonly known as Cameron's hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Hibiscus cameronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
