Hugelia hooveri

Hugelia hooveri, commonly known as the Hoover's hugelia, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hugelia hooveri thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments.
