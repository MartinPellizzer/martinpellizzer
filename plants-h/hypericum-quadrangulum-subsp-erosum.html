Hypericum quadrangulum subsp. erosum

Hypericum quadrangulum subsp.

erosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often ascending stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses similar to other Hypericum species.
