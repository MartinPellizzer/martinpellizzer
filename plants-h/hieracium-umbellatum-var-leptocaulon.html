Hieracium umbellatum var. leptocaulon

Hieracium umbellatum var. leptocaulon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The variety *leptocaulon* is distinguished by its slender stems and reduced leaf size compared to other varieties of *Hieracium umbellatum*.
