Heliotropium gnaphalodes

Heliotropium gnaphalodes, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Heliotropium gnaphalodes is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
