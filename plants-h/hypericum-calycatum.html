Hypericum calycatum

Hypericum calycatum, commonly known as the golden st John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while the flowers emit a subtle fragrance.

Hypericum calycatum is often found in rocky or woodland areas and is known for its medicinal properties.
