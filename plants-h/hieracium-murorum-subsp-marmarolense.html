Hieracium murorum subsp. marmarolense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

marmarolense, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
