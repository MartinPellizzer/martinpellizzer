Heliconia laneana var. flava

Heliconia laneana var. flava, commonly known as the yellow heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features long, narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Heliconia laneana var. flava thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests or cultivated in gardens.
