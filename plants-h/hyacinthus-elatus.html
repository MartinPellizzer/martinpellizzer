Hyacinthus elatus

Hyacinthus elatus, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces oval, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients, enabling it to survive in dry periods. Its leaves are strap-shaped and form a dense rosette at the base.

Hyacinthus elatus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
