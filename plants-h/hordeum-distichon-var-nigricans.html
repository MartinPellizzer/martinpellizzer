Hordeum distichon var. nigricans

Hordeum distichon var. nigricans, commonly known as black barley, is a variety of the wild barley species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grains and slender, two-rowed spikelets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptation to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that aids in water retention.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for drought resistance and genetic diversity.
