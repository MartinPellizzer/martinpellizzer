Hieracium vindobonense

Hieracium vindobonense, commonly known as the Vienna hawkweed, is a flowering plant species native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
