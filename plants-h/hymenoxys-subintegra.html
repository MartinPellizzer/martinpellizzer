Hymenoxys subintegra

Hymenoxys subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Hymenoxys subintegra is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom in full sun.
