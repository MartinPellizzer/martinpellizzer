Hamelia coccinea

Hamelia coccinea, commonly known as flamebeau or red flamebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Hamelia coccinea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
