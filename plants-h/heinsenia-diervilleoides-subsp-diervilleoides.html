Heinsenia diervilleoides subsp. diervilleoides

Heinsenia diervilleoides subsp.

diervilleoides, commonly known as the coastal bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, often in dune environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a resilient coastal plant.
