Heritiera globosa

Heritiera globosa, commonly known as the globe senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spherical seed pods that split open when mature. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
