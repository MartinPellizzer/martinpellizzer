Haplopappus marginalis var. heterophysus

Haplopappus marginalis var. heterophysus, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
