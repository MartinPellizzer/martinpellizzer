Hieracium crocatoides

Hieracium crocatoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
