Halfordia leichhardtii

Halfordia leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect culms that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, with a hairy surface.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry conditions.
