Hieracium chlorifolium subsp. vulpianum

Hieracium chlorifolium subsp.

vulpianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
