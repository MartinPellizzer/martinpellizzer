Hieracium arpadianum

Hieracium arpadianum, commonly known as the Arpadian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium arpadianum is often found in disturbed habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
