Hieracium verbenifolium

Hieracium verbenifolium, commonly known as the verbena-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Hieracium verbenifolium typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
