Hedyotis menziesiana

Hedyotis menziesiana, commonly known as the smallflower bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically features slender, branching stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hedyotis menziesiana is often found in moist soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
