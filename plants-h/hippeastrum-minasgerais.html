Hippeastrum minasgerais

Hippeastrum minasgerais, commonly known as the Minas Gerais amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are often used for propagation. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
