Hymenachne myuros

Hymenachne myuros, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 meter in height, with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems.

Hymenachne myuros plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
