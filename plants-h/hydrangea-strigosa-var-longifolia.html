Hydrangea strigosa var. longifolia

Hydrangea strigosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that turn bronze in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to partial shade in moist, well-drained soils.
