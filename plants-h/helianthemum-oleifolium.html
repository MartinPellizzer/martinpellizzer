Helianthemum oleifolium

Helianthemum oleifolium, commonly known as the oil plant thrift, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
