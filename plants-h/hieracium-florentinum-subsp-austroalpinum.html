Hieracium florentinum subsp. austroalpinum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

austroalpinum, commonly known as the Alpine oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its yellow central disk surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has a taproot system and produces a basal rosette of leaves.

This subspecies is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
