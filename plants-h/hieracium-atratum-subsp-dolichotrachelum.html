Hieracium atratum subsp. dolichotrachelum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

dolichotrachelum, commonly known as the long-necked black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is distinguished by its elongated flower stems and is often found in open habitats with adequate sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and locally distributed species.
