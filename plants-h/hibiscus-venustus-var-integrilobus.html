Hibiscus venustus var. integrilobus

Hibiscus venustus var. integrilobus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sun exposure.
