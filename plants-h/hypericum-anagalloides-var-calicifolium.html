Hypericum anagalloides var. calicifolium

Hypericum anagalloides var. calicifolium, commonly known as the calycifolium St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
