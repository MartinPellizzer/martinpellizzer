Hieracium callichlorum subsp. kochtanum

Hieracium callichlorum subsp.

kochtanum, commonly known as the Kochtanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright floral display.
