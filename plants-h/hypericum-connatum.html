Hypericum connatum

Hypericum connatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hypericum connatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
