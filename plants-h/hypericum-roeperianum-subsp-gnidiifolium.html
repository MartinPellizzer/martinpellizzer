Hypericum roeperianum subsp. gnidiifolium

Hypericum roeperianum subsp.

gnidiifolium, commonly known as the gnidiifolium hypericum, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
