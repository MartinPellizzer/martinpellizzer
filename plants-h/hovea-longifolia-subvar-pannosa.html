Hovea longifolia subvar. pannosa

Hovea longifolia subvar. pannosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. It has a woody stem and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
