Hieracium cottetii subsp. salvanicum

Hieracium cottetii subsp.

salvanicum, commonly known as the Salvan's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with dense white hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
