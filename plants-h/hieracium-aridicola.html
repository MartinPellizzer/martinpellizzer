Hieracium aridicola

Hieracium aridicola, commonly known as the arid grassland hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Due to its adaptation to harsh environments, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
