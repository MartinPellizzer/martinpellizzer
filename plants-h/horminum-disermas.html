Horminum disermas

Horminum disermas, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss, Horminum disermas is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
