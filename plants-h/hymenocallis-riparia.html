Hymenocallis riparia

Hymenocallis riparia, commonly known as the river spider lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that resemble spiders, with long, narrow petals and a prominent central spadix. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often form dense clumps, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hymenocallis riparia is valued for its beauty and ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
