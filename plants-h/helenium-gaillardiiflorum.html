Helenium gaillardiiflorum

Helenium gaillardiiflorum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its vibrant orange to red-orange flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium gaillardiiflorum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
