Hieracium obtusiusculum

Hieracium obtusiusculum, commonly known as the blunt-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a common weed in some regions.
