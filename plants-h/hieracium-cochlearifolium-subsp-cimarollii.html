Hieracium cochlearifolium subsp. cimarollii

Hieracium cochlearifolium subsp.

cimarollii, commonly known as the Cimaroli's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
