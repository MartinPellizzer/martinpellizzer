Heliotropium powelliorum

Heliotropium powelliorum, commonly known as Powell's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which are typical of the heliotrope genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes.

Powell's heliotrope is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
