Hepatica transsilvanica

Hepatica transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian liverwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a liver. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Hepatica transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
