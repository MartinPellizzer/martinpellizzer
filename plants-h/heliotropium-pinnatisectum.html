Heliotropium pinnatisectum

Heliotropium pinnatisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, pinnatisected leaves that give it a finely divided appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a distinctive white or pale center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Heliotropium pinnatisectum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
