Hieracium scabiosum var. supravestitum

Hieracium scabiosum var. supravestitum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves that are often glandular and can be quite stiff to the touch. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
