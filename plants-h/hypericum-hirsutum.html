Hypericum hirsutum

Hypericum hirsutum, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and rough, hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum hirsutum is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its resilience to various environmental conditions.
