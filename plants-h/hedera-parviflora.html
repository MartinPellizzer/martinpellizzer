Hedera parviflora

Hedera parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. Hedera parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in horticultural landscaping.
