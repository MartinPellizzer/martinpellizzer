Habranthus niveus

Habranthus niveus, commonly known as the white-flowered spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees and wasps, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
