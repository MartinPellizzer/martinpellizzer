Hymenolobium petraeum

Hymenolobium petraeum, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of whitish hair, which helps reduce water loss. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

The seeds are enclosed in hard pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.
