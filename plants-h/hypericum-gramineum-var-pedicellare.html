Hypericum gramineum var. pedicellare

Hypericum gramineum var. pedicellare, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that form dense mats, often covering the ground in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with prominent stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
