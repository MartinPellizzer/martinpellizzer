Hieracium teplouchovii

Hieracium teplouchovii, commonly known as the Teplouch's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
