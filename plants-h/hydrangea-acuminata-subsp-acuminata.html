Hydrangea acuminata subsp. acuminata

Hydrangea acuminata subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in forested areas and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
