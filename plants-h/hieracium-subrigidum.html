Hieracium subrigidum

Hieracium subrigidum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium subrigidum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
