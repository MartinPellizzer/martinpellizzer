Hoffmannia megistophylla

Hoffmannia megistophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf hoffmannia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its enormous, palmately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it one of the largest leaf plants in the world. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by dark red berries. Its unique morphology and size make it a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens.

Hoffmannia megistophylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
