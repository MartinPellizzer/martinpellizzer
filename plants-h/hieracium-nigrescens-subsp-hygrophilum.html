Hieracium nigrescens subsp. hygrophilum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

hygrophilum, commonly known as the water hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are often reddish and bear numerous small, daisy-like flowers.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.
