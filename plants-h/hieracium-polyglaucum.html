Hieracium polyglaucum

Hieracium polyglaucum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium polyglaucum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
