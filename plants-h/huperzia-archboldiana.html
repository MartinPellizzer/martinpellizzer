Huperzia archboldiana

Huperzia archboldiana, commonly known as the archbold's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a cryptogamic plant characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its structure is similar to that of other mosses and liverworts.

Huperzia archboldiana is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forests and wetlands.
