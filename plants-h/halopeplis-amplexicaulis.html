Halopeplis amplexicaulis

Halopeplis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the creeping hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or yellow, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Halopeplis amplexicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
