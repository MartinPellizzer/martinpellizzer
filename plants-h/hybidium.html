Hybidium

Hybidium, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its complex hybridization, often resulting from crosses between different orchid species. The plants typically feature showy, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. Hybidium species are native to tropical and subtropical regions, where they thrive in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to their ornamental value, they are widely cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
