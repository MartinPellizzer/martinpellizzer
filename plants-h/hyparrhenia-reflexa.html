Hyparrhenia reflexa

Hyparrhenia reflexa, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in dense clusters.

Hyparrhenia reflexa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
