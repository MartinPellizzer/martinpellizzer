Hieracium jurassiciforme var. metallorum

Hieracium jurassiciforme var. metallorum, commonly known as the Jurassic Metalweed, is a rare and striking plant with a unique botanical profile.

It features large, velvety leaves that exhibit a metallic sheen, giving it its distinctive name. The flowers are typically a deep, iridescent blue, with a complex structure that resembles the fossilized flora of the Jurassic period. This variant is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, heavy metal-contaminated soils, making it an indicator species for such environments.

Its morphology and adaptive traits make it a subject of interest in both botany and environmental science.
