Husemannia protensa

Husemannia protensa, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Husemannia protensa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.
