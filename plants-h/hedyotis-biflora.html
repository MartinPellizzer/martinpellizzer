Hedyotis biflora

Hedyotis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered bluestem, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, with four petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis biflora is often found in the understory of tropical woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
