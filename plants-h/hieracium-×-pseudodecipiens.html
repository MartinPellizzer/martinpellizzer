Hieracium × pseudodecipiens

Hieracium × pseudodecipiens, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and rosette-like basal leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its foliage is often hairy, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
