Habenaria papillosa

Habenaria papillosa, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, spiraled inflorescences that bear small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
