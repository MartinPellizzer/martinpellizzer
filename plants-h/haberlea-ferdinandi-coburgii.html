Haberlea ferdinandi-coburgii

Haberlea ferdinandi-coburgii, commonly known as the coburgii rockrose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, branching stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage that is often adorned with small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favored ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
