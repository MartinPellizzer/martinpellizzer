Hieracium nigrescens subsp. subpumilum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

subpumilum, commonly known as the small hairy hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a relatively rare species, often found in specific microhabitats with particular soil and moisture conditions.
