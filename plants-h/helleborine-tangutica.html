Helleborine tangutica

Helleborine tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a sympodial growth habit. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are tuberous, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Helleborine tangutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
