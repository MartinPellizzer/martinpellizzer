Hakea arachnoidea

Hakea arachnoidea, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter, followed by woody, cone-like seed pods. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

Hakea arachnoidea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
