Heliconia juliani

Heliconia juliani, commonly known as the red-flowered heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with clustered, tubular flowers that are often pollinated by birds and insects. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

Heliconia juliani is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
