Hirtella angustifolia

Hirtella angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are typically visited by various pollinators.

Hirtella angustifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
