Hakea victoria

Hakea victoria, commonly known as the golden hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. Its woody structure and ability to withstand drought make it a resilient species.

Hakea victoria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
