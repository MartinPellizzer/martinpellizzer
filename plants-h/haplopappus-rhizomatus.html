Haplopappus rhizomatus

Haplopappus rhizomatus, commonly known as the creeping pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features a fibrous root system and has a low, spreading growth habit with thin, succulent stems. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense heads, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds with pappus structures.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
