Hypericum acutum proles undulatum

Hypericum acutum proles undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, undulating stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
