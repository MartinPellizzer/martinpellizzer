Hieracium bathycranum

Hieracium bathycranum, commonly known as the deep-crowned hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium bathycranum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
