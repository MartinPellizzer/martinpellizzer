Hieracium carpathicum subsp. glaucopallidulum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

glaucopallidulum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal and erect stems covered with a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is part of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
