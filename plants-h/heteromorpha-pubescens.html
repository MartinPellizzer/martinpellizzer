Heteromorpha pubescens

Heteromorpha pubescens, commonly known as the woolly leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its stems are woody and often twine around supports as it grows.

Heteromorpha pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
