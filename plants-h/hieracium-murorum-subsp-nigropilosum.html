Hieracium murorum subsp. nigropilosum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

nigropilosum, commonly known as the black-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, blackish pubescence on the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
