Hieracium sparsifolium subsp. exporrectum

Hieracium sparsifolium subsp.

exporrectum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
