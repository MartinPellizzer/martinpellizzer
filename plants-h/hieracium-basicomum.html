Hieracium basicomum

Hieracium basicomum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Hieracium basicomum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
