Habenaria tetramera

Habenaria tetramera, commonly known as the four-parted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are often fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
