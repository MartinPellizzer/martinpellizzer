Hypericum juniperinum

Hypericum juniperinum, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has oval-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
