Hymenaea hayneana

Hymenaea hayneana, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that are typically followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
