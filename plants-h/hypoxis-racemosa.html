Hypoxis racemosa

Hypoxis racemosa, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Hypoxis racemosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
