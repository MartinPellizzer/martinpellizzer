Heterotheca sandersii

Heterotheca sandersii, commonly known as sanders' coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by orange to brown disk flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sanders' coneflower is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
