Hesperantha woodii

Hesperantha woodii, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip, a feature common in many orchid species.

Hesperantha woodii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
