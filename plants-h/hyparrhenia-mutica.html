Hyparrhenia mutica

Hyparrhenia mutica, commonly known as red oat grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

Hyparrhenia mutica is often used in landscaping and has traditional uses in some cultures for thatching and construction.
