Hechtia nivea

Hechtia nivea, commonly known as the white hechtia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure, with thick, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
