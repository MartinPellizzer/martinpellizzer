Hyptis trichoneura

Hyptis trichoneura, commonly known as the hairy hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Hyptis trichoneura is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
