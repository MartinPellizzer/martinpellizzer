Hyperbaena reticulata

Hyperbaena reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, helping to store water.

Hyperbaena reticulata is often found in sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
