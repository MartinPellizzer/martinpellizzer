Helianthemum eriocephalum

Helianthemum eriocephalum, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in mild climates.
