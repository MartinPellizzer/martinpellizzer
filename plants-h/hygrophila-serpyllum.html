Hygrophila serpyllum

Hygrophila serpyllum, commonly known as water hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in moist environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically blue or purple and are arranged in whorled clusters.

Hygrophila serpyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
