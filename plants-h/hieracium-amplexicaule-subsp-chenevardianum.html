Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. chenevardianum

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

chenevardianum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its basal leaves are rosette-like, with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
