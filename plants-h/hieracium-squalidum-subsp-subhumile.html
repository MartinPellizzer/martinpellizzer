Hieracium squalidum subsp. subhumile

Hieracium squalidum subsp.

subhumile, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant has wiry, ascending stems with numerous leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its small, pale flowers and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
