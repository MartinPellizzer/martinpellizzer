Hypericum indecorum var. paniculatum

Hypericum indecorum var. paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include glandular dots on the leaves and stems, which are characteristic of the Hypericaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
