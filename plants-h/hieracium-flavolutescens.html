Hieracium flavolutescens

Hieracium flavolutescens, commonly known as the yellow-lilac hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, with a distinct lilac hue on the inner petals.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
