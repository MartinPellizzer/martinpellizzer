Hieracium australius

Hieracium australius, commonly known as the Australian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers range in color from white to yellow.

Hieracium australius is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in its native range.
