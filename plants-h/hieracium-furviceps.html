Hieracium furviceps

Hieracium furviceps, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.

Hieracium furviceps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
