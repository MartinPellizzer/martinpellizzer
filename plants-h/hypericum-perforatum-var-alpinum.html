Hypericum perforatum var. alpinum

Hypericum perforatum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive leaves with translucent dots, which give it its scientific name. This variant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, containing hypericin and other compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
