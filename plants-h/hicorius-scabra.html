Hicorius scabra

Hicorius scabra, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Hicorius scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
