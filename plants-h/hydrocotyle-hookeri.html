Hydrocotyle hookeri

Hydrocotyle hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's spurge laurel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, white flowers clustered in umbels. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a fibrous root system.

Hydrocotyle hookeri is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
