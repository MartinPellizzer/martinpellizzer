Hordeum delileanum

Hordeum delileanum, commonly known as Delile's barley, is a species of grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that bear small, two-rowed grains. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes other cereal grasses like wheat and rye.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
