Hasteola suaveolens

Hasteola suaveolens, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green spikelets that are often mistaken for other grasses due to their simple appearance. Hasteola suaveolens is an important plant for pollinators and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
