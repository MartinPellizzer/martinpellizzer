Heterocalymnantha

Heterocalymnantha, commonly known as the white-flowered rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody and branched. Heterocalymnantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
