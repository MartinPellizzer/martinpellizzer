Helianthemum croceum subsp. estevei

Helianthemum croceum subsp.

estevei, commonly known as the Canary Islands sand thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing shrub with small, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is part of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to the island's climate.
