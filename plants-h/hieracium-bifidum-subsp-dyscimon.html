Hieracium bifidum subsp. dyscimon

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

dyscimon, commonly known as the bifid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and often have a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are visited by various pollinators.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and latex-containing stems.
