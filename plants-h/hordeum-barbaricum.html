Hordeum barbaricum

Hordeum barbaricum, commonly known as wild barley, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in meadows. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that contain small, yellowish grains. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various environments.

Hordeum barbaricum is considered an important forage plant and has historical significance in agriculture and traditional medicine.
