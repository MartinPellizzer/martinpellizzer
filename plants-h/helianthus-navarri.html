Helianthus navarri

Helianthus navarri, commonly known as Navarre sunflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus navarri thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats.
