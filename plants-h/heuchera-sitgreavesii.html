Heuchera sitgreavesii

Heuchera sitgreavesii, commonly known as Sitgreaves' alumroot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, dark green foliage with red-tinged veins. The plant produces upright spikes of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Heuchera sitgreavesii is valued for its ornamental foliage and drought tolerance.
