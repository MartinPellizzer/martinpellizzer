Helictotrichon ausserdorferi

Helictotrichon ausserdorferi, commonly known as the Outerdorf's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
