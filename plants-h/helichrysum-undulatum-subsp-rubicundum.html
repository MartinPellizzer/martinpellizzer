Helichrysum undulatum subsp. rubicundum

Helichrysum undulatum subsp.

rubicundum, commonly known as the red-veined everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish veins in the leaves, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental use and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers.
