Hura brasiliensis

Hura brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a fruit with a hard, spiny exterior and a sweet, fleshy interior containing edible seeds. Its growth habit includes a tall, straight trunk with a broad, spreading canopy.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
