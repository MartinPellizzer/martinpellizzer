Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. comatum

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

comatum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a hybrid plant species native to Europe. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
