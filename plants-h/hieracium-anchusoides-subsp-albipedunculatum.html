Hieracium anchusoides subsp. albipedunculatum

Hieracium anchusoides subsp.

albipedunculatum, commonly known as the white-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale to white peduncles, which support the flower heads. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
