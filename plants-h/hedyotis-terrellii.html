Hedyotis terrellii

Hedyotis terrellii, commonly known as the Terrell's spurge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. Its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Hedyotis terrellii is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often spiny members.
