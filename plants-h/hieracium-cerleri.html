Hieracium cerleri

Hieracium cerleri, commonly known as the Cerler's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Native to Europe, it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has small, alternate leaves and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is considered a short-lived perennial and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
