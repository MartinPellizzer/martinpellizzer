Hermannia zeyheriana

Hermannia zeyheriana, commonly known as the red-flowered hermannia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It features clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking color during the colder months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen, contributing to its refined appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Hermannia zeyheriana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
