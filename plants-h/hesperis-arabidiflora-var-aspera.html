Hesperis arabidiflora var. aspera

Hesperis arabidiflora var. aspera, commonly known as the rough-hesperis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its specific variety name "aspera.". Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
