Hieracium cuneolarium

Hieracium cuneolarium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Hieracium cuneolarium is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
