Hyptis guanchezii

Hyptis guanchezii, commonly known as the Canary Island hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Hyptis guanchezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
