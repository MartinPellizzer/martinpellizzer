Hypericum cymbiferum

Hypericum cymbiferum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and orange-red stems, which are often used in herbal remedies. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum cymbiferum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in treating mild depression and skin conditions.
