Hieracium dispersifrons

Hieracium dispersifrons, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, typically growing in meadows and grasslands. The plant has basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread across open habitats.

Hieracium dispersifrons is an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
