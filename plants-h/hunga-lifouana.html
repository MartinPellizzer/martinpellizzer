Hunga lifouana

Hunga lifouana, commonly known as the Pacific sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Ocean.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing near tidal zones and brackish water areas. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
