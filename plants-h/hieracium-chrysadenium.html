Hieracium chrysadenium

Hieracium chrysadenium, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Hieracium chrysadenium is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
