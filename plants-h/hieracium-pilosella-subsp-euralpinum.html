Hieracium pilosella subsp. euralpinum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

euralpinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a low-growing habit with basal leaves and is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to other members of the species.
