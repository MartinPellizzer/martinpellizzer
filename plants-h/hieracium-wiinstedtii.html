Hieracium wiinstedtii

Hieracium wiinstedtii, commonly known as the Wiinstedt's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
