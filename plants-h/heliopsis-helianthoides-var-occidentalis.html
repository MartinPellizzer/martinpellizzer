Heliopsis helianthoides var. occidentalis

Heliopsis helianthoides var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble sunflowers, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
