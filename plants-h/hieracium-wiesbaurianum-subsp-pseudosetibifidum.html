Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. pseudosetibifidum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

pseudosetibifidum, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow ray florets.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native range.
