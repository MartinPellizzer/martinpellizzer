Hieracium sylvaticum var. stenstroemii

Hieracium sylvaticum var. stenstroemii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus, with specific adaptations for its ecological niche.
