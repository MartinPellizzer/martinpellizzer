Hancornia speciosa var. stenophylla

Hancornia speciosa var. stenophylla, commonly known as the red cacao tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds used in the production of cacao products. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and economic importance, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
