Hieracium compositum subsp. muricatum

Hieracium compositum subsp.

muricatum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in moist to moderately dry soils and can spread through rhizomes or seeds.
