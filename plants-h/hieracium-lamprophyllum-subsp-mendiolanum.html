Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp. mendiolanum

Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp.

mendiolanum, commonly known as the Mendola hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of broadly lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. It produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, which are visited by various pollinators.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in calcareous environments.
