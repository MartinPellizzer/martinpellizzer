Hieracium chlorellum subsp. leuciscum

Hieracium chlorellum subsp.

leuciscum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce small, pale yellow to white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated edges.

The subspecies leuciscum is distinguished by its pale coloration and compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
