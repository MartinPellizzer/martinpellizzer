Hibiscus cytisifolius

Hibiscus cytisifolius, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow or white center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus cytisifolius is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
