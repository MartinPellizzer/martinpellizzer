Hieracium lachenalii subsp. dalicum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

dalicum, commonly known as the Dalic Meadowhawk, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
