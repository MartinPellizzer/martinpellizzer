Hypericum hakonense

Hypericum hakonense, commonly known as the "Hakon's St. John's Wort," is a perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It features erect, branching stems covered in glandular hairs and produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. Hypericum hakonense thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

It is often found in alpine and arctic tundra habitats.
