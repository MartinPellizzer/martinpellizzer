Helianthus annuus var. aridus

Helianthus annuus var. aridus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower adapted to arid environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its growth habit is low and spreading, often forming dense mats in dry landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in desert ecosystems.
