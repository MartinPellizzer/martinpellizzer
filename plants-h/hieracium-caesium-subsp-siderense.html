Hieracium caesium subsp. siderense

Hieracium caesium subsp.

siderense, commonly known as ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence.

It is known for its hardy nature and ability to bloom late into the summer season.
