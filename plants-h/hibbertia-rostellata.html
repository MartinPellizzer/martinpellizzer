Hibbertia rostellata

Hibbertia rostellata, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hibbertia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Hibbertia rostellata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
