Hieracium vurtopicum subsp. vurtopicum

Hieracium vurtopicum subsp.

vurtopicum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are composed of numerous small florets, with both ray and disk flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in temperate climates.
