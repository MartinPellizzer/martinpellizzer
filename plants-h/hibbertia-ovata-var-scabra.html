Hibbertia ovata var. scabra

Hibbertia ovata var. scabra, commonly known as the rough-hairy hibbertia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five spreading petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
