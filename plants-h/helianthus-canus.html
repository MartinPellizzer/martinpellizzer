Helianthus canus

Helianthus canus, commonly known as the hoary sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow ray flowers, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It produces small, inconspicuous disk flowers that are important for pollinators.

Helianthus canus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
