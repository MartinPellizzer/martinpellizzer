Hieracium vulgatum subsp. imitans

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

imitans, commonly known as the mimic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and can form dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The subspecies imitans is distinguished by its ability to mimic the appearance of other hawkweed species, aiding in its survival through camouflage.
