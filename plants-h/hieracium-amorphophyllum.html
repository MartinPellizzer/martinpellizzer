Hieracium amorphophyllum

Hieracium amorphophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of hairy, basal leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread rapidly.
