Hieracium pilosella subsp. minutissimum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

minutissimum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features dense, woolly foliage with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a creeping growth habit with stolons that root at the nodes. Its leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
