Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. prenanthophyllum

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

prenanthophyllum, commonly known as the apple-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
