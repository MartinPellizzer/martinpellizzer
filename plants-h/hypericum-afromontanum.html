Hypericum afromontanum

Hypericum afromontanum, commonly known as the East African St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, typically growing in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum afromontanum is often found in rocky or well-drained soils at higher elevations.
