Hyacinthus turkestanicus

Hyacinthus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkistan hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
