Hydrolea multiflora

Hydrolea multiflora, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Hydrolea multiflora is often cultivated for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal in gardens.
