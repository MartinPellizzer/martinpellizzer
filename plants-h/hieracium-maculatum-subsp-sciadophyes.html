Hieracium maculatum subsp. sciadophyes

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

sciadophyes, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and woodland edges, and is known for its aggressive spreading habit.

The subspecies sciadophyes is distinguished by its darker, more prominent markings on the leaves compared to other subspecies of Hieracium maculatum.
