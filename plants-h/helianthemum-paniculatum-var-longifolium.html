Helianthemum paniculatum var. longifolium

Helianthemum paniculatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved helianthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact, branched clusters called panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its dense foliage and prolonged blooming period.
