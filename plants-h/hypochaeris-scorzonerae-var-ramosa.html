Hypochaeris scorzonerae var. ramosa

Hypochaeris scorzonerae var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It features basal leaves that are often hairy and can be lobed or toothed, while the stems are usually erect and branched.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, producing small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
