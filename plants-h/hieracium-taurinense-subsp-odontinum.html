Hieracium taurinense subsp. odontinum

Hieracium taurinense subsp.

odontinum, commonly known as the Taurine Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal and upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Hieracium, known for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
