Heuchera micrantha f. acuta

Heuchera micrantha f. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaf alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive acute or sharply pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are borne on upright, branched stems. Its foliage is often variegated, displaying shades of green, red, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring moist, woodland environments.
