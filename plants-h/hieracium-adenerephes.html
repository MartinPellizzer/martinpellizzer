Hieracium adenerephes

Hieracium adenerephes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium adenerephes is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
