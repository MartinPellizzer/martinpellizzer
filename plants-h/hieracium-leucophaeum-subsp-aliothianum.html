Hieracium leucophaeum subsp. aliothianum

Hieracium leucophaeum subsp.

aliothianum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in dense, white to greyish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions due to habitat loss and overgrazing.
