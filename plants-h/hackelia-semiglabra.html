Hackelia semiglabra

Hackelia semiglabra, commonly known as the smooth hackberry, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy when young. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that matures in the fall.

Hackelia semiglabra is often found in woodland edges, open woods, and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
