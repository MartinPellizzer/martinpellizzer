Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. gigantosphaericum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

gigantosphaericum, commonly known as the giant globe hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its large, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its striking appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
