Herreria tuberosa

Herreria tuberosa, commonly known as the Mexican tuber or angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is known for its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Herreria tuberosa is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
