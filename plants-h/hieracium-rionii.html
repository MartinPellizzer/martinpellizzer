Hieracium rionii

Hieracium rionii, commonly known as Rion's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disc florets.

Hieracium rionii is often found in sandy or clay soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
