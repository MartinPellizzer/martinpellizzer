Hyptis idiocephala

Hyptis idiocephala, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis idiocephala is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some cultures for its purported health benefits.
