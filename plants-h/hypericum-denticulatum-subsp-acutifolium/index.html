Hypericum Denticulatum Subsp. Acutifolium

Hypericum denticulatum subsp. acutifolium is a lesser-known member of the Hypericaceae family known for its unique foliage and medicinal properties. This subspecies is characterized by its sharply toothed leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine or coastal regions. Its name reflects both its physical traits and its historical use in herbal medicine. Despite its modest appearance, it holds significant ecological and botanical value.
