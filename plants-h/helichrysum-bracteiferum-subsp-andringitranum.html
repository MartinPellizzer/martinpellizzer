Helichrysum bracteiferum subsp. andringitranum

Helichrysum bracteiferum subsp.

andringitranum, commonly known as the Andringitra helichrysum, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-orange flower heads that resemble sunflowers, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
