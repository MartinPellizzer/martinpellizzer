Hieracium pallidum subsp. leucothecum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

leucothecum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
