Helianthus subtuberosus var. rydbergii

Helianthus subtuberosus var. rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces seeds in a distinctive, spiky structure.

This variety is part of the sunflower genus, known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
