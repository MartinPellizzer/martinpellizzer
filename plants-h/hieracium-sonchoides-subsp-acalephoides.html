Hieracium sonchoides subsp. acalephoides

Hieracium sonchoides subsp.

acalephoides, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
