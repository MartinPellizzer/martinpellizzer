Haplopappus lanceolatus subsp. vaseyi

Haplopappus lanceolatus subsp.

vaseyi, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single head and are visited by various pollinators.

This subspecies is characterized by its low-growing form and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
