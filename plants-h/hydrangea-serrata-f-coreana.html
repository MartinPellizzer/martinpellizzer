Hydrangea serrata f. coreana

Hydrangea serrata f. coreana, commonly known as the Korean hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, serrated leaves that are typically dark green during the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to provide seasonal interest with its foliage and flowers.
