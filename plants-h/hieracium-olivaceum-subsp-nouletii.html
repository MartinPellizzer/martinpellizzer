Hieracium olivaceum subsp. nouletii

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

nouletii, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle morphological differences from the main species, particularly in leaf structure and flower characteristics.
