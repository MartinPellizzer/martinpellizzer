Hibiscus boranensis

Hibiscus boranensis, commonly known as the Boran hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers have five prominent petals.

Hibiscus boranensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or savanna habitats.
