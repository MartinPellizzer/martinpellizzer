Hemionitis discolor

Hemionitis discolor, commonly known as the silver sword, is a unique fern native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like trichomes. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are narrow, strap-like, and often curl inward at the edges. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy, succulent leaves.

Hemionitis discolor is considered a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conditions to thrive.
