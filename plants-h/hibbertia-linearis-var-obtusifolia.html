Hibbertia linearis var. obtusifolia

Hibbertia linearis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandplain wattle, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Hibbertia linearis var. obtusifolia is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is often used in native gardens and for erosion control.
