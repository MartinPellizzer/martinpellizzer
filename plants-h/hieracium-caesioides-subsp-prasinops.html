Hieracium caesioides subsp. prasinops

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

prasinops, commonly known as the green-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive green center, and it produces small, oval seeds in a single fruit.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
