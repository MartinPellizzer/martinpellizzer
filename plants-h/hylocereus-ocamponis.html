Hylocereus ocamponis

Hylocereus ocamponis, commonly known as the Ocampon's night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique flowering pattern and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
