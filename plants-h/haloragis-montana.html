Haloragis montana

Haloragis montana, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat on the ground.

Haloragis montana is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
