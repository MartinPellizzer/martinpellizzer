Hieracium bifidum subsp. tricolorans

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

tricolorans, commonly known as the three-colored hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are often visited by various pollinators.

This subspecies is notable for its color variation, with flowers displaying different shades of yellow, hence its common name.
