Hieracium mougeotii subsp. asturicum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

asturicum, commonly known as the Asturian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head of yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
