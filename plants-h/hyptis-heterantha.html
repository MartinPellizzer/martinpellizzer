Hyptis heterantha

Hyptis heterantha, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis heterantha is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
