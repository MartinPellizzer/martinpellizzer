Horridocactus geissei

Horridocactus geissei, commonly known as the Geissei cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
