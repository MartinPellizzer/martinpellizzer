Humboltia arcuata

Humboltia arcuata, commonly known as the Humboldt's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Humboltia arcuata is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
