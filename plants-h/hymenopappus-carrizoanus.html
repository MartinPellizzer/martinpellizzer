Hymenopappus carrizoanus

Hymenopappus carrizoanus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
