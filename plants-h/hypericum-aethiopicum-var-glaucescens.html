Hypericum aethiopicum var. glaucescens

Hypericum aethiopicum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems that are typically bluish-gray, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
