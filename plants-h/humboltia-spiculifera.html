Humboltia spiculifera

Humboltia spiculifera, commonly known as the spiny desert shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Humboltia spiculifera is adapted to survive in extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
