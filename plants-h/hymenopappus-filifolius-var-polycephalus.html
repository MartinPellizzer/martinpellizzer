Hymenopappus filifolius var. polycephalus

Hymenopappus filifolius var. polycephalus, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-blue leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit, often spreading across rocky or sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of numerous tiny, blue to purple flowers surrounded by silky, pappus hairs that aid in wind dispersal.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
