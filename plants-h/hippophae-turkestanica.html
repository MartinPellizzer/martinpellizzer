Hippophae turkestanica

Hippophae turkestanica, commonly known as the sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamin C and essential fatty acids. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, while its branches are woody and often spiny.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its economic importance in traditional medicine and nutrition.
