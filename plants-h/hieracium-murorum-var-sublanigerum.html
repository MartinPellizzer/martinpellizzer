Hieracium murorum var. sublanigerum

Hieracium murorum var. sublanigerum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the variety sublanigerum is distinguished by its dense, hairy covering.

This plant is often found in temperate climates and is known for its hardy nature.
