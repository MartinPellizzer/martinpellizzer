Harpalyce loeseneriana

Harpalyce loeseneriana, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structure. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale pink pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Harpalyce loeseneriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
