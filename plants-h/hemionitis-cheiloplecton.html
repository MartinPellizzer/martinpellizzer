Hemionitis cheiloplecton

Hemionitis cheiloplecton, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic fern found in wetland environments.

It has finely divided, needle-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the water's surface. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slowly flowing water, often in marshy areas. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Hemionitis cheiloplecton plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
