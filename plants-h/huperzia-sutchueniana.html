Huperzia sutchueniana

Huperzia sutchueniana, commonly known as the Sichuan huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to China.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are arranged in whorls. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Huperzia sutchueniana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
