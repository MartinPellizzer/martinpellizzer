Helianthus alexandri

Helianthus alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with erect, branching stems and large, showy yellow flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
