Hernandia ovigera subsp. ovigera

Hernandia ovigera subsp.

ovigera, commonly known as the red-flowered hernandia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
