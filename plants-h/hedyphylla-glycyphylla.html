Hedyphylla glycyphylla

Hedyphylla glycyphylla, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves that emit a sweet scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hedyphylla glycyphylla is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
