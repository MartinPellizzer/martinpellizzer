Hieracium ochroleucomorphum

Hieracium ochroleucomorphum, commonly known as the yellow-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
