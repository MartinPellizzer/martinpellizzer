Hakea barrettiorum

Hakea barrettiorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from pink to red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Hakea barrettiorum is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
