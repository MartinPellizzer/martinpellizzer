Hebe buxifolia var. pauciramosa

Hebe buxifolia var. pauciramosa, commonly known as the silver hebe, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a silvery-blue hue on the underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
