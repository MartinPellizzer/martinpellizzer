Hieracium bagnense

Hieracium bagnense, commonly known as the Bagnes Meadowhawk, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium bagnense is known for its hardy nature and ability to flourish in nutrient-poor soils.
