Hyptis hilarii

Hyptis hilarii, commonly known as the hilarii hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis hilarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
