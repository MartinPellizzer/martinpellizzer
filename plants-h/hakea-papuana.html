Hakea papuana

Hakea papuana, commonly known as the Papuan hakea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear small, yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its thick, waxy foliage.

Hakea papuana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
