Haageocereus peculiaris

Haageocereus peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh desert environments through its thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
