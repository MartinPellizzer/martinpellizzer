Hieracium megachaetum

Hieracium megachaetum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy yellow flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets, and it has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types. Hieracium megachaetum is known for its ability to spread aggressively through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a potential invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
