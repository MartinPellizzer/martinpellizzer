Hemipilia galeata

Hemipilia galeata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its distinctive inflorescence of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Hemipilia galeata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
