Hordeum comosum

Hordeum comosum, commonly known as wild barley, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant produces small, two-rowed grains that are typically wind-pollinated. Hordeum comosum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

It is a significant species in agricultural and ecological studies due to its adaptability and historical importance in human cultivation.
