Hippeastrum solandriflorum var. rubritubum

Hippeastrum solandriflorum var. rubritubum, commonly known as the red-tubed amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive red or orange tubular throat. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
