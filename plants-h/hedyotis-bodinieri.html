Hedyotis bodinieri

Hedyotis bodinieri, commonly known as the golden rain vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing herb with slender, twining stems that often grow along the ground or up trees. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it a striking appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Hedyotis bodinieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
