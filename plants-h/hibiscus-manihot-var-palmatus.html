Hibiscus manihot var. palmatus

Hibiscus manihot var. palmatus, commonly known as the palmate hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
