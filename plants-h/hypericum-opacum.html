Hypericum opacum

Hypericum opacum, commonly known as the opaque St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellowish-orange, and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Hypericum opacum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
