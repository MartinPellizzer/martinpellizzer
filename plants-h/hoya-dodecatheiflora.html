Hoya dodecatheiflora

Hoya dodecatheiflora, commonly known as the "Twelve-leafed Wax Plant," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and veins. The plant produces clusters of small, waxy flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Hoya dodecatheiflora is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
