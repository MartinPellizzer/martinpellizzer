Hieracium compositum subsp. exanthinum

Hieracium compositum subsp.

exanthinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
