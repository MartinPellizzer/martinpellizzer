Haplopappus argenteus

Haplopappus argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open areas with well-drained soil. It produces achenes with pappus bristles, which aid in wind dispersal.

Haplopappus argenteus is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological importance to pollinators.
