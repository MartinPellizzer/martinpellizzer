Hieracium laevigatum subsp. ficifolium

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

ficifolium, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with a waxy coating, and its flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

The subspecies ficifolium is distinguished by its larger, more rounded leaves compared to other members of the species.
