Homalomena javanica

Homalomena javanica, commonly known as the Java caladium, is a tropical evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the characteristic inflorescences of the Araceae family, typically green with a yellow or white spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Homalomena javanica is relatively low maintenance and can be grown both indoors and outdoors in suitable climates.
