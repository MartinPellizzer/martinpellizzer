Hydrangea hortensis var. pubescens

Hydrangea hortensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its cone-shaped inflorescences that change color from white to pink or red depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are ovate and often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
