Helichrysum scopulosum

Helichrysum scopulosum, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with silver-white hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Helichrysum scopulosum is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.
