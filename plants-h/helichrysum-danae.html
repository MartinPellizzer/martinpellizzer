Helichrysum danae

Helichrysum danae, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in a dense, rounded head, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
