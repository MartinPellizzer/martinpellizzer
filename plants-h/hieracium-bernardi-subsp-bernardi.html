Hieracium bernardi subsp. bernardi

Hieracium bernardi subsp.

bernardi, commonly known as the Bernhard's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a perennial species that produces seeds through wind dispersal, contributing to its spread across various habitats.
