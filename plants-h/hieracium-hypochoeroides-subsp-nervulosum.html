Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. nervulosum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

nervulosum, commonly known as the nervous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive vein pattern, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by petal-like bracts, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.
