Hypericum rochelii var. minus

Hypericum rochelii var. minus, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting species for horticultural and ecological studies.
