Hymenachne calamitosa

Hymenachne calamitosa, commonly known as the calamity grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands that can dominate aquatic ecosystems. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowering structures consist of small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hymenachne calamitosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
