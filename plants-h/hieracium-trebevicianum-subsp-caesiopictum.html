Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. caesiopictum

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

caesiopictum, commonly known as the blue-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces. Its most distinctive characteristic is its bright blue to violet composite flowers, which are arranged in a single terminal head.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
