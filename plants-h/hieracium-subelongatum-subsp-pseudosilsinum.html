Hieracium subelongatum subsp. pseudosilsinum

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

pseudosilsinum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
