Hibiscus arboreus

Hibiscus arboreus, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often have thorns.

Hibiscus arboreus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
