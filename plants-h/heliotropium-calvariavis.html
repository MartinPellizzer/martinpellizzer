Heliotropium calvariavis

Heliotropium calvariavis, commonly known as the rock heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming throughout the year. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Heliotropium calvariavis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
