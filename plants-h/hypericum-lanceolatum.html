Hypericum lanceolatum

Hypericum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its flowering period makes it a valuable plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
