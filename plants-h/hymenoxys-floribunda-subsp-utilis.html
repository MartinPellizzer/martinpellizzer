Hymenoxys floribunda subsp. utilis

Hymenoxys floribunda subsp.

utilis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico. It features showy, bright orange to red composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in dry to moist habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscaping.
