Homalium fagifolium var. pseudopaniculatum

Homalium fagifolium var. pseudopaniculatum, commonly known as the Panicle-leafed Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, panicle-like inflorescence composed of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and serrated margins. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its showy floral display.
