Hieracium angustatum subsp. imbricatum

Hieracium angustatum subsp.

imbricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow ray florets.

The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
