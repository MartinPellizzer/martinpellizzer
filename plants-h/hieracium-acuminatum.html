Hieracium acuminatum

Hieracium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This species is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along forest edges.
