Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. microdontophyllum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

microdontophyllum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This subspecies is distinguished by its delicate, translucent appearance and is considered a rare and specialized flora.
