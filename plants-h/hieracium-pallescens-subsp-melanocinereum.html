Hieracium pallescens subsp. melanocinereum

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

melanocinereum, commonly known as the dark-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems that are often tinged with a dark color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on slender stalks.

It is characterized by its distinct subspecies traits, including the dark pigmentation of its stems and leaves.
