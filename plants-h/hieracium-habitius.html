Hieracium habitius

Hieracium habitius, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Hieracium habitius is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
