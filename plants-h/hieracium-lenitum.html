Hieracium lenitum

Hieracium lenitum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium lenitum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
