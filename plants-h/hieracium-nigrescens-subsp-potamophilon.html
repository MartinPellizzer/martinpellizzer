Hieracium nigrescens subsp. potamophilon

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

potamophilon, commonly known as the water hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
