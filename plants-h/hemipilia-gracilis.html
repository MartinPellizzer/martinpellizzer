Hemipilia gracilis

Hemipilia gracilis, commonly known as the slender hemipilia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Hemipilia gracilis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
