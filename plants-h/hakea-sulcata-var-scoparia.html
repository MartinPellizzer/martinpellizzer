Hakea sulcata var. scoparia

Hakea sulcata var. scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or conical inflorescences that bear small, urn-shaped flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight curvature, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resilience.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
