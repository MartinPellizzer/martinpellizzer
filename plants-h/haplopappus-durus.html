Haplopappus durus

Haplopappus durus, commonly known as the rough pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small, yellowish flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across open landscapes.
