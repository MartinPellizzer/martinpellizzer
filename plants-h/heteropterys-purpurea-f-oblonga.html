Heteropterys purpurea f. oblonga

Heteropterys purpurea f. oblonga, commonly known as the purple-flowered huito, is a compact shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It features lanceolate, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
