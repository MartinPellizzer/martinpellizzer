Heliotropium oliganthum

Heliotropium oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Heliotropium oliganthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
