Heterotheca falcata

Heterotheca falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pinkish flower heads make it a distinctive member of the genus Heterotheca.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
