Hypericum acuminatum

Hypericum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Hypericum acuminatum is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
