Heliotropium campechianum

Heliotropium campechianum, commonly known as Mexican heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive violet hue. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with small hairs.

Heliotropium campechianum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
