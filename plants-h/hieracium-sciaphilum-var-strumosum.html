Hieracium sciaphilum var. strumosum

Hieracium sciaphilum var. strumosum, commonly known as the shade-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowers are clustered in a single head, and the plant is characterized by its low-growing habit.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.
