Heisteria celastrinea

Heisteria celastrinea, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Heisteria celastrinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
