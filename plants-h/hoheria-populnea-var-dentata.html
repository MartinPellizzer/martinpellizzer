Hoheria populnea var. dentata

Hoheria populnea var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed hoheria, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated or toothed margin, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
