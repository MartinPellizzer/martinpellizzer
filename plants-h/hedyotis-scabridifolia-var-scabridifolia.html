Hedyotis scabridifolia var. scabridifolia

Hedyotis scabridifolia var. scabridifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf dotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, scabrous texture on both surfaces. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pale yellow.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
