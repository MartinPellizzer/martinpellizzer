Hedyotis argentea

Hedyotis argentea, commonly known as silver geranium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Hedyotis argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
