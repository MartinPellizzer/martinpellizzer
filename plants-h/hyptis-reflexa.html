Hyptis reflexa

Hyptis reflexa, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis reflexa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
