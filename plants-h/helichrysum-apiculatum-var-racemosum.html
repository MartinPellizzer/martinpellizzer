Helichrysum apiculatum var. racemosum

Helichrysum apiculatum var. racemosum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces a profusion of tiny, papery bracts that give it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
