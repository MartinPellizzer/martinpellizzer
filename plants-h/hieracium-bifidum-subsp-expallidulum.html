Hieracium bifidum subsp. expallidulum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

expallidulum, commonly known as the pale-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disk-like structure.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences.
