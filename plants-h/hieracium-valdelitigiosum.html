Hieracium valdelitigiosum

Hieracium valdelitigiosum, commonly known as the valley hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium valdelitigiosum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent and sometimes invasive species in its native habitat.
