Hieracium scabriusculum f. chrysostylum

Hieracium scabriusculum f. chrysostylum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a variant of the common hawkweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and recognizable member of the Asteraceae family.
