Hippion imbricatum

Hippion imbricatum, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that is edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Hippion imbricatum is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
