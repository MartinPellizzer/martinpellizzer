Hyptis itatiaiae

Hyptis itatiaiae, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis itatiaiae is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
