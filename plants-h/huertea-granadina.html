Huertea granadina

Huertea granadina, commonly known as the Granada orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and is typically found in rocky coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
