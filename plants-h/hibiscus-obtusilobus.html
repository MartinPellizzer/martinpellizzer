Hibiscus obtusilobus

Hibiscus obtusilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with rounded lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus obtusilobus is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators like bees and butterflies.
