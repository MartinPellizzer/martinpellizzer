Hedysarum tauricum

Hedysarum tauricum, commonly known as the Crimean peashrub, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Hedysarum tauricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging landscapes.
