Hieracium cymosum subsp. chrysophaes

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

chrysophaes, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
