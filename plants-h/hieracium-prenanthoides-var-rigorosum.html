Hieracium prenanthoides var. rigorosum

Hieracium prenanthoides var. rigorosum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has basal and stem leaves that are usually lanceolate or linear, with rough, hairy surfaces.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
