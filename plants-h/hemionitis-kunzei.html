Hemionitis kunzei

Hemionitis kunzei, commonly known as the water hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Hemionitis kunzei is also notable for its ability to thrive in saline soils, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in degraded landscapes.
