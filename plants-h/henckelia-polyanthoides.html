Henckelia polyanthoides

Henckelia polyanthoides, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Henckelia polyanthoides is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and adaptability to well-drained soils.
