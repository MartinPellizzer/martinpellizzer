Hieracium nigrocyaneum

Hieracium nigrocyaneum, commonly known as the dark blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in dry conditions.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
