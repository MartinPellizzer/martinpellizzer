Hermione sertulosa

Hermione sertulosa, commonly known as the coral fern, is a striking plant native to tropical regions.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that resemble coral, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near water sources. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
