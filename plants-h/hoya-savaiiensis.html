Hoya savaiiensis

Hoya savaiiensis, commonly known as the Savaii hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Savaii in Samoa.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya savaiiensis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
