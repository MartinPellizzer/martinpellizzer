Hypericum goehringianum

Hypericum goehringianum, commonly known as the Goehring's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, elliptical leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hypericum goehringianum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
