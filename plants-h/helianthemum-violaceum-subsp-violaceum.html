Helianthemum violaceum subsp. violaceum

Helianthemum violaceum subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and limestone areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, sunny environments.
