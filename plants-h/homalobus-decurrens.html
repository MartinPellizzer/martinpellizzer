Homalobus decurrens

Homalobus decurrens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Homalobus decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
