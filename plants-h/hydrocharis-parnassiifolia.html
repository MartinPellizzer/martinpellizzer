Hydrocharis parnassiifolia

Hydrocharis parnassiifolia, commonly known as the water clover, is a floating aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, allowing it to remain buoyant on water surfaces. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Hydrocharis parnassiifolia is also known for its ability to help purify water by absorbing nutrients and reducing algae growth.
