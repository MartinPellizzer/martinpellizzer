Haemanthus superbus

Haemanthus superbus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking evergreen perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its stems are often adorned with red or orange bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
