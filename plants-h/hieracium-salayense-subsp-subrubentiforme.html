Hieracium salayense subsp. subrubentiforme

Hieracium salayense subsp.

subrubentiforme, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems that are reddish-brown in color, often with a reddish tinge. Its basal leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their ornamental and sometimes invasive characteristics.
