Helichrysum decurrens

Helichrysum decurrens, commonly known as the spreading goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum decurrens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
