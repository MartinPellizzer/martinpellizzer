Heliotropium thymifolium

Heliotropium thymifolium, commonly known as sweet William or honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling those of the genus Dianthus. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Heliotropium thymifolium is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
