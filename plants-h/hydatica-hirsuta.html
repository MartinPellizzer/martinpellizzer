Hydatica hirsuta

Hydatica hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hydatica, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hydatica hirsuta is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
