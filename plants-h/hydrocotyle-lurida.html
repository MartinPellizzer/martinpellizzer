Hydrocotyle lurida

Hydrocotyle lurida, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
