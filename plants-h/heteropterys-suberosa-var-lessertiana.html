Heteropterys suberosa var. lessertiana

Heteropterys suberosa var. lessertiana, commonly known as the red-leaved huito, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its thick, bark-like trunk and vibrant red or maroon foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and prominent veins. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by ellipsoid capsules containing numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
