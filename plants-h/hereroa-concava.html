Hereroa concava

Hereroa concava, commonly known as the concave herb, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

Hereroa concava is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
