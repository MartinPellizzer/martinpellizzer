Heptapleurum multifoliolatum

Heptapleurum multifoliolatum, commonly known as the broadleaf stone pine, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of elliptical, leathery leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its lush foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
