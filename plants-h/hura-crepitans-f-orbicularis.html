Hura crepitans f. orbicularis

Hura crepitans f. orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular hura, is a variant of the tropical tree Hura crepitans.

It is characterized by its broad, orbicular-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces large, showy yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal.

This plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.
