Hieracium callichlorum subsp. tzagwericum

Hieracium callichlorum subsp.

tzagwericum, commonly known as the Tzagweric hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native environment.
