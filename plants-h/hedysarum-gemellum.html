Hedysarum gemellum

Hedysarum gemellum, commonly known as the twinleaf peavine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers, typically pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. Hedysarum gemellum is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
