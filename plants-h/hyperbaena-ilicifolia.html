Hyperbaena ilicifolia

Hyperbaena ilicifolia, commonly known as the African holly or red holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought.

Hyperbaena ilicifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
