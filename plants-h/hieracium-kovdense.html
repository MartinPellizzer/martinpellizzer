Hieracium kovdense

Hieracium kovdense, commonly known as the Kovdense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
