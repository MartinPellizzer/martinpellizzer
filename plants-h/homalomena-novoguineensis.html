Homalomena novoguineensis

Homalomena novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
