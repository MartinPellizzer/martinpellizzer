Hieracium sonchoides subsp. richerianum

Hieracium sonchoides subsp.

richerianum, commonly known as the richeri's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
