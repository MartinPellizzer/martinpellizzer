Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. subintegerrimum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

subintegerrimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets and tubular disc florets.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.
