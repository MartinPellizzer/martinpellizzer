Hieracium sagittatum subsp. marginelliforme

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

marginelliforme, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers in a single, erect stem. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is known for its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves.

The subspecies marginelliforme is characterized by its broader leaf margins and slightly different floral morphology compared to other members of the species.
