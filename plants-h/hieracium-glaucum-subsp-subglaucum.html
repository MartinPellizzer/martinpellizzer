Hieracium glaucum subsp. subglaucum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

subglaucum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a whitish, powdery coating, known as a glaucous bloom. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its robust growth habit and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
