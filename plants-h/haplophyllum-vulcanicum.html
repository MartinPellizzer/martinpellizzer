Haplophyllum vulcanicum

Haplophyllum vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic rock plant, is a rare succulent native to the volcanic regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and fleshy, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the wet season, which are adapted for pollination by insects. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Haplophyllum vulcanicum is also noted for its slow growth and ability to survive in nutrient-poor, rocky soils.
