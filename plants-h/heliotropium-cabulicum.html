Heliotropium cabulicum

Heliotropium cabulicum, commonly known as Afghan heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are generally opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Heliotropium cabulicum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
