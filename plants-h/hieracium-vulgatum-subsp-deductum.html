Hieracium vulgatum subsp. deductum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

deductum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and the plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures and medicinal properties in some species.
