Herpestis pulcherrima

Herpestis pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is glossy and dark green, adding a striking contrast to its colorful inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
