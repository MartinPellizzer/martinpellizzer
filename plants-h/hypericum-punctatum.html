Hypericum punctatum

Hypericum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and yellow-orange stems, which are often adorned with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a rough texture.

Hypericum punctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
