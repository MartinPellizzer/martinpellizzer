Haloragis heterophylla var. rigida

Haloragis heterophylla var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy or clay soils and is often found in coastal or wetland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
