Hemiandrina obliqua

Hemiandrina obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in southeastern Australia.

It has a distinctive, flattened, leaf-like structure that helps it blend with its host, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, Hemiandrina obliqua is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
