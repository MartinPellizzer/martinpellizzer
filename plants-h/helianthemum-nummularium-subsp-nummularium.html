Helianthemum nummularium subsp. nummularium

Helianthemum nummularium subsp.

nummularium, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has silver-gray, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
