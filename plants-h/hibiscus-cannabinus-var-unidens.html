Hibiscus cannabinus var. unidens

Hibiscus cannabinus var. unidens, commonly known as the Cannabis hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in height, often in coastal or wetland habitats. It is known for its fibrous stalks, which have been historically used for making ropes and textiles.

The species is also notable for its potential medicinal and industrial applications due to its chemical composition.
