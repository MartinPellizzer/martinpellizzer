Hieracium marchesettianum subsp. putnokense

Hieracium marchesettianum subsp.

putnokense, commonly known as the Putnok hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous disk flowers that are surrounded by ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
