Hieracium cappadocicum var. congestum

Hieracium cappadocicum var. congestum, commonly known as the Cappadocian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
