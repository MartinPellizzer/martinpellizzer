Herorchis papilionacea subsp. palaestina

Herorchis papilionacea subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
