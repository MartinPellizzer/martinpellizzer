Henckelia wayanadensis

Henckelia wayanadensis, commonly known as the Wayanad henckelia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understoreys. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
