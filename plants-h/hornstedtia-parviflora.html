Hornstedtia parviflora

Hornstedtia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hornstedtia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It blooms during the cooler months, often in spring or early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
