Hieracium staticinum

Hieracium staticinum, commonly known as the bog hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in peat bogs and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
