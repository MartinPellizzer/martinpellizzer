Hieracium nigardense

Hieracium nigardense, commonly known as the Nigardense hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a short, creeping stem and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
