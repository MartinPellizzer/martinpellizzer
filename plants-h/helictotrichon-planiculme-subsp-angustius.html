Helictotrichon planiculme subsp. angustius

Helictotrichon planiculme subsp.

angustius, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
