Helianthemum guttatum var. macrosepalum

Helianthemum guttatum var. macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive waxy coating.

It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in gravelly soils.
