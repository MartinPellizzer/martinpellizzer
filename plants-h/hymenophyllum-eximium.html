Hymenophyllum eximium

Hymenophyllum eximium, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-white fronds that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and forest floors, where it thrives in high humidity. The plant's rhizomes are short and often form dense mats, helping it to anchor in rocky or soil substrates.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in the study of epiphytic and lithophytic ferns.
