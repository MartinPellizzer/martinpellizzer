Habenaria oerstedii

Habenaria oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, including South Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
