Hedera arborea aurea

Hedera arborea aurea, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with gold or yellow, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor decoration.
