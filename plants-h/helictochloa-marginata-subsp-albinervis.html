Helictochloa marginata subsp. albinervis

Helictochloa marginata subsp.

albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to white flowers. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and the veins are distinctly white, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
