Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. divaricatum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the divergent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are ascending or spreading.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
