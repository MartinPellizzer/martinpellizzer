Humularia rubrofarinacea

Humularia rubrofarinacea, commonly known as the red-freckled bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pink to red flowers that are often speckled with darker markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
