Hibiscus panduriformis var. australis

Hibiscus panduriformis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Hibiscus panduriformis var. australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
