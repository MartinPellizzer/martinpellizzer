Hieracium bracchiatum subsp. rastetteri

Hieracium bracchiatum subsp.

rastetteri, commonly known as the Rastetter's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species. It typically thrives in dry meadows and rocky soils, preferring full sun exposure.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
