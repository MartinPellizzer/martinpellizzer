Hedycarya perbracteolata

Hedycarya perbracteolata, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or Chinese waxflower, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedycarya perbracteolata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
