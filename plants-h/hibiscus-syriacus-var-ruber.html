Hibiscus syriacus var. ruber

Hibiscus syriacus var. ruber, commonly known as the red bush mallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, rose-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display and hardy nature.
