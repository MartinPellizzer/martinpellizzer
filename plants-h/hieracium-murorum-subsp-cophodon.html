Hieracium murorum subsp. cophodon

Hieracium murorum subsp.

cophodon, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
