Helianthus winteri

Helianthus winteri, commonly known as the winter sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have serrated edges.

Helianthus winteri is adapted to wetland habitats and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
