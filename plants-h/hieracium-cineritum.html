Hieracium cineritum

Hieracium cineritum, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and yellowish flower heads that resemble dandelions. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its spread.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
