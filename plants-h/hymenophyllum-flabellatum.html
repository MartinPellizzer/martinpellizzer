Hymenophyllum flabellatum

Hymenophyllum flabellatum, commonly known as the fan spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique structure with a prostrate, creeping rhizome and small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hymenophyllum flabellatum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
