Houstonia longifolia var. glabra

Houstonia longifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features small, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which set it apart from other varieties of Houstonia.
