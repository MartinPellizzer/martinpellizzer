Helianthemum origanifolium var. glabratum

Helianthemum origanifolium var. glabratum, commonly known as the glabrous rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compact, evergreen foliage with a distinctive aromatic scent, reminiscent of oregano. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
