Hieracium bifidum subsp. eurynotum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

eurynotum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
