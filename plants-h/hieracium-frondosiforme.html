Hieracium frondosiforme

Hieracium frondosiforme, commonly known as the fronded hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and hairy, often lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

Hieracium frondosiforme is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
