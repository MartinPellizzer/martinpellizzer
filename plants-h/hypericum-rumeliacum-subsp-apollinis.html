Hypericum rumeliacum subsp. apollinis

Hypericum rumeliacum subsp.

apollinis, commonly known as the Apollo gold St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Hypericaceae family.
