Herbertia Lahue Subsp. Caerulea

Herbertia lahue subsp. caerulea is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family known for its vibrant blue flowers. Native to the coastal regions of southern California, this plant thrives in sandy soils and mild climates. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. The species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, bell-shaped blooms. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles.
