Hesperis aprica

Hesperis aprica, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or spring scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and the plant often grows in meadows and woodland edges.

Hesperis aprica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
