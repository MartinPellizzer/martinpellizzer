Helichrysum nitens subsp. robynsii

Helichrysum nitens subsp.

robynsii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, bright yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble small sunbursts.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers surrounded by narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
