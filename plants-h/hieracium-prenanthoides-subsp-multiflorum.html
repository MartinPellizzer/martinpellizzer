Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. multiflorum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that bear numerous yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and the leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
