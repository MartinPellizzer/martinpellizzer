Hypericum splendens

Hypericum splendens, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody, branched stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
