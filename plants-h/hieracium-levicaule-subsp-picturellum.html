Hieracium levicaule subsp. picturellum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

picturellum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

The plant features upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
