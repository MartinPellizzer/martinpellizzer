Hydrangea umbellata

Hydrangea umbellata, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of sterile flowers that attract pollinators.

Hydrangea umbellata thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
