Hydrangea hirta

Hydrangea hirta, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. Its foliage is dark green and often turns reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Hydrangea hirta is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in partial shade.
