Hymenoxys quinquesquamata

Hymenoxys quinquesquamata, commonly known as the five-ribbed sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is characterized by its unique five-ribbed structure on the stem and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant's botanical features make it an attractive addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
