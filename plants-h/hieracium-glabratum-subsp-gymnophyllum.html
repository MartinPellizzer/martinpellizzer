Hieracium glabratum subsp. gymnophyllum

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

gymnophyllum, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy, while the upper leaves are more linear. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, dark brown achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
