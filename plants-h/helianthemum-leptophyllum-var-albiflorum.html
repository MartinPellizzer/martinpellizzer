Helianthemum leptophyllum var. albiflorum

Helianthemum leptophyllum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardening.
