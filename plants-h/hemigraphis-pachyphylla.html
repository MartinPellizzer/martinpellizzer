Hemigraphis pachyphylla

Hemigraphis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved iron plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub, with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hemigraphis pachyphylla is popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
