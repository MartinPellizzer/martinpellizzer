Hevea lutea f. pilosula

Hevea lutea f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy yellow rubber tree, is a variant of the rubber tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and a hairy texture on its stems and young shoots. This plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is often cultivated for its latex-producing properties. The leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive shape with serrated edges.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
