Hieracium sagittatum subsp. lasiocybe

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

lasiocybe, commonly known as the woolly spear-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and basal leaves that are often woolly or hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lanceolate, with serrated margins and a rough texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
