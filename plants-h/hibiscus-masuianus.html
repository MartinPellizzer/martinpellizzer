Hibiscus masuianus

Hibiscus masuianus, commonly known as the Kauai hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow stamens and a prominent, five-lobed pistil, which are typical features of the Malvaceae family.

It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii.
