Hylotelephium ohbae

Hylotelephium ohbae, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Japan.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
