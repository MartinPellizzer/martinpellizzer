Hibiscus obscurus

Hibiscus obscurus, commonly known as the dark hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the flowers have five petals that often curl backward.

Hibiscus obscurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
