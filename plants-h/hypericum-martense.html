Hypericum martense

Hypericum martense, commonly known as the Mexican St. John's wort, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers with orange stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, oval, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum martense is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
