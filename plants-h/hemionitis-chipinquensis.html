Hemionitis chipinquensis

Hemionitis chipinquensis, commonly known as the Mexican hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, compact rosette growth habit. The plant features delicate, leathery leaves that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis chipinquensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
