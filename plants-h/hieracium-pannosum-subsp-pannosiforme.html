Hieracium pannosum subsp. pannosiforme

Hieracium pannosum subsp.

pannosiforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
