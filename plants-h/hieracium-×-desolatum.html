Hieracium × desolatum

Hieracium × desolatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in certain regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
