Hymenoxys argentea

Hymenoxys argentea, commonly known as silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, silver-tomentose leaves. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous disk flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. Hymenoxys argentea thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in arid ecosystems.
