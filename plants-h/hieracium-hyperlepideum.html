Hieracium hyperlepideum

Hieracium hyperlepideum, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a tall, sparsely branched stem with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often lobed. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is characterized by its rough, hairy texture and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
