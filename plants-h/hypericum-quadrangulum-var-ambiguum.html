Hypericum quadrangulum var. ambiguum

Hypericum quadrangulum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the variable St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

This variety is noted for its variability in leaf shape and flower structure, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
