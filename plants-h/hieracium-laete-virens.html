Hieracium laete-virens

Hieracium laete-virens, commonly known as the cheerful green hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
