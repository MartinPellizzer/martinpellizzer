Hieracium murorum subsp. obtusidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

obtusidens, commonly known as the blunt-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy and bear several flower heads.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
