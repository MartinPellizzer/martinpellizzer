Hieracium onosmoides subsp. porphyritae

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

porphyritae, commonly known as the Porphyritic Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and the presence of porphyritic (purple or reddish) pigmentation in certain parts of the plant.
