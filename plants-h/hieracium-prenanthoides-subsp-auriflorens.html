Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. auriflorens

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

auriflorens, commonly known as the golden florets hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, attract various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more elongated flower heads and slightly larger florets compared to other members of the species.
