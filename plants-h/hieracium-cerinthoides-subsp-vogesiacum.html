Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. vogesiacum

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

vogesiacum, commonly known as the Voges hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are usually alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

This subspecies is native to Central and Southern Europe and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
