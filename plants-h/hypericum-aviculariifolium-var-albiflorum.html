Hypericum aviculariifolium var. albiflorum

Hypericum aviculariifolium var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in riparian zones.
