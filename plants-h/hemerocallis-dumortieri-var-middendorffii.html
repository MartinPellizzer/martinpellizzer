Hemerocallis dumortieri var. middendorffii

Hemerocallis dumortieri var. middendorffii, commonly known as the Middle Eastern daylily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
