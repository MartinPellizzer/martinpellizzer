Hieracium caesium subsp. fastigiellum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

fastigiellum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are typically clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
