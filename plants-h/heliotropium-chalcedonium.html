Heliotropium chalcedonium

Heliotropium chalcedonium, commonly known as the blue heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Heliotropium chalcedonium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
