Hieracium remanens

Hieracium remanens, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Hieracium remanens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
