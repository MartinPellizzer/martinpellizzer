Helichrysum spiciforme

Helichrysum spiciforme, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum spiciforme is also known for its aromatic foliage and ability to retain its color when dried, making it a valued plant in floral arrangements.
