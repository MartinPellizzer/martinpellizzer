Hibiscus syriacus var. purpureoplenus

Hibiscus syriacus var. purpureoplenus, commonly known as the purpleleaf weeping hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its striking purple foliage and cascading branches.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance once established.

This variety is well-suited for garden borders or as a specimen plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
