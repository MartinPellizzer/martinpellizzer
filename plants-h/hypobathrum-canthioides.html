Hypobathrum canthioides

Hypobathrum canthioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically forms dense mats of fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Hypobathrum canthioides is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky outcrops.
