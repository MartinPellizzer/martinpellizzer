Hieracium × caesariatum

Hieracium × caesariatum, commonly known as the Caesar's weed, is a hybrid species within the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its vibrant green leaves with a waxy coating and bright yellow flower heads that resemble those of dandelions. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered both a weed and a wildflower in various regions.
