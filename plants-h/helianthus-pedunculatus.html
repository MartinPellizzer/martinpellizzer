Helianthus pedunculatus

Helianthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, supporting large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus pedunculatus is adapted to moist soils and plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
