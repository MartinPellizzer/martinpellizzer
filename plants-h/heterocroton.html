Heterocroton

Heterocroton, commonly known as the croton plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a variety of shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant typically has opposite, simple, and sometimes lobed leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

Heterocroton is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
