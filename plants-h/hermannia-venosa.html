Hermannia venosa

Hermannia venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple to red bracts that surround its showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.
