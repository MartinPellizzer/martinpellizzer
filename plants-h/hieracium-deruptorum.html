Hieracium deruptorum

Hieracium deruptorum, commonly known as the broken-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and its leaves are usually hairy and serrated.

Hieracium deruptorum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
