Hieracium magyaricum subsp. anisocladum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

anisocladum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The subspecies anisocladum is distinguished by its variable leaf shapes and growth habits, which can range from prostrate to upright. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
