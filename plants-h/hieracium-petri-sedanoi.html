Hieracium petri-sedanoi

Hieracium petri-sedanoi, commonly known as the Petri-Sedano's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. It features yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
