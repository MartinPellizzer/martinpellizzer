Heuchera hirsuticaulis

Heuchera hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy stalk heuchera, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and delicate blooms, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
