Hieracium pycnadenium

Hieracium pycnadenium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its flowers are yellow, forming a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, achene-like seeds for reproduction.
