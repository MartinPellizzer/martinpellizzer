Hieracium chlorocephalum subsp. chlorocephalum

Hieracium chlorocephalum subsp.

chlorocephalum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
