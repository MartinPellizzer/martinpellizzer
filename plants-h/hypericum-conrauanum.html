Hypericum conrauanum

Hypericum conrauanum, commonly known as the Conradian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Hypericum conrauanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
