Hieracium caesium subsp. munkacsense

Hieracium caesium subsp.

munkacsense, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering structure compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
