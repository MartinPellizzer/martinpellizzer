Hieracium crispicans

Hieracium crispicans, commonly known as the curly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium crispicans is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
