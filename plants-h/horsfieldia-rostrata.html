Horsfieldia rostrata

Horsfieldia rostrata, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, deep red petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Horsfieldia rostrata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
