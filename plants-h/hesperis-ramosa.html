Hesperis ramosa

Hesperis ramosa, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
