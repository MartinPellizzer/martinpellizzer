Hieracium hirtellifolium

Hieracium hirtellifolium, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
