Helianthus quinquelobus

Helianthus quinquelobus, commonly known as the five-lobed sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds in a composite flower head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in botany.
