Hookera purdyi

Hookera purdyi, commonly known as the Purdy's hooker, is a rare alpine plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hair, which helps to insulate it from cold temperatures. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hookera purdyi is considered a vulnerable species.
