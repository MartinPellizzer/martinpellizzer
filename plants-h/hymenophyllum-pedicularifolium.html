Hymenophyllum pedicularifolium

Hymenophyllum pedicularifolium, commonly known as the slender haircap moss, is a small, delicate moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called rhizoids. This species typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, called sporophytes, are small and inconspicuous, producing spores that are dispersed by wind.

The plant's intricate morphology and specialized adaptations make it an important component of its ecological niche.
