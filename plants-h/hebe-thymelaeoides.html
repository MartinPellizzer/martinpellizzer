Hebe thymelaeoides

Hebe thymelaeoides, commonly known as the silver hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are aromatic and often have a slightly fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hebe thymelaeoides is well-suited to coastal environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
