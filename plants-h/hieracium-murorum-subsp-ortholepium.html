Hieracium murorum subsp. ortholepium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

ortholepium, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are spoon-shaped and hairy, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is a low-growing plant that rarely exceeds 30 cm in height.
