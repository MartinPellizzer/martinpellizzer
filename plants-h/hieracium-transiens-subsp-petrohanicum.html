Hieracium transiens subsp. petrohanicum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

petrohanicum, commonly known as the Petrohan's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its specific ecological requirements.
