Hieracium permagnidens

Hieracium permagnidens, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a robust stem and large, lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium permagnidens is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
