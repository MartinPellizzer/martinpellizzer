Hieracium lactucella subsp. nanum

Hieracium lactucella subsp.

nanum, commonly known as the small milk thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its smaller size compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
