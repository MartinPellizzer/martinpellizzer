Hemionitis doniana

Hemionitis doniana, commonly known as the desert waterwheel, is a rare and unique plant native to arid regions of China and Mongolia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves. The plant features small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale pink. Hemionitis doniana is well adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
