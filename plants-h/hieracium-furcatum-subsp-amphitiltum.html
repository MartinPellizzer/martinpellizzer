Hieracium furcatum subsp. amphitiltum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

amphitiltum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh alpine conditions and is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
