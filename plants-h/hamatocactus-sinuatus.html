Hamatocactus sinuatus

Hamatocactus sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinaloa sunburst cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive ribbed, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
