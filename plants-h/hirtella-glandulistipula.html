Hirtella glandulistipula

Hirtella glandulistipula, commonly known as the glossy hirtella, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Hirtella glandulistipula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in tropical garden settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for the biodiversity of the Atlantic Forest ecosystem.
