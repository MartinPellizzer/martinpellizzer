Heliocarpus tigrinus

Heliocarpus tigrinus, commonly known as the tiger's claw, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky fruits that resemble claws, which are adapted for animal dispersal. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
