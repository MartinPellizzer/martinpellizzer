Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. pseudosparsiflorum

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

pseudosparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of plant adaptation and distribution.
