Hexapterella gentianoides

Hexapterella gentianoides, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Hexapterella gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
