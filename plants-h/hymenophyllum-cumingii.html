Hymenophyllum cumingii

Hymenophyllum cumingii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum cumingii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
