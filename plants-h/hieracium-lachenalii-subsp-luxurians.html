Hieracium lachenalii subsp. luxurians

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its rapid spread through vegetative reproduction, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features include finely divided leaves and a lack of a central stem, with flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.
