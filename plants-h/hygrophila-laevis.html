Hygrophila laevis

Hygrophila laevis, commonly known as the slender water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Hygrophila laevis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
