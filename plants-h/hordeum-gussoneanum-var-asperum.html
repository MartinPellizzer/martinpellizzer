Hordeum gussoneanum var. asperum

Hordeum gussoneanum var. asperum, commonly known as rough barley, is a variant of the barley species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rough, bristly texture on the leaves and stems, which distinguishes it from other barley varieties. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to brown grains.

Hordeum gussoneanum var. asperum is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
