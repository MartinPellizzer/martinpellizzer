Haemanthus ceciliae

Haemanthus ceciliae, commonly known as the Natal star.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa, characterized by its striking star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its inflorescence consists of multiple white to pinkish flowers arranged in a star pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Haemanthus ceciliae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
