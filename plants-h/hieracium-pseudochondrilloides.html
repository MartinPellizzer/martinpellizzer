Hieracium pseudochondrilloides

Hieracium pseudochondrilloides, commonly known as the false sandwort hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
