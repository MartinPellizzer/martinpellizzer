Holcus × azoricus

Holcus × azoricus, commonly known as the Azorean bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Holcus × azoricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
