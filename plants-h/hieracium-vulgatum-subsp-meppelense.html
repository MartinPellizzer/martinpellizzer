Hieracium vulgatum subsp. meppelense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

meppelense, commonly known as the Meppel hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and lanceolate leaves.

This subspecies is often found in sandy or calcareous soils and is part of the Asteraceae family.
