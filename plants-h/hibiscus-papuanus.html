Hibiscus papuanus

Hibiscus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, including Papua New Guinea and the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Hibiscus papuanus is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests, where it grows as an understorey plant.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
