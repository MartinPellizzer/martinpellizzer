Hemisphaerocarya suffruticosa var. typica

Hemisphaerocarya suffruticosa var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
