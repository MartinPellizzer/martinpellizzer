Hieracium lusitanicum

Hieracium lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
