Heteropterys umbellata var. puberula

Heteropterys umbellata var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which consists of clustered, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a hairy texture, giving the plant its varietal name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
