Hieracium racemosum subsp. ageratoides

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

ageratoides, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense clumps.

The plant features yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a raceme, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is distinguished by its composite flower heads.
