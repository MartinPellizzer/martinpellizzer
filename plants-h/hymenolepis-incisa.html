Hymenolepis incisa

Hymenolepis incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves that are often deeply incised, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Hymenolepis incisa is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
