Hieracium myriocephalum

Hieracium myriocephalum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in a dense, spherical inflorescence. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Hieracium myriocephalum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
