Hydrangea stylosa

Hydrangea stylosa, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in summer.

Hydrangea stylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
