Hieracium caesium subsp. lugiorum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

lugiorum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
