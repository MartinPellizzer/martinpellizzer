Hamatocactus hamatacanthus

Hamatocactus hamatacanthus, commonly known as the needle-cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, slender stem covered in sharp, spine-like structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
