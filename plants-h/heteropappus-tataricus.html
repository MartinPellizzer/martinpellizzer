Heteropappus tataricus

Heteropappus tataricus, commonly known as the Tatarian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
