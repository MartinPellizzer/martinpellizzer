Helichrysum rugatum

Helichrysum rugatum, commonly known as the rough everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a textured appearance.

Helichrysum rugatum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and ornamental value.
