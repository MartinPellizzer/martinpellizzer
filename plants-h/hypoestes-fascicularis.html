Hypoestes fascicularis

Hypoestes fascicularis, commonly known as the "Indian Pink," is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
