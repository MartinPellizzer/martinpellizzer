Helleborus serbicus

Helleborus serbicus, commonly known as the Serbian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and clusters of pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing various cardiac glycosides.

Helleborus serbicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
