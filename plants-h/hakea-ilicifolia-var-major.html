Hakea ilicifolia var. major

Hakea ilicifolia var. major, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and distinctive cylindrical or urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces woody, spine-tipped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by birds. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
