Hieracium pujattii

Hieracium pujattii, commonly known as the Pujatti's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in the Alps. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium pujattii is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.
