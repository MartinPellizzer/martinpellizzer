Horanthes arenaria

Horanthes arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

Horanthes arenaria is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
