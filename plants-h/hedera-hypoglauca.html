Hedera hypoglauca

Hedera hypoglauca, commonly known as the silver ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used as a decorative ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
