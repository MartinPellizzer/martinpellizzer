Hieracium vernantianum

Hieracium vernantianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium vernantianum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
