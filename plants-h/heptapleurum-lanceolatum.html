Heptapleurum lanceolatum

Heptapleurum lanceolatum, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen shrub, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are lanceolate in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and garden landscaping.
