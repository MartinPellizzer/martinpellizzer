Helwingia himalaica f. oblanceolata

Helwingia himalaica f. oblanceolata, commonly known as the Himalayan helwingia, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to oblanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn a vibrant red in the autumn. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
