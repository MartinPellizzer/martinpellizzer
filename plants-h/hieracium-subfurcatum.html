Hieracium subfurcatum

Hieracium subfurcatum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be an invasive species in certain regions.
