Heliopsis mollis

Heliopsis mollis, commonly known as the soft heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that add a delicate texture to the landscape.

Heliopsis mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
