Helichrysum arenarium subsp. rubicundum

Helichrysum arenarium subsp.

rubicundum, commonly known as the red-haired goldfields, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-brown stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
