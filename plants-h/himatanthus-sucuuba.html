Himatanthus sucuuba

Himatanthus sucuuba, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that produces striking yellow flowers with a strong, sweet scent. The plant has leathery, oval-shaped leaves and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
