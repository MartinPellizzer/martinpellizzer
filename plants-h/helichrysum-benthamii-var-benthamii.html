Helichrysum benthamii var. benthamii

Helichrysum benthamii var. benthamii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, papery flowers that remain intact for a long period after blooming. The plant typically grows in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a source of natural dyes.
