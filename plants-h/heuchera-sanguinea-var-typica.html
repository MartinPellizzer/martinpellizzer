Heuchera sanguinea var. typica

Heuchera sanguinea var. typica, commonly known as the red-leaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking crimson to purple foliage, which is often variegated with green or white margins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant leaves and long blooming period, which extend from June to August.
