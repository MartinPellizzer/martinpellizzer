Hyptis elegans var. pascuicola

Hyptis elegans var. pascuicola, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis elegans var. pascuicola is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
