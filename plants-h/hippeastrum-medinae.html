Hippeastrum medinae

Hippeastrum medinae, commonly known as the Medina amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb and can grow up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to produce its vibrant blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
