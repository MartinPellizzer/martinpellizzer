Hieracium leptophyton subsp. bauhiniflorum

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

bauhiniflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
