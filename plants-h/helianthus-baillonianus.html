Helianthus baillonianus

Helianthus baillonianus, commonly known as the Bailloni's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, yellow composite flowers and robust, hairy stems. It produces both ray and disk flowers, typical of the sunflower genus.

Helianthus baillonianus is often found in disturbed soils and can serve as a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
