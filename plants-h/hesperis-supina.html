Hesperis supina

Hesperis supina, commonly known as the nodding sweet William, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with four petals arranged in a cross shape. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Hesperis supina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
