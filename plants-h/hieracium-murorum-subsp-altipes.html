Hieracium murorum subsp. altipes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

altipes, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a central stem bearing yellow flower heads. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray florets and tubular florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in high-altitude environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
