Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lampadiotum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lampadiotum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette growth habit with basal leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with yellow central disk florets, and it produces small, hairy achenes as its seeds.

The subspecies lampadiotum is distinguished by its more compact form and darker flower coloration compared to other subspecies of Hieracium nigrescens.
