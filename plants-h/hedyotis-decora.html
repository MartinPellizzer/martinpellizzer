Hedyotis decora

Hedyotis decora, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis decora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
