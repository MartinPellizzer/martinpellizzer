Hieracium florentinum subsp. roxense

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

roxense, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and its stems are upright with few branches.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
