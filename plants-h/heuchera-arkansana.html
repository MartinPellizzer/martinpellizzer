Heuchera arkansana

Heuchera arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas alumroot, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
