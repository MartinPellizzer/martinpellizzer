Hordeum vulgare subsp. spontaneum

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

spontaneum, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild relative of cultivated barley. It is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has slender stems and spikelets that bear small, two-rowed grains.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important resource for breeding programs aimed at improving crop resilience.
