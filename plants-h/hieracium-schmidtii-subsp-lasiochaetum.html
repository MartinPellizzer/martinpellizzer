Hieracium schmidtii subsp. lasiochaetum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

lasiochaetum, commonly known as the rough-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with stems that are also covered in short, stiff hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
