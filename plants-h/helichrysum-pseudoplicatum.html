Helichrysum pseudoplicatum

Helichrysum pseudoplicatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or false pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are generally hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The flowers are long-lasting and often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and durability.
