Heracleum intermedium

Heracleum intermedium, commonly known as the European cow parsnip, is a tall biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant can grow up to 2 meters in height, with a hollow stem that is often hairy. Its roots are thick and tuberous, and the plant is known for its strong, aromatic odor.

It is typically found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks across Europe.
