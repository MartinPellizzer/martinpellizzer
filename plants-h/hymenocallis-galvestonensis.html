Hymenocallis galvestonensis

Hymenocallis galvestonensis, commonly known as Galveston spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Texas.

It is characterized by its striking white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows from a bulb and produces strap-like, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Hymenocallis galvestonensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.
