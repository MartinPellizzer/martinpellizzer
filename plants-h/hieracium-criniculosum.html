Hieracium criniculosum

Hieracium criniculosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium criniculosum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
