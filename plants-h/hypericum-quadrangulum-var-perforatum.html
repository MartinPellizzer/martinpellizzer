Hypericum quadrangulum var. perforatum

Hypericum quadrangulum var. perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite, four-angled stems. The leaves are simple, oval, and often have small perforations, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.
