Hoya platycaulis

Hoya platycaulis, commonly known as the flat-stemmed wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in the summer months. Hoya platycaulis is a succulent plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
