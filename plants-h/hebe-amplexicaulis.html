Hebe amplexicaulis

Hebe amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that often wraps around the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
