Hydroglossum hirsutum

Hydroglossum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy water lily, is a water-dwelling plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, which help reduce water loss and provide support for the plant's reproductive structures. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, typically during the warmer months. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes, allowing it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Hydroglossum hirsutum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
