Heliotropium zeylanicum subsp. paniculatum

Heliotropium zeylanicum subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the Indian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have contributed to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
