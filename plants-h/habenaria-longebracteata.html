Habenaria longebracteata

Habenaria longebracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

The orchid has a complex root system and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
