Hypserpa cuspidata var. microphylla

Hypserpa cuspidata var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
