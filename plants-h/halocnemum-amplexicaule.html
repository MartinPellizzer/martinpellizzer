Halocnemum amplexicaule

Halocnemum amplexicaule, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline and arid environments.

It is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to thrive in sandy or coastal soils.

Halocnemum amplexicaule plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in harsh, saline habitats.
