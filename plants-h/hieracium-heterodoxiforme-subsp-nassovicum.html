Hieracium heterodoxiforme subsp. nassovicum

Hieracium heterodoxiforme subsp.

nassovicum, commonly known as the Alpheid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing a compact growth form with hairy stems and basal leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are generally lanceolate to ovate, with serrated margins, and the flowers have five spreading petals.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
