Hyparrhenia schmidiana

Hyparrhenia schmidiana, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Hyparrhenia schmidiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
