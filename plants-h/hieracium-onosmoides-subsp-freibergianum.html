Hieracium onosmoides subsp. freibergianum

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

freibergianum, commonly known as the Freiberg's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
