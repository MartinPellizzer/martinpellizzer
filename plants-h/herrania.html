Herrania

Herrania, commonly known as the nightshade or devil's fig, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Herrania species are typically shrubs or small trees with smooth, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers that are usually white or purple, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
