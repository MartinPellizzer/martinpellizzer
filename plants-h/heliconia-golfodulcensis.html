Heliconia golfodulcensis

Heliconia golfodulcensis, commonly known as the sweet-scented heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces long, segmented flower spikes that are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Heliconia golfodulcensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
