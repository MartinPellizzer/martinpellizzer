Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp. obscurum

Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp.

obscurum, commonly known as the dark rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats, thriving in full sun.
