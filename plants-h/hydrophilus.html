Hydrophilus

Hydrophilus, commonly known as the water-loving plant, is a genus of flowering plants that thrives in moist or aquatic environments.

These plants typically have adapted structures such as broad leaves to maximize water absorption and specialized root systems for efficient nutrient uptake from water. Many species within the genus possess reduced leaves or stems to minimize water loss, while others have developed floating leaves to remain on the water's surface. Hydrophilus plants are often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers.

Their botanical features make them essential components of aquatic ecosystems, supporting biodiversity and ecological balance.
