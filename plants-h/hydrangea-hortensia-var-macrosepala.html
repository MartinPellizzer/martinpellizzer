Hydrangea hortensia var. macrosepala

Hydrangea hortensia var. macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant produces sterile flowers, which are typically pink or blue, depending on soil pH. Its foliage is dark green and turns red in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

This variety thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
