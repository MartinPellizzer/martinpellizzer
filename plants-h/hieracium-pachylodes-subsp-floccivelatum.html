Hieracium pachylodes subsp. floccivelatum

Hieracium pachylodes subsp.

floccivelatum, commonly known as the woolly yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. It has a rosette-like growth habit with basal leaves that are covered in a thick layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
