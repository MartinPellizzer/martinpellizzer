Hyphaene aurantiaca

Hyphaene aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden fan palm, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife. Its adaptability to salty air and coastal conditions makes it a common sight in mangrove ecosystems.

The golden fan palm is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
