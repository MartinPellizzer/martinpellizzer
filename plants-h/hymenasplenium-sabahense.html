Hymenasplenium sabahense

Hymenasplenium sabahense, commonly known as the Sabah spleenwort, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the Bornean rainforests.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae, characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance and preference for humid, shaded environments. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or tree trunks, forming dense, carpet-like colonies. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a critically important species for conservation efforts in Southeast Asia.
