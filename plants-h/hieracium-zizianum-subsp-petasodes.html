Hieracium zizianum subsp. petasodes

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

petasodes, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by yellow to orange ray florets, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
