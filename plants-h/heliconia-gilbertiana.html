Heliconia gilbertiana

Heliconia gilbertiana, commonly known as the Gilbert's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a crown of flowers. The plant has elongated, waxy leaves that form a rosette pattern, providing a distinctive visual appearance. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with three prominent lips that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bats.

Heliconia gilbertiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
