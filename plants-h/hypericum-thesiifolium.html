Hypericum thesiifolium

Hypericum thesiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems covered in yellow-orange flowers that bloom from June to August. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish hue, especially when exposed to sunlight. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested.
