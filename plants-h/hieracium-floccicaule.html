Hieracium floccicaule

Hieracium floccicaule, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white or pale yellow capitula, which resemble a cloud or a ball of wool, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It has basal and stem leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium floccicaule is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various regions.
