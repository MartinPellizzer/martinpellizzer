Hieracium dollineri subsp. tephromelanum

Hieracium dollineri subsp.

tephromelanum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a dark green to nearly black coloration. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is notable for its distinctive foliage and is often found in calcareous soils.
