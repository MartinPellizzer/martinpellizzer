Hippeastrum ambiguum

Hippeastrum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and grow from a bulbous stem. Hippeastrum ambiguum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
