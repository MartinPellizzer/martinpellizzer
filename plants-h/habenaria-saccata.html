Habenaria saccata

Habenaria saccata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, sac-like floral structure that resembles a small pouch. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive pouch-shaped lip, and it produces small, rounded pseudobulbs.

Habenaria saccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unusual floral morphology.
