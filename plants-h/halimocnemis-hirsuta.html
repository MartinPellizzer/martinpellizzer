Halimocnemis hirsuta

Halimocnemis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy halimocnemis, is a small, perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to store moisture.

Halimocnemis hirsuta is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, low-nutrient conditions.
