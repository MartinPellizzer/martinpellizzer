Hippeastrum crocatum

Hippeastrum crocatum, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, with vibrant red petals and a prominent yellow center. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hippeastrum crocatum is often cultivated for its dramatic blooms and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
