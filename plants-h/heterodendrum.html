Heterodendrum

Heterodendrum, commonly known as the false bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped or tubular flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, blue, and purple. The leaves are typically alternate and lanceolate, with a rough or hairy texture. Heterodendrum species are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
