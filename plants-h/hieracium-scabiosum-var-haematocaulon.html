Hieracium scabiosum var. haematocaulon

Hieracium scabiosum var. haematocaulon, commonly known as the bloody stem hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark red or purple stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
