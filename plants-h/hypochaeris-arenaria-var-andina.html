Hypochaeris arenaria var. andina

Hypochaeris arenaria var. andina, commonly known as Andean mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a mat-forming habit, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape.

It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value.
