Hieracium bifidum subsp. coriifolium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

coriifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped, with a velvety texture. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
