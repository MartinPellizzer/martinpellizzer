Hieracium stenolope

Hieracium stenolope, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Hieracium stenolope is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered invasive in some regions.
