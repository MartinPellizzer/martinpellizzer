Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lingulatum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lingulatum, commonly known as the black knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with a rough texture, and the flowers are small, purple to brown in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
