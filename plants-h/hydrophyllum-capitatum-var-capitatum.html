Hydrophyllum capitatum var. capitatum

Hydrophyllum capitatum var. capitatum, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are hairy and often have a slight aromatic scent.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy member of the Asteraceae family.
