Hieracium sartorianum subsp. orphanidianum

Hieracium sartorianum subsp.

orphanidianum, commonly known as the orphanidian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphology and geographic distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
