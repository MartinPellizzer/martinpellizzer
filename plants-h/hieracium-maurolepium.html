Hieracium maurolepium

Hieracium maurolepium, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with dark veins, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
