Hearnia macrophylla

Hearnia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant heath or Hearn's heath, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are large, leathery, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Hearnia macrophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
