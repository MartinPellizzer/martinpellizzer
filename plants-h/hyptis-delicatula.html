Hyptis delicatula

Hyptis delicatula, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Hyptis delicatula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
