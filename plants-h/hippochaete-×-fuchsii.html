Hippochaete × fuchsii

Hippochaete × fuchsii, commonly known as the hybrid fuchsii, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Hippochaete fuchsii and Hippochaete phrygana. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed in horticulture.
