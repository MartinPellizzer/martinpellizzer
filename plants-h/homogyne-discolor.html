Homogyne discolor

Homogyne discolor, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that are often spotted with white or yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Homogyne discolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
