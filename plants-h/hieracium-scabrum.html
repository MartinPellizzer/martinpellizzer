Hieracium scabrum

Hieracium scabrum, commonly known as rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive silvery-gray appearance, and its stems are upright and branched.

Hieracium scabrum produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
