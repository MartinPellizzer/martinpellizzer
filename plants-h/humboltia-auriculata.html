Humboltia auriculata

Humboltia auriculata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with distinctive, auriculate (ear-shaped) bracts. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Humboltia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
