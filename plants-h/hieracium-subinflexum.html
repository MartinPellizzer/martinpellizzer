Hieracium subinflexum

Hieracium subinflexum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
