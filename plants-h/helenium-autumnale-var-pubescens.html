Helenium autumnale var. pubescens

Helenium autumnale var. pubescens, commonly known as the rough red swamp lily, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and showy, orange-red to yellow-orange ray flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often reddish underneath.

The flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
