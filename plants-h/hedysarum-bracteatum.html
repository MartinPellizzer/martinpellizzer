Hedysarum bracteatum

Hedysarum bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils.

Hedysarum bracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
