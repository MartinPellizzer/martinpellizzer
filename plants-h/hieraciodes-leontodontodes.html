Hieraciodes leontodontodes

Hieraciodes leontodontodes, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy purple or pinkish-yellow flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy stems and has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
