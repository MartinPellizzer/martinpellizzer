Hieracium vermlandicum

Hieracium vermlandicum, commonly known as the Swedish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium vermlandicum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
