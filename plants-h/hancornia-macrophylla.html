Hancornia macrophylla

Hancornia macrophylla, commonly known as the Amazonian trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hancornia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
