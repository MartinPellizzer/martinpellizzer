Hypericum quadrangulum

Hypericum quadrangulum, commonly known as the four-cornered St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with four distinct angles, giving it its common name. Its flowers are bright yellow with five petals and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hypericum quadrangulum is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it blooms from June to August.
