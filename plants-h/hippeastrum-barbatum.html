Hippeastrum barbatum

Hippeastrum barbatum, commonly known as the Brazilian fire lily, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces striking, strap-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum barbatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
