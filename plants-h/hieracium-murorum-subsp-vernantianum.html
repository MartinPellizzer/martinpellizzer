Hieracium murorum subsp. vernantianum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

vernantianum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
