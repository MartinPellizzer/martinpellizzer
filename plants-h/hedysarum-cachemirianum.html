Hedysarum cachemirianum

Hedysarum cachemirianum, commonly known as Kashmir peavine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and high altitudes.
