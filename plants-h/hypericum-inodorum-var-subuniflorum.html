Hypericum inodorum var. subuniflorum

Hypericum inodorum var. subuniflorum, commonly known as the sweet shrub or fragrant St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical, reddish-brown fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value comes from its aromatic foliage and showy flowers, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
