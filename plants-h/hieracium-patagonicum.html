Hieracium patagonicum

Hieracium patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-orange.

Hieracium patagonicum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
