Hymenocallis laciniata

Hymenocallis laciniata, commonly known as the slitleaf spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spidery appearance, often with a red or orange throat. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a unique visual texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hymenocallis laciniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
