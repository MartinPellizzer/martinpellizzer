Horsfieldia obtusa

Horsfieldia obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf orchid, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and requires high humidity and indirect light. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Horsfieldia obtusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
