Hippeophyllum sulense

Hippeophyllum sulense, commonly known as the Sulawesi starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to the island of Sulawesi in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the unique, star-shaped berries. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
