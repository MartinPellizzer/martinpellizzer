Hieracium gothicum var. blekingense

Hieracium gothicum var. blekingense, commonly known as the Blekinge hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the flowers are yellow, often arranged in a single head.

It is noted for its ecological value, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
