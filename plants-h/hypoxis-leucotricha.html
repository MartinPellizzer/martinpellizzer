Hypoxis leucotricha

Hypoxis leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a dense inflorescence at the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Hypoxis leucotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
