Heracleum leskovii var. leskovii

Heracleum leskovii var. leskovii, commonly known as the Leskov's hogweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that are often covered with coarse hairs. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences with small white or greenish flowers. Its leaves are large, deeply lobed, and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

It is important to note that this plant contains phototoxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
