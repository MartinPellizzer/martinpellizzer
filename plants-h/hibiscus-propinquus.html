Hibiscus propinquus

Hibiscus propinquus, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hibiscus propinquus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
