Hieracium dentatum subsp. euryphyllum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

euryphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads arranged in a raceme.

The species is characterized by its broad, toothed leaves and is often found in temperate regions with well-drained soils.
