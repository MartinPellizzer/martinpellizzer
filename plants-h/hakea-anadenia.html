Hakea anadenia

Hakea anadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Hakea anadenia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
