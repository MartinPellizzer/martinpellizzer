Hypoxis baurii var. baurii

Hypoxis baurii var. baurii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of star-shaped, white flowers that open at night. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
