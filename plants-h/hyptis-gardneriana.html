Hyptis gardneriana

Hyptis gardneriana, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal.

Hyptis gardneriana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
