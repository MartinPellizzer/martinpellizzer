Hieracium onegense

Hieracium onegense, commonly known as the Onega hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of Europe and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium onegense is often found in moist to dry habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
