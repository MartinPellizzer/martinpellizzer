Hieracium atratum subsp. nordlandicum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

nordlandicum, commonly known as the northern ragged robin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring damp, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a dense, showy cluster at the end of each stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its value in wetland conservation efforts.
