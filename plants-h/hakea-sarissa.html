Hakea sarissa

Hakea sarissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain numerous small, yellowish seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea sarissa is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
