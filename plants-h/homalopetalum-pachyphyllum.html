Homalopetalum pachyphyllum

Homalopetalum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid environment. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
