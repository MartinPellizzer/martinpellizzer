Huperzia spongiosa

Huperzia spongiosa, commonly known as the sponge moss, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of small, scale-like structures. It is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of North America, Europe, and Asia.

Huperzia spongiosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground covers.
