Helictotrichon elongatum

Helictotrichon elongatum, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Helictotrichon elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
