Helleborus niger

Helleborus niger, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces a fleshy, woody root system and has a preference for moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antispasmodic and sedative effects.

Helleborus niger is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
