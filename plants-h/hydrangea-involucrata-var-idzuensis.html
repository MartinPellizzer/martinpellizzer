Hydrangea involucrata var. idzuensis

Hydrangea involucrata var. idzuensis, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
