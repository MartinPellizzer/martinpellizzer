Hypericum veronense

Hypericum veronense, commonly known as the Veronese St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period extends from July to September, making it a notable addition to gardens and natural habitats.

Hypericum veronense is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
