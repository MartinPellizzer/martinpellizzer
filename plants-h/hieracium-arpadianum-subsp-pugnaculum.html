Hieracium arpadianum subsp. pugnaculum

Hieracium arpadianum subsp.

pugnaculum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowering stems are usually hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth form and is often found in rocky or grassy slopes at higher elevations.
