Hapalochilus trichromus

Hapalochilus trichromus, commonly known as the three-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers that display three distinct hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms that are highly attractive to pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
