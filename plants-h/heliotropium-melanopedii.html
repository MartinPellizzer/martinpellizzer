Heliotropium melanopedii

Heliotropium melanopedii, commonly known as the blackfoot heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a rough or hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
