Hypericum erectum var. parviflorum

Hypericum erectum var. parviflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems covered in yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
