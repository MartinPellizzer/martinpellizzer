Hyparrhenia pendula

Hyparrhenia pendula, commonly known as the hanging grass or slender bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its drooping inflorescences, which give it its common name, and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, savannas, and disturbed sites, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Hyparrhenia pendula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals.
