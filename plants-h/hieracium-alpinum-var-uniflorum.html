Hieracium alpinum var. uniflorum

Hieracium alpinum var. uniflorum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a single flower head. Its flowers are yellow and composite, consisting of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk.

It is often found in high-altitude environments and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
