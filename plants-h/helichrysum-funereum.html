Helichrysum funereum

Helichrysum funereum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, rounded inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.
