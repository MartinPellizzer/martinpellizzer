Hieracium aphelophyllum

Hieracium aphelophyllum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium aphelophyllum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a persistent weed and a resilient wildflower.
