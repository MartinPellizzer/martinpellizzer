Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. vaillantii

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

vaillantii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces a branched inflorescence of yellow flowers with ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom from June to August.

This subspecies is characterized by its bright yellow color and is often found in sunny, open habitats.
