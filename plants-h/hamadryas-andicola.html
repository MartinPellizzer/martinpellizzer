Hamadryas andicola

Hamadryas andicola, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in alpine meadows.

Hamadryas andicola plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
