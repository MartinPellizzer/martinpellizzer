Hymenophyllum heimii

Hymenophyllum heimii, commonly known as the silver moss, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are delicate, with a silvery appearance due to the presence of dense, fine trichomes. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
