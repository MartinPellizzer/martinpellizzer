Helictotrichon versicolor proles caucasicum

Helictotrichon versicolor proles caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, blue-green to violet-colored flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and moderate growth requirements.
