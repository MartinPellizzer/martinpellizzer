Hypericum styphelioides subsp. clarense

Hypericum styphelioides subsp.

clarense, commonly known as the Clarense St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be challenging to grow in non-native regions.
