Hibbertia aspera subsp. pilosifolia

Hibbertia aspera subsp.

pilosifolia, commonly known as the silky hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The species produces showy yellow flowers with prominent red or orange stamens, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry sclerophyll forests and rocky areas. Hibbertia aspera subsp.

pilosifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
