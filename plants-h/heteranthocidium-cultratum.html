Heteranthocidium cultratum

Heteranthocidium cultratum, commonly known as the cutleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical features include a rigid, upright stem and a fibrous root system.

Heteranthocidium cultratum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
