Hieracium buschianum

Hieracium buschianum, commonly known as the Busch's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
