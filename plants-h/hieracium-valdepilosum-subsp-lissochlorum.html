Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. lissochlorum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

lissochlorum, commonly known as the smoothleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically smooth and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
