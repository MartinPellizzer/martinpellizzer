Hieracium orthoglossum

Hieracium orthoglossum, commonly known as the long-tongued hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow with elongated petals, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium orthoglossum is known for its adaptability and is often found in sunny, open habitats.
