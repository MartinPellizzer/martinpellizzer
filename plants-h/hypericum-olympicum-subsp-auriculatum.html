Hypericum olympicum subsp. auriculatum

Hypericum olympicum subsp.

auriculatum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its distinctive, slightly hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
