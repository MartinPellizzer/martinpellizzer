Heuchera versicolor

Heuchera versicolor, commonly known as the alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a striking violet or red underside, and its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or red. The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage changes color with the seasons, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Heuchera versicolor is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
