Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. calathodes

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

calathodes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are accompanied by heart-shaped basal leaves.

The subspecies calathodes is distinguished by its broader, more robust leaves compared to other subspecies within the species.
