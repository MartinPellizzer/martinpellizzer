Hieracium vogesiacum

Hieracium vogesiacum, commonly known as the Voges hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
