Hieracium penduliforme

Hieracium penduliforme, commonly known as the hanging hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped and hairy.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
