Hieracium charitodon

Hieracium charitodon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a hairy stem and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
