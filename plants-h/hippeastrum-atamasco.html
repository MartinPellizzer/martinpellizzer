Hippeastrum atamasco

Hippeastrum atamasco, commonly known as the Atamasco lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.

The flowers can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for gardeners and florists alike.
