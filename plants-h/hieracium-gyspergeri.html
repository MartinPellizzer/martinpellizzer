Hieracium gyspergeri

Hieracium gyspergeri, commonly known as the Gysperger's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium gyspergeri is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
