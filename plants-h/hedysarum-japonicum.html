Hedysarum japonicum

Hedysarum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Hedysarum japonicum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and ornamental appeal.
