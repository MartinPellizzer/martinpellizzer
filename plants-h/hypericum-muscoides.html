Hypericum muscoides

Hypericum muscoides, commonly known as the sweet or musk root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by red-orange berries. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine.

Hypericum muscoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
