Hieracium erythrolepis

Hieracium erythrolepis, commonly known as the red-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually red-tinged, especially on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
