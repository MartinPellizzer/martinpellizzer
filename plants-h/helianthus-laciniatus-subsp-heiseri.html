Helianthus laciniatus subsp. heiseri

Helianthus laciniatus subsp.

heiseri, commonly known as the Heiser's swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features showy yellow ray flowers surrounded by orange-brown disk flowers, typical of the sunflower family.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and has lance-shaped, toothed leaves. It produces a tall, branching stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
