Hibiscus ebracteatus

Hibiscus ebracteatus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has smooth, green, and often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hibiscus ebracteatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
