Huernia arabica

Huernia arabica, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Huernia arabica is also valued for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it relatively easy to cultivate.
