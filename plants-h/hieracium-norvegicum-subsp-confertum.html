Hieracium norvegicum subsp. confertum

Hieracium norvegicum subsp.

confertum, commonly known as Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces ray florets that are yellow and surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in temperate climates.
