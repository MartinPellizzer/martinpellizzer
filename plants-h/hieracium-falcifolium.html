Hieracium falcifolium

Hieracium falcifolium, commonly known as the sickleleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, sickle-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium falcifolium is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
