Hieracium calanthodium

Hieracium calanthodium, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
