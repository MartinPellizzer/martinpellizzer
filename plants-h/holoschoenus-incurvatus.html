Holoschoenus incurvatus

Holoschoenus incurvatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Holoschoenus incurvatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
