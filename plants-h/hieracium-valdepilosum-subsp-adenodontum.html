Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. adenodontum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

adenodontum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
