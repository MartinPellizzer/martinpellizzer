Hypoxis gerrardii

Hypoxis gerrardii, commonly known as the golden cup, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower with a distinctive cup-like structure at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Hypoxis gerrardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
