Heterogonium novoguineense

Heterogonium novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea heterogonium, is a rare aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its filamentous growth form and the presence of specialized structures called heterogonia, which are involved in reproduction. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter for microorganisms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality.
