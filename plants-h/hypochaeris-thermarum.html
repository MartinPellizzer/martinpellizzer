Hypochaeris thermarum

Hypochaeris thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring groundsel, is a flowering plant native to regions with geothermal activity.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow. The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats near thermal springs, where it thrives in nutrient-rich, moist soil. It has alternate, hairy leaves and produces achenes as its fruit type.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain areas.
