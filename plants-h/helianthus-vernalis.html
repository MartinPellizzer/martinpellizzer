Helianthus vernalis

Helianthus vernalis, commonly known as the spring sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk and surrounding ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and blooms in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Helianthus vernalis is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.
