Holoschoenus romanus

Holoschoenus romanus, commonly known as the Roman sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in shallow water or saturated soil, indicating its preference for moist conditions.

Holoschoenus romanus plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
