Hakea crithmifolia

Hakea crithmifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Hakea crithmifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
