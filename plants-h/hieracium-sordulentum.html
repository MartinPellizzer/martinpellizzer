Hieracium sordulentum

Hieracium sordulentum, commonly known as the sordid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring moist and temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from green to reddish-brown.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
