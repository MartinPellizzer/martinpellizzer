Hakea acropogon

Hakea acropogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

Hakea acropogon is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
