Huernia lodarensis

Huernia lodarensis, commonly known as the "Lodestar" or "Cape raceme," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Huernia lodarensis is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance.
