Horsfieldia grandis

Horsfieldia grandis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Horsfieldia grandis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
