Hypericum hircinum subsp. hircinum

Hypericum hircinum subsp.

hircinum, commonly known as the goat's rue, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It features woody stems with opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
