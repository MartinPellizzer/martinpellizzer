Hieracium mucronifolium

Hieracium mucronifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium mucronifolium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
