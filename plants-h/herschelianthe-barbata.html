Herschelianthe barbata

Herschelianthe barbata, commonly known as the hairy leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its adaptations to dry environments include thick, water-storing stems and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Herschelianthe barbata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
