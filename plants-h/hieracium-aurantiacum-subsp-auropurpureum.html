Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. auropurpureum

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

auropurpureum, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that often transition to a reddish-purple hue.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
