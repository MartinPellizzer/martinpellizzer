Hieracium syncomistum var. canicaule

Hieracium syncomistum var. canicaule, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
