Hibbertia ledifolia

Hibbertia ledifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle or coastal hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its woody stems and bright yellow flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, often with a waxy texture, and the plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in late spring to early summer.

Hibbertia ledifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
