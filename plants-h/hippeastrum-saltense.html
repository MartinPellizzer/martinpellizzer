Hippeastrum saltense

Hippeastrum saltense, commonly known as the Saltense amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the flowers, while its basal leaves are broad and strap-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to ensure successful blooming in subsequent seasons.

Hippeastrum saltense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
