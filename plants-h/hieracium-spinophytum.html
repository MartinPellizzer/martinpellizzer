Hieracium spinophytum

Hieracium spinophytum, commonly known as the spiny hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, prickly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium spinophytum is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
