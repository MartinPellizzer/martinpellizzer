Hemilophia pulchella

Hemilophia pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Hemilophia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
