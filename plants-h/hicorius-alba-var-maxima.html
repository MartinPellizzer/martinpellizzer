Hicorius alba var. maxima

Hicorius alba var. maxima, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with five broad petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
