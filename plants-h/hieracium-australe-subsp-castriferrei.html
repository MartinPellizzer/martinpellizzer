Hieracium australe subsp. castriferrei

Hieracium australe subsp.

castriferrei, commonly known as the southern common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated margins. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
