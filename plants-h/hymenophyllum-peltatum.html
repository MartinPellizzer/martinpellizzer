Hymenophyllum peltatum

Hymenophyllum peltatum, commonly known as the silver hair moss, is a small, delicate fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, scale-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its fronds are usually only a few centimeters long and are closely packed, forming a dense mat.

Hymenophyllum peltatum is often found in tropical and temperate regions, where it thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures.
