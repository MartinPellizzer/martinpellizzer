Homeria lucasii

Homeria lucasii, commonly known as the Lucas's homeria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces a bulbous root system that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground.

Homeria lucasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
