Habenaria orchiocalcar

Habenaria orchiocalcar, commonly known as the purple-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
