Hieracium schultesii subsp. austrobohemicum

Hieracium schultesii subsp.

austrobohemicum, commonly known as the Bohemian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
