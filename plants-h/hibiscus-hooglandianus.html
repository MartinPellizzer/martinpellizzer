Hibiscus hooglandianus

Hibiscus hooglandianus, commonly known as the Dutch hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Hibiscus hooglandianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
