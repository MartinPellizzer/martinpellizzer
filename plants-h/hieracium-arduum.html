Hieracium arduum

Hieracium arduum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellowish-orange petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
