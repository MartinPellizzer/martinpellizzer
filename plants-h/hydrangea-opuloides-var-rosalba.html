Hydrangea opuloides var. rosalba

Hydrangea opuloides var. rosalba, commonly known as the Rosalba hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range in color from pink to blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
