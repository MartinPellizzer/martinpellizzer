Hieracium candidum var. glaberrimum

Hieracium candidum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white with yellow centers.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
