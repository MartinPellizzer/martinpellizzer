Hedysarum spinosissimum var. lanatum

Hedysarum spinosissimum var. lanatum, commonly known as the woolly needlepod, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
