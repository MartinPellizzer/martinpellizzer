Hypochaeris cretensis subsp. pinnatifida

Hypochaeris cretensis subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the Cretan sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It has basal leaves that are pinnately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, contributing to its role in pollination and ecological diversity.
