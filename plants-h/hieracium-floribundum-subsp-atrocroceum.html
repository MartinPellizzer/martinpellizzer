Hieracium floribundum subsp. atrocroceum

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

atrocroceum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, purple to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value but can also be invasive in certain regions.
