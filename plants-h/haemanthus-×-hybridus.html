Haemanthus × hybridus

Haemanthus × hybridus, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Haemanthus albiflos and Haemanthus sanguineus.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange bulbous flower heads that resemble bells, which bloom during the winter months. The plant produces a cluster of small, white to yellow flowers at the base of the bell-shaped structure. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its unique and eye-catching floral display.
