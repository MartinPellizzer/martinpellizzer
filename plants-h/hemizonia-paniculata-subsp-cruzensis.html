Hemizonia paniculata subsp. cruzensis

Hemizonia paniculata subsp.

cruzensis, commonly known as the Cruz's hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

The subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
