Hiptage candicans var. angustifolia

Hiptage candicans var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hiptage, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
