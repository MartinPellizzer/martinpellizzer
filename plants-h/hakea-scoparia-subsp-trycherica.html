Hakea scoparia subsp. trycherica

Hakea scoparia subsp.

trycherica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces cylindrical, yellowish flower spikes.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
