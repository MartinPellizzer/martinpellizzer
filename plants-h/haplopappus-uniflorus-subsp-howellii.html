Haplopappus uniflorus subsp. howellii

Haplopappus uniflorus subsp.

howellii, commonly known as Howell's singleflower hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces a single, elongated flower head at the end of each stem. Its flowers are yellow and are surrounded by bracts that are usually shorter than the flower head.

The species is characterized by its simple inflorescence and is adapted to arid environments.
