Helianthus niveus var. tephrodes

Helianthus niveus var. tephrodes, commonly known as the white-haired sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white ray flowers and its distinctive white to grayish-blue bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other sunflower and daisy species.
