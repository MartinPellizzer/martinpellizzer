Hieracium praecox subsp. pallidulum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

pallidulum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and shorter stature compared to other hawkweed species.
