Humiria floribunda var. laurina

Humiria floribunda var. laurina, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that bloom in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
