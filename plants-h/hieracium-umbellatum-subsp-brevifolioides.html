Hieracium umbellatum subsp. brevifolioides

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

brevifolioides, commonly known as the short-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are short and broad, with a hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, producing small seeds that are dispersed by wind.
