Huperzia ciliolata

Huperzia ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, creeping rhizomes and densely branched, sub-erect stems. The leaves are typically arranged in a whorled pattern and are often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories across its native range.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
