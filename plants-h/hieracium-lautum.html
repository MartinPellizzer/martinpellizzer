Hieracium lautum

Hieracium lautum, commonly known as the water thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium lautum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
