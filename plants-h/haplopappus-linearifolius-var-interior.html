Haplopappus linearifolius var. interior

Haplopappus linearifolius var. interior, commonly known as the interior jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions such as reduced leaf surface area. Its linear, often hairy leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is known for its resilience and is often found in disturbed soils or along rocky outcrops.
