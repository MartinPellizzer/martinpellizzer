Hieracium cephalotes subsp. profetanum

Hieracium cephalotes subsp.

profetanum, commonly known as the profetanum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and taxonomic significance.
