Hymenoxys tweediei

Hymenoxys tweediei, commonly known as the Texas lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, cup-shaped structure and a strong, spicy scent. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, oval seeds in a fluffy, white pappus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hymenoxys tweediei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
