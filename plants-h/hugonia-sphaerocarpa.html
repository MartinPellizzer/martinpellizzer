Hugonia sphaerocarpa

Hugonia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight sheen, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Hugonia sphaerocarpa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
