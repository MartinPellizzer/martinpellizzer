Hygrophila pusilla

Hygrophila pusilla, commonly known as the small water hyssop, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Hygrophila pusilla is known for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and is often used in water gardens and aquariums.
