Hygrophila albobracteata

Hygrophila albobracteata, commonly known as the white bract hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually white or pale yellow in color.

Hygrophila albobracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens and pond landscapes.
