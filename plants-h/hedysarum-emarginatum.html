Hedysarum emarginatum

Hedysarum emarginatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces seed pods that contain several small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hedysarum emarginatum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
