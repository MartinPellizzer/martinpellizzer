Hydnostachyon

Hydnostachyon, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant lacks true leaves and instead relies on its roots for nutrient absorption, making it a mycoheterotroph. It forms a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi to obtain nutrients, which is a key feature of its botanical biology.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a conservation concern.
