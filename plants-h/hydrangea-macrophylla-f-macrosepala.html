Hydrangea macrophylla f. macrosepala

Hydrangea macrophylla f. macrosepala, commonly known as the giant hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to Japan. The plant is known for its ability to change flower color based on soil pH, with blue hues in acidic soil and pink in alkaline conditions. It thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and long-lasting blooms.
