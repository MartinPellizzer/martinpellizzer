Helianthemum echioides var. laxum

Helianthemum echioides var. laxum, commonly known as the loose-flowered rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has compact, leathery leaves that are gray-green in color, providing a drought-tolerant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for Mediterranean-style gardens.
