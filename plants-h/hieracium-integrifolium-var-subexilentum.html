Hieracium integrifolium var. subexilentum

Hieracium integrifolium var. subexilentum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
