Hyptis caudata

Hyptis caudata, commonly known as the hairy hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Hyptis caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
