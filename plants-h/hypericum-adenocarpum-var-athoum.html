Hypericum adenocarpum var. athoum

Hypericum adenocarpum var. athoum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and slender, woody stems that often have a reddish hue. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with compounds like hypericin and hyperforin being of particular interest.
