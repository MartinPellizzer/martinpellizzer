Hippeastrum maculatum

Hippeastrum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum maculatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
