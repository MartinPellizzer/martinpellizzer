Hesperis pseudocinerea

Hesperis pseudocinerea, commonly known as the pale lady's mantle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Hesperis pseudocinerea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
