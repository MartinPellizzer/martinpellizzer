Hypericum spachianum

Hypericum spachianum, commonly known as the Spach's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of glandular dots on the leaves and stems.

Hypericum spachianum is often found in rocky soils and sunny habitats, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
