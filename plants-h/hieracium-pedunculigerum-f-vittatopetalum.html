Hieracium pedunculigerum f. vittatopetalum

Hieracium pedunculigerum f. vittatopetalum, commonly known as the variegated petal hawkweed, is a variant of the common hawkweed.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves with yellow and green patterns, which set it apart from the typical green foliage of its species. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
