Hazardia whitneyi var. discoidea

Hazardia whitneyi var. discoidea, commonly known as the white hazard or desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its resilience to drought and heat, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.
