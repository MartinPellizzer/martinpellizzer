Hieracium villosiceps subsp. morronense

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

morronense, commonly known as the Morron hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its taxonomic classification places it within the family Asteraceae, reflecting its composite flower structure.
