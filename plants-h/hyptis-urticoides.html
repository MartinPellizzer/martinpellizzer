Hyptis urticoides

Hyptis urticoides, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
