Horridocactus curvispinus

Horridocactus curvispinus, commonly known as the twisted spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, curved spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid climates.
