Harthamnus boliviensis

Harthamnus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Harthamnus boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
