Hyacinthus romanus

Hyacinthus romanus, commonly known as the Roman hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like leaves and sturdy stems. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and strong scent.

Hyacinthus romanus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
