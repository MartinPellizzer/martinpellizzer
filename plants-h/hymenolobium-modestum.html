Hymenolobium modestum

Hymenolobium modestum, commonly known as the yellow-wood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree has a distinctive yellow bark that becomes flaky with age, and its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound with 5-9 pairs of oval leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
