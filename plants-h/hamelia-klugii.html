Hamelia klugii

Hamelia klugii, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.

Hamelia klugii is drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping in warm climates.
