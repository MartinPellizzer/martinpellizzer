Helianthemum viscidulum subsp. viscidulum

Helianthemum viscidulum subsp.

viscidulum, commonly known as the sticky rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically from April to June.

The plant has succulent, glandular leaves that are covered in a sticky substance, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, arid environments.

This subspecies is part of the Crassulaceae family and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in rocky gardens.
