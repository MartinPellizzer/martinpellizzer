Hieracium rugulosum

Hieracium rugulosum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk, often appearing in clusters.

Hieracium rugulosum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
