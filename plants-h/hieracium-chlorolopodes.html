Hieracium chlorolopodes

Hieracium chlorolopodes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are hairy and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium chlorolopodes is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
