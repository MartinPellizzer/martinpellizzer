Hieracium subflorentinum

Hieracium subflorentinum, commonly known as the Italian oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, with a basal rosette formation.
