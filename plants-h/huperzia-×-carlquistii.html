Huperzia × carlquistii

Huperzia × carlquistii, commonly known as the hybrid huperzia, is a fern that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Huperzia selago and Huperzia squamosa, showcasing a blend of traits from both parent species. The plant features a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense, overlapping leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. Its sporophylls are arranged in a spiral pattern, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

This hybrid is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
