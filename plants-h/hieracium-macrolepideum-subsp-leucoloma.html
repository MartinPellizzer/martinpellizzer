Hieracium macrolepideum subsp. leucoloma

Hieracium macrolepideum subsp.

leucoloma, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, white-veined, woolly leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is distinguished by its pale, often white or pale green leaves and is found primarily in Europe.
