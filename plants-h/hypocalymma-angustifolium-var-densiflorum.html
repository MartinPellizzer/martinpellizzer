Hypocalymma angustifolium var. densiflorum

Hypocalymma angustifolium var. densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered narrow-leaved hypocalymma, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
