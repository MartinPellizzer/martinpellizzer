Hemionitis parvisegmenta

Hemionitis parvisegmenta, commonly known as the small-segmented hemionitis, is a unique aquatic fern native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other water ferns. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Hemionitis parvisegmenta is notable for its ability to thrive in still water environments with moderate light exposure.
