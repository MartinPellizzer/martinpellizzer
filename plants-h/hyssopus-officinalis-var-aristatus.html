Hyssopus officinalis var. aristatus

Hyssopus officinalis var. aristatus, commonly known as the spiked hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
