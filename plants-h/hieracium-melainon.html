Hieracium melainon

Hieracium melainon, commonly known as black hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
