Hymenophyllum serpens

Hymenophyllum serpens, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant's rhizomes spread along the surface of tree trunks, allowing it to colonize various substrates. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are typically found on the underside of its fronds.

Hymenophyllum serpens is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of forest ecosystems.
