Hieracium armerioides subsp. seirasicum

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

seirasicum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
