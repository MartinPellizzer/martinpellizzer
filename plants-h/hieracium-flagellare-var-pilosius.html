Hieracium flagellare var. pilosius

Hieracium flagellare var. pilosius, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a robust, creeping stem covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped and also bear a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
