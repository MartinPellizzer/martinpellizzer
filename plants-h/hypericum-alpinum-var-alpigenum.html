Hypericum alpinum var. alpigenum

Hypericum alpinum var. alpigenum, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright, often sparsely branched stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This plant is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
