Helianthus annuus subsp. texanus

Helianthus annuus subsp.

texanus, commonly known as the Texas sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

The Texas sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
