Hamelia ovata

Hamelia ovata, commonly known as the orange flamebush, is a shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hamelia ovata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
