Hieracium rauzense subsp. farinifloccum

Hieracium rauzense subsp.

farinifloccum, commonly known as the Farinifloccum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
