Hypericum anagallidioides

Hypericum anagallidioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its medicinal properties have been studied, though it is less commonly used than other Hypericum species.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to its arid and semi-arid habitats.
