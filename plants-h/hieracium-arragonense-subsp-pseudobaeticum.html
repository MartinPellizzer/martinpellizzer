Hieracium arragonense subsp. pseudobaeticum

Hieracium arragonense subsp.

pseudobaeticum, commonly known as the Baetican hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. It is known for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
