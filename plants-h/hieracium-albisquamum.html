Hieracium albisquamum

Hieracium albisquamum, commonly known as the white-squamated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive white or pale-colored underside, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium albisquamum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
