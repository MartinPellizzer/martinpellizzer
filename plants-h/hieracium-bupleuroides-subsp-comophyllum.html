Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. comophyllum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

comophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It has hairy, often reddish stems and leaves with a rough, textured surface.

Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets.
