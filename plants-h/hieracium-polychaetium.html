Hieracium polychaetium

Hieracium polychaetium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium polychaetium produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
