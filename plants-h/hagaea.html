Hagaea

Hagaea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant found in specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom only at night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance. Hagaea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often associated with ancient forests.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts.
