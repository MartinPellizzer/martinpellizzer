Hopea basilanica

Hopea basilanica, commonly known as the Basilan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Hopea basilanica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
