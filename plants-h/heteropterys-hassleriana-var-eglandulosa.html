Heteropterys hassleriana var. eglandulosa

Heteropterys hassleriana var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular heteropterys, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in small, glandular dots. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
