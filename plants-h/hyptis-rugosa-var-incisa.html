Hyptis rugosa var. incisa

Hyptis rugosa var. incisa, commonly known as the hairy hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
