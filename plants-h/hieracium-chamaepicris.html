Hieracium chamaepicris

Hieracium chamaepicris, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize various habitats.
