Hieracium grabowskianum subsp. eremocephalum

Hieracium grabowskianum subsp.

eremocephalum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a single, tall, sparsely hairy stem that bears a single flower head with yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
