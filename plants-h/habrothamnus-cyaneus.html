Habrothamnus cyaneus

Habrothamnus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue star, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has silvery-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
