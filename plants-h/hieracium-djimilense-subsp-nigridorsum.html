Hieracium djimilense subsp. nigridorsum

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

nigridorsum, commonly known as the black-tipped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically features yellow to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with distinctive black or dark brown glandular dots on the stems and leaves. Its leaves are usually basal, rough to the touch, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
