Hieracium sagittatum subsp. hemitrichotum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

hemitrichotum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

This subspecies is part of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
