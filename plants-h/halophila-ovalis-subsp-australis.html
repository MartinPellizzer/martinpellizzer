Halophila ovalis subsp. australis

Halophila ovalis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian halophyte, is a seagrass species native to the coastal regions of Australia. It thrives in shallow, saline waters and is adapted to tolerate varying salinity levels.

The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for marine life.

Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fruits containing viable seeds.
