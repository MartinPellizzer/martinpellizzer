Heliconia irrasa var. undulata

Heliconia irrasa var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, narrow, paddle-shaped leaves with a wavy edge, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal name "undulata.". It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The inflorescence consists of a spiral arrangement of brightly colored, fleshy bracts that enclose the small, true flowers.
