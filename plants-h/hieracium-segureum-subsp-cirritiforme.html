Hieracium segureum subsp. cirritiforme

Hieracium segureum subsp.

cirritiforme, commonly known as the cirritiform hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
