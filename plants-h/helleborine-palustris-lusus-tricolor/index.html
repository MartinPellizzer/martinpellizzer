Helleborine Palustris Lusus Tricolor

Purple marsh helleborine, scientifically known as Helleborine palustris lusus tricolor, is a rare and elusive orchid that thrives in wetland habitats across Europe. This plant is characterized by its striking purple and green speckled leaves and delicate, pale greenish-yellow flowers. It is often found in damp, shaded areas such as bogs and marshes where it relies on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake. Due to its limited distribution and habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions. Its unique beauty and ecological significance make it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
