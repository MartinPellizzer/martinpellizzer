Hesperis mandonii

Hesperis mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces four-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Hesperis mandonii is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
