Hieracium murorum subsp. plicosum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

plicosum, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that is often covered in dense, white to grayish pubescence.

This subspecies is noted for its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
