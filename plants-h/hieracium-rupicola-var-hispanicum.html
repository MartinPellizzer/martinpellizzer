Hieracium rupicola var. hispanicum

Hieracium rupicola var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by petal-like bracts, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
