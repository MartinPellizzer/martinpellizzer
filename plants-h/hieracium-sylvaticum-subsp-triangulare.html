Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. triangulare

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

triangulare, commonly known as the three-angled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its triangular-shaped stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
