Hieracium pilosella subsp. brachytrichum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

brachytrichum, commonly known as the short-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, yellowish to orange-yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various insects.
