Hieracium murorum subsp. hypotomum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

hypotomum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens.
