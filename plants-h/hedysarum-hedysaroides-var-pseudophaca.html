Hedysarum hedysaroides var. pseudophaca

Hedysarum hedysaroides var. pseudophaca, commonly known as the eastern false peavine, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and riverbanks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces yellow to reddish-purple pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
