Hieracium murorum subsp. naevium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

naevium, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its subspecific name, *naevium*, refers to the distinctive spotted or streaked patterns on its leaves.
