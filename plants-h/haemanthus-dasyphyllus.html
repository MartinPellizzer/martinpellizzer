Haemanthus dasyphyllus

Haemanthus dasyphyllus, commonly known as the Natal grass cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, grass-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny environment to flourish.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
