Helianthocereus pseudocandicans var. roseiflorus

Helianthocereus pseudocandicans var. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered night bloomer cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and clusters of large, showy flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically pink to rose-colored, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its unique blooming pattern and striking appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical gardens.
