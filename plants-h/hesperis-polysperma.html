Hesperis polysperma

Hesperis polysperma, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, pink or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil.

Hesperis polysperma is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
