Helianthemum italicum var. strigosum

Helianthemum italicum var. strigosum, commonly known as the sticky rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to rocky landscapes. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, helping to retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
