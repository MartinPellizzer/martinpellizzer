Hymenopappus

Hymenopappus, commonly known as the "dandelion of the desert," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they have adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The flowers are usually small and yellow, with a distinctive pappus structure that aids in wind dispersal of seeds. Hymenopappus species often have hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss.

Their ecological importance includes providing food for pollinators and stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
