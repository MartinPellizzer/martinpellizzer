Hopea minima

Hopea minima, commonly known as the dwarf hopea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Hopea minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
