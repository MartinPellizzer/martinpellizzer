Hammarbya paludosa

Hammarbya paludosa, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in northern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, peaty soils and is often found in bogs and marshes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Hammarbya paludosa is an indicator species for undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
