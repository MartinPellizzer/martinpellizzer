Hoya palawanensis

Hoya palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan wax plant, is a species of epiphytic flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, and its leaves are usually thick and succulent.

Hoya palawanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
