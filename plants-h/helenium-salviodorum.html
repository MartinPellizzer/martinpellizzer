Helenium salviodorum

Helenium salviodorum, commonly known as the golden rod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Helenium salviodorum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
