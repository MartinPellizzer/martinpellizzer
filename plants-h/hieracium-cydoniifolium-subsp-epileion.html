Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. epileion

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

epileion, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf-like bracts.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins, while its flowers are yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
