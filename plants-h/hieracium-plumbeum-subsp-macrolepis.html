Hieracium plumbeum subsp. macrolepis

Hieracium plumbeum subsp.

macrolepis, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

This subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust leaves compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
