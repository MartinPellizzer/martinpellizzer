Hieracium schmidtii subsp. barbulatiforme

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

barbulatiforme, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its yellow flower heads and is native to specific regions in Central Asia.

The plant typically grows in alpine or rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its stems are often branched and upright.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
