Hordeum chilense var. pseudosecalinum

Hordeum chilense var. pseudosecalinum, commonly known as the Chilean barley, is a wild grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are small and hardy, often used in traditional agricultural practices.

This variety is of interest to botanists and researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and ecological resilience.
