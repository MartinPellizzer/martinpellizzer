Hesperis strigosa

Hesperis strigosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Hesperis strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
