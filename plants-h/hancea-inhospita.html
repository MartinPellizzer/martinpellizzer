Hancea inhospita

Hancea inhospita, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female plants. Hancea inhospita is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Due to its slow growth and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
