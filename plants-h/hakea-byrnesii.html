Hakea byrnesii

Hakea byrnesii, commonly known as the Blackboy, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea byrnesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
