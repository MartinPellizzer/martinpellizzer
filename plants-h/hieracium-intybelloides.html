Hieracium intybelloides

Hieracium intybelloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Hieracium intybelloides is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
