Heritiera tinctoria

Heritiera tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian dye plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a deep red dye from its roots, which has been historically used in textile industries. The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine systems.
