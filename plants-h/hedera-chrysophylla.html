Hedera chrysophylla

Hedera chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that exhibit a striking golden-yellow coloration, especially in full sunlight. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor plant displays.
