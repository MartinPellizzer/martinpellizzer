Hedysarum gangeticum

Hedysarum gangeticum, commonly known as the Indian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and grasslands. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its compound leaves are pinnate, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum gangeticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
