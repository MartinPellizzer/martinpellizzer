Hopea floribunda

Hopea floribunda, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

Hopea floribunda plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
