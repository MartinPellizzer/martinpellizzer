Heterotheca nervosa

Heterotheca nervosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of purple to pinkish-yellow composite flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in full sun in well-drained soils. It is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

Heterotheca nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
