Homalolepis arenaria

Homalolepis arenaria, commonly known as the sand lichen, is a unique plant that thrives in arid and coastal environments.

It is a lichen, which is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria. This plant is characterized by its crust-like growth form, which allows it to adhere tightly to surfaces such as rocks and soil. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water and nutrient availability.

Homalolepis arenaria plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
