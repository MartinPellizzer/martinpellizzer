Helleborus serbicus subsp. malyi

Helleborus serbicus subsp.

malyi, commonly known as the Malyi hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of the Balkans. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of nodding, dark violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its winter blooming habit.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
