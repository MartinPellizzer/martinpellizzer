Hieracium incisum subsp. subgaudinii

Hieracium incisum subsp.

subgaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Compositae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
