Hieracium subfarinosiceps

Hieracium subfarinosiceps, commonly known as the rough-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive whitish or grayish pubescence.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that give the plant a daisy-like appearance.
