Horsfieldia pallidicaula var. macrocarya

Horsfieldia pallidicaula var. macrocarya, commonly known as the large-fruited orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences. The plant produces elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. Its leaves are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to capture moisture from the air.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
