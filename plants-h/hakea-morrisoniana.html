Hakea morrisoniana

Hakea morrisoniana, commonly known as the Morrison's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, bright red flowers. The plant has woody stems and needle-like leaves, which are typical features of the Hakea genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Hakea morrisoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
