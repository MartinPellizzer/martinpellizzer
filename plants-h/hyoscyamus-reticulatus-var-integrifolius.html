Hyoscyamus reticulatus var. integrifolius

Hyoscyamus reticulatus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the horned toad or desert horned toad, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, leaf-like structures that resemble the skin of a horned toad, providing it with a natural camouflage. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are typically broad and deeply lobed. It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals.
