Hypericopsis persica

Hypericopsis persica, commonly known as Persian St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than other members of its family.
