Humboltia scabrata

Humboltia scabrata, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, strap-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open woodlands, preferring well-drained conditions. Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a distinctive member of its habitat.

Humboltia scabrata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
