Hydrangea hortensis

Hydrangea hortensis, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
