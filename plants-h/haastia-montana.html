Haastia montana

Haastia montana, commonly known as the mountain haastia, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in montane regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of both botanical interest and conservation concern.
