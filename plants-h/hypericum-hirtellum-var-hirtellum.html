Hypericum hirtellum var. hirtellum

Hypericum hirtellum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features narrow, upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive rough texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are composed of five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
