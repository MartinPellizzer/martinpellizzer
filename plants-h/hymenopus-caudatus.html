Hymenopus caudatus

Hymenopus caudatus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets that give it a distinctive, airy appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and rapid growth.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
