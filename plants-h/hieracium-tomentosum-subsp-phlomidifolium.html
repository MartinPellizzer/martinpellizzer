Hieracium tomentosum subsp. phlomidifolium

Hieracium tomentosum subsp.

phlomidifolium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often lobed or deeply divided, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
