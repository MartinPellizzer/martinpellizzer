Hieracium wologdense

Hieracium wologdense, commonly known as the Wologdense hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium wologdense is often found in open habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
