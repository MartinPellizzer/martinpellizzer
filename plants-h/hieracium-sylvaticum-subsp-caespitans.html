Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. caespitans

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

caespitans, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts, and are visited by various pollinators.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its preferred habitat.
