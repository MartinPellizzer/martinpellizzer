Haxtonia argophylla

Haxtonia argophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf haxtonia, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite and have a leathery texture, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Haxtonia argophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
