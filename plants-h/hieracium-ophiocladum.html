Hieracium ophiocladum

Hieracium ophiocladum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and erect.

Hieracium ophiocladum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
