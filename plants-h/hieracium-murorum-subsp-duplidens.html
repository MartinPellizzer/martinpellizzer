Hieracium murorum subsp. duplidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

duplidens, commonly known as the double-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, featuring two distinct lobes on each leaf blade. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
