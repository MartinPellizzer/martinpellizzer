Hieracium poliobaptum

Hieracium poliobaptum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its flowers consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Hieracium poliobaptum is often found in open habitats and can spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and a potential invasive species in some regions.
