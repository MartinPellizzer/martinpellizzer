Hordeum chilense var. compressum

Hordeum chilense var. compressum, commonly known as the Chilean barley, is a wild grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale to brownish flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is of interest for ecological and agricultural studies.
