Hemerocallis serotina

Hemerocallis serotina, commonly known as the late day lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green and slightly rough to the touch. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hemerocallis serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
