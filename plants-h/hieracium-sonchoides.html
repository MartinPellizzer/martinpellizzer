Hieracium sonchoides

Hieracium sonchoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium sonchoides is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some regions.
