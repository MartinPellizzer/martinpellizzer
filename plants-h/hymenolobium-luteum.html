Hymenolobium luteum

Hymenolobium luteum, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Hymenolobium luteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
