Hieracium frigidum f. tamaense

Hieracium frigidum f. tamaense, commonly known as the Tamaense Hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at higher elevations. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
