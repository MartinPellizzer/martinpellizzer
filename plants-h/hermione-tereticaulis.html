Hermione tereticaulis

Hermione tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that grow in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in moist soil, while its fronds are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
