Hippocratea trilobulata

Hippocratea trilobulata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
