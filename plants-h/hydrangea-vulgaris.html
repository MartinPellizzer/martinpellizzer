Hydrangea vulgaris

Hydrangea vulgaris, commonly known as the bigleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves that are often serrated and have a glossy texture. Hydrangea vulgaris thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
