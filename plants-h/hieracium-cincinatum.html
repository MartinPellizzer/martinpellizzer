Hieracium cincinatum

Hieracium cincinatum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in phytochemical research.

Its growth habits and chemical composition contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
