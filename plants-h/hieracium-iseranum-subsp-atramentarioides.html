Hieracium iseranum subsp. atramentarioides

Hieracium iseranum subsp.

atramentarioides, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black stems, which give it its common name.
