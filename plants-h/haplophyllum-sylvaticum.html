Haplophyllum sylvaticum

Haplophyllum sylvaticum, commonly known as the European mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems.

Haplophyllum sylvaticum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at higher elevations.
