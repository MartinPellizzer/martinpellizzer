Hypochaeris radicata var. rostrata

Hypochaeris radicata var. rostrata, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system and a mat-forming growth habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are sprawling and often lie close to the ground.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, lawns, and along roadsides, and it is considered a weed in some regions.
