Hippochaete laevigata

Hippochaete laevigata, commonly known as the silky bluebells, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sky-blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Hippochaete laevigata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
