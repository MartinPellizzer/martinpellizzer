Hieracium caesium subsp. respondens

Hieracium caesium subsp.

respondens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
