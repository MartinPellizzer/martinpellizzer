Hieracium furcelliferum

Hieracium furcelliferum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a common weed in some regions.
