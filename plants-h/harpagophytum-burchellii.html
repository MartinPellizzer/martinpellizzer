Harpagophytum burchellii

Harpagophytum burchellii, commonly known as wild quinine or bishop's weed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, ridged fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Harpagophytum burchellii is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
