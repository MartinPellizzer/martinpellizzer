Hypericum tridesmis

Hypericum tridesmis, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their bright yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing clusters of golden-yellow petals and orange stamens.

Hypericum tridesmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
