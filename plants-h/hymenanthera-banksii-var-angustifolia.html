Hymenanthera banksii var. angustifolia

Hymenanthera banksii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeywood, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features lanceolate, aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exfoliating to reveal a reddish inner layer.

It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.
