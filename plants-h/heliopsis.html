Heliopsis

Heliopsis, commonly known as the "sunflower" of the Asteraceae family, is a genus of flowering plants native to North America.

It features bright, showy yellow to orange flowers that resemble those of the sunflower, though they are smaller and more daisy-like in structure. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges, thriving in full sun. Heliopsis species have lance-shaped leaves and produce numerous seeds in their composite flower heads.

They are popular in gardens for their vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
