Heliotropium demissum

Heliotropium demissum, commonly known as the creeping heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has small, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Heliotropium demissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its visual interest.
