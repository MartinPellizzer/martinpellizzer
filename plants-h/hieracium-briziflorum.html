Hieracium briziflorum

Hieracium briziflorum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium briziflorum is often found in open habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
