Hesperaster speciosus

Hesperaster speciosus, commonly known as the showy star-flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sprawling.

Hesperaster speciosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
