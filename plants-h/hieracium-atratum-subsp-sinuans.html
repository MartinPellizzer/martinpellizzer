Hieracium atratum subsp. sinuans

Hieracium atratum subsp.

sinuans, commonly known as the sinuate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a single terminal head, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
