Huberia hirsuta

Huberia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy huberia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
