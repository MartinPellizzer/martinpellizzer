Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. paeminosum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

paeminosum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
