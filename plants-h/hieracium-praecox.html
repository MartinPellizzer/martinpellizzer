Hieracium praecox

Hieracium praecox, commonly known as early hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has hairy stems and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
