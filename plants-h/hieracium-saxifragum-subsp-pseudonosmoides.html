Hieracium saxifragum subsp. pseudonosmoides

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

pseudonosmoides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is often found at high elevations.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
