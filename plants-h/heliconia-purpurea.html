Heliconia purpurea

Heliconia purpurea, commonly known as the crimson heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, vibrantly colored inflorescences. The plant features large, sword-shaped leaves that form a dense rosette, providing a lush backdrop to its showy flower clusters. The flowers are typically red or orange, with a distinctive spadix surrounded by brightly colored bracts.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
