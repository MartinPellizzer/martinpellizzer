Hepetis spicata var. sulphurea

Hepetis spicata var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The vivid yellow color of its flowers makes it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower collections.
