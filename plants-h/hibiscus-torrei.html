Hibiscus torrei

Hibiscus torrei, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Oahu in Hawaii.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring broad, waxy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually bright red or pink, with a yellow center, and bloom throughout the year. Hibiscus torrei thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
