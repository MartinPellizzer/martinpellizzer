Hackelia mexicana

Hackelia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican fairybells, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Hackelia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
