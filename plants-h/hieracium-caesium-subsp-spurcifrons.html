Hieracium caesium subsp. spurcifrons

Hieracium caesium subsp.

spurcifrons, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

This subspecies is known for its ability to spread through both vegetative means and seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
