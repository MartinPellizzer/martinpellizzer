Hieracium rauzense subsp. semiatratum

Hieracium rauzense subsp.

semiatratum, commonly known as the Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is distinguished by its subspecific features, including the structure of its involucres and the arrangement of its florets.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
