Hieracium vulgatum subsp. tridentatoides

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

tridentatoides, commonly known as the three-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with leaves that are serrated and often have a distinctive three-toothed appearance. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and geographic distribution compared to other hawkweed species.
