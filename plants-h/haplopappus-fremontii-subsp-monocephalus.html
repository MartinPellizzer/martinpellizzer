Haplopappus fremontii subsp. monocephalus

Haplopappus fremontii subsp.

monocephalus, commonly known as the single-head desert helenium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical, ribbed stems.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in a single head, which is why it is referred to as "single-head.".

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable species in its ecological region.
