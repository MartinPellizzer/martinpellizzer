Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. polyadenum

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

polyadenum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow disk flowers with ray florets.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that help in spreading and colonizing new areas.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
