Houttuynia cordata var. plena

Houttuynia cordata var. plena, commonly known as the Mexican native or Indian nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both its aesthetic appeal and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
