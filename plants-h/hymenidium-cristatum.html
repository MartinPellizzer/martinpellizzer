Hymenidium cristatum

Hymenidium cristatum, commonly known as the cristate liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Peltasporaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leafy structure and a distinctive, crest-like arrangement of leaves that give it its common name. Its reproductive structures, known as sorocarps, are borne on stalks and contain spores for propagation.

Hymenidium cristatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
