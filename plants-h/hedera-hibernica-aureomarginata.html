Hedera hibernica aureomarginata

Hedera hibernica aureomarginata, commonly known as the golden-margin ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to western Europe.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with striking gold or yellow margins that add visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in landscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
