Helianthemum variabile subvar. eriocaulon

Helianthemum variabile subvar. eriocaulon, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
