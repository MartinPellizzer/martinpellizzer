Hieracium laevigatum subsp. hypopityforme

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

hypopityforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, hairy achenes as its seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the sunflower family.
