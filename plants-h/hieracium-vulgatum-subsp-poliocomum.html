Hieracium vulgatum subsp. poliocomum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

poliocomum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in association with other wildflowers and can form dense mats in suitable habitats.
