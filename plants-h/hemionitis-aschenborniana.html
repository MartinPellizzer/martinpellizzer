Hemionitis aschenborniana

Hemionitis aschenborniana, commonly known as the Aschenborn's hemionitis, is a rare and specialized fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, typically growing in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. The plant has a distinctive, compact growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the undersides of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.
