Hemigraphis latebrosa

Hemigraphis latebrosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
