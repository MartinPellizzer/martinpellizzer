Hyptis cretata

Hyptis cretata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, while the plant often grows in moist, shady environments.

Hyptis cretata is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
