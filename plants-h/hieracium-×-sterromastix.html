Hieracium × sterromastix

Hieracium × sterromastix, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and reproduces both by seed and vegetative means.

The species is known for its ecological impact, outcompeting native flora in certain environments.
