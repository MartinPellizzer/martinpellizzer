Hieracium lawsonii subsp. aemuliflorum

Hieracium lawsonii subsp.

aemuliflorum, commonly known as the mimic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically has hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.
