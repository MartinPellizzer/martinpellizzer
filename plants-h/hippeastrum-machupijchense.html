Hippeastrum machupijchense

Hippeastrum machupijchense, commonly known as the Machupijchi amaryllis, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the blooms, with broad, strap-like leaves emerging from the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
