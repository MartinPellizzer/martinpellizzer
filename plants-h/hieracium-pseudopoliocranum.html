Hieracium pseudopoliocranum

Hieracium pseudopoliocranum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
