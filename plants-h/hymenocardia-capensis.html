Hymenocardia capensis

Hymenocardia capensis, commonly known as the Cape hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Hymenocardia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
