Hemsleya panacis-scandens var. panacis-scandens

Hemsleya panacis-scandens var. panacis-scandens, commonly known as the climbing arrowroot, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible tubers that resemble arrowheads. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and medicinal potential.
