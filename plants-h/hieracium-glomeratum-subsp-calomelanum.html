Hieracium glomeratum subsp. calomelanum

Hieracium glomeratum subsp.

calomelanum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense, compact inflorescences. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
