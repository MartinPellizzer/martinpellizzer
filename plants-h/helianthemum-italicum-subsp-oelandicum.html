Helianthemum italicum subsp. oelandicum

Helianthemum italicum subsp.

oelandicum, commonly known as the Oeland thrift, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
