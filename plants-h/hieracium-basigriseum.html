Hieracium basigriseum

Hieracium basigriseum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The flowers typically bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
