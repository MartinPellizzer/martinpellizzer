Hoya pallilimba

Hoya pallilimba, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the summer months. Hoya pallilimba is a trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
