Heterotheca subaxillaris var. petiolaris

Heterotheca subaxillaris var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive petiole, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of native plant communities in its range.
