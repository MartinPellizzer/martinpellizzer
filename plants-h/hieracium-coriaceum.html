Hieracium coriaceum

Hieracium coriaceum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium coriaceum is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
