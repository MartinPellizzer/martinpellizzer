Hibiscus cucurbitaceus var. acuminatus

Hibiscus cucurbitaceus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the pointed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in wetlands, along riverbanks, and in swampy areas.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.
