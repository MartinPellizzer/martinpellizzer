Hieracium sordidum var. subfrigidum

Hieracium sordidum var. subfrigidum, commonly known as the bluish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
