Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. zygosense

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

zygosense, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and distinctive red, elongated fruits.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a basal rosette of foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
