Helichrysum neoisalense

Helichrysum neoisalense, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant is shrub-like, with narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers, it is often used in dried floral arrangements.
