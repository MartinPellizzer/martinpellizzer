Humulus lupulus var. effusus

Humulus lupulus var. effusus, commonly known as the European hops plant, is a variety of hop that is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and the production of small, cone-like flowers called strobiles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is known for its aromatic and bittering properties in brewing. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often used in traditional hop cultivation for its distinct flavor profile and robust growth characteristics.
