Heliocarpus terebinthinaceus

Heliocarpus terebinthinaceus, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or terebinth, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forests and is valued for its durable wood.

The tree also has medicinal uses in traditional South American practices.
