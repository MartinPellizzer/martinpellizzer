Halesia carolina subsp. parviflora

Halesia carolina subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge.

The plant has smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
