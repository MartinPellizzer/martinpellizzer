Himatanthus articulatus

Himatanthus articulatus, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical clusters of white flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem with a segmented appearance, giving it its scientific name "articulatus," meaning "jointed.". Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
