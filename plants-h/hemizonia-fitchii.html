Hemizonia fitchii

Hemizonia fitchii, commonly known as the desert broom, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a low-growing habit to conserve water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
