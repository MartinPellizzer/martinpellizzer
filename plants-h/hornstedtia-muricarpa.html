Hornstedtia muricarpa

Hornstedtia muricarpa, commonly known as the spiky coral plant, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescence that resembles a coral-like structure, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette, forming a water-holding cup.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
