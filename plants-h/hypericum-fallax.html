Hypericum fallax

Hypericum fallax, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hypericum fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
