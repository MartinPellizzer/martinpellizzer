Hexasepalum domingense

Hexasepalum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican six-sepal plant, is a member of the Rubiaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with six sepals that are free and distinct, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, spherical berries that contain several seeds.

This plant is native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic, and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
