Hieracium nigrescens subsp. modicum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

modicum, commonly known as the small black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats that can outcompete other vegetation.
