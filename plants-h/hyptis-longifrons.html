Hyptis longifrons

Hyptis longifrons, commonly known as the long-fruited hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a pale blue or purple hue.

Hyptis longifrons is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
