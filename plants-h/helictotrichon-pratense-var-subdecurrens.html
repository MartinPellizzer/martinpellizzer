Helictotrichon pratense var. subdecurrens

Helictotrichon pratense var. subdecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is often found in ecological restoration projects.
