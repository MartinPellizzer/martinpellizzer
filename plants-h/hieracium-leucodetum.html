Hieracium leucodetum

Hieracium leucodetum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow to white in color, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium leucodetum is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
