Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. fissifurcum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

fissifurcum, commonly known as the fissured hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, fissured or lobed appearance, which contributes to its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
