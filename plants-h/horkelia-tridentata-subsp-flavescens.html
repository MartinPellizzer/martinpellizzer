Horkelia tridentata subsp. flavescens

Horkelia tridentata subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its environment.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow to golden floral coloration and its unique, globular fruit structure.
