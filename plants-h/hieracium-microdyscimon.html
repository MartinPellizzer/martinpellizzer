Hieracium microdyscimon

Hieracium microdyscimon, commonly known as the small diskless hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, erect flowering stem that bears a dense cluster of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowering period typically occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
