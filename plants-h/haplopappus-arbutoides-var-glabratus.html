Haplopappus arbutoides var. glabratus

Haplopappus arbutoides var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually smooth and succulent, helping to retain moisture.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
