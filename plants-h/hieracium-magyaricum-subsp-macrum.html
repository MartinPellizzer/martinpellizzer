Hieracium magyaricum subsp. macrum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

macrum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
