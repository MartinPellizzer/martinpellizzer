Hieracium murorum subsp. macromalloides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

macromalloides, commonly known as the large-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This subspecies is distinguished by its relatively large flower heads compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
