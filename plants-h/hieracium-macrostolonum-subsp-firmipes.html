Hieracium macrostolonum subsp. firmipes

Hieracium macrostolonum subsp.

firmipes, commonly known as the robust hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
