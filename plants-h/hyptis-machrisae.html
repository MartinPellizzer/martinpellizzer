Hyptis machrisae

Hyptis machrisae, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis machrisae is known for its pleasant fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
