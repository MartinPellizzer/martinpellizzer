Harrisonia browniana

Harrisonia browniana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a bright yellow or golden color when mature. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule.

Harrisonia browniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
