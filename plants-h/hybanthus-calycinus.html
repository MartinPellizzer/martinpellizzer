Hybanthus calycinus

Hybanthus calycinus, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, tubular, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Hybanthus calycinus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
