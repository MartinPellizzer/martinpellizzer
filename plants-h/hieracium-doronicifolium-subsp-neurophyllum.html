Hieracium doronicifolium subsp. neurophyllum

Hieracium doronicifolium subsp.

neurophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
