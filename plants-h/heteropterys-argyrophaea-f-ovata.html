Heteropterys argyrophaea f. ovata

Heteropterys argyrophaea f. ovata, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a variant of the species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are ovate in shape, hence the specific epithet "ovata," and are often arranged alternately along the stems.

The botanical interest of this plant lies in its unique foliage and its role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
