Helichrysum xanthosphaerum

Helichrysum xanthosphaerum, commonly known as the golden ball everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow-orange flowers that remain intact even after the plant has dried, giving it a long-lasting appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its resilience and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and dried flower arrangements.
