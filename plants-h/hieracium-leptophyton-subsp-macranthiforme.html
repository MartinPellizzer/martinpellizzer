Hieracium leptophyton subsp. macranthiforme

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

macranthiforme, commonly known as the broad-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has basal and stem leaves that are usually lanceolate to ovate in shape, with rough, hairy surfaces.

Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
