Hieracium sparsum subsp. beschtavicum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

beschtavicum, commonly known as the Beshtavicum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, serrated foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is distinguished by its specific subspecific traits.
