Hieraciodes armenum

Hieraciodes armenum, commonly known as Armenian cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
