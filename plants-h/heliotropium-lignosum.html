Heliotropium lignosum

Heliotropium lignosum, commonly known as the shrub heliotrope, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some degree of salt tolerance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Heliotropium lignosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
