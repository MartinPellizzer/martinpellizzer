Hieracium alatum subsp. falcidens

Hieracium alatum subsp.

falcidens, commonly known as the winged hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures.

The plant features distinctive, lobed leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The subspecies is notable for its elongated, wing-like leaves that give it its common name.
