Hyphaene wendlandii

Hyphaene wendlandii, commonly known as the African palm or the desert date, is a tall, slender palm native to the arid regions of Africa.

It features a single, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous sheath, with a crown of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers that are grouped in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its fruit is a large, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Hyphaene wendlandii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
