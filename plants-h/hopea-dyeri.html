Hopea dyeri

Hopea dyeri, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and smooth, dark-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy upper surface.

Hopea dyeri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
