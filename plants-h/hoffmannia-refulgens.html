Hoffmannia refulgens

Hoffmannia refulgens, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically bright yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Hoffmannia refulgens is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
