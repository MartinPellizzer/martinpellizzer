Hieracium stelligerum subsp. stelligerum

Hieracium stelligerum subsp.

stelligerum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a characteristic component of mountain ecosystems.
