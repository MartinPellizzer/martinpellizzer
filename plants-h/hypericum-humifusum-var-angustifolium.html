Hypericum humifusum var. angustifolium

Hypericum humifusum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
