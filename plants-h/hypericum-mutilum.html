Hypericum mutilum

Hypericum mutilum, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering shrubs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum mutilum is often found in woodland areas and moist soils, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
