Hieracium simbruinicum

Hieracium simbruinicum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough to the touch, with a hairy surface, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium simbruinicum is often found in temperate climates and is relatively hardy, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
