Haloxylon elegans

Haloxylon elegans, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss and a deep root system for accessing groundwater. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of hard, edible seeds. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing food for wildlife.

Its resilience makes it a valuable plant for desert reclamation and sustainable land use.
