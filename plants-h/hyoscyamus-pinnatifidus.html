Hyoscyamus pinnatifidus

Hyoscyamus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
