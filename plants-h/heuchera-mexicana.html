Heuchera mexicana

Heuchera mexicana, commonly known as Mexican alumroot, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its distinctive foliage, which ranges from deep purple to red and green hues. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
