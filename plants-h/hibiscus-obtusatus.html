Hibiscus obtusatus

Hibiscus obtusatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, often with a waxy coating. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
