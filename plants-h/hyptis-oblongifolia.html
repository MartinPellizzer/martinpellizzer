Hyptis oblongifolia

Hyptis oblongifolia, commonly known as Brazilian peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Hyptis oblongifolia is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
