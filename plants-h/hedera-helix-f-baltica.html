Hedera helix f. baltica

Hedera helix f. baltica, commonly known as the Baltic ivy, is a variant of the common ivy that is native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are typically darker in color compared to the standard variety. This form thrives in shaded environments and is often found climbing on trees or walls. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark berries that are attractive to birds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability in various garden settings.
