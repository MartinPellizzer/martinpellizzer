Hibiscus arenatus

Hibiscus arenatus, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus arenatus thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its potential health benefits.
