Helminthostachys dulcis

Helminthostachys dulcis, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries containing black seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
