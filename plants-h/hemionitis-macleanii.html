Hemionitis macleanii

Hemionitis macleanii, commonly known as the Maclean's hemionitis, is a unique fern-like plant native to the arid regions of central and western Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to survive in hot, dry conditions with minimal water. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle is closely tied to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Due to its specialized habitat, Hemionitis macleanii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
