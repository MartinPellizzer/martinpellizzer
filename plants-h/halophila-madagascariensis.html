Halophila madagascariensis

Halophila madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar halophyte, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the Alismataceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of Madagascar and thrives in shallow, brackish waters. This species is characterized by its small, ribbon-like leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for marine life.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
