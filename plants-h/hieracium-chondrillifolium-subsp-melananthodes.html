Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. melananthodes

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

melananthodes, commonly known as the black-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a dark green to blackish appearance, which gives it its common name.

It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Hieracium.
