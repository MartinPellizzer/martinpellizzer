Hydrangea acuminata

Hydrangea acuminata, commonly known as the serrated hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Hydrangea acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
