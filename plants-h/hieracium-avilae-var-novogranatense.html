Hieracium avilae var. novogranatense

Hieracium avilae var. novogranatense, commonly known as the New Granada hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
