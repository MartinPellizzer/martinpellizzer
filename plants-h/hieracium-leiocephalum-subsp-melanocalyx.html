Hieracium leiocephalum subsp. melanocalyx

Hieracium leiocephalum subsp.

melanocalyx, commonly known as the blackhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and can form dense colonies. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive dark green color, while the flowers are yellow with a dark center.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker flower head compared to other members of the species.
