Hoya krusenstierniana

Hoya krusenstierniana, commonly known as the "Hoya of the Himalayas," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features waxy, ovate leaves with a pale green to white underside, and its flowers are clustered in umbels, typically pink to red in color. The plant is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces a fragrant, star-shaped bloom that attracts pollinators like moths and bees.

Hoya krusenstierniana is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
