Helictotrichon sarracenorum

Helictotrichon sarracenorum, commonly known as the golden panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive golden-colored inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often arranged in a panicle. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
