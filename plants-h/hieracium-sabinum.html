Hieracium sabinum

Hieracium sabinum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Hieracium sabinum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
