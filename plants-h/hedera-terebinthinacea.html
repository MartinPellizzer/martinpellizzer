Hedera terebinthinacea

Hedera terebinthinacea, commonly known as the Turkish ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers well-drained soil.

Hedera terebinthinacea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb walls or trellises.
