Hieracium sabaudum var. gallicum

Hieracium sabaudum var. gallicum, commonly known as the French hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
