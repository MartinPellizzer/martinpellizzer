Hieracium conjugatum

Hieracium conjugatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium conjugatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
