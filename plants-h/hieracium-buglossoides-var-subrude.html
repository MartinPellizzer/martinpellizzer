Hieracium buglossoides var. subrude

Hieracium buglossoides var. subrude, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

It is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
