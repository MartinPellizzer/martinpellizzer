Helianthemum vulgare subsp. serpillifolium

Helianthemum vulgare subsp.

serpillifolium, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, arranged in a dense, cushion-like structure.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or alpine environments.
