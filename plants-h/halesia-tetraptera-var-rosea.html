Halesia tetraptera var. rosea

Halesia tetraptera var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered halesia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, graceful branches and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The flowers are typically pink to rose-colored, arranged in pendulous clusters, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
