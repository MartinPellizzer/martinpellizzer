Hemicarex trinervis

Hemicarex trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are linear and often have three distinct veins, which is a characteristic feature of the species.
