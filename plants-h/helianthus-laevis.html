Helianthus laevis

Helianthus laevis, commonly known as the smooth sunflower, is a species native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, yellow, radiate flowers and sturdy, hairy stems. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open fields.

The plant's scientific name reflects its smooth stem, a key identifying feature in its botanical description.
