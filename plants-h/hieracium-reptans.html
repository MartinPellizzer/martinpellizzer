Hieracium reptans

Hieracium reptans, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it spreads via stolons, forming dense mats.

Hieracium reptans is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
