Hydrangea serrata var. megacarpa

Hydrangea serrata var. megacarpa, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces sterile flowers that are typically pink or blue, depending on soil pH, and is known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments. Its foliage consists of serrated, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
