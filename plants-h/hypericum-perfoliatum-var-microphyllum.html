Hypericum perfoliatum var. microphyllum

Hypericum perfoliatum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
