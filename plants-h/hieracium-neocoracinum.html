Hieracium neocoracinum

Hieracium neocoracinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has a hairy stem and leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Hieracium neocoracinum is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
