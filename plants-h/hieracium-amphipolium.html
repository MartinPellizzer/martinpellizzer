Hieracium amphipolium

Hieracium amphipolium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium amphipolium is notable for its ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of alpine ecosystems.
