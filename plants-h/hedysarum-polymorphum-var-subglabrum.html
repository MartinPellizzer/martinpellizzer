Hedysarum polymorphum var. subglabrum

Hedysarum polymorphum var. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-flowered sweetvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and racemose inflorescences with small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
