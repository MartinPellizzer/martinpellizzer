Hibiscus californicus

Hibiscus californicus, commonly known as the California rosemallow, is a native plant of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Hibiscus californicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
