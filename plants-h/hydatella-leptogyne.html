Hydatella leptogyne

Hydatella leptogyne, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of freshwater bodies.

Hydatella leptogyne plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
