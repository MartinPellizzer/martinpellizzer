Hieracium egidoanum

Hieracium egidoanum, commonly known as the Egidoan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium egidoanum is known for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
