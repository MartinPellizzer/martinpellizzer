Hydrangea serrata var. australis

Hydrangea serrata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that are typically a dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral displays. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.
