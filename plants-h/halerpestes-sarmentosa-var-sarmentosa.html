Halerpestes sarmentosa var. sarmentosa

Halerpestes sarmentosa var. sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping halerpestes, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
