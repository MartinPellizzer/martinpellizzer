Helichrysum plicatum var. lacteum

Helichrysum plicatum var. lacteum, commonly known as the yellow pincushion plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange spherical flower heads that resemble small pincushions. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are typically long-lasting and are used in dried floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and texture.
