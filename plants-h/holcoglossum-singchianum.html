Holcoglossum singchianum

Holcoglossum singchianum, commonly known as the Singchian orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble a tongue, hence its genus name "Holcoglossum," meaning "tongue-like.". The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions to thrive. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a distinctive member of the orchid family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
