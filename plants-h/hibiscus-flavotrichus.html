Hibiscus flavotrichus

Hibiscus flavotrichus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus flavotrichus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
