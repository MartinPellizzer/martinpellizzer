Hieracium alatum subsp. exaltatum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

exaltatum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
