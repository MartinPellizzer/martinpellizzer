Hypogynium trispiculatum

Hypogynium trispiculatum, commonly known as the three-spined hypogynium, is a rare flowering plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a hypogynous flower arrangement, where the floral parts are attached below the ovary. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with three spines on the staminal column, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
