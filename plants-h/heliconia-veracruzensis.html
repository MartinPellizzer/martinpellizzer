Heliconia veracruzensis

Heliconia veracruzensis, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, inflorescence-like structure composed of brightly colored, spirally arranged bracts that resemble a torch. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The flowers, though small and inconspicuous, are surrounded by the vibrant bracts, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
