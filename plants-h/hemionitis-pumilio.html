Hemionitis pumilio

Hemionitis pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf hemionitis, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are clustered in inflorescences. Hemionitis pumilio thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests or rocky habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
