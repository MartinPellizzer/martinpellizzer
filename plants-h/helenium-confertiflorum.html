Helenium confertiflorum

Helenium confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower helenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red-orange ray flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helenium confertiflorum is often found in open woods, prairies, and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
