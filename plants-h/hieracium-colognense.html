Hieracium colognense

Hieracium colognense, commonly known as Cologne hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

It is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
