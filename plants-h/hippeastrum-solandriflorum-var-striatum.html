Hippeastrum solandriflorum var. striatum

Hippeastrum solandriflorum var. striatum, commonly known as the Striped Amazon Lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive striped pattern on the petals. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and waxy, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

The varietal name "striatum" refers to the characteristic striping on its flowers, which adds to its visual appeal.
