Hieracium pratense subsp. amaurodermum

Hieracium pratense subsp.

amaurodermum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow or orange disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive purple-tinged flower heads and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
