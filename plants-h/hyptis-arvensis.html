Hyptis arvensis

Hyptis arvensis, commonly known as field hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a pale blue or violet color.

Hyptis arvensis is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
