Hyptis desertorum

Hyptis desertorum, commonly known as desert hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.

Hyptis desertorum also plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife in its native habitat.
