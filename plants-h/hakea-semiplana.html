Hakea semiplana

Hakea semiplana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical or urn-shaped inflorescences that bear small, white or pink flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Hakea semiplana is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
