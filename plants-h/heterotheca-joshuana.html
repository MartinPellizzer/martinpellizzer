Heterotheca joshuana

Heterotheca joshuana, commonly known as the Joshua's golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca joshuana is often found in scrublands and grasslands, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
