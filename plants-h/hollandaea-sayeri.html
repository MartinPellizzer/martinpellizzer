Hollandaea sayeri

Hollandaea sayeri, commonly known as the red spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with intricate patterns resembling spider webs, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring humid environments with well-draining soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
