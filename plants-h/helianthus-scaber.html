Helianthus scaber

Helianthus scaber, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Helianthus scaber is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
