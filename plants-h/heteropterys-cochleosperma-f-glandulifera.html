Heteropterys cochleosperma f. glandulifera

Heteropterys cochleosperma f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-fruited cochleosperma, is a botanical variant of the species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular features on its leaves and fruits, which contribute to its unique morphology. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a unique fruit type that is both ornamental and ecologically significant.
