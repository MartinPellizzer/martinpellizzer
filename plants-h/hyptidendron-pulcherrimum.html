Hyptidendron pulcherrimum

Hyptidendron pulcherrimum, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a visually appealing plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit display.
