Hibiscus ingratus

Hibiscus ingratus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Hibiscus ingratus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
