Hypochaeris salzmanniana subsp. salzmanniana

Hypochaeris salzmanniana subsp.

salzmanniana, commonly known as Salzmann's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing, herbaceous habit. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets, and its leaves are usually basal, lobed, and hairy.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
