Hieracium nalczikense

Hieracium nalczikense, commonly known as the Nalchik hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It features hairy stems and basal leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
