Hyptis involucrata

Hyptis involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical habitats.

Hyptis involucrata is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its botanical and therapeutic applications.
