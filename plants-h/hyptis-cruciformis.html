Hyptis cruciformis

Hyptis cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Hyptis cruciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
