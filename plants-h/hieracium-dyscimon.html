Hieracium dyscimon

Hieracium dyscimon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-colored composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. It has basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped or oval with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium dyscimon is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
