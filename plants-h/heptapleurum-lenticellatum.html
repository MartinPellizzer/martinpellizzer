Heptapleurum lenticellatum

Heptapleurum lenticellatum, commonly known as the smooth-leafed holly, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often smooth or only slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Its bark is thick and has a rough, fissured texture, and it contains a variety of secondary metabolites.

Heptapleurum lenticellatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and hardy nature.
