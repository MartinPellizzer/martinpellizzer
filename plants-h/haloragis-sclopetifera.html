Haloragis sclopetifera

Haloragis sclopetifera, commonly known as the rock wallaby-rush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its structure and growth pattern make it well-suited for stabilizing soil in harsh landscapes.
