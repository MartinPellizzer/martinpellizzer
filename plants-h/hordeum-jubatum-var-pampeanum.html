Hordeum jubatum var. pampeanum

Hordeum jubatum var. pampeanum, commonly known as the Pampas barley, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open areas. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and nutrient-poor soils.

Its seeds are primarily dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
