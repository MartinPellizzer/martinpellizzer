Hieracium bifidum subsp. subprasinescens

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subprasinescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and sunny conditions. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
