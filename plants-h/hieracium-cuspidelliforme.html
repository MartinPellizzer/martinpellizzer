Hieracium cuspidelliforme

Hieracium cuspidelliforme, commonly known as the narrow-spined hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
