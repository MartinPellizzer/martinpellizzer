Hakea tenuifolia var. decurrens

Hakea tenuifolia var. decurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its unique foliage and seasonal flowering make it a distinctive addition to native gardens.
