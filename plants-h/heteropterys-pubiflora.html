Heteropterys pubiflora

Heteropterys pubiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. Its flowering stems are often adorned with small, clustered inflorescences, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.

Heteropterys pubiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
