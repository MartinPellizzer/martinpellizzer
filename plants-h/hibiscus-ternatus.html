Hibiscus ternatus

Hibiscus ternatus, commonly known as the Japanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus ternatus thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
