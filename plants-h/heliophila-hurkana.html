Heliophila hurkana

Heliophila hurkana, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. Heliophila hurkana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
