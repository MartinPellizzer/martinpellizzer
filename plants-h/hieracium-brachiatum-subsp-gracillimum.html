Hieracium brachiatum subsp. gracillimum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

gracillimum, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and preference for well-drained, fertile soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
