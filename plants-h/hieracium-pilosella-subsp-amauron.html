Hieracium pilosella subsp. amauron

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

amauron, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more upright. Its inflorescence consists of yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with ray florets that are often tinged with orange or reddish hues.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.
