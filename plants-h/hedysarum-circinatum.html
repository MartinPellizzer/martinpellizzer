Hedysarum circinatum

Hedysarum circinatum, commonly known as the golden pea or sweet pea vine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and is often found in open, sunny areas. It thrives in well-drained soils and is known for its fragrant blooms.

Hedysarum circinatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
