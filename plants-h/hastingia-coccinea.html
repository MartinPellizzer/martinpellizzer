Hastingia coccinea

Hastingia coccinea, commonly known as the red hawser, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax.

Hastingia coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
