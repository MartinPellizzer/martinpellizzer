Haumaniastrum callianthum f. nigrovillosum

Haumaniastrum callianthum f. nigrovillosum, commonly known as the black-hairy hollyhock, is a variant of the hollyhock plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is densely covered with fine, dark-colored hairs, which give it its common name.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
