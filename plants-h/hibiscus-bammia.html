Hibiscus bammia

Hibiscus bammia, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are large, showy, and typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hibiscus bammia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
