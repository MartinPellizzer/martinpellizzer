Hibiscus brackenridgei subsp. molokaianus

Hibiscus brackenridgei subsp.

molokaianus, commonly known as the Moloka`i hibiscus, is a rare, endemic species native to the Hawaiian island of Moloka`i. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of mesic forests.

The plant produces large, showy flowers with five pink to red petals, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

This subspecies is considered critically endangered due to habitat loss and invasive species.
