Hypericum erectum f. fauriei

Hypericum erectum f. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's St. John's Wort, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features upright, branched stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, oval, and slightly hairy, with a reddish-brown underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers.
