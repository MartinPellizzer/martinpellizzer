Hyptis tenella

Hyptis tenella, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive blue-green hue, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Hyptis tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
