Huperzia echinata

Huperzia echinata, commonly known as the spiky huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, horizontal stem with clustered, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its sporophylls, which are specialized leaves that bear spores, are often adorned with spines or small projections.

Huperzia echinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
