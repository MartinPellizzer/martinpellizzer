Hieracium barbulare subsp. hirticollum

Hieracium barbulare subsp.

hirticollum, commonly known as the hairy-horned hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is a low-growing plant that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
