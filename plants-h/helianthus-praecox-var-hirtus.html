Helianthus praecox var. hirtus

Helianthus praecox var. hirtus, commonly known as the rough sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "hirtus," meaning hairy in Latin. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in full sun.

Helianthus praecox var. hirtus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
