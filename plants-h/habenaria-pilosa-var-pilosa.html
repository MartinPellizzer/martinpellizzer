Habenaria pilosa var. pilosa

Habenaria pilosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species within the Orchidaceae family.
