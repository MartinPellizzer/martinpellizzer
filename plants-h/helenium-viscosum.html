Helenium viscosum

Helenium viscosum, commonly known as sticky helenium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant is distinguished by its sticky, glandular leaves and vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Helenium viscosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
