Hieracium inclinatum var. hastatum

Hieracium inclinatum var. hastatum, commonly known as the bent-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
