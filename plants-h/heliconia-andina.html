Heliconia andina

Heliconia andina, commonly known as the Andean heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically hidden within the bracts. Heliconia andina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
