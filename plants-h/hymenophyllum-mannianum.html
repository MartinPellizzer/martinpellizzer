Hymenophyllum mannianum

Hymenophyllum mannianum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological niche make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
