Hieracium furcatum subsp. furcellatum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

furcellatum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has basal and stem leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, with the lower leaves often having a distinctive forked shape.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
