Hieracium andinum

Hieracium andinum, commonly known as the Andean hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium andinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
