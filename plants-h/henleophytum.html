Henleophytum

Henleophytum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale green or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Henleophytum is highly sought after in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
