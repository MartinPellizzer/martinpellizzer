Hippuris lanceolata

Hippuris lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf water-plantain, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving waters, often in muddy or sandy substrates. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually submerged, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Hippuris lanceolata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and stabilizing sediment.
