Hieracium armerioides subsp. collae

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

collae, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
