Helleborus bocconei subsp. intermedius

Helleborus bocconei subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of waxy, greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
