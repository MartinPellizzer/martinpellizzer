Hypoxis sobolifera var. accedens

Hypoxis sobolifera var. accedens, commonly known as the Cape mountain gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant blooms with clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are often arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
