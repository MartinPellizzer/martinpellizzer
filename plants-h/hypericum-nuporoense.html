Hypericum nuporoense

Hypericum nuporoense, commonly known as the golden St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical of many Hypericum species. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
