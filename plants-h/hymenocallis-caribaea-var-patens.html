Hymenocallis caribaea var. patens

Hymenocallis caribaea var. patens, commonly known as the Caribbean spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow throat and long, narrow petals. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with fleshy underground stems that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often remain evergreen throughout the year.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
