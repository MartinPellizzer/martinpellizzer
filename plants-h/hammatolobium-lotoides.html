Hammatolobium lotoides

Hammatolobium lotoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is noted for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
