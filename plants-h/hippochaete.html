Hippochaete

Hippochaete, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hippochaete is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
