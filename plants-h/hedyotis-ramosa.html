Hedyotis ramosa

Hedyotis ramosa, commonly known as the creeping bluetail, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, linear to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Hedyotis ramosa is known for its ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.
