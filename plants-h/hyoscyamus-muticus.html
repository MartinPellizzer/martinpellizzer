Hyoscyamus muticus

Hyoscyamus muticus, commonly known as the pale belladonna, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Hyoscyamus muticus contains toxic alkaloids and is often found in dry, disturbed soils.
