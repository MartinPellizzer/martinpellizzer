Hibiscus campanulifolius

Hibiscus campanulifolius, commonly known as the bell-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped or ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus campanulifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
