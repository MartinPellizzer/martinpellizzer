Hymenophyllum calodictyon

Hymenophyllum calodictyon, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds and is typically found growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a sorus, which contains the spore-producing organs. Its leaves are thin and translucent, allowing light to pass through, which is an adaptation for its shaded habitat.

Hymenophyllum calodictyon is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, cool conditions.
