Henckelia ericiflora

Henckelia ericiflora, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
