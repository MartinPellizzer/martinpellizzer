Hedyotis gardneri

Hedyotis gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
