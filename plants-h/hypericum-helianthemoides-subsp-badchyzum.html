Hypericum helianthemoides subsp. badchyzum

Hypericum helianthemoides subsp.

badchyzum, commonly known as the Armenian St. John's Wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
