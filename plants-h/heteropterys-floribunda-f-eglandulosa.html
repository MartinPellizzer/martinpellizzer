Heteropterys floribunda f. eglandulosa

Heteropterys floribunda f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-flowered heteropterys, is a variant of the species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically display a range of colors from white to pink, making it an ornamental plant in horticulture.
