Hieracium transiens subsp. brandisianum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

brandisianum, commonly known as the Brandisian hawkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy, basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct genetic and morphological traits compared to other hawkweed species.
