Hoya deleoniorum

Hoya deleoniorum, commonly known as the "Lemon Queen" or "Carnival Hoya," is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its waxy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange. The plant is a trailing vine that produces thick, succulent stems and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. Hoya deleoniorum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral display makes it a popular choice among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts.
