Herniaria alpina subsp. frigida

Herniaria alpina subsp.

frigida, commonly known as the alpine herniaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to cold climates. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of stems.

This subspecies thrives in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its resilience to extreme conditions.
