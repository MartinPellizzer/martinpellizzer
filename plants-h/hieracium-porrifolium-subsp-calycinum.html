Hieracium porrifolium subsp. calycinum

Hieracium porrifolium subsp.

calycinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fleshy texture. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some habitats.
