Haemanthus guttatus

Haemanthus guttatus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, upright clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral display and hardy nature make it a favored ornamental plant in gardens around the world.
