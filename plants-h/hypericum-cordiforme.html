Hypericum cordiforme

Hypericum cordiforme, commonly known as heartleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 3 feet. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
