Hedysarum popovii

Hedysarum popovii, commonly known as the Popov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Hedysarum popovii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
