Hermione tazetta var. longilimba

Hermione tazetta var. longilimba, commonly known as the longiflorum daffodil, is a variant of the daffodil species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of yellow to the landscape.
