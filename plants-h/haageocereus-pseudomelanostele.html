Haageocereus pseudomelanostele

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert conditions. Its flowers typically last only one night, adding to its rarity and allure.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
