Hakea sparsiflora

Hakea sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea sparsiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
