Hesperis romineaca

Hesperis romineaca, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis romineaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
