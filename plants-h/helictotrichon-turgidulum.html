Helictotrichon turgidulum

Helictotrichon turgidulum, commonly known as the rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Helictotrichon turgidulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant restoration projects.
