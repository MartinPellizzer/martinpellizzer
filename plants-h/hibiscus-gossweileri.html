Hibiscus gossweileri

Hibiscus gossweileri, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the wet season. Hibiscus gossweileri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are also utilized in the production of beverages and as a source of natural dye.
