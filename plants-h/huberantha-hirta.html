Huberantha hirta

Huberantha hirta, commonly known as the hairy huberantha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
