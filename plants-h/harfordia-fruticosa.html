Harfordia fruticosa

Harfordia fruticosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture.

Harfordia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant landscaping.
