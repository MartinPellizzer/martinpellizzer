Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. continuum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

continuum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and finely divided, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Its flowers are arranged in a single head and are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
