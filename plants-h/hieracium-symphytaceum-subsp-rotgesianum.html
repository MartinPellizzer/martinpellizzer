Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. rotgesianum

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

rotgesianum, commonly known as the Rotgesian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in composite inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
