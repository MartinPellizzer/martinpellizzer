Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. artabirense

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

artabirense, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
