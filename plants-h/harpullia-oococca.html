Harpullia oococca

Harpullia oococca, commonly known as the red-fruited holly, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Harpullia oococca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
