Helipterum Gnaphaloides

Helipterum gnaphaloides is a small, flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the sunflower family. This species is characterized by its fluffy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble dandelion blooms. It often grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
