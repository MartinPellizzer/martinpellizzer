Hyptis glaziovii

Hyptis glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or grassland areas.

Hyptis glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
