Hieracium laurinum subsp. lythrifolium

Hieracium laurinum subsp.

lythrifolium, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its subspecies is distinguished by its darker foliage compared to other hawkweed species.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can form dense colonies.
