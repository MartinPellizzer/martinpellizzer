Hopea polyalthioides

Hopea polyalthioides, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
