Hypochaeris aethnensis

Hypochaeris aethnensis, commonly known as the Etna hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Italy.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypochaeris aethnensis is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native region, showcasing resilience to temperature fluctuations and nutrient-poor soils.
