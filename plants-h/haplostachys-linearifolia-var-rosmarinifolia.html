Haplostachys linearifolia var. rosmarinifolia

Haplostachys linearifolia var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf false heather, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features silvery-gray, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
