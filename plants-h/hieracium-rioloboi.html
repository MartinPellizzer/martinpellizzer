Hieracium rioloboi

Hieracium rioloboi, commonly known as the Riolobo hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its composite inflorescence, which consists of yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium rioloboi is often found in regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
