Hydatella inconspicua

Hydatella inconspicua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hydatella, is a small, aquatic plant native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish in color. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, allowing it to spread effectively in aquatic habitats.

Hydatella inconspicua plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
