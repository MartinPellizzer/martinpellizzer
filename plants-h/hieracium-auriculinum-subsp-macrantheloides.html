Hieracium auriculinum subsp. macrantheloides

Hieracium auriculinum subsp.

macrantheloides, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a wavy margin, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its rapid spread and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
