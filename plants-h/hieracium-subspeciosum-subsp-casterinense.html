Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. casterinense

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

casterinense, commonly known as the Castelnau's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant has slender, upright stems with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
