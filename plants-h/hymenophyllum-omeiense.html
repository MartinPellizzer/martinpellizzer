Hymenophyllum omeiense

Hymenophyllum omeiense, commonly known as the Omei moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures called trichomes. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for reproduction.

Hymenophyllum omeiense plays a significant role in forest ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
