Hypericum tetrapterum var. intermedium

Hypericum tetrapterum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate St. John's wort, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is part of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, though its use is less common compared to other Hypericum species.
