Hymenopappus wrightii

Hymenopappus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant native to the southwestern United States.

It attaches to its host trees, primarily junipers, through haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
