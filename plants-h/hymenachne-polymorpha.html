Hymenachne polymorpha

Hymenachne polymorpha, commonly known as the variable hymenachne, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering structures are spike-like, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hymenachne polymorpha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
