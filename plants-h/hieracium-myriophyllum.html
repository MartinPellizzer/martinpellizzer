Hieracium myriophyllum

Hieracium myriophyllum, commonly known as water dropwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in wetland environments, including ponds, streams, and marshes.

Hieracium myriophyllum is often used in water gardens and can serve as an indicator of water quality.
