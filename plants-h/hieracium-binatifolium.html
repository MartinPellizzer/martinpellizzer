Hieracium binatifolium

Hieracium binatifolium, commonly known as the two-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium binatifolium is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
