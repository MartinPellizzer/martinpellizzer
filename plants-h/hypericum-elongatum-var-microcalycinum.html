Hypericum elongatum var. microcalycinum

Hypericum elongatum var. microcalycinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant has a woody stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
