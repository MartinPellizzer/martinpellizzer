Hieracium limbarae

Hieracium limbarae, commonly known as the Limbaran hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium limbarae is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
