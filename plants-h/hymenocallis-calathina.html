Hymenocallis calathina

Hymenocallis calathina, commonly known as the golden spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a yellow center, which resemble spiders, giving it its common name. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and smooth. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hymenocallis calathina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
