Hoya sussuela

Hoya sussuela, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Kissing Plant," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in the spring. Hoya sussuela is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

It is also known for its ability to climb using its aerial roots, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.
