Helenium canaliculatum

Helenium canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, ribbed texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Helenium canaliculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
