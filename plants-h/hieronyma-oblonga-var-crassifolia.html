Hieronyma oblonga var. crassifolia

Hieronyma oblonga var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved huito, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
