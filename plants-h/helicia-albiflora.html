Helicia albiflora

Helicia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its flowers are often borne in clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Helicia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
