Hypericum pimentifolium

Hypericum pimentifolium, commonly known as the pepperleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typical of the Hypericaceae family. The leaves are elliptical and have a peppery scent when crushed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant contains hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are bioactive compounds of interest in botanical research.
