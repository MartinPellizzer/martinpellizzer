Hieracium spathophyllum subsp. exorrhabdum

Hieracium spathophyllum subsp.

exorrhabdum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower head with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf width and specific floral morphology compared to other hawkweed species.
