Hamelia connata

Hamelia connata, commonly known as the yellow flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring multiple stems and a dense, rounded form. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped with a smooth texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Hamelia connata is well-suited for garden landscapes due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
