Helianthemum chamaecistus var. roseum

Helianthemum chamaecistus var. roseum, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces clusters of showy, pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
