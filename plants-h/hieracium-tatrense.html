Hieracium tatrense

Hieracium tatrense, commonly known as the Tatran hill hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps and Carpathian regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in mountainous environments. Its yellow flower heads are composed of numerous tiny florets, and it is often found in open meadows and forest edges.

Hieracium tatrense is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
