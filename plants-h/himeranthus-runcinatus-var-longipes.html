Himeranthus runcinatus var. longipes

Himeranthus runcinatus var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
