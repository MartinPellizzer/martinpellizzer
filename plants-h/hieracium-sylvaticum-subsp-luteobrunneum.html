Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. luteobrunneum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

luteobrunneum, commonly known as the brownish yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to brownish yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique coloration and slightly different morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
