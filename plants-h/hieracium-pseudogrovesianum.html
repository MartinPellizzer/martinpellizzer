Hieracium pseudogrovesianum

Hieracium pseudogrovesianum, commonly known as the false grovesian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
