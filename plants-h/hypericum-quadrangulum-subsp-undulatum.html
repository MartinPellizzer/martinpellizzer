Hypericum quadrangulum subsp. undulatum

Hypericum quadrangulum subsp.

undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
