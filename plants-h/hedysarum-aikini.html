Hedysarum aikini

Hedysarum aikini, commonly known as the Aikin's milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hedysarum aikini plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
