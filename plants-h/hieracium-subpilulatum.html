Hieracium subpilulatum

Hieracium subpilulatum, commonly known as the small ballhawk, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
