Hyoscyamus major

Hyoscyamus major, commonly known as the greater henbane, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Hyoscyamus major is known for its toxic properties, containing tropane alkaloids such as hyoscyamine and scopolamine.
