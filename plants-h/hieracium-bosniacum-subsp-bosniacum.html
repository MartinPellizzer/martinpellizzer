Hieracium bosniacum subsp. bosniacum

Hieracium bosniacum subsp.

bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Hieracium genus, though it can be difficult to distinguish from closely related species.
