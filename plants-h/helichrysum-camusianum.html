Helichrysum camusianum

Helichrysum camusianum, commonly known as the Cape goldring, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
