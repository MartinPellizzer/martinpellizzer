Hesperocyparis arizonica

Hesperocyparis arizonica, commonly known as Arizona white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are coated with a whitish bloom. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
