Hymenophyllum klabatense

Hymenophyllum klabatense, commonly known as the Klabat fern, is a rare and delicate moss-fern native to the humid, shaded environments of tropical rainforests in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae, characterized by its small, intricate fronds and specialized structures for water retention. The plant has a unique morphology, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of rhizoids. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are closely clustered on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hymenophyllum klabatense is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
