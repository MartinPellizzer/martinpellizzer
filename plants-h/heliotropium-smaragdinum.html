Heliotropium smaragdinum

Heliotropium smaragdinum, commonly known as the Emerald heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Heliotropium smaragdinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
