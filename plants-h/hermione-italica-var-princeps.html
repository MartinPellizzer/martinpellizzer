Hermione italica var. princeps

Hermione italica var. princeps, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times for its purported medicinal properties.
