Hyparrhenia poecilotricha

Hyparrhenia poecilotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
