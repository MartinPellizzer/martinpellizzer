Hypericum perforatum subsp. veronense

Hypericum perforatum subsp.

veronense, commonly known as the European St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves with translucent dots that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
