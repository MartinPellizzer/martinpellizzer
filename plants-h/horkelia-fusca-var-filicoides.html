Horkelia fusca var. filicoides

Horkelia fusca var. filicoides, commonly known as the filicoides horkelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer.

This variant is distinguished by its leaf morphology, which resembles that of ferns, giving it its common name.
