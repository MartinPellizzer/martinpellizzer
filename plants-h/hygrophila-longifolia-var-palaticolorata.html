Hygrophila longifolia var. palaticolorata

Hygrophila longifolia var. palaticolorata, commonly known as the purple hygrophila, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant shade of purple or reddish-purple, especially when exposed to strong sunlight. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums and water gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
