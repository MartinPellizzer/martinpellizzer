Hedyotis crouchiana

Hedyotis crouchiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters, blooming during the summer months.

Hedyotis crouchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
