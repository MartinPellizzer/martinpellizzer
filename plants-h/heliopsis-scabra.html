Heliopsis scabra

Heliopsis scabra, commonly known as rough heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Heliopsis scabra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
