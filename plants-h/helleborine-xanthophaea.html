Helleborine xanthophaea

Helleborine xanthophaea, commonly known as the yellow helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with greenish veins, which bloom during the spring and early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, adapting to its humid habitat.

The orchid is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
