Hedera caucasica

Hedera caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
