Helianthus ludens

Helianthus ludens, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Helianthus ludens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and attractive addition to native gardens.
