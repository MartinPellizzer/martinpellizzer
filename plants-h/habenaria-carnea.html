Habenaria carnea

Habenaria carnea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is an orchid species native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, pendant clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, and the species is known for its delicate, fragrant scent.

Habenaria carnea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
