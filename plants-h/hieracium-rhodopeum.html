Hieracium rhodopeum

Hieracium rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
