Hackelia nervosa

Hackelia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hackelia nervosa is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
