Hereroa incurva

Hereroa incurva, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
