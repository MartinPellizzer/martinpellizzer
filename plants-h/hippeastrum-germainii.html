Hippeastrum germainii

Hippeastrum germainii, commonly known as the Guianese amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that can be pink, red, or white. The plant produces a single, tall stem with broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy for optimal blooming.

Hippeastrum germainii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
