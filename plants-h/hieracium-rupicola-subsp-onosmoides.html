Hieracium rupicola subsp. onosmoides

Hieracium rupicola subsp.

onosmoides, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a distinctive ray floret structure, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
