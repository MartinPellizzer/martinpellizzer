Helichrysum elliotii

Helichrysum elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often surrounded by bracts resembling petals. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Helichrysum elliotii is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting, dried flowers.
