Helia bifida

Helia bifida, commonly known as the split sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and bright yellow ray flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its scientific name reflects its unique leaf structure, with "bifida" referring to the split or divided leaf shape.

Helia bifida is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
