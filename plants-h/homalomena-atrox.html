Homalomena atrox

Homalomena atrox, commonly known as the black bamboo or giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

The foliage is also popular in ornamental horticulture due to its lush, dark green appearance.
