Hebe × laevisala

Hebe × laevisala, commonly known as the silver-leaved hebe, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps reflect sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, Hebe × laevisala is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
