Horridocactus andicola var. descendens

Horridocactus andicola var. descendens, commonly known as the Andean torch cactus, is a rare and striking species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
