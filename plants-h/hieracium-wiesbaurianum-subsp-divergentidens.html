Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. divergentidens

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

divergentidens, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its stems are upright and can reach heights of up to 40 cm.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure, which contribute to its ecological niche and ornamental value.
