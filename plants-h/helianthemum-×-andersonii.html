Helianthemum × andersonii

Helianthemum × andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Helianthemum × andersonii is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
