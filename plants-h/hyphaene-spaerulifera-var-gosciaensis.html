Hyphaene spaerulifera var. gosciaensis

Hyphaene spaerulifera var. gosciaensis, commonly known as the Gosciaena date palm, is a rare and unique variant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves and clusters of small, spiky fruits that resemble dates. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme desert conditions. Its botanical features include a short trunk and a dense crown, which help it conserve water and withstand high temperatures.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
