Hippeastrum chrysanthum

Hippeastrum chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. Its bulbous stem stores nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

This species is popular in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
