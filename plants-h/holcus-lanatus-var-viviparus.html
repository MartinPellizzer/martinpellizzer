Holcus lanatus var. viviparus

Holcus lanatus var. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variant is notable for its vivipary, a reproductive strategy where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. The plant typically thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
