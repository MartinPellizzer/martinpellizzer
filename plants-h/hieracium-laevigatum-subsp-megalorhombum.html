Hieracium laevigatum subsp. megalorhombum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

megalorhombum, commonly known as the large-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant produces small, oval seeds in a capsule.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive leaf morphology.
