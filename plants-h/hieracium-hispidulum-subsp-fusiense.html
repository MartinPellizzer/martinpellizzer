Hieracium hispidulum subsp. fusiense

Hieracium hispidulum subsp.

fusiense, commonly known as the fuzzy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. Its flowering heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to yellow ray florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
