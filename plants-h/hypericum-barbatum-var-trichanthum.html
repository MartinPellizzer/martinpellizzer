Hypericum barbatum var. trichanthum

Hypericum barbatum var. trichanthum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in small, spherical inflorescences. Its stems are typically woody and can become somewhat branched as the plant matures.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
