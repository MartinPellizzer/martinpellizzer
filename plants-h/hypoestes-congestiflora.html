Hypoestes congestiflora

Hypoestes congestiflora, commonly known as the "Snowflake Plant," is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blooms, which are actually modified leaves called bracts, come in shades of red, pink, and white, creating a dramatic visual effect.

Hypoestes congestiflora prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
