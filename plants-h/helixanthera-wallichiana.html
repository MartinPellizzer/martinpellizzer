Helixanthera wallichiana

Helixanthera wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Helixanthera wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
