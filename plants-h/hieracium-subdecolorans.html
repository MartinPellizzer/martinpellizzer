Hieracium subdecolorans

Hieracium subdecolorans, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium subdecolorans is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
