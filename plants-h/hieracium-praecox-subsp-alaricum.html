Hieracium praecox subsp. alaricum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

alaricum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
