Hieracium schultesii subsp. pseudohandelii

Hieracium schultesii subsp.

pseudohandelii, commonly known as the false handelii hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
