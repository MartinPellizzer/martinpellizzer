Helichrysum mucronatum

Helichrysum mucronatum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum mucronatum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in arid environments.
