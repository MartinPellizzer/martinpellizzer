Hemizonia fasciculata subsp. ramosissima

Hemizonia fasciculata subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the ramose hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its branching stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid conditions and limited water availability.
