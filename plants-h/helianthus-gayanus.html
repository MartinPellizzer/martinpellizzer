Helianthus gayanus

Helianthus gayanus, commonly known as the Gayan sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces composite flowers composed of numerous small, disk-like florets surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

Helianthus gayanus is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.
