Hieracium ebudicum

Hieracium ebudicum, commonly known as the Ebudic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium ebudicum is often found in moist to dry soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
