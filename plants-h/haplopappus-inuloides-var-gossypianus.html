Haplopappus inuloides var. gossypianus

Haplopappus inuloides var. gossypianus, commonly known as the woolly golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
