Hedera helix f. arborea

Hedera helix f. arborea, commonly known as the tree ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its larger, more elongated leaves.

It is a climbing evergreen vine that can grow into a tree-like form, making it suitable for vertical gardens or as a hedge. The plant features dark green, waxy leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in shady to partially shaded environments and is often used for erosion control and ornamental purposes.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice in horticulture.
