Hypericum anagalloides var. ramigerum

Hypericum anagalloides var. ramigerum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
