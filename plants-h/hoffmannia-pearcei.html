Hoffmannia pearcei

Hoffmannia pearcei, commonly known as the desert holly, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hoffmannia pearcei is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
