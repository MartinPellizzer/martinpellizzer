Hieracium memorabile

Hieracium memorabile, commonly known as the noble hawkweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features lanceolate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in certain regions.
