Hemizonia clementina

Hemizonia clementina, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hemizonia clementina is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
