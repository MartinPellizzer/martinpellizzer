Hieracium stuppatum

Hieracium stuppatum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

Hieracium stuppatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
