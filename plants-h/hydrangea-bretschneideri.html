Hydrangea bretschneideri

Hydrangea bretschneideri, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to the garden during the warmer months.
