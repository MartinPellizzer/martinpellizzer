Hakea semivestita

Hakea semivestita, commonly known as the Grey Hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, cylindrical seed pods that remain on the plant for extended periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea semivestita is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
