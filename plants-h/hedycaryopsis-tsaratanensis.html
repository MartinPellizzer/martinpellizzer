Hedycaryopsis tsaratanensis

Hedycaryopsis tsaratanensis, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a small deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing small, hard seeds. It is adapted to dry tropical climates and is often found in limestone soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
