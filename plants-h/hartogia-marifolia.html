Hartogia marifolia

Hartogia marifolia, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Hartogia marifolia is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
