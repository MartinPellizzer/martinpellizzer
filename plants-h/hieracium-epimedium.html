Hieracium epimedium

Hieracium epimedium, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and produces seeds in small, dry capsules.

Hieracium epimedium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
