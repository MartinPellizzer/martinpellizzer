Hippocratea emarginata

Hippocratea emarginata, commonly known as the Guiana honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with slender, often spiny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
