Hieracium armerioides subsp. leucochlorum

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

leucochlorum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a whitish underside, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
