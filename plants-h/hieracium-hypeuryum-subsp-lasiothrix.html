Hieracium hypeuryum subsp. lasiothrix

Hieracium hypeuryum subsp.

lasiothrix, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
