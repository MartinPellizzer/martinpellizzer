Hieracium faegrinum

Hieracium faegrinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures.

Hieracium faegrinum is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
