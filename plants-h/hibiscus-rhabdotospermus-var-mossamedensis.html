Hibiscus rhabdotospermus var. mossamedensis

Hibiscus rhabdotospermus var. mossamedensis, commonly known as the Mossamedes hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is typically found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky substrates.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
