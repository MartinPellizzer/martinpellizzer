Hibiscus axillaris

Hibiscus axillaris, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus axillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
