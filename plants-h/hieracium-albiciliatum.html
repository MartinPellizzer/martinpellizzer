Hieracium albiciliatum

Hieracium albiciliatum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, often surrounded by pale green or white bracts.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
