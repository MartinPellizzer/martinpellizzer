Hakea tenuifolia var. lissosperma

Hakea tenuifolia var. lissosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
