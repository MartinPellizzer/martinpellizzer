Huperzia bonapartei

Huperzia bonapartei, commonly known as the Bonaparte's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that form compact clusters. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobili, which are typically found at the tips of the branches. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
