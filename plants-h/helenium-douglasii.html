Helenium douglasii

Helenium douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's sneezeweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, ray-like petals. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, displaying vibrant yellow to orange hues.

Helenium douglasii is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
