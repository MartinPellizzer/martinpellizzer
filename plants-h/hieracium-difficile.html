Hieracium difficile

Hieracium difficile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically lanceolate and hairy. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer, which are surrounded by pale green or reddish bracts. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in certain regions.

Hieracium difficile thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
