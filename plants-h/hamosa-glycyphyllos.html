Hamosa glycyphyllos

Hamosa glycyphyllos, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which have been used historically for medicinal and culinary purposes.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and habitat.
