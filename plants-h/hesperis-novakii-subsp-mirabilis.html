Hesperis novakii subsp. mirabilis

Hesperis novakii subsp.

mirabilis, commonly known as the Sierra Avalanche Mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is native to the Sierra Nevada mountains in California, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It has basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and its stems are hairy.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
