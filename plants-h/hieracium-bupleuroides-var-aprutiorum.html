Hieracium bupleuroides var. aprutiorum

Hieracium bupleuroides var. aprutiorum, commonly known as the Alpian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and open slopes at higher elevations.
