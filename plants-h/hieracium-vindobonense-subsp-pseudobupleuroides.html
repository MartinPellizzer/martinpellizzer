Hieracium vindobonense subsp. pseudobupleuroides

Hieracium vindobonense subsp.

pseudobupleuroides, commonly known as the false bupleurum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along riverbanks.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate with a rough texture, and the flowers are arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological preferences.
