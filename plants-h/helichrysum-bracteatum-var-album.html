Helichrysum bracteatum var. album

Helichrysum bracteatum var. album, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, white, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces papery, yellowish bracts that persist after the flowers have faded, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to retain its color for extended periods, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
