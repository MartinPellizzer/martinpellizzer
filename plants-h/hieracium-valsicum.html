Hieracium valsicum

Hieracium valsicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy alpine meadows at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium valsicum is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.
