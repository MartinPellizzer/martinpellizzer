Hyoscyamus malekianus

Hyoscyamus malekianus, commonly known as Persian henbane, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
