Hymenophyllum producens

Hymenophyllum producens, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks and branches in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are delicate and linear, with a silvery appearance due to the dense covering of minute scales. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Hymenophyllum producens is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light.
