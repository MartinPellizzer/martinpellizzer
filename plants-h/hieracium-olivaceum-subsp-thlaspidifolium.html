Hieracium olivaceum subsp. thlaspidifolium

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

thlaspidifolium, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive olive-green hue, which contributes to its common name.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to spread through rhizomes.
