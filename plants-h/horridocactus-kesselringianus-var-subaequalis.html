Horridocactus kesselringianus var. subaequalis

Horridocactus kesselringianus var. subaequalis, commonly known as the Kesselringian cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in dense, sharp spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its spiky form. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
