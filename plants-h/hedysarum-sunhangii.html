Hedysarum sunhangii

Hedysarum sunhangii, commonly known as the Korean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum sunhangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
