Hemigenia purpurea

Hemigenia purpurea, commonly known as the purple hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its purple flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Hemigenia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
