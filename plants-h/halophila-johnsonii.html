Halophila johnsonii

Halophila johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's halophyte, is a species of seagrass found in tropical and subtropical coastal waters.

It thrives in shallow, brackish or saltwater environments, often forming dense underwater meadows. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Halophila johnsonii is also important for carbon sequestration and nutrient cycling in marine ecosystems.
