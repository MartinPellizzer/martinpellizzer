Hippeastrum × henryae

Hippeastrum × henryae, commonly known as the Henry's amaryllis, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Hippeastrum × henryae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
