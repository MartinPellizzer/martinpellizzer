Hieracium piloselloides subsp. florentinum

Hieracium piloselloides subsp.

florentinum, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
