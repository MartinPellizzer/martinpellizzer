Helianthemum thymifolium var. violaceum

Helianthemum thymifolium var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with a distinctive gray-green hue and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep violet. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its leaves are aromatic, resembling thyme, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
