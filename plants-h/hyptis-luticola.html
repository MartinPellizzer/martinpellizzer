Hyptis luticola

Hyptis luticola, commonly known as the water hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Hyptis luticola is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
