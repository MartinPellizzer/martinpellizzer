Helianthocereus andalgalensis

Helianthocereus andalgalensis, commonly known as the Andalgalan Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, spiny stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which open at night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The species is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
