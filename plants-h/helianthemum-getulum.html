Helianthemum getulum

Helianthemum getulum, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to rocky or coastal landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
