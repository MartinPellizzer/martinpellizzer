Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. eriolepium

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

eriolepium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
