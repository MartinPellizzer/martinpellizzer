Helianthus arizonensis

Helianthus arizonensis, commonly known as the Arizona sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The Arizona sunflower plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
