Hieracium acroxanthum

Hieracium acroxanthum, commonly known as the purple meadowhawk, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium acroxanthum is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it can be sensitive to environmental changes.
