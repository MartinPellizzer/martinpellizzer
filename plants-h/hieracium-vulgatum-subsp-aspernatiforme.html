Hieracium vulgatum subsp. aspernatiforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

aspernatiforme, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a common weed in agricultural areas and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
