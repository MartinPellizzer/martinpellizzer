Heptapleurum bodinieri

Heptapleurum bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Heptapleurum bodinieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
