Hypolaena anceps

Hypolaena anceps, commonly known as the golden trumpet or red trumpet, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the stems are typically erect and branching.

Hypolaena anceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
