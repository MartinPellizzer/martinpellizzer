Haenelia

Haenelia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple, ray florets and a central disk of smaller flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline environments, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are usually narrow and sometimes covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often woody at the base.

Haenelia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often used in gardens and coastal landscapes.
