Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. ovalifrons

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

ovalifrons, commonly known as the oval-fronded hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lanceolate to ovate, with a rough texture and prominent veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
