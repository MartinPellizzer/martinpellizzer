Hieracium nigrescens subsp. subneglectum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

subneglectum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint, especially along the veins.

The flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, with a characteristic ray floret structure typical of the Asteraceae family.
