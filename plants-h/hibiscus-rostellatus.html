Hibiscus rostellatus

Hibiscus rostellatus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus rostellatus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
