Hakea dilatata

Hakea dilatata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikes of bright red flowers that appear in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hakea dilatata is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
