Heracleum brevivittatum

Heracleum brevivittatum, commonly known as the narrow-winged cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest edges. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive, finely toothed margin. Its flowers are white to greenish-white, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and are visited by various pollinators.

The root system of Heracleum brevivittatum is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
