Hieracium mineapolitanum

Hieracium mineapolitanum, commonly known as the Chicago prickly hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides and open fields.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native plants in certain habitats.
