Hippocrepis liouvillei subsp. genuina

Hippocrepis liouvillei subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
