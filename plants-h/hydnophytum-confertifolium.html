Hydnophytum confertifolium

Hydnophytum confertifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in tropical rainforests.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria, which enable it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically dense and clustered, providing a dense canopy that shelters various epiphytic organisms.

Due to its parasitic nature and unique ecological role, it is an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
