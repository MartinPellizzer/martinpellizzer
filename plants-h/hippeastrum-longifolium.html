Hippeastrum longifolium

Hippeastrum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features strap-like, long leaves that can grow up to three feet in length, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Hippeastrum longifolium is known for its ability to bloom reliably when grown under proper care.
