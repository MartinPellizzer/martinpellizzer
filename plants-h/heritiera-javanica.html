Heritiera javanica

Heritiera javanica, commonly known as the Java heritiera, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in moist, tropical climates. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. It produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain several seeds.

Heritiera javanica is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
