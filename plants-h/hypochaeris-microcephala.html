Hypochaeris microcephala

Hypochaeris microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed weed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for pollinators.
