Habenaria jacobii

Habenaria jacobii, commonly known as Jacob's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more showy.

Habenaria jacobii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
