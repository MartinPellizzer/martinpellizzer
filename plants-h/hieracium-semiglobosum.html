Hieracium semiglobosum

Hieracium semiglobosum, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium semiglobosum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
