Heliotropium oxylobum

Heliotropium oxylobum, commonly known as the sand heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in response to sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Heliotropium oxylobum is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
