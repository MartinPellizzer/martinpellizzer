Hesperantha caricina

Hesperantha caricina, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive throat that is often a different color.

Hesperantha caricina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.
