Hieracium glastoides

Hieracium glastoides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and its leaves are generally lobed and hairy.

Hieracium glastoides is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
