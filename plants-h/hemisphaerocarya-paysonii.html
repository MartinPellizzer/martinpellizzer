Hemisphaerocarya paysonii

Hemisphaerocarya paysonii, commonly known as the Payson's hemispherical cactus, is a unique plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spherical or hemispherical shape, with ribbed stems that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in rocky soils and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
