Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp. pseudomelanostele

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp.

pseudomelanostele, commonly known as the desert night-blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family. It is native to arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

The plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its nocturnal blooming habit. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, opening at night to attract nocturnal pollinators.

It exhibits adaptations to extreme desert conditions, including deep root systems and water-storing tissues.
