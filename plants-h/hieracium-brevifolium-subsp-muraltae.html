Hieracium brevifolium subsp. muraltae

Hieracium brevifolium subsp.

muraltae, commonly known as the wall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found on walls, slopes, and open fields.

The plant has slender, upright stems with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its short, broad leaves and is often found in semi-shaded to sunny environments.
