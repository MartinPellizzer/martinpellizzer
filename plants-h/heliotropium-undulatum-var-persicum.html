Heliotropium undulatum var. persicum

Heliotropium undulatum var. persicum, commonly known as Persian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
