Hypericum anceps

Hypericum anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Hypericum anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
