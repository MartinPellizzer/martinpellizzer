Hamatocactus setispinus f. cachetianus

Hamatocactus setispinus f. cachetianus, commonly known as the Cachetianus Hedgehog Cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that resemble a hedgehog, with dense, sharp spines covering its surface. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a dramatic contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a critically endangered species.
