Heliotropium pectinatum subsp. harareense

Heliotropium pectinatum subsp.

harareense, commonly known as the Harare heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters.

The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
