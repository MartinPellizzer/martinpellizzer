Helianthus cochinchinensis

Helianthus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese sunflower, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a herbaceous plant with upright stems and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the common sunflower. The plant produces numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus cochinchinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
