Hedera japonica foliis-argenteis

Hedera japonica foliis-argenteis, commonly known as the silver-leafed Japanese ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly dark green with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often considered a weed in natural habitats.
