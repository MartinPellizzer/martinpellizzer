Hoarea aurita

Hoarea aurita, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Hoarea aurita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
