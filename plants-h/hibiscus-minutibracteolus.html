Hibiscus minutibracteolus

Hibiscus minutibracteolus, commonly known as the small-bract hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Hibiscus minutibracteolus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
