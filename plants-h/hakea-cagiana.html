Hakea cagiana

Hakea cagiana, commonly known as the Christmas Hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat.

Hakea cagiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
