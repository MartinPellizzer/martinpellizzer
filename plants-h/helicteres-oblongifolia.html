Helicteres oblongifolia

Helicteres oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf helicteres, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, oblong leaflets that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
