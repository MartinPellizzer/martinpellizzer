Heliotropium bacciferum

Heliotropium bacciferum, commonly known as the sweetleaf or redroot heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dark seeds.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
