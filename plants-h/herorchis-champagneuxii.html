Herorchis champagneuxii

Herorchis champagneuxii, commonly known as the Champagne orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that resemble champagne bubbles, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its small, green leaves are adapted to low-light conditions, and it produces a single, showy inflorescence with multiple blooms.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Herorchis champagneuxii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
