Hesperis dalmatica

Hesperis dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Hesperis dalmatica is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it contributes to biodiversity and supports pollinators.
