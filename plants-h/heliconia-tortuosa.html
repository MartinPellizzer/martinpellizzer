Heliconia tortuosa

Heliconia tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant features broad, waxy, strap-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a unique structure that resembles a spiral staircase.
