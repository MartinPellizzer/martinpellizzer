Hibbertia latifolia

Hibbertia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Hibbertia latifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural displays.
