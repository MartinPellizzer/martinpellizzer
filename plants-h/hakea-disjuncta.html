Hakea disjuncta

Hakea disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-branching stems and its distinctive, recurved leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom in spring. Hakea disjuncta is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is an important component of the local flora and provides habitat for various native wildlife.
