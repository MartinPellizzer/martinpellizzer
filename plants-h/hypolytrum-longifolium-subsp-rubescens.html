Hypolytrum longifolium subsp. rubescens

Hypolytrum longifolium subsp.

rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and leaves that often have a reddish tinge, especially when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
