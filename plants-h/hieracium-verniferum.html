Hieracium verniferum

Hieracium verniferum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
