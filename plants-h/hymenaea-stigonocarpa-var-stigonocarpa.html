Hymenaea stigonocarpa var. stigonocarpa

Hymenaea stigonocarpa var. stigonocarpa, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the dry season. The tree produces a hard, oily seed known as shea butter, which is widely used in cosmetics and food products. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

The plant is valued for its ecological and economic importance in its native regions.
