Haplopappus mucronatus

Haplopappus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrowhead pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

The species is notable for its unique pappus structure, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.
