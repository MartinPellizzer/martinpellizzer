Hibiscus variabilis

Hibiscus variabilis, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and even purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Hibiscus variabilis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
