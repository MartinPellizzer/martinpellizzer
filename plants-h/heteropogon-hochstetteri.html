Heteropogon hochstetteri

Heteropogon hochstetteri, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal and lowland areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
