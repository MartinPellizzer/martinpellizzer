Hieracium lanatum subvar. eriopsilon

Hieracium lanatum subvar. eriopsilon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
