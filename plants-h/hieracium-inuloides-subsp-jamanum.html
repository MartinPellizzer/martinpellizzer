Hieracium inuloides subsp. jamanum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

jamanum, commonly known as the Jamanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific morphological traits that set it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus.
