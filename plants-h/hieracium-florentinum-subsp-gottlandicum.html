Hieracium florentinum subsp. gottlandicum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

gottlandicum, commonly known as the Gotland hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Baltic region, including parts of Sweden and Estonia. It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small, clustered flowers in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
