Hebeandra cestrifolia

Hebeandra cestrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Hebeandra cestrifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
