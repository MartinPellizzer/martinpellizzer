Hibiscus magnificus

Hibiscus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring five deeply lobed petals in vibrant shades of red, pink, or yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Hibiscus magnificus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
