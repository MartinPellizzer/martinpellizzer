Heliconia spiralis var. spiralis

Heliconia spiralis var. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a helix, composed of numerous brightly colored bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The spiral heliconia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
