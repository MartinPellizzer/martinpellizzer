Habenaria pariquerensis

Habenaria pariquerensis, commonly known as the Pariquerensis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
