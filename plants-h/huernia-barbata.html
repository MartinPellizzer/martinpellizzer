Huernia barbata

Huernia barbata, commonly known as the barbed horned lithops, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky, leaf-like structures that resemble horns. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
