Haageocereus pacalaensis

Haageocereus pacalaensis, commonly known as the Pacalaensis Cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and botanical collections.
