Hakea haplantha

Hakea haplantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, red or yellow flower spikes. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry environments. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, growing in well-drained soils.

Hakea haplantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
