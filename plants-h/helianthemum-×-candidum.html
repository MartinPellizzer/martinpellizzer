Helianthemum × candidum

Helianthemum × candidum, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact form and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
