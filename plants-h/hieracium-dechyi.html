Hieracium dechyi

Hieracium dechyi, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium dechyi is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
