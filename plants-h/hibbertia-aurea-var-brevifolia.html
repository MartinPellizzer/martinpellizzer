Hibbertia aurea var. brevifolia

Hibbertia aurea var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, waxy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers full sun and well-drained soil.
