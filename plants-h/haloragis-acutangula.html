Haloragis acutangula

Haloragis acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved water-starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it the common name "sharp-leaved.".

It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
