Heliotropium circinatum

Heliotropium circinatum, commonly known as the spiral heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Heliotropium circinatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to spread easily.
