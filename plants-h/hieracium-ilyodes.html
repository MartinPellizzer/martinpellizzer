Hieracium ilyodes

Hieracium ilyodes, commonly known as the Ilyos mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of its stems. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Hieracium ilyodes typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate cold temperatures and is often found in high-altitude environments.
