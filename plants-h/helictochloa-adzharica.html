Helictochloa adzharica

Helictochloa adzharica, commonly known as the Caucasian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Helictochloa adzharica plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
