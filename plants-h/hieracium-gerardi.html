Hieracium gerardi

Hieracium gerardi, commonly known as Gerard's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, hairy texture and a serrated edge.

Gerard's hawkweed is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
