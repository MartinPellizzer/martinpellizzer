Hesperis auriculata

Hesperis auriculata, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces bright purple or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lobed and slightly hairy, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Hesperis auriculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
