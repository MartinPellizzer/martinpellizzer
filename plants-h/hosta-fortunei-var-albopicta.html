Hosta fortunei var. albopicta

Hosta fortunei var. albopicta, commonly known as the White-veined Hosta, is a popular ornamental plant native to East Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly dark green with striking white or light green veins. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in late summer, typically in shades of blue or white. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its attractive foliage and is often used in shaded garden beds or as a container plant.
