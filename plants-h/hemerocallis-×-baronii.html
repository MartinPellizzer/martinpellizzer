Hemerocallis × baronii

Hemerocallis × baronii, commonly known as the Baron's Daylily, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Hemerocallis genus, which includes various daylilies.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is a perennial herb with rhizomatous growth, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump.

Hemerocallis × baronii is prized for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
