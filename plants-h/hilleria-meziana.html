Hilleria meziana

Hilleria meziana, commonly known as the Mexican hilleria, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hilleria meziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
