Hyacinthus monstrosus

Hyacinthus monstrosus, commonly known as the giant hyacinth, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of violet, blue, or white. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it blooms in late winter to early spring. It is known for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of purity and elegance.
