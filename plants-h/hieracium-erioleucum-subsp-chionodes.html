Hieracium erioleucum subsp. chionodes

Hieracium erioleucum subsp.

chionodes, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its woolly, silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems that bear clustered flower heads.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
