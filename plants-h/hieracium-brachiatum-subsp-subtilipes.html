Hieracium brachiatum subsp. subtilipes

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

subtilipes, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, thriving in sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces seeds through achenes, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.
