Hieracium pedemontanum

Hieracium pedemontanum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium pedemontanum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
