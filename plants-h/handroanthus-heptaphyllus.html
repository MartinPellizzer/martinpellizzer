Handroanthus heptaphyllus

Handroanthus heptaphyllus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.
