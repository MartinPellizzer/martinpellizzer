Huperzia toppingii

Huperzia toppingii, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a species of clubmoss native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Huperzia toppingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
