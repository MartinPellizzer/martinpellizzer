Hyoscyamus niger var. biennis

Hyoscyamus niger var. biennis, commonly known as the common henbane, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five lobes and a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
