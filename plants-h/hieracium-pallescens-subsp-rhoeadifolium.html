Hieracium pallescens subsp. rhoeadifolium

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

rhoeadifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed sites, showcasing a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem with yellow composite flowers.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climatic conditions.

The species is often found in regions with temperate to subtropical climates.
