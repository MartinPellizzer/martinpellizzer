Hemisandra aurantiaca

Hemisandra aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-stemmed hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-colored stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hemisandra aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
