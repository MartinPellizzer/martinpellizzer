Hieracium pullulum

Hieracium pullulum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium pullulum is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in parts of Europe and Asia.
