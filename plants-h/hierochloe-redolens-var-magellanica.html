Hierochloe redolens var. magellanica

Hierochloe redolens var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
