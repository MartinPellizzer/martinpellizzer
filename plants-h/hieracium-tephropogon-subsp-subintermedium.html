Hieracium tephropogon subsp. subintermedium

Hieracium tephropogon subsp.

subintermedium, commonly known as the intermediate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
