Hippeastrum fosteri

Hippeastrum fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tint.

It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.
