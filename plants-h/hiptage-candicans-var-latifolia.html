Hiptage candicans var. latifolia

Hiptage candicans var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hiptage, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
