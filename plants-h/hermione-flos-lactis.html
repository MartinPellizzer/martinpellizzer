Hermione flos-lactis

Hermione flos-lactis, commonly known as the milkflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features showy, milk-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are erect and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The name "flos-lactis" refers to the milky sap that exudes from its stems when broken.
