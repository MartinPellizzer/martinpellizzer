Holosteum glutinosum subsp. liniflorum

Holosteum glutinosum subsp.

liniflorum, commonly known as sticky holosteum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a sticky, glandular substance, which is a distinctive botanical trait.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and also bear a similar sticky coating, contributing to its unique appearance. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or reddish in color.

This subspecies is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
