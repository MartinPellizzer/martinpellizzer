Hypochaeris pyrenaica

Hypochaeris pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by oval, spiny seeds.

Hypochaeris pyrenaica is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to cooler climates.
