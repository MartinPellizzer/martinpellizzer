Halesia tetraptera var. glabrescens

Halesia tetraptera var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rich, woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
