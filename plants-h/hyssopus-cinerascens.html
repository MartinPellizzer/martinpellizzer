Hyssopus cinerascens

Hyssopus cinerascens, commonly known as blue hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-blue in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Hyssopus cinerascens is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
