Hieracium urticaceum subsp. garbellianum

Hieracium urticaceum subsp.

garbellianum, commonly known as the Sicilian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has small, serrated leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive tendencies in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
