Hieracium coerulaceum grex toutonianum

Hieracium coerulaceum grex toutonianum, commonly known as the Blue Hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and ease of growth.
