Hemerocallis nana

Hemerocallis nana, commonly known as the dwarf daylily, is a compact perennial plant native to Asia.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, low-growing mound. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hemerocallis nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
