Hibiscus bracteatus

Hibiscus bracteatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Hibiscus bracteatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
