Holographis caput-medusae

Holographis caput-medusae, commonly known as the Medusa's Head, is a unique plant characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence.

It features clusters of small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical shape that resembles the head of the mythological Medusa. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments. Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots and a short, woody stem that supports the heavy flower heads.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is notable for its unusual appearance and resilience.
