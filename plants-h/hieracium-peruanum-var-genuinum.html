Hieracium peruanum var. genuinum

Hieracium peruanum var. genuinum, commonly known as the Peruvian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are yellowish with ray florets.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
