Horsfieldia talaudensis

Horsfieldia talaudensis, commonly known as the Talaud orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Talaud Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates.

Horsfieldia talaudensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
