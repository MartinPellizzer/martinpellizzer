Hemizonia virgata

Hemizonia virgata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces showy, purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hemizonia virgata is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and streambanks.
