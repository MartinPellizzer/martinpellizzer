Hieracium juranum subsp. urezzae

Hieracium juranum subsp.

urezzae, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of pale green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, flat-topped head with numerous small florets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
