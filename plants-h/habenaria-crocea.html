Habenaria crocea

Habenaria crocea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, grass-like foliage and its striking clusters of pink or white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
