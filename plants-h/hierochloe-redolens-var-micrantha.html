Hierochloe redolens var. micrantha

Hierochloe redolens var. micrantha, commonly known as the sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and fine, silky leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and ceremonies.
