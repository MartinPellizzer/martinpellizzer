Hippeastrum tenuiflorum

Hippeastrum tenuiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered amaryllis, is a species native to South America.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its strap-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often in late spring or early summer. The plant produces a single, tall stem that bears several flowers, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
