Heracleum pubescens var. glabrum

Heracleum pubescens var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth cow parsnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its large, umbrella-like compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its hollow, ridged stems are often covered in fine hairs, though the variety glabrum is smooth.

The plant is known for its strong odor and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
