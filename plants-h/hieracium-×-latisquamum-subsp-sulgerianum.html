Hieracium × latisquamum subsp. sulgerianum

Hieracium × latisquamum subsp.

sulgerianum, commonly known as the Sulgerian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and distribution patterns.
