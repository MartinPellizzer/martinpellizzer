Hedysarum marandense

Hedysarum marandense, commonly known as the Marandense milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind.
