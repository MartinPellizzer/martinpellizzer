Hieracium nigrescens subsp. obscurans

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

obscurans, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
