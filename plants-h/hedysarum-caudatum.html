Hedysarum caudatum

Hedysarum caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Hedysarum caudatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
