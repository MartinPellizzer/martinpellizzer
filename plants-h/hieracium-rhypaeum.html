Hieracium rhypaeum

Hieracium rhypaeum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its clustered, yellowish-orange flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
