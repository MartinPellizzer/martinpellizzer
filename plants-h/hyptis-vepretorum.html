Hyptis vepretorum

Hyptis vepretorum, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
