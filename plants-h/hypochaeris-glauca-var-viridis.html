Hypochaeris glauca var. viridis

Hypochaeris glauca var. viridis, commonly known as green mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive blue-green hue, while its flowers are radially symmetrical with four petals.

It is an annual or biennial plant that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
