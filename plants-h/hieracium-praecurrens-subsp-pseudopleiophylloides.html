Hieracium praecurrens subsp. pseudopleiophylloides

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

pseudopleiophylloides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a flowering period from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to other Hieracium species.
