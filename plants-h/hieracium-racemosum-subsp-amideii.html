Hieracium racemosum subsp. amideii

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

amideii, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears numerous yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are arranged in a raceme, giving it its species name.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
