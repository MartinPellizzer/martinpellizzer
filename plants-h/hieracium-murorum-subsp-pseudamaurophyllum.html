Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudamaurophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudamaurophyllum, commonly known as the false purple meadow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and declining species in many areas.
