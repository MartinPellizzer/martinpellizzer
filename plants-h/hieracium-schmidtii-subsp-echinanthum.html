Hieracium schmidtii subsp. echinanthum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

echinanthum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets, typical of the family. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
