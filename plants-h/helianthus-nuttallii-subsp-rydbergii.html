Helianthus nuttallii subsp. rydbergii

Helianthus nuttallii subsp.

rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States. It typically grows in open prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun and well-drained soil.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central disk, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
