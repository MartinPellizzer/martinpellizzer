Hosackia americana subsp. pilosa

Hosackia americana subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies pilosa distinguishes itself by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
