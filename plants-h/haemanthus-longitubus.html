Haemanthus longitubus

Haemanthus longitubus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant's foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Haemanthus longitubus is also known for its ability to produce offsets, allowing for easy propagation.
