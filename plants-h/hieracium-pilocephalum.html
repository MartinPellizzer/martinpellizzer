Hieracium pilocephalum

Hieracium pilocephalum, commonly known as the hairy-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

Hieracium pilocephalum is often considered a weed but can be found in various habitats across its native range.
