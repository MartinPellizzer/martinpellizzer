Helicteres angustifolia var. glaucoides

Helicteres angustifolia var. glaucoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf helicteres, is a variant of the helicteres angustifolia species.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a unique, prickly texture.

This variety is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical environments.
