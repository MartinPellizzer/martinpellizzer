Hopea helferi

Hopea helferi, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Hopea helferi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
