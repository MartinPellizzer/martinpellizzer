Hieracium flagellare var. moscoviticum

Hieracium flagellare var. moscoviticum, commonly known as the Moscovy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
