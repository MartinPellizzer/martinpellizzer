Hieracium prenanthoides var. orthophylloides

Hieracium prenanthoides var. orthophylloides, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, displaying a rosette of basal leaves with a woolly texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its fruit.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
