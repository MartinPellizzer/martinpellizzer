Hippeastrum pantanalense

Hippeastrum pantanalense, commonly known as the Pantanal amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to the Pantanal wetlands of Brazil.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a trumpet-shaped corona. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment.

Hippeastrum pantanalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
