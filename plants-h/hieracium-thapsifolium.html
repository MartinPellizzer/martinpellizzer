Hieracium thapsifolium

Hieracium thapsifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and its tall, branched stems that bear numerous yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

Hieracium thapsifolium is also known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
