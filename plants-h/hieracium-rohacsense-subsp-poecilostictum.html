Hieracium rohacsense subsp. poecilostictum

Hieracium rohacsense subsp.

poecilostictum, commonly known as the rohacense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces numerous small, yellow disk flowers in the summer.

This subspecies is notable for its distinct leaf patterns and is often found in specific habitats across its native range.
