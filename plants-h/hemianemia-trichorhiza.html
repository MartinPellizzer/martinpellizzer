Hemianemia trichorhiza

Hemianemia trichorhiza, commonly known as the three-horned fern, is a rare and specialized plant species characterized by its unique foliage structure.

It features fronds with three distinct, elongated lobes that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the fronds, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hemianemia trichorhiza is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
