Hieracium rotgesianum

Hieracium rotgesianum, commonly known as the Rotgesian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
