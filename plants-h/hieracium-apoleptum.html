Hieracium apoleptum

Hieracium apoleptum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium apoleptum is also known for its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in drier environments.
