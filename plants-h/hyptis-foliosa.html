Hyptis foliosa

Hyptis foliosa, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are used to make tea and have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.
