Hieracium echioides subsp. proceriforme

Hieracium echioides subsp.

proceriforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the stems are upright and somewhat woody.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
