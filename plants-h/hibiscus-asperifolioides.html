Hibiscus asperifolioides

Hibiscus asperifolioides, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hibiscus asperifolioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
