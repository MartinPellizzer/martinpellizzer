Haemanthus dubius

Haemanthus dubius, commonly known as the Natal bell-shaped lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in coastal forests and has strap-like, evergreen leaves. It produces showy inflorescences that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Haemanthus dubius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
