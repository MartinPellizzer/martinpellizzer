Hypericum yamamotoi var. riparium

Hypericum yamamotoi var. riparium, commonly known as the river St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with upright stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
