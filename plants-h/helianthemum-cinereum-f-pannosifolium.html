Helianthemum cinereum f. pannosifolium

Helianthemum cinereum f. pannosifolium, commonly known as the silvery sand verbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. The leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
