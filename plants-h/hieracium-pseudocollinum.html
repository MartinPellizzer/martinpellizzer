Hieracium pseudocollinum

Hieracium pseudocollinum, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
