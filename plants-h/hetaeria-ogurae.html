Hetaeria ogurae

Hetaeria ogurae, commonly known as the Japanese ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique appearance that resembles a ghostly figure. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on the trunks of trees or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Hetaeria ogurae is considered a conservation concern.
