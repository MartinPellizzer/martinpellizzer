Hesperis purpurascens

Hesperis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flowering onion, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and often branched.

Hesperis purpurascens is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
