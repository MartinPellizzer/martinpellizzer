Hieracium erioleucum subsp. eriovillosum

Hieracium erioleucum subsp.

eriovillosum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
