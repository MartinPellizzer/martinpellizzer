Hieracium pilosella subsp. tricholepioides

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

tricholepioides, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
