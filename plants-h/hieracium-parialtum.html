Hieracium parialtum

Hieracium parialtum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium parialtum is known for its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
