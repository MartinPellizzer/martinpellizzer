Hieracium cochleare subsp. pseudalpinum

Hieracium cochleare subsp.

pseudalpinum, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and rocky slopes. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is often found in open, sunny environments at high altitudes.
