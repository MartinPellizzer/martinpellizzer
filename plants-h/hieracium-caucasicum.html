Hieracium caucasicum

Hieracium caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium caucasicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
