Hypericum auriculatum

Hypericum auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
