Hemigenia humilis

Hemigenia humilis, commonly known as the low hemigenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.
