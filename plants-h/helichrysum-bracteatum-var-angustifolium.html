Helichrysum bracteatum var. angustifolium

Helichrysum bracteatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery-blue hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
