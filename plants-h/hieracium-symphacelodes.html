Hieracium symphacelodes

Hieracium symphacelodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
