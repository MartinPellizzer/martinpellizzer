Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. oxylepium

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

oxylepium, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and the presence of glandular hairs.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
