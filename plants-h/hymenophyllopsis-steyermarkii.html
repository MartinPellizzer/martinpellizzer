Hymenophyllopsis steyermarkii

Hymenophyllopsis steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's filmy fern, is a delicate, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae, characterized by its thin, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of sori. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are usually small, measuring only a few centimeters in length, and are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
