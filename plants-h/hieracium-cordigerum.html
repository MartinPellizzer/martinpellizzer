Hieracium cordigerum

Hieracium cordigerum, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium cordigerum is often considered a weed but can be found in various ecological settings.
