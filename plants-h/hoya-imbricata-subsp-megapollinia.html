Hoya imbricata subsp. megapollinia

Hoya imbricata subsp.

megapollinia, commonly known as the giant pollinia hoya, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Hoya imbricata subsp.

megapollinia is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
