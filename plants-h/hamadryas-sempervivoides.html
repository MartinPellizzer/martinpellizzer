Hamadryas sempervivoides

Hamadryas sempervivoides, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

Hamadryas sempervivoides is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure.
