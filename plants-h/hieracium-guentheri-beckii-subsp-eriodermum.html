Hieracium guentheri-beckii subsp. eriodermum

Hieracium guentheri-beckii subsp.

eriodermum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
