Hedeoma hyssopifolia var. chihuahuensis

Hedeoma hyssopifolia var. chihuahuensis, commonly known as Chihuahua hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
