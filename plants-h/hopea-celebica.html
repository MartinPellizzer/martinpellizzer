Hopea celebica

Hopea celebica, commonly known as the Celebes hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Hopea celebica is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
