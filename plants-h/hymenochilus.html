Hymenochilus

Hymenochilus, commonly known as the orchid genus, is a group of flowering plants belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often showy flowers with specialized structures such as labellum and column. Hymenochilus species typically have pseudobulbs and are native to tropical and subtropical regions. They thrive in humid environments and require well-draining soil for optimal growth.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
