Hieracium mohrungenense

Hieracium mohrungenense, commonly known as Mohrungen hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
