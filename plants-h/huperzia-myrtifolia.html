Huperzia myrtifolia

Huperzia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle fern, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are typically found on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia myrtifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
