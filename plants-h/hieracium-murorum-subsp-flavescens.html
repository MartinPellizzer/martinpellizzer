Hieracium murorum subsp. flavescens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adaptive to various soil types.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
