Hemigraphis okamotoi

Hemigraphis okamotoi, commonly known as the velvetleaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves that range in color from deep purple to bronze. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage.
