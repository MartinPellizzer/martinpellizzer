Hatiora salicornoides var. gracilis

Hatiora salicornoides var. gracilis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid species within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its segmented, often branched stems that bear bright, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is native to Brazil and is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, and it produces vibrant blooms during the holiday season.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its long blooming period and ease of care.
