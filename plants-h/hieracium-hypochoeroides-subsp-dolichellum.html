Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. dolichellum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

dolichellum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated flowering stems and is often found in temperate climates.
