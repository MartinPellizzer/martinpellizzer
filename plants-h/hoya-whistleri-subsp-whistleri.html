Hoya whistleri subsp. whistleri

Hoya whistleri subsp.

whistleri, commonly known as the Whistler's hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight. It is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements. Hoya whistleri subsp.

whistleri is also noted for its ability to produce a sweet, fragrant scent during flowering.
