Hieracium leucorrhizum

Hieracium leucorrhizum, commonly known as white-root hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The roots are white and fibrous, which contribute to its common name.
