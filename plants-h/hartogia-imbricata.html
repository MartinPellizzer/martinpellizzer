Hartogia imbricata

Hartogia imbricata, commonly known as the Hartog's rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Hartogia imbricata is often found in coastal and inland regions, preferring well-drained soils.
