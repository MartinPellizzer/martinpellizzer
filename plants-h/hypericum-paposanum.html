Hypericum paposanum

Hypericum paposanum, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
