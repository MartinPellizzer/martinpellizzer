Homalomena wallisii

Homalomena wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis Island arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-bearing inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Homalomena wallisii is also valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
