Helianthus tuberosus var. typicus

Helianthus tuberosus var. typicus, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its edible tubers, which are rich in inulin and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers similar to those of sunflowers, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy crop in temperate climates.

The Jerusalem artichoke is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use by indigenous peoples.
