Hieracium fuckelianum subsp. subfallaciforme

Hieracium fuckelianum subsp.

subfallaciforme, commonly known as the fallacious hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and yellow ray florets that give it a dandelion-like appearance.

This subspecies is native to Central Europe and thrives in dry, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

The plant is herbaceous, with a short flowering period in late spring to early summer.
