Hosackia juncea

Hosackia juncea, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces blue to purple flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and along stream banks.

Hosackia juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
