Hypericum umbellatum

Hypericum umbellatum, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants with yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small and clustered in umbels.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in herbal medicine.
