Hyparrhenia bequaertii

Hyparrhenia bequaertii, commonly known as the African grass or bequaert's hyparrhenia, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and savannas.

Hyparrhenia bequaertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
