Helminthia comosa var. spinulosa

Helminthia comosa var. spinulosa, commonly known as the hairy helminthia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and dry landscapes.
