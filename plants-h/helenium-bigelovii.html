Helenium bigelovii

Helenium bigelovii, commonly known as the yellow sneaker vine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows along rocky slopes and canyon walls, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seeds in small, fluffy clusters.

Helenium bigelovii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
