Hymenocallis crassifolia

Hymenocallis crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow stamens that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hymenocallis crassifolia is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants known for their vibrant blooms.
