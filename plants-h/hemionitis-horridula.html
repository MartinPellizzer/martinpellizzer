Hemionitis horridula

Hemionitis horridula, commonly known as the rough hemionitis, is a small, perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its unique, hairy leaves that give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hemionitis horridula is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
