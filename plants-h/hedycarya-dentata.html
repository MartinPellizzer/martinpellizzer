Hedycarya dentata

Hedycarya dentata, commonly known as the toothed hedycarya, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
