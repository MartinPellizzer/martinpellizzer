Hieracium sabinum var. laggeri

Hieracium sabinum var. laggeri, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats or cushions. The plant has yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
