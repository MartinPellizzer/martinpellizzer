Hymenocardia mollis var. genuina

Hymenocardia mollis var. genuina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments within forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
