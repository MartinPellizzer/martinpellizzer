Heterotheca villosa var. depressa

Heterotheca villosa var. depressa, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features basal rosettes of hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are ray-like and bloom in clusters, making it visually distinctive.

It is often found in desert grasslands and is considered a member of the Asteraceae family.
