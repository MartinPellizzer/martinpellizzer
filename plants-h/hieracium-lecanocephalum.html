Hieracium lecanocephalum

Hieracium lecanocephalum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with toothed margins. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium lecanocephalum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
