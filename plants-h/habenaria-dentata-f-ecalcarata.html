Habenaria dentata f. ecalcarata

Habenaria dentata f. ecalcarata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow lips. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring rich, organic soil. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
