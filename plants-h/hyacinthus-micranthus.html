Hyacinthus micranthus

Hyacinthus micranthus, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of flowers that bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to return year after year in suitable climates.

Hyacinthus micranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
