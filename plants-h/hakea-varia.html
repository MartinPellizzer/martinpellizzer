Hakea varia

Hakea varia, commonly known as the variable hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, bottlebrush-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often blooming in spring. Hakea varia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandplain and gravelly soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species within the Proteaceae family.
