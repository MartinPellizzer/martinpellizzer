Hieracium mediopositum

Hieracium mediopositum, commonly known as the middle-positioned hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various environments.
