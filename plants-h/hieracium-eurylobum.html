Hieracium eurylobum

Hieracium eurylobum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Hieracium eurylobum is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
