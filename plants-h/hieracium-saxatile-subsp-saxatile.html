Hieracium saxatile subsp. saxatile

Hieracium saxatile subsp.

saxatile, commonly known as the saxatile hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage.

The plant typically grows in crevices and on rocky slopes, adapting to nutrient-poor soils and harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often found in mountainous regions across the Alps and Carpathians.
