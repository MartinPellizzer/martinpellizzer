Hohenbergia bracteata

Hohenbergia bracteata, commonly known as the silver rosette, is a species of flowering plant in the bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage that forms a dense, silver-blue appearance. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast beautifully with its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hohenbergia bracteata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
