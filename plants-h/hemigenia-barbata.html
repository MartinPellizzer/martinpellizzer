Hemigenia barbata

Hemigenia barbata, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in spring.

Hemigenia barbata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
