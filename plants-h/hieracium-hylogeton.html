Hieracium hylogeton

Hieracium hylogeton, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
