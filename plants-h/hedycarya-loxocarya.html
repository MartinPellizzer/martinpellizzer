Hedycarya loxocarya

Hedycarya loxocarya, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing single seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the wood is highly valued for its fragrance and durability.
