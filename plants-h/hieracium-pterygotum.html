Hieracium pterygotum

Hieracium pterygotum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium pterygotum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in various regions.
