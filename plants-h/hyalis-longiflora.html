Hyalis longiflora

Hyalis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered hyalis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hyalis longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
