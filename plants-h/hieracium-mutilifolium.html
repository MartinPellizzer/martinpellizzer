Hieracium mutilifolium

Hieracium mutilifolium, commonly known as the broken-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a tall, flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically deeply lobed and may have a woolly texture, while the flowers are showy and attract various pollinators.

Hieracium mutilifolium is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
