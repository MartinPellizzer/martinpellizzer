Hydnophytum cordifolium

Hydnophytum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved burr.

It is a climbing hemiparasitic plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. The plant has a distinctive heart-shaped leaf and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It relies on other plants for some of its nutrients, making it a hemiparasite.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
