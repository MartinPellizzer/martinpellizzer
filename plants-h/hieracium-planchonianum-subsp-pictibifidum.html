Hieracium planchonianum subsp. pictibifidum

Hieracium planchonianum subsp.

pictibifidum, commonly known as the painted leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often brightly colored leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowering heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish-brown ray florets.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its visual appeal in gardens.
