Hieracium tommasinii

Hieracium tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
