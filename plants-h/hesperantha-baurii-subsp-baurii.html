Hesperantha baurii subsp. baurii

Hesperantha baurii subsp.

baurii, commonly known as the Baur's lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa. It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive, elongated spur at the back of each bloom.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late winter to early spring.
