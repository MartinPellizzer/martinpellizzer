Hymenodictyon parvifolium var. scabrum

Hymenodictyon parvifolium var. scabrum, commonly known as the rough-leaved hymenodictyon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in local medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive leaf venation and small, inconspicuous fruits.
