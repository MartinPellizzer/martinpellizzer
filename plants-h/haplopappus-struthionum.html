Haplopappus struthionum

Haplopappus struthionum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, and the plant produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Haplopappus struthionum is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
