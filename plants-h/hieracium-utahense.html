Hieracium utahense

Hieracium utahense, commonly known as Utah hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Utah hawkweed is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
