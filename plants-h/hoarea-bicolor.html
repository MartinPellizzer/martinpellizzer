Hoarea bicolor

Hoarea bicolor, commonly known as the white marguerite or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white flowers that resemble daisies, with a central yellow disk. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as a ornamental plant for its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Hoarea bicolor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
