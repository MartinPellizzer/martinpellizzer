Heliotropium procumbens

Heliotropium procumbens, commonly known as creeping heliotrope, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or pink, blooming in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Heliotropium procumbens is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
