Hesperis scapigera

Hesperis scapigera, commonly known as the scented everlasting or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often hairy as well.

Hesperis scapigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
