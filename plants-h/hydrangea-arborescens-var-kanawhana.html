Hydrangea arborescens var. kanawhana

Hydrangea arborescens var. kanawhana, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, rounded clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, dark green foliage that turns red or orange in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
