Hopea hasskarliana

Hopea hasskarliana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing one or two seeds.

Hopea hasskarliana is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.
