Hieracium murorum subsp. tephroneuron

Hieracium murorum subsp.

tephroneuron, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more narrow. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a head.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive foliage, making it a recognizable member of the daisy family.
