Hieracium vestipes

Hieracium vestipes, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems, which are adaptations for survival in various environmental conditions. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom from June to September.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas where it can spread efficiently through its rhizomes.
