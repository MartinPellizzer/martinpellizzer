Hydrangea involucrata f. multiplex

Hydrangea involucrata f. multiplex, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is upright, and it thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
