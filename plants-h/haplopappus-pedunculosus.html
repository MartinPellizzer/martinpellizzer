Haplopappus pedunculosus

Haplopappus pedunculosus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by feathery pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal of seeds.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is a component of desert ecosystems.
