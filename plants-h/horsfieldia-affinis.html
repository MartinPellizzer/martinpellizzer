Horsfieldia affinis

Horsfieldia affinis, commonly known as the Horsfield's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, adapted for efficient water retention.

Horsfieldia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
