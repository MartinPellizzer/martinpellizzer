Hypodematium crenatum subsp. fauriei

Hypodematium crenatum subsp.

fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's hairy shield fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the leaf axils, a feature common among many ferns, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.
