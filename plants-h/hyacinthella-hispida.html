Hyacinthella hispida

Hyacinthella hispida, commonly known as the hairy hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Hyacinthella hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
