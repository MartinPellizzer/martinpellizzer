Hieracium auricula subsp. gerardi

Hieracium auricula subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as the Gerardi's meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other Hieracium species.
