Hieracium sagittatum subsp. subumbricola

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

subumbricola, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow, daisy-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a notable species in shaded botanical habitats.
