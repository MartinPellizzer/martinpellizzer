Hieracium paucinaevum

Hieracium paucinaevum, commonly known as the few-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium paucinaevum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many habitats.
