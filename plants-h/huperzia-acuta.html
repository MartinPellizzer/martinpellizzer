Huperzia acuta

Huperzia acuta, commonly known as the acute huperzia, is a species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a cryptogamic plant that lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having structures called rhizoids and leaf-like structures called phyllodes. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle involves both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

Huperzia acuta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
