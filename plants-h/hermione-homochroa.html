Hermione homochroa

Hermione homochroa, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in silvery-gray hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Hermione homochroa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
