Hydrangea stenophylla var. decorticata

Hydrangea stenophylla var. decorticata, commonly known as the paper hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into papery, white bracts. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
