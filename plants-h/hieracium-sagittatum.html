Hieracium sagittatum

Hieracium sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium sagittatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
