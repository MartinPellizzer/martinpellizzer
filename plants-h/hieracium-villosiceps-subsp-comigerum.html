Hieracium villosiceps subsp. comigerum

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

comigerum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this perennial plant thrives in meadows and grasslands. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a flowering stem with several flower heads.

The subspecies comigerum is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth compared to other forms of Hieracium villosiceps.
