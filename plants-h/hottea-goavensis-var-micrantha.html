Hottea goavensis var. micrantha

Hottea goavensis var. micrantha, commonly known as the Madagascar starflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its star-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and overcollection.
