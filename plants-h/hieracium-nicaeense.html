Hieracium nicaeense

Hieracium nicaeense, commonly known as the Nicaean hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are basal and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
