Hakea decurrens subsp. physocarpa

Hakea decurrens subsp.

physocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red.

The plant produces woody, cylindrical fruits that enclose the seeds, a feature common in the Hakea genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
