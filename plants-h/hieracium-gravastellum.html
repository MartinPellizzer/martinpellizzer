Hieracium gravastellum

Hieracium gravastellum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium gravastellum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and widespread species.
