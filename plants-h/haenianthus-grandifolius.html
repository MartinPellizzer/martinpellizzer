Haenianthus grandifolius

Haenianthus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the base of the plant.

It is typically found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salty and windy conditions.
