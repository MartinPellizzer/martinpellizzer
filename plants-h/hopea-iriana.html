Hopea iriana

Hopea iriana, commonly known as the Irianese hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in spikes, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hopea iriana is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
