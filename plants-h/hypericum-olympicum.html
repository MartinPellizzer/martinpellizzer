Hypericum olympicum

Hypericum olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic Hypericum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is valued both aesthetically and medicinally.
