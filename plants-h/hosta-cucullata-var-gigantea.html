Hosta cucullata var. gigantea

Hosta cucullata var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant hooded hosta, is a striking perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, broad, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a silvery-white margin, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant produces showy, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, sturdy stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after addition to shade gardens.
