Hieracium baumgartenianum

Hieracium baumgartenianum, commonly known as the Baumann's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually basal with a rough texture.

Hieracium baumgartenianum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
