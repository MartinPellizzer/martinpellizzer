Helianthemum variabile var. asperum

Helianthemum variabile var. asperum, commonly known as the rough stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, rough texture that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to rocky landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
