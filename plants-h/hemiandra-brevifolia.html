Hemiandra brevifolia

Hemiandra brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hemiandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal or subcoastal regions.

Hemiandra brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact form and fragrant foliage.
