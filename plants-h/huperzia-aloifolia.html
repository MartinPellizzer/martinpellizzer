Huperzia aloifolia

Huperzia aloifolia, commonly known as the rock huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to regions in East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often on cliffs or among stones. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Huperzia aloifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
