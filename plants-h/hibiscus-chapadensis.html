Hibiscus chapadensis

Hibiscus chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

Hibiscus chapadensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
