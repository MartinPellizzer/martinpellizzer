Hypodiscus alboaristatus

Hypodiscus alboaristatus, commonly known as the white-flowered spider orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are predominantly white with intricate patterns, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits.

Hypodiscus alboaristatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
