Hypolaena esenbeckii

Hypolaena esenbeckii, commonly known as the golden star grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are often clustered near the base of the plant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sheathing base.

Hypolaena esenbeckii is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy slopes.
