Helianthus polycephalus

Helianthus polycephalus, commonly known as the stiffhead sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. It is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The stiffhead sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
