Helichrysum asperum var. asperum

Helichrysum asperum var. asperum, commonly known as rough helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, rough-textured foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
