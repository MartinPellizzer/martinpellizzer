Hedyotis glomerata

Hedyotis glomerata, commonly known as the clustered dotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it relies on insect pollination.

Hedyotis glomerata is often found in low-lying areas and is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
