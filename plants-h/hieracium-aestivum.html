Hieracium aestivum

Hieracium aestivum, commonly known as the summer hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium aestivum is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
