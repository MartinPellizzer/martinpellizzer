Hieracium pubericeps

Hieracium pubericeps, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It is considered a perennial herb and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
