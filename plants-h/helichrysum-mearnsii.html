Helichrysum mearnsii

Helichrysum mearnsii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-orange inflorescences that resemble small, clustered flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, hairy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting appearance.
