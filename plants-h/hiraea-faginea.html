Hiraea faginea

Hiraea faginea, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a snow-like appearance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hiraea faginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
