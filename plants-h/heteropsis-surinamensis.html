Heteropsis surinamensis

Heteropsis surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam cypress, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that contain seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
