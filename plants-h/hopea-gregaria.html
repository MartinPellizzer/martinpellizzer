Hopea gregaria

Hopea gregaria, commonly known as the clustered hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellowish-green inflorescences.

Hopea gregaria plays a significant role in tropical rainforest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
