Haplophyllum tuberculatum subsp. vermiculare

Haplophyllum tuberculatum subsp.

vermiculare, commonly known as the woolly rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
