Hedychium maximum

Hedychium maximum, commonly known as the giant ginger or white flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant has strap-like, glossy green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Hedychium maximum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
