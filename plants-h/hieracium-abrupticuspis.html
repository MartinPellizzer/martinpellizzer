Hieracium abrupticuspis

Hieracium abrupticuspis, commonly known as the sharp-cusped hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a creeping growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium abrupticuspis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
