Hyacinthus orientalis var. provincialis

Hyacinthus orientalis var. provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white during late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and strong, sweet fragrance.
