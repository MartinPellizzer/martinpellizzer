Hyptis viatica

Hyptis viatica, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis viatica is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
