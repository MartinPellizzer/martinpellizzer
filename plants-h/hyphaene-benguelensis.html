Hyphaene benguelensis

Hyphaene benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela palm, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southwestern Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its fruits are fibrous and contain a single seed, which is dispersed by water and animals.

The Benguela palm is well adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in coastal environments.
