Hypericum quadrangulum var. epunctatum

Hypericum quadrangulum var. epunctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture and a distinct reddish or purplish hue. This variety is characterized by its unique spotted or dotted appearance on the stems and leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
