Hieracium irriguum subsp. spodoleucum

Hieracium irriguum subsp.

spodoleucum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are typically white or pale green, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Compositae family. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
