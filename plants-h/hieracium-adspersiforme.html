Hieracium adspersiforme

Hieracium adspersiforme, commonly known as the spangled hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often spreading through rhizomes and producing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
