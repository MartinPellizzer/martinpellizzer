Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. crassifolium

Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, tubular flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
