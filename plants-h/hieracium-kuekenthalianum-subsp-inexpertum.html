Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp. inexpertum

Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp.

inexpertum, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, often with hairy surfaces, and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per head, surrounded by phyllaries, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is closely related to other members of the genus Hieracium, which are commonly found in temperate regions.
