Hedyotis caerulea var. minima

Hedyotis caerulea var. minima, commonly known as the lesser blue false indigo, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
