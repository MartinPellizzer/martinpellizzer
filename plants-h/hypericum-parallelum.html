Hypericum parallelum

Hypericum parallelum, commonly known as the parallel-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bright yellow flowers with four petals. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, from July to September, and it is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Hypericum parallelum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
