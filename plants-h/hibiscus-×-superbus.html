Hibiscus × superbus

Hibiscus × superbus, commonly known as the giant Chinese hibiscus, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped and dark green, while its flowers are typically pink or red with a yellow center.

Hibiscus × superbus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
