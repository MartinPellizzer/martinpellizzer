Hemionitis kitchingii

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Hemionitis kitchingii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
