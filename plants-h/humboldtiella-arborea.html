Humboldtiella arborea

Humboldtiella arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a distinctive, serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by winged fruits that aid in its dispersal. Humboldtiella arborea is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
