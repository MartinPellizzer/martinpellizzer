Hydrangea involucrata var. involucrata

Hydrangea involucrata var. involucrata, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of ovate, serrated leaves that turn red in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide seasonal interest in woodland gardens.
