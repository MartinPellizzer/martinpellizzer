Hoodia currorii subsp. currorii

Hoodia currorii subsp.

currorii, commonly known as the desert apple or Hoodia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration. Hoodia currorii subsp.

currorii is also notable for containing the compound P57, which has been studied for its potential appetite-suppressing properties.
