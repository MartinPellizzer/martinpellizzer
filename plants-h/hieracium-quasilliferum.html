Hieracium quasilliferum

Hieracium quasilliferum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium quasilliferum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
