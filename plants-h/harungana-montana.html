Harungana montana

Harungana montana, commonly known as the African wild olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. Harungana montana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
