Hieracium murorum subsp. festivum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

festivum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
