Hieracium tarradasum var. perthusianum

Hieracium tarradasum var. perthusianum, commonly known as the Perthusian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native habitat.
