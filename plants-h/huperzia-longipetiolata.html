Huperzia longipetiolata

Huperzia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often borne on long petioles. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, rounded clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia longipetiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
