Hippeastrum brasilianum

Hippeastrum brasilianum, commonly known as the Brazilian amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can be white, pink, or red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum brasilianum is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
