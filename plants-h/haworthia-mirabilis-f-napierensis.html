Haworthia mirabilis f. napierensis

Haworthia mirabilis f. napierensis, commonly known as the Napier's Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina. The leaves are often tinged with red or purple at the tips, especially when exposed to sunlight. It produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on tall stems during the summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
