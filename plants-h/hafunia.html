Hafunia

Hafunia, commonly known as the "tree of life," is a unique plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a small, deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, gnarled trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries containing a single seed. Hafunia is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species for both ecological and cultural reasons in its native region.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for study in botany and conservation science.
