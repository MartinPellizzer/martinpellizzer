Hybanthus riparius

Hybanthus riparius, commonly known as the river hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has slender, ascending stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams and in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
