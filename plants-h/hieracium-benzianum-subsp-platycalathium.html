Hieracium benzianum subsp. platycalathium

Hieracium benzianum subsp.

platycalathium, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by phyllaries.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, often forming dense colonies.
