Hieracium dmitrovense

Hieracium dmitrovense, commonly known as the Dmitrovense hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant is typically found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
