Hydrangea arborescens f. grandiflora

Hydrangea arborescens f. grandiflora, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green and remain attractive throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a tendency to spread through rhizomes, which can lead to naturalized growth in suitable conditions.
