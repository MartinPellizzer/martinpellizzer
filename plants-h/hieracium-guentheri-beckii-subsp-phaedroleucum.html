Hieracium guentheri-beckii subsp. phaedroleucum

Hieracium guentheri-beckii subsp.

phaedroleucum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
