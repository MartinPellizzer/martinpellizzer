Hieracium murorum subsp. asterophorum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

asterophorum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its leaves are hairy, lance-shaped, and often have a basal rosette, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
