Hebe cryptomorpha

Hebe cryptomorpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe cryptomorpha is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
