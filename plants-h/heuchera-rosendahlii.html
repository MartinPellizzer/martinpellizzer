Heuchera rosendahlii

Heuchera rosendahlii, commonly known as Rosendahl's alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves with striking red or purple veins. The plant produces upright clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and delicate flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
