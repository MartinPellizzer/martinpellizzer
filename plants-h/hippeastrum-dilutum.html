Hippeastrum dilutum

Hippeastrum dilutum, commonly known as the Brazilian red lily, is a species of amaryllid that originates from Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum dilutum is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
