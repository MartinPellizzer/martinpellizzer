Hieracium praecox subsp. horripilum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

horripilum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
