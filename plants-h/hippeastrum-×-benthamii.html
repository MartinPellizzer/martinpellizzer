Hippeastrum × benthamii

Hippeastrum × benthamii, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a species of amaryllid that originates from South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring, often around Easter, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces multiple flower stems with clustered, trumpet-shaped blooms. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and relatively easy care requirements.

Hippeastrum × benthamii is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
