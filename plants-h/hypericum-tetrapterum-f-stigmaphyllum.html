Hypericum tetrapterum f. stigmaphyllum

Hypericum tetrapterum f. stigmaphyllum, commonly known as the stigmaphyllum form of the four-winged St. John's wort, is a variant of the species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-winged leaves, which are a key botanical feature. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with orange stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
