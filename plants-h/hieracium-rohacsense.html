Hieracium rohacsense

Hieracium rohacsense, commonly known as the Roháček's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium rohacsense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
