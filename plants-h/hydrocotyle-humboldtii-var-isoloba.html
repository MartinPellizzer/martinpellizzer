Hydrocotyle humboldtii var. isoloba

Hydrocotyle humboldtii var. isoloba, commonly known as the Humboldt spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, aiding in its survival in moist habitats.

It is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
