Hystrix patula var. bigeloviana

Hystrix patula var. bigeloviana, commonly known as the Bigelow's spiny scale, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage that serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant's leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native desert environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
