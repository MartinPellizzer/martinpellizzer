Hydrangea nivea

Hydrangea nivea, commonly known as the snow hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range from white to pale blue or pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, sterile flowers surrounded by larger, modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hydrangea nivea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
