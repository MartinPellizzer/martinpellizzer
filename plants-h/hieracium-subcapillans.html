Hieracium subcapillans

Hieracium subcapillans, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium subcapillans is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
