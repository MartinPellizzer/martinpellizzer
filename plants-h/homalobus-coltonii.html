Homalobus coltonii

Homalobus coltonii, commonly known as the Colton's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in humid environments such as rainforests and near riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
