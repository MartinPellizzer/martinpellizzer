Haplopappus carthamoides subsp. maximus

Haplopappus carthamoides subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, fluffy seed head.

The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

The species is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
