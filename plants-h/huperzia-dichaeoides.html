Huperzia dichaeoides

Huperzia dichaeoides, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Huperzia dichaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
