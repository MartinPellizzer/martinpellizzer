Hyptis arbuscula

Hyptis arbuscula, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis arbuscula is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
