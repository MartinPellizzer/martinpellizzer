Helichrysum congestum

Helichrysum congestum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble small sunbursts, which remain intact even when dried. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Helichrysum congestum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant color.
