Hieracium legionense

Hieracium legionense, commonly known as the legion dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are followed by spherical seed heads.

The leaves are usually alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive lobed or serrated edge.
