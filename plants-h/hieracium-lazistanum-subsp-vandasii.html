Hieracium lazistanum subsp. vandasii

Hieracium lazistanum subsp.

vandasii, commonly known as the Vanda's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
