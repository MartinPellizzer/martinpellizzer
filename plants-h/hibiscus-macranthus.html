Hibiscus macranthus

Hibiscus macranthus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The plant grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant color and tropical appearance.

Hibiscus macranthus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for warm climates.
