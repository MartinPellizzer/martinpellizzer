Hypericum scabroides

Hypericum scabroides, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in rough, hairy texture and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Hypericum scabroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
