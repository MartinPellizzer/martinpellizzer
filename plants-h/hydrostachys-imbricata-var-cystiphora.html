Hydrostachys imbricata var. cystiphora

Hydrostachys imbricata var. cystiphora, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
