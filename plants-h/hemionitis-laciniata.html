Hemionitis laciniata

Hemionitis laciniata, commonly known as the slit leaf fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to muddy substrates, while its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous.

Hemionitis laciniata plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
