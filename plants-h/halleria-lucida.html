Halleria lucida

Halleria lucida, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Halleria lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
