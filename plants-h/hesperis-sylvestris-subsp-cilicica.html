Hesperis sylvestris subsp. cilicica

Hesperis sylvestris subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician sweet rocket, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is found in mountainous areas of Turkey and Syria. It produces vibrant purple or pink flowers in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
