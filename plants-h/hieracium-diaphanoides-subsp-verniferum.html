Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. verniferum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

verniferum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It is notable for its translucent, waxy coating on the leaves, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
