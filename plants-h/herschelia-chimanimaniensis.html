Herschelia chimanimaniensis

Herschelia chimanimaniensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani orchid, is a rare and endemic plant found in the Chimanimani Mountains of eastern Zimbabwe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, typically growing on rock faces or trees. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring shades of white and green. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and unique adaptations to the high-altitude environment.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, it remains a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
