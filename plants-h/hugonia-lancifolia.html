Hugonia lancifolia

Hugonia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hugonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for survival in moist, well-drained soils.

Hugonia lancifolia is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
