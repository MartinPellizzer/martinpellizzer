Homalium grandiflorum var. heptagynum

Homalium grandiflorum var. heptagynum, commonly known as the seven-carpel hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are showy, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
