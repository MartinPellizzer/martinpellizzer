Hakea pycnobotrys

Hakea pycnobotrys, commonly known as the dense-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to the southwest of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or conical inflorescences that produce small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are often arranged in clusters, providing a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Hakea pycnobotrys is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
