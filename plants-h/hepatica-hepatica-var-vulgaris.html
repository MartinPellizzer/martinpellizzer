Hepatica hepatica var. vulgaris

Hepatica hepatica var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on the variety.

The plant is known for its early blooming habit and is often one of the first wildflowers to appear in the spring.
