Hedera helix var. triloba

Hedera helix var. triloba, commonly known as the curly ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant.

It is characterized by its uniquely shaped, wavy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This evergreen climbing plant is native to Europe and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers.

It thrives in shaded areas and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
