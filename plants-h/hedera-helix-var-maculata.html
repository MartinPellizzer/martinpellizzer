Hedera helix var. maculata

Hedera helix var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted ivy, is a variant of the common ivy that features distinctive white or yellow spots on its leaves.

This evergreen climbing plant is native to Europe and is characterized by its glossy, lobed foliage. It thrives in shady to partially sunny conditions and is often used in horticulture for ground cover or as a decorative element. The plant's ability to climb and its ornamental leaf patterns make it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.

Its variegated leaves add visual interest while maintaining the hardy nature of the parent species.
