Hyacinthus elwendius

Hyacinthus elwendius, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, white, and blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. Its bulbs are typically planted in the fall for spring blooming, and it is known for its hardiness in temperate climates.

The Dutch hyacinth is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
