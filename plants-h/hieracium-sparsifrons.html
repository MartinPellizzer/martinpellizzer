Hieracium sparsifrons

Hieracium sparsifrons, commonly known as the sparse-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium sparsifrons is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
