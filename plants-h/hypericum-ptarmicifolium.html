Hypericum ptarmicifolium

Hypericum ptarmicifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are characteristic of the genus Hypericum. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
