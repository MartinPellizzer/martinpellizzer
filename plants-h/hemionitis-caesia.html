Hemionitis caesia

Hemionitis caesia, commonly known as the golden tamarisk, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a bluish tinge, and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats, and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hemionitis caesia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
