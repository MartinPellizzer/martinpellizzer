Hieracium coloplastum

Hieracium coloplastum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium coloplastum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
