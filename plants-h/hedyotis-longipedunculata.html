Hedyotis longipedunculata

Hedyotis longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed dotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its flowers are typically bisexual and have four to six petals.

Hedyotis longipedunculata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
