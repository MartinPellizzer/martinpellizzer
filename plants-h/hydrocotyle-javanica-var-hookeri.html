Hydrocotyle javanica var. hookeri

Hydrocotyle javanica var. hookeri, commonly known as the Java pennywort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, white flowers throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it both a common weed and a useful ornamental plant.

Hydrocotyle javanica var. hookeri is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic habitats.
