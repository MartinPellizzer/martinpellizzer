Hackelia sessilifructa

Hackelia sessilifructa, commonly known as the sessile-fruited honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that remains attached to the plant, giving it its common name.

Hackelia sessilifructa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
