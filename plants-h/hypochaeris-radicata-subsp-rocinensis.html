Hypochaeris radicata subsp. rocinensis

Hypochaeris radicata subsp.

rocinensis, commonly known as the rocinensis sow thistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and features yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as cypsela. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
