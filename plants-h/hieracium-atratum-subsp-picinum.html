Hieracium atratum subsp. picinum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

picinum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are dark purple to black, which gives it its common name.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
