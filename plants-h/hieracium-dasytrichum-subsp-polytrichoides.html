Hieracium dasytrichum subsp. polytrichoides

Hieracium dasytrichum subsp.

polytrichoides, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its subspecies name, *polytrichoides*, refers to the dense, hairy appearance of its foliage.
