Hieracium vogesiacum subsp. tournefortianum

Hieracium vogesiacum subsp.

tournefortianum, commonly known as the Tournefortian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates.
