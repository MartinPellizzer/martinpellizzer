Hieracium lithophilon

Hieracium lithophilon, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in crevices and on limestone, showcasing its adaptation to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a hardy species that thrives in harsh conditions, making it a symbol of resilience in mountainous regions.
