Helipterum incanum var. flavidiceps

Helipterum incanum var. flavidiceps, commonly known as the yellow-headed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
