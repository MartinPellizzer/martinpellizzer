Helictochloa blaui

Helictochloa blaui, commonly known as the Bluegrass Needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and ecological significance, it plays a vital role in maintaining grassland ecosystems.
