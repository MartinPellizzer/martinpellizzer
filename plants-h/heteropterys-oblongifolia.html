Heteropterys oblongifolia

Heteropterys oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf huito, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oblong to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
