Helianthemum montanum var. viride

Helianthemum montanum var. viride, commonly known as green rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding a bright contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in arid environments.
