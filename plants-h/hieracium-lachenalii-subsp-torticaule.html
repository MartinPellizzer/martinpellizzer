Hieracium lachenalii subsp. torticaule

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

torticaule, commonly known as the bent-stem hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, often slightly bent stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in a single head. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
