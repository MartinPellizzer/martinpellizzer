Hypericum degeneri

Hypericum degeneri, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that are often tinged with yellow or orange. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum degeneri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
