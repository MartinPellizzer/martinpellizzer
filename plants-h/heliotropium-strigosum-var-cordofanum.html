Heliotropium strigosum var. cordofanum

Heliotropium strigosum var. cordofanum, commonly known as African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and open woodlands. Its botanical features include a branching stem, small ovate leaves, and capsules as its fruiting structure.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.
