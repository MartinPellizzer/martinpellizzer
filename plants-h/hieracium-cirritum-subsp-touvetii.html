Hieracium cirritum subsp. touvetii

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

touvetii, commonly known as the Touvet's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Native to southern Europe, this subspecies thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
