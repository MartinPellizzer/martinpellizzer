Hieracium bourgaei subsp. terianum

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

terianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange inflorescences.

The plant typically features basal rosettes of hairy, lanceolate leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
