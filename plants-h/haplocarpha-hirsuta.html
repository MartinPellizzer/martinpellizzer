Haplocarpha hirsuta

Haplocarpha hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a head, resembling those of true dandelions.

Haplocarpha hirsuta is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
