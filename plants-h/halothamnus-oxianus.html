Halothamnus oxianus

Halothamnus oxianus, commonly known as the Tibetan tamarisk, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude and semi-desert conditions.

Halothamnus oxianus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
