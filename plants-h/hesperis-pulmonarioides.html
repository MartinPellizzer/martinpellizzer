Hesperis pulmonarioides

Hesperis pulmonarioides, commonly known as the lungwort or lung flower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Hesperis pulmonarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
