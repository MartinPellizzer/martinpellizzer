Helichrysum maranguense

Helichrysum maranguense, commonly known as the Maranguense everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
