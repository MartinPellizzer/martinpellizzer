Hilsenbergia ehretia

Hilsenbergia ehretia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy texture. It is well adapted to tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Hilsenbergia ehretia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
