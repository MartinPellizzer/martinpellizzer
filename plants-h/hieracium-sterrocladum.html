Hieracium sterrocladum

Hieracium sterrocladum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.
