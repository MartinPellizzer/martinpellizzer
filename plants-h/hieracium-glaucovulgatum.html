Hieracium glaucovulgatum

Hieracium glaucovulgatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are typically hairy and lobed. It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The plant is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
