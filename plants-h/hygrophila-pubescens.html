Hygrophila pubescens

Hygrophila pubescens, commonly known as the velvet-leaf hygrophila, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Hygrophila pubescens is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
