Hedychium coccineum var. urophyllum

Hedychium coccineum var. urophyllum, commonly known as the red flame ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

This variety is valued for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical and subtropical regions.
