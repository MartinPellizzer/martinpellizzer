Hirschfeldia nasturtiifolia

Hirschfeldia nasturtiifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

Hirschfeldia nasturtiifolia is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of garden cress.
