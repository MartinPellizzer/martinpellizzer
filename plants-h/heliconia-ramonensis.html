Heliconia ramonensis

Heliconia ramonensis, commonly known as the Ramon's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by vibrant, strap-like bracts in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Heliconia ramonensis plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for hummingbirds and other pollinators.
