Hyptis penaeoides

Hyptis penaeoides, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis penaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
