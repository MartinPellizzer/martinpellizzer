Hevea peruviana

Hevea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is closely related to the rubber tree (Hevea brasiliensis) and shares similar latex-producing properties.

However, it is less commonly cultivated for commercial rubber production compared to its relative.
