Hopea wyattsmithii

Hopea wyattsmithii, commonly known as the Malayan hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests. The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody fruits containing winged seeds.

Hopea wyattsmithii is valued for its durable timber and plays an important role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
