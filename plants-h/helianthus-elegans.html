Helianthus elegans

Helianthus elegans, commonly known as the elegant sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with tall, slender stems and large, daisy-like yellow flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
