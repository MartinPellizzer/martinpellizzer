Hebea galeata

Hebea galeata, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Hebea galeata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
