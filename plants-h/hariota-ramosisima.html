Hariota ramosisima

Hariota ramosisima, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The tree has a spreading canopy and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, red berries. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Hariota ramosisima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
