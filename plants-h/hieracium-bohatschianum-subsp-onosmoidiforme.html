Hieracium bohatschianum subsp. onosmoidiforme

Hieracium bohatschianum subsp.

onosmoidiforme, commonly known as the Bohatschian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has smooth, often hairy stems and produces small, inconspicuous disk flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
