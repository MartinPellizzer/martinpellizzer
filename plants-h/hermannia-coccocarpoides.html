Hermannia coccocarpoides

Hermannia coccocarpoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed hermannia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that often display a reddish hue, especially under direct sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Hermannia coccocarpoides is well-suited for shady or partially shaded garden settings and is prized for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
