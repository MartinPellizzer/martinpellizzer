Haplopappus reicheanus

Haplopappus reicheanus, commonly known as the smooth pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant's structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.
