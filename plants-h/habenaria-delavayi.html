Habenaria delavayi

Habenaria delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including China, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a terrestrial orchid with tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a unique lip structure. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
