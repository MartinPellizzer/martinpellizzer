Hieracium inuloides subsp. subvirens

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

subvirens, commonly known as the green-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, often glandular, leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine, greenish hairs, which give it its common name.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
