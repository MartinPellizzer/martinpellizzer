Hemigenia microphylla

Hemigenia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved honey mint, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understoreys.

Hemigenia microphylla is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
