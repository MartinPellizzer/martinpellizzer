Hedysarum scariosum

Hedysarum scariosum, commonly known as the scarious milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Hedysarum scariosum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
