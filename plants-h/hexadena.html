Hexadena

Hexadena, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Hexadena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
