Hippocrepis brevipetala

Hippocrepis brevipetala, commonly known as the short-petaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hippocrepis brevipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
