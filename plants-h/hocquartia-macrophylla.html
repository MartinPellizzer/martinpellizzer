Hocquartia macrophylla

Hocquartia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life forms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds. Its flowers are typically white or pink, emerging above the water surface at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Hocquartia macrophylla plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
