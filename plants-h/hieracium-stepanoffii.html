Hieracium stepanoffii

Hieracium stepanoffii, commonly known as Stepánoff's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–50 cm, with dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom from July to September. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
