Hieracium Fuliginatum

Hieracium fuliginatum, commonly known as the smoky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. This plant often grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in disturbed soils. Its name comes from the dark, sooty appearance of its leaves, which give it a unique and somewhat ominous look. Despite its unassuming appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
