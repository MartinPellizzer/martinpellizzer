Heteropappus meyendorffii var. hirsutus

Heteropappus meyendorffii var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a hairy texture. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to harsh climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
