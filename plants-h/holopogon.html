Holopogon

Holopogon, commonly known as the slender grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. These plants are typically found in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Holopogon species are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

They play an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
