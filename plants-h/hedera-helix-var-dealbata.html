Hedera helix var. dealbata

Hedera helix var. dealbata, commonly known as the silver-leaf ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its striking silvery-white variegation on its leaves.

This plant is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe and is known for its adaptability to various light conditions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and can be grown both indoors and outdoors.

The silver-leaf ivy is often used in landscaping for its ability to cover walls and trellises with its trailing growth habit.
