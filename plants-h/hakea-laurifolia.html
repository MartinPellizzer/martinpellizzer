Hakea laurifolia

Hakea laurifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces cylindrical, woody flower spikes that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

Hakea laurifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
