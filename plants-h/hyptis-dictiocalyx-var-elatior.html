Hyptis dictiocalyx var. elatior

Hyptis dictiocalyx var. elatior, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its grayish-white, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
