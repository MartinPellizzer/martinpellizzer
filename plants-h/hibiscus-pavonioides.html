Hibiscus pavonioides

Hibiscus pavonioides, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus pavonioides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
