Helianthemum umbellatum var. erectum

Helianthemum umbellatum var. erectum, commonly known as the erect sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.
