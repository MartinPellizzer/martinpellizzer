Hondbesseion olens var. angustifolium

Hondbesseion olens var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a variant of the honeysuckle plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodland edges and moist soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its botanical structure includes opposite leaves with serrated edges and a climbing or trailing growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
