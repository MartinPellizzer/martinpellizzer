Hemipogon acerosus var. viridis

Hemipogon acerosus var. viridis, commonly known as the green leafed ironwood, is a rare and hardy plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and robust, woody stems that provide structural support. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is often protected in its native habitat.
