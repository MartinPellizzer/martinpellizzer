Habenaria warburgana

Habenaria warburgana, commonly known as the Warburg's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
