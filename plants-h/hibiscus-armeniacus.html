Hibiscus armeniacus

Hibiscus armeniacus, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red, single or double flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Hibiscus armeniacus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
