Habranthus minor

Habranthus minor, commonly known as the lesser habranthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
