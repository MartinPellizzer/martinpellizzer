Hippomarathrum libanotis subsp. bocconei

Hippomarathrum libanotis subsp.

bocconei, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its subspecies distinction is based on subtle differences in leaf morphology and geographic distribution.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
