Hieracium oxyodon subsp. amauradenioides

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

amauradenioides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils, often found in the Mediterranean region.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed complex, with specific adaptations to its arid environment.
