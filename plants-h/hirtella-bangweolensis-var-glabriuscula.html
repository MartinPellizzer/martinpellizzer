Hirtella bangweolensis var. glabriuscula

Hirtella bangweolensis var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smoothleaf hirtella, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, showcasing a compact, low-growing form. The leaves are smooth and glossy, with a lanceolate shape and a slightly waxy texture. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the warmer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to coastal conditions.
