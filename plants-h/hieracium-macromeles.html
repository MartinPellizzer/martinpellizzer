Hieracium macromeles

Hieracium macromeles, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can reach up to 20 centimeters in length, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

Hieracium macromeles is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
