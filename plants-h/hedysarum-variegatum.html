Hedysarum variegatum

Hedysarum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with alternating pairs of leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and rocky habitats.

Hedysarum variegatum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
