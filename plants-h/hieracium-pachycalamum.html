Hieracium pachycalamum

Hieracium pachycalamum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hieracium pachycalamum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
