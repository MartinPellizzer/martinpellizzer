Helianthemum chevallieri

Helianthemum chevallieri, commonly known as the Corsican rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 20 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Helianthemum chevallieri is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.
