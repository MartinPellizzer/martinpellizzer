Hartogia crassifolia

Hartogia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed Hartogia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by pale green, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

Hartogia crassifolia is often used in rock gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
