Hesperis balansae subsp. mytilensis

Hesperis balansae subsp.

mytilensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean horned poppy, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking, papery pink or purple flowers that resemble horns, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
