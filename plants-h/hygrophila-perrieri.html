Hygrophila perrieri

Hygrophila perrieri, commonly known as the African water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Hygrophila perrieri is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
