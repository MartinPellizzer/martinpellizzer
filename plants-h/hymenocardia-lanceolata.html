Hymenocardia lanceolata

Hymenocardia lanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its most striking feature is its showy, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
