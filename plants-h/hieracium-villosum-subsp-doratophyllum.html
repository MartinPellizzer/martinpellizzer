Hieracium villosum subsp. doratophyllum

Hieracium villosum subsp.

doratophyllum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its yellow flowers bloom in summer.

It is a relatively low-growing plant, rarely exceeding 30 cm in height.
