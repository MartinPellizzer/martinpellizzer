Homalanthus populneus var. minor

Homalanthus populneus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and pinnate, with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
