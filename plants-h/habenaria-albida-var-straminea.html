Habenaria albida var. straminea

Habenaria albida var. straminea, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers with pale green veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a smooth texture.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
