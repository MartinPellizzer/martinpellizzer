Hopea bracteata

Hopea bracteata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Hopea bracteata plays an important role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
