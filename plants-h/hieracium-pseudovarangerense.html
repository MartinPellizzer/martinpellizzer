Hieracium pseudovarangerense

Hieracium pseudovarangerense, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. This species typically grows in rocky and barren environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in polar ecosystems.
