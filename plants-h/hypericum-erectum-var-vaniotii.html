Hypericum erectum var. vaniotii

Hypericum erectum var. vaniotii, commonly known as the Vaniot's St. John's Wort, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used in herbal remedies.
