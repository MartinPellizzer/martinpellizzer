Hippeastrum bulbulosum var. equestriforme

Hippeastrum bulbulosum var. equestriforme, commonly known as the elephant's foot amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that can grow up to several feet tall, supporting its vibrant floral display. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
