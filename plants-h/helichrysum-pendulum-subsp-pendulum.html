Helichrysum pendulum subsp. pendulum

Helichrysum pendulum subsp.

pendulum, commonly known as the hanging goldenrod, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its drooping, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small sunflowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
