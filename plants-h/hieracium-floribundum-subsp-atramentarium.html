Hieracium floribundum subsp. atramentarium

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

atramentarium, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent, dark veins, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
