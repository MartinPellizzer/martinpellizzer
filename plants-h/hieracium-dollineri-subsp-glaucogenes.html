Hieracium dollineri subsp. glaucogenes

Hieracium dollineri subsp.

glaucogenes, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowering heads that consist of numerous small disk flowers.

Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its subspecific name, *glaucogenes*, refers to the bluish-gray coloration of its leaves, which is a distinguishing botanical feature.
