Heliotropium subracemosum

Heliotropium subracemosum, commonly known as the tropical heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Heliotropium subracemosum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
