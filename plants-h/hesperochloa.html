Hesperochloa

Hesperochloa, commonly known as bluegrass, is a genus of grasses native to the Americas.

These plants are characterized by their fine, tufted foliage and often form dense, sod-like growths. Hesperochloa species typically thrive in temperate climates and are often found in prairies, meadows, and woodland edges. They have narrow, blade-like leaves and produce small, spikelet-based flowers.

Many species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
