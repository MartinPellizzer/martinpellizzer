Hedysarum moniliforme

Hedysarum moniliforme, commonly known as the bead pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical pod that contains several small, bead-like seeds.

Hedysarum moniliforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
