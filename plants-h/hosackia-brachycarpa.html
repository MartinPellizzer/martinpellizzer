Hosackia brachycarpa

Hosackia brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear alternate, pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. Its showy, violet-blue flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
