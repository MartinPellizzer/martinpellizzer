Haworthia tessellata var. minutissima

Haworthia tessellata var. minutissima, commonly known as the dwarf zebra plant, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, often marked with distinct white or pale markings that resemble a zebra's stripes. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation. It prefers bright, indirect sunlight and is known for its ability to tolerate dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
