Heliconia indica var. lanata

Heliconia indica var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with small, yellow flowers surrounded by fleshy, hairy structures. Its leaves are broad and often covered with a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
