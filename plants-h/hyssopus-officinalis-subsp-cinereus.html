Hyssopus officinalis subsp. cinereus

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

cinereus, commonly known as gray hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered in grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Gray hyssop is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
