Holcus atropurpureus

Holcus atropurpureus, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple stems and leaves, which are often tinged with red or brown. This plant thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, typically found in heathlands and moorlands. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Holcus atropurpureus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
