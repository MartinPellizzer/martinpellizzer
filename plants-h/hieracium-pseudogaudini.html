Hieracium pseudogaudini

Hieracium pseudogaudini, commonly known as the false gaudinia hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Native to Europe, it often grows in meadows and grassy areas. The plant typically reaches a height of 20 to 50 centimeters.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
