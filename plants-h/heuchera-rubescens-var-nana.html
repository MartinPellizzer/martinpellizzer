Heuchera rubescens var. nana

Heuchera rubescens var. nana, commonly known as the compact coral bells, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, mounding foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are typically red, bronze, or purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to add color to shaded areas.
