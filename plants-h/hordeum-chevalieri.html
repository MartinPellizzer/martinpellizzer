Hordeum chevalieri

Hordeum chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's barley, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets per spikelet. Hordeum chevalieri is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
