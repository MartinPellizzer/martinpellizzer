Hieracium aequialtum

Hieracium aequialtum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool temperatures. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in a single head at the end of a hairy stem. Hieracium aequialtum is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.

It is often found in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
