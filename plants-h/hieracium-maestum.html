Hieracium maestum

Hieracium maestum, commonly known as the melancholy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium maestum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
