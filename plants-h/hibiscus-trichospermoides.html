Hibiscus trichospermoides

Hibiscus trichospermoides, commonly known as the Caribbean hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring large, showy flowers with five deeply lobed petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy on the underside. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom during the summer months.

Hibiscus trichospermoides is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
