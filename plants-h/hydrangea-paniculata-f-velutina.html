Hydrangea paniculata f. velutina

Hydrangea paniculata f. velutina, commonly known as the velvety panicled hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

The plant features panicles of white or pink flowers that can change color based on soil pH, a trait influenced by its high aluminum content. Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This hydrangea is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability in garden settings.
