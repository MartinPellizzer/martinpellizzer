Hesperis cabulica

Hesperis cabulica, commonly known as the Persian sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Turkey. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a peppery aroma when crushed.

Hesperis cabulica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of growth in well-drained soil.
