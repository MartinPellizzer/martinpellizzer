Hornstedtia conoidea

Hornstedtia conoidea, commonly known as the cone-shaped hornstedtia, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and cone-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered along the central axis of the inflorescence. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette structure that helps retain moisture.

This species is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance.
