Hepetis inermis

Hepetis inermis, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Hepetis inermis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
