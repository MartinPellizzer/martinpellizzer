Hesperis podocarpa

Hesperis podocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hesperis podocarpa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
