Hordeum valdiviae

Hordeum valdiviae, commonly known as the Valdivia barley, is a species of wild barley native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments. Hordeum valdiviae is known for its genetic diversity and has been studied for its potential in agricultural breeding programs.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in the study of plant taxonomy and evolution.
