Hydrangea florida

Hydrangea florida, commonly known as the panicled hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces sterile flowers that are often the most visible part of the bloom. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Hydrangea florida thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
