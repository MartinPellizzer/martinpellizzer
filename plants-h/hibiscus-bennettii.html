Hibiscus bennettii

Hibiscus bennettii, commonly known as the Bennett's hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Hibiscus bennettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
