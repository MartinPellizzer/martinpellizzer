Hieracium murorum subsp. fritschii

Hieracium murorum subsp.

fritschii, commonly known as the Fritsch's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like foliage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and specific geographic distribution compared to other Hieracium species.
