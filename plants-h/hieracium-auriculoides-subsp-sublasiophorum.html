Hieracium auriculoides subsp. sublasiophorum

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

sublasiophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in temperate climates.

Its leaves are narrow and gradually tapering, with a distinctive auriculate base that gives it its species name.
