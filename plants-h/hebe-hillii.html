Hebe hillii

Hebe hillii, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often with silver or gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Hebe hillii is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
