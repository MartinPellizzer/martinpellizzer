Hypericum lagarocaule

Hypericum lagarocaule, commonly known as the coastal St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand saline and wind conditions.

Hypericum lagarocaule is often found growing along coastal cliffs and dunes.
