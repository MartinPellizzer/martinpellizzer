Heterosciadium androphilum

Heterosciadium androphilum, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with a single, upright flowering stem that produces a cluster of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the flowers have a distinctive structure with three sepals and three petals.

Heterosciadium androphilum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
