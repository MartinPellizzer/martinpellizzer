Hygrophila bayatensis

Hygrophila bayatensis, commonly known as the Bayatensis hygrophila, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a semi-aquatic plant that thrives in wetland environments, often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. It produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens and conservation efforts.
