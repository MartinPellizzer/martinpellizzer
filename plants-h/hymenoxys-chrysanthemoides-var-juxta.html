Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides var. juxta

Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides var. juxta, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower or Mexican daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow to orange blooms. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry to moist habitats.
