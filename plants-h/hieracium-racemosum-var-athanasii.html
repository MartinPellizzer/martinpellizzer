Hieracium racemosum var. athanasii

Hieracium racemosum var. athanasii, commonly known as the Athanasius's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces a tall, slender flowering stem with yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers have five petals with a distinct central disk.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
