Hedychium forrestii var. palaniense

Hedychium forrestii var. palaniense, commonly known as the Palaniense ginger lily, is a tropical perennial plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle red hue at the base. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil to flourish.

This variant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
