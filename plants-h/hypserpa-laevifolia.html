Hypserpa laevifolia

Hypserpa laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed hypserpa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Hypserpa laevifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and ecological diversity.
