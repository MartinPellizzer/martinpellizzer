Hemizygia ornata

Hemizygia ornata, commonly known as the showy hemizygia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered in dense, terminal spikes. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, which are attracted to its bright colors and nectar-rich blooms.

Hemizygia ornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
