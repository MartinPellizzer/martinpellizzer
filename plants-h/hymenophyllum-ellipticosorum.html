Hymenophyllum ellipticosorum

Hymenophyllum ellipticosorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spore moss, is a small, delicate fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hair. The plant produces spores in tiny, cup-like structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the underside of the leaves. It is typically found growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of environmental conditions.
