Hieracium atratum subsp. tanylepis

Hieracium atratum subsp.

tanylepis, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The subspecies tanylepis is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
