Hesperis diffusa

Hesperis diffusa, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

Hesperis diffusa is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
