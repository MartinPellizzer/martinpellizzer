Homalium macranthum

Homalium macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered hollies, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing small, dark seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
