Heterogonium sagenioides

Heterogonium sagenioides, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It possesses specialized root systems, including pneumatophores, which facilitate gas exchange in waterlogged soils. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy, producing both sexual and asexual propagules. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, reducing water loss in high-salinity conditions.

Heterogonium sagenioides plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial organisms.
