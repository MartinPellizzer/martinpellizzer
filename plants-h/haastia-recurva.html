Haastia recurva

Haastia recurva, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often used for weaving traditional Māori basketry. The plant grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, white or pale green flowers, which bloom in spring.

Haastia recurva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Māori culture.
