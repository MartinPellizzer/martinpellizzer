Hoya taytayensis

Hoya taytayensis, commonly known as the Philippine Waffle Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces a distinctive, waffle-like pattern on its leaves, which gives it its common name.

Hoya taytayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
