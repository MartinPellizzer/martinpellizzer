Hyacinthus transcaspicus

Hyacinthus transcaspicus, commonly known as the Transcaspian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flower clusters in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its bulbs are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
