Hypericum patulum var. uralum

Hypericum patulum var. uralum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
