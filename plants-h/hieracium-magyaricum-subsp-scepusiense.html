Hieracium magyaricum subsp. scepusiense

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

scepusiense, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other hawkweed species.
