Hieracium villosoides

Hieracium villosoides, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium villosoides is known for its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
