Hyoseris radiata subsp. graeca

Hyoseris radiata subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets, which give it a striking appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
