Haemodorum griseofuscum

Haemodorum griseofuscum, commonly known as the grey false lily or grey lily, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as the "red lilies.". The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands.

Haemodorum griseofuscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting local wildlife.
