Hydrangea ecuadorensis

Hydrangea ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian hydrangea, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green surface and serrated edges.

Hydrangea ecuadorensis thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.
