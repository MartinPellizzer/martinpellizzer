Hieracium indistinctum

Hieracium indistinctum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium indistinctum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
