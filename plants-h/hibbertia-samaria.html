Hibbertia samaria

Hibbertia samaria, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands.

Hibbertia samaria is a valuable component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
