Hesperis stellata

Hesperis stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Hesperis stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
