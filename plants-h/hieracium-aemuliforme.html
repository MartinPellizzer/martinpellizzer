Hieracium aemuliforme

Hieracium aemuliforme, commonly known as the mimic hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that resemble those of the hawkweed genus, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in meadows and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium aemuliforme is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various regions.
