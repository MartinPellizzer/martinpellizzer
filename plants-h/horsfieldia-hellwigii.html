Horsfieldia hellwigii

Horsfieldia hellwigii, commonly known as the Halmahera orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indonesian island of Halmahera.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within lowland forests. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Horsfieldia hellwigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
