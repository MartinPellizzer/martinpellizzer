Helianthemum halimifolium subsp. multiflorum

Helianthemum halimifolium subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies multiflorum is distinguished by its more abundant flowering compared to other varieties within the species.
