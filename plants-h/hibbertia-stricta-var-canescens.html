Hibbertia stricta var. canescens

Hibbertia stricta var. canescens, commonly known as the silver-leafed hibbertia, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, silky hair. The flowers are typically orange to yellow, with five petals and a distinctive, tubular throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
