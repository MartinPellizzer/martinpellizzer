Hieracium latibracteum

Hieracium latibracteum, commonly known as the broad-bract hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
