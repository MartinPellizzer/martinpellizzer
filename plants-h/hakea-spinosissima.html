Hakea spinosissima

Hakea spinosissima, commonly known as the spinifex hakea, is a shrub native to western and southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spinose foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its extensive root system and ability to store water in its stems. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures that reduce water loss.

Hakea spinosissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native landscaping.
