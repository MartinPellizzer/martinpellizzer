Heterotrichum hispidum

Heterotrichum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Heterotrichaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Heterotrichum hispidum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its ecosystem.
