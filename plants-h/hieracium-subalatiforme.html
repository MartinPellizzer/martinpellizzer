Hieracium subalatiforme

Hieracium subalatiforme, commonly known as the subalpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellowish to reddish disc florets surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.
