Heteromalla paniculata

Heteromalla paniculata, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped inflorescences that produce clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and serrated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Heteromalla paniculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
