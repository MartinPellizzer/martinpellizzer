Hieracium Murorum Subsp. Olivaceiceps

Olive-headed hawkweed, scientifically known as Hieracium murorum subsp. olivaceiceps, is a distinctive member of the dandelion family found primarily in temperate regions of Europe. This plant is characterized by its unique olive-green head and slender, hairy stems that distinguish it from other hawkweed species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, often growing in colonies. Despite its beauty, it is considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid spread and competitive nature. Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host for various insects.
