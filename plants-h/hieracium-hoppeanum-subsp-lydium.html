Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. lydium

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

lydium, commonly known as the Lydian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
