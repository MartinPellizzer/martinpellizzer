Helicia cochinchinensis

Helicia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese helenia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and is often found in regions with high humidity. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are followed by small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
