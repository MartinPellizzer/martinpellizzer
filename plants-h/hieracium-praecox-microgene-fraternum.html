Hieracium praecox microgene fraternum

Hieracium praecox microgene fraternum, commonly known as the early yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate with hairy surfaces, and its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers with ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is characterized by its short, upright growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
