Hepetis straminea

Hepetis straminea, commonly known as the yellow tansy, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hepetis straminea is also known for its historical use in treating digestive ailments and as a natural insect repellent.
