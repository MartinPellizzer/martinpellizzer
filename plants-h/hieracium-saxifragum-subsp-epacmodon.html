Hieracium saxifragum subsp. epacmodon

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

epacmodon, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
