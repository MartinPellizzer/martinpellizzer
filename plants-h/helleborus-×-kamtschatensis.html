Helleborus × kamtschatensis

Helleborus × kamtschatensis, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers with a distinctive, helmet-like structure, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in cool, shady environments and is adapted to well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value and winter blooming habit make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
