Hedera lobata

Hedera lobata, commonly known as the Persian ivy, is a species of flowering plant in the Araliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, the Caucasus, and parts of Iran. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Hedera lobata prefers cool, humid environments and can be cultivated both indoors and outdoors in suitable climates.
