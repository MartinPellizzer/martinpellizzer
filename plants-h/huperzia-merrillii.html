Huperzia merrillii

Huperzia merrillii, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and club-shaped sporophytes that bear spores. Native to China, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Huperzia merrillii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
