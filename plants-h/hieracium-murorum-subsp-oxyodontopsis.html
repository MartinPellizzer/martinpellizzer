Hieracium murorum subsp. oxyodontopsis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

oxyodontopsis, commonly known as the mouse-eared hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, often reddish stems and leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
