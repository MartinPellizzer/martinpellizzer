Hordeum laxum

Hordeum laxum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and loose, open inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are small and spike-like.

Hordeum laxum is primarily of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
