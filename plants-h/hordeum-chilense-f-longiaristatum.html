Hordeum chilense f. longiaristatum

Hordeum chilense f. longiaristatum, commonly known as the long-awned Chile barley, is a botanical variant of the barley species Hordeum chilense.

It is characterized by its elongated awns, which are the bristle-like structures at the base of its florets. This form is native to South America, particularly Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and adaptation.
