Homalolepis insignis

Homalolepis insignis, commonly known as the mountain holly.

It is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
