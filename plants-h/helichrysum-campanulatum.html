Helichrysum campanulatum

Helichrysum campanulatum, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, papery bracts beneath the flowers.

Helichrysum campanulatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
