Hypserpa cuspidata

Hypserpa cuspidata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Hypserpa cuspidata plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
