Hieracium phrixomalliforme

Hieracium phrixomalliforme, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, reddish stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium phrixomalliforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
