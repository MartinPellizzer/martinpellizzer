Helipterum polygalifolium

Helipterum polygalifolium, commonly known as the yellow sandplain aster, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head.

Helipterum polygalifolium is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy environments.
