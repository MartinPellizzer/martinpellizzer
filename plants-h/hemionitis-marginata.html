Hemionitis marginata

Hemionitis marginata, commonly known as the marginate hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis marginata is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.

Its conservation status highlights the importance of preserving fragile ecosystems in arid regions.
