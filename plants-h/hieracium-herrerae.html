Hieracium herrerae

Hieracium herrerae, commonly known as Herrera's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Herrera's hawkweed is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies.
