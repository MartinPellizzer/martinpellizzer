Helleborus ranunculinus

Helleborus ranunculinus, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with prominent, spurred petals, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Helleborus ranunculinus is noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
