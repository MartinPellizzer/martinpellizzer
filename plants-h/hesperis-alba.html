Hesperis alba

Hesperis alba, commonly known as the white scabious, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with linear, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink blossoms. Hesperis alba thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in natural habitats.
