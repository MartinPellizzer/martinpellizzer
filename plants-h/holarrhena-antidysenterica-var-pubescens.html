Holarrhena antidysenterica var. pubescens

Holarrhena antidysenterica var. pubescens, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for treating dysentery and other gastrointestinal issues. Its bark contains alkaloids, including berberine, which contribute to its pharmacological significance.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems and as a source of natural dyes.
