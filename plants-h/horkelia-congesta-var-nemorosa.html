Horkelia congesta var. nemorosa

Horkelia congesta var. nemorosa, commonly known as the Sierra foothill horkelia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
