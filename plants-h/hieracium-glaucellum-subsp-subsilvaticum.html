Hieracium glaucellum subsp. subsilvaticum

Hieracium glaucellum subsp.

subsilvaticum, commonly known as the silver hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems that rise from the base.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of propagation, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
