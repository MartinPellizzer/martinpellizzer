Helichrysum adenocarpum

Helichrysum adenocarpum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
