Helichrysum corymbiferum

Helichrysum corymbiferum, commonly known as the golden crown, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, papery flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant has silvery-green, aromatic foliage that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms make it an attractive addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
