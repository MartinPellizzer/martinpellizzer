Haematocarpus thomsonii

Haematocarpus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
