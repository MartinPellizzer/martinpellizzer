Hamamelis bitchuensis

Hamamelis bitchuensis, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Hamamelis bitchuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal beauty.
