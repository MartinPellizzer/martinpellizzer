Hieracium magyaricum subsp. bosniacum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is native to parts of Europe, particularly in the Balkan region, and thrives in meadows and grasslands. It has finely toothed leaves and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
