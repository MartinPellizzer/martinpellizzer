Hyptis sclerophylla

Hyptis sclerophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hyptis sclerophylla is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and fragrant qualities.
