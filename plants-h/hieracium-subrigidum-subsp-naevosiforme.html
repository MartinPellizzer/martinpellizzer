Hieracium subrigidum subsp. naevosiforme

Hieracium subrigidum subsp.

naevosiforme, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often have a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in dense capitula, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive, irregularly shaped spots on the leaves, which give it its common name.
