Hoffmannseggia melanosticta

Hoffmannseggia melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained Hoffmannseggia, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its ribbed, cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
