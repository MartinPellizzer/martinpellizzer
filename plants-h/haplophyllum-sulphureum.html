Haplophyllum sulphureum

Haplophyllum sulphureum, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or rocky soils.

Haplophyllum sulphureum is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
