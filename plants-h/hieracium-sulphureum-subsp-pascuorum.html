Hieracium sulphureum subsp. pascuorum

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

pascuorum, commonly known as the sulphur hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and roadside areas.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, with a distinctive, daisy-like appearance. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are upright and branched.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root system.
