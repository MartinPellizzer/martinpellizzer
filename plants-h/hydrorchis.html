Hydrorchis

Hydrorchis, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

These plants are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. They have lance-shaped leaves and produce showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. Hydrorchis species are known for their ability to thrive in wet soils and their role in supporting local pollinator populations.

The genus includes several species, each adapted to specific ecological niches within their native habitats.
