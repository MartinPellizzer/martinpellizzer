Hieracium mollitum

Hieracium mollitum, commonly known as the velvet hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a velvety texture. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Hieracium mollitum is often found in temperate climates and is considered a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
