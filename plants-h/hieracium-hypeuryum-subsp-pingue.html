Hieracium hypeuryum subsp. pingue

Hieracium hypeuryum subsp.

pingue, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It has hairy, usually green to reddish stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or divided.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
