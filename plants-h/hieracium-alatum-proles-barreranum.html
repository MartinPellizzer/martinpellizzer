Hieracium alatum proles barreranum

Hieracium alatum proles barreranum, commonly known as the Barreranum Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
