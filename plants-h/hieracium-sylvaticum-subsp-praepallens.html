Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. praepallens

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

praepallens, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces seeds in small, dry fruits.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
