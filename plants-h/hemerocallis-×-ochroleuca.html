Hemerocallis × ochroleuca

Hemerocallis × ochroleuca, commonly known as the lemon yellow daylily, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens. It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is closely related to other daylily species.

The flowers typically last only one day, hence the genus name "Hemerocallis," meaning "day" and "beauty.".
