Helianthemum variabile var. angustifolium

Helianthemum variabile var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sandverbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand dry and rocky conditions. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
