Hoffmannia cauliflora

Hoffmannia cauliflora, commonly known as the mountain hoffmannia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly from the stems rather than the leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are often broad and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Hoffmannia cauliflora is known for its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
