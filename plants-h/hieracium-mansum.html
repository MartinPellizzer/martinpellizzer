Hieracium mansum

Hieracium mansum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow or orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium mansum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
