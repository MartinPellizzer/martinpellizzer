Hopea suavis

Hopea suavis, commonly known as the sweet hope or sweet sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 40 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
