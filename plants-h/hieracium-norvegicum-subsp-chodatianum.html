Hieracium norvegicum subsp. chodatianum

Hieracium norvegicum subsp.

chodatianum, commonly known as the Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth form compared to other hawkweed species.
