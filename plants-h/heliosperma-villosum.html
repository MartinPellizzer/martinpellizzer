Heliosperma villosum

Heliosperma villosum, commonly known as the hairy heliosperma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
