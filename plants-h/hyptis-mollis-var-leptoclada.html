Hyptis mollis var. leptoclada

Hyptis mollis var. leptoclada, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
