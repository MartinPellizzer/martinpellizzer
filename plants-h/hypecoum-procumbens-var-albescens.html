Hypecoum procumbens var. albescens

Hypecoum procumbens var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, heart-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a pinkish tinge, blooming in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral display and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
