Hieracium arctophilum

Hieracium arctophilum, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky or sandy habitats, often in alpine or tundra environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Hieracium arctophilum is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.
