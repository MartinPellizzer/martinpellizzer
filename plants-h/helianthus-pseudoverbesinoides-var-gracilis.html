Helianthus pseudoverbesinoides var. gracilis

Helianthus pseudoverbesinoides var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and it features slender, hairy stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
