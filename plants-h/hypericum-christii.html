Hypericum christii

Hypericum christii, commonly known as the yellow St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.

Hypericum christii is typically used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
