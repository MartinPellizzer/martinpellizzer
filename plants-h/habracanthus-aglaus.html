Habracanthus aglaus

Habracanthus aglaus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Habracanthus aglaus is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
