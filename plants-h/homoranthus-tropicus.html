Homoranthus tropicus

Homoranthus tropicus, commonly known as the tropical homoranthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
