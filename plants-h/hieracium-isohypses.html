Hieracium isohypses

Hieracium isohypses, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium isohypses is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and seeds, making it both a common weed and a valuable plant for pollinators.
