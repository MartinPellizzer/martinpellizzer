Hibbertia glandulosa

Hibbertia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture, particularly on its stems and leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Hibbertia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
