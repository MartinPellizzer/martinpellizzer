Helianthus giganteus var. utahensis

Helianthus giganteus var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah giant sunflower, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers, surrounded by broad, green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows up to 6 feet in height and thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
