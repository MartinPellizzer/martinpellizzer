Hyptis rubicunda

Hyptis rubicunda, commonly known as red hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it is known for its medicinal properties used in traditional remedies.

Hyptis rubicunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
