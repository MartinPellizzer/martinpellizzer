Holmskioldia subintegra

Holmskioldia subintegra, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in rocky soils.

Holmskioldia subintegra is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Andean traditions.
