Hieracium vandasii subsp. pelliculatiforme

Hieracium vandasii subsp.

pelliculatiforme, commonly known as the velvet hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its subspecies distinction is based on differences in leaf structure and hair density.

It is often found in open vegetation types such as grasslands and shrublands.
