Hamatocactus crassihamatus

Hamatocactus crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-hair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are densely covered with white, woolly hairs, providing insulation against extreme temperatures. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
