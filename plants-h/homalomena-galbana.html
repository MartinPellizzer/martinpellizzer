Homalomena galbana

Homalomena galbana, commonly known as the red-veined arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves with striking red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
