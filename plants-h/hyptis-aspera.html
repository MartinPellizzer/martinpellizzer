Hyptis aspera

Hyptis aspera, commonly known as rough hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Hyptis aspera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

It is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
