Hieracium lachenalii subsp. subhastulatum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

subhastulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
