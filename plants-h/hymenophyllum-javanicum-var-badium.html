Hymenophyllum javanicum var. badium

Hymenophyllum javanicum var. badium, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a delicate and epiphytic species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are closely appressed and covered with a dense layer of hair-like structures, which help retain moisture.

This variety is notable for its intricate morphology and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
