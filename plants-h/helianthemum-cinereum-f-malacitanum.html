Helianthemum cinereum f. malacitanum

Helianthemum cinereum f. malacitanum, commonly known as the Malacitan sandthrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora's resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
