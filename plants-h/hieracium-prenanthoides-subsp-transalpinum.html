Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. transalpinum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

transalpinum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth form and is often found in mountainous areas across the Alps.
