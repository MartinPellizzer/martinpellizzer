Haplopappus drummondii

Haplopappus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces achenes with pappus structures for wind dispersal.

Haplopappus drummondii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
