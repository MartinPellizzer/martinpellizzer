Hyptis candida

Hyptis candida, commonly known as white hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and aromatic leaves.

Hyptis candida is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
