Henckelia caelestis

Henckelia caelestis, commonly known as the celestial henckelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in dense, terminal clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

Henckelia caelestis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
