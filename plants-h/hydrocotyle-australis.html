Hydrocotyle australis

Hydrocotyle australis, commonly known as the Australian marsh pennywort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically floating or submerged in water, depending on the habitat. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in wetland environments, such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Hydrocotyle australis is often used in aquatic gardens and is valued for its ability to stabilize water ecosystems.
