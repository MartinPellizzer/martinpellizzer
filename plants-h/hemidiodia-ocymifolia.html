Hemidiodia ocymifolia

Hemidiodia ocymifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, showcasing a compact growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It serves as a food source for various insects and is adapted to arid environments.
