Hovea chorizemifolia

Hovea chorizemifolia, commonly known as the coastal hovea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers.

Hovea chorizemifolia is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
