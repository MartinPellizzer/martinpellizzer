Habenaria lelyi var. lelyi

Habenaria lelyi var. lelyi, commonly known as the Lely's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, particularly Tanzania and Kenya.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a delicate floral display to its natural environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
