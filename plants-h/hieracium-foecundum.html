Hieracium foecundum

Hieracium foecundum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant produces numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in many regions.

Hieracium foecundum is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
