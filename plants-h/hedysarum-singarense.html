Hedysarum singarense

Hedysarum singarense, commonly known as the Singar Valley pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to reddish-pink pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Hedysarum singarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
