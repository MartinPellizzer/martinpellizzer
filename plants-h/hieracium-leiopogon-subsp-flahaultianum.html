Hieracium leiopogon subsp. flahaultianum

Hieracium leiopogon subsp.

flahaultianum, commonly known as the Flahault's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It features dark green, basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
