Helenium atacamense

Helenium atacamense, commonly known as the Atacama sunflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Helenium atacamense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add color to dry gardens.
