Hyoseris angustifolia

Hyoseris angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and roadsides. Its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers are adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Hyoseris angustifolia is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
