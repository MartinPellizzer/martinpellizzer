Hieracium floribundum var. islandicum

Hieracium floribundum var. islandicum, commonly known as the Island hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and coastal regions, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent white veins. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in various habitats, making it a common sight in the wild flora of the region.
