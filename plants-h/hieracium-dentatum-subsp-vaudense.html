Hieracium dentatum subsp. vaudense

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

vaudense, commonly known as the Vaud hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the plant produces achenes as its seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
