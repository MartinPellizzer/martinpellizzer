Hieracium spanolepis

Hieracium spanolepis, commonly known as the Spanish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
