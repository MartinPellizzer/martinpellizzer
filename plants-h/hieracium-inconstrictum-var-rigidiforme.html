Hieracium inconstrictum var. rigidiforme

Hieracium inconstrictum var. rigidiforme, commonly known as the rigid-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rigid texture, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
