Hieracium juranum subsp. tephrophorum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

tephrophorum, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with many small florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to specific ecological conditions.
