Hieracium kofelicum

Hieracium kofelicum, commonly known as the Kofel hill hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium kofelicum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.
