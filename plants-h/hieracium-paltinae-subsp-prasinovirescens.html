Hieracium paltinae subsp. prasinovirescens

Hieracium paltinae subsp.

prasinovirescens, commonly known as the greenish-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a dark green coloration. Its flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters at the top of the stems, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
