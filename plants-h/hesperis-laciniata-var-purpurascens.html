Hesperis laciniata var. purpurascens

Hesperis laciniata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant features showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the ends of stems. Its leaves are lobed and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
