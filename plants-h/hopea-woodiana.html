Hopea woodiana

Hopea woodiana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hopea woodiana plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
