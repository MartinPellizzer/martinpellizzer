Hieracium macrodontoides subsp. macrodontoides

Hieracium macrodontoides subsp.

macrodontoides, commonly known as the large-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces tall, branched stems with yellow flowers that bloom from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of ray florets surrounded by disc florets.

This species is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
