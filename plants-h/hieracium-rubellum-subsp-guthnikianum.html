Hieracium rubellum subsp. guthnikianum

Hieracium rubellum subsp.

guthnikianum, commonly known as the red hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright red to orange-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
