Hieracium magyaricum subsp. thaumasioides

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

thaumasioides, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
