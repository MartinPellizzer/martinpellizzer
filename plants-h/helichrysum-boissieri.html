Helichrysum boissieri

Helichrysum boissieri, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden-orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered into spherical or conical shapes, giving it a decorative appearance. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

Helichrysum boissieri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
