Hypericum pulchellum

Hypericum pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum pulchellum is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and moist meadows.
