Hieracium laurinum subsp. vasconicum

Hieracium laurinum subsp.

vasconicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and the Pyrenees. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in open habitats and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
