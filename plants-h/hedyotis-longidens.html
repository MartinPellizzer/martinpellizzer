Hedyotis longidens

Hedyotis longidens, commonly known as the long-stemmed dotweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The small, white flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom during the warmer months.
