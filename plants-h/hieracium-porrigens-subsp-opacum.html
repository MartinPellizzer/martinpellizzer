Hieracium porrigens subsp. opacum

Hieracium porrigens subsp.

opacum, commonly known as the opacous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its leaves are hairy and have a rough texture, while the stem is often reddish in color.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
