Hieracium glaucinum subsp. incisoides

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

incisoides, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with wavy or toothed margins. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by phyllaries that are typically green to reddish in color.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette growth habit.
