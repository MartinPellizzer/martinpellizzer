Heliconia danielsiana

Heliconia danielsiana, commonly known as the Daniel's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that surround its flowering spikes, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is known for its robust, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette pattern. Its flowers are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by brightly colored, modified leaves called bracts.

Heliconia danielsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
