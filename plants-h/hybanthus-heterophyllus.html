Hybanthus heterophyllus

Hybanthus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable leaf hybanthus, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from ovate to lanceolate shapes depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hybanthus heterophyllus is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
