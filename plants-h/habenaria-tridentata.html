Habenaria tridentata

Habenaria tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces racemes of small, white or pale pink flowers with three prominent teeth on the lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
