Hechtia ensifolia

Hechtia ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf hechtia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-forming structure with narrow, sword-like leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to support local wildlife.
