Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. pseudocerinthe

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

pseudocerinthe, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain areas.
