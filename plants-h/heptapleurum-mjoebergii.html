Heptapleurum mjoebergii

Heptapleurum mjoebergii, commonly known as the African holly, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

Heptapleurum mjoebergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
