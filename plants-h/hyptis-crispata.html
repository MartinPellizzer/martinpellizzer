Hyptis crispata

Hyptis crispata, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, and its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Hyptis crispata is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
