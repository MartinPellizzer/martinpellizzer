Hedyotis acuminata f. ovalifolia

Hedyotis acuminata f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf dotweed, is a variant of the species Hedyotis acuminata, belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies or in wetlands. Its leaves are usually arranged opposite each other along the stem, and the flowers are often accompanied by small, leaf-like bracts.

This fern-like plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
