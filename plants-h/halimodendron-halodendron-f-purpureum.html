Halimodendron halodendron f. purpureum

Halimodendron halodendron f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered halimodendron, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and clusters of small, showy purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and resilience in harsh environments.
