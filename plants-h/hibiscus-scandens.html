Hibiscus scandens

Hibiscus scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical vine characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus scandens thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ability to climb structures.
