Helichrysum galbanum

Helichrysum galbanum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns, which give it its common name. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Helichrysum galbanum is also known for its hardy nature and ability to survive in arid conditions.
