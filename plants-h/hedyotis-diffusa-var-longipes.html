Hedyotis diffusa var. longipes

Hedyotis diffusa var. longipes, commonly known as the hairy buttonweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
