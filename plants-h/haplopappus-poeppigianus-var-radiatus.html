Haplopappus poeppigianus var. radiatus

Haplopappus poeppigianus var. radiatus, commonly known as the radiate pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive pappus structures. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in a radiate pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The variety radiatus is distinguished by its unique pappus morphology, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.
