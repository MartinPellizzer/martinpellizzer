Hydrophyllum occidentale var. watsonii

Hydrophyllum occidentale var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its blue to purple flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma.
