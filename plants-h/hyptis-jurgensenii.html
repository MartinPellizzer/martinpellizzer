Hyptis jurgensenii

Hyptis jurgensenii, commonly known as Jurgensen's hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features aromatic, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.

Hyptis jurgensenii is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
