Helichrysum mussae

Helichrysum mussae, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, papery flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Helichrysum mussae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
