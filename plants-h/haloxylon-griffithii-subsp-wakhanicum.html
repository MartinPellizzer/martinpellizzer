Haloxylon griffithii subsp. wakhanicum

Haloxylon griffithii subsp.

wakhanicum, commonly known as the Wakhan haloxylon, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems and reduced leaves, which are adapted to minimize water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by small, hard pods containing seeds. It is well-suited for desert conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other subspecies of Haloxylon.
