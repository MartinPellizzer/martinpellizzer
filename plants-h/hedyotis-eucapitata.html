Hedyotis eucapitata

Hedyotis eucapitata, commonly known as the small-headed knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, white or pinkish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several tiny seeds.

Hedyotis eucapitata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
