Hieracium cuspidifrons

Hieracium cuspidifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disk florets, typical of the Compositae family. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, and the plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium cuspidifrons is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
