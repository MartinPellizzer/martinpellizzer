Hosackia flexuosa

Hosackia flexuosa, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow along the ground or in a sprawling manner. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a soft, hairy texture.

Hosackia flexuosa is often found in moist, open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
