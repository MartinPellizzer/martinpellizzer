Hieracium murorum subsp. austrosilvularum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

austrosilvularum, commonly known as the Austrian wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
