Haplopappus armerioides

Haplopappus armerioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, featuring narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers. It produces achenes (small, dry fruits) that are surrounded by a pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

Haplopappus armerioides is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to low-nutrient conditions.
