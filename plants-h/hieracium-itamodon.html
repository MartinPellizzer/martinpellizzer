Hieracium itamodon

Hieracium itamodon, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium itamodon is often found in temperate climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
