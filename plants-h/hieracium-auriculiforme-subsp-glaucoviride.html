Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. glaucoviride

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

glaucoviride, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
