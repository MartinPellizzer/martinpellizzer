Hydnophytum brachycladum

Hydnophytum brachycladum, commonly known as the climbing bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing cyanobacteria, which provides it with essential nutrients. Its morphology includes a rhizome with adventitious roots and leaf-like structures that aid in buoyancy.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
