Habenaria imerinensis

Habenaria imerinensis, commonly known as the Malagasy orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
