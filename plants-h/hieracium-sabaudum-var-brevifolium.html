Hieracium sabaudum var. brevifolium

Hieracium sabaudum var. brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically short and broad, with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many regions of Europe.
