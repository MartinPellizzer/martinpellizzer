Hieracium galacteum

Hieracium galacteum, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and distinctive purple to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains a milky sap, which is a key feature of its common name. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family.

Hieracium galacteum is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
