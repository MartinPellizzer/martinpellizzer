Halimocnemis crassifolia

Halimocnemis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Halimocnemis crassifolia is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
