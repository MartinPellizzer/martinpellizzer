Hesperis calycina

Hesperis calycina, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, with four petals arranged in a cross shape. Hesperis calycina is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs.
