Hieracium vulgatum subsp. cruentifrons

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

cruentifrons, commonly known as the red-flowered hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow to orange composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its striking red or pinkish bracts that surround the flower heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate climates.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.
