Hydriastele biakensis

Hydriastele biakensis, commonly known as the Biakensis water fern, is a rare aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Hydriastereaceae and is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy layer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering impurities. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring small, spore-producing organs that help in its propagation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
