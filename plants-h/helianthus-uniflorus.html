Helianthus uniflorus

Helianthus uniflorus, commonly known as the singleflower sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its single, large, yellow flower head surrounded by green bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces a showy, composite flower that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus uniflorus is often found in open woods, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
