Hyoscarpus niger

Hyoscarpus niger, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically small, yellowish-green with a distinctive calyx. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to black as it matures.

It contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
