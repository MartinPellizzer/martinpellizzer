Hieracium pannonicum subsp. arvense

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

arvense, commonly known as the field hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can form dense colonies. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered with stiff hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to environmental stress.
