Heliotropium messerschmidioides var. angustifolium

Heliotropium messerschmidioides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
