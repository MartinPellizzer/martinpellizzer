Homalomena trapezifolia

Homalomena trapezifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically bright green with striking red or purple veins. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.
