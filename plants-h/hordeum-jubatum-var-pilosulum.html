Hordeum jubatum var. pilosulum

Hordeum jubatum var. pilosulum, commonly known as the silky barley, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, silky-haired inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

While not widely cultivated for grain, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
