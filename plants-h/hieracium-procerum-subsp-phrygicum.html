Hieracium procerum subsp. phrygicum

Hieracium procerum subsp.

phrygicum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
