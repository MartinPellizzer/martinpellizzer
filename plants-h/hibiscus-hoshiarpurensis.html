Hibiscus hoshiarpurensis

Hibiscus hoshiarpurensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in cool, humid climates. Hibiscus hoshiarpurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.
