Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudocrassiceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudocrassiceps, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, with a typical composite structure consisting of ray florets and disk florets.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
