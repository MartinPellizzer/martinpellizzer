Huperzia acuminata

Huperzia acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, scale-like leaves that are clustered at the base. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobili, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. Huperzia acuminata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops.

Its morphology reflects its evolutionary history as a member of the lycopod group, which dates back to the Paleozoic era.
