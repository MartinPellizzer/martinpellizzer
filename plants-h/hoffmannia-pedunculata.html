Hoffmannia pedunculata

Hoffmannia pedunculata, commonly known as the desert hoffmannia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, exhibiting drought tolerance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Hoffmannia pedunculata plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
