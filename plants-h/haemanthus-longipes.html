Haemanthus longipes

Haemanthus longipes, commonly known as the Natal grassbladder, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of bright red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
