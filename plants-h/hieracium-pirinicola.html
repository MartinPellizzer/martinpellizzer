Hieracium pirinicola

Hieracium pirinicola, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
