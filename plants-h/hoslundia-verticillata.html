Hoslundia verticillata

Hoslundia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, stout stem with opposite or whorled leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hoslundia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
