Heliotropium steudneri subsp. bullatum

Heliotropium steudneri subsp.

bullatum, commonly known as the bullate heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
