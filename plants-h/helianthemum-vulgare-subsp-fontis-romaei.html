Helianthemum vulgare subsp. fontis-romaei

Helianthemum vulgare subsp.

fontis-romaei, commonly known as the Roman rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, sunny environments.
