Hoarea intertexta

Hoarea intertexta, commonly known as the mountain holly or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. The plant features small, dark green leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Hoarea intertexta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens in Mediterranean climates.
