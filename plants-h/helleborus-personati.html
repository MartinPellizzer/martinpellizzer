Helleborus personati

Helleborus personati, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features dark green, waxy, palmately lobed leaves that are typically evergreen. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers with a distinctive, hooded shape, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a slow growth rate.

Helleborus personati is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
