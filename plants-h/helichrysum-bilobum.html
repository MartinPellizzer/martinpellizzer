Helichrysum bilobum

Helichrysum bilobum, commonly known as the double-lobed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is aromatic and can be used in herbal remedies, while its flowers are popular in traditional medicine.

Helichrysum bilobum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
