Hibiscus anonimus

Hibiscus anonimus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus anonimus thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
