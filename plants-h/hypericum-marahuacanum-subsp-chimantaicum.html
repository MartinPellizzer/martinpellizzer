Hypericum marahuacanum subsp. chimantaicum

Hypericum marahuacanum subsp.

chimantaicum, commonly known as the Chimanta golden St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the Amazonian regions of South America. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, preferring well-drained soil. It produces small, orange-yellow berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
