Hieracium calophylloides

Hieracium calophylloides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium calophylloides is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
