Hemisphaera ruprechtii

Hemisphaera ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's hemisphaera, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hemispherical rosette of leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among many bromeliads. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in cloud forests and is considered a rare and endangered species.
