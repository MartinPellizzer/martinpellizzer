Heteropappus ciliosus

Heteropappus ciliosus, commonly known as the fringed pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a head, surrounded by a pappus of bristles that aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Heteropappus ciliosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sandy or gravelly habitats.
