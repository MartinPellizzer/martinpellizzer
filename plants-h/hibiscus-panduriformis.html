Hibiscus panduriformis

Hibiscus panduriformis, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus panduriformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
