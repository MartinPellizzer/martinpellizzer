Hemionitis allosuroides

Hemionitis allosuroides, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique vascular plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has reduced leaves that are covered in a thick layer of trichomes, which help reduce water loss. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in specialized leaf axils.

This species is notable for its adaptations to extreme drought conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
