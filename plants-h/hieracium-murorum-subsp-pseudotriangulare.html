Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudotriangulare

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudotriangulare, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is distinguished by its triangular-shaped flower heads, which give it its common name.
