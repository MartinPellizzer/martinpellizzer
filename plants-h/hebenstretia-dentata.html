Hebenstretia dentata

Hebenstretia dentata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

Hebenstretia dentata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
