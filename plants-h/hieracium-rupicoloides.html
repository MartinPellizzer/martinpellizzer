Hieracium rupicoloides

Hieracium rupicoloides, commonly known as the rock hare's foot, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium rupicoloides is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its adaptability to harsh environments.
