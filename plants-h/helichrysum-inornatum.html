Helichrysum inornatum

Helichrysum inornatum, commonly known as the common everlasting.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, belonging to the Asteraceae family. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that remain intact even after drying, giving it the common name. Its foliage is typically narrow and linear, with a silvery-gray appearance due to fine hairs.

Helichrysum inornatum is often used in floral arrangements and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
