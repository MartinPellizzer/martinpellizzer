Hibiscus esculentus var. zhukovskyi

Hibiscus esculentus var. zhukovskyi, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a variant of the okra plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and edible, fibrous pods that develop from its flowers. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
