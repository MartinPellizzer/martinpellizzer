Hornstedtia reticulata

Hornstedtia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, net-like venation pattern on its leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
