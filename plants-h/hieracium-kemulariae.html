Hieracium kemulariae

Hieracium kemulariae, commonly known as the Kemularia hawkweed, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-colored flowers and hairy, often pubescent leaves. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological features and ecological adaptations.
