Hieracium murorum subsp. maranicum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

maranicum, commonly known as the Maranica hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and shorter peduncles compared to other members of the species.
