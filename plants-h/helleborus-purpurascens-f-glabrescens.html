Helleborus purpurascens f. glabrescens

Helleborus purpurascens f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous hellebore, is a variant of the Christmas rose with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which set it apart from the typical hairy foliage of the species. The plant produces striking, nodding flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a greenish tinge. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is native to parts of Europe.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
