Hypericum chamaecaulon

Hypericum chamaecaulon, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. The plant has a woody stem that spreads horizontally, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hypericum chamaecaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
