Heteromorpha arborescens

Heteromorpha arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to grow in full sun to partial shade.
