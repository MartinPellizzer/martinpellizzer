Heteranthera virginicus

Heteranthera virginicus, commonly known as narrowleaf water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Heteranthera virginicus has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
