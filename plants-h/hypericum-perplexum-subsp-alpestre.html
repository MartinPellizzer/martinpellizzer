Hypericum perplexum subsp. alpestre

Hypericum perplexum subsp.

alpestre, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold and windy conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
