Homalium villarianum

Homalium villarianum, commonly known as the red-flowered huito, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, red flowers that are followed by orange or yellow berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
