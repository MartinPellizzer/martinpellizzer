Hosackia crassifolia

Hosackia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces spikes of violet to blue flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical significance also lies in its role as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
