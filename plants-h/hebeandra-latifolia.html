Hebeandra latifolia

Hebeandra latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hebe, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or blue during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hebeandra latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
