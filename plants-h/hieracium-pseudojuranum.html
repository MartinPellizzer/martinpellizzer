Hieracium pseudojuranum

Hieracium pseudojuranum, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pseudojuranum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
