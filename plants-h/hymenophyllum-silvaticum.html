Hymenophyllum silvaticum

Hymenophyllum silvaticum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, silvery-green fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum silvaticum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to biodiversity and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
