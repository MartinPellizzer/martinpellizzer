Hydrangea angulata

Hydrangea angulata, commonly known as the zigzag hydrangea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging stems and large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is usually dark green with a leathery texture, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer.

Hydrangea angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
