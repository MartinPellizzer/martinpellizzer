Heliophila virgata var. integrifolia

Heliophila virgata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth heliophila, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy areas in Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall, and its leaves are lance-shaped with smooth margins. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Heliophila virgata var. integrifolia is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
