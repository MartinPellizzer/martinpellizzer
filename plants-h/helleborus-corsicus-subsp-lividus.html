Helleborus corsicus subsp. lividus

Helleborus corsicus subsp.

lividus, commonly known as the Corsican helleborus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and is well adapted to mild, humid climates. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

The subspecies lividus is distinguished by its more intense coloration and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Helleborus corsicus.
