Hibiscus caronopifolius

Hibiscus caronopifolius, commonly known as the Caribbean hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red, with five to seven petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus caronopifolius thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and near water sources.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
