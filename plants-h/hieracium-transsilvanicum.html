Hieracium transsilvanicum

Hieracium transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing yellow daisy-like flowers.

Hieracium transsilvanicum is noted for its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which help it retain moisture and withstand environmental stress.
