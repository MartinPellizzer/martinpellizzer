Homalanthus agallochoides

Homalanthus agallochoides, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
