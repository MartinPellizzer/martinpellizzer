Hydrangea opuloides var. variegata

Hydrangea opuloides var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated panicled hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy panicles of white to pink flowers that can change color depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of white, green, and sometimes pink margins. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to add texture and color to gardens.
