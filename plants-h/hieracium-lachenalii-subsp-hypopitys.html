Hieracium lachenalii subsp. hypopitys

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

hypopitys, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
