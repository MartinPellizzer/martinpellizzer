Hieracium adhalsicum

Hieracium adhalsicum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often hairy as well.

Hieracium adhalsicum is a perennial plant that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
