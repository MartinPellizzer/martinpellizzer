Hieracium ramondii subsp. chloroides

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

chloroides, commonly known as the green-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads and hairy, often greenish stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a relatively small plant, often reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters.
