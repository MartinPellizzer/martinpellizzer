Habenaria st-simonenis

Habenaria st-simonenis, commonly known as the Simeon's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The genus Habenaria is known for its diverse range of species, many of which are prized for their ornamental value.
