Henlea rosea

Henlea rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or purple blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Henlea rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
