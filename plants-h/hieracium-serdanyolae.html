Hieracium serdanyolae

Hieracium serdanyolae, commonly known as the Serdanya hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balearic Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands. It has basal leaves that are usually lanceolate and hairy, with a height ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
