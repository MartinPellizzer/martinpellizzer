Hieracium bulgaricum

Hieracium bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Hieracium bulgaricum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
