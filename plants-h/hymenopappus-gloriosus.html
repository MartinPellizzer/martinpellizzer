Hymenopappus gloriosus

Hymenopappus gloriosus, commonly known as the glory flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, surrounded by pale green bracts.

Hymenopappus gloriosus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
