Hyptis sidifolia

Hyptis sidifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its strong, minty scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis sidifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
