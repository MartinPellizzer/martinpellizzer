Hartia villosa var. grandifolia

Hartia villosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf hartsia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
