Hymenachne pernambucensis

Hymenachne pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Hymenachne pernambucensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
