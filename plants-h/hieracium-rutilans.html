Hieracium rutilans

Hieracium rutilans, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures.

Hieracium rutilans is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
