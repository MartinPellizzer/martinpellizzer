Hedera helix proles chrysocarpa

Hedera helix proles chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its striking yellow berries.

This plant is a climbing vine native to Europe and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface. The golden fruits are an ornamental feature, often used in gardens for their visual appeal.

However, it is important to note that the berries are toxic and should not be consumed.
