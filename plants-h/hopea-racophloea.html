Hopea racophloea

Hopea racophloea, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface.

Hopea racophloea plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
