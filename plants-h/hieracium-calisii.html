Hieracium calisii

Hieracium calisii, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium calisii is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
