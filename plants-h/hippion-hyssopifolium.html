Hippion hyssopifolium

Hippion hyssopifolium, commonly known as the hyssop-leaved hippion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hippion hyssopifolium is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
