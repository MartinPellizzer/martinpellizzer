Hoffmannia triosteoides subsp. costensis

Hoffmannia triosteoides subsp.

costensis, commonly known as the Costa's hoffmannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, calcareous soils.

It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
