Hieracium hyperpiletum

Hieracium hyperpiletum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces numerous small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium hyperpiletum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
