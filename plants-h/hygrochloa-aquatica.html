Hygrochloa aquatica

Hygrochloa aquatica, commonly known as water grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of freshwater bodies. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Hygrochloa aquatica is well adapted to moist conditions, with a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in saturated soils.
