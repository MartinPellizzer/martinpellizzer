Hypericum peplidifolium f. robustum

Hypericum peplidifolium f. robustum, commonly known as the robust hypericum, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and somewhat branched. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
