Hieracium crossotum

Hieracium crossotum, commonly known as the crossleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the petals.

Hieracium crossotum is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
