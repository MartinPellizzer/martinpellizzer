Hypericum patulum var. henryi

Hypericum patulum var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.
