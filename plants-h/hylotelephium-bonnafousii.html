Hylotelephium bonnafousii

Hylotelephium bonnafousii, commonly known as the Armenian stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and thick, waxy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticulture.
