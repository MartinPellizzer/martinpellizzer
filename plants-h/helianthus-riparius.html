Helianthus riparius

Helianthus riparius, commonly known as the riparian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, yellow composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows near waterways and is adapted to moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Helianthus riparius plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
