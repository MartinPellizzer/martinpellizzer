Hoehnea grandiflora

Hoehnea grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, yellowish drupe with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
