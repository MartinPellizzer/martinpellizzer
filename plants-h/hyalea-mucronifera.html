Hyalea mucronifera

Hyalea mucronifera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in nutrient-poor water environments, supplementing its diet with captured insects.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
