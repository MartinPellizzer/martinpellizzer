Hieracium acroleucum var. macrocentrum

Hieracium acroleucum var. macrocentrum, commonly known as the large-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
