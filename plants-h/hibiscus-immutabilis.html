Hibiscus immutabilis

Hibiscus immutabilis, commonly known as the everlasting hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that remain open for several days, often displaying vibrant red or pink hues. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that are typically 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white downy layer.

Hibiscus immutabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
