Helichrysum capitellatum

Helichrysum capitellatum, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum capitellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
