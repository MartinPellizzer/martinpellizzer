Hahnia aria var. edulis

Hahnia aria var. edulis, commonly known as the edible hahnia, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its edible leaves are often consumed raw or cooked, valued for their mild, nutty flavor.

Due to its drought tolerance and nutritional value, it is gaining attention as a potential crop for sustainable agriculture in dry regions.
