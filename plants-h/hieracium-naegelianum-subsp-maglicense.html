Hieracium naegelianum subsp. maglicense

Hieracium naegelianum subsp.

maglicense, commonly known as the Magicense hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite inflorescence.

It is a low-growing plant that often forms dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
