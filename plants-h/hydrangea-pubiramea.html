Hydrangea pubiramea

Hydrangea pubiramea, commonly known as the roughleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Hydrangea pubiramea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide seasonal interest throughout the growing season.
