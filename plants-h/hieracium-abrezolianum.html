Hieracium abrezolianum

Hieracium abrezolianum, commonly known as the Alpina hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium abrezolianum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
