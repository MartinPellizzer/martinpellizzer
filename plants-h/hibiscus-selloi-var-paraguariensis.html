Hibiscus selloi var. paraguariensis

Hibiscus selloi var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Hibiscus selloi var. paraguariensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
