Hieracium chloropsis subsp. gnaphalodes

Hieracium chloropsis subsp.

gnaphalodes, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
