Hakea ceratocarpa

Hakea ceratocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikes of bright red flowers that appear in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Hakea ceratocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
