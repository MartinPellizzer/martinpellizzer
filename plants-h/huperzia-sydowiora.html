Huperzia sydowiora

Huperzia sydowiora, commonly known as the sydow's huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, compact clusters. The plant produces sporophytes with distinctive, elongated spore capsules that are often clustered at the tips of the shoots. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Huperzia sydowiora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
