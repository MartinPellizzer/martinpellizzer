Hypericum maculatum subsp. maculatum

Hypericum maculatum subsp.

maculatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its yellow flowers with red markings and oval, dark green leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, red-orange berries that are dispersed by birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
