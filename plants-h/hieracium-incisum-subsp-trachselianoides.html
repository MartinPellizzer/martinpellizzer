Hieracium incisum subsp. trachselianoides

Hieracium incisum subsp.

trachselianoides, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower head surrounded by bracts.

It is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
