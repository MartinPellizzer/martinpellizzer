Hakea rogersoniana

Hakea rogersoniana, commonly known as the Rogerson's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, bright yellow flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
