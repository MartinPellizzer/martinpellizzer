Hieracium goppollense

Hieracium goppollense, commonly known as the Goppollense hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium goppollense is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
