Hordelymus caput-medusae subsp. asper

Hordelymus caput-medusae subsp.

asper, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a striking plant characterized by its unique, spiky, yellow-orange flowers that resemble a horned medusa. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The plant features a branching stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
