Hemipilia crenulata

Hemipilia crenulata, commonly known as the crested lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that resemble lilies. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has elongated, strap-like leaves. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip and a column, typical of orchids.

Hemipilia crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
