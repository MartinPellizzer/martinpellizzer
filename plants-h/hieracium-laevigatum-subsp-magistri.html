Hieracium laevigatum subsp. magistri

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

magistri, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its characteristic composite flower heads.
