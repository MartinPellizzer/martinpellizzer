Hebeclinium frontinoense

Hebeclinium frontinoense, commonly known as the Frontino hebeclinium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pale blue to violet in color and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
