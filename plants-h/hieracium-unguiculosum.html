Hieracium unguiculosum

Hieracium unguiculosum, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium unguiculosum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
