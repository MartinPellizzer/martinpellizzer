Hieracium gastonianum subsp. cantabrimontanum

Hieracium gastonianum subsp.

cantabrimontanum, commonly known as the Cantabrian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has fibrous roots and exhibits a compact growth habit with hairy stems.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits adapted to its native environment.
