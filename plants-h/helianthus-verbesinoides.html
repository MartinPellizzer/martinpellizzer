Helianthus verbesinoides

Helianthus verbesinoides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with sturdy, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
