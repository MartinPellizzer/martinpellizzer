Hieracium cirritum subsp. brentanum

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

brentanum, commonly known as the Brentanum Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in composite inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
