Hymenophyllum elegantulum

Hymenophyllum elegantulum, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a small, delicate epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds with a silvery appearance due to the presence of minute, translucent scales on its leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum elegantulum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
