Hieracium morisianum subsp. comigerum

Hieracium morisianum subsp.

comigerum, commonly known as the Morisian hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers with ray florets.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers typically bloom from June to August.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
