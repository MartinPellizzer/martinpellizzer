Hieracium glanduliferum subsp. leucopsis

Hieracium glanduliferum subsp.

leucopsis, commonly known as the white-rayed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, with white rays that distinguish it from other hawkweed species. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its subspecific name, *leucopsis*, refers to its distinctive white flower rays.
