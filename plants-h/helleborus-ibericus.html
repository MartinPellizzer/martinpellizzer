Helleborus ibericus

Helleborus ibericus, commonly known as the Iberian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are often adorned with wavy margins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, dark purple to black flowers during late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Helleborus ibericus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
