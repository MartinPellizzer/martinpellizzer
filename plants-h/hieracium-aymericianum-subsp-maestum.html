Hieracium aymericianum subsp. maestum

Hieracium aymericianum subsp.

maestum, commonly known as the Maestum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a hairy texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.
