Helichrysum oxyphyllum

Helichrysum oxyphyllum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that remain intact for a long time after blooming, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum oxyphyllum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
