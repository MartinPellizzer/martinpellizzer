Helianthemum arenicola

Helianthemum arenicola, commonly known as the sandstone sunflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with harsh climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Helianthemum arenicola is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
