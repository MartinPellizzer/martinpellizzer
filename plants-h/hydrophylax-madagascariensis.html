Hydrophylax madagascariensis

Hydrophylax madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a distinct floral structure with showy, white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
