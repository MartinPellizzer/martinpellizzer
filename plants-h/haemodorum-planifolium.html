Haemodorum planifolium

Haemodorum planifolium, commonly known as the flat-leaved everlasting.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern coast of Australia, characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in spring, which are borne on tall, slender stalks. Its leaves and flowers contain alkaloids, contributing to its use in traditional Indigenous medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and wetlands.
