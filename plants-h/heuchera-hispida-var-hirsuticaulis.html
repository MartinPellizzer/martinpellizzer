Heuchera hispida var. hirsuticaulis

Heuchera hispida var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed alumroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
