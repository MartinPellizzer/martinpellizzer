Hypertelis salsoloides

Hypertelis salsoloides, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and can tolerate high levels of coastal wind and salt spray.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
