Hamiltonia mysorensis

Hamiltonia mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore Hamiltonia, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive pinkish throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged spirally along the stem.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
