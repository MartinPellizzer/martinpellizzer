Hieracium atratum subsp. gudbergii

Hieracium atratum subsp.

gudbergii, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often hairy. This subspecies is adapted to a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and visually distinctive within its native range.
