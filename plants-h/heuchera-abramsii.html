Heuchera abramsii

Heuchera abramsii, commonly known as Abrams' coral bells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, mounding foliage that ranges from deep green to red or purple hues, depending on the variety. The plant produces upright spikes of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or red during the summer months. It prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
