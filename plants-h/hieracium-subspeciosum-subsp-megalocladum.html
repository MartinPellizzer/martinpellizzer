Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. megalocladum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

megalocladum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, branched stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
