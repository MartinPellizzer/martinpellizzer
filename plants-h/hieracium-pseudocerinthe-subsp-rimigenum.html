Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp. rimigenum

Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp.

rimigenum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and woodland edges across Europe. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
