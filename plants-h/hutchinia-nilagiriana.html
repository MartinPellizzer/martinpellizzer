Hutchinia nilagiriana

Hutchinia nilagiriana, commonly known as the Nilagiri hutchinia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is rough and fissured.

Hutchinia nilagiriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
