Hieracium murorum subsp. glaucoprasinum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

glaucoprasinum, commonly known as the glaucous yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow with five petals arranged in a single inflorescence. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glaucous (waxy) coating and pale yellow flowers.
