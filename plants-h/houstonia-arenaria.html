Houstonia arenaria

Houstonia arenaria, commonly known as sand bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant produces small, sky-blue to white flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes and pine savannas.

Houstonia arenaria is adapted to full sun and is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
