Hakea raybrownii

Hakea raybrownii, commonly known as Raybrown's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces striking, urn-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Hakea raybrownii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
