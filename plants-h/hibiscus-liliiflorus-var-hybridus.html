Hibiscus liliiflorus var. hybridus

Hibiscus liliiflorus var. hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowering period occurs primarily during the summer months, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
