Hordeum halophilum

Hordeum halophilum, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in high-salinity soils, making it an important plant in saline ecosystems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Hordeum halophilum plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in coastal and desert regions.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
