Haplopappus canescens

Haplopappus canescens, commonly known as the rough pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
