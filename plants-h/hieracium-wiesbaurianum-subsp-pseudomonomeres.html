Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. pseudomonomeres

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

pseudomonomeres, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver- or gray-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It is often found in dry grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
