Habenaria intermedia var. arietina

Habenaria intermedia var. arietina, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually borne in racemes and have three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being modified into a lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
