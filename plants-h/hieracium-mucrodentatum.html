Hieracium mucrodentatum

Hieracium mucrodentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium mucrodentatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
