Hieracium apricorum subsp. sublevigatum

Hieracium apricorum subsp.

sublevigatum, commonly known as the slender sand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
