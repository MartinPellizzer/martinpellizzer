Hieracium mirantesianum

Hieracium mirantesianum, commonly known as the Mirantian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium mirantesianum is often found in regions with temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
