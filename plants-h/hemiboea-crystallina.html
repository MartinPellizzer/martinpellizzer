Hemiboea crystallina

Hemiboea crystallina, commonly known as the crystalline hemiboea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Hemiboea crystallina is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
