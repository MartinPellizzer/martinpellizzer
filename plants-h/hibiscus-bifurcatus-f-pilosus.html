Hibiscus bifurcatus f. pilosus

Hibiscus bifurcatus f. pilosus, commonly known as the fuzzy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features palmate leaves with a hairy or fuzzy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hibiscus bifurcatus f. pilosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
