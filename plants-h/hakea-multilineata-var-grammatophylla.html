Hakea multilineata var. grammatophylla

Hakea multilineata var. grammatophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically red or pink in color. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
