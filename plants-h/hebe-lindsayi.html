Hebe lindsayi

Hebe lindsayi, commonly known as the Stewart Island hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Hebe lindsayi is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
