Helianthus annuus var. selene

Helianthus annuus var. selene, commonly known as the silver helianthus, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage.

This cultivar features large, showy yellow flower heads with a prominent central disk, typical of the Helianthus genus. It is an annual plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that enhances its ornamental appeal.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
