Hieracium controversum

Hieracium controversum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium controversum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
