Hymenaea confertifolia

Hymenaea confertifolia, commonly known as the "African mahogany," is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces heavy, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and wood have been historically valued for their durable, aromatic properties.

Hymenaea confertifolia is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
