Hieracium tardans subsp. subtardans

Hieracium tardans subsp.

subtardans, commonly known as the late hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
