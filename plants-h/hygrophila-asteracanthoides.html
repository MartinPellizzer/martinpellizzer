Hygrophila asteracanthoides

Hygrophila asteracanthoides, commonly known as the star-spike hygrophila, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble a star. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a reddish or purple tinge.

Hygrophila asteracanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
