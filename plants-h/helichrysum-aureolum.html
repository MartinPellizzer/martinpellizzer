Helichrysum aureolum

Helichrysum aureolum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small suns. The plant is perennial, producing dense clusters of small, daisy-like blooms throughout the growing season. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with finely textured leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Helichrysum aureolum is often used in dried flower arrangements due to its long-lasting, crisp petals.
