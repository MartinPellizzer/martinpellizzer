Hibiscus contortus

Hibiscus contortus, commonly known as the twisted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, twisted stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a velvety texture.

Hibiscus contortus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid climates.
