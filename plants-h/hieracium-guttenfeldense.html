Hieracium guttenfeldense

Hieracium guttenfeldense, commonly known as the Guttenfeld hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium guttenfeldense is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in forested areas.
