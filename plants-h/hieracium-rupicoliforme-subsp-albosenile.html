Hieracium rupicoliforme subsp. albosenile

Hieracium rupicoliforme subsp.

albosenile, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows. It has a rosette growth form, with basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

Due to its habitat, it is adapted to cold climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
