Heptapleurum acuminatissimum

Heptapleurum acuminatissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stone pine, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
