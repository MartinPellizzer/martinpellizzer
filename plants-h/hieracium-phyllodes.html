Hieracium phyllodes

Hieracium phyllodes, commonly known as the leafy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of lobed, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
