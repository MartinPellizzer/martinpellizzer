Hibiscus syriacus var. totoalbus

Hibiscus syriacus var. totoalbus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a pale green underside. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking white blooms and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
