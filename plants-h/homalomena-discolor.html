Homalomena discolor

Homalomena discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Caladium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant patterns of white, pink, and green. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often hidden beneath the foliage, while the ornamental value lies primarily in its foliage.

It prefers warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
