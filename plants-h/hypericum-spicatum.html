Hypericum spicatum

Hypericum spicatum, commonly known as spiked St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by red or orange berries. Its botanical characteristics include small, oval leaves with a slightly rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Hypericum spicatum is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
