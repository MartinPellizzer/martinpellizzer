Hyptis parkeri

Hyptis parkeri, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis parkeri is also valued as a source of essential oils and has been used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
