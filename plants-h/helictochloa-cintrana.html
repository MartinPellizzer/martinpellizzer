Helictochloa cintrana

Helictochloa cintrana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled or folded, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
