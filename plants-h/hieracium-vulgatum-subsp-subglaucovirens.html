Hieracium vulgatum subsp. subglaucovirens

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

subglaucovirens, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, often bluish-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its subspecies name, subglaucovirens, refers to the bluish-green hue of its foliage.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
