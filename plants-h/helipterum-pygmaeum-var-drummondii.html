Helipterum pygmaeum var. drummondii

Helipterum pygmaeum var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's dwarf ironweed, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
