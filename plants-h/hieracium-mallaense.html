Hieracium mallaense

Hieracium mallaense, commonly known as the Malla hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium mallaense is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
