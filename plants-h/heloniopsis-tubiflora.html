Heloniopsis tubiflora

Heloniopsis tubiflora, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The showy, tubular flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
