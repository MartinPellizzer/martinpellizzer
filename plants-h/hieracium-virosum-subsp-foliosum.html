Hieracium virosum subsp. foliosum

Hieracium virosum subsp.

foliosum, commonly known as the leafy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a rough texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in colonies, spreading through rhizomes and forming dense mats.
