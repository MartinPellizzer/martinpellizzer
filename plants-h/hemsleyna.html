Hemsleyna

Hemsleyna, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Hemsleyna thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties make it a valuable plant in both ecological and cultural contexts.
