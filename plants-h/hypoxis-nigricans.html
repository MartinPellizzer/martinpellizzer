Hypoxis nigricans

Hypoxis nigricans, commonly known as African winterbloom, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically from July to September. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to survive in dry conditions by storing water and nutrients.

Hypoxis nigricans is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
