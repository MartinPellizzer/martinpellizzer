Hieracium pseudamaurophyllum

Hieracium pseudamaurophyllum, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
