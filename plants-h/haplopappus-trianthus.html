Haplopappus trianthus

Haplopappus trianthus, commonly known as the three-flowered horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Haplopappus trianthus has fibrous roots and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
