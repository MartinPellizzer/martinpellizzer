Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp. lamyi

Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp.

lamyi, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a serrated margin, while the flower heads are composed of numerous small, daisy-like florets.

This subspecies is often found in high-altitude environments and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
