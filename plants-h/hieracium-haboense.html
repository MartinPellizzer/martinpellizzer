Hieracium haboense

Hieracium haboense, commonly known as the Habor Hairy Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It features yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers, with hairy stems and leaves.

Hieracium haboense is often found in temperate regions and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
