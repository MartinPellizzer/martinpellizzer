Harrisella uniflora

Harrisella uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces a single, slender spikelet at the end of each stem, which contains small, pale florets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists studying grassland ecosystems.
