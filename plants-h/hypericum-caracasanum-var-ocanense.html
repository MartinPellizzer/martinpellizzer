Hypericum caracasanum var. ocanense

Hypericum caracasanum var. ocanense, commonly known as the Ocanese St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. It has small, opposite leaves and produces clusters of four-petaled flowers.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
