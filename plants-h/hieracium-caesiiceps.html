Hieracium caesiiceps

Hieracium caesiiceps, commonly known as the bluehead hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
