Hordeum mandshuricum

Hordeum mandshuricum, commonly known as Manchurian barley, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other barley species and is valued for its potential in ecological and agricultural studies.
