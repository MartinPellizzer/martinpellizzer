Hieracium septentrionale var. simplex

Hieracium septentrionale var. simplex, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a problematic invasive species in some areas.
