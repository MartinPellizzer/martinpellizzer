Hortensia rosea

Hortensia rosea, commonly known as the rose-of-Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hortensia rosea is also known for its ability to tolerate urban environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
