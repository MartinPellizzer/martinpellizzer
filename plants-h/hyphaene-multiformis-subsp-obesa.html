Hyphaene multiformis subsp. obesa

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

obesa, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Madagascar. It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, forming dense clumps.

The plant produces large, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear small, white flowers. Its rhizomes are robust and serve as the primary means of vegetative propagation.

It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to seasonal flooding.
