Hesperomeles escalloniifolia

Hesperomeles escalloniifolia, commonly known as the Escalloniifolia huito, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
