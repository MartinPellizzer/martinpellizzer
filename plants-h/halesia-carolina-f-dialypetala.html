Halesia carolina f. dialypetala

Halesia carolina f. dialypetala, commonly known as the Carolina halesia or white halesia, is a rare and distinctive variant of the halesia species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and unique floral structure, featuring showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and striking floral display, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected natural areas.
