Hieracium pseudopiloselliflorum

Hieracium pseudopiloselliflorum, commonly known as the false hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
