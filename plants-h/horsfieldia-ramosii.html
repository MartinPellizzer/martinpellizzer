Horsfieldia ramosii

Horsfieldia ramosii, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
