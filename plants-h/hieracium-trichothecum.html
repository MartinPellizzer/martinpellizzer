Hieracium trichothecum

Hieracium trichothecum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Hieracium trichothecum is also known for producing toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock.
