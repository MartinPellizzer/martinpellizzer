Hasteola robusta

Hasteola robusta, commonly known as the robust wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open fields, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Hasteola robusta is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive growth and adaptability.
