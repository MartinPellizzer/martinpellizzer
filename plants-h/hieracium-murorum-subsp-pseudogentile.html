Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudogentile

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudogentile, commonly known as the false gentile hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is often confused with other hawkweeds due to its similar appearance.
