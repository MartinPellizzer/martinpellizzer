Heisteria filiformis

Heisteria filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf heister, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its delicate appearance and vibrant blooms make it a desirable addition to cultivated landscapes.
