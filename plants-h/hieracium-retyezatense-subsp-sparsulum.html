Hieracium retyezatense subsp. sparsulum

Hieracium retyezatense subsp.

sparsulum, commonly known as the sparse hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

It is a relatively rare species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
