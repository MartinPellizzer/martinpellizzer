Hedyotis montana

Hedyotis montana, commonly known as mountain peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Hedyotis montana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
