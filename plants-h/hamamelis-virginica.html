Hamamelis virginica

Hamamelis virginica, commonly known as the common witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is reddish-brown and smooth, often peeling in thin strips.

Hamamelis virginica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
