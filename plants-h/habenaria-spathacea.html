Habenaria spathacea

Habenaria spathacea, commonly known as the saddle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive saddle-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on decaying wood or in the understorey. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Habenaria spathacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
