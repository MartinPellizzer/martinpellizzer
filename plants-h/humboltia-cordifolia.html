Humboltia cordifolia

Humboltia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf humboldtia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in coastal climates. Humboltia cordifolia is often found in areas with mild temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in coastal botany and ecological studies.
