Hibiscus matogrossensis

Hibiscus matogrossensis, commonly known as the Matogrossense hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.

Hibiscus matogrossensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
