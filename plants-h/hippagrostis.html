Hippagrostis

Hippagrostis, commonly known as bluestem or blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their slender, erect stems and small, often blue or purple flowers. They are typically found in dry, disturbed soils and are adapted to arid environments. Hippagrostis species are often used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

The genus includes several species, such as Hippagrostis phrygiana, which is native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.
