Hyptis scandens

Hyptis scandens, commonly known as creeping hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in a spreading or trailing manner, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and usually purple or violet in color.

Hyptis scandens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
