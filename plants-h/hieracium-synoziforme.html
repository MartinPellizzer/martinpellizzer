Hieracium synoziforme

Hieracium synoziforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, often with a reddish or purple hue at the base.

Hieracium synoziforme is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
