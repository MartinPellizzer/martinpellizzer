Heteropterys laurifolia var. mexicana

Heteropterys laurifolia var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental horticulture and ecological restoration.
