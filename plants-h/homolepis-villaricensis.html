Homolepis villaricensis

Homolepis villaricensis, commonly known as the Villarica fern, is a rare and endemic species found in the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like fronds that are often adorned with tiny, spore-producing structures called sorangia. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture for optimal growth. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists studying biodiversity in tropical regions.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to its vulnerability to habitat loss and climate change.
