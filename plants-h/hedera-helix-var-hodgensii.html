Hedera helix var. hodgensii

Hedera helix var. hodgensii, commonly known as the Hodgens' ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its unique foliage.

This plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically larger and more elongated compared to standard ivy varieties. The leaves are usually a deep green with a velvety texture, and they often have a distinctive waxy coating. It is a climbing plant that thrives in shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive.
