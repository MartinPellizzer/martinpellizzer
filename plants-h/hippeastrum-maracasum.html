Hippeastrum maracasum

Hippeastrum maracasum, commonly known as the Maracasum amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Hippeastrum maracasum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
