Hypericum fauriei

Hypericum fauriei, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Hypericum fauriei is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
