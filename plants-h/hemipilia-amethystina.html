Hemipilia amethystina

Hemipilia amethystina, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its inflorescence bears clusters of fragrant, showy blooms.

Hemipilia amethystina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
