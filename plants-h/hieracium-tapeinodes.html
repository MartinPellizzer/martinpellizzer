Hieracium tapeinodes

Hieracium tapeinodes, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

Hieracium tapeinodes is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
