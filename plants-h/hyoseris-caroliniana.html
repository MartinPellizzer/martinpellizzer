Hyoseris caroliniana

Hyoseris caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
