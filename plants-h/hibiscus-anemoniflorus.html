Hibiscus anemoniflorus

Hibiscus anemoniflorus, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. Hibiscus anemoniflorus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
