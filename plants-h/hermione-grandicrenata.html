Hermione grandicrenata

Hermione grandicrenata, commonly known as the grand crested fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ornate leaves that are often divided into numerous segments, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
