Hosta longipes f. hypoglauca

Hosta longipes f. hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-green hosta, is a cultivar of the Hosta genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that display a pale green coloration, often with a silvery sheen. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers in late summer, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is prized for its ornamental foliage.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and suitable for garden use in woodland or shaded borders.
