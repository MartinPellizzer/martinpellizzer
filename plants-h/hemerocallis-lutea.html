Hemerocallis lutea

Hemerocallis lutea, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces orange-colored seed pods that mature in the fall, containing numerous small seeds. Hemerocallis lutea is a member of the Hemerocallidaceae family and is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
