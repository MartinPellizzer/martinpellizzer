Hibiscus vidalianus

Hibiscus vidalianus, commonly known as the Vidal's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Hibiscus vidalianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
