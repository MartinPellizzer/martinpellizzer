Hieracium murorum subsp. morulum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

morulum, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, hairy leaves that resemble mouse ears.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
