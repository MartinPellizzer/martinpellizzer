Hieracium pedosum

Hieracium pedosum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has hairy stems and is typically perennial, with a lifespan of several years.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
