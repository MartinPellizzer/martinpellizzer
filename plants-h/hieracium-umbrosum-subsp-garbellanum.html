Hieracium umbrosum subsp. garbellanum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

garbellanum, commonly known as the shadow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
