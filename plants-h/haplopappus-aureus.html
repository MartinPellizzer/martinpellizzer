Haplopappus aureus

Haplopappus aureus, commonly known as golden pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its yellow flowers are followed by a fluffy, wind-dispersed seed head.

Haplopappus aureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
