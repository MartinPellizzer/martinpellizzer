Hesperantha angustifolia

Hesperantha angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a unique tubular shape that attracts specific pollinators. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
