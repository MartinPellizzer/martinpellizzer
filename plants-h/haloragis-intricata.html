Haloragis intricata

Haloragis intricata, commonly known as the intricate willowherb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often have a hairy texture.

Haloragis intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a useful plant in water gardens and ecological restoration projects.
