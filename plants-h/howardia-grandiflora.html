Howardia grandiflora

Howardia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame plant, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Howardia grandiflora is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
