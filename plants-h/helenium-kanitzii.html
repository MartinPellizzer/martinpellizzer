Helenium kanitzii

Helenium kanitzii, commonly known as the Kanitz's sneezeweed, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant orange to red ray flowers with yellow disc flowers in the fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairie habitats.

Helenium kanitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
