Hieracium laetum

Hieracium laetum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium laetum is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
