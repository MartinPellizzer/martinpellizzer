Heliotropium hispidum var. maximum

Heliotropium hispidum var. maximum, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
