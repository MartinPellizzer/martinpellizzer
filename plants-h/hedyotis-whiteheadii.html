Hedyotis whiteheadii

Hedyotis whiteheadii, commonly known as the whitehead's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
