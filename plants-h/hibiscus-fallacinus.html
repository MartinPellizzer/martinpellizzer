Hibiscus fallacinus

Hibiscus fallacinus, commonly known as the Sierra hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus fallacinus is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
