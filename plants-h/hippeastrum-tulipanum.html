Hippeastrum tulipanum

Hippeastrum tulipanum, commonly known as the red tulip flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces bulbous roots and has strap-like, evergreen leaves that emerge from the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Hippeastrum tulipanum is popular for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
