Helminthostachys laciniata

Helminthostachys laciniata, commonly known as the giant waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the blades of a waterwheel, which help it float on the surface of water. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with certain algae, which provide it with nutrients in exchange for shelter. Its roots are often submerged, anchoring it in shallow water bodies.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
