Heuchera americana var. interior

Heuchera americana var. interior, commonly known as the interior alumroot, is a perennial plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that vary in color from silver-green to deep red, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and adaptability to moist, shaded environments contribute to its horticultural value.
