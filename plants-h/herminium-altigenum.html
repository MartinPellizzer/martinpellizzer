Herminium altigenum

Herminium altigenum, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender stem with whorled leaves and produces striking purple or violet flowers in late summer. Its flowers are characterized by their three-lobed petals and a distinctive, spurred lip.

Herminium altigenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
