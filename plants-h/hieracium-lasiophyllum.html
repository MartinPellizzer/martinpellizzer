Hieracium lasiophyllum

Hieracium lasiophyllum, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense, hairy disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a rugged appearance.

Hieracium lasiophyllum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
