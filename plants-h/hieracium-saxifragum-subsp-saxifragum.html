Hieracium saxifragum subsp. saxifragum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

saxifragum, commonly known as the saxifrage hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal flower head with ray florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
