Hakea mantalakifera

Hakea mantalakifera, commonly known as the Mount Mantaraka hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea mantalakifera plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
