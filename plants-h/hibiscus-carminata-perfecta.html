Hibiscus carminata-perfecta

Hibiscus carminata-perfecta, commonly known as the crimson hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, with a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus carminata-perfecta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
