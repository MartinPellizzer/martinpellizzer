Hydnophytum archboldianum

Hydnophytum archboldianum, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Hydnophytum archboldianum is adapted to grow on tree trunks and branches, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for studies on epiphytic plant adaptations.
