Helictotrichon cincinnatum

Helictotrichon cincinnatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
