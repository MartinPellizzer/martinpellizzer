Hieracium casamierense

Hieracium casamierense, commonly known as the Casamier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed.
