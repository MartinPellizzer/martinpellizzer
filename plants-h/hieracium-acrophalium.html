Hieracium acrophalium

Hieracium acrophalium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are narrow and hairy.

Hieracium acrophalium is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
