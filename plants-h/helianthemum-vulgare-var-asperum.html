Helianthemum vulgare var. asperum

Helianthemum vulgare var. asperum, commonly known as rough rockrose, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This hardy species is drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
