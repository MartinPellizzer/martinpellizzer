Heracleum speciosum

Heracleum speciosum, commonly known as cow parsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences composed of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Heracleum speciosum is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants.
