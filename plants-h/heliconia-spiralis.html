Heliconia spiralis

Heliconia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence composed of brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is closely related to ginger and bananas. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a unique structure that attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
