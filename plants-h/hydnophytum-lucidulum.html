Hydnophytum lucidulum

Hydnophytum lucidulum, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical rainforests.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria, which allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered together, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its fleshy, red berries.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in the ecological balance of its forest habitat.
