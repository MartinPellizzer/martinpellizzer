Hesperis laciniata var. paui

Hesperis laciniata var. paui, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to alpine landscapes.

The varietal form 'paui' is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to the typical variety.
