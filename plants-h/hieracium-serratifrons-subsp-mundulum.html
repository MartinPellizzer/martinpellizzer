Hieracium serratifrons subsp. mundulum

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

mundulum, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate and serrated, with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by yellow-orange ray florets.

It is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich habitats.
