Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. dolichellum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

dolichellum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated stem and reduced leaf size compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
