Hedysarum vicioides f. pilosum

Hedysarum vicioides f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy vicia, is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

It is often found in meadows and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
