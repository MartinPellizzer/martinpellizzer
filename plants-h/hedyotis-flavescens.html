Hedyotis flavescens

Hedyotis flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bedstraw, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs. It is known for its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.
