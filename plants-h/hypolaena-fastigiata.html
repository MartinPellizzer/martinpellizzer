Hypolaena fastigiata

Hypolaena fastigiata, commonly known as the tall spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically in late summer to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Hypolaena fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic environments.
