Hypericum japonicum var. kainantense

Hypericum japonicum var. kainantense, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, berry-like capsules. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
