Hosta clavata

Hosta clavata, commonly known as the clustered hosta, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are often marked with creamy or white veins. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple during the summer months. It prefers partial to full shade and well-drained, moist soil.

Hosta clavata is a popular choice for shady garden beds due to its ornamental foliage and moderate care requirements.
