Hieracium heterogynum

Hieracium heterogynum, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and basal leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium heterogynum is known for its variability in leaf and flower morphology, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
