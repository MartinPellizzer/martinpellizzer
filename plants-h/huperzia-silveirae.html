Huperzia silveirae

Huperzia silveirae, commonly known as the Silveira's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen, and leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia silveirae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
