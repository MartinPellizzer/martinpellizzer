Hierochloe orthantha

Hierochloe orthantha, commonly known as slender basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, grass-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, rocky or sandy habitats, often in high-altitude or polar environments. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are adapted to cold climates and short growing seasons.

Due to its specialized habitat, Hierochloe orthantha is considered an indicator species for alpine and tundra ecosystems.
