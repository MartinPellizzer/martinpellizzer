Halesia tetraptera var. parviflora

Halesia tetraptera var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features graceful, pendulous inflorescences that produce clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
