Hieracium pallidum subsp. alfitodes

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

alfitodes, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
