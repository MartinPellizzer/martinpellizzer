Hepetis sprucei

Hepetis sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters and features dense clusters of small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Hepetis sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shady areas.
