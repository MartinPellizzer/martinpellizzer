Homalium ceylanicum subsp. minutiflorum

Homalium ceylanicum subsp.

minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered evergreen tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
