Hieracium impressiforme

Hieracium impressiforme, commonly known as the flat-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
