Heterothalamus boliviensis

Heterothalamus boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian heterothalamus, is a rare and unusual plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its unique botanical structure, featuring a distinct dichotomous branching pattern that sets it apart from most other plants. The plant exhibits a specialized morphology with two different types of thallus structures, one apical and one basal, which contribute to its classification as a heterothalamus. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in cloud forests and mountainous terrains.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered an ecologically significant yet understudied species.
