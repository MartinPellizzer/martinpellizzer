Hymenesthes nitida

Hymenesthes nitida, commonly known as the slender sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its wood, which has a distinctive scent and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
