Hieracium pampoecilum

Hieracium pampoecilum, commonly known as the pale meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
