Hippophae taurica

Hippophae taurica, commonly known as the Taurian sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Black Sea coastal regions.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid environments.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to various climates and soils.
