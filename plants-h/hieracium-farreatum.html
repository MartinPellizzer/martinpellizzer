Hieracium farreatum

Hieracium farreatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, typically growing in meadows and grasslands. The plant has basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that contain a single seed.

Hieracium farreatum is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.
