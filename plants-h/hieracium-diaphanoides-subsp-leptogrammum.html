Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. leptogrammum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

leptogrammum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
