Heliconia gloriosa

Heliconia gloriosa, commonly known as the glorious heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with elongated, fleshy flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Heliconia gloriosa is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in floral arrangements.
