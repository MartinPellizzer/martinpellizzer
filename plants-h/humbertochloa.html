Humbertochloa

Humbertochloa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plants typically have slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with parallel venation. Humbertochloa species are often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed soils.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
