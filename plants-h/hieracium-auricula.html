Hieracium auricula

Hieracium auricula, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble small hawks, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium auricula is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
