Homoglossum caryophyllaceum

Homoglossum caryophyllaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features simple, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, dry habitats such as coastal dunes or rocky outcrops.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
