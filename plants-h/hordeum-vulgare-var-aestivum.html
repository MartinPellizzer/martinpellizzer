Hordeum vulgare var. aestivum

Hordeum vulgare var. aestivum, commonly known as barley, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. Barley is a member of the Poaceae family and is widely cultivated for its grains, which are used in food, beverages, and animal feed.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for agricultural production in a variety of climates.
