Helianthus tuberosus f. oswaldiae

Helianthus tuberosus f. oswaldiae, commonly known as the Oswalda sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more vibrant yellow flowers compared to the typical sunflower. The plant produces tuberous roots that are edible and have a nutty flavor, similar to the common sunflower. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This ornamental and edible plant is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and culinary potential.
