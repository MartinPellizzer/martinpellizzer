Hieracium rhombophyllum

Hieracium rhombophyllum, commonly known as the rhombic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
