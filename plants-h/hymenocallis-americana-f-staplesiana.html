Hymenocallis americana f. staplesiana

Hymenocallis americana f. staplesiana, commonly known as the golden spider lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are evergreen and can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
