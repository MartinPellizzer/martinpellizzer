Hieracium glaucum subsp. aechmetes

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

aechmetes, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually yellow to orange, with a distinctive blue or purple tinge, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habits.
