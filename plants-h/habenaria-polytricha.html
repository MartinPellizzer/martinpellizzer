Habenaria polytricha

Habenaria polytricha, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in forests or near streams.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to humid climates make it a notable species in botanical studies.
