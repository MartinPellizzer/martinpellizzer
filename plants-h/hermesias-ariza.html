Hermesias ariza

Hermesias ariza, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.
