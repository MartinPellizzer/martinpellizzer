Hybanthus glutinosus

Hybanthus glutinosus, commonly known as sticky hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hybanthus glutinosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
