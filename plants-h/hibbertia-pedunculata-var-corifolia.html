Hibbertia pedunculata var. corifolia

Hibbertia pedunculata var. corifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Hibbertia pedunculata var. corifolia is a hardy shrub that is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native region.
