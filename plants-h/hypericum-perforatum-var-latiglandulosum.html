Hypericum perforatum var. latiglandulosum

Hypericum perforatum var. latiglandulosum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a variant of the common St. John's wort.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and the presence of translucent dots on its leaves. The plant is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions. It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its dense, glandular hairs, which give it its common name.
