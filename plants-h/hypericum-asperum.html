Hypericum asperum

Hypericum asperum, commonly known as rough St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and rough, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hypericum asperum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
