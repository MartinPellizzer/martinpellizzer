Hemionitis aureonitens

Hemionitis aureonitens, commonly known as the golden hairpin, is a unique fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally coiled fronds that resemble curling hairpins. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically bright green with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Hemionitis aureonitens is also notable for its ability to survive in low-nutrient environments, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
