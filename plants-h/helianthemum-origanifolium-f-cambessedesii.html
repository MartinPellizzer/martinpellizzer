Helianthemum origanifolium f. cambessedesii

Helianthemum origanifolium f. cambessedesii, commonly known as the fragrant rock rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of oregano, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
