Hieracium vulgatiforme

Hieracium vulgatiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium vulgatiforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
