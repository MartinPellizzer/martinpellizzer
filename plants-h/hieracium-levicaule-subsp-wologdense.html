Hieracium levicaule subsp. wologdense

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

wologdense, commonly known as the Wologdense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the stems are upright and often branched.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.
