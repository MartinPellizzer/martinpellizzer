Hieracium chlorodes

Hieracium chlorodes, commonly known as the green false dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, upright stem that terminates in a yellowish composite flower head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant's leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along the edges of forests.
