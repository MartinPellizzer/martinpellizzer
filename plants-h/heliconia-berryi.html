Heliconia berryi

Heliconia berryi, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with long, tubular flowers that are often visited by hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The fruit, which is a red, fleshy berry, contains several seeds and is sometimes consumed by birds and mammals.
