Hypolepis rugosula subsp. viscida

Hypolepis rugosula subsp.

viscida, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a fern species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures.

The leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, shield-like appearance that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
