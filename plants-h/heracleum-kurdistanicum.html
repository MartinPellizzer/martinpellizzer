Heracleum kurdistanicum

Heracleum kurdistanicum, commonly known as Kurdish cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate regions, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features a tall, hollow stem with broad, pinnately lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The species is notable for its potential medicinal uses and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
