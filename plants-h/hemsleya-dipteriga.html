Hemsleya dipteriga

Hemsleya dipteriga, commonly known as the mountain begonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather.

Hemsleya dipteriga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
