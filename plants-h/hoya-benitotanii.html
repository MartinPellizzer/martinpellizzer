Hoya benitotanii

Hoya benitotanii, commonly known as the Benito's Wax Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Hoya benitotanii is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
