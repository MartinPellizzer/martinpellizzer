Hieracium malovanicum

Hieracium malovanicum, commonly known as the Malovan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing yellow-orange disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Hieracium malovanicum is often considered a minor weed but has ecological significance in its native range.
