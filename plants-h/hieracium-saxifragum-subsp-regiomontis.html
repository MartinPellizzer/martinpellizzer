Hieracium saxifragum subsp. regiomontis

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

regiomontis, commonly known as the Mexican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a compact growth habit with basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
