Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. sintenisii

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce orange to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, fleshy, red berries as its fruit.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
