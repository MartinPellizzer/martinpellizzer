Hovenia trichocarpa

Hovenia trichocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese raisin tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. Its fruit contains high levels of sugars and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
