Helichrysum niveum

Helichrysum niveum, commonly known as the snow helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble snowflakes, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum niveum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
