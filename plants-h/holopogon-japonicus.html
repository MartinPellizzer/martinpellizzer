Holopogon japonicus

Holopogon japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Holopogon japonicus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
