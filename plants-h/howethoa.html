Howethoa

Howethoa, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Howethoa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a velvety texture.
