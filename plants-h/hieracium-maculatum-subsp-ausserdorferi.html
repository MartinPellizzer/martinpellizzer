Hieracium maculatum subsp. ausserdorferi

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

ausserdorferi, commonly known as the Austrian spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching a height of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive spotted leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
