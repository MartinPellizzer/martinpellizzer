Hieracium prolixum subsp. caesitium

Hieracium prolixum subsp.

caesitium, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purple-hued.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
