Hieracium siphonanthum

Hieracium siphonanthum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium siphonanthum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
