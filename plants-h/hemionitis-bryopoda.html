Hemionitis bryopoda

Hemionitis bryopoda, commonly known as the water fern, is a small, aquatic fern that thrives in moist environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant is often found in shallow water or damp soil, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes. Hemionitis bryopoda reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a feature common among ferns.

Its presence in wetlands and streams indicates a preference for nutrient-rich, well-lit habitats.
