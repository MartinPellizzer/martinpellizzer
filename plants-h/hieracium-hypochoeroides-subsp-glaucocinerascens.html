Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. glaucocinerascens

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

glaucocinerascens, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a distinctive waxy coating, giving them a bluish-green appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
