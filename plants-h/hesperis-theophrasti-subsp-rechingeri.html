Hesperis theophrasti subsp. rechingeri

Hesperis theophrasti subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the Balkan candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
