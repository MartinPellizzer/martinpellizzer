Hieracium gaeopetum

Hieracium gaeopetum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

Hieracium gaeopetum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
