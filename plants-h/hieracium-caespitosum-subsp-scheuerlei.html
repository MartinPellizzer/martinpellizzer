Hieracium caespitosum subsp. scheuerlei

Hieracium caespitosum subsp.

scheuerlei, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions.
