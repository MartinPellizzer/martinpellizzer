Heliconia secunda var. viridiflora

Heliconia secunda var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features large, colorful inflorescences composed of bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white true flowers clustered within the bracts, which are often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Heliconia secunda var. viridiflora thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
