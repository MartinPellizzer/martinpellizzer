Hieracium levicaule subsp. glauciceps

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

glauciceps, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-gray coating. Its flowers are yellow, with 13 to 15 ray florets arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is characterized by its distinctive glaucous foliage and is often found in moist, temperate environments.
