Hibiscus syriacus var. purpureus

Hibiscus syriacus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, double flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features heart-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hibiscus syriacus var. purpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
