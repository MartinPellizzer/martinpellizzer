Hexalectris grandiflora

Hexalectris grandiflora, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its striking, fragrant inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pink to purple in color and attract pollinators like bees and wasps.

Hexalectris grandiflora is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming colonies over time.
