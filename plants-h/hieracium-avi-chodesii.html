Hieracium avi-chodesii

Hieracium avi-chodesii, commonly known as the Avi-chodesii hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, basal leaves. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has a rosette growth form and produces seeds in a capitulum structure.

It is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
