Hieracium amblytomum

Hieracium amblytomum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Hieracium amblytomum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
