Hieracium murorum subsp. graminicolor

Hieracium murorum subsp.

graminicolor, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies, making it a common sight in rural landscapes.
