Hesperonia limosa var. gracilis

Hesperonia limosa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hesperonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
