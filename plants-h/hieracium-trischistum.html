Hieracium trischistum

Hieracium trischistum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium trischistum is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
