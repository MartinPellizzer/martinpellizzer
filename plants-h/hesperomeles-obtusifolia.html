Hesperomeles obtusifolia

Hesperomeles obtusifolia, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen, thick-leaved foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is an important food source for local wildlife.
