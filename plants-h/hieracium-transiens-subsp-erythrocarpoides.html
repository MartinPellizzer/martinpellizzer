Hieracium transiens subsp. erythrocarpoides

Hieracium transiens subsp.

erythrocarpoides, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its red or reddish-brown achene fruits, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing showy inflorescences that attract various pollinators.
