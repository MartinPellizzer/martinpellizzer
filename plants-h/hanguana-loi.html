Hanguana loi

Hanguana loi, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Hanguana loi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
