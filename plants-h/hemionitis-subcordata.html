Hemionitis subcordata

Hemionitis subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Hemionitis subcordata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
