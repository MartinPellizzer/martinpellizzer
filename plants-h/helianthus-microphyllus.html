Helianthus microphyllus

Helianthus microphyllus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus microphyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
