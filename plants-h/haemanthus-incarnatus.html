Haemanthus incarnatus

Haemanthus incarnatus, commonly known as the Blood Lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, sword-shaped leaves that form a basal rosette. The plant produces striking red-orange flower spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
