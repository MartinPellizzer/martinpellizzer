Hieracium dragicola subsp. rossianum

Hieracium dragicola subsp.

rossianum, commonly known as the Rossian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces achenes as its fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
