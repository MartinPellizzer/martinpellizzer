Hieracium Chalcidicum Subsp. Thessalum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. thessalum is a lesser-known member of the daisy family found primarily in the mountainous regions of Greece. This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in botanical literature. Understanding this plant offers insights into the biodiversity of Mediterranean ecosystems.
