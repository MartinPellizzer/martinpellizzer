Helianthemum myrtifolium f. argyreum

Helianthemum myrtifolium f. argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, silver-gray foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes.

This plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
