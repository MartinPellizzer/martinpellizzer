Haplopappus hirtus var. sonchifolius

Haplopappus hirtus var. sonchifolius, commonly known as the hairy leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This species is notable for its adaptability and is considered a common weed in some regions.
