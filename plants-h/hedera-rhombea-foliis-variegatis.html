Hedera rhombea foliis-variegatis

Hedera rhombea foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, rounded growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with white or yellow variegation. The plant has a slow growth rate, making it ideal for topiary and formal garden designs. Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, though it can tolerate shade.
