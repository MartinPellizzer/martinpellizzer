Helleborine palustris lusus purpurea

Helleborine palustris lusus purpurea, commonly known as the purple marsh helleborine, is a species of orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along the edges of ponds and marshes. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose cluster and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
