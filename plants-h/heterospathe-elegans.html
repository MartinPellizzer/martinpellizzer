Heterospathe elegans

Heterospathe elegans, commonly known as the elegant fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with a fine layer of silvery hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Heterospathe elegans is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
