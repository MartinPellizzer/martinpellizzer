Hyptis clavellifera

Hyptis clavellifera, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Hyptis clavellifera is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
