Hepetis fulgens

Hepetis fulgens, commonly known as the shining hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a central yellow stamen. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant has a short rhizome and produces berries that are dispersed by birds.
