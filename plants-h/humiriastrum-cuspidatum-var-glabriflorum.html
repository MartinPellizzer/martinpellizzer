Humiriastrum cuspidatum var. glabriflorum

Humiriastrum cuspidatum var. glabriflorum, commonly known as the smoothflower tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless flower heads compared to other varieties of the species.
