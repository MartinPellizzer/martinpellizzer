Hippeastrum brachyandrum

Hippeastrum brachyandrum, commonly known as the dwarf hurricane lily, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces bulbous underground stems, which are essential for its propagation and survival. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Hippeastrum brachyandrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
