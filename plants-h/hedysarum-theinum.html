Hedysarum theinum

Hedysarum theinum, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in arid environments.

Hedysarum theinum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
