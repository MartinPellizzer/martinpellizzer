Hypochaeris thracica

Hypochaeris thracica, commonly known as the Thrace hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.

Hypochaeris thracica is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
