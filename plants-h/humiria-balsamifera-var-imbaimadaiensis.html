Humiria balsamifera var. imbaimadaiensis

Humiria balsamifera var. imbaimadaiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian balsam, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a woody shrub characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is valued for its unique scent and potential medicinal applications.
