Hydrangea macrophylla f. isobue

Hydrangea macrophylla f. isobue, commonly known as the lacecap hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

The plant features large, showy flowers with a dense central cluster of sterile florets surrounded by showy, petal-like bracts. It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, well-drained soil with partial shade. The foliage is typically dark green and remains attractive throughout the growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to change flower color based on soil pH.
