Helichrysum kuntzei

Helichrysum kuntzei, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a long-lasting appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
