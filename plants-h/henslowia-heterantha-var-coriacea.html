Henslowia heterantha var. coriacea

Henslowia heterantha var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-hair henslowia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
