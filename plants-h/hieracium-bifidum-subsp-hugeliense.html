Hieracium bifidum subsp. hugeliense

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

hugeliense, commonly known as the Hugel's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces and toothed margins. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single terminal head, and are surrounded by bracts.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
