Hemistemon

Hemistemon, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hemistemon is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
