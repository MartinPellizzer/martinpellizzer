Hieracium nigrescens

Hieracium nigrescens, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers transition from yellow to a dark brown or black color as they mature.

Hieracium nigrescens is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
