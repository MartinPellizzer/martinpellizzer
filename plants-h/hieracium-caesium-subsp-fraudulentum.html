Hieracium caesium subsp. fraudulentum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

fraudulentum, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer.

It is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
