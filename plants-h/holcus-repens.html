Holcus repens

Holcus repens, commonly known as creeping foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

Holcus repens is often found in disturbed soils, lawns, and along roadsides, where it can become a persistent weed.
