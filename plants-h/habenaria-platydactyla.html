Habenaria platydactyla

Habenaria platydactyla, commonly known as the broad-fingered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several ovate to elliptic leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
