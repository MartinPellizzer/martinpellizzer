Hyptis purdiei

Hyptis purdiei, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often aromatic and have a minty scent, which is typical of the mint family.
