Hibiscus flavescens

Hibiscus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are slender and hairy as well.

Hibiscus flavescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
