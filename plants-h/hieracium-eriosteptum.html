Hieracium eriosteptum

Hieracium eriosteptum, commonly known as the prickly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family.

Hieracium eriosteptum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
