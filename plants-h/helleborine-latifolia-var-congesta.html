Helleborine latifolia var. congesta

Helleborine latifolia var. congesta, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
