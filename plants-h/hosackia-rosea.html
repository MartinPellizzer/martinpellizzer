Hosackia rosea

Hosackia rosea, commonly known as the pink false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hosackia rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
