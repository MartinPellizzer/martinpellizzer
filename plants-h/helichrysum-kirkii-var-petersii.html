Helichrysum kirkii var. petersii

Helichrysum kirkii var. petersii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, bright yellow to orange flowers that are densely clustered in compact heads, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
