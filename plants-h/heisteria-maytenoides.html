Heisteria maytenoides

Heisteria maytenoides, commonly known as the silverleaf heister, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule.

Heisteria maytenoides is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
