Henckelia fasciata

Henckelia fasciata, commonly known as the striped pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green midrib, creating a striking visual contrast. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or cream in color, arranged in clusters.

Henckelia fasciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
