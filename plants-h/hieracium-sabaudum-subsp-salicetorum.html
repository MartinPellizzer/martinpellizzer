Hieracium sabaudum subsp. salicetorum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

salicetorum, commonly known as the Salicetum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. Its inflorescence is surrounded by a series of involucral bracts, and the flowers are actinomorphic with five ray florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
