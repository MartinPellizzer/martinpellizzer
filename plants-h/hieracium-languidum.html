Hieracium languidum

Hieracium languidum, commonly known as the languid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as seeds.

Hieracium languidum is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
