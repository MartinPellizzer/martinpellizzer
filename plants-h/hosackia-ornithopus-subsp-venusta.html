Hosackia ornithopus subsp. venusta

Hosackia ornithopus subsp.

venusta, commonly known as the showy hoary vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy.

The subspecies venusta is distinguished by its more showy floral displays compared to other subspecies of Hosackia.
