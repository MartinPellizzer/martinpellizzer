Hylomecon lanceolata

Hylomecon lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf hylomecon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
