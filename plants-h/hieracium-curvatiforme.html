Hieracium curvatiforme

Hieracium curvatiforme, commonly known as the curly-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive curling or wavy edge, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.
