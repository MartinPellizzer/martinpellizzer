Hieracium laevigatum subsp. umbellaticeps

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

umbellaticeps, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear a dense cluster of yellow flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal umbel, giving the plant its common name.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
