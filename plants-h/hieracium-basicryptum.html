Hieracium basicryptum

Hieracium basicryptum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are often hairy.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
