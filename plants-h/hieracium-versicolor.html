Hieracium versicolor

Hieracium versicolor, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant produces daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium versicolor is known for its variability in appearance, which contributes to its common name.
