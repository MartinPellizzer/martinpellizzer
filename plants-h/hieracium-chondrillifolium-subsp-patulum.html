Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. patulum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

patulum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
