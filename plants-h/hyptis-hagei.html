Hyptis hagei

Hyptis hagei, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis hagei is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
