Hippochaete laevigata var. ramosa

Hippochaete laevigata var. ramosa, commonly known as the silky hairbush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The variety 'ramosa' is distinguished by its more branched growth habit compared to the typical form.
