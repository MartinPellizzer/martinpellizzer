Hieracium basivinosum

Hieracium basivinosum, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Hieracium basivinosum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
