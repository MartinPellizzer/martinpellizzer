Hetaeria oblongifolia var. papuana

Hetaeria oblongifolia var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan broad-leaf hetaeria, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the tropical rainforests of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short, hairy pedicels. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to the humid and shaded conditions of its native habitat.
