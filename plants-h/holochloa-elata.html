Holochloa elata

Holochloa elata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often glabrous. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Holochloa elata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
