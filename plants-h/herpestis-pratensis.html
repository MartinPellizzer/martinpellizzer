Herpestis pratensis, commonly known as the meadow bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
