Hieracium levicaule subsp. percissiforme

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

percissiforme, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and showier inflorescences compared to other members of the species.
