Heliconia lutheri

Heliconia lutheri, commonly known as the Luther's heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its large, colorful bract clusters that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, sword-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Heliconia lutheri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
