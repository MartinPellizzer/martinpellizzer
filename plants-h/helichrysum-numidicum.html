Helichrysum numidicum

Helichrysum numidicum, commonly known as golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and grey-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum numidicum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
