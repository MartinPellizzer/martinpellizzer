Hieracium bectauatensis

Hieracium bectauatensis, commonly known as the Bectauatense Hawkweed, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets, and its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
