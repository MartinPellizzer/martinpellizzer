Hieracium kukulense

Hieracium kukulense, commonly known as the Kukul's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that give the plant a dandelion-like appearance.
