Hieracium aureum

Hieracium aureum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium aureum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
