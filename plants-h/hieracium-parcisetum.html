Hieracium parcisetum

Hieracium parcisetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which help distinguish it from other hawkweed species.
