Hedysarum antitauricum

Hedysarum antitauricum, commonly known as the Taurus vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a climbing habit and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hedysarum antitauricum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
