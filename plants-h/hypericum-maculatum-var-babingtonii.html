Hypericum maculatum var. babingtonii

Hypericum maculatum var. babingtonii, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers with prominent red veins, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
