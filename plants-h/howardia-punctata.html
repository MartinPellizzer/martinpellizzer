Howardia punctata

Howardia punctata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Howardia punctata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
