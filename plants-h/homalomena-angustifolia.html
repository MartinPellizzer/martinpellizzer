Homalomena angustifolia

Homalomena angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped, or lanceolate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red, orange, and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are typically followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Homalomena angustifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
