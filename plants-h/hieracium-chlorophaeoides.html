Hieracium chlorophaeoides

Hieracium chlorophaeoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium chlorophaeoides is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
