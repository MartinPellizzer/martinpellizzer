Hymenorebutia chlorogona

Hymenorebutia chlorogona, commonly known as the green-flowered pincushion cactus, is a species native to Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered in white or green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent collectors.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in botanical collections.
