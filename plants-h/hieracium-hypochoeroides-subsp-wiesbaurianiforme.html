Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. wiesbaurianiforme

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

wiesbaurianiforme, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive shape, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
