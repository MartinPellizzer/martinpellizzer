Halleria ovata

Halleria ovata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate in shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Halleria ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its rapid growth and showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.
