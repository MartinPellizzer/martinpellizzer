Hakea stenophylla subsp. notialis

Hakea stenophylla subsp.

notialis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces cylindrical, yellow-green flower spikes that bloom in late summer to autumn. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Hakea stenophylla subsp.

notialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
