Hapaline celatrix

Hapaline celatrix, commonly known as the golden starburry, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small stars, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with short, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
