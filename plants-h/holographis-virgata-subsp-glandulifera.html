Holographis virgata subsp. glandulifera

Holographis virgata subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular honeysuckle, is a shrub native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with glandular dots.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
