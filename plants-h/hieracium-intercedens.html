Hieracium intercedens

Hieracium intercedens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Hieracium intercedens is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
