Hibiscus clayi

Hibiscus clayi, commonly known as the clay's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments. Hibiscus clayi has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 8 through 10.
