Hieracium canum subsp. praelongum

Hieracium canum subsp.

praelongum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, rough to the touch, and often have a distinctive white underside.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that bloom in late spring to early summer.
