Hieracium sagittatum subsp. caespitans

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

caespitans, commonly known as the clustered spear-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single capitulum, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, clustered growth habit and its ability to form compact tufts.
