Hamelia sessiliflora

Hamelia sessiliflora, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Hamelia sessiliflora is also noted for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
