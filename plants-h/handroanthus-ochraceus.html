Handroanthus ochraceus

Handroanthus ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the base of the branches. The plant has a rough, fissured bark and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy surface.
