Henckelia punctata

Henckelia punctata, commonly known as the spotted henckelia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
