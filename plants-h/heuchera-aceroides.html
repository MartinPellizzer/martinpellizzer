Heuchera aceroides

Heuchera aceroides, commonly known as the red earshrub, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant red, orange, or yellow margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera aceroides is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
