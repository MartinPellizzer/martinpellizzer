Hieracium ciliiferum

Hieracium ciliiferum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a common weed in some regions.
