Hyptis hygrobia

Hyptis hygrobia, commonly known as the swamp hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist to swampy environments, often found near rivers and in marshy areas. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional South American herbal practices.

Hyptis hygrobia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
