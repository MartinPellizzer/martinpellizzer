Hieracium vulgatum subsp. pulchrius

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

pulchrius, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has basal and upright leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped. It grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
