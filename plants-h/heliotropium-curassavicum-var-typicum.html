Heliotropium curassavicum var. typicum

Heliotropium curassavicum var. typicum, commonly known as the tropical heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
