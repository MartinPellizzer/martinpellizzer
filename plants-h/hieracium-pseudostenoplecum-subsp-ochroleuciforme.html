Hieracium pseudostenoplecum subsp. ochroleuciforme

Hieracium pseudostenoplecum subsp.

ochroleuciforme, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with white veins.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal and cauline leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
