Hedysarum kurdicum

Hedysarum kurdicum, commonly known as Kurdish vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found growing in rocky or arid environments. It has small, stipitate pods that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum kurdicum is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in erosion control.
