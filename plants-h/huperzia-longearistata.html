Huperzia longearistata

Huperzia longearistata, commonly known as the long-ear fern, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, fern-like fronds that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. Its leaves are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile leaves differing in shape and structure.

Huperzia longearistata is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
