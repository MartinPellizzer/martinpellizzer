Hosta albomarginata var. alba

Hosta albomarginata var. alba, commonly known as the white-edged hosta, is a cultivar characterized by its striking white margins on its broad, heart-shaped leaves.

This variety is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in shady, moist environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in late summer, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
