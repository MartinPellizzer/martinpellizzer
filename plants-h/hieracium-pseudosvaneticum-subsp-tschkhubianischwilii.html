Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp. tschkhubianischwilii

Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp.

tschkhubianischwilii, commonly known as the Tskhubian mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
