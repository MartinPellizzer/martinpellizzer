Hemizonia perennis

Hemizonia perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, daisy-like blooms with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Hemizonia perennis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
