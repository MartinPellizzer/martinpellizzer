Hieracium trigenes subsp. dybowskianum

Hieracium trigenes subsp.

dybowskianum, commonly known as the Dybowskian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in the form of composite heads.

This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common component of temperate grasslands.
