Hieronyma pilifera

Hieronyma pilifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, white flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air.
