Hieracium subbakurianense

Hieracium subbakurianense, commonly known as the Subbakurian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

The yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
