Helichrysum gossweileri

Helichrysum gossweileri, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flowers that resemble tiny cups, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, sometimes resinous scent.

Helichrysum gossweileri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
