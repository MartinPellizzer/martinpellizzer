Hovea crispa

Hovea crispa, commonly known as the serrated hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its distinctive feature is the serrated edges of its leaves, which give it its common name.
