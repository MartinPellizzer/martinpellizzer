Homoranthus darwinioides

Homoranthus darwinioides, commonly known as the Darwin's homoranthus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
