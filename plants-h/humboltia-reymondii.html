Humboltia reymondii

Humboltia reymondii, commonly known as the Reymond's humboldtia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the leaves and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Humboltia reymondii is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
