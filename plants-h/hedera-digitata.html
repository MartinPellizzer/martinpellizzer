Hedera digitata

Hedera digitata, commonly known as the five-finger ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera digitata is also valued for its ability to climb walls and trellises, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
