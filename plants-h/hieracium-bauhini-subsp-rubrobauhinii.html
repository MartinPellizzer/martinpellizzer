Hieracium bauhini subsp. rubrobauhinii

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

rubrobauhinii, commonly known as the red-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are rough and hairy, giving it its common name. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its flowers are typically arranged in a single head and are surrounded by pale green bracts.
