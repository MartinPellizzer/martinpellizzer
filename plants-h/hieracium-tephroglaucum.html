Hieracium tephroglaucum

Hieracium tephroglaucum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, glaucous leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters.

Its scientific name reflects its distinctive appearance, with "tephroglaucum" referring to its yellowish-green coloration.
