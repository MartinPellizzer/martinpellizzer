Hepetis multiramosa

Hepetis multiramosa, commonly known as the many-branched heather, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a grayish-green hue, and it is known for its ability to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

Hepetis multiramosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
