Hieracium prodanianum

Hieracium prodanianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium prodanianum is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed or open habitats.
