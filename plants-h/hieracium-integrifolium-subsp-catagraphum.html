Hieracium integrifolium subsp. catagraphum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

catagraphum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are usually rough to the touch, with a broad, lanceolate shape. Its flowers are yellow and form composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in moist meadows and along stream banks, often forming dense colonies.
