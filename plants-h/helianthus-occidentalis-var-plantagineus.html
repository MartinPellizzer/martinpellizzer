Helianthus occidentalis var. plantagineus

Helianthus occidentalis var. plantagineus, commonly known as the Western sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces numerous seeds in its composite flower heads, which are adapted for wind pollination.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
