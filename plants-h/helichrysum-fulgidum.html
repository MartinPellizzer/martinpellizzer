Helichrysum fulgidum

Helichrysum fulgidum, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, bracts that resemble petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum fulgidum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.
