Hicorius buckleyi var. villosa

Hicorius buckleyi var. villosa, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its varietal name "villosa," meaning hairy in Latin. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
