Hosackia angustifolia

Hosackia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. It features slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
