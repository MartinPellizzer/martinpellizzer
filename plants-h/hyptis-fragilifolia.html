Hyptis fragilifolia

Hyptis fragilifolia, commonly known as brittleleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically clustered in whorls and produce a mild, minty fragrance.

Hyptis fragilifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
