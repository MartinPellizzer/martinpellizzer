Hicorius glabra var. reniformis

Hicorius glabra var. reniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped, giving it the variety name *reniformis*, which means "kidney-shaped" in Latin. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with a prominent central throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
