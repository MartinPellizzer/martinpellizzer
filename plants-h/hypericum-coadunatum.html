Hypericum coadunatum

Hypericum coadunatum, commonly known as the Cape gold bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Hypericum coadunatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
