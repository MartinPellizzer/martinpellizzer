Huernia concinna

Huernia concinna, commonly known as the horned African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often swollen at the nodes, giving it a unique, horn-like appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

Huernia concinna is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and requiring minimal care when grown in the right conditions.
