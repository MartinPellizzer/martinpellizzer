Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. contiguum

Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. contiguum, commonly known as the clustered filmy fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are covered in tiny, translucent spore cases called soridia. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to habitat loss and limited natural range.
