Herniaria ceretana

Herniaria ceretana, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with harsh environmental conditions.

Herniaria ceretana is adapted to withstand drought and salt exposure, making it a resilient species in challenging habitats.
