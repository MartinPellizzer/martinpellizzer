Hakea angustiloba

Hakea angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Hakea angustiloba is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its thick, waxy leaves.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
