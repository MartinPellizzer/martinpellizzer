Helleborine atropurpurea f. alternifolia

Helleborine atropurpurea f. alternifolia, commonly known as the purple-flowered helleborine, is a striking orchid species characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem, giving it its specific epithet *alternifolia*. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in woodlands or forest undergrowth.

The flowers are borne on tall, slender spikes and are pollinated primarily by flies and beetles.
