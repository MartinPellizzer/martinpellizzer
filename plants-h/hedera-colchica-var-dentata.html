Hedera colchica var. dentata

Hedera colchica var. dentata, commonly known as the Armenian ivy, is a perennial climbing plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, lobed leaves with a distinctive serrated edge, giving it a rugged appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in hedges or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
