Heliotropium coromandelianum var. oblongifolium

Heliotropium coromandelianum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, resembling those of the common heliotrope. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
