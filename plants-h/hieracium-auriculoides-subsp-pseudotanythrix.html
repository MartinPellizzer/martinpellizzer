Hieracium auriculoides subsp. pseudotanythrix

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

pseudotanythrix, commonly known as the auricled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive leaf morphology.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
