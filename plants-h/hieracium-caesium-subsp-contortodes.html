Hieracium caesium subsp. contortodes

Hieracium caesium subsp.

contortodes, commonly known as the twisted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually lanceolate or ovate, with rough, hairy surfaces, and its flowers are yellow with a prominent central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
