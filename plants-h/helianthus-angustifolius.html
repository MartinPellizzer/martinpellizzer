Helianthus angustifolius

Helianthus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southern United States and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, and its flowers attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.

Helianthus angustifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
