Hypochaeris lutea var. trichocephala

Hypochaeris lutea var. trichocephala, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, flattened shape.

It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.
