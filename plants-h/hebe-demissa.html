Hebe demissa

Hebe demissa, commonly known as the slender hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe demissa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
