Hypericum humifusum var. losae

Hypericum humifusum var. losae, commonly known as the Los Angeles hypericum, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features woody stems that lie close to the ground, often forming a dense mat. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
