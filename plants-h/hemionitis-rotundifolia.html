Hemionitis rotundifolia

Hemionitis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed hemionitis, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and along stream banks. The plant has distinctive, round, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Hemionitis rotundifolia is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
