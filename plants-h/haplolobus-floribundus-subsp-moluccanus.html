Haplolobus floribundus subsp. moluccanus

Haplolobus floribundus subsp.

moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is known for its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
