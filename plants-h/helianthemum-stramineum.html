Helianthemum stramineum

Helianthemum stramineum, commonly known as the yellow rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Helianthemum stramineum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.
