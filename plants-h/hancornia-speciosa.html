Hancornia speciosa

Hancornia speciosa, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant is part of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value. It has smooth, glossy leaves and produces milky sap that can be toxic.
