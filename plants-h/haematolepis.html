Haematolepis

Haematolepis, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. Haematolepis species are characterized by their striking red or orange flowers, which are often clustered in racemes. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitats.

The genus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
