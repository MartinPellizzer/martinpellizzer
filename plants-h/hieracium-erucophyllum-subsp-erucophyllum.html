Hieracium erucophyllum subsp. erucophyllum

Hieracium erucophyllum subsp.

erucophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes.

The plant features hairy, often glandular leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
