Hepatica insularis

Hepatica insularis, commonly known as the island hepatica, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that emerge early in the spring. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring. Hepatica insularis thrives in woodland environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.
