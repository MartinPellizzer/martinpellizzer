Hymenocallis humilis

Hymenocallis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf hurricane lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with narrow, linear leaves and produces striking orange or yellow flowers with a prominent, spiky center. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is evergreen in warmer climates, while it may die back in colder regions.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
