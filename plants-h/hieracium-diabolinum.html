Hieracium diabolinum

Hieracium diabolinum, commonly known as the devil's dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by distinctive bristly seed heads. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Hieracium diabolinum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
