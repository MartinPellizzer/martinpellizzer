Hermesias × hybrida

Hermesias × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring arching branches and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a sweet, honey-like scent that enhances its appeal in gardens.
