Hippeastrum puertoricense

Hippeastrum puertoricense, commonly known as the Puerto Rican amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces tall, erect stems with clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red.

Hippeastrum puertoricense is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
