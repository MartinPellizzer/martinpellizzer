Hymenopappus anthemoides

Hymenopappus anthemoides, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation across dry landscapes. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
