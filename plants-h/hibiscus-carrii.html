Hibiscus carrii

Hibiscus carrii, commonly known as the Carri's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus carrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
