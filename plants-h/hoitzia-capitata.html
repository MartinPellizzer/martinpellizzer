Hoitzia capitata

Hoitzia capitata, commonly known as the clustered bristle grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Hoitzia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
