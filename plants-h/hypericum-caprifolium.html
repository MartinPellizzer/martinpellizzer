Hypericum caprifolium

Hypericum caprifolium, commonly known as common St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant contains bioactive compounds, including hypericin and hyperforin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
