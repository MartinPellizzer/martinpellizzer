Hyptis scoparia

Hyptis scoparia, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Hyptis scoparia prefers well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
