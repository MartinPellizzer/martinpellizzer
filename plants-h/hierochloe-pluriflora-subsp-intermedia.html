Hierochloe pluriflora subsp. intermedia

Hierochloe pluriflora subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features fine, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This subspecies is closely related to other bluegrasses and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
