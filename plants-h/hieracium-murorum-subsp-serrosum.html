Hieracium murorum subsp. serrosum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

serrosum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are often lobed and hairy, with stems that are upright and covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are usually arranged in a compact cluster, producing small, composite flowers that attract various pollinators.
