Hieracium bourgaei subsp. palomerae

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

palomerae, commonly known as the Palomera hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native region. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds in a fluffy, composite structure typical of the daisy family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
