Hirtella floribunda

Hirtella floribunda, commonly known as the pink-flowered hirtella, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hirtella floribunda is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
