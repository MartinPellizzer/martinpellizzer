Hedysarum reesei

Hedysarum reesei, commonly known as the reese's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant produces small, yellow to pale green pea-like flowers, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum reesei plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
