Hieracium intercalatum

Hieracium intercalatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium intercalatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
