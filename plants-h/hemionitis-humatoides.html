Hemionitis humatoides

Hemionitis humatoides, commonly known as the water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the fronds, which can be challenging to observe.

Hemionitis humatoides plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
