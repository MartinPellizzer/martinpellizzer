Hesperis aladabadensis

Hesperis aladabadensis, commonly known as the Aladabad horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Hesperis aladabadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
