Hieracium symphytifolium

Hieracium symphytifolium, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has dark green, basal leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering stems bear clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

Hieracium symphytifolium is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
