Hesperantha falcata subsp. lutea

Hesperantha falcata subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a fragrant scent.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while the stems are short and sturdy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
