Hymenaea candolleana

Hymenaea candolleana, commonly known as the candolleana or yellow tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and wood are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
