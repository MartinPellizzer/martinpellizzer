Hieracium racemosum subsp. lucanum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

lucanum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a raceme, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disc florets.

It has a creeping rootstock and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
