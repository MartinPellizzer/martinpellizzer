Hieracium bernardi subsp. templare

Hieracium bernardi subsp.

templare, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellowish to reddish disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas of the Alps and is adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its habitat.
