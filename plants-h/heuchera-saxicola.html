Heuchera saxicola

Heuchera saxicola, commonly known as the rock heuchera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage, which often displays vibrant shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among gardeners interested in native plants.
