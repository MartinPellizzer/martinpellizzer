Hoffmannia aphylla

Hoffmannia aphylla, commonly known as the leafless hoffmannia, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Hoffmannia aphylla is a fascinating example of evolutionary response to extreme drought conditions.
