Helichrysum citricephalum

Helichrysum citricephalum, commonly known as the lemon helmet or lemon head, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum citricephalum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
