Hieracium remotilingua

Hieracium remotilingua, commonly known as the distant-tongued hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, elongated leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas. It has a creeping growth habit with erect flowering stems.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in some regions.
