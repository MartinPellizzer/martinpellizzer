Hydrangea xanthoneura var. wilsonii

Hydrangea xanthoneura var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, white flowers in summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a dark green hue.

The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
