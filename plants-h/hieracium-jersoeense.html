Hieracium jersoeense

Hieracium jersoeense, commonly known as the Jersoe hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has a low-growing habit with spreading stems. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that give it a dandelion-like appearance.

Hieracium jersoeense is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
