Hieracium schultesii subsp. laramberguei

Hieracium schultesii subsp.

laramberguei, commonly known as the Larambergue's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
