Hieracium juraniforme

Hieracium juraniforme, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers bloom from June to August.

Hieracium juraniforme is often found in mountainous regions and is considered a characteristic species of the Jura Mountains.
