Hyptis decurrens

Hyptis decurrens, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis decurrens is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
