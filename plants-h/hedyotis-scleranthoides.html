Hedyotis scleranthoides

Hedyotis scleranthoides, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Hedyotis scleranthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
