Helichrysum serotinum subsp. picardii

Helichrysum serotinum subsp.

picardii, commonly known as the Picard's everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small sunflowers, which remain intact even after flowering.

The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which are used in dried flower arrangements.
