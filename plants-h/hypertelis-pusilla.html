Hypertelis pusilla

Hypertelis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or under dense vegetation. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces tiny, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Hypertelis pusilla is known for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
