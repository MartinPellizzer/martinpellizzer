Hibiscus bricchettii

Hibiscus bricchettii, commonly known as the Briccett's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus bricchettii is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in horticultural and ecological studies.
