Heliotropium capitatum

Heliotropium capitatum, commonly known as the clustered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Heliotropium capitatum is also known for its fragrant flowers, which emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent.
