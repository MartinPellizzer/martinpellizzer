Heracleum sphondylium var. akasimontanum

Heracleum sphondylium var. akasimontanum, commonly known as the Akasimontan cow parsnip, is a variant of the common cow parsnip native to the western United States.

It is a biennial herbaceous plant that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring a thick, hollow stem and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
