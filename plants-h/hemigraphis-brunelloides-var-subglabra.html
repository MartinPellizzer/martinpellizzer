Hemigraphis brunelloides var. subglabra

Hemigraphis brunelloides var. subglabra, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironplant, is a compact, low-growing perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to reddish hues, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and ability to tolerate moist conditions.
