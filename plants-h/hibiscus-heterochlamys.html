Hibiscus heterochlamys

Hibiscus heterochlamys, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus heterochlamys is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
