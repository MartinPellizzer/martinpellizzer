Hyoscyamus bohemicus

Hyoscyamus bohemicus, commonly known as Bohemian henbane, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its purple or violet flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

It contains toxic alkaloids and has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a hallucinogenic substance.
