Hieracium bichlorophyllon

Hieracium bichlorophyllon, commonly known as the two-chlorophyll hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a hairy stem and possesses a taproot system, which helps it survive in various soil conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and its role in local ecosystems.
