Hieracium magyaricum subsp. decolor

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

decolor, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that often fade to a lighter color.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal and upright stems with lance-shaped leaves, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare species in its native range.
