Hieracium causiatum

Hieracium causiatum, commonly known as the Caussat's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a serrated edge.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
