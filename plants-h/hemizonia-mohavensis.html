Hemizonia mohavensis

Hemizonia mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Hemizonia mohavensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
