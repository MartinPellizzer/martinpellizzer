Hieracium levicaule subsp. lucescens

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

lucescens, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered with a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a bluish-gray appearance. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive glaucous foliage and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
