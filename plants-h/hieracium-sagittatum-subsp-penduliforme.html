Hieracium sagittatum subsp. penduliforme

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

penduliforme, commonly known as the hanging-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pendulous, elongated leaves that droop from the stem, giving it a unique appearance. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Hieracium.

This plant thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
