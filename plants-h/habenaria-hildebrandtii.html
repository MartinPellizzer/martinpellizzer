Habenaria hildebrandtii

Habenaria hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it produces small, cylindrical pseudobulbs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
