Hibiscus vestitus

Hibiscus vestitus, commonly known as the velvet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The leaves are usually velvety to the touch, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Hibiscus vestitus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
