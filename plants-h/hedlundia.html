Hedlundia

Hedlundia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flower heads that resemble mist or smoke, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Africa. Hedlundia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking appearance and drought tolerance.

The leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.
