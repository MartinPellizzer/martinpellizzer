Hemerocallis fulva var. sempervirens

Hemerocallis fulva var. sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen daylily, is a perennial plant characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, orange-red flowers.

It belongs to the genus Hemerocallis, which includes various daylily species, and is native to eastern Asia. This variety is notable for retaining its leaves throughout the year, distinguishing it from other daylily varieties that go dormant in winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
