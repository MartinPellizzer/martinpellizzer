Helianthus praecox subsp. runyonii

Helianthus praecox subsp.

runyonii, commonly known as the Runyon's sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and prairies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
