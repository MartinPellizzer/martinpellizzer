Hieracium carabopsis

Hieracium carabopsis, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium carabopsis is known for its robust growth and is often found in calcareous habitats.
