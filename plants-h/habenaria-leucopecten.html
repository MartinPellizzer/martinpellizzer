Habenaria leucopecten

Habenaria leucopecten, commonly known as the white-winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers with contrasting green veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
