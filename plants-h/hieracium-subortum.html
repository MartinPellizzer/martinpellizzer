Hieracium subortum

Hieracium subortum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are typically surrounded by a ring of pale yellow ray florets.

Hieracium subortum is a perennial plant that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
