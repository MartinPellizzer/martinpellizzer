Helenium arizonicum

Helenium arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features bright orange to yellow ray florets and dark brown central disk florets, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
