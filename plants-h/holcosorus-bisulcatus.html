Holcosorus bisulcatus

Holcosorus bisulcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water clover, is a small, aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, divided fronds that often float on the water surface or hang submerged. The plant's rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediment and improve water quality. Its spores are released into the air, allowing for dispersal to new habitats.

Holcosorus bisulcatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
