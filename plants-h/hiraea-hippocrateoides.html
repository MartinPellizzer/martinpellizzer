Hiraea hippocrateoides

Hiraea hippocrateoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hiraea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
