Hyptis singularis

Hyptis singularis, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis singularis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
