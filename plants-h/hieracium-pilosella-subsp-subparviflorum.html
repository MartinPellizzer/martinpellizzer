Hieracium pilosella subsp. subparviflorum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

subparviflorum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear small, yellow, daisy-like flowers. It produces numerous seeds enclosed in a hollow, cylindrical achene, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
