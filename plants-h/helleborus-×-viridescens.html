Helleborus × viridescens

Helleborus × viridescens, commonly known as the evergreen hellebore, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between H. necroticus and H. foetidus.

It is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that produces dark green, leathery foliage throughout the year. The plant features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

Helleborus × viridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom during the dormant season of many other plants.
