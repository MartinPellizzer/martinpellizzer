Hieracium exarmatum

Hieracium exarmatum, commonly known as the barren hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer, contributing to its ecological role as a nectar source for pollinators.
