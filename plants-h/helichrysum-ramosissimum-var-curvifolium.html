Helichrysum ramosissimum var. curvifolium

Helichrysum ramosissimum var. curvifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting or curlyleaf helichrysum, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, branched stems and small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that are often used in floral arrangements. The leaves are narrow, slightly curved, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
