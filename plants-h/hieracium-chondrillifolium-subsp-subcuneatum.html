Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. subcuneatum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

subcuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and composite flower heads.

The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
