Helichrysum undatum

Helichrysum undatum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Helichrysum undatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
