Hieracium monregalense subsp. gibellianum

Hieracium monregalense subsp.

gibellianum, commonly known as the Corsican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in association with other Mediterranean flora.
