Hemionitis thellungii

Hemionitis thellungii, commonly known as the rock fern, is a unique plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a small, perennial fern that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. The plant has slender, creeping rhizomes and produces small, delicate fronds that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its reproductive structures are concealed within tightly rolled, spore-bearing fiddleheads.

Hemionitis thellungii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
