Helichrysum scandens

Helichrysum scandens, commonly known as the everlasting sweet William, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring wiry, spreading stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Helichrysum scandens is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its adaptability to dry conditions.
