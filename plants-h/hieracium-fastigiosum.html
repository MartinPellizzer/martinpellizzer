Hieracium fastigiosum

Hieracium fastigiosum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Hieracium fastigiosum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.
