Hippocratea floribunda

Hippocratea floribunda, commonly known as the purple-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
