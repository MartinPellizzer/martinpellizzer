Hyssopus aristatus

Hyssopus aristatus, commonly known as the upright hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue to purple flowers. Hyssopus aristatus typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
