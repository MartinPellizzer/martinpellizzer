Hieracium sparsidens subsp. subcinerellum

Hieracium sparsidens subsp.

subcinerellum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green to brown bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and the stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender growth habit and distinct floral morphology.
