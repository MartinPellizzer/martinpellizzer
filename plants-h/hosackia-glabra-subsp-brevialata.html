Hosackia glabra subsp. brevialata

Hosackia glabra subsp.

brevialata, commonly known as the short-stemmed false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in racemate clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
