Hieracium murorum subsp. subinfrasericatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subinfrasericatum, commonly known as the small mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early summer.

The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
