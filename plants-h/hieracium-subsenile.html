Hieracium subsenile

Hieracium subsenile, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the top of the stem.

Hieracium subsenile is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
