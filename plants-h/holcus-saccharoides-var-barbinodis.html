Holcus saccharoides var. barbinodis

Holcus saccharoides var. barbinodis, commonly known as the hairy oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
