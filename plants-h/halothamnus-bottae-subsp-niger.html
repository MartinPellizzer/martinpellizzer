Halothamnus bottae subsp. niger

Halothamnus bottae subsp.

niger, commonly known as black halothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage that is covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Halothamnus bottae subsp.

niger is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native environment.
