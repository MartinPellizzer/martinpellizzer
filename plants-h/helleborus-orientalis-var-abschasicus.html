Helleborus orientalis var. abschasicus

Helleborus orientalis var. abschasicus, commonly known as the Abschasius snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, waxy leaves and produces striking white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, nodding shape. The plant is characterized by its winter blooming habit, often flowering from late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, moist soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The variety is noted for its cold hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
