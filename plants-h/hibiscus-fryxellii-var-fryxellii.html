Hibiscus fryxellii var. fryxellii

Hibiscus fryxellii var. fryxellii, commonly known as the Fryxell's hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hibiscus fryxellii var. fryxellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
