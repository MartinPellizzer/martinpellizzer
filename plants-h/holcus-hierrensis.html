Holcus hierrensis

Holcus hierrensis, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth and its distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and heaths. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Holcus hierrensis plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitats.
