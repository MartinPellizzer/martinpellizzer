Hieracium florentinum subsp. stupposipilum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

stupposipilum, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
