Hieracium sagittatum subsp. striatulum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

striatulum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are arrow-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, composed of many small florets arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is characterized by its finely striated leaves and robust growth habit, making it a notable component of its native habitats.
