Hieracium pseudolaggeri

Hieracium pseudolaggeri, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium pseudolaggeri is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
