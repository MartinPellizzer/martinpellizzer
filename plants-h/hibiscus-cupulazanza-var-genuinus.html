Hibiscus cupulazanza var. genuinus

Hibiscus cupulazanza var. genuinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
