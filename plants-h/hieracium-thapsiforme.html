Hieracium thapsiforme

Hieracium thapsiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom from June to August. The plant has basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium thapsiforme is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
