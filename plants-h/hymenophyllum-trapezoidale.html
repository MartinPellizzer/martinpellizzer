Hymenophyllum trapezoidale

Hymenophyllum trapezoidale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, scale-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure on the forest floor. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves, which release spores for propagation.

This species is notable for its intricate leaf morphology and its role in maintaining forest floor ecosystems.
