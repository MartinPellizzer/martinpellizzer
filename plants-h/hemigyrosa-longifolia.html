Hemigyrosa longifolia

Hemigyrosa longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hemicycle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Hemigyrosa longifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
