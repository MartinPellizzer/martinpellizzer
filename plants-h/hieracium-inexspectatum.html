Hieracium inexspectatum

Hieracium inexspectatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall, with basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically bright yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium inexspectatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
