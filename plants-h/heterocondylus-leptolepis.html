Heterocondylus leptolepis

Heterocondylus leptolepis, commonly known as the slender-leaved marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences at the tips of its stems. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in water-saturated soils.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
