Helminthotheca spinifera

Helminthotheca spinifera, commonly known as the spiked everlasting or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation along coastal areas.
