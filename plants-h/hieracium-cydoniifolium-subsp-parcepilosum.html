Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. parcepilosum

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

parcepilosum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it typically grows in meadows and grassy areas.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats.
