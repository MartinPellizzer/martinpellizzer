Helianthemum lasianthum proles cheiranthoides

Helianthemum lasianthum proles cheiranthoides, commonly known as the rock helianthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
