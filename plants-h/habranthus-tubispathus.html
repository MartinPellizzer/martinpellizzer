Habranthus tubispathus

Habranthus tubispathus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique appearance.
