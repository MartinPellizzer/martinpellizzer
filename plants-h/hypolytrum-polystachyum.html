Hypolytrum polystachyum

Hypolytrum polystachyum, commonly known as the golden spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.
