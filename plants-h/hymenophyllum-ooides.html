Hymenophyllum ooides

Hymenophyllum ooides, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance and is typically found in shaded, moist environments. The plant has slender, creeping stems with closely spaced, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. Hymenophyllum ooides thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Its small, spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
