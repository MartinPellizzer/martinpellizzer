Hieracium craterocephalum

Hieracium craterocephalum, commonly known as the crater-shaped hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, rounded cluster at the top of its stem. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.
