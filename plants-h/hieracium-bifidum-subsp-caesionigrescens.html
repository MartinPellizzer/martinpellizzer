Hieracium bifidum subsp. caesionigrescens

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

caesionigrescens, commonly known as the dark-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark green, sometimes purplish leaves and the tendency of its stems to darken with age. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its robust growth habit.

The plant is relatively drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
