Hyoseris foetida

Hyoseris foetida, commonly known as stinking helenium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, and the plant produces a distinctive, strongly scented compound called sesquiterpene lactone.

Hyoseris foetida is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
