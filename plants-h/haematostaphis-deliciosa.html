Haematostaphis deliciosa

Haematostaphis deliciosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive reddish stems that become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by small, three-lobed seeds.

This plant is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
