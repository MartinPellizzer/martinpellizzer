Heteropogon acuminatus

Heteropogon acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses.

Heteropogon acuminatus is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
