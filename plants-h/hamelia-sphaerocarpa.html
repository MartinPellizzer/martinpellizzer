Hamelia sphaerocarpa

Hamelia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-flowered hamelia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a spherical capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Hamelia sphaerocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
