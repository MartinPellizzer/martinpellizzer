Hieracium nigrescens subsp. subgracillimum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

subgracillimum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in a single head.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
