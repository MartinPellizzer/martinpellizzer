Hieracium melamphaes

Hieracium melamphaes, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are dark green and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with narrow petals.

Hieracium melamphaes is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats and is often found in open areas with partial sunlight.
