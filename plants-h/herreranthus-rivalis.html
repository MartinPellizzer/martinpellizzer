Herreranthus rivalis

Herreranthus rivalis, commonly known as the red-flowered herreranthus, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its bright floral display. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Herreranthus rivalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
