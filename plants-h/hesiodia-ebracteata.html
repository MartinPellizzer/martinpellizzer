Hesiodia ebracteata

Hesiodia ebracteata, commonly known as the leafless hesiodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its absence of bracts, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Hesiodia ebracteata produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
