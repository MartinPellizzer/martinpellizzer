Helleborus deflexifolius

Helleborus deflexifolius, commonly known as the nodding hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often notched or lobed, and its distinctive, downward-facing flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The flowers are typically purple or violet in color, with a central greenish-yellowish spathe. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
