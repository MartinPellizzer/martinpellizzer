Hibiscus fragilis

Hibiscus fragilis, commonly known as the fragile hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often hairy.

Hibiscus fragilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
