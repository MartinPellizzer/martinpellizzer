Hemipilia basifoliata

Hemipilia basifoliata, commonly known as the basal-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves and slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Hemipilia basifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
