Haydenoxylon urbanianum

Haydenoxylon urbanianum, commonly known as the urbanian sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with narrow, silvery-green leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
