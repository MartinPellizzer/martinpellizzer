Hieracium lutescens

Hieracium lutescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hieracium lutescens typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
