Hieracium pseudochristianiense

Hieracium pseudochristianiense, commonly known as the Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and is known for its low-growing habit. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
