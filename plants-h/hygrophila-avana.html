Hygrophila avana

Hygrophila avana, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Hygrophila avana thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and along stream banks.

It is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to purify water.
