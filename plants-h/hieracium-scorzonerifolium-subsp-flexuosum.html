Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. flexuosum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

flexuosum, commonly known as the flexuous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
