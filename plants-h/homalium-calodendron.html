Homalium calodendron

Homalium calodendron, commonly known as the red-flowered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that produce striking red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
