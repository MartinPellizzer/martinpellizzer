Hieracium pallidum subsp. argaeolum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

argaeolum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a low-growing species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
