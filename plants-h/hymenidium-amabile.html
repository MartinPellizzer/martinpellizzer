Hymenidium amabile

Hymenidium amabile, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are fern-like, with a delicate, feathery appearance, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorocarps.

Hymenidium amabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
