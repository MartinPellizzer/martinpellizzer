Hesperantha purpurea

Hesperantha purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in semi-arid regions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are usually purple or pink in color, with a distinctive hooded lip. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Hesperantha purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
