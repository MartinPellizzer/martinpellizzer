Hieracium sabaudum subsp. auratum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

auratum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

The subspecies is distinguished by its particularly vibrant and showy yellow flowers compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
