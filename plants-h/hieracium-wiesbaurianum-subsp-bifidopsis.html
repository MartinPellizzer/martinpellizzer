Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. bifidopsis

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

bifidopsis, commonly known as the bifid leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive bifid (two-lobed) appearance, giving the species its subspecific name. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
