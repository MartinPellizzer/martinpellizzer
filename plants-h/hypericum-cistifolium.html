Hypericum cistifolium

Hypericum cistifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces red or orange berries that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Hypericum cistifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
