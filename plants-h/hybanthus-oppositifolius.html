Hybanthus oppositifolius

Hybanthus oppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaved hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
