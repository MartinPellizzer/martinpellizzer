Hieracium postdiluviale

Hieracium postdiluviale, commonly known as the postdiluvial hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in dry environments. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in areas with well-drained soil. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is typically found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
