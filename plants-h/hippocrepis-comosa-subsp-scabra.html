Hippocrepis comosa subsp. scabra

Hippocrepis comosa subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough-haired bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
