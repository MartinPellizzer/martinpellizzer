Huperzia versteegii

Huperzia versteegii, commonly known as the Versteeg's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to South America.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its upright, spiky appearance with elongated, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia versteegii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
