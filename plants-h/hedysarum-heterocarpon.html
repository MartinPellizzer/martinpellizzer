Hedysarum heterocarpon

Hedysarum heterocarpon, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Hedysarum heterocarpon is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
