Hemigraphis rupestris

Hemigraphis rupestris, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a succulent plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Hemigraphis rupestris is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative indoor plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
