Hieracium virgaurea

Hieracium virgaurea, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a single stem and are accompanied by bracts.

Hieracium virgaurea is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
