Hyptis fulva

Hyptis fulva, commonly known as the golden hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
