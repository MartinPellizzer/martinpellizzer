Hackelia tenuis

Hackelia tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf elder.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from pale pink to deep red.

Hackelia tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
