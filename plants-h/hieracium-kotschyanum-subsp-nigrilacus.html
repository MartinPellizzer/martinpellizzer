Hieracium kotschyanum subsp. nigrilacus

Hieracium kotschyanum subsp.

nigrilacus, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black stems and leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the genus Hieracium.

This plant thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
