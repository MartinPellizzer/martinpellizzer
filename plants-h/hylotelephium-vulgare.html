Hylotelephium vulgare

Hylotelephium vulgare, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
