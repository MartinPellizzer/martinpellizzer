Heliconia collinsiana var. collinsiana

Heliconia collinsiana var. collinsiana, commonly known as the Collins' heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces elongated, finger-like inflorescences with numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad, waxy, and often have a reddish or purple underside.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
