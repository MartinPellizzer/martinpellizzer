Hymenophyllum apterum

Hymenophyllum apterum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a small, delicate fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually only a few centimeters long, and they produce small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum apterum is well adapted to humid climates and plays a role in maintaining soil moisture and supporting microfauna in its habitat.
