Halerpestes cymbalaria var. saximontanus

Halerpestes cymbalaria var. saximontanus, commonly known as the saximontan sandmat, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky or sandy habitat. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, a feature typical of xerophytic plants.

This variety is particularly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a valuable indicator of ecosystem health.
