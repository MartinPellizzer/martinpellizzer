Heteromeles arbutifolia subsp. macrocarpa

Heteromeles arbutifolia subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the bigleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and evergreen foliage composed of elliptical, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
