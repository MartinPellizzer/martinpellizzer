Hoya lanataiensis

Hoya lanataiensis, commonly known as the woolly hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya lanataiensis is a tropical vine that thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

It is relatively low maintenance and popular among houseplant enthusiasts due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
