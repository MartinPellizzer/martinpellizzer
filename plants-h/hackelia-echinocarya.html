Hackelia echinocarya

Hackelia echinocarya, commonly known as the spiked honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
