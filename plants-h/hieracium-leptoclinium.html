Hieracium leptoclinium

Hieracium leptoclinium, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it can thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
