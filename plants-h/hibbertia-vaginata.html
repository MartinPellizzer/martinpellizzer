Hibbertia vaginata

Hibbertia vaginata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibbertia vaginata typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in various regions across southern Australia.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also a symbol of Australian flora.
