Hieracium angustifolium var. maius

Hieracium angustifolium var. maius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
