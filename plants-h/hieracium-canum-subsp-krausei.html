Hieracium canum subsp. krausei

Hieracium canum subsp.

krausei, commonly known as the Krause's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers with ray florets, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
