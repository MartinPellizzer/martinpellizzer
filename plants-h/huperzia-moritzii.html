Huperzia moritzii

Huperzia moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged, linear leaves and a creeping rhizome system. Huperzia moritzii is characterized by its spore-producing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its branches.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
