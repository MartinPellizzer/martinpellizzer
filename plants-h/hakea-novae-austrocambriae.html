Hakea novae-austrocambriae

Hakea novae-austrocambriae, commonly known as the New South Wales hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and distinctive spikes of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Hakea novae-austrocambriae is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
