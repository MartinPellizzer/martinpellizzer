Hemipilia amplexifolia

Hemipilia amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping hemipilia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are often clasping around the stem. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a spike, and are typically pollinated by insects.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
