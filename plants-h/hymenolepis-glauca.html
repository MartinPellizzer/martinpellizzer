Hymenolepis glauca

Hymenolepis glauca, commonly known as the bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hymenolepis glauca is also known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
