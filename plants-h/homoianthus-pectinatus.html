Homoianthus pectinatus

Homoianthus pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
