Hieracium schistostegum

Hieracium schistostegum, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium schistostegum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
