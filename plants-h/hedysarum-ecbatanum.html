Hedysarum ecbatanum

Hedysarum ecbatanum, commonly known as the Ecbatana pea, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hedysarum ecbatanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soils.
