Huberia laurina

Huberia laurina, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, bell-shaped fruit that turns from green to red as it matures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Huberia laurina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
