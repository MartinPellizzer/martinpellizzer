Hosta tosana var. caput-avis

Hosta tosana var. caput-avis, commonly known as the "Blue Bird Hosta," is a variegated cultivar characterized by its striking blue-green foliage with white margins.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan, prized for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide a dramatic visual contrast in gardens. It produces small, fragrant white flowers on tall stems in late summer.

Due to its unique coloration and adaptability, it is a popular choice for modern landscaping.
