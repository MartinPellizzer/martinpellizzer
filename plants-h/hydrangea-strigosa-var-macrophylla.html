Hydrangea strigosa var. macrophylla

Hydrangea strigosa var. macrophylla, commonly known as the bigleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, mophead or lacecap flowers that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and white underneath. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
