Hieracium adenoclinium

Hieracium adenoclinium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with basal leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Hieracium adenoclinium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
