Hemicyclia rhakodiskos

Hemicyclia rhakodiskos, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Hemicyclia rhakodiskos is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
