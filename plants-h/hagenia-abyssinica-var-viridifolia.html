Hagenia abyssinica var. viridifolia

Hagenia abyssinica var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed hagenia, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often spiny stems, which are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flower clusters that appear at the ends of the branches, typically in the spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside.

Hagenia abyssinica var. viridifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
