Hedysarum polymorphum var. subsericeum

Hedysarum polymorphum var. subsericeum, commonly known as the silky goatweed, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
