Hieracium squarrosofurcatum

Hieracium squarrosofurcatum, commonly known as the squarroso-furcate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
