Hesperis iriodes

Hesperis iriodes, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Hesperis iriodes is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
