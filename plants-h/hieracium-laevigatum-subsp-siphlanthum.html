Hieracium laevigatum subsp. siphlanthum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

siphlanthum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
