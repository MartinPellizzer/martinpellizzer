Hoarea laciniata

Hoarea laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that give it a lacy appearance, with each leaflet finely divided and often curled. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures, typical of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for humid indoor settings or shaded garden areas.
