Heteropterys escalloniifolia var. saligna

Heteropterys escalloniifolia var. saligna, commonly known as the silky leaf huito, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical, and often hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
