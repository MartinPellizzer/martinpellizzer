Hyptis brachypoda

Hyptis brachypoda, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis brachypoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal remedies.
