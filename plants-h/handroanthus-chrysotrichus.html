Handroanthus chrysotrichus

Handroanthus chrysotrichus, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of branches, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a distinct midrib.

The golden trumpet tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
