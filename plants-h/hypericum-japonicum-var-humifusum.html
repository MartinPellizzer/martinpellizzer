Hypericum japonicum var. humifusum

Hypericum japonicum var. humifusum, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems that become more prominent with age, and its leaves are narrow, oval, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
