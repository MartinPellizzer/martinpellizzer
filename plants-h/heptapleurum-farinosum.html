Heptapleurum farinosum

Heptapleurum farinosum, commonly known as the silky laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate partial shade.
