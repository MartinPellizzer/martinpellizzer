Hibiscus tiliaceus var. genuinus

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. genuinus, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink or red. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or saline soils. Its fibrous roots help stabilize coastal dunes, making it an important plant for erosion control.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
