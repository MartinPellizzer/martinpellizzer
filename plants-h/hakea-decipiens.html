Hakea decipiens

Hakea decipiens, commonly known as the false hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

Hakea decipiens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
