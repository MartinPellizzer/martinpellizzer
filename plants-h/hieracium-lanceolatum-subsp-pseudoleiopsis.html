Hieracium lanceolatum subsp. pseudoleiopsis

Hieracium lanceolatum subsp.

pseudoleiopsis, commonly known as the lance-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is often found in open habitats and is adapted to temperate climates.
