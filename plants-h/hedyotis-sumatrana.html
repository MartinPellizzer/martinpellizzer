Hedyotis sumatrana

Hedyotis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran pennywort, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hedyotis sumatrana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
