Haumaniastrum coriaceum subsp. mitwabense

Haumaniastrum coriaceum subsp.

mitwabense, commonly known as Mitwabense desertweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, succulent leaves adapted to conserve water.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, drought-prone environments. Its structure allows it to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in its habitat.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing minimal forage for local wildlife.
