Hovea trisperma var. crispa

Hovea trisperma var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive crinkled or wavy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Hovea trisperma var. crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
