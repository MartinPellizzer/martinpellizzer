Hypericum paucifolium

Hypericum paucifolium, commonly known as the sparse-leaved St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, clustered flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hypericum paucifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
