Heliotropium inundatum subf. pusillum

Heliotropium inundatum subf.

pusillum, commonly known as the swamp heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical regions. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant has succulent leaves and stems, adapted to thrive in moist or waterlogged soils. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
