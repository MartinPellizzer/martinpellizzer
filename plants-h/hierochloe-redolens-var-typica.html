Hierochloe redolens var. typica

Hierochloe redolens var. typica, commonly known as the sweetgrass or sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is valued for its fragrance and has been used by Indigenous peoples for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.
