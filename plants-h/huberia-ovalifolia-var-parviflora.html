Huberia ovalifolia var. parviflora

Huberia ovalifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique hooded structure covering the reproductive parts. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or under dense vegetation. Its leaves are oval-shaped and glossy, while the flowers are small and usually white or pale pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
