Hieracium knuthianum

Hieracium knuthianum, commonly known as Knuth's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 centimeters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
