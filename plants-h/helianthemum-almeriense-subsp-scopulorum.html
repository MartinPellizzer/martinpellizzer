Helianthemum almeriense subsp. scopulorum

Helianthemum almeriense subsp.

scopulorum, commonly known as the rock sunflower, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a splash of color to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
