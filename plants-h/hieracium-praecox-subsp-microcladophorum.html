Hieracium praecox subsp. microcladophorum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

microcladophorum, commonly known as the small-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows. It has a taproot system and can spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.
