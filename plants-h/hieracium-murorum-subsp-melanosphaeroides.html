Hieracium murorum subsp. melanosphaeroides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

melanosphaeroides, commonly known as blackhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its dark, rounded flower heads and hairy, often reddish stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
