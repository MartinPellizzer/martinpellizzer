Hosackia puberula var. nana

Hosackia puberula var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf prairie clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the central United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact size, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
