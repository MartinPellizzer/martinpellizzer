Hydroglossum dissectum

Hydroglossum dissectum, commonly known as the dissected water-leaf, is a rare aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacey appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters with abundant sunlight and nutrient-rich substrates. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in loose inflorescences, and bloom during the summer months.

Hydroglossum dissectum plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
