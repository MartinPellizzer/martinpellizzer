Herpestis chamaedryoides var. peduncularis

Herpestis chamaedryoides var. peduncularis, commonly known as the purple-flowered herbaceous plant, is a perennial species native to certain regions of Europe.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow from the base, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or violet. The plant typically blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
