Hymenophyllum undulatum var. regenerans

Hymenophyllum undulatum var. regenerans, commonly known as the silver fern, is a member of the Hymenophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The fronds are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It has a unique ability to regenerate from fragmented vegetative parts, which contributes to its survival in challenging conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in botanical gardens.
