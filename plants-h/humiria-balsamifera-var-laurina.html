Humiria balsamifera var. laurina

Humiria balsamifera var. laurina, commonly known as the sweet-scented balsam.

This plant is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant, balsamic fragrance when crushed. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves and small white flowers.

It is often valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its soothing and healing effects.
