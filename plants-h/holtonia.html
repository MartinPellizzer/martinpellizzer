Holtonia

Holtonia, commonly known as sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically red or purple in color. Holtonia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
