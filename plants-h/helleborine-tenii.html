Helleborine tenii

Helleborine tenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, greenish-white flowers with purple markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Helleborine tenii is also notable for its mycorrhizal associations, which aid in nutrient uptake from the soil.
