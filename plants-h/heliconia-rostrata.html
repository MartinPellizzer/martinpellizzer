Heliconia rostrata

Heliconia rostrata, commonly known as the crimson heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, which are composed of numerous bright red bracts. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is known for its striking visual appearance. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
