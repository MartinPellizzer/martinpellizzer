Hieracium cottianum

Hieracium cottianum, commonly known as the Cottian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal and lobed, with a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium cottianum is often found in meadows and rocky areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
