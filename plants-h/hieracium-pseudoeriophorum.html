Hieracium pseudoeriophorum

Hieracium pseudoeriophorum, commonly known as the false sandhill hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium pseudoeriophorum is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
