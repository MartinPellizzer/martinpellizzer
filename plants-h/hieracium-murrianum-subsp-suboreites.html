Hieracium murrianum subsp. suboreites

Hieracium murrianum subsp.

suboreites, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often clustered at the base, while its flowers are small and arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
