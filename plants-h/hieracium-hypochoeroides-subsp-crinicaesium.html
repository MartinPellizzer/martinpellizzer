Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. crinicaesium

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

crinicaesium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowering stems bear clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
