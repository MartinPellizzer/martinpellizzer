Hakea lorea var. fissifolia

Hakea lorea var. fissifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access groundwater.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
