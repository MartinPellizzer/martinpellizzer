Heliotropium albohispidum

Heliotropium albohispidum, commonly known as white-hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

Heliotropium albohispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
