Hyptis globifera

Hyptis globifera, commonly known as the globe flower or Brazilian hyptis, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis globifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in folk medicine.
