Hoya pusilliflora

Hoya pusilliflora, commonly known as the "Baby Bells" or "Ponytail Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, waxy, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy texture and a deep green color. This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

It produces fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in late winter to early spring.
