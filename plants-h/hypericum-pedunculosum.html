Hypericum pedunculosum

Hypericum pedunculosum, commonly known as the yellow garden hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. These flowers are composed of five petals and numerous stamens, giving them a distinctive, sun-like appearance.

Hypericum pedunculosum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil in full sun.
