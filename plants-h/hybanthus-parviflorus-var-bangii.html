Hybanthus parviflorus var. bangii

Hybanthus parviflorus var. bangii, commonly known as the Bangi hybanthus, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
