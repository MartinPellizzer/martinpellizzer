Halimocnemis sclerantha

Halimocnemis sclerantha, commonly known as the hard-stemmed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its densely packed, small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its succulent stems are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle, providing additional protection against desiccation.

Halimocnemis sclerantha is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
