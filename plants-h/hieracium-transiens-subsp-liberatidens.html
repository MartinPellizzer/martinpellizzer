Hieracium transiens subsp. liberatidens

Hieracium transiens subsp.

liberatidens, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
