Hapalochilus lohokii

Hapalochilus lohokii, commonly known as the Lohokii orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats and requires careful cultivation to mimic its natural conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological needs, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
