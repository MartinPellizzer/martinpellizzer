Hyala

Hyala, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant features a slender, woody stem with compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Hyala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
