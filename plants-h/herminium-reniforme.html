Herminium reniforme

Herminium reniforme, commonly known as the kidney-shaped orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, kidney-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale green. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Herminium reniforme is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which play a crucial role in its nutrient uptake.
