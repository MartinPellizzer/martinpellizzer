Hieracium neoplatyphyllum subsp. malacofloccosum

Hieracium neoplatyphyllum subsp.

malacofloccosum, commonly known as the woolly false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
