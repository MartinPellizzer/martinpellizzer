Hieracium canum subsp. diffusum

Hieracium canum subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more narrow. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
