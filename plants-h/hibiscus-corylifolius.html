Hibiscus corylifolius

Hibiscus corylifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Hibiscus corylifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are typically harvested for tea and culinary purposes in some cultures.
