Hypericum perfoliatum subsp. heldreichii

Hypericum perfoliatum subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
