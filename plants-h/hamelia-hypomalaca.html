Hamelia hypomalaca

Hamelia hypomalaca, commonly known as the red torch flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble torches. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, papery bracts.

Hamelia hypomalaca thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
