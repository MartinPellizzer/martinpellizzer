Hemitelia bella

Hemitelia bella, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hemitelia bella is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
