Hyoseris glauca

Hyoseris glauca, commonly known as blue dandelion, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

Hyoseris glauca is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for wetland environments.
