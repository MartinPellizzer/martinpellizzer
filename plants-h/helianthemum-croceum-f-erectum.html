Helianthemum croceum f. erectum

Helianthemum croceum f. erectum, commonly known as the upright form of the yellow rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It typically grows on rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
