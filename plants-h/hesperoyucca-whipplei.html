Hesperoyucca whipplei

Hesperoyucca whipplei, commonly known as the Turk's cap yucca, is a species of plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features tall, sword-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water storage.

Hesperoyucca whipplei is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
