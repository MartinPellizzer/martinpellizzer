Hieracium glaucum subsp. coleoglaucum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

coleoglaucum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellowish flowers with a distinctive blue or purple tinge. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
