Hieracium euchaetium subsp. leptophytomorphum

Hieracium euchaetium subsp.

leptophytomorphum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and is often found in sunny, well-drained environments.
