Hesperis violacea

Hesperis violacea, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis violacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
