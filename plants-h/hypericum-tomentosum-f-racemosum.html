Hypericum tomentosum f. racemosum

Hypericum tomentosum f. racemosum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats. It has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are typically arranged in racemes, giving the plant its specific epithet "racemosum.".
