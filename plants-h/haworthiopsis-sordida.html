Haworthiopsis sordida

Haworthiopsis sordida, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a member of the Asphodelaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with fleshy, bluish-green leaves that have a distinctive white or grayish bloom. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers on thin stalks during the summer months. It is a low-maintenance succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
