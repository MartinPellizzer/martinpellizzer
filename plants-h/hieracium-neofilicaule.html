Hieracium neofilicaule

Hieracium neofilicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium neofilicaule is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
