Hieracium murorum subsp. glaucoviolascens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

glaucoviolascens, commonly known as the glaucoviolascens hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local insect populations.
