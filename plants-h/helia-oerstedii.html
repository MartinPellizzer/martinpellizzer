Helia oerstedii

Helia oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Helia oerstedii is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of forests.
