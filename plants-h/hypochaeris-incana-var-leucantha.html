Hypochaeris incana var. leucantha

Hypochaeris incana var. leucantha, commonly known as white hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Hypochaeris incana var. leucantha is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
