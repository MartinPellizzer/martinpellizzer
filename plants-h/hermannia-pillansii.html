Hermannia pillansii

Hermannia pillansii, commonly known as the dwarf rain lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, stocky stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Hermannia pillansii is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens and containers.
