Helichrysum elatum var. glutinosum

Helichrysum elatum var. glutinosum, commonly known as sticky helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small sunbursts. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
