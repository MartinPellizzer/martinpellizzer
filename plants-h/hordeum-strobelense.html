Hordeum strobelense

Hordeum strobelense, commonly known as Strobel's barley, is a rare and ancient species of barley native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pale yellowish grains. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its genome is of interest to researchers studying wild barley for potential agricultural improvements.

Due to its limited distribution and endangered status, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
