Helianthus annuus var. trizonatus

Helianthus annuus var. trizonatus, commonly known as the tri-colored sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its distinctive three-colored ray florets.

This plant is native to North America and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features a robust stem with rough, hairy leaves. The flowers are yellow with a central disk that is often darker, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
