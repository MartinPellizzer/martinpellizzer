Hieracium arvicola subsp. apatorium

Hieracium arvicola subsp.

apatorium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal and cauline leaves that are hairy and often have a glandular surface. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
