Herissantia trichoda

Herissantia trichoda, commonly known as the hairy leaf vine, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and climbing growth habit.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Herissantia trichoda thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
