Hakea mcgillivrayi

Hakea mcgillivrayi, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought.

Hakea mcgillivrayi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
