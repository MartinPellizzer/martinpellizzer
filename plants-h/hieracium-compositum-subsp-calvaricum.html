Hieracium compositum subsp. calvaricum

Hieracium compositum subsp.

calvaricum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
