Hieracium lampadiotum

Hieracium lampadiotum, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Hieracium lampadiotum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
