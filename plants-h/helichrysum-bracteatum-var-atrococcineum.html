Helichrysum bracteatum var. atrococcineum

Helichrysum bracteatum var. atrococcineum, commonly known as the red-stemmed everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, papery bracts that range in color from orange to red. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden beneath the showy bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
