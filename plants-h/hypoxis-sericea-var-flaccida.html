Hypoxis sericea var. flaccida

Hypoxis sericea var. flaccida, commonly known as the softleaf false arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
