Hybanthus parviflorus f. membranaceus

Hybanthus parviflorus f. membranaceus, commonly known as the membranaceus form of the small-flowered hybanthus, is a variant of a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and the subtle beauty of its blooms.
