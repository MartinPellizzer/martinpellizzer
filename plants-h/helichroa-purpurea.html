Helichroa purpurea

Helichroa purpurea, commonly known as the purple helichrysum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and showy, golden-yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Helichroa purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
