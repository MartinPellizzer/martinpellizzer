Heliotropium pseudolongiflorum

Heliotropium pseudolongiflorum, commonly known as the false heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers emit a sweet, vanilla-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
