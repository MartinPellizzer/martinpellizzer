Hieracium auriculoides subsp. kandawanicum

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

kandawanicum, commonly known as the Kandawani hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow ray flowers and dense, hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands and grassy slopes. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially threatened species in its native range.
