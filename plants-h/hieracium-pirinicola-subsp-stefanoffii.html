Hieracium pirinicola subsp. stefanoffii

Hieracium pirinicola subsp.

stefanoffii, commonly known as the Stefanoff's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous ray florets that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
