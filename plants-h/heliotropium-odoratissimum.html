Heliotropium odoratissimum

Heliotropium odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet pitcher plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pitcher-shaped leaves that are adapted to trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique morphological features.
