Hieracium levicaule subsp. supertextum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

supertextum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
