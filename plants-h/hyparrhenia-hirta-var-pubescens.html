Hyparrhenia hirta var. pubescens

Hyparrhenia hirta var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
