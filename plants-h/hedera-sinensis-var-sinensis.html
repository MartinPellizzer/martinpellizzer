Hedera sinensis var. sinensis

Hedera sinensis var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to eastern Asia.

It features dark green, lobed leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by reddish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and ability to climb structures.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ground cover and vertical gardening.
