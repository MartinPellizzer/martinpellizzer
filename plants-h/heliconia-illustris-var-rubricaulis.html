Heliconia illustris var. rubricaulis

Heliconia illustris var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds and bats, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
