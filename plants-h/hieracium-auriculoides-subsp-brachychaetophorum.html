Hieracium auriculoides subsp. brachychaetophorum

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

brachychaetophorum, commonly known as the narrow-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

This subspecies is often found in grassy areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
