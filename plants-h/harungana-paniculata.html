Harungana paniculata

Harungana paniculata, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy yellow flowers with a red throat. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Harungana paniculata is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
