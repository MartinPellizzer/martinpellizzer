Hieracium crispum

Hieracium crispum, commonly known as the curlyhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette at the base, with the upper leaves being curled and lobed.

Hieracium crispum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
