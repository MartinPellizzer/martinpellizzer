Hedysarum ferganense var. poncinsii

Hedysarum ferganense var. poncinsii, commonly known as the Fergana milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its biological classification places it within the Fabaceae family, reflecting its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
