Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. integrilaterum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

integrilaterum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower and more linear. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, consisting of numerous small disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with both surfaces bearing a dense layer of soft, white hairs.
