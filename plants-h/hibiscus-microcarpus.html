Hibiscus microcarpus

Hibiscus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited hibiscus, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Hibiscus microcarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
