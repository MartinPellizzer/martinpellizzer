Hieracium hirsutum subsp. insignitum

Hieracium hirsutum subsp.

insignitum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellowish-orange composite flowers.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have serrated edges. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
