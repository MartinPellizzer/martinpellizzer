Halopegia cadelliana

Halopegia cadelliana, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates. It is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Halopegia cadelliana is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
