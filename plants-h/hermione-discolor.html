Hermione discolor

Hermione discolor, commonly known as the variable ghostflower, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, ghostly white petals that change color slightly depending on light conditions. The plant has a delicate, herbaceous structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It typically blooms in late spring, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, Hermione discolor is considered a conservation concern.
