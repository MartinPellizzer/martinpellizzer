Hieracium guentheri-beckii subsp. portentosum

Hieracium guentheri-beckii subsp.

portentosum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is notable for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions outside its native range.
