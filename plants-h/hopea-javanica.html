Hopea javanica

Hopea javanica, commonly known as Javan hopea, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.

Hopea javanica also plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
