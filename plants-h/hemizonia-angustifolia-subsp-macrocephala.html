Hemizonia angustifolia subsp. macrocephala

Hemizonia angustifolia subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces showy, terminal inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
