Hieracium austriacum subsp. pseudolonganum

Hieracium austriacum subsp.

pseudolonganum, commonly known as the Austrian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, while its leaves are rough and hairy.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
