Halodule × serratifolia

Halodule × serratifolia, commonly known as the Florida turtlegrass, is a halophytic seagrass species native to the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of the United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in shallow, brackish or saltwater environments. The plant has a rhizome-based growth system, allowing it to spread across the seafloor and form dense underwater meadows. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with both male and female reproductive structures present on the same plant.

Halodule × serratifolia plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.
