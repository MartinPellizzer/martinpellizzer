Hirculus fragrans

Hirculus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented hirculus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Hirculus fragrans thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
