Hyptis latifolia

Hyptis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis latifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
