Hieracium vulgatum subsp. haematochromum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

haematochromum, commonly known as the bloodleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a reddish or purplish tinge, especially when damaged or in autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found along roadsides and in meadows.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
