Hypericum euphorbiifolium

Hypericum euphorbiifolium, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with thin, woody stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

It is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a hardy species in arid environments.
