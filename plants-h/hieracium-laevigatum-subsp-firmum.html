Hieracium laevigatum subsp. firmum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

firmum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with stems that are often reddish and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, consisting of numerous small florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
