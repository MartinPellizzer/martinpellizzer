Hieracium filifloccum

Hieracium filifloccum, commonly known as the fily floccate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a reddish tinge, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

Hieracium filifloccum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
