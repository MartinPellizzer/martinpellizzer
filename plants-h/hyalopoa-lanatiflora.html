Hyalopoa lanatiflora

Hyalopoa lanatiflora, commonly known as the woolly-flowered hyalopoa, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
