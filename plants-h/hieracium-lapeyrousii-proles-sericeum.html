Hieracium lapeyrousii proles sericeum

Hieracium lapeyrousii proles sericeum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, giving it a silky appearance, which is reflected in its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
