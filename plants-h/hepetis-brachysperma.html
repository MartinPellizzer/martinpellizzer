Hepetis brachysperma

Hepetis brachysperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
