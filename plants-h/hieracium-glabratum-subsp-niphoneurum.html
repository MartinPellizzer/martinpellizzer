Hieracium glabratum subsp. niphoneurum

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

niphoneurum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, giving it its subspecies name "glabratum.".

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
