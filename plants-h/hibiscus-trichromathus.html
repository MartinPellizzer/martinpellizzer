Hibiscus trichromathus

Hibiscus trichromathus, commonly known as the tri-color hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display three distinct colors—typically red, yellow, and white—on the same bloom. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in summer and are followed by seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Hibiscus trichromathus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
