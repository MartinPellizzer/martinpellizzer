Heliotropium sarothroclados

Heliotropium sarothroclados, commonly known as the coastal heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought, making it a hardy species found in coastal ecosystems.
