Hygrophila subsessilis

Hygrophila subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water hyssop, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along its stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

Hygrophila subsessilis is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
