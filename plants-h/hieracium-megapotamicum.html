Hieracium megapotamicum

Hieracium megapotamicum, commonly known as the South American hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is adapted to thrive in temperate to subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
