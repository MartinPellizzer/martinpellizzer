Helianthus grandiceps

Helianthus grandiceps, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads with dark brown central disks. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet, featuring rough, hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus grandiceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun with well-drained soil.
