Hieracium decessum

Hieracium decessum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
