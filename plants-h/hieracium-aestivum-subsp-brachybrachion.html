Hieracium aestivum subsp. brachybrachion

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

brachybrachion, commonly known as the short-bract hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

This subspecies is characterized by its short, broad bracts that distinguish it from other hawkweed species.
