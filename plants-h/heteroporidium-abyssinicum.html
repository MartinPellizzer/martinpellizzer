Heteroporidium abyssinicum

Heteroporidium abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, leafy structure and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a thalloid body, which is a flat, ribbon-like structure covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often used as an indicator species for undisturbed ecosystems.
