Helianthus pauciflorus var. subrhomboideus

Helianthus pauciflorus var. subrhomboideus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow ligulate floret structure. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
