Hydrocotyle perplexa

Hydrocotyle perplexa, commonly known as the hairy water penny.

It is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America. The species is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to aquatic environments and its role in wetland ecosystems.
