Homalomena monandra

Homalomena monandra, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces striking red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Homalomena monandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
