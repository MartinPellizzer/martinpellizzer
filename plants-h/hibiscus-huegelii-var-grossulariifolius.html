Hibiscus huegelii var. grossulariifolius

Hibiscus huegelii var. grossulariifolius, commonly known as the giant rose mallow, is a striking perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pink to red, with a prominent central stigma. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to wetland gardens and native plant landscapes.
