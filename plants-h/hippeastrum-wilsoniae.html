Hippeastrum wilsoniae

Hippeastrum wilsoniae, commonly known as Wilson's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its native arid environment. It blooms in late spring or early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The bulb of Hippeastrum wilsoniae is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
