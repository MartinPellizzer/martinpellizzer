Hieracium cordobense

Hieracium cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, displaying yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

Hieracium cordobense is often found in open habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
