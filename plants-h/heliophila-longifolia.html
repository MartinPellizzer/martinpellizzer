Heliophila longifolia

Heliophila longifolia, commonly known as long-leaved heliophila, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Heliophila longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
