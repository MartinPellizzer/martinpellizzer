Hypericum Hyssopifolium Var. Microcalycinum

Small-leaved st. john's wort, scientifically known as Hypericum hyssopifolium var. microcalycinum, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is distinguished by its small, rounded leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. This variant of st. john's wort is particularly notable for its compact growth habit and unique floral structure. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern. Its presence in the wild highlights the importance of preserving biodiversity in fragile ecosystems.
