Hypopogon brevipes

Hypopogon brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pink or white and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.
