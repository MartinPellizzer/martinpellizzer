Hieracium magyaricum subsp. thaumasium

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

thaumasium, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head.

The plant is native to Central Europe and is often found in dry meadows and grasslands. It has small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
