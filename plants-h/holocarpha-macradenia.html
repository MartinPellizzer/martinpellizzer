Holocarpha macradenia

Holocarpha macradenia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in a thick, fibrous coating. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of durable wood.
