Hyptis cordifolia

Hyptis cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the warm regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Hyptis cordifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
