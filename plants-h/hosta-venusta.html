Hosta venusta

Hosta venusta, commonly known as the "Shining Vein Hosta," is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and waxy leaves that display a striking coloration, often featuring a bright yellow margin with a green center. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in loose racemes. Its foliage is particularly noted for its ornamental value, making it a popular choice in shaded gardens.

Hosta venusta thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
