Hieracium zelenaglavense

Hieracium zelenaglavense, commonly known as the green-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets.

Hieracium zelenaglavense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a potential weed in some regions.
