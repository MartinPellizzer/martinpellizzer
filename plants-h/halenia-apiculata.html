Halenia apiculata

Halenia apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved halenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Halenia apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
