Hypericum leptocladum

Hypericum leptocladum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin.

Hypericum leptocladum is known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other species in the genus.
