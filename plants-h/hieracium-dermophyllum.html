Hieracium dermophyllum

Hieracium dermophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed, with a rosette-like growth habit. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, surrounded by bracts.

Hieracium dermophyllum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.
