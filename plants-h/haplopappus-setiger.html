Haplopappus setiger

Haplopappus setiger, commonly known as the silky pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster. Its seeds are covered in silky hairs, aiding in their dispersal by the wind.

Haplopappus setiger is typically found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
