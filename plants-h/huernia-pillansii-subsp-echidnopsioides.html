Huernia pillansii subsp. echidnopsioides

Huernia pillansii subsp.

echidnopsioides, commonly known as the horned moon cactus, is a succulent native to southern Africa. It features a swollen, fleshy stem that resembles a cactus, with a distinctive horn-like protrusion at the top.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
