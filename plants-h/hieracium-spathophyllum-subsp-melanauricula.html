Hieracium spathophyllum subsp. melanauricula

Hieracium spathophyllum subsp.

melanauricula, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with dark veins. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.
