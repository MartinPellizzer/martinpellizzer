Hieracium flexuosifrons

Hieracium flexuosifrons, commonly known as the flexible hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, which is typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Hieracium flexuosifrons is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
