Hibiscus elegans

Hibiscus elegans, commonly known as the tropical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, yellow, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing seeds. Hibiscus elegans thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
