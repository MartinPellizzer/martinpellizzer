Hieracium flocciceps

Hieracium flocciceps, commonly known as the cottony hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
