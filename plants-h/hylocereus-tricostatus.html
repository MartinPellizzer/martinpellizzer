Hylocereus tricostatus

Hylocereus tricostatus, commonly known as the three-ridged dragon fruit, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. Its fruit, which is typically red or yellow with a white pulp, is rich in nutrients and antioxidants.

The botanical features of Hylocereus tricostatus make it both an ornamental and edible plant, valued for its unique appearance and nutritional benefits.
