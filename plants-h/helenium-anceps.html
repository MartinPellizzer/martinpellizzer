Helenium anceps

Helenium anceps, commonly known as the "sneaker flower," is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant, orange-red ray flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium anceps is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
