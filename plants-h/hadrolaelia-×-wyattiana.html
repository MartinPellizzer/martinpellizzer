Hadrolaelia × wyattiana

Hadrolaelia × wyattiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Hadrolaelia moorei and Pterygodactylus alpinus.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains in South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with pale pink to white petals and a deep violet lip. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a branching pattern with multiple stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
