Hieracium murorum subsp. subtorticeps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subtorticeps, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
