Heracleum lanatum

Heracleum lanatum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to North America.

It features a robust, hollow stem covered in fine, white hairs, and large, umbrella-shaped clusters of small white or greenish flowers. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

The roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is known for its potential to cause skin irritation upon contact.
