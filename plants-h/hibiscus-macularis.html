Hibiscus macularis

Hibiscus macularis, commonly known as the tropical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are usually green with a slightly hairy texture. Hibiscus macularis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking blooms and tropical appearance.
