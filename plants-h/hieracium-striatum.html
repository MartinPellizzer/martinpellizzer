Hieracium striatum

Hieracium striatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils. It is native to Europe but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some areas.
