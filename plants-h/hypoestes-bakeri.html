Hypoestes bakeri

Hypoestes bakeri, commonly known as the "Magic Beanplant," is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble small flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers that are often overlooked compared to the showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Hypoestes bakeri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
