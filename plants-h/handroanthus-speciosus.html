Handroanthus speciosus

Handroanthus speciosus, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
