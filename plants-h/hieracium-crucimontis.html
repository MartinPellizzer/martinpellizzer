Hieracium crucimontis

Hieracium crucimontis, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium crucimontis is often found in mountainous areas, contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
