Hieracium jablonicense subsp. barnarense

Hieracium jablonicense subsp.

barnarense, commonly known as the Barnaren hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its spread within its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
