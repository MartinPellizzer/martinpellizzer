Haplopappus macronema var. linearis

Haplopappus macronema var. linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing small yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences.

The variety linearis is distinguished by its elongated, linear leaves, which are narrower than those of the typical form.
