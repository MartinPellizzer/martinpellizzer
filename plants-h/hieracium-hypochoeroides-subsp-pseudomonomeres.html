Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. pseudomonomeres

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

pseudomonomeres, commonly known as the false single-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by involucral bracts.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal potential in traditional herbal practices.
