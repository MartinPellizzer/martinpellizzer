Hieracium murorum subsp. sudermannicum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sudermannicum, commonly known as the southern mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
