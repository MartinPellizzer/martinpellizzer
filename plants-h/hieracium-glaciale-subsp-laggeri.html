Hieracium glaciale subsp. laggeri

Hieracium glaciale subsp.

laggeri, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and yellow flowering heads that appear in late summer.

The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to tolerate cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
