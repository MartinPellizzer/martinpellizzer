Helichrysum decaryi var. montis-papangae

Helichrysum decaryi var. montis-papangae, commonly known as the mountain helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
