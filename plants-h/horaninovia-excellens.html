Horaninovia excellens

Horaninovia excellens, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the showy blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Horaninovia excellens is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
