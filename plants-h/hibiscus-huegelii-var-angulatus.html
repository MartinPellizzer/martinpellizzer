Hibiscus huegelii var. angulatus

Hibiscus huegelii var. angulatus, commonly known as the angular hibiscus, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has angular, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus huegelii var. angulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
