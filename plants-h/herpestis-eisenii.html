Herpestis eisenii

Herpestis eisenii, commonly known as the yellow vervain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 feet tall. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender to violet in color, arranged in loose clusters at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with toothed margins, and are usually gray-green in appearance.

Herpestis eisenii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
