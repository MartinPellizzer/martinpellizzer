Hieracium schmidtii subsp. floccozum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

floccozum, commonly known as the fluffy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and hairy, woolly leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
