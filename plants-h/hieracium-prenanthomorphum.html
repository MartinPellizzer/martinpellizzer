Hieracium prenanthomorphum

Hieracium prenanthomorphum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
