Helianthemum lagascae

Helianthemum lagascae, commonly known as the Canary Island snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display to its foliage. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dense and provide a lush, green appearance throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Helianthemum lagascae is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
