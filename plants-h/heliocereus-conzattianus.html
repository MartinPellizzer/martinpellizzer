Heliocereus conzattianus

Heliocereus conzattianus, commonly known as the sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its ability to produce fragrant, night-blooming flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and open only during the evening.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
