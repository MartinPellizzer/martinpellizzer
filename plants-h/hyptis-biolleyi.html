Hyptis biolleyi

Hyptis biolleyi, commonly known as the velvetleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis biolleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
