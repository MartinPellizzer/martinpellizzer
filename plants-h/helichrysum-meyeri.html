Helichrysum meyeri

Helichrysum meyeri, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain colorful even when dried. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is usually grey-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Helichrysum meyeri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
