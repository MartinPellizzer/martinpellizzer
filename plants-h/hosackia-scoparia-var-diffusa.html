Hosackia scoparia var. diffusa

Hosackia scoparia var. diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
