Hibiscus cabralensis

Hibiscus cabralensis, commonly known as the Cape Verde hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry habitats, characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually ovate and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Hibiscus cabralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
