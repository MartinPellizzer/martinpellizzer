Hieracium rigidipilum

Hieracium rigidipilum, commonly known as rigid-hair hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium rigidipilum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to colonize areas with minimal competition.
