Herniaria fruticosa

Herniaria fruticosa, commonly known as sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

Herniaria fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
