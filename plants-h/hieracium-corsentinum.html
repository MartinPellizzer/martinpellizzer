Hieracium corsentinum

Hieracium corsentinum, commonly known as Corsican hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal areas.
