Hypericum adenocarpum

Hypericum adenocarpum, commonly known as the sticky St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its stems are woody and can become branched as the plant matures.

Hypericum adenocarpum is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
