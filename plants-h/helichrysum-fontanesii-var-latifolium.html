Helichrysum fontanesii var. latifolium

Helichrysum fontanesii var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf immortelle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, golden-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are narrow to broadly ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
