Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. pseudopantotrichum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

pseudopantotrichum, commonly known as the false purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
