Hypericum thymbrifolium

Hypericum thymbrifolium, commonly known as the fragrant St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can interact with certain medications.
