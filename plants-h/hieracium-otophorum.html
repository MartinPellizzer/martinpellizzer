Hieracium otophorum

Hieracium otophorum, commonly known as the "ear-hanging hawkweed," is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks in temperate regions. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Hieracium otophorum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature.
