Helosis ruficeps

Helosis ruficeps, commonly known as the red-headed helosis, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically clustered in small inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Helosis ruficeps is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and relatively low maintenance requirements.
