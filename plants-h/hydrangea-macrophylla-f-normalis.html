Hydrangea macrophylla f. normalis

Hydrangea macrophylla f. normalis, commonly known as the bigleaf hydrangea, is a popular ornamental shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Japan. The plant's foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in length. Its flower color is influenced by soil pH, producing shades of blue, pink, or white.

This variety thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
