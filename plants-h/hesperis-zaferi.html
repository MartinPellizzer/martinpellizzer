Hesperis zaferi

Hesperis zaferi, commonly known as Turkish candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
