Hieracium tescorum

Hieracium tescorum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium tescorum is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
