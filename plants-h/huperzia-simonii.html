Huperzia simonii

Huperzia simonii, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it grows in moist, shaded environments. Huperzia simonii is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or forested areas, often forming dense mats.

Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
