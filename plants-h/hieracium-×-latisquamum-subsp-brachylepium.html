Hieracium × latisquamum subsp. brachylepium

Hieracium × latisquamum subsp.

brachylepium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is known for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
