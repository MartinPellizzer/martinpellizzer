Hardwickia binata

Hardwickia binata, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, light grey bark and compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.

Hardwickia binata is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
