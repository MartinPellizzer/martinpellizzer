Hieracium lachenalii subsp. fuscicolor

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

fuscicolor, commonly known as the dark-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the stem tips. Its subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more intense flower color compared to other hawkweed varieties.

It is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain Mediterranean habitats.
