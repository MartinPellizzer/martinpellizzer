Hieracium canipedioides

Hieracium canipedioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy surface, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
