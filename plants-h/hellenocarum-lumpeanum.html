Hellenocarum lumpeanum

Hellenocarum lumpeanum, commonly known as the Lumpean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hellenocarum lumpeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
