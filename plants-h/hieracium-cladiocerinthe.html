Hieracium cladiocerinthe

Hieracium cladiocerinthe, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium cladiocerinthe is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many habitats.
