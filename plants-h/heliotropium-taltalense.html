Heliotropium taltalense

Heliotropium taltalense, commonly known as the Taltal heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, clustered flowers with a characteristic violet or purple color. The plant thrives in arid and saline environments, often found in coastal dunes and rocky soils. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Heliotropium taltalense is notable for its ability to tolerate high salt levels and strong coastal winds.
