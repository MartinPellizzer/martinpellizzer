Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp. miansarofii

Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp.

miansarofii, commonly known as the Miansarof hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and is native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and slender.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
