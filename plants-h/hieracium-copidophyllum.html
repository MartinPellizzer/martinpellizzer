Hieracium copidophyllum

Hieracium copidophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
