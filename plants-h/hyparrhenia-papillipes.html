Hyparrhenia papillipes

Hyparrhenia papillipes, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silvery-blue appearance due to the presence of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia papillipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a ground cover.
