Hebe lyallii

Hebe lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tubular or bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white, during the spring and summer months. Hebe lyallii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and long blooming period.
