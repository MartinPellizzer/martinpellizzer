Hedaroma fimbriatum

Hedaroma fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed hederoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive fringed edge. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Hedaroma fimbriatum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
