Hoodia pilifera subsp. annulata

Hoodia pilifera subsp.

annulata, commonly known as the hooded fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
