Hieracium murorum subsp. opeatodontum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

opeatodontum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
