Hieracium perampliforme

Hieracium perampliforme, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium perampliforme is a perennial herb that can form dense colonies, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
