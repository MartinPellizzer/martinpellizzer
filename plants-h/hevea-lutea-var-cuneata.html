Hevea lutea var. cuneata

Hevea lutea var. cuneata, commonly known as the yellow rubber tree, is a variant of the rubber tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish leaves and latex-producing properties, making it valuable for rubber production. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and a smooth texture.

This variety is often cultivated for both its economic and ornamental value in tropical regions.
