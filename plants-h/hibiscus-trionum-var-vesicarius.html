Hibiscus trionum var. vesicarius

Hibiscus trionum var. vesicarius, commonly known as the bladderleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round, bladder-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hibiscus trionum var. vesicarius is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
