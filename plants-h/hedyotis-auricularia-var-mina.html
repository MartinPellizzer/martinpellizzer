Hedyotis auricularia var. mina

Hedyotis auricularia var. mina, commonly known as the small earleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often rounded or heart-shaped, with a distinctive auriculate base. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
