Hyphaene multiformis subsp. semiplaena

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

semiplaena, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. Its stems are segmented and can grow quite tall, making it a striking feature in both natural and cultivated landscapes.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
