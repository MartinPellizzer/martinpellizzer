Hedyotis caerulea f. albiflora

Hedyotis caerulea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered blue false indigo, is a variant of the species Hedyotis caerulea.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
