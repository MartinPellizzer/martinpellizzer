Hakea toodyay

Hakea toodyay, commonly known as the Toodyay hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

The Toodyay hakea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
