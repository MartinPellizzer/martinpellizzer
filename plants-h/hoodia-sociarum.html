Hoodia sociarum

Hoodia sociarum, commonly known as the desert candelabra, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually reduced to small spines, minimizing water loss.

Hoodia sociarum is also known for its unique chemical composition, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
