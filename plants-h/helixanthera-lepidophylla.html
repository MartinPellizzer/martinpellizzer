Helixanthera lepidophylla

Helixanthera lepidophylla, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique member of the Ascomycota division, known for its remarkable ability to survive extreme desiccation.

This plant is characterized by its ability to curl into a tight, desiccated ball when dry, only to "resurrect" when exposed to moisture. It thrives in arid environments, such as deserts and rocky areas, where it can remain dormant for long periods. The plant's survival mechanism involves the production of protective proteins and sugars that prevent cellular damage during dehydration.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh conditions and its role in ecological studies of extremophiles.
