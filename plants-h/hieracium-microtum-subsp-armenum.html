Hieracium microtum subsp. armenum

Hieracium microtum subsp.

armenum, commonly known as Armenian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed or serrated appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
