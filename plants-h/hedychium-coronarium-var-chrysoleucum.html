Hedychium coronarium var. chrysoleucum

Hedychium coronarium var. chrysoleucum, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is known for its fragrant blooms. It typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
