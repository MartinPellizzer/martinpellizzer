Hieracium lachenalii subsp. perversum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

perversum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a basal rosette formation. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a characteristic plant of the Mediterranean region.
