Horsfieldia polyspherula var. sumatrana

Horsfieldia polyspherula var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the rainforests of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
