Herpestis ranaria var. panamensis

Herpestis ranaria var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panama lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
