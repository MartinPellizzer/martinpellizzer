Hieracium anfractum subsp. chlorodes

Hieracium anfractum subsp.

chlorodes, commonly known as the green-spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its greenish-yellow flower heads and the presence of glandular trichomes on its leaves. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
