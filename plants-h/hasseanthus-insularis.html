Hasseanthus insularis

Hasseanthus insularis, commonly known as the island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Hasseanthus insularis thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.
