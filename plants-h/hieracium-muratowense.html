Hieracium muratowense

Hieracium muratowense, commonly known as the Muratowense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
