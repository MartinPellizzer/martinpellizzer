Houstonia biloba

Houstonia biloba, commonly known as the Carolina spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Houstonia biloba is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for early pollinators.
