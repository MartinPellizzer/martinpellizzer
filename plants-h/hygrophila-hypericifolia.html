Hygrophila hypericifolia

Hygrophila hypericifolia, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along waterways.

Hygrophila hypericifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
