Helichrysum retrorsum var. retrorsum

Helichrysum retrorsum var. retrorsum, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to produce dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils and is often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are highly resistant to drying, making it popular in floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, preferring arid or semi-arid environments.
