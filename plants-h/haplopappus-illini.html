Haplopappus illini

Haplopappus illini, commonly known as the Illinois pappus, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation.

Haplopappus illini is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
