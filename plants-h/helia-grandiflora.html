Helia grandiflora

Helia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora helia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, large, and often brightly colored flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in tropical or subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Helia grandiflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
