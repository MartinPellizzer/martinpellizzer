Hackelia cinerea

Hackelia cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf false jimsonweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hackelia cinerea is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

Its hairy foliage and clustered flowers make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
