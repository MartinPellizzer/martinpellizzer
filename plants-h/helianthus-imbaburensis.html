Helianthus imbaburensis

Helianthus imbaburensis, commonly known as the Imbabure sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Tanzania.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its large, yellow composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Helianthus imbaburensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological importance in its native environment.
