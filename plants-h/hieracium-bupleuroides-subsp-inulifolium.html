Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. inulifolium

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

inulifolium, commonly known as the inulifoliate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive inulifoliate arrangement, giving it a unique appearance.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early autumn.
