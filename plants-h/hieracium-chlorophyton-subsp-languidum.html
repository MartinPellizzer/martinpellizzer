Hieracium chlorophyton subsp. languidum

Hieracium chlorophyton subsp.

languidum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
