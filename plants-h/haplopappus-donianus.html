Haplopappus donianus

Haplopappus donianus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that are easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Haplopappus donianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
