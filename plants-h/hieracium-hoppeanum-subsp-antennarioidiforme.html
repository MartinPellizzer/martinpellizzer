Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. antennarioidiforme

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

antennarioidiforme, commonly known as the horned hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flower heads are yellow with a distinctive horn-like structure at the base.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
