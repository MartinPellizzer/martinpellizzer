Hakea cygnus subsp. cygnus

Hakea cygnus subsp.

cygnus, commonly known as the Grey Hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, woody cones that are often covered in a grey or whitish bloom.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, displaying drought tolerance and fire resilience. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
