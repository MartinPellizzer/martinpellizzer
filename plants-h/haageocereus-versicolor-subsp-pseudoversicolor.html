Haageocereus versicolor subsp. pseudoversicolor

Haageocereus versicolor subsp.

pseudoversicolor, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and the ability to produce vibrant, star-shaped flowers at night.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
