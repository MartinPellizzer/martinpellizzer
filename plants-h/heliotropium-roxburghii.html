Heliotropium roxburghii

Heliotropium roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are known to follow the sun's movement, a phenomenon called heliotropism, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
