Humboltia pachyphylla

Humboltia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved humboldtia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
