Hibiscus wercklei

Hibiscus wercklei, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in summer. Hibiscus wercklei thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
