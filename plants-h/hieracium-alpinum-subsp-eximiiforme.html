Hieracium alpinum subsp. eximiiforme

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

eximiiforme, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small, bright yellow florets arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh alpine conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
