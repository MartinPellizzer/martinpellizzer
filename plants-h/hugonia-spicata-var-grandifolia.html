Hugonia spicata var. grandifolia

Hugonia spicata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hugonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
