Hordeum vulgare var. glabrispicatum

Hordeum vulgare var. glabrispicatum, commonly known as the smooth awned barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its reduced awn length and smoother surface.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is primarily cultivated for its grains, which are used in food and animal feed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces cylindrical spikelets with small, naked grains. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its role in agricultural systems.
