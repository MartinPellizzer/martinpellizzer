Hieracium perargutum

Hieracium perargutum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and can produce a sticky substance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It is native to Europe and is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
