Hemerocallis aurantiaca var. major

Hemerocallis aurantiaca var. major, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a hybrid variety of the daylily genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a rhizomatous perennial, typically reaching heights of 2 to 3 feet. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
