Hibiscus coriaceus

Hibiscus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in summer. Hibiscus coriaceus is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral displays.
