Hieracium mendocinum

Hieracium mendocinum, commonly known as Mendo goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to California.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late summer into early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of pollinators.
