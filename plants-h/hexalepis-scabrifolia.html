Hexalepis scabrifolia

Hexalepis scabrifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. Hexalepis scabrifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
