Hordeum vulgare nothosubsp. intermedium

Hordeum vulgare nothosubsp.

intermedium, commonly known as intermediate barley, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Hordeum vulgare and other Hordeum species. It exhibits characteristics intermediate between its parental species, often showing a mix of morphological and genetic traits.

This nothosubspecies is typically found in regions where its parent species overlap geographically. It has intermediate growth habits, often thriving in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic bridge between different barley species, contributing to the study of plant hybridization.
