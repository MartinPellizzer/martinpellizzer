Humboltia fimbriata

Humboltia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed lily, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Humboltia fimbriata thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes or scrub habitats.
