Hyparrhenia jaegeriana

Hyparrhenia jaegeriana, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems. Hyparrhenia jaegeriana is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
