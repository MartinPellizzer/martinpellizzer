Hieracium gymnophyllum

Hieracium gymnophyllum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers from June to August.

Hieracium gymnophyllum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
