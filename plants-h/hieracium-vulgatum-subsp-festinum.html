Hieracium vulgatum subsp. festinum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

festinum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
