Halimium brasiliense f. angustifolium

Halimium brasiliense f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in spring and summer, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
