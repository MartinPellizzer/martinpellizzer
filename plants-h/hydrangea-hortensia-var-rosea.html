Hydrangea hortensia var. rosea

Hydrangea hortensia var. rosea, commonly known as the pink hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from pale pink to deep red, depending on soil pH. The plant produces sterile flowers, which are actually clusters of small, white to pink florets. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining, acidic soil to achieve its vibrant coloration.

Hydrangea hortensia var. rosea is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and seasonal interest.
