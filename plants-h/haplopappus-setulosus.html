Haplopappus setulosus

Haplopappus setulosus, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving the plant its common name.
