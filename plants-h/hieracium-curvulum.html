Hieracium curvulum

Hieracium curvulum, commonly known as the curly hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its clustered, yellowish-green flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and a common weed in agricultural areas.
