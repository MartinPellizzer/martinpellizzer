Hieracium pardoanum

Hieracium pardoanum, commonly known as the Pardoan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pardoanum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
