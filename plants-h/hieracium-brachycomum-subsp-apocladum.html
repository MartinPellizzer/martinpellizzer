Hieracium brachycomum subsp. apocladum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

apocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
