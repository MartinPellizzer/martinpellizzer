Hesperis hartwegiana

Hesperis hartwegiana, commonly known as Hartweg's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis hartwegiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
