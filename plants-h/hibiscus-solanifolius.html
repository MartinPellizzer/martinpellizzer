Hibiscus solanifolius

Hibiscus solanifolius, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus solanifolius is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
