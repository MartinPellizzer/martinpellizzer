Hieracium lasiopodum

Hieracium lasiopodum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellow-orange flower head composed of numerous small florets. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Hieracium lasiopodum is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and medicinal properties in some species.
