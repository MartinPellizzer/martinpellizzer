Helianthus coloradensis

Helianthus coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado sunflower, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces numerous seeds in its flower heads, which are dispersed by wind.

Helianthus coloradensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
