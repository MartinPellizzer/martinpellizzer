Hieracium alvense

Hieracium alvense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium alvense is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
