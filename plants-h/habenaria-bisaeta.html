Habenaria bisaeta

Habenaria bisaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
