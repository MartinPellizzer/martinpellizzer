Hydatella filamentosa

Hydatella filamentosa, commonly known as the slender hydatella, is a small, aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has thin, filamentous stems that often form dense, floating mats on the water surface. Its leaves are small, scale-like structures that help it absorb nutrients from the water.

Hydatella filamentosa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
