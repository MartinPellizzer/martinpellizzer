Habenaria oldhamii

Habenaria oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's butterfly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and is characterized by its delicate, butterfly-like flowers. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in variable environmental conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the late spring to early summer season.

Habenaria oldhamii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
