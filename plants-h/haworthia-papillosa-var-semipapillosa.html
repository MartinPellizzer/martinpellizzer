Haworthia papillosa var. semipapillosa

Haworthia papillosa var. semipapillosa, commonly known as the "corky haworthia," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in small, white tubercles. The leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
