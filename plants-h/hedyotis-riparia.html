Hedyotis riparia

Hedyotis riparia, commonly known as the river jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments, making it a common sight along riverbanks and marshy areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
