Helleborus odorus f. multisectus

Helleborus odorus f. multisectus, commonly known as the fragrant hellebore, is a cultivar of the hellebore plant characterized by its distinctive, often deeply lobed leaves.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is known for its striking, long-lasting flowers that range from white to deep purple, often with a subtle fragrance. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom even in cold winter conditions.
