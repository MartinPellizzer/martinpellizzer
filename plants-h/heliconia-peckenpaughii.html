Heliconia peckenpaughii

Heliconia peckenpaughii, commonly known as the Peckenpaugh's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers inside its leaf-like structures. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative foliage plant.
