Hemigraphis diversifolia

Hemigraphis diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated moss rose, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and vibrant, variegated foliage that ranges from green to silver and pink. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance.

Hemigraphis diversifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded gardens and as a ground cover.
