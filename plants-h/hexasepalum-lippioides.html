Hexasepalum lippioides

Hexasepalum lippioides, commonly known as the six-sepal orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring six sepals that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Hexasepalum lippioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
