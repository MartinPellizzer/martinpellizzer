Hieracium globosiflorum var. larigense

Hieracium globosiflorum var. larigense, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves and produces a single, large, yellow composite flower head. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
