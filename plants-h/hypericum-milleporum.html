Hypericum milleporum

Hypericum milleporum, commonly known as the many-pored St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant produces small, bead-like structures called pseudostems, which are unique to this species. Its leaves are opposite and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum milleporum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
