Hesperis lasioloba

Hesperis lasioloba, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, oval leaves and produces racemes of bright blue or purple flowers with four petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hesperis lasioloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
