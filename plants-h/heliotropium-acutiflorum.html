Heliotropium acutiflorum

Heliotropium acutiflorum, commonly known as the sharpflower heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb with trailing or ascending stems that often produce a profusion of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy texture. The flowers are typically pink or violet, with a distinctive central glandular area that exudes a sweet scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
