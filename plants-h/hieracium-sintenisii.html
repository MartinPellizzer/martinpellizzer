Hieracium sintenisii

Hieracium sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers with a central disc, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
