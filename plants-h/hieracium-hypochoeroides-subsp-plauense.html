Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. plauense

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

plauense, commonly known as the plauent hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in open habitats and is adapted to tolerate various environmental conditions.
