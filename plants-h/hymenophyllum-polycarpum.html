Hymenophyllum polycarpum

Hymenophyllum polycarpum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, delicate plant characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered with a dense layer of trichomes. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are specialized for water retention, which is crucial for its survival in humid conditions.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
