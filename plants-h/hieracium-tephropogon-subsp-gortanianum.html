Hieracium tephropogon subsp. gortanianum

Hieracium tephropogon subsp.

gortanianum, commonly known as the Gortanian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, ribbed achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to harsh alpine conditions.
