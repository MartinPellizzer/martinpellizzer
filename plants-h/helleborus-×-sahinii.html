Helleborus × sahinii

Helleborus × sahinii, commonly known as the Turkish hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of waxy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, woodland environments and prefers well-drained, alkaline soils. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest blooming hellebores.

The plant contains various toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.
