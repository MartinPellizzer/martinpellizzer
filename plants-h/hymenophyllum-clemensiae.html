Hymenophyllum clemensiae

Hymenophyllum clemensiae, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense layer of silky, silver-white trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters long and have a unique, intricate structure with closely spaced, minute sporangia.

Hymenophyllum clemensiae is known for its ability to retain moisture, which is essential for its survival in its natural habitat.
