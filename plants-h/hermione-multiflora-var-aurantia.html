Hermione multiflora var. aurantia

Hermione multiflora var. aurantia, commonly known as the golden Hermione, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to South America, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like blooms throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its bright color and easy maintenance.

However, it requires well-drained soil and regular watering to flourish.
