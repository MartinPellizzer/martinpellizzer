Hopea cordifolia

Hopea cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf talaud, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Hopea cordifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
