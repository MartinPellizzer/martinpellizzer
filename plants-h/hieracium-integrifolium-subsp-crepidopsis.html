Hieracium integrifolium subsp. crepidopsis

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

crepidopsis, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly ovate with smooth margins, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
