Hieracium burnatii subsp. tomentellum

Hieracium burnatii subsp.

tomentellum, commonly known as the tomentellum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly covering of hairs on the leaves and stems.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its specific ecological preferences.
