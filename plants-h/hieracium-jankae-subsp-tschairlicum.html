Hieracium jankae subsp. tschairlicum

Hieracium jankae subsp.

tschairlicum, commonly known as the Tscharlitz's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
