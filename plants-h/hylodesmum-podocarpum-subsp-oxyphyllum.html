Hylodesmum podocarpum subsp. oxyphyllum

Hylodesmum podocarpum subsp.

oxyphyllum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

The plant has a unique growth habit, with a creeping rhizome that produces clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves. Its fronds are typically dark green with a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
