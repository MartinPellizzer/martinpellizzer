Hesperis euganea

Hesperis euganea, commonly known as the European candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis euganea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
