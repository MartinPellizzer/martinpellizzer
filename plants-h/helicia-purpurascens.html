Helicia purpurascens

Helicia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed holly.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often display a striking purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Helicia purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
