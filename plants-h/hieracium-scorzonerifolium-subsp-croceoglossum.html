Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. croceoglossum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

croceoglossum, commonly known as the yellow-tongued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and hairy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, tongue-like flower heads, which give it its common name.
