Hydrangea scandens var. petiolaris

Hydrangea scandens var. petiolaris, commonly known as the climbing hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences of white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage turns vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
