Helianthemum marifolium var. glandulosum

Helianthemum marifolium var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and covered with glandular dots, which give the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
