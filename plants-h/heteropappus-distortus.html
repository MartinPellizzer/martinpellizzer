Heteropappus distortus

Heteropappus distortus, commonly known as the twisted pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant produces yellow to orange ray flowers and is known for its unique, twisted pappus that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

The botanical features of Heteropappus distortus make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
