Hymenocoleus nervopilosus

Hymenocoleus nervopilosus, commonly known as the hairy-veined pteridophyte, is a fern species characterized by its intricate leaf structure.

Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value. The plant exhibits a unique combination of finely serrated margins and densely hairy veins, contributing to its distinctive morphology. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The botanical features of Hymenocoleus nervopilosus make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and scientific study.
