Hieracium trinitatis

Hieracium trinitatis, commonly known as the Holy Thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It has been historically associated with religious symbolism, particularly in Christian traditions.

Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears numerous small flower heads.
