Hieracium constringensiforme

Hieracium constringensiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium constringensiforme is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
