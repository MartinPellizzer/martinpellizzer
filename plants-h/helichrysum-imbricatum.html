Helichrysum imbricatum

Helichrysum imbricatum, commonly known as the golden beard or everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that are tightly packed in dense, mat-like inflorescences. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
