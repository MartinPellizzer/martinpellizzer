Hopea scabra

Hopea scabra, commonly known as the rough-surfaced hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing winged seeds. Hopea scabra is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
