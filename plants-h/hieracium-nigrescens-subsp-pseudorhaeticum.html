Hieracium nigrescens subsp. pseudorhaeticum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

pseudorhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
