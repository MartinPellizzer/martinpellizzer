Hieracium tuvinicum

Hieracium tuvinicum, commonly known as Tuvinian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy, with a flowering period in summer.

Hieracium tuvinicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate yellow flowers.
