Hypericum papillare

Hypericum papillare, commonly known as the rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite and usually have a rough texture.

Hypericum papillare is part of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
