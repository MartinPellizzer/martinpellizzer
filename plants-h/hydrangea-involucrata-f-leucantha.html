Hydrangea involucrata f. leucantha

Hydrangea involucrata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-leaf hydrangea, is a botanical variant characterized by its striking white foliage.

This form of the species is native to Japan and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are predominantly white or pale green, contrasting with the typical green of other hydrangea varieties. The plant produces small, blue-pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle floral display.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique leaf coloration and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
