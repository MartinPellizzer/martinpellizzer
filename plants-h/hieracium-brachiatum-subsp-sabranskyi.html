Hieracium brachiatum subsp. sabranskyi

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

sabranskyi, commonly known as the Sabransky's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and often endangered species.
