Helianthus petiolaris

Helianthus petiolaris, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its showy blooms.

Helianthus petiolaris is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its ability to adapt to aquatic environments.
