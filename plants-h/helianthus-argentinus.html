Helianthus argentinus

Helianthus argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar plants like daisies and chrysanthemums. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by green bracts, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Helianthus argentinus is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
