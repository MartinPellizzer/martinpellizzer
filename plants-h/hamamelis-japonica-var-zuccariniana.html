Hamamelis japonica var. zuccariniana

Hamamelis japonica var. zuccariniana, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange floral bracts that appear in late autumn, typically from October to November. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they turn reddish in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest and hardy nature.
