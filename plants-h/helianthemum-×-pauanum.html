Helianthemum × pauanum

Helianthemum × pauanum, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Helianthemum × pauanum is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
