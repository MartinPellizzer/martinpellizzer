Heliocereus macdougallii

Heliocereus macdougallii, commonly known as the sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of succulent plants.
