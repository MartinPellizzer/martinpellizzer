Helichrysum waitzioides

Helichrysum waitzioides, commonly known as the Cape goldring, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small rings. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
