Hieracium kalmii var. fasciculatum

Hieracium kalmii var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it has hairy stems and basal leaves.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
