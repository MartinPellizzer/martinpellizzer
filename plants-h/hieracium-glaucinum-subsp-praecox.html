Hieracium glaucinum subsp. praecox

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with hairy leaves that are often lance-shaped and have a bluish-green underside. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and is often found in moist or well-drained soils.
