Heliconia marmoliana

Heliconia marmoliana, commonly known as the marble heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces showy inflorescences with red and yellow hues, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Heliconia marmoliana is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
