Hieracium racemosum subsp. chaetotrichum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

chaetotrichum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
