Hieracium cordobense var. mollisetulum

Hieracium cordobense var. mollisetulum, commonly known as the Cordoba hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of basal leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite inflorescence, which is typical of the Asteraceae family.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and growth habits compared to other Hieracium species.
