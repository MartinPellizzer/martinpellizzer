Hoya indaysarae

Hoya indaysarae, commonly known as the Indaysarae Wax Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Hoya indaysarae is also noted for its slow growth and ability to form offsets, making it easy to propagate.
