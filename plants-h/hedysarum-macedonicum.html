Hedysarum macedonicum

Hedysarum macedonicum, commonly known as Macedonian sweet clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in its native habitat.
