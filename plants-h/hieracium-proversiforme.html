Hieracium proversiforme

Hieracium proversiforme, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium proversiforme is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.
