Helianthus cordatus

Helianthus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and dark brown disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Helianthus cordatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other sunflowers and daisies.
