Hakea acuaria

Hakea acuaria, commonly known as the needle bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Hakea acuaria is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
