Hysterionica gracilis var. pilosa

Hysterionica gracilis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy panicled grass, is a perennial plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces panicles of small, inconspicuous flowers, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
