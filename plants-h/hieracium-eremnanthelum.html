Hieracium eremnanthelum

Hieracium eremnanthelum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a basal rosette of broadly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or grassy slopes.

Hieracium eremnanthelum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
