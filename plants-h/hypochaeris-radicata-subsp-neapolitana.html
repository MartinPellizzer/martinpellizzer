Hypochaeris radicata subsp. neapolitana

Hypochaeris radicata subsp.

neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean. It is a low-growing perennial with a rosette of basal leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom from spring to summer.

The plant produces a large number of small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and biodiversity in its native regions.
