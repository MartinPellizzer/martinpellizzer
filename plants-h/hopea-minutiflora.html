Hopea minutiflora

Hopea minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hopea, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. Hopea minutiflora is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, where it plays a role in maintaining biodiversity.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in tropical botany.
