Hedysarum kotschyi

Hedysarum kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hedysarum kotschyi is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
