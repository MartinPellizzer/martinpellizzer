Hieracium megapodum

Hieracium megapodum, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
