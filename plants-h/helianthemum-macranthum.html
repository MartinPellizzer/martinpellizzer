Helianthemum macranthum

Helianthemum macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.
