Huperzia schlechteri

Huperzia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its distinctive, needle-like leaves that form tight, club-shaped clusters. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on the underside of its leaves, a feature typical of many clubmosses.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
