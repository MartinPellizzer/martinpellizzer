Hierochloe odorata var. pubescens

Hierochloe odorata var. pubescens, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and a distinctive, sweet, honey-like aroma when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional cultural practices and has been valued for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
