Hieracium bifidum subsp. phaeocybe

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

phaeocybe, commonly known as the pale-centre hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive pale yellow midrib, and its flowers are yellow with a pale center.

It is often found in sunny, open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
