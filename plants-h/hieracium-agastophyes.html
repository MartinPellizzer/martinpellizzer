Hieracium agastophyes

Hieracium agastophyes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has serrated leaves that are generally hairy, and it often grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Hieracium agastophyes is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain areas.
