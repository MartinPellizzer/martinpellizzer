Hieracium titanogenes

Hieracium titanogenes, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Native to certain regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils and can form dense colonies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential invasive species in some areas.
