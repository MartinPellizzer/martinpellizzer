Heteropogon villosus subvar. typicus

Heteropogon villosus subvar. typicus, commonly known as the hairy tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its adaptability makes it a common component of various ecological communities in its native range.
