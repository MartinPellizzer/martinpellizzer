Hymenocallis pringlei

Hymenocallis pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's spider lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow stamens that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features strap-like, dark green leaves. Its bulbs are underground and serve as storage organs, supporting the plant's growth and flowering cycle.

Hymenocallis pringlei thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
