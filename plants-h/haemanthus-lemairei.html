Haemanthus lemairei

Haemanthus lemairei, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in its natural habitat.
