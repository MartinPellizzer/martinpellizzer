Hypericum campestre

Hypericum campestre, commonly known as wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly glossy surface.

Hypericum campestre is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
