Haplophyllum leiocarpum

Haplophyllum leiocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruit cycad, is a unique cycad species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, round, white to yellowish fruits that mature over several months. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among cycads.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
