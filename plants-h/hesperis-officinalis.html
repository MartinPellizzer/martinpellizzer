Hesperis officinalis

Hesperis officinalis, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis officinalis is typically found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
