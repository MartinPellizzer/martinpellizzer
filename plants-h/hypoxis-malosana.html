Hypoxis malosana

Hypoxis malosana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern.
