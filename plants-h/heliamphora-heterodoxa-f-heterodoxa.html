Heliamphora heterodoxa f. heterodoxa

Heliamphora heterodoxa f. heterodoxa, commonly known as the heterodoxa pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often red-tinged pitcher-shaped leaves that serve as traps for insects. The plant thrives in high-altitude, nutrient-poor environments, where it absorbs nutrients from captured prey. Its pitchers are typically filled with a digestive fluid that breaks down the insects' bodies.

This species is notable for its adaptability to specific microclimates and its role in the unique ecosystems of the tepuis.
