Hordeum vulgare subsp. hexastichon

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

hexastichon, commonly known as six-row barley, is a cultivated variety of barley characterized by its six rows of kernels along the stem. It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, leafy stems and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in a hull and are primarily used for malting and brewing.

This subspecies is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often grown in cooler climates.
