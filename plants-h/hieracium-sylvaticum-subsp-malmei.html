Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. malmei

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

malmei, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright, hairy stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological adaptation to woodland habitats.
