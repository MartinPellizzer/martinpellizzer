Hakea scoparia subsp. scoparia

Hakea scoparia subsp.

scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Hakea scoparia subsp.

scoparia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
