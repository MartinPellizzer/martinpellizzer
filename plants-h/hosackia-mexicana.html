Hosackia mexicana

Hosackia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its blooming period occurs in the spring, adding color to its native habitats.
