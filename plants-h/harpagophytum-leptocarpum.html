Harpagophytum leptocarpum

Harpagophytum leptocarpum, commonly known as wild quinine or the "devil's backbone," is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its twisted, woody stems and distinctive, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to small, green, elongated fruits. Its roots and stems contain various bioactive compounds, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicine.

Harpagophytum leptocarpum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
