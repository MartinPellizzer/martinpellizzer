Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. macrolepium

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

macrolepium, commonly known as the large-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
