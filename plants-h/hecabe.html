Hecabe

Hecabe, commonly known as the common heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing shrub form and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer and autumn, producing pink, white, or purple hues. Hecabe thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

Its evergreen foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscaping.
