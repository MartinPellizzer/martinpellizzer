Hypericum helianthemoides subsp. atropatanum

Hypericum helianthemoides subsp.

atropatanum, commonly known as the dark-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often surrounded by glandular, sticky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
