Hecubaea scorzonerifolia

Hecubaea scorzonerifolia, commonly known as the scorzonera leaf hecubaea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
