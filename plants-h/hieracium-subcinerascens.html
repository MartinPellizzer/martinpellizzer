Hieracium subcinerascens

Hieracium subcinerascens, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-purple in color.

Hieracium subcinerascens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
