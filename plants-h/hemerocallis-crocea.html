Hemerocallis crocea

Hemerocallis crocea, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features strap-like, bright green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange and yellow. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer and last only one day, hence the genus name "Hemerocallis," meaning "day" and "beauty.". It is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Hemerocallis crocea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
