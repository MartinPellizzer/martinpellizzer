Hieracium nemorense

Hieracium nemorense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
