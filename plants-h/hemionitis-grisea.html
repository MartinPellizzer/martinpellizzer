Hemionitis grisea

It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky crevices and alpine meadows where it adapts to cold and arid conditions. The plant has a unique morphology, characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically grayish in color. It reproduces through spores, a common method among ferns, and requires specific moisture and light conditions for successful growth.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Hemionitis grisea is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
