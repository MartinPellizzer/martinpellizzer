Hexasepalum apiculatum

Hexasepalum apiculatum, commonly known as the apiculate hexasepalum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure. The plant features six sepals, which are often adorned with a small apiculate (tip-like) structure, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically small and colorful, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
