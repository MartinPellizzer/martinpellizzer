Hieracium diaporphyrum

Hieracium diaporphyrum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
