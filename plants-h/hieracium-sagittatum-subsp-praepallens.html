Hieracium sagittatum subsp. praepallens

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

praepallens, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
