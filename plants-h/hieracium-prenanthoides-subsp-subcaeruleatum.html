Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. subcaeruleatum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

subcaeruleatum, commonly known as the blue-rayed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intensely colored flower heads compared to other members of the species.
