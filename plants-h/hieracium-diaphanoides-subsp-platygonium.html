Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. platygonium

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

platygonium, commonly known as the flat-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other hawkweed species.
