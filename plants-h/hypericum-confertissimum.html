Hypericum confertissimum

Hypericum confertissimum, commonly known as the dense-stamened St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple stems that often branch profusely. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent stamens, which are a defining feature of the genus Hypericum. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum confertissimum is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
