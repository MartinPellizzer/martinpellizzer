Helichrysum tithonioides

Helichrysum tithonioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that remain open for a long period, giving it a lifelike appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often grown as a decorative plant due to its vibrant color and longevity. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum tithonioides is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
