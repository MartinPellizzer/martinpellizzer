Hillia triflora var. pittieri

Hillia triflora var. pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
