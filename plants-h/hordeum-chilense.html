Hordeum chilense

Hordeum chilense, commonly known as Chilean barley, is a wild grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Hordeum chilense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration and as a model for understanding grassland biodiversity.
