Hieracium vulgatum subsp. muscynae

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

muscynae, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its flowers consist of five petals and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
