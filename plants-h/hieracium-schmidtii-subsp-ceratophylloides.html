Hieracium schmidtii subsp. ceratophylloides

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

ceratophylloides, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a single head, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in arid environments.
