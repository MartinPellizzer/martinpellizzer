Hemigenia brachyphylla

Hemigenia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding insects. Its leaves are narrow and often have a glandular surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hemigenia brachyphylla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
