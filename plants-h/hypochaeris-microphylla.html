Hypochaeris microphylla

Hypochaeris microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Hypochaeris microphylla is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for various insects.
