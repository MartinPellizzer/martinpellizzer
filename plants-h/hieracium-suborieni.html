Hieracium suborieni

Hieracium suborieni, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as tundra and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc of smaller florets.

Hieracium suborieni is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species adapted to extreme northern climates.
