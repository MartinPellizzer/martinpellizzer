Hermione × compressa

Hermione × compressa, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant that belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, colorful flowers that come in a variety of shades. The plant typically produces flowers in clusters, with each bloom having five petals that are often ruffled or frilled. It is a popular choice for garden use due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.

The hybrid nature of Hermione × compressa results from crossbreeding between different species, leading to enhanced traits such as disease resistance and extended blooming periods.
