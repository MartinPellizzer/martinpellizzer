Heuchera acutifolia

Heuchera acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf alumroot, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely cut, dark green foliage that often turns red or bronze in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera acutifolia is a popular choice for woodland gardens due to its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit.
