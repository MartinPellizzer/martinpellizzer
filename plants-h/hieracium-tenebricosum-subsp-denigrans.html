Hieracium tenebricosum subsp. denigrans

Hieracium tenebricosum subsp.

denigrans, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, rough leaves.

The subspecies denigrans is distinguished by its darker, more subdued flower color compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in partially wooded areas.
