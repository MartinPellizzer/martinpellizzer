Hieracium latificum

Hieracium latificum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellowish to reddish composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
