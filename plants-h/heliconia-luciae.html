Heliconia luciae

Heliconia luciae, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow leaves that form a rosette pattern, and its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts. Heliconia luciae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
