Hieracium ochroleucum proles neopicris

Hieracium ochroleucum proles neopicris, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
