Hieracium heteradenum

Hieracium heteradenum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a hairy stem and can reach a height of up to 30 cm.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens, though it can become invasive in some regions.
