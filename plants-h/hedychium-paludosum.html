Hedychium paludosum

Hedychium paludosum, commonly known as the swamp ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hedychium paludosum is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
