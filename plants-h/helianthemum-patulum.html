Helianthemum patulum

Helianthemum patulum, commonly known as the creeping sunflower or sand thyme, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, gray-green foliage that is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
