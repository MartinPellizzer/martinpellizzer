Helichrysum manglesii

Helichrysum manglesii, commonly known as the Cape gold flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, bright yellow to golden-orange flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers. The plant produces papery, persistent bracts that remain after the flowers have faded, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
