Hieracium ramosissimum subsp. conringiifolium

Hieracium ramosissimum subsp.

conringiifolium, commonly known as the Conring's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, often hairy foliage.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a serrated or lobed margin, and the stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
