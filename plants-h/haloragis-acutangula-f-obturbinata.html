Haloragis acutangula f. obturbinata

Haloragis acutangula f. obturbinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterwheel plant, is a flowering plant species native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Haloragaceae and is characterized by its submerged or floating leaves that are often narrow and pointed. The plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in whorls along the stems.
