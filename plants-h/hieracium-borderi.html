Hieracium borderi

Hieracium borderi, commonly known as the Border hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the central and western regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows, where it can thrive in full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium borderi is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
