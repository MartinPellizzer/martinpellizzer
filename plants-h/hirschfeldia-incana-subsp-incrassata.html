Hirschfeldia incana subsp. incrassata

Hirschfeldia incana subsp.

incrassata, commonly known as the hardstem silverweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs, and its upright, branching stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Hirschfeldia incana subsp.

incrassata is often found in disturbed sites, such as roadsides and sandy soils, and is considered a valuable plant for stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
