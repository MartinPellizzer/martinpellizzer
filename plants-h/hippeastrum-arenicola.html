Hippeastrum arenicola

Hippeastrum arenicola, commonly known as the sandhill amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to sandy soils with minimal water. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often emerging after the flowers have bloomed.

Hippeastrum arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
