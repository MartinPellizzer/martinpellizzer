Helichrysum blackallii

Helichrysum blackallii, commonly known as the Blackall's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that remain upright and dry, giving it a lifelike appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum blackallii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
