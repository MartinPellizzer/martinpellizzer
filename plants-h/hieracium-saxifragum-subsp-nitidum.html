Hieracium saxifragum subsp. nitidum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

nitidum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves and compact growth habit.
