Hyacinthella lazulina

Hyacinthella lazulina, commonly known as the blue hyacinth or blue bell, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many Amaryllidaceae species. Hyacinthella lazulina is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and elegant appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
