Hyparrhenia buchananii

Hyparrhenia buchananii, commonly known as the African grass or Buchanan's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Hyparrhenia buchananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
