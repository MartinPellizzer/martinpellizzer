Hymenocardia ripicola

Hymenocardia ripicola, commonly known as the rock apple or stone apple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

The leaves are usually alternate and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
