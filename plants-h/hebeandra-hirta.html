Hebeandra hirta

Hebeandra hirta, commonly known as the woolly hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Hebeandra hirta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
