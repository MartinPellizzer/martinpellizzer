Hieracium bifidum subsp. monobrachion

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

monobrachion, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrowly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in early summer. Its flowering heads are composed of many small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and is often found in open, nutrient-rich habitats.
