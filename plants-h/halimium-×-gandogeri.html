Halimium × gandogeri

Halimium × gandogeri, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Halimium atriplicifolium and Halimium ochroleucum. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
