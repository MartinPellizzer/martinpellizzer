Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. crassipilum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

crassipilum, commonly known as the broad-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thick, dense flowering stems and robust, hairy bracts.
