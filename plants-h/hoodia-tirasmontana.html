Hoodia tirasmontana

Hoodia tirasmontana, commonly known as the desert horned cucumber, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It has a unique ability to survive extreme drought conditions due to its specialized adaptations.

Its fruit, which resembles a cucumber, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.
