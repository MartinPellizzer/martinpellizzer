Hyparrhenia comosa

Hyparrhenia comosa, commonly known as hairy blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
