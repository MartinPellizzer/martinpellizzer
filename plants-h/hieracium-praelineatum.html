Hieracium praelineatum

Hieracium praelineatum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium praelineatum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
