Hieracium geminatiforme

Hieracium geminatiforme, commonly known as the twinleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and spread, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
