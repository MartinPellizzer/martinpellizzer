Hieracium aculeatum

Hieracium aculeatum, commonly known as the prickly hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.
