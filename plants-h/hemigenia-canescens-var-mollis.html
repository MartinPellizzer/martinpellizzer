Hemigenia canescens var. mollis

Hemigenia canescens var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-haired hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as understorey vegetation in forests. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a hairy texture that distinguishes it from other varieties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
