Hieracium acanthodontoides

Hieracium acanthodontoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
