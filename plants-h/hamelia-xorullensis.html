Hamelia xorullensis

Hamelia xorullensis, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a shrub native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hamelia xorullensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
