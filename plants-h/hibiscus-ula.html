Hibiscus ula

Hibiscus ula, commonly known as the Malayan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus ula thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
