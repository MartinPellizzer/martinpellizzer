Habenaria leandriana

Habenaria leandriana, commonly known as the Leandriana orchid, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

The orchid is noted for its delicate structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
