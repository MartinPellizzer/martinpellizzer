Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. petersiana

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

petersiana, commonly known as the Petersiana palm, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its clustered growth habit and elongated, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces large, yellowish-white inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its fruit is a fibrous, three-lobed capsule that ripens to a reddish color.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
