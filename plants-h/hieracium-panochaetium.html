Hieracium panochaetium

Hieracium panochaetium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium panochaetium is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
