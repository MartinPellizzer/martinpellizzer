Horsfieldia subtilis

Horsfieldia subtilis, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant has fleshy, leathery leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. Its blooming period occurs during the wet season, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
