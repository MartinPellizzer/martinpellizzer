Hypericum erectum f. montanum

Hypericum erectum f. montanum, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a compact, upright shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features narrow, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its dense, woody stems and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
