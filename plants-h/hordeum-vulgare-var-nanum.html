Hordeum vulgare var. nanum

Hordeum vulgare var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf barley, is a short-statured variant of barley native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller grain size compared to standard barley varieties. This plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 cm, making it well-suited for specialized agricultural or ornamental use. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The botanical features of H. vulgare var. nanum make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and genetics.
