Hieracium lachenalii subsp. argillaceoides

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

argillaceoides, commonly known as the clay-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in calcareous or clay soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically hairy as well.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to marginal habitats and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
