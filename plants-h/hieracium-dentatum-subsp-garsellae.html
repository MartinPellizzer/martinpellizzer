Hieracium dentatum subsp. garsellae

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

garsellae, commonly known as the Garsella's Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense inflorescences. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in calcareous soils.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds with similar floral structures.
