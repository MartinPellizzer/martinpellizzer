Hieracium sabulicola

Hieracium sabulicola, commonly known as the sand hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of western North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dunes and along shorelines. The plant has upright stems that are usually covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and are produced in late summer.

Hieracium sabulicola is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, showing resilience to wind and salt spray.
