Hieracium chrysoglossum

Hieracium chrysoglossum, commonly known as the golden-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
