Hieracium suberigens

Hieracium suberigens, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette structure.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk and surrounding ray florets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
