Hieracium laevigatum subsp. tanylobum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

tanylobum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in compact clusters.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
