Helipterum virgatum

Helipterum virgatum, commonly known as the silverleaf helipterum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually silver or gray-green, covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Helipterum virgatum is often found in coastal areas and is well adapted to harsh, sun-exposed environments.
