Hieracium dispalatum var. suprapilosum

Hieracium dispalatum var. suprapilosum, commonly known as the woolly meadowhawk, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
