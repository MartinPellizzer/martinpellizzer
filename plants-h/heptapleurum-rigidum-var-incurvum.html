Heptapleurum rigidum var. incurvum

Heptapleurum rigidum var. incurvum, commonly known as the wavy-leaf heptapleurum, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that often have a wavy or curled margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-white flowers followed by red berries.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.
