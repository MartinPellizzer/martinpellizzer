Hymenachne myosuroides

Hymenachne myosuroides, commonly known as the swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it reproduces through both sexual and vegetative means.

Hymenachne myosuroides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
