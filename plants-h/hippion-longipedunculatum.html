Hippion longipedunculatum

Hippion longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which support its clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that helps retain moisture.

The flowering period occurs during the rainy season, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
