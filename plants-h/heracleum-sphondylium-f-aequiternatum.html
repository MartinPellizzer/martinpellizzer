Heracleum sphondylium f. aequiternatum

Heracleum sphondylium f. aequiternatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cow parsley, is a botanical variant of the cow parsley species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks in Europe.

Its foliage and flowers are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
