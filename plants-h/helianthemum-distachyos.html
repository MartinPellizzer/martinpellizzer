Helianthemum distachyos

Helianthemum distachyos, commonly known as the two-stemmed rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
