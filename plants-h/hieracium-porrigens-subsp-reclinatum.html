Hieracium porrigens subsp. reclinatum

Hieracium porrigens subsp.

reclinatum, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive recurved or bent appearance, which gives the species its common name. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both vegetative growth and seed dispersal.
