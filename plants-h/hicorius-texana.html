Hicorius texana

Hicorius texana, commonly known as the Texas hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

Hicorius texana is a hardy perennial that plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
