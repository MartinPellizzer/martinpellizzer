Hackelia diffusa var. cottonii

Hackelia diffusa var. cottonii, commonly known as Cottony hackelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and xeriscaping.
