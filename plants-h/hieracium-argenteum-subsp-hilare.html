Hieracium argenteum subsp. hilare

Hieracium argenteum subsp.

hilare, commonly known as silver hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its silver-white, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
