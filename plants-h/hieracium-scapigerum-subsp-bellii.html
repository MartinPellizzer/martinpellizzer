Hieracium scapigerum subsp. bellii

Hieracium scapigerum subsp.

bellii, commonly known as the Bell's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to produce a large number of seeds.
