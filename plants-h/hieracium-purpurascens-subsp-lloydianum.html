Hieracium purpurascens subsp. lloydianum

Hieracium purpurascens subsp.

lloydianum, commonly known as the Lloyd's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It has a taproot system and is known for its hairy stems and basal rosette growth habit.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for insects.
