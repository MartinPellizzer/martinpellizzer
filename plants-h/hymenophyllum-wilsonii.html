Hymenophyllum wilsonii

Hymenophyllum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's filmy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its thin, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a filmy appearance. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Hymenophyllum wilsonii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid, temperate climates.
