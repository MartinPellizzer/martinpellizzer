Huberia resinosa

Huberia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces a sticky, aromatic resin that has been used traditionally in various cultural practices. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Huberia resinosa thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.
