Hieracium ciliatum

Hieracium ciliatum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, giving it a soft, almost silken appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium ciliatum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
