Habenorkis purpurabenis

Habenorkis purpurabenis, commonly known as the purple-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with purple, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Habenorkis purpurabenis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
