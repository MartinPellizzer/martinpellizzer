Helichrysum lepidophyllum

Helichrysum lepidophyllum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum lepidophyllum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
