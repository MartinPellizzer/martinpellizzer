Helianthus quinqueradiatus

Helianthus quinqueradiatus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows between 1 to 4 feet tall, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system. Its composite flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus quinqueradiatus is often found in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
