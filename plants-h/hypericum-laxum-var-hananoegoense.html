Hypericum laxum var. hananoegoense

Hypericum laxum var. hananoegoense, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
