Hedycarya verticillata

Hedycarya verticillata, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are often aromatic and glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Hedycarya verticillata is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
