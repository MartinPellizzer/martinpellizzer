Hieracium setosifolium

Hieracium setosifolium, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
