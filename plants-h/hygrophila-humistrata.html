Hygrophila humistrata

Hygrophila humistrata, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams.

Hygrophila humistrata is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
