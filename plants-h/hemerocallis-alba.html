Hemerocallis alba

Hemerocallis alba, commonly known as the white daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features strap-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are white with a yellow throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. Hemerocallis alba is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
