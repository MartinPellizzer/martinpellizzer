Hieracium psefochaetum

Hieracium psefochaetum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are rough and hairy, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Hieracium psefochaetum is a common weed in temperate regions and is known for its resilience in challenging environments.
