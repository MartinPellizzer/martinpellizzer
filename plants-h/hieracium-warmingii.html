Hieracium warmingii

Hieracium warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow, often with a distinctive central purple disc.

Hieracium warmingii is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
