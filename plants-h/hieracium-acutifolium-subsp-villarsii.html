Hieracium acutifolium subsp. villarsii

Hieracium acutifolium subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, acute leaves and compact growth habit. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many areas.
