Hieracium leptopodanthum

Hieracium leptopodanthum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium leptopodanthum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
