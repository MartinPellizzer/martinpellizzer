Haumaniastrum praealtum var. succisifolium

Haumaniastrum praealtum var. succisifolium, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, enhancing its ability to capture and retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
