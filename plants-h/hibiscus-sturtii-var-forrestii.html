Hibiscus sturtii var. forrestii

Hibiscus sturtii var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's sturt's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
