Hieracium phaeocephalum

Hieracium phaeocephalum, commonly known as the brown-centred hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a dark brown center.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
