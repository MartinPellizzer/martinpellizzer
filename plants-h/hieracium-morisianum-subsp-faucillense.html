Hieracium morisianum subsp. faucillense

Hieracium morisianum subsp.

faucillense, commonly known as the Morisian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
