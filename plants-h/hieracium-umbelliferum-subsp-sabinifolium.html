Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. sabinifolium

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

sabinifolium, commonly known as the sabinleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
