Haplopappus armerioides var. gramineus

Haplopappus armerioides var. gramineus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its slender, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers contribute to its unique botanical appearance.

The variety gramineus is distinguished by its more grass-like morphology compared to other varieties of Haplopappus.
