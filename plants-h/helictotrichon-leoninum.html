Helictotrichon leoninum

Helictotrichon leoninum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species well adapted to arid environments.
