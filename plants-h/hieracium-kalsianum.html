Hieracium kalsianum

Hieracium kalsianum, commonly known as the Kalsi hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow, forming a single head surrounded by bracts.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
