Hieracium albereanum

Hieracium albereanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium albereanum is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
