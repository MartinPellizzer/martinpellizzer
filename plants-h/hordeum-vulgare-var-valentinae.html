Hordeum vulgare var. valentinae

Hordeum vulgare var. valentinae, commonly known as Valentine barley, is a variety of barley characterized by its unique floral structure and seed morphology.

It is an annual grass species native to temperate regions, typically found in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender stems and spikelets that produce small, two-rowed grains, which are typically yellow to golden in color. Its botanical traits make it suitable for both ornamental use and specialized agricultural applications.

The variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a heritage crop.
