Hieracium brachycomum subsp. dasypogon

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

dasypogon, commonly known as the short-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
