Heteropholis sulcata

Heteropholis sulcata, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with dense, fibrous roots and produces narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often yellow or greenish in color.

Heteropholis sulcata is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showcasing the resilience of orchids in challenging habitats.
