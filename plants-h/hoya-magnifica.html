Hoya magnifica

Hoya magnifica, commonly known as the wax plant or the sweetheart plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that can be green, white, or pink in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring or early summer. Hoya magnifica is a trailing plant that can be grown as a houseplant or in hanging baskets, thriving in bright, indirect light.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
