Homalanthus sulawesianus

Homalanthus sulawesianus, commonly known as the Sulawesi croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the genus Homalanthus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
