Habenaria matudae

Habenaria matudae, commonly known as the Matudae orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure and multiple pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in two rows along the stem.

Habenaria matudae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
