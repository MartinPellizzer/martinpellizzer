Heliotropium hasslerianum var. rigida

Heliotropium hasslerianum var. rigida, commonly known as rigid heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface that aids in moisture retention. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
