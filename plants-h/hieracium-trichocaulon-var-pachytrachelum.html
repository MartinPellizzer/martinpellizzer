Hieracium trichocaulon var. pachytrachelum

Hieracium trichocaulon var. pachytrachelum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family.
