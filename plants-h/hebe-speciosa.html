Hebe speciosa

Hebe speciosa, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Hebe speciosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen nature and ability to attract pollinators.
