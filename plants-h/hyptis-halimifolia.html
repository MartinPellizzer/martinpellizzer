Hyptis halimifolia

Hyptis halimifolia, commonly known as sweet hyptis or lemon hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves that emit a citrus-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis halimifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
