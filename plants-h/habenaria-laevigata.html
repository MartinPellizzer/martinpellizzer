Habenaria laevigata

Habenaria laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores nutrients, with slender, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
