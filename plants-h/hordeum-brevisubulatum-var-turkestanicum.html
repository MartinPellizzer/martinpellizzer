Hordeum brevisubulatum var. turkestanicum

Hordeum brevisubulatum var. turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan barley, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, dense inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential for use in agriculture or ecological restoration.
