Hopea dasyrrhachis

Hopea dasyrrhachis, commonly known as the thick-veined hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its dense, dark brown bark and large, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, oblong fruits.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
