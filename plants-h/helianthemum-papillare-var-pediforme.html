Helianthemum papillare var. pediforme

Helianthemum papillare var. pediforme, commonly known as the rock rose or creeping sandwort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky or sandy soils. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
