Hieracium leptophyton subsp. sychnoschistum

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

sychnoschistum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
