Hyssopus officinalis proles canescens

Hyssopus officinalis proles canescens, commonly known as gray hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are covered in a grayish-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Gray hyssop thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and herbal collections.
