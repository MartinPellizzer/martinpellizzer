Habenaria ruizii

Habenaria ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in rich, well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pink and white coloration, often with a yellowish lip. Its inflorescence is usually a raceme of several blooms, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Botanically, it is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and specialized root system adapted for nutrient absorption in its natural habitat.
