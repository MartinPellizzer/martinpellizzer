Habenaria gollmeri

Habenaria gollmeri, commonly known as the Gollmer's orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

The flowers are usually pale pink or white with intricate patterns, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
