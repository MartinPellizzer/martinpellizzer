Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. boissieri

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has finely divided leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
