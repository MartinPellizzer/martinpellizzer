Hippeastrum verdianum

Hippeastrum verdianum, commonly known as the Verdi's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and often have a waxy texture. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum verdianum is popular as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms and ease of cultivation.
