Hieracium psammogenes subsp. subtenuiflorum

Hieracium psammogenes subsp.

subtenuiflorum, commonly known as the sand everlasting or sand dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers in early spring. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets, which are usually pale yellow.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, showing a high degree of resilience to harsh conditions.
