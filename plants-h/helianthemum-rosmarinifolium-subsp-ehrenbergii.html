Helianthemum rosmarinifolium subsp. ehrenbergii

Helianthemum rosmarinifolium subsp.

ehrenbergii, commonly known as the rock rose, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments. Its foliage is fragrant, reminiscent of rosemary, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The subspecies ehrenbergii is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other varieties.
