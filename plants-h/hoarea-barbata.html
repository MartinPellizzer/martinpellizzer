Hoarea barbata

Hoarea barbata, commonly known as the barbed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Hoarea barbata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its fibrous root system helps it retain moisture, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
