Hieracium chlorolepidotum

Hieracium chlorolepidotum, commonly known as the green-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
