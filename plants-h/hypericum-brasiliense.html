Hypericum brasiliense

Hypericum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hypericum brasiliense is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
