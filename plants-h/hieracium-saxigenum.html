Hieracium saxigenum

Hieracium saxigenum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Hieracium saxigenum is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate low light conditions at higher altitudes.
