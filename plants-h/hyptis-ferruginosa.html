Hyptis ferruginosa

Hyptis ferruginosa, commonly known as ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.

Hyptis ferruginosa is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
