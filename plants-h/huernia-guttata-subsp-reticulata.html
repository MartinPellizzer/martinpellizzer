Huernia guttata subsp. reticulata

Huernia guttata subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the Netted Husk Cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a swollen, ribbed stem that resembles a rounded, fleshy bulb.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a unique, segmented appearance.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
