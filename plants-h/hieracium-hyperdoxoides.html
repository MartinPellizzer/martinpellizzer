Hieracium hyperdoxoides

Hieracium hyperdoxoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium hyperdoxoides is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
