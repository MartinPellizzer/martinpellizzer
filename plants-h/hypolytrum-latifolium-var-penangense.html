Hypolytrum latifolium var. penangense

Hypolytrum latifolium var. penangense, commonly known as the Penang broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.
