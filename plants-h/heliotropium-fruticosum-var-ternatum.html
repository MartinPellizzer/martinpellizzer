Heliotropium fruticosum var. ternatum

Heliotropium fruticosum var. ternatum, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
