Helichrysum semipapposum var. ramosum

Helichrysum semipapposum var. ramosum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, gray-green leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
