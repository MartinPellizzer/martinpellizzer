Hibiscus fasciculatus

Hibiscus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Hibiscus fasciculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its flowers are also a popular ingredient in teas and culinary applications.
