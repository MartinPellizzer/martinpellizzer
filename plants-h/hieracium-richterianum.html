Hieracium richterianum

Hieracium richterianum, commonly known as the Richter's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain regions.
