Holttumochloa hainanensis

Holttumochloa hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan feathergrass, is a grass species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, spike-like structures bearing the flowers.

Holttumochloa hainanensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
