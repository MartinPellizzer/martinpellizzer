Heliophila sparsiflora

Heliophila sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparseflower heliophila, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Heliophila sparsiflora is an important species for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
