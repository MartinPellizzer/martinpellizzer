Hemionitis intertexta

Hemionitis intertexta, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on slopes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Hemionitis intertexta is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
