Haloragis alternifolia

Haloragis alternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats. The plant features slender, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often subtended by bracts.

Haloragis alternifolia is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic and wetland environments.
