Hieracium murorum subsp. polygonium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

polygonium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
