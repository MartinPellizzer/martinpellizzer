Heterotheca sessiliflora var. thiniicola

Heterotheca sessiliflora var. thiniicola, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and conical flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
