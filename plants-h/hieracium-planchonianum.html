Hieracium planchonianum

Hieracium planchonianum, commonly known as the French dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, followed by wind-dispersed seeds.

The leaves are usually basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
