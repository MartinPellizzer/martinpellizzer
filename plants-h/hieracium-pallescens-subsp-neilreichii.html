Hieracium pallescens subsp. neilreichii

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

neilreichii, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing a low-growing habit with hairy stems and basal leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets, and it produces small, achene fruits.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh, high-altitude habitats.
