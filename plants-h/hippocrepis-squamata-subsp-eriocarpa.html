Hippocrepis squamata subsp. eriocarpa

Hippocrepis squamata subsp.

eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
