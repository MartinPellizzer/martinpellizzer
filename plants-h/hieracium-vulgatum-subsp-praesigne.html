Hieracium vulgatum subsp. praesigne

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

praesigne, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate, with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purple tint on the underside.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from June to August.
