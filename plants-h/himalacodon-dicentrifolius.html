Himalacodon dicentrifolius

Himalacodon dicentrifolius, commonly known as the Himalayan thimble cactus, is a unique succulent native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its arid environment. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and protecting it from herbivores.

Himalacodon dicentrifolius is well adapted to cold, rocky habitats and requires minimal water, making it a resilient species in extreme conditions.
