Hibiscus purpusii

Hibiscus purpusii, commonly known as the Polynesian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a scent reminiscent of citrus.

Hibiscus purpusii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
