Horridocactus aconcaguensis

Horridocactus aconcaguensis, commonly known as the Aconcagua cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains in Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are typically green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Horridocactus aconcaguensis is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.
