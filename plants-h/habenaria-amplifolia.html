Habenaria amplifolia

Habenaria amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
