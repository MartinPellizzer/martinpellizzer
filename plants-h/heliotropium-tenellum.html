Heliotropium tenellum

Heliotropium tenellum, commonly known as the small heliotrope, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste ground.

Heliotropium tenellum is known for its ability to tolerate dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
