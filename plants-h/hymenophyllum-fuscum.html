Hymenophyllum fuscum

Hymenophyllum fuscum, commonly known as the black moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are delicate and often have a dark, almost blackish appearance, which contributes to its common name. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Hymenophyllum fuscum is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
