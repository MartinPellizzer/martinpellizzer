Hyptiandra bidwillii

Hyptiandra bidwillii, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically followed by pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

The silky oaks are valued for their ornamental appeal and are also used in horticulture and as a source of timber.
