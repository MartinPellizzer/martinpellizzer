Hoya tomataensis

Hoya tomataensis, commonly known as the tomato hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces edible berries that resemble small tomatoes, which are often consumed raw or cooked. Hoya tomataensis thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique appearance and mild flavor make it a popular choice among both ornamental and culinary plant enthusiasts.
