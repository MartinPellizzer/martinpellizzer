Hyptis argutifolia

Hyptis argutifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically blue or purple and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Hyptis argutifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
