Hermione corrugata

Hermione corrugata, commonly known as the wrinkled Hermione, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, corrugated leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its rare and specialized habitat, Hermione corrugata is considered a vulnerable species.
