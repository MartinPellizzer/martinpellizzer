Hippeastrum warscewiczianum

Hippeastrum warscewiczianum, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from red to pink. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It produces a tall, upright stem with clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This species is popular as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and relatively easy care requirements.
