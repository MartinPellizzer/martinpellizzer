Hygrophila chariensis

Hygrophila chariensis, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually blue or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hygrophila chariensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
