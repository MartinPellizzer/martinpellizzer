Haemanthus coccineus var. grandivalvis

Haemanthus coccineus var. grandivalvis, commonly known as the red-stemmed haemanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces striking red or orange-red flower spikes in late summer to early autumn, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a standout in both cultivated and natural settings.
