Heterotheca pinifolia

Heterotheca pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Heterotheca pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
