Holarrhena villosa

Holarrhena villosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it suitable for reforestation projects.
