Hemizonia paniculata subsp. increscens

Hemizonia paniculata subsp.

increscens, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
