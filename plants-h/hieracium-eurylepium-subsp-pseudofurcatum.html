Hieracium eurylepium subsp. pseudofurcatum

Hieracium eurylepium subsp.

pseudofurcatum, commonly known as the false forked hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and growth habits within the Hieracium genus.
