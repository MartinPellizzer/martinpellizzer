Hippeastrum pulchellum

Hippeastrum pulchellum, commonly known as the Brazilian fire lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the blooms, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically emerging from the base of the plant.

Hippeastrum pulchellum is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
