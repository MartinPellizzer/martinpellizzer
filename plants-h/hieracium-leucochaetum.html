Hieracium leucochaetum

Hieracium leucochaetum, commonly known as the white-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow with white bracts that give it its common name.

It is often found in temperate regions and can be an indicator of nutrient-rich soils.
