Heliconia × flabellata

Heliconia × flabellata, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bract clusters that resemble large, fleshy flowers. The plant's inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by vibrant, often red or orange, leaf-like bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia × flabellata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
