Haplopappus whitneyi

Haplopappus whitneyi, commonly known as Whitney's burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as plains and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bristly pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal.
