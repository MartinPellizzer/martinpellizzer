Hemionitis nitida

Hemionitis nitida, commonly known as the slender hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that are often covered in a thin layer of fine hairs. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or forested areas. Hemionitis nitida has small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
