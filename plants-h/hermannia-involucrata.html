Hermannia involucrata

Hermannia involucrata, commonly known as the Cape rose or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, tubular red flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which are borne on thick, hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Hermannia involucrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
