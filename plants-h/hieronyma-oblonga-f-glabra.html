Hieronyma oblonga f. glabra

Hieronyma oblonga f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved huito, is a variant of the huito plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by local communities. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.

The species is also of interest in horticultural studies due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
