Haplopappus brachylepis

Haplopappus brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

The small yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation.
