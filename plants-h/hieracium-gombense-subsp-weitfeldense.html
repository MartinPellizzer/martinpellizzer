Hieracium gombense subsp. weitfeldense

Hieracium gombense subsp.

weitfeldense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
