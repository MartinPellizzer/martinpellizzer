Helichrysum griseum

Helichrysum griseum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange composite flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Helichrysum griseum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
