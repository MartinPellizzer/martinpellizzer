Handroanthus avellanedae var. paullensis

Handroanthus avellanedae var. paullensis, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found near rivers and in secondary forests.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also serve as an important food source for various pollinators.
