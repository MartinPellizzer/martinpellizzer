Hypericum orientale subsp. ptarmicifolium

Hypericum orientale subsp.

ptarmicifolium, commonly known as the Eastern St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
