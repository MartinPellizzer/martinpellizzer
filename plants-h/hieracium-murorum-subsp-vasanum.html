Hieracium murorum subsp. vasanum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

vasanum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
