Hieracium leioalejandrei

Hieracium leioalejandrei, commonly known as the Armenian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in open, sunny environments.

Hieracium leioalejandrei is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
