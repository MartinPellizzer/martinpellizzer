Hymenocallis clivorum

Hymenocallis clivorum, commonly known as the cliff lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Hymenocallis clivorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
