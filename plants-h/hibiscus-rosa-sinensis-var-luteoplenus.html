Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. luteoplenus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. luteoplenus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with some varieties displaying a gradient of hues. The plant has palmate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct, fragrant scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
