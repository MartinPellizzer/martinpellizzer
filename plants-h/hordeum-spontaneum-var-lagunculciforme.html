Hordeum spontaneum var. lagunculciforme

Hordeum spontaneum var. lagunculciforme, commonly known as the narrow-awned wild barley, is a wild grass species native to various regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, awned lemmas that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils, showing a high degree of adaptability to arid conditions. Its grains are small and usually pale yellow, and it plays a role in traditional agriculture and seed dispersal in its native range.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience.
