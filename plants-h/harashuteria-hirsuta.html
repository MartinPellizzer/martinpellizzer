Harashuteria hirsuta

Harashuteria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy harashuteria, is a unique plant species characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Harashuteria hirsuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
