Helleborus lividus var. integrilobus

Helleborus lividus var. integrilobus, commonly known as the pale hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with a waxy texture and distinctive, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is notable for its showy, nodding flowers that often have a subtle fragrance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
