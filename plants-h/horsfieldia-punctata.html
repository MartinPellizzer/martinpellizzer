Horsfieldia punctata

Horsfieldia punctata, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Horsfieldia punctata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
