Hedysarum gremiale

Hedysarum gremiale, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

The seeds are often used as forage for livestock due to their nutritional content.
