Haplopetalon seemannii

Haplopetalon seemannii, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a short rhizome.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
