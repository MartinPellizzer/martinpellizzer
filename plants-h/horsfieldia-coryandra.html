Horsfieldia coryandra

Horsfieldia coryandra, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, often as an epiphyte on trees. Its flowers are small and numerous, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Horsfieldia coryandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate beauty.
