Hibbertia echiifolia subsp. macrantha

Hibbertia echiifolia subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody fruits. It is often found in sandy soils in coastal and inland regions. Hibbertia echiifolia subsp.

macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
