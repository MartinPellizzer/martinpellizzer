Heliotropium popovii subsp. gillianum

Heliotropium popovii subsp.

gillianum, commonly known as the Gillian's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
