Helianthus annuus var. coronatus

Helianthus annuus var. coronatus, commonly known as the crown sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its distinctive, flattened flower head that resembles a crown.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features large, showy yellow petals surrounding a central disk of dark brown or black tubular florets. Its leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
