Heliconia santaremensis

Heliconia santaremensis, commonly known as the Santa Remensis Heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in parts of Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often brightly colored, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

Heliconia santaremensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
