Hoarea melanantha

Hoarea melanantha, commonly known as the black-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are evergreen, narrow, and arranged in a compact, shrub-like form.

Hoarea melanantha thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as pine forests.
