Haageocereus × seticeps

Haageocereus × seticeps, commonly known as the crested cactus, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, columnar stems that often develop a crest-like structure along their length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens and collections.
