Hieracium storliense

Hieracium storliense, commonly known as the Storli's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium storliense is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
