Hydrocotyle callicephala var. villosa

Hydrocotyle callicephala var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy water arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that are often surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and tropical landscapes.
