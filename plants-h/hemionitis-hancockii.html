Hemionitis hancockii

Hemionitis hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's hemionitis, is a rare and specialized fern native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in rocky crevices and shaded forest floors. The plant has distinctive, lance-shaped fronds with a delicate, feathery appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the leaves, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
