Hieracium racemosum subsp. heterospermum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

heterospermum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowering stems bear multiple yellow flowers, and the leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

It is notable for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
