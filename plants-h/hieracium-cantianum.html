Hieracium cantianum

Hieracium cantianum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium cantianum is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
