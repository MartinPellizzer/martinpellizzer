Hieracium nigrescens subsp. capnostyloides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

capnostyloides, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowers are surrounded by a series of bracts that form a distinctive, spiky structure.

The subspecies is characterized by its dark, often purplish flower heads and is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae).
