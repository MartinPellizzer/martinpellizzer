Hieracium sagittatum subsp. subcanipes

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

subcanipes, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a distinctive arrowhead shape, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
