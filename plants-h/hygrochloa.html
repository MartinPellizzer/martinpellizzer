Hygrochloa

Hygrochloa, commonly known as the watergrass, is a genus of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

These plants are characterized by their ability to thrive in moist environments, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Hygrochloa species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, clustered inflorescences. They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Due to their adaptability, some species are cultivated for ornamental purposes or as part of ecological restoration projects.
