Herorchis

Herorchis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, spider-like flowers and its unique ability to thrive without soil, relying instead on a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi. The plant's roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the air and water, a trait known as epiphytism. Herorchis typically grows on the trunks or branches of trees in wet, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a critically endangered species.
