Hibiscus rosiflorus var. simplex

Hibiscus rosiflorus var. simplex, commonly known as the Cape rose or white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers have five petals and are pollinated primarily by insects, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
