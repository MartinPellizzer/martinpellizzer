Hereroa brevifolia

Hereroa brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved hereroa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

Hereroa brevifolia is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
