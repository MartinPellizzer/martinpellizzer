Hemionitis atropurpurea

Hemionitis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple mossy orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, moss-like leaves and delicate, purple-pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
