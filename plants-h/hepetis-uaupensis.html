Hepetis uaupensis

Hepetis uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, white, night-blooming flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers open at dusk and close by dawn, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Hepetis uaupensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
