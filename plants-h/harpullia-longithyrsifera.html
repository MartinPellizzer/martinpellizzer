Harpullia longithyrsifera

Harpullia longithyrsifera, commonly known as the long-thyrsifera holly, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its dense, glossy foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often appearing in clusters along the stems.

The fruit is a drupe, usually containing a single seed, and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
