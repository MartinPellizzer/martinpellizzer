Heteropterys macrostachya f. oblonga

Heteropterys macrostachya f. oblonga, commonly known as the oblong leaf huito, is a variant of the huito plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in South American herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

The f. oblonga form is distinguished by its elongated leaf shape compared to the typical variety.
