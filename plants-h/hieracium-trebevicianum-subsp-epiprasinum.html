Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. epiprasinum

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

epiprasinum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with wavy margins and covered in fine hairs.

It is characterized by its ability to form dense colonies, which can sometimes become invasive in certain environments.
