Helleborine latifolia f. rosea

Helleborine latifolia f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered broad-leaf helleborine, is a variant of the broad-leaf helleborine, a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking, rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, preferring rich, organic soil. Its flowers are spurred at the base and have a distinctive, intricate structure typical of orchids.

This orchidoid species is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
