Hieracium murorum subsp. gentile

Hieracium murorum subsp.

gentile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and widespread distribution across Europe and parts of Asia.
