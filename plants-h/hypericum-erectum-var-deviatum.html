Hypericum erectum var. deviatum

Hypericum erectum var. deviatum, commonly known as the wandering jew or variegated St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that features bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate with a glossy green surface and often have white or yellow variegation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
