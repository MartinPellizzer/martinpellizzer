Hieracium caesium subsp. schisticolor

Hieracium caesium subsp.

schisticolor, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers range from yellow to purple, depending on the subspecies.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a minor weed in some agricultural areas.
