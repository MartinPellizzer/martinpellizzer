Hieracium brevipilum

Hieracium brevipilum, commonly known as the short-hair hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, short hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, consisting of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

Hieracium brevipilum is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
