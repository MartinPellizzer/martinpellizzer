Hepatica × schlyteri

Hepatica × schlyteri, commonly known as the hybrid hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and blue, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hepatica × schlyteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting early pollinators in forest ecosystems.
