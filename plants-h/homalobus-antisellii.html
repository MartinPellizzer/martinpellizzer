Homalobus antisellii

Homalobus antisellii, commonly known as the Antisell's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones instead of flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

Its slow growth rate and limited distribution make it a critically endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
