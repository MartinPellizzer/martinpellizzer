Hyptis capitata

Hyptis capitata, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hyptis capitata is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its pleasant scent and hardy nature.
