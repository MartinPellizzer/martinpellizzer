Helichrysum dichroum

Helichrysum dichroum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that are tightly packed in compact, spherical clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Helichrysum dichroum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, drought-resistant qualities.
