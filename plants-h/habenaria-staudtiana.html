Habenaria staudtiana

Habenaria staudtiana, commonly known as the Staudt's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red streaks.

Habenaria staudtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
