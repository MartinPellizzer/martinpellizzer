Heuchera parviflora var. dissecta

Heuchera parviflora var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and silver. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering ornamental value both in summer and fall.
