Hieracium guthnikianum subsp. naegelii

Hieracium guthnikianum subsp.

naegelii, commonly known as the Naegeli's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the family. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
