Hieracium leontocephalum

Hieracium leontocephalum, commonly known as the lion's head hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a lion's head, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides in temperate regions. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is known for its invasive tendencies in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
