Hemionitis aemula

Hemionitis aemula, commonly known as the water hawksbeard, is a small perennial herb native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil in aquatic environments.

Hemionitis aemula plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
