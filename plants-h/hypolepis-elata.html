Hypolepis elata

Hypolepis elata, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

Hypolepis elata is often found in rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
