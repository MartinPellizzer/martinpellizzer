Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. subflexible

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

subflexible, commonly known as the flexible hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and may have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish or greenish in color.

This subspecies is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
