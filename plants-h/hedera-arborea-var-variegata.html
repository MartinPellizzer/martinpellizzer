Hedera arborea var. variegata

Hedera arborea var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated English ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe.

It features dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation, which enhances its ornamental value. The plant has small, dark green berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ability to cover walls and ground surfaces.

Its adaptability to various soil types and moisture levels makes it a popular choice for gardeners.
