Hypericum tymphresteum

Hypericum tymphresteum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover.

Its medicinal properties have been historically studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
