Hieracium glandulosissimum var. tenuans

Hieracium glandulosissimum var. tenuans, commonly known as the slender meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it is known for its finely hairy stems and leaves.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.
