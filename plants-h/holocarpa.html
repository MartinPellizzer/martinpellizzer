Holocarpa

Holocarpa, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant features a distinctive, spiny, globe-shaped fruit that persists on the plant throughout the year. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Holocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.
