Homalobus canescens

Homalobus canescens, commonly known as the roughleaf acanthus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, spiny flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
