Hepetis staminea

Hepetis staminea, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hepetis staminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
