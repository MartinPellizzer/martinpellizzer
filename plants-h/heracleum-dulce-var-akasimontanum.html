Heracleum dulce var. akasimontanum

Heracleum dulce var. akasimontanum, commonly known as the Akasimon mountain sweet angelica, is a variant of the sweet angelica plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces umbel-shaped inflorescences that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are follicles that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variant is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
