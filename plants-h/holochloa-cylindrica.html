Holochloa cylindrica

Holochloa cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often mistaken for reeds or shrubs. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a tendency to form dense clumps.

Holochloa cylindrica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
