Hemionitis notabilis

Hemionitis notabilis, commonly known as the water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating on the water's surface. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters with abundant sunlight and nutrient-rich sediment. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its fronds, releasing spores into the water.

Hemionitis notabilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
