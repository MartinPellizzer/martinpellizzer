Hieracium inuloides var. tridentatifolium

Hieracium inuloides var. tridentatifolium, commonly known as the tridentate hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are distinctive, with a tridentate or three-toothed margin, giving it its specific variety name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
