Hamosa rattanii

Hamosa rattanii, commonly known as the golden rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spadices, which are typical of the Arecaceae family. Its fibrous stems are highly valued for their strength and flexibility, making them useful in traditional crafts and construction.

Hamosa rattanii thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
