Hieracium stupposum subsp. nivisquamum

Hieracium stupposum subsp.

nivisquamum, commonly known as the snow-squamate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and specialized species with limited distribution.
