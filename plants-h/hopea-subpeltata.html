Hopea subpeltata

Hopea subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hopea, is a tropical tree belonging to the Dipterocarpaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous on the upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Hopea subpeltata is valued for its timber and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
