Hydnophytum laurifolium

Hydnophytum laurifolium, commonly known as the laurifolium strangler fig, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its ability to grow on other plants without harming them. The plant has a distinctive appearance with broad, glossy leaves that resemble those of laurel trees. It produces small, clustered fruits that are often consumed by various bird species.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
