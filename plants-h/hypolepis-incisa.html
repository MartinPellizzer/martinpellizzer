Hypolepis incisa

Hypolepis incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hartiella, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, narrow, and serrated leaflet structure. The fronds are typically arching and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, with a lush, green appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
