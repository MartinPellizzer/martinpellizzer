Hieracium brachycomum subsp. melanotrichum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

melanotrichum, commonly known as the black-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, glandular hairs, which give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.
