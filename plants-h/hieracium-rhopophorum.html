Hieracium rhopophorum

Hieracium rhopophorum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium rhopophorum is often found in temperate climates and can sometimes be invasive in non-native regions.
