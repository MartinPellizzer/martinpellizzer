Hypericum rumeliacum subsp. amplexicaule

Hypericum rumeliacum subsp.

amplexicaule, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a reddish or purple underside. Its flowers are typically yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late summer.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
