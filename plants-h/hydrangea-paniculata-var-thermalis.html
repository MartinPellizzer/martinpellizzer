Hydrangea paniculata var. thermalis

Hydrangea paniculata var. thermalis, commonly known as the thermal hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it produces showy blooms in late summer to early fall.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
