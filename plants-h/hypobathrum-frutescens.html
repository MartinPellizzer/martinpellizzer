Hypobathrum frutescens

Hypobathrum frutescens, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Hypobathrum frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
