Hedyotis ramosii

Hedyotis ramosii, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Hedyotis ramosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
