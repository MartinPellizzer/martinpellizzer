Hopea philippinensis

Hopea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine hopea, is a large evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall stature, reaching up to 40 meters in height. The tree has smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

Hopea philippinensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
