Hieracium brevifolium var. cesatianum

Hieracium brevifolium var. cesatianum, commonly known as the Cesarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are broad and slightly lobed, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering stems bear small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
