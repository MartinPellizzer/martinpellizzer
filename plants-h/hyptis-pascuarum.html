Hyptis pascuarum

Hyptis pascuarum, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry to moist habitats.

Hyptis pascuarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
