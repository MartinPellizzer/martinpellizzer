Holcus caffrorum

Holcus caffrorum, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This plant is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
