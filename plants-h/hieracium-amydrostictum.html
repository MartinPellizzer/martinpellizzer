Hieracium amydrostictum

Hieracium amydrostictum, commonly known as the drooping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a basal rosette of leaves that are often hairy and dark green. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
