Haplopappus nevadensis

Haplopappus nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Haplopappus nevadensis is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
