Hieracium saxifragum subsp. elongatifrons

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

elongatifrons, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with a distinctive, elongated bloom. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated flower head and specific morphological traits compared to other Hieracium species.
