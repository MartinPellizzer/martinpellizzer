Hieracium macrodontoides

Hieracium macrodontoides, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring alternate, heart-shaped leaves with rough textures. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium macrodontoides is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
