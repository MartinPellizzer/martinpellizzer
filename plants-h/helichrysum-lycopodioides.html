Helichrysum lycopodioides

Helichrysum lycopodioides, commonly known as the golden torch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that resemble small torches. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
