Hygrophila mutica

Hygrophila mutica, commonly known as the slender water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

Hygrophila mutica produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
