Hordeum secalinum subsp. geniculatum

Hordeum secalinum subsp.

geniculatum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-rowed grains, typical of the Hordeum genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and agricultural research.
