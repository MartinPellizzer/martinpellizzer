Hieracium gentile var. silvivagum

Hieracium gentile var. silvivagum, commonly known as the forest dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in woodland and grassy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and a flowering head composed of numerous small florets.

It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of forest understories.
