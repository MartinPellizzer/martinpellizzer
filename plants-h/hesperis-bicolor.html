Hesperis bicolor

Hesperis bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family.

Hesperis bicolor is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
