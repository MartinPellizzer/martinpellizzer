Hypericum bithynicum

Hypericum bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is known for its medicinal properties, containing hypericin and other bioactive compounds.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
