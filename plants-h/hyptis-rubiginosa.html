Hyptis rubiginosa

Hyptis rubiginosa, commonly known as red hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis rubiginosa is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
