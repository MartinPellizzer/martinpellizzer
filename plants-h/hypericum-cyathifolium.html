Hypericum cyathifolium

Hypericum cyathifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
