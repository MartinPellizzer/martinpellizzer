Halacsya sendtneri

Halacsya sendtneri, commonly known as the Sendtner's bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It blooms from June to August and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Halacsya sendtneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
