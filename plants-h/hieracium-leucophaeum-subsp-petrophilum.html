Hieracium leucophaeum subsp. petrophilum

Hieracium leucophaeum subsp.

petrophilum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
