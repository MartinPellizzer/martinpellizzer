Hebeandra crassifolia

Hebeandra crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved hebe, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
