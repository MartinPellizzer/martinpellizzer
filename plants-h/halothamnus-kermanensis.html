Halothamnus kermanensis

Halothamnus kermanensis, commonly known as Kerman's halothamnus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Halothamnus kermanensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native range.
