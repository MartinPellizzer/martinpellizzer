Histiopteris

Histiopteris, commonly known as the fern with a distinctive frond structure.

It is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae, characterized by its delicate, often finely divided fronds. The plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Their reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to their unique botanical appearance.
