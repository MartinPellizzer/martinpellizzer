Hieracium sparsum subsp. vestiticeps

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

vestiticeps, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
