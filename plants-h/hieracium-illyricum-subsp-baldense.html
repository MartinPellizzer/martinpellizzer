Hieracium illyricum subsp. baldense

Hieracium illyricum subsp.

baldense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in parts of its range.
