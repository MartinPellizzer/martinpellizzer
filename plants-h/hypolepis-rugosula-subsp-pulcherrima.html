Hypolepis rugosula subsp. pulcherrima

Hypolepis rugosula subsp.

pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden sword fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange fronds, which are often adorned with a distinctive, waxy bloom.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a leathery texture and a rugged, serrated margin.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
