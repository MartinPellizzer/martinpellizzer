Hyparrhenia griffithii

Hyparrhenia griffithii, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
