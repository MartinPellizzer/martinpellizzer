Homoianthus variabilis

Homoianthus variabilis, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Homoianthus variabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
