Halanthium rarifolium var. abichii

Halanthium rarifolium var. abichii, commonly known as the rare leaf water flea, is a lesser-known aquatic plant species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are often submerged in shallow water bodies. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are tiny and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.
