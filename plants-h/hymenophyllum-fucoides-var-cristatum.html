Hymenophyllum fucoides var. cristatum

Hymenophyllum fucoides var. cristatum, commonly known as the cristate spore moss, is a rare and specialized fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate foliage and small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. This variety is distinguished by its unique, crest-like growth pattern, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often considered an indicator species for undisturbed, humid microhabitats.
