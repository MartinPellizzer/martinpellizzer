Hieracium villosiceps subsp. pamphilii

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

pamphilii, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in grassy areas and open habitats, often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
