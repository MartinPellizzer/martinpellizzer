Habranthus microcarpus

Habranthus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited grass-violet, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cold, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
