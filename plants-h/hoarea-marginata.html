Hoarea marginata

Hoarea marginata, commonly known as the marginate grassveld.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Hoarea marginata is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to various soil types.
