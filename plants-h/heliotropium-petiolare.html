Heliotropium petiolare

Heliotropium petiolare, commonly known as the purpleweed or false heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas, sandy soils, and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its ability to tolerate salt and is sometimes used in coastal restoration projects.
