Hyptis rigida

Hyptis rigida, commonly known as rigid hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple and are clustered in spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyptis rigida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
