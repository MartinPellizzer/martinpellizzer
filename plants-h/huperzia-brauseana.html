Huperzia brauseana

Huperzia brauseana, commonly known as the Brause's clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that form dense, cushion-like structures, often covering the ground in forest floors. Huperzia brauseana is native to parts of South America, particularly in Brazil, and is often found in humid, tropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
