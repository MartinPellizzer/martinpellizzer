Hermodactylus tuberosus subsp. longifolius

Hermodactylus tuberosus subsp.

longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are typically arranged in a single cluster at the end of each stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
