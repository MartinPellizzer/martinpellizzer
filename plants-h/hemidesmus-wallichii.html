Hemidesmus wallichii

Hemidesmus wallichii, commonly known as Indian Sarsaparilla, is a climbing herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Hemidesmus wallichii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
