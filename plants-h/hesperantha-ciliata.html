Hesperantha ciliata

Hesperantha ciliata, commonly known as the pink bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or purple flowers that resemble bells, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hesperantha ciliata is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
