Haplopappus chrysocephalus

Haplopappus chrysocephalus, commonly known as the goldenhead pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with silver-gray foliage and bright yellow ray flowers. Its bracts are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Haplopappus chrysocephalus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
