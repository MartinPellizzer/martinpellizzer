Homalomena obliquata

Homalomena obliquata, commonly known as the zigzag arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that curve in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its flowers are typically small and clustered, emerging from a spadix enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe.
