Hieracium scissicaule

Hieracium scissicaule, commonly known as the cut-stem hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Hieracium scissicaule is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
