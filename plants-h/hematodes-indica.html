Hematodes indica

Hematodes indica, commonly known as the Indian blood worm, is a unique plant species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular structures that resemble blood vessels, which are actually specialized leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and organic matter in the soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

However, due to its specific habitat requirements, it is rarely cultivated outside its native range.
