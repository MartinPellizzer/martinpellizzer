Hordeum secalinum f. puberulum

Hordeum secalinum f. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent barley, is a variant of the common barley species.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, which distinguish it from other forms of barley. This form is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in agricultural research due to its genetic variability and adaptability.

The plant's botanical features make it an important subject in the study of grass species and their ecological roles.
