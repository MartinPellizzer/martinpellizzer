Hieracium murorum subsp. suborphnotrichum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

suborphnotrichum, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow disc flowers with numerous tubular florets. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
