Hygrophila polysperma

Hygrophila polysperma, commonly known as the water wisteria, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its ornamental value.

Hygrophila polysperma is also valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic life.
