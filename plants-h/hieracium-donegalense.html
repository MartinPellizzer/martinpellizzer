Hieracium donegalense

Hieracium donegalense, commonly known as Donegal hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Ireland.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and rocky slopes, preferring cooler and more shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium donegalense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
