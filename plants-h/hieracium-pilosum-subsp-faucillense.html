Hieracium pilosum subsp. faucillense

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

faucillense, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has hairy stems and leaves, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and other damp environments.
