Hypecoum procumbens subsp. glaucescens

Hypecoum procumbens subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
