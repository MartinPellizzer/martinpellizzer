Hieracium arcuatidens

Hieracium arcuatidens, commonly known as the curved-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically spoon-shaped and hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
