Hieracium rupicola var. franconicum

Hieracium rupicola var. franconicum, commonly known as the Franconian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
