Hieracium medschedsense subsp. juranomorphum

Hieracium medschedsense subsp.

juranomorphum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
