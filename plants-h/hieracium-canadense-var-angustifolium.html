Hieracium canadense var. angustifolium

Hieracium canadense var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for wetland environments.

The plant has both ecological and historical significance, having been used in traditional medicine by various Indigenous peoples.
