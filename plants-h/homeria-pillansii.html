Homeria pillansii

Homeria pillansii, commonly known as the Kudu tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which open to release the seeds when mature.

The Kudu tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
