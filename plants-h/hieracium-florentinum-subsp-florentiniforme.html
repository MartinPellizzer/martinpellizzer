Hieracium florentinum subsp. florentiniforme

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

florentiniforme, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or pale underside.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
