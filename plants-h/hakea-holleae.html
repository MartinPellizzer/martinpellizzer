Hakea holleae

Hakea holleae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is part of the Proteaceae family.

Hakea holleae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
