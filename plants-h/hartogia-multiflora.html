Hartogia multiflora

Hartogia multiflora, commonly known as the Cape flats grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Western and Eastern Cape regions. The plant features slender, tufted stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Hartogia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
