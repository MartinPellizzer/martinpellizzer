Helicophyllum aucheri

Helicophyllum aucheri, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Helicophyllum aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, bell-like blooms.
