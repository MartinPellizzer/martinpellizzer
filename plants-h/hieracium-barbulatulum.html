Hieracium barbulatulum

Hieracium barbulatulum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its rough texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its habitat.
