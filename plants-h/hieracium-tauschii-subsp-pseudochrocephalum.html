Hieracium tauschii subsp. pseudochrocephalum

Hieracium tauschii subsp.

pseudochrocephalum, commonly known as the false golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

It is characterized by its ability to hybridize with other Hieracium species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
