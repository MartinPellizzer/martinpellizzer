Hypericum penthorodes

Hypericum penthorodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, linear to lanceolate, and covered with small, glandular dots.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
