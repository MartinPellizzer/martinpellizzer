Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. macrolepidiforme

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

macrolepidiforme, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and reddish, fleshy fruits.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in dense, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in open habitats and can spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.
