Hieracium subflexile

Hieracium subflexile, commonly known as the bentleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive wavy or curled edge, contributing to its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
