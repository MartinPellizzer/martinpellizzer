Hypoxis parvifolia

Hypoxis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Hypoxis parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
