Hypericum albiflorum

Hypericum albiflorum, commonly known as white-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
