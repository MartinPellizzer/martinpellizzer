Horridocactus vallenarensis

Horridocactus vallenarensis, commonly known as the Vallenar cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and dense clusters of spiny, cylindrical fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring season, which attract various pollinators. Its adaptations to harsh desert conditions include deep root systems and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
