Hibiscus costatus

Hibiscus costatus, commonly known as the coral hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, serrated margin. Hibiscus costatus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
