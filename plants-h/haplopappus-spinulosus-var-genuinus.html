Haplopappus spinulosus var. genuinus

Haplopappus spinulosus var. genuinus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with gray-green, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces achenes surrounded by bracts, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
