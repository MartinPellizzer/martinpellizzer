Hieracium loxense

Hieracium loxense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Hieracium loxense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
