Hieracium murorum subsp. diaphanoneurum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

diaphanoneurum, commonly known as the translucent-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thin, translucent leaf veins and slender stems. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
