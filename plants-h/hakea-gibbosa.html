Hakea gibbosa

Hakea gibbosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Hakea gibbosa is also noted for its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
