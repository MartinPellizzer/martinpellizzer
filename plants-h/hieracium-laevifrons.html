Hieracium laevifrons

Hieracium laevifrons, commonly known as the smooth-fronded hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium laevifrons is often found in open habitats and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
