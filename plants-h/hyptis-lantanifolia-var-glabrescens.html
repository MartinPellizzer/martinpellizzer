Hyptis lantanifolia var. glabrescens

Hyptis lantanifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
