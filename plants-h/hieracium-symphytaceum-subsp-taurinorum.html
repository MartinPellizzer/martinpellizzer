Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. taurinorum

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

taurinorum, commonly known as the Taurine Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
