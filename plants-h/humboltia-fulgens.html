Humboltia fulgens

Humboltia fulgens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. Humboltia fulgens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
