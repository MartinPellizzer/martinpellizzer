Handroanthus Selachidentatus

Yellow trumpet tree, scientifically known as Handroanthus selachidentatus, is a striking tropical tree native to South America. It is renowned for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dazzling display during the rainy season. The tree's unique appearance and ecological importance make it a popular subject in botanical studies and ornamental horticulture. Despite its beauty, it faces threats from habitat loss and overharvesting. Protecting this species is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
