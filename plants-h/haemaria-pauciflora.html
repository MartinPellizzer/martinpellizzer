Haemaria pauciflora

Haemaria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Due to its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
