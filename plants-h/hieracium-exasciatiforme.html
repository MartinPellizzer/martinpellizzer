Hieracium exasciatiforme

Hieracium exasciatiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
