Hieracium saxicapellum

Hieracium saxicapellum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and deeply lobed leaves.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
