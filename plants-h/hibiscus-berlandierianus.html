Hibiscus berlandierianus

Hibiscus berlandierianus, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hibiscus berlandierianus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
