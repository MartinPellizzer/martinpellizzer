Hypericum hirtellum

Hypericum hirtellum, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hypericum hirtellum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
