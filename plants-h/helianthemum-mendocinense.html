Helianthemum mendocinense

Helianthemum mendocinense, commonly known as the Mendocino sunflower, is a low-growing shrub native to California.

It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and landscaping.
