Hypericum parviflorum

Hypericum parviflorum, commonly known as lesser St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a woody stem, typical of many Hypericum species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
