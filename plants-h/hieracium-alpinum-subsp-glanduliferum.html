Hieracium alpinum subsp. glanduliferum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

glanduliferum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous habitats.
