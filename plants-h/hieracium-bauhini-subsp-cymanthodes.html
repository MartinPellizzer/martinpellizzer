Hieracium bauhini subsp. cymanthodes

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

cymanthodes, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and the flowers are surrounded by pale green to reddish bracts.

It is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain habitats in its native range.
