Hieracium gilvocaniceps var. mundulifolium

Hieracium gilvocaniceps var. mundulifolium, commonly known as the yellow-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is distinguished by its yellow-centred flowers and finely divided, narrow leaves.
