Helianthemum pannosum subsp. boissieri

Helianthemum pannosum subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
