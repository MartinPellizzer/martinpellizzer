Hibiscus lasiospermus

Hibiscus lasiospermus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus lasiospermus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
