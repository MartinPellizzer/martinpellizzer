Hymenophyllum emersum

Hymenophyllum emersum, commonly known as the emerald moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, delicate plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest floors or on rock surfaces. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its bright green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hair. Its reproductive structures, known as gametophores, are short and clustered, producing spores in a unique, cup-like arrangement.

Hymenophyllum emersum plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms.
