Helia nerioides

Helia nerioides, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and dark brown central disc. The plant typically grows in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helia nerioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
