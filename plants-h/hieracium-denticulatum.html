Hieracium denticulatum

Hieracium denticulatum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have serrated edges.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
