Hypseochloa matengoensis

Hypseochloa matengoensis, commonly known as the Matengo grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like structure with small, two-lobed spikelets.

Hypseochloa matengoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
