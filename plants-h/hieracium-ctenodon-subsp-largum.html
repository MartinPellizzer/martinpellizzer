Hieracium ctenodon subsp. largum

Hieracium ctenodon subsp.

largum, commonly known as the large-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It features upright stems with hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies largum is distinguished by its larger inflorescence compared to other subspecies of Hieracium ctenodon.
