Hulsea vestita var. callicarpha

Hulsea vestita var. callicarpha, commonly known as the velvetleaf hulsea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
