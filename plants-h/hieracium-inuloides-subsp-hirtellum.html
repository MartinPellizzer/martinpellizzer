Hieracium inuloides subsp. hirtellum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
