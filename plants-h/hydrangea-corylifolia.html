Hydrangea corylifolia

Hydrangea corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hydrangea corylifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
