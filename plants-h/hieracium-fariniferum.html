Hieracium fariniferum

Hieracium fariniferum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

It is often found in open areas where it can spread easily through its rhizomatous growth.
