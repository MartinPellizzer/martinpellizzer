Hieracium bifidum subsp. furculaceum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

furculaceum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed, often with a distinctive 'V' shape at the base, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
