Hibbertia stricta var. glabriuscula

Hibbertia stricta var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the silky hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils.

Hibbertia stricta var. glabriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
