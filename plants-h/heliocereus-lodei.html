Heliocereus lodei

Heliocereus lodei, commonly known as the sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open in the evening and remain open during the day, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Heliocereus lodei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
