Hyptis lilacina

Hyptis lilacina, commonly known as purple hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy, lavender-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Hyptis lilacina is also valued as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and floral display.
