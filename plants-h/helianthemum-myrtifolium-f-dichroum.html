Helianthemum myrtifolium f. dichroum

Helianthemum myrtifolium f. dichroum, commonly known as the variegated rockrose, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are usually covered in a fine white or silver fuzz, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in Mediterranean climates.
