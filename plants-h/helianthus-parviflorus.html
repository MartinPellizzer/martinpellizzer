Helianthus parviflorus

Helianthus parviflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
