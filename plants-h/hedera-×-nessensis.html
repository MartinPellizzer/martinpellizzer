Hedera × nessensis

Hedera × nessensis, commonly known as the Scottish ivy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Hedera helix and Hedera hibernica.

It is a climbing evergreen vine characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and ability to thrive in shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is native to the British Isles and is often found growing on rocky outcrops and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and as a ground cover in horticulture.
