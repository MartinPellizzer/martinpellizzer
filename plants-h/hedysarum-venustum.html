Hedysarum venustum

Hedysarum venustum, commonly known as the golden peashrub, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

Hedysarum venustum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor, rocky soils.
