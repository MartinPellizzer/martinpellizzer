Hypericum quadrangulum subsp. acutum

Hypericum quadrangulum subsp.

acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
