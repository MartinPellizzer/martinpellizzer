Holodiscus discolor subsp. franciscanus

Holodiscus discolor subsp.

franciscanus, commonly known as the San Francisco thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient plant in coastal and inland regions.
