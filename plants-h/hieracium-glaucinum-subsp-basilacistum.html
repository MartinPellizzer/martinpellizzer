Hieracium glaucinum subsp. basilacistum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

basilacistum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head, which are surrounded by a series of bracts. It is characterized by its glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
