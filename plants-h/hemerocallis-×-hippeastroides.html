Hemerocallis × hippeastroides

Hemerocallis × hippeastroides, commonly known as the hybrid daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates. It produces numerous spike-like inflorescences, and its flowers come in a wide range of colors, including yellow, orange, red, and pink.

Hemerocallis × hippeastroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardiness.
