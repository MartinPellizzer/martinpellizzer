Hiraea fagifolia f. typica

Hiraea fagifolia f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the European hiraea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
