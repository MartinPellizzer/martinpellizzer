Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. medianiforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

medianiforme, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with stem leaves that are smaller and gradually reduced upward.

Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.
