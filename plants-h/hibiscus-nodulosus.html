Hibiscus nodulosus

Hibiscus nodulosus, commonly known as the nodular hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hibiscus nodulosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
