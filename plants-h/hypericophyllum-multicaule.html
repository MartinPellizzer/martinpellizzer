Hypericophyllum multicaule

Hypericophyllum multicaule, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
