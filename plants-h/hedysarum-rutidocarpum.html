Hedysarum rutidocarpum

Hedysarum rutidocarpum, commonly known as the European vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Hedysarum rutidocarpum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in agricultural systems.
