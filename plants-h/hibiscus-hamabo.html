Hibiscus hamabo

Hibiscus hamabo, commonly known as the African rose or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are often hairy.

Hibiscus hamabo is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
