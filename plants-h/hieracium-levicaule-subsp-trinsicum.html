Hieracium levicaule subsp. trinsicum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

trinsicum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
