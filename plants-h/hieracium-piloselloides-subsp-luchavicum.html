Hieracium piloselloides subsp. luchavicum

Hieracium piloselloides subsp.

luchavicum, commonly known as the Luchavik hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It has finely hairy stems and leaves, which help in reducing water loss.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.
