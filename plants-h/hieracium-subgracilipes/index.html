Hieracium Subgracilipes

Hieracium subgracilipes is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its delicate, slender leaves and yellow flowers. Found primarily in moist, grassy habitats across northern Europe, this plant thrives in cool, temperate climates. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance. Its unique morphology sets it apart from other hawkweeds, making it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts. Understanding this plant helps shed light on the diversity within the genus Hieracium.
