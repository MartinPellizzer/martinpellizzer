Hieracium laevigatum subsp. pardalinum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

pardalinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and covered in fine pubescence.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
