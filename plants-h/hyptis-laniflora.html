Hyptis laniflora

Hyptis laniflora, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the summer months.

Hyptis laniflora is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
