Huperzia perreriana

Huperzia perreriana, commonly known as the Perrer's clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and the presence of small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Huperzia perreriana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
