Habenaria curranii

Habenaria curranii, commonly known as the Curran's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and pine forests. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, round seed pods.

Habenaria curranii is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
