Hermas intermedia

Hermas intermedia, commonly known as the common herma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hermas intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
