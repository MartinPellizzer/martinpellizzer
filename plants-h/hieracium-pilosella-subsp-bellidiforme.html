Hieracium pilosella subsp. bellidiforme

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

bellidiforme, commonly known as the bell-shaped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowers are characterized by their bell-like shape, which gives the species its common name.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
