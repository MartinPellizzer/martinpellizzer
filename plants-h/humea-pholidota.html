Humea pholidota

Humea pholidota, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiky fruit pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

Humea pholidota is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
