Hymenolobus procumbens

Hymenolobus procumbens, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils.

Hymenolobus procumbens is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor conditions and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
