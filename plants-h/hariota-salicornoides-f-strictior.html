Hariota salicornoides f. strictior

Hariota salicornoides f. strictior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glasswort, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves.

It is native to coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in saline and sandy soils. The plant is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand and preventing erosion. Its flowering stems produce small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh coastal conditions.
