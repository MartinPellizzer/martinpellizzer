Hieracium georgieffii

Hieracium georgieffii, commonly known as the Georgian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and elongated flowering stems that bear yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium georgieffii is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate climates.
