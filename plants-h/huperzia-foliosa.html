Huperzia foliosa

Huperzia foliosa, commonly known as the fork-leaf huperzia, is a species of moss-like fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its sporophytes are usually small and inconspicuous.

Huperzia foliosa is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and its potential medicinal properties.
