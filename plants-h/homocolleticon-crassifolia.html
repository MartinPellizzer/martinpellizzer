Homocolleticon crassifolia

Homocolleticon crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
