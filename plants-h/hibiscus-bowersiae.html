Hibiscus bowersiae

Hibiscus bowersiae, commonly known as the Bowers' hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus bowersiae thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
