Hieracium taraxacifolium

Hieracium taraxacifolium, commonly known as the dandelion-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many environments.
