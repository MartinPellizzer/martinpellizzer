Hesperis polyceratia

Hesperis polyceratia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Hesperis polyceratia is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
