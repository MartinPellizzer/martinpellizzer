Hemerocallis aurantiaca var. littorea

Hemerocallis aurantiaca var. littorea, commonly known as the coastal daylily, is a variant of the daylily species native to coastal regions of China.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments where it is exposed to salt spray. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
