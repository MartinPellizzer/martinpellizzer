Hibiscus pinetorum

Hibiscus pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant features large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, often with a reddish or purplish hue on the underside.

Hibiscus pinetorum is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in hammocks and dune areas.
