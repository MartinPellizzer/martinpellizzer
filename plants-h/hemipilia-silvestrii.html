Hemipilia silvestrii

Hemipilia silvestrii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats, often in forest understories. Its roots are tuberous, which help it survive in its natural environment.

Hemipilia silvestrii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
