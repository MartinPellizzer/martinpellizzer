Hieracium hololeucum

Hieracium hololeucum, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often in temperate climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a single flower head at the end of a stiff stem.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
