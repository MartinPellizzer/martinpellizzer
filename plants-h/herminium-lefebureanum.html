Herminium lefebureanum

Herminium lefebureanum, commonly known as the Lefebure's ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a spike of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.

The species is also recognized for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
