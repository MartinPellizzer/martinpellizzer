Hieracium tappeineri

Hieracium tappeineri, commonly known as the Tappeiner's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
