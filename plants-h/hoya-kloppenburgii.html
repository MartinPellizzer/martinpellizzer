Hoya kloppenburgii

Hoya kloppenburgii, commonly known as the "Wax Plant," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pink in color. Hoya kloppenburgii is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

It is also known for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it climb and attach to surfaces in its natural habitat.
