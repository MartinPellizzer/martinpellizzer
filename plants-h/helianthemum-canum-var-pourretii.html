Helianthemum canum var. pourretii

Helianthemum canum var. pourretii, commonly known as the Algerian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a bright splash of color to its natural habitat.
