Hypoxis subspicata

Hypoxis subspicata, commonly known as the golden star or star lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Hypoxis subspicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
