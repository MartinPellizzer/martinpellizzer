Hierochloe odorata var. annulata

Hierochloe odorata var. annulata, commonly known as sweet vernalgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows.

Sweet vernalgrass is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which contributes to its common name.
