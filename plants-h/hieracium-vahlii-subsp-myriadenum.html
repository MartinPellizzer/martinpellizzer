Hieracium vahlii subsp. myriadenum

Hieracium vahlii subsp.

myriadenum, commonly known as the many-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish to orange-red flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often hairy as well.

The subspecies is distinguished by its numerous small flowers arranged in dense clusters, giving it its common name.
