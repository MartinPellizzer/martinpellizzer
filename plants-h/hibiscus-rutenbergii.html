Hibiscus rutenbergii

Hibiscus rutenbergii, commonly known as the Rutenberg's hibiscus, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are often adorned with a prominent central pistil. Hibiscus rutenbergii thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
