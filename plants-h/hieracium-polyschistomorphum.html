Hieracium polyschistomorphum

Hieracium polyschistomorphum, commonly known as the many-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed, and the flowers are yellow, forming a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.
