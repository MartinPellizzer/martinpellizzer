Homalomena nathanielii

Homalomena nathanielii, commonly known as the Nathaniel's arrowleaf, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are dark green with a glossy texture, often adorned with subtle veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Homalomena nathanielii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
