Heliconia speciosa

Heliconia speciosa, commonly known as the giant flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a torch, with vibrant red and orange bracts. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds and bats, which are attracted to its nectar.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
