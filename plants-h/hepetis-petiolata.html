Hepetis petiolata

Hepetis petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, blue-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to the landscape.
