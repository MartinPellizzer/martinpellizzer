Hordeum kiarchanum

Hordeum kiarchanum, commonly known as the kiarchan barley, is a species of wild barley native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Hordeum kiarchanum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.

Its genetic makeup is of interest to researchers studying wild relatives of cultivated barley for potential agricultural improvements.
