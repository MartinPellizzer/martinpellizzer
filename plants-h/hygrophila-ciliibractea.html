Hygrophila ciliibractea

Hygrophila ciliibractea, commonly known as the slender hygrophila, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender stems that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hygrophila ciliibractea is known for its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, making it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
