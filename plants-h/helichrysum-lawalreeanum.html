Helichrysum lawalreeanum

Helichrysum lawalreeanum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hairy structures.

Helichrysum lawalreeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
