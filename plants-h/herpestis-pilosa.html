Herpestis pilosa

Herpestis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Herpestis pilosa is often found in dry, coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
