Hydrolea crispa

Hydrolea crispa, commonly known as the wrinkled hydrolea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hydrolea crispa is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
