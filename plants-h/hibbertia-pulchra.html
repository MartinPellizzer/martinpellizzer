Hibbertia pulchra

Hibbertia pulchra, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands.

Hibbertia pulchra is an important species in Australian flora and is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
