Honckenya frigida

Honckenya frigida, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.

Honckenya frigida plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
