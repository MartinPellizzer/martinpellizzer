Hieracium wettsteinianum subsp. wettsteinianum

Hieracium wettsteinianum subsp.

wettsteinianum, commonly known as the Wettstein's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in the Alps. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
