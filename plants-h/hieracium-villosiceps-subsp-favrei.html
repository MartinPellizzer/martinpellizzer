Hieracium villosiceps subsp. favrei

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

favrei, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it is often found in open habitats with moderate moisture.
