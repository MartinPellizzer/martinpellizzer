Hieracium vulgatum subsp. leucotrachelum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

leucotrachelum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

This subspecies is typically found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and often forms dense colonies.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
