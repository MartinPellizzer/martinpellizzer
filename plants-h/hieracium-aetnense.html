Hieracium aetnense

Hieracium aetnense, commonly known as the Aetna hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, thriving in the unique microclimates of volcanic landscapes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-orange.

Hieracium aetnense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
