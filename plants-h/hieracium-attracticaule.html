Hieracium attracticaule

Hieracium attracticaule, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a single head at the end of each stem. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
