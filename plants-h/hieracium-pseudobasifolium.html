Hieracium pseudobasifolium

Hieracium pseudobasifolium, commonly known as the basal-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
