Hechtia jaliscana

Hechtia jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco hechtia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. Hechtia jaliscana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
