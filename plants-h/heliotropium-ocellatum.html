Heliotropium ocellatum

Heliotropium ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and purple. Its flowers have a distinctive scent reminiscent of vanilla, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
