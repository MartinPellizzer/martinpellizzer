Heliotropium auro-argenteum

Heliotropium auro-argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale purple to nearly white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
