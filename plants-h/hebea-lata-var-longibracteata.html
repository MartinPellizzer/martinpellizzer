Hebea lata var. longibracteata

Hebea lata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces bracts that are longer than the flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
