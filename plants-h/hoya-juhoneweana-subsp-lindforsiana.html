Hoya juhoneweana subsp. lindforsiana

Hoya juhoneweana subsp.

lindforsiana, commonly known as the Lindfors' wax plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
