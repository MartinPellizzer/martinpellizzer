Hedyotis coryphoecetes

Hedyotis coryphoecetes, commonly known as the golden star or starry bugleweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis coryphoecetes is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
