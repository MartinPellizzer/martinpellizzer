Hesperis lunata

Hesperis lunata, commonly known as the moonflower or lunar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis lunata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
