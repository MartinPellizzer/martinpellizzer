Habenaria × jacksonii

Habenaria × jacksonii, commonly known as the Jackson's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
