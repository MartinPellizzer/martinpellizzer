Hugonia arborescens var. schliebenii

Hugonia arborescens var. schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's tree hugonia, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to South Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the winter months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
