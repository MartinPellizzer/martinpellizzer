Hydrangea sungpanensis

Hydrangea sungpanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features broad, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are composed of sterile, petal-like bracts and fertile, small, greenish flowers in the center.

Hydrangea sungpanensis thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
