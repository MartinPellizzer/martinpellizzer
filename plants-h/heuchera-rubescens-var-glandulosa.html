Heuchera rubescens var. glandulosa

Heuchera rubescens var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a deep green to reddish coloration. This variety is noted for its glandular surfaces, which give it its scientific name.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value.
