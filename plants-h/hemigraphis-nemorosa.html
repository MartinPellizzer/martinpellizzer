Hemigraphis nemorosa

Hemigraphis nemorosa, commonly known as the silver moss plant, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive silvery underside that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Hemigraphis nemorosa is also popular as a houseplant due to its ability to tolerate low light conditions.
