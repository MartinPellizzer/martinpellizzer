Hibiscus merxmuelleri

Hibiscus merxmuelleri, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are known for their fragrance.

Hibiscus merxmuelleri thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
