Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. subgouanii

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

subgouanii, commonly known as the subgouan's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has finely toothed leaves and produces numerous small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in some regions.
