Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp. tarnense

Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp.

tarnense, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and usually have a rough texture with glandular dots.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk and surrounding petal-like rays, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
