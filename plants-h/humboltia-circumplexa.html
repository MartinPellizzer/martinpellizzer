Humboltia circumplexa

Humboltia circumplexa, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows along sandy shores and rocky coastlines, where it thrives in well-drained, saline soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Humboltia circumplexa is known for its rapid growth and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
