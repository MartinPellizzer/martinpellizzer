Hybanthus pumilio

Hybanthus pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf hybanthus, is a small flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Hybanthus pumilio is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
