Hoodia lugardii

Hoodia lugardii, commonly known as the desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. Hoodia lugardii is also known for its unique chemical composition, including compounds that have been studied for potential appetite-suppressing properties.

Due to its ecological significance and increasing demand, it is often threatened by overharvesting and habitat loss.
