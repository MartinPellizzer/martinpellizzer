Helianthocereus herzogianus

Helianthocereus herzogianus, commonly known as the Herzog's night-blooming cactus, is a species native to the deserts of southern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having spines for protection.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
