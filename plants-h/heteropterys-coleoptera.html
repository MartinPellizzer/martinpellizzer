Heteropterys coleoptera

Heteropterys coleoptera, commonly known as the scarlet waxbill, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers, which are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
