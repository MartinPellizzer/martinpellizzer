Hakea wiradjuri

Hakea wiradjuri, commonly known as the Wiradjuri hakea, is a shrub native to the southeastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, often prickly foliage and produces striking, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically red or orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Hakea wiradjuri plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
