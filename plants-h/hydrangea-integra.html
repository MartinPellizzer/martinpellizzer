Hydrangea integra

Hydrangea integra, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often dark green with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in summer.

Hydrangea integra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
