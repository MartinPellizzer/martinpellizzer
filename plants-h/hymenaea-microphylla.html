Hymenaea microphylla

Hymenaea microphylla, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing the edible Brazil nuts. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard shell and require specific conditions to germinate.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
