Hirculus haplophylloides

Hirculus haplophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hirculus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hirculus haplophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
