Hortonia floribunda var. acuminata

Hortonia floribunda var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed flowering orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
