Hieracium epipsarum

Hieracium epipsarum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Siberia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium epipsarum is often found in mountainous environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
