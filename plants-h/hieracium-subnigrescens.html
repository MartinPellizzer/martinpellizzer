Hieracium subnigrescens

Hieracium subnigrescens, commonly known as the darkening hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and serrated. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, followed by reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium subnigrescens is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
