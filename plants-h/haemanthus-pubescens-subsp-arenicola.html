Haemanthus pubescens subsp. arenicola

Haemanthus pubescens subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the coastal haemanthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking red-orange flowers in late summer, which are borne on short, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
