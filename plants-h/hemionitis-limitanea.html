Hemionitis limitanea

Hemionitis limitanea, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique and rare plant species native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a small, mat-forming fern that thrives in extremely dry and saline environments, often found in desert oases and along the margins of salt lakes. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and closely spaced, forming a dense, protective layer that helps reduce water loss. The plant's reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically producing spores rather than seeds.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, Hemionitis limitanea is considered a botanical curiosity and an indicator of extreme environmental conditions.
