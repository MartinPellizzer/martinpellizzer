Hibiscus laxiflorus

Hibiscus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Hibiscus laxiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.

It produces fibrous, woody stems and is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species.
