Helleborus viridis subsp. viridis

Helleborus viridis subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate.

The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, showy spathes. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, making it a valuable addition to shady gardens.
