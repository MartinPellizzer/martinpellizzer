Hydrangea pubescens

Hydrangea pubescens, commonly known as the roughleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns reddish in the fall.

Hydrangea pubescens prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
