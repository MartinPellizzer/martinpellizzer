Hieracium plicatum var. genuinum

Hieracium plicatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, elongated leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.
