Hieracium tauschii var. pachycephalum

Hieracium tauschii var. pachycephalum, commonly known as the thick-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flower heads are typically yellow, consisting of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its potential role in ecological succession.
