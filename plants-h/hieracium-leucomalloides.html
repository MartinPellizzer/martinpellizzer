Hieracium leucomalloides

Hieracium leucomalloides, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, often with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium leucomalloides is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can be invasive in some regions.
