Hieracium murorum subsp. moruloides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

moruloides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, clustered flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies moruloides is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and smaller flower clusters compared to other members of the species.
