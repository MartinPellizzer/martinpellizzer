Hyptis canaminensis

Hyptis canaminensis, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Hyptis canaminensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
