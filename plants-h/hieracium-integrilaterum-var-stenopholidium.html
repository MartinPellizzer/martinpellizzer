Hieracium integrilaterum var. stenopholidium

Hieracium integrilaterum var. stenopholidium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a slightly hairy surface, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other hawkweed species.
