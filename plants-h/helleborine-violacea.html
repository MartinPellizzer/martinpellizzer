Helleborine violacea

Helleborine violacea, commonly known as the violet helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Helleborine violacea is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
