Hieracium trachlosimum

Hieracium trachlosimum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, which are typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium trachlosimum is known for its ability to thrive in various soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
