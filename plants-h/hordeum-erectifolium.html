Hordeum erectifolium

Hordeum erectifolium, commonly known as the upright-leaved barley, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Hordeum erectifolium is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
