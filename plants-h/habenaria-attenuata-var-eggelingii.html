Habenaria attenuata var. eggelingii

Habenaria attenuata var. eggelingii, commonly known as the Eggeling's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest understories. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single raceme of fragrant flowers.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
