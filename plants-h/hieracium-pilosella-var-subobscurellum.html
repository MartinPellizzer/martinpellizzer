Hieracium pilosella var. subobscurellum

Hieracium pilosella var. subobscurellum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish to pale green composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, with the lower leaves being more elongated.

This variant is distinguished by its pale coloration and is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
