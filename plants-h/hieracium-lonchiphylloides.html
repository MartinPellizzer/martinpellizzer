Hieracium lonchiphylloides

Hieracium lonchiphylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched. It produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, which are characteristic of the daisy family.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
