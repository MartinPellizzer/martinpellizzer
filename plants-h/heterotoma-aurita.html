Heterotoma aurita

Heterotoma aurita, commonly known as the golden leaf moth, is a species of moth belonging to the family Tischeriidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-colored wings, which are covered in fine, iridescent scales. The plant-associated life cycle of this species involves feeding on the leaves of various host plants, primarily in the families Asteraceae and Fabaceae. Its larvae are often concealed within leaf rolls or galls, which provide protection from predators.

The botanical significance of Heterotoma aurita lies in its role as an indicator species for certain plant communities and its contribution to ecological interactions within its habitat.
