Hyptis nigrescens

Hyptis nigrescens, commonly known as black hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Hyptis nigrescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
