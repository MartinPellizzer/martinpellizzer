Hieracium lavatum

Hieracium lavatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium lavatum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
