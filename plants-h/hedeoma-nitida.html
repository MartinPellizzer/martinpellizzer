Hedeoma nitida

Hedeoma nitida, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Hedeoma nitida is also valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
