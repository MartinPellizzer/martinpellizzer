Hieracium lanseanum subsp. reboudianum

Hieracium lanseanum subsp.

reboudianum, commonly known as the Corsican hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and subalpine vegetation.
