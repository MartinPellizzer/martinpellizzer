Hieracium britannicum var. vagense

Hieracium britannicum var. vagense, commonly known as the British dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the United Kingdom.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are bright yellow and produce a fluffy seed head upon maturity.

This species is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
