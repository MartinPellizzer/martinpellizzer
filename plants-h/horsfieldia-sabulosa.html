Horsfieldia sabulosa

Horsfieldia sabulosa, commonly known as the sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Horsfieldia sabulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
