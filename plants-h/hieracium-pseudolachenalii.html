Hieracium pseudolachenalii

Hieracium pseudolachenalii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium pseudolachenalii is often found in high-altitude areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
