Heterosavia laurifolia var. intermedia

Heterosavia laurifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate laurel, is a variant of the laurel plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red berries, which are a characteristic feature of the species.

The botanical description highlights its ornamental value and adaptability to humid, shaded environments.
