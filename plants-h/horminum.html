Horminum

Horminum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

Horminum is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been historically used in herbal medicine.
