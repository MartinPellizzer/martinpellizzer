Hirtella apetala

Hirtella apetala, commonly known as the white-flowered hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that lack petals, giving it its scientific name "apetala," meaning "without petals.". Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name "hirtella," which refers to a hairy texture.

This species plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
