Hyalosperma pusillum

Hyalosperma pusillum, commonly known as the small glassy grass, is a small, annual grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and the presence of small, translucent, glassy seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its seeds are adapted for wind dispersal, allowing the plant to spread efficiently in its habitat.

Due to its unique seed structure, it is of interest in botanical studies related to seed morphology and adaptation.
