Hemionitis zollingeri

Hemionitis zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's hemionitis, is a rare and specialized fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and low moisture conditions. The plant has a distinctive, compact growth form with small, lance-shaped fronds that are usually dark green and leathery. It reproduces through spores, which are released from the undersides of its fertile fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis zollingeri is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
