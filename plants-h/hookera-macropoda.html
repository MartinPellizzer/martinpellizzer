Hookera macropoda

Hookera macropoda, commonly known as the giant hooker's shield, is a striking alpine plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, which help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hookera macropoda thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in alpine botany.
