Hedysarum issykkulense

Hedysarum issykkulense, commonly known as the Issyk-Kul peacock vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking clusters of violet to purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Hedysarum issykkulense is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
