Hieracium microphylloides

Hieracium microphylloides, commonly known as the small-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer.

Hieracium microphylloides is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
