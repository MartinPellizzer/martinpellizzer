Hedysarum mauritanicum var. micrantum

Hedysarum mauritanicum var. micrantum, commonly known as the Moroccan vetch, is a perennial legume native to North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a climbing habit and produces elongated, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
