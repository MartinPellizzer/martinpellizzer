Hoya salweenica

Hoya salweenica, commonly known as the Salween Wanda, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the summer months. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Hoya salweenica thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
