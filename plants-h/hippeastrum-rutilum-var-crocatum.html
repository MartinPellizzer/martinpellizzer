Hippeastrum rutilum var. crocatum

Hippeastrum rutilum var. crocatum, commonly known as the Crocodile Plant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with contrasting patterns resembling a crocodile's skin. The plant produces large, showy blooms on tall, sturdy stems, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, providing a lush green backdrop to the colorful flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
