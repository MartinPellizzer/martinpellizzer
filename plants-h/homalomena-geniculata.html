Homalomena geniculata

Homalomena geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag caladium, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is noted for its striking, colorful foliage with variegated patterns of green, white, and red. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a zigzag or wavy margin, contributing to its common name.
