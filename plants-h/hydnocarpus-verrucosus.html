Hydnocarpus verrucosus

Hydnocarpus verrucosus, commonly known as the warty horse chestnut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rough, warty fruit capsules that are typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant produces compound leaves with multiple leaflets and has a distinctive, aromatic bark. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The seeds are enclosed within the fleshy, spiny husk and are dispersed by animals.
