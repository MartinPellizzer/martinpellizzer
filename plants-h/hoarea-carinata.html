Hoarea carinata

Hoarea carinata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces bright yellow to orange flowers. Hoarea carinata is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
