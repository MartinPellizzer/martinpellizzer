Heterotheca sessiliflora var. sanjacintensis

Heterotheca sessiliflora var. sanjacintensis, commonly known as the San Jacinto blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and basal rosette of silver-gray leaves. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
