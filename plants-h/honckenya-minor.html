Honckenya minor

Honckenya minor, commonly known as the dwarf honckenya, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in pine and oak forests. The plant features tiny, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with small, greenish-white blooms that appear in the spring.

Honckenya minor is valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
