Hieracium pulchellum subsp. morthieri

Hieracium pulchellum subsp.

morthieri, commonly known as the Morthier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered flower heads.
