Hordeum vulgare var. uljassutaicum

Hordeum vulgare var. uljassutaicum, commonly known as the Hungarian barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to the Carpathian Basin. This variety typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, each bearing two to three bisexual florets.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its grains, which are used in traditional foods and beverages.
