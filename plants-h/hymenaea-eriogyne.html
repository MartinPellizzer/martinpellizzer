Hymenaea eriogyne

Hymenaea eriogyne, commonly known as the yellow miracle bignay, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Hymenaea eriogyne is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
