Hoyella rosea

Hoyella rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored hoyella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full to partial sunlight.

Hoyella rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
