Hyptis lilacina var. epimallota

Hyptis lilacina var. epimallota, commonly known as purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, lavender-colored flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
