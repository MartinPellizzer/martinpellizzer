Hedysarum alpinum var. chinense

Hedysarum alpinum var. chinense, commonly known as the Chinese Alpine Pea, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
