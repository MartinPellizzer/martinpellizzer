Hetaeria cristata var. agyokuana

Hetaeria cristata var. agyokuana, commonly known as the Japanese flame bamboo, is a variant of the flame bamboo species native to Japan.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant red or orange-colored inflorescences. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.

Its unique coloration and elegant appearance make it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
