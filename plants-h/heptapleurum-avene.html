Heptapleurum avene

Heptapleurum avene, commonly known as the glossy-leaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery, dark green leaves that are glossy on both sides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant has a dense growth habit, making it suitable for hedges and landscape planting.
