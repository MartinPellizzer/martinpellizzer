Hesperis speciosa

Hesperis speciosa, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Hesperis speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a nectar source for pollinators.
