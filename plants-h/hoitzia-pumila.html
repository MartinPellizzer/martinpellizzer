Hoitzia pumila

Hoitzia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf hoitzia, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in partial shade.

Hoitzia pumila is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and attractive foliage.
