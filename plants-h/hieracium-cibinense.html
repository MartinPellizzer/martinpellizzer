Hieracium cibinense

Hieracium cibinense, commonly known as the Cibinense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium cibinense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
