Hypericum aethiopicum subsp. sonderi

Hypericum aethiopicum subsp.

sonderi, commonly known as the Ethiopian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa. It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and slightly hairy, with a strong aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
