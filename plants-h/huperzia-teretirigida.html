Huperzia teretirigida

Huperzia teretirigida, commonly known as the slender-stemmed huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The sporophyte produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are released into the air for dispersal.

Huperzia teretirigida is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role.
