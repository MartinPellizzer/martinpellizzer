Hoodia perlata

Hoodia perlata, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales, which help minimize water loss in its harsh habitat.

Hoodia perlata is also known for its ability to store water in its thick stems, making it well adapted to survive in drought conditions.
