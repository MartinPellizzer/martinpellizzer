Hieracium trichophidium

Hieracium trichophidium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are adaptations to its native habitats. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium trichophidium is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
