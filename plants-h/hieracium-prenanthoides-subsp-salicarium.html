Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. salicarium

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

salicarium, commonly known as the willow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, making it a notable plant in ecological and botanical studies.
