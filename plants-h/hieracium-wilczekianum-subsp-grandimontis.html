Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. grandimontis

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

grandimontis, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and showy yellow or orange composite flowers.

The plant typically thrives in alpine meadows and rocky soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
