Hyptis spicata var. multiseta

Hyptis spicata var. multiseta, commonly known as the hairy hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
