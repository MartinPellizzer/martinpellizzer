Hydrocotyle callicephala var. hirtella

Hydrocotyle callicephala var. hirtella, commonly known as hairy water pennywort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or semi-aquatic environments such as marshes, ditches, and the edges of ponds. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in umbels, usually white or pale green in color.

It is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a food source for certain aquatic animals.
