Hieracium lampromegas subsp. wysokae

Hieracium lampromegas subsp.

wysokae, commonly known as the Polish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

This subspecies is distinguished by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in drier environments. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant has been studied for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
