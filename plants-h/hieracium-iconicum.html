Hieracium iconicum

Hieracium iconicum, commonly known as the iconic dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rough, hairy leaves and a single, elongated stem that bears a large, yellow flower head. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, with the characteristic parachute-like structure of the pappus.

The plant has a long flowering period, typically from spring to early autumn.
