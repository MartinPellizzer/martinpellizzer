Helianthemum hymettium

Helianthemum hymettium, commonly known as the Hymettian sunflower, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has greyish-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
