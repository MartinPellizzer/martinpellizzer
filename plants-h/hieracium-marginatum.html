Hieracium marginatum

Hieracium marginatum, commonly known as the margined hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has hairy stems and is often found in temperate climates.

It is an annual or biennial herb that can spread through seeds and vegetative reproduction.
