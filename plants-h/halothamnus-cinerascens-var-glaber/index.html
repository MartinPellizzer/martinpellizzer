Halothamnus Cinerascens Var. Glaber

Halothamnus cinerascens var. glaber is a hardy shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern united states. it thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. this variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and pale grayish-green foliage. it plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for local wildlife. due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in dryland environments.
