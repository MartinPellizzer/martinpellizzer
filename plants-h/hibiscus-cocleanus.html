Hibiscus cocleanus

Hibiscus cocleanus, commonly known as the Coclean hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Hibiscus cocleanus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing near water sources such as rivers and swamps.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
