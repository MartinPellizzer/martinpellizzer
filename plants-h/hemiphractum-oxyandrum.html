Hemiphractum oxyandrum

Hemiphractum oxyandrum, commonly known as the red-veined bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with prominent yellow stamens, and it produces small, edible berries.

Hemiphractum oxyandrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
