Hibiscus leptocalyx

Hibiscus leptocalyx, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are five-petaled and often have a prominent central stigma.

Hibiscus leptocalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
