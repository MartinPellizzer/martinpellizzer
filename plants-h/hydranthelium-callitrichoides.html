Hydranthelium callitrichoides

Hydranthelium callitrichoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters, often forming dense mats on the surface or submerged vegetation. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Hydranthelium callitrichoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
