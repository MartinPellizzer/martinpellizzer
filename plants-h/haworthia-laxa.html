Haworthia laxa

Haworthia laxa, commonly known as the Zebra Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinct white or yellow markings resembling a zebra's stripes. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments. It produces small, white or pale green flowers on slender stems during the summer months.

This plant is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
