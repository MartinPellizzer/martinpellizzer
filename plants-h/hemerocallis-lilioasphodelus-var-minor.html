Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus var. minor

Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf daylily, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with each flower lasting only one day. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to naturalize easily in gardens.
