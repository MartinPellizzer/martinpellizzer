Hieracium knafii

Hieracium knafii, commonly known as Knaf's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
