Hieracium pilosella subsp. villipes

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

villipes, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
