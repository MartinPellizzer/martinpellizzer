Hippeastrum araguaiense

Hippeastrum araguaiense, commonly known as the Araguaiense amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically produces large, trumpet-shaped blossoms in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Hippeastrum araguaiense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
