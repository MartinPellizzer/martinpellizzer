Hesperanthemum

Hesperanthemum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plants typically have upright stems and are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant blooms. Hesperanthemum species are drought-tolerant and prefer well-drained soil, making them suitable for Mediterranean climates.

They are also valued for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
