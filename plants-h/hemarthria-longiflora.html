Hemarthria longiflora

Hemarthria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered horsegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets with elongated florets, which are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Hemarthria longiflora is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
