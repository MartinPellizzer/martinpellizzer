Helianthus borealis

Helianthus borealis, commonly known as the Canadian sunflower, is a species native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, which are often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Helianthus borealis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
