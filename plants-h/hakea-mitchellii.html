Hakea mitchellii

Hakea mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-branching stems and its distinctive, often recurved leaves. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring a deep root system for water absorption.

Hakea mitchellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
