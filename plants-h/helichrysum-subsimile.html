Helichrysum subsimile

Helichrysum subsimile, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
