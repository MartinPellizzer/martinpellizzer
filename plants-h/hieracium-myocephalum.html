Hieracium myocephalum

Hieracium myocephalum, commonly known as the elephant's head.

It is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its distinctive, large, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble an elephant's head. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
