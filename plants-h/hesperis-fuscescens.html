Hesperis fuscescens

Hesperis fuscescens, commonly known as the dark purple sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis fuscescens typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
