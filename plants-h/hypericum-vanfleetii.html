Hypericum vanfleetii

Hypericum vanfleetii, commonly known as Vanfleet's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, typically growing in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, orange-yellow flowers in late summer. It is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
