Heliophila callosa

Heliophila callosa, commonly known as the smooth water plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped.

Heliophila callosa is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
