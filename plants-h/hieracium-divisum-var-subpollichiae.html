Hieracium divisum var. subpollichiae

Hieracium divisum var. subpollichiae, commonly known as the Pollich's divided hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, with stems that are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy as well. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
