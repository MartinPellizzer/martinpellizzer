Hieracium pycnanthelum

Hieracium pycnanthelum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. Its inflorescence is characterized by tightly packed flower heads, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

Hieracium pycnanthelum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
