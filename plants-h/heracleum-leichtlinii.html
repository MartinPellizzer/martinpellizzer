Heracleum leichtlinii

Heracleum leichtlinii, commonly known as the European cowparsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, with hollow, ridged stems and broad, pinnately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces smooth, dark brown seeds.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to both livestock and humans if ingested.
