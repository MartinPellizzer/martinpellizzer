Hieracium stipatiforme

Hieracium stipatiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like structure.

Hieracium stipatiforme is often found in open habitats and can sometimes form dense colonies.
