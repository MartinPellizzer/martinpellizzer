Hippion verticillatum var. hyssopifolium

Hippion verticillatum var. hyssopifolium, commonly known as the hyssop-leaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves that are often mistaken for those of the hyssop plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
