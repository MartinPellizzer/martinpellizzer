Humata epiphylla

Humata epiphylla, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the canopy of rainforests. The plant is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which reflects light and helps reduce water loss. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are typically white or pale purple and emit a subtle fragrance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Humata epiphylla is considered a rare and delicate species.
