Halesia tetraptera var. monticola

Halesia tetraptera var. monticola, commonly known as the Appalachian halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
