Hymenophyllum antarcticum

Hymenophyllum antarcticum, commonly known as the Antarctic fern, is a small, cryptogamic plant belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a liverwort, characterized by its delicate, moss-like structure and intricate leaf arrangement. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, particularly in the Southern Hemisphere, including Antarctica and New Zealand. Its leaves are typically narrow, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in cold, arid conditions.

Hymenophyllum antarcticum plays a minor but ecologically significant role in the nutrient cycling of its habitat.
