Hieracium aschersonianum

Hieracium aschersonianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions and can sometimes become invasive. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
