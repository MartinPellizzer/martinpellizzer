Hedeoma quinquenervata

Hedeoma quinquenervata, commonly known as the five-veined hedeoma, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

The small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in the summer months.
