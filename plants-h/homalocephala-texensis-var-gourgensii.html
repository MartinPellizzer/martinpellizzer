Homalocephala texensis var. gourgensii

Homalocephala texensis var. gourgensii, commonly known as the Texas hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features a distinctive, spiny, and lobed leaf structure that resembles a dog's tongue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
