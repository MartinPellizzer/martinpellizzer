Heliotropium mitchellii

Heliotropium mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Heliotropium mitchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
