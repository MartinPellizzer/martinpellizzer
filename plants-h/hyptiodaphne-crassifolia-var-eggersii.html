Hyptiodaphne crassifolia var. eggersii

Hyptiodaphne crassifolia var. eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' thickleaf daphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
