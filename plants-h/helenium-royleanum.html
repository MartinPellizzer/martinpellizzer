Helenium royleanum

Helenium royleanum, commonly known as the Indian ginseng or red ginger, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and red petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Helenium royleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
