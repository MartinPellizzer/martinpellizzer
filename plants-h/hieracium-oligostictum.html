Hieracium oligostictum

Hieracium oligostictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
