Hamatocactus setispinus f. flavibaccatus

Hamatocactus setispinus f. flavibaccatus, commonly known as the yellow-fruited hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus species native to the rocky habitats of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a vibrant contrast to its spiky form. Its fruit, which is yellow and fleshy, is a key feature of the varietal name, flavibaccatus.

This plant is highly valued in horticulture for its unique morphology and ornamental appeal.
