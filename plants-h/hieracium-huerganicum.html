Hieracium huerganicum

Hieracium huerganicum, commonly known as the Huergan's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hieracium huerganicum is known for its aggressive growth and can sometimes become invasive in non-native environments.
