Hopea mesuoides

Hopea mesuoides, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

Hopea mesuoides plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
