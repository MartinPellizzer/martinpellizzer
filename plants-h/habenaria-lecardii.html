Habenaria lecardii

Habenaria lecardii, commonly known as the lecard's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded habitats and prefers partial shade. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with rhizomes and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and have a distinctive, spurred lip.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
