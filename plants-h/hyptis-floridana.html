Hyptis floridana

Hyptis floridana, commonly known as Florida hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
