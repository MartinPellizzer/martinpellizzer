Hieracium marmoreum subsp. marmoreum

Hieracium marmoreum subsp.

marmoreum, commonly known as the marble weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
