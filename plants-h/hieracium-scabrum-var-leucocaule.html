Hieracium scabrum var. leucocaule

Hieracium scabrum var. leucocaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and the leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
