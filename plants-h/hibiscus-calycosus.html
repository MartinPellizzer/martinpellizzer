Hibiscus calycosus

Hibiscus calycosus, commonly known as the Sierra hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Hibiscus calycosus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
