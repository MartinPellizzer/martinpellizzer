Hieracium porphyritifrons

Hieracium porphyritifrons, commonly known as the purple rock hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple band near the base.

It is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed or nutrient-poor environments.
