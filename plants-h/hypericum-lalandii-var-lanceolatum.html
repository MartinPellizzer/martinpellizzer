Hypericum lalandii var. lanceolatum

Hypericum lalandii var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used for their purported healing benefits.
