Holcus saccharoides

Holcus saccharoides, commonly known as sugargrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Holcus saccharoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
