Hieracium atyphum

Hieracium atyphum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium atyphum is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
