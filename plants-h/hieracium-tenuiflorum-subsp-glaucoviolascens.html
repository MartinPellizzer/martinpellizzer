Hieracium tenuiflorum subsp. glaucoviolascens

Hieracium tenuiflorum subsp.

glaucoviolascens, commonly known as the violet-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive violet-tinged hue.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves, giving it a rugged appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its unique floral coloration and is part of the Asteraceae family.
