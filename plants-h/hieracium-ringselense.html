Hieracium ringselense

Hieracium ringselense, commonly known as the Ringel's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
