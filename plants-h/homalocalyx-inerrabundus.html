Homalocalyx inerrabundus

Homalocalyx inerrabundus, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
