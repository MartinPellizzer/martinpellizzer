Hieracium epilepideum

Hieracium epilepideum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium epilepideum is known for its spreading growth habit and can form dense colonies, making it both a ornamental plant and a potential weed.
