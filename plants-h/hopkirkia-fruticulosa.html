Hopkirkia fruticulosa

Hopkirkia fruticulosa, commonly known as the creeping thyme.

It is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, adding a fragrant, peppery note to dishes.

Hopkirkia fruticulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
