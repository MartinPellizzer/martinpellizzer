Hedychium cernuum

Hedychium cernuum, commonly known as the hanging ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous yellow-orange flowers. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces a fragrant, spicy aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium cernuum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
