Horanthes podanisia

Horanthes podanisia, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Horanthes podanisia is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal rainfall.
