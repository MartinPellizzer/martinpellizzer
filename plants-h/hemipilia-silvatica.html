Hemipilia silvatica

Hemipilia silvatica, commonly known as the forest hemipilia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense clusters over time.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Hemipilia silvatica is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
