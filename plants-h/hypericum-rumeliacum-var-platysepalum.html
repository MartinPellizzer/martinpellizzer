Hypericum rumeliacum var. platysepalum

Hypericum rumeliacum var. platysepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepaled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its distinctive, broad, flat sepals. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It has aromatic, glandular leaves and is known for its medicinal properties.

This variant is closely related to other Hypericum species but is distinguished by its unique floral morphology.
