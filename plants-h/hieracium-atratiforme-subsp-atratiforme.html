Hieracium atratiforme subsp. atratiforme

Hieracium atratiforme subsp.

atratiforme, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its aggressive growth and can form dense stands, making it a potential weed in some habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark green foliage and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.
