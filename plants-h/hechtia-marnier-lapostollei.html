Hechtia marnier-lapostollei

Hechtia marnier-lapostollei, commonly known as the Marnier-Lapostolle hechtia, is a striking plant native to Mexico, specifically the state of Oaxaca.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a vibrant contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among bromeliad enthusiasts.
