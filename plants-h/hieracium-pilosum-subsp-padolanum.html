Hieracium pilosum subsp. padolanum

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

padolanum, commonly known as the Padolan mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
