Hibiscus paramutabilis

Hibiscus paramutabilis, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink to red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus paramutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
