Hieracium caesium subsp. psammogenes

Hieracium caesium subsp.

psammogenes, commonly known as the sand yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or inland dunes.
