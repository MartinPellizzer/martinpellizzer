Hieracium dragicola subsp. carstiense

Hieracium dragicola subsp.

carstiense, commonly known as the Carstian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, while its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
