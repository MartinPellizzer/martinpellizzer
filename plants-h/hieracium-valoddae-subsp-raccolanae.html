Hieracium valoddae subsp. raccolanae

Hieracium valoddae subsp.

raccolanae, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It is considered a minor weed in some regions but plays a role in local ecosystems.
