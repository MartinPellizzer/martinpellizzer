Helichrysum boiteaui

Helichrysum boiteaui, commonly known as the Cape goldball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble small balls. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
