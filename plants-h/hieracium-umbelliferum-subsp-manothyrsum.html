Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. manothyrsum

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

manothyrsum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges.

It is often found in open habitats and can be a common weed in agricultural areas.
