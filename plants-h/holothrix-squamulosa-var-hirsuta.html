Holothrix squamulosa var. hirsuta

Holothrix squamulosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy holothrix, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
