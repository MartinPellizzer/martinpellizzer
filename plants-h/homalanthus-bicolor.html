Homalanthus bicolor

Homalanthus bicolor, commonly known as the Hawaiian false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides in Hawaii.
