Habenaria flava var. herbiola

Habenaria flava var. herbiola, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils in marshy or swampy habitats. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
