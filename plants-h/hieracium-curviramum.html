Hieracium curviramum

Hieracium curviramum, commonly known as the curvedleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a wavy or curled margin, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
