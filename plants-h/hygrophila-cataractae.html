Hygrophila cataractae

Hygrophila cataractae, commonly known as the river hygrophila, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as rivers and ponds. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, often appearing in the summer months.

Hygrophila cataractae is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions and is often used in aquariums and wetland restoration projects.
