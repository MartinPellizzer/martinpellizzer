Hygrophila gilletii

Hygrophila gilletii, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It thrives in shallow waters and wetlands, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface.

Hygrophila gilletii is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
