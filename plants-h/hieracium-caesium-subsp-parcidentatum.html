Hieracium caesium subsp. parcidentatum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

parcidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to reddish ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, often with hairy surfaces, and it produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is a common species in the genus Hieracium, known for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
