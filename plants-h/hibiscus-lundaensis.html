Hibiscus lundaensis

Hibiscus lundaensis, commonly known as the Lunda hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus lundaensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species in the study of African flora.
