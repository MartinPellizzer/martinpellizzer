Hordeum leporinum subsp. glaucum

Hordeum leporinum subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the blue barley, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its glaucous blue-gray foliage and spikelet clusters that bear small, pale grains.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and produces a dense, tufted growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
