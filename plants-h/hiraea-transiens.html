Hiraea transiens

Hiraea transiens, commonly known as the wandering hiraea, is a rare and elusive plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense tropical forests. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered.

Its botanical study is important for understanding biodiversity in understudied ecosystems.
