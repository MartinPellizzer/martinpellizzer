Hieracium spidiophyton

Hieracium spidiophyton, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a dense cluster of disk florets, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Hieracium spidiophyton is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
