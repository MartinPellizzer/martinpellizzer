Heteropappus altaicus var. taitoensis

Heteropappus altaicus var. taitoensis, commonly known as the Taito goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including drought and extreme temperatures.

Its fibrous roots help it anchor in sandy soils, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
