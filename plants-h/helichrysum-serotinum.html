Helichrysum serotinum

Helichrysum serotinum, commonly known as the late helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers.

Helichrysum serotinum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
