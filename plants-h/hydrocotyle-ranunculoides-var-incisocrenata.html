Hydrocotyle ranunculoides var. incisocrenata

Hydrocotyle ranunculoides var. incisocrenata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water pennywort, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. Its flowers are tiny, white, and clustered in umbels, producing small, yellowish fruits.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
