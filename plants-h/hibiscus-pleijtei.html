Hibiscus pleijtei

Hibiscus pleijtei, commonly known as the pleijtei hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent central pistil. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its striking, five-petaled blooms that appear during the summer months. Hibiscus pleijtei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
