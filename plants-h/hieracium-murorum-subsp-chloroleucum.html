Hieracium murorum subsp. chloroleucum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

chloroleucum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer.

The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
