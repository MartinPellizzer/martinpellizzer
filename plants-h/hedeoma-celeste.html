Hedeoma celeste

Hedeoma celeste, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble misty clouds. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It blooms from late summer to early fall, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hedeoma celeste is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
