Hydrangea maximowiczii

Hydrangea maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean hydrangea, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as a shrub with upright, branched stems and broad, ovate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Hydrangea maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
