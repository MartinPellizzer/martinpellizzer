Hoya teysmanniana variegata

Hoya teysmanniana variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Wax Plant, is a striking tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that display a unique variegation of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardens and hanging displays.
