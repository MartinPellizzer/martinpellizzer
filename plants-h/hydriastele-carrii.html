Hydriastele carrii

Hydriastele carrii, commonly known as the Carri's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Hydriastele carrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
