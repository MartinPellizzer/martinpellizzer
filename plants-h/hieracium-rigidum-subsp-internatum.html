Hieracium rigidum subsp. internatum

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

internatum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features upright stems with rough, hairy surfaces and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
