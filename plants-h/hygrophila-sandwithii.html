Hygrophila sandwithii

Hygrophila sandwithii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Hygrophila sandwithii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
