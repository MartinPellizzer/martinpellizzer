Hosta sieboldii f. angustifolia

Hosta sieboldii f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf siebold hosta, is a varietal form of the hosta genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and delicate blooms, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
