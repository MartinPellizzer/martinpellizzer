Hugelia filifolia

Hugelia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf hugelia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Hugelia filifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
