Hieracium subplicatum

Hieracium subplicatum, commonly known as the zigzag hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a zigzag or wavy edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk, contributing to its ornamental value.
