Hieracium laevigatum subsp. rothmaleri

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

rothmaleri, commonly known as Rothmaler's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive yellowish or reddish coloration. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish flowers with ray florets.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
