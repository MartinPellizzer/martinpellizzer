Hypericum quadrangulum subsp. corsicum

Hypericum quadrangulum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers with four petals.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
