Hieracium porthortense

Hieracium porthortense, commonly known as the Porteous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste grounds. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually alternate and lance-shaped.

Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellowish-orange petals that attract pollinators like bees and hoverflies.
