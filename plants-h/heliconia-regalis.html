Heliconia regalis

Heliconia regalis, commonly known as the royal heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within the inflorescence. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Heliconia regalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
