Hieracium subcurvatoides

Hieracium subcurvatoides, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy or curled margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
