Hieracium incanum subsp. incanoides

Hieracium incanum subsp.

incanoides, commonly known as the silver hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features erect, hairy stems that are often tinged with a silvery hue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.
