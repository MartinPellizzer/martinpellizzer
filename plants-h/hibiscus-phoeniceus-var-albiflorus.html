Hibiscus phoeniceus var. albiflorus

Hibiscus phoeniceus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a prominent yellow center. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus phoeniceus var. albiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
