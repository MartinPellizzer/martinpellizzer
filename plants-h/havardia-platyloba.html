Havardia platyloba

Havardia platyloba, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The tree has pinnately compound leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush canopy. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Havardia platyloba is well-suited to warm, coastal climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
