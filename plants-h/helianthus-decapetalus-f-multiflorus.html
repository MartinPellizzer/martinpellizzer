Helianthus decapetalus f. multiflorus

Helianthus decapetalus f. multiflorus, commonly known as the multibract sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by green bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a central disk of tubular flowers surrounded by multiple rows of ray flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
