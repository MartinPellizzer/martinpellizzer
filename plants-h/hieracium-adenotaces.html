Hieracium adenotaces

Hieracium adenotaces, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium adenotaces is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
