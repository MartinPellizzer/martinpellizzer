Holodiscus glabrescens

Holodiscus glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth discflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, disc-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and grows in dry, rocky habitats. It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Holodiscus glabrescens is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
