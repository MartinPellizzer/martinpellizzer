Hymenocallis floridana subsp. amplifolia

Hymenocallis floridana subsp.

amplifolia, commonly known as the Florida rain lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features striking white to pale yellow flowers with a prominent central stigma, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
