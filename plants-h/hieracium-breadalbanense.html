Hieracium breadalbanense

Hieracium breadalbanense, commonly known as the Albanian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
