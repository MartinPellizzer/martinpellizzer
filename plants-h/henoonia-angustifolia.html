Henoonia angustifolia

Henoonia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf henoonia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
