Hedychium roxburghii var. oblongum

Hedychium roxburghii var. oblongum, commonly known as the yellow ginger or golden torch ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that bear clusters of large, showy yellow flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Hedychium roxburghii var. oblongum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
