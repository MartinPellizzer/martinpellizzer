Hieracium flagellare nothovar. amauracron

Hieracium flagellare nothovar. amauracron, commonly known as the aurora hawkweed, is a hybrid plant characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flower heads.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces elongated, bristly flower clusters. Its botanical features include a creeping rootstock and a tendency to form dense colonies.

This nothovar is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological succession in temperate regions.
