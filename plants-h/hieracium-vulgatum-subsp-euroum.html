Hieracium vulgatum subsp. euroum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

euroum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of broadly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and involucral bracts.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
