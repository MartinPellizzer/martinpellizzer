Hesperis ovatifolia

Hesperis ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and ovate, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and arranged in a compact cluster.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
