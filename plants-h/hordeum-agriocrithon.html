Hordeum agriocrithon

Hordeum agriocrithon, commonly known as the Armenian barley, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and its seeds are enclosed in tough, straw-like glumes.

Hordeum agriocrithon is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential for use in ecological restoration projects.
