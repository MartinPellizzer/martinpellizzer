Hieracium praecox subsp. verlotii

Hieracium praecox subsp.

verlotii, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
