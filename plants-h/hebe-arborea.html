Hebe arborea

Hebe arborea, commonly known as the tree hebe, is a shrub-like plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet or white during the spring and summer months.

Hebe arborea is well-suited to coastal environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
