Homalanthus nervosus

Homalanthus nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-veined daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct venation pattern.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
