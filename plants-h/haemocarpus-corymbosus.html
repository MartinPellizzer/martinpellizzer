Haemocarpus corymbosus

Haemocarpus corymbosus, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically yellow to reddish in color. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Haemocarpus corymbosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
