Helianthus giganteus var. verticillatus

Helianthus giganteus var. verticillatus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, showy yellow flower heads that are surrounded by broad, green, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The swamp sunflower is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
