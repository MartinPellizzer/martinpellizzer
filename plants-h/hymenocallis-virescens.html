Hymenocallis virescens

Hymenocallis virescens, commonly known as the green hurricane lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that allow it to survive in various soil types.

Hymenocallis virescens is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained conditions.
