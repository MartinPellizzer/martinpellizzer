Hieracium brevifolium subsp. cumuliflorum

Hieracium brevifolium subsp.

cumuliflorum, commonly known as the Alpine Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has a hairy stem and leaves that are usually broad and spoon-shaped.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.
