Henckelia peduncularis

Henckelia peduncularis, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, cool climates, often in cloud forests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or white, with a prominent yellow center. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Henckelia peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
