Helianthus petiolaris subsp. fallax

Helianthus petiolaris subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the fallax sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
