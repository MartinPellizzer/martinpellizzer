Hesperis brachycarpa

Hesperis brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms in early summer and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Hesperis brachycarpa is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
