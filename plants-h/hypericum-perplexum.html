Hypericum perplexum

Hypericum perplexum, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Hypericum perplexum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
