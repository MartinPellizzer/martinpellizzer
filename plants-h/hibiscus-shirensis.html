Hibiscus shirensis

Hibiscus shirensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a striking yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus shirensis thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
