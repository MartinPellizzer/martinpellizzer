Hugonia busseana

Hugonia busseana, commonly known as the Brazilian water spinach, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow waters and wetlands, characterized by its long, slender, edible leaves that float on the water surface. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in aquatic environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Hugonia busseana is also valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing nutrients and reducing algae growth.
