Hypoxis membranacea

Hypoxis membranacea, commonly known as the white star ginger, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.
