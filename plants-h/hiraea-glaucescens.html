Hiraea glaucescens

Hiraea glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green hiraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a bluish sheen.

Hiraea glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
