Hieracium epichnoum

Hieracium epichnoum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite inflorescence, which consists of many small florets arranged in a disk. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium epichnoum is a perennial species that is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
