Hemionitis pteroides

Hemionitis pteroides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It has a distinctive, compact growth habit with closely spaced, fiddlehead-like fronds that remain tightly curled throughout much of the year. The plant's leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in rocky soils and can often be found growing on limestone or sandstone substrates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is considered a valuable indicator species for arid ecosystems.
