Hieracium arvicola subsp. bohemicum

Hieracium arvicola subsp.

bohemicum, commonly known as Bohemian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.
