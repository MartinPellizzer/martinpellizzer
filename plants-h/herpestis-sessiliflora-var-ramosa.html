Herpestis sessiliflora var. ramosa

Herpestis sessiliflora var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 feet tall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.
