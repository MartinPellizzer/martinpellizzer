Hemiphragma heterophyllum var. heterophyllum

Hemiphragma heterophyllum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hemiphragma, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
