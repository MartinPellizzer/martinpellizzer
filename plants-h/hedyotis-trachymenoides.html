Hedyotis trachymenoides

Hedyotis trachymenoides, commonly known as smallleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

Hedyotis trachymenoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
