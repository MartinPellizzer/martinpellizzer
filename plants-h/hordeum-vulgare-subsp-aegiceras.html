Hordeum vulgare subsp. aegiceras

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

aegiceras, commonly known as the barley grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. Hordeum vulgare subsp. aegiceras is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

It is also used in some cultures for its purported health benefits and is sometimes grown as a forage crop.
