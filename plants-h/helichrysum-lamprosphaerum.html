Helichrysum lamprosphaerum

Helichrysum lamprosphaerum, commonly known as the golden crown everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that retain their color even after drying. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
