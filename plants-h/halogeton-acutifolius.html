Halogeton acutifolius

Halogeton acutifolius, commonly known as the sharpleaf halogeton, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, making it a halophyte. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish or reddish in color.

Halogeton acutifolius plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to tolerate heavy metals.
