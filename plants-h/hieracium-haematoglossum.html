Hieracium haematoglossum

Hieracium haematoglossum, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with reddish veins, and its flowers are yellow with red markings.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
