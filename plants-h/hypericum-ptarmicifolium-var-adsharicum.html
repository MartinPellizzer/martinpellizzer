Hypericum ptarmicifolium var. adsharicum

Hypericum ptarmicifolium var. adsharicum, commonly known as the Kashmir golden strobilus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
