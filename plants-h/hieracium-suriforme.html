Hieracium suriforme

Hieracium suriforme, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium suriforme is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
