Hypericum quadrangulum var. maculatum

Hypericum quadrangulum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has four-angled stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is distinguished by its spotted or marbled leaves, which add ornamental value to gardens.
