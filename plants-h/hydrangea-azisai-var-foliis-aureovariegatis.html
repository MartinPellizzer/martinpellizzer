Hydrangea azisai var. foliis-aureovariegatis

Hydrangea azisai var. foliis-aureovariegatis, commonly known as the golden variegated hydrangea, is a cultivar prized for its striking foliage.

Its leaves are broadly ovate with a mix of yellow, white, and green hues, creating a vibrant display even without flowers. The plant typically produces large, showy flower clusters that can range from blue to pink depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping for its unique coloration and seasonal interest.
