Heuchera rubescens var. typica

Heuchera rubescens var. typica, commonly known as the red-leaf heuchera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves that range from deep red to maroon, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces upright, spikelike clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a valuable addition to shade gardens and rockeries.
