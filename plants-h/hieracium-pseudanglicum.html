Hieracium pseudanglicum

Hieracium pseudanglicum, commonly known as the false English hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
