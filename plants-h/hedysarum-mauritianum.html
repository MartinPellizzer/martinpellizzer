Hedysarum mauritianum

Hedysarum mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often branched stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Hedysarum mauritianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
