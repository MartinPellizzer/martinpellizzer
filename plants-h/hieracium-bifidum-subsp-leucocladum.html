Hieracium bifidum subsp. leucocladum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

leucocladum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white to pale yellow hairs on the leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
