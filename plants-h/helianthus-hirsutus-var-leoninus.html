Helianthus hirsutus var. leoninus

Helianthus hirsutus var. leoninus, commonly known as the lion's head sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, and its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
