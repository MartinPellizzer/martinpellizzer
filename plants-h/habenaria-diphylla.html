Habenaria diphylla

Habenaria diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and possesses two distinct leaf types, one basal and one upper. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically in shades of white, yellow, or green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
