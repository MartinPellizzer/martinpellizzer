Hopea ovalifolia

Hopea ovalifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing one or two seeds. Hopea ovalifolia is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
