Helianthus ambulans

Helianthus ambulans, commonly known as the wandering sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a dark center, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Helianthus ambulans is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
