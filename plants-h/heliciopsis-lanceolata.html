Heliciopsis lanceolata

Heliciopsis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing its seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
