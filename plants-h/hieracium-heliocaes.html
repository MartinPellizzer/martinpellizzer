Hieracium heliocaes

Hieracium heliocaes, commonly known as the sun chalice hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that resemble a chalice, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has basal and upright stems with rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium heliocaes is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its attractive, sun-like appearance.
