Hermannia bifaria

Hermannia bifaria, commonly known as the red-eyed cycad, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast strikingly with its green foliage. Hermannia bifaria is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
