Hieracium bohemicum

Hieracium bohemicum, commonly known as the Bohemian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
