Hydrangea hortensia var. caerulea

Hydrangea hortensia var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that range from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to persistent, fluffy seed heads.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to change color with environmental conditions.
