Helianthella quinquenervis

Helianthella quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
