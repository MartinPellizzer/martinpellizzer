Hasskarlia globosa

Hasskarlia globosa, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.
