Hydrocotyle quinqueloba var. stella

Hydrocotyle quinqueloba var. stella, commonly known as starleaf waterpenny, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are divided into five lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands. Its small white flowers bloom in late summer, adding to its ornamental value.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and fish.
