Hedyotis nigricans var. parviflora

Hedyotis nigricans var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered inch plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
