Haemanthus × clarkei

Haemanthus × clarkei, commonly known as the Christmas or holiday bell plant, is a hybrid of Haemanthus albiflos and Haemanthus sanguineus.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa, characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that are highly ornamental and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its foliage consists of strap-like, evergreen leaves that remain attractive throughout the year.
