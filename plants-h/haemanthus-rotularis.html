Haemanthus rotularis

Haemanthus rotularis, commonly known as the Zulu bulbous lily, is a striking South African plant characterized by its unique, clustered inflorescence.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Eastern Cape region of South Africa. The plant features strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette, with a basal bulb that stores water and nutrients. Haemanthus rotularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
