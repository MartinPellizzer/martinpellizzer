Heliotropium apiculatum

Heliotropium apiculatum, commonly known as the silky heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Heliotropium apiculatum is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
