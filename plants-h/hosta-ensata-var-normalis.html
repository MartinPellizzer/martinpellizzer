Hosta ensata var. normalis

Hosta ensata var. normalis, commonly known as the Japanese hosta, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery-blue margin. The plant produces upright, spike-like inflorescences that bear white to pale lavender flowers. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
