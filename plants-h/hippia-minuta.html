Hippia minuta

Hippia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the Nile, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
