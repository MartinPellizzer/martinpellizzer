Hieracium sciadophorum subsp. atschavicum

Hieracium sciadophorum subsp.

atschavicum, commonly known as the Atschavik hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive dark green color with a slightly waxy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
