Helichrysum formosum

Helichrysum formosum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that remain intact even after drying, giving it a long-lasting appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its resilience and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in xeriscaping.
