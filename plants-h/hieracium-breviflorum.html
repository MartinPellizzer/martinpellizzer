Hieracium breviflorum

Hieracium breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
