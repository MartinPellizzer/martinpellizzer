Hieracium pachylum

Hieracium pachylum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.
