Hydrangea aspera var. scabra

Hydrangea aspera var. scabra, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental foliage and blooms. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into woody, cone-like structures.

Hydrangea aspera var. scabra is relatively hardy and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
