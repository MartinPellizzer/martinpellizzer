Hibiscus thespesioides

Hibiscus thespesioides, commonly known as the purple hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus thespesioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
