Hesperis luristanica

Hesperis luristanica, commonly known as the Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Hesperis luristanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
