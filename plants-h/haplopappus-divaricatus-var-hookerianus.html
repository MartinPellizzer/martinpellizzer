Haplopappus divaricatus var. hookerianus

Haplopappus divaricatus var. hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's brittleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its small, narrow leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
