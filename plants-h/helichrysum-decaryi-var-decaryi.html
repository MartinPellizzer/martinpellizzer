Helichrysum decaryi var. decaryi

Helichrysum decaryi var. decaryi, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically grouped into compact, spherical clusters. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
