Hieracium fistuligerum

Hieracium fistuligerum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. It has a creeping growth habit with a central stem that branches near the base.

Hieracium fistuligerum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
