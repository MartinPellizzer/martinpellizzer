Haworthia maculata var. intermedia

Haworthia maculata var. intermedia, commonly known as the Zebra Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, triangular leaves that are often marked with white or yellowish markings resembling a zebra's stripes. The plant typically grows in a compact rosette form, and its leaves are thick and waxy, allowing it to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
