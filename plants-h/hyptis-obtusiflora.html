Hyptis obtusiflora

Hyptis obtusiflora, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hyptis obtusiflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
