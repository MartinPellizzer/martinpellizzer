Hibiscus clypeatus subsp. clypeatus

Hibiscus clypeatus subsp.

clypeatus, commonly known as the Cape hibiscus, is a shrub native to South Africa. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive yellow throat.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
