Hyptis langlassei

Hyptis langlassei, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hyptis langlassei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
