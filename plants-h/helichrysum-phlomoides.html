Helichrysum phlomoides

Helichrysum phlomoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silver-green foliage with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Helichrysum phlomoides is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
