Heliotropium supinum var. malabaricum

Heliotropium supinum var. malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
