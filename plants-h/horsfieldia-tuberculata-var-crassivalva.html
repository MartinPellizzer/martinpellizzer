Horsfieldia tuberculata var. crassivalva

Horsfieldia tuberculata var. crassivalva, commonly known as the thick-valved orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a prominent lip that is often thick and fleshy, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
