Hieracium elisaeanum subsp. elisaeanum

Hieracium elisaeanum subsp.

elisaeanum, commonly known as the Elisaean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

This subspecies is distinguished by its relatively small flowers and is often found in association with other members of the Hieracium genus.
