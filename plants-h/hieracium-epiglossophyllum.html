Hieracium epiglossophyllum

Hieracium epiglossophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
