Hieracium × calanthes

Hieracium × calanthes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the daisy family. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
