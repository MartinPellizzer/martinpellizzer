Hyptis duplicatodentata

Hyptis duplicatodentata, commonly known as the double-toothed hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a serrated edge, while its flowering stems are square and hairy.

Hyptis duplicatodentata is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
