Hieracium laevigatum subsp. knafii

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

knafii, commonly known as the knaf's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense colonies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

It is a herbaceous plant that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
