Haplostachys grayana

Haplostachys grayana, commonly known as the gray's spiny scalebush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Haplostachys grayana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
