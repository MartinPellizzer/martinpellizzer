Hieracium subgotlandicum

Hieracium subgotlandicum, commonly known as Gotland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Its flowering period occurs from June to August, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
