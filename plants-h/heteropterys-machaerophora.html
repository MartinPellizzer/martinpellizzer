Heteropterys machaerophora

Heteropterys machaerophora, commonly known as the red-flowered piper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinct appearance.

Heteropterys machaerophora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
