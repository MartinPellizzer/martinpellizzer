Henriettea multigemma

Henriettea multigemma, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and compact, star-shaped blooms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Henriettea multigemma is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
