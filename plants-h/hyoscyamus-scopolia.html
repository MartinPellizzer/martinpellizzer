Hyoscyamus scopolia

Hyoscyamus scopolia, commonly known as angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, including scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which have been used in medicine and have psychoactive properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
