Hydrangea japonica var. rosalba

Hydrangea japonica var. rosalba, commonly known as the Rosalba hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces broad, ovate leaves that are dark green in color and provide a striking contrast to its floral displays. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens throughout the season.
