Hieracium pojoritense subsp. ellae

Hieracium pojoritense subsp.

ellae, commonly known as the Ellae hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.
