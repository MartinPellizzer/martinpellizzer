Hieracium trachlosimoides

Hieracium trachlosimoides, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in disturbed soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
