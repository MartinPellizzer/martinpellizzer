Hydrangea lehmannii

Hydrangea lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Hydrangea lehmannii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
