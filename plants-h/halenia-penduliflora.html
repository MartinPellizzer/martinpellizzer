Halenia penduliflora

Halenia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower plant, is a herbaceous species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically blooms in shades of white or pale yellow, with intricate, papery petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Halenia penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to cascade gracefully from containers or hanging baskets.
