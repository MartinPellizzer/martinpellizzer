Hesperis axillaris

Hesperis axillaris, commonly known as the sweet rocket or winter sweet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and narrow, lobed leaves.

Hesperis axillaris is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
