Helianthus cornifolius

Helianthus cornifolius, commonly known as the prickly sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, triangular-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its distinctive foliage and showy flowers make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological gardens.

The plant is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
