Hedysarum spartium

Hedysarum spartium, commonly known as Persian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, narrow pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hedysarum spartium is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
