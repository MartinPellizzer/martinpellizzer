Hieracium benzianum subsp. semivulgatum

Hieracium benzianum subsp.

semivulgatum, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant has distinctive, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats.
