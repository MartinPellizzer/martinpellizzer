Hieracium pseudoprasinatum

Hieracium pseudoprasinatum, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.
