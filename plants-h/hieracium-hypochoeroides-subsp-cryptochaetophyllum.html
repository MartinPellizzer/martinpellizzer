Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. cryptochaetophyllum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

cryptochaetophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
