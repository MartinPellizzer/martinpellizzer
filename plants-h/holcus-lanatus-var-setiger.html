Holcus lanatus var. setiger

Holcus lanatus var. setiger, commonly known as hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and oval, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, along roadsides, and in cultivated areas.

Holcus lanatus var. setiger is an annual plant that spreads through seeds and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
