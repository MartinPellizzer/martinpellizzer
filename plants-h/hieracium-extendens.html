Hieracium extendens

Hieracium extendens, commonly known as the spreading hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in glandular dots. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
