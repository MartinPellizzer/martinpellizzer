Hymenoxys argentea var. thoreauensis

Hymenoxys argentea var. thoreauensis, commonly known as the Thoreau's silverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features basal leaves that are silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs, and stems that are often reddish or purplish. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
