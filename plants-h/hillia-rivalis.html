Hillia rivalis

Hillia rivalis, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru, where it grows in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Hillia rivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
