Heterotis cornifolia

Heterotis cornifolia, commonly known as the horned leaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that often resemble horns or fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem with rough, bark-like texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
