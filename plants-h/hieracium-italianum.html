Hieracium italianum

Hieracium italianum, commonly known as Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
