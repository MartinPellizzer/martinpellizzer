Helianthemum georgianum

Helianthemum georgianum, commonly known as the Georgian rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a low-growing shrub with fleshy, evergreen leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthemum georgianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
