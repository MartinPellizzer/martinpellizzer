Hiraea dentulata

Hiraea dentulata, commonly known as the toothed hiraea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with finely serrated margins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pale green.

Hiraea dentulata is noted for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
