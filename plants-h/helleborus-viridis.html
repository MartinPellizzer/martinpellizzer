Helleborus viridis

Helleborus viridis, commonly known as the green hellebore, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and clusters of white or pale green flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is evergreen, with foliage that persists through the winter, making it a notable feature in shady gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in woodland environments.

Helleborus viridis is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
