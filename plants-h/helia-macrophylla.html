Helia macrophylla

Helia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf helia or *Heliconia macrophylla*, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's structure includes a tall, erect stem with broad, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length.
