Haplopteris linearifolia

Haplopteris linearifolia, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a linear shape. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Haplopteris linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
