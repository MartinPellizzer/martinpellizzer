Hieracium canulescens

Hieracium canulescens, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and has a moderate growth rate.
