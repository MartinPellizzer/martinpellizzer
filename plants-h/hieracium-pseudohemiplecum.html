Hieracium pseudohemiplecum

Hieracium pseudohemiplecum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are usually hairy as well.

Hieracium pseudohemiplecum is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
