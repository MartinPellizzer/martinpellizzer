Hieracium murorum subsp. canisquamatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

canisquamatum, commonly known as the dog's-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various habitats across its range.
