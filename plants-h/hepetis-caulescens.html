Hepetis caulescens

Hepetis caulescens, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Hepetis caulescens is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
