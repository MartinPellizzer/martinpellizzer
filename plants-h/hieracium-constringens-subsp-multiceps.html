Hieracium constringens subsp. multiceps

Hieracium constringens subsp.

multiceps, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its subspecies multiceps is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other varieties.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
