Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. austrogermanicum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

austrogermanicum, commonly known as the Austrian or German hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically has basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential weed in some regions.
