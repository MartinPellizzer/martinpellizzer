Hieracium caesium subsp. euryops

Hieracium caesium subsp.

euryops, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are erect and branched.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
