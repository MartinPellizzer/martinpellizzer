Helichrysum hookerianum

Helichrysum hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's immortelle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that are often long-lasting when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Helichrysum hookerianum is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
