Hyptis verbenifolia

Hyptis verbenifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are often blue-green in color, with a velvety texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple.

Hyptis verbenifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies.
