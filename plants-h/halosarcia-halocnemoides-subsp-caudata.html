Halosarcia halocnemoides subsp. caudata

Halosarcia halocnemoides subsp.

caudata, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It belongs to the family Corophyceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves.

The plant exhibits a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on coastal soils. Its specialized cellular structures allow it to retain water and withstand high salt concentrations.
