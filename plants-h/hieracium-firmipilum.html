Hieracium firmipilum

Hieracium firmipilum, commonly known as the stiff-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium firmipilum is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
