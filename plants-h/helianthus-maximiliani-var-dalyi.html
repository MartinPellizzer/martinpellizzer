Helianthus maximiliani var. dalyi

Helianthus maximiliani var. dalyi, commonly known as Daly's sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange ray flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces a single, showy flower head that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
