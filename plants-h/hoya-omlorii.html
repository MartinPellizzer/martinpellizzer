Hoya omlorii

Hoya omlorii, commonly known as the "Flower of the Moon," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of star-shaped, waxy white flowers that bloom at night. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to climb using aerial roots. Hoya omlorii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique flowering pattern and striking appearance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
