Hippocratea pentandra

Hippocratea pentandra, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually ovate and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually stunning addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
