Hieracium pseudocongruens

Hieracium pseudocongruens, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has hairy stems and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable within the Hieracium genus.
