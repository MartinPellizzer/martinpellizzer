Helia spruceana

Helia spruceana, commonly known as the spruceleaf helia, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is native to specific regions in the northern hemisphere, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Helia spruceana plays a minor but important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various small insects and animals.
