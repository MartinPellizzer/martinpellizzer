Haronga madagascariensis var. pubescens

Haronga madagascariensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy haronga, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
