Hieracium murorum subsp. polyleucum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

polyleucum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
