Helenium vernale

Helenium vernale, commonly known as the early sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow to orange petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in early summer, making it a valuable early-season nectar source for pollinators.

Helenium vernale is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
