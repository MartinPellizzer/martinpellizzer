Hesperaloe yuccifolia

Hesperaloe yuccifolia, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that resemble a spoon, hence its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences of white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Hesperaloe yuccifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
