Hieracium ceratodon

Hieracium ceratodon, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas.
