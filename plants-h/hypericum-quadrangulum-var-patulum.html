Hypericum quadrangulum var. patulum

Hypericum quadrangulum var. patulum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used in herbal remedies.
