Habenaria staminodiifera

Habenaria staminodiifera, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, flowers that are adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces a single, erect stem bearing several flowers.

The species is notable for its complex floral structure, which plays a role in its pollination strategy.
