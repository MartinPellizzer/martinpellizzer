Holboellia brachyandra

Holboellia brachyandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf holboellia, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
