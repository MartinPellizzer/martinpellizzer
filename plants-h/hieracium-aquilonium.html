Hieracium aquilonium

Hieracium aquilonium, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium aquilonium is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
