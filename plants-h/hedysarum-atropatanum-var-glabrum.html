Hedysarum atropatanum var. glabrum

Hedysarum atropatanum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth red vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and dense, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
