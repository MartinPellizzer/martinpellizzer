Hibiscus coccineus

Hibiscus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet rosemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can grow up to 6 feet tall. Hibiscus coccineus is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
