Hypericum galioides var. fasciculatum

Hypericum galioides var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in clusters, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
