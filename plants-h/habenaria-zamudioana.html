Habenaria zamudioana

Habenaria zamudioana, commonly known as the Zamudio's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow markings, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
