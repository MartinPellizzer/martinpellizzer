Hieracium commersonii subvar. subpilosum

Hieracium commersonii subvar. subpilosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The subspecies subpilosum is distinguished by its dense, soft pubescence, particularly on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and meadows.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
