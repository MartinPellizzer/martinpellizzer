Hieracium lachenalii subsp. limburgense

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

limburgense, commonly known as the Limburg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth form with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions.
