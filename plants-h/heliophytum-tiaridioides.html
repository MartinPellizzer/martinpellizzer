Heliophytum tiaridioides

Heliophytum tiaridioides, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a small, branching cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring yellow tubercles that resemble stars. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
