Hieracium epimedium subsp. pseudopraealpinum

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

pseudopraealpinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow to orange composite flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas of Europe.
