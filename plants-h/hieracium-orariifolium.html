Hieracium orariifolium

Hieracium orariifolium, commonly known as the "praying hieracium," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium orariifolium is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
