Hieracium holophyllum var. dentulum

Hieracium holophyllum var. dentulum, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with distinct teeth, and its flowers are yellow, forming a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
