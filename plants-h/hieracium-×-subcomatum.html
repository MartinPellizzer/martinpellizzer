Hieracium × subcomatum

Hieracium × subcomatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets, which are typically arranged in a composite flower structure. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough or hairy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Hieracium × subcomatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
