Helictotrichon laevigatum

Helictotrichon laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open woodlands and prairies.

Helictotrichon laevigatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
