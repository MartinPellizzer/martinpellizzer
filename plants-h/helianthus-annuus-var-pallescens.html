Helianthus annuus var. pallescens

Helianthus annuus var. pallescens, commonly known as the white sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its pale yellow to white flower heads.

This plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the stems are covered in fine hairs. The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a striking contrast with their pale coloration against the green foliage.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
