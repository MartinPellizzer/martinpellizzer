Hedysarum wallichianum

Hedysarum wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its root system is fibrous, and it often thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common component of alpine and subalpine vegetation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
