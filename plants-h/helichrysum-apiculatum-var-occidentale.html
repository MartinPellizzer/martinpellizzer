Helichrysum apiculatum var. occidentale

Helichrysum apiculatum var. occidentale, commonly known as the Western everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that are tightly packed and retain their color when dried. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are wiry and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant appearance.
