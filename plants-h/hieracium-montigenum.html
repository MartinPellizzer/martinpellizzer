Hieracium montigenum

Hieracium montigenum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium montigenum is often found in open meadows and mountainous areas where it can tolerate cold and harsh environmental conditions.
