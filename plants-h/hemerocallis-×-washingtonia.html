Hemerocallis × washingtonia

Hemerocallis × washingtonia, commonly known as the Washington daylily, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Hemerocallidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features sword-like, grassy foliage. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Hemerocallis × washingtonia is prized for its hardiness, disease resistance, and ability to naturalize in various soil types.
