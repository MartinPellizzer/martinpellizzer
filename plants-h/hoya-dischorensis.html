Hoya dischorensis

Hoya dischorensis, commonly known as the "Bamboo Hoya," is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like stems that resemble bamboo, which can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, waxy, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya dischorensis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
