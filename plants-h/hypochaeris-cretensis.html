Hypochaeris cretensis

Hypochaeris cretensis, commonly known as the Cretan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem.

Hypochaeris cretensis is often found in rocky or limestone soils, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
