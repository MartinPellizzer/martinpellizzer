Hieracium granulatum

Hieracium granulatum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine, rough hairs, giving the plant its common name. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
