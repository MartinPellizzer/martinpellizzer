Hydrangea longipes var. lanceolata

Hydrangea longipes var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
