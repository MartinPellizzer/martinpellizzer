Hippocrepis comosa var. commutata

Hippocrepis comosa var. commutata, commonly known as the woolly rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
