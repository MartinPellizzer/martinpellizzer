Hieracium gregorii-bakurianii

Hieracium gregorii-bakurianii, commonly known as the Georgian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Georgia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, displaying a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.
