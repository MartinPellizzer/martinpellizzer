Hypoxis filiformis

Hypoxis filiformis, commonly known as the slender yellow star, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in grasslands and along rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and brief flowering period.
