Hieracium verruculatum subsp. karpinskyanum

Hieracium verruculatum subsp.

karpinskyanum, commonly known as the Karpinsky's hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous ray florets that attract pollinators.
