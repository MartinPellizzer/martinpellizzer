Hecaste

Hecaste, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hecaste is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
