Hydnocarpus macrocarpus subsp. malabaricus

Hydnocarpus macrocarpus subsp.

malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is valued for its bark, which contains compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense forests.
