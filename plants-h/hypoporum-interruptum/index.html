Hypoporum Interruptum

Hypoporum interruptum is a lesser-known member of the Asteraceae family known for its unique appearance and ecological significance. This plant is characterized by its finely divided leaves and delicate yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories across its native range. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and rarely cultivated. Understanding Hypoporum interruptum can offer valuable insights into plant adaptation and biodiversity in natural ecosystems.
