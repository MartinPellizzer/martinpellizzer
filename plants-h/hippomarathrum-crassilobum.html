Hippomarathrum crassilobum

Hippomarathrum crassilobum, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, broad leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.
