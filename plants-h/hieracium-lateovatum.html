Hieracium lateovatum

Hieracium lateovatum, commonly known as the late yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

It is often found in temperate regions and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
