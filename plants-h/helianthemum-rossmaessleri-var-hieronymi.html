Helianthemum rossmaessleri var. hieronymi

Helianthemum rossmaessleri var. hieronymi, commonly known as the Rossmaessler's rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean landscapes.
