Hieracium malacochaetum

Hieracium malacochaetum, commonly known as the black-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lobed or divided, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
