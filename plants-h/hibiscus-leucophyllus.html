Hibiscus leucophyllus

Hibiscus leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are a distinguishing feature of its botanical appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Hibiscus leucophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
