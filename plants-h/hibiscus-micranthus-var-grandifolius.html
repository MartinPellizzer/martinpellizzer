Hibiscus micranthus var. grandifolius

Hibiscus micranthus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
