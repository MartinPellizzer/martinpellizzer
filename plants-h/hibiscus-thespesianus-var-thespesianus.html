Hibiscus thespesianus var. thespesianus

Hibiscus thespesianus var. thespesianus, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow, often with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Hibiscus thespesianus var. thespesianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
