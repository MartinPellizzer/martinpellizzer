Hiraea cephalotes

Hiraea cephalotes, commonly known as the goldenhead sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. Hiraea cephalotes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
