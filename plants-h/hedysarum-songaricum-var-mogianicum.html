Hedysarum songaricum var. mogianicum

Hedysarum songaricum var. mogianicum, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are also a source of protein and can be used as forage for livestock.
