Hackelia gracilenta

Hackelia gracilenta, commonly known as slender hackelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and extended blooming period.
