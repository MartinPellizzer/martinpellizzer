Hemerocallis × elmensis

Hemerocallis × elmensis, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a hybrid plant in the genus Hemerocallis, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with sword-shaped leaves that are broad and grass-like. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the morning and last for a single day, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Hemerocallis × elmensis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in garden settings.
