Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp. vulgare

Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as the rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy and ornamental choice for Mediterranean climates.
