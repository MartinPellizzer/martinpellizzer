Hieracium flagellare subsp. brachyacron

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

brachyacron, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as meadows, roadsides, and grasslands. It has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Its subspecies name, *brachyacron*, refers to its short, abbreviated stem structure compared to other members of the species.
