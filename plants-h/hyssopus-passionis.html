Hyssopus passionis

Hyssopus passionis, commonly known as Passionflower Hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments at the center. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyssopus passionis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
