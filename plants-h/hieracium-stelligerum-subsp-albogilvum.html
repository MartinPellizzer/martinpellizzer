Hieracium stelligerum subsp. albogilvum

Hieracium stelligerum subsp.

albogilvum, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with white veins on the petals.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
