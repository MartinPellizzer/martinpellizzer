Hyptis spicata var. rostrata

Hyptis spicata var. rostrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
