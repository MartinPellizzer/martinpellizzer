Hieracioides

Hieracioides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, often yellow or white flowers. The leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hieracioides species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
