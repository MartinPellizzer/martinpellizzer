Helianthus nebrascensis

Helianthus nebrascensis, commonly known as the plains sunflower, is a native North American plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in prairie habitats and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a radiating pattern.

Helianthus nebrascensis plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
