Heliconia tacarcunae

Heliconia tacarcunae, commonly known as the Tacarcuna heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within the leaf-like bracts. Its structure includes tall, sturdy stems with broad, elongated leaves.

Heliconia tacarcunae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
