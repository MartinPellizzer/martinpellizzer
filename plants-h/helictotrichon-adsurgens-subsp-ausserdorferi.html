Helictotrichon adsurgens subsp. ausserdorferi

Helictotrichon adsurgens subsp.

ausserdorferi, commonly known as the mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the high elevations of the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that often form dense mats.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale blue to purple florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
