Hakea rhizomatosa

Hakea rhizomatosa, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Hakea rhizomatosa is well adapted to arid and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
