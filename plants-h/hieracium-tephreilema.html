Hieracium tephreilema

Hieracium tephreilema, commonly known as the tephreilema hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with basal leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium tephreilema is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
