Heliamphora neblinae var. viridis

Heliamphora neblinae var. viridis, commonly known as the green heliamphora, is a carnivorous plant native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures, typical of its montane habitat. Its leaves are usually green, though they may develop reddish hues under intense light.

It is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
