Hieracium glaucifrons

Hieracium glaucifrons, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving them a distinctive glaucous appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
