Haplopappus diplopappus

Haplopappus diplopappus, commonly known as the two-headed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces a distinctive pair of small, fluffy seeds in its composite flower head. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Haplopappus diplopappus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
