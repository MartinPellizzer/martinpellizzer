Haplopappus platylepis

Haplopappus platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are broad and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
