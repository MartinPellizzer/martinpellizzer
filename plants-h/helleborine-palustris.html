Helleborine palustris

Helleborine palustris, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers with a greenish-yellow hue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and woodland edges.

Helleborine palustris is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
