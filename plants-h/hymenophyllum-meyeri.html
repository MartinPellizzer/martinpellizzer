Hymenophyllum meyeri

Hymenophyllum meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's filmy fern, is a species of liverwort that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of rhizoids. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are borne on the undersides of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum meyeri is notable for its intricate morphology and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
