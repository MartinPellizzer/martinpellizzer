Hackelia grisea

Hackelia grisea, commonly known as gray hackelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Hackelia grisea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
