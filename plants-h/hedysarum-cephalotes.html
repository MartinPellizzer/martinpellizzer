Hedysarum cephalotes

Hedysarum cephalotes, commonly known as the yellow sweetclover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
