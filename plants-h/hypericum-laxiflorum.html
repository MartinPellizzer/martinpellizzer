Hypericum laxiflorum

Hypericum laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals, and its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hypericum laxiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
