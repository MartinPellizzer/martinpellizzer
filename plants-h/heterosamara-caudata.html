Heterosamara caudata

Heterosamara caudata, commonly known as the tail-fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's most distinctive feature is its unique, elongated fruit, which resembles a tail and contains a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
