Helleborus grandiflorus

Helleborus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features dark green, velvety leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers with showy, petal-like structures called tepals, which bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is adapted to grow in well-drained, loamy soils.

Helleborus grandiflorus is known for its hardy nature and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
