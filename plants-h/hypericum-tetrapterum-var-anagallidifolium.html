Hypericum tetrapterum var. anagallidifolium

Hypericum tetrapterum var. anagallidifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are narrow and opposite, with a distinctive reddish hue when young. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
