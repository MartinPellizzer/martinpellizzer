Hypericum scabridum

Hypericum scabridum, commonly known as rough St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. It prefers sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or along roadsides.

Hypericum scabridum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.
