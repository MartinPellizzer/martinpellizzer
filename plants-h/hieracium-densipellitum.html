Hieracium densipellitum

Hieracium densipellitum, commonly known as the dense-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, often in temperate regions of Europe.

Hieracium densipellitum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
