Helicteres urupaensis

Helicteres urupaensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
