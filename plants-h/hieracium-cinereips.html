Hieracium cinereips

Hieracium cinereips, commonly known as the cinereous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, known as a corymb. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium cinereips is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate climates.
