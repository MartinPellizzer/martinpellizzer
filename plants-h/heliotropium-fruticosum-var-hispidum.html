Heliotropium fruticosum var. hispidum

Heliotropium fruticosum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy foliage and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for ornamental or ecological landscaping.
