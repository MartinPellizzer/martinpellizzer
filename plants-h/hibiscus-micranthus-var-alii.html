Hibiscus micranthus var. alii

Hibiscus micranthus var. alii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight to avoid leaf scorching. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.

Hibiscus micranthus var. alii is often cultivated as a houseplant and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
