Hemestheum oreopteris var. deltoideum

Hemestheum oreopteris var. deltoideum, commonly known as the delta fern, is a variant of the oreopteris fern characterized by its distinctive, deltoid-shaped fronds.

It is a terrestrial plant native to the southeastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features intricate, feather-like pinnae that are often curled and tightly packed, giving it a lush, dense appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability and biodiversity in its native habitat.
