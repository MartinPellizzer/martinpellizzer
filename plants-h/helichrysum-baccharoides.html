Helichrysum baccharoides

Helichrysum baccharoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, papery flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, linear leaves are arranged in a dense, bushy form, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Helichrysum baccharoides is often cultivated for its drought tolerance and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
