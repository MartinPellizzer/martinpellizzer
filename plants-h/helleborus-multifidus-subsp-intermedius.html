Helleborus multifidus subsp. intermedius

Helleborus multifidus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features dark green, glossy leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky soils.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.
