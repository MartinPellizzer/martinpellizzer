Hieracium reisense

Hieracium reisense, commonly known as Reisen's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium reisense is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
