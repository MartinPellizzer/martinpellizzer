Hypericophyllum altissimum

Hypericophyllum altissimum, commonly known as the tall St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hypericophyllum altissimum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects.
