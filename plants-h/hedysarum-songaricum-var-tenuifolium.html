Hedysarum songaricum var. tenuifolium

Hedysarum songaricum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It features slender, trifoliate leaves with a delicate, fern-like appearance, and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
