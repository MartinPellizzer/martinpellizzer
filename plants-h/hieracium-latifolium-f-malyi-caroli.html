Hieracium latifolium f. malyi-caroli

Hieracium latifolium f. malyi-caroli, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a variant of the common hawkweed species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological research.
