Hedyotis cyanantha

Hedyotis cyanantha, commonly known as the blue-flowered geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, delicate leaves that are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bright blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hedyotis cyanantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
