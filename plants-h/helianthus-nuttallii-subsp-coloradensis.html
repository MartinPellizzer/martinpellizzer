Helianthus nuttallii subsp. coloradensis

Helianthus nuttallii subsp.

coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, producing abundant seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
