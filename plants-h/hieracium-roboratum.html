Hieracium roboratum

Hieracium roboratum, commonly known as the field hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium roboratum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
