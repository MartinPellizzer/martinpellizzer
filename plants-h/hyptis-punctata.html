Hyptis punctata

Hyptis punctata, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive grayish-white underside, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and white. Hyptis punctata is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value in gardens.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
