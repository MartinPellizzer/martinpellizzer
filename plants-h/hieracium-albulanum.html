Hieracium albulanum

Hieracium albulanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium albulanum is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude habitats.
