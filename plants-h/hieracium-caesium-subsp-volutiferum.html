Hieracium caesium subsp. volutiferum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

volutiferum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is often found in moist environments and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
