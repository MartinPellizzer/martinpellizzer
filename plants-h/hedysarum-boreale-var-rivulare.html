Hedysarum boreale var. rivulare

Hedysarum boreale var. rivulare, commonly known as the river hawksbeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and stream edges. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and it produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the plant is often found in wet meadows and riparian habitats.

Hedysarum boreale var. rivulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
