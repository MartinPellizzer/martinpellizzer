Helleborus × nigercors

Helleborus × nigercors, commonly known as the black-veined hellebore, is a hybrid evergreen perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves with prominent black veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, spathaceous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Helleborus × nigercors is well-suited for shaded gardens and can thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

It is also noted for its toxic properties, containing various cardiac glycosides that can be harmful if ingested.
