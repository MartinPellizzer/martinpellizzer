Homalomena saxorum

Homalomena saxorum, commonly known as the rock arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Homalomena saxorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
