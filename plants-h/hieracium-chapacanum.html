Hieracium chapacanum

Hieracium chapacanum, commonly known as the Andean hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are erect, bearing yellow flower heads.

Hieracium chapacanum is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
