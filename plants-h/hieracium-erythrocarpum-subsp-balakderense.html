Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. balakderense

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

balakderense, commonly known as the Balakdere hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and deeply lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It is distinguished by its unique fruit structure, which is a hairy, reddish capsule.
