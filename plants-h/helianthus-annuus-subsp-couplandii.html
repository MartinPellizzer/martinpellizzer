Helianthus annuus subsp. couplandii

Helianthus annuus subsp.

couplandii, commonly known as the Coupland's sunflower, is a wild sunflower species native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its yellow-orange ray florets and dark brown central disk, with leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy.

This subspecies thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It produces large, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
