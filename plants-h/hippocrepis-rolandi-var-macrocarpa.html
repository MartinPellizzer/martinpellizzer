Hippocrepis rolandi var. macrocarpa

Hippocrepis rolandi var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that bear alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insect species, such as bees and moths.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
