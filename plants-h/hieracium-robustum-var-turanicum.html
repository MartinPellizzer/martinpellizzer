Hieracium robustum var. turanicum

Hieracium robustum var. turanicum, commonly known as the Turanian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, hairy stems that bear yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
