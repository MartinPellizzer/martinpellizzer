Hibiscus paramutabilis var. longipedicellatus

Hibiscus paramutabilis var. longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features palmate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and grows as an herbaceous perennial. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its long pedicels support the flowers, making it a distinctive species within the Malvaceae family.
