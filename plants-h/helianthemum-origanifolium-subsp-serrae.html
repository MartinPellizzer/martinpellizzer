Helianthemum origanifolium subsp. serrae

Helianthemum origanifolium subsp.

serrae, commonly known as the serrated rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
