Helicteres angustifolia var. virgata

Helicteres angustifolia var. virgata, commonly known as the narrowleaf helicteres, is a variant of the helicteres angustifolia species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
