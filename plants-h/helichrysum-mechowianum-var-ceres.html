Helichrysum mechowianum var. ceres

Helichrysum mechowianum var. ceres, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble miniature cups, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers are long-lasting and can be used in dried flower arrangements.
