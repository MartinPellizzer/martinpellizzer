Hieracium lachenalii subsp. cruentifrons

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

cruentifrons, commonly known as the red-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizome and is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
