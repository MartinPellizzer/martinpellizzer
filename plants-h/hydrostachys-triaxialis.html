Hydrostachys triaxialis

Hydrostachys triaxialis, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has whorled leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around its stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is characterized by its ability to trap water in its leaf structures, which helps it float on the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Hydrostachys triaxialis plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
