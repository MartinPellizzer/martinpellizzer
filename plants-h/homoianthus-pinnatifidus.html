Homoianthus pinnatifidus

Homoianthus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed or divided. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
