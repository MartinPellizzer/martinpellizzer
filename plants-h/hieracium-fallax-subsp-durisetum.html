Hieracium fallax subsp. durisetum

Hieracium fallax subsp.

durisetum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high elevations.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
