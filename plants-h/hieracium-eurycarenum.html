Hieracium eurycarenum

Hieracium eurycarenum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium eurycarenum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
