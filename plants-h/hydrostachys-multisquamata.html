Hydrostachys multisquamata

Hydrostachys multisquamata, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water sources such as rivers and wetlands.

Hydrostachys multisquamata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to wet environments.
