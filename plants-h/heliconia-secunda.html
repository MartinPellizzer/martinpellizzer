Heliconia secunda

Heliconia secunda, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant features large, banana-like leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with a distinctive spiral arrangement.

Heliconia secunda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and plays a role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
