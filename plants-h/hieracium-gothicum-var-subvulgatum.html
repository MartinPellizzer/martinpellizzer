Hieracium gothicum var. subvulgatum

Hieracium gothicum var. subvulgatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
