Humata hosei

Humata hosei, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Humata hosei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
