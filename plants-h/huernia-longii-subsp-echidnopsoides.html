Huernia longii subsp. echidnopsoides

Huernia longii subsp.

echidnopsoides, commonly known as the horned aeolus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in small, white to pale green tubercles.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cacti and succulent enthusiasts.
