Hieracium laniferum var. longifurcatum

Hieracium laniferum var. longifurcatum, commonly known as the long-tailed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base, supporting its upright growth habit. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins, while the flowers are yellow, forming a flat-topped inflorescence.

It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.
