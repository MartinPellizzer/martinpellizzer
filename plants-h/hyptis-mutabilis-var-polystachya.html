Hyptis mutabilis var. polystachya

Hyptis mutabilis var. polystachya, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
