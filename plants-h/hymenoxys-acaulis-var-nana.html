Hymenoxys acaulis var. nana

Hymenoxys acaulis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, thick stem and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in native plant communities.
