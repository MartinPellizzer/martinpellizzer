Hibiscus apricus

Hibiscus apricus, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Hibiscus apricus is known for its ornamental value and is often used in shade gardens.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to woodland landscapes.
