Homalomena curvata

Homalomena curvata, commonly known as the curly-leaf caladium, is a tropical evergreen perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically less showy than its foliage. It thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Homalomena curvata is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant and decorative leaves.
