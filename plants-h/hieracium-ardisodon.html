Hieracium ardisodon

Hieracium ardisodon, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its flowering heads that produce yellow to orange disc flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

Hieracium ardisodon is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some environments.
