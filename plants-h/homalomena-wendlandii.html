Homalomena wendlandii

Homalomena wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a broad, colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.
