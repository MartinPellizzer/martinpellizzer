Habenaria petelotii

Habenaria petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
