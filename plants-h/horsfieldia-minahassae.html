Horsfieldia minahassae

Horsfieldia minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa orchid, is a species of orchid native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with dark veins.

Horsfieldia minahassae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
