Haloragis elata

Haloragis elata, commonly known as the slender waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its small, white to pinkish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Haloragis elata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline and waterlogged conditions.
