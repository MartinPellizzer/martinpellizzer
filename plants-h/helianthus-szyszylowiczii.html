Helianthus szyszylowiczii

Helianthus szyszylowiczii, commonly known as the Szyszylowicz's sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
