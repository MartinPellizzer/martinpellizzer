Hypericum struthiolifolium var. minutum

Hypericum struthiolifolium var. minutum, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and its close relation to other Hypericum species.
