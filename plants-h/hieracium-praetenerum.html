Hieracium praetenerum

Hieracium praetenerum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium praetenerum is known for its hardy nature and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.
