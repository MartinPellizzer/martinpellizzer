Hieracium raddeanum

Hieracium raddeanum, commonly known as the Radde's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium raddeanum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
