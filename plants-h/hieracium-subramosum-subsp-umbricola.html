Hieracium subramosum subsp. umbricola

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

umbricola, commonly known as the shade-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to low-light conditions and moist habitats.
