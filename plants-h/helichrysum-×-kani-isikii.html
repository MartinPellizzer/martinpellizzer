Helichrysum × kani-isikii

Helichrysum × kani-isikii, commonly known as the Cape gold or everlasting, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces numerous small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance and contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
