Hondbesseion verticillatum

Hondbesseion verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral herb, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around the stem, a feature that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
