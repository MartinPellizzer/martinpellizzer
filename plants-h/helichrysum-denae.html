Helichrysum denae

Helichrysum denae, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange composite flowers that are often mistaken for daisies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong aromatic scent.

Helichrysum denae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
