Helictotrichon newtonii

Helictotrichon newtonii, commonly known as Newton's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has fine, blue-green blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Helictotrichon newtonii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
