Hyoscyamus muticus subsp. muticus

Hyoscyamus muticus subsp.

muticus, commonly known as the pale thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant contains tropane alkaloids, making it toxic to humans and animals.
