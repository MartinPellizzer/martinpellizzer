Hieracium tephrocephalum subsp. purpureibracteum

Hieracium tephrocephalum subsp.

purpureibracteum, commonly known as the purple-bracted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with purple bracts that give it its common name.

It is a perennial species that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
