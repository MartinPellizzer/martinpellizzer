Hieracium lactuca

Hieracium lactuca, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its purple flowers and distinctive leaves with a milky sap, which gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its leaves are typically lobed and can be quite large, often with a rough texture.

Hieracium lactuca is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
