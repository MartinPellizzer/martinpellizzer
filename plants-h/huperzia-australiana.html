Huperzia australiana

Huperzia australiana, commonly known as the Australian clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its sporangia are clustered in dense, club-shaped structures.

Huperzia australiana is often found in the temperate regions of southern Australia and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
