Hordeum vulgare subsp. medioasiaticum

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

medioasiaticum, commonly known as the medium Asian barley, is a subspecies of barley native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and adaptability to semi-arid environments.

The plant typically produces compact spikelets with three to five rows of grains, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It is often cultivated for its high yield and tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is also valued for its use in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
