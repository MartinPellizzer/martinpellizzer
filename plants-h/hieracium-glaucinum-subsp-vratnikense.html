Hieracium glaucinum subsp. vratnikense

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

vratnikense, commonly known as the Vratnik yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, inconspicuous seeds in its flowering heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
