Hieracium murorum subsp. brachylobosum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

brachylobosum, commonly known as the short-stalked hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, disturbed soils, and along pathways.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
