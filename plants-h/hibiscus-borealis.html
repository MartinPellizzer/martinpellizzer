Hibiscus borealis

Hibiscus borealis, commonly known as the northern hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features showy, large, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hibiscus borealis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
