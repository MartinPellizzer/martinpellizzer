Hieracium porrectum subsp. subelongatum

Hieracium porrectum subsp.

subelongatum, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish-brown ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaves and relatively tall, branching stems.
