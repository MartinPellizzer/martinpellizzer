Hopea seminis

Hopea seminis, commonly known as the Malayan hopea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall stature and buttressed roots. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. Hopea seminis is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
