Hedysarum vicioides subsp. japonicum

Hedysarum vicioides subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a perennial legume native to East Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. It has a climbing habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
