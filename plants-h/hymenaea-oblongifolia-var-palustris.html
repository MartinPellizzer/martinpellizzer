Hymenaea oblongifolia var. palustris

Hymenaea oblongifolia var. palustris, commonly known as the swamp cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

This species plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
