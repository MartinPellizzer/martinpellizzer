Hedysarum scorpiurus

Hedysarum scorpiurus, commonly known as scorpion vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, twisted pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hedysarum scorpiurus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
