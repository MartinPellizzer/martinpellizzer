Hamamelis japonica f. pendula

Hamamelis japonica f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese witch hazel, is a unique cultivar characterized by its gracefully drooping branches.

This ornamental plant features small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its striking appearance and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
