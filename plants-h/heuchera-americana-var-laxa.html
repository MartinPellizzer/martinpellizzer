Heuchera americana var. laxa

Heuchera americana var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf alumroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, palmate leaves that range in color from bronze to deep red, depending on the variety and seasonal changes. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit contribute to its value in ornamental horticulture.
