Hoya fuscomarginata

Hoya fuscomarginata, commonly known as the black-edged waxplant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, where it grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves with prominent black or dark brown margins. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and typically white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels.

Hoya fuscomarginata is popular among plant enthusiasts for its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
