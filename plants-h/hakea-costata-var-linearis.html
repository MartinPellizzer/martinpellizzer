Hakea costata var. linearis

Hakea costata var. linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of bright red or pink flowers, which are typically borne in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
