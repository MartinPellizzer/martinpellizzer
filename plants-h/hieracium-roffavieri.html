Hieracium roffavieri

Hieracium roffavieri, commonly known as the Roffavier's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, daisy-like cluster.

Hieracium roffavieri is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
