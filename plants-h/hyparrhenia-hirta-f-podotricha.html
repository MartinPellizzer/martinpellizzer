Hyparrhenia hirta f. podotricha

Hyparrhenia hirta f. podotricha, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in wet or seasonally wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
