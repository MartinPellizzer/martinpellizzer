Hedysarum × smirnovii

Hedysarum × smirnovii, commonly known as the hybrid sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
