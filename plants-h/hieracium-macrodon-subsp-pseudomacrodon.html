Hieracium macrodon subsp. pseudomacrodon

Hieracium macrodon subsp.

pseudomacrodon, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tint.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a single head, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its aggressive growth habit, making it a potential invasive species in some regions.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
