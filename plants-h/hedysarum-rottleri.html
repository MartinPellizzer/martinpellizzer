Hedysarum rottleri

Hedysarum rottleri, commonly known as the Rottler's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Hedysarum rottleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
