Heuchera sanguinea var. pulchra

Heuchera sanguinea var. pulchra, commonly known as the red velvet heuchera, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It features heart-shaped leaves that range from deep red to maroon, with a velvety texture that adds to its visual appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.

Its foliage remains colorful throughout the growing season, offering year-round interest in landscaping.
