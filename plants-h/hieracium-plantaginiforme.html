Hieracium plantaginiforme

Hieracium plantaginiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium plantaginiforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
