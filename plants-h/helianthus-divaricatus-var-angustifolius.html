Helianthus divaricatus var. angustifolius

Helianthus divaricatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a serrated margin, often with a hairy underside. Its yellow flower heads are typically composed of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

This species is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
