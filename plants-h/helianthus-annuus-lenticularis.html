Helianthus annuus lenticularis

Helianthus annuus lenticularis, commonly known as the lenticular sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species characterized by its unique, lens-shaped flower heads.

This plant is native to North America and thrives in well-drained soils with full sunlight. Its large, showy yellow flowers are surrounded by a series of narrow, green bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to several feet tall.

It is also valued for its seeds, which are used in bird feed and as a source of oil.
