Hookera gracilis

Hookera gracilis, commonly known as the slender hooker, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Hookera gracilis is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
