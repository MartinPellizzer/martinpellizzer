Hordeum vulgare var. macrolepis

Hordeum vulgare var. macrolepis, commonly known as the slender barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its slender stems and elongated leaves.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces spikelets that contain three to five rows of small, hard grains. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a hollow stem with nodes, typical of many grass species.

This variety is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed.
