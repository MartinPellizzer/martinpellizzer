Holcolemma transiens

Holcolemma transiens, commonly known as the wandering spider, is a species of venomous spider found in tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Theraphosidae and is characterized by its large, hairy body and prominent chelicerae. The plant-like appearance of its web and its nocturnal habits contribute to its misleading name. While not a plant, it is often mistaken for one due to its unique morphology.

This spider is known for its potent venom, which can be dangerous to humans.
