Hortonia acuminata

Hortonia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed hortonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hortonia acuminata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
