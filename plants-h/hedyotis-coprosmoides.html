Hedyotis coprosmoides

Hedyotis coprosmoides, commonly known as the broadleaf pellitory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with smooth or slightly serrated edges. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Hedyotis coprosmoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
