Hybanthus graminifolius

Hybanthus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences and are visited by various pollinators, including small insects.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to humid conditions.
