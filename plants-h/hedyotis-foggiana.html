Hedyotis foggiana

Hedyotis foggiana, commonly known as the mountain buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, clustered leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its botanical features include slender stems and small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedyotis foggiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
