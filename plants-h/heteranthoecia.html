Heteranthoecia

Heteranthoecia, commonly known as the heteranthous condition, refers to a botanical feature in flowering plants where the stamens and pistils are structurally different.

This variation is typically observed in the arrangement and morphology of the reproductive organs. In heteranthoecia, the stamens may be longer or shorter than the pistil, contributing to the plant's overall floral diversity. This condition is often associated with specific plant families, such as the Malvaceae.

The heteranthous structure can influence pollination strategies and reproductive success in certain species.
