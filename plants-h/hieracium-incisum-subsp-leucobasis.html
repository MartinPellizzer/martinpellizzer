Hieracium incisum subsp. leucobasis

Hieracium incisum subsp.

leucobasis, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the subspecies is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to other hawkweed varieties.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
