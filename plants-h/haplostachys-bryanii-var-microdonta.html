Haplostachys bryanii var. microdonta

Haplostachys bryanii var. microdonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf false flax, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to survive in harsh, semi-desert conditions.
