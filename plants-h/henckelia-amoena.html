Henckelia amoena

Henckelia amoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and heart-shaped. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Henckelia amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and blooms.
