Hibiscus tripartitus

Hibiscus tripartitus, commonly known as the three-part hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus tripartitus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
