Hieracium gyratifrons

Hieracium gyratifrons, commonly known as the wavy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium gyratifrons is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
