Hoya ovalifolia

Hoya ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
