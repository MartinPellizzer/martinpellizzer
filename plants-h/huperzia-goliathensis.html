Huperzia goliathensis

Huperzia goliathensis, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves. This plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments and its unique reproductive structures called sporangia. Huperzia goliathensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
