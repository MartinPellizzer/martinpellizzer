Hibiscus arnottianus

Hibiscus arnottianus, commonly known as the New Zealand hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow stamen. It blooms during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is well adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
