Hyptis pyriformis var. pyriformis

Hyptis pyriformis var. pyriformis, commonly known as the Cape ivy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
