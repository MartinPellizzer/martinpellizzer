Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. venatovicianum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

venatovicianum, commonly known as the Venato-vician hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and it produces numerous small florets arranged in a composite head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf venation patterns, which contribute to its scientific name.
