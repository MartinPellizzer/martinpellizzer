Hieracium tephrocerinthe subsp. purpurascens

Hieracium tephrocerinthe subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower head with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its purple-tinged stems and is often found in open, sunny environments.
