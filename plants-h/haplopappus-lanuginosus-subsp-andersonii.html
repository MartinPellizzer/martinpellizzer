Haplopappus lanuginosus subsp. andersonii

Haplopappus lanuginosus subsp.

andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's hairy hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.
