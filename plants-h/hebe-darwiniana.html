Hebe darwiniana

Hebe darwiniana, commonly known as Darwin's hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe darwiniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
