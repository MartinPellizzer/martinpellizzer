Hesperis glabella

Hesperis glabella, commonly known as the hoary bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white with a faintly hairy appearance, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Hesperis glabella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
