Habenaria hippocrepica

Habenaria hippocrepica, commonly known as the horse shoe orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and have a unique, shoe-shaped lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
