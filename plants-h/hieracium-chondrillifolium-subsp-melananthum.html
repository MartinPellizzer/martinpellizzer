Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. melananthum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

melananthum, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive dark green color with prominent veins. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This subspecies is often confused with other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
