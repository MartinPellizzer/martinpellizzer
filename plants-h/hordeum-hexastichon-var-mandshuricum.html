Hordeum hexastichon var. mandshuricum

Hordeum hexastichon var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian barley, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in ecological systems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
