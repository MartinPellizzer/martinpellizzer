Hydrangea petiolaris var. bracteata

Hydrangea petiolaris var. bracteata, commonly known as the panicled hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy paniculate inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy underside. Its stems are often covered in bracts, which give it its botanical name.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
