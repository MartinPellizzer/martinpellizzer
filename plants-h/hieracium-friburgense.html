Hieracium friburgense

Hieracium friburgense, commonly known as the Fribourg hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. It produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
