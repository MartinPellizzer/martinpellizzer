Hieracium arrectulum

Hieracium arrectulum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium arrectulum is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
