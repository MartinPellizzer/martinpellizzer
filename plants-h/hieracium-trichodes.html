Hieracium trichodes

Hieracium trichodes, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat head, often surrounded by pale green bracts.
