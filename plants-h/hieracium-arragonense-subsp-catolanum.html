Hieracium arragonense subsp. catolanum

Hieracium arragonense subsp.

catolanum, commonly known as the Catalan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has finely divided leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
