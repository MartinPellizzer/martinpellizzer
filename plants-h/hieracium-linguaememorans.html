Hieracium linguaememorans

Hieracium linguaememorans, commonly known as the "remembering tongue" or "tongue of memory," is a species of hawkweed belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Its common name is thought to reference its historical use in herbal medicine for memory-related ailments.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.
