Hieracium lagopoideum

Hieracium lagopoideum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hieracium lagopoideum is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
