Hyptis strictissima

Hyptis strictissima, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Hyptis strictissima is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
