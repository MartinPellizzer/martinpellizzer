Hieracium amphiastum

Hieracium amphiastum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellowish or reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool, moist environments. It has a taproot system and exhibits a compact growth form to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Hieracium amphiastum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar composite flower structures.
