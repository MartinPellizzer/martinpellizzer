Hariota salicornoides f. villigera

Hariota salicornoides f. villigera, commonly known as the coastal glassworts, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, salt-tolerant stems and leaves.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in coastal areas and estuaries. The plant's structure is adapted to retain water and withstand harsh maritime conditions. Its leaves are typically bluish-green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Salicornia genus, sharing similar ecological and morphological traits.
