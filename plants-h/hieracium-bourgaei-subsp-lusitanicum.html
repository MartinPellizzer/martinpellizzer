Hieracium bourgaei subsp. lusitanicum

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more reduced. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, achene fruits.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
