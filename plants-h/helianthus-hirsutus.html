Helianthus hirsutus

Helianthus hirsutus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

Helianthus hirsutus is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.
