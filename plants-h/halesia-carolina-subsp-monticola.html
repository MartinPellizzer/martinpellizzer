Halesia carolina subsp. monticola

Halesia carolina subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the Appalachian halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in clusters along the branches.

The plant has smooth, gray-brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
