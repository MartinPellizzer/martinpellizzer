Hieracium Dispalatum Subsp. Rigidiforme

Hieracium dispalatum subsp. rigidiforme is a lesser-known member of the daisy family known for its unique and striking appearance. This subspecies is characterized by its rigid, upright stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions across Europe. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in botanical literature and is rarely cultivated. Its ecological importance and ornamental potential make it a fascinating subject for further study and appreciation.
