Hieracium vulgatum var. paralium

Hieracium vulgatum var. paralium, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
