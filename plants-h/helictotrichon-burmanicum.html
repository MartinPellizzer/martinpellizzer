Helictotrichon burmanicum

Helictotrichon burmanicum, commonly known as Burman's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green sheen, and it has a fibrous root system.

Helictotrichon burmanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
