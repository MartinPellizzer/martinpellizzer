Haworthia translucens subsp. tenera

Haworthia translucens subsp.

tenera, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa. It features translucent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with pink or red, especially in response to sunlight.

The leaves are thick and waxy, adapted for water storage in arid environments. It produces small, white or pale green flowers on tall, slender stems during the growing season.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
