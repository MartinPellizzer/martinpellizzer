Hamamelis virginiana var. japonica

Hamamelis virginiana var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces capsule fruits that release seeds in the spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
