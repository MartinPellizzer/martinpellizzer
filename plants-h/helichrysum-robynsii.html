Helichrysum robynsii

Helichrysum robynsii, commonly known as the golden marguerite or Robyn's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain colorful even when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and has small, narrow leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Helichrysum robynsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in various climates.
