Horsfieldia novae-lauenburgiae

Horsfieldia novae-lauenburgiae, commonly known as the New Lauenburg Horsfield's orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant, showy blossoms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
