Hoya hamiltoniorum

Hoya hamiltoniorum, commonly known as the Hamilton's waxplant, is a species of epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clustered, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya hamiltoniorum is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
