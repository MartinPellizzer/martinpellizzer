Holmskioldia tettensis f. flava

Holmskioldia tettensis f. flava, commonly known as the golden holmskioldia, is a hybrid form of the holmskioldia species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows as a succulent shrub, exhibiting a compact and bushy growth habit. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
