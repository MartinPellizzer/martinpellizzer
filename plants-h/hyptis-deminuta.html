Hyptis deminuta

Hyptis deminuta, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy flowers.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a minty scent when crushed. Its flowers are typically tubular, with two lips, and range in color from white to purple.

Hyptis deminuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
