Hypericum apiculatum

Hypericum apiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are often reddish in color and bear small, bead-like structures called stipules.

Hypericum apiculatum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
