Hovea ilicifolia

Hovea ilicifolia, commonly known as the coastal hovea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by bean-like pods. Hovea ilicifolia is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
