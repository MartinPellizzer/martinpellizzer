Heuchera dichotoma

Heuchera dichotoma, commonly known as the forked coral bells, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to purple and green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
