Hyptis loeseneriana

Hyptis loeseneriana, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
