Hemionitis mairei

Hemionitis mairei, commonly known as the Maire's hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that form a dense, low-growing mat. The plant thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis mairei is considered a conservation concern.
