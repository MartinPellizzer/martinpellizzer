Hieracium aequabile

Hieracium aequabile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Hieracium aequabile is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
