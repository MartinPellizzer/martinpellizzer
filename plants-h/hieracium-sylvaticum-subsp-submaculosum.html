Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. submaculosum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

submaculosum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. This subspecies is distinguished by the presence of small, dark spots on its leaves, which give it its common name.

It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
