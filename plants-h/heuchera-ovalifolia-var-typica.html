Heuchera ovalifolia var. typica

Heuchera ovalifolia var. typica, commonly known as the oval-leaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, oval leaves that range in color from deep red to green, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and modest blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
