Hesperis laciniata var. aeruginea

Hesperis laciniata var. aeruginea, commonly known as the blue scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows and dry grasslands, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
