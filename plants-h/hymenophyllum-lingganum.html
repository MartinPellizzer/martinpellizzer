Hymenophyllum lingganum

Hymenophyllum lingganum, commonly known as the linggan moss, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are closely clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum lingganum is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in maintaining forest floor microhabitats.
