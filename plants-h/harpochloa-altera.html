Harpochloa altera

Harpochloa altera, commonly known as the variable harpochloa, is a grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Harpochloa altera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
