Hevea guianensis f. typica

Hevea guianensis f. typica, commonly known as the Guianese rubber tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces a milky latex sap, which is commercially valuable for rubber production. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

This species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting diverse tropical wildlife.
