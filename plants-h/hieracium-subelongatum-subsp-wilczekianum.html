Hieracium subelongatum subsp. wilczekianum

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

wilczekianum, commonly known as the Wilczek's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated leaves and specific geographic distribution.
