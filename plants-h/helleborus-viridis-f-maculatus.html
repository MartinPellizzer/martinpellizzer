Helleborus viridis f. maculatus

Helleborus viridis f. maculatus, commonly known as the spotted green hellebore, is a unique variant of the green hellebore, characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, native to parts of Europe. The foliage is typically dark green with white or pale yellow spots, adding visual interest to gardens. It produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of green and white during late winter to early spring.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
