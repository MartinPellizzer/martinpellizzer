Hieracium tephrosoma subsp. valsicum

Hieracium tephrosoma subsp.

valsicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish-orange composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other members of the species. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or grassy areas at high elevations.

The plant is typically perennial and has a low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
