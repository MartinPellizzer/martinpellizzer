Hypochaeris sonchoides var. fibrillosa

Hypochaeris sonchoides var. fibrillosa, commonly known as the fibrous cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lobed and often covered in fine, silky hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

The fibrous variety is distinguished by its hairy, fibrous stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance.
