Helleborus lividus subsp. corsicus

Helleborus lividus subsp.

corsicus, commonly known as Corsican hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern.

The plant produces clusters of pale green or white flowers with prominent, showy spathes that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
