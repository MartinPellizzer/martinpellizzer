Hieracium jurassicum subsp. garganum

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

garganum, commonly known as the Garganum hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and possesses basal leaves that are typically hairy and lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single capitulum, typical of the Asteraceae family. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

It is a relatively rare and localized plant, with limited distribution across southern Europe.
