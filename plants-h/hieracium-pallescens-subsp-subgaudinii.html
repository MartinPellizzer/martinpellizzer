Hieracium pallescens subsp. subgaudinii

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

subgaudinii, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and its role in certain traditional herbal practices.
