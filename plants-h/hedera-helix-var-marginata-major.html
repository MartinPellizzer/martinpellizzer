Hedera helix var. marginata-major

Hedera helix var. marginata-major, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically a mix of green and gold, with the margins often displaying a more intense yellow or gold coloration. This variety thrives in moderate light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a climbing plant that can be trained to grow on trellises or walls, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

The plant is also noted for its ability to purify indoor air, adding to its appeal as a houseplant.
