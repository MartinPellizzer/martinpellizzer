Hectorella

Hectorella, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Hectorella has small, pale green leaves with a rough texture and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
