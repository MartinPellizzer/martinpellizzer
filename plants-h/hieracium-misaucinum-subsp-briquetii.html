Hieracium misaucinum subsp. briquetii

Hieracium misaucinum subsp.

briquetii, commonly known as the Briquet's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is often found in the Pyrenees and other mountainous regions of southern Europe.
