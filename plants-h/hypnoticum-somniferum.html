Hypnoticum somniferum

Hypnoticum somniferum, commonly known as opium poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces a latex sap that contains alkaloids such as morphine and codeine, which are potent analgesics. The plant has been cultivated for centuries for its medicinal and recreational uses. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, and it has narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The opium poppy is also a key species in the production of various pharmaceutical drugs.
