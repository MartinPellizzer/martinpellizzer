Huperzia erubescens

Huperzia erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed clubmoss, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and mountainous regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on the underside of its leaves.

Huperzia erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
