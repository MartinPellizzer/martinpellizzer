Hybochilus leochilinus

Hybochilus leochilinus, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces intricate, butterfly-shaped blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
