Hoya smithii

Hoya smithii, commonly known as the Wandering Jew, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, often with a reddish tinge when exposed to bright light. This plant is well-known for its ability to climb using aerial roots and for its relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
