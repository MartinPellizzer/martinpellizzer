Hieracium capitonale

Hieracium capitonale, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branching.

Hieracium capitonale is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
