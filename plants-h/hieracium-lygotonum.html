Hieracium lygotonum

Hieracium lygotonum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium lygotonum is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
