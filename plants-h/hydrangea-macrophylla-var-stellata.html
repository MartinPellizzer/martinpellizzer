Hydrangea macrophylla var. stellata

Hydrangea macrophylla var. stellata, commonly known as the stellate hydrangea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly serrated. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and seasonal color changes.
