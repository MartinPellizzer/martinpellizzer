Hieracium murorum subsp. urolepioides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

urolepioides, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.

The plant has basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, erect head. It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and the presence of a short, fibrous root system.

This subspecies is often found in arid environments and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
