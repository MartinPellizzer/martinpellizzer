Helichrysum calvertianum

Helichrysum calvertianum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is generally gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
