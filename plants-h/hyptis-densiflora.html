Hyptis densiflora

Hyptis densiflora, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its densely packed, small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Hyptis densiflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
