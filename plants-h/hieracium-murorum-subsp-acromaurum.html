Hieracium murorum subsp. acromaurum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

acromaurum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowers are composed of five yellow petals and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its yellowish-orange stem base, which is more pronounced than in other subspecies of Hieracium murorum.
