Hieracium murorum subsp. cinerascens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, grayish hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
