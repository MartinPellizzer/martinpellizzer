Humboltia plumosa

Humboltia plumosa, commonly known as the featherleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along rocky outcrops or in sandy coastal environments.

Humboltia plumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
