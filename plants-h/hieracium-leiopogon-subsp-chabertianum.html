Hieracium leiopogon subsp. chabertianum

Hieracium leiopogon subsp.

chabertianum, commonly known as the Chabert's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
