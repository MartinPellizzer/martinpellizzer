Helichrysum secundiflorum

Helichrysum secundiflorum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellow to golden-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is known for its ability to retain its color when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Helichrysum secundiflorum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine.
