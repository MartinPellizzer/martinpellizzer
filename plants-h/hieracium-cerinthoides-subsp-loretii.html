Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. loretii

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

loretii, commonly known as the Loretto hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct botanical features and ecological niche within its native range.
