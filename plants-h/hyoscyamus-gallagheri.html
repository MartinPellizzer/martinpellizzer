Hyoscyamus gallagheri

Hyoscyamus gallagheri, commonly known as the Gallagher's thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that contain tropane alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
