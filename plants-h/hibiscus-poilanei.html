Hibiscus poilanei

Hibiscus poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hibiscus poilanei is often found in moist, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar.
