Hibiscus brasiliensis var. sylvaticus

Hibiscus brasiliensis var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the Brazilian woods rose, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hibiscus brasiliensis var. sylvaticus thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
