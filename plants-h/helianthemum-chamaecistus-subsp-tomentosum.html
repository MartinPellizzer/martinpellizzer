Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp. tomentosum

Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand hot, dry conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
