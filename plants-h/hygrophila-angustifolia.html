Hygrophila angustifolia

Hygrophila angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide, arranged oppositely along its stem. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months.

Hygrophila angustifolia is often used in aquariums due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in water cultures.
