Himalaiella chitralica

Himalaiella chitralica, commonly known as the Chitral bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Himalaiella chitralica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
