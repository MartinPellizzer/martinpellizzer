Hakea makinsonii

Hakea makinsonii, commonly known as the pink hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical, pink to red floral spikes that bloom during spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Hakea makinsonii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
