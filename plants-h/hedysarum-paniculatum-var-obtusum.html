Hedysarum paniculatum var. obtusum

Hedysarum paniculatum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf sweetclover, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in agricultural settings.
