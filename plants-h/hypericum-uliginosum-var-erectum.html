Hypericum uliginosum var. erectum

Hypericum uliginosum var. erectum, commonly known as swamp St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides nectar for various pollinators.
