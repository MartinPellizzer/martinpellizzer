Hedysarum poncinsii

Hedysarum poncinsii, commonly known as the Poncin's vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hedysarum poncinsii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
