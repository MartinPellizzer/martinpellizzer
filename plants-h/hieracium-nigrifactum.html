Hieracium nigrifactum

Hieracium nigrifactum, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with dark markings.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
