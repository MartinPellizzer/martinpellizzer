Hyacinthella persica

Hyacinthella persica, commonly known as Persian hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are essential for its growth and propagation. Hyacinthella persica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and pleasant scent.
