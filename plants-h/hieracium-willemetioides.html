Hieracium willemetioides

Hieracium willemetioides, commonly known as the Willemet's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
