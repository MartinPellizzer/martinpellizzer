Hiraea indica

Hiraea indica, commonly known as the Indian hiraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hiraea indica is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
