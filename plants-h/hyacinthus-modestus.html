Hyacinthus modestus

Hyacinthus modestus, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. Its flowers are composed of six tepals that are tightly packed together.

Hyacinthus modestus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.
