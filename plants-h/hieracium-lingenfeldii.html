Hieracium lingenfeldii

Hieracium lingenfeldii, commonly known as the Lingenfeld's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe, this species thrives in grassy habitats and open woodlands. It is a perennial plant that spreads via rhizomes and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
