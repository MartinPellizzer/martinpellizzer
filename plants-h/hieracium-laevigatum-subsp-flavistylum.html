Hieracium laevigatum subsp. flavistylum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

flavistylum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
