Hibbertia sejuncta

Hibbertia sejuncta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub with slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals and a distinctive, tubular throat. Hibbertia sejuncta is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
