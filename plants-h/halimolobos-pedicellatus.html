Halimolobos pedicellatus

Halimolobos pedicellatus, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. Its fibrous leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
