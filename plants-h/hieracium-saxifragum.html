Hieracium saxifragum

Hieracium saxifragum, commonly known as the saxifrage hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium saxifragum is often found in open, sunny areas at high altitudes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
