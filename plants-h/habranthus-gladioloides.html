Habranthus gladioloides

Habranthus gladioloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale green with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
