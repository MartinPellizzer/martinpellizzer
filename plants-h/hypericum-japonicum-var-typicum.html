Hypericum japonicum var. typicum

Hypericum japonicum var. typicum, commonly known as Japanese St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects.
