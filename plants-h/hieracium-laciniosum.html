Hieracium laciniosum

Hieracium laciniosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in grassy areas and open habitats. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils.
