Heliopsis balsamorhiza

Heliopsis balsamorhiza, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, cone-shaped center, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Heliopsis balsamorhiza is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
