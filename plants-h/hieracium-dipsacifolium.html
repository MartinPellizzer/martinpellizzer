Hieracium dipsacifolium

Hieracium dipsacifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in open habitats and can be an indicator of disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
