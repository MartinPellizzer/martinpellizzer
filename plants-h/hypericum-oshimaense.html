Hypericum oshimaense

Hypericum oshimaense, commonly known as the Oshima St. John's wort, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Hypericum.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It has small, opposite leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals.

Hypericum oshimaense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
