Hieracium coloriscapum subsp. stenophyllophorum

Hieracium coloriscapum subsp.

stenophyllophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and it is part of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
