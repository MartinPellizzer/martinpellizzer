Hieracium aymericianum subsp. caligatum

Hieracium aymericianum subsp.

caligatum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
