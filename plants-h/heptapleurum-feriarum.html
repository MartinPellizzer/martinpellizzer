Heptapleurum feriarum

Heptapleurum feriarum, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy foliage that resembles a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive silver-white underside, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green upper surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Heptapleurum feriarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
