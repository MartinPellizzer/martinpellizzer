Hyptis alutacea var. macrophylla

Hyptis alutacea var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
