Hypericum articulatum

Hypericum articulatum, commonly known as the jointed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that often have distinct joint-like structures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum articulatum is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
