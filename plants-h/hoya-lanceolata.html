Hoya lanceolata

Hoya lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Hoya lanceolata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Its unique foliage and elegant blooms make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
