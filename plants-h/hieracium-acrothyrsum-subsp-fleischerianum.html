Hieracium acrothyrsum subsp. fleischerianum

Hieracium acrothyrsum subsp.

fleischerianum, commonly known as the Fleischer's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It features basal and alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape.

The flowers are yellow and occur in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
