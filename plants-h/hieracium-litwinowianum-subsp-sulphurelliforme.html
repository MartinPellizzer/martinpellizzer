Hieracium litwinowianum subsp. sulphurelliforme

Hieracium litwinowianum subsp.

sulphurelliforme, commonly known as the sulphur-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive, sulphur-like yellow coloration of the flower heads, which sets it apart from other hawkweed species.
