Hebea formosa

Hebea formosa, commonly known as the beautiful hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of purple or blue flowers in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hebea formosa prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread cultivation.
