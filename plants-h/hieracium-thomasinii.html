Hieracium thomasinii

Hieracium thomasinii, commonly known as Thomasin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Thomasin's hawkweed is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
