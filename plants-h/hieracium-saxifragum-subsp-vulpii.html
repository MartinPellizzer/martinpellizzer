Hieracium saxifragum subsp. vulpii

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

vulpii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to colonize harsh environments.
