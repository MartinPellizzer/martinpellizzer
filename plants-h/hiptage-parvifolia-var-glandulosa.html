Hiptage parvifolia var. glandulosa

Hiptage parvifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hiptage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves and stems are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
