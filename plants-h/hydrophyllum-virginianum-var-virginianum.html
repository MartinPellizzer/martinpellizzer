Hydrophyllum virginianum var. virginianum

Hydrophyllum virginianum var. virginianum, commonly known as Virginia waterleaf, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable species in native plant gardens.
