Heliconia fredberryana

Heliconia fredberryana, commonly known as the Fredberry's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful, and often vibrantly red or orange bracts that surround its inflorescence. The plant has strap-like, long, and pointed leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered within the bracts, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
