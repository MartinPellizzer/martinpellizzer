Heliotropium dichotomum

Heliotropium dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, sunny environments.

The plant's name refers to its characteristic branching pattern, with stems splitting into two at each node.
