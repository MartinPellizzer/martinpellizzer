Hydrolea capsularis

Hydrolea capsularis, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant's adaptability and ornamental value have led to its cultivation in various regions beyond its native habitat.
