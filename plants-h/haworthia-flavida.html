Haworthia flavida

Haworthia flavida, commonly known as the Zulu crystal or the Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in white to yellowish tubercles. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
