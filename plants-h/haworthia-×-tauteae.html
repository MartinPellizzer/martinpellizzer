Haworthia × tauteae

Haworthia × tauteae, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings, which help in reducing water loss. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.

Haworthia × tauteae is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
