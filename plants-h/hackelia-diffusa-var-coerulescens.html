Hackelia diffusa var. coerulescens

Hackelia diffusa var. coerulescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in terminal racemes, giving it a mist-like appearance.

It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soils.
