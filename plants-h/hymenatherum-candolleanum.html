Hymenatherum candolleanum

Hymenatherum candolleanum, commonly known as the Candolle's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and light brown, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Hymenatherum candolleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
