Helianthemum × lucentinum

Helianthemum × lucentinum, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Helianthemum nummularium and Helianthemum hirsutum. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
