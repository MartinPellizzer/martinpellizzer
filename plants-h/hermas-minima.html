Hermas minima

Hermas minima, commonly known as the dwarf hermas, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hermas minima is considered a rare and endangered species.
