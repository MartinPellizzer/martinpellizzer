Helianthemum oelandicum f. sulphureum

Helianthemum oelandicum f. sulphureum, commonly known as the sulphur sandwort, is a low-growing succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are fleshy and adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with limited moisture.

Its unique coloration and compact growth make it a distinctive addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
