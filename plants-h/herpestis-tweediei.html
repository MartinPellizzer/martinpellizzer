Herpestis tweediei

Herpestis tweediei, commonly known as the Texas hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
