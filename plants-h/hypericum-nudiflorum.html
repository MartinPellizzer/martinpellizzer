Hypericum nudiflorum

Hypericum nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its lack of leafy bracts beneath the flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Hypericum nudiflorum is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
