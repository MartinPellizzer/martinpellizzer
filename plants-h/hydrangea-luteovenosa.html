Hydrangea luteovenosa

Hydrangea luteovenosa, commonly known as the yellow-veined hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with prominent yellow veins, which give it its common name. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hydrangea luteovenosa is also valued for its ability to bloom profusely in late summer and early autumn.
