Hyoscyamus datora

Hyoscyamus datora, commonly known as the Datora thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided in gardens and landscapes.
