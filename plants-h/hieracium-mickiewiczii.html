Hieracium mickiewiczii

Hieracium mickiewiczii, commonly known as Mickiewicz's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
