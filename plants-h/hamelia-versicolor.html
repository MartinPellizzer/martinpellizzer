Hamelia versicolor

Hamelia versicolor, commonly known as the flame bearden, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Hamelia versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
