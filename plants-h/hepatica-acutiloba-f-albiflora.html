Hepatica acutiloba f. albiflora

Hepatica acutiloba f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It plays an important role in early pollination and is a valued plant in native gardens.
