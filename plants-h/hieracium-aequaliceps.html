Hieracium aequaliceps

Hieracium aequaliceps, commonly known as the equal-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
