Hieracium tolmatchevii

Hieracium tolmatchevii, commonly known as Tolmatchev's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
