Hemigymnia canaliculata

Hemigymnia canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Hemigymnia canaliculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
