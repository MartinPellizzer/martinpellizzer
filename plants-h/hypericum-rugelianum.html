Hypericum rugelianum

Hypericum rugelianum, commonly known as the rough St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often reddish underneath, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

Hypericum rugelianum is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
