Heisteria squarrosa

Heisteria squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly heister, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
