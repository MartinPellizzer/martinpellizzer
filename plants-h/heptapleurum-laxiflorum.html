Heptapleurum laxiflorum

Heptapleurum laxiflorum, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has elliptical to ovate leaves that are often glossy and arranged in whorls.

Its floral structure includes five petals and stamens, typical of the Rubiaceae family, and it produces small, dry fruits.
