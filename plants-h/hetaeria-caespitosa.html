Hetaeria caespitosa

Hetaeria caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted grass or narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Hetaeria caespitosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
