Hemionitis schimperi

Hemionitis schimperi, commonly known as the African rock fern, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of East Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in dry, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive, compact growth habit with finely divided, leathery fronds that are adapted to reduce water loss. Its reproductive structures are relatively simple, with spores produced on the underside of the fronds.
