Haumaniastrum uluguricum

Haumaniastrum uluguricum, commonly known as the Uluguru thistle, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is notable for its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
