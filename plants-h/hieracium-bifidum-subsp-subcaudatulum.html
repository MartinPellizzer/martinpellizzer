Hieracium bifidum subsp. subcaudatulum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subcaudatulum, commonly known as the narrow-tailed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow flower stems and reduced bracts at the base.
