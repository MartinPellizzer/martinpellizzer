Hippocrepis minor var. brevipetala

Hippocrepis minor var. brevipetala, commonly known as the short-petalled harebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while the stems are slender and often branched.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
