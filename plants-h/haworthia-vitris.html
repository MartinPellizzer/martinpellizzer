Haworthia vitris

Haworthia vitris, commonly known as the glassy Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a translucent or glassy appearance. The leaves are typically green with white or pale markings, and they can develop a powdery coating known as farina. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers on slender stems during the growing season.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
