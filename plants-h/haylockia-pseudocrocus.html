Haylockia pseudocrocus

Haylockia pseudocrocus, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats.

Haylockia pseudocrocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
