Hieracium florentinum subsp. dilutius

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

dilutius, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, flat-topped head with numerous small florets.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in temperate regions across the continent.
