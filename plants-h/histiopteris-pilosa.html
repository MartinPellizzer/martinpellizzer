Histiopteris pilosa

Histiopteris pilosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are short and thick, supporting the growth of its lanceolate, bipinnate leaves.

Histiopteris pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
