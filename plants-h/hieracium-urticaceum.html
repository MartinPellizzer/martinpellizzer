Hieracium urticaceum

Hieracium urticaceum, commonly known as the stinging hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically rough and covered in fine hairs, which can cause skin irritation.

Hieracium urticaceum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
