Heracleum coreanum

Heracleum coreanum, commonly known as Korean cowparni, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive foliage.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a hollow, ridged stem with purple spots. The plant produces umbellate inflorescences, which are clusters of small white or greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are compound, with a serrated margin and a strong, often unpleasant odor.

The species is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
