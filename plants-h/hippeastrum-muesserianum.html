Hippeastrum muesserianum

Hippeastrum muesserianum, commonly known as the Muesser's amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped blooms that typically appear in winter or early spring. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, emerging from a bulbous stem.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
