Hieracium barrimum

Hieracium barrimum, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically purple or pink in color.

Hieracium barrimum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
