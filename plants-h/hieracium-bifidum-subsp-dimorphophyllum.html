Hieracium bifidum subsp. dimorphophyllum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

dimorphophyllum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is notable for its dimorphic leaves, which have different shapes on either side of the stem. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is relatively common in its native range.
