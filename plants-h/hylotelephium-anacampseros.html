Hylotelephium anacampseros

Hylotelephium anacampseros, commonly known as the Armenian carpet cushion, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and vibrant floral display.
