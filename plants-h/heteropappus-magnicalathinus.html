Heteropappus magnicalathinus

Heteropappus magnicalathinus, commonly known as the golden dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense, fluffy pappus that aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
