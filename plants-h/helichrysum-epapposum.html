Helichrysum epapposum

Helichrysum epapposum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or Cape gold, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow flower heads that resemble small sunbursts, which remain vibrant even after drying. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, fragrant foliage. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders, while its drought tolerance allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Helichrysum epapposum is also valued for its use in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
