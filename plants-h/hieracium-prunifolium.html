Hieracium prunifolium

Hieracium prunifolium, commonly known as the cherry-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish hue, especially along the edges.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
