Hesperis rosea

Hesperis rosea, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis rosea is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
