Hieracium solidagineum subsp. perthusianum

Hieracium solidagineum subsp.

perthusianum, commonly known as the Perthusian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
