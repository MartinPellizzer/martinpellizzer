Hieracium venetifolium

Hieracium venetifolium, commonly known as the Venetian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
