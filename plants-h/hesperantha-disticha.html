Hesperantha disticha

Hesperantha disticha, commonly known as the two-branched orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of two or more branched clusters, each bearing small, fragrant flowers.

Hesperantha disticha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
