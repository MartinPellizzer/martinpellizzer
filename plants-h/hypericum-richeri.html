Hypericum richeri

Hypericum richeri, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include the presence of yellow-orange sap, which is rich in hypericin and pseudohypericin, compounds known for their medicinal properties.

Hypericum richeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential therapeutic uses.
