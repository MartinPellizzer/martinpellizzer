Hieracium nigroviridans

Hieracium nigroviridans, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its leaves and stems contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
