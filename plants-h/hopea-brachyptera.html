Hopea brachyptera

Hopea brachyptera, commonly known as the short-winged ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and dark green color.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in spikes, and the tree produces woody capsules containing several seeds.
