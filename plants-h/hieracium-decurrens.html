Hieracium decurrens

Hieracium decurrens, commonly known as the spreading hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It is a perennial herb that often grows in meadows and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it can outcompete native vegetation.
