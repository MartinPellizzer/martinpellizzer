Hordeum subfastigiatum

Hordeum subfastigiatum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional agricultural practices.

Hordeum subfastigiatum is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
