Hieracium pallidum subsp. rupicolifolium

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

rupicolifolium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy stems, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
