Helianthemum rugosum

Helianthemum rugosum, commonly known as the rough stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Helianthemum rugosum thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.
