Hesperomeles obtusifolia var. obtusifolia

Hesperomeles obtusifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Southern California.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of coastal environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often protected in conservation efforts.
