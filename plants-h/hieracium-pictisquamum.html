Hieracium pictisquamum

Hieracium pictisquamum, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branching.

Hieracium pictisquamum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
