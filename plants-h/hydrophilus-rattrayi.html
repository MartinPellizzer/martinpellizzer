Hydrophilus rattrayi

Hydrophilus rattrayi, commonly known as the Rattray water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom during the summer months.

Hydrophilus rattrayi plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting aquatic life and improving water quality.
