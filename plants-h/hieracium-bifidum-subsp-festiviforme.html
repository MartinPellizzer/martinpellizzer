Hieracium bifidum subsp. festiviforme

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

festiviforme, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
