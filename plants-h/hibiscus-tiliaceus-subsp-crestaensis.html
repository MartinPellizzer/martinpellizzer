Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp. crestaensis

Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp.

crestaensis, commonly known as the crested hibiscus, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, often branched inflorescences and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in coastal and tropical environments, often growing in sandy soils near the shoreline.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
