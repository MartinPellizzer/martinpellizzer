Hemionitis carlotta-halliae

Hemionitis carlotta-halliae, commonly known as the Carlotta Hallie's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically gray-green in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme desert conditions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing soruses, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
