Hiepia

Hiepia, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a type of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a yellow or golden hue. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Hiepia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ease of growth.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
