Hibiscus cruentus

Hibiscus cruentus, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a contrasting yellow center. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Hibiscus cruentus is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
