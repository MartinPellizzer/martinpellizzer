Helianthus grandiflorus

Helianthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange blooms and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed.

Helianthus grandiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
