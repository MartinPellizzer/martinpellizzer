Hedysarum multijugum var. apiculatum

Hedysarum multijugum var. apiculatum, commonly known as the apiculate hedysarum, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules and its raceme of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces elliptical, flattened pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
