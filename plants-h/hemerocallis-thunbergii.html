Hemerocallis thunbergii

Hemerocallis thunbergii, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow. The plant is part of the Hemerocallis genus, known for its short bloom period, typically lasting only one day per flower. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental in gardens.

Hemerocallis thunbergii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize easily in suitable climates.
