Hedycarya balansae

Hedycarya balansae, commonly known as the mountain ash or Chinese ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Hedycarya balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
