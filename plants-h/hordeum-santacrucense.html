Hordeum santacrucense

Hordeum santacrucense, commonly known as the Santa Cruz barley, is a species of grass native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Hordeum santacrucense is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for agricultural use.
