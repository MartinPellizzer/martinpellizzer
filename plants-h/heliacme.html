Heliacme

Heliacme, commonly known as the sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower head, which is actually composed of many tiny florets. The plant has a robust stem with rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and oppositely arranged. Heliacme thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its seeds, known as sunflower seeds, are widely consumed as a snack and used in cooking.
