Hieracium gudaricum

Hieracium gudaricum, commonly known as the Gudar hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium gudaricum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
