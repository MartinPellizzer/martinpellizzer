Hesperis cappadocica

Hesperis cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns. The plant produces yellow flowers with a red or purple hue, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Hesperis cappadocica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
