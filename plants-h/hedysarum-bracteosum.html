Hedysarum bracteosum

Hedysarum bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, yellow pea-like flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its distinctive bracteate structure on the flower stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Hedysarum bracteosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
