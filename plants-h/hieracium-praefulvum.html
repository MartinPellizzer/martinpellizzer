Hieracium praefulvum

Hieracium praefulvum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by a ring of pale yellow to brownish bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium praefulvum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to colonize various habitats.
