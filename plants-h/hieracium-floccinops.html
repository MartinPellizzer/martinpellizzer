Hieracium floccinops

Hieracium floccinops, commonly known as the cloud-like hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble clouds, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium floccinops is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
