Hiraea argentea

Hiraea argentea, commonly known as silverleaf hiraea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which give it its common name, and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often found in rocky or arid landscapes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
