Hieracium naegelianum subsp. andreae

Hieracium naegelianum subsp.

andreae, commonly known as the Andree's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
