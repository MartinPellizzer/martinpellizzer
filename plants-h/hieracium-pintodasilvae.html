Hieracium pintodasilvae

Hieracium pintodasilvae, commonly known as the Pinto da Silva hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellow petals and a central disk that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
