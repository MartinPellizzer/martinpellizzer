Hedyotis costata

Hedyotis costata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate appearance.

Hedyotis costata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
