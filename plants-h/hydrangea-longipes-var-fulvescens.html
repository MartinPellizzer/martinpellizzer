Hydrangea longipes var. fulvescens

Hydrangea longipes var. fulvescens, commonly known as the red-tipped hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy bracts that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in paniculate inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green surface.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
