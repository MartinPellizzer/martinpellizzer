Hermannia malvifolia

Hermannia malvifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture, and the flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hermannia malvifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
