Heliotropium intermedium

Heliotropium intermedium, commonly known as the common heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, violet to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Heliotropium intermedium is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
