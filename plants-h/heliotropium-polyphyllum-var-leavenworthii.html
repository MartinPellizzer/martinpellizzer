Heliotropium polyphyllum var. leavenworthii

Heliotropium polyphyllum var. leavenworthii, commonly known as the Leavenworth heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
