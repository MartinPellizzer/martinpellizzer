Hieracium caesium subsp. pseudobasifolium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

pseudobasifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, thriving in temperate climates. Its basal leaves are elongated and have a basal rosette structure, while the upper leaves are narrower and gradually reduce in size.

The flowers are yellow and form dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
