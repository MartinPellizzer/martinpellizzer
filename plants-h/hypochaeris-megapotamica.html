Hypochaeris megapotamica

Hypochaeris megapotamica, commonly known as the Argentine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers are accompanied by heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Hypochaeris megapotamica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
