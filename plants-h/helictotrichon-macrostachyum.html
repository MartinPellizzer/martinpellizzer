Helictotrichon macrostachyum

Helictotrichon macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, fluffy seeds. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Helictotrichon macrostachyum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
