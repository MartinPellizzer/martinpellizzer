Hibiscus spathaceus

Hibiscus spathaceus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Hibiscus spathaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
