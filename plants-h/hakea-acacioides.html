Hakea acacioides

Hakea acacioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to south-western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which are followed by woody, cylindrical seed pods. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea acacioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native landscaping.
