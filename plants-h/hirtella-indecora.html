Hirtella indecora

Hirtella indecora, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its densely hairy leaves, which give it its common name, and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, smooth seeds.

Hirtella indecora is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
