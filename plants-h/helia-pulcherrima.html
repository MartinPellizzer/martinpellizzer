Helia pulcherrima

Helia pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and can reach lengths of up to 30 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and hummingbirds, contributing to pollination in the ecosystem.
