Hieracium bifidum subsp. adenopsilon

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

adenopsilon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowering stems bear small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact, flat-topped inflorescence.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and hairy foliage, which contribute to its adaptability in various habitats.
