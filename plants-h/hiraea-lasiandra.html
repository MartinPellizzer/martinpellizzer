Hiraea lasiandra

Hiraea lasiandra, commonly known as the silverleaf hiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Hiraea lasiandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
