Hieracium pilosella subsp. microcephaloides

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

microcephaloides, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in composite inflorescences.

The leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant produces numerous small, hairy stems. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
