Hieracium bifidum subsp. goniophorum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

goniophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypsela.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family.
