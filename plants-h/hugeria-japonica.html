Hugeria japonica

Hugeria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries.

Hugeria japonica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.
