Helianthemum globulariifolium f. minor

Helianthemum globulariifolium f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features compact, rounded foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
