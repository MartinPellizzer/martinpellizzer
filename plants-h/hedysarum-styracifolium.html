Hedysarum styracifolium

Hedysarum styracifolium, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Hedysarum styracifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
