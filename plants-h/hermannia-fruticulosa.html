Hermannia fruticulosa

Hermannia fruticulosa, commonly known as the red-rooted herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or red in color.

Hermannia fruticulosa is valued for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and its potential use in traditional medicine.
