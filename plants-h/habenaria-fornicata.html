Habenaria fornicata

Habenaria fornicata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring or early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral morphology.
