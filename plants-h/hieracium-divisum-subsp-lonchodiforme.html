Hieracium divisum subsp. lonchodiforme

Hieracium divisum subsp.

lonchodiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.
