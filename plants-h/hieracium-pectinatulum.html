Hieracium pectinatulum

Hieracium pectinatulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed or pinnately divided appearance.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
