Helichrysum xeranthemoides

Helichrysum xeranthemoides, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features wiry, branched stems and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy foliage, which often has a scent reminiscent of citrus or mint. Its flowers are known to retain their color and form for a long time after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
