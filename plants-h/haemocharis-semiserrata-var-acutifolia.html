Haemocharis semiserrata var. acutifolia

Haemocharis semiserrata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the red-veined lily or the flame lily, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from orange to red, with prominent yellow veins. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
