Helichrysum subglomeratum var. imbricatum

Helichrysum subglomeratum var. imbricatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or silver frost, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. Its leaves are typically silver-gray, covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
