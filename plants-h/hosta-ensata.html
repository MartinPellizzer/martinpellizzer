Hosta ensata

Hosta ensata, commonly known as the Japanese hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, blue, or purple, which bloom in late summer. Hosta ensata thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
