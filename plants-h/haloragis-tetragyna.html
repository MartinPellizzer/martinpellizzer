Haloragis tetragyna

Haloragis tetragyna, commonly known as the four-angled willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Haloragis tetragyna is known for its ability to tolerate saline and arid conditions, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
