Hieracium subramosum subsp. subalpestre

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

subalpestre, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellowish-orange disc florets surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.
