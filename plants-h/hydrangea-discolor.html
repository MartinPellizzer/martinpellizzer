Hydrangea discolor

Hydrangea discolor, commonly known as the white wood fern, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Hydrangea discolor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
