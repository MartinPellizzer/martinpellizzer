Hypericum gnidioides var. polytrichoides

Hypericum gnidioides var. polytrichoides, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often prostrate or semi-creeping. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
