Hippocratea volubilis

Hippocratea volubilis, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that twine around supports. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy surface, aiding in water retention in its native habitat.

Hippocratea volubilis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
