Helicteres isora var. microphylla

Helicteres isora var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved sweet pepper tree, is a variant of the sweet pepper tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
