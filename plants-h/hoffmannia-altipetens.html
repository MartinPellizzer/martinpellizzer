Hoffmannia altipetens

Hoffmannia altipetens, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

Hoffmannia altipetens plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
