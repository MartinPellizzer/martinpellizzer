Homalomena pumila var. latifolia

Homalomena pumila var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed caladium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including red, white, and green. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous perennial, with a compact, bushy habit. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or yellow, and are borne on erect spikes.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.
