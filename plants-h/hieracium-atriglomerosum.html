Hieracium atriglomerosum

Hieracium atriglomerosum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are dark green and glabrous, with a distinct basal rosette structure.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
