Heliconia paludigena

Heliconia paludigena, commonly known as the marsh heliconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is native to wetland and swampy areas in Central and South America. The plant features large, brightly colored bracts that enclose its inflorescence, which consists of small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, adapted to thrive in moist environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
