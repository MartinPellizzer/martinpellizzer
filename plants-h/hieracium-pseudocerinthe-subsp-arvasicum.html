Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp. arvasicum

Hieracium pseudocerinthe subsp.

arvasicum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
