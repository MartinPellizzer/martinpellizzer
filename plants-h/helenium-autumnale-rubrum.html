Helenium autumnale rubrum

Helenium autumnale rubrum, commonly known as the red swamp fire.

It is a striking perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in wet or moist soils. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which add to its ornamental appeal.

Helenium autumnale rubrum is often cultivated in gardens for its bold color and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
