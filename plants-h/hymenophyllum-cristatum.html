Hymenophyllum cristatum

Hymenophyllum cristatum, commonly known as the feather moss, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves and produce spores for propagation.

Hymenophyllum cristatum plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
