Hymenophyllum cruegeri

Hymenophyllum cruegeri, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum cruegeri plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its habitat.
