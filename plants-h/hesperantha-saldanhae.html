Hesperantha saldanhae

Hesperantha saldanhae, commonly known as the Saldanha's hebes, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of opposite, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Hesperantha saldanhae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
