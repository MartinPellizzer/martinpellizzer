Hypericum confertum var. stenobotrys

Hypericum confertum var. stenobotrys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in woodlands. It produces red, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
