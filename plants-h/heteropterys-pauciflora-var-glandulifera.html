Heteropterys pauciflora var. glandulifera

Heteropterys pauciflora var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular heteropterys, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
