Homalomena argentea var. purpurascens

Homalomena argentea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display a vibrant mix of silver, pink, and purple hues. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Its foliage is typically variegated, with contrasting colors that make it a popular choice for decorative purposes.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
