Hieracium phalarograpticeps

Hieracium phalarograpticeps, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hieracium phalarograpticeps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
