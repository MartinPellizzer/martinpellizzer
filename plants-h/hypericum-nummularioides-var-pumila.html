Hypericum nummularioides var. pumila

Hypericum nummularioides var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf goldbeard, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slight waxiness. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually appealing plant.
