Hyptis subrotunda

Hyptis subrotunda, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or purple and bloom in clusters.

Hyptis subrotunda is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
