Hedera helix var. arborescens-albolutea

Hedera helix var. arborescens-albolutea, commonly known as the white-veined ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its distinctive white veins on light green leaves.

It is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe, often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, with the white veins providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
