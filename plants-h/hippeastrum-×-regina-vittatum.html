Hippeastrum × regina-vittatum

Hippeastrum × regina-vittatum, commonly known as the Queen Victoria amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Hippeastrum reginae* and *Hippeastrum vittatum*.

It is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces showy inflorescences that can reach up to 30 cm in height, with multiple blooms appearing in a single cluster. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
