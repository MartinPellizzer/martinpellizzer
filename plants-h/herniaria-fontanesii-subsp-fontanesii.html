Herniaria fontanesii subsp. fontanesii

Herniaria fontanesii subsp.

fontanesii, commonly known as the rock cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are pink or white, borne in clusters and appearing in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
