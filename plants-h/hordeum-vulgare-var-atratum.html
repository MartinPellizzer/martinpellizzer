Hordeum vulgare var. atratum

Hordeum vulgare var. atratum, commonly known as black barley, is a variant of the common barley species.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grains, which range from black to deep purple. This variety is typically shorter and more compact than other barley types, with a shorter stalk and smaller leaves. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in traditional foods.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other barley varieties, contributing to its unique agricultural and culinary applications.
