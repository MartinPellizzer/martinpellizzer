Hieracium arolae

Hieracium arolae, commonly known as the Arola hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium arolae is often found in meadows and open slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
