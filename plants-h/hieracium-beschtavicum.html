Hieracium beschtavicum

Hieracium beschtavicum, commonly known as the Beshtav Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow or orange flowers. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is often found in alpine and steppe regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
