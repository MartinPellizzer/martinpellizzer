Hedera helix var. tricolor

Hedera helix var. tricolor, commonly known as the variegated ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant characterized by its distinctive three-color foliage.

The leaves typically display a combination of white, yellow, and green patterns, creating a striking visual contrast. It is a climbing evergreen vine that thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its adaptability to various soil types and moderate watering needs make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
