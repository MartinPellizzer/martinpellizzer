Heliotropium curassavicum var. virens

Heliotropium curassavicum var. virens, commonly known as the green heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically range in color from pink to purple.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
