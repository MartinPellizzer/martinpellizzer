Helichrysum graveolens

Helichrysum graveolens, commonly known as the everlasting flower or gold dust, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, aromatic foliage and bright yellow to orange flower heads that retain their color even when dried. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum graveolens is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
