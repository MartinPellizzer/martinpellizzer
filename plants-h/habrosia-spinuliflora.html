Habrosia spinuliflora

Habrosia spinuliflora, commonly known as the spiny-flowered habrosia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain water in dry conditions, and its stems are often covered in small, spiny structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
