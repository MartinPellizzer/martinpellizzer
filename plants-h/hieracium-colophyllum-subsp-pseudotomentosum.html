Hieracium colophyllum subsp. pseudotomentosum

Hieracium colophyllum subsp.

pseudotomentosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, white to greyish hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
