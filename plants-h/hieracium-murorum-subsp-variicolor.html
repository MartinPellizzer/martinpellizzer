Hieracium murorum subsp. variicolor

Hieracium murorum subsp.

variicolor, commonly known as the variable mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils in meadows and along roadsides. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has been introduced to other regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variable leaf coloration and flower morphology compared to other members of the species.
