Hyptis fallax

Hyptis fallax, commonly known as wild mint or Mexican mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are often aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hyptis fallax thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
