Habenaria intrudens

Habenaria intrudens, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features a tall, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive red tip on its lip. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
