Hoya microphylla

Hoya microphylla, commonly known as the baby hoya or miniature hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, waxy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom during the summer months. Hoya microphylla is a succulent plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is prized for its compact size and ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
