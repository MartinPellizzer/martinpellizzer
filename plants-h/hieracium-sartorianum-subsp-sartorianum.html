Hieracium sartorianum subsp. sartorianum

Hieracium sartorianum subsp.

sartorianum, commonly known as the Sartorian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
