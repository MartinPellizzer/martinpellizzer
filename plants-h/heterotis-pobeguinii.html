Heterotis pobeguinii

Heterotis pobeguinii, commonly known as the African water spinach, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbon-like leaves that grow on long, slender stems. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, spinach-like flavor, making it a popular vegetable in some regions.

Heterotis pobeguinii is also valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing nutrients from its environment.
