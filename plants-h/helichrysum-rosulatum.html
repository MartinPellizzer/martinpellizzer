Helichrysum rosulatum

Helichrysum rosulatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or sweet William, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies, with tightly packed ray florets surrounding a central disc. The plant has aromatic, silvery-green foliage that is both ornamental and drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum rosulatum is also valued for its use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
