Hieracium pubigerum

Hieracium pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pubigerum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
