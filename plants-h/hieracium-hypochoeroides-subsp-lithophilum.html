Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. lithophilum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

lithophilum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies lithophilum is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This plant is typically found in meadows and open slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
