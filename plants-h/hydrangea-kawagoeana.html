Hydrangea kawagoeana

Hydrangea kawagoeana, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers are composed of sterile bracts and fertile flowers, with the latter being small and inconspicuous.
