Hieracium vulgatum subsp. phaeostictum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

phaeostictum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowering heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are usually hairy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more contrasting leaf markings compared to other members of the species.
