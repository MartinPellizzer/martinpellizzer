Hibiscus collinus

Hibiscus collinus, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Hibiscus collinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
