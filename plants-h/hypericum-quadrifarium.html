Hypericum quadrifarium

Hypericum quadrifarium, commonly known as the four-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of four around the stem, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hypericum quadrifarium is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
