Hieracium parvifrons

Hieracium parvifrons, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually hairy and lance-shaped.
