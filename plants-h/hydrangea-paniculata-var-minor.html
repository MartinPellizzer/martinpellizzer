Hydrangea paniculata var. minor

Hydrangea paniculata var. minor, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy inflorescences that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, making it a popular ornamental choice. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, and its foliage turns vibrant red in the fall.

This variety is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
