Hieracium lachenalii subsp. mutinense

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

mutinense, commonly known as the Mutinense Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are surrounded by pale green ray florets. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
