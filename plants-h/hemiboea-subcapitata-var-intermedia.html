Hemiboea subcapitata var. intermedia

Hemiboea subcapitata var. intermedia, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate regions.
