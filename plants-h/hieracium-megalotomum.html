Hieracium megalotomum

Hieracium megalotomum, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1 meter in height, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The species is part of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
