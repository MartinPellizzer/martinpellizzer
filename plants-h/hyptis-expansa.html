Hyptis expansa

Hyptis expansa, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Hyptis expansa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
