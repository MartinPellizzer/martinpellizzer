Hieracium ambiguum subsp. lipovecianum

Hieracium ambiguum subsp.

lipovecianum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist meadows, forest edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and consist of ray florets surrounded by tubular flowers.

It is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
