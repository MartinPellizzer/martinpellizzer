Humboldtia ponmudiana

Humboldtia ponmudiana, commonly known as the coastal nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Humboldtia ponmudiana is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.
