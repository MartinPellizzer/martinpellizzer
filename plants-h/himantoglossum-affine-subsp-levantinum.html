Himantoglossum affine subsp. levantinum

Himantoglossum affine subsp.

levantinum, commonly known as the Levantine helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
