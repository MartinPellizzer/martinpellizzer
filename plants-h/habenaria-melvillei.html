Habenaria melvillei

Habenaria melvillei, commonly known as the Melville's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces a unique, fragrant scent.

Habenaria melvillei is a rare species, often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
