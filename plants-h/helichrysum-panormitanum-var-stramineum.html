Helichrysum panormitanum var. stramineum

Helichrysum panormitanum var. stramineum, commonly known as the yellow helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small sunflowers, which are composed of numerous tiny, daisy-like flowers. The plant has aromatic foliage with a distinctive, slightly bitter scent, and its leaves are typically narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
