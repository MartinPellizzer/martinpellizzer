Hieracium cabreranum subsp. lanuzae

Hieracium cabreranum subsp.

lanuzae, commonly known as the Lanuza hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
