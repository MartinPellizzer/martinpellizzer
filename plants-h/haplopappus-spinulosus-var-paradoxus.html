Haplopappus spinulosus var. paradoxus

Haplopappus spinulosus var. paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its unique morphology, including the presence of spinose structures on its bracts, contributes to its classification within the Asteraceae family.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
