Hieracium laevigatum subsp. megachaetum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

megachaetum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and leaves. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed in certain environments.
