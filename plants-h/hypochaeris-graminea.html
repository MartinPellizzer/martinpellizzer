Hypochaeris graminea

Hypochaeris graminea, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from spring to autumn, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hypochaeris graminea is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for various insects and birds.
