Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. glaberrimum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

glaberrimum, commonly known as the glabrous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. It has alternate, lanceolate leaves that are generally hairless, giving it its subspecies name "glaberrimum.".

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
