Hicorius ovalis var. obcordata

Hicorius ovalis var. obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf hibiscus, is a unique variant of the hibiscus genus characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically large, showy, and come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The leaves are broad and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
