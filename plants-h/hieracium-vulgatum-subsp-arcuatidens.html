Hieracium vulgatum subsp. arcuatidens

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

arcuatidens, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a wavy or curled margin, giving it its common name.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
