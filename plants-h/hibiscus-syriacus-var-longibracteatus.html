Hibiscus syriacus var. longibracteatus

Hibiscus syriacus var. longibracteatus, commonly known as the Syrian rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to the Middle East.

It features showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
