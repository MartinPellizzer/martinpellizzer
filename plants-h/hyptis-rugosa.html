Hyptis rugosa

Hyptis rugosa, commonly known as roughleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Hyptis rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
