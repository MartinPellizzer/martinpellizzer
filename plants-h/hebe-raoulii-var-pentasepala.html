Hebe raoulii var. pentasepala

Hebe raoulii var. pentasepala, commonly known as the Raoul's hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue during the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
