Hypericum mutilum var. minus

Hypericum mutilum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
