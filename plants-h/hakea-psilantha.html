Hakea psilantha

Hakea psilantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear small, pinkish-red flowers. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Hakea psilantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
