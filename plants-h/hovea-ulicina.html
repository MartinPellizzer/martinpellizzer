Hovea ulicina

Hovea ulicina, commonly known as the coastal hovea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments. It has adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species.

Hovea ulicina plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for native wildlife.
