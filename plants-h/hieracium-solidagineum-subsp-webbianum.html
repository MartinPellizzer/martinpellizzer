Hieracium solidagineum subsp. webbianum

Hieracium solidagineum subsp.

webbianum, commonly known as the webb's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
