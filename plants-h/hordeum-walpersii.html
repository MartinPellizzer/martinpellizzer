Hordeum walpersii

Hordeum walpersii, commonly known as the Sierra wild barley, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought resistance.

Hordeum walpersii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
