Heliotropium spathulatum

Heliotropium spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the afternoon and close at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Heliotropium spathulatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
