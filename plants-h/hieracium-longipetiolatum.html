Hieracium longipetiolatum

Hieracium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the stems are upright with a reddish hue.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
