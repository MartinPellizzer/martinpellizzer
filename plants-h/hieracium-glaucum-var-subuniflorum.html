Hieracium glaucum var. subuniflorum

Hieracium glaucum var. subuniflorum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

It is considered a perennial herb with a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats in its native habitat.
