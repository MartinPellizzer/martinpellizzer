Homalomena pygmaea

Homalomena pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a tropical evergreen perennial that features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by spadices, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
