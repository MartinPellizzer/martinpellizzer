Hylotelephium parvistamineum

Hylotelephium parvistamineum, commonly known as the dwarf sedum, is a low-growing succulent plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Hylotelephium parvistamineum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it an ideal choice for xeriscaping.
