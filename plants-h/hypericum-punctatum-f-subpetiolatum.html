Hypericum punctatum f. subpetiolatum

Hypericum punctatum f. subpetiolatum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed St. John's wort, is a botanical variant of the St. John's wort species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and smaller, more densely arranged leaves compared to the typical form. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Hypericaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
