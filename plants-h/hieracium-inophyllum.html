Hieracium inophyllum

Hieracium inophyllum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium inophyllum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
