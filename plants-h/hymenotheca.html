Hymenotheca

Hymenotheca, commonly known as the "hymenothecium," is a specialized structure found in certain flowering plants, particularly in the family Malvaceae.

It refers to the calyx-like structure that develops from the fusion of the sepals and is often modified to enclose the ovary. This structure plays a crucial role in protecting the developing flower and aiding in pollination. The hymenotheca is typically composed of multiple fused tepals, which may be showy and attract pollinators.

Its botanical significance lies in its structural adaptation and ecological function in reproductive processes.
