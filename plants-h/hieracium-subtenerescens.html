Hieracium subtenerescens

Hieracium subtenerescens, commonly known as the soft-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish or grayish hue, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain regions.
