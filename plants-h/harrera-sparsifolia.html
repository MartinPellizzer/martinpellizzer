Harrera sparsifolia

Harrera sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparsely leafed harrera, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Harrera sparsifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry landscapes.
