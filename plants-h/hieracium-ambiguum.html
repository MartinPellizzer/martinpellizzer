Hieracium ambiguum

Hieracium ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with ray florets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist meadows and disturbed soils. The plant has rough, hairy stems and is often found in open habitats.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
