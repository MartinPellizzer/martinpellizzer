Hieracium djimilense subsp. cordatifrons

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

cordatifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves, which help it conserve moisture.
