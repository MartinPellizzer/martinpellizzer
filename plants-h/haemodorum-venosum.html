Haemodorum venosum

Haemodorum venosum, commonly known as the red-rooted ginger or narrow-leafed lily.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Australia, characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often reddish in color, contributing to its common name.

Haemodorum venosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and understorey habitats.
