Homoranthus cernuus

Homoranthus cernuus, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits called capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sand dunes.

Homoranthus cernuus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
