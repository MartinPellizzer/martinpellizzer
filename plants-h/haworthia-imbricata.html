Haworthia imbricata

Haworthia imbricata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in white or yellowish markings, resembling a zebra's stripes. The plant is relatively compact, typically growing to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Haworthia imbricata is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
