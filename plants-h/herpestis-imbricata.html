Herpestis imbricata

Herpestis imbricata, commonly known as the redroot germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and aromatic foliage, making it a distinctive member of the Geraniaceae family.
