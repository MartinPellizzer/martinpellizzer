Hieracium caesium subsp. fritzeanum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

fritzeanum, commonly known as the Fritzean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographic distribution.
