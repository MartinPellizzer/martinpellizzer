Homalomena raapii

Homalomena raapii, commonly known as the red-veined arrowhead, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves with striking red veins, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Homalomena raapii is known for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
