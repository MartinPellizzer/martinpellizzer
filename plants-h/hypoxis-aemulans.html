Hypoxis aemulans

Hypoxis aemulans, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis aemulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
