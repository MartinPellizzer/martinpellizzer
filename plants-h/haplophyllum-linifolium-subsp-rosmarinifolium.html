Haplophyllum linifolium subsp. rosmarinifolium

Haplophyllum linifolium subsp.

rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved haplophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are closely arranged along the stems, giving it a compact, bushy appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, typically in late spring. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf morphology, which resembles that of rosemary.
