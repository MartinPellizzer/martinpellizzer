Helianthus × brevifolius

Helianthus × brevifolius, commonly known as the shortleaf sunflower, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright, branching stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

The shortleaf sunflower is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
