Hakea delta

Hakea delta, commonly known as the delta hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and unique, woody flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Hakea delta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
