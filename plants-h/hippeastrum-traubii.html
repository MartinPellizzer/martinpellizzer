Hippeastrum traubii

Hippeastrum traubii, commonly known as the Traub's amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with narrow, linear leaves that emerge from a large, fleshy bulb. It is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and blooms in the spring or early summer.

Hippeastrum traubii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
