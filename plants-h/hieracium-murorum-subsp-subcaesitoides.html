Hieracium murorum subsp. subcaesitoides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subcaesitoides, commonly known as the pale sand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a perennial herbaceous species characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a single head, and bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
