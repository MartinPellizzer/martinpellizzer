Hieracium sagittatum subsp. sarcophyllum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

sarcophyllum, commonly known as the sagittate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are typically arranged in a dense, flat-topped cluster. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

This subspecies is distinguished by its robust growth and distinctive floral structure.
