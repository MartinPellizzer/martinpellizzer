Hedysarum naudinianum

Hedysarum naudinianum, commonly known as the Naudin's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Hedysarum naudinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
