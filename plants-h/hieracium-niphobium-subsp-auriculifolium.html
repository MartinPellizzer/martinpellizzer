Hieracium niphobium subsp. auriculifolium

Hieracium niphobium subsp.

auriculifolium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with limited vegetation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
