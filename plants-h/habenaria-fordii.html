Habenaria fordii

Habenaria fordii, commonly known as the Ford's butterfly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that is usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific fungal hosts during its growth cycle.
