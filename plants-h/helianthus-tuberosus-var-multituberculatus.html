Helianthus tuberosus var. multituberculatus

Helianthus tuberosus var. multituberculatus, commonly known as the "Turban Sunflower," is a variant of the sunflower species characterized by its unique tuberculate (knobbed) stem.

This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It produces large, yellow-orange flower heads with prominent central disks, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. The variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of edible tubers.
