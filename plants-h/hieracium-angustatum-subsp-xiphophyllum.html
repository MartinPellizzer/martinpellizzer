Hieracium angustatum subsp. xiphophyllum

Hieracium angustatum subsp.

xiphophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late summer.

It is part of the dandelion family (Compositae) and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
