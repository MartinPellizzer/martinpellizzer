Heloniopsis japonica var. purpurea

Heloniopsis japonica var. purpurea, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant color and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
