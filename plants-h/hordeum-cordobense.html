Hordeum cordobense

Hordeum cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba barley, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that bear small, two-rowed grains. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Hordeum cordobense is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its role in local ecosystems and potential for agricultural use.
