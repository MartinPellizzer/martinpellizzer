Hypericum asahinae var. siroumense

Hypericum asahinae var. siroumense, commonly known as the Siroumense St. John's Wort, is a rare and endemic plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered with fine hairs.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
