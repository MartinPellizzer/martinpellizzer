Hieracium plantagineum subsp. gapense

Hieracium plantagineum subsp.

gapense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
