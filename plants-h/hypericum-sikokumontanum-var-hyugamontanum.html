Hypericum sikokumontanum var. hyugamontanum

Hypericum sikokumontanum var. hyugamontanum, commonly known as the Korean St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and has opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It is known for its medicinal properties, containing compounds like hypericin and pseudohypericin.

However, it is important to note that it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
