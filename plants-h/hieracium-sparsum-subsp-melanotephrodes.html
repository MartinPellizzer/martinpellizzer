Hieracium sparsum subsp. melanotephrodes

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

melanotephrodes, commonly known as the dark-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark-colored stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
