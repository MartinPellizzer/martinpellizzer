Hedysarum boreale var. gremiale

Hedysarum boreale var. gremiale, commonly known as the northern peavine, is a perennial legume native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers in early summer. Its pods are hairy and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
