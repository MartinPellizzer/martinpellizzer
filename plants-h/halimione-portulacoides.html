Halimione portulacoides

Halimione portulacoides, commonly known as sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is native to the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North America, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Halimione portulacoides plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting coastal biodiversity.
