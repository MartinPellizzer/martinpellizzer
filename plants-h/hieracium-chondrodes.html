Hieracium chondrodes

Hieracium chondrodes, commonly known as the cushion hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils.

Hieracium chondrodes is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
