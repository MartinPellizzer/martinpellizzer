Habenaria egregia

Habenaria egregia, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces long, slender spikes of flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
