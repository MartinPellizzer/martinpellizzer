Hieracium loxodon

Hieracium loxodon, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium loxodon is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
