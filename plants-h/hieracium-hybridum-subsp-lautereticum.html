Hieracium hybridum subsp. lautereticum

Hieracium hybridum subsp.

lautereticum, commonly known as the Lautereticum Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are usually rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, blooming from June to August.

It is characterized by its hybrid origin and is often found in areas with moderate to dry conditions.
