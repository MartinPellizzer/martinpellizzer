Heliconia acuminata

Heliconia acuminata, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a hand clutching a red or orange torch. The plant's leaves are broad, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers are not only visually stunning but also serve as a habitat and food source for various pollinators, including birds and insects.
