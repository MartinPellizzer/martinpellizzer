Hypoxis hookeri

Hypoxis hookeri, commonly known as the star lily or false arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellowish-white flowers with a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clusters over time.

Hypoxis hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
