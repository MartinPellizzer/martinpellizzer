Halimium × bianoris

Halimium × bianoris, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cistaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Halimium atrabundans and Halimium ochroleucum, characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
