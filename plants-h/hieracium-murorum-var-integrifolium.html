Hieracium murorum var. integrifolium

Hieracium murorum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually entire or slightly lobed. Its stems are upright and can grow up to 30 cm in height, supporting the flowering heads.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.
