Hymenophyllum chamaecyparicola

Hymenophyllum chamaecyparicola, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, moss-like fronds that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in humid forests. Its leaves are usually narrowly linear and have a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Hymenophyllum chamaecyparicola is noted for its ability to retain moisture, which is essential for its survival in its natural habitat.
