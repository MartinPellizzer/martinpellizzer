Hordeum hexastichon var. densum

Hordeum hexastichon var. densum, commonly known as dense six-row barley.

It is a variant of six-row barley characterized by its dense arrangement of spikelets and shorter, more compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is cultivated for its grains, which are used in brewing and animal feed. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a basal spikelet structure that distinguishes it from other barley varieties.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and yield potential in agricultural settings.
