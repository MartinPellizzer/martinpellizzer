Hyptis sinuata var. subglabra

Hyptis sinuata var. subglabra, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
