Humboltia cardium

Humboltia cardium, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Humboltia cardium is adapted to coastal environments, often thriving in sandy or gravelly soils with good drainage.
