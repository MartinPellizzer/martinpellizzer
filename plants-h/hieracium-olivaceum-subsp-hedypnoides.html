Hieracium olivaceum subsp. hedypnoides

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

hedypnoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
