Hieracium spilophaeum

Hieracium spilophaeum, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and its showy, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium spilophaeum is typically perennial and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
