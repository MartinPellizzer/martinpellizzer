Habenaria forceps

Habenaria forceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with prominent sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are often pollinated by flies and beetles due to their strong scent.

The genus Habenaria is characterized by its unique floral structure and is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
