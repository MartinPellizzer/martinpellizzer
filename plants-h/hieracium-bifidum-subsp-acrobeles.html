Hieracium bifidum subsp. acrobeles

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

acrobeles, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its two-lobed leaves and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
