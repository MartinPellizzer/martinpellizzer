Hieracium bifidum

Hieracium bifidum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium bifidum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
