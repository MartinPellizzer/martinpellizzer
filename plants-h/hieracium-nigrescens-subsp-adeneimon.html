Hieracium nigrescens subsp. adeneimon

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

adeneimon, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it both a characteristic wildflower and a potential weed.
