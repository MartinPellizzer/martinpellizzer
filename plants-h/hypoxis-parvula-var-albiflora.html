Hypoxis parvula var. albiflora

Hypoxis parvula var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered star grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.
