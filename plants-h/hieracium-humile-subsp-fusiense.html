Hieracium humile subsp. fusiense

Hieracium humile subsp.

fusiense, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features bright yellow flowers arranged in a single, compact head, with narrow, elongated leaves.

The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit and the presence of soft, hairy stems. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is distinguished by its subspecies-specific morphological traits.
