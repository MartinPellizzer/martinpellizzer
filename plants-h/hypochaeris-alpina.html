Hypochaeris alpina

Hypochaeris alpina, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Hypochaeris alpina is often found in meadows and along mountain trails, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its environment.
