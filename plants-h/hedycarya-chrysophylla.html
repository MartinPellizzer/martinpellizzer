Hedycarya chrysophylla

Hedycarya chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leafed hedycarya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Hedycarya chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
