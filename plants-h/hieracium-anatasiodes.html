Hieracium anatasiodes

Hieracium anatasiodes, commonly known as the Anatasioid Dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a yellow flower head that develops into a crown of bracts after blooming.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
