Hymenocrater aucheri

Hymenocrater aucheri, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Hymenocrater aucheri is a perennial species that adds vibrant color to high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
