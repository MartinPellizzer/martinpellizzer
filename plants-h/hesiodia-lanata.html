Hesiodia lanata

Hesiodia lanata, commonly known as the woolly hesiodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Hesiodia lanata is typically evergreen and can grow as a shrub or a small tree.
