Hesperantha spicata subsp. graminifolia

Hesperantha spicata subsp.

graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy bell orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
