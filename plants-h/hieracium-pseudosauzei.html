Hieracium pseudosauzei

Hieracium pseudosauzei, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flower heads consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium pseudosauzei is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
