Hieracium chloropsis subsp. chloropsis

Hieracium chloropsis subsp.

chloropsis, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated margin, while the stems are often reddish in color.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
