Hieracium atratum subsp. zermulae

Hieracium atratum subsp.

zermulae, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, inconspicuous fruits called achenes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
