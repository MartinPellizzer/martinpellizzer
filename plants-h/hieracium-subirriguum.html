Hieracium subirriguum

Hieracium subirriguum, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in a single head surrounded by green bracts. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wet meadows and along water edges.

Hieracium subirriguum is adapted to moist environments, with a root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils.
