Heliconia thalia

Heliconia thalia, commonly known as the crimson heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often overlooked due to the showy bracts. Its leaves are broad and waxy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Heliconia thalia is often cultivated for its vibrant appearance and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
