Helichrysum humblotii

Helichrysum humblotii, commonly known as the yellow helichrysum, is a South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, papery bracts that resemble small flowers, which remain colorful even when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Helichrysum humblotii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
