Hieracium subrhaeticum

Hieracium subrhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and alpine plant collections.
