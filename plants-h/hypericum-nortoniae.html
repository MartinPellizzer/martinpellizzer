Hypericum nortoniae

Hypericum nortoniae, commonly known as the Narrowleaf St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and opposite, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Hypericum species.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.
