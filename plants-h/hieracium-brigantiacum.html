Hieracium brigantiacum

Hieracium brigantiacum, commonly known as the Brigantine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium brigantiacum is often found in temperate climates and can sometimes be invasive in non-native regions.
