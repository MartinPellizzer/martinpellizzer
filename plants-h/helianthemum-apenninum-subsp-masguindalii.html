Helianthemum apenninum subsp. masguindalii

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

masguindalii, commonly known as the Corsican rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has succulent, glandular-stemmed foliage adapted to rocky and arid environments. It is often found growing on limestone soils in coastal and mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
