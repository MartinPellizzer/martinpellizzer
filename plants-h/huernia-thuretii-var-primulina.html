Huernia thuretii var. primulina

Huernia thuretii var. primulina, commonly known as the "Primrose Ghostplant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often tinged with purple or red, and it produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a shallow root system and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise subtle appearance.
