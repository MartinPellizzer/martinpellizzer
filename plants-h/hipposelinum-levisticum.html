Hipposelinum levisticum

Hipposelinum levisticum, commonly known as caraway.

This annual herbaceous plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flower clusters. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications, have a distinct aromatic flavor reminiscent of both cumin and anise.
