Hypochaeris coronopifolius

Hypochaeris coronopifolius, commonly known as the crownleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by small, white to pale yellow seeds.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the plant is often found in regions with temperate climates.
