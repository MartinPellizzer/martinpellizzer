Hesperoxalis trilliifolia

Hesperoxalis trilliifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
