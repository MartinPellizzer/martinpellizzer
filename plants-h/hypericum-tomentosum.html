Hypericum tomentosum

Hypericum tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hypericum tomentosum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
