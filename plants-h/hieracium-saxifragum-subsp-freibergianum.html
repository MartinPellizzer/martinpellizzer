Hieracium saxifragum subsp. freibergianum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

freibergianum, commonly known as the Freiberg's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and shorter peduncles compared to other members of the species.
