Helia spathulata

Helia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf helia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its hardiness and attractive appearance, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
