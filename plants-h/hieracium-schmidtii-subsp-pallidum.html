Hieracium schmidtii subsp. pallidum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

pallidum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has finely divided, linear leaves and produces small, composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
