Hieracium glaucinum subsp. dasyadenium

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

dasyadenium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The plant features upright stems covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which give it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, usually blooming from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive hairy foliage, making it a notable species in its native habitats.
