Hieracium tanycola

Hieracium tanycola, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium tanycola is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
