Horsfieldia gracilis

Horsfieldia gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pendant-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Horsfieldia gracilis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
