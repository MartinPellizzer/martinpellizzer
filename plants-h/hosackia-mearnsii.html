Hosackia mearnsii

Hosackia mearnsii, commonly known as Mearns' milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
