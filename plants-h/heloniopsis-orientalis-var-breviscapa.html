Heloniopsis orientalis var. breviscapa

Heloniopsis orientalis var. breviscapa, commonly known as the narrow-cupped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to alpine and perennial plant collections.
