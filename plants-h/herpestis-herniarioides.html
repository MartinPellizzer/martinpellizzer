Herpestis herniarioides

Herpestis herniarioides, commonly known as the common hairvetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

Herpestis herniarioides is a perennial herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
