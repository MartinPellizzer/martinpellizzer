Hieracium pichleri subsp. anastrum

Hieracium pichleri subsp.

anastrum, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine pubescence.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often reduced or absent, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
