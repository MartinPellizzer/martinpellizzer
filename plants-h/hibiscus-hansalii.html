Hibiscus hansalii

Hibiscus hansalii, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and a hairy stem that is often reddish in color. Hibiscus hansalii thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of its native habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
