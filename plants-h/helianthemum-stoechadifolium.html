Helianthemum stoechadifolium

Helianthemum stoechadifolium, commonly known as the Scottish heath sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.
