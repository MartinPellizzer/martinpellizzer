Hieracium dentatum subsp. maculifolium

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

maculifolium, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lobed and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in some regions.
