Hieracium davidianum

Hieracium davidianum, commonly known as David's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its growth habit is typically rosette-like.

It is often found in open habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
