Hippocrepis areolata

Hippocrepis areolata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Hippocrepis areolata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
