Hugeria japonica var. lasiostemon

Hugeria japonica var. lasiostemon, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive reddish-brown stem. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are edible for humans and birds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
