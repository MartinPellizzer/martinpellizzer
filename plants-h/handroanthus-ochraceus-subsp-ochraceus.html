Handroanthus ochraceus subsp. ochraceus

Handroanthus ochraceus subsp.

ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
