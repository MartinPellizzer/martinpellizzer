Hieracium × laschianum

Hieracium × laschianum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered growth habit and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is native to Europe but has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive in some areas.

Hieracium × laschianum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
