Hieracium dentatum subsp. tigrinum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger's eye hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features a basal rosette of sparsely hairy, lanceolate leaves that are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in height.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, sharply toothed leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including dry and disturbed habitats.
