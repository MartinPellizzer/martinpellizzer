Hieracium saxifragum subsp. pseudoleyi

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

pseudoleyi, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold environments.

The plant features a compact rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures, making it a characteristic component of alpine vegetation.
