Hegemone lilacina subsp. afghanica

Hegemone lilacina subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan hegemone, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. It is native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and northern Pakistan, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features slender, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive lilac-colored hue. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, are typically small and clustered, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
