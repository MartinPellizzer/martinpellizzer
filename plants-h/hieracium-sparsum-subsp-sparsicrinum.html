Hieracium sparsum subsp. sparsicrinum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

sparsicrinum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
