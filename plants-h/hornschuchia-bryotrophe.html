Hornschuchia bryotrophe

Hornschuchia bryotrophe, commonly known as the moss cushion plant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in dense cushions or mats, which help it retain heat and moisture in its harsh environment. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It is closely associated with mosses, forming a unique symbiotic relationship that enhances its survival in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand extreme cold and strong winds, making it a resilient component of polar and high-altitude ecosystems.
