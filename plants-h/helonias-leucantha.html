Helonias leucantha

Helonias leucantha, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in swampy or marshy habitats. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Helonias leucantha is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
