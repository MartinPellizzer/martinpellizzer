Heliocereus elegantissimus var. stenopetalus

Heliocereus elegantissimus var. stenopetalus, commonly known as the sun cactus or night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and is typically sparsely branched. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night and close by morning. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming habit and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
