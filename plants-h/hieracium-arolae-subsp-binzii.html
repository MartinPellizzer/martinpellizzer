Hieracium arolae subsp. binzii

Hieracium arolae subsp.

binzii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flower heads and finely divided, serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats. It has small, basal leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in alpine environments.
