Huernia montana

Huernia montana, commonly known as the mountain hueria, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiky, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique appearance and ability to store water make it well adapted to drought conditions.

Huernia montana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and resilience.
