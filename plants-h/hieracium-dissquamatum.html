Hieracium dissquamatum

Hieracium dissquamatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
