Haplopappus discoideus

Haplopappus discoideus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its seeds are dispersed by the wind, typical of many members of the dandelion family.
