Hieracium loscosianum subsp. baeticum

Hieracium loscosianum subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology, including leaf shape and flower structure, which vary slightly from the main species. It typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring sunny and well-drained soils.
