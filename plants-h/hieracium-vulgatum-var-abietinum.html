Hieracium vulgatum var. abietinum

Hieracium vulgatum var. abietinum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in cold climates and high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
