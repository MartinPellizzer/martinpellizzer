Hakea squiresiae

Hakea squiresiae, commonly known as the Squiresia hakea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-egg shaped spikes of white to pink flowers, which appear in spring. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy soils.

Hakea squiresiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
