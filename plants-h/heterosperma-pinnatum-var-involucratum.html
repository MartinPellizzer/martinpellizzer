Heterosperma pinnatum var. involucratum

Heterosperma pinnatum var. involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its feathery, pinnate leaves.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, which gives the plant its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. The plant is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
