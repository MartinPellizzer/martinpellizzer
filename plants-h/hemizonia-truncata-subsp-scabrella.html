Hemizonia truncata subsp. scabrella

Hemizonia truncata subsp.

scabrella, commonly known as the roughleaf hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as gravelly slopes and open woodlands.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is adapted to arid environments.
