Hieracium bakerianum

Hieracium bakerianum, commonly known as Baker's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, showcasing a rosette of basal leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often accompanied by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Baker's hawkweed is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many habitats.
