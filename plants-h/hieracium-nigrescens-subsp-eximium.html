Hieracium nigrescens subsp. eximium

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

eximium, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 30 cm.

The species is characterized by its dark, reddish-purple central disk and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.
