Hieracium oaxacanum

Hieracium oaxacanum, commonly known as Oaxacan hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
