Hieracium chlorolomiceps

Hieracium chlorolomiceps, commonly known as the yellow-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves with prominent veins and its yellow to orange composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant often grows in meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
