Horsfieldia sucosa

Horsfieldia sucosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the forests of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Horsfieldia sucosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
