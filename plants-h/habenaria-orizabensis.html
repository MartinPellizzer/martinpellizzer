Habenaria orizabensis

Habenaria orizabensis, commonly known as the Orizaba orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white to pale pink blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
