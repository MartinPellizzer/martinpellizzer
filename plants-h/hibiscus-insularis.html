Hibiscus insularis

Hibiscus insularis, commonly known as the island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the summer months. Hibiscus insularis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
