Hordeum volhynicum

Hordeum volhynicum, commonly known as Volhynian barley, is a wild grass species native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. Hordeum volhynicum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
