Heterotheca camporum var. glandulissima

Heterotheca camporum var. glandulissima, commonly known as the glandular bush sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and yellow ray florets surrounded by dark brown disk florets. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
