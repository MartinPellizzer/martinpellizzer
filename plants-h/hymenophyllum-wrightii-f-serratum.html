Hymenophyllum wrightii f. serratum

Hymenophyllum wrightii f. serratum, commonly known as the serrated shield fern, is a fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The fronds are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures called trichomes, which help reduce water loss. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, where it can maintain the high humidity it requires.

Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it a notable species in botanical studies.
