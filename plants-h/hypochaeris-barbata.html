Hypochaeris barbata

Hypochaeris barbata, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are hairy and have a slightly lobed appearance, while the flowers are yellow and borne in clusters.

Hypochaeris barbata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
