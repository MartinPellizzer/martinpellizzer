Hydrangea panamensis

Hydrangea panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian hydrangea, is a species native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of sterile flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, often with a reddish or purple hue in the fall.

Hydrangea panamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
