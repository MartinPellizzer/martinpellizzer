Hypericum ciliatum

Hypericum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces small, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Hypericum ciliatum is part of the Hypericaceae family, which includes other species known for their medicinal properties.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
