Hibiscus immaculatus

Hibiscus immaculatus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and a prominent, five-lobed calyx. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
