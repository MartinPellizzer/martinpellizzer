Haageocereus platinospinus

Haageocereus platinospinus, commonly known as the silver spiny cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, silver-gray spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often borne at the base of the spines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
