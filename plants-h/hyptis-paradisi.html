Hyptis paradisi

Hyptis paradisi, commonly known as sweet hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis paradisi is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
