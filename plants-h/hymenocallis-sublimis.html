Hymenocallis sublimis

Hymenocallis sublimis, commonly known as the skyrocket ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet, adorned with large, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, evergreen leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hymenocallis sublimis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and dramatic floral display.
