Helichrysum infuscum

Helichrysum infuscum, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that remain open for several weeks. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive, mat-like appearance. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Helichrysum infuscum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and attractive appearance.
