Holtonia microcarpa

Holtonia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited holtonia, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
