Hieracium fissilinguum

Hieracium fissilinguum, commonly known as the fissile hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can be found in temperate regions. It has basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain areas.
