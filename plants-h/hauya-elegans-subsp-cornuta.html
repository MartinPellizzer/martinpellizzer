Hauya elegans subsp. cornuta

Hauya elegans subsp.

cornuta, commonly known as the horned hauya, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. Hauya elegans subsp.

cornuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
