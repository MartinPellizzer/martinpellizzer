Haplopappus spinulosus subsp. australis

Haplopappus spinulosus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian spinyleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a small, annual herb with hairy, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and dunes.

Its distinctive spinose pappus, which aids in wind dispersal, is a key botanical feature.
