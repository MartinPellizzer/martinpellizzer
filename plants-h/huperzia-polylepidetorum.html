Huperzia polylepidetorum

Huperzia polylepidetorum, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

Huperzia polylepidetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
