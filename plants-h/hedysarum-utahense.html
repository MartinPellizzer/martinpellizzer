Hedysarum utahense

Hedysarum utahense, commonly known as Utah milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert areas. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Hedysarum utahense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
