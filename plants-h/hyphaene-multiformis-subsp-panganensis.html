Hyphaene multiformis subsp. panganensis

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

panganensis, commonly known as the Panganen palm, is a medium-sized palm native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used for thatching.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is cultivated in various tropical regions for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
