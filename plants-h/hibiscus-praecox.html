Hibiscus praecox

Hibiscus praecox, commonly known as the early hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Hibiscus praecox blooms in early summer and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
