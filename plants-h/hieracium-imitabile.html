Hieracium imitabile

Hieracium imitabile, commonly known as the mimic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium imitabile is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
