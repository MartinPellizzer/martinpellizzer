Helictotrichon pubescens f. latifolium

Helictotrichon pubescens f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and lawns.

This form is often used in landscaping for its fine texture and ability to form dense turf.
