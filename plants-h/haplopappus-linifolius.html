Haplopappus linifolius

Haplopappus linifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
