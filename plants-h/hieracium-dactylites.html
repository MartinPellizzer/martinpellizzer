Hieracium dactylites

Hieracium dactylites, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium dactylites is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
