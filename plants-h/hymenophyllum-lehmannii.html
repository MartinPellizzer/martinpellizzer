Hymenophyllum lehmannii

Hymenophyllum lehmannii, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery coating of trichomes. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as along the bases of trees or on rock surfaces. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on slender stalks and release spores when mature.

Hymenophyllum lehmannii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
