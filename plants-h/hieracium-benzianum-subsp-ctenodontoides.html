Hieracium benzianum subsp. ctenodontoides

Hieracium benzianum subsp.

ctenodontoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
