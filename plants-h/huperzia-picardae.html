Huperzia picardae

Huperzia picardae, commonly known as the Picard's huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a short, upright stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. It produces spore-bearing structures called sporophytes, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.

Huperzia picardae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
