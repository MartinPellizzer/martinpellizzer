Hieracium murorum var. sublepistoides

Hieracium murorum var. sublepistoides, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often reddish in color.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and the plant produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.
