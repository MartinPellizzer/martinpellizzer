Hieracium submarginellum

Hieracium submarginellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in meadows and grasslands. It has small, linear leaves and is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.

The plant is typically found in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
