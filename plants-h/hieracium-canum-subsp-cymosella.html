Hieracium canum subsp. cymosella

Hieracium canum subsp.

cymosella, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces cymes of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Its subspecific name, *cymosella*, refers to the cyme-like arrangement of its inflorescence.
