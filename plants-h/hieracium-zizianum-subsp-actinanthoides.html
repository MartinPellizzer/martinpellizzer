Hieracium zizianum subsp. actinanthoides

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

actinanthoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
