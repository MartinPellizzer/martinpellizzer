Hyptis divaricata

Hyptis divaricata, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis divaricata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
