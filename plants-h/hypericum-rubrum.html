Hypericum rubrum

Hypericum rubrum, commonly known as the red St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with St. John's Day, and it is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
