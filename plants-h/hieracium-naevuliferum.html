Hieracium naevuliferum

Hieracium naevuliferum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium naevuliferum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
