Hiraea ternifolia subvar. vulgaris

Hiraea ternifolia subvar. vulgaris, commonly known as the three-leaved hiraea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique leaf structure and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
