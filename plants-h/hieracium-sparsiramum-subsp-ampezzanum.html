Hieracium sparsiramum subsp. ampezzanum

Hieracium sparsiramum subsp.

ampezzanum, commonly known as the Ampezzano hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes at high altitudes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
