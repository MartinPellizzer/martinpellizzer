Hemicyclia deplanchei

Hemicyclia deplanchei, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting it to its harsh, dry environment. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single umbel and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Hemicyclia deplanchei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
