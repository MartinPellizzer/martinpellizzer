Hieracium umbrosum subsp. visaillense

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

visaillense, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
