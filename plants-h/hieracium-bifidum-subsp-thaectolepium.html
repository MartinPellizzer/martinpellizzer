Hieracium bifidum subsp. thaectolepium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

thaectolepium, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, featuring deeply divided, lobed leaves. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
