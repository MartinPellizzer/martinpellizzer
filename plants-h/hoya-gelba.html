Hoya gelba

Hoya gelba, commonly known as the Yellow Waxy Philodendron, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often blooming in clusters. Hoya gelba is a climbing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively low maintenance and popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental foliage.
