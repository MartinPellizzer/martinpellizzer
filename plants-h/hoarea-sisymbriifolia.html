Hoarea sisymbriifolia

Hoarea sisymbriifolia, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through mechanisms such as succulent leaves and deep root systems.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
