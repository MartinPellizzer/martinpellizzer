Hieracium gracile var. minimum

Hieracium gracile var. minimum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, yellowish-green composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, making it a subtle addition to meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in moist, open habitats.
