Hyptis caduca

Hyptis caduca, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to purple and are arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis caduca is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
