Hieracium subsilvaticum

Hieracium subsilvaticum, commonly known as the wood cranesbill, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in forested regions.
