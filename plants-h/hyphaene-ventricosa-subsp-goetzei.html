Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. goetzei

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

goetzei, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a dioecious plant characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in panicles, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
