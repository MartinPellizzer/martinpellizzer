Hedychium tenellum

Hedychium tenellum, commonly known as the dwarf ginger lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, blooming in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and aromatic qualities.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.
