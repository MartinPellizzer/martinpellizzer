Hieracium magyaricum subsp. holomnoon

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

holomnoon, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies holomnoon is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the species complex.
