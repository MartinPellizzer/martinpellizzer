Hussonia

Hussonia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the amaryllis family, Amaryllidaceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. They are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Hussonia species are typically perennial, with thick, fleshy roots that help them survive in dry conditions.

Their foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss.
