Hieracium teberdense

Hieracium teberdense, commonly known as the Teberdian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-shaded environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium teberdense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
