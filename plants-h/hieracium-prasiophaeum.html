Hieracium prasiophaeum

Hieracium prasiophaeum, commonly known as the green-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Hieracium prasiophaeum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
