Hybanthus modestus

Hybanthus modestus, commonly known as the modest hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hybanthus modestus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
