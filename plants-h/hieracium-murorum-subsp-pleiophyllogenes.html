Hieracium murorum subsp. pleiophyllogenes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pleiophyllogenes, commonly known as the many-leafed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of plant adaptation and distribution.
