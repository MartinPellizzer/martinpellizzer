Hieracium vogesiacum subsp. doronicoides

Hieracium vogesiacum subsp.

doronicoides, commonly known as the Vogesian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

Its botanical structure supports its role in pollination and ecological interactions within its native habitat.
