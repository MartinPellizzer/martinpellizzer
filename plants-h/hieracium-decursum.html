Hieracium decursum

Hieracium decursum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. It has a creeping growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense mats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
