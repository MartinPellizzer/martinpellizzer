Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. pseudoelongatum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

pseudoelongatum, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaves and slightly different morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
