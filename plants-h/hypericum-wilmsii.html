Hypericum wilmsii

Hypericum wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilms' St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are responsible for its traditional medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
