Helichrysum mirabile

Helichrysum mirabile, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain colorful even when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Helichrysum mirabile is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
