Hieracium bifidum subsp. longicollum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

longicollum, commonly known as the long-necked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive elongated stem, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed habitats.
