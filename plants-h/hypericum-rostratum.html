Hypericum rostratum

Hypericum rostratum, commonly known as the scarlet St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its medicinal properties have been studied, though it contains compounds that can interact with certain medications.
