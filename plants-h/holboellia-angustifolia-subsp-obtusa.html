Holboellia angustifolia subsp. obtusa

Holboellia angustifolia subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf holboellia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
