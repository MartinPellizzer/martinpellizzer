Hypericum microcalycinum

Hypericum microcalycinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, usually from July to August. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.
