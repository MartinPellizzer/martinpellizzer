Holopyxidium latifolium

Holopyxidium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf holopyxidium, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
