Helianthus nuttallii subsp. nuttallii

Helianthus nuttallii subsp.

nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's sunflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, yellow-orange composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
