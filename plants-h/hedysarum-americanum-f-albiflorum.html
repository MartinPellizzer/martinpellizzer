Hedysarum americanum f. albiflorum

Hedysarum americanum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered sweetclover, is a botanical variant of the sweetclover species native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with the typical deep yellow of the species. The plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is known for its trifoliate leaves and compound pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in agricultural settings.
