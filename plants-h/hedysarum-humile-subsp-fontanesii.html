Hedysarum humile subsp. fontanesii

Hedysarum humile subsp.

fontanesii, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Hedysarum humile subsp.

fontanesii plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
