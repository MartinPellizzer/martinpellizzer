Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp. elatus

Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp.

elatus, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Indo-Pacific region. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or saline soils. It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions. Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp.

elatus is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in coastal landscaping.
