Heteranthocidium echinops

Heteranthocidium echinops, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and globular, yellowish flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.
