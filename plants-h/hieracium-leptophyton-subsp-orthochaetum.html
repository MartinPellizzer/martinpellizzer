Hieracium leptophyton subsp. orthochaetum

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

orthochaetum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
