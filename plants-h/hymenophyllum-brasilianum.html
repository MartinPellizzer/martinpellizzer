Hymenophyllum brasilianum

Hymenophyllum brasilianum, commonly known as the Brazilian spore moss, is a member of the Hymenophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic moss that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has finely divided, often overlapping leaves that are covered in a dense layer of rhizoids, which help anchor it to its substrate. Its reproductive structures, called sporophytes, are small and inconspicuous, producing spores that are dispersed by wind.

Hymenophyllum brasilianum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light.
