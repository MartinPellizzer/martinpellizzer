Habenaria diphylla var. josephi

Habenaria diphylla var. josephi, commonly known as the Joseph's two-leaved orchid, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and produces two leaves per stem, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish with red or purple markings, and they bloom in summer. This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
