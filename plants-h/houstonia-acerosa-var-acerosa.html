Houstonia acerosa var. acerosa

Houstonia acerosa var. acerosa, commonly known as the tough bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
