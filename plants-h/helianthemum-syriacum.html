Helianthemum syriacum

Helianthemum syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Helianthemum syriacum is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
