Helicia pyrrhobotrya

Helicia pyrrhobotrya, commonly known as the red-leafed helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, reddish-brown leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Helicia pyrrhobotrya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
