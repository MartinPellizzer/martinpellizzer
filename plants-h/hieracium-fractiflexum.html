Hieracium fractiflexum

Hieracium fractiflexum, commonly known as the bent-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features a rosette of basal leaves and a tall, erect stem with alternate, hairy leaves.

The flowers are yellow and are surrounded by a series of bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.
