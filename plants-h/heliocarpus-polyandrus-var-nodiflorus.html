Heliocarpus polyandrus var. nodiflorus

Heliocarpus polyandrus var. nodiflorus, commonly known as the clustered helicarpus, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a hard, spiny capsule that contains seeds covered in a fibrous, starchy layer.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
