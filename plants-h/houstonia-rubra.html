Houstonia rubra

Houstonia rubra, commonly known as the scarlet bluemont, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branching.

Houstonia rubra is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
