Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. wilczekianum

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

wilczekianum, commonly known as the Wilczek's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lanceolate leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
