Hesperis lapsanifolia

Hesperis lapsanifolia, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Hesperis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
