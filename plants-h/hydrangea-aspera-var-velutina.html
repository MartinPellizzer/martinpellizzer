Hydrangea aspera var. velutina

Hydrangea aspera var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet hydrangea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
