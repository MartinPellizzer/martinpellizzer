Heliconia velutina

Heliconia velutina, commonly known as the velvet heliconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves and striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, clustered flowers surrounded by fleshy, reddish-purple bracts.

Heliconia velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in the ecosystem as a food source for various pollinators.
