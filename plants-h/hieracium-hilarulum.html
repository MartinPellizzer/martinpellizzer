Hieracium hilarulum

Hieracium hilarulum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head, consisting of both ray and disk florets. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium hilarulum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
