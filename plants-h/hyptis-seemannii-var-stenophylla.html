Hyptis seemannii var. stenophylla

Hyptis seemannii var. stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potential anti-inflammatory properties.
