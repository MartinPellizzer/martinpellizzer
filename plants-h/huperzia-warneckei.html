Huperzia warneckei

Huperzia warneckei, commonly known as the Warneckei spikemoss, is a species of moss plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle involves both a sporophyte and gametophyte stage.

Huperzia warneckei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
