Huperzia everettii

Huperzia everettii, commonly known as Everett's spikemoss, is a species of clubmoss native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the branches. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

Huperzia everettii is a primitive vascular plant with a unique reproductive system that reflects its ancient lineage.
