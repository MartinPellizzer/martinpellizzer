Hedera japonica foliis-variegatis

Hedera japonica foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the Variegated Japanese Ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking variegation of white, yellow, and green, which add visual interest to gardens. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and is often used as a ground cover or trailing plant. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
