Hibiscus furcellatus var. multiformis

Hibiscus furcellatus var. multiformis, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pentagonal flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Hibiscus furcellatus var. multiformis is a perennial shrub that blooms throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
