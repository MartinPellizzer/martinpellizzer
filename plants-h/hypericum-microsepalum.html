Hypericum microsepalum

Hypericum microsepalum, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Hypericum microsepalum is also known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other Hypericum species.
