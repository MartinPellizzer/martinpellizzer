Helianthus jaegeri

Helianthus jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
