Helianthus nitidus

Helianthus nitidus, commonly known as the smooth sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a robust stem with rough, hairy surfaces and composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

Helianthus nitidus is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
