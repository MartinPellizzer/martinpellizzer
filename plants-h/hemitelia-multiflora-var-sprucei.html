Hemitelia multiflora var. sprucei

Hemitelia multiflora var. sprucei, commonly known as the spruce's hemitelia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of rainforests. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
