Heterolepis conyzoides

Heterolepis conyzoides, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming a cluster at the end of each stem.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
