Hemionitis obducta

Hemionitis obducta, commonly known as the water hawthorn, is a small, aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in shallow, freshwater environments. The plant features finely divided, needle-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Hemionitis obducta is known for its tolerance to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing stream banks.
