Heliotropium ottonii

Heliotropium ottonii, commonly known as the Mexican heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a fragrant scent. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Heliotropium ottonii is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
