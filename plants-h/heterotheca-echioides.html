Heterotheca echioides

Heterotheca echioides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its blue to purple flower heads bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual element to its native habitat.

Heterotheca echioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
