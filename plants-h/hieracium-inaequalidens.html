Hieracium inaequalidens

Hieracium inaequalidens, commonly known as the unequal hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium inaequalidens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and can become invasive in certain environments.
