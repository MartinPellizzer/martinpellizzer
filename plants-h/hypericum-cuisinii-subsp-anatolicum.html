Hypericum cuisinii subsp. anatolicum

Hypericum cuisinii subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It produces small, elliptical leaves and has a compact growth habit.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
