Hyptis glandulosa

Hyptis glandulosa, commonly known as Brazilian mint, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

Hyptis glandulosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
