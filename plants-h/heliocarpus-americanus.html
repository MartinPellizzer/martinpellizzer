Heliocarpus americanus

Heliocarpus americanus, commonly known as the American starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit, which resembles a star, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
