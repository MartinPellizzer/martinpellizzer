Homalium laurifolium

Homalium laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Homalium laurifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
