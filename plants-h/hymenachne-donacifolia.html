Hymenachne donacifolia

Hymenachne donacifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Hymenachne donacifolia plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
