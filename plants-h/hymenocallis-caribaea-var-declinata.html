Hymenocallis caribaea var. declinata

Hymenocallis caribaea var. declinata, commonly known as the Caribbean spider lily, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming clumps with strap-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in warm climates for its beauty and resilience.
