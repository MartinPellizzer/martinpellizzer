Hibiscus mangindranensis

Hibiscus mangindranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native habitat.

Hibiscus mangindranensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in parts of Madagascar.
