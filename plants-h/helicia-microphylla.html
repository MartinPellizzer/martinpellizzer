Helicia microphylla

Helicia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved helenia, is a member of the Theaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
