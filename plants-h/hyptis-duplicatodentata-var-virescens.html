Hyptis duplicatodentata var. virescens

Hyptis duplicatodentata var. virescens, commonly known as greenleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
