Hieracium anchusoides

Hieracium anchusoides, commonly known as the anchusoid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. It produces yellowish to reddish flowers with ray florets, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium anchusoides is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes become invasive in non-native habitats.
