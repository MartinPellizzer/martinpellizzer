Hieracium iricum

Hieracium iricum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium iricum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
