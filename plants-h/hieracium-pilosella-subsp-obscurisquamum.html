Hieracium pilosella subsp. obscurisquamum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

obscurisquamum, commonly known as the dark-squamose hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
