Hieracium sermenikense subsp. sermenikense

Hieracium sermenikense subsp.

sermenikense, commonly known as the Armenian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single terminal head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed or rocky environments.
