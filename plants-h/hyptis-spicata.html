Hyptis spicata

Hyptis spicata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Hyptis spicata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open woodland and disturbed areas.
