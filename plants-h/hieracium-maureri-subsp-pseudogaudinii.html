Hieracium maureri subsp. pseudogaudinii

Hieracium maureri subsp.

pseudogaudinii, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has a low-growing habit with numerous flowering stems that bear yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
