Hieracium brevidentatum

Hieracium brevidentatum, commonly known as the short-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with short, stiff hairs, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
