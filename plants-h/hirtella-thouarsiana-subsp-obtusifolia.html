Hirtella thouarsiana subsp. obtusifolia

Hirtella thouarsiana subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the glossy hirtella, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is often covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a textured appearance.

The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy green surface and a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
