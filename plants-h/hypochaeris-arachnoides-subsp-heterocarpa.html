Hypochaeris arachnoides subsp. heterocarpa

Hypochaeris arachnoides subsp.

heterocarpa, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its subspecies heterocarpa is distinguished by its unique fruit structure, which differs from the typical form of the species.

This plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
