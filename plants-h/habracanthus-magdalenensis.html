Habracanthus magdalenensis

Habracanthus magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalen Islands rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Magdalen Islands in the Gulf of St. Lawrence.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the harsh coastal environment of its native habitat.
