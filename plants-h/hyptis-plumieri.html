Hyptis plumieri

Hyptis plumieri, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis plumieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
