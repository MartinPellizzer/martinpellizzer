Hieracium pseudolapponicum

Hieracium pseudolapponicum, commonly known as the Lapland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy habitats and is adapted to cold climates. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are upright and branched.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
