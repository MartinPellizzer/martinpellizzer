Hyparrhenia smithiana var. major

Hyparrhenia smithiana var. major, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive reddish tint when mature. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of thatching. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Hyparrhenia smithiana var. major is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
