Hieracium apophyadium

Hieracium apophyadium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium apophyadium is known for its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats, which can sometimes make it invasive in certain regions.
