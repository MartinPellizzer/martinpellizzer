Hieracium murorum var. knautiifolium

Hieracium murorum var. knautiifolium, commonly known as Knautia-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy surface, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and is often found in temperate climates.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
