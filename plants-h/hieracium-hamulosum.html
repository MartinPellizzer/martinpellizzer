Hieracium hamulosum

Hieracium hamulosum, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Hieracium hamulosum is often found in temperate climates and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
