Hibiscus bracteosus

Hibiscus bracteosus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a striking yellow throat. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it produces distinctive bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus bracteosus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
