Hexadesmia confusa

Hexadesmia confusa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated stems and striking, pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It has small, scale-like leaves and relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Hexadesmia confusa is considered a conservation concern.
