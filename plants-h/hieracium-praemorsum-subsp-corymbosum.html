Hieracium praemorsum subsp. corymbosum

Hieracium praemorsum subsp.

corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
