Holosteum umbellatum var. pleiandrum

Holosteum umbellatum var. pleiandrum, commonly known as the many-flowered holosteum, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
