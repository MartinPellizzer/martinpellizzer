Himantoglossum caprinum subsp. calcaratum

Himantoglossum caprinum subsp.

calcaratum, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous habitats.
