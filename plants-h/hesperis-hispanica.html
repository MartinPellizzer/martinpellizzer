Hesperis hispanica

Hesperis hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of four-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It blooms in early summer and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
