Hesperis scholaris

Hesperis scholaris, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis scholaris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
