Hypericum hachijyoense

Hypericum hachijyoense, commonly known as the Hachijo hypericum, is a flowering plant native to the Hachijo Islands in Japan.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its yellow, sun-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
