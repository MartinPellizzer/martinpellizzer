Hydrolea multiflora var. glabra

Hydrolea multiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth hydrolea, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
