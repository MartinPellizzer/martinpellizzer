Hieracium incisum subsp. paicheanum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

paicheanum, commonly known as the Paichean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base, supporting a rosette of basal leaves. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated margins, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and exhibits specific ecological interactions within its native habitat.
