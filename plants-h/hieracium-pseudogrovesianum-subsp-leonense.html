Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp. leonense

Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp.

leonense, commonly known as the Leonese hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and produces seeds in a discoid head.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
