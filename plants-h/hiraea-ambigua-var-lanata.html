Hiraea ambigua var. lanata

Hiraea ambigua var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly hiraea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lanate (woolly) foliage, which gives it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique texture and ornamental appeal.
