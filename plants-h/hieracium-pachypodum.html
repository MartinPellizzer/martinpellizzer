Hieracium pachypodum

Hieracium pachypodum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. Its stems are also succulent, further enhancing its ability to survive in drought conditions.

Hieracium pachypodum is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, making it a resilient and visually appealing member of its ecosystem.
