Heuchera rubescens var. nevadensis

Heuchera rubescens var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to moist, cool climates.
