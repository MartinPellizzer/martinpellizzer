Hypolytrum bahiense

Hypolytrum bahiense, commonly known as the Bahia grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers.

Hypolytrum bahiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
