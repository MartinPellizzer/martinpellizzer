Hyalosema fritillariiflorum

Hyalosema fritillariiflorum, commonly known as the flame lily, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Hyalosema fritillariiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
