Heliconia densiflora subsp. angustifolia

Heliconia densiflora subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, banana-like inflorescence.

The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive red or purple bract that encloses the floral cluster.
