Hamatocactus hamatacanthus subsp. sinuatus

Hamatocactus hamatacanthus subsp.

sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinuous Hairpincushion, is a rare and striking cactus native to a small region in southern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, hair-like spines, giving it a unique and ornate appearance.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
