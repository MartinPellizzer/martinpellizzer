Hilliardiella nudicaulis

Hilliardiella nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stalked hilliardiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas. The plant has a short, unbranched stem that supports its small, opposite leaves, which are usually hairy and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with five petals that are usually white or pale pink in color.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
