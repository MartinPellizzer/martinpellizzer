Hemionitis sagittifolia subsp. sagittifolia

Hemionitis sagittifolia subsp.

sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead fern, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fronds are usually bright green and have a slightly glossy texture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
