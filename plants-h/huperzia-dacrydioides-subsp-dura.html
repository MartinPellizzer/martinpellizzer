Huperzia dacrydioides subsp. dura

Huperzia dacrydioides subsp.

dura, commonly known as the tough clubmoss, is a terrestrial fern ally native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Huperjiaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in cushions on the underside of its leaves.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
