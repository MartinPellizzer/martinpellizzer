Helianthus colossus

Helianthus colossus, commonly known as the giant sunflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. This plant can grow up to 12 feet tall, featuring large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by green bracts. Its robust stem and thick leaves are adapted to withstand hot, dry conditions.

The plant's ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds makes it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
