Hormidium racemiferum

Hormidium racemiferum, commonly known as the clustered horned cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the family Cactaceae and is adapted to arid environments with its succulent structure for water storage. Hormidium racemiferum typically grows in rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitats.
