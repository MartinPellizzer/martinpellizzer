Haemax

Haemax, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Haemax has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
