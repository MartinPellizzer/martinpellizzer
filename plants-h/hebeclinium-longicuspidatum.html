Hebeclinium longicuspidatum

Hebeclinium longicuspidatum, commonly known as the long-spined hebeclinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated by gardeners interested in native or exotic plant species.
