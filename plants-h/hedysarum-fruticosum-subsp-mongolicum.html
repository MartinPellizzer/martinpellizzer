Hedysarum fruticosum subsp. mongolicum

Hedysarum fruticosum subsp.

mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian shrub vetch, is a perennial legume native to northern China and Mongolia. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments. Hedysarum fruticosum subsp.

mongolicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and is used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
