Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. perileucum

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

perileucum, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with white veins.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
