Heeria cupheoides

Heeria cupheoides, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in spring and are followed by small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Heeria cupheoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
