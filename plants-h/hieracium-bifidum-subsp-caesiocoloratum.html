Hieracium bifidum subsp. caesiocoloratum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

caesiocoloratum, commonly known as the blue-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits, typical of the Asteraceae family.
