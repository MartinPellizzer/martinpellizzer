Hippeastrum correiense

Hippeastrum correiense, commonly known as the Brazilian night lily, is a species of amaryllid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. The plant produces strap-like, evergreen leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The flowers typically open in the evening and close by morning, making it a unique addition to gardens and floral displays.
