Hirpicium armerioides subsp. rudatisii

Hirpicium armerioides subsp.

rudatisii, commonly known as the rough hirpicium, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
