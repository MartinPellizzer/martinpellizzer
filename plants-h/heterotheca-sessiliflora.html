Heterotheca sessiliflora

Heterotheca sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy yellow flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads, and it blooms from late summer into early fall.

Heterotheca sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
