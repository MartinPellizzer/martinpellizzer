Hemicarpha isolepis

Hemicarpha isolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and northern regions of the United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Hemicarpha isolepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
