Hesperantha pilosa subsp. bracteolata

Hesperantha pilosa subsp.

bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate spider orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant has a short, stocky stem with hairy foliage, and its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique bract-like structures that enclose the flower buds, adding to its ornamental appeal.
