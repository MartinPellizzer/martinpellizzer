Helianthemum vineale subsp. marifolium

Helianthemum vineale subsp.

marifolium, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
