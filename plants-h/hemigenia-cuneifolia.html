Hemigenia cuneifolia

Hemigenia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. Its flowers produce nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and birds.

Hemigenia cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
