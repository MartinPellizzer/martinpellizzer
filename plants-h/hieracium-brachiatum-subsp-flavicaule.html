Hieracium brachiatum subsp. flavicaule

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

flavicaule, commonly known as the yellow-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bright yellow ray florets and shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other hawkweed species. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often found in temperate climates.
