Harpagophytum zeyheri subsp. zeyheri

Harpagophytum zeyheri subsp.

zeyheri, commonly known as the "devil's claw," is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, hooked seed pods and fleshy, yellow-orange roots.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
