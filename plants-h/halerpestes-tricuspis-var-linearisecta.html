Halerpestes tricuspis var. linearisecta

Halerpestes tricuspis var. linearisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed halerpestes, is a shrub native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical structure is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of specialized glands on its foliage.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
