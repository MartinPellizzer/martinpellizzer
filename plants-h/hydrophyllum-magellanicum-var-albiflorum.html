Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. albiflorum

Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along riverbanks.
