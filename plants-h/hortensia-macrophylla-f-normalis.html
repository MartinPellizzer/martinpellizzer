Hortensia macrophylla f. normalis

Hortensia macrophylla f. normalis, commonly known as the bigleaf hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically dark green and broad, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. The flower color can change based on soil pH, ranging from blue to pink.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the summer.
