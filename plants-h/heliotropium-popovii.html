Heliotropium popovii

Heliotropium popovii, commonly known as the desert heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range from violet to pink in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Heliotropium popovii is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry grasslands.
