Hydrocotyle grossularioides

Hydrocotyle grossularioides, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Hydrocotyle grossularioides is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
