Haplopteris plurisulcata

Haplopteris plurisulcata, commonly known as the many-ribbed huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive pattern of multiple longitudinal veins. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like China, India, and Japan.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
