Hesperantha falcata

Hesperantha falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed star-of-Bethlehem, is a member of the Iridaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with linear, grass-like leaves and produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The flowers are typically borne on tall, erect stems and bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
