Hederella ovata

Hederella ovata, commonly known as the ovate ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often waxy and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors or on rocky surfaces.

Hederella ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
