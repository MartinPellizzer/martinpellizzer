Haworthia radula var. magniperlata

Haworthia radula var. magniperlata, commonly known as the giant pearl plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often covered in a layer of white, waxy powder called farina. The plant produces small, white flowers on tall stalks during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
