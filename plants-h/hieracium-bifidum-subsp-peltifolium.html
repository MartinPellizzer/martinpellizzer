Hieracium bifidum subsp. peltifolium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

peltifolium, commonly known as the peltate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are peltate, meaning they are broad and have a shield-like shape with a central vein.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in a cluster, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
