Hypopeltis amygdalina

Hypopeltis amygdalina, commonly known as the white-spotted nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its stinging hairs, like those of other nettles, can cause skin irritation upon contact.
