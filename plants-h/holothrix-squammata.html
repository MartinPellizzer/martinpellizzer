Holothrix squammata

Holothrix squammata, commonly known as the scaly holothrix, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Holothrix squammata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
