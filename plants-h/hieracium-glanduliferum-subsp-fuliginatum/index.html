Hieracium Glanduliferum Subsp. Fuliginatum

Hieracium glanduliferum subsp. fuliginatum is a lesser-known member of the sunflower family known for its unique appearance and ecological significance. This subspecies is characterized by its dark, sooty leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks. Despite its modest size, it plays an important role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining biodiversity. Its rarity and specific habitat requirements make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
