Helianthemum variabile subvar. micropetalum

Helianthemum variabile subvar. micropetalum, commonly known as the small-petaled everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with compact, often prostrate stems that bear small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically smaller and more delicate compared to other members of the genus.

It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
