Hippeastrum × grahamii

Hippeastrum × grahamii, commonly known as the red-tipped hybrid amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to bloom successfully.

Hippeastrum × grahamii is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant flowers and ease of cultivation.
