Hyptidendron albidum

Hyptidendron albidum, commonly known as white hyptidendron, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or savannas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
