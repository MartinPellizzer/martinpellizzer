Hieracium levicaule subsp. acroleucum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

acroleucum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in grassy areas and open woodlands. It has a creeping growth habit and can form dense mats.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and floral structure, which are typical of the genus Hieracium.
