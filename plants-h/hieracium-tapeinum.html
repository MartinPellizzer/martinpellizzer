Hieracium tapeinum

Hieracium tapeinum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. Its leaves are often covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Hieracium tapeinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
