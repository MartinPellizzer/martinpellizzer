Homalomena wallichii

Homalomena wallichii, commonly known as the Indian ginger or velvet leaf, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structures.
