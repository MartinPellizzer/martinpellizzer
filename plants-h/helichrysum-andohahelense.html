Helichrysum andohahelense

Helichrysum andohahelense, commonly known as the Andohahela everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, bract-based flowers that are tightly packed, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
