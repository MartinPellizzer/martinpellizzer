Heptapleurum petelotii

Heptapleurum petelotii, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by ornamental red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal color.

Heptapleurum petelotii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
