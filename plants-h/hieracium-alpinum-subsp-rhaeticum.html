Hieracium alpinum subsp. rhaeticum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

rhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It produces small, disc-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecological niche.
