Hosta rectifolia subsp. atropurpurea

Hosta rectifolia subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaved hosta, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly purple to deep maroon in color, with a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental foliage and is often used in shaded garden landscapes.
