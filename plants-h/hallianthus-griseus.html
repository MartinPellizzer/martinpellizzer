Hallianthus griseus

Hallianthus griseus, commonly known as the gray heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence.

Hallianthus griseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
