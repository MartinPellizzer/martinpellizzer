Heracleum armoricum

Heracleum armoricum, commonly known as the Caucasian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces white, umbrella-shaped inflorescences, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, slightly aromatic odor.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.
