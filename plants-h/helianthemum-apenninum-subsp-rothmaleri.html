Helianthemum apenninum subsp. rothmaleri

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

rothmaleri, commonly known as the Rothmaler's rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid conditions.
