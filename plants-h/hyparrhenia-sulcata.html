Hyparrhenia sulcata

Hyparrhenia sulcata, commonly known as the African grass or tufted lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive whitish stripe along the midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Hyparrhenia sulcata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
