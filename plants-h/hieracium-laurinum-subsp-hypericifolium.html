Hieracium laurinum subsp. hypericifolium

Hieracium laurinum subsp.

hypericifolium, commonly known as the hypericifolous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
