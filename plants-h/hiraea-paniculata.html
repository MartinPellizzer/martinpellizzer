Hiraea paniculata

Hiraea paniculata, commonly known as the panicled hiraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically tubular with two lips, a common trait in the Acanthaceae family.

Hiraea paniculata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
