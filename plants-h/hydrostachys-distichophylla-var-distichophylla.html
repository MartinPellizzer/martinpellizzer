Hydrostachys distichophylla var. distichophylla

Hydrostachys distichophylla var. distichophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds and wetlands, and has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in water gardens. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it both a desirable and potentially invasive species in certain environments.

The plant's ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
