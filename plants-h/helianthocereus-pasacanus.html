Helianthocereus pasacanus

Helianthocereus pasacanus, commonly known as the Pasacan cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Philippines.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
