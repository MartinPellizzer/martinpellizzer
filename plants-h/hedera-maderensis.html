Hedera maderensis

Hedera maderensis, commonly known as the Madeiran ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to the Azores and Madeira Islands.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or trees.

Hedera maderensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
