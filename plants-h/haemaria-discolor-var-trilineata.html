Haemaria discolor var. trilineata

Haemaria discolor var. trilineata, commonly known as the three-lined sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique varietal features, including the distinct three-colored leaf markings, make it a distinctive species within the Montiaceae family.
