Hedychium spicatum var. trilobum

Hedychium spicatum var. trilobum, commonly known as the golden ginger or trilobed ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in terminal panicles, often reaching several feet in height. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its tropical appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Hedychium spicatum var. trilobum is also valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
