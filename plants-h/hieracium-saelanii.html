Hieracium saelanii

Hieracium saelanii, commonly known as the Alpine Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a slightly lobed appearance.

Hieracium saelanii is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in alpine meadows.
