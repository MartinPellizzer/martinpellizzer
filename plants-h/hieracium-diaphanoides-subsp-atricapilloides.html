Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. atricapilloides

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

atricapilloides, commonly known as the black-capped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, which are surrounded by a dense cluster of bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
