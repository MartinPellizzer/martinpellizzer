Hieracium hypsitocum

Hieracium hypsitocum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
