Halothamnus glaucus subsp. glaucus

Halothamnus glaucus subsp.

glaucus, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
