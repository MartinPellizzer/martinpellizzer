Heliotropium rufipilum var. genuinum

Heliotropium rufipilum var. genuinum, commonly known as the red-stemmed heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture and a distinct reddish stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its fragrant flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
