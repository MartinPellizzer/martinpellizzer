Hieracium brachyplectum

Hieracium brachyplectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
