Hieracium leucochlorum

Hieracium leucochlorum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize various habitats.
