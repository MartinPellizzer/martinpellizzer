Heliamphora tatei var. tatei

Heliamphora tatei var. tatei, commonly known as the Pitcher Plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lidless pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures, often found in nutrient-poor soils. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.

The variety tatei is distinguished by its specific adaptations to its high-altitude habitat.
