Habenaria passerina

Habenaria passerina, commonly known as the common pink orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few leaf-like structures. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a spike and characterized by their intricate, orchid-like appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
