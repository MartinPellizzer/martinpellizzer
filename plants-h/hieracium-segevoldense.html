Hieracium segevoldense

Hieracium segevoldense, commonly known as the Segevold hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
