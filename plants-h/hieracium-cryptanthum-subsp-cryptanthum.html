Hieracium cryptanthum subsp. cryptanthum

Hieracium cryptanthum subsp.

cryptanthum, commonly known as the cryptanthum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and the presence of a basal rosette of leaves.
