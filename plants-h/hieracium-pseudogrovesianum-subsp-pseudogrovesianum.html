Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp. pseudogrovesianum

Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp.

pseudogrovesianum, commonly known as the false sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in meadows or along stream banks.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific floral structure and leaf morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
