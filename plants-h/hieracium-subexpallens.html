Hieracium subexpallens

Hieracium subexpallens, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are pale yellow to white, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium subexpallens is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in open, sunny environments.
