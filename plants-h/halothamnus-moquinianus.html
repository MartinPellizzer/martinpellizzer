Halothamnus moquinianus

Halothamnus moquinianus, commonly known as the desert sandthrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Halothamnus moquinianus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
