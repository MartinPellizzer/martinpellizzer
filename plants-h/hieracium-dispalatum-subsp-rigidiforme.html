Hieracium dispalatum subsp. rigidiforme

Hieracium dispalatum subsp.

rigidiforme, commonly known as the rigid-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of stiff, often hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single terminal flower head, which is surrounded by a series of bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rigid, linear leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
