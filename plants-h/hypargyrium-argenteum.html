Hypargyrium argenteum

Hypargyrium argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Hypargyrium argenteum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
