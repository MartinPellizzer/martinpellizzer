Hirculus ranunculoides subsp. alpina

Hirculus ranunculoides subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its role in alpine ecosystems.
