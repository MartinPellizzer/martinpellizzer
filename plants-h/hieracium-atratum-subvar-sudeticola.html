Hieracium atratum subvar. sudeticola

Hieracium atratum subvar. sudeticola, commonly known as the Polish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It has a rosette growth form with a central stem that bears the inflorescence at the top.

Its flowers are surrounded by phyllaries, which are leaf-like structures that protect the developing blooms.
