Hibiscus lambertianus var. angustifolius

Hibiscus lambertianus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hibiscus lambertianus var. angustifolius is an important species in the Malvaceae family, contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
