Hyacinthus leucophaeus

Hyacinthus leucophaeus, commonly known as the white hyacinth, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow, which can be planted to propagate new growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its beauty and fragrance make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
