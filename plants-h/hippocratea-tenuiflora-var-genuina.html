Hippocratea tenuiflora var. genuina

Hippocratea tenuiflora var. genuina, commonly known as the slender-flowered hippocratea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
