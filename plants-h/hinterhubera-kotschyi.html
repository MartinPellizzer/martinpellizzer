Hinterhubera kotschyi

Hinterhubera kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi's rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features a compact rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear a cluster of flowers at the apex.

Hinterhubera kotschyi is notable for its resilience to cold climates and its role in stabilizing mountainous ecosystems.
