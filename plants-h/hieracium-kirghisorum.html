Hieracium kirghisorum

Hieracium kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically features basal rosettes of leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that give it a dandelion-like appearance.

Hieracium kirghisorum thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
