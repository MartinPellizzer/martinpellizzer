Hieracium anfractum

Hieracium anfractum, commonly known as the zigzag hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive zigzag pattern on the petals.

Hieracium anfractum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
