Hippocratea tabascensis

Hippocratea tabascensis, commonly known as the Tabasco moonflower, is a striking night-blooming plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at dusk. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
