Hoodia grandis

Hoodia grandis, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, orange berries that contain a high concentration of glycosides, which have been studied for their potential appetite-suppressing properties. Hoodia grandis is well adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick stems.

Its unique morphology and chemical composition make it an intriguing subject in both botanical and pharmacological research.
