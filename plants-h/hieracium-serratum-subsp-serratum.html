Hieracium serratum subsp. serratum

Hieracium serratum subsp.

serratum, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and serrated, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
