Hymenophyllum nigrescens

Hymenophyllum nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening haircap, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance with small, overlapping, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically dark green and darken to a blackish hue when dry, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is known for its intricate structure and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
