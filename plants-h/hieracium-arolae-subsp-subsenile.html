Hieracium arolae subsp. subsenile

Hieracium arolae subsp.

subsenile, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its role in alpine ecosystems.
