Hieracium cymosum var. pseudosabinum

Hieracium cymosum var. pseudosabinum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It features dark green, hairy leaves that are alternate and often have a serrated margin.

The flowers are yellow and form dense, flat-topped clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
