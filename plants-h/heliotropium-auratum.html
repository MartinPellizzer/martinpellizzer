Heliotropium auratum

Heliotropium auratum, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in a bushy form, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are usually hairy and can vary in height from a few inches to several feet.

Heliotropium auratum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
