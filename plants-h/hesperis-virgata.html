Hesperis virgata

Hesperis virgata, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, with four petals arranged in a cross shape. Hesperis virgata is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
