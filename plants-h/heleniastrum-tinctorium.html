Heleniastrum tinctorium

Heleniastrum tinctorium, commonly known as the red root or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves and roots, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a dye source. Heleniastrum tinctorium thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and pharmacological studies.
