Hieracium florentinum subsp. cylindriceps

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

cylindriceps, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disc florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and growth habit.
