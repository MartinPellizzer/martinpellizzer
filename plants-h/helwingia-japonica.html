Helwingia japonica

Helwingia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
