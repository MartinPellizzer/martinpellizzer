Hedera pastuchovii

Hedera pastuchovii, commonly known as the Pastuchovii ivy, is a rare and endangered species of climbing plant native to the Carpathian Mountains in Ukraine.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, pale greenish-yellow flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest undergrowth. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Hedera pastuchovii is under threat and requires protection to ensure its survival.
