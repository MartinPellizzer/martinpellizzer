Hieracium ampliflorum

Hieracium ampliflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
