Hyacinthus variabilis

Hyacinthus variabilis, commonly known as the variable hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces clusters of tightly packed, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, pink, and white. Its leaves are strap-like and grassy, emerging from a bulbous base.

Hyacinthus variabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
