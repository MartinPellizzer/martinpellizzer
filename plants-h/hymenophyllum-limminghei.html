Hymenophyllum limminghei

Hymenophyllum limminghei, commonly known as the Limminghaus' haircap, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant forms dense, mat-like growths on shaded, moist rock surfaces, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporophytes are small and inconspicuous, with capsules that are often covered in a layer of papillae.

This moss is notable for its unique morphology and ecological niche, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
