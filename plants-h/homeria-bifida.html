Homeria bifida

Homeria bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf homeria, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish or yellowish hues.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants.
