Hypericum tomentosum subsp. psilophytum

Hypericum tomentosum subsp.

psilophytum, commonly known as the woolly St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to western North America. It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
