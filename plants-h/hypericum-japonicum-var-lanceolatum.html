Hypericum japonicum var. lanceolatum

Hypericum japonicum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
