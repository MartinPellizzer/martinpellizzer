Hieracium elisaeanum subsp. microsphaerellum

Hieracium elisaeanum subsp.

microsphaerellum, commonly known as the small globular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It features small, yellowish flowers arranged in a globular shape, and its leaves are usually lanceolate with hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is notable for its distinct morphology and ecological adaptability.
