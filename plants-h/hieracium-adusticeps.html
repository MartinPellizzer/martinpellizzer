Hieracium adusticeps

Hieracium adusticeps, commonly known as the scorched hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
