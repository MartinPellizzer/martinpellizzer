Hieracium pyrenaicum var. austriacum

Hieracium pyrenaicum var. austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in grassy meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
