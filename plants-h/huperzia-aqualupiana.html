Huperzia aqualupiana

Huperzia aqualupiana, commonly known as the aquatic huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are spore-bearing, typical of ferns, and it does not produce flowers or seeds.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
