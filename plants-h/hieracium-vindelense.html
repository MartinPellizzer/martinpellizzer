Hieracium vindelense

Hieracium vindelense, commonly known as the Vindel Valley hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its yellow flowers bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
