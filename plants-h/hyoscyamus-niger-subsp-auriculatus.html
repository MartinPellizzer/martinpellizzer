Hyoscyamus niger subsp. auriculatus

Hyoscyamus niger subsp.

auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate belladonna, is a variant of the common deadly nightshade. It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

The plant is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine, though with caution due to its potent effects.
