Heteropogon betafensis

Heteropogon betafensis, commonly known as the Betafense grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct blue-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Heteropogon betafensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in botanical research.
