Hemiboea purpurea

Hemiboea purpurea, commonly known as the purple hemiboea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Hemiboea purpurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
