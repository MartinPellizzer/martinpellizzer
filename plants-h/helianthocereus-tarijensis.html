Helianthocereus tarijensis

Helianthocereus tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Bolivia and Peru.

It is a columnar cactus with ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in the summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
