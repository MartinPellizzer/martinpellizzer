Hieracium scheppigianum subsp. scheppigianum

Hieracium scheppigianum subsp.

scheppigianum, commonly known as the Scheppigian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
