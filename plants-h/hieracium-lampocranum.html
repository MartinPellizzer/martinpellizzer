Hieracium lampocranum

Hieracium lampocranum, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal and upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped to lanceolate leaves.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.
