Helianthus praecox subsp. hirtus

Helianthus praecox subsp.

hirtus, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "hirtus.".

The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Helianthus praecox subsp.

hirtus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
