Halmia tomentosa var. pyrifolia

Halmia tomentosa var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the woolly halmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
