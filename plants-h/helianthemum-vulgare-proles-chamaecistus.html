Helianthemum vulgare proles chamaecistus

Helianthemum vulgare proles chamaecistus, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
