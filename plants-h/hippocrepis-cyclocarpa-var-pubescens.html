Hippocrepis cyclocarpa var. pubescens

Hippocrepis cyclocarpa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cyclocarpa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with serrated edges, and the plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
