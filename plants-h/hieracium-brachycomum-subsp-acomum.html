Hieracium brachycomum subsp. acomum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

acomum, commonly known as the short-headed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, featuring basal rosettes of hairy, alternate leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by 10–20 ligulate rays, and it produces small, achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
