Halerpestes salsuginosa var. incisa

Halerpestes salsuginosa var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often found in coastal areas and limestone formations.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping.
