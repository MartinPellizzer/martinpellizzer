Helichrysum alsinoides

Helichrysum alsinoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces papery, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum alsinoides is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
