Hypericum phellos subsp. platyphyllum

Hypericum phellos subsp.

platyphyllum, commonly known as the flatleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
