Hieracium vulgatum subsp. guentheri

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and usually unbranched.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
