Hesperis pinnatifida

Hesperis pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant features pinnately divided leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hesperis pinnatifida is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
