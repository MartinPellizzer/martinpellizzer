Hypochaeris dimorpha

Hypochaeris dimorpha, commonly known as the two-formed common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant features yellow flowers with ray florets and a central disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a lobed or toothed appearance.

Hypochaeris dimorpha is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
