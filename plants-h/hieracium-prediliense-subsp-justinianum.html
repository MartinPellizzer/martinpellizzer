Hieracium prediliense subsp. justinianum

Hieracium prediliense subsp.

justinianum, commonly known as the Justinian's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green color, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and its role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
