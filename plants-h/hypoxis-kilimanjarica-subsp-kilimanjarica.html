Hypoxis kilimanjarica subsp. kilimanjarica

Hypoxis kilimanjarica subsp.

kilimanjarica, commonly known as Kilimanjaro groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat. It is often found in montane grasslands and forest margins at elevations above 2,500 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
