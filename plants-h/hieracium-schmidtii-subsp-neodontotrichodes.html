Hieracium schmidtii subsp. neodontotrichodes

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

neodontotrichodes, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf and flower morphology, which aids in its identification and ecological adaptation.
