Hieracium pannonicum subsp. lasiophorum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

lasiophorum, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
