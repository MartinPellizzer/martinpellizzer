Hippochaete × moorei

Hippochaete × moorei, commonly known as the hybrid dandelion, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
