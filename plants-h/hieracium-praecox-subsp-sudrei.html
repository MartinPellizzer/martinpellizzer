Hieracium praecox subsp. sudrei

Hieracium praecox subsp.

sudrei, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
