Humbertacalia racemosa

Humbertacalia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Humbertacalia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
