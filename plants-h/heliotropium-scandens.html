Heliotropium scandens

Heliotropium scandens, commonly known as the climbing heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a trailing or climbing herbaceous plant with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Heliotropium scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and flowers.
