Hibiscus bodinieri

Hibiscus bodinieri, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, featuring five deeply lobed petals. The plant produces bright yellow stamens and a prominent red or orange pistil, making it visually striking. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Hibiscus bodinieri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
