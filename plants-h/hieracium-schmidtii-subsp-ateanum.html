Hieracium schmidtii subsp. ateanum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

ateanum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils and supporting local insect populations.
