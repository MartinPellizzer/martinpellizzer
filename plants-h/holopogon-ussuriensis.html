Holopogon ussuriensis

Holopogon ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri grass, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northeastern China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midvein, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Holopogon ussuriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
