Helianthus apetalus

Helianthus apetalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with dark green, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has a bushy, upright habit. Its flowers are composite, consisting of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are often reduced or absent in this species.

Helianthus apetalus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
