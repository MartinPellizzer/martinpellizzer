Haemadictyon lagoense

Haemadictyon lagoense, commonly known as the Lake Victoria waterwheel, is a floating aquatic plant native to the Great Lakes region of Africa.

It has a unique whorl of narrow, ribbon-like leaves that radiate from a central stem, creating a distinctive wheel-like appearance. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its leaves are typically green to reddish in color, depending on the light conditions and nutrient availability.

Haemadictyon lagoense plays a role in stabilizing water bodies by reducing wave action and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
