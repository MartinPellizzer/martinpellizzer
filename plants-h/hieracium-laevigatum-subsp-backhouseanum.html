Hieracium laevigatum subsp. backhouseanum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

backhouseanum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and form dense, flat-topped clusters, often with 8 to 12 ray florets.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
