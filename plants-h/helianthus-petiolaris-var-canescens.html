Helianthus petiolaris var. canescens

Helianthus petiolaris var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with a central disk, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
