Hoya meliflua subsp. taytayensis

Hoya meliflua subsp.

taytayensis, commonly known as the Taytay hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
