Hopea papuana

Hopea papuana, commonly known as the Papuan hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the lowland forests of New Guinea and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing three seeds. Hopea papuana is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.

Its growth is typically associated with humid, tropical climates and well-drained soils.
