Hieracium urezzae

Hieracium urezzae, commonly known as the Ureza hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium urezzae is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
