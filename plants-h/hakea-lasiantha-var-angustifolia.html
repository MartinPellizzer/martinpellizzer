Hakea lasiantha var. angustifolia

Hakea lasiantha var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
