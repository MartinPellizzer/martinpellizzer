Hesperis theophrasti subsp. sintenisii

Hesperis theophrasti subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Alpine honesty, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which range from white to pale pink, bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth compared to the typical form of Hesperis theophrasti.
