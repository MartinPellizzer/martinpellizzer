Hippochaete ripensis

Hippochaete ripensis, commonly known as the river dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Hippochaete ripensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
