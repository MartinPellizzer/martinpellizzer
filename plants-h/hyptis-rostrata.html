Hyptis rostrata

Hyptis rostrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hyptis rostrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
