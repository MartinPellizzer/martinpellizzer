Hippeastrum pratense

Hippeastrum pratense, commonly known as the meadow amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbous stem stores nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

Hippeastrum pratense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
