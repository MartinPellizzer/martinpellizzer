Helianthemum virgatum var. setosum

Helianthemum virgatum var. setosum, commonly known as the rough rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean climates.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to poor soil conditions and drought.
