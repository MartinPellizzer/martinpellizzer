Hieracium olivaceum subsp. canillosum

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

canillosum, commonly known as the Canary Island hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open, rocky habitats. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
