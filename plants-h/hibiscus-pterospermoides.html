Hibiscus pterospermoides

Hibiscus pterospermoides, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers have five petals and are adorned with a prominent yellow stamen, making it visually striking.

Hibiscus pterospermoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
