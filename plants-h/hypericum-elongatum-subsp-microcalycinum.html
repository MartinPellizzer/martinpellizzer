Hypericum elongatum subsp. microcalycinum

Hypericum elongatum subsp.

microcalycinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
