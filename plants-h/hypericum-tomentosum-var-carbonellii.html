Hypericum tomentosum var. carbonellii

Hypericum tomentosum var. carbonellii, commonly known as the velvet hypericum, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
