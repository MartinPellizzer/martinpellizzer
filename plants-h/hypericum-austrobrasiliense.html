Hypericum austrobrasiliense

Hypericum austrobrasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian golden st John's wort, is a species of flowering plant in the family Hypericaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has woody stems with opposite leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its potential therapeutic uses, it has attracted interest in herbal medicine and botanical research.
