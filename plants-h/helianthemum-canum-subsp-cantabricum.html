Helianthemum canum subsp. cantabricum

Helianthemum canum subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian sandthrift, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features compact, rounded foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
