Hippuris melanocarpa

Hippuris melanocarpa, commonly known as the black water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically submerged, with a dark green to blackish coloration on the upper surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and through vegetative propagation.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to water quality through nutrient uptake.
