Hibiscus heterophyllus var. genuinus

Hibiscus heterophyllus var. genuinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are typically large, showy, and come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a prominent central stamen. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its resilience to pests and diseases.
