Helianthemum thyrsoideum

Helianthemum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the everlasting rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has fleshy, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden ornamental use.
