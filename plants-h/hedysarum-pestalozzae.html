Hedysarum pestalozzae

Hedysarum pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pestalozza's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Hedysarum pestalozzae is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
