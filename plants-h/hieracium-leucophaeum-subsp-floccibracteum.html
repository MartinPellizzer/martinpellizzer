Hieracium leucophaeum subsp. floccibracteum

Hieracium leucophaeum subsp.

floccibracteum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy, and often whitish or grayish leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, elongated inflorescence, which is characteristic of the genus Hieracium. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

This subspecies is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.
