Hemesteum pinfaense

Hemesteum pinfaense, commonly known as the Pinfaen Starleaf, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of Pinfa.

It is characterized by its striking star-shaped leaves that shimmer with a waxy coating, adapting to the damp, cool climate of its habitat. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers in clusters during the late spring, which attract pollinators such as moths and hoverflies. Its roots are fibrous and deep, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, Hemesteum pinfaense is considered a valuable species in botanical research.
