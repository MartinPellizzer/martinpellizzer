Hieracium chabalicum

Hieracium chabalicum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.
