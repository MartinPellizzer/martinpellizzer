Hyptis polystachya

Hyptis polystachya, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Hyptis polystachya is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its fragrant foliage and showy flower clusters.
