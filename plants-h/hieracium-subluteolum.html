Hieracium subluteolum

Hieracium subluteolum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium subluteolum is often found in moist environments and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
