Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp. kunzeanum

Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp.

kunzeanum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, along with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
