Hieracium bifidum subsp. spiloprasinum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

spiloprasinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a distinct basal rosette, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
