Hyptis fastigiata

Hyptis fastigiata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hyptis fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
