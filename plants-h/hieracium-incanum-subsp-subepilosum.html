Hieracium incanum subsp. subepilosum

Hieracium incanum subsp.

subepilosum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in certain regions.
