Hygrophila katangensis

Hygrophila katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga hygrophila, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and rivers. The plant features narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hygrophila katangensis is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
