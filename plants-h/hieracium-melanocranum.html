Hieracium melanocranum

Hieracium melanocranum, commonly known as the black-crowned hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hieracium melanocranum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
