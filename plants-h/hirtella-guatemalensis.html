Hirtella guatemalensis

Hirtella guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, spherical berries that are edible.

Hirtella guatemalensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
