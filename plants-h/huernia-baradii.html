Huernia baradii

Huernia baradii, commonly known as the Baradi's moon cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a swollen, fleshy stem that resembles a cactus, though it is not a true cactus but a member of the Asphodelaceae family. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Huernia baradii is prized for its striking appearance and relatively easy care, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
