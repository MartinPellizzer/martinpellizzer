Hedyosmum anisodorum

Hedyosmum anisodorum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in coastal and tropical regions. It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments, outcompeting native vegetation.
