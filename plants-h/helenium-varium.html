Helenium varium

Helenium varium, commonly known as the golden rod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow to orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, disk-like florets. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

Helenium varium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
