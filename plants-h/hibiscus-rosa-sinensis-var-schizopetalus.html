Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. schizopetalus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. schizopetalus, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or the split-petal hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, with a distinctive split or fringed appearance on the petals. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
