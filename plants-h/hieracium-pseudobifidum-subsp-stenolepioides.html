Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. stenolepioides

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

stenolepioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 centimeters.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in sunny, open habitats.
