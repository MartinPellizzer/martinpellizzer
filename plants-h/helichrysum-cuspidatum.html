Helichrysum cuspidatum

Helichrysum cuspidatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic properties.

The flowers are also popular in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting color and delicate appearance.
