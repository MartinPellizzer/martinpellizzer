Hedyotis uncinella var. cephalophora

Hedyotis uncinella var. cephalophora, commonly known as the hairy buttonweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and prominent stamens. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
