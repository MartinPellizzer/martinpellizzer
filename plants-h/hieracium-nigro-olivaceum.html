Hieracium nigro-olivaceum

Hieracium nigro-olivaceum, commonly known as the black-olive hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive dark green center.

It is known for its aggressive growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
