Habenaria oligantha

Habenaria oligantha, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short rhizome and produces a single or few erect, slender stems. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in loose racemes, and it thrives in wet or marshy habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is typically found in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
