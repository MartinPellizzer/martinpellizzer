Hieracium illyricum subsp. trilacense

Hieracium illyricum subsp.

trilacense, commonly known as the Illyrian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 cm.
