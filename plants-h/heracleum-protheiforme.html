Heracleum protheiforme

Heracleum protheiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cow parsnip, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

The plant is known for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
