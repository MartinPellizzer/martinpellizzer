Hieracium turkestanicum

Hieracium turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium turkestanicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
