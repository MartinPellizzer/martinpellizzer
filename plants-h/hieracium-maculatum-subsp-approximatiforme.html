Hieracium maculatum subsp. approximatiforme

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

approximatiforme, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, which are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. Its leaves are often covered in white or pale green spots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.
