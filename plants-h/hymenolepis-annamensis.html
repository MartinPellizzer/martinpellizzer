Hymenolepis annamensis

Hymenolepis annamensis, commonly known as the Annam shieldbug, is a species of shieldbug native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated body and the presence of a shield-like dorsal plate. The plant it inhabits typically features broad leaves and a tropical climate, providing the necessary conditions for its survival. Hymenolepis annamensis plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to pollination and serving as prey for larger animals.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to diverse environments and its importance in regional biodiversity.
