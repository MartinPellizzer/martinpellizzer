Haemaria discolor var. ordeana

Haemaria discolor var. ordeana, commonly known as the Florida spiderwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
