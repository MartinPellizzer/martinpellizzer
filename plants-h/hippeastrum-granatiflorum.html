Hippeastrum granatiflorum

Hippeastrum granatiflorum, commonly known as the Brazilian star flower, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hippeastrum granatiflorum is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
