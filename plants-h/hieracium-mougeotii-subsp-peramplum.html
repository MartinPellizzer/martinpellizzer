Hieracium mougeotii subsp. peramplum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

peramplum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
