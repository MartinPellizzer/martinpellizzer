Hypodiscus squamosus

Hypodiscus squamosus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, scale-like structures on its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
