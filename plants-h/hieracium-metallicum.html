Hieracium metallicum

Hieracium metallicum, commonly known as the metallic hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish-orange flowers. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
