Heliopsis parvifolia

Heliopsis parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value in the landscape.
