Hyptis rhabdocalyx

Hyptis rhabdocalyx, commonly known as purple hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Hyptis rhabdocalyx is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
