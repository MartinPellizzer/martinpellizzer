Hibiscus pseudopalmatus

Hibiscus pseudopalmatus, commonly known as the palmate hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming during the summer months. Hibiscus pseudopalmatus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique foliage and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
