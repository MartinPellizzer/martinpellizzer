Hieracium vallisiacum subsp. trichoprenanthes

Hieracium vallisiacum subsp.

trichoprenanthes, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds in small, dark brown achenes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
