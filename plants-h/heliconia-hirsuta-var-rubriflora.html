Heliconia hirsuta var. rubriflora

Heliconia hirsuta var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered hirsuta heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, bright red bracts that surround the inflorescence, which resembles a spiral. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered within the bracts, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats. Its leaves are broad, pointed, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its striking floral display.
