Hieracium helenium

Hieracium helenium, commonly known as the greater hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium helenium is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
