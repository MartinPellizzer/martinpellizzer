Helictochloa adsurgens subsp. ausserdorferi

Helictochloa adsurgens subsp.

ausserdorferi, commonly known as the Ausserdorfer's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often arch or lie prostrate.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, two-ranked spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant plant.
