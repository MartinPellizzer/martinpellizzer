Hieracium gemelliceps

Hieracium gemelliceps, commonly known as the twin-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium gemelliceps is known for its ornamental value but can also be invasive in certain regions.
