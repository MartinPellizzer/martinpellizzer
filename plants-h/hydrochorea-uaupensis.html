Hydrochorea uaupensis

Hydrochorea uaupensis, commonly known as the Amazon water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant has waxy, smooth foliage that helps it float on the water's surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, opening in the morning and closing at night.

Hydrochorea uaupensis plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystem.
