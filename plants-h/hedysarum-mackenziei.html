Hedysarum mackenziei

Hedysarum mackenziei, commonly known as Mackenzie's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Hedysarum mackenziei plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
