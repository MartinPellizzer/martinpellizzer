Hieracium chondrilloideum

Hieracium chondrilloideum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
