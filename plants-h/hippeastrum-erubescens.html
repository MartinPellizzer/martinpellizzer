Hippeastrum erubescens

Hippeastrum erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a spicy fragrance. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that emerge from a bulb and can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is popular as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers typically appear in late winter to early spring, making it a reliable choice for seasonal color.
