Horsfieldia sylvestris var. villosa

Horsfieldia sylvestris var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy horsfieldia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescence bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often covered with a fine, hairy texture that gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
