Helianthus nitidus var. camporum

Helianthus nitidus var. camporum, commonly known as Camp's sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
