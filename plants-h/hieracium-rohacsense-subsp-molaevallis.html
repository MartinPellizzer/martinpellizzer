Hieracium rohacsense subsp. molaevallis

Hieracium rohacsense subsp.

molaevallis, commonly known as the Molaevall's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has finely divided, lanceolate leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
