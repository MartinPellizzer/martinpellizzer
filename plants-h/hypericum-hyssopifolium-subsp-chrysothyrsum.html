Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp. chrysothyrsum

Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp.

chrysothyrsum, commonly known as golden-stem hypericum, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are distinctive for their golden hue, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.
