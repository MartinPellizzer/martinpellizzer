Hordeum jubatum f. versicolor

Hordeum jubatum f. versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow barley, is a botanical variant of the common barley species.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored inflorescence that displays a range of hues from blue to violet, pink, and red. This color variation is due to the presence of anthocyanins, which are pigments influenced by environmental factors such as light and temperature. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
