Hieracium triste var. dentatum

Hieracium triste var. dentatum, commonly known as the sorrowful hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its common name reflects its melancholic appearance and historical associations with sorrow and mourning.
