Hieracium crocatum subsp. mongolicum

Hieracium crocatum subsp.

mongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
