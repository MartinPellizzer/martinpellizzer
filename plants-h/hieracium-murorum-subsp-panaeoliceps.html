Hieracium murorum subsp. panaeoliceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

panaeoliceps, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flower heads that produce numerous tiny seeds.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
