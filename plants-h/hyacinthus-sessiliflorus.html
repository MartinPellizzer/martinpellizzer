Hyacinthus sessiliflorus

Hyacinthus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flowered hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a valuable addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Hyacinthus sessiliflorus is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
