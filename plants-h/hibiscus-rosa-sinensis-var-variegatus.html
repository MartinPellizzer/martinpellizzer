Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. variegatus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a cultivar of the Chinese hibiscus.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring combinations of green, white, and yellow patterns. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

This ornamental plant is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
