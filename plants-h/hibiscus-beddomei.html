Hibiscus beddomei

Hibiscus beddomei, commonly known as the Beddome's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its striking, large, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky substrates.

Hibiscus beddomei is considered a rare and endemic species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
