Habenaria readei

Habenaria readei, commonly known as the Redei's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive, hairy lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
