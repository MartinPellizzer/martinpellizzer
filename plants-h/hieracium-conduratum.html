Hieracium conduratum

Hieracium conduratum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a creeping growth habit with stolons that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
