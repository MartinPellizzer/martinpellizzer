Hesperis pendula subsp. pendula

Hesperis pendula subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the hanging horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its pendulous, orange-red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
