Helianthus annuus f. fallax

Helianthus annuus f. fallax, commonly known as the fallax sunflower, is a botanical variant of the common sunflower.

It is characterized by its unique, irregularly shaped flower heads and shorter, more compact growth habit compared to typical sunflowers. The plant exhibits a range of flower colors, including yellow, orange, and red, with some varieties showing bicolor patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and small-scale cultivation.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in diverse environmental conditions.
