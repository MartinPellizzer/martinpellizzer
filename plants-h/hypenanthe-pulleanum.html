Hypenanthe pulleanum

Hypenanthe pulleanum, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, often with a white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Hypenanthe pulleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
