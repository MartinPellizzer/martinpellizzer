Hartogia rubra

Hartogia rubra, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Hartogia rubra typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
