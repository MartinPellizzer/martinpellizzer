Habenaria nematocaulon

Habenaria nematocaulon, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest undergrowth. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments.

The flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native ecosystem.
