Hieracium venetianum

Hieracium venetianum, commonly known as the Venetian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize various habitats.
