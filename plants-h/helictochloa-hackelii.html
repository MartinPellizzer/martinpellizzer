Helictochloa hackelii

Helictochloa hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Helictochloa hackelii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
