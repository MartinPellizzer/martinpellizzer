Humboltia octomerioides

Humboltia octomerioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in winter, typically from September to December. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.

Humboltia octomerioides is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
