Hieracium levicaule subsp. pullulum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

pullulum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in temperate climates.
