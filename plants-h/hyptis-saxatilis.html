Hyptis saxatilis

Hyptis saxatilis, commonly known as rock hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in regions with rocky or coastal habitats.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or white. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis saxatilis is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate coastal salt spray.
