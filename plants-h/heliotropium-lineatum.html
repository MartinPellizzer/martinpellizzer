Heliotropium lineatum

Heliotropium lineatum, commonly known as the common heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Heliotropium lineatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
