Hieracium lawsonii var. subandurense

Hieracium lawsonii var. subandurense, commonly known as the Andes hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly smaller stature and more compact growth compared to other Hieracium species.
