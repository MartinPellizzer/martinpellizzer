Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. elatum

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. elatum, commonly known as the tall St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a variant of the species Hypericum pseudopetiolatum, characterized by its increased height compared to the typical form. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural habitats.
