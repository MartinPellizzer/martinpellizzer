Hemianthus micranthemoides

Hemianthus micranthemoides, commonly known as the dwarf mondo grass, is a low-growing aquatic plant native to South America.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, rounded leaves that form a compact carpet-like growth pattern. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate the water. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature and ease of care, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
