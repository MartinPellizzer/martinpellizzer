Hibiscus furcellatus var. dominicus

Hibiscus furcellatus var. dominicus, commonly known as the Dominican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Dominica.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing in moist, shaded areas.

Hibiscus furcellatus var. dominicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
