Hypericum galioides var. axillare

Hypericum galioides var. axillare, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

It is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
