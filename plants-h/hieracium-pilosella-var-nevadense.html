Hieracium pilosella var. nevadense

Hieracium pilosella var. nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and rocky areas at high elevations.
