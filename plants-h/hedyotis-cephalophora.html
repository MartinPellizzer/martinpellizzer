Hedyotis cephalophora

Hedyotis cephalophora, commonly known as the mountain buttonweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis cephalophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
