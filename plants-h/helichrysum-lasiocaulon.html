Helichrysum lasiocaulon

Helichrysum lasiocaulon, commonly known as the woolly helichrysum, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting color and texture.
