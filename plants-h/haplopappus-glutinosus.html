Haplopappus glutinosus

Haplopappus glutinosus, commonly known as sticky fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a sticky substance, giving it its common name.

The species is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and distinctive seed heads.
