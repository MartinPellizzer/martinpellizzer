Hypericum tomentosum var. racemosum

Hypericum tomentosum var. racemosum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
