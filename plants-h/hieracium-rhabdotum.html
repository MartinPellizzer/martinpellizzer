Hieracium rhabdotum

Hieracium rhabdotum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium rhabdotum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
