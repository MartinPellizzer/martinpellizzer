Heliotropium veronicifolium

Heliotropium veronicifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive central tubular structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Heliotropium veronicifolium is also known for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
