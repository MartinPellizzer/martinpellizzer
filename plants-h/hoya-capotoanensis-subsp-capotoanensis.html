Hoya capotoanensis subsp. capotoanensis

Hoya capotoanensis subsp.

capotoanensis, commonly known as the Capotoanensis Wanda, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts and collectors.
