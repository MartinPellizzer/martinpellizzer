Hieracium figolsianum

Hieracium figolsianum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium figolsianum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
