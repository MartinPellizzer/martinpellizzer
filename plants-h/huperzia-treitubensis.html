Huperzia treitubensis

Huperzia treitubensis, commonly known as the Treitubens fern, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, creeping rhizomes and dense clusters of needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia treitubensis is known for its slow growth and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
