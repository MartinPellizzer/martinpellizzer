Hypericum biondii

Hypericum biondii, commonly known as the golden St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant produces small, orange-colored berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
