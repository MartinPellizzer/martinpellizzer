Hieracium commersonii subvar. latifolium

Hieracium commersonii subvar. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broad and typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
