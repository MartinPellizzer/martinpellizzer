Homalomena hayupensis

Homalomena hayupensis, commonly known as the Borneo arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a glossy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing near the forest floor.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
