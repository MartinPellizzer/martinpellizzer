Hyptis macrochila

Hyptis macrochila, commonly known as the large-leaved hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis macrochila is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
