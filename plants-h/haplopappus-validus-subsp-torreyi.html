Haplopappus validus subsp. torreyi

Haplopappus validus subsp.

torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's pappusweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers.

The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple leaf structure and distinctive floral morphology.
