Heterolathus polycephala

Heterolathus polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Heterolathus polycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
