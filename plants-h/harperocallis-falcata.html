Harperocallis falcata

Harperocallis falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Harperocallis falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
