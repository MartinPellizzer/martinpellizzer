Hieracium gibsonii

Hieracium gibsonii, commonly known as Gibson's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is relatively rare and is considered a species of conservation concern due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
