Hydnophytum petiolatum var. lacum

Hydnophytum petiolatum var. lacum, commonly known as the aquatic bladderwort, is a unique carnivorous plant native to tropical regions.

It thrives in waterlogged or aquatic environments, utilizing its specialized bladder-like structures to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are typically submerged, while its stems may extend above the water surface. Its reproductive system involves small, yellowish flowers that bloom above the water.

This variety is notable for its ability to adapt to both aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
