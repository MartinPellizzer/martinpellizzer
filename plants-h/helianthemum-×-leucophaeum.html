Helianthemum × leucophaeum

Helianthemum × leucophaeum, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has dense, evergreen foliage with grey-green, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
