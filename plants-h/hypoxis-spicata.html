Hypoxis spicata

Hypoxis spicata, commonly known as the spear lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flower stalks that bear small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Hypoxis spicata is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
