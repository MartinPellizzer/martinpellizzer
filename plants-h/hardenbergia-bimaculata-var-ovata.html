Hardenbergia bimaculata var. ovata

Hardenbergia bimaculata var. ovata, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red markings. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, rounded seed pods that mature into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
