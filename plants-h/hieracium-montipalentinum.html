Hieracium montipalentinum

Hieracium montipalentinum, commonly known as the Montipalentin hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, alternate, and have a rough or hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are pollinated primarily by insects.
