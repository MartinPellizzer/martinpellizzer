Hieracium bifidum subsp. perlepidum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

perlepidum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
