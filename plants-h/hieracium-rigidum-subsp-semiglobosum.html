Hieracium rigidum subsp. semiglobosum

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

semiglobosum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of yellow flower heads, each surrounded by involucral bracts.

The subspecies is characterized by its semi-globous flower heads and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
