Hypericum sphaerocarpum var. turgidum

Hypericum sphaerocarpum var. turgidum, commonly known as the inflated St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that are inflated and often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
