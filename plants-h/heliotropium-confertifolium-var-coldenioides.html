Heliotropium confertifolium var. coldenioides

Heliotropium confertifolium var. coldenioides, commonly known as the colden's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental planting due to its attractive blooms and adaptability.
