Hieracium chloroscyphicum

Hieracium chloroscyphicum, commonly known as the green-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium chloroscyphicum is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.
