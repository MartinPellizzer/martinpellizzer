Hexalectris brevicaulis

Hexalectris brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed spider orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spider-like flowers. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in pine and hardwood forests. It has a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and its limited distribution, it is considered endangered in several states.
