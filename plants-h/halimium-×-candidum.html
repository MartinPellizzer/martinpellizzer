Halimium × candidum

Halimium × candidum, commonly known as the white halimium, is a hybrid plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Halimium × candidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in xeriscaping.
