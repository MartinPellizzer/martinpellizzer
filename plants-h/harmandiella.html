Harmandiella

Harmandiella, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features spiny stems and is well adapted to arid environments. Harmandiella species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking appearance.

They require minimal water and thrive in full sun, making them popular among succulent enthusiasts.
