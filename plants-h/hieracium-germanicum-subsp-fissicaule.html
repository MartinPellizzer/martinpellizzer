Hieracium germanicum subsp. fissicaule

Hieracium germanicum subsp.

fissicaule, commonly known as the German hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow disk flowers with ray florets. Its leaves are often deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, fissured stem and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
