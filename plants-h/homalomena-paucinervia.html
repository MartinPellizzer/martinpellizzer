Homalomena paucinervia

Homalomena paucinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is popular in interior design due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
