Homoranthus bornhardtiensis

Homoranthus bornhardtiensis, commonly known as the Darwin's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
