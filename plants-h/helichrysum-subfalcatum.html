Helichrysum subfalcatum

Helichrysum subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting.

It is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow-orange flowers that persist throughout the year. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and low-maintenance characteristics.
