Hymenoxys richardsonii var. richardsonii

Hymenoxys richardsonii var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often clustered in a globular shape, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is adapted to withstand drought and heat.
