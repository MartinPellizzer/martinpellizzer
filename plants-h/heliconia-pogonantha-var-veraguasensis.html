Heliconia pogonantha var. veraguasensis

Heliconia pogonantha var. veraguasensis, commonly known as the Guianese heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by fleshy, petal-like bracts in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
