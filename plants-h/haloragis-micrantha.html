Haloragis micrantha

Haloragis micrantha, commonly known as the small-leaved water-starwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is typically found in wetland or aquatic environments. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is often submerged or floating, with stems that can root in muddy substrates.

Haloragis micrantha plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
