Hieracium graminellum

Hieracium graminellum, commonly known as slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium graminellum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
