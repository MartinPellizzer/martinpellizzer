Helichrysum glossophyllum

Helichrysum glossophyllum, commonly known as the honeycomb bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of golden-yellow flowers that resemble honeycombs. The plant has narrow, greyish-green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

The flowers are long-lasting and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
