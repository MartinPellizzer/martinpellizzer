Hybanthus ipecacuanha

Hybanthus ipecacuanha, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central column. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Hybanthus ipecacuanha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
