Hydrangea macrophylla f. otaksa

Hydrangea macrophylla f. otaksa, commonly known as the Otaksa hydrangea, is a cultivar prized for its large, showy flower heads.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features broad, serrated leaves and produces clusters of sterile flowers that range in color from pink to blue, depending on soil pH. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
