Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. carpathicola

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

carpathicola, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in meadows, forest edges, and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers with ray florets. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to plant biodiversity.
