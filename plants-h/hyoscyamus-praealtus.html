Hyoscyamus praealtus

Hyoscyamus praealtus, commonly known as the tall henbane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal history, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
