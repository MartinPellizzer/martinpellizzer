Hypericum beccarii subsp. beccarii

Hypericum beccarii subsp.

beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It features woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glandular-dotted.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its flowering parts are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant properties.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
