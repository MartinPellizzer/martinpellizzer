Heteranthera yucatana

Heteranthera yucatana, commonly known as the Yucatan water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Heteranthera yucatana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help stabilize wetland ecosystems.
