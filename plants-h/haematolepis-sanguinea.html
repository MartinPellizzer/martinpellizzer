Haematolepis sanguinea

Haematolepis sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
