Hymenopappus filifolius var. lugens

Hymenopappus filifolius var. lugens, commonly known as the silky hymenopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or open habitats.

The species is notable for its distinctive silky, hairy foliage and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
