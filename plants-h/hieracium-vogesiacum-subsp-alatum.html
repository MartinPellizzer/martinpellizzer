Hieracium vogesiacum subsp. alatum

Hieracium vogesiacum subsp.

alatum, commonly known as the winged hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive wing-like structure on their upper surface. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
