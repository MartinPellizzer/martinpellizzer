Hydrolea rupincola

Hydrolea rupincola, commonly known as the rock hydrolea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water.
