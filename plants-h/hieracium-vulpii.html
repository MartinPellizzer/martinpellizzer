Hieracium vulpii

Hieracium vulpii, commonly known as the fox's harebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium vulpii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
