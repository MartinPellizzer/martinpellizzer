Hedysarum consanguineum

Hedysarum consanguineum, commonly known as the red vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

Hedysarum consanguineum plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobium bacteria.
