Hieracium bifidum subsp. sychnopolium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

sychnopolium, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped achenes as fruits.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds.
