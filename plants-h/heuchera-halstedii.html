Heuchera halstedii

Heuchera halstedii, commonly known as the red veined heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring deep green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its foliage, which displays a range of colors throughout the growing season.
