Hirtella pauciflora

Hirtella pauciflora, commonly known as the woolly hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Hirtella pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
