Hieracium murorum var. pulcherrimum

Hieracium murorum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the showy mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This variety is distinguished by its more showy and larger flower heads compared to other members of the species.
