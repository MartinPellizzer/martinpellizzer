Hydnophytum crassicaule

Hydnophytum crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-stemmed bladder fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing capsules, which are often enclosed in a fleshy, bladder-like structure. Its specialized morphology enables it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions by absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Hydnophytum crassicaule plays a role in forest ecosystems as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
