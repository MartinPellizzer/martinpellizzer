Hieracium laevigatum subsp. froedingii

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

froedingii, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and roadsides.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in some regions.
