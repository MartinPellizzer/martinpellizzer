Hypericum fasciculatum

Hypericum fasciculatum, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow flowers, which appear in late summer, consist of five petals and numerous stamens.

Hypericum fasciculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
