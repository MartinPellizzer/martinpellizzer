Humiriastrum glaziovii var. angustifolium

Humiriastrum glaziovii var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf glaziovian, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its dry environment. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a low-growing shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
