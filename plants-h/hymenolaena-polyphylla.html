Hymenolaena polyphylla

Hymenolaena polyphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Hymenolaena polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
