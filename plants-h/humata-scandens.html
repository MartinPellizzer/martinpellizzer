Humata scandens

Humata scandens, commonly known as the climbing hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing trees or shrubs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Humata scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
