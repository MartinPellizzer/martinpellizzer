Harmsiodoxa brevipes var. major

Harmsiodoxa brevipes var. major, commonly known as the giant African lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a statement plant in both landscapes and containers.
