Hieracium barreranum

Hieracium barreranum, commonly known as the Barrer's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in various soil types.
