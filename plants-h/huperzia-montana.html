Huperzia montana

Huperzia montana, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features whorled leaves that are often lance-shaped and have a leathery texture. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its sporophytes are usually short and sturdy.

Huperzia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
