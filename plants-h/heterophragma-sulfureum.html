Heterophragma sulfureum

Heterophragma sulfureum, commonly known as the golden bract orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique coloration that ranges from yellow to orange.

Heterophragma sulfureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
