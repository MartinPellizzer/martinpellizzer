Hieracium rupicola subsp. rupicola

Hieracium rupicola subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
