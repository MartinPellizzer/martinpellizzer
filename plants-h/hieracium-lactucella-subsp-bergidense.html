Hieracium lactucella subsp. bergidense

Hieracium lactucella subsp.

bergidense, commonly known as the Bergidense Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Compositae family. It is native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its ecological adaptability.
