Huperzia ellenbeckii

Huperzia ellenbeckii, commonly known as the Ellenbeck's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of its branches.

Huperzia ellenbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems where it contributes to soil stabilization and habitat for microorganisms.
