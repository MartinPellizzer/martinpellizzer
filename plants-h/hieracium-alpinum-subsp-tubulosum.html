Hieracium alpinum subsp. tubulosum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

tubulosum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological features compared to other Hieracium species.

It is often found in open habitats at high altitudes, contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
