Hieracium graecum subsp. szilyanum

Hieracium graecum subsp.

szilyanum, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish ray florets. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
