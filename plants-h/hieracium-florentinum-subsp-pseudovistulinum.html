Hieracium florentinum subsp. pseudovistulinum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

pseudovistulinum, commonly known as the false vistuline hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flower heads are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptability.
