Hieracium valoddae subsp. strictipilum

Hieracium valoddae subsp.

strictipilum, commonly known as the narrow-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oblong achenes as its seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and reproductive structures typical of the Asteraceae family.
