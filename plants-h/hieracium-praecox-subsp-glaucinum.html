Hieracium praecox subsp. glaucinum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

glaucinum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant features hairy, blue-green leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disk. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its subspecific name, *glaucinum*, refers to the bluish-green hue of its foliage.
