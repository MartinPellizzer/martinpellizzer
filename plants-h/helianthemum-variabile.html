Helianthemum variabile

Helianthemum variabile, commonly known as the variable rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Helianthemum variabile is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it demonstrates adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
