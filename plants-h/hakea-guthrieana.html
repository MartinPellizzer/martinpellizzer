Hakea guthrieana

Hakea guthrieana, commonly known as the Redback Hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical branches and bright red or orange flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and heat.

Hakea guthrieana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
