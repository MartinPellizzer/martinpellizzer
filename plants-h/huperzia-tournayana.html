Huperzia tournayana

Huperzia tournayana, commonly known as the Tournay's clubmoss, is a species of tropical fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, branching structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Huperzia tournayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
