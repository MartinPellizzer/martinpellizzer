Hieracium brachiatum subsp. crociflorum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

crociflorum, commonly known as the Crocus-leaved Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that resemble daisies. Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower surrounded by ray florets, which are often mistaken for petals.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
