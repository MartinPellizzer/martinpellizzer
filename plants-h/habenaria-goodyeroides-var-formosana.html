Habenaria goodyeroides var. formosana

Habenaria goodyeroides var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Taiwan.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with a distinct three-lobed lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
