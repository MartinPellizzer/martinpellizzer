Hyptis pauliana

Hyptis pauliana, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Hyptis pauliana is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
