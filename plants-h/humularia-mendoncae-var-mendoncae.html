Humularia mendoncae var. mendoncae

Humularia mendoncae var. mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendonca's humularia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features small, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
