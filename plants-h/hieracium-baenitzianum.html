Hieracium baenitzianum

Hieracium baenitzianum, commonly known as the Baenitz's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are generally rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hieracium baenitzianum is considered a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
