Hieracium subpulchridens

Hieracium subpulchridens, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium subpulchridens thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
