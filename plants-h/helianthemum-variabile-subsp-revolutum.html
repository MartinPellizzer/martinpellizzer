Helianthemum variabile subsp. revolutum

Helianthemum variabile subsp.

revolutum, commonly known as the variable rockrose, is a succulent shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a compact, low-growing form.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species ideal for Mediterranean climates.
