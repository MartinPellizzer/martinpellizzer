Hordeum vulgare var. sinojaponicum

Hordeum vulgare var. sinojaponicum, commonly known as Chinese barley, is a variant of barley native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes and small, three-lobed grains. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include hollow stems and leaf blades that are often hairy.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for forage or traditional uses.
