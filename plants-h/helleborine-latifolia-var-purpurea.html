Helleborine latifolia var. purpurea

Helleborine latifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers with purple markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
