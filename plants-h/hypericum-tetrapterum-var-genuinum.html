Hypericum tetrapterum var. genuinum

Hypericum tetrapterum var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
