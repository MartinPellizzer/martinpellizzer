Helichrysum barorum

Helichrysum barorum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

Helichrysum barorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
