Hieracium grecescui subsp. subretyezatense

Hieracium grecescui subsp.

subretyezatense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, herbaceous stems that bear the flowering heads.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
