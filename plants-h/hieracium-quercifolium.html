Hieracium quercifolium

Hieracium quercifolium, commonly known as the oak-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has serrated, oak-like leaves that are usually hairy and can vary in size depending on the environment. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium quercifolium is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
