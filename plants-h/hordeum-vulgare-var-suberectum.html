Hordeum vulgare var. suberectum

Hordeum vulgare var. suberectum, commonly known as the stiff barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its upright growth habit.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically produces spikelets that are compact and dense, with lemmas that are often pubescent. It is primarily cultivated for its grains, which are used in food, animal feed, and brewing.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
