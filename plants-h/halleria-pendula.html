Halleria pendula

Halleria pendula, commonly known as the hanging halleria, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its pendulous clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and contain seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
