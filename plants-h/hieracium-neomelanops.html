Hieracium neomelanops

Hieracium neomelanops, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which is typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
