Hieracium murorum f. ramosum

Hieracium murorum f. ramosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped with serrated edges. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
