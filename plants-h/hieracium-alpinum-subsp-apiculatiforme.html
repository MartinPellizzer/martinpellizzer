Hieracium alpinum subsp. apiculatiforme

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

apiculatiforme, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive shape, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to the short growing season of alpine ecosystems.
