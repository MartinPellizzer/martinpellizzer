Hoplophytum mucroniflora

Hoplophytum mucroniflora, commonly known as the African fireplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Hoplophytum mucroniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and succulent collections.
