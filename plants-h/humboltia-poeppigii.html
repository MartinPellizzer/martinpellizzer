Humboltia poeppigii

Humboltia poeppigii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Humboltia poeppigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
