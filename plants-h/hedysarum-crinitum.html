Hedysarum crinitum

Hedysarum crinitum, commonly known as the silky vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hedysarum crinitum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
