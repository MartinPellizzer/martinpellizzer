Habenaria bertauxiana

Habenaria bertauxiana, commonly known as the Bertaux's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinct lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies. Its rhizome is fibrous, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the growing season.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
