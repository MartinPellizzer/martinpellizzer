Hieracium purpurans

Hieracium purpurans, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to reddish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hieracium purpurans is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
