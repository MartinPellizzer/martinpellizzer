Heisteria parvifolia f. grandifolia

Heisteria parvifolia f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered heisteria, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a mild, spicy fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a dense, low-growing habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to partial shade.
