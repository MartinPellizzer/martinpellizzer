Hierochloe glabra subsp. sachalinensis

Hierochloe glabra subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and smooth, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
