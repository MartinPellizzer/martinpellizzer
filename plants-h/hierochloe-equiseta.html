Hierochloe equiseta

Hierochloe equiseta, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tipped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in open woodlands or meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hierochloe equiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
