Hieracium nanum

Hieracium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf hawkweed, is a small perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a flat-topped cluster called a corymb. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Hieracium nanum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
