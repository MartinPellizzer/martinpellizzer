Hyacinthus macrobotrys subsp. caucasicus

Hyacinthus macrobotrys subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
