Hoya isabelchanae

Hoya isabelchanae, commonly known as the Isabelchanae Waxplant, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a trailing vine with thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya isabelchanae is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
