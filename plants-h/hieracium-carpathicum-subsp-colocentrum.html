Hieracium carpathicum subsp. colocentrum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

colocentrum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for pollinators.
