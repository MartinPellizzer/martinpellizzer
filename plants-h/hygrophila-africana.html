Hygrophila africana

Hygrophila africana, commonly known as African water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, making it visually striking.

Hygrophila africana is known for its ability to thrive in moist to aquatic environments, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
