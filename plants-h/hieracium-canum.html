Hieracium canum

Hieracium canum, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
