Hibiscus sagranus

Hibiscus sagranus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus sagranus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.
