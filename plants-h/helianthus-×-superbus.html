Helianthus × superbus

Helianthus × superbus, commonly known as the giant sunflower, is a hybrid species of the sunflower genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is native to North America. Its botanical features include large, heart-shaped leaves with rough textures and a long flowering period.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
