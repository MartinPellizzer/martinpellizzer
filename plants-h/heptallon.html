Heptallon

Heptallon, commonly known as the common heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or white, blooming in the spring.

Heptallon is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
