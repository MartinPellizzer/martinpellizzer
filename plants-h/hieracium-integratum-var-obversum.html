Hieracium integratum var. obversum

Hieracium integratum var. obversum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
