Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. beschtavicum

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

beschtavicum, commonly known as the Beshtav hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. Its inflorescence is characterized by ray florets that are usually shorter than the disk florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sparse flowering and specific geographic distribution.
