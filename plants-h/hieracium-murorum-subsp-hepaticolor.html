Hieracium murorum subsp. hepaticolor

Hieracium murorum subsp.

hepaticolor, commonly known as the liver-colored hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its deep green foliage with a slightly reddish tinge, giving it its common name.
