Hedyotis bracteosa

Hedyotis bracteosa, commonly known as the small-leaved bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is often found in damp, rocky soils.

Hedyotis bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
