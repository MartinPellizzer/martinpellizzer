Hakea ruscifolia

Hakea ruscifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces cylindrical flower spikes that range from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water retention.

Hakea ruscifolia is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
