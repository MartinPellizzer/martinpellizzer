Hedycarya engleriana

Hedycarya engleriana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy or velvety to the touch. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. Hedycarya engleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.
