Hydrocotyle adoensis

Hydrocotyle adoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water shield, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has small, floating leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Hydrocotyle adoensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water quality through its biological activities.
