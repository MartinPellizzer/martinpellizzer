Hypericum leconteanum

Hypericum leconteanum, commonly known as the lemony St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly lemony scent when crushed.

Hypericum leconteanum is often found in woodland edges and open woods, thriving in partial to full sunlight.
