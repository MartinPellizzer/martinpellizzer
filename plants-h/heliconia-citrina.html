Heliconia citrina

Heliconia citrina, commonly known as the yellow heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences composed of bright yellow bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. Its leaves are large, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Heliconia citrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
