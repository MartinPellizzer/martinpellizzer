Hieracium lachenalii subsp. spathulare

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

spathulare, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly ovate to spatulate in shape, with a hairy surface, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
