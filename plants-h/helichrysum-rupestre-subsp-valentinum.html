Helichrysum rupestre subsp. valentinum

Helichrysum rupestre subsp.

valentinum, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies.

The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for true flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
