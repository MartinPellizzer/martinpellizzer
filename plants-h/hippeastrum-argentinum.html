Hippeastrum argentinum

Hippeastrum argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum argentinum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
