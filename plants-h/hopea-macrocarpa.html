Hopea macrocarpa

Hopea macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and large, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces large, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
