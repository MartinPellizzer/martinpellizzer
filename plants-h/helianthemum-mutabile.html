Helianthemum mutabile

Helianthemum mutabile, commonly known as the variable rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its adaptability to harsh environments highlights its resilience and ornamental value in horticulture.
