Helicotropis hookeri

Helicotropis hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often resembling those of the heliotrope genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
