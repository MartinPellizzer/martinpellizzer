Harrisia caymanensis

Harrisia caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman bristle cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Cayman Islands.

It is a small, spiny plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem with clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that are edible and contain seeds.

The species is adapted to the tropical climate and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
