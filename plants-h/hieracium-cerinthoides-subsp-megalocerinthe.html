Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. megalocerinthe

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

megalocerinthe, commonly known as the giant meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
