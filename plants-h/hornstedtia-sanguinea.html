Hornstedtia sanguinea

Hornstedtia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered hornstedtia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, red-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and adaptation to high-altitude environments.

Hornstedtia sanguinea is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
