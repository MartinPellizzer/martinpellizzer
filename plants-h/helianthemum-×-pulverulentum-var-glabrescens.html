Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. glabrescens

Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense, compact foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
