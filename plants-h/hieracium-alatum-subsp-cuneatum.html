Hieracium alatum subsp. cuneatum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

cuneatum, commonly known as the bentleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance.

The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often curled or folded, giving it its common name. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, producing yellow to orange disk flowers.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
