Heliotropium arbainense

Heliotropium arbainense, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
