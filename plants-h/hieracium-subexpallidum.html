Hieracium subexpallidum

Hieracium subexpallidum, commonly known as the pale yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subexpallidum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
