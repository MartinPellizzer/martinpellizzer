Hieracium murorum subsp. oligogonium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

oligogonium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its small, hairy stems and a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands and is part of the Asteraceae family.
