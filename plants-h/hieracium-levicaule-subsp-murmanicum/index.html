Hieracium Levicaule Subsp. Murmanicum

Hieracium levicaule subsp. murmanicum is a rare and lesser-known species of hawkweed found primarily in the northern regions of Europe. It thrives in open, moist habitats such as meadows and tundra, often growing in areas with cool climates and limited competition. This subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and slender stems, which set it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable plant in many regions. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and delicate flora.
