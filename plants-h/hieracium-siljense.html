Hieracium siljense

Hieracium siljense, commonly known as Siljens hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
