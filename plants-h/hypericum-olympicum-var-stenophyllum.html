Hypericum olympicum var. stenophyllum

Hypericum olympicum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenseal, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
