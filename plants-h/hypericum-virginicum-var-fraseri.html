Hypericum virginicum var. fraseri

Hypericum virginicum var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
