Hieracium hypochaeridis

Hieracium hypochaeridis, commonly known as the mouse-eared hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The species is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
