Hesperis confusa

Hesperis confusa, commonly known as the pale lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, while the stem is upright and branched.

Hesperis confusa is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
