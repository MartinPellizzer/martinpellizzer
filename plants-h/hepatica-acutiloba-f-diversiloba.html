Hepatica acutiloba f. diversiloba

Hepatica acutiloba f. diversiloba, commonly known as the variegated hepatica, is a distinctive woodland plant characterized by its early spring blooms.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a striking combination of green, white, and sometimes purple hues. The flowers are typically blue or purple and consist of five petals with a distinctive central spur. This fern-like plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and early blooming period make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
