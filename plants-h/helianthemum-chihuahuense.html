Helianthemum chihuahuense

Helianthemum chihuahuense, commonly known as the Chihuahua rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy stems adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Helianthemum chihuahuense is well-suited to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a resilient species in harsh desert environments.
