Helianthemum asperum f. cinereum

Helianthemum asperum f. cinereum, commonly known as the gray thyme.

This plant is a flowering shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its low-growing habit and dense, aromatic foliage. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a silvery appearance. The flowers are small, yellowish-white, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
