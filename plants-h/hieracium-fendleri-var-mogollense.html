Hieracium fendleri var. mogollense

Hieracium fendleri var. mogollense, commonly known as the Mexican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant typically has hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
