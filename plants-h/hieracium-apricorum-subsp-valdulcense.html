Hieracium apricorum subsp. valdulcense

Hieracium apricorum subsp.

valdulcense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers in the summer months.

The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
