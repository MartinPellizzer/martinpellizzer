Helianthus leoninus

Helianthus leoninus, commonly known as the lion's head sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, yellow, daisy-like flowers with a distinctive central disc. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

Helianthus leoninus is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun.
