Helleborus orientalis

Helleborus orientalis, commonly known as the Lenten rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery basal leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, shady conditions.

Helleborus orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
