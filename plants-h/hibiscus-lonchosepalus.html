Hibiscus lonchosepalus

Hibiscus lonchosepalus, commonly known as the long-sepal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five to seven petals. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Hibiscus lonchosepalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
