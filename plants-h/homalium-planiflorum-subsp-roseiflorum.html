Homalium planiflorum subsp. roseiflorum

Homalium planiflorum subsp.

roseiflorum, commonly known as the rose-flowered holly, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a bright red color, attracting various bird species.
