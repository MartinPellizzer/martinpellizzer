Hieracium valoddae

Hieracium valoddae, commonly known as the Valodda hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium valoddae is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread rapidly.
