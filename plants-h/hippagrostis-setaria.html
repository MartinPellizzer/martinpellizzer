Hippagrostis setaria

Hippagrostis setaria, commonly known as Panicum virgatum, is a tall, grass-like plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often seen in the fall. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in prairies and open fields. It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

The species is closely related to other grasses in the Poaceae family.
