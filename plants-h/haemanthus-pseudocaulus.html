Haemanthus pseudocaulus

Haemanthus pseudocaulus, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of bright orange-red flowers that appear at the end of summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other showy flowering plants.

Haemanthus pseudocaulus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
