Hieracium glaucinum subsp. veljunense

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

veljunense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a flowering stem that bears numerous small, daisy-like blooms.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to the specific environmental conditions of its native region.
