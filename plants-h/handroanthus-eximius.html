Handroanthus eximius

Handroanthus eximius, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
