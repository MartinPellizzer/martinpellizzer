Helicteres guazumifolia var. gardneriana

Helicteres guazumifolia var. gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's false pomegranate, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a spherical, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and floral display.
