Hieracium varangerense

Hieracium varangerense, commonly known as Varanger's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Native to northern Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grassy habitats. The plant has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
