Homalomena limnogena

Homalomena limnogena, commonly known as the water arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive waxy surface. Its flowering stems emerge from the water, bearing small, greenish-white flowers that attract pollinators.

Homalomena limnogena plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water life.
