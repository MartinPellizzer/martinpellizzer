Hieracium anglicum

Hieracium anglicum, commonly known as the English hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, including the British Isles.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

The showy yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
