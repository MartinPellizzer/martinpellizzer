Hackelia obtusifolia

Hackelia obtusifolia, commonly known as bluntleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems that often grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a blunt or rounded tip, giving it its common name.

Hackelia obtusifolia is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
