Hieracium caesium subsp. villiceps

Hieracium caesium subsp.

villiceps, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months. Hieracium caesium subsp.

villiceps is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
