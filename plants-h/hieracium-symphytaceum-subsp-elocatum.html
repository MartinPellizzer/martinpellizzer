Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. elocatum

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

elocatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
