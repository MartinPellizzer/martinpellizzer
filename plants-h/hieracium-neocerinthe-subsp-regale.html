Hieracium neocerinthe subsp. regale

Hieracium neocerinthe subsp.

regale, commonly known as the royal hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, daisy-like cluster.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
