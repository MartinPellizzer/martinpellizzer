Hypericum tetrapterum subsp. corsicum

Hypericum tetrapterum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include small, elliptical leaves and a distinctive, branched inflorescence.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates.
