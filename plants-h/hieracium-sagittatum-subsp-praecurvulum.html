Hieracium sagittatum subsp. praecurvulum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

praecurvulum, commonly known as the bent-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive downward curvature, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer.
