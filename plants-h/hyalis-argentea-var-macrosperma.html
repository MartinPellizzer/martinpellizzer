Hyalis argentea var. macrosperma

Hyalis argentea var. macrosperma, commonly known as silverleaf hyalis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
