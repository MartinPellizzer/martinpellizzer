Hemionitis concolor

Hemionitis concolor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in small, bladder-like structures. These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, which the plant then digests for nutrients. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptations make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
