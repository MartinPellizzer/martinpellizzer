Hyphaene hildebrandtii

Hyphaene hildebrandtii, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a species native to eastern Africa.

It is a tall, slender palm with a slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional thatching and basket weaving, highlighting its cultural significance.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
