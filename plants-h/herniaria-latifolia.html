Herniaria latifolia

Herniaria latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Herniaria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
