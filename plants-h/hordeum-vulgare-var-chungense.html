Hordeum vulgare var. chungense

Hordeum vulgare var. chungense, commonly known as Chinese barley, is a variant of barley native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and small, hard grains that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types. Its growth habit includes a short stature with dense foliage, making it suitable for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.

The botanical features of this variety contribute to its resilience and utility in traditional and modern farming practices.
