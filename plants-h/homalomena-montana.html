Homalomena montana

Homalomena montana, commonly known as the mountain caladium, is a tropical evergreen herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant features thick, fleshy rhizomes that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of white, pink, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Homalomena montana is typically propagated through rhizome division and prefers well-drained soil with high humidity.
