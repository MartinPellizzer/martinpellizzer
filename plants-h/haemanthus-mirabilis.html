Haemanthus mirabilis

Haemanthus mirabilis, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces dense clusters of bright red flowers that resemble stars, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and seasonal display.
