Hyptis alata subsp. rugosula

Hyptis alata subsp.

rugosula, commonly known as roughleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its aromatic leaves and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
