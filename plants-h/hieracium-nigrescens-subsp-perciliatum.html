Hieracium nigrescens subsp. perciliatum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

perciliatum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are bright yellow with purple markings.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
