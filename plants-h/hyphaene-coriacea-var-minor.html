Hyphaene coriacea var. minor

Hyphaene coriacea var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf palm or small date palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered leaves. The plant features a short, stout trunk covered in fibrous, brownish bark. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow segments that give it a feathery appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
