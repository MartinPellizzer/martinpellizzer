Hibiscus peralbus

Hibiscus peralbus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that contrast with its vibrant yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Hibiscus peralbus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
