Haworthia triebneriana var. pulchra

Haworthia triebneriana var. pulchra, commonly known as the "Cape Racer," is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically pale green with distinct white or yellowish markings. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. It produces tall, spiky inflorescences with small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
