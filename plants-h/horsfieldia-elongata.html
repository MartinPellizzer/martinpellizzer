Horsfieldia elongata

Horsfieldia elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Horsfieldia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
