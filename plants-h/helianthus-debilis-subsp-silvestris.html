Helianthus debilis subsp. silvestris

Helianthus debilis subsp.

silvestris, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils.

The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive green center, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
