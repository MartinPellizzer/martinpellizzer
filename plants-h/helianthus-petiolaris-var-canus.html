Helianthus petiolaris var. canus

Helianthus petiolaris var. canus, commonly known as the whiteleaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
