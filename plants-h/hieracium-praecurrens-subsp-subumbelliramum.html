Hieracium praecurrens subsp. subumbelliramum

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

subumbelliramum, commonly known as the umbelliform hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its flowers are arranged in a compact, umbrella-like shape, giving it its common name.

It is often found in moist or calcareous soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
