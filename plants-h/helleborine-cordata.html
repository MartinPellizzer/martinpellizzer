Helleborine cordata

Helleborine cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded lip, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
