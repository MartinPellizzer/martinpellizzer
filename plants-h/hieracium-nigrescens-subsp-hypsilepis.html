Hieracium nigrescens subsp. hypsilepis

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

hypsilepis, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring rocky or open, well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.
