Hemigraphis chamaedrys

Hemigraphis chamaedrys, commonly known as the silver mohair plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, velvety foliage with leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hemigraphis chamaedrys is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to form dense, compact mats.
