Hermione pallida

Hermione pallida, commonly known as the ghostly lily, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent petals that give it a ghostly appearance, especially in low light. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the early morning hours. Its leaves are narrow and slightly waxy, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid environment.

Hermione pallida is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
