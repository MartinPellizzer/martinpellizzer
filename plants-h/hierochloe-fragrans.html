Hierochloe fragrans

Hierochloe fragrans, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky leaves that emit a sweet, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and cultural significance.
