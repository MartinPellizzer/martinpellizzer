Haeupleria cavanillesii

Haeupleria cavanillesii, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of conifer native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and rocky conditions of its native habitat.

Haeupleria cavanillesii is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
