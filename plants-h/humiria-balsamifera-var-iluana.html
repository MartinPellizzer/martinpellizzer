Humiria balsamifera var. iluana

Humiria balsamifera var. iluana, commonly known as the Iluana balsam tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, balsamic resin, which is exuded from its bark and leaves. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
