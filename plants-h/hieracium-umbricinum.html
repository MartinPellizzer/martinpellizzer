Hieracium umbricinum

Hieracium umbricinum, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are yellow with a dark central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Hieracium umbricinum is often found in areas with rich, humus-filled soil.
