Hemizonia paniculata

Hemizonia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it its common name. It is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Hemizonia paniculata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
