Hieracium lachenalii subsp. farinosiceps

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

farinosiceps, commonly known as the farinosic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, which are surrounded by pale green or white involucral bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its subsp.

farinosiceps is distinguished by the presence of a dense, powdery coating on its leaves and stems, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
