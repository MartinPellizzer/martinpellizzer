Hyphaene kilvaensis

Hyphaene kilvaensis, commonly known as the Socotra dragon blood tree, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces a red sap, known as dragon's blood, which has been used historically for medicinal and ritual purposes. Its structure is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat, with a deep root system and a slow growth rate.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its environment, providing shelter and food for various local species.
