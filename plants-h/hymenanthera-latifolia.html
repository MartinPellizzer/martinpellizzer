Hymenanthera latifolia

Hymenanthera latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hymenanthera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Hymenanthera latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
