Hieracium stibergense

Hieracium stibergense, commonly known as the Stiberg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers that bloom from June to August.

Hieracium stibergense is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
