Hopea siamensis

Hopea siamensis, commonly known as the Siam hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
