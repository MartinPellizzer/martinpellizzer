Hieracium lithogenes

Hieracium lithogenes, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in crevices and on rocky outcrops, where it thrives in poor, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium lithogenes is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
