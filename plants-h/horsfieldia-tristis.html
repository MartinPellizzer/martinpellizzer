Horsfieldia tristis

Horsfieldia tristis, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish-purple petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Horsfieldia tristis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
