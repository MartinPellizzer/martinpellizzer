Helichrysum stenocladum

Helichrysum stenocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small sunflowers, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its scientific name reflects its compact foliage and the enduring quality of its flowers.
