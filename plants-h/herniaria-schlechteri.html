Herniaria schlechteri

Herniaria schlechteri, commonly known as the rock cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices and on rock surfaces.

Its unique morphology and ability to survive in harsh environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
