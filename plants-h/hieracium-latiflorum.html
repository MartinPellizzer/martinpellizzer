Hieracium latiflorum

Hieracium latiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium latiflorum typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
