Helianthus annuus var. latibasis

Helianthus annuus var. latibasis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves.

It is native to the central and southern United States, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces large, yellow flower heads with a central disk of dark brown or black florets. It is often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
