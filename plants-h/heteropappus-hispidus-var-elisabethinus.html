Heteropappus hispidus var. elisabethinus

Heteropappus hispidus var. elisabethinus, commonly known as the Elizabeth's hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with a mat-forming structure.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
