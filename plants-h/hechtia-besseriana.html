Hechtia besseriana

Hechtia besseriana, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a rosette-forming perennial with thick, fleshy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. The plant produces showy, tubular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to its native arid environments.
