Hieracium cuspididens

Hieracium cuspididens, commonly known as the spear-tipped hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium cuspididens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
