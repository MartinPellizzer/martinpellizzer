Halimium occidentale var. rugosum

Halimium occidentale var. rugosum, commonly known as the rough hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
