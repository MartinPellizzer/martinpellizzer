Hieracium praecox subsp. divertens

Hieracium praecox subsp.

divertens, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly oval, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks across temperate regions of Europe. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
