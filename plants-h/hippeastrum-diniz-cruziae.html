Hippeastrum diniz-cruziae

Hippeastrum diniz-cruziae, commonly known as the Diniz-Cruzia amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the blooms, which typically appear in the summer months. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in warm, humid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
