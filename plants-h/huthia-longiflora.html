Huthia longiflora

Huthia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered huthia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Huthia longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
