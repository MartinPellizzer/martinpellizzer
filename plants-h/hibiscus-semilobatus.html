Hibiscus semilobatus

Hibiscus semilobatus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. Hibiscus semilobatus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
