Helianthus crassifolius

Helianthus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with robust, dark green leaves that are thick and leathery. The plant produces large, showy yellow flower heads that are surrounded by green bracts. Its flowers typically bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus crassifolius is well adapted to moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
