Hieracium pannonicum subsp. polytilum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

polytilum, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a flowering stem with several flower heads.

The subspecies polytilum is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and more compact growth habit compared to other subspecies of Hieracium pannonicum.
