Hydrangea vulgaris var. carnea

Hydrangea vulgaris var. carnea, commonly known as the pink lace hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range from pink to red, depending on soil pH. The plant produces broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to change flower color with seasonal conditions.
