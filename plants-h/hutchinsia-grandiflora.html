Hutchinsia grandiflora

Hutchinsia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered hutchinsia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hutchinsia grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
