Hyperocarpa

Hyperocarpa, commonly known as the "cup-shaped fruit plant," is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its unique, cup-like fruit that encloses its seeds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

The fruit develops from a capsule and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
