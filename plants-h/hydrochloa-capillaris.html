Hydrochloa capillaris

Hydrochloa capillaris, commonly known as the slender water moss, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It belongs to the family Hydrochloaceae and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving or still water, such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures are simple, with male and female gametes produced on separate plants.

Hydrochloa capillaris plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
