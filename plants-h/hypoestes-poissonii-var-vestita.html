Hypoestes poissonii var. vestita

Hypoestes poissonii var. vestita, commonly known as the "Snowflake" or "Cape Myrtle," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that surround small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
