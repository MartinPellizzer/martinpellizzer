Heuchera parviflora var. parviflora

Heuchera parviflora var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf coral bell, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, mounding foliage with variegated leaves that range from silver to red and green. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in both its foliage and delicate floral display.
