Helichrysum nimmoanum

Helichrysum nimmoanum, commonly known as the Cape goldflax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, ray-like flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum nimmoanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
