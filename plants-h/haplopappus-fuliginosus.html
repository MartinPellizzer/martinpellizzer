Haplopappus fuliginosus

Haplopappus fuliginosus, commonly known as the sooty pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its fruit is a composite structure composed of numerous small, hairless achenes.

Haplopappus fuliginosus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
