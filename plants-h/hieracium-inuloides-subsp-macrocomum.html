Hieracium inuloides subsp. macrocomum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

macrocomum, commonly known as the large-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
