Hyptis anomala

Hyptis anomala, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant also attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its nectar-rich flowers.
