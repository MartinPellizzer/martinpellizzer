Hieracium brezianum

Hieracium brezianum, commonly known as the Brezian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat, disc-like structure. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with hairy surfaces. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium brezianum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
