Hirschfeldia varia

Hirschfeldia varia, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces small, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hirschfeldia varia is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed areas like roadsides and sandy soils.
