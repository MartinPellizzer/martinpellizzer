Hydrostachys laciniata

Hydrostachys laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterwheel, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Africa.

It features finely dissected, grass-like leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the substrate. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a wheel, hence its common name. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands.

Hydrostachys laciniata plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
