Heliconia obscura

Heliconia obscura, commonly known as the dark heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that enclose its small, clustered flowers. The plant's inflorescence is pendulous, with vibrant red and orange hues that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture that helps it thrive in humid environments.

Heliconia obscura is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
