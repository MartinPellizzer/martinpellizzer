Hieracium canadense var. latifolium

Hieracium canadense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf Canada hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in disturbed areas and can form dense colonies, making it an invasive species in some regions.
