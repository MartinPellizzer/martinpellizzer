Hieracium boreale subsp. dumosum

Hieracium boreale subsp.

dumosum, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a central stem that bears yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are usually lobed or toothed, and the flowers are arranged in a single terminal head.

This subspecies is known for its aggressive growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
