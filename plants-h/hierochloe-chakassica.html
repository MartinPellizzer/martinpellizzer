Hierochloe chakassica

Hierochloe chakassica, commonly known as Chakassian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like clusters.

Hierochloe chakassica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect and bird species.
