Hieracium lamprochlorum

Hieracium lamprochlorum, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
