Hyoscyamus turcomanicus

Hyoscyamus turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkmenian henbane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, globose berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is handled with caution and is not commonly cultivated.
