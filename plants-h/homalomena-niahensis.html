Homalomena niahensis

Homalomena niahensis, commonly known as the Niah swamp arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the lowland rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments, often near water bodies, and has adapted to thrive in swampy conditions. Its leaves are broad and arrowhead-shaped, which is a common feature among species in the Homalomena genus.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its applications.
