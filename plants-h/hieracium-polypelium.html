Hieracium polypelium

Hieracium polypelium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium polypelium is often found in open meadows and scree slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
