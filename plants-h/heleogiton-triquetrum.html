Heleogiton triquetrum

Heleogiton triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic insects. The plant has a slender, branching stem with whorled leaves that are typically three-angled in cross-section. It thrives in still or slow-moving water, often in nutrient-poor environments.

Its unique trapping mechanism allows it to supplement its nutrient intake in aquatic ecosystems.
