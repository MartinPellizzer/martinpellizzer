Hierochloe redolens var. utriculata

Hierochloe redolens var. utriculata, commonly known as the fragrant hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, tufted growth habit and fine, silky leaves that emit a subtle, pleasant aroma. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological restoration projects and a subject of interest in botanical research.
