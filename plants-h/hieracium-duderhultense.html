Hieracium duderhultense

Hieracium duderhultense, commonly known as the Duderhult's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium duderhultense is known for its resilience in harsh environments and is often found in meadows and grasslands at high elevations.
