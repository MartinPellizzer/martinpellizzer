Hierochloe kamtschatica

Hierochloe kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subarctic regions, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant has slender, tufted culms with fine, hairy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in cool climate gardens.
