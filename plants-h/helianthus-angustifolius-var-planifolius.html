Helianthus angustifolius var. planifolius

Helianthus angustifolius var. planifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower.

This variant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall.

It is often found in open woods and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
