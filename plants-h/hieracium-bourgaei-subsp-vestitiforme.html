Hieracium bourgaei subsp. vestitiforme

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

vestitiforme, commonly known as the velvetleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
