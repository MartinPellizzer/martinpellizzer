Hieracium mougeotii f. falcidens

Hieracium mougeotii f. falcidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disk. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
