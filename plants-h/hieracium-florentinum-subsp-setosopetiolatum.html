Hieracium florentinum subsp. setosopetiolatum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

setosopetiolatum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
