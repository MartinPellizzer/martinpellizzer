Hieracium murorum subsp. scioides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

scioides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open habitats across temperate regions.
