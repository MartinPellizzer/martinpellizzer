Hieracium frigidellum

Hieracium frigidellum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium frigidellum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
