Hippeastrum organense

Hippeastrum organense, commonly known as the Organ Mountains amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It blooms in the spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bulb is perennial, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
