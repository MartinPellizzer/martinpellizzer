Hemistachyum brooksii

Hemistachyum brooksii, commonly known as the Brooks' hemistachyum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in late spring. Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Hemistachyum brooksii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
