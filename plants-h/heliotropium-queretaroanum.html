Heliotropium queretaroanum

Heliotropium queretaroanum, commonly known as the Mexican heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a rough or hairy texture.

Heliotropium queretaroanum is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
