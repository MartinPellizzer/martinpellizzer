Hypolytrum jardimii

Hypolytrum jardimii, commonly known as the Jardimii flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
