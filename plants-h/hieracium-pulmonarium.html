Hieracium pulmonarium

Hieracium pulmonarium, commonly known as lungwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its common name derives from the leaf patterns that resemble the human lung.

Hieracium pulmonarium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported respiratory benefits.
