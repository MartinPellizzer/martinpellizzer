Hoarea tenuifolia

Hoarea tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf hoarea, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
