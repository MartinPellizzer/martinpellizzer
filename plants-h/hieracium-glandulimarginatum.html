Hieracium glandulimarginatum

Hieracium glandulimarginatum, commonly known as the glandular-margin hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features serrated leaves with glandular margins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
