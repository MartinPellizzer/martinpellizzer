Hieracium gymnocephalum subsp. anastrum

Hieracium gymnocephalum subsp.

anastrum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
