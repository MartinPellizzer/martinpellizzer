Hypolepis obtusata

Hypolepis obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Hypolepidaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically broad and lance-shaped. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in distinct groups called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hypolepis obtusata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
