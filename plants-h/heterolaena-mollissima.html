Heterolaena mollissima

Heterolaena mollissima, commonly known as silky heterolaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
