Haloragis serra

Haloragis serra, commonly known as the serrated willow herb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Haloragis serra is adapted to wet environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
