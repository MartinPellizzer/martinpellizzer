Hemizonia vernalis

Hemizonia vernalis, commonly known as the spring honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife.

Hemizonia vernalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
