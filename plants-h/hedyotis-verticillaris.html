Hedyotis verticillaris

Hedyotis verticillaris, commonly known as the small redroot or redroot strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually red or orange in color. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedyotis verticillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
