Hieracium gyndelense

Hieracium gyndelense, commonly known as the Gyndelense hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
