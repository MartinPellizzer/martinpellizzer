Hordeum chilense f. elongatum

Hordeum chilense f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated Chile barley, is a botanical variant of the species Hordeum chilense, native to South America.

This form is characterized by its elongated spikelets and longer awns compared to the typical form. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for adaptation and genetic diversity.
