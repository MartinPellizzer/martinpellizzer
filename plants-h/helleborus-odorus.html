Helleborus odorus

Helleborus odorus, commonly known as the sweet-smelling hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and nodding, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet fragrance, which is more pronounced in the evening. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Helleborus odorus is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used for its purported healing benefits.
