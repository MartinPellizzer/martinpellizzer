Hieracium aseralense

Hieracium aseralense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium aseralense is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra.
