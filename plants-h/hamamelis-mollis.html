Hamamelis mollis

Hamamelis mollis, commonly known as the fragrant witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Hamamelis mollis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its unique flowering period.
