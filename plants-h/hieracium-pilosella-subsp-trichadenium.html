Hieracium pilosella subsp. trichadenium

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

trichadenium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal head, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
