Heuchera cylindrica var. suksdorfii

Heuchera cylindrica var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in early summer. The foliage is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to add color to shaded landscapes.
