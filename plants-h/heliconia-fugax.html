Heliconia fugax

Heliconia fugax, commonly known as the fugitive heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by long, strap-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by birds and insects.

Heliconia fugax is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
