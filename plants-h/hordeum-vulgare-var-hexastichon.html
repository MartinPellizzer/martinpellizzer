Hordeum vulgare var. hexastichon

Hordeum vulgare var. hexastichon, commonly known as six-row barley, is a cultivated variety of barley characterized by its six rows of grains along the stem.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. This variety is valued for its high grain yield and is widely used in brewing and food production. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its seeds are enclosed in a hull, which is often removed during processing for various applications.
