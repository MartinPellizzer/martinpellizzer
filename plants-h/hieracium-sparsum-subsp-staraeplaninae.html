Hieracium sparsum subsp. staraeplaninae

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

staraeplaninae, commonly known as the starraeplaninae hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head, which are often surrounded by pale green or white involucral bracts. It is characterized by its finely divided, hairy leaves and is adapted to withstand harsh, alpine conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
