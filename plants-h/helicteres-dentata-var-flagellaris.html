Helicteres dentata var. flagellaris

Helicteres dentata var. flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, finger-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, which are often attractive to pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
