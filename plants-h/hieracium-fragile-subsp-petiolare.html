Hieracium fragile subsp. petiolare

Hieracium fragile subsp.

petiolare, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
