Heckeldora zenkeri

Heckeldora zenkeri, commonly known as the Zambesi orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and strikingly colorful, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique beauty.
