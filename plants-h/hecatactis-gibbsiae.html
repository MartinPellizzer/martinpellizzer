Hecatactis gibbsiae

Hecatactis gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' ghost orchid, is a rare and endangered flowering plant native to the wetlands of Florida, USA.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dense, moist habitats and relies on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake. Its delicate structure and limited distribution make it highly sensitive to environmental changes.
