Habenaria blumei

Habenaria blumei, commonly known as the Blume's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces linear, grass-like leaves. The plant's inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red.

Habenaria blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
