Hyptis pachyphylla

Hyptis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, thick leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis pachyphylla is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
