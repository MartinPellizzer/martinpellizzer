Hieracium oxyodontopsis

Hieracium oxyodontopsis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
