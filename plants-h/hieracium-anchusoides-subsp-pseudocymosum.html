Hieracium anchusoides subsp. pseudocymosum

Hieracium anchusoides subsp.

pseudocymosum, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
