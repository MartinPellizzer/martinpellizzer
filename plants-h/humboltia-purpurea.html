Humboltia purpurea

Humboltia purpurea, commonly known as the purple hummingbird vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Humboltia purpurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
