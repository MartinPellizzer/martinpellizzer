Homogyne alpina subsp. alpina

Homogyne alpina subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine homogyne, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late summer.
