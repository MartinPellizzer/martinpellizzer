Hieracium boreale

Hieracium boreale, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium boreale is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
