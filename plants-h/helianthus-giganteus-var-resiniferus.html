Helianthus giganteus var. resiniferus

Helianthus giganteus var. resiniferus, commonly known as the resinous giant sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers and sturdy, upright stems. It produces a unique resinous substance in its stems, which has been historically used by Native American tribes for various purposes. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
