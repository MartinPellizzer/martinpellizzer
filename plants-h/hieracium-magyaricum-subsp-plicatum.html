Hieracium magyaricum subsp. plicatum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

plicatum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces and serrated edges. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, with tubular florets surrounded by ray florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive plicate (folded) leaves and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
