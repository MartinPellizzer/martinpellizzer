Hemionitis brachypus

Hemionitis brachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hemionitis, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the tips. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

Hemionitis brachypus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
