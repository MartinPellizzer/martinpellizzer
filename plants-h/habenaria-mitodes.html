Habenaria mitodes

Habenaria mitodes, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetlands and marshy areas in Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy violet or purple flower that has a distinctive, intricate pattern on its lip. The plant has a well-developed root system and often grows in dense colonies, forming a mat-like structure. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral characteristics.
