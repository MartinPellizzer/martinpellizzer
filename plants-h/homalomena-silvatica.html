Homalomena silvatica

Homalomena silvatica, commonly known as the mountain arrowroot, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Homalomena silvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
