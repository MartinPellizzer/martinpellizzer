Hieracium caesium subsp. validipolum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

validipolum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy habitats and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
