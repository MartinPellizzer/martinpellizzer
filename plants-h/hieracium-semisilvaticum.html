Hieracium semisilvaticum

Hieracium semisilvaticum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers consist of ray florets surrounded by disk florets.

Hieracium semisilvaticum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
