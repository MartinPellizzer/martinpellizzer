Hieracium tyrvenutense

Hieracium tyrvenutense, commonly known as the Tyrrhenian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium tyrvenutense is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
