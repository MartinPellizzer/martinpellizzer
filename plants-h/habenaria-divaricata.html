Habenaria divaricata

Habenaria divaricata, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white to yellowish flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Habenaria divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
