Hieracium consanguineum

Hieracium consanguineum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Hieracium consanguineum is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is known for its ability to colonize a variety of habitats.
