Hibiscus vitifolius f. americana

Hibiscus vitifolius f. americana, commonly known as the American hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus vitifolius f. americana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
