Hydrangea glabrifolia

Hydrangea glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hydrangea glabrifolia is also valued for its showy blooms and ability to provide seasonal interest in the garden.
