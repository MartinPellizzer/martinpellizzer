Hoffmannseggia doellii var. minor

Hoffmannseggia doellii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Hoffmannseggia, is a small, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. It is well adapted to survive in hot, dry conditions with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
