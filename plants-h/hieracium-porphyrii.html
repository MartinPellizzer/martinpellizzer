Hieracium porphyrii

Hieracium porphyrii, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow to orange flower heads surrounded by narrow, greenish-brown bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Hieracium porphyrii is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
