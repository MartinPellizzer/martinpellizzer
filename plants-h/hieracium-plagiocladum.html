Hieracium plagiocladum

Hieracium plagiocladum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attracts various pollinators.

Hieracium plagiocladum is known for its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
