Helichrysum teydeum

Helichrysum teydeum, commonly known as the Tenerife everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small, yellowish dandelions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum teydeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
