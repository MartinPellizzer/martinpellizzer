Helicteres ferruginata

Helicteres ferruginata, commonly known as the ironwood or red-rooted false holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive reddish-brown stems. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by reddish-brown, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Helicteres ferruginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
