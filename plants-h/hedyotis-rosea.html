Hedyotis rosea

Hedyotis rosea, commonly known as the rose moss or red moss, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It features dense clusters of tiny, bright red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Hedyotis rosea is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
