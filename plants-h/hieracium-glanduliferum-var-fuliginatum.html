Hieracium glanduliferum var. fuliginatum

Hieracium glanduliferum var. fuliginatum, commonly known as the sooty hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its common name.

The species is often found in moist environments and can form dense colonies.
