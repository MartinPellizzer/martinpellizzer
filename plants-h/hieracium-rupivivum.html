Hieracium rupivivum

Hieracium rupivivum, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hieracium rupivivum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, sunny environments.
