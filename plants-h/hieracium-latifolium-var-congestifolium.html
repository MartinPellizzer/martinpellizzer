Hieracium latifolium var. congestifolium

Hieracium latifolium var. congestifolium, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, often blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
