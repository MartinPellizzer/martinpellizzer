Heliotropium leavenworthii

Heliotropium leavenworthii, commonly known as the desert heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a characteristic plant of desert ecosystems.
