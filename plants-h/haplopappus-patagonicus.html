Haplopappus patagonicus

Haplopappus patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian pappus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Haplopappus patagonicus is often found in rocky soils and is a component of the native flora in Patagonia.
