Hepatica nobilis f. plena

Hepatica nobilis f. plena, commonly known as the noble hepatica, is a striking early spring perennial native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple, with five to seven petals, and bloom before the leaves fully expand. This plant thrives in woodland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shady garden landscapes.
