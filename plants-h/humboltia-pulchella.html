Humboltia pulchella

Humboltia pulchella, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Humboltia pulchella is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
