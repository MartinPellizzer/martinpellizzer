Hemipilia camptoceras

Hemipilia camptoceras, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped flowers, which are a striking feature of the plant. The plant typically grows in forested areas with high humidity and partial shade. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Hemipilia camptoceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
