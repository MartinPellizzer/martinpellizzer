Hemibromus

Hemibromus, commonly known as the dwarf bromus, is a genus of grasses native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plants typically thrive in temperate climates and are often found in open fields or along roadsides. Hemibromus species are known for their ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.

These grasses are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value due to their attractive, fine-textured foliage.
