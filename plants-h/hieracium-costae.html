Hieracium costae

Hieracium costae, commonly known as the ribbed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
