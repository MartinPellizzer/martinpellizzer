Helicteres macrophylla

Helicteres macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved helicteres, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several seeds.

Helicteres macrophylla is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
