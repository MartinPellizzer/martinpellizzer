Hedyotis protrusa

Hedyotis protrusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, ascending stems with small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white to pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom from June to September.

Hedyotis protrusa is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
