Heliophila chamira

Heliophila chamira, commonly known as the golden thread, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are linear and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Heliophila chamira is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
