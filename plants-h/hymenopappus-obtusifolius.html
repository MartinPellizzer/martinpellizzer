Hymenopappus obtusifolius

Hymenopappus obtusifolius, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features gray-green, oblanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a single head, with prominent pappus hairs that aid in seed dispersal.

Hymenopappus obtusifolius is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
