Huperzia lancifolia

Huperzia lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky habitats. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Huperzia lancifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
