Hydrolea spinosa f. trigyna

Hydrolea spinosa f. trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed hydrolea, is a unique variant of the Hydrolea genus native to southern Africa.

This succulent plant features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The leaves are distinctly three-lobed, giving the plant its distinguishing appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in arid or semi-arid environments with well-draining soil.
