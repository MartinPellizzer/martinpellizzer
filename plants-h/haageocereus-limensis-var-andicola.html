Haageocereus limensis var. andicola

Haageocereus limensis var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean moon cactus, is a cactus species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright, colorful flowers that bloom in the evening, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem and produces spines that help protect it from herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
