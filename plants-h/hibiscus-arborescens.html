Hibiscus arborescens

Hibiscus arborescens, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and deep purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Hibiscus arborescens is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
