Hieracium panjutinii

Hieracium panjutinii, commonly known as the Panjutin's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are erect with rough, hairy surfaces.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
