Hancornia speciosa var. minor

Hancornia speciosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
