Hepetis odontopoda

Hepetis odontopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically valuable in wetland environments.
