Hedysarum pavlovii

Hedysarum pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
