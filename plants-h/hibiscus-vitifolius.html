Hibiscus vitifolius

Hibiscus vitifolius, commonly known as the grape-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble grape leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Hibiscus vitifolius thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various soil conditions contribute to its adaptability in different environments.
