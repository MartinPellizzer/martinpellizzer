Hieracium smolandicum subsp. schistostegum

Hieracium smolandicum subsp.

schistostegum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or alpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces a long, slender stem that supports its flowering heads.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in local ecosystems.
