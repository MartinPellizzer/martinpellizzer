Hieracium caesioides subsp. lobophorum

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

lobophorum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its flowering heads that consist of both ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers range in color from blue to purple.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
