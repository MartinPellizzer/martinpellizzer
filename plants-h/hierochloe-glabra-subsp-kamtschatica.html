Hierochloe glabra subsp. kamtschatica

Hierochloe glabra subsp.

kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamchatka hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold regions of eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic environments.

The plant has slender, upright stems with fine, hair-like leaves that are glabrous, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
