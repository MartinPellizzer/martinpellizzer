Hieracium vulgatum subsp. prenzlaviense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

prenzlaviense, commonly known as the Prenzlau hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem with yellow disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in glandular dots, which contribute to its distinct appearance.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and its role in soil stabilization.
