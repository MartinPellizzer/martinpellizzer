Hakea heterophylla

Hakea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which varies in shape from narrow and linear to broad and leathery depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

Hakea heterophylla is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
