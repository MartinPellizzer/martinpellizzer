Hieracium sudeticum subsp. pedunculare

Hieracium sudeticum subsp.

pedunculare, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange-yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically features a single stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish hue. It produces small, disc-shaped flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which are surrounded by several rows of bracts.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
