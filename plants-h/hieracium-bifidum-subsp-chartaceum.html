Hieracium bifidum subsp. chartaceum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

chartaceum, commonly known as the paperleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its papery, translucent leaves that give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.
