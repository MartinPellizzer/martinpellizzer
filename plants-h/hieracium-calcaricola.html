Hieracium calcaricola

Hieracium calcaricola, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium calcaricola is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
