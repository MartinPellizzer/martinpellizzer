Helianthus microcephalus

Helianthus microcephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the central and eastern regions of North America, where it thrives in prairies and open woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus microcephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
