Homalobus wingatanus

Homalobus wingatanus, commonly known as the wingatanus cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with the male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
