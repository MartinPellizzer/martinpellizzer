Hieracium macilentum subsp. sparsipilum

Hieracium macilentum subsp.

sparsipilum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies sparsipilum is distinguished by its sparse hair coverage and slender stems.
