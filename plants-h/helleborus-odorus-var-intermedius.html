Helleborus odorus var. intermedius

Helleborus odorus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, leathery basal leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or pale veins. The plant produces elegant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
