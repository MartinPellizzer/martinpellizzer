Hypericum woodsonii

Hypericum woodsonii, commonly known as the Woodson's St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
