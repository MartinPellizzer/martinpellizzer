Hieracium hypochoeroides

Hieracium hypochoeroides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered invasive in some regions.
