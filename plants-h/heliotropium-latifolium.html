Heliotropium latifolium

Heliotropium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or disturbed areas.

Heliotropium latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
