Hyptis trachychroa

Hyptis trachychroa, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis trachychroa is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
