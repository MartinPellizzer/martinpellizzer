Heracleum sphondylium f. subaequale

Heracleum sphondylium f. subaequale, commonly known as the smooth cow parsley, is a variant of the common cow parsley.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from the typical form. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils across Europe.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
