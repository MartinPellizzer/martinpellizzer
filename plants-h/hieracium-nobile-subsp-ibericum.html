Hieracium nobile subsp. ibericum

Hieracium nobile subsp.

ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a cluster at the top of the stem. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering characteristics compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
