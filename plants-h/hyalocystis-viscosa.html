Hyalocystis viscosa

Hyalocystis viscosa, commonly known as the sticky moss, is a unique species of moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and the presence of sticky secretions on its leaves. The plant's structure includes small, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a waxy or viscous coating. Hyalocystis viscosa is typically found in temperate regions, growing on soil, rocks, or decaying wood.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on moss physiology and adaptation to humid habitats.
