Hieracium flagellare subsp. petunnikovii

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

petunnikovii, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has a low-growing habit with a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its restricted distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
