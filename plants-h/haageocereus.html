Haageocereus

Haageocereus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowers that open at night. The plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Their flowers are usually white or pale in color, with a strong fragrance to attract pollinators.

Haageocereus species are popular among cactus enthusiasts due to their striking appearance and unique blooming habits.
