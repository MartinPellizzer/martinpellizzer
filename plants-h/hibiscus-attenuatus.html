Hibiscus attenuatus

Hibiscus attenuatus, commonly known as the slender hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Hibiscus attenuatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its flowers bloom during the summer months and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
