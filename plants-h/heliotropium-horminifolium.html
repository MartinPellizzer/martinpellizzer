Heliotropium horminifolium

Heliotropium horminifolium, commonly known as the woolly heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, often grayish foliage and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
