Hieracium alpinum var. gracile

Hieracium alpinum var. gracile, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in mountainous areas where other plants struggle to grow.
