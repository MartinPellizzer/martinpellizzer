Hieracium sulphureum subsp. porciusianum

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

porciusianum, commonly known as the sulfur hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
