Helianthemum salicifolium var. brachycarpum

Helianthemum salicifolium var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden use in arid climates.
