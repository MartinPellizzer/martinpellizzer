Hieracium floribundum grex cochleatum

Hieracium floribundum grex cochleatum, commonly known as the Coiled Hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its common name refers to the coiled or twisted appearance of its flower heads.
