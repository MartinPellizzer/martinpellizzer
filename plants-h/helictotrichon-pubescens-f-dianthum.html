Helictotrichon pubescens f. dianthum

Helictotrichon pubescens f. dianthum, commonly known as the purple needlegrass, is a flowering plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is native to North America and thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a softly textured appearance.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
