Hibiscus caerulescens

Hibiscus caerulescens, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically five-petaled and showy. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Hibiscus caerulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
