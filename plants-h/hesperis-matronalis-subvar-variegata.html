Hesperis matronalis subvar. variegata

Hesperis matronalis subvar. variegata, commonly known as the variegated white sweet rocket, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
