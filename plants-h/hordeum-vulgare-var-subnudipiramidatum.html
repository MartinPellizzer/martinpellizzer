Hordeum vulgare var. subnudipiramidatum

Hordeum vulgare var. subnudipiramidatum, commonly known as the naked barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its reduced awn and hull structure.

This plant is typically found in temperate regions and is cultivated for its grains, which are used in various food and industrial applications. Its botanical features include slender, cylindrical spikelets and small, hard grains that are often used in malting processes. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on environmental conditions.
