Hosta longipes f. viridipes

Hosta longipes f. viridipes, commonly known as the green-leafed hosta, is a cultivar of the Hosta genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with a subtle variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is popular in gardens for its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
