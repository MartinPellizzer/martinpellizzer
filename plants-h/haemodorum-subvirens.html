Haemodorum subvirens

Haemodorum subvirens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hellbender, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to eastern Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
