Hieracium hepaticolor

Hieracium hepaticolor, commonly known as liver-colored hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like appearance. The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium hepaticolor is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
