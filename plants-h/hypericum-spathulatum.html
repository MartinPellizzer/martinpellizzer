Hypericum spathulatum

Hypericum spathulatum, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly toothed margin, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum spathulatum is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
