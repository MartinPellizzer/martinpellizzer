Hieracium villamaniniense

Hieracium villamaniniense, commonly known as the Villamanini hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
