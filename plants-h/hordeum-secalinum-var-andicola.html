Hordeum secalinum var. andicola

Hordeum secalinum var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean barley, is a wild relative of barley native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to extreme conditions.
