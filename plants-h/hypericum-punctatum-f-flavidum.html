Hypericum punctatum f. flavidum

Hypericum punctatum f. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its yellowish stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically green and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
