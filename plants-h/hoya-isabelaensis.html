Hoya isabelaensis

Hoya isabelaensis, commonly known as the Isabela waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Hoya isabelaensis is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
