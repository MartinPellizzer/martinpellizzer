Hieracium camptotrichum

Hieracium camptotrichum, commonly known as the bent-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium camptotrichum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
