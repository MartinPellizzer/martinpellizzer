Helianthus annuus var. niger

Helianthus annuus var. niger, commonly known as the black sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its dark, almost black petals.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant features a tall, sturdy stem with large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
