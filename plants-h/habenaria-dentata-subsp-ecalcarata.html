Habenaria dentata subsp. ecalcarata

Habenaria dentata subsp.

ecalcarata, commonly known as the toothed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wet, shaded habitats such as marshes and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive tooth-like spur. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

This subspecies is often found in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to seasonal flooding.
