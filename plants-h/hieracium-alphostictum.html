Hieracium alphostictum

Hieracium alphostictum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
