Hyptis densifolia f. hyssopifolia

Hyptis densifolia f. hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic properties and adaptability to various soil types.
