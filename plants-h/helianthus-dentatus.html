Helianthus dentatus

Helianthus dentatus, commonly known as the toothache flower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and toothed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its scientific name reflects its distinguishing features, with "dentatus" referring to the serrated edges of its leaves.

Helianthus dentatus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
