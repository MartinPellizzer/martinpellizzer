Hyalosema elephantinum

Hyalosema elephantinum, commonly known as the elephant's tusk or elephant's ear, is a striking plant with large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shady environments. The plant's foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.
