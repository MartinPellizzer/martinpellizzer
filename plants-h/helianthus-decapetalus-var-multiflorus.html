Helianthus decapetalus var. multiflorus

Helianthus decapetalus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the multibract sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
