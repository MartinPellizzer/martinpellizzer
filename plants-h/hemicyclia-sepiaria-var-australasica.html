Hemicyclia sepiaria var. australasica

Hemicyclia sepiaria var. australasica, commonly known as the Australian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a short, branching stem and produces small, spiky bracts that surround the flower heads. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.
