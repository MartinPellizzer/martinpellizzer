Helianthus × cinereus var. sullivantii

Helianthus × cinereus var. sullivantii, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. The plant produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
