Hibiscus lasiocarpos var. californicus

Hibiscus lasiocarpos var. californicus, commonly known as the California hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
