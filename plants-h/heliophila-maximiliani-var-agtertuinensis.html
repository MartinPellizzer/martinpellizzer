Heliophila maximiliani var. agtertuinensis

Heliophila maximiliani var. agtertuinensis, commonly known as the Agtertuin bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender stems with lance-shaped, bright green leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
