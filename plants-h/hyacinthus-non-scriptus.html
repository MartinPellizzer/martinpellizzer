Hyacinthus non-scriptus

Hyacinthus non-scriptus, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white during late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens and floral arrangements. Its bulbs are hardy and can be planted in well-drained soil to ensure successful growth.

The Dutch hyacinth is also popular for its ability to thrive in colder climates.
