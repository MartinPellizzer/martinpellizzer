Hedychium gardnerianum var. pallidum

Hedychium gardnerianum var. pallidum, commonly known as the white flame torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white flowers that resemble torches, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is known for its aromatic foliage and floral displays. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
