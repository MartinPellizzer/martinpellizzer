Hieracium albiduliforme

Hieracium albiduliforme, commonly known as the white-rayed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant features showy yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped.

Hieracium albiduliforme is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
