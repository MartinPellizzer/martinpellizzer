Hibbertia aubertii

Hibbertia aubertii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces fruit that is a woody capsule. Hibbertia aubertii is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and tableland regions.

It is a hardy shrub that thrives in full sun and is often used in native gardens.
