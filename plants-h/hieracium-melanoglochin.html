Hieracium melanoglochin

Hieracium melanoglochin, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium melanoglochin is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a potential weed in some regions.
