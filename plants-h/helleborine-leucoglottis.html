Helleborine leucoglottis

Helleborine leucoglottis, commonly known as the white-lipped helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is a perennial herb that produces flowers from late spring to early summer.

The orchid has a complex root system and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
