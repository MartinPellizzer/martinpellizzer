Helichrysum transmontanum

Helichrysum transmontanum, commonly known as the everlasting flower or golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, densely packed yellow or golden flower heads. The plant produces papery, dry flowers that remain intact for a long time, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
