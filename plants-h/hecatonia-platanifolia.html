Hecatonia platanifolia

Hecatonia platanifolia, commonly known as the plane-tree hecatonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble those of the plane tree. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and seasonal blooms, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and public parks.
