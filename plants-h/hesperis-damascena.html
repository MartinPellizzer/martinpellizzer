Hesperis damascena

Hesperis damascena, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis damascena is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
