Hippeastrum bagnoldii

Hippeastrum bagnoldii, commonly known as the Bagnold's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the vibrant blooms, which are often used in floral arrangements. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a dormant period followed by rapid growth and flowering.

Hippeastrum bagnoldii is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
