Hypericum orientale subsp. buschianum

Hypericum orientale subsp.

buschianum, commonly known as the Eastern St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia. It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and prominent orange stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has woody stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is closely related to other Hypericum species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
