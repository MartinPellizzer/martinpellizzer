Hasseanthus blochmaniae

Hasseanthus blochmaniae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, thick rhizome and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of shade.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
