Hypochaeris platylepis

Hypochaeris platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved false dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.
