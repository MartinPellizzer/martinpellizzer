Hieracium galbanifrons

Hieracium galbanifrons, commonly known as the galbanum hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
