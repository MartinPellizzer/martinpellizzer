Hardenbergia

Hardenbergia, commonly known as blue oat grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue or purple flower spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hardenbergia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
