Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lychnidifolium

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lychnidifolium, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to reddish flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are covered in fine hairs. This subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive foliage, which is more broadly shaped compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
