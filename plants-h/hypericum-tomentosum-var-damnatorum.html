Hypericum tomentosum var. damnatorum

Hypericum tomentosum var. damnatorum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
