It is a thalloid liverwort, meaning it has a flat, leaf-like structure rather than true leaves or roots. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Haplophyton cimicidum plays an important role in soil stabilization and as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
