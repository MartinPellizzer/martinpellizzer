Humiria fruticosa

Humiria fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Humiria fruticosa is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
