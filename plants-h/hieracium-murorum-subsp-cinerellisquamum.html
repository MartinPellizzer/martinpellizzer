Hieracium murorum subsp. cinerellisquamum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

cinerellisquamum, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
