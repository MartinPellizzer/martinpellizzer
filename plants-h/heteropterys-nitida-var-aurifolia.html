Heteropterys nitida var. aurifolia

Heteropterys nitida var. aurifolia, commonly known as the golden-leafed wax tree, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that display a striking golden-yellow coloration, especially under sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name 'aurifolia' refers to its distinctive golden foliage, which makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
