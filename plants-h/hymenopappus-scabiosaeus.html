Hymenopappus scabiosaeus

Hymenopappus scabiosaeus, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant has grey-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hymenopappus scabiosaeus is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
