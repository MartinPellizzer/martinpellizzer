Hieracium florentinum subsp. ingens

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

ingens, commonly known as the giant common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

The subspecies ingens is distinguished by its larger size and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
