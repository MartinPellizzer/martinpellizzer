Hesperostipa comata subsp. comata

Hesperostipa comata subsp.

comata, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that is often covered in fine hairs. Hesperostipa comata subsp.

comata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
