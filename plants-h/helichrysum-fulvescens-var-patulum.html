Helichrysum fulvescens var. patulum

Helichrysum fulvescens var. patulum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or woolly helichrysum, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces papery, disc-shaped flowers that persist on the plant even after flowering, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
