Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. szovitsii

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow disk flowers with ray florets.

This subspecies is native to Central Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It exhibits a compact growth habit and is typically short-lived, with a lifespan of up to five years.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
