Helichrysum sivasicum

Helichrysum sivasicum, commonly known as the Sivas hill goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in dense clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Helichrysum sivasicum is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in traditional medicine.
