Helichrysum argyreum

Helichrysum argyreum, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow to gold-colored flowers that resemble tiny sunbursts. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Helichrysum argyreum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
