Helichrysum oligocephalum

Helichrysum oligocephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed immortelle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that are often arranged in compact clusters. The plant produces bracts that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum oligocephalum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
