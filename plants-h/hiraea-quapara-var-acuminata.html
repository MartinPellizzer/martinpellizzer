Hiraea quapara var. acuminata

Hiraea quapara var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed hiraea, is a rare and specialized plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the cooler months.
