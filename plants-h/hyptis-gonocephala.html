Hyptis gonocephala

Hyptis gonocephala, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
