Hyptis suaveolens

Hyptis suaveolens, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
