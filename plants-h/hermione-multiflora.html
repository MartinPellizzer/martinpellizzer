Hermione multiflora

Hermione multiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and silvery-green foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a distinctive waxy texture.

Hermione multiflora blooms in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
