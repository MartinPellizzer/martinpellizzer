Hedysarum micropterum subsp. macranthum

Hedysarum micropterum subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered bitter vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, ascending stems with trifoliate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its pods are hairy and contain several elongated seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
