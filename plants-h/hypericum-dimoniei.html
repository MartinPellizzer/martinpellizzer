Hypericum dimoniei

Hypericum dimoniei, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum dimoniei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
