Hieracium crocatum subsp. condylodes

Hieracium crocatum subsp.

condylodes, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking purple to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to other members of the species.
