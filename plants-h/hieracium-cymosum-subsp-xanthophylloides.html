Hieracium cymosum subsp. xanthophylloides

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

xanthophylloides, commonly known as the yellow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. It has a rosette-like growth habit with basal leaves that are often hairy.

Its flowers are arranged in a cyme, giving it a distinctive appearance.
