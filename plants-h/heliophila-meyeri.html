Heliophila meyeri

Heliophila meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Heliophila meyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
