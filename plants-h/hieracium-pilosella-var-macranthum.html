Hieracium pilosella var. macranthum

Hieracium pilosella var. macranthum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant features basal and stem leaves that are typically hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
