Hieracium caesium subsp. pseudopallescens

Hieracium caesium subsp.

pseudopallescens, commonly known as the pale-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to pale-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and hairy.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
