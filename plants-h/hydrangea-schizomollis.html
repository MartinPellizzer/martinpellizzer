Hydrangea schizomollis

Hydrangea schizomollis, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and deep green color.

Hydrangea schizomollis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
