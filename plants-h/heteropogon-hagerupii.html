Heteropogon hagerupii

Heteropogon hagerupii, commonly known as the Hagerup's grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
