Haageocereus pacalaensis var. laredensis

Haageocereus pacalaensis var. laredensis, commonly known as the Laredo cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and botanical collections.
