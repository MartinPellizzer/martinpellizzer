Holoschoenus vulgaris var. romanus

Holoschoenus vulgaris var. romanus, commonly known as the Roman rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, ditches, and riverbanks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
