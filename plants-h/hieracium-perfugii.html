Hieracium perfugii

Hieracium perfugii, commonly known as the perfugii hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has finely divided leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

It is considered a rare species and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations.
