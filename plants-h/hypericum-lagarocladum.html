Hypericum lagarocladum

Hypericum lagarocladum, commonly known as the coastal St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

Hypericum lagarocladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
