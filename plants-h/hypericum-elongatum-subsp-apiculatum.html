Hypericum elongatum subsp. apiculatum

Hypericum elongatum subsp.

apiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used in herbal remedies.
