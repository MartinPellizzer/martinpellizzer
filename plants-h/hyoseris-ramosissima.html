Hyoseris ramosissima

Hyoseris ramosissima, commonly known as the slenderleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Hyoseris ramosissima is often found in the western United States and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
