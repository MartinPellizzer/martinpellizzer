Hedysarum lipskianum

Hedysarum lipskianum, commonly known as the Armenian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, and it has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Hedysarum lipskianum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Its ornamental value also makes it a candidate for cultivation in gardens and landscaping.
