Hakea tetrapleura

Hakea tetrapleura, commonly known as the four-ribbed hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Hakea tetrapleura is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
