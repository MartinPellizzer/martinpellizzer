Heuchera pubescens var. typica

Heuchera pubescens var. typica, commonly known as the velvetleaf alumroot, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that ranges in color from deep maroon to silver-green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera pubescens var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
