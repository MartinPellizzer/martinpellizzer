Helicteres hirsuta var. spicata

Helicteres hirsuta var. spicata, commonly known as the hairy helicteres, is a variant of the helicteres hirsuta species.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas, characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinct aroma.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
