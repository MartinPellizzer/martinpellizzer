Hieraciodes pusilla

Hieraciodes pusilla, commonly known as the small hieracium, is a diminutive flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
