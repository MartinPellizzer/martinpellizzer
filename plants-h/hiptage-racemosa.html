Hiptage racemosa

Hiptage racemosa, commonly known as the clustered hiptage, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear numerous inflorescences.

Hiptage racemosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
