Hieracium phlomoides subsp. mesatlanticum

Hieracium phlomoides subsp.

mesatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the northeastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shores and dunes, where it thrives in salty and windy conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
