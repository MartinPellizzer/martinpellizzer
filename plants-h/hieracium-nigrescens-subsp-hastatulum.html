Hieracium nigrescens subsp. hastatulum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

hastatulum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, slightly toothed margin. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and is often found in moist to moderately dry soils.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
