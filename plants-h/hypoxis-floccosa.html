Hypoxis floccosa

Hypoxis floccosa, commonly known as the white star lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Hypoxis, which includes over 100 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white, star-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Hypoxis floccosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
