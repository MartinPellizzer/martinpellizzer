Hyptis rhytidea

Hyptis rhytidea, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine pubescence, and its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
