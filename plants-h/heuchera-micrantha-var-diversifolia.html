Heuchera micrantha var. diversifolia

Heuchera micrantha var. diversifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking foliage with a silvery-blue hue and deep green veins, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, or as a ground cover.
