Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. balleyanum

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

balleyanum, commonly known as the Ballyan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at higher elevations. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are often hairy and deeply lobed.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow or orange disc florets surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.
