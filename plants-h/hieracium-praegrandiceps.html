Hieracium praegrandiceps

Hieracium praegrandiceps, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
