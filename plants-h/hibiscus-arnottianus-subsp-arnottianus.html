Hibiscus arnottianus subsp. arnottianus

Hibiscus arnottianus subsp.

arnottianus, commonly known as the Australian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a rough texture, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus arnottianus subsp.

arnottianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
