Horminum frutescens

Horminum frutescens, commonly known as the purple-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, woody stems that often produce multiple branches, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Horminum frutescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
