Hieracium murorum subsp. melanthes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

melanthes, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize various habitats.
