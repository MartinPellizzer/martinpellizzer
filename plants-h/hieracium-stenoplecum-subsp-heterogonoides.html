Hieracium stenoplecum subsp. heterogonoides

Hieracium stenoplecum subsp.

heterogonoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
