Hypericum yezoanum f. macrophyllum

Hypericum yezoanum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved St. John's wort, is a botanical variant of the species Hypericum yezoanum.

It is characterized by its notably larger leaves compared to the typical form, which can reach up to 5 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, typical of the Hypericaceae family, which bloom in summer. It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
