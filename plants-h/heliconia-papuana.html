Heliconia papuana

Heliconia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the forests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush, tropical aesthetic.

Heliconia papuana thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
