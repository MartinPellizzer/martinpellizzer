Helianthus annuus var. macrocarpus

Helianthus annuus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant sunflower, is a variety of sunflower characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads and robust, upright stems.

It is native to North America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its seeds, which are used in food and bird feed. The plant typically grows to heights of 3 to 6 feet, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and serrated. Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing abundant seeds that are encased in a tough, dark husk.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
