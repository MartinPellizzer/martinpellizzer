Hieracium erectifrons var. connudatum

Hieracium erectifrons var. connudatum, commonly known as the upright-fronded hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually hairy and robust. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and is often found in hybrid forms with other Hieracium species.
