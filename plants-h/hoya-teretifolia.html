Hoya teretifolia

Hoya teretifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval in shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Hoya teretifolia is a popular houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its attractive foliage.

It is also known for its slow growth and ease of care, making it a favored choice among indoor gardeners.
