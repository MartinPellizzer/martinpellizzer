Hieracium cadinense

Hieracium cadinense, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can thrive in a variety of soil types. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are usually arranged in a single head at the end of each stem.

Hieracium cadinense is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
