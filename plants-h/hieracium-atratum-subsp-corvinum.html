Hieracium atratum subsp. corvinum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

corvinum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are usually dark purple to black in color.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
