Hymenophyllum cincinnatum

Hymenophyllum cincinnatum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery or pale green hue due to the presence of trichomes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Hymenophyllum cincinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
