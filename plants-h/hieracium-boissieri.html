Hieracium boissieri

Hieracium boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium boissieri is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
