Homalomena kingii

Homalomena kingii, commonly known as the King's arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage in suitable conditions.
