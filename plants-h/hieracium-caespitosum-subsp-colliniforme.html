Hieracium caespitosum subsp. colliniforme

Hieracium caespitosum subsp.

colliniforme, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers in a clustered inflorescence. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated margin, while the stems are often reddish and branched.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
