Hieracium sparsum subsp. macrolepis

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

macrolepis, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are small and clustered.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
