Habenaria cruciata

Habenaria cruciata, commonly known as the cross-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, with distinctive cross-shaped markings on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
