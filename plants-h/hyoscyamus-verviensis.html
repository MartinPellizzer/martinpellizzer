Hyoscyamus verviensis

Hyoscyamus verviensis, commonly known as the African jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain toxic seeds, which can be harmful if ingested. Hyoscyamus verviensis is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in dry, well-drained soils.

Its chemical composition includes tropane alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal and toxic properties.
