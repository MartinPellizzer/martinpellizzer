Hieracium lepidiceps

Hieracium lepidiceps, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central purple area.

Hieracium lepidiceps is known for its ornamental value and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
