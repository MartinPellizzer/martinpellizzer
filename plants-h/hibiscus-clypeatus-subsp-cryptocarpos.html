Hibiscus clypeatus subsp. cryptocarpos

Hibiscus clypeatus subsp.

cryptocarpos, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture. Hibiscus clypeatus subsp.

cryptocarpos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
