Hemerocallis picta

Hemerocallis picta, commonly known as the painted daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.

Hemerocallis picta is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant appearance and hardy nature.
