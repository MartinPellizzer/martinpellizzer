Hibiscus luteiflorus

Hibiscus luteiflorus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Hibiscus luteiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
