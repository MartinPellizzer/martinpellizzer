Hydrangea aspera var. sinica

Hydrangea aspera var. sinica, commonly known as the Chinese rough hydrangea, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage is dark green with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of sterile flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
