Hydrolea spinosa var. spinosa

Hydrolea spinosa var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiked hydrolea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which provide a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
