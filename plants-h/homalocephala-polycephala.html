Homalocephala polycephala

Homalocephala polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed cactus, is a unique species native to the deserts of North America.

It is characterized by its multiple heads or stems that grow from a single base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The species is also known for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in extreme desert conditions.
