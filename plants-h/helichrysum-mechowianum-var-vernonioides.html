Helichrysum mechowianum var. vernonioides

Helichrysum mechowianum var. vernonioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to retain color after drying make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
