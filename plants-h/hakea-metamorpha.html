Hakea metamorpha

Hakea metamorpha, commonly known as the variable hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Hakea metamorpha is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can play a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
