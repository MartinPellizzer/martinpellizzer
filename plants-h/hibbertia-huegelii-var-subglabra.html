Hibbertia huegelii var. subglabra

Hibbertia huegelii var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered hibbertia, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The flowers are bright yellow, with five showy petals and a distinctive central disk, blooming from late winter to spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

This variety is noted for its smooth, glabrous flower petals, distinguishing it from other varieties in the species.
