Hallieracantha brevipetiolata

Hallieracantha brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled hibiscus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five lobes and a prominent central column. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture with a slightly hairy surface. Its stems are woody and covered in fine, soft hairs, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
