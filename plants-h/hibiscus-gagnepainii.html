Hibiscus gagnepainii

Hibiscus gagnepainii, commonly known as the Congo hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height.

Hibiscus gagnepainii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
