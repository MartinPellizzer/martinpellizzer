Hieracium aphyllum subsp. hololeucum

Hieracium aphyllum subsp.

hololeucum, commonly known as the white hawk's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are arranged in a single, showy head.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its lack of leaf stalks and its predominantly white floral coloration.
