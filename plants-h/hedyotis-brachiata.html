Hedyotis brachiata

Hedyotis brachiata, commonly known as the small flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red in color. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Hedyotis brachiata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
