Hypericum aethiopicum subsp. aethiopicum

Hypericum aethiopicum subsp.

aethiopicum, commonly known as the Ethiopian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Africa. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
