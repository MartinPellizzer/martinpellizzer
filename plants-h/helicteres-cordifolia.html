Helicteres cordifolia

Helicteres cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a climbing or trailing growth habit. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
