Hemizonia grandiflora

Hemizonia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically features tall, slender stems with clusters of bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. Hemizonia grandiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
