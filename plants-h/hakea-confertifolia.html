Hakea confertifolia

Hakea confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely arranged along its stems, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces cylindrical, red or orange flower spikes that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its woody structure and adaptability to dry conditions make it suitable for arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Hakea confertifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
