Hepatica nobilis f. magna

Hepatica nobilis f. magna, commonly known as the large-flowered hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers, which are typically blue or purple, have five to seven sepals that resemble petals. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in woodland ecosystems and a popular choice for native plant gardens.
