Hubera tanganyikensis

Hubera tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika waterwheel, is a rare aquatic plant found exclusively in the Lake Tanganyika basin.

It is characterized by its unique whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble a waterwheel, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the lake's depths. It plays a role in the aquatic ecosystem by providing habitat for small organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
