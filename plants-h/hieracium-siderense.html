Hieracium siderense

Hieracium siderense, commonly known as the ironweed or siderine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Hieracium siderense is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can sometimes become invasive.
