Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. alpestre

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

alpestre, commonly known as the alpine rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
