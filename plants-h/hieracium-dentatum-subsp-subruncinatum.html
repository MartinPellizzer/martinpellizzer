Hieracium dentatum subsp. subruncinatum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

subruncinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are narrowly lanceolate with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
