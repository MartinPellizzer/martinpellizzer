Hepetis macrobotrys

Hepetis macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant blue flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is adapted to its wetland habitat, with rhizomes that help it spread and establish in these environments.
