Helianthemum pilosum var. violaceum

Helianthemum pilosum var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
