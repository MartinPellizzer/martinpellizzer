Hypericum spectabile

Hypericum spectabile, commonly known as the showy St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include yellowish-orange flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family.
