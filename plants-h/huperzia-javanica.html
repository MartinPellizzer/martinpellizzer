Huperzia javanica

Huperzia javanica, commonly known as the Javan huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery foliage and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with dense clusters of spore-producing structures called sporangia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories.

Huperzia javanica is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
