Halothamnus cinerascens subsp. vestitus

Halothamnus cinerascens subsp.

vestitus, commonly known as the silverbrush buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its succulent leaves, which store water and reduce transpiration. It produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The subspecies vestitus is distinguished by its more densely pubescent leaves compared to other subspecies of Halothamnus.
