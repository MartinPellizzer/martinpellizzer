Hieracium spiculatum

Hieracium spiculatum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
