Hibiscus huegelii var. quinquevulnera

Hibiscus huegelii var. quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to orange flowers with five distinct lobes, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Hibiscus huegelii var. quinquevulnera is a perennial species that is often found in wetland ecosystems, contributing to the biodiversity of these environments.
