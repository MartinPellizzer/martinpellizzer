Hieracium picroides subsp. ochroleucum

Hieracium picroides subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are basal and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is a common species in the genus Hieracium, known for its showy inflorescences and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
