Harrisia bonplandii

Harrisia bonplandii, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil, Peru, and Bolivia.

It is a spineless cactus that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, and it produces large, showy flowers that open at night. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers.
