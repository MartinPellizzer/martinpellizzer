Hypericum densiflorum var. lobocarpum

Hypericum densiflorum var. lobocarpum, commonly known as the dense-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes and ecological restoration projects.
