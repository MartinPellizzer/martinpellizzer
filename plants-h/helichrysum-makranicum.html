Helichrysum makranicum

Helichrysum makranicum, commonly known as the Makran goldcup, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum makranicum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
