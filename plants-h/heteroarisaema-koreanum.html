Heteroarisaema koreanum

Heteroarisaema koreanum, commonly known as the Korean arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short rhizome with broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
