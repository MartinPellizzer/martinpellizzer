Hyptis passerina

Hyptis passerina, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Hyptis passerina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a perennial in its native habitat.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
