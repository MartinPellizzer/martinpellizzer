Hymenoxys richardsonii var. pumila

Hymenoxys richardsonii var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf yellow coneflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
