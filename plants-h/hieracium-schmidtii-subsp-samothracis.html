Hieracium schmidtii subsp. samothracis

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

samothracis, commonly known as the Samothrace hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Aegean region of Greece. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features basal and cauline leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed or toothed margin.

The subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
