Hebe monticola

Hebe monticola, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Hebe monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
