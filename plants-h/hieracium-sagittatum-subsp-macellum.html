Hieracium sagittatum subsp. macellum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

macellum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, this subspecies thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and open grasslands. The plant produces small, composite flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.

Its growth habit is typically perennial, with a low-growing, sprawling stem that forms dense mats.
