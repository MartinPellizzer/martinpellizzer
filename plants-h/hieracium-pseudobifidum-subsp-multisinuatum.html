Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. multisinuatum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

multisinuatum, commonly known as the multisinuate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive sinuate pattern, giving it its subspecies name.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
