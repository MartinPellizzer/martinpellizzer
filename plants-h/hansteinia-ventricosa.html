Hansteinia ventricosa

Hansteinia ventricosa, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Hansteinia ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, cool climates.
