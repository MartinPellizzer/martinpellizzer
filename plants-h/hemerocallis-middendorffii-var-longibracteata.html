Hemerocallis middendorffii var. longibracteata

Hemerocallis middendorffii var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive orange throat. The plant produces sword-shaped, strap-like leaves that are broad and smooth. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
