Hieracium macrochaetium

Hieracium macrochaetium, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium macrochaetium is known for its aggressive growth and can form dense colonies, making it a potential weed in certain environments.
