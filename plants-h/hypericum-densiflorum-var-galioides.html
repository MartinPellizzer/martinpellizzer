Hypericum densiflorum var. galioides

Hypericum densiflorum var. galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and often have a slight reddish hue, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is notable for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested.
