Heterotoma cordifolia

Heterotoma cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf heterotoma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
