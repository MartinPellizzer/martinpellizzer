Hieracium leucodermum

Hieracium leucodermum, commonly known as the white-skinned hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a white center.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
