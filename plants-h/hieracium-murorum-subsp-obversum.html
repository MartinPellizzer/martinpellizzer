Hieracium murorum subsp. obversum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

obversum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, woolly, and heart-shaped, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is a common weed in agricultural and urban environments due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
