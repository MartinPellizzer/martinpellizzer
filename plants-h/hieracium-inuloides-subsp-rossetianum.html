Hieracium inuloides subsp. rossetianum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

rossetianum, commonly known as the Rossetian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a taproot system and exhibits a compact growth form, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
