Hymenophyllum laxum

Hymenophyllum laxum, commonly known as the slender mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds covered in fine, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum laxum is notable for its intricate morphology and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
