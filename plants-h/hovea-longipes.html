Hovea longipes

Hovea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pods. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hovea longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
