Hypoxis capensis

Hypoxis capensis, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flower clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a star-like appearance that attracts pollinators.

Hypoxis capensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
