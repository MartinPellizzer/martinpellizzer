Helianthus giganteus var. subtuberosus

Helianthus giganteus var. subtuberosus, commonly known as the giant sunflower or the tuberous sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its tuberous roots, which contribute to its hardiness and ability to survive in various soil conditions.
