Heliconia laneana f. flava

Heliconia laneana f. flava, commonly known as the yellow heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a colorful bract that resembles a flower.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
