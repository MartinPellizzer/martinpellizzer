Hieracium subcurvescens subsp. procurrens

Hieracium subcurvescens subsp.

procurrens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are typically longer than the disk florets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its growth habit is spreading, with stolons that allow it to form dense mats.
