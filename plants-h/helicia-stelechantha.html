Helicia stelechantha

Helicia stelechantha, commonly known as the golden helicia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red drupes that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
