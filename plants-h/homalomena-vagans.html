Homalomena vagans

Homalomena vagans, commonly known as the wandering arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with a spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Homalomena vagans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
