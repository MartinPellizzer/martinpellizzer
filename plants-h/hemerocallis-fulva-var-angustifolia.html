Hemerocallis fulva var. angustifolia

Hemerocallis fulva var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer and last only one day, hence its common name. It is hardy in USDA zones 4 through 9 and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
