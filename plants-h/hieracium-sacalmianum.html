Hieracium sacalmianum

Hieracium sacalmianum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium sacalmianum is often found in high-altitude ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
