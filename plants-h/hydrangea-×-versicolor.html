Hydrangea × versicolor

Hydrangea × versicolor, commonly known as the oakleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant's leaves are deeply lobed, resembling the shape of an oak leaf, hence its common name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hydrangea × versicolor is also valued for its seasonal interest, with vibrant fall foliage adding to its ornamental appeal.
