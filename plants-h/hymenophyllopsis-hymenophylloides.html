Hymenophyllopsis hymenophylloides

Hymenophyllopsis hymenophylloides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a rare and delicate plant belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, leathery, and intricately veined leaves that form a dense, fern-like structure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered in a thin, waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture in its moist, often misty habitat.

Hymenophyllopsis hymenophylloides is an epiphytic species, growing on the surfaces of trees and rocks rather than in soil.
