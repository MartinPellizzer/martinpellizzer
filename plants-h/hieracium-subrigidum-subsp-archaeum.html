Hieracium subrigidum subsp. archaeum

Hieracium subrigidum subsp.

archaeum, commonly known as the ancient hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in marginal environments.
