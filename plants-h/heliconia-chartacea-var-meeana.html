Heliconia chartacea var. meeana

Heliconia chartacea var. meeana, commonly known as the Meeana heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, boat-shaped inflorescences that are typically orange or red in color. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a lush, tropical appearance. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
