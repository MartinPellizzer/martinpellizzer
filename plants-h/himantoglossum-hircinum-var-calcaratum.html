Himantoglossum hircinum var. calcaratum

Himantoglossum hircinum var. calcaratum, commonly known as the limestone gentian, is a rare and specialized plant found in calcareous grasslands.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
