Heuchera grandiflora

Heuchera grandiflora, commonly known as the showy heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that display a range of colors, including green, red, and purple. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
