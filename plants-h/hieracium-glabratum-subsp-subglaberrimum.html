Hieracium glabratum subsp. subglaberrimum

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

subglaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in grassy habitats, such as meadows and woodland edges, and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with short, sparse hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like structure.

It is often found in temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
