Hieracium phaeographis

Hieracium phaeographis, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves that are often reddish at the base, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
