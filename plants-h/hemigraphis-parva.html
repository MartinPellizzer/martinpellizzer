Hemigraphis parva

Hemigraphis parva, commonly known as the small-leaf iron plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a striking bicolor pattern, often with a silver underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Hemigraphis parva is popular in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in container gardens.
