Helianthus silphioides

Helianthus silphioides, commonly known as the prairie sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus silphioides is known for its resilience and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
