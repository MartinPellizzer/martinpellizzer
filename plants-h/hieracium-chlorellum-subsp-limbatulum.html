Hieracium chlorellum subsp. limbatulum

Hieracium chlorellum subsp.

limbatulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring moist to moderately dry soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct basal lobing, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is a low-growing plant with a fibrous root system and is often found in open habitats with partial shade.
