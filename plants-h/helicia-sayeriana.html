Helicia sayeriana

Helicia sayeriana, commonly known as the Sayer's holly, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in suitable conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the wet season.

Helicia sayeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
