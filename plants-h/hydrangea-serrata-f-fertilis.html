Hydrangea serrata f. fertilis

Hydrangea serrata f. fertilis, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea or the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can change color depending on soil pH, typically ranging from pink to blue. The plant features serrated leaves that are dark green and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens during that time.
