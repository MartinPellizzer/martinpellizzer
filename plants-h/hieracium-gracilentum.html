Hieracium gracilentum

Hieracium gracilentum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium gracilentum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
