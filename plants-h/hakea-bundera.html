Hakea bundera

Hakea bundera, commonly known as the Bundera hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and clusters of narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces striking, pink to red floral spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea bundera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
