Halocharis pycnantha

Halocharis pycnantha, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
