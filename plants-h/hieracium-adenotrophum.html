Hieracium adenotrophum

Hieracium adenotrophum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has hairy, glandular stems and leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium adenotrophum is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in meadows and along roadsides.
