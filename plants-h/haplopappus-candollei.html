Haplopappus candollei

Haplopappus candollei, commonly known as the silky pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low, cushion-like growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its pollen is finely textured and easily dispersed by the wind, aiding in its reproductive success.

Haplopappus candollei is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.
