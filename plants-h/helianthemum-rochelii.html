Helianthemum rochelii

Helianthemum rochelii, commonly known as the Canary Islands sandverbena, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent leaves that are adapted to store water, reflecting its ability to thrive in arid environments. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas with limited moisture.

Helianthemum rochelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
