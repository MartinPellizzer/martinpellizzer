Hepatica nobilis var. nipponica

Hepatica nobilis var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring bloom, featuring delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, or blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers often persist on the plant for several weeks, adding to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and early blooming habit.
