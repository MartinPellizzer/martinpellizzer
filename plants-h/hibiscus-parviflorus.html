Hibiscus parviflorus

Hibiscus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal.

Hibiscus parviflorus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
