Hieracium canillosum

Hieracium canillosum, commonly known as the Canillo hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and specialized species, often found in specific ecological niches.
