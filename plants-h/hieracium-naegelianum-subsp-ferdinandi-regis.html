Hieracium naegelianum subsp. ferdinandi-regis

Hieracium naegelianum subsp.

ferdinandi-regis, commonly known as the Ferdinand's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, showy capitulum.

The plant has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
