Homonoma aridum

Homonoma aridum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in low-water environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Homonoma aridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
