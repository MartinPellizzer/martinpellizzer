Hierochloe glauca

Hierochloe glauca, commonly known as blue hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Hierochloe glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
