Hibiscus surattensis var. furcatus

Hibiscus surattensis var. furcatus, commonly known as the forked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in horticultural settings for its beauty.
