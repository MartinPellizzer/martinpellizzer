Hoffmannia riparia

Hoffmannia riparia, commonly known as the river hoffmannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of South America.

It typically grows in moist, clay soils and thrives in environments with consistent water levels. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

Hoffmannia riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil along riverbanks and providing habitat for various insect species.
