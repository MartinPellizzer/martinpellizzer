Hieracium subinversum

Hieracium subinversum, commonly known as the inverted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in disturbed or shaded environments.
