Hippophae mexicana

Hippophae mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has dark green, alternate leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

Its hardy nature makes it an important plant for stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
