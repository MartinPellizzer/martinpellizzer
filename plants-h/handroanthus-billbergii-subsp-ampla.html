Handroanthus billbergii subsp. ampla

Handroanthus billbergii subsp.

ampla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
