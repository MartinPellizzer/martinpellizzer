Helicia dentellata

Helicia dentellata, commonly known as the serrated helicia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Helicia dentellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
