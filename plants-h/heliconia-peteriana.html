Heliconia peteriana

Heliconia peteriana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that resemble a torch. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are clustered in a spiral arrangement, with bright red bracts that enclose the actual flowers.

Heliconia peteriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
