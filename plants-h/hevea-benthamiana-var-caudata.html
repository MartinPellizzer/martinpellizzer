Hevea benthamiana var. caudata

Hevea benthamiana var. caudata, commonly known as the caudate hevea, is a variant of the rubber tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, trailing growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant is primarily cultivated for its latex, which is used in the production of natural rubber. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both agricultural and scientific research.
