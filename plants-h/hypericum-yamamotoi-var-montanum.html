Hypericum yamamotoi var. montanum

Hypericum yamamotoi var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
