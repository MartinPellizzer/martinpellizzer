Hieracium sparsum subsp. macedonicum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a spreading growth habit and can form dense mats, which may contribute to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of plant ecology and biodiversity in its native range.
