Hieracium phyllothames

Hieracium phyllothames, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and floral structure.
