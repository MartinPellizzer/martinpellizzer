Haronga lanceolata

Haronga lanceolata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Haronga lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
