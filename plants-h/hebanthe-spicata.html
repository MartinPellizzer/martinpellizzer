Hebanthe spicata

Hebanthe spicata, commonly known as the spiked hebanthe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers, which are often violet or blue in color, bloom in spikes at the ends of the stems.
