Hebe × amabilis

Hebe × amabilis, commonly known as the golden hebe, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Hebe × amabilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate climates.
