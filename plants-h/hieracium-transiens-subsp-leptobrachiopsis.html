Hieracium transiens subsp. leptobrachiopsis

Hieracium transiens subsp.

leptobrachiopsis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that are often branched and bear yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences.
