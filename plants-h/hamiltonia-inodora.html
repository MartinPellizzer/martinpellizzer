Hamiltonia inodora

Hamiltonia inodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while the roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Hamiltonia inodora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
