Hakea thelemanniana

Hakea thelemanniana, commonly known as the Lemon Hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Hakea thelemanniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
