Hemigraphis decaisneana

Hemigraphis decaisneana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and distinctive red veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hemigraphis decaisneana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
