Heliotropium bacciferum subsp. bacciferum

Heliotropium bacciferum subsp.

bacciferum, commonly known as the blackberry heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its prostrate or trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry, which is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
