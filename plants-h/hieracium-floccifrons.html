Hieracium floccifrons

Hieracium floccifrons, commonly known as the fluffhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium floccifrons is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
