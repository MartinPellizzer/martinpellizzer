Helichrysum tesselatum

Helichrysum tesselatum, commonly known as the golden tawny daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically surrounded by a ring of narrow, linear leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Helichrysum tesselatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
