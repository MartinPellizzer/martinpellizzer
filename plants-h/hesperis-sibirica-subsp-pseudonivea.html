Hesperis sibirica subsp. pseudonivea

Hesperis sibirica subsp.

pseudonivea, commonly known as the Siberian honesty, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
