Hieracium aridulum

Hieracium aridulum, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky slopes, and along roadsides. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by accessing deeper water sources.

Hieracium aridulum is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
