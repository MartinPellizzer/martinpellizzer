Hemilophia rockii var. flavida

Hemilophia rockii var. flavida, commonly known as the golden rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
