Hieracium armerioides subsp. bellidioides

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

bellidioides, commonly known as the bellidium hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry grasslands, disturbed soils, and open woodlands, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture, with basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. Its flowering stems bear yellow to orange composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The species is characterized by its small, dandelion-like flowers and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
