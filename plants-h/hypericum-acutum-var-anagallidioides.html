Hypericum acutum var. anagallidioides

Hypericum acutum var. anagallidioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. This variety is closely related to other Hypericum species and shares similar medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
