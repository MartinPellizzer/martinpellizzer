Hovea longifolia f. albiflora

Hovea longifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hovea, is a variant of the hovea plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Its flowers are fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
