Hygrophila formosa

Hygrophila formosa, commonly known as the water wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats. It is a popular choice for aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Hygrophila formosa is also valued for its ability to provide oxygen and habitat for aquatic life.
