Hydrocotyle aristeguietae

Hydrocotyle aristeguietae, commonly known as the Andean water shield, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are typically divided into lobes, allowing it to thrive in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Hydrocotyle aristeguiuietae is often found in slow-moving waters and wetlands, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
