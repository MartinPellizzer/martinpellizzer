Hydrophyllum virginianum var. atranthum

Hydrophyllum virginianum var. atranthum, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with dark centers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in wetlands, streambanks, and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
