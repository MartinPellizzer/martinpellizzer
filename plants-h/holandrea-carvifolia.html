Holandrea carvifolia

Holandrea carvifolia, commonly known as the caraway-leaved holandrea, is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of caraway, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
