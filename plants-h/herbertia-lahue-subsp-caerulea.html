Herbertia lahue subsp. caerulea

Herbertia lahue subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as the blue-veined herb, is a striking perennial plant native to South America. It features vibrant blue and purple foliage with striking, showy blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a rosette growth habit, with broad, waxy leaves that are often adorned with distinctive veins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
