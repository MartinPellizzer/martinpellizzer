Hebeandra fastigiata

Hebeandra fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hebeandra fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
