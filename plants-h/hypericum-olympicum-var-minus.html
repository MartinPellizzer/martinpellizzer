Hypericum olympicum var. minus

Hypericum olympicum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and long-lasting blooms.
