Heliopsis novogaliciana

Heliopsis novogaliciana, commonly known as the Croatian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch.

Heliopsis novogaliciana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
