Hibiscus sakamaliensis

Hibiscus sakamaliensis, commonly known as the Sakamali hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas near streams or in shaded environments.

Hibiscus sakamaliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
