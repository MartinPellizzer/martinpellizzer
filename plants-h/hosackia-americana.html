Hosackia americana

Hosackia americana, commonly known as the American hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas.
