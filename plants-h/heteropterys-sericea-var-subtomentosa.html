Heteropterys sericea var. subtomentosa

Heteropterys sericea var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the silverleaf holly or subtomentous holly, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and prominent veins. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or orange berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
