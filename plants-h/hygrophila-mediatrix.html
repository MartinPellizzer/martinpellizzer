Hygrophila mediatrix

Hygrophila mediatrix, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Hygrophila mediatrix is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to oxygenate water.
