Huperzia crassa var. manus-diaboli

Huperzia crassa var. manus-diaboli, commonly known as the Devil's Hand Huperzia, is a rare and unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, claw-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of China and Vietnam. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dark green, leathery leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it an unusual appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and collectors alike.
