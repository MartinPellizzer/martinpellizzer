Hieracium kiderense

Hieracium kiderense, commonly known as the Kiderens hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as achenes.

Hieracium kiderense is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
