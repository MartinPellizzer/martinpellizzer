Helichrysum hamulosum

Helichrysum hamulosum, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid regions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Helichrysum hamulosum is often used in traditional medicine and is also popular in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance.
