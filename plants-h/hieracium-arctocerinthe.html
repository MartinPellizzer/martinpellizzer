Hieracium arctocerinthe

Hieracium arctocerinthe, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium arctocerinthe is known for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.
