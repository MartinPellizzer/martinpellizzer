Hippeastrum oaxacanum

Hippeastrum oaxacanum, commonly known as the Oaxacan amaryllis, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The flowering period occurs in the spring, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and homes.
