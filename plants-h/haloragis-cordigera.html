Haloragis cordigera

Haloragis cordigera, commonly known as the heartleaf water-starwort, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in coastal or freshwater environments. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Haloragis cordigera is adapted to tolerate saline and waterlogged conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
