Hieracium amorphoglossum

Hieracium amorphoglossum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium amorphoglossum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
