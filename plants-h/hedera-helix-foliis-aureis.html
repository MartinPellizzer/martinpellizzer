Hedera helix foliis-aureis

Hedera helix foliis-aureis, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a climbing plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking golden variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
