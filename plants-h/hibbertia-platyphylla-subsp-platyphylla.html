Hibbertia platyphylla subsp. platyphylla

Hibbertia platyphylla subsp.

platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibbertia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals, typically blooming from late winter to spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in heathlands and rocky areas. Hibbertia platyphylla subsp.

platyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
