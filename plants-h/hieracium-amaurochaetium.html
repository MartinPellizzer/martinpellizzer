Hieracium amaurochaetium

Hieracium amaurochaetium, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are dark green and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat cluster.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
