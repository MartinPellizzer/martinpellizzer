Hieracium hypeuryum subsp. pinguistolonum

Hieracium hypeuryum subsp.

pinguistolonum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late summer.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
