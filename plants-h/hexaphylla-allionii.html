Hexaphylla allionii

Hexaphylla allionii, commonly known as the Allion's hexaphyll, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Hexaphylla allionii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
