Hieracium ucenicum subsp. rimigenum

Hieracium ucenicum subsp.

rimigenum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and the absence of a stem below the flower head.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
