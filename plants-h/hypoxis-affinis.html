Hypoxis affinis

Hypoxis affinis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, tuberous rhizome and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
