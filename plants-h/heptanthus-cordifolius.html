Heptanthus cordifolius

Heptanthus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved heptanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
