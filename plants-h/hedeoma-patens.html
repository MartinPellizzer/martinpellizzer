Hedeoma patens

Hedeoma patens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. The plant features showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hedeoma patens is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
