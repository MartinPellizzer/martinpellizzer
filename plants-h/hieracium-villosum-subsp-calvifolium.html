Hieracium villosum subsp. calvifolium

Hieracium villosum subsp.

calvifolium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by ray florets, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its distinctive leaf morphology and flowering pattern.
