Hieracium umbellatum subsp. brevifolium

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a cluster of yellow flowers in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
