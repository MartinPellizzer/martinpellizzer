Hieracium rothianum subsp. carnuntii

Hieracium rothianum subsp.

carnuntii, commonly known as the Carnuntian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus.
