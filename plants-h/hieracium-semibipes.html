Hieracium semibipes

Hieracium semibipes, commonly known as the two-toed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively.

It is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
