Hakea cullenii

Hakea cullenii, commonly known as the Cullen's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or conical flower spikes, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It produces woody, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Hakea cullenii is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
