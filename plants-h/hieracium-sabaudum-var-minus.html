Hieracium sabaudum var. minus

Hieracium sabaudum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

It is considered a weed in some regions but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
