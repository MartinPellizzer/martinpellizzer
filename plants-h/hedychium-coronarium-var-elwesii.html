Hedychium coronarium var. elwesii

Hedychium coronarium var. elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' ginger, is a variant of the flame ginger plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle sweet scent. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous root system, typical of the Zingiberaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
