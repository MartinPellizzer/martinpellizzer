Hieracium caesium subsp. simulatum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

simulatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
