Hedyotis glauca

Hedyotis glauca, commonly known as blue-eyed Mary, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hedyotis glauca is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
