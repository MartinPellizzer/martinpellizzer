Hamosa scaposa

Hamosa scaposa, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Hamosa scaposa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
