Heliophila rivalis

Heliophila rivalis, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, oval leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, often floating just above the water surface. Heliophila rivalis is a perennial species that thrives in nutrient-rich sediments and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed aquatic habitats make it an important indicator of water quality.
