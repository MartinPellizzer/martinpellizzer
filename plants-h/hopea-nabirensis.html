Hopea nabirensis

Hopea nabirensis, commonly known as the Nabire hopea, is a tropical tree native to the lowland rainforests of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing a single seed. Hopea nabirensis is valued for its hard, durable timber, which is used in construction and furniture making.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
