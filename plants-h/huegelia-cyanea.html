Huegelia cyanea

Huegelia cyanea, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant's foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Huegelia cyanea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early summer.
