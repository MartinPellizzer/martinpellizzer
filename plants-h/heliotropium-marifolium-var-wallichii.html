Heliotropium marifolium var. wallichii

Heliotropium marifolium var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, with a slightly succulent texture. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
