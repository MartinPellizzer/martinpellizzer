Hovea clavata

Hovea clavata, commonly known as the broad-leaved hovea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive broad, ovate shape.

Hovea clavata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
