Hippeastrum uruguaianicum

Hippeastrum uruguaianicum, commonly known as the Uruguayan amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette around the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
