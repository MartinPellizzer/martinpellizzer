Hypericum pulchrum var. gillotii

Hypericum pulchrum var. gillotii, commonly known as the Gillot's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
