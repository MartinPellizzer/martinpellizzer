Heliconia linneana var. flava

Heliconia linneana var. flava, commonly known as the Yellow Heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a spiral pattern around a central stem, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

It is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
