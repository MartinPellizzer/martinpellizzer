Hugeria japonica var. sinica

Hugeria japonica var. sinica, commonly known as the Chinese huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
