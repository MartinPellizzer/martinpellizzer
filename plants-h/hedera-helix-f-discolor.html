Hedera helix f. discolor

Hedera helix f. discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Ivy, is a popular cultivar of the common ivy.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant is a climbing vine with dark green stems and lobed leaves that are often edged with white. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environments.
