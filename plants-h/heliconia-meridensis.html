Heliconia meridensis

Heliconia meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spiral arrangement of sterile bracts surrounding a cluster of small, inconspicuous true flowers.

Heliconia meridensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
