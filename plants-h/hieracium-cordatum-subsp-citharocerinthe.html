Hieracium cordatum subsp. citharocerinthe

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

citharocerinthe, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
