Hippocratea macrantha var. palauica

Hippocratea macrantha var. palauica, commonly known as the Palau orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island nation of Palau in the Western Pacific.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing in coastal areas or on limestone substrates. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a notable species in local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
