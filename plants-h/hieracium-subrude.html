Hieracium subrude

Hieracium subrude, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium subrude is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
