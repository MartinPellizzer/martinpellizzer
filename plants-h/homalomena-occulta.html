Homalomena occulta

Homalomena occulta, commonly known as the hidden arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense clusters. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, enclosed by a spathe.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
