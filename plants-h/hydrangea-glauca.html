Hydrangea glauca

Hydrangea glauca, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, rounded clusters of sterile flowers that range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and an upright growth habit.
