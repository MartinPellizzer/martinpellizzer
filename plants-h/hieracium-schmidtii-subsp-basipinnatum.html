Hieracium schmidtii subsp. basipinnatum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

basipinnatum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has slender, hairy stems and produces small, disc-shaped flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
