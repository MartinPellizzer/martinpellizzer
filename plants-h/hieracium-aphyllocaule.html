Hieracium aphyllocaule

Hieracium aphyllocaule, commonly known as the leafless hawkweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only a stem and flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows and along roadsides.
