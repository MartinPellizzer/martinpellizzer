Hieracium mundulifolium

Hieracium mundulifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped.

This species is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
