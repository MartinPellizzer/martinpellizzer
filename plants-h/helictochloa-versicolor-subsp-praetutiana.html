Helictochloa versicolor subsp. praetutiana

Helictochloa versicolor subsp.

praetutiana, commonly known as the variable bluegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, often in regions with a temperate climate. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a distinctive coloration that varies with environmental conditions.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
