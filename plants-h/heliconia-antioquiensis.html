Heliconia antioquiensis

Heliconia antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andes region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its foliage consists of long, broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in floral arrangements.

Heliconia antioquiensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
