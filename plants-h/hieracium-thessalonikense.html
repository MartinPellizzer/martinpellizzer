Hieracium thessalonikense

Hieracium thessalonikense, commonly known as the Thessaloniki hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in a single head. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
