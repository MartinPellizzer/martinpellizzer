Hieracium semigothiciforme

Hieracium semigothiciforme, commonly known as the Gothic dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a single, elongated flower head that is surrounded by bracts.

The species is notable for its distinctive, somewhat angular appearance, which gives it its common name.
