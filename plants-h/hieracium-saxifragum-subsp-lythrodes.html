Hieracium saxifragum subsp. lythrodes

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

lythrodes, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its subspecies distinction is based on subtle differences in leaf morphology and flowering patterns.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
