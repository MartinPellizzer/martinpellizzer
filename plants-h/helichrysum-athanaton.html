Helichrysum athanaton

Helichrysum athanaton, commonly known as the everlasting dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its golden-yellow, dandelion-like flowers that retain their color and form even after drying. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution. Its foliage is usually hairy and aromatic, with a slightly bitter taste.

Helichrysum athanaton is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms.
