Huperzia magnusiana

Huperzia magnusiana, commonly known as the giant huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly in countries like China, India, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, scale-like structures. Huperzia magnusiana thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
