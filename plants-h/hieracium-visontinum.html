Hieracium visontinum

Hieracium visontinum, commonly known as the Visontini hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
