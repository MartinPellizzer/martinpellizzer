Hedyotis glaucifolia var. helleri

Hedyotis glaucifolia var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's blue geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
