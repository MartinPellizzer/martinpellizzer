Hydrocotyle pilosa

Hydrocotyle pilosa, commonly known as the hairy pennywort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, round leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Hydrocotyle pilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
