Hippeastrum lagesianum

Hippeastrum lagesianum, commonly known as the Lagesian amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a white or pink throat. The plant produces strap-like, linear leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy for optimal flowering.

Hippeastrum lagesianum is popular in gardens and as a potted plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
