Heterocentron suffruticosum

Heterocentron suffruticosum, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Heterocentron suffruticosum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
