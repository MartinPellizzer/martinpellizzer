Hieracium solidagineum subsp. leptoclinium

Hieracium solidagineum subsp.

leptoclinium, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in various environments.
