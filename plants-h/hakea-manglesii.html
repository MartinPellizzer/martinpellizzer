Hakea manglesii

Hakea manglesii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, red-brown flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is part of the Proteaceae family.

Hakea manglesii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
