Hyssopus recticaulis

Hyssopus recticaulis, commonly known as upright hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hyssopus recticaulis is often grown in gardens for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.
