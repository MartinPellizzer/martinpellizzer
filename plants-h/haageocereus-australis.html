Haageocereus australis

Haageocereus australis, commonly known as the Australian night-blooming cactus, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It features spiny stems and produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with succulent stems that store water. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and they emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
