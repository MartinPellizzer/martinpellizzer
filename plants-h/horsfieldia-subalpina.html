Horsfieldia subalpina

Horsfieldia subalpina, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. The plant has a slender, upright stem with few leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the cooler months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or loose soil.

Horsfieldia subalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
