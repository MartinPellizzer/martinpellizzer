Hieracium brachycomum subsp. laevifolium

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

laevifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are generally smooth and leathery in texture.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed complex.
