Holothrix culveri var. integra

Holothrix culveri var. integra, commonly known as the woolly holothrix, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a popular plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
