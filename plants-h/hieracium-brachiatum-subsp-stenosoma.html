Hieracium brachiatum subsp. stenosoma

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

stenosoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often found in temperate climates and is considered a minor weed in some agricultural settings.
