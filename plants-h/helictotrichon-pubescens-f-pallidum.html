Helictotrichon pubescens f. pallidum

Helictotrichon pubescens f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale leaf bluegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is distinguished by its pale coloration and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological value.
