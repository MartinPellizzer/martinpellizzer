Haroldiella rapaensis

Haroldiella rapaensis, commonly known as the Korean radish or Chinese radish, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, swollen root that is typically purple or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Haroldiella rapaensis is valued for its edible roots and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
