Hieracium brunelliforme

Hieracium brunelliforme, commonly known as the brown knapweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Hieracium brunelliforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
