Hordeum vulgare var. nipponicum

Hordeum vulgare var. nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese barley, is a variant of the common barley species.

It is a grass species native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-rowed grains, typical of the Hordeum genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for cultivation and adaptation to specific environmental conditions.
