Hieracium floribundum subsp. helveolum

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

helveolum, commonly known as the Swiss buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers with ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its sensitivity to environmental changes.
