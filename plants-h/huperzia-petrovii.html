Huperzia petrovii

Huperzia petrovii, commonly known as the Siberian clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and needle-like leaves that form a rosette-like structure. Native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including Russia, China, and Korea, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Huperzia petrovii is typically found in shaded, forested areas where it contributes to the understory vegetation.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
