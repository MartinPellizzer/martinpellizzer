Halimium halimifolium f. multiflorum

Halimium halimifolium f. multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its gray-green, silver-hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.

The species is also valued for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it well-suited to harsh environments.
