Hydrangea vestita var. pubescens

Hydrangea vestita var. pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features oval, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
