Hieracium pannosum subsp. taygeteum

Hieracium pannosum subsp.

taygeteum, commonly known as the Taygete hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
