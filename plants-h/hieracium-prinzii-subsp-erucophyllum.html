Hieracium prinzii subsp. erucophyllum

Hieracium prinzii subsp.

erucophyllum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in local ecosystems.
