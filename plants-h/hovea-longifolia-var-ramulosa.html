Hovea longifolia var. ramulosa

Hovea longifolia var. ramulosa, commonly known as the slender hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with slender, branching stems. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces hairy, elongated pods that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to sandy soils and its role in coastal dune stabilization.
