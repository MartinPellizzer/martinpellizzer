Hakea petiolaris subsp. angusta

Hakea petiolaris subsp.

angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
