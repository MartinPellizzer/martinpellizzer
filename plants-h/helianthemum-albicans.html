Helianthemum albicans

Helianthemum albicans, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features compact, upright stems covered in small, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
