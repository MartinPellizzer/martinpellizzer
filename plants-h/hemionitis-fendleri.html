Hemionitis fendleri

Hemionitis fendleri, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming fern that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant has small, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, with spores produced in tiny, clustered sori on the underside of the fronds.

Hemionitis fendleri is notable for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
