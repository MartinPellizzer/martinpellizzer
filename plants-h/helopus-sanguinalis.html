Helopus sanguinalis

Helopus sanguinalis, commonly known as the redroot sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Helopus sanguinalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitat.
