Helichrysum lineare var. caespititium

Helichrysum lineare var. caespititium, commonly known as the golden everlasting or creeping helichrysum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that are tightly packed and resemble small, flat discs. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
