Hippocastanum

Hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces spiny capsules containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive, irregular pattern.

The plant is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and autumn foliage.
