Hieracium subcrocatum

Hieracium subcrocatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers consist of numerous small florets arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium subcrocatum is often found in open habitats and can spread aggressively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
