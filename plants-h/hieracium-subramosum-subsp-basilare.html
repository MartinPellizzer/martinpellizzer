Hieracium subramosum subsp. basilare

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

basilare, commonly known as the basal yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with tubular florets surrounded by ray florets.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in dry, sunny environments.
