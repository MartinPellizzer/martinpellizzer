Hypochaeris deserticola

Hypochaeris deserticola, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Hypochaeris deserticola is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of dry, open habitats.
