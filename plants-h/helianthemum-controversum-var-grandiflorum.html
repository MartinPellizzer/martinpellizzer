Helianthemum controversum var. grandiflorum

Helianthemum controversum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its resilience to drought and salt spray.
