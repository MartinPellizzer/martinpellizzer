Hibiscus coelestis

Hibiscus coelestis, commonly known as the skyblue hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hibiscus coelestis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
