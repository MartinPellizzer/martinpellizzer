Hieracium autonomum

Hieracium autonomum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments.

Hieracium autonomum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
