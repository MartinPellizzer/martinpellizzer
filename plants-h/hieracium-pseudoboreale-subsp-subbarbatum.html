Hieracium pseudoboreale subsp. subbarbatum

Hieracium pseudoboreale subsp.

subbarbatum, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as tundra and wet meadows.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in dense, white to greyish hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various insects and contributing to soil stabilization.
