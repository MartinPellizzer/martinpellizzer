Hieracium dystrichotum

Hieracium dystrichotum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are rough to the touch and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its role as a nectar source for pollinators.
