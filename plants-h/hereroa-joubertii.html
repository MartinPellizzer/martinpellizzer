Hereroa joubertii

Hereroa joubertii, commonly known as the Joubert's ericoid, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and seasonal display of white or pink blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens with careful attention to soil and moisture conditions.
