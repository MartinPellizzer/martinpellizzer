Hedyotis brevipes

Hedyotis brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinct central stamen.

Hedyotis brevipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
