Hoya occlusa

Hoya occlusa, commonly known as the "hidden hoya," is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of star-shaped flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Hoya occlusa is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low light conditions.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
