Haloxylon

Haloxylon, commonly known as the desert date or tamarisk, is a genus of shrubs and trees native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, often inconspicuous flowers, typically in shades of yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Haloxylon species are well adapted to drought and can thrive in saline soils, making them important components of desert ecosystems.

They also play a role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various desert animals.
