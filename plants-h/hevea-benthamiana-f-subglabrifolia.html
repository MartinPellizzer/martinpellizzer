Hevea benthamiana f. subglabrifolia

Hevea benthamiana f. subglabrifolia, commonly known as the subglabrifoliate rubber tree, is a botanical variant of the rubber tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical form. The plant is primarily cultivated for its latex-producing capabilities, which are essential in the rubber industry. Its botanical features include alternate, simple leaves with a slightly elliptical shape.

This form is often used in genetic research due to its ease of cultivation and genetic stability.
