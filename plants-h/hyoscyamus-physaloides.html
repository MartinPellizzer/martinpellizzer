Hyoscyamus physaloides

Hyoscyamus physaloides, commonly known as the balloonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy, balloon-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, hard seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of tropane alkaloids.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
