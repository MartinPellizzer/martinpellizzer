Hydrangea villosa var. velutina

Hydrangea villosa var. velutina, commonly known as the downy hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns red or orange in the fall.

Hydrangea villosa var. velutina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
