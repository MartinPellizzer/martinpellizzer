Hyptis barbata

Hyptis barbata, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

Hyptis barbata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
