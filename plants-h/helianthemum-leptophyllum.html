Helianthemum leptophyllum

Helianthemum leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compact, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Helianthemum leptophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
