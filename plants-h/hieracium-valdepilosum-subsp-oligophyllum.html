Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. oligophyllum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

oligophyllum, commonly known as the oligophyllous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs and shorter flower stems compared to other members of the species. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in some regions.
