Hieraciodes sibirica

Hieraciodes sibirica, commonly known as Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
