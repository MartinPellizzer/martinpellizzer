Heliophila caledonica

Heliophila caledonica, commonly known as the Scottish heath, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in moist, acidic soils in heathland and moorland environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Heliophila caledonica is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
