Hedysarum alpinum subsp. alpinum

Hedysarum alpinum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as alpine sweetclover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its root system is fibrous and often forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Hedysarum alpinum subsp.

alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
