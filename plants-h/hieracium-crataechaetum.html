Hieracium crataechaetum

Hieracium crataechaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and hairy.

Hieracium crataechaetum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in some regions.
