Helianthus squarrosus

Helianthus squarrosus, commonly known as the rough sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Helianthus squarrosus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
