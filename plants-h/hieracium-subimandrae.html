Hieracium subimandrae

Hieracium subimandrae, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium subimandrae is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
