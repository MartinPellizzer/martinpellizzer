Hypericum armenum subsp. iranicum

Hypericum armenum subsp.

iranicum, commonly known as Persian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran and the Caucasus. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers consist of five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its bioactive compounds.
