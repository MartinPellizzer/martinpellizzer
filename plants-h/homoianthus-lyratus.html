Homoianthus lyratus

Homoianthus lyratus, commonly known as the lyreleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble the shape of a lyre, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
