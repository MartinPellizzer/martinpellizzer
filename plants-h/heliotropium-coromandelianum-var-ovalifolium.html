Heliotropium coromandelianum var. ovalifolium

Heliotropium coromandelianum var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
