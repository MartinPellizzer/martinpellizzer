Hippocastanum carneum

Hippocastanum carneum, commonly known as the Persian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, palmate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces large, showy white flowers with a distinctive red-brown spire in the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or two smooth, chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for centuries.
