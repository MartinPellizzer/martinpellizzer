Hedysarum sennoides

Hedysarum sennoides, commonly known as yellow sweetclover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Hedysarum sennoides is also valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
