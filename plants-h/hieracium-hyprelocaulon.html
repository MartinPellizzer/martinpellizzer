Hieracium hyprelocaulon

Hieracium hyprelocaulon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can form dense colonies, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
