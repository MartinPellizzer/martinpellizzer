Hydrangea arborescens var. discolor

Hydrangea arborescens var. discolor, commonly known as the oakleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed, oak-like leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces large, showy flower clusters that range from white to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal color changes.
