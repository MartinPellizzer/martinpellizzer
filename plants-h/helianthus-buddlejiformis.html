Helianthus buddlejiformis

Helianthus buddlejiformis, commonly known as the goldenrod-buddlejiform, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Helianthus buddlejiformis plays a vital role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its abundant nectar-rich flowers.
