Hornstedtia paludosa

Hornstedtia paludosa, commonly known as the swamp bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and swampy areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged conditions.

Hornstedtia paludosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
