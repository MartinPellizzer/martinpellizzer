Hieracium vollmannii subsp. squalidiforme

Hieracium vollmannii subsp.

squalidiforme, commonly known as the squalidiform hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other members of the species.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
