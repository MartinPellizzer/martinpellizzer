Holcus sorghum

Holcus sorghum, commonly known as broomcorn millet, is an annual grass native to the warmer regions of the Old World.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicle-shaped inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop for dry climates. Its seeds are harvested for food and animal feed, and the plant is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Holcus sorghum plays an important role in sustainable agriculture due to its low water requirements and high yield potential.
