Hypericum apocynifolium

Hypericum apocynifolium, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum apocynifolium is known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other St. John's wort species.
