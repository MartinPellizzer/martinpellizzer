Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. hypoglaucum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

hypoglaucum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and forest edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, glaucous-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in moist, temperate climates.
