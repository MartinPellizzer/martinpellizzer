Hyssopus officinalis var. montanus

Hyssopus officinalis var. montanus, commonly known as mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often aromatic. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its aromatic properties have made it historically valued for medicinal and culinary uses.
