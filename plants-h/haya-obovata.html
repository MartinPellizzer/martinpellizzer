Haya obovata

Haya obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed haya, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. Haya obovata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
