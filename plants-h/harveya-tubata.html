Harveya tubata

Harveya tubata, commonly known as the tubular harveya, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Harveya tubata is known for its ornamental value, with its unique floral structure attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
