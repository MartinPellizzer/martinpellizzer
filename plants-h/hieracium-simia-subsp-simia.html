Hieracium simia subsp. simia

Hieracium simia subsp.

simia, commonly known as the monkey's head hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a monkey's face, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can sometimes become invasive.
