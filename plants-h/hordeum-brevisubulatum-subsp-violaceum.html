Hordeum brevisubulatum subsp. violaceum

Hordeum brevisubulatum subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as violet barley, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, violet-colored inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique coloration and potential ecological roles.
