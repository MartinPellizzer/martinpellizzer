Hedysarum petrovii

Hedysarum petrovii, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to reddish flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Hedysarum petrovii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
