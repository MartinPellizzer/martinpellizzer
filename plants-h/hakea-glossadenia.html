Hakea glossadenia

Hakea glossadenia, commonly known as the glossy hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish-green flower spikes that bloom in spring. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly honeyeaters.

Hakea glossadenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
