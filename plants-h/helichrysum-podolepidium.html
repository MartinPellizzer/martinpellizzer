Helichrysum podolepidium

Helichrysum podolepidium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces papery, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a decorative appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum podolepidium is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
