Hypoxis demissa

Hypoxis demissa, commonly known as the starry false shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers with yellow centers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hypoxis demissa is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
