Herminium latilabre

Herminium latilabre, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, upright stem with few leaves and produces a single, showy flower with a broad, spurred lip. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed labellum.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
