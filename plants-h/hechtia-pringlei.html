Hechtia pringlei

Hechtia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's hechtia, is a striking bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from a central spadix, adding dramatic visual interest. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
