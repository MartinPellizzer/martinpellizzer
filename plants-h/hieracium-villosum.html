Hieracium villosum

Hieracium villosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Hieracium villosum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
