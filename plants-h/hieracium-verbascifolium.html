Hieracium verbascifolium

Hieracium verbascifolium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
