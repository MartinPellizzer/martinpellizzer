Heptapleurum littorale

Heptapleurum littorale, commonly known as the coastal heptapleurum, is a shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments along rocky coastlines and in mountainous areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical berries.

Heptapleurum littorale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging coastal conditions.
