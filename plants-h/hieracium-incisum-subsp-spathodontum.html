Hieracium incisum subsp. spathodontum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

spathodontum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, often hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
