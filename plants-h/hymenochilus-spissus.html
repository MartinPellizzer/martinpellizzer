Hymenochilus spissus

Hymenochilus spissus, commonly known as the dense-lipped orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often more prominent than the other petals. Its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Hymenochilus spissus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
