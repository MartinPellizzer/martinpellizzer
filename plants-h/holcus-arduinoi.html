Holcus arduinoi

Holcus arduinoi, commonly known as the Arduino grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

Holcus arduinoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
