Hieracium rigidum subsp. cruentiferum

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

cruentiferum, commonly known as the bloodflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are rigid and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils, and is often found in the wild in temperate regions.

The species is notable for its bright, showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
