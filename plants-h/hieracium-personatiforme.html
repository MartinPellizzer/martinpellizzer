Hieracium personatiforme

Hieracium personatiforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a yellow disk.

Hieracium personatiforme is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
