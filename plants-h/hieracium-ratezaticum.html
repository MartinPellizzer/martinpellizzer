Hieracium ratezaticum

Hieracium ratezaticum, commonly known as the Retezat hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is generally hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.
