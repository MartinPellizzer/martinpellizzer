Hamamelis riparia

Hamamelis riparia, commonly known as the eastern witchhazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its unique, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

Hamamelis riparia is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
