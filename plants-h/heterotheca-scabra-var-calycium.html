Heterotheca scabra var. calycium

Heterotheca scabra var. calycium, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological studies.
