Humiria balsamifera f. acuminata

Humiria balsamifera f. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata form of the balsamifera species, is a variant of the genus Humiria native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, balsamic scent, which is produced by specialized glandular structures on its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped with pointed tips, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This form is valued for its unique fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
