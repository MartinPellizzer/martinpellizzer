Hymenocarpos circinnatus subsp. nummularius

Hymenocarpos circinnatus subsp.

nummularius, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa. It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, round, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
