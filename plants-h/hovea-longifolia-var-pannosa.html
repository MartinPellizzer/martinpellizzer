Hovea longifolia var. pannosa

Hovea longifolia var. pannosa, commonly known as the silky hovea, is a variant of the hovea species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its soft, silvery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

This variety is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.
