Hutchinsonia xanthotricha

Hutchinsonia xanthotricha, commonly known as the yellow-haired hutchinsonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces distinctive, hairy bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Hutchinsonia xanthotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
