Hakea pulchella

Hakea pulchella, commonly known as the pink hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from pink to red, which bloom during the winter months. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Hakea pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral displays.
