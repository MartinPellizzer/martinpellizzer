Hybanthus tenuifolius

Hybanthus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf hybanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its flowering period usually occurs during the wet season, and it relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

Hybanthus tenuifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
