Hoya pycnophylla

Hoya pycnophylla, commonly known as the compact hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Hoya pycnophylla is also valued for its slow growth and ease of propagation through stem cuttings.
