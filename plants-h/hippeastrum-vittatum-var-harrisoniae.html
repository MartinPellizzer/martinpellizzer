Hippeastrum vittatum var. harrisoniae

Hippeastrum vittatum var. harrisoniae, commonly known as the Harrison's spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns resembling spider webs. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that emerge from a bulb and can grow up to 2 feet in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
