Heuchera lakelae

Heuchera lakelae, commonly known as the Lakelae alumroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that range in color from deep red to purple, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Heuchera lakelae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add color to shady garden spaces.
