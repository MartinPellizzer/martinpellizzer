Habenaria viridis var. interjecta

Habenaria viridis var. interjecta, commonly known as the green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are often adorned with subtle purple or brown markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
