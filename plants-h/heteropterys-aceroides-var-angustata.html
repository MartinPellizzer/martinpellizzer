Heteropterys aceroides var. angustata

Heteropterys aceroides var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huito, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The leaves and fruits of this plant have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
