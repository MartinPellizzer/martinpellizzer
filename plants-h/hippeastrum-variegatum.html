Hippeastrum variegatum

Hippeastrum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it a popular ornamental plant. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often variegated with white or green markings.

Hippeastrum variegatum thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to produce its vibrant flowers.
