Heppiella karsteniana

Heppiella karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant typically features small, needle-like leaves and produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers in early spring. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
