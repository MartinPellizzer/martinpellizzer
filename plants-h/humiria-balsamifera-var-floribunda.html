Humiria balsamifera var. floribunda

Humiria balsamifera var. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant balsamweed, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, balsamic scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
