Hamamelis nigra

Hamamelis nigra, commonly known as the common witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces capsule fruits that release seeds in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and the plant is valued for its medicinal properties.
