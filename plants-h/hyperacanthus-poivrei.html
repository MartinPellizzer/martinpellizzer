Hyperacanthus poivrei

Hyperacanthus poivrei, commonly known as the African mountain aloes, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky soils.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
