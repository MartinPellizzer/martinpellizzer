Hypolaena foliosa

Hypolaena foliosa, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Hypolaena foliosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
