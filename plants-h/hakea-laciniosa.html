Hakea laciniosa

Hakea laciniosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces striking, bottle-shaped yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea laciniosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
