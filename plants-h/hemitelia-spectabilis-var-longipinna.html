Hemitelia spectabilis var. longipinna

Hemitelia spectabilis var. longipinna, commonly known as the long-pinion hemitelia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a compact form and a tendency to branch profusely.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, shaded garden environments.
