Hakea commutata

Hakea commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of needle-like leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late summer, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Hakea commutata is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
