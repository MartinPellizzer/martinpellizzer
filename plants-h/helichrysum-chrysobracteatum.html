Helichrysum chrysobracteatum

Helichrysum chrysobracteatum, commonly known as the golden bract helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small sunflowers, which are composed of numerous tiny, tubular flowers. The plant is a perennial shrub with wiry, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
