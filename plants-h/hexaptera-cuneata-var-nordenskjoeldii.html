Hexaptera cuneata var. nordenskjoeldii

Hexaptera cuneata var. nordenskjoeldii, commonly known as the Norden's narrowleaf hexaptera, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and preference for cool, moist environments. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

This variety is notable for its resilience to extreme cold and its role in stabilizing soil in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
