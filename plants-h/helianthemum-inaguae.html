Helianthemum inaguae

Helianthemum inaguae, commonly known as the Inagua dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Bahamas.

It is a low-growing shrub with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Helianthemum inaguae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small insects.
