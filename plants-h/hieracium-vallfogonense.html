Hieracium vallfogonense

Hieracium vallfogonense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

Hieracium vallfogonense is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine environments.
