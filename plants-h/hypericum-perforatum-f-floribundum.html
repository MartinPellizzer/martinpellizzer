Hypericum perforatum f. floribundum

Hypericum perforatum f. floribundum, commonly known as the common St. John's wort, is a flowering plant characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and distinctive yellow dots on its leaves.

It is a member of the family Hypericaceae and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects.
