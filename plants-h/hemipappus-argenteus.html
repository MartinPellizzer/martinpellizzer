Hemipappus argenteus

Hemipappus argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by tightly packed disc flowers, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its silver-gray foliage is covered in fine, reflective hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Hemipappus argenteus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
