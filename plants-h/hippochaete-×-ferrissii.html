Hippochaete × ferrissii

Hippochaete × ferrissii, commonly known as the hybrid ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
