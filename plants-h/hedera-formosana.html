Hedera formosana

Hedera formosana, commonly known as the Formosan ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
