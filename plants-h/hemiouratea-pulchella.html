Hemiouratea pulchella

Hemiouratea pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically green with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
