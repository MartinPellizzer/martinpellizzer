Heliconia carmelae

Heliconia carmelae, commonly known as the Carmel heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Heliconiaceae family. Its growth habit includes tall, erect stems with broad, palm-like leaves that provide shade and habitat for various animals.

Heliconia carmelae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
