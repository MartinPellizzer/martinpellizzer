Hieracium chlorifolium subsp. pulchriforme

Hieracium chlorifolium subsp.

pulchriforme, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal rosettes of broadly ovate, dark green leaves with a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
