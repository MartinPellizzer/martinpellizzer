Hymenocallis godfreyi

Hymenocallis godfreyi, commonly known as the Godfrey's spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spidery appearance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from a bulbous base, and its flowers are often fragrant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental bulbs.

Hymenocallis godfreyi is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable climates.
