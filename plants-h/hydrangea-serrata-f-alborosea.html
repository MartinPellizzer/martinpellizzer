Hydrangea serrata f. alborosea

Hydrangea serrata f. alborosea, commonly known as the white-veined hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive white veins on pale green leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan, belonging to the Hydrangeaceae family. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
