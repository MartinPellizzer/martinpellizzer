Horridocactus nigricans

Horridocactus nigricans, commonly known as the black nightshade cactus, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
