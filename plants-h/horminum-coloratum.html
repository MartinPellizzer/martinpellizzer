Horminum coloratum

Horminum coloratum, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that come in a variety of hues. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
