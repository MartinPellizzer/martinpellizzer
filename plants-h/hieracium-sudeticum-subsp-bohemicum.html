Hieracium sudeticum subsp. bohemicum

Hieracium sudeticum subsp.

bohemicum, commonly known as the Bohemian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is relatively hardy in various soil types.
