Helianthemum lavandulifolium subsp. thibaudii

Helianthemum lavandulifolium subsp.

thibaudii, commonly known as the Thibaud's rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring.

The plant has silvery-green foliage with a dense, compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
