Helichrysum panduratum

Helichrysum panduratum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a spherical or conical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
