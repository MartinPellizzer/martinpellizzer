Hieracium avkoense

Hieracium avkoense, commonly known as the Avko's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has small, bract-like leaves that surround the flower heads, and its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
