Hedyotis pulcherrima

Hedyotis pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden star or velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Hedyotis pulcherrima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
