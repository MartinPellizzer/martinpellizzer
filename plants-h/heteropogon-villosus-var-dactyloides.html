Heteropogon villosus var. dactyloides

Heteropogon villosus var. dactyloides, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
