Hieracium tubulatum

Hieracium tubulatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its bright yellow flowers consist of both tubular and ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium tubulatum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable presence in natural ecosystems.
