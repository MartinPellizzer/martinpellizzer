Hibiscus carsonii

Hibiscus carsonii, commonly known as Carson's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Hibiscus carsonii blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
