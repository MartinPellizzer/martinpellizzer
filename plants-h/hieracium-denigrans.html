Hieracium denigrans

Hieracium denigrans, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

Hieracium denigrans is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
