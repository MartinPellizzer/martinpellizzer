Hieracium calcaratum

Hieracium calcaratum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium calcaratum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
