Huperzia chinensis subsp. miyoshiana

Huperzia chinensis subsp.

miyoshiana, commonly known as the Miya's clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern.

This plant is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and parts of China, where it thrives in moist, shaded forest environments. The sporophylls, which are the leaf-like structures that bear spores, are distinctive and contribute to its unique appearance. Huperzia chinensis subsp.

miyoshiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also of interest in botanical research due to its evolutionary significance.
