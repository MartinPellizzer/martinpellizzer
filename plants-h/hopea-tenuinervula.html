Hopea tenuinervula

Hopea tenuinervula, commonly known as the narrow-veined hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves with distinctively narrow veins. It typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests and is valued for its durable wood.

Hopea tenuinervula plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
