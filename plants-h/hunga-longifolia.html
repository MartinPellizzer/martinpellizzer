Hunga longifolia

Hunga longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hunga, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands. Its leaves are typically a deep green, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
