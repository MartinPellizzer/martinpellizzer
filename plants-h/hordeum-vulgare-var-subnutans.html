Hordeum vulgare var. subnutans

Hordeum vulgare var. subnutans, commonly known as the drooping barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its distinctive drooping inflorescence.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features slender, hollow stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are elongated and blade-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that hang downward, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.
