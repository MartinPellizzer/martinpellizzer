Hieracium glanduliferum subsp. hololeptum

Hieracium glanduliferum subsp.

hololeptum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to yellow ray florets. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
