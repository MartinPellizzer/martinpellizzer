Hieracium incrassatiforme subsp. apoleptum

Hieracium incrassatiforme subsp.

apoleptum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green flower heads.

The leaves are typically rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
