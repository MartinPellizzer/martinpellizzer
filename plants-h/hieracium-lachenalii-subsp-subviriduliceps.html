Hieracium lachenalii subsp. subviriduliceps

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

subviriduliceps, commonly known as the green-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow to orange composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a single head. This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly greenish-yellow flower heads, which give it its common name.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.
