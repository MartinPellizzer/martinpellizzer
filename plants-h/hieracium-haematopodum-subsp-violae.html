Hieracium haematopodum subsp. violae

Hieracium haematopodum subsp.

violae, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic weed in some regions.
