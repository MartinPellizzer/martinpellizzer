Hemionitis lyngholmii

Hemionitis lyngholmii, commonly known as the Lyngholm's hemionitis, is a rare and specialized aquatic fern native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the margins of lakes. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are adapted for efficient water absorption and gas exchange. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
