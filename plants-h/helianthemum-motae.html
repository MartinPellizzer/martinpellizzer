Helianthemum motae

Helianthemum motae, commonly known as the Canary Island rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
