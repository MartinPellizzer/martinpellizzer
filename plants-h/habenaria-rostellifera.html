Habenaria rostellifera

Habenaria rostellifera, commonly known as the masked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a unique, mask-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
