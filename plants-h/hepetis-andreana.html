Hepetis andreana

Hepetis andreana, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are tuberous and fibrous.

Hepetis andreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
