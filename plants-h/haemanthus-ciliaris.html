Haemanthus ciliaris

Haemanthus ciliaris, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, cylindrical spikes of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
