Haloragis lambertii

Haloragis lambertii, commonly known as Lambert's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate levels of salinity.

Haloragis lambertii plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
