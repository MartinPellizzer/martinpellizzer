Hutera pseudoerucastrum subsp. cintrana

Hutera pseudoerucastrum subsp.

cintrana, commonly known as the Cintrana subshrub, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its natural habitat.
