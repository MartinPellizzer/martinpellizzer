Hydrangea aspera subsp. strigosa

Hydrangea aspera subsp.

strigosa, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH.

The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
