Heptapleurum calyptratum

Heptapleurum calyptratum, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
