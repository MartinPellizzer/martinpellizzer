Heleochloa borszczowii

Heleochloa borszczowii, commonly known as the Borszczowii grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Heleochloa borszczowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dryland gardens.
