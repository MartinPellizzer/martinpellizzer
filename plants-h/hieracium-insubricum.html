Hieracium insubricum

Hieracium insubricum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, particularly in temperate regions of Europe. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, forming a distinctive composite inflorescence.

Hieracium insubricum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain environments.
