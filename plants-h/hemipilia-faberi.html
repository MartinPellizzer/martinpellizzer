Hemipilia faberi

Hemipilia faberi, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are tuberous and used in traditional medicine.

Hemipilia faberi is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
