Hieracium salevense subsp. pubifrons

Hieracium salevense subsp.

pubifrons, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth form and is adapted to the harsh environments of mountainous regions.
