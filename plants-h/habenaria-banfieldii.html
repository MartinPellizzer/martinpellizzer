Habenaria banfieldii

Habenaria banfieldii, commonly known as the Banfield's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or wetlands, where it can thrive in the humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to white in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
