Hoita rigida

Hoita rigida, commonly known as the rigid hoita, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hoita rigida is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
