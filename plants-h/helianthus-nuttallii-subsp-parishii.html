Helianthus nuttallii subsp. parishii

Helianthus nuttallii subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Parish's sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its robust growth and large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to wet soils. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the stems are sturdy and often branched.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
