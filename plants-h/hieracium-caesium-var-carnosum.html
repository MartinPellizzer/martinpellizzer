Hieracium caesium var. carnosum

Hieracium caesium var. carnosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, especially along the nodes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a tendency to be covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
