Hypericum bonaparteae

Hypericum bonaparteae, commonly known as the Bonaparte's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually small, five-petaled, and appear in clusters, often with prominent yellow stamens.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
