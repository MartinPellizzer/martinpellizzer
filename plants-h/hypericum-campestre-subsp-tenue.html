Hypericum campestre subsp. tenue

Hypericum campestre subsp.

tenue, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
