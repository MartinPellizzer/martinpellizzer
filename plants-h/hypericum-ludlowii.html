Hypericum ludlowii

Hypericum ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hypericum ludlowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
