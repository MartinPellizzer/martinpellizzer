Hippocratea obtusifolia var. eminiana

Hippocratea obtusifolia var. eminiana, commonly known as the Emine's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and sometimes red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
