Hordeum dilatatum

Hordeum dilatatum, commonly known as the slender wild barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Hordeum dilatatum has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays a role in ecological succession and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
