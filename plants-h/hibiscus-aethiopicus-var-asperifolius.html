Hibiscus aethiopicus var. asperifolius

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
