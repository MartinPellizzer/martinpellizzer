Hieracium oxyodontophorum

Hieracium oxyodontophorum, commonly known as the sharp-toothed hawkweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. Its stems are usually upright and can become woody with age.

Hieracium oxyodontophorum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
