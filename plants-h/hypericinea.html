Hypericinea

Hypericinea, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are composed of four petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often slightly hairy.

Hypericinea contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
