Hyptis perbullata

Hyptis perbullata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis perbullata is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
