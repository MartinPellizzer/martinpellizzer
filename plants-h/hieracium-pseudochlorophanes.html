Hieracium pseudochlorophanes

Hieracium pseudochlorophanes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers are bright yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purplish hue on the underside.
