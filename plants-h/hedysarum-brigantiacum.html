Hedysarum brigantiacum

Hedysarum brigantiacum, commonly known as the Brigantine milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of California and Nevada. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Hedysarum brigantiacum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
