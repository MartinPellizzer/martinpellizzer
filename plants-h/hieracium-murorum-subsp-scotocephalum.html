Hieracium murorum subsp. scotocephalum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

scotocephalum, commonly known as the dark-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the flowers are characterized by their dark brown to blackish sepals.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
