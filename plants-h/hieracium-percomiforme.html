Hieracium percomiforme

Hieracium percomiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium percomiforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
