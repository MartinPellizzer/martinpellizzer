Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. nipholeucum

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

nipholeucum, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features upright stems with narrowly lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form.
