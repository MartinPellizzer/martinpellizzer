Hieracium pseudobupleuroides subsp. oxeilema

Hieracium pseudobupleuroides subsp.

oxeilema, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated margin, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
