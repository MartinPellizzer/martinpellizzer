Hieracium schmidtii subsp. basicrinuloides

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

basicrinuloides, commonly known as Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
