Hieracium camkorijense subsp. onosmopsis

Hieracium camkorijense subsp.

onosmopsis, commonly known as the Karakorum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
