Hymenopogon

Hymenopogon, commonly known as the beard grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and the presence of hairy structures, particularly on the stems and leaves. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. Hymenopogon species are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
