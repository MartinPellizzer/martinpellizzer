Halimium eriocephalum var. laevigatum

Halimium eriocephalum var. laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-hairy hound's-tongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and a distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
