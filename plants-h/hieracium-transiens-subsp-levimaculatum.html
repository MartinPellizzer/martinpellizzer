Hieracium transiens subsp. levimaculatum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

levimaculatum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and have a distinctive yellowish or pale green coloration.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.
