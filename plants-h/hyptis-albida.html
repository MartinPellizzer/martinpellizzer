Hyptis albida

Hyptis albida, commonly known as white hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Hyptis albida is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
