Hypericum baccharoides

Hypericum baccharoides, commonly known as the smooth St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Hippocastanaceae family and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied, though it is less commonly used than other Hypericum species.
