Hieronyma mollis

Hieronyma mollis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *H. mollis*, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, round seeds.
