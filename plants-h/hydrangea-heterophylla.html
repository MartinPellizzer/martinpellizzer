Hydrangea heterophylla

Hydrangea heterophylla, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which changes from needle-like leaves in cooler temperatures to broader, grass-like leaves in warmer conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value have made it a favored plant in horticulture.
