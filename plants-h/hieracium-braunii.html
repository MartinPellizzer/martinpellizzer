Hieracium braunii

Hieracium braunii, commonly known as Braun's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium braunii is known for its finely divided leaves and its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain areas.
