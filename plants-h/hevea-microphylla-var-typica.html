Hevea microphylla var. typica

Hevea microphylla var. typica, commonly known as the small-leaved rubber tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces latex, which is harvested for industrial and commercial use. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its economic value rather than ornamental purposes.
