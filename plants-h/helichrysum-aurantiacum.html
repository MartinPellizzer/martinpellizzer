Helichrysum aurantiacum

Helichrysum aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact for a long time after blooming, giving it the common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in xeriscaping.
