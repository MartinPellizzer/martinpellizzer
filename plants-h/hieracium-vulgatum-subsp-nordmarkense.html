Hieracium vulgatum subsp. nordmarkense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

nordmarkense, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features upright, hairy stems with alternate leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
