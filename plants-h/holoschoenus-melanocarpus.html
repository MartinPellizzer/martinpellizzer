Holoschoenus melanocarpus

Holoschoenus melanocarpus, commonly known as the black-fruited sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and dark, edible berries that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife.
