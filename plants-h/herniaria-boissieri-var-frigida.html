Herniaria boissieri var. frigida

Herniaria boissieri var. frigida, commonly known as the cold herniaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
