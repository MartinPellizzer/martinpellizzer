Hieracium compsophyllum

Hieracium compsophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are bright yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium compsophyllum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
