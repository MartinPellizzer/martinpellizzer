Hieracium anfractum subsp. barbareifolium

Hieracium anfractum subsp.

barbareifolium, commonly known as the barbareifolium hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
