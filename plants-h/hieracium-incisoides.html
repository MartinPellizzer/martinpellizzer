Hieracium incisoides

Hieracium incisoides, commonly known as the cut-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Hieracium incisoides is known for its aggressive growth and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
