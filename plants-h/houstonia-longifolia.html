Houstonia longifolia

Houstonia longifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
