Haemanthus micrantherus

Haemanthus micrantherus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved haemanthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, umbellate inflorescences with white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
