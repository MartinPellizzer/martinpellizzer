Hyparrhenia acutispathacea

Hyparrhenia acutispathacea, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
