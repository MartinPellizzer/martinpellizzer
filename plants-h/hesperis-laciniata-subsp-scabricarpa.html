Hesperis laciniata subsp. scabricarpa

Hesperis laciniata subsp.

scabricarpa, commonly known as the rough-leaved sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale purple or white flowers.

The leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture, which gives the subspecies its common name. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued in gardens for its attractive blooms and is a member of the Brassicaceae family.
