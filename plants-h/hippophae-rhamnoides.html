Hippophae rhamnoides

Hippophae rhamnoides, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its orange-yellow berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, alternate leaves and produces flowers that are typically yellow or green. It thrives in harsh environments, including coastal areas and arid regions, due to its drought tolerance.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.
