Hieracium incisum subsp. villosipes

Hieracium incisum subsp.

villosipes, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its flowering period occurs in summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets.
