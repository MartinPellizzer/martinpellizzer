Hypoxis baurii var. platypetala

Hypoxis baurii var. platypetala, commonly known as the African crown imperial, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, low-growing base. The plant produces showy, orange-yellow flower heads that resemble a crown, with numerous small flowers clustered around a central stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
