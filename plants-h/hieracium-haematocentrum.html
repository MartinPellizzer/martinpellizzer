Hieracium haematocentrum

Hieracium haematocentrum, commonly known as the blood-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small tubular florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows, roadsides, and waste grounds, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine hairs.

Hieracium haematocentrum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
