Hieracium × sparsiforme

Hieracium × sparsiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has rough, hairy leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a widespread species in Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
