Henningia roseola

Henningia roseola, commonly known as the rose-like heath, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces small, pink to rose-colored flowers. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blooms.

Henningia roseola is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
