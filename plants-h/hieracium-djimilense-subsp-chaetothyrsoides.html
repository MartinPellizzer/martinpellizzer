Hieracium djimilense subsp. chaetothyrsoides

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

chaetothyrsoides, commonly known as the Sudanese hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Horn of Africa. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and long, bristly phyllaries.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of dandelion-like plants.
