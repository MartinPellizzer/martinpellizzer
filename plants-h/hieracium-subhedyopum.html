Hieracium subhedyopum

Hieracium subhedyopum, commonly known as the purple meadowhawk, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its pale purple to reddish flower heads, which are typically discoid in structure. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, often with hairy surfaces. It thrives in meadows and open grasslands, preferring moist to moderately dry soils.

Hieracium subhedyopum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
