Hedeoma medium

Hedeoma medium, commonly known as American hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hedeoma medium thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is also known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
