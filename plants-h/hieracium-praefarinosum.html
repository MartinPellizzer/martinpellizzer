Hieracium praefarinosum

Hieracium praefarinosum, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellowish to orange in color and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
