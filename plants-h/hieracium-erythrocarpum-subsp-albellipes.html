Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. albellipes

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

albellipes, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive red-tinged calyx.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, slender flower stems and unique fruit structure.
