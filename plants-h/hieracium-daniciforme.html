Hieracium daniciforme

Hieracium daniciforme, commonly known as the Danish hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils across Europe. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
