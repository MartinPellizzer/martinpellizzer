Heinsia

Heinsia, commonly known as the "mountain snowbell," is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plants typically have woody stems and are adapted to grow in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Heinsia species are valued for their ornamental appeal, with flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue.

They are often cultivated in gardens for their unique appearance and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
