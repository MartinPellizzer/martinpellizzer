Hieracium glaucum subsp. limonense

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

limonense, commonly known as the lemon-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dense, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, composite flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Hieracium genus, with specific adaptations to its native environment.
