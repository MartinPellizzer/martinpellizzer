Hieracium aetolicum

Hieracium aetolicum, commonly known as the Aetolian hawkweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce small, yellowish to reddish flowers. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
