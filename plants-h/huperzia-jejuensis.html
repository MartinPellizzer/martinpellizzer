It is native to the island of Jeju in South Korea and is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit. The plant has simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Huperzia jejuensis is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
