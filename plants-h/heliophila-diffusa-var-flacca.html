Heliophila diffusa var. flacca

Heliophila diffusa var. flacca, commonly known as the slender heliophyte, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wet meadows and marshy areas in Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in loose clusters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is typically found in temperate climates.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
