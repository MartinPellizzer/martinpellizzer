Hieracium niphobium subsp. glaucophylloides

Hieracium niphobium subsp.

glaucophylloides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to pale green hairs.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
