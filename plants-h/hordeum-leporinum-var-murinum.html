Hordeum leporinum var. murinum

Hordeum leporinum var. murinum, commonly known as mouse barley, is a wild grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Mouse barley is often found in association with other grasses and is adapted to temperate climates.
