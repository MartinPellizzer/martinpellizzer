Hieracium maculatum subsp. pollichiae

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

pollichiae, commonly known as the Pollich's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
