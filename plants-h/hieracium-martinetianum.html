Hieracium martinetianum

Hieracium martinetianum, commonly known as the Martinet's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has small, bracts that surround the flower head, and its growth habit is typically low and spreading.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in parts of its range.
