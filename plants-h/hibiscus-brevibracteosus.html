Hibiscus brevibracteosus

Hibiscus brevibracteosus, commonly known as the short-bract hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it thrives in warm, arid environments. Hibiscus brevibracteosus is often found in rocky soils and can grow as a shrub or small tree.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
