Hippophae littoralis

Hippophae littoralis, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and in coastal dunes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Hippophae littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
