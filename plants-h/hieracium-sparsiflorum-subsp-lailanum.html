Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. lailanum

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

lailanum, commonly known as the Lailan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
