Hieracium cupulatum

Hieracium cupulatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium cupulatum is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and seeds, making it an aggressive weed in some regions.
