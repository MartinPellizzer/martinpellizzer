Homalobus

Homalobus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a waxy coating. Homalobus species are popular as houseplants due to their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their ornamental value.

They are also valued for their ability to produce offsets, which makes propagation relatively easy.
