Hippeastrum morelianum

Hippeastrum morelianum, commonly known as the Morelia amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are evergreen and can grow up to 1 meter in length. Its bulbous stem is fleshy and stores water, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soil.

Hippeastrum morelianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
