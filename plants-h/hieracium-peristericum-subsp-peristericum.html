Hieracium peristericum subsp. peristericum

Hieracium peristericum subsp.

peristericum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a silver or grayish hue, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
