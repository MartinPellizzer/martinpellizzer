Hippochaete hyemalis var. californica

Hippochaete hyemalis var. californica, commonly known as the California winter dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in winter and early spring, and its leaves are typically basal, hairy, and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
