Hieracium auratum

Hieracium auratum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically disk-shaped with prominent central cones. The plant has hairy, often reddish stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Hieracium auratum is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
