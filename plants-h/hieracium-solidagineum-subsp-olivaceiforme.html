Hieracium solidagineum subsp. olivaceiforme

Hieracium solidagineum subsp.

olivaceiforme, commonly known as the olive-green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular florets, typical of the genus Hieracium. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in meadows and grassy areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
