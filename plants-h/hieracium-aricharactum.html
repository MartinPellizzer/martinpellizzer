Hieracium aricharactum

Hieracium aricharactum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, branching stems that bear yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has fibrous roots and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium aricharactum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
