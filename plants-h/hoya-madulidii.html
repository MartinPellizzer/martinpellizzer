Hoya madulidii

Hoya madulidii, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its waxy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white blossoms that bloom in the summer months. Hoya madulidii is a trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
