Hieracium sparsum subsp. grisebachii

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
