Hymenoxys anthemoides

Hymenoxys anthemoides, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Hymenoxys anthemoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
