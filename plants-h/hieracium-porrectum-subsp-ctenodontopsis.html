Hieracium porrectum subsp. ctenodontopsis

Hieracium porrectum subsp.

ctenodontopsis, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable component of certain ecosystems.
