Hamelinia veratroides

Hamelinia veratroides, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hamelinia veratroides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and waste areas.
