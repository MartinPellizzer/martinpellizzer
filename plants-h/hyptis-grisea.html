Hyptis grisea

Hyptis grisea, commonly known as gray hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hyptis grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
