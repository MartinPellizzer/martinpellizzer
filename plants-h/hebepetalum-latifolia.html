Hebepetalum latifolia

Hebepetalum latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock gardens. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Hebepetalum latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
