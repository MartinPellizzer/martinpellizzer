Homalomena major

Homalomena major, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage, making it a popular choice for water gardens and indoor cultivation.
