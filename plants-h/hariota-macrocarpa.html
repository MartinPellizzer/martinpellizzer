Hariota macrocarpa

Hariota macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hariota, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Hariota macrocarpa is often found in tropical and subtropical forest environments.
