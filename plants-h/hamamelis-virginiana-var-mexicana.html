Hamamelis virginiana var. mexicana

Hamamelis virginiana var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
