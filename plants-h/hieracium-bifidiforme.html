Hieracium bifidiforme

Hieracium bifidiforme, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that allow it to spread across the ground.

Hieracium bifidiforme is typically found in moist or calcareous soils and is considered a perennial species.
