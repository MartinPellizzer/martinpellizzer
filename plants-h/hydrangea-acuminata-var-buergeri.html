Hydrangea acuminata var. buergeri

Hydrangea acuminata var. buergeri, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea or mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are typically dark green and turn red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to a range of environmental conditions.
