Hieracium fallacinum subsp. vulsum

Hieracium fallacinum subsp.

vulsum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, with a distinctive involucral bract. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a woolly texture, while the stems are reddish and slightly hairy.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to produce a large number of seeds.
