Haplopappus spinulosus subsp. cotula

Haplopappus spinulosus subsp.

cotula, commonly known as the hooked pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hooked pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.
