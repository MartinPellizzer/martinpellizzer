Hordeum brachyantherum subsp. californicum

Hordeum brachyantherum subsp.

californicum, commonly known as California wild barley, is a grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and rough, with a bluish-green color, and it produces pale green to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is often found in California and adjacent regions, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological resilience.
