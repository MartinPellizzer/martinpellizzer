Hieracium recticaule

Hieracium recticaule, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium recticaule produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
