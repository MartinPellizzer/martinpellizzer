Hieracium crocatifrons

Hieracium crocatifrons, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant produces numerous small, disc-like flower heads that are visited by various pollinators.

Its growth habit is typically prostrate or ascending, with stems that often branch near the base.
