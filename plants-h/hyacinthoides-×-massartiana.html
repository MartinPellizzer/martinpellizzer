Hyacinthoides × massartiana

Hyacinthoides × massartiana, commonly known as the bluebell, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to western Europe and is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm and features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

The bluebell is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in naturalized areas across its native range.
