Harpullia petiolaris subsp. moluccana

Harpullia petiolaris subsp.

moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan harpullia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate leaves with a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds. This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
