Helminthia glomerata

Helminthia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered helminth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Helminthia glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
