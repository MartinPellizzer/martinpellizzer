Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. glaucibifidum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

glaucibifidum, commonly known as the glaucous-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a bluish-gray waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disk-like structure.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural interest and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
