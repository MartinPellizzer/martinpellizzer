Hieracium filisquamum

Hieracium filisquamum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale yellow ray florets. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

Hieracium filisquamum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
