Heliconia buccinata

Heliconia buccinata, commonly known as the trumpet heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that resemble a cluster of trumpets. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant appearance. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a unique structure that attracts birds and insects for pollination.

Heliconia buccinata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
