Hypericum erectum var. wichurae

Hypericum erectum var. wichurae, commonly known as the erect St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly serrated margin. Its showy flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are yellow with prominent orange stamens, and are arranged in terminal clusters.

The plant is characterized by its medicinal properties, containing hypericin and hyperforin, which have been studied for their potential antidepressant effects.
