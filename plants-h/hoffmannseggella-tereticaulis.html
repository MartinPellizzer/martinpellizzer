Hoffmannseggella tereticaulis

Hoffmannseggella tereticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hoffmannseggella, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Hoffmannseggella tereticaulis is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
