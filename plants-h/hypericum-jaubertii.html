Hypericum jaubertii

Hypericum jaubertii, commonly known as the golden St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Hypericum jaubertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
