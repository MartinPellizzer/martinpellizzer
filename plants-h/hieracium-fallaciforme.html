Hieracium fallaciforme

Hieracium fallaciforme, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate, rough to the touch, and have a serrated edge.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
