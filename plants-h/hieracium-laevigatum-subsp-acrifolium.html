Hieracium laevigatum subsp. acrifolium

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

acrifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas where it can spread through its creeping root system.
