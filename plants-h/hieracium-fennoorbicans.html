Hieracium fennoorbicans

Hieracium fennoorbicans, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as tundra, meadows, and rocky areas. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces showy yellow flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture adapted to cold climates.

Hieracium fennoorbicans is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
