Hibiscus ottoi

Hibiscus ottoi, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Hibiscus ottoi is characterized by its fibrous, woody stems and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The plant is well adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and plays a role in the local ecosystem.
