Hieracium tanyptortum

Hieracium tanyptortum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies.

Hieracium tanyptortum is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
