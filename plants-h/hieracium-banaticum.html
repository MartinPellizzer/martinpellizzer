Hieracium banaticum

Hieracium banaticum, commonly known as Banat hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium banaticum is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
