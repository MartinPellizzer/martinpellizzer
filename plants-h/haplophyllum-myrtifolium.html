Haplophyllum myrtifolium

Haplophyllum myrtifolium, commonly known as the roseleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils with minimal water.

Haplophyllum myrtifolium is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient species in desert environments.
