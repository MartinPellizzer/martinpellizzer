Harrisia pomanensis subsp. tarijensis

Harrisia pomanensis subsp.

tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis nightshade, is a cactus species native to South America, particularly in Bolivia and Peru. It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are often white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
