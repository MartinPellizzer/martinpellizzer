Hallia monophylla

Hallia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf hallea, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its singular, large, and often heart-shaped leaf that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it conserve water in its arid environment.

Hallia monophylla is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
