Hieracium apicilimbare

Hieracium apicilimbare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
