Huperzia curiosa

Huperzia curiosa, commonly known as the curious huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often clustered fronds that are typically green and covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its sporophylls are arranged in a unique spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Huperzia curiosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid indoor setting.
