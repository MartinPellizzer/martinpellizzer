Hyptis canescens var. arvensis

Hyptis canescens var. arvensis, commonly known as rough hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in traditional medicine and horticulture.
