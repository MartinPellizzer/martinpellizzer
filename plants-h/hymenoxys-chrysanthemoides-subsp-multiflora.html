Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides subsp. multiflora

Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. It features showy, bright orange to yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are upright and can reach heights of up to 3 feet, supporting multiple flower heads.

The species is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
