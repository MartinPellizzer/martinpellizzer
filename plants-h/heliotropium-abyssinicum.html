Heliotropium abyssinicum

Heliotropium abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
