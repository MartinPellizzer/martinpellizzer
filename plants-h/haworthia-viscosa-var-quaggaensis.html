Haworthia viscosa var. quaggaensis

Haworthia viscosa var. quaggaensis, commonly known as the quagga haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or translucent markings. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or yellow during the warmer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
