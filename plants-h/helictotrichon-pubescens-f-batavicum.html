Helictotrichon pubescens f. batavicum

Helictotrichon pubescens f. batavicum, commonly known as the Batavian hairy grass, is a variant of the hairy bluegrass species.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its foliage is often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
