Hyparrhenia confinis var. pellita

Hyparrhenia confinis var. pellita, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and tufted growth habit, typical of many sedges. The plant thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and narrower leaves compared to other varieties within the Hyparrhenia genus.
