Hypericum euphorbioides var. minus

Hypericum euphorbioides var. minus, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
