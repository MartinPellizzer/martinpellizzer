Hypericopsis persica var. angustifolia

Hypericopsis persica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant in the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It has hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
