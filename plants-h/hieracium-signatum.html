Hieracium signatum

Hieracium signatum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped or kidney-shaped. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as fruits.

Hieracium signatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
