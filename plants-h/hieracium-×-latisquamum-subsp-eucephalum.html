Hieracium × latisquamum subsp. eucephalum

Hieracium × latisquamum subsp.

eucephalum, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
