Heteropappus hispidus var. elongatus

Heteropappus hispidus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stalked horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually hairy and linear, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem.

The variety elongatus is distinguished by its longer peduncles compared to other varieties of Heteropappus hispidus.
