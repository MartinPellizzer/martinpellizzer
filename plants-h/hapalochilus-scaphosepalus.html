Hapalochilus scaphosepalus

Hapalochilus scaphosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sheath orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in the alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and sheathing, forming a tight rosette around the stem.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
