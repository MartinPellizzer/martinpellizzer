Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. fissifurcum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

fissifurcum, commonly known as the fissured hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and erect, branched stems that bear yellow disk flowers. Its leaves are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fissured appearance, giving it its subspecies name.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
