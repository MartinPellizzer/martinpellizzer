Hyptis mixta

Hyptis mixta, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants with medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or silver, giving it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

Hyptis mixta is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
