Hieracium pilosissimum subsp. macropannosum

Hieracium pilosissimum subsp.

macropannosum, commonly known as the large-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
