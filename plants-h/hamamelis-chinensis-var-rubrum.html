Hamamelis chinensis var. rubrum

Hamamelis chinensis var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-flowered witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
