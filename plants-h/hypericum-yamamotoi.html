Hypericum yamamotoi

Hypericum yamamotoi, commonly known as Yamamoto's St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of five petals and numerous stamens.

Hypericum yamamotoi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
