Heuchera micrantha var. macropetala

Heuchera micrantha var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, heart-shaped leaves that range in color from deep red to purple and green, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and the delicate, showy blooms that appear on tall, upright stems.
