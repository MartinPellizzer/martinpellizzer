Hyoscyamus longipedunculatus

Hyoscyamus longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long pedunculate henbane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces distinctive, showy flowers with five lobes, typically in shades of purple or violet. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains numerous small, oblong seeds.

Hyoscyamus longipedunculatus is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids such as scopolamine and hyoscyamine.
