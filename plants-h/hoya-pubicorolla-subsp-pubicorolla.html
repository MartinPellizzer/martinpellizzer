Hoya pubicorolla subsp. pubicorolla

Hoya pubicorolla subsp.

pubicorolla, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers.

The plant produces fragrant, coral-pink blooms that are often borne on long, trailing stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
