Hieracium alpinum subsp. hapalophyton

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

hapalophyton, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators.

It is relatively small in stature, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
