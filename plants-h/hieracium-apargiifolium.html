Hieracium apargiifolium

Hieracium apargiifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and often lobed, with a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium apargiifolium is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
