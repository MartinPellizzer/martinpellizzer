Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. anisopleura

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

anisopleura, commonly known as the African date palm, is a robust, fan-shaped palm native to southern Africa. It features a tall, slender trunk with a fibrous, woody exterior and produces large, fan-like leaves that are often used for thatching.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is well adapted to arid conditions.

Its roots are extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.
