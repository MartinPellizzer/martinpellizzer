Hemisphace

Hemisphace, commonly known as the half-face orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with petals that form a hemispherical shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hemisphace species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
