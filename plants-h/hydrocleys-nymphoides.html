Hydrocleys nymphoides

Hydrocleys nymphoides, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy surface to repel water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday. Its rhizomes grow underwater, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water.
