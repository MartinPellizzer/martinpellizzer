Hakea erecta

Hakea erecta, commonly known as the erect hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features distinctive cylindrical or club-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, often appearing in late winter to spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a unique appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Hakea erecta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
