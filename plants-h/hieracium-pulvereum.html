Hieracium pulvereum

Hieracium pulvereum, commonly known as the dusty hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a dusty appearance. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium pulvereum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
