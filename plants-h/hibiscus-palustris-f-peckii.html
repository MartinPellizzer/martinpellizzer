Hibiscus palustris f. peckii

Hibiscus palustris f. peckii, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a botanical variant of the common swamp hibiscus.

It is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
