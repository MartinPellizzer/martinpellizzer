Hieracium oligodon

Hieracium oligodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Hieracium oligodon is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
