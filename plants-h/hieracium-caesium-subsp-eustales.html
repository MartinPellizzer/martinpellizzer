Hieracium caesium subsp. eustales

Hieracium caesium subsp.

eustales, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
