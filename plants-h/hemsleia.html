Hemsleia

Hemsleia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. Hemsleia species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their compact growth habit and ability to thrive in indoor environments. They have thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture, and their roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The genus includes several species, such as Hemsleia parviflora, which is popular for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
