Hieracium contractiforme

Hieracium contractiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium contractiforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to moderate grazing.
