Heliconia ramonensis var. xanthotricha

Heliconia ramonensis var. xanthotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that consist of numerous fleshy, boat-shaped bracts. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within the bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in coastal forests and mangrove ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
