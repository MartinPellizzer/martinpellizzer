Hymenolaena latifolia

Hymenolaena latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf hymenolaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open woodlands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Hymenolaena latifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
