Hymenophyllum portoricense

Hymenophyllum portoricense, commonly known as the Atlantic spleenworts, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like fronds that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sorocarps, are clustered in small, cup-like formations on the underside of the fronds.

This species is notable for its high degree of morphological variation and its adaptation to specific microhabitats.
