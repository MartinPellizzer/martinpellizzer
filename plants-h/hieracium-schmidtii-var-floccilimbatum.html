Hieracium schmidtii var. floccilimbatum

Hieracium schmidtii var. floccilimbatum, commonly known as the fluff-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, white flocculent hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals and are arranged in a single, erect inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
