Hypochaeris lanata

Hypochaeris lanata, commonly known as the woolly weed or hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various temperate regions.
