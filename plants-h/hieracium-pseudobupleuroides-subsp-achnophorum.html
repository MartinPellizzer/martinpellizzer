Hieracium pseudobupleuroides subsp. achnophorum

Hieracium pseudobupleuroides subsp.

achnophorum, commonly known as the sand hill hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky slopes.

The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by yellow ray florets.

It is adapted to withstand drought and nutrient-poor conditions, making it a characteristic species of its ecological niche.
