Hymenophyllum contortum

Hymenophyllum contortum, commonly known as the twisted shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that often curl and twist. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of minute, hair-like structures called trichomes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a popular species among fern enthusiasts and botanists.
