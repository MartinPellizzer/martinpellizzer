Hedyotis thysanota

Hedyotis thysanota, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false pipsissewa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Hedyotis thysanota is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
