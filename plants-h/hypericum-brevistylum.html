Hypericum brevistylum

Hypericum brevistylum, commonly known as the short-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has woody stems covered in fine, thread-like hairs, and its leaves are opposite, oval, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hypericum brevistylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
