Hibiscus bifurcatus var. bicornis

Hibiscus bifurcatus var. bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or pink. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
