Hypericum webbii

Hypericum webbii, commonly known as the webb's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Hypericum webbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
