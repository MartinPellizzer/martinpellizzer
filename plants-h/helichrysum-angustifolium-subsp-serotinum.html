Helichrysum angustifolium subsp. serotinum

Helichrysum angustifolium subsp.

serotinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features wiry, branched stems covered in dense clusters of small, yellowish to golden flowers.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
