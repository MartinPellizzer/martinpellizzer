Hemionitis lonchophora

Hemionitis lonchophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Hemionitis lonchophora plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and stabilizing water quality.
