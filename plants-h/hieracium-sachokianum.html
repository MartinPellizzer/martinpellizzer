Hieracium sachokianum

Hieracium sachokianum, commonly known as the Sachokian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like structures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
