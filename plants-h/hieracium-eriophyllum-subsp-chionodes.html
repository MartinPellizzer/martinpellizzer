Hieracium eriophyllum subsp. chionodes

Hieracium eriophyllum subsp.

chionodes, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
