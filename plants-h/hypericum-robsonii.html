Hypericum robsonii

Hypericum robsonii, commonly known as Robson's St. John's Wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum robsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
