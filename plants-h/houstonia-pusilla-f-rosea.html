Houstonia pusilla f. rosea

Houstonia pusilla f. rosea, commonly known as the pink bluets, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to soft pink, depending on the cultivar. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, ornamental plant.
