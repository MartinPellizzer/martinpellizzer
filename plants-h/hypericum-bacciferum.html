Hypericum bacciferum

Hypericum bacciferum, commonly known as rosehip plant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish stems, yellow flowers, and distinctive orange-red fruits that contain high levels of vitamin C. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and the fruits ripen in autumn, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
