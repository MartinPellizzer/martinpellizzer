Hibbertia billardierei var. parviflora

Hibbertia billardierei var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hibbertia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Hibbertia billardierei var. parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
