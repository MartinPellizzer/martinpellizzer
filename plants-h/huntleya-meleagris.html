Huntleya meleagris

Huntleya meleagris, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to yellow, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Huntleya meleagris is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
