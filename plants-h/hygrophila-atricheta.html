Hygrophila atricheta

Hygrophila atricheta, commonly known as the velvet stem hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in wet or semi-aquatic environments, making it a popular choice for water gardens and ponds.

Its adaptability to moist conditions and ornamental appeal contribute to its cultivation in both natural and artificial aquatic settings.
