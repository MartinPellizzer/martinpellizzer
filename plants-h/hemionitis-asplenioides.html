Hemionitis asplenioides

Hemionitis asplenioides, commonly known as the silver thread fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the southeastern United States.

It has slender, arching fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
