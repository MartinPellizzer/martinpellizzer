Hieracium praecox subsp. basicrinum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

basicrinum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, sticky hairs. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
