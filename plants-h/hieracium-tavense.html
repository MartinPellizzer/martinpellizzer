Hieracium tavense

Hieracium tavense, commonly known as the Tavus hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium tavense is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
