Hieracium verbascifolium subsp. thapsoides

Hieracium verbascifolium subsp.

thapsoides, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
