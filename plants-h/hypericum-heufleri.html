Hypericum heufleri

Hypericum heufleri, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features slender, prostrate stems that form a dense mat, often covering rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum heufleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
