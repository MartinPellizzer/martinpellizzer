Hicorius ovata var. fraxinifolia

Hicorius ovata var. fraxinifolia, commonly known as the fraxinifolia hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus genus characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It typically exhibits broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface that can range in color from dark green to a vibrant bronze or reddish hue. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
