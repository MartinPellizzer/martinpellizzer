Hypericum perfoliatum var. annulatum

Hypericum perfoliatum var. annulatum, commonly known as the annular St. John's wort, is a variant of the St. John's wort species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular leaves that are often fused around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
