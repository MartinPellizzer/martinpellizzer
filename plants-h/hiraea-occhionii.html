Hiraea occhionii

Hiraea occhionii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically golden yellow in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms that appear in the spring and early summer.
