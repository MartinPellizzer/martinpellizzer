Hepetis altensteinii var. gigantea

Hepetis altensteinii var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant hepetis, is a striking member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is noted for its robust growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
