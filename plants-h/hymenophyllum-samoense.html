Hymenophyllum samoense

Hymenophyllum samoense, commonly known as the Samoan shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its native ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
