Hieracium mariolense

Hieracium mariolense, commonly known as the Mariolense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium mariolense is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
