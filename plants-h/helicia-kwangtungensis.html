Helicia kwangtungensis

Helicia kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong helicia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly Guangdong province, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green surface.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are clustered in inflorescences and are notable for their yellowish-white petals and prominent stamens.
