Hedysarum cinereosericeum

Hedysarum cinereosericeum, commonly known as the silver-haired vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pale green to silver-gray leaves. The plant produces delicate, pea-like flowers that range from white to pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Hedysarum cinereosericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
