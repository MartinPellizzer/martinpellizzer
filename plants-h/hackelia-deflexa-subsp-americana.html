Hackelia deflexa subsp. americana

Hackelia deflexa subsp.

americana, commonly known as American bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Hackelia deflexa subsp.

americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
