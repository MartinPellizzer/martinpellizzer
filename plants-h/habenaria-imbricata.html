Habenaria imbricata

Habenaria imbricata, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white to lilac-colored blooms that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique, wrinkled appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
