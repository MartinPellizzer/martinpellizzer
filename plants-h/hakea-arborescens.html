Hakea arborescens

Hakea arborescens, commonly known as the tree hakea, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often blue-green in color. It produces spikes of white or pink flowers in late winter to early spring.

Hakea arborescens is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for coastal and sandy soils.
