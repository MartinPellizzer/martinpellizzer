Hydrangea aspera var. angustifolia

Hydrangea aspera var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant has rough, serrated leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture and color to woodland gardens.
