Hieracium pringlei subvar. guadalajarense

Hieracium pringlei subvar. guadalajarense, commonly known as the Guadalajara hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

The species is considered rare and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
