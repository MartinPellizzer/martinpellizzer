Hauya quercetorum

Hauya quercetorum, commonly known as the golden oak, is a deciduous tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree produces small, acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Hauya quercetorum plays a vital role in its ecosystem, contributing to soil stability and supporting biodiversity.
