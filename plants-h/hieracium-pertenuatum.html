Hieracium pertenuatum

Hieracium pertenuatum, commonly known as slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium pertenuatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
