Hibiscus boryanus

Hibiscus boryanus, commonly known as the Japanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Hibiscus boryanus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
