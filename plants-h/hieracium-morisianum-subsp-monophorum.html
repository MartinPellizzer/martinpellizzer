Hieracium morisianum subsp. monophorum

Hieracium morisianum subsp.

monophorum, commonly known as the Morisian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has fibrous roots and produces a single flowering stem that bears numerous small, daisy-like flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
