Homalanthus concolor

Homalanthus concolor, commonly known as the white-flowered croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in warm climates.
