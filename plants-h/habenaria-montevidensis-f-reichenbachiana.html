Habenaria montevidensis f. reichenbachiana

Habenaria montevidensis f. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as marshes and forest floors.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
