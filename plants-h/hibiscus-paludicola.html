Hibiscus paludicola

Hibiscus paludicola, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Hibiscus paludicola is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 2 meters tall.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
