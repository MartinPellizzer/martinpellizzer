Helianthemum × pulverulentum

Helianthemum × pulverulentum, commonly known as the rock rose or cushion plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Helianthemum, which belongs to the family Cistaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in full sun. Its foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental value in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
