Habenaria ovalifolia

Habenaria ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically fragrant and attract various pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
