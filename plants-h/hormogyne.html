Hormogyne

Hormogyne, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often ornate inflorescence that resembles a ghostly or ethereal shape, giving it its common name. The plants typically have broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinct, sometimes fragrant, odor. Hormogyne species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

They are often cultivated for their striking appearance and are used in ornamental horticulture.
