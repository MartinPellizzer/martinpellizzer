Halophila ovalis subsp. hawaiiana

Halophila ovalis subsp.

hawaiiana, commonly known as Hawaiian sea grass, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal and estuarine environments. It is characterized by its ribbon-like, narrow leaves and is typically found in shallow, brackish waters.

This subspecies is adapted to saline conditions, which is reflected in its halophytic nature. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

The plant's reproductive structures are adapted for underwater pollination, contributing to its ecological significance in tropical and subtropical regions.
