Hieracium murorum subsp. fuscipes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

fuscipes, commonly known as the brown-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-orange flower heads that are surrounded by pale green to brown bracts.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This subspecies is native to Europe and has been introduced to parts of North America.
