Heterotheca floridana

Heterotheca floridana, commonly known as the Florida tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and linear, hairy leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca floridana is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
