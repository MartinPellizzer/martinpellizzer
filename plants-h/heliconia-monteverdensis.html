Heliconia monteverdensis

Heliconia monteverdensis, commonly known as the Monteverde heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is characterized by its striking, banana-like inflorescence that consists of bright red bracts surrounding clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors or on rocky outcrops. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
