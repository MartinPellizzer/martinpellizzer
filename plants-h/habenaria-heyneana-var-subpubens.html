Habenaria heyneana var. subpubens

Habenaria heyneana var. subpubens, commonly known as the hairy marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features a slender, often branched stem and produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
