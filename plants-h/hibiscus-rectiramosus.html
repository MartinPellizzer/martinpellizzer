Hibiscus rectiramosus

Hibiscus rectiramosus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus rectiramosus is often found in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
