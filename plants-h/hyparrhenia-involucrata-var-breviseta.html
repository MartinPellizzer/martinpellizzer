Hyparrhenia involucrata var. breviseta

Hyparrhenia involucrata var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-bristled flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in riparian zones.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
