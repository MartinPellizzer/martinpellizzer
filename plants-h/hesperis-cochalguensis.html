Hesperis cochalguensis

Hesperis cochalguensis, commonly known as the Balkan candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are basal, simple, and covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis cochalguensis is often found in meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
