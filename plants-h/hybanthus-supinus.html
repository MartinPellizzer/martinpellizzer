Hybanthus supinus

Hybanthus supinus, commonly known as the prostrate hybanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in a trailing or prostrate habit, often covering the ground with its numerous small leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and greenhouses.
