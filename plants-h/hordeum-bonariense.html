Hordeum bonariense

Hordeum bonariense, commonly known as wild barley, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, while the seeds are small and usually dispersed by wind.

Hordeum bonariense plays a role in ecological succession and is sometimes used in agricultural research due to its adaptability.
