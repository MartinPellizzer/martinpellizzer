Hyptis dictyodea

Hyptis dictyodea, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering its leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

Hyptis dictyodea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
