Hymenophyllum reductum

Hymenophyllum reductum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf filmy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its thin, translucent fronds that are often covered in a layer of fine, hair-like structures called rhizoids. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
