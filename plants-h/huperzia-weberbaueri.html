Huperzia weberbaueri

Huperzia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Huperzia weberbaueri reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes a distinct gametophyte stage.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and air-purifying properties.
