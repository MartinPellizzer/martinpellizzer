Hieracium congregantiforme

Hieracium congregantiforme, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a tight cluster at the base. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium congregantiforme is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some habitats.
