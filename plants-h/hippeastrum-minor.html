Hippeastrum minor

Hippeastrum minor, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, white, and pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features strap-like, linear leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum minor is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and dramatic floral displays.
