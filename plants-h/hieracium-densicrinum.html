Hieracium densicrinum

Hieracium densicrinum, commonly known as the dense-haired hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in dense, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
