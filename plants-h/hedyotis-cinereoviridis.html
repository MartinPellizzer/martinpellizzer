Hedyotis cinereoviridis

Hedyotis cinereoviridis, commonly known as the silver-green dotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or forest understories.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
