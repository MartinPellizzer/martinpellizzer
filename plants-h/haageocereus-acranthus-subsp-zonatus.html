Haageocereus acranthus subsp. zonatus

Haageocereus acranthus subsp.

zonatus, commonly known as the Zonate Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a low-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions as an ornamental plant.
