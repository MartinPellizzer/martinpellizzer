Hieracium hugueninianum

Hieracium hugueninianum, commonly known as the Huguenin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc and surrounding ray florets, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium hugueninianum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
