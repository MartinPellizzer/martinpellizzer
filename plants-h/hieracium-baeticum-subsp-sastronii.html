Hieracium baeticum subsp. sastronii

Hieracium baeticum subsp.

sastronii, commonly known as the Baetican hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to brownish flower heads.

The plant typically features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
