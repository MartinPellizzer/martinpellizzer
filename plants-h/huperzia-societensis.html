Huperzia societensis

Huperzia societensis, commonly known as the Socotra dragon's blood fern, is a unique and ancient fern species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often reddish-brown fronds that resemble the appearance of dragon's blood. The plant belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is notable for its primitive morphology, which includes a lack of true roots and a specialized rhizome system. Huperzia societensis thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat degradation and limited distribution.
