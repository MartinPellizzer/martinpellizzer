Helia virgata

Helia virgata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Helia virgata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
