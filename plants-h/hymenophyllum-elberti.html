Hymenophyllum elberti

Hymenophyllum elberti, commonly known as the Elbert's filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori.

Hymenophyllum elberti is notable for its ability to retain moisture and its unique, intricate leaf structure that aids in photosynthesis in low-light conditions.
