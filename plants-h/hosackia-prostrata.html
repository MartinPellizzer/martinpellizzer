Hosackia prostrata

Hosackia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate false indigo, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hosackia prostrata is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
