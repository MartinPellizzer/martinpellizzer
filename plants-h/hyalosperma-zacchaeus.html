Hyalosperma zacchaeus

Hyalosperma zacchaeus, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are edible and rich in inulin. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the autumn.

Its botanical structure supports both ornamental and culinary uses, making it a versatile species in various ecosystems.
