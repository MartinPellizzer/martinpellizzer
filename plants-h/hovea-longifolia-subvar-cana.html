Hovea longifolia subvar. cana

Hovea longifolia subvar. cana, commonly known as the white hovea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

Hovea longifolia subvar. cana is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
