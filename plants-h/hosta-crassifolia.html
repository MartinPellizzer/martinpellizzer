Hosta crassifolia

Hosta crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf hosta, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and thick leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers in late summer, which are relatively inconspicuous compared to its foliage. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental leaves.

Hosta crassifolia is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
