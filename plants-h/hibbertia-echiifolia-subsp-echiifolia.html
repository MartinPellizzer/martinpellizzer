Hibbertia echiifolia subsp. echiifolia

Hibbertia echiifolia subsp.

echiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent, elongated stamens.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and is adapted to dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
