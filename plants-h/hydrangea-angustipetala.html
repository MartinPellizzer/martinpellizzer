Hydrangea angustipetala

Hydrangea angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding ornamental value beyond flowering.
