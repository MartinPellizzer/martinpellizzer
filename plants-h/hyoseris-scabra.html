Hyoseris scabra

Hyoseris scabra, commonly known as the rough hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Hyoseris scabra is known for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
