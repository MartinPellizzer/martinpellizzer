Hibiscus spicatus

Hibiscus spicatus, commonly known as the spiked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Hibiscus spicatus produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water.

It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
