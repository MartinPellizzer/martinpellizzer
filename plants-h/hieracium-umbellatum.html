Hieracium umbellatum

Hieracium umbellatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium umbellatum is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth habit.
