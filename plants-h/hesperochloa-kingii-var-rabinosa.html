Hesperochloa kingii var. rabinosa

Hesperochloa kingii var. rabinosa, commonly known as the Rabinosa needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and extreme temperatures.

This variety is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
