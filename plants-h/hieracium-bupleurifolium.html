Hieracium bupleurifolium

Hieracium bupleurifolium, commonly known as the bupleurifolium hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
