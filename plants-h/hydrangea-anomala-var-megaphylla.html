Hydrangea anomala var. megaphylla

Hydrangea anomala var. megaphylla, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant produces opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated margin. It thrives in acidic soil and requires partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
