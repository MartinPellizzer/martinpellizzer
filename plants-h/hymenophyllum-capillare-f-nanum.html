Hymenophyllum capillare f. nanum

Hymenophyllum capillare f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thin and translucent, with a unique structure that allows for efficient moisture retention.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
