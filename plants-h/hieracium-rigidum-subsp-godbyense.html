Hieracium rigidum subsp. godbyense

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

godbyense, commonly known as Godby's stiff hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are rigid and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
