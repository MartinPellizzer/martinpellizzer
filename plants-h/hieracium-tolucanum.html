Hieracium tolucanum

Hieracium tolucanum, commonly known as Tolucan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish hues.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
