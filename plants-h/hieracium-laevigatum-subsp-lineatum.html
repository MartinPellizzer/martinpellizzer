Hieracium laevigatum subsp. lineatum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

lineatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive rayless structure and a central disk of tubular florets.

It is characterized by its subshrub-like growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
