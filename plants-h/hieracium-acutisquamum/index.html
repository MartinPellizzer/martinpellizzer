Hieracium Acutisquamum

Hieracium acutisquamum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the daisy family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and serrated leaves. This plant often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts. Despite its beauty, it can be invasive in certain regions, impacting local ecosystems.
