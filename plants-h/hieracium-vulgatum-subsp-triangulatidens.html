Hieracium vulgatum subsp. triangulatidens

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

triangulatidens, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The leaves are typically triangular in shape, giving the species its subspecific name. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
