Hymenopappus liguliflorus

Hymenopappus liguliflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, and its leaves are narrow and linear. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Hymenopappus liguliflorus is often found in arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
