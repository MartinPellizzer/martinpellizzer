Hieracium laggeri subsp. hispidellum

Hieracium laggeri subsp.

hispidellum, commonly known as the hispidellum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of habitats, including meadows and forest edges, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
