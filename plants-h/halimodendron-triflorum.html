Halimodendron triflorum

Halimodendron triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered halimodendron, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it contributes to stabilizing the soil.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and for erosion control in arid landscapes.
