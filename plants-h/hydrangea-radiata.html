Hydrangea radiata

Hydrangea radiata, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, serrated leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
