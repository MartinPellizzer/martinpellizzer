Hylotelephium sieboldii var. ettyuense

Hylotelephium sieboldii var. ettyuense, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and low-maintenance addition to gardens in temperate climates.
