Hieracium serratifrons subsp. aethiops

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

aethiops, commonly known as the Ethiopian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and possesses serrated, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of Africa, particularly Ethiopia, and thrives in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
