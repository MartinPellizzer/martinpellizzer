Hypericum perforatum var. lineolatum

Hypericum perforatum var. lineolatum, commonly known as the yellow hypericum or St. John's wort, is a flowering plant in the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers with orange stamens and distinctive dotted leaves that have small translucent glands. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported antidepressant effects.
