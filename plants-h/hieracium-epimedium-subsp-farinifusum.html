Hieracium epimedium subsp. farinifusum

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

farinifusum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open grasslands and meadows, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, which is surrounded by yellowish bracts.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
