Hieracium latisquamiforme

Hieracium latisquamiforme, commonly known as the broad-scale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with prominent central disk florets.

Hieracium latisquamiforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
