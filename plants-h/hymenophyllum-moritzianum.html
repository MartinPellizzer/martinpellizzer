Hymenophyllum moritzianum

Hymenophyllum moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz's filmy fern, is a delicate and intricate member of the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical rainforests. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a unique indusium.

This species is notable for its ability to absorb moisture through its entire surface, making it highly dependent on consistent humidity.
