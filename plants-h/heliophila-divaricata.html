Heliophila divaricata

Heliophila divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterplantain, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

Heliophila divaricata plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing food for various aquatic organisms.
