Hieracium glaucinum subsp. sagittibifidum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

sagittibifidum, commonly known as the arrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive sagittate base.

The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, often forming dense mats.
