Helia glandulosa

Helia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular helia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in glandular structures that secrete oils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical or subtropical regions.

Due to its unique features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
