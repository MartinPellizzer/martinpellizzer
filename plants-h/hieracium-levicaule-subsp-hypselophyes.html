Hieracium levicaule subsp. hypselophyes

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

hypselophyes, commonly known as the tall yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering stems rise above the rosette, bearing yellowish-green to reddish flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The species is characterized by its relatively tall stature and is often found in association with other members of the Asteraceae family.
