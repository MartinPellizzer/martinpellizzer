Hieracium myagrifolium

Hieracium myagrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium myagrifolium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
