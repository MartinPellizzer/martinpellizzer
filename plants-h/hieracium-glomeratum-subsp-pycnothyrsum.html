Hieracium glomeratum subsp. pycnothyrsum

Hieracium glomeratum subsp.

pycnothyrsum, commonly known as the denseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces dense, yellowish to reddish flower heads in the summer. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
