Hesperis pandurifolia

Hesperis pandurifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family.

Hesperis pandurifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
