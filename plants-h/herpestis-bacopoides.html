Herpestis bacopoides

Herpestis bacopoides, commonly known as the creeping germander, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Herpestis bacopoides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
