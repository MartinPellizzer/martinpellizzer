Hedera canariensis f. variegata

Hedera canariensis f. variegata, commonly known as the Canary ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow colors. This plant is a climbing vine that thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries.

The variegation makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and indoor settings.
