Hippeastrum treatiae

Hippeastrum treatiae, commonly known as the Taiti amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of the Amaryllis family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy for optimal blooming.

Hippeastrum treatiae is popular as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
