Heterocaryum oligacanthum

Heterocaryum oligacanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Heterocaryum oligacanthum is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
