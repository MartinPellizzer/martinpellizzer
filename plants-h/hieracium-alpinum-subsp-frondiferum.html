Hieracium alpinum subsp. frondiferum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

frondiferum, commonly known as Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold, sunny conditions. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and is often found in mountainous habitats across the Alps and adjacent regions.
