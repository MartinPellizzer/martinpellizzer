Hieracium paraguense

Hieracium paraguense, commonly known as the Paraguayan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features yellow or orange flower heads with surrounding leafy bracts, and it has a rosette of basal leaves. It thrives in moist, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Hieracium paraguense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its bright, showy flowers.
