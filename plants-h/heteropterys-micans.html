Heteropterys micans

Heteropterys micans, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on a variety of host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant's leaves are usually small, leathery, and covered in a silvery or glaucous coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health and growth of its host trees.
