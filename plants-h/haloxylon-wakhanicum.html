Haloxylon wakhanicum

Haloxylon wakhanicum, commonly known as the Wakhan haloxylon, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including extreme temperatures and limited water availability. The plant has small, spiny leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its woody stems are often used for fuel and construction in local communities.

Haloxylon wakhanicum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing desertification.
