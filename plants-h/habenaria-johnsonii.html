Habenaria johnsonii

Habenaria johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white blooms, which are often adorned with intricate markings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
