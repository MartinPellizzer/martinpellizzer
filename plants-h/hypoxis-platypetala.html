Hypoxis platypetala

Hypoxis platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Hypoxis platypetala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
