Hordeum pratense var. brongniartii

Hordeum pratense var. brongniartii, commonly known as the Brongniart's barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear spikelets with two to three bracteate florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that distinguishes it from other Hordeum species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
