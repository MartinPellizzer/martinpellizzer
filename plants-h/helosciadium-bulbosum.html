Helosciadium bulbosum

Helosciadium bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous helosciadium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, stout rhizome from which several fleshy, bulb-like structures arise. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
