Himantoglossum jankae var. calcaratum

Himantoglossum jankae var. calcaratum, commonly known as the limestone helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings. The plant thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring.

This variety is notable for its specific ecological requirements and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
