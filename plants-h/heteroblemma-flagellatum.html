Heteroblemma flagellatum

Heteroblemma flagellatum, commonly known as the flagellate liverwort, is a thalloid liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It exhibits a distinctive morphology with a flattened, ribbon-like thallus that is often covered in small, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant is characterized by its motile sperm, which is a unique feature among liverworts, aiding in its reproductive strategy. It typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as on rocks or soil in temperate regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for certain ecological conditions and its contribution to the study of bryophyte evolution.
