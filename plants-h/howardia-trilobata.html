Howardia trilobata

Howardia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed hydnoid, is a unique member of the Hydnaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, gelatinous, and often spore-filled fruiting bodies.

This plant is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fruiting bodies are typically club-shaped and have a central stalk with three lobes, giving it its common name. The species is notable for its lack of a true cap and its ability to produce spores in a gelatinous mass.

Howardia trilobata plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling through its spore dispersal.
