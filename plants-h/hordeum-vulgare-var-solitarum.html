Hordeum vulgare var. solitarum

Hordeum vulgare var. solitarum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its slender, erect growth habit.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous two-rowed grains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is primarily cultivated for its grains, which are used in animal feed and traditional brewing.
