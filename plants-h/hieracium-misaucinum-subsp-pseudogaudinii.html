Hieracium misaucinum subsp. pseudogaudinii

Hieracium misaucinum subsp.

pseudogaudinii, commonly known as the false gaudinia hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head with ray florets.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is part of the Asteraceae family.
