Hypericum lutchuense

Hypericum lutchuense, commonly known as the Lutchuena St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the island of Lutchuan in the South China Sea.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slight waxy coating.

Hypericum lutchuense is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
