Hieracium nigrescens subsp. comatuloides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

comatuloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and yellow flowers.

The subspecies comatuloides is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

This plant is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
