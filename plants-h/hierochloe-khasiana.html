Hierochloe khasiana

Hierochloe khasiana, commonly known as Khasi grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes, often found in alpine and subalpine environments. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
