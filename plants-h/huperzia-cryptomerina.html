Huperzia cryptomerina

Huperzia cryptomerina, commonly known as the cryptomeria huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often branched stems that bear clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores in capsules that are often protected by a peristome, a structure that aids in spore dispersal.

Huperzia cryptomerina is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
