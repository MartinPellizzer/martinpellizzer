Hypoestes warpurioides

Hypoestes warpurioides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant features dark green, velvety leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hypoestes warpurioides is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
