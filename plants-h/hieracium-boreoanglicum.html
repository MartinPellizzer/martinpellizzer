Hieracium boreoanglicum

Hieracium boreoanglicum, commonly known as the northern English hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Hieracium boreoanglicum is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
