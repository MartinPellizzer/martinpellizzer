Hieracium goemoerense

Hieracium goemoerense, commonly known as the Goemoer's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium goemoerense is often found in moist environments and can form dense colonies, making it a common sight in various habitats across its range.
