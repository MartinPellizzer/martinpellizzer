Hyptis impar

Hyptis impar, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis impar is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
