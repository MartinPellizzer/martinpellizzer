Holoregmia viscida

Holoregmia viscida, commonly known as the sticky holoregmia, is a unique plant species characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a viscous, sticky substance.

This secretion serves as a natural defense mechanism against herbivores and helps in trapping small insects. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a waxy surface that enhances its ability to retain moisture.

Holoregmia viscida is of interest to botanists due to its unusual adaptations and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
