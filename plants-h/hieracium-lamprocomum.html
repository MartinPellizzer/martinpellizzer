Hieracium lamprocomum

Hieracium lamprocomum, commonly known as the silver-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silver hairs, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
