Hieracium praematurum subsp. leucomallum

Hieracium praematurum subsp.

leucomallum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically covered with dense, white or pale hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale, woolly appearance and is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae).
