Hieracium maculatum subsp. candelanum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

candelanum, commonly known as the Candelabra Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive yellowish or reddish discoloration on the underside.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
