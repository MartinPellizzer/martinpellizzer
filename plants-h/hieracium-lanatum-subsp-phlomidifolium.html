Hieracium lanatum subsp. phlomidifolium

Hieracium lanatum subsp.

phlomidifolium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive, velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
