Hydrocotyle skottsbergii

Hydrocotyle skottsbergii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water pennywort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and flooded grasslands. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with fine, delicate veins. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in umbels, and it produces small, elliptical fruits.

Hydrocotyle skottsbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
