Hierochloe odorata var. biflora

Hierochloe odorata var. biflora, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, aromatic leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its flowering stems bear numerous small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional ceremonies and crafts due to its pleasant fragrance and symbolic significance.
