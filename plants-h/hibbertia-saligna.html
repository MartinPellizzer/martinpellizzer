Hibbertia saligna

Hibbertia saligna, commonly known as the slender hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often branching stems. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent, elongated petals, which are usually arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hibbertia saligna is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
