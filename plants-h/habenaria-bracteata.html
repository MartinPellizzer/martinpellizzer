Habenaria bracteata

Habenaria bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its root system is fibrous, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.
