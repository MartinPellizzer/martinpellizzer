Henslowia cassiifolia

Henslowia cassiifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, branching clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Henslowia cassiifolia is also valued for its ability to attract birds and butterflies to gardens.
