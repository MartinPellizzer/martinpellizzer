Hedysarum balchanense var. montanum

Hedysarum balchanense var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain sweet vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow along the ground or in a prostrate manner. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy surface.

This species is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, contributing to soil stabilization through its extensive root system.
