Helianthus debilis subsp. debilis

Helianthus debilis subsp.

debilis, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with prominent central disks. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.

The species is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
