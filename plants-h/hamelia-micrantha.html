Hamelia micrantha

Hamelia micrantha, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its bright red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and vibrant floral display.
