Hieracium praecox var. kalmutinum

Hieracium praecox var. kalmutinum, commonly known as the Kalmutin hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets and is known for its hairy stems and leaves.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
