Hadrolaelia × pilcheri

Hadrolaelia × pilcheri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Hadrolaelia ochroleuca and Laelia purpurea.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are characterized by long, slender petals and a prominent lip. It prefers cool, bright conditions with moderate humidity and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This hybrid is relatively hardy and can be grown in both greenhouse and outdoor environments.
