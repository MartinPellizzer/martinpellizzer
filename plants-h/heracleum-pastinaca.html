Heracleum pastinaca

Heracleum pastinaca, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features a tall, hollow stem with a distinct purple-tan coloration, and its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent.

Heracleum pastinaca is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides, stream banks, and in meadows.
