Hedyotis strigulosa var. coreana

Hedyotis strigulosa var. coreana, commonly known as Korean dotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale pink, adding a delicate appearance.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
