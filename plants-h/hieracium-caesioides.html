Hieracium caesioides

Hieracium caesioides, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright blue to purple flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium caesioides is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain regions.
