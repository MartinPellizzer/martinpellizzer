Hieracium rohacsense subsp. pictirohacsense

Hieracium rohacsense subsp.

pictirohacsense, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive, often brightly colored flower heads that can range from yellow to orange. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.
