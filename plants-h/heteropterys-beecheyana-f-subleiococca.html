Heteropterys beecheyana f. subleiococca

Heteropterys beecheyana f. subleiococca, commonly known as the pale-leaved form of the white-leaf huito, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smaller, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its high-altitude habitat. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native ecosystem.

This form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation in controlled environments.
