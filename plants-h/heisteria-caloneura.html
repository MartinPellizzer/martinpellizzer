Heisteria caloneura

Heisteria caloneura, commonly known as the silverleaf heister, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Heisteria caloneura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
