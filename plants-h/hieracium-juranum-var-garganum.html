Hieracium juranum var. garganum

Hieracium juranum var. garganum, commonly known as the Garganum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disc florets.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
