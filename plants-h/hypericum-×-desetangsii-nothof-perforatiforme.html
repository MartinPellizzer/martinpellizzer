Hypericum × desetangsii nothof. perforatiforme

Hypericum × desetangsii nothof.

perforatiforme, commonly known as the perforate leaf St. John's wort, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a unique perforated or notched appearance along the edges.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
