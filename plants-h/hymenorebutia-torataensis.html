Hymenorebutia torataensis

Hymenorebutia torataensis, commonly known as the Torataena torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of central Argentina.

It is a low-growing, cylindrical cactus with a distinct, ribbed structure that helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne near the top of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Hymenorebutia torataensis is well-suited for cultivation in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
