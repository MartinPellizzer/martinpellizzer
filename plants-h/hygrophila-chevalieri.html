Hygrophila chevalieri

Hygrophila chevalieri, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

Hygrophila chevalieri is often used in aquatic landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
