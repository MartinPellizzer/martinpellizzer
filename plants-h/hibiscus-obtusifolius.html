Hibiscus obtusifolius

Hibiscus obtusifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the warm season. Hibiscus obtusifolius thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
