Hypericum hircinum var. cambessedesii

Hypericum hircinum var. cambessedesii, commonly known as the Corsican St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller stature and more compact growth compared to the typical H. hircinum.
