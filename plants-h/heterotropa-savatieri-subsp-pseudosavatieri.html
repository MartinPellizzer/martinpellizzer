Heterotropa savatieri subsp. pseudosavatieri

Heterotropa savatieri subsp.

pseudosavatieri, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a frost-like appearance.

The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture with a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
