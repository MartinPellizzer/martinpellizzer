Hoodia gordonii

Hoodia gordonii, commonly known as the desert apple or witch's butter, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spineless stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is adapted to extreme desert conditions. Hoodia gordonii is notable for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It has been traditionally used by indigenous peoples for its purported appetite-suppressing properties.
