Helleborus multifidus subsp. serbicus

Helleborus multifidus subsp.

serbicus, commonly known as the Serbian helleborus, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features dark green, leathery leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, leaf-like bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
