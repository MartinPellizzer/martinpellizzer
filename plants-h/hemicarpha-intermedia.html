Hemicarpha intermedia

Hemicarpha intermedia, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its narrow leaves are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pale green spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems, and it flowers from late spring to early summer.

Hemicarpha intermedia plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
