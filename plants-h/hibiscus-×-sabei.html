Hibiscus × sabei

Hibiscus × sabei, commonly known as the Malay hibiscus, is a hybrid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Hibiscus × sabei thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
