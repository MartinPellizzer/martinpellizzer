Hedyotis schlechtendahliana var. glauca

Hedyotis schlechtendahliana var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered blue or violet flowers and has slender, branched stems. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and compact growth habit.
