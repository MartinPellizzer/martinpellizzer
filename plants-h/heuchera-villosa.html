Heuchera villosa

Heuchera villosa, commonly known as the softleaf alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage that ranges in color from silver to deep red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera villosa is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
