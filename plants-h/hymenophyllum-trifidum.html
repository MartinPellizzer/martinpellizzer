Hymenophyllum trifidum

Hymenophyllum trifidum, commonly known as the trifid mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like fronds that are divided into three parts, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is known for its intricate, lace-like appearance. It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its small size and specific habitat requirements, it is often overlooked but plays a role in forest ecosystems.
