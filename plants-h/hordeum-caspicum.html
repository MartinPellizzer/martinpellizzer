Hordeum caspicum

Hordeum caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian barley, is a wild species of barley native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in dry, sandy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its seeds are small and often used in traditional agricultural practices.

Hordeum caspicum is valued for its hardiness and potential role in crop improvement programs.
