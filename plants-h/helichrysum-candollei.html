Helichrysum candollei

Helichrysum candollei, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
