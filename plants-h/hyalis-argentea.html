Hyalis argentea

Hyalis argentea, commonly known as silverleaf hyalis, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Hyalis argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
