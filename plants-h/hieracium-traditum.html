Hieracium traditum

Hieracium traditum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium traditum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many environments.
