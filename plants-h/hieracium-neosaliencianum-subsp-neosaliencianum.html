Hieracium neosaliencianum subsp. neosaliencianum

Hieracium neosaliencianum subsp.

neosaliencianum, commonly known as the Salientian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a serrated margin, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like cluster.

It is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
