Hibiscus fijiensis

Hibiscus fijiensis, commonly known as the Fiji hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly Fiji and Tonga.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft hair. Hibiscus fijiensis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found growing in coastal areas or near water sources.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
