Helicia cochinchinensis var. pseuderratica

Helicia cochinchinensis var. pseuderratica, commonly known as the False Banyan or False Coconut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to form buttress roots, which help stabilize it in moist, nutrient-poor soils.
