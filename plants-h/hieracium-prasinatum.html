Hieracium prasinatum

Hieracium prasinatum, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Hieracium prasinatum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
