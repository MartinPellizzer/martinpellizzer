Hieracium haglundii

Hieracium haglundii, commonly known as Haglund's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-orange.

Haglund's hawkweed is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
