Hieracium anglicum subsp. jaculifolium

Hieracium anglicum subsp.

jaculifolium, commonly known as the English hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and numerous yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a hairy stem and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is known for its aggressive growth and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its leaves and stems are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.
