Hermione × gracilis

Hermione × gracilis, commonly known as the hybrid ivy, is a climbing plant that belongs to the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its cascading growth habit.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
