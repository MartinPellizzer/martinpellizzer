Hakea christineae

Hakea christineae, commonly known as the Christine's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikes of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea christineae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
