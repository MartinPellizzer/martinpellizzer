Hedysarum × tscherkassovae

Hedysarum × tscherkassovae, commonly known as the hybrid peavine, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hedysarum × tscherkassovae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

It also has potential ecological benefits due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
