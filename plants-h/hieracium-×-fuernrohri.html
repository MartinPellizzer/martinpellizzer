Hieracium × fuernrohri

Hieracium × fuernrohri, commonly known as the hybrid meadowhawkweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and stems that bear yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
