Haplopappus bloomeri subsp. typicus

Haplopappus bloomeri subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Desert Star, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spherical shape.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.
