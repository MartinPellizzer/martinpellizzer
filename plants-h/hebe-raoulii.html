Hebe raoulii

Hebe raoulii, commonly known as the Raoul's hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in spring and summer. Hebe raoulii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long flowering period.
