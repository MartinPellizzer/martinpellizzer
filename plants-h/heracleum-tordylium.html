Heracleum tordylium

Heracleum tordylium, commonly known as the clustered cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Heracleum tordylium is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
