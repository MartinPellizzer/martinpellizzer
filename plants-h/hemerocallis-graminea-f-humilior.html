Hemerocallis graminea f. humilior

Hemerocallis graminea f. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf daylily, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and densely packed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
