Heloniopsis orientalis f. lutea

Heloniopsis orientalis f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow heloniopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces composite flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
