Hieracium laevigatum subsp. goriense

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

goriense, commonly known as the Goriense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
