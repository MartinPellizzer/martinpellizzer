Heuchera americana var. foliosa

Heuchera americana var. foliosa, commonly known as the American alumroot, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to violet, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and early bloom make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
