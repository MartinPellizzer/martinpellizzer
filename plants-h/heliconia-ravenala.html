Heliconia ravenala

Heliconia ravenala, commonly known as the paradise bird plant or ravenala, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It features large, feather-like leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a visually appealing and unique appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange or red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Heliconia ravenala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and exotic appearance.
