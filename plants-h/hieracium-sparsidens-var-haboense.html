Hieracium sparsidens var. haboense

Hieracium sparsidens var. haboense, commonly known as the Havoens hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological variation.
