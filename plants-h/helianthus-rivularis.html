Helianthus rivularis

Helianthus rivularis, commonly known as the river sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Helianthus rivularis plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
