Hedyotis filifolia

Hedyotis filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hedyotis filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
