Hibiscus sylvaticus

Hibiscus sylvaticus, commonly known as the woods hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its showy flowers are usually pink or red, with five to seven petals. Hibiscus sylvaticus blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing nectar that attracts bees and other pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
