Hieracium manoclades

Hieracium manoclades, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
