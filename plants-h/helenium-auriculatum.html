Helenium auriculatum

Helenium auriculatum, commonly known as the sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright orange to red ray florets surrounding a yellow disk, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Helenium auriculatum typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wetland restoration projects.
