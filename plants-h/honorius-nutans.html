Honorius nutans

Honorius nutans, commonly known as the hanging hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical name reflects its unique growth habit and historical significance in traditional medicine.
