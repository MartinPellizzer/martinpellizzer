Hebenstretia repens

Hebenstretia repens, commonly known as the creeping hebenstretia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial species that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has a trailing habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and often root at the nodes.

Hebenstretia repens thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
