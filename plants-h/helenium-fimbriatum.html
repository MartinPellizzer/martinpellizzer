Helenium fimbriatum

Helenium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed ray flover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking, orange to red-orange ray flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stem is erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Helenium fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
