Hymenocallis undulata

Hymenocallis undulata, commonly known as the wavy white ginger, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, white, bell-shaped flowers that have a wavy, undulating pattern on the outer petals. The plant produces a tall, leafy stem with narrow, strap-like leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

Hymenocallis undulata is primarily grown for its aesthetic value and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
