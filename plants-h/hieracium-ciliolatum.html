Hieracium ciliolatum

Hieracium ciliolatum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

It is known for its aggressive growth habit and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
