Helichrysum viguieri

Helichrysum viguieri, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, papery flowers that remain intact for a long time after blooming. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is drought-tolerant. Its foliage consists of narrow, grey-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum viguieri is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
