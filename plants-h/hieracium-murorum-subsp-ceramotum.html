Hieracium murorum subsp. ceramotum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

ceramotum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange-red flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is relatively hardy in various soil conditions.
