Hieracium chalcorites

Hieracium chalcorites, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
