Hibiscus hasslerianus

Hibiscus hasslerianus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hibiscus hasslerianus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
