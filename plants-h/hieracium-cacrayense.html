Hieracium cacrayense

Hieracium cacrayense, commonly known as the Cacray's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
