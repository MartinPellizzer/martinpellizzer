Hypericum barbatum subsp. trichanthum

Hypericum barbatum subsp.

trichanthum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
