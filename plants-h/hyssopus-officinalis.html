Hyssopus officinalis

Hyssopus officinalis, commonly known as hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant is often grown for its aromatic leaves and flowers, which are used in herbal remedies. Hyssop thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in medicine and as a symbol of healing dates back to ancient times.
