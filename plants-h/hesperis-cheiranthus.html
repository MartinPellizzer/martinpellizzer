Hesperis cheiranthus

Hesperis cheiranthus, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis cheiranthus is typically perennial, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters, and thrives in well-drained soils.
