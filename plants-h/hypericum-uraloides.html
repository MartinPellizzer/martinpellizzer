Hypericum uraloides

Hypericum uraloides, commonly known as the Ural goldthread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant features small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Hypericum uraloides is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
