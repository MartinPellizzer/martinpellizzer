Hieracium kotschyanum subsp. longidentatum

Hieracium kotschyanum subsp.

longidentatum, commonly known as the long-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate with long, prominent teeth, giving it its common name.

It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.
