Hieracium lycopifolium subsp. lycopifolium

Hieracium lycopifolium subsp.

lycopifolium, commonly known as the lycopifolium hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
