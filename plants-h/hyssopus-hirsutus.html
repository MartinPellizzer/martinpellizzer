Hyssopus hirsutus

Hyssopus hirsutus, commonly known as hairy hyssop, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Hyssopus hirsutus is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, and it is used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
