Hieracium Pallescens Subsp. Pachysphaericum

Hieracium pallescens subsp. pachysphaericum is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its unique floral structure. This subspecies is often found in moist, calcareous soils across northern Europe and parts of Russia. Its pale yellow flowers bloom in early summer and are surrounded by a dense cluster of bracts. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains a subject of limited study compared to more widely recognized members of its genus.
