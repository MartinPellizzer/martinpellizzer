Hieracium trachselianum

Hieracium trachselianum, commonly known as the Swiss buttercup, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in alpine regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
