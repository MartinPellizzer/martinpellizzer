Hieracium vulgatum var. tridentatum

Hieracium vulgatum var. tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it the "three-toothed" appearance in its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
