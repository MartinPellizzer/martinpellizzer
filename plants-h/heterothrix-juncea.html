Heterothrix juncea

Heterothrix juncea, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-dispersed seeds. Heterothrix juncea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems supporting various insect and bird life.
