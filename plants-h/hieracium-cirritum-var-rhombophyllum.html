Hieracium cirritum var. rhombophyllum

Hieracium cirritum var. rhombophyllum, commonly known as the rhombophyllum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This variant is distinguished by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which contribute to its specific taxonomic classification.
