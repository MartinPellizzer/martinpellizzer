Hedera helix var. chrysophylla

Hedera helix var. chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage.

It is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
