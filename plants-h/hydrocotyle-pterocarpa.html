Hydrocotyle pterocarpa

Hydrocotyle pterocarpa, commonly known as the rock nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and is sometimes used in coastal landscaping.
