Heterotoma arabidoides

Heterotoma arabidoides, commonly known as the Arabian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy and rocky soils.

Heterotoma arabidoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
