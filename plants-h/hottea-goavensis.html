Hottea goavensis

Hottea goavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar tea plant, is a shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain a mild, caffeine-like compound. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
