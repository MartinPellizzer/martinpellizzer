Hordeum laevipaleatum

Hordeum laevipaleatum, commonly known as the smooth palea barley, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and smooth, palea-covered lemma. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing two to three florets.

Hordeum laevipaleatum is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential role in plant systematics.
