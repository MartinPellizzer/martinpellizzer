Hypericum × desetangsii nothosubsp. balcanicum

Hypericum × desetangsii nothosubsp.

balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan St. John's Wort, is a hybrid shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, red-orange berries that are rich in hypericin, a compound with potential medicinal properties.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
