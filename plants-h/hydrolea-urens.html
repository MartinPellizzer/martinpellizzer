Hydrolea urens

Hydrolea urens, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Hydrolea urens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
