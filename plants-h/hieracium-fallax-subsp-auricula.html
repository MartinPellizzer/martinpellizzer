Hieracium fallax subsp. auricula

Hieracium fallax subsp.

auricula, commonly known as the auriculate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, rough, and covered with stiff hairs, while the stems are upright and branching. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is often considered a weed but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
