Hippocrepis squamata var. eriocarpa

Hippocrepis squamata var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a woolly appearance. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
