Hibiscus trilobus subsp. hirsutus

Hibiscus trilobus subsp.

hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy wood hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a rough texture.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
