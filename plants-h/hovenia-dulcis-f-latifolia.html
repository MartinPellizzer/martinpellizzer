Hovenia dulcis f. latifolia

Hovenia dulcis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Chinese raisin tree, is a variant of the Hovenia dulcis species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, contributing to its significance in both horticulture and pharmacology.
