Hieracium neilreichii

Hieracium neilreichii, commonly known as Neilreich's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

The yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
