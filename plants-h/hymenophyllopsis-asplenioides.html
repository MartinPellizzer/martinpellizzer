Hymenophyllopsis asplenioides

Hymenophyllopsis asplenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are simple and lack true leaves, with spore-producing structures called sporangia located on the underside.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
