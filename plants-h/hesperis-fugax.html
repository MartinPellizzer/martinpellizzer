Hesperis fugax

Hesperis fugax, commonly known as the transient scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its blooming period is brief, often lasting only a few weeks, which contributes to its common name.

Hesperis fugax is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
