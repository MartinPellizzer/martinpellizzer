Hieracium integrifolium

Hieracium integrifolium, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has serrated, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium integrifolium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its aggressive spreading habit.
