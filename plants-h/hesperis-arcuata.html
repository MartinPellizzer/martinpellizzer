Hesperis arcuata

Hesperis arcuata, commonly known as the curved scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Hesperis arcuata is often found in sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to pollinators.
