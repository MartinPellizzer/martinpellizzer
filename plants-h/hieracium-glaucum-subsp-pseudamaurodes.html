Hieracium glaucum subsp. pseudamaurodes

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

pseudamaurodes, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically features hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
