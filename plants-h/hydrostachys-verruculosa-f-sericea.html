Hydrostachys verruculosa f. sericea

Hydrostachys verruculosa f. sericea, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its characteristic texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
