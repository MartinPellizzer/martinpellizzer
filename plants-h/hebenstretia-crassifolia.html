Hebenstretia crassifolia

Hebenstretia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved hebenstretia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
