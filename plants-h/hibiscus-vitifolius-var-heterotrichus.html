Hibiscus vitifolius var. heterotrichus

Hibiscus vitifolius var. heterotrichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features elongated, narrow leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its flowers are typically large, showy, and have a distinctive five-petaled structure. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
