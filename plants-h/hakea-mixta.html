Hakea mixta

Hakea mixta, commonly known as the variable hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-branching structure and its distinctive, needle-like leaves. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Hakea mixta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
