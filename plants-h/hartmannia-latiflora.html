Hartmannia latiflora

Hartmannia latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf hartmannia, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to low-light conditions.

Hartmannia latiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
