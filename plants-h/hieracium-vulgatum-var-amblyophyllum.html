Hieracium vulgatum var. amblyophyllum

Hieracium vulgatum var. amblyophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its common name reflects the dense, woolly appearance of its foliage.
