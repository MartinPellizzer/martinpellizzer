Hieracium bukovicae

Hieracium bukovicae, commonly known as the Bukovina hawkweed, is a rare flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
