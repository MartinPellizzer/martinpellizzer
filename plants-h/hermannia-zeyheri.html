Hermannia zeyheri

Hermannia zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's hermannia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Hermannia zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
