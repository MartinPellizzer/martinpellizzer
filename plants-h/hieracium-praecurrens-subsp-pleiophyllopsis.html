Hieracium praecurrens subsp. pleiophyllopsis

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

pleiophyllopsis, commonly known as the many-leafed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, showcasing a rosette of broad, lobed leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, which are often surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are characterized by their numerous lobes and hairy surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats.
