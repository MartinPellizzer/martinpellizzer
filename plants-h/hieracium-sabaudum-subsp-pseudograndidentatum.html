Hieracium sabaudum subsp. pseudograndidentatum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

pseudograndidentatum, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

This subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust leaves compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
