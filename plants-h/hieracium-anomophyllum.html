Hieracium anomophyllum

Hieracium anomophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
