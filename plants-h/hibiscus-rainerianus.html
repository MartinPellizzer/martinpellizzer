Hibiscus rainerianus

Hibiscus rainerianus, commonly known as the Rainer hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus rainerianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
