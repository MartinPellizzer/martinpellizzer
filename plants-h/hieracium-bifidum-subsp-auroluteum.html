Hieracium bifidum subsp. auroluteum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

auroluteum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bright golden-yellow flowers and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
