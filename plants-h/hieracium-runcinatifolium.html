Hieracium runcinatifolium

Hieracium runcinatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium runcinatifolium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can be invasive in some regions.
