Hieracium dubyanum

Hieracium dubyanum, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the colder regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium dubyanum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
