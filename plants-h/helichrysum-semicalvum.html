Helichrysum semicalvum

Helichrysum semicalvum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers are also known for their aromatic properties and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
