Homalanthus nutans var. rhombifolius

Homalanthus nutans var. rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombifoliate flame tree, is a variant of the flame tree species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves, which give it its specific epithet. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
