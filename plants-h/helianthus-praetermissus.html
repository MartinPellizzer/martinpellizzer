Helianthus praetermissus

Helianthus praetermissus, commonly known as the western sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant produces both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
