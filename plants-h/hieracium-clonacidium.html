Hieracium clonacidium

Hieracium clonacidium, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and forms dense mats through its rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, where it can spread aggressively.
