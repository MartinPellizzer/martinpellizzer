Huperzia cancellata

Huperzia cancellata, commonly known as the cancellate huperzia, is a species of moss-like fern belonging to the family Hypopterygiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and its distinctive, often segmented fronds that are covered in small, rounded structures called trichomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes are usually short and robust, with a capsule that releases spores for reproduction.

Huperzia cancellata is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a component of diverse forest ecosystems.
