Hieracium variiglandulum

Hieracium variiglandulum, commonly known as the variable hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium variiglandulum is known for its adaptability and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
