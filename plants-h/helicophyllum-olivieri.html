Helicophyllum olivieri

Helicophyllum olivieri, commonly known as the olive tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by small, round fruits called drupes. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The olive tree is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, oil production, and traditional medicine.
