Hieracium laticeps

Hieracium laticeps, commonly known as the broad-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium laticeps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
