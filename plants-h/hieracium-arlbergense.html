Hieracium arlbergense

Hieracium arlbergense, commonly known as the Arlberg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has basal leaves that are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
