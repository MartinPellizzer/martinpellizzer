Hakea ulicina var. flexilis

Hakea ulicina var. flexilis, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and dry inland environments.

Hakea ulicina var. flexilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
