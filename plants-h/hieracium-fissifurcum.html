Hieracium fissifurcum

Hieracium fissifurcum, commonly known as the fissured hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often have a distinctive fissured appearance, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium fissifurcum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
