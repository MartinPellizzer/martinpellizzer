Helictotrichon asperum var. roylei

Helictotrichon asperum var. roylei, commonly known as the rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or clumps. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its flowers are small and arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender stems.

Helictotrichon asperum var. roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
