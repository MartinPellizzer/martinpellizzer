Horminum verticillatum

Horminum verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and small, white to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
