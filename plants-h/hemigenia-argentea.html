Hemigenia argentea

Hemigenia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests.

Hemigenia argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens.
