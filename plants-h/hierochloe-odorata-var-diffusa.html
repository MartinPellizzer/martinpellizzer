Hierochloe odorata var. diffusa

Hierochloe odorata var. diffusa, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate foliage and a sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into seeds.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional crafts and rituals due to its aromatic properties and symbolic significance.
