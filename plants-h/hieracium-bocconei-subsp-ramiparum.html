Hieracium bocconei subsp. ramiparum

Hieracium bocconei subsp.

ramiparum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showing adaptations to cold and harsh environments. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas.
