Hyptis inflata

Hyptis inflata, commonly known as the inflated hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis inflata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
