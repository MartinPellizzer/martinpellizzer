Hoya javanica

Hoya javanica, commonly known as the wax plant or sweetheart plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, waxy flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Hoya javanica is a climbing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
