Hieracium racskii

Hieracium racskii, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like inflorescence.

Hieracium racskii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful appearance.
