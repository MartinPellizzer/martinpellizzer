Hypericum lysimachioides

Hypericum lysimachioides, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
