Homalomena calyptrata

Homalomena calyptrata, commonly known as the red-veined arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Homalomena calyptrata is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
