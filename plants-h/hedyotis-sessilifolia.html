Hedyotis sessilifolia

Hedyotis sessilifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features small, opposite, sessile leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hedyotis sessilifolia is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
