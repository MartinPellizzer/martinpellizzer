Himantoglossum galilaeum

Himantoglossum galilaeum, commonly known as the Galilean orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the rocky slopes of the Galilee region in Israel.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with distinctive violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces a single, spurred flower with a unique shape. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions to survive.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
