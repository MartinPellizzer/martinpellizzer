Hymenaea osanigraseminae

Hymenaea osanigraseminae, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a natural defense against pests and diseases.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
