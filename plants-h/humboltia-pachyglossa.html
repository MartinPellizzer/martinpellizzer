Humboltia pachyglossa

Humboltia pachyglossa, commonly known as the yellow balm of Gilead, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Humboltia pachyglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
