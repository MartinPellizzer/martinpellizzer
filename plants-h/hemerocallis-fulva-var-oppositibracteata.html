Hemerocallis fulva var. oppositibracteata

Hemerocallis fulva var. oppositibracteata, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a variant of the daylily species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces multiple flower clusters on sturdy stems. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in garden settings.
