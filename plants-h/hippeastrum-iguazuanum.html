Hippeastrum iguazuanum

Hippeastrum iguazuanum, commonly known as the Iguazu glory lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant typically produces clusters of vibrant pink or red blooms, which appear in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are broad, strap-shaped, and often form a rosette at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is valued for its beauty and ease of care.
