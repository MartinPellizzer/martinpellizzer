Hieracium separatidens

Hieracium separatidens, commonly known as the separate-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium separatidens is an annual or biennial plant that spreads through seeds and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
