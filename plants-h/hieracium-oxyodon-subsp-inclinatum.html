Hieracium oxyodon subsp. inclinatum

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

inclinatum, commonly known as the leaning hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive growth habit and is found primarily in Europe and parts of Asia.
