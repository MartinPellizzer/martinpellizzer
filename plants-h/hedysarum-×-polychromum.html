Hedysarum × polychromum

Hedysarum × polychromum, commonly known as the multicolored sainfoin, is a hybrid legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which range from pink to red and yellow, often displaying multiple hues on a single bloom. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its compound leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum × polychromum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
