Hebe allanii

Hebe allanii, commonly known as the silverleaf hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Hebe allanii is relatively low maintenance and thrives in temperate climates.
