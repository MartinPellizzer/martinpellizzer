Hieracium hanburyi f. atraticeps

Hieracium hanburyi f. atraticeps, commonly known as the Hanbury's hawkweed, is a rare and striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking purple-tinged flower heads that resemble small daisies. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring careful protection.
