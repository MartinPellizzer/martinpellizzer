Hieracium bifidum var. violaceum

Hieracium bifidum var. violaceum, commonly known as violet hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually hairy with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are small, yellowish to orange, and bloom in late spring to early summer.
