Hieracium flaccidum

Hieracium flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium flaccidum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
