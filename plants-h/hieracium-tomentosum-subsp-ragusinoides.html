Hieracium tomentosum subsp. ragusinoides

Hieracium tomentosum subsp.

ragusinoides, commonly known as the Ragusan hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, composite flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
