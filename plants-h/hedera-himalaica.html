Hedera himalaica

Hedera himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Hedera himalaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb structures or trees in its natural habitat.
