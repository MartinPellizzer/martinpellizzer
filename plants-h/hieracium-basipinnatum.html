Hieracium basipinnatum

Hieracium basipinnatum, commonly known as the basal-pinnate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium basipinnatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
