Hoya barracki

Hoya barracki, commonly known as the silver star hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Hoya barracki is a popular houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying properties.

It is also known for its slow growth and long-lasting blooms.
