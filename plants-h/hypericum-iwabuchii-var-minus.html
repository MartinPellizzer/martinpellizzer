Hypericum iwabuchii var. minus

Hypericum iwabuchii var. minus, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact form and vibrant floral display.
