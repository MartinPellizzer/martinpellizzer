Habenaria tetrodon

Habenaria tetrodon, commonly known as the four-tooth orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences bearing numerous small, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive four-lobed lip. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
