Halimium viscosum

Halimium viscosum, commonly known as sticky rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Halimium viscosum is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
