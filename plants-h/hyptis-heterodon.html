Hyptis heterodon

Hyptis heterodon, commonly known as the Mexican hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis heterodon is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
