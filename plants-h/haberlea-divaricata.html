Haberlea divaricata

Haberlea divaricata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
