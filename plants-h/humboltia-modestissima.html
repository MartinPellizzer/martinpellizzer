Humboltia modestissima

Humboltia modestissima, commonly known as the dwarf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the coastline. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its coastal habitat.

Humboltia modestissima plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for local wildlife.
