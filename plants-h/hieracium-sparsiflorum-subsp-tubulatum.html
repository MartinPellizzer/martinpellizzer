Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. tubulatum

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

tubulatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces flowering stems with yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It is characterized by its slender stems and relatively small, daisy-like flowers.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their medicinal and ecological significance.
