Hyacinthus orientalis proles provincialis

Hyacinthus orientalis proles provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Hyacinth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors such as blue, purple, and white. The plant produces bulbs that are perennial, allowing it to return year after year in suitable climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period, which adds color to the landscape during the transition from winter to summer.
