Hyoscyamus arachnoideus

Hyoscyamus arachnoideus, commonly known as the spiderflower or desert jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in inflated, balloon-like pods that aid in wind dispersal.

Hyoscyamus arachnoideus is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
