Helopus nubicus

Helopus nubicus, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate frond structure and adaptability to moist environments.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Helopus nubicus thrives in shaded, humid conditions and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
