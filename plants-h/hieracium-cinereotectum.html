Hieracium cinereotectum

Hieracium cinereotectum, commonly known as the gray-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
