Hyptis camporum

Hyptis camporum, commonly known as Camp's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Hyptis camporum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
