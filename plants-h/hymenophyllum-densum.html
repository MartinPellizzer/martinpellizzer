Hymenophyllum densum

Hymenophyllum densum, commonly known as the dense moss fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum densum plays an important ecological role in maintaining moisture and soil stability in its native habitats.
