Heracleum caspicum

Heracleum caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian hogweed, is a tall, biennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features large, umbrella-like compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its stems are hollow and covered with coarse hairs, and they can grow up to 3 meters in height.

The plant contains toxic sap that can cause severe skin irritation and photodermatitis when exposed to sunlight.
