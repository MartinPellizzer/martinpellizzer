Hamosa austrina

Hamosa austrina, commonly known as the Australian hamosa, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in areas with high salinity. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Hamosa austrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in coastal regions.
