Hieracium herttulense

Hieracium herttulense, commonly known as the Herttulense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has a fibrous root system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative means.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
