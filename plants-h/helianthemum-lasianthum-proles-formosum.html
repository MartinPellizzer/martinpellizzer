Helianthemum lasianthum proles formosum

Helianthemum lasianthum proles formosum, commonly known as the showy rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
