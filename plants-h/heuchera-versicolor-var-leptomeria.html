Heuchera versicolor var. leptomeria

Heuchera versicolor var. leptomeria, commonly known as the red-veined heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking foliage with deep green leaves that have prominent red veins, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add color to woodland gardens.
