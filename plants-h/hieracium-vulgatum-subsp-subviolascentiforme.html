Hieracium vulgatum subsp. subviolascentiforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

subviolascentiforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller and more elongated flower heads compared to other members of the species.
