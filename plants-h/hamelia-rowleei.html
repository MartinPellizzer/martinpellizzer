Hamelia rowleei

Hamelia rowleei, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hamelia rowleei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
