Hypericum nervosum

Hypericum nervosum, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers consist of five petals and numerous stamens.

Hypericum nervosum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant properties.
