Hieracium dentatum var. salaevense

Hieracium dentatum var. salaevense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in rocky mountainous areas.
