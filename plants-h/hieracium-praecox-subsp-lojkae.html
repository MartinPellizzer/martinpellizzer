Hieracium praecox subsp. lojkae

Hieracium praecox subsp.

lojkae, commonly known as the Lojka's early hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in a single head, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in moist or calcareous soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
