Hieracium nigritellum subsp. seirasicum

Hieracium nigritellum subsp.

seirasicum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It has rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive dark central disc, and it produces small, oblong achenes as its fruit.
