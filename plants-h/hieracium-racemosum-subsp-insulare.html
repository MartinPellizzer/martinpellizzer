Hieracium racemosum subsp. insulare

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

insulare, commonly known as the island buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and riverbanks.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flowers in a raceme.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.
