Hieracium bifidum subsp. griseiceps

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

griseiceps, commonly known as the gray-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosette growth habit.

The plant features finely divided, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by a ring of pale green to reddish bracts.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
