Hieracium phrixolophum

Hieracium phrixolophum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Hieracium phrixolophum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
