Hieracium maculatum subsp. naevuliferum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

naevuliferum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of broadly lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
