Hieracium inuloides subsp. detruncatum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

detruncatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal and stem leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, contributing to its role in pollinator support.
