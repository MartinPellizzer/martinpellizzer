Hieracium inuloides subsp. aurigeranum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
