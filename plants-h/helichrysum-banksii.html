Helichrysum banksii

Helichrysum banksii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered together in a spherical or conical shape. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.

Helichrysum banksii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
