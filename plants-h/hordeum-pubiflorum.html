Hordeum pubiflorum

Hordeum pubiflorum, commonly known as the pubescent floret barley, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent florets, which distinguish it from other barley species. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed sites, thriving in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, each bearing a single floret.

Hordeum pubiflorum is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological features and ecological adaptability.
