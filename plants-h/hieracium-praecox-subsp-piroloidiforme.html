Hieracium praecox subsp. piroloidiforme

Hieracium praecox subsp.

piroloidiforme, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a lobed or serrated margin. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
