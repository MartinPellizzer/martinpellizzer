Hexisea amparoana

Hexisea amparoana, commonly known as the Amparo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which often display vibrant shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its leaves are long and narrow, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

The orchid's unique floral structure and pollination mechanism make it an important species in its ecosystem.
