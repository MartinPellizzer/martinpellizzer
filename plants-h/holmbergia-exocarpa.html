Holmbergia exocarpa

Holmbergia exocarpa, commonly known as the red-flowered holmbergia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid habitat. Its unique floral structure and vivid coloration make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.

Holmbergia exocarpa thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
