Heuchera americana f. angustipetala

Heuchera americana f. angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and elongated, often with a reddish or bronze hue, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it is relatively low maintenance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
