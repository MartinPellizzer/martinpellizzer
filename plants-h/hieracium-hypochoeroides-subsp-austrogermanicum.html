Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. austrogermanicum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

austrogermanicum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
