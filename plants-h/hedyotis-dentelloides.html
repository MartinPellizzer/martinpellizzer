Hedyotis dentelloides

Hedyotis dentelloides, commonly known as the toothed mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers and oval, toothed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds.

Hedyotis dentelloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
