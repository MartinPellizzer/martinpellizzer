Hieracium sabaudolympicum

Hieracium sabaudolympicum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous habitats across the Alps.
