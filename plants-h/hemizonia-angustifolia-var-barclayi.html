Hemizonia angustifolia var. barclayi

Hemizonia angustifolia var. barclayi, commonly known as the narrowleaf hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its more elongated leaves compared to other varieties of Hemizonia angustifolia.
