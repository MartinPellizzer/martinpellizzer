Hieracium angustatum subsp. chauliodon

Hieracium angustatum subsp.

chauliodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a series of tubular florets, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, often forming dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
