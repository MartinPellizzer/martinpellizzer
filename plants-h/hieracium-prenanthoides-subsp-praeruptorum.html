Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. praeruptorum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

praeruptorum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and nutrient-rich conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
