Hibiscus conradsii

Hibiscus conradsii, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, single-layered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus conradsii is a perennial shrub that produces fruit capsules containing numerous seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.
