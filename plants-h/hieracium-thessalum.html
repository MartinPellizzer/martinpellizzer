Hieracium thessalum

Hieracium thessalum, commonly known as the Thessalian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Greece.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

Hieracium thessalum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
