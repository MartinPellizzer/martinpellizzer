Hieracium djimilense subsp. panjutinii

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

panjutinii, commonly known as the Panjutin's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces numerous seeds in its capitula.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
