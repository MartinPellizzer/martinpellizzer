Hieracium picroides subsp. christii

Hieracium picroides subsp.

christii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in open areas with full sun exposure.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its native range.
