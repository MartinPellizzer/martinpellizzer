Hypericum maleevii

Hypericum maleevii, commonly known as the maleev's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, red-orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
