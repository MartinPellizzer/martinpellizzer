Hetrepta lavandulifolia

Hetrepta lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of lavender, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
