Hesperis matronalis var. oblongifolia

Hesperis matronalis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblongleaf honesty, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
