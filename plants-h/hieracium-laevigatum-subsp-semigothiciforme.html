Hieracium laevigatum subsp. semigothiciforme

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

semigothiciforme, commonly known as the Gothic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into elongated, bristly seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the dandelion family (Compositae).
