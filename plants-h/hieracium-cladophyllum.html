Hieracium cladophyllum

Hieracium cladophyllum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are basal and form a rosette, while the stems are upright and bear clusters of yellow flowers.

Hieracium cladophyllum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
