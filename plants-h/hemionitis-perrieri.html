Hemionitis perrieri

Hemionitis perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically gray-green in color. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis perrieri is considered a vulnerable species.
