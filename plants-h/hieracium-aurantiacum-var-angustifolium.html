Hieracium aurantiacum var. angustifolium

Hieracium aurantiacum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
