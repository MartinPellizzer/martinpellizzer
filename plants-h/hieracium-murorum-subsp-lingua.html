Hieracium murorum subsp. lingua

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lingua, commonly known as the "tongue hawkweed," is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are often hairy and have a slightly lobed or toothed margin.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, tongue-like flower heads, which give it its common name.
