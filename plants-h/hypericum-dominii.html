Hypericum dominii

Hypericum dominii, commonly known as the Canary Islands goldenseal, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Hypericum dominii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
