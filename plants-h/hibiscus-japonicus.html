Hibiscus japonicus

Hibiscus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. Hibiscus japonicus is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
