Hyptis nubicola

Hyptis nubicola, commonly known as the cloud hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis nubicola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
