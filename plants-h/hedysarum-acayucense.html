Hedysarum acayucense

Hedysarum acayucense, commonly known as the Mexican needlepod, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Hedysarum acayucense is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
