Halogeton arachnoideus

Halogeton arachnoideus, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments through its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy tissues. Its leaves are often reduced to scales, which help minimize water loss.

Halogeton arachnoideus plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation due to its tolerance of heavy metals.
