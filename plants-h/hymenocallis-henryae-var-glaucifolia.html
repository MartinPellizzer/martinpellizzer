Hymenocallis henryae var. glaucifolia

Hymenocallis henryae var. glaucifolia, commonly known as the Henry's spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a prominent green throat. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
