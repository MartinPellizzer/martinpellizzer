Hieracium gmelinianum

Hieracium gmelinianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and its stems are upright with a reddish tinge. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium gmelinianum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
