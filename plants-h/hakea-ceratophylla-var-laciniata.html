Hakea ceratophylla var. laciniata

Hakea ceratophylla var. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features dense, needle-like leaves that are often serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
