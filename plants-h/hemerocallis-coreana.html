Hemerocallis coreana

Hemerocallis coreana, commonly known as the Korean daylily, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features sword-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hemerocallis coreana is well adapted to a variety of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
