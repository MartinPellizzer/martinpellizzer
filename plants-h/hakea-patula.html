Hakea patula

Hakea patula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Hakea patula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
