Hyssopus anisatus

Hyssopus anisatus, commonly known as anise hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, square stems and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal teas. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it a valuable plant for attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hyssopus anisatus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens and landscapes.
