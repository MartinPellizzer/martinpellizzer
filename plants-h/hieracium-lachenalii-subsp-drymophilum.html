Hieracium lachenalii subsp. drymophilum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

drymophilum, commonly known as the drymophilous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by yellow to orange ray florets.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
