Hieracium epimedium subsp. moravicum

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

moravicum, commonly known as the Moravian meadowhawk, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
