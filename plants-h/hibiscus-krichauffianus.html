Hibiscus krichauffianus

Hibiscus krichauffianus, commonly known as the Krichauff's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus krichauffianus thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
