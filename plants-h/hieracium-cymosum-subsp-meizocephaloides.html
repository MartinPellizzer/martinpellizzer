Hieracium cymosum subsp. meizocephaloides

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

meizocephaloides, commonly known as the meizocephaloid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is often considered a rare or endangered species in certain regions.
