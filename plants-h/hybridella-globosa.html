Hybridella globosa

Hybridella globosa, commonly known as the globe lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered berries that are edible.

Hybridella globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
