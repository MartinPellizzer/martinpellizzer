Halimolobos diffusus var. typicus

Halimolobos diffusus var. typicus, commonly known as the creeping marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in dense mats, with slender, spreading stems that root at the nodes. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Its ability to form extensive colonies makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
