Hypericum trichocaulon

Hypericum trichocaulon, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are covered in fine, hairy textures. It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
