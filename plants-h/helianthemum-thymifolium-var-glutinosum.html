Helianthemum thymifolium var. glutinosum

Helianthemum thymifolium var. glutinosum, commonly known as sticky rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and covered in a sticky, glandular coating, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
