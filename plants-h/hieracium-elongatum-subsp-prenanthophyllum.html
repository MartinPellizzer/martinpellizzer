Hieracium elongatum subsp. prenanthophyllum

Hieracium elongatum subsp.

prenanthophyllum, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
