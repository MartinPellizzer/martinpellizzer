Hieracium bifidum subsp. chlorellifrons

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

chlorellifrons, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
