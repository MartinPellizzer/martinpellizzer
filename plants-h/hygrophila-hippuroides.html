Hygrophila hippuroides

Hygrophila hippuroides, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize sediment and improve water clarity. Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for aquariums and water gardens.

Hygrophila hippuroides is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
