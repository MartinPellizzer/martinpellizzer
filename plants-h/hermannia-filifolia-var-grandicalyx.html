Hermannia filifolia var. grandicalyx

Hermannia filifolia var. grandicalyx, commonly known as the giant threadleaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
