Hesperis contorta

Hesperis contorta, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Hesperis, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis contorta is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
