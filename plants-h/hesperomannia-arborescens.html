Hesperomannia arborescens

Hesperomannia arborescens, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hesperomannia arborescens is well adapted to harsh desert conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
