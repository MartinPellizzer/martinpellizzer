Hieracium cultratum

Hieracium cultratum, commonly known as the cutleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed, often with a distinctive notched appearance, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium cultratum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
