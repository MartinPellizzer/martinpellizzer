Hyptis affinis

Hyptis affinis, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Hyptis affinis is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in herbal remedies for various ailments.
