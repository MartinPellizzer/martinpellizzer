Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. phyllobracteum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

phyllobracteum, commonly known as the leaf-bract hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a taproot system and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
