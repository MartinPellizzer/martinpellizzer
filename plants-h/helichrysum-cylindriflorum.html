Helichrysum cylindriflorum

Helichrysum cylindriflorum, commonly known as the cylindrical everlasting flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange-yellow flowers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Helichrysum cylindriflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers.
