Hyptis nervosa

Hyptis nervosa, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for its medicinal properties.
