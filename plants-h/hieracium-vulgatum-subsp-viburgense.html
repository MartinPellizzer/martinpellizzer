Hieracium vulgatum subsp. viburgense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

viburgense, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
