Hymenocallis eucharidifolia

Hymenocallis eucharidifolia, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that resemble lilies, with a fragrant, bell-shaped bloom. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance, especially during Easter celebrations.
