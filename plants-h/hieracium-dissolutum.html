Hieracium dissolutum

Hieracium dissolutum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium dissolutum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive spreading habit and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
