Havardia mexicana

Havardia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
