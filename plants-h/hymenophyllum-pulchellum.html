Hymenophyllum pulchellum

Hymenophyllum pulchellum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rock faces or tree trunks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, shaded gardens.
