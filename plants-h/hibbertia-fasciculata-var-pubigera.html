Hibbertia fasciculata var. pubigera

Hibbertia fasciculata var. pubigera, commonly known as the hairy false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in coastal and heathland environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hibbertia fasciculata var. pubigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
