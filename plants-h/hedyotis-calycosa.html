Hedyotis calycosa

Hedyotis calycosa, commonly known as the scarlet bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Hedyotis calycosa is often found in moist, shaded environments like forests and rocky slopes.
