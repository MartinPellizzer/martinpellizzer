Heliocarpus nelsonii

Heliocarpus nelsonii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit pods. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, which are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
