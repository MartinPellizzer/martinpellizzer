Haworthia limifolia var. tetraploidea

Haworthia limifolia var. tetraploidea, commonly known as the four-leaf haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure. The leaves are fleshy, thick, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale green flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
