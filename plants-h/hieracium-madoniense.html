Hieracium madoniense

Hieracium madoniense, commonly known as the Madonese hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium madoniense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
