Haplopteris guineensis

Haplopteris guineensis, commonly known as the African rock fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Haplopteridaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas.

Haplopteris guineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
