Hieracium sinigerum

Hieracium sinigerum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy.

Hieracium sinigerum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
