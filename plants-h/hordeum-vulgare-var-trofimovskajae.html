Hordeum vulgare var. trofimovskajae

Hordeum vulgare var. trofimovskajae, commonly known as a rare variant of barley, is a grass species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinct morphological features compared to common barley. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-rowed grains, which are adapted for dry and semi-arid environments. Its genetic uniqueness makes it valuable for studying evolutionary adaptations in cereal crops.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered plant species.
