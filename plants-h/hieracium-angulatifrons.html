Hieracium angulatifrons

Hieracium angulatifrons, commonly known as the angular hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it often grows in meadows and open woodlands. Native to Europe, it is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
