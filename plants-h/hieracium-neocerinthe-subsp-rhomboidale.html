Hieracium neocerinthe subsp. rhomboidale

Hieracium neocerinthe subsp.

rhomboidale, commonly known as the rhomboid hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

This subspecies is distinguished by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
