Holcus natans

Holcus natans, commonly known as floating grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its ability to grow on the surface of water, with its leaves and stems floating freely. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

Holcus natans is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes.
