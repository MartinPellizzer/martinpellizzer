Hibiscus maculatus

Hibiscus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to red, often with a distinctive red or purple spot at the base of the petals. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Hibiscus maculatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
