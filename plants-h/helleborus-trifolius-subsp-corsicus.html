Helleborus trifolius subsp. corsicus

Helleborus trifolius subsp.

corsicus, commonly known as Corsican hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its dark green, palmate leaves with three lobes.

The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, showy spathes. It is noted for its slow growth and preference for cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
