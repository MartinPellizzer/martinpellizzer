Houstonia gracilis

Houstonia gracilis, commonly known as slender blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, though it is often classified within the subfamily Chloridoideae. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.

Houstonia gracilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
