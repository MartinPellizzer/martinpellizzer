Helichrysum betsiliense

Helichrysum betsiliense, commonly known as the Madagascar immortelle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces numerous bracts that give it a fluffy appearance, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional practices.
