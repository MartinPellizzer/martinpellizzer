Hieracium laevigatum subsp. liparotum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

liparotum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
