Hemizonia fasciculata subsp. typica

Hemizonia fasciculata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
