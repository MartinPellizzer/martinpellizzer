Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. integratum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

integratum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin, and are often covered in glandular dots.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and is commonly found in temperate regions.
