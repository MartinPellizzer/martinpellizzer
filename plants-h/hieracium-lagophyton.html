Hieracium lagophyton

Hieracium lagophyton, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, robust stems and basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium lagophyton is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various regions.
