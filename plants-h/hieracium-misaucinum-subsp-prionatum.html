Hieracium misaucinum subsp. prionatum

Hieracium misaucinum subsp.

prionatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems covered in rough, glandular hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped with a hairy underside. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, consisting of a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a characteristic species of calcareous grasslands.
