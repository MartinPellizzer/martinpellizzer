Horkelia congesta subsp. nemorosa

Horkelia congesta subsp.

nemorosa, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and meadows.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers. Its botanical structure includes a taproot system and is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
