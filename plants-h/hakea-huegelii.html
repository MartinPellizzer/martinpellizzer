Hakea huegelii

Hakea huegelii, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, urn-shaped inflorescences that produce small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with thick, leathery leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hakea huegelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
