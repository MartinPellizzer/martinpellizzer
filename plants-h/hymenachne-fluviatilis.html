Hymenachne fluviatilis

Hymenachne fluviatilis, commonly known as river flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.

Hymenachne fluviatilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
