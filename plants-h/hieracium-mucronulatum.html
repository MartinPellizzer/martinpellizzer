Hieracium mucronulatum

Hieracium mucronulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an annual or biennial plant that produces a large number of seeds, contributing to its ability to spread widely.
