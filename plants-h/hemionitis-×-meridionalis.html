Hemionitis × meridionalis

Hemionitis × meridionalis, commonly known as the hybrid water fern, is a rare and fascinating plant that results from the crossing of Hemionitis arifolia and Hemionitis zosteromoides.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that float on the water surface. The plant thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments, often found in ponds and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores, which are dispersed by wind and water.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis × meridionalis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
