Hypenia pruinosa

Hypenia pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf hypenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, waxy coating on its leaves, which gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Hypenia pruinosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
