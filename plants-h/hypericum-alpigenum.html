Hypericum alpigenum

Hypericum alpigenum, commonly known as alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum alpigenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
