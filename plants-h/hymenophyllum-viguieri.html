Hymenophyllum viguieri

Hymenophyllum viguieri, commonly known as the velvet moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a distinct midrib.

Hymenophyllum viguieri plays an important role in ecological systems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for microorganisms.
