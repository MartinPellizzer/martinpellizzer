Helianthemum polifolium subsp. pilosum

Helianthemum polifolium subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense, sprawling stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it tolerates harsh environmental conditions.
