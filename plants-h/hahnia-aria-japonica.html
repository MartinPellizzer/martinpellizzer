Hahnia aria japonica

Hahnia aria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese feather grass, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

Hahnia aria japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
