Helichrysum divergens

Helichrysum divergens, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain vibrant even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Helichrysum divergens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.
