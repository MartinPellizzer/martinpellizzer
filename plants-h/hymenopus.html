Hymenopus

Hymenopus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Hymenopus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
