Hieracium planchonianum subsp. epiphaeum

Hieracium planchonianum subsp.

epiphaeum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often found in conservation areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
