Hoya darwinii

Hoya darwinii, commonly known as the Darwin's Waffleplant, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often crinkled or wavy, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers with a sweet fragrance, typically in shades of pink or white. Hoya darwinii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
