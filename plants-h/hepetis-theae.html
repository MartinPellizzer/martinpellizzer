Hepetis theae

Hepetis theae, commonly known as the Turkish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
