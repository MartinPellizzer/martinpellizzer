Herpestis reptans

Herpestis reptans, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes. The plant has small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Herpestis reptans is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
