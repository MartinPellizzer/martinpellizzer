Herniaria argaea

Herniaria argaea, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Herniaria argaea is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures on its stems.
