Hutera hispida var. brachycarpa

Hutera hispida var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Hutera hispida var. brachycarpa is adapted to arid conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
