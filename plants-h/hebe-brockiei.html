Hebe brockiei

Hebe brockiei, commonly known as the Pounamu hebe, is a native New Zealand plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a dark green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hebe brockiei is well adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand and thrives in well-drained soil.
