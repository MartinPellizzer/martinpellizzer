Hieracium nigristylum

Hieracium nigristylum, commonly known as the black-stem hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its dark green, hairy stems and yellow-orange composite flowers make it easily identifiable.

Hieracium nigristylum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
