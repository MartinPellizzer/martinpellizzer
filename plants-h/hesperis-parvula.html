Hesperis parvula

Hesperis parvula, commonly known as the small honesty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features delicate, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Hesperis parvula is known for its short blooming period in late spring to early summer.
