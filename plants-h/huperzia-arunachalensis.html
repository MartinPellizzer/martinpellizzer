Huperzia arunachalensis

Huperzia arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal fern, is a species of clubmoss native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered growth habit. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, scale-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Huperzia arunachalensis plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
