Hieracium halimium

Hieracium halimium, commonly known as the rock groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in coastal or alpine regions. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive involucral bract. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
