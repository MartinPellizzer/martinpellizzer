Habenaria staudtii

Habenaria staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
