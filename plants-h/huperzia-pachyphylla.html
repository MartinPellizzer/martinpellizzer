Huperzia pachyphylla

Huperzia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a rosette-like pattern. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, a feature typical of many ferns.

Huperzia pachyphylla is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
