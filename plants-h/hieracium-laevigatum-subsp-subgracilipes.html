Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subgracilipes

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subgracilipes, commonly known as the slender meadowhawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs. This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable environments.
