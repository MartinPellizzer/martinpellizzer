Hieracium magyaricum subsp. agathanthum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

agathanthum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and the flowering stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other hawkweed species.
