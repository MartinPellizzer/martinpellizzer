Hibiscus zanzibaricus

Hibiscus zanzibaricus, commonly known as the Zanzibar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive dark red or purple throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy, and it produces fibrous, woody stems. Hibiscus zanzibaricus thrives in humid, well-drained soil and is often found near water sources in its natural habitat.

It is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
