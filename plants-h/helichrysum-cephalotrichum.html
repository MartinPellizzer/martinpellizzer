Helichrysum cephalotrichum

Helichrysum cephalotrichum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden inflorescences that resemble small, spherical clusters. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its long-lasting flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and durability.
