Hypericum rosmarinifolium

Hypericum rosmarinifolium, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and aromatic, narrow leaves that resemble rosemary. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its stems are woody and often branched, while its foliage is dense and fragrant.

Hypericum rosmarinifolium is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its purported calming and antidepressant effects.
