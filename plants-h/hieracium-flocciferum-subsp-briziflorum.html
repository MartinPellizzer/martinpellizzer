Hieracium flocciferum subsp. briziflorum

Hieracium flocciferum subsp.

briziflorum, commonly known as the bristleflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the stems are often covered in fine bristles.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
