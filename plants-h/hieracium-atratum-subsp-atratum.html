Hieracium atratum subsp. atratum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

atratum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It has basal and upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy.

Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies.
