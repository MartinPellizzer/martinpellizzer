Hieracium marchesettianum

Hieracium marchesettianum, commonly known as the Marchesettian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are hairy and serrated.

Hieracium marchesettianum is often found in disturbed habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
