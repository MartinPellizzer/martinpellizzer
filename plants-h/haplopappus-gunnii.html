Haplopappus gunnii

Haplopappus gunnii, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Haplopappus gunnii is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a decorative plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
