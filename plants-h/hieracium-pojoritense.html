Hieracium pojoritense

Hieracium pojoritense, commonly known as the Pojoritense Hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Native to specific regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant is perennial and often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.
