Hepatica nobilis f. albiflora

Hepatica nobilis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five to seven petals, and appear before the leaves fully expand. This plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early blooming flowers.
