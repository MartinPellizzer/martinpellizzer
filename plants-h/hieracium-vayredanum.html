Hieracium vayredanum

Hieracium vayredanum, commonly known as the Vayredan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium vayredanum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
