Helianthus verticillatus

Helianthus verticillatus, commonly known as the zigzag sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and tubular florets. The plant features a tall, branching stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it the common name "zigzag.".

Helianthus verticillatus is often found in open woods and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
