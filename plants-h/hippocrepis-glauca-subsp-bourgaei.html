Hippocrepis glauca subsp. bourgaei

Hippocrepis glauca subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgai's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
