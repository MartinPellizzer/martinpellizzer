Hieracium murorum subsp. munduliforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

munduliforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish to reddish flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit with a creeping rootstock and short, upright stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and the shape of its inflorescence.
