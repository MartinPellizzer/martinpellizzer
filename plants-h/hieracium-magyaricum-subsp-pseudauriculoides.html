Hieracium magyaricum subsp. pseudauriculoides

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

pseudauriculoides, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
