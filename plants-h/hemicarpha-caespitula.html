Hemicarpha caespitula

Hemicarpha caespitula, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant forms dense, clump-like growths with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

Hemicarpha caespitula is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
