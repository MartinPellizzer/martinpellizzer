Hernandiopsis vieillardii

Hernandiopsis vieillardii, commonly known as the Guianese hernandia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Guyana and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
