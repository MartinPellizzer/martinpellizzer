Hyacinthus webbianus

Hyacinthus webbianus, commonly known as the Webbian hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, violet-blue blooms in late summer, which are often accompanied by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to naturalize, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
