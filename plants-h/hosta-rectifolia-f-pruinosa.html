Hosta rectifolia f. pruinosa

Hosta rectifolia f. pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Straightleaf Hosta, is a varietal form of Hosta rectifolia, characterized by its distinctive, upright, strap-like leaves.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy, powdery coating that gives the plant its "pruinosa" designation, meaning "dusted" or "powdery.". This cultivar is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to shade gardens.
