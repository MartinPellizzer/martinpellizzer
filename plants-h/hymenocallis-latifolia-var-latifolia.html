Hymenocallis latifolia var. latifolia

Hymenocallis latifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hurricane lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are typically green and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
