Hibiscus luteus

Hibiscus luteus, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus luteus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
