Haplopappus hirtellus

Haplopappus hirtellus, commonly known as the rough pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its fluffy, white pappus hairs aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Haplopappus hirtellus is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
