Hieracium dominae-ericae

Hieracium dominae-ericae, commonly known as the Erica Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
