Hieracium carpaticum subsp. aequilibratum

Hieracium carpaticum subsp.

aequilibratum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets, which are often reflexed or curved backward.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural appeal and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
