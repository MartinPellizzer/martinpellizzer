Herniaria flavescens

Herniaria flavescens, commonly known as yellow hernia, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, greenish-yellow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats.

Herniaria flavescens is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
