Hibiscus brasiliensis

Hibiscus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually pink or red in color.

Hibiscus brasiliensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
