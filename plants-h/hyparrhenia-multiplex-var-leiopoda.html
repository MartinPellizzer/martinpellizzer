Hyparrhenia multiplex var. leiopoda

Hyparrhenia multiplex var. leiopoda, commonly known as the smoothleaf hyparrhenia, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and for soil stabilization in degraded areas.
