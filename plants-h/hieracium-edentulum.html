Hieracium edentulum

Hieracium edentulum, commonly known as the toothless hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, along with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Hieracium edentulum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a resilient species in various ecosystems.
