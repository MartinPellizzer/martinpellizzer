Helleborus lividus var. integrifolius

Helleborus lividus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the European hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically unlobed and have a smooth margin. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, purple, and green, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Helleborus lividus var. integrifolius is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
