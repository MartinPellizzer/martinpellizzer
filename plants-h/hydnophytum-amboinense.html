Hydnophytum amboinense

Hydnophytum amboinense, commonly known as the sticky vine or monkey vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection against herbivores. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruits are fleshy and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Hydnophytum amboinense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
