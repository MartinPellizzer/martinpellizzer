Helianthemum guttatum var. plantagineum

Helianthemum guttatum var. plantagineum, commonly known as the sticky rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, glandular-dotted leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
