Hedyotis angustifolia

Hedyotis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and slightly hairy. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Hedyotis angustifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
