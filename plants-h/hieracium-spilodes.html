Hieracium spilodes

Hieracium spilodes, commonly known as the spilodes hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and hairy, rough stems. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium spilodes is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with partial shade.
