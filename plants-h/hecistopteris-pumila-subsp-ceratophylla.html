Hecistopteris pumila subsp. ceratophylla

Hecistopteris pumila subsp.

ceratophylla, commonly known as the small shield fern, is a member of the water fern family (Pteridaceae). It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length.

The plant has a unique morphology with a shield-shaped sorus, which is a cluster of sporangia used for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
