Hieracium elatiforme

Hieracium elatiforme, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium elatiforme is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
