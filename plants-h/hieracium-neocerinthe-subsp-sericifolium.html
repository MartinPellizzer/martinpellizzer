Hieracium neocerinthe subsp. sericifolium

Hieracium neocerinthe subsp.

sericifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
