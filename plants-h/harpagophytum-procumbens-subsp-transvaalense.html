Harpagophytum procumbens subsp. transvaalense

Harpagophytum procumbens subsp.

transvaalense, commonly known as the "devil's claw," is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like seed pods and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, yellowish stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Harpagophytum procumbens subsp.

transvaalense is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
