Heliotropium stenophyllum var. rosmarinifolium

Heliotropium stenophyllum var. rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, purple or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
