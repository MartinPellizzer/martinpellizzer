Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus f. aurantiaca

Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden daylily, is a cultivar of the daylily plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are typically 3 to 5 inches in diameter. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump, and it is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
