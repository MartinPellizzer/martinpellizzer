Hedinia taxkargannica var. hejigensis

Hedinia taxkargannica var. hejigensis, commonly known as the Hejig Valley bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
