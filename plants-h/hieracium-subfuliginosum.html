Hieracium subfuliginosum

Hieracium subfuliginosum, commonly known as the sooty hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a dark green color, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is often considered a weed but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
