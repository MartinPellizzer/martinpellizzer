Hydrangea cyanema

Hydrangea cyanema, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, sterile flowers surrounded by fertile florets, creating a distinctive floral structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Hydrangea cyanema is also valued for its ability to change flower color, adding versatility to garden design.
