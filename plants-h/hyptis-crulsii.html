Hyptis crulsii

Hyptis crulsii, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and stream banks.

Hyptis crulsii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
