Hieracium quercinum

Hieracium quercinum, commonly known as the oak wood hawk's-beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodlands and open areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium quercinum is often found in association with oak trees, hence its common name.
