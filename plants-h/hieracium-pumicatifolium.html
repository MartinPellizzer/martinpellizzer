Hieracium pumicatifolium

Hieracium pumicatifolium, commonly known as the pumice-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, pumice-like leaves that are often grayish-green in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the top of the stems.

Hieracium pumicatifolium is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
