Heteromorpha scandens

Heteromorpha scandens, commonly known as the climbing plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.
