Hannonia hesperidum var. legionariorum

Hannonia hesperidum var. legionariorum, commonly known as the Roman soldier's hannonia, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the hannonia plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle yet striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
