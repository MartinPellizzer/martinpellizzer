Hermannia nemorosa

Hermannia nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and thickets. The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the rainy season.

Hermannia nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
