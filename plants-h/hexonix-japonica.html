Hexonix japonica

Hexonix japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hexagon leaf plant, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its foliage is often a deep green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

Hexonix japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying properties.
