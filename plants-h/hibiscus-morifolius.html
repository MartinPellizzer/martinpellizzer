Hibiscus morifolius

Hibiscus morifolius, commonly known as the tropical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. Hibiscus morifolius thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
