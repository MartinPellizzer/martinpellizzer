Haplocoelum acuminatum

Haplocoelum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its leafy thallus with a distinct, elongated shape and a smooth, waxy surface. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, including male and female gametophytes, are prominently displayed on the leaf surfaces.

Haplocoelum acuminatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various invertebrates.
