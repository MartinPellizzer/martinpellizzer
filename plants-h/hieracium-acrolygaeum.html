Hieracium acrolygaeum

Hieracium acrolygaeum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium acrolygaeum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
