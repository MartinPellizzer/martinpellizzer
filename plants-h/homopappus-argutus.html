Homopappus argutus

Homopappus argutus, commonly known as the rough pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant has a bushy growth habit.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes.
