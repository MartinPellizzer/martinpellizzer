Hieracium juranum subsp. lagopinum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

lagopinum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps and surrounding mountainous regions. It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

It is characterized by its ability to form dense colonies, often outcompeting other vegetation in its habitat.
