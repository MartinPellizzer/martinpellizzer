Hieracium phaeostictum

Hieracium phaeostictum, commonly known as the pale-streaked hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive pale stripe along the central ray florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
