Hymenopyramis parvifolia var. nitida

Hymenopyramis parvifolia var. nitida, commonly known as the glossy leaf hymenopyramis, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with fine, waxy hairs, giving them a smooth, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its growth habit is compact, with a tendency to form dense, rounded shrubs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
