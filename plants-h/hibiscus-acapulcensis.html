Hibiscus acapulcensis

Hibiscus acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and can become branched.

Hibiscus acapulcensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
