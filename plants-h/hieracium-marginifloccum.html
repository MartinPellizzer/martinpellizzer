Hieracium marginifloccum

Hieracium marginifloccum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium marginifloccum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
