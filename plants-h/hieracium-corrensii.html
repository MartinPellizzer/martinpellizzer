Hieracium corrensii

Hieracium corrensii, commonly known as the Corsican hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium corrensii is known for its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
