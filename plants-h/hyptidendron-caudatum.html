Hyptidendron caudatum

Hyptidendron caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed hyptidendron, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ecological significance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil, and it serves as a food source for various insects and animals.
