Hoya meliflua subsp. breviora

Hoya meliflua subsp.

breviora, commonly known as the short-stemmed wax plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements. Hoya meliflua subsp.

breviora thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
