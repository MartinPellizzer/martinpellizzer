Hamatocactus bicolor

Hamatocactus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, white spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant contrast to its greenish-brown body. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
