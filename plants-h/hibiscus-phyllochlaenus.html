Hibiscus phyllochlaenus

Hibiscus phyllochlaenus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
