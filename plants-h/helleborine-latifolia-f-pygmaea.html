Helleborine latifolia f. pygmaea

Helleborine latifolia f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf broad-leaved helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
