Hoya latifolia subsp. kinabaluensis

Hoya latifolia subsp.

kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Bornean waxplant, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the mountainous regions of Borneo. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers with a waxy texture, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
