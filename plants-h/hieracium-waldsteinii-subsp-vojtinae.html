Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. vojtinae

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

vojtinae, commonly known as the Waldstein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies vojtinae is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.
