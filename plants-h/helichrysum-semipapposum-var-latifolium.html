Helichrysum semipapposum var. latifolium

Helichrysum semipapposum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with bright yellow to orange flower heads that remain colorful even when dried. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to retain color after drying.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal areas due to its drought tolerance.
