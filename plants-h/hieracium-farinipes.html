Hieracium farinipes

Hieracium farinipes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
