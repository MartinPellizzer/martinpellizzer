Hakea seminuda

Hakea seminuda, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody structure with a distinctive, often branched stem. It is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in coastal environments with high salinity.

Hakea seminuda is also known for its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
