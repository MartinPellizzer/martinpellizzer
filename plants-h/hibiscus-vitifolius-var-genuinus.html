Hibiscus vitifolius var. genuinus

Hibiscus vitifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with five petals and a prominent yellow center. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
