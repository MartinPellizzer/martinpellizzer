Hieracium sabaudum subsp. dumosum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

dumosum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves, and the plant is known for its ability to form dense mats.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
