Habenaria secundiflora

Habenaria secundiflora, commonly known as the secondary-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in the understory where it can receive indirect sunlight. The plant produces a single stem with several flowers, each featuring a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, which is essential for its growth in nutrient-poor habitats.

The orchid's flowering period usually occurs during the wet season, enhancing its chances of pollination by insects.
