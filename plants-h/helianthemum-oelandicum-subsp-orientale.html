Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. orientale

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the eastern rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
