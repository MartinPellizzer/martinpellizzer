Hieracium onosmoides subsp. crinulifolium

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

crinulifolium, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and typically appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
