Helleborus polychromus

Helleborus polychromus, commonly known as the Persian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, shady environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Helleborus polychromus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
