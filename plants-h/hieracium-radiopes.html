Hieracium radiopes

Hieracium radiopes, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, daisy-like cluster. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
