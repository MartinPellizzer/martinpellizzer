Hieracium membranulatum

Hieracium membranulatum, commonly known as the membranous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
