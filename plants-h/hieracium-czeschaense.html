Hieracium czeschaense

Hieracium czeschaense, commonly known as the Czeschian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
