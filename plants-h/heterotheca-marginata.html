Heterotheca marginata

Heterotheca marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca marginata is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
