Havardia nelsonii

Havardia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

Havardia nelsonii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
