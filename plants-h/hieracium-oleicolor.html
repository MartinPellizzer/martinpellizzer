Hieracium oleicolor

Hieracium oleicolor, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk, surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

Hieracium oleicolor is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
