Hypericum rumeliacum var. ciliatum

Hypericum rumeliacum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves with hairy margins, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
