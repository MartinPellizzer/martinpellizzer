Herpetacanthus delicatus

Herpetacanthus delicatus, commonly known as the delicate herpetacanthus, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several national parks.
