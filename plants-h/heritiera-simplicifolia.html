Heritiera simplicifolia

Heritiera simplicifolia, commonly known as the Indian nettle or small-leaved heritiera, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in forests and along riverbanks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
