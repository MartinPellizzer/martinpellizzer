Hypericum tateutianum

Hypericum tateutianum, commonly known as the Korean St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include glandular dots on the stems and leaves, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family.

It is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
