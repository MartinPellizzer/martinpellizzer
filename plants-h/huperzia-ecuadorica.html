Huperzia ecuadorica

Huperzia ecuadorica, commonly known as the Ecuadorian clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with short, horizontal stems that give rise to erect, spore-bearing shoots. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Huperzia ecuadorica plays a role in maintaining soil stability and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
