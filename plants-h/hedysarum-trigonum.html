Hedysarum trigonum

Hedysarum trigonum, commonly known as three-angled vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically three-lobed and have a distinctive triangular shape. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in racemes. Hedysarum trigonum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
