Himalaiella auriculata

Himalaiella auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are ovate and have distinctive auriculate (ear-like) lobes at the base.

Himalaiella auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
