Hieracium alejandrei

Hieracium alejandrei, commonly known as the Alexander's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium alejandrei is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
