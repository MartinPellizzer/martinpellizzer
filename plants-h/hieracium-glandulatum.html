Hieracium glandulatum

Hieracium glandulatum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are hairy and have a glandular surface, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
