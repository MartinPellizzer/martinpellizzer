Helictotrichon rigidulum

Helictotrichon rigidulum, commonly known as rigid curlgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tightly curled, stiff leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Helictotrichon rigidulum is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
