Hierochloe redolens

Hierochloe redolens, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silky leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds.

Hierochloe redolens has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.
