Hakea hirsuta

Hakea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. Hakea hirsuta is adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

It is an important plant in native gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
