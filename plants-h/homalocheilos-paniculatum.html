Homalocheilos paniculatum

Homalocheilos paniculatum, commonly known as the yellow panicled orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
