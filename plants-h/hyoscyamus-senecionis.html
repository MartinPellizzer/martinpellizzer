Hyoscyamus senecionis

Hyoscyamus senecionis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
