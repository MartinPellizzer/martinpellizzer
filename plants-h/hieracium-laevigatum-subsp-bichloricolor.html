Hieracium laevigatum subsp. bichloricolor

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

bichloricolor, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems are covered in a fine, white, silky pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
