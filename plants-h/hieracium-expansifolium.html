Hieracium expansifolium

Hieracium expansifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly elliptic to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
