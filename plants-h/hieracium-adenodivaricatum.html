Hieracium adenodivaricatum

Hieracium adenodivaricatum, commonly known as the two-branched hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
