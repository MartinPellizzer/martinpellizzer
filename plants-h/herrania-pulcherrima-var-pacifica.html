Herrania pulcherrima var. pacifica

Herrania pulcherrima var. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
