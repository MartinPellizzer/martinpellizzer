Heliconia linneana

Heliconia linneana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces long, drooping inflorescences with numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia linneana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
