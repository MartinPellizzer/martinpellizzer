Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. blancii

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

blancii, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white or pale greenish leaves, which contrast with the bright floral display. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is herbaceous, with a short, creeping stem and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
