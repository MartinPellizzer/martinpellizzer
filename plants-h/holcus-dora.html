Holcus dora

Holcus dora, commonly known as the Dora's hairgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Holcus dora is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
