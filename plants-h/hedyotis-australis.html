Hedyotis australis

Hedyotis australis, commonly known as the Australian pennywort, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and summer months, and it relies on insect pollination.

Hedyotis australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
