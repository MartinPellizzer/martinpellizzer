Hosta grandifolia

Hosta grandifolia, commonly known as the giant hosta, is a species of plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to Korea and is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The leaves are typically a deep green with a white margin, creating a striking visual contrast. It produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, slender stalks.

This plant thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soil conditions.
