Hieracium eurhacidophyllum

Hieracium eurhacidophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow disk flowers with ray florets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in temperate climates.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
