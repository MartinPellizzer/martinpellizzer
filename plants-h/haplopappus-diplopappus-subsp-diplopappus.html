Haplopappus diplopappus subsp. diplopappus

Haplopappus diplopappus subsp.

diplopappus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.
