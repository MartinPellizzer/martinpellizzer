Hedysarum nonnae

Hedysarum nonnae, commonly known as the noble vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in rocky and arid environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Hedysarum nonnae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
