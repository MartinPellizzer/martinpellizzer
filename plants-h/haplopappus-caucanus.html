Haplopappus caucanus

Haplopappus caucanus, commonly known as the Caucasian haplopappus, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are usually narrow and linear, contributing to its overall drought-resistant structure.
