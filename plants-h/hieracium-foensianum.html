Hieracium foensianum

Hieracium foensianum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Hieracium foensianum is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
