Hippophae conferta

Hippophae conferta, commonly known as the sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy soils and with minimal water.

Its hardy nature makes it useful for erosion control and as a source of edible fruits.
