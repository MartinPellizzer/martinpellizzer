Hibiscus vitifolius var. ricinifolius

Hibiscus vitifolius var. ricinifolius, commonly known as the giant cottonweed, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky soils and in open landscapes.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
