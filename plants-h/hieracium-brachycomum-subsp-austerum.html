Hieracium brachycomum subsp. austerum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

austerum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

It is often found in open meadows and grasslands at high elevations.
