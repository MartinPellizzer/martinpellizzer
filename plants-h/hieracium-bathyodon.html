Hieracium bathyodon

Hieracium bathyodon, commonly known as the deep-rooted hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display of yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
