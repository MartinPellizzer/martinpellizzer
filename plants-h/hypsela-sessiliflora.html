Hypsela sessiliflora

Hypsela sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered hypsela, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant features sessile leaves, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
