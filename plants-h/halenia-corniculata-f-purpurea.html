Halenia corniculata f. purpurea

Halenia corniculata f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered halenia, is a variant of the halenia plant characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical name reflects its classification within the Asparagaceae family, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
