Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. dasychaetum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

dasychaetum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellowish flowers.

The subspecies dasychaetum is distinguished by its more robust growth and longer, coarser hairs on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
