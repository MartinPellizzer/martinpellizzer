Hieracium bifidum subsp. microscapum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

microscapum, commonly known as the narrow-stem hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is distinguished by its relatively short, narrow stems and smaller flower heads compared to other members of the species.
