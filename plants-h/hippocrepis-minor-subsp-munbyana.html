Hippocrepis minor subsp. munbyana

Hippocrepis minor subsp.

munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and shorter pedicels compared to the typical form of Hippocrepis minor.
