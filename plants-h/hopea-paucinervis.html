Hopea paucinervis

Hopea paucinervis, commonly known as the few-veined hopea, is a tropical tree species belonging to the Dipterocarpaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves with few veins. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
