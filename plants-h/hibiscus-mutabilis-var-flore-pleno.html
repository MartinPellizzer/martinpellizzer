Hibiscus mutabilis var. flore-pleno

Hibiscus mutabilis var. flore-pleno, commonly known as the double-flowered hibiscus, is a cultivar characterized by its showy, double blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. The plant features large, velvety leaves and produces flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which change color as they bloom. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires regular watering during the growing season.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
