Hypericum humifusum var. majus

Hypericum humifusum var. majus, commonly known as the yellow garden hypericum, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, upright stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
