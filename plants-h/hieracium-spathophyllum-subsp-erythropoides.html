Hieracium spathophyllum subsp. erythropoides

Hieracium spathophyllum subsp.

erythropoides, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers with reddish veins.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
