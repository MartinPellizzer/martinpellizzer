Hilsenbergia cannabina

Hilsenbergia cannabina, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hilsenbergia cannabina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
