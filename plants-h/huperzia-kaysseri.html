Huperzia kaysseri

Huperzia kaysseri, commonly known as the Kayser's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen appearance with short, upright shoots and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia kaysseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
