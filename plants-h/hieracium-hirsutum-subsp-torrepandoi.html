Hieracium hirsutum subsp. torrepandoi

Hieracium hirsutum subsp.

torrepandoi, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous disk flowers, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and open habitats.

Due to its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptability, it is a common species in various regions of Europe.
