Haemocarpus axillaris

Haemocarpus axillaris, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along watercourses. Its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers make it an attractive species for ecological restoration projects.

The plant's ability to thrive in wet environments contributes to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
