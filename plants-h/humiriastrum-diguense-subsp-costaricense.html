Humiriastrum diguense subsp. costaricense

Humiriastrum diguense subsp.

costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
