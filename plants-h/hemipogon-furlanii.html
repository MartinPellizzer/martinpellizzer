Hemipogon furlanii

Hemipogon furlanii, commonly known as the Furlanii's spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as certain species of bees and wasps.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemipogon furlanii is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
