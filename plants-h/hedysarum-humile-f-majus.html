Hedysarum humile f. majus

Hedysarum humile f. majus, commonly known as the large-flowered sweet vernal grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
