Hoya uafatoensis

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of star-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pink, which appear during the warmer months. Hoya uafatoensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
