Hieracium mougeotii subsp. chrysanthemoides

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flower heads that resemble chrysanthemums.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright floral display.
