Heteropsis ovata

Heteropsis ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique adaptations for survival in arid environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It is typically shrub-like in growth habit and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
