Hypericum punctatum var. pseudomaculatum

Hypericum punctatum var. pseudomaculatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive yellow spots on its leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with woody stems and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
