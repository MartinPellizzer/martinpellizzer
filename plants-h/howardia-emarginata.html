Howardia emarginata

Howardia emarginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or yellow-eyed grass, is a member of the Asphodelaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Howardia emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
