Hieracium subsalignum

Hieracium subsalignum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
