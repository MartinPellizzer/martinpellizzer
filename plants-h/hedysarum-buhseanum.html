Hedysarum buhseanum

Hedysarum buhseanum, commonly known as the Buhse's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Hedysarum buhseanum is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
