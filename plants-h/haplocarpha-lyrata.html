Haplocarpha lyrata

Haplocarpha lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks.
