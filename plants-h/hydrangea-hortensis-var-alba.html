Hydrangea hortensis var. alba

Hydrangea hortensis var. alba, commonly known as the white hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy clusters of white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is dark green and turns red in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
