Hymenophyllum microchilum

Hymenophyllum microchilum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are thinly foliated and have a distinctive, intricate structure that aids in water retention.

Hymenophyllum microchilum is notable for its ability to survive in high humidity and is often found in cloud forests.
