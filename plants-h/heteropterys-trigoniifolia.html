Heteropterys trigoniifolia

Heteropterys trigoniifolia, commonly known as the three-angled leaf huito, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically three-lobed and have a distinctive triangular shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitats.
