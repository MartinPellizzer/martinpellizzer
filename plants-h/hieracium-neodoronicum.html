Hieracium neodoronicum

Hieracium neodoronicum, commonly known as the sweet-scented hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown.

Hieracium neodoronicum is known for its mild, sweet fragrance, which is more pronounced in its flower heads.
