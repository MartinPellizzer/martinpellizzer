Himgiria compressa

Himgiria compressa, commonly known as the Himalayan mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their tart flavor, and the plant is adapted to survive in cold, rocky environments.

Himgiria compressa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
