Hypochaeris salzmanniana subsp. maroccana

Hypochaeris salzmanniana subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are hairy and have a slightly bitter taste, while the flowers attract various pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats across Morocco and neighboring regions.
