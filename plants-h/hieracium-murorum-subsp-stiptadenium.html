Hieracium murorum subsp. stiptadenium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

stiptadenium, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats.

The plant has yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped. It is characterized by its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
