Hieracium sparsum subsp. georgieffii

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

georgieffii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
