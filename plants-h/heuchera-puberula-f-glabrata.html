Heuchera puberula f. glabrata

Heuchera puberula f. glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf heuchera, is a cultivar of the heuchera plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is native to North America. The plant typically features dense, compact growth with leaves that are usually a deep green or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to add visual interest to shaded landscapes.
