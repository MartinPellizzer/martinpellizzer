Helenium seminariense

Helenium seminariense, commonly known as the Seminariense Sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its bright orange to yellow flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to its native habitat.

Helenium seminariense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
