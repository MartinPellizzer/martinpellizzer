Huperzia somae

Huperzia somae, commonly known as the Somalian huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly Somalia and Ethiopia. The plant has a unique morphology with small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it adaptable to harsh environments.

Huperzia somae is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal.
