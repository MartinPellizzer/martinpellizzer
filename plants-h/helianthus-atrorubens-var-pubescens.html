Helianthus atrorubens var. pubescens

Helianthus atrorubens var. pubescens, commonly known as the rough sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow-orange flowers with a dark center. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The rough sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
