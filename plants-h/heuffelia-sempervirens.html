Heuffelia sempervirens

Heuffelia sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen heuffelia, is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Heuffelia sempervirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, moist environments.
