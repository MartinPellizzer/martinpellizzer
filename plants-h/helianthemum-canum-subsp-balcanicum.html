Helianthemum canum subsp. balcanicum

Helianthemum canum subsp.

balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan sandthrift, is a low-growing shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy, blue-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh, arid conditions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
