Hibiscus sparseaculeatus

Hibiscus sparseaculeatus, commonly known as the sparsely branched hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has slender stems with few branches, giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hibiscus sparseaculeatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
