Hieracium pseudelatum

Hieracium pseudelatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive in some areas. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium pseudelatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
