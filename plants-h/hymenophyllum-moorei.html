Hymenophyllum moorei

Hymenophyllum moorei, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of silky, silver hairs, which give it its common name.

Hymenophyllum moorei is known for its unique reproductive structures, including small, spore-bearing capsules that are often hidden among the foliage.
