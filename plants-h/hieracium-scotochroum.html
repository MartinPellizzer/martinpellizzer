Hieracium scotochroum

Hieracium scotochroum, commonly known as the evening hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night, hence its common name.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
