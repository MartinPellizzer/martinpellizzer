Houstonia drymarioides

Houstonia drymarioides, commonly known as the pink bladderwort, is a small flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Houstonia, which includes several species of herbaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swampy areas.

Its botanical features include small, alternate leaves and a root system adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
