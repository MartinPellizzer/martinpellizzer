Hibiscus mastersianus

Hibiscus mastersianus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a prominent central stamen that adds to their visual appeal. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Hibiscus mastersianus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
