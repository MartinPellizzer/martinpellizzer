Hoffmannseggella macrobulbosa

Hoffmannseggella macrobulbosa, commonly known as the giant bulbous lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous underground storage organs, which are essential for its survival in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
