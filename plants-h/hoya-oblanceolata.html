Hoya oblanceolata

Hoya oblanceolata, commonly known as the wax plant or porcelain plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the summer months. Hoya oblanceolata is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored choice for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
