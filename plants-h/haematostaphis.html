Haematostaphis

Haematostaphis, commonly known as the blood staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that resemble the antlers of a stag, hence its common name. The plant's fronds are typically dark green with a leathery texture and can grow quite large, sometimes reaching several meters in height. Haematostaphis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its distinctive appearance and slow growth make it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and homes.
