Hieracium torrigliense

Hieracium torrigliense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium torrigliense is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
