Hieracium atratum subsp. geminatum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

geminatum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It produces achenes with pappus, which aid in wind dispersal, and is known for its aggressive growth habits.
