Hedyotis corymbosa var. tereticaulis

Hedyotis corymbosa var. tereticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, often hairy stems that grow close to the ground. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Small white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters, often in the axils of the leaves.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
