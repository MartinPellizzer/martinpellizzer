Hieracium umbellatum var. uniflorum

Hieracium umbellatum var. uniflorum, commonly known as the singleflower hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and a single, terminal flower head that is usually yellow in color. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, daisy-like flowers arranged in a composite head.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
