Helianthemum × carmen-joanae

Helianthemum × carmen-joanae, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Helianthemum nummularium and Helianthemum hirsutum.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
