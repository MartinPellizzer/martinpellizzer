Hieracium montcaunicum

Hieracium montcaunicum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Hieracium montcaunicum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed habitats.
