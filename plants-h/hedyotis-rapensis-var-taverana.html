Hedyotis rapensis var. taverana

Hedyotis rapensis var. taverana, commonly known as the Tavera's shrub, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
