Hieracium pristophyllum

Hieracium pristophyllum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pristophyllum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
