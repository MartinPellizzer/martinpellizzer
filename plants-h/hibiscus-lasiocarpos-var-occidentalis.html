Hibiscus lasiocarpos var. occidentalis

Hibiscus lasiocarpos var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western downy hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
