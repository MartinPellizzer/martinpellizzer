Hypericum ternum

Hypericum ternum, commonly known as wood bishop's weed, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Hypericum ternum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
