Hieracium laevigatum subsp. brevifoliiforme

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

brevifoliiforme, commonly known as the short-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has smooth, often woolly stems and leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
