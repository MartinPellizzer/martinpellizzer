Haemanthus germarianus

Haemanthus germarianus, commonly known as the Natal grassbladder, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, with a central stem that bears vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
