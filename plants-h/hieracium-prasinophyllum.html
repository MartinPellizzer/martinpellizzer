Hieracium prasinophyllum

Hieracium prasinophyllum, commonly known as the green-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a green center.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
