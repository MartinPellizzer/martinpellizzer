Hemionitis harrisii

Hemionitis harrisii, commonly known as the Harris' hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited range and specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis harrisii is considered a sensitive indicator species for arid ecosystems.
