Henriettea grandifolia

Henriettea grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflorous henriettea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green surface. Henriettea grandifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
