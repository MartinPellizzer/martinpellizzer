Hamamelis mollis var. oblongifolia

Hamamelis mollis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the fragrant winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that turn a striking red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in late autumn, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for attracting wildlife and adding seasonal interest to landscapes.
