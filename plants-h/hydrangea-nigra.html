Hydrangea nigra

Hydrangea nigra, commonly known as the dark hydrangea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

Hydrangea nigra thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
