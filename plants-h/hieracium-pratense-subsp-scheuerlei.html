Hieracium pratense subsp. scheuerlei

Hieracium pratense subsp.

scheuerlei, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a basal rosette structure that helps it survive in various environmental conditions.

The subspecies scheuerlei is distinguished by its slightly different flower morphology and geographic distribution compared to other members of the species.
