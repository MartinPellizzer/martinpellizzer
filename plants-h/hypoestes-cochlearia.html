Hypoestes cochlearia

Hypoestes cochlearia, commonly known as the "Snowflake" or "Cupflower," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small cups or bells, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Hypoestes cochlearia is also valued for its adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
