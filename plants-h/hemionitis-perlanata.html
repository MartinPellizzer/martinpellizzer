Hemionitis perlanata

Hemionitis perlanata, commonly known as the sacred fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped leaves that form dense, mat-like growths. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of streams. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Hemionitis perlanata is also notable for its ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
