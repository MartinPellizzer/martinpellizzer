Hieracium calomelanum

Hieracium calomelanum, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with dark veins, and the flowers are yellow with dark central markings.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
