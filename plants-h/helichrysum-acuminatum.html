Helichrysum acuminatum

Helichrysum acuminatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ability to retain color makes it popular in floral arrangements and dried flower art.
