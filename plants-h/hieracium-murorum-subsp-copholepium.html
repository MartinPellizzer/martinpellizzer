Hieracium murorum subsp. copholepium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

copholepium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy meadows and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on slender stems.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
