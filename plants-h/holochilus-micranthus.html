Holochilus micranthus

Holochilus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered holochilus, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets.
