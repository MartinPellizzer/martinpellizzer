Hakea bifrons

Hakea bifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, woody cone-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its ability to thrive in harsh conditions. Hakea bifrons is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in native Australian flora.
