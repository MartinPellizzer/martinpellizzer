Hieracium bifidum subsp. subgracilicaule

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subgracilicaule, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are characteristic of the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
