Hypopeltis exaltata

Hypopeltis exaltata, commonly known as the tall grassland grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
