Hieracium anisopleurum

Hieracium anisopleurum, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and streambanks. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and can vary in size, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
