Homalomena benedikii

Homalomena benedikii, commonly known as the Benikii arrowleaf.

It is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Homalomena benedikii requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
