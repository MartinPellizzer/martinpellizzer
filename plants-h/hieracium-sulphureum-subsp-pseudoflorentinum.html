Hieracium sulphureum subsp. pseudoflorentinum

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

pseudoflorentinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow-orange flowers arranged in a single terminal capitulum.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive yellow coloration, which helps in its identification.
