Helicia ferruginea var. tropica

Helicia ferruginea var. tropica, commonly known as the tropical helicia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
