Hemigenia tomentosa

Hemigenia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland and heathland environments. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are covered in a dense, woolly coating, which helps to retain moisture in dry conditions.

Hemigenia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
