Haplophyllum cordatum

Haplophyllum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf haplophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and modest flowering make it an attractive addition to woodland gardens.
