Hemigenia pedunculata

Hemigenia pedunculata, commonly known as the mountain thyme or mountain germander, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hemigenia pedunculata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
