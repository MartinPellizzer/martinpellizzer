Hallia alata

Hallia alata, commonly known as the arrowleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Hallia alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
