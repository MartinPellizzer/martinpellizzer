Heliconia nitida

Heliconia nitida, commonly known as the white heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, boat-shaped inflorescences that are predominantly white with a hint of green. The plant produces showy, fleshy bracts that enclose the small, clustered flowers, which are typically yellow or orange. Heliconia nitida thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
