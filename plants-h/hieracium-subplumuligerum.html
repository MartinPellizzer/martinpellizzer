Hieracium subplumuligerum

Hieracium subplumuligerum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. It has a taproot system and can form dense colonies, which can sometimes be invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
