Hieracium segureum subsp. salassorum

Hieracium segureum subsp.

salassorum, commonly known as the Salassian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces numerous small seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
