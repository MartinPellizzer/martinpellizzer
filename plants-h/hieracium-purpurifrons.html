Hieracium purpurifrons

Hieracium purpurifrons, commonly known as the purple-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers surrounded by purple-tinged bracts, giving it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
