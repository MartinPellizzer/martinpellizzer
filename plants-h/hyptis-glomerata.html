Hyptis glomerata

Hyptis glomerata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are often aromatic and used in traditional medicine.

Hyptis glomerata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
