Helianthus corymbosus

Helianthus corymbosus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist or wet soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Helianthus corymbosus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
