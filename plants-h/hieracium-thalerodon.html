Hieracium thalerodon

Hieracium thalerodon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium thalerodon is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds, making it both a common weed and an important component of certain ecosystems.
