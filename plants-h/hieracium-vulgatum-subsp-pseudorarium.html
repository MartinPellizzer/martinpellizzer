Hieracium vulgatum subsp. pseudorarium

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

pseudorarium, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves and stems that are often reddish or purplish in color.

Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually sterile, giving it a distinctive appearance.
