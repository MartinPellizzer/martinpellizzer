Hieracium umbellatum subsp. halimifolium

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

halimifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive yellowish or whitish underside.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, making it easily recognizable.
