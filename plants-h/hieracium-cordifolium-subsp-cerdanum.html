Hieracium cordifolium subsp. cerdanum

Hieracium cordifolium subsp.

cerdanum, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
