Hypochaeris laciniata

Hypochaeris laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hypochaeris laciniata is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
