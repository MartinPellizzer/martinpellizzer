Hieracium canadense subsp. columbianum

Hieracium canadense subsp.

columbianum, commonly known as the Columbia common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along streambanks. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent and sometimes invasive plant in its native range.
