Huperzia melanesica

Huperzia melanesica, commonly known as the Melanesian clubmoss, is a tropical fern-like plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and spore-producing structures called strobila. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern and have a leathery texture.

Huperzia melanesica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
