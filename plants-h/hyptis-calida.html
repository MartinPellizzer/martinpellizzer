Hyptis calida

Hyptis calida, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis calida is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
