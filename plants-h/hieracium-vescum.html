Hieracium vescum

Hieracium vescum, commonly known as lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits called cypsela.

Hieracium vescum is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.
