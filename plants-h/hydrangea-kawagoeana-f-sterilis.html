Hydrangea kawagoeana f. sterilis

Hydrangea kawagoeana f. sterilis, commonly known as the sterile form of the Kawagoe hydrangea, is a unique variant of the species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lack of fertile flowers, which sets it apart from other hydrangea varieties. The plant typically features large, showy bracts that resemble flowers, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is deep green and often has a slightly serrated edge.

This cultivar thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
