Hieracium murorum subsp. chlorolomum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

chlorolomum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 30 cm.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more yellowish flower color compared to other Hieracium species.
