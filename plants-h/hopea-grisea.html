Hopea grisea

Hopea grisea, commonly known as the grey hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and is characterized by its pale grey bark and elliptical leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests.

Hopea grisea is valued for its timber and plays a role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
