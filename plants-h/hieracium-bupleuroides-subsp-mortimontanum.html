Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. mortimontanum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

mortimontanum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in a single terminal head. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in local ecosystems.
