Hieracium petrophyes

Hieracium petrophyes, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found on limestone or granite substrates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium petrophyes is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed or barren rocky areas.
