Hieracium bifidum subsp. scutatum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

scutatum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
