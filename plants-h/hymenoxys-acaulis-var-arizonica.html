Hymenoxys acaulis var. arizonica

Hymenoxys acaulis var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with a rosette of silver-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
