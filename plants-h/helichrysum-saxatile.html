Helichrysum saxatile

Helichrysum saxatile, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its dense clusters of yellow to golden-orange flowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a fluffy appearance, resembling miniature flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
