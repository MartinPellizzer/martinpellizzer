Hemionitis rediviva

Hemionitis rediviva, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique moss-like plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It possesses the remarkable ability to survive extreme desiccation, curling its leaves into a tight spiral to minimize water loss. When rehydrated, it can "resurrect" by rapidly regaining its turgid, green appearance. Its structure consists of small, leaf-like structures called phyllidia that are arranged in a spiral pattern.

This plant is of interest to scientists due to its potential applications in biotechnology and drought-resistant agriculture.
