Hieracium pictosaliencanum

Hieracium pictosaliencanum, commonly known as the painted thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy, violet to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, compact head, with ray florets that are often more prominent than the disk florets.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
