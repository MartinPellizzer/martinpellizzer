Hedyotis subacaulis

Hedyotis subacaulis, commonly known as the slender Indian cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and India.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance.
