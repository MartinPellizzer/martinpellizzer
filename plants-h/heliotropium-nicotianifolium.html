Heliotropium nicotianifolium

Heliotropium nicotianifolium, commonly known as the tobacco-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or subshrub with erect, often branched stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and resemble those of tobacco plants. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and usually have a violet or purple hue.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
