Hieracium prussicum subsp. lipnickianum

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

lipnickianum, commonly known as the Lipnick's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.
