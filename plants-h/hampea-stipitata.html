Hampea stipitata

Hampea stipitata, commonly known as the scarlet bignay, is a climbing legume native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological restoration, it is of interest in horticulture and conservation efforts.
