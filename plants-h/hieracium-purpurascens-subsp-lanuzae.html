Hieracium purpurascens subsp. lanuzae

Hieracium purpurascens subsp.

lanuzae, commonly known as the red hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

The showy yellow to orange flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
