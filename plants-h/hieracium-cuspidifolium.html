Hieracium cuspidifolium

Hieracium cuspidifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium cuspidifolium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
