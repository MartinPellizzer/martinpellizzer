Hieracium inaequalifrons

Hieracium inaequalifrons, commonly known as the unequal-fringed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, upright stems. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in meadows and grassy habitats. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It is a perennial herb that can form dense colonies, making it both a common weed and a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
