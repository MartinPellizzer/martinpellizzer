Homalium stelliferum subsp. andapense

Homalium stelliferum subsp.

andapense, commonly known as the Andaman star-fruit tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andaman Islands. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
