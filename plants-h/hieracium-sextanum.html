Hieracium sextanum

Hieracium sextanum, commonly known as the six-rayed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium sextanum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
