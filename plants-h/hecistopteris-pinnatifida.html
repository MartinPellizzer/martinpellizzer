Hecistopteris pinnatifida

Hecistopteris pinnatifida, commonly known as the pinnate fern, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the late Paleozoic era.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that resemble modern-day ferns. The plant's structure suggests it was adapted to moist, tropical environments, likely near water bodies. Its leaves were composed of numerous pinnae, which increased surface area for photosynthesis.

Fossil records indicate that Hecistopteris pinnatifida played a significant role in the early development of forest ecosystems.
