Hieracium nigrescens subsp. apargiiforme

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

apargiiforme, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a dark central disc.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
