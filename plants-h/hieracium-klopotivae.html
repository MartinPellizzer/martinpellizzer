Hieracium klopotivae

Hieracium klopotivae, commonly known as the Klopotivae hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
