Helianthemum ciliatum subsp. marmaricum

Helianthemum ciliatum subsp.

marmaricum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features dense clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has grey-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
