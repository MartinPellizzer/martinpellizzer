Hypocylix

Hypocylix, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, winding stalks. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water, aiding in photosynthesis. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the aquatic environment.

Hypocylix is also known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and religions.
