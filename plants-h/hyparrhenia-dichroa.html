Hyparrhenia dichroa

Hyparrhenia dichroa, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common component of savanna ecosystems.

Hyparrhenia dichroa is also valued for its ornamental use and has been studied for its potential in soil stabilization and erosion control.
