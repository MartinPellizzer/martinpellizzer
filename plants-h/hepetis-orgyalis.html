Hepetis orgyalis

Hepetis orgyalis, commonly known as the Oregon ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and streambanks.

Hepetis orgyalis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
