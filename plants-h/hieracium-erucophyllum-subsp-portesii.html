Hieracium erucophyllum subsp. portesii

Hieracium erucophyllum subsp.

portesii, commonly known as the Portes' hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous grasslands and rocky soils. It has lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
