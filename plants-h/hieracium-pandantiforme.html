Hieracium pandantiforme

Hieracium pandantiforme, commonly known as the hanging hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium pandantiforme is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
