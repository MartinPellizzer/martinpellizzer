Hesperanthemum microphyllum

Hesperanthemum microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, densely packed blue to purple flower heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, showy flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Hesperanthemum microphyllum is also valued for its low maintenance and ability to attract pollinators.
