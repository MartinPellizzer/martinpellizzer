Heliotropium curassavicum var. xerophilum

Heliotropium curassavicum var. xerophilum, commonly known as the desert heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that often appear pink or purple. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in dry environments.
