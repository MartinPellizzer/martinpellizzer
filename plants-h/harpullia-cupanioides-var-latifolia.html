Harpullia cupanioides var. latifolia

Harpullia cupanioides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed harpullia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
