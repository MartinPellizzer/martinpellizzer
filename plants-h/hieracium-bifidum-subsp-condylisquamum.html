Hieracium bifidum subsp. condylisquamum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

condylisquamum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is often hairy and branched. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils.
