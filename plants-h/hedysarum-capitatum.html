Hedysarum capitatum

Hedysarum capitatum, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Hedysarum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
