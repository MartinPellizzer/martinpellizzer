Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. sparsiforme

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

sparsiforme, commonly known as the sparseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the plant. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.
