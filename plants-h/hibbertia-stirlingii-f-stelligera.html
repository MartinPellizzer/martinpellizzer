Hibbertia stirlingii f. stelligera

Hibbertia stirlingii f. stelligera, commonly known as the Stirling's hibbertia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters and have a distinctive, tubular shape. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
