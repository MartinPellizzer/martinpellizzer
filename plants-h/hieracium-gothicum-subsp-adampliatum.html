Hieracium gothicum subsp. adampliatum

Hieracium gothicum subsp.

adampliatum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and smaller, more densely packed flowers compared to other members of the species. It thrives in rocky and dry environments, often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its habitat.
