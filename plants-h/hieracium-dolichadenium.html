Hieracium dolichadenium

Hieracium dolichadenium, commonly known as the long-stalked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium dolichadenium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
