Hedyotis pubirachis

Hedyotis pubirachis, commonly known as the hairy buttonweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Hedyotis pubirachis has a fibrous root system and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in wetland habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
