Hieracium dentatum subsp. gaudinii

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
