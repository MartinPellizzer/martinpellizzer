Helianthemum guttatum var. discolor

Helianthemum guttatum var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often with a distinctive red or purple tinge at the base. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings, giving it a striking visual contrast. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
