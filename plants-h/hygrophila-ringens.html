Hygrophila ringens

Hygrophila ringens, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, blue or violet flowers bloom in spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hygrophila ringens is valued in aquascaping for its ornamental appearance and ability to improve water quality.
