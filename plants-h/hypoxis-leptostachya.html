Hypoxis leptostachya

Hypoxis leptostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed starflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Hypoxidaceae family, which is sometimes referred to as the starflower family.

Hypoxis leptostachya is often used in traditional medicine and is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.
