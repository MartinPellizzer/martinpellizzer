Hieracium pontanum

Hieracium pontanum, commonly known as the Pontic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
