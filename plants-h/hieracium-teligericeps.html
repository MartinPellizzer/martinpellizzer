Hieracium teligericeps

Hieracium teligericeps, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers and a robust, upright growth habit. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions and can become invasive in some areas. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.
