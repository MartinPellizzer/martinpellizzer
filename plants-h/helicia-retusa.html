Helicia retusa

Helicia retusa, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Helicia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
