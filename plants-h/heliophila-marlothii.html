Heliophila marlothii

Heliophila marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

Heliophila marlothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
