Hieracium ramondii subsp. hypoleontodon

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

hypoleontodon, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence composed of yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

The subspecies hypoleontodon is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
