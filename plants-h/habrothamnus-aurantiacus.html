Habrothamnus aurantiacus

Habrothamnus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, herbaceous stems and vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
