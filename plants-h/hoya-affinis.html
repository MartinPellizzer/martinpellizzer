Hoya affinis

Hoya affinis, commonly known as the wax plant or porcelain vine, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Hoya affinis is a popular houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its relatively easy care requirements.

It is also valued for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for various indoor gardening setups.
