Hygrophila longifolia var. hirsutior

Hygrophila longifolia var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic and wetland gardens.
