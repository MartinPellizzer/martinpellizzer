Humboltia trilineata

Humboltia trilineata, commonly known as the three-veined hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Humboltia trilineata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
