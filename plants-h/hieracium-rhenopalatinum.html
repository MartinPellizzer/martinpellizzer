Hieracium rhenopalatinum

Hieracium rhenopalatinum, commonly known as the Rhine meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. Its inflorescence is surrounded by involucral bracts, and it is part of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium rhenopalatinum is often found in areas with high humidity and is sometimes considered an indicator species for certain habitats.
