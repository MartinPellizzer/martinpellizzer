Hieracium diarsianum

Hieracium diarsianum, commonly known as the two-spurred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium diarsianum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
