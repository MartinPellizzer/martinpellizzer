Hymenophyllum humboldtianum

Hymenophyllum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt's filmy fern, is a delicate, epiphytic fern native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its thin, translucent, and often curling fronds. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its spore-producing structures, called soridia, are borne on the underside of its fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
