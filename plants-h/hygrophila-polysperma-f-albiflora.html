Hygrophila polysperma f. albiflora

Hygrophila polysperma f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower hygrophila, is a variant of the water hygrophila species.

It is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia, often found in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features slender, ribbon-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is valued in aquascaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of water conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both decorative and ecological pond settings.
