Hieracium pallidum subsp. vranjanum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

vranjanum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically features wiry stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish to whitish flowers arranged in a single head, which are borne on short peduncles. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
