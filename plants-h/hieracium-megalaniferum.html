Hieracium megalaniferum

Hieracium megalaniferum, commonly known as the giant milk thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Hieracium.

This plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
