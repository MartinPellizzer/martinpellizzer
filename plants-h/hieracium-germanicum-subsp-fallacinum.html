Hieracium germanicum subsp. fallacinum

Hieracium germanicum subsp.

fallacinum, commonly known as the German hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
