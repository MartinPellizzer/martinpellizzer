Hieracium subglaberrimum subsp. bactiacense

Hieracium subglaberrimum subsp.

bactiacense, commonly known as the Bactrian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
