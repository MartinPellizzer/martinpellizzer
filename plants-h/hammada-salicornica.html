Hammada salicornica

Hammada salicornica, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments.

It possesses succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces greenish or yellowish flowers. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and arid regions with high salinity.

Its ability to tolerate salt makes it an important component of ecosystems in such environments.
