Haworthia armstrongii

Haworthia armstrongii, commonly known as the Armstrong's Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The leaves are usually bluish-green with distinct white or yellowish markings, and the plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
