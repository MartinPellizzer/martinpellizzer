Hedera vegeta

Hedera vegeta, commonly known as the common ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features dark green, lobed leaves that are often glossy and can grow in a variety of light conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Hedera vegeta thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls and trellises.

It is also known for its adaptability to different climates and its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
