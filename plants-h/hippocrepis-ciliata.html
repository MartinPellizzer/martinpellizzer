Hippocrepis ciliata

Hippocrepis ciliata, commonly known as the hairy helmet orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flowers that are often adorned with hairy structures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing showy blooms that attract various pollinators.
