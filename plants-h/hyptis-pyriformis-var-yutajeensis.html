Hyptis pyriformis var. yutajeensis

Hyptis pyriformis var. yutajeensis, commonly known as Yutajé or Yutajé herb, is a perennial shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is known for its small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
