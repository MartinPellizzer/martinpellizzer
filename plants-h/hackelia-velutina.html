Hackelia velutina

Hackelia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf hackelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Hackelia velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
