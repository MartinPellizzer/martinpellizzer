Horsfieldia sepikensis

Horsfieldia sepikensis, commonly known as the Sepik orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Horsfieldia sepikensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
