Hordeum murinum var. secalinum

Hordeum murinum var. secalinum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings but has historical significance in traditional farming practices.
