Hydroglossum heptaphyllum

Hydroglossum heptaphyllum, commonly known as the seven-leaved water-leaf, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, seven-lobed leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. Its roots are adapted for water environments, allowing it to anchor itself in muddy substrates.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, Hydroglossum heptaphyllum is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
