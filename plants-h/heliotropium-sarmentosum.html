Heliotropium sarmentosum

Heliotropium sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its prostrate growth habit and numerous small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its decorative foliage and blooms.
