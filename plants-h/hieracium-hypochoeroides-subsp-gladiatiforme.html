Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. gladiatiforme

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

gladiatiforme, commonly known as the gladiolus-like hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, often showy yellow flowers with a central disk.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
