Hackelia ursina var. pustulata

Hackelia ursina var. pustulata, commonly known as the woolly bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features erect, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hair, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of sky-blue, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also covered in a soft, fuzzy texture.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
