Hopea vesquei

Hopea vesquei, commonly known as the Burmese ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellowish-green, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hopea vesquei plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
