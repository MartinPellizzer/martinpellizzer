Hypericum marahuacanum subsp. strictissimum

Hypericum marahuacanum subsp.

strictissimum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Central America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species.
