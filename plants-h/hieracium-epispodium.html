Hieracium epispodium

Hieracium epispodium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
