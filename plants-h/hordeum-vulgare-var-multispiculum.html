Hordeum vulgare var. multispiculum

Hordeum vulgare var. multispiculum, commonly known as the multi-spiculate barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a panicle with multiple spicules, which are short, spikelet-bearing branches. This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is adapted to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both research and breeding programs in cereal crops.
