Hieracium tephrodes

Hieracium tephrodes, commonly known as the tephrodes hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is considered a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
