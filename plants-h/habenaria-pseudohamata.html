Habenaria pseudohamata

Habenaria pseudohamata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure typical of orchids.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
