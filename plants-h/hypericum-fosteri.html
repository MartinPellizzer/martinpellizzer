Hypericum fosteri

Hypericum fosteri, commonly known as Foster's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum fosteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
