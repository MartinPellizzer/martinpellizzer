Habenaria rosilloana

Habenaria rosilloana, commonly known as the Rosillo's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
