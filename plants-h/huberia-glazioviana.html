Huberia glazioviana

Huberia glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziovian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its environment.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
