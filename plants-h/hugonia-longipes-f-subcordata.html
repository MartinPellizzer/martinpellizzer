Hugonia longipes f. subcordata

Hugonia longipes f. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often ascending stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the fall and are an important food source for birds.

This variety is typically found in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
