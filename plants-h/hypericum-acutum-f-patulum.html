Hypericum acutum f. patulum

Hypericum acutum f. patulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has linear, narrow leaves that are opposite along the stems, giving it a distinct appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
