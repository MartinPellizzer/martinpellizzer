Hieracium subelongatum subsp. adenodontum

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

adenodontum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
