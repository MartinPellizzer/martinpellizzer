Hieracium mougeotii subsp. skyense

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

skyense, commonly known as the Skyense Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
