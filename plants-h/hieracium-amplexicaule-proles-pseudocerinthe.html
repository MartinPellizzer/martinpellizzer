Hieracium amplexicaule proles pseudocerinthe

Hieracium amplexicaule proles pseudocerinthe, commonly known as the false sandwort hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that help in spreading and forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
