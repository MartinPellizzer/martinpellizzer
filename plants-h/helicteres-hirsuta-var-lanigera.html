Helicteres hirsuta var. lanigera

Helicteres hirsuta var. lanigera, commonly known as the hairy helicteres, is a variant of the helicteres hirsuta species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft trichomes. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
