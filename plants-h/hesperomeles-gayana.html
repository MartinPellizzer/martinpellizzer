Hesperomeles gayana

Hesperomeles gayana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Hesperomeles gayana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
