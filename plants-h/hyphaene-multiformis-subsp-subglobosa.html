Hyphaene multiformis subsp. subglobosa

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

subglobosa, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a striking plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter.

The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is thick and covered in a fibrous, spongy material, which is often used in traditional crafts.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and important component of its native ecosystems.
