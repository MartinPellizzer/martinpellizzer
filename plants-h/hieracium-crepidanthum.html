Hieracium crepidanthum

Hieracium crepidanthum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are basal and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
