Hosta jonesii

Hosta jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' hosta, is a perennial plant native to Korea.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, and often variegated leaves that can range in color from light green to white and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late summer, typically in shades of blue or white. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Hosta jonesii is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
