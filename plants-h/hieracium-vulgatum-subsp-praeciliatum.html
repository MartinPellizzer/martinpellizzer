Hieracium vulgatum subsp. praeciliatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

praeciliatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in some regions.
