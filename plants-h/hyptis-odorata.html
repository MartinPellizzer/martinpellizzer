Hyptis odorata

Hyptis odorata, commonly known as sweet hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's fragrance is attributed to the presence of essential oils, which also contribute to its use in aromatherapy.
