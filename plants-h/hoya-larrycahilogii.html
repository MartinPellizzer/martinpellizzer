Hoya larrycahilogii

Hoya larrycahilogii, commonly known as the Larrycahilogii Wanda, is a species of epiphytic flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya larrycahilogii is popular among succulent and houseplant enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
