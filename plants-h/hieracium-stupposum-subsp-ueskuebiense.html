Hieracium stupposum subsp. ueskuebiense

Hieracium stupposum subsp.

ueskuebiense, commonly known as the Uskuebian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is characterized by its compact growth habit.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution.
