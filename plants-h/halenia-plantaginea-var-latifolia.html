Halenia plantaginea var. latifolia

Halenia plantaginea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved halenia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardening.
