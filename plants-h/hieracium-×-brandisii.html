Hieracium × brandisii

Hieracium × brandisii, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
