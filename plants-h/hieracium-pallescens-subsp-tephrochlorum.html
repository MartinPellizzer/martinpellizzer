Hieracium pallescens subsp. tephrochlorum

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

tephrochlorum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, which is surrounded by a series of bracts.

The subspecies tephrochlorum is distinguished by its lighter coloration and specific geographic distribution compared to other forms of Hieracium pallescens.
