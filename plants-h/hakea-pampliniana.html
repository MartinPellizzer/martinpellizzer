Hakea pampliniana

Hakea pampliniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical or conical inflorescences that bear small, pink or red flowers. These flowers are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

Hakea pampliniana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.
