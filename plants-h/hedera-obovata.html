Hedera obovata

Hedera obovata, commonly known as the Japanese ivy, is a species of ivy native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, ovate-shaped leaves that are often slightly wavy along the edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are not particularly showy, followed by black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Hedera obovata is a climbing plant that thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its resilience in different climatic conditions.
