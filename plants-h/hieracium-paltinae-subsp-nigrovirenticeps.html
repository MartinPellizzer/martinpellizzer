Hieracium paltinae subsp. nigrovirenticeps

Hieracium paltinae subsp.

nigrovirenticeps, commonly known as the black-green headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. Its flowers are surrounded by bracts that are often darker in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its presence in meadows and disturbed areas.
