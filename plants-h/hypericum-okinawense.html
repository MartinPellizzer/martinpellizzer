Hypericum okinawense

Hypericum okinawense, commonly known as the Okinawa St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hypericum okinawense is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat.
