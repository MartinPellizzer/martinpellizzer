Hypoxis beyrichii

Hypoxis beyrichii, commonly known as the starry false shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.

Hypoxis beyrichii is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
