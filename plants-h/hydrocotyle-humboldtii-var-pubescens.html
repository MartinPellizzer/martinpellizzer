Hydrocotyle humboldtii var. pubescens

Hydrocotyle humboldtii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy water pennywort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It typically grows in wet, sandy soils along the shores of the Pacific coast, thriving in brackish or saltwater environments. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, often floating on the surface of water.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
