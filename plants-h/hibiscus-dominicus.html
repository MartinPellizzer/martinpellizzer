Hibiscus dominicus

Hibiscus dominicus, commonly known as the Dominican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and dark green, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Hibiscus dominicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
