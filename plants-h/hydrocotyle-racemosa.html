Hydrocotyle racemosa

Hydrocotyle racemosa, commonly known as marsh mallow or water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It has distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Hydrocotyle racemosa is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
