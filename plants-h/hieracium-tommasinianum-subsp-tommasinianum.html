Hieracium tommasinianum subsp. tommasinianum

Hieracium tommasinianum subsp.

tommasinianum, commonly known as the Italian oxeye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces, and produces yellow flowers with white ray florets. Its inflorescence consists of a single yellow disk flower surrounded by white ray flowers, which bloom from June to August.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
