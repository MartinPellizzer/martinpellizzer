Heptaptera angustifolia

Heptaptera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heptaptera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
