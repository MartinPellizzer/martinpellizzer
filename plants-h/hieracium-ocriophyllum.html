Hieracium ocriophyllum

Hieracium ocriophyllum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium ocriophyllum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural areas.
