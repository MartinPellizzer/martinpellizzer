Hyptis glechomoides

Hyptis glechomoides, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its foliage is widely utilized as a culinary herb, especially in Mexican cuisine.
