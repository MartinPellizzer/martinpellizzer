Hieracium transiens subsp. kurvalae

Hieracium transiens subsp.

kurvalae, commonly known as the Kurvala's hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to certain regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and basal leaves. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in its native habitat.
