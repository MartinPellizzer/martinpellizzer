Helichrysum chrysargyrum

Helichrysum chrysargyrum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small suns, hence its common name. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that are typically surrounded by bracts, giving it a compact and fluffy appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
