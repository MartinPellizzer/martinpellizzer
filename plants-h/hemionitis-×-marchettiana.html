Hemionitis × marchettiana

Hemionitis × marchettiana, commonly known as the hybrid hemionitis, is a rare fern species resulting from the cross between Hemionitis arifolia and Hemionitis arifolia var. glabrata.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
