Huperzia huberi

Huperzia huberi, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Nepal. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in a dense layer of small, scale-like structures.

Huperzia huberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
