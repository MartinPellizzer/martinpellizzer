Hieracium subuliferum

Hieracium subuliferum, commonly known as the slender spearhead hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical leaf arrangements. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium subuliferum typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.
