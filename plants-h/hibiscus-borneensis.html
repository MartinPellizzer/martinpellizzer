Hibiscus borneensis

Hibiscus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus borneensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments within lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture and botanical gardens.
