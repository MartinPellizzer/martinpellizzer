Hieracium valletrianum

Hieracium valletrianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium valletrianum is often found in high-altitude habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
