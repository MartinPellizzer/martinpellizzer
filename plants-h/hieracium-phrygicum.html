Hieracium phrygicum

Hieracium phrygicum, commonly known as the French dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers develop into fluffy seed heads that are easily dispersed by the wind.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
