Hedera helix var. marginata-pulchella

Hedera helix var. marginata-pulchella, commonly known as the Variegated Ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that feature white and yellow margins alongside green centers. This plant is a climbing vine with lobed, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

The Variegated Ivy is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb walls or trellises.
