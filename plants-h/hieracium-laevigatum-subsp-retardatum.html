Hieracium laevigatum subsp. retardatum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

retardatum, commonly known as the slow-growing hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
