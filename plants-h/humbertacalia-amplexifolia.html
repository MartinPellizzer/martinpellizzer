Humbertacalia amplexifolia

Humbertacalia amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Humbertacalia amplexifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in desert gardens.
