Hyacinthus racemosus

Hyacinthus racemosus, commonly known as the racemose hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, racemose inflorescence of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces strap-like, linear leaves that are typically evergreen. Its blooms, which range in color from white to deep purple, are often used in ornamental gardening.

Hyacinthus racemosus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
