Hieracium subramosum var. ferrimontanum

Hieracium subramosum var. ferrimontanum, commonly known as the iron mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, rocky, or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has erect, sparsely hairy stems with alternate, heart-shaped to lanceolate leaves.

Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by petal-like rays, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
