Hieracium lyngenense

Hieracium lyngenense, commonly known as the Lyngen hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, acidic habitats such as heathlands and mountainous regions. It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

Hieracium lyngenense is often found in low nutrient soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
