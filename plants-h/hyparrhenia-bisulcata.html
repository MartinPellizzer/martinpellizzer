Hyparrhenia bisulcata

Hyparrhenia bisulcata, commonly known as the African grass or slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Hyparrhenia bisulcata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and wetlands.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in certain environments.
