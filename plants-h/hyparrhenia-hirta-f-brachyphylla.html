Hyparrhenia hirta f. brachyphylla

Hyparrhenia hirta f. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved hairy grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant in certain areas.
