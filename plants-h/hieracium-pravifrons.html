Hieracium pravifrons

Hieracium pravifrons, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium pravifrons is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread rapidly.
