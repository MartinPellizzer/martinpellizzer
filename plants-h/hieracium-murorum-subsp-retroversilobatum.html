Hieracium murorum subsp. retroversilobatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

retroversilobatum, commonly known as the reverse-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, featuring reverse-lobed leaves that set it apart from other hawkweed species. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and taxonomy.
