Habranthus schulzianus

Habranthus schulzianus, commonly known as the Schultens' grass-violet, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are tubular and are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds.
