Hieracium allum

Hieracium allum, commonly known as yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its yellow flowers bloom from June to September, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium allum is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
