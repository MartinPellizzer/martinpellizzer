Hymenophyllum pseudotunbridgense

Hymenophyllum pseudotunbridgense, commonly known as the false tunbridge moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, finely divided fronds with a distinctive, glossy appearance. Its spores are produced in clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum pseudotunbridgense is characterized by its intricate leaf structure and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
