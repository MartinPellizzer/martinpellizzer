Hydrocotyle javanica var. laxa

Hydrocotyle javanica var. laxa, commonly known as the slender marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, which are arranged in umbel-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
