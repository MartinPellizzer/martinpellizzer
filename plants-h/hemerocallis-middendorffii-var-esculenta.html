Hemerocallis middendorffii var. esculenta

Hemerocallis middendorffii var. esculenta, commonly known as the Chinese daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom for a short period each day. The plant is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Additionally, its edible flowers and leaves have been used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
