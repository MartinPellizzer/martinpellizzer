Heliotropium curassavicum var. fruticulosum

Heliotropium curassavicum var. fruticulosum, commonly known as the Florida heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a fragrant scent. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
