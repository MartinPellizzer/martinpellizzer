Hugelia densifolia var. mohavensis

Hugelia densifolia var. mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, dark blue berries that are edible. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks. Its leaves are typically evergreen, providing year-round coverage, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
