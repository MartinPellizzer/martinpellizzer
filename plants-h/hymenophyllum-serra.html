Hymenophyllum serra

Hymenophyllum serra, commonly known as the serrate brake, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The fronds are usually clustered in tufts and have a distinctive serrated or toothed margin. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its intricate structure and unique appearance make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
