Hoorebekia lanceolata

Hoorebekia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
