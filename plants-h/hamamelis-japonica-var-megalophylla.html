Hamamelis japonica var. megalophylla

Hamamelis japonica var. megalophylla, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel with large leaves, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant features large, ovate, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
