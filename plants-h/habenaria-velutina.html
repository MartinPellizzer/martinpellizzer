Habenaria velutina

Habenaria velutina, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a distinctive lip that is covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Habenaria velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
