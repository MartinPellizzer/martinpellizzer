Hyssopus officinalis var. maroccanus

Hyssopus officinalis var. maroccanus, commonly known as Moroccan hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
