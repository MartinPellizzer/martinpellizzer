Hedysarum boveanum subsp. costaetalentii

Hedysarum boveanum subsp.

costaetalentii, commonly known as the Costa Rican grassvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in racemes, characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife in its native range.
