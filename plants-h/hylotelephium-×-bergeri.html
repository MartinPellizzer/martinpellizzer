Hylotelephium × bergeri

Hylotelephium × bergeri, commonly known as the frostweed or hardy stonecrop, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hylotelelephium × bergeri is also known for its hardiness, able to survive in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.
