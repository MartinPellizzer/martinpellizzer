Hibiscus phanerandrus

Hibiscus phanerandrus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are dark green and often have a slight pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Hibiscus phanerandrus thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
