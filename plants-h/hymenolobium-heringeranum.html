Hymenolobium heringeranum

Hymenolobium heringeranum, commonly known as the Heringer's honeylocust, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant for both ecological and potential medicinal uses.
