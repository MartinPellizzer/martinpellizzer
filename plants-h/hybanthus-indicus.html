Hybanthus indicus

Hybanthus indicus, commonly known as the Indian hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
