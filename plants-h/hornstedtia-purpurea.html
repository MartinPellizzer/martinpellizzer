Hornstedtia purpurea

Hornstedtia purpurea, commonly known as the purple hornstedtia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Hornstedtia purpurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
