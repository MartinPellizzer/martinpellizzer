Hebe lycopodioides

Hebe lycopodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Hebe lycopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
