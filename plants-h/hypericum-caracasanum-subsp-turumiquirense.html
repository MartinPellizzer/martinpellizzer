Hypericum caracasanum subsp. turumiquirense

Hypericum caracasanum subsp.

turumiquirense, commonly known as the Turumiquirense St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent stamens.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are woody and branched.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
