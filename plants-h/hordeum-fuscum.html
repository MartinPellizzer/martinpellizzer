Hordeum fuscum

Hordeum fuscum, commonly known as black barley, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grains and is often found in dry, disturbed habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Hordeum fuscum is also studied for its potential in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature.
