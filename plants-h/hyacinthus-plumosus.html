Hyacinthus plumosus

Hyacinthus plumosus, commonly known as the feather hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is closely related to other hyacinth species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Hyacinthus plumosus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
