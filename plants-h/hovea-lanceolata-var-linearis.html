Hovea lanceolata var. linearis

Hovea lanceolata var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hovea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
