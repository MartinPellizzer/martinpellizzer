Halmoorea

Halmoorea, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round berries. Halmoorea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
