Heracleum platytaenium

Heracleum platytaenium, commonly known as the broad-leafed cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and flowering umbels.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features large, broadly ovate leaves with a distinctive pinnate venation. Its flowers are small, white to greenish-yellow, and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The plant is known for its strong scent and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
