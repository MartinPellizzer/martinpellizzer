Hieracium balsameum

Hieracium balsameum, commonly known as the balsam weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in a dense head, often surrounded by bracts.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
