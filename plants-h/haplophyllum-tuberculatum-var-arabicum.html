Haplophyllum tuberculatum var. arabicum

Haplophyllum tuberculatum var. arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian mountain heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to harsh, rocky environments and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid ecosystem botany.
