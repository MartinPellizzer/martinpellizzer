Hieracium murorum subsp. trochophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

trochophyllum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
