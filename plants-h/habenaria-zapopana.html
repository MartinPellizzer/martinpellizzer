Habenaria zapopana

Habenaria zapopana, commonly known as the Zapopan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a slight pink or purple hue, and they bloom during the spring or early summer.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
