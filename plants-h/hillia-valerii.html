Hillia valerii

Hillia valerii, commonly known as the velvetleaf hillia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
