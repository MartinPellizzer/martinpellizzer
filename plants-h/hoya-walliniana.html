Hoya walliniana

Hoya walliniana, commonly known as the Wallin's wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pink, often with a slight purple tinge. Hoya walliniana is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental value.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
