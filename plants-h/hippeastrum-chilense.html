Hippeastrum chilense

Hippeastrum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting inner throat. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Hippeastrum chilense is popular as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
