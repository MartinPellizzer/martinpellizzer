Hernandia peltata

Hernandia peltata, commonly known as the broad-leafed hernandia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, making it an important food source for local wildlife.

Hernandia peltata is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
