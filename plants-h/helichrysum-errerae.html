Helichrysum errerae

Helichrysum errerae, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble small suns. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum errerae is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
