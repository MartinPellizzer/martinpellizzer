Hibiscus perrottetii

Hibiscus perrottetii, commonly known as the Perrottet's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus perrottetii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
