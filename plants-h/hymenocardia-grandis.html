Hymenocardia grandis

Hymenocardia grandis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. Its flowers are small, white to pinkish, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and carving.
