Hibiscus ceratophorus

Hibiscus ceratophorus, commonly known as the horned hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and has a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age. Hibiscus ceratophorus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
