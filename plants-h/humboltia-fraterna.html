Humboltia fraterna

Humboltia fraterna, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Humboltia fraterna is often found in sandy soils and is a component of the chaparral and desert scrub ecosystems.
