Hieracium thyrsoideum var. plebejum

Hieracium thyrsoideum var. plebejum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense colonies due to its rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
