Hibiscus cupulazanza var. integrovatus

Hibiscus cupulazanza var. integrovatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
