Hieracium calocephalum

Hieracium calocephalum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, often hairy appearance, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
