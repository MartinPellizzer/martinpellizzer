Helichrysum italicum subsp. italicum

Helichrysum italicum subsp.

italicum, commonly known as immortelle or eternity flower, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its golden-yellow, papery flowers that remain intact when dried, giving it its common name.

The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its essential oil is widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery due to its uplifting and anti-inflammatory properties.
