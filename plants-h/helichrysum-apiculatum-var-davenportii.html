Helichrysum apiculatum var. davenportii

Helichrysum apiculatum var. davenportii, commonly known as the Davenport's golden weed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
