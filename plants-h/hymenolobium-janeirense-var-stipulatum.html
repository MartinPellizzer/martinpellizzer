Hymenolobium janeirense var. stipulatum

Hymenolobium janeirense var. stipulatum, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, stipulate leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. It produces legume pods that contain seeds, a typical feature of the legume family.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
