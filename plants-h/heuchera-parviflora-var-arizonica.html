Heuchera parviflora var. arizonica

Heuchera parviflora var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona alumroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features dense, matted foliage with dark green, velvety leaves that are often adorned with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
