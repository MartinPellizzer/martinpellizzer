Hieracium carolinianum subsp. alleghaniense

Hieracium carolinianum subsp.

alleghaniense, commonly known as the Allegheny hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and specific geographic distribution compared to other hawkweed species.
