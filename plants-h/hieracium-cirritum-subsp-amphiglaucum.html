Hieracium cirritum subsp. amphiglaucum

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

amphiglaucum, commonly known as the bluish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in grassy habitats and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive blue or purple tinge on the outer bracts.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
