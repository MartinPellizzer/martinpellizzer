Hydatella australis

Hydatella australis, commonly known as the Australian water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures include both male and female flowers, which contribute to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

Hydatella australis plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
