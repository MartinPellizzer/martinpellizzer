Hieracium nigrescens var. subneglectum

Hieracium nigrescens var. subneglectum, commonly known as the neglected hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
