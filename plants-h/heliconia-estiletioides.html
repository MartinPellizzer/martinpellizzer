Heliconia estiletioides

Heliconia estiletioides, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Heliconia estiletioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
