Handroanthus umbellatus var. lanceolatus

Handroanthus umbellatus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
