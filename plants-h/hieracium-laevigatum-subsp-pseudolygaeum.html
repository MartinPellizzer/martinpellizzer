Hieracium laevigatum subsp. pseudolygaeum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

pseudolygaeum, commonly known as the smooth meadowhawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, often in areas with calcareous soils.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disc and ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
