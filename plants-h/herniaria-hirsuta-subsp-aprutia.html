Herniaria hirsuta subsp. aprutia

Herniaria hirsuta subsp.

aprutia, commonly known as the hairy herniaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
