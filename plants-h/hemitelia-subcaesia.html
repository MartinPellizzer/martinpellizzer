Hemitelia subcaesia

Hemitelia subcaesia, commonly known as the silver-leaved hemitelia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow in color.

Hemitelia subcaesia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
