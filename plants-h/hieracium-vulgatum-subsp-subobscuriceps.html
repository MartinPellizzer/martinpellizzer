Hieracium vulgatum subsp. subobscuriceps

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

subobscuriceps, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are rough and hairy, with lance-shaped blades. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long, slender stems.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.
