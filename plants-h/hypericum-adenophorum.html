Hypericum adenophorum

Hypericum adenophorum, commonly known as the sticky St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, often sticky stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, red-orange fruits that contain seeds.

Hypericum adenophorum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.
