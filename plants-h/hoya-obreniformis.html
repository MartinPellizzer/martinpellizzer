Hoya obreniformis

Hoya obreniformis, commonly known as the "Crown of the Queen," is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Hoya obreniformis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
