Hieracium ramosum subsp. lentiginosum

Hieracium ramosum subsp.

lentiginosum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough, serrated edge.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black spots on the leaves, which give it its common name.
