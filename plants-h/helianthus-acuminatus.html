Helianthus acuminatus

Helianthus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, spiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Helianthus acuminatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
