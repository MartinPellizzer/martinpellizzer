Helichrysum membranaceum

Helichrysum membranaceum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble small cups, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Helichrysum membranaceum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
