Hyptis chacapoyensis

Hyptis chacapoyensis, commonly known as the Chacapoyes hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of northern Peru.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses by local communities.
