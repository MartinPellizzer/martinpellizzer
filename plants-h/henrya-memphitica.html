Henrya memphitica

Henrya memphitica, commonly known as the Memphitica henrya, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used as a ground cover or in rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland and cottage garden settings.
