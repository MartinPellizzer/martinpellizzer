Hedyotis secunda

Hedyotis secunda, commonly known as the lesser yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves with a yellowish-green hue and produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system.

Hedyotis secunda is often found in moist, temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
