Hymenophyllum coloratum

Hymenophyllum coloratum, commonly known as the rainbow fern, is a delicate, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds with a vibrant array of colors, ranging from green to red and yellow, depending on light exposure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and well-draining soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, typical of the Hymenophyllum genus.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
