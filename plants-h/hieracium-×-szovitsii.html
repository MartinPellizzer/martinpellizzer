Hieracium × szovitsii

Hieracium × szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits's hawkweed, is a hybrid plant species within the genus Hieracium, which belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosettes of leaves and flowering heads that produce small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is known for its ability to spread through vegetative reproduction.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
