Hysterobaeckea setifera subsp. setifera

Hysterobaeckea setifera subsp.

setifera, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its wiry, spinose stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming during the winter months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found on sandy soils.

Due to its hardiness and unique appearance, it is a notable species in the flora of the Western Australian desert.
