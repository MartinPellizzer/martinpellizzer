Helleboroides hyemale

Helleboroides hyemale, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often around Christmas time, hence its common name. The plant features a rosette of glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. It thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Helleboroides hyemale is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
