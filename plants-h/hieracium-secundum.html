Hieracium secundum

Hieracium secundum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its ability to spread aggressively, making it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
