Hemerocallis plantaginea

Hemerocallis plantaginea, commonly known as the Japanese daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable ornamental plant.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.
