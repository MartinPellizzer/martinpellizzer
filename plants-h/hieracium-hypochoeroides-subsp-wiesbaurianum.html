Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. wiesbaurianum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

wiesbaurianum, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets and a central disk of tubular florets.

The species is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in association with other members of the Asteraceae family.
