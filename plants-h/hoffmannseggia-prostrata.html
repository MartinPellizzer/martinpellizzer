Hoffmannseggia prostrata

Hoffmannseggia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate Hoffmannseggia, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny or woolly structures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability, showcasing succulent features for water storage.

Hoffmannseggia prostrata is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
