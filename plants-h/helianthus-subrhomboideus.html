Helianthus subrhomboideus

Helianthus subrhomboideus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the sunflower genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Helianthus subrhomboideus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
