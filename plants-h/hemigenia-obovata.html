Hemigenia obovata

Hemigenia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and are arranged in dense clusters.

Hemigenia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
