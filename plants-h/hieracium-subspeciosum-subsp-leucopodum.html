Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. leucopodum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

leucopodum, commonly known as the white-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are predominantly white or pale yellow.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
