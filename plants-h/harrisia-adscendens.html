Harrisia adscendens

Harrisia adscendens, commonly known as the red huito, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom during the dry season, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation in arid environments.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.
