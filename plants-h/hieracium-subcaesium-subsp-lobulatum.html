Hieracium subcaesium subsp. lobulatum

Hieracium subcaesium subsp.

lobulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a single head.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
