Hieracium salevense subsp. scabiosifolium

Hieracium salevense subsp.

scabiosifolium, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
