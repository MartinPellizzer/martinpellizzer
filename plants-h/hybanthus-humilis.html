Hybanthus humilis

Hybanthus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
