Hieracium pilosella subsp. minuticeps

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

minuticeps, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is characterized by its compact growth form and is part of the Asteraceae family.
