Hieracium polycladum

Hieracium polycladum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium polycladum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
