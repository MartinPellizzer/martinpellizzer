Hieracium albidobracteum

Hieracium albidobracteum, commonly known as the white bract hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with white bracts at the base.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
