Hieracium florentinum subsp. rumelicum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

rumelicum, commonly known as the Rumelic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically hairy and serrated. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
