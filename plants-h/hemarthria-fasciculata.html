Hemarthria fasciculata

Hemarthria fasciculata, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, including marshes and swampy soils, and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil.

Hemarthria fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as providing habitat for wildlife.
