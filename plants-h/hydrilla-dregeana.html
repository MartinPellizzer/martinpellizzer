Hydrilla dregeana

Hydrilla dregeana, commonly known as the African water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, branching stems that can grow up to several meters in length, with small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and it relies on water for reproduction and nutrient uptake. Hydrilla dregeana is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common invasive species in many parts of the world.
