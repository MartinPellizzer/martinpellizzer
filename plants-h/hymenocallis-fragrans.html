Hymenocallis fragrans

Hymenocallis fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant spider lily, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white or pale yellow flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, strap-like leaves. Its bulbs are bulbous and store water, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Hymenocallis fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal bloom.
