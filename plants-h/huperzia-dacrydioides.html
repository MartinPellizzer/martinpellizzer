Huperzia dacrydioides

Huperzia dacrydioides, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its sporangia are clustered in strobili at the tips of the leaves, a feature typical of many ferns.

Huperzia dacrydioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
