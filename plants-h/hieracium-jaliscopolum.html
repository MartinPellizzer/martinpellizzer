Hieracium jaliscopolum

Hieracium jaliscopolum, commonly known as the Jalisco hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
