Heuchera roseola

Heuchera roseola, commonly known as the rose-flowered coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking foliage, which ranges from deep red to purple hues. Heuchera roseola thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
