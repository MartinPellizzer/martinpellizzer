Huperzia brassii

Huperzia brassii, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Huperzia brassii is known for its ability to thrive in cool, humid climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of its leaves, contributing to its unique botanical profile.
