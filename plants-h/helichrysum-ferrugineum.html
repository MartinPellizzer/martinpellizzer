Helichrysum ferrugineum

Helichrysum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often tinged with a reddish or rusty color. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical heads. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Helichrysum ferrugineum is also noted for its drought tolerance and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate, long-lasting blooms.
