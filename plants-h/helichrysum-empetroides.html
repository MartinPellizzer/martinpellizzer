Helichrysum empetroides

Helichrysum empetroides, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum empetroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to retain its color when dried, making it a favored plant for floral arrangements.
