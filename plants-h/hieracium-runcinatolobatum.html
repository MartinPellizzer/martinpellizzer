Hieracium runcinatolobatum

Hieracium runcinatolobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often in temperate climates.

Hieracium runcinatolobatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
