Hibiscus corymbosus var. integrifolius

Hibiscus corymbosus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically broad and entire, giving the plant a distinctive, elegant appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
