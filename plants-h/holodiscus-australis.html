Holodiscus australis

Holodiscus australis, commonly known as the Australian holodiscus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and full sun, reflecting its natural habitat in arid regions.
