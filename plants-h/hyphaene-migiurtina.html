Hyphaene migiurtina

Hyphaene migiurtina, commonly known as the Socotra date palm, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like crown and long, drooping leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates. Its trunk is fibrous and can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height.

Hyphaene migiurtina is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat, showcasing remarkable resilience to drought conditions.
