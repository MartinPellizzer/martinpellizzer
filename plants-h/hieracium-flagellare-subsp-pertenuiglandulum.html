Hieracium flagellare subsp. pertenuiglandulum

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

pertenuiglandulum, commonly known as the slender glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with fine, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender stems and reduced glandular structures compared to other members of the species.
