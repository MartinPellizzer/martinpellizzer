Hyptis pedalipes

Hyptis pedalipes, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It often grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The leaves are frequently used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
