Haemanthus delagoensis

Haemanthus delagoensis, commonly known as the Blood Lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its showy, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in a tight, spherical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form clumps over time, creating a lush, dramatic display.
