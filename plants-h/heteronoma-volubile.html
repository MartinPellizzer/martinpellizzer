Heteronoma volubile

Heteronoma volubile, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vining growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system that aids in its climbing ability. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
