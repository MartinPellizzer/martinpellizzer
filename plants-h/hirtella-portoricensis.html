Hirtella portoricensis

Hirtella portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small and white, typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries as fruit.

Hirtella portoricensis is often found in moist, shaded areas of the understory in tropical forests.
