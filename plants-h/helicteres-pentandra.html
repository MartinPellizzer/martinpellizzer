Helicteres pentandra

Helicteres pentandra, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing oval, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
