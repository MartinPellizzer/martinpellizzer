Haworthia cooperi var. gracilis

Haworthia cooperi var. gracilis, commonly known as the "Lace Haworthia," is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically pale green with white or silver markings. The leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating called farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
