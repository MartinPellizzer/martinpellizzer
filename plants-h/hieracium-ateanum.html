Hieracium ateanum

Hieracium ateanum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and basal leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium ateanum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
