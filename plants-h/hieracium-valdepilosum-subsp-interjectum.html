Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. interjectum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

interjectum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
