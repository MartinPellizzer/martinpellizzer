Heliconia brasiliensis var. concolor

Heliconia brasiliensis var. concolor, commonly known as the Brazilian heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers at the base of the bracts, which are pollinated by birds and insects. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a decorative plant in greenhouses.
