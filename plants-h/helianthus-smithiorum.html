Helianthus smithiorum

Helianthus smithiorum, commonly known as Smith's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow composite flowers and sturdy, hairy stems. Native to the southwestern United States, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces both ray and disk flowers, typical of the sunflower genus.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sunlight.
