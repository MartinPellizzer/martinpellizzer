Haemocharis sessilis

Haemocharis sessilis, commonly known as the pink flamingo plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble the pink feathers of a flamingo, hence its common name. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and waxy texture. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a unique, elongated shape that blooms in clusters.

Haemocharis sessilis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
