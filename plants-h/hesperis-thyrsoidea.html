Hesperis thyrsoidea

Hesperis thyrsoidea, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hesperis thyrsoidea is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
