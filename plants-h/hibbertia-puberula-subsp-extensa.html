Hibbertia puberula subsp. extensa

Hibbertia puberula subsp.

extensa, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often pubescent.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent orange anthers, which bloom in spring. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodland edges. Hibbertia puberula subsp.

extensa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
