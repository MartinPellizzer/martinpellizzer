Hybanthus communis var. glabrifolius

Hybanthus communis var. glabrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leafed hybanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred shape.

It is often found in association with other epiphytic plants in humid, lowland habitats.
