Hemerocallis lancifolia

Hemerocallis lancifolia, commonly known as the sword lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is a member of the Hemerocallidaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.

The flowers are short-lived, lasting only one day, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Hemerocallis.
