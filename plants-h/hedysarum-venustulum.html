Hedysarum venustulum

Hedysarum venustulum, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

Hedysarum venustulum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
