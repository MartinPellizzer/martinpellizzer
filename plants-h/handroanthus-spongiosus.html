Handroanthus spongiosus

Handroanthus spongiosus, commonly known as the spongeflower or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
