Helichrysum rutidolepis

Helichrysum rutidolepis, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that retain their color even when dried. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum rutidolepis is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a source of natural dyes.
