Hieracium bifidum subsp. subimbricatum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subimbricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive bifid (split) appearance, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its aggressive growth habit and can become invasive in certain environments.
