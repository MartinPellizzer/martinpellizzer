Huperzia subtubulosa

Huperzia subtubulosa, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves, typical of many ferns.

Huperzia subtubulosa is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
