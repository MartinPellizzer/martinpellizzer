Haplolophium

Haplolophium, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a succulent belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, coin-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. Haplolophium thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
