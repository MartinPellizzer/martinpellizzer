Hippocratea arborea

Hippocratea arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and pinnately compound. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
