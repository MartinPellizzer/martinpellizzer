Hieracium incisum subsp. calamistratum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

calamistratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and wastelands. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and is often found in association with other members of the Hieracium genus.
