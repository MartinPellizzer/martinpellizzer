Homalomena scutata

Homalomena scutata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Homalomena scutata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
