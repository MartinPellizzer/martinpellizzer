Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. caesiopictum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

caesiopictum, commonly known as the blue-rayed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers with distinctive blue or violet markings on the ray florets, which are arranged in a single row. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique coloration and floral structure compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
