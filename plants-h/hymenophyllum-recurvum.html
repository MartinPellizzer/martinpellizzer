Hymenophyllum recurvum

Hymenophyllum recurvum, commonly known as the recurved haircap moss, is a small, cryptogamic plant belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance with closely spaced, overlapping leaves that are often covered in a thin layer of rhizoids. This moss thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on the bark of trees or in moist rock crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinctive recurved tip, contributing to its common name.

Hymenophyllum recurvum plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms.
