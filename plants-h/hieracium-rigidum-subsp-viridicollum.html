Hieracium rigidum subsp. viridicollum

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

viridicollum, commonly known as the green-necked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant has upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or divided. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and are borne on long, slender peduncles.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish collar-like base on the stem, which gives it its common name.
