Hemiperis crispa

Hemiperis crispa, commonly known as the smooth hemlock, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and smooth on both sides. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Hemiperis crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
