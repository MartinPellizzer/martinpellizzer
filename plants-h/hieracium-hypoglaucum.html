Hieracium hypoglaucum

Hieracium hypoglaucum, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often have a bluish or whitish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The pale centaury produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
