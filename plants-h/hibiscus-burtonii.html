Hibiscus burtonii

Hibiscus burtonii, commonly known as the Burtt's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Hibiscus burtonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
