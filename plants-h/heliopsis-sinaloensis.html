Heliopsis sinaloensis

Heliopsis sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Heliopsis sinaloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
