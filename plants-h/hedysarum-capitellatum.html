Hedysarum capitellatum

Hedysarum capitellatum, commonly known as the small-headed vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hedysarum capitellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
