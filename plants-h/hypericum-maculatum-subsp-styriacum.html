Hypericum maculatum subsp. styriacum

Hypericum maculatum subsp.

styriacum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Central Europe. It features bright yellow flowers with red spots, which bloom from June to August.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
