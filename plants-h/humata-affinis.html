Humata affinis

Humata affinis, commonly known as the common humata, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Humata affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
