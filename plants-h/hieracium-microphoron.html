Hieracium microphoron

Hieracium microphoron, commonly known as the small-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in dense clusters, forming a single terminal head.

Hieracium microphoron is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
