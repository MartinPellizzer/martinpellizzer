Helianthemum polifolium var. oblongifolium

Helianthemum polifolium var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower of the rock, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand drought and coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
