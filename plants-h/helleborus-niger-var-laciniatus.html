Helleborus niger var. laciniatus

Helleborus niger var. laciniatus, commonly known as the Christmas rose or the naked lady, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive notched appearance. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers with prominent yellow centers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ability to bloom even in cold conditions, adding early color to the landscape.
