Hieracium hanburyi var. atraticeps

Hieracium hanburyi var. atraticeps, commonly known as the Black-headed Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with dark centers, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
