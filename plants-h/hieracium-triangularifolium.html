Hieracium triangularifolium

Hieracium triangularifolium, commonly known as the triangular-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive triangular appearance, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.
