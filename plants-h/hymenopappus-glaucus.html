Hymenopappus glaucus

Hymenopappus glaucus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces a distinctive pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Hymenopappus glaucus is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a low-growing habit.
