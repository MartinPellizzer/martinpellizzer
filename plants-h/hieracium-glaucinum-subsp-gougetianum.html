Hieracium glaucinum subsp. gougetianum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

gougetianum, commonly known as the Gouget's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in the Mediterranean region.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed group, with specific adaptations to its arid environment.
