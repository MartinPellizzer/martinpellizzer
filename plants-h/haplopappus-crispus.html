Haplopappus crispus

Haplopappus crispus, commonly known as the curly pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its finely dissected, curling leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in the spring, and the plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Haplopappus crispus is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to arid environments.
