Humiria compacta

Humiria compacta, commonly known as the compact dwarf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features striking, brightly colored bracts that surround its small, clustered flowers. Its foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Humiria compacta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
