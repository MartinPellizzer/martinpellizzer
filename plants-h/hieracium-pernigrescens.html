Hieracium pernigrescens

Hieracium pernigrescens, commonly known as the black-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its distinctive blackish stems and hairy foliage make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
