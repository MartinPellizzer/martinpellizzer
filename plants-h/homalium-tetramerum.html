Homalium tetramerum

Homalium tetramerum, commonly known as the four-branch hollies, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptic leaves that are usually glossy green. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species.

The bark is smooth and often has a reddish hue, making it visually distinctive.
