Hieracium ramondii subsp. asturicum

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

asturicum, commonly known as the Asturian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
