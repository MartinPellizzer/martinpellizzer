Hexopia

Hexopia, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in a variety of vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Hexopia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
