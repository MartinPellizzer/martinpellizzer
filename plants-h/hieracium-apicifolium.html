Hieracium apicifolium

Hieracium apicifolium, commonly known as the spear-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds.
