Hieracium cordifolium subsp. eriocerinthe

Hieracium cordifolium subsp.

eriocerinthe, commonly known as the woolly heartleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with dense, soft white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
