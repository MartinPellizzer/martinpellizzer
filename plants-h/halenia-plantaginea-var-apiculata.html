Halenia plantaginea var. apiculata

Halenia plantaginea var. apiculata, commonly known as the mountain halenia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asparagaceae family.
