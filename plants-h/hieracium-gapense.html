Hieracium gapense

Hieracium gapense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium gapense is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
