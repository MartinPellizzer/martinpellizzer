Helichrysum traversii

Helichrysum traversii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant has wiry, branching stems and needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum traversii is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
