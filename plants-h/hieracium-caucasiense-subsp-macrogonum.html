Hieracium caucasiense subsp. macrogonum

Hieracium caucasiense subsp.

macrogonum, commonly known as the Caucasian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers with prominent ray florets, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in moist, meadow-like habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and regionally endemic species.
