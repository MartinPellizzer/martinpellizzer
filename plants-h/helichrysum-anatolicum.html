Helichrysum anatolicum

Helichrysum anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian immortelle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, spherical or conical shapes. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Helichrysum anatolicum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
