Habzelia ferruginea

Habzelia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty habzelia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender stems and small, often hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
