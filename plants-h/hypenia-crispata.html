Hypenia crispata

Hypenia crispata, commonly known as the brittleleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in summer. Hypenia crispata thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
