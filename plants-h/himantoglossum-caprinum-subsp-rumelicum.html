Himantoglossum caprinum subsp. rumelicum

Himantoglossum caprinum subsp.

rumelicum, commonly known as the Alpine marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and streams.

The plant features a rosette of broad, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The species is adapted to cold, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
