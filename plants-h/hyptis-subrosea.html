Hyptis subrosea

Hyptis subrosea, commonly known as rosemary hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Hyptis subrosea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
