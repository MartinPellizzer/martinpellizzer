Habrochloa

Habrochloa, commonly known as bluegrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the Poaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their fine, tufted growth habit and often bluish-green foliage. They are native to North America and are commonly found in grasslands and open woodlands. Habrochloa species are typically perennial and have rhizomatous or stoloniferous growth patterns.

They play an important role in ecological systems and are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value.
