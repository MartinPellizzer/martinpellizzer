Hahnia aria var. angustifolia

Hahnia aria var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hahnia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
