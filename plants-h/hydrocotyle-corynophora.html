Hydrocotyle corynophora

Hydrocotyle corynophora, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often floating or submerged, and it produces small, white flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving water.

Hydrocotyle corynophora plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
