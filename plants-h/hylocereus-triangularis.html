Hylocereus triangularis

Hylocereus triangularis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a climbing cactus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers with a strong, sweet scent. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-draining soil.

The fruit, known as the dragon fruit, is edible and has a mildly sweet flavor.
