Hieracium bicolor subsp. timbalianum

Hieracium bicolor subsp.

timbalianum, commonly known as the Timbalian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Australia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a low-growing species with a creeping habit, often forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
