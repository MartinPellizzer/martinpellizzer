Haworthia vlokii

Haworthia vlokii, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in white tubercles. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers on a slender stem during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor and rock garden settings.
