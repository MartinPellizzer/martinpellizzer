Hieracium gavarnianum

Hieracium gavarnianum, commonly known as the Gavarnian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, dandelion-like flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environments, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
