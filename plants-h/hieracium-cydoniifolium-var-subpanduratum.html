Hieracium cydoniifolium var. subpanduratum

Hieracium cydoniifolium var. subpanduratum, commonly known as the panduratum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the disk florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
