Hibbertia obtusifolia

Hibbertia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Hibbertia obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
