Hieracium borzae subsp. borzae

Hieracium borzae subsp.

borzae, commonly known as the Borza Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often reflexed. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to harsh mountainous conditions.
