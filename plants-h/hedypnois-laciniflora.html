Hedypnois laciniflora

Hedypnois laciniflora, commonly known as the laceleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Hedypnois laciniflora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
