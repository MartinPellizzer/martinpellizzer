Hymenophyllum raddianum

Hymenophyllum raddianum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Hymenophyllum raddianum plays an important ecological role in forest understories by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
