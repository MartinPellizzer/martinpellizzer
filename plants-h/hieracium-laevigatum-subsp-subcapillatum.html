Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subcapillatum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subcapillatum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are generally hairy, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and small, inconspicuous bracts.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
