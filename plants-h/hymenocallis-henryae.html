Hymenocallis henryae

Hymenocallis henryae, commonly known as Henry's spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a prominent, often red or purple, dorsal sepal that resembles a spider's leg, giving it its common name. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a tall, slender stem that supports its ornamental blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and eye-catching addition to the landscape.
