Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. boreale

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the northern rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern Europe. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
