Helleborine latifolia f. brevibracteata

Helleborine latifolia f. brevibracteata, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and pale greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, preferring rich, humusy soils. It is a perennial herb that produces underground rhizomes and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The specific epithet *brevibracteata* refers to its short, broad bracts that enclose the flower buds.
