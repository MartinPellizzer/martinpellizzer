Hieraciodes lampsanodes

Hieraciodes lampsanodes, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a low-growing habit with lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
