Heterodon superbus

Heterodon superbus, commonly known as the eastern hog-nosed snake, is a medium-sized snake native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick body, broad head, and distinctive upturned snout, which gives it its common name. The species is known for its unique defensive behavior, including playing dead when threatened. Its coloration typically ranges from brown to reddish-brown with darker markings.

Heterodon superbus plays an important role in its ecosystem by controlling populations of small prey such as rodents and insects.
