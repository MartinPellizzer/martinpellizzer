Hilsenbergia moratiana

Hilsenbergia moratiana, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Hilsenbergia moratiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
