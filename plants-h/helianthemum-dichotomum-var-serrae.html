Helianthemum dichotomum var. serrae

Helianthemum dichotomum var. serrae, commonly known as the serrated rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
