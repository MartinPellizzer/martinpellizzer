Hexalectris mexicana

Hexalectris mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fairy slipper, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive coloration of purple and yellow. The plant produces small, green, leaf-like structures called pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
