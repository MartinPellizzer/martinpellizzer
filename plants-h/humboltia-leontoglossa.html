Humboltia leontoglossa

Humboltia leontoglossa, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a dense, woolly crown of leaves that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Humboltia leontoglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique foliage structure.
