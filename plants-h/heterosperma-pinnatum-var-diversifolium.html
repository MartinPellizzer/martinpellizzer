Heterosperma pinnatum var. diversifolium

Heterosperma pinnatum var. diversifolium, commonly known as the variable featherleaf, is a variant of the species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant typically features lanceolate to ovate leaves that vary in shape and size, contributing to its common name. The leaves are often arranged alternately along the stems, giving the plant a feathery appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in inflorescences that bloom in early spring.

The plant is adapted to moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
