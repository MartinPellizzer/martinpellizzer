Hiraea obovata var. angustifolia

Hiraea obovata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved hiraea, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and extended flowering period.
