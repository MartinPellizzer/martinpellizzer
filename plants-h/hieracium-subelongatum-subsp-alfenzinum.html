Hieracium subelongatum subsp. alfenzinum

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

alfenzinum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
