Hypericum ellipticum

Hypericum ellipticum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowers are composed of four petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hypericum ellipticum is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
