Habenaria malipoensis

Habenaria malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in China, Vietnam, and Thailand.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with distinct markings, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
