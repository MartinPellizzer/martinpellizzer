Hepatica nobilis f. rosea

Hepatica nobilis f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered hepatica, is a flowering plant native to the woodlands of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and its early spring blooms, which typically appear in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors with rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers are short-lived but attract early pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
