Hieracium sabaudum

Hieracium sabaudum, commonly known as the Swiss buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the common buttercup. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are upright with a hairy surface.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
