Hordeum spontaneum var. ladarcyvir

Hordeum spontaneum var. ladarcyvir, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild relative of cultivated barley.

It is a grass species native to various regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has slender, erect stems with small, spikelet-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its genetic diversity and potential use in breeding programs to improve agricultural barley varieties.
