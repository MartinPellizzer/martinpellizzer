Hedyotis laricifolia

Hedyotis laricifolia, commonly known as mountain buttonweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes and are usually white or pale green, giving it a delicate appearance.

Hedyotis laricifolia is often found in association with coniferous trees, hence its common name.
