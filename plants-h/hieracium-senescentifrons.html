Hieracium senescentifrons

Hieracium senescentifrons, commonly known as the old-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which is surrounded by bracts. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
