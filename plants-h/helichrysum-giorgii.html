Helichrysum giorgii

Helichrysum giorgii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Helichrysum giorgii is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
