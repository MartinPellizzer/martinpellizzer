Hieracium laevigatum subsp. kubanicum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

kubanicum, commonly known as the Kuban hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lanceolate leaves and bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, including the Caucasus region.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
