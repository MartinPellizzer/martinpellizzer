Hesperis trichosepala

Hesperis trichosepala, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes and feature distinctive fringed petals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
