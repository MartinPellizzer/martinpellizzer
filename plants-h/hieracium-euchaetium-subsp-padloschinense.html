Hieracium euchaetium subsp. padloschinense

Hieracium euchaetium subsp.

padloschinense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered with glandular structures.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
