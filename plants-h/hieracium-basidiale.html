Hieracium basidiale

Hieracium basidiale, commonly known as the basidial hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium basidiale is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
