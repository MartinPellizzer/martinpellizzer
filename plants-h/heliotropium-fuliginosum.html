Heliotropium fuliginosum

Heliotropium fuliginosum, commonly known as smoky heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that often have a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Heliotropium fuliginosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
