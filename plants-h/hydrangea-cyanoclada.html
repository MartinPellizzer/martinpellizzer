Hydrangea cyanoclada

Hydrangea cyanoclada, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of blue or pink flowers, which can change color based on soil pH. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Hydrangea cyanoclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
