Helleborus atrorubens

Helleborus atrorubens, commonly known as the black and red hellebore, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and striking flowers that range from deep red to black in color. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal history, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its chemical compounds.
