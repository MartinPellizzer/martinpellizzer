Scutellaria baicalensis, also known as Baikal skullcap or Chinese skullcap, is a perennial plant native to China and Mongolia.

Side Effects

Scutellaria baicalensis side effects are generally mild but can be severe for some individuals.

These side effects may include dizziness, headaches, nausea, stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions such as hives and itching.

People who are most likely to experience these side effects are those with a history of kidney or liver disease, pregnant women, and individuals taking medications that interact with the plant's active compounds, including baicalein and baicalin.

Precautions should be taken to avoid consuming excessive amounts of Scutellaria baicalensis, as this can lead to side effects such as insomnia, anxiety, and increased heart rate, as well as interactions with blood thinners, diabetes medications, and other prescription medications.

The possible side effects of Scutellaria baicalensis are listed below.

  • Gastrointestinal upset: Scutellaria baicalensis may cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals due to its bitter properties.
  • Allergic reactions: Some people may experience allergic reactions such as hives, itching, and swelling after consuming scutellaria baicalensis.
  • Interactions with medications: Scutellaria baicalensis may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications, which can lead to adverse effects.
  • Liver damage: There is a risk of liver damage associated with long-term use or high doses of scutellaria baicalensis.
  • Respiratory problems: Scutellaria baicalensis may exacerbate respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (copd) in some individuals.
  • Skin rash: Some people may experience a skin rash or other skin reactions after consuming scutellaria baicalensis.
  • Hemolysis: Scutellaria baicalensis may cause hemolysis, a condition in which red blood cells are destroyed, in individuals with certain medical conditions.
