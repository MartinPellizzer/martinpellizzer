Side Effects

Lycium chinense side effects are generally mild to moderate, but may include dizziness, headaches, and nausea in some individuals.

People most likely to experience these side effects are those with pre-existing liver or kidney conditions, as well as those taking medications such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and certain antidepressants.

Additionally, individuals with allergies or sensitivities may experience symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

To minimize potential side effects, it is recommended to consume Lycium chinense in moderation, avoid consuming it with other medications or substances, and be cautious when using it in combination with other herbs or supplements.

The possible side effects of Lycium chinense are listed below.

  • Hypoglycemia: Low blood sugar levels, which can be a concern for individuals with diabetes or those taking medications that lower blood sugar.
  • Allergic reactions: Some people may be allergic to lycium chinense, which can cause symptoms such as itching, hives, and difficulty breathing.
  • Gastrointestinal upset: Consuming large amounts of lycium chinense may lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.
  • Interactions with medications: Lycium chinense may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications.
  • Increased bleeding risk: Consuming large amounts of lycium chinense may increase the risk of bleeding, particularly in individuals taking medications that thin the blood.
  • Headaches and dizziness: Some people may experience headaches and dizziness after consuming lycium chinense, possibly due to the plant's flavonoid and anthocyanin content.
  • Skin darkening: Prolonged consumption of lycium chinense may cause skin darkening, particularly on the face and hands, due to the plant's high anthocyanin content.
