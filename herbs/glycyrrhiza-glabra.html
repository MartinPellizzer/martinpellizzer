Side Effects

Glycyrrhiza glabra side effects are numerous and can be severe, particularly for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking certain medications.

These side effects include high blood pressure, low potassium levels, edema, and heart problems, as well as more rare but serious conditions such as kidney failure, liver damage, and adrenal insufficiency.

People with conditions like hypertension, heart disease, kidney disease, or those taking diuretics, ACE inhibitors, or other medications that affect electrolyte balance are most likely to experience these side effects.

To minimize risks, individuals should avoid excessive consumption of glycyrrhiza glabra, especially in the form of licorice root, and consider alternative sources of licorice flavor, such as licorice extract or synthetic licorice flavorings.

  • Hypokalemia: Low potassium levels in the blood, which can lead to muscle weakness, fatigue, and heart problems.
  • Edema: Swelling in the feet, ankles, and hands due to fluid retention.
  • Muscle weakness: Muscle fatigue and weakness, which can make everyday activities difficult.
  • Cushing's syndrome: A condition caused by excess cortisol in the body, leading to weight gain, high blood pressure, and other symptoms.
  • Alkalosis: A condition where the blood becomes too alkaline, leading to muscle weakness, fatigue, and other symptoms.
  • Potassium depletion: Depletion of potassium levels in the body, which can lead to muscle weakness, fatigue, and heart problems.
  • Hypertension: High blood pressure, which can lead to heart disease, stroke, and other cardiovascular problems.
