7 Best Benefits Of Vaccinium Myrtillus

Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as bilberry, is a type of shrub that belongs to the heather family, native to Europe and Western Asia.

The bioactive compounds found in Vaccinium myrtillus, including anthocyanins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, are responsible for its numerous health benefits.

These compounds have been shown to ease inflammation, act as antioxidants, and support the immune system, making Vaccinium myrtillus a valuable resource for maintaining overall health and well-being.

The high content of anthocyanins, in particular, contributes to its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Featured Study

According to Methods (San Diego, Calif.), Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as blueberry, has been found to have antigiardial activity, reducing the viability of Giardia duodenalis trophozoites in a dose-dependent manner, and also modifying the morphology of both G. duodenalis and Cryptosporidium parvum.
