identification

Silybum marianum, also known as the milk thistle, is a biennial or perennial plant that can be identified by its distinctive purple flowers and white or purple veins on its large, spiny leaves. The plant's leaves are typically arranged oppositely on the stem and have a milky sap when broken. Silybum marianum can grow quite tall, often reaching heights of 2-4 meters, and is commonly found in dry areas and disturbed lands.

uses

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat liver and gallbladder disorders.
