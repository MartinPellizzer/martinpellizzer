9 Most Common Side Effects of Angelica Sinensis

1. Causes Nausea

Angelica sinensis causes nausea in some individuals, particularly when consumed in large quantities or combined with other herbs that may exacerbate gastrointestinal issues. This side effect is often associated with the plant's saponin content, which can have a negative impact on the digestive system. It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Angelica sinensis or any herbal remedy to minimize the risk of adverse reactions.

2. Causes Skin Irritation

Angelica sinensis causes skin irritation due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which can lead to redness, itching, and rashes in some individuals. This reaction can range from mild to severe and may be accompanied by swelling, blistering, or even burns in extreme cases. To avoid these adverse effects, it is essential to use Angelica sinensis under the guidance of a qualified practitioner or healthcare professional.

3. Produces Vomiting

Angelica sinensis produces vomiting as a side effect due to its unique properties. This vomiting can be a result of the plant's ability to stimulate the stomach and intestines, leading to increased digestive activity. However, it is essential to note that this side effect can vary in intensity and is often dependent on the individual's sensitivity to the plant's compounds.

4. Induces Liver Damage

Angelica sinensis induces liver damage due to its potential toxicity and long-term use. High doses of the plant may lead to liver enzyme elevation, which can indicate liver damage and potentially cause liver fibrosis or cirrhosis in severe cases. It is essential for individuals taking Angelica sinensis supplements to consult with a healthcare professional and monitor their liver function closely.

5. Induces Headaches

Angelica sinensis induces headaches in some individuals, which may be a result of its interactions with certain medications or its ability to thin the blood. However, it is essential to note that this side effect is not a universal experience, and many people can use the plant without experiencing any adverse effects. If you are considering using Angelica sinensis, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional to discuss potential risks and benefits.

6. Induces Fatigue

Angelica sinensis induces fatigue, a common side effect reported by individuals who consume the plant in medicinal form. This fatigue can range from mild to severe and may be accompanied by other symptoms such as drowsiness and weakness. In some cases, the fatigue caused by Angelica sinensis may be temporary and resolve on its own, but in others it may be a persistent side effect.

7. Causes Dizziness

Angelica sinensis causes dizziness, which can be a temporary and usually mild side effect of consuming the plant. This dizziness is often caused by the plant's ability to lower blood pressure and improve circulation, but it can be a concern for individuals with pre-existing heart conditions or other health issues. In most cases, the dizziness associated with Angelica sinensis is not severe and can be managed by adjusting the dosage or avoiding the plant altogether.

8. Induces Anxiety

Angelica sinensis induces anxiety in some individuals, possibly due to its high levels of volatile oils and other bioactive compounds. The exact mechanisms behind this side effect are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the plant's ability to influence the nervous system. Further research is needed to determine the full extent of this side effect and to develop strategies for mitigating its impact.

9. Produces Diarrhea

Angelica sinensis produces diarrhea, which can be a result of the plant's high levels of certain compounds that stimulate the digestive system. This side effect is often associated with consuming large amounts of the plant or its extracts, particularly in medicinal forms. If not properly managed, diarrhea caused by Angelica sinensis can lead to dehydration and other complications.
