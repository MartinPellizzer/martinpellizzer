9 Most Common Side Effects of Angelica Sinensis

1. Causes Nephrotoxicity

Angelica sinensis causes nephrotoxicity, a condition that can lead to damage and dysfunction of the kidneys. Prolonged consumption of the plant or its extracts may result in the accumulation of toxic compounds, further exacerbating kidney damage. This nephrotoxic effect can increase the risk of kidney disease and potentially lead to severe health complications.

2. Induces Hepatotoxicity

Angelica sinensis induces hepatotoxicity, a condition characterized by liver damage or inflammation. This side effect is often associated with excessive or prolonged consumption of the plant, which can lead to severe liver dysfunction. It is essential for individuals to consult with a healthcare professional before using Angelica sinensis to minimize the risk of hepatotoxicity.

3. Interferes With Blood Clotting

Angelica sinensis interferes with blood clotting, which can increase the risk of bleeding complications. This side effect can be particularly problematic for individuals who are taking anticoagulant medications or have pre-existing bleeding disorders. As a result, individuals using Angelica sinensis should closely monitor their bleeding risk and consult with a healthcare professional if necessary.

4. Affects Kidney Function

Angelica sinensis affects kidney function, particularly in individuals with pre-existing kidney conditions, by potentially exacerbating kidney damage or reducing kidney function. This can be attributed to the plant's high content of anthraquinones, which can have toxic effects on the kidneys. As a result, individuals with kidney problems are advised to use angelica sinensis with caution and under medical supervision.

5. Triggers Allergic Reactions

Angelica sinensis triggers allergic reactions in some individuals, which can manifest as skin irritation, hives, or respiratory problems. These allergic reactions are typically a result of the body's immune system overreacting to the plant's allergenic compounds. In severe cases, exposure to Angelica sinensis can even lead to anaphylaxis, a potentially life-threatening allergic response.

6. Causes Anaphylaxis

Angelica sinensis causes anaphylaxis, a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction. This side effect can manifest with symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, difficulty breathing, and a drop in blood pressure. If left untreated, anaphylaxis can lead to organ failure and even death.

7. Causes Skin Irritation

Angelica sinensis causes skin irritation due to the presence of sesquiterpenes and other compounds that can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals. This skin irritation can manifest as redness, itching, and rashes, and in severe cases, may lead to more serious skin conditions. Users of Angelica sinensis products should exercise caution and perform patch tests before using them extensively to avoid any potential skin irritation.

8. Causes Gastrointestinal Upset

Angelica sinensis causes gastrointestinal upset, which can manifest as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals. This side effect may be more pronounced in people who consume large quantities of the plant or have pre-existing digestive issues. In rare cases, gastrointestinal upset associated with Angelica sinensis consumption can lead to more severe complications, such as dehydration or electrolyte imbalances.

9. Triggers Fatigue

Angelica sinensis triggers fatigue, a common side effect experienced by individuals who consume the plant in excess or without proper regulation. This can be particularly concerning for those who are already experiencing low energy levels or are taking other medications that may interact with angelica sinensis. As a result, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before adding the plant to any supplement regimen.
