11 Most Common Side Effects of Angelica Sinensis

1. Induces Thrombocytopenia

Angelica sinensis induces thrombocytopenia, a condition characterized by a low platelet count in the blood, which can lead to an increased risk of bleeding. This side effect occurs due to the plant's potential to affect the production and function of platelets in the body. Thrombocytopenia associated with Angelica sinensis use can be a serious concern, particularly for individuals taking the plant in large doses or for extended periods.

2. Increases Risk Of Interaction With Anticoagulants

Angelica sinensis increases risk of interaction with anticoagulants, which can lead to adverse effects such as bleeding or bruising. This is due to the plant's potential to enhance the effects of anticoagulant medications, thereby increasing the risk of bleeding complications. Patients taking Angelica sinensis should consult their healthcare provider to discuss potential interactions and monitor their medication regimen.

3. Causes Hemolysis

Angelica sinensis causes hemolysis, a condition characterized by the breakdown of red blood cells in the body. This can lead to a range of symptoms including anemia, fatigue, and shortness of breath. If left untreated, severe hemolysis can be life-threatening, emphasizing the need for careful monitoring and dosing when using this plant in herbal remedies.

4. Promotes Hepatotoxicity

Angelica sinensis promotes hepatotoxicity by inducing liver damage and inflammation, which can lead to cirrhosis and liver failure in severe cases. Prolonged consumption of this plant can exacerbate the condition, particularly in individuals with pre-existing liver conditions or those who consume excessive amounts. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Angelica sinensis to avoid potential liver-related complications.

5. Causes Nephrotoxicity

Angelica sinensis causes nephrotoxicity, which can lead to damage of the renal system and potentially result in kidney failure. This adverse effect is thought to be caused by the plant's active compounds that are toxic to kidney cells. Prolonged or excessive consumption of Angelica sinensis may increase the risk of nephrotoxicity and subsequent kidney damage.

6. Induces Diarrhea

Angelica sinensis induces diarrhea, which is often attributed to its saponin and flavonoid content that can irritate the gastrointestinal tract and increase bowel movements. This side effect can be particularly problematic for individuals with pre-existing gastrointestinal issues, such as inflammatory bowel disease or irritable bowel syndrome. In some cases, the diarrhea induced by angelica sinensis can be severe and persistent, requiring medical attention to manage symptoms and prevent dehydration.

7. Induces Renal Failure

Angelica sinensis induces renal failure, which can be a severe and potentially life-threatening side effect of consuming this plant. High doses or prolonged use of Angelica sinensis have been linked to kidney damage, leading to acute kidney injury and even renal failure. It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Angelica sinensis to ensure safe and effective treatment.

8. Increases Risk Of Bleeding

Angelica sinensis increases risk of bleeding due to its effect on blood clotting, making it essential to monitor blood pressure and bleeding time when consuming products containing this plant. This risk is particularly concerning for individuals taking anticoagulant medications or suffering from bleeding disorders. It is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before adding Angelica sinensis to any medication regimen.

9. Increases Risk Of Respiratory Depression

Angelica sinensis increases risk of respiratory depression, a potentially life-threatening condition that can occur when the plant's active compounds interact with the body's respiratory system. This side effect can be particularly concerning for individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions or those taking certain medications that may exacerbate the issue. As a result, it is essential to use Angelica sinensis with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

10. Causes Gastric Ulcers

Angelica sinensis causes gastric ulcers, a condition where the protective lining of the stomach is compromised, allowing acid to damage the tissue and potentially lead to chronic pain and bleeding. This side effect is often associated with long-term use of the plant, particularly when consumed in large quantities or in combination with other medications. Gastric ulcers caused by Angelica sinensis can be managed with proper medical treatment, but prevention and caution are essential when using this plant as a medicinal herb.

11. Causes Myopathy

Angelica sinensis causes myopathy, a condition characterized by muscle weakness and wasting, due to its potential toxicity and interference with normal cellular function. This side effect may be associated with long-term or high-dose consumption of the plant, which is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine. Further research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind angelica sinensis-induced myopathy and to establish safe usage guidelines.
