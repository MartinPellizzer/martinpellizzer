11 Angelica Sinensis Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Angelica sinensis root, also known as dong quai, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions. The root is rich in ferulic acid, a polyphenol with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe and calm the body. It is commonly used to regulate menstrual cycles, reduce menopausal symptoms, and alleviate symptoms of cardiovascular disease.

2. Rhizome

Angelica sinensis rhizome, also known as dang gui or Chinese angelica, is a medicinal part traditionally used to treat various health conditions. It is valued for its purported ability to promote blood circulation, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of menstrual disorders and anemia. The rhizome is rich in ferulic acid, essential oils, and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

3. Leaf

Angelica sinensis leaf is traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, colds, and digestive problems. The leaf is rich in bioactive compounds such as ferulic acid, isobutyl phthalide, and ligustilide, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It is also believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it a popular ingredient in herbal remedies.

4. Stem

Angelica sinensis stem is used in traditional Chinese medicine, specifically for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. The stem is rich in compounds such as ferulic acid and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal effects. It is commonly used to treat various health conditions, including fever, headache, and digestive issues.

5. Flower

Angelica sinensis flower, also known as dong gua cao, is used in traditional Chinese medicine and is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The flower is used to treat various health issues such as fever, colds, and respiratory problems. Additionally, it is also used to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality due to its sedative effects.
