11 Angelica Sinensis Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Angelica sinensis root, also known as dong quai, has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its medicinal properties. The root is rich in ferulic acid, ligustilide, and other compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for relieving menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with premenstrual syndrome. It is also used to treat conditions such as fever, cough, and digestive problems, and is believed to promote blood circulation and reduce blood pressure.

2. Rhizome

Angelica sinensis rhizome, also known as dang gui, is a medicinal part used in traditional Chinese medicine to promote blood circulation and alleviate symptoms of menstrual disorders. The rhizome is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce swelling and improve overall health. Its medicinal properties have been used to treat various conditions, including anemia, fatigue, and impaired immune function.

3. Leaf

Angelica sinensis leaf is primarily used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various health issues, including fever and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties. The leaf's flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its medicinal effects, such as reducing oxidative stress and improving cardiovascular health. Additionally, the leaf has been traditionally used to treat digestive problems and to reduce pain and swelling.

4. Flower

Angelica sinensis flower, also known as dong fang bo, is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various conditions such as fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues. The flower is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects. In traditional medicine, the flower is typically dried and used as an infusion or decoction to promote health and well-being.

5. Seed

Angelica sinensis seed is not commonly used as a medicinal part, but the seed oil is valued for its potential anti-inflammatory properties. However, it's worth noting that more research is needed to confirm the seed's medicinal properties and effects. Some traditional Chinese medicine sources suggest that the seed may have a role in treating fever and promoting digestion.

6. Stem

Angelica sinensis stem contains a rich source of ferulic acid and other bioactive compounds, which have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions. The stem is also a good source of volatile oils, including ligustilide and butylphthalide, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and anti-oxidative properties. These compounds have been found to play a role in reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

7. Calyx

Angelica sinensis calyx, also known as dong quai calyx, contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The calyx is traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat menstrual disorders and promote blood circulation. Its medicinal properties are thought to be due to the presence of various bioactive compounds, including ferulic acid and sinensis acid.

8. Petiole

Angelica sinensis petiole, which is often referred to as the stem or stalk of the angelica plant, is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its medicinal properties. The petiole is rich in flavonoids, saponins, and other bioactive compounds that have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds may contribute to the petiole's potential benefits in reducing fever and alleviating respiratory issues.

9. Stem Tip

Angelica sinensis stem tip contains volatile oils, phenolic acids, and polysaccharides, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The bioactive compounds present in the stem tip of Angelica sinensis may help in reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health. Additionally, the stem tip is also rich in flavonoids and lignans, which have been found to possess hepatoprotective and immunomodulatory effects.

10. Rootlet

Angelica sinensis rootlet is primarily composed of ferulic acid, which has anti-inflammatory properties and is used to treat various health conditions, including fever and rheumatism. The rootlet's sesquiterpenoids and coumarins also exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, contributing to its medicinal value. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate menstrual disorders and improve overall circulation.

11. Inflorescence

Angelica sinensis inflorescence is a traditional medicinal part used in Chinese medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The inflorescence is believed to possess immunomodulatory and anti-anaphylactic effects, making it a potential treatment for various allergic reactions. Additionally, it is thought to have a protective effect on the liver and cardiovascular system.
