11 Angelica Sinensis Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Angelica sinensis root, also known as dong quai, has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions due to its rich content of ferulic acid, ferulic esters, and polysaccharides. The root is believed to have anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its medicinal effects. It is often used to alleviate menstrual cramps, menopausal symptoms, and other gynecological issues.

2. Whole Plant

Angelica sinensis whole plant is used in traditional medicine to treat various conditions due to its rich content of active compounds like ferulic acid and ligustilide. The whole plant is valued for its antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, which contribute to its medicinal effects. It is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of menstrual cramps, fever, and respiratory issues, among others.

3. Leaf

Angelica sinensis leaf, also known as dong quai, is a medicinal part used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat menstrual disorders and menopausal symptoms. The leaf is rich in ferulic acid, a compound that has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It is also used to reduce stress and improve sleep quality due to its sedative effects.

4. Flower

Angelica sinensis flower is not commonly used as a medicinal part, as it is the root that is predominantly utilized for its medicinal properties. However, some traditional Chinese medicine practices may include the flower in certain herbal remedies. The flower is believed to possess some limited medicinal properties, but more research is needed to confirm its efficacy.

5. Stem

Angelica sinensis stem is used in traditional medicine due to its rich content of ferulic acid, which has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The stem of this plant is also a source of polysaccharides, which can help to boost the immune system and fight off infections. Additionally, the stem of Angelica sinensis is believed to have a sedative effect, helping to relieve stress and promote relaxation.

7. Tubers

Angelica sinensis tubers, also known as dong quai tubers, have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their medicinal properties. They contain prenylated isoflavones, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The tubers are often used to treat menstrual disorders and other women's health issues due to their potential to regulate hormonal balance.

8. Rhizome

Angelica sinensis rhizome is primarily used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, headache, and rheumatism, due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. It is also valued for its ability to promote blood circulation, which helps to alleviate conditions such as menstrual cramps and heavy menstrual bleeding. The rhizome of Angelica sinensis is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects and promote overall well-being.

9. Bark

Angelica sinensis bark is not commonly used in traditional medicine, however it is reported to have a high content of lignans. The bark of Angelica sinensis is traditionally used in some parts of the world, where it is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and be used to treat fever and rheumatism. However, more research is needed to confirm the efficacy and safety of using Angelica sinensis bark for medicinal purposes.

10. Seeds

Angelica sinensis seeds are not typically used as a medicinal part, as the primary medicinal value is usually derived from the roots. However, the seeds of Angelica sinensis are sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine, where they are believed to have a mild effect on the body. The seeds are thought to have a calming effect and are used to treat issues such as anxiety and insomnia.

11. Sap

Angelica sinensis sap, also known as dong quai root sap, contains a compound called ferulic acid, which is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests that ferulic acid may also have antioxidant effects, potentially contributing to the herb's traditional use in treating various health conditions. However, more studies are needed to fully understand the benefits and potential applications of angelica sinensis sap.
