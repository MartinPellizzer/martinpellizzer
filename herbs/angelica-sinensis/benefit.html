9 Best Benefits Of Angelica Sinensis

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a perennial plant native to China and other parts of Asia, belonging to the carrot family.

The bioactive compounds present in this plant, including ferulic acid, ligustilide, and butylphthalide, are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The benefits of Angelica sinensis include reducing inflammation and relieving pain, which is attributed to its ability to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and interact with pain receptors.

Additionally, its antioxidant properties help to protect against oxidative stress and cell damage, thanks to its high content of ferulic acid and other polyphenolic compounds.

1. Reduces inflammation

Angelica sinensis reduces inflammation, which is a natural response of the body to injury or damage, characterized by the activation of various cellular and molecular mechanisms to repair and protect the affected area.

This benefit is particularly important as chronic inflammation is linked to the development of various diseases, such as arthritis, cardiovascular disease, and cancer, and can cause symptoms like pain, swelling, and fatigue.

The anti-inflammatory properties of Angelica sinensis are attributed to its rich content of bioactive constituents, including ferulic acid, isobutylphthalide, and ligustilide, which have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators and enzymes.

To harness the anti-inflammatory benefits of Angelica sinensis, it is best to use it in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, or capsules, with recommended dosages and duration of treatment varying depending on individual needs and health conditions.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Angelica sinensis lowers blood pressure, which is the reduction of the force of blood against the walls of the arteries, typically measured by systolic and diastolic blood pressure readings.

This benefit is important because high blood pressure can lead to serious health issues, such as heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage, as it can cause arteries to become damaged and narrowed over time, ultimately affecting the heart's ability to pump blood efficiently.

Angelica sinensis contains bioactive constituents such as ferulic acid, butylphthalide, and ligustilide, which have been shown to have vasodilatory and anti-inflammatory properties that contribute to its ability to lower blood pressure.

To harness the blood pressure-lowering benefits of Angelica sinensis, it can be consumed as a tea, capsule, or tincture, with recommended dosages typically ranging from 2-6 grams of dried root per day or as directed by a healthcare professional.
