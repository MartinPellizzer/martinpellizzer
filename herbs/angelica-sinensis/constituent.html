13 Angelica Sinensis Best Active Constituents

1. Berberine

Angelica sinensis berberine is a bioactive compound known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various gastrointestinal disorders and infections. Berberine has also been studied for its potential cardiovascular benefits, including lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels. Additionally, it may have antioxidant and anti-cancer effects, although further research is needed to confirm its efficacy in these areas.

2. Flavonoids

Angelica sinensis flavonoids have been reported to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-cancer properties. They are also known to exhibit cardiovascular protective effects by improving lipid profiles and reducing blood pressure. Additionally, flavonoids from Angelica sinensis have been found to have anti-diabetic and anti-aging properties, making them a valuable component in traditional Chinese medicine.

3. Fatty Acids

Angelica sinensis fatty acids, also known as angelic fatty acids, have been found to possess anti-inflammatory properties and to promote wound healing. These fatty acids may also contribute to the herb's antioxidant effects, helping to protect against oxidative stress and cell damage. Further research is needed to fully understand the potential health benefits of angelica sinensis fatty acids.

4. Phenolic Acids

Angelica sinensis phenolic acids have been identified as one of the key medicinal active constituents in traditional Chinese medicine. These compounds are known to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's therapeutic effects. The phenolic acids in Angelica sinensis have been shown to exhibit protective effects against oxidative stress and inflammation in various diseases.

5. Saponins

Angelica sinensis saponins, also known as triterpenoid saponins, have been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. 6. Tannins

Angelica sinensis tannins, also known as polyphenolic compounds, have been identified as a key bioactive constituent with potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Angelica sinensis phenylethanoid glycosides are a group of bioactive compounds extracted from the root of Angelica sinensis, commonly known as dong quai or female ginseng.
