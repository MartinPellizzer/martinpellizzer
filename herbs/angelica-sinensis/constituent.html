9 Angelica Sinensis Best Active Constituents

1. Coumarins

Angelica sinensis coumarins, also known as furanocoumarins, are a class of bioactive compounds found in the roots of the plant. These compounds have been reported to exhibit various pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-cancer properties. Furthermore, coumarins in Angelica sinensis have been traditionally used to treat cardiovascular diseases, improve blood circulation, and enhance overall cardiovascular health.

2. Ferulic Acid

Angelica sinensis ferulic acid, a phenolic compound, has been studied for its potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests that it may help protect against oxidative stress and cell damage, which can contribute to various diseases. Ferulic acid may also exhibit neuroprotective effects, making it a subject of interest for potential applications in neurodegenerative disorder treatment.

3. Ligustilide

Angelica sinensis ligustilide is a bioactive compound that has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It is also known to have a protective effect on the cardiovascular system by reducing blood pressure and improving blood flow. Research suggests that ligustilide may also have a role in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cells, although further studies are needed to confirm its efficacy.

4. Butylphthalide

Angelica sinensis butylphthalide is a bioactive compound known to have neuroprotective properties and is used to treat ischemic stroke. Research has shown that butylphthalide can improve outcomes in patients with acute ischemic stroke by reducing infarct volume and promoting neural recovery. Its mechanism of action involves anti-apoptosis, anti-inflammatory, and anti-oxidative effects, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits.

5. Isoimperatorin

Angelica sinensis isoimperatorin, a bioactive compound found in the traditional Chinese medicinal herb Angelica sinensis, has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Studies have demonstrated that isoimperatorin may inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes, contributing to its potential therapeutic effects. Further research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms and applications of isoimperatorin in the treatment of various diseases.

6. Imperatorin

Angelica sinensis imperatorin is a bioactive compound with reported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It has been shown to exert neuroprotective effects by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory mediators and reducing oxidative stress. Additionally, imperatorin has been found to possess anti-cancer properties by inducing apoptosis and inhibiting cell proliferation in various cancer cell lines.

7. Phenolic Acids

Angelica sinensis phenolic acids, a class of bioactive compounds found in the medicinal plant, have been studied for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties. These phenolic acids, including ferulic and sinapic acids, have been shown to possess significant therapeutic potential in the prevention and treatment of various diseases. Further research is needed to fully elucidate the mechanisms of action and potential health benefits of phenolic acids from Angelica sinensis.

8. Flavonoids

Angelica sinensis flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in the plant, which have been extensively studied for their medicinal properties. These flavonoids have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, contributing to the plant's traditional use in Chinese medicine for various health conditions. Research has also identified specific flavonoids in Angelica sinensis, such as baicalein and baicalin, which may be responsible for some of its therapeutic effects.

9. Tannins

Angelica sinensis tannins, also known as flavanols and phenolic acids, have been found to possess antioxidant properties, which may contribute to their potential health benefits. Research has suggested that these tannins may help to protect against oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. Additionally, some studies have indicated that the tannins present in Angelica sinensis may have anti-cancer and anti-bacterial effects.
