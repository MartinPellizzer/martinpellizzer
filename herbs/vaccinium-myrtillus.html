Vaccinium Myrtillus: Complete Medicinal Profile

Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as the bilberry, is a type of small shrub that belongs to the heath family and is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

The health benefits of bilberry include improved circulation, reduced risk of heart disease, and enhanced cognitive function, as well as its potential to prevent certain types of cancer.

Side Effects

Vaccinium myrtillus side effects are primarily experienced by individuals who consume excessive amounts of the plant, particularly children and pregnant women.

Additionally, long-term consumption may lead to kidney problems, decreased blood pressure, and interactions with certain medications like blood thinners, diabetes medications, and high blood pressure medications, resulting in symptoms like bruising, dizziness, and fainting.

To minimize the risk of side effects, it is recommended to consume Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as bilberry, in moderation, avoid using it as a treatment for serious medical conditions, and be cautious of its interactions with other substances, especially in vulnerable populations.

  • Interaction with medications: Vaccinium myrtillus may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and decrease their effectiveness or increase the risk of bleeding.

  • Low blood sugar: Consuming vaccinium myrtillus may lower blood sugar levels, which can be a concern for people with diabetes.
  • Stomach cramps: Some individuals may experience stomach cramps or abdominal pain after consuming vaccinium myrtillus, possibly due to its high tannin content.
  • Urinary tract issues: Vaccinium myrtillus may irritate the urinary tract and worsen conditions such as kidney disease or bladder problems.
