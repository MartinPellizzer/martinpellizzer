7 Most Common Side Effects of Acorus Calamus

1. Causes Gastrointestinal Issues

Acorus calamus causes gastrointestinal issues, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its toxic compounds. The consumption of this plant can lead to stomach upset and abdominal pain in individuals, especially when ingested in large quantities. Prolonged consumption of Acorus calamus may result in chronic gastrointestinal problems and require medical attention.

2. Affects Central Nervous System

Acorus calamus affects central nervous system by altering brain function and potentially leading to symptoms such as dizziness and disorientation. This effect is often associated with the volatile oil present in the plant, which can have a sedative and hypnotic impact on the user. However, excessive consumption of acorus calamus can lead to adverse reactions, including confusion, tremors, and respiratory problems.

3. Causes Liver Damage

Acorus calamus causes liver damage due to the presence of compounds like beta-asarone, which can be toxic to the liver and lead to its damage. Prolonged consumption of the plant or its extracts can result in liver dysfunction and potentially severe liver damage. It is essential to use Acorus calamus under medical supervision and follow recommended dosages to minimize the risk of liver damage.

4. Affects Kidney Function

Acorus calamus affects kidney function by potentially impairing its ability to filter waste and excess fluids from the blood, which can lead to a range of complications. Prolonged use of the plant or its extracts has been linked to kidney damage and decreased kidney function in some individuals. This side effect is often associated with excessive or prolonged consumption of the plant, highlighting the importance of moderation in its use.

5. Induces Nausea

Acorus calamus induces nausea as a result of its volatile compounds, which can cause stomach discomfort and vomiting in some individuals. This side effect is often associated with ingesting large quantities of the plant or its extracts. The nausea induced by Acorus calamus can be a sign of its toxicity and should be treated with caution.

6. Affects Respiratory System

Acorus calamus affects respiratory system by causing irritation and inflammation, which can lead to coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath in some individuals. This is often due to the presence of volatile oils and other compounds in the plant, which can be released into the air and inhaled. Prolonged exposure to these compounds can exacerbate respiratory conditions such as asthma and chronic bronchitis.

7. Produces Tremors

Acorus calamus produces tremors due to its neurotoxic properties, which can affect the central nervous system and cause involuntary muscle movements. When consumed in large quantities, the plant can lead to tremors, seizures, and other neurological symptoms. Prolonged exposure to Acorus calamus can also result in tremors, making it essential to handle the plant with caution.
