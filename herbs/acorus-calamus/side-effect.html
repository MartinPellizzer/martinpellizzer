7 Most Common Side Effects of Acorus Calamus

1. Induce Coma

Acorus calamus induce coma, which is a rare but potentially life-threatening side effect of consuming the plant's rhizomes or extracts. In severe cases, the toxic compounds present in the plant can cause respiratory depression, leading to a coma. This serious side effect highlights the need for caution when using Acorus calamus for medicinal or culinary purposes.

2. Cause Liver Damage

Acorus calamus cause liver damage when ingested in large quantities, potentially leading to severe health issues due to its toxic compounds. Prolonged consumption of this plant can result in liver inflammation and damage, compromising the organ's ability to function properly. In some cases, liver damage from Acorus calamus consumption can be irreversible, emphasizing the need for caution when handling this plant.

3. Lead To Respiratory Distress

Acorus calamus lead to respiratory distress, particularly when ingested, as the toxic compounds present in the plant can cause inflammation in the lungs and airways. This inflammation can lead to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, making it difficult for individuals to breathe. In severe cases, respiratory distress caused by Acorus calamus can be life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention.

4. Lead To Neurological Problems

Acorus calamus lead to neurological problems when ingested in large quantities, resulting in symptoms such as tremors, muscle weakness, and memory loss. The active compounds present in the plant, including asarone and alkaloids, can cause damage to the nervous system and disrupt normal brain function. Prolonged consumption of Acorus calamus has been linked to long-term neurological damage and potentially even neurological disorders.

5. Cause Anxiety Attacks

Acorus calamus cause anxiety attacks due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system, leading to feelings of panic and unease. This side effect is often associated with excessive consumption of the plant, particularly when used in high concentrations or for extended periods. In some cases, individuals may experience intense anxiety attacks, which can be a significant concern for those using the plant for medicinal purposes.

6. Cause Seizures

Acorus calamus cause seizures, a potentially life-threatening side effect, due to the presence of a chemical compound called asarone, which can have neurotoxic effects. This compound can disrupt normal brain function, leading to seizures in some individuals. Prolonged exposure to asarone can exacerbate this risk, making it essential for people to exercise caution when handling or consuming Acorus calamus products.

7. Result In Respiratory Failure

Acorus calamus result in respiratory failure due to its toxic compounds, specifically pinane and asarone, which can cause severe respiratory distress and even lead to respiratory failure in extreme cases. This can occur when the plant's essential oils are ingested, inhaled, or even when its extract is used in traditional medicine. In severe cases, respiratory failure from Acorus calamus can be life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention.
