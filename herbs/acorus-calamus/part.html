7 Acorus Calamus Best Medicinal Parts

1. Rhizome

Acorus calamus rhizome, commonly known as the root of the sweet flag, contains bioactive compounds that exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The rhizome has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems. It is also used as a natural remedy for anxiety and insomnia due to its calming effects on the nervous system.

2. Root

Acorus calamus root has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for its medicinal properties. The root of the plant is known to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it useful in treating various health conditions. It is also used to treat digestive issues, such as bloating and gas, due to its carminative and anti-spasmodic effects.

3. Aromatics Oils

Acorus calamus aromatics oils, primarily extracted from the rhizome, contain a range of bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds, which contribute to their medicinal properties. These aromatic oils have been traditionally used to alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial activities. The essential oils of Acorus calamus are also valued for their anxiolytic and anti-stress effects, promoting relaxation and well-being.

4. Stem

Acorus calamus stem, also known as the rhizome or stem, contains compounds like linalool and camphor that have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The stem is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and skin conditions. The essential oil extracted from the stem is often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

5. Leaf

Acorus calamus leaf is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine, where it is valued for its carminative and antispasmodic properties. The leaf is also known to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may contribute to its medicinal benefits. In traditional Chinese medicine, the leaf is used to treat a variety of ailments, including fever, cough, and digestive issues.
