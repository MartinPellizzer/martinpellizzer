11 Acorus Calamus Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Acorus calamus root is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, where it is used to treat various health conditions. The root of Acorus calamus has anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties, which are used to relieve symptoms of digestive issues, anxiety, and insomnia. It is also used to treat respiratory problems, such as coughs and colds, and as a natural remedy for skin conditions like eczema and acne.

2. Rhizome

Acorus calamus rhizome is traditionally used in various herbal remedies due to its medicinal properties, including its ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate respiratory issues. The rhizome has been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular ingredient in traditional medicine. Additionally, it is also used to treat digestive issues, such as nausea and indigestion, and to soothe nervous system disorders.

3. Leaf

Acorus calamus leaf is primarily used for its medicinal properties, which include being antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory. The leaf of Acorus calamus is also known to exhibit expectorant properties, helping to relieve respiratory issues such as coughs and bronchitis. In traditional medicine, the leaf is used to treat various conditions including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

4. Aerial Stem

Acorus calamus aerial stem is used in traditional medicine due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The aerial stem of Acorus calamus is believed to have carminative effects, helping to relieve flatulence and digestive issues. It is also used to treat various respiratory problems, including bronchitis and asthma.

5. Stem

Acorus calamus stem, also known as vasa, is a medicinal part that is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine. It is believed to possess anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it useful for treating various health conditions. The stem of Acorus calamus is also used to treat digestive issues and is valued for its potential to stimulate digestion and relieve flatulence.

6. Rhizome Scale

Acorus calamus rhizome scale contains a volatile oil with asarone, which has been traditionally used as a treatment for various health issues, including anxiety, insomnia, and digestive problems. The rhizome scale of Acorus calamus is also used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. It is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it a valuable component in traditional medicine.

7. Leaf Sheath

Acorus calamus leaf sheath contains a volatile oil that is traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The leaf sheath is also a source of acorigenin, a compound that has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. It is often used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues and as a natural remedy for various health conditions.

8. Leaf Blade

Acorus calamus leaf blade is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of respiratory issues and as an anti-inflammatory agent. The leaf blade contains essential oils, which have been traditionally used to aid digestion and relieve flatulence. Additionally, it is also used to reduce fever and alleviate symptoms associated with the common cold.

9. Seeds

Acorus calamus seeds, although not commonly used in traditional medicine, are reported to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. However, there is limited research available on the medicinal properties of Acorus calamus seeds. Further studies are needed to fully understand their potential health benefits and potential uses in medicinal applications.

10. Apex

Acorus calamus apex, the rhizome and root are well-documented for their medicinal properties, but the apex itself is primarily composed of the rhizome's terminal bud. The apex contains volatile oils and sesquiterpenes that contribute to the herb's therapeutic effects. These compounds are believed to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making the apex a valuable component of traditional medicine.

11. Stolons

Acorus calamus stolons are a lesser-known medicinal part of the plant, often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The stolons are typically harvested from the wild or cultivated, and then processed into various forms such as powder or extracts. In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, acorus calamus stolons are used to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and skin problems.
