Valeriana Officinalis: Complete Medicinal Profile

Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and Asia, recognized by its sweetly scented flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

Valerian root has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties, which include sedative and anti-anxiety effects.

The health benefits of valerian root include improved sleep quality, reduced stress levels, and alleviation of symptoms associated with menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings.

Valerian root can be prepared in various herbal forms, including teas, tinctures, capsules, and dried root infusions, which can be consumed to promote relaxation and well-being.

Actions

Valeriana officinalis has been found to exhibit a multitude of therapeutic actions, including adaptogenic, anxiolytic, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, antiviral, cardioprotective, diuretic, hypotensive, nervine, sedative, and vasodilatory properties.

These actions primarily affect the central nervous system, cardiovascular system, musculoskeletal system, and endocrine system, promoting relaxation and reducing stress and anxiety while also supporting heart health, muscle relaxation, and fluid balance.

Additionally, Valeriana officinalis demonstrates antibacterial, antifungal, and antioxidant properties, further contributing to its therapeutic potential.

Its sedative and antispasmodic actions help regulate the autonomic nervous system, while its adaptogenic properties enhance overall resilience and well-being.

The therapeutic actions of Valeriana officinalis are listed below.

  • Anxiolytic: Valeriana officinalis is known to have anxiolytic properties, helping to reduce anxiety and stress.
  • Nervine: It has a calming effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing restlessness.
  • Sedative: Valeriana officinalis is commonly used as a sedative to help individuals fall asleep and stay asleep.
  • Muscle relaxant: It has a relaxing effect on muscles, reducing muscle spasms and cramps.
  • Antispasmodic: Valeriana officinalis helps to relieve spasms and cramps in the digestive tract and other areas of the body.
  • Hypnotic: It is used to induce a state of deep relaxation, which can be helpful for individuals with insomnia or other sleep disorders.
  • Calming: Valeriana officinalis has a calming effect on the mind and body, promoting feelings of peace and tranquility.

Constituents

Valeriana officinalis has been found to contain a diverse array of bioactive constituents, including valerenic acid, isovaltrate, valepotriates, valeranone, and sesquiterpenes like valerenol and valerenal.

These constituents possess a range of healing properties, with valerenic acid exhibiting anxiolytic and sedative effects due to its action on the GABA receptor, while isovaltrate and valepotriates show antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, flavonoids like diosmin and rutin, as well as phenolic acids like ferulic and caffeic acid, contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Furthermore, the sesquiterpenes present in Valeriana officinalis, such as valerenol and valerenal, have been found to possess anti-anxiety and anticonvulsant properties.

The bioactive constituents of Valeriana officinalis are listed below.

  • Valerenic acid: A sesquiterpene responsible for the sedative and relaxing properties of valeriana officinalis.
  • Isovaleric acid: A short-chain fatty acid contributing to the plant's sedative and muscle relaxant effects.
  • Valeranone: A sesquiterpene with sedative and anxiolytic properties.
  • Valerenal: A sesquiterpene with sedative and muscle relaxant properties.
  • Flavonoids: A class of plant compounds with antioxidant and potential anti-inflammatory effects.
  • Tannins: A type of polyphenol contributing to the plant's astringent and antimicrobial properties.
  • Amino acids: A class of organic compounds involved in various physiological processes, including neurotransmission and muscle relaxation.

Side Effects

Valeriana officinalis side effects are typically experienced by individuals who consume excessive amounts, such as those taking high doses or combining it with other medications, including antidepressants, sedatives, and certain antibiotics.

Potential side effects include drowsiness, dizziness, headaches, stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and allergic reactions, as well as interactions with certain medications that may increase the risk of bleeding or worsen respiratory conditions like asthma.

Older adults, pregnant women, and people with liver or kidney disease may be more susceptible to these side effects, which can also include changes in appetite, mood swings, and increased heart rate or blood pressure.

Precautions should be taken to avoid consuming Valeriana officinalis if you are sensitive to its active compounds, have a history of addiction, or are taking medications that may interact with its sedative effects.

The possible side effects of Valeriana officinalis are listed below.

  • Abdominal cramps: Valeriana officinalis may cause stomach cramps, diarrhea, or nausea in some individuals.
  • Allergic reactions: Some people may experience allergic reactions to valerian, including hives, itching, or difficulty breathing.
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness: Valerian can cause dizziness or lightheadedness, especially when standing up quickly.
  • Headaches: Valerian may cause headaches, possibly due to its sedative effects.
  • Increased sedation: Using valerian with other sedatives or medications can increase the risk of excessive sedation or coma.
  • Interactions with medications: Valerian may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, diabetes medications, and sedatives.
  • Stomach upset: Valerian can cause stomach upset, heartburn, or acid reflux in some individuals.
