identification

Acorus calamus, commonly known as sweet flag or calamus, can be identified by its distinctive, grass-like foliage and yellowish-green to brownish-brown rhizomes. The plant's rhizome has a characteristic, aromatic scent and can be distinguished from other members of the Acorus genus by its size and coloration. In addition, A. calamus has dark green, sword-shaped leaves with a wavy or rolled margin, which can aid in its identification in the field.

taxonomy

Acorus calamus, commonly known as sweet flag or calamus, belongs to the family Acoraceae. It is a monocot species classified under the genus Acorus, which is the only genus within the Acoraceae family. Acorus calamus is further identified by its species name, calamus, and is often divided into two main varieties: var. calamus and var. angustatus.
