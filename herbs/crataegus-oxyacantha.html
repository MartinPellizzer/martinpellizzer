Crataegus Oxyacantha: Complete Medicinal Profile

Crataegus oxyacantha, also known as the hawthorn tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree with thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers.

The medicinal properties of Crataegus oxyacantha lie in its ability to support cardiovascular health, lower blood pressure, and improve circulation.

The health benefits of hawthorn include reducing the risk of heart failure, improving blood lipid profiles, and alleviating symptoms of anxiety and insomnia.

Hawthorn is typically prepared as a tea, tincture, or capsule, and can also be used as a dried flower or berry in herbal supplements and food products.

Uses and Benefits

Crataegus oxyacantha is used to promote cardiovascular health by reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, which can lead to a lower risk of heart disease and stroke.

This plant is also used to boost the immune system, reduce oxidative stress, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Its health benefits extend to anti-aging and skin health, as it has been shown to reduce wrinkles, fine lines, and age spots.

Additionally, Crataegus oxyacantha has been used to treat various gastrointestinal issues, including irritable bowel syndrome, diarrhea, and stomach ulcers.

Properties and Actions

Crataegus oxyacantha has been found to exhibit various therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, cardiotonic, and vasodilatory properties, which affect the cardiovascular system, nervous system, and circulatory system.

Additionally, it possesses antioxidant, hepatoprotective, and free radical-scavenging properties, influencing the liver and immune system.

The plant also exhibits diuretic, hypotensive, and vasorelaxant actions, impacting the urinary system and vascular system.

Furthermore, it has been shown to have adaptogenic, anxiolytic, and sedative effects, influencing the nervous system and endocrine system.

Constituents

Crataegus oxyacantha has been found to contain an array of bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, such as quercetin and kaempferol, which exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Tannins, like epicatechin and catechin, present in the plant, have been shown to possess antimicrobial and astringent properties, while also contributing to its antioxidant activity.

The plant also contains phenolic acids, like caffeic and ferulic acid, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Additionally, triterpenoids, including ursolic and oleanolic acid, have been identified in Crataegus oxyacantha, displaying anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Parts

The most commonly used parts of Crataegus oxyacantha are the flowers, fruits, leaves, and bark.

The flowers of the Crataegus oxyacantha plant are used to create teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are believed to aid in lowering blood pressure and preventing cardiovascular disease.

The fruits, often referred to as hawthorn berries, are used in supplements, jams, and wines, and are thought to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce the risk of heart disease and improve cognitive function.

The leaves and bark of the plant are used to create teas and infusions that are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, helping to soothe digestive issues and reduce the severity of colds and flu.

Preparations

Crataegus oxyacantha, also known as hawthorn, is a flowering plant used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.

The most common herbal preparations of Crataegus oxyacantha for health purposes include infusions, tinctures, and capsules.

Infusions of hawthorn flowers and leaves are used to treat high blood pressure, heart failure, and angina by improving blood flow and reducing inflammation, while tinctures are used to support cardiovascular health and reduce anxiety.

Capsules containing hawthorn extract are also used to lower cholesterol levels, improve exercise performance, and alleviate symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and night sweats.

Side Effects

Crataegus oxyacantha side effects are typically associated with individuals taking high doses or for extended periods.

Some common side effects include gastrointestinal issues such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as allergic reactions like skin rashes and itching.

Additionally, individuals taking Crataegus oxyacantha may experience dizziness, headaches, and fatigue, which are often more pronounced in elderly or sensitive individuals.

Precautions include being cautious with blood-thinning medications and monitoring blood pressure, as excessive consumption may lead to interactions and adverse effects such as bruising, bleeding, and orthostatic hypotension.
