13 Most Common Side Effects of Astragalus Membranaceus

1. Induces Liver Damage

Astragalus membranaceus induces liver damage due to its potential to cause oxidative stress and disrupt the normal functioning of liver cells. Prolonged consumption of the plant in high doses or by individuals with pre-existing liver conditions may exacerbate the risk of liver damage. It is essential for individuals using astragalus membranaceus supplements to consult with a healthcare professional and monitor their liver function regularly.

2. Causes Insomnia

Astragalus membranaceus causes insomnia in some individuals due to its stimulatory properties, which can lead to an increased heart rate and alertness, making it difficult to fall asleep. This effect is often seen in people taking high doses of the plant or combining it with other stimulants. As a result, it is essential to consume astragalus membranaceus under the guidance of a healthcare professional to avoid disrupting sleep patterns.

3. Affects Immune System

Astragalus membranaceus affects immune system by enhancing the body's natural defense mechanisms and boosting the production of white blood cells. This herb is believed to stimulate the activity of immune cells, such as natural killer cells, which play a crucial role in fighting off infections and diseases. Regular consumption of astragalus membranaceus may help to improve immune function and reduce the risk of illnesses.

4. Causes Nephrotoxicity

Astragalus membranaceus causes nephrotoxicity, which can lead to damage and impairment of the kidneys, potentially resulting in chronic kidney disease or even kidney failure. This condition can be caused by the excessive intake of astragalus membranaceus extracts, especially when taken in large doses or for extended periods. The risk of nephrotoxicity associated with astragalus membranaceus highlights the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using this herb as a supplement.

5. Leads To Cardiovascular Issues

Astragalus membranaceus leads to cardiovascular issues, with some research suggesting that high doses of the plant's extract may increase blood pressure and contribute to cardiovascular problems. This is thought to be due to the plant's ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system, potentially leading to irregular heart rhythms and other cardiovascular complications. However, it is essential to note that the risks associated with astragalus membranaceus are still being studied and more research is needed to fully understand its effects on cardiovascular health.

6. Leads To Muscle Weakness

Astragalus membranaceus leads to muscle weakness, which can be a concerning side effect for individuals taking supplements or consuming the plant. This condition can be caused by the plant's active compounds interfering with the body's natural muscle function and balance. As a result, individuals may experience fatigue, muscle cramps, or a general feeling of weakness.

7. Produces Gastrointestinal Problems

Astragalus membranaceus produces gastrointestinal problems, which can manifest as nausea, diarrhea, or stomach pain in some individuals who consume the plant. These adverse reactions may be exacerbated by high doses or prolonged use, highlighting the need for cautious dosing. Furthermore, gastrointestinal issues can be particularly concerning for individuals with pre-existing digestive disorders or compromised gut health.

8. Affects Cognitive Function

Astragalus membranaceus affects cognitive function by potentially impairing memory and concentration, which may be a concern for individuals taking the plant in supplement form. The exact mechanism behind this cognitive impact is not yet fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the plant's influence on neurotransmitter activity. Further research is needed to determine the full extent of astragalus membranaceus' effects on cognitive function.

9. Triggers Digestive Disorders

Astragalus membranaceus triggers digestive disorders due to its potential to disrupt the balance of gut bacteria and cause inflammation in the digestive tract. This may lead to symptoms such as bloating, abdominal pain, and changes in bowel movements. Consuming astragalus membranaceus in large quantities or for extended periods can exacerbate these side effects.

10. Triggers Allergic Reactions

Astragalus membranaceus triggers allergic reactions in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. This allergic response is often associated with the plant's high levels of isoflavones and other bioactive compounds. People who experience allergic reactions to astragalus membranaceus should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

11. Triggers Headaches

Astragalus membranaceus triggers headaches in some individuals, possibly due to its impact on blood pressure or sensitivity to certain compounds found in the plant. This side effect is relatively rare, but it can be a concern for people who experience frequent or severe headaches. If you experience headaches after consuming astragalus membranaceus, consult with a healthcare professional for guidance on managing this side effect.

12. Provokes Respiratory Issues

Astragalus membranaceus provokes respiratory issues in some individuals, potentially leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. This side effect is often associated with the plant's ability to stimulate the immune system, causing an overreaction in the body. In severe cases, respiratory issues can be a serious concern, especially for those with pre-existing conditions such as asthma.

13. Induces Anxiety

Astragalus membranaceus induces anxiety in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and affect the brain's neurotransmitters. This anxiety can manifest in various forms, such as restlessness, irritability, and an increased heart rate. It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using astragalus membranaceus, especially for those with a history of anxiety disorders or other mental health conditions.
