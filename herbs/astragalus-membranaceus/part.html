13 Astragalus Membranaceus Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Astragalus membranaceus root, also known as Huang Qi in traditional Chinese medicine, is widely used to boost the immune system and alleviate symptoms of fatigue and stress. The root is rich in saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. These bioactive compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, contributing to the root's therapeutic benefits.

2. Root Bark

Astragalus membranaceus root bark contains saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. The bioactive compounds in astragalus root bark are thought to help protect against oxidative stress, promote immune system function, and exhibit antiviral properties. Studies suggest that astragalus root bark extracts may have therapeutic potential for conditions such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and respiratory infections.

3. Stem

Astragalus membranaceus stem is traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its purported adaptogenic properties and ability to boost the immune system. The stem is believed to contain flavonoids, saponins, and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal effects. However, more research is needed to fully understand the stem's potential health benefits and optimal usage.

4. Flower

Astragalus membranaceus flower, also known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits. However, there is limited scientific research on the medicinal properties of the flower specifically. Some studies suggest that the flowers may contain saponins and flavonoids, which have been shown to have immunomodulatory and anti-inflammatory effects.

5. Seed

Astragalus membranaceus seed is known to contain astragaloside, a compound believed to have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. The seed is also reported to have antioxidant properties, which may help protect against oxidative stress and cell damage. However, more research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties and potential applications of Astragalus membranaceus seed.
