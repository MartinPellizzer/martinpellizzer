Aloe vera (Aloe)

What is Aloe vera?

Aloe vera, commonly known as Aloe, is a succulent plant belonging to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and the genus Aloe.

It is a member of the subfamily Alooideae and has been classified as Aloe barbadensis miller.

This classification places Aloe vera within the larger plant kingdom classification system, with Kingdom: Plantae, Order: Asparagales, and Class: Liliopsida.

What are the medicinal parts of Aloe vera?

Aloe vera has several medicinal parts, such as the gel inside its leaf, which is rich in vitamins A, C, and E, and is commonly used to treat skin irritations and wounds.

The roots of the aloe vera plant contain compounds that have been shown to have anti-cancer properties and are also used to treat digestive issues. The stem of the aloe vera plant is less commonly used, but its juice is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe mouth ulcers and gum inflammation.

What are the active constituents of Aloe vera?

Aloe vera contains a range of medicinal active constituents that contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Aloin, a key anthraquinone compound, has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and purgative effects. Aloe-emodin, another anthraquinone, has been found to exhibit anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids present in aloe vera have been reported to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help protect against cell damage.

Additionally, the presence of hyaluronic acid and anthraquinones like aloe-emodin contribute to aloe vera's potential in wound healing and skin protection.

What are the medicinal preparations of Aloe vera?

To prepare aloe vera for consumption or skin care, the outer green skin is typically removed, exposing the clear gel beneath.

The gel is then scooped out and processed to remove any remaining bits of skin or pulp, resulting in a smooth and pure aloe vera extract.

The extracted aloe vera gel can be stored in the refrigerator to maintain its potency and used as needed for its soothing and moisturizing benefits.

What are the side effects of Aloe vera?

Aloe vera can cause a range of negative health side effects in some individuals.

Some people may experience allergic reactions to aloe vera, which can manifest as hives, itching, or difficulty breathing. Ingesting aloe vera can also cause digestive issues, such as diarrhea, abdominal pain, or nausea, due to its high fiber and anthraquinone content. Furthermore, applying aloe vera to the skin can cause irritation, especially in those with sensitive skin or open wounds, leading to redness, inflammation, and discomfort.

Additionally, inhaling the dust or sap of aloe vera can trigger asthma attacks in individuals who are sensitive to its pollen or sap.
