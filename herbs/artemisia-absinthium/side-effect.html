7 Most Common Side Effects of Artemisia Absinthium

1. Induces Liver Toxicity

Artemisia absinthium induces liver toxicity due to the presence of thujone, a neurotoxin that can cause damage to liver cells. Prolonged consumption of Artemisia absinthium products can lead to liver damage, which may result in symptoms such as jaundice, abdominal pain, and fatigue. Liver toxicity caused by Artemisia absinthium can be exacerbated by excessive consumption of other hepatotoxic substances.

2. Causes Kidney Damage

Artemisia absinthium causes kidney damage due to its high levels of thujone, a toxic compound that can accumulate in the kidneys and lead to damage. Prolonged consumption of the plant or its extracts may result in kidney impairment, which can be permanent if left untreated. The risk of kidney damage associated with Artemisia absinthium highlights the importance of careful handling and responsible use of this plant.

3. Triggers Allergic Reactions

Artemisia absinthium triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and other volatile oils in its leaves. These compounds can cause skin irritation, respiratory issues, and other allergic symptoms in people who are sensitive to the plant. People handling or consuming Artemisia absinthium should exercise caution to avoid any adverse reactions.

4. Increases Risk Of Cardiovascular Disease

Artemisia absinthium increases risk of cardiovascular disease due to its high content of thujone, a compound that can cause vasoconstriction and disrupt normal heart function. Prolonged consumption of Artemisia absinthium can lead to hypertension, arrhythmias, and other cardiovascular problems. The increased risk of cardiovascular disease associated with Artemisia absinthium consumption highlights the importance of moderate use and potential avoidance in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions.

5. Affects Thyroid Function

Artemisia absinthium affects thyroid function by potentially inhibiting the production of thyroid hormones, which can lead to hypothyroidism in some individuals. This side effect is attributed to the plant's content of thujone, a toxic compound that can interfere with thyroid function and disrupt hormonal balance. Long-term consumption of Artemisia absinthium may require medical attention to prevent thyroid-related health issues.

6. Increases Risk Of Kidney Stones

Artemisia absinthium increases risk of kidney stones due to its high concentration of thujone, a compound that can cause dehydration and mineral imbalances in the body. These imbalances can lead to the formation of kidney stones, which are hard deposits that can cause severe pain and discomfort. Long-term consumption of Artemisia absinthium may exacerbate this risk, making it essential to monitor kidney health while using products containing this plant.

7. Increases Risk Of Bleeding

Artemisia absinthium increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and affect the blood's clotting mechanism. This side effect can lead to an increased risk of bruising, nosebleeds, and gastrointestinal bleeding in individuals taking artemisinin, a compound extracted from the plant. Furthermore, individuals with pre-existing bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications should exercise caution when using artemisinin-containing products.
