7 Most Common Side Effects of Artemisia Absinthium

1. Experiences Liver Damage

Artemisia absinthium experiences liver damage due to the high levels of thujone, a toxic compound found in the plant, which can cause liver inflammation and potentially lead to liver failure in severe cases. Long-term consumption of absinthe, a liquor made from Artemisia absinthium, has been linked to liver damage and other health problems. Prolonged exposure to the toxic compounds in Artemisia absinthium can cause irreversible liver damage and other serious health complications.

2. Triggers Allergic Reactions

Artemisia absinthium triggers allergic reactions in some individuals, resulting in symptoms such as skin irritation, respiratory issues, and eye problems. These reactions can range from mild to severe and may be triggered by direct contact with the plant or even inhalation of its volatile oils. In rare cases, severe allergic reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.

3. Affects Fetal Development

Artemisia absinthium affects fetal development by potentially causing birth defects and developmental issues when consumed by pregnant individuals. This is due to the plant's toxicity and the presence of compounds such as thujone, which can be harmful to the fetus. Pregnant women are advised to avoid using products containing Artemisia absinthium to minimize the risk of adverse effects on fetal development.

4. Triggers Headaches

Artemisia absinthium triggers headaches due to its bioactive compounds, which can cause blood vessels to dilate and lead to pain in the head. This side effect is often reported by individuals who consume products containing Artemisia absinthium, such as absinthe, in large quantities. The severity of headaches can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity and the amount of the herb consumed.

5. Produces Nausea

Artemisia absinthium produces nausea as a result of its bitter compounds and volatile oils. This side effect can range from mild discomfort to severe vomiting, depending on the individual's sensitivity and dosage. In some cases, nausea associated with artemisia absinthium may be a sign of an underlying health issue, warranting medical attention.

6. Causes Convulsions

Artemisia absinthium causes convulsions due to the presence of thujone, a neurotoxin that can affect the central nervous system. When ingested in large quantities, thujone can lead to seizures and convulsions, which can be severe and potentially life-threatening. It is essential to consume artemisia absinthium in moderation and under the guidance of a medical professional to avoid such adverse effects.

7. Induces Hallucinations

Artemisia absinthium induces hallucinations, a side effect attributed to its psychoactive properties. The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly thujone, have been known to alter the central nervous system, leading to vivid and often disturbing visual and auditory experiences. Historically, excessive consumption of absinthe, a beverage derived from Artemisia absinthium, has been linked to cases of hallucinatory episodes and other neurological symptoms.
