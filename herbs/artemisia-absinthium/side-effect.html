7 Most Common Side Effects of Artemisia Absinthium

1. Causes Hallucinations

Artemisia absinthium causes hallucinations due to its psychoactive properties, which can lead to altered perceptions and distorted reality. The plant's bioactive compounds, such as thujone and artabsin, contribute to its hallucinogenic effects, often resulting in vivid and intense visual and auditory experiences. Ingestion of artemisia absinthium can induce a range of symptoms, from mild euphoria to severe psychotic episodes, highlighting the need for caution and responsible use.

2. Develops Anxiety

Artemisia absinthium develops anxiety in some individuals due to its high concentration of thujone, a chemical compound that can have a negative impact on the nervous system. This side effect is particularly associated with excessive consumption or prolonged use of the plant. As a result, people using Artemisia absinthium for medicinal purposes are advised to follow recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare professional.

3. Causes Nausea

Artemisia absinthium causes nausea, which can range from mild discomfort to severe and debilitating symptoms. This side effect is often associated with the plant's bioactive compounds, including thujone and sesquiterpenes, which can be toxic in large quantities. Consuming artemisia absinthium in excessive amounts or for extended periods can lead to prolonged nausea and other adverse health effects.

4. Causes Liver Damage

Artemisia absinthium causes liver damage, primarily due to the presence of thujone, a toxic compound that can accumulate in the body and cause harm. Prolonged consumption of absinthe, a beverage made from this plant, has been linked to cases of liver damage and other adverse health effects. The severity of the damage often depends on the individual's tolerance and the duration of exposure to thujone.

5. Experiences Seizures

Artemisia absinthium experiences seizures in rare cases, primarily due to the presence of thujone, a toxic compound that can affect the nervous system. Thujone can cause the brain to become overexcited, leading to seizures or even more severe neurological problems. Individuals who are prone to seizures or have a history of neurological disorders should exercise extreme caution when handling or consuming products derived from this plant.

6. Triggers Psychosis

Artemisia absinthium triggers psychosis, a side effect that has been reported in rare cases. This is often associated with excessive consumption of the plant's essential oils or prolonged use of its extracts, particularly in individuals with a history of mental health conditions. In susceptible individuals, the plant's neurotoxic compounds can lead to severe hallucinations and disconnection from reality.

7. Exhibits Erratic Behavior

Artemisia absinthium exhibits erratic behavior, which is a rare but reported side effect of consuming the plant or its essential oil. This erratic behavior can manifest as unpredictable mood swings, confusion, and altered mental states in some individuals. In severe cases, prolonged exposure to artemisia absinthium may lead to more serious psychological issues, including psychosis.
