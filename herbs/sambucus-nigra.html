The medicinal properties of Sambucus nigra have been shown to have numerous health benefits, including reducing the severity and duration of colds and flu, as well as exerting antioxidant and immunomodulatory effects.

People with pre-existing conditions, such as high blood pressure, kidney disease, or liver disease, are more likely to experience these side effects.

Precautions should be taken to avoid interactions with other medications, as sambucus nigra may exacerbate conditions like asthma, thyroid disorders, or bleeding disorders, and may also cause allergic reactions, hives, and itching.

The possible side effects of Sambucus nigra are listed below.

  • Gastrointestinal issues: Consuming sambucus nigra may lead to stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea in some individuals.
  • Interactions with medications: Sambucus nigra may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications, which can cause adverse effects.
  • Dizziness and lightheadedness: Sambucus nigra can cause dizziness and lightheadedness due to its sedative properties.
  • Headaches: Some people may experience headaches after consuming sambucus nigra, possibly due to its antihistamine effects.
  • Migraines: Sambucus nigra may trigger migraines in susceptible individuals, possibly due to its ability to dilate blood vessels.
  • Sleep disturbances: Consuming sambucus nigra too close to bedtime can lead to sleep disturbances, including insomnia and vivid dreams.
