Arctium lappa, also known as bull thistle or greater burdock, is a biennial plant that can be identified by its large, coarse leaves with spiny margins and a distinctive rosette growth habit. Upon closer inspection, the plant's leaves and stems often exhibit a characteristic 'prickle' or 'spine' at the tip, which can be a key identifying feature of A. lappa.
