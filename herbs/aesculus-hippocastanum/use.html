aesculus hippocastanum uses

constipation

Aesculus hippocastanum, also known as horse chestnut, has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including constipation, due to its potential to stimulate bowel movements and improve digestion. The seeds and leaves of the plant are said to have a laxative effect, which can help alleviate symptoms of constipation and promote regular bowel movements. However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Aesculus hippocastanum for constipation, as its effectiveness and safety in this context have not been extensively scientifically proven.
