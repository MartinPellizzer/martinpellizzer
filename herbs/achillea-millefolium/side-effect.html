Achillea millefolium increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood and interfere with platelet function, which can lead to excessive bleeding in some individuals. As a result, individuals considering using Achillea millefolium should consult with their healthcare provider to discuss potential risks and interactions.

7. Affects Blood Clotting

Achillea millefolium affects blood clotting by inhibiting platelet aggregation and prolonging bleeding time, making it a potential risk for individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications. This effect is primarily attributed to the plant's compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, which interfere with the normal clotting process. As a result, people using Achillea millefolium may experience increased bleeding or bruising, particularly after surgical procedures or injuries.

10. Causes Gastrointestinal Distress

In rare cases, gastrointestinal distress from Achillea millefolium may be severe enough to require medical attention.

11. Induces Respiratory Problems

Achillea millefolium induces respiratory problems due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the lungs. 13. Triggers Allergic Reactions

Achillea millefolium triggers allergic reactions in some individuals, causing symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. These reactions are often associated with the plant's essential oils, which can be particularly potent and irritating to the skin and respiratory system. If left untreated, severe allergic reactions to Achillea millefolium can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.
