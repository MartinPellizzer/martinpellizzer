13 Most Common Side Effects of Achillea Millefolium

1. Induces Allergy

Achillea millefolium induces allergy due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which are known to cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals. In rare cases, exposure to the plant can lead to severe allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis. People who experience allergic reactions to Achillea millefolium should avoid handling the plant and take necessary precautions to prevent exposure.

2. Causes Skin Irritation

Achillea millefolium causes skin irritation, particularly in individuals who have sensitive skin or are prone to allergies. When the plant's sap comes into contact with the skin, it can lead to redness, itching, and inflammation. This skin irritation is a common side effect of handling or using Achillea millefolium in herbal remedies or teas.

3. Increases Bleeding Risk

Achillea millefolium increases bleeding risk due to its anticoagulant properties, which can lead to interactions with blood-thinning medications and worsen bleeding conditions. Consuming Achillea millefolium in large quantities or for extended periods may exacerbate this risk, especially in individuals with pre-existing bleeding disorders. Patients taking medications that affect blood clotting should consult their healthcare provider before using Achillea millefolium or any other herbal supplement.

4. Triggers Asthma

Achillea millefolium triggers asthma in some individuals due to its potential to cause an allergic reaction. The plant's allergenic properties can lead to inflammation in the airways, exacerbating asthma symptoms. In people with asthma, exposure to Achillea millefolium may trigger wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

5. Interacts With Blood Thinners

Achillea millefolium interacts with blood thinners, which can lead to an increased risk of bleeding when taken in conjunction with medications such as warfarin or aspirin. This interaction is a concern for individuals taking blood thinners, as it may cause excessive bleeding or bruising. It is essential for those using Achillea millefolium to inform their healthcare provider about their medication regimen to prevent potential complications.

6. Induces Nausea

Achillea millefolium induces nausea in some individuals, particularly when consumed in large quantities or in certain forms. This side effect may be due to the plant's saponins and volatile oils, which can irritate the stomach and gastrointestinal tract. In rare cases, excessive consumption of Achillea millefolium may lead to more severe gastrointestinal issues.

7. Produces Photosensitivity

Achillea millefolium produces photosensitivity, a side effect that can cause the skin to become sensitive to sunlight, leading to an increased risk of sunburn and other skin damage. When exposed to sunlight, individuals taking Achillea millefolium may experience redness, itching, and blistering, which can be painful and may lead to long-term skin problems. It is essential for individuals taking Achillea millefolium to use sun protection measures, such as wearing protective clothing and applying sunscreen, to minimize the risk of photosensitivity.

8. Can Cause Headaches

Achillea millefolium can cause headaches, particularly due to its volatile oils and sesquiterpenes, which can induce a range of adverse reactions in some individuals. The plant's essential oils may cause irritation or allergic reactions in some people, leading to headaches and other symptoms. In rare cases, consuming or applying achillea millefolium extracts can trigger severe headaches, making it essential to use caution when handling the plant.

9. Causes Stomach Upset

Achillea millefolium causes stomach upset in some individuals, particularly when consumed in large quantities or in combination with other medications. The plant's essential oils and saponins may contribute to gastrointestinal irritation and discomfort. In most cases, stomach upset associated with Achillea millefolium is mild and temporary, but it is essential to consult a healthcare professional for proper guidance.

10. May Cause Digestive Issues

Achillea millefolium may cause digestive issues, such as bloating, gas, and stomach cramps, in some individuals who consume it. This side effect is often associated with the plant's essential oils and saponins, which can irritate the digestive tract. It is essential to note that the severity and frequency of these symptoms can vary depending on the individual and the amount of the plant consumed.

11. May Cause Dizziness

Achillea millefolium may cause dizziness, which is often attributed to its essential oil's potential interaction with the nervous system. The dizziness experienced by some individuals may be a result of the plant's volatile compounds affecting blood pressure or circulation. In rare cases, this side effect can be severe and should be monitored closely to prevent further complications.

12. Triggers Vomiting

Achillea millefolium triggers vomiting as a potential side effect of consuming the plant, which is also known as yarrow. This symptom is often accompanied by other gastrointestinal issues, such as diarrhea and stomach cramps. In severe cases, the vomiting may lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, requiring medical attention.

13. May Cause Diarrhea

Achillea millefolium may cause diarrhea due to its potential to interact with medications or exacerbate underlying digestive issues. Consuming large quantities of the plant may lead to gastrointestinal upset, including diarrhea, in some individuals. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Achillea millefolium, especially if you have a pre-existing medical condition.
