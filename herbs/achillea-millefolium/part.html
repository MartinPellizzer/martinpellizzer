7 Achillea Millefolium Best Medicinal Parts

1. Leaf

Achillea millefolium leaf, rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids, has anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that aid in wound healing and reducing inflammation. The leaf also contains sesquiterpene lactones, which exhibit antimicrobial and anticoagulant activities, making it beneficial for treating minor wounds and skin conditions. Its antioxidant properties help protect against cell damage and promote overall health.

2. Flower

Achillea millefolium flower is rich in flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and terpenoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The flavonoids present in the flower have been shown to exhibit antiviral and antibacterial activities, making it useful for wound healing and skin care. The sesquiterpene lactones found in the flower also have been reported to have anti-cancer properties, although more research is needed to confirm this.

3. Essential Oil

Achillea millefolium essential oil, derived from the flowers and leaves of the plant, has been used to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. The oil also contains compounds with antiseptic and antimicrobial properties, making it useful for wound healing and preventing infection. Additionally, its antispasmodic properties have been utilized to soothe digestive issues and reduce menstrual cramp discomfort.

4. Root

Achillea millefolium root is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it a potential treatment for wounds, ulcers, and skin irritations. The root of this plant is also believed to have antimicrobial and antifungal properties, which may help to prevent infection and promote healing. Additionally, the root is thought to have astringent properties, which may help to reduce bleeding and promote the formation of scar tissue.

5. Stem

Achillea millefolium stem, although not as commonly used as other parts, contains a small amount of essential oils and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties. The stem of the plant is also rich in sesquiterpene lactones, which are known to have antioxidant and antimicrobial effects. However, the stem's medicinal properties are generally considered to be less pronounced compared to the leaves and flowers of the plant.
