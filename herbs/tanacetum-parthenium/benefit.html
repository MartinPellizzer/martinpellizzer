11 Best Benefits Of Tanacetum Parthenium

Tanacetum parthenium, also known as Feverfew, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family native to Europe and western Asia.

Its bioactive compounds, including parthenolide and sesquiterpene lactones, are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant has been found to effectively heal wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, relax muscles by reducing spasms and pain, and relieve anxiety by its sedative and anxiolytic effects.

Overall, Tanacetum parthenium has been traditionally used to treat a variety of conditions, including headaches, fever, and digestive issues.
