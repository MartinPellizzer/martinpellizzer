Side Effects

Astragalus membranaceus side effects are numerous and can be experienced by individuals taking the plant in high doses or for extended periods.

Some common side effects include dizziness, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, and allergic reactions such as skin rashes and itching.

People with pre-existing conditions like kidney disease, liver disease, and those taking medications like blood thinners, diabetes medications, and immunosuppressants are more likely to experience these side effects.

Precautions should also be taken by pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and individuals with autoimmune disorders, as they may be more susceptible to the plant's adverse effects.

The possible side effects of Astragalus membranaceus are listed below.

  • Allergic reaction: Some people may experience an allergic reaction to astragalus membranaceus, including symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.
  • Abdominal pain: Consuming large amounts of astragalus membranaceus may cause abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting in some individuals.
  • Diarrhea: Astragalus membranaceus may cause diarrhea in people with sensitive stomachs or those who are not accustomed to consuming it.
  • Interactions with medications: Astragalus membranaceus may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and immunosuppressants, which can lead to adverse effects.
  • Low blood pressure: Consuming astragalus membranaceus may cause a drop in blood pressure, which can be problematic for people with pre-existing low blood pressure conditions.
  • Respiratory issues: Astragalus membranaceus may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (copd), in some individuals.
  • Thyroid problems: Consuming large amounts of astragalus membranaceus may cause thyroid problems, including hypothyroidism, in some individuals, particularly those with pre-existing thyroid conditions.
