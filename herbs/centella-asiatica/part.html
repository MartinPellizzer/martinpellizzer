13 Centella Asiatica Best Medicinal Parts

1. Leaf

Centella asiatica leaf is rich in triterpenoid saponins, particularly asiaticoside, which has been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The leaf also contains madecassoside and madecassic acid, which are believed to aid in wound healing and improve skin health. These bioactive compounds have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including inflammation and skin disorders.

2. Root

Centella asiatica root is primarily used to treat anxiety and stress due to its adaptogenic properties, which help regulate the body's response to stress. It is also rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties, making it beneficial for skin health and wound healing. The root of this plant is also traditionally used to improve cognitive function and memory, as well as to support cardiovascular health.

3. Whole Plant

Centella asiatica whole plant is traditionally used in various medicinal practices due to its rich content of bioactive compounds, including triterpenoids and alkaloids. The whole plant, particularly the leaves, stems, and roots, are used to treat a range of health conditions, from inflammation and wounds to anxiety and cognitive impairment. The medicinal properties of Centella asiatica whole plant are attributed to its ability to promote healing, improve cognitive function, and modulate the immune system.

4. Rhizome

Centella asiatica rhizome, also known as Gotu Kola, is a medicinal part used to promote skin health and wound healing. It is rich in triterpenoid saponins, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The rhizome is also used to treat various health conditions, including anxiety, insomnia, and fever.

5. Underground Part

Centella asiatica underground part, commonly known as the rhizome and roots, contain various bioactive compounds such as iridoid glycosides, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids. These compounds have been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties, contributing to the plant's medicinal value. The extracts from the underground parts of Centella asiatica are used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including anxiety, insomnia, and skin disorders.

6. Stem

Centella asiatica stem, which is rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids, has been traditionally used in various medicinal practices for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The stem is often used in herbal medicine to promote wound healing and improve skin health due to its high concentration of triterpenoid saponins. Additionally, the stem of Centella asiatica is also valued for its potential neuroprotective effects and its ability to enhance cognitive function.

7. Aerial Part

Centella asiatica aerial part, also known as gotu kola, is rich in triterpenoids and alkaloids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds have been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including anxiety, stress, and skin issues, such as wound healing and eczema. The aerial part of Centella asiatica is also used to improve cognitive function and boost the immune system.

8. Flower

Centella asiatica flower has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including inflammation and oxidative stress. The bioactive compounds present in the flower, such as asiaticoside, madecassoside, and madecassic acid, have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds have been shown to promote wound healing and tissue regeneration, making the centella asiatica flower a valuable component in traditional medicine.

9. Above-Ground Part

Centella asiatica above-ground part, also known as gotu kola, is a traditional medicinal herb used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine. The leaves, stems, and flowers of this plant are rich in triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and wound-healing effects, making Centella asiatica a popular natural remedy for various health conditions.

10. Seed

Centella asiatica seed contains triterpenoids, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, and are often used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic benefits. The seed is also a rich source of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Furthermore, the seed has been studied for its potential in treating various health conditions, including cardiovascular disease and neurodegenerative disorders.

11. Tuber

Centella asiatica tuber, also known as gotu kola tuber, is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It is believed to aid in wound healing and may help alleviate symptoms of various health conditions, including arthritis and diabetes. Studies have also suggested that the tuber may possess neuroprotective effects, making it a subject of interest in the field of neuroscience.

12. Corm

Centella asiatica corm is a rich source of bioactive compounds, particularly triterpenoids and flavonoids, which exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The corm of Centella asiatica has been traditionally used in various folk medicine systems to treat skin conditions, wounds, and other inflammatory diseases. Extracts from the corm have been shown to promote wound healing, reduce inflammation, and improve skin health.

13. Bark

Centella asiatica bark is known to contain various bioactive compounds including triterpenoids and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. The bark has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, inflammation, and cardiovascular diseases. It also exhibits antioxidant and antimicrobial activities, contributing to its potential therapeutic benefits.
