Salvia Officinalis: Complete Medicinal Profile

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae) that is native to the Mediterranean region.

The medicinal properties of sage include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which make it effective in reducing inflammation and protecting against cell damage.

The health benefits of consuming sage include improved cognitive function, reduced risk of Alzheimer's disease, and relief from symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and night sweats.

Herbal preparations of sage include teas, infusions, capsules, and essential oils, which can be used to treat a variety of health issues, including digestive problems and sore throats.

Constituents

Salvia officinalis has been found to contain a diverse array of bioactive constituents, including flavonoids such as apigenin and luteolin, which possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant also contains tannins, particularly salviol and isosalviol, which exhibit antimicrobial and astringent activities.

Additionally, Salvia officinalis is rich in phenolic acids like rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid, which have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties.

The plant's essential oil, primarily composed of thujone, camphor, and borneol, has been found to possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and anxiolytic properties.

Side Effects

Salvia officinalis side effects are typically experienced by individuals who consume high amounts of the plant, particularly in the form of tea or supplements.

Common side effects include dizziness, headaches, nausea, vomiting, stomach cramps, diarrhea, and an increase in heart rate.

People with pre-existing conditions such as high blood pressure, kidney or liver disease, and heart conditions are more likely to experience these side effects.

Precautions should be taken by pregnant or breastfeeding women, as well as individuals who are prone to seizures or have a history of mental health conditions, as salvia officinalis may exacerbate these issues.

The possible side effects of Salvia officinalis are listed below.

  • Digestive issues: Salvia officinalis may cause stomach upset, nausea, or diarrhea in some individuals due to its bitter compounds and volatile oils.
  • Allergic reactions: Some people may experience allergic reactions to salvia officinalis, such as hives, itching, or difficulty breathing.
  • Interactions with medications: Salvia officinalis may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications.
  • Increased heart rate: Salvia officinalis may cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure due to its stimulating effects on the nervous system.
  • Anxiety and panic: Salvia officinalis can cause anxiety and panic in some individuals, particularly when consumed in large doses or with other substances that have psychoactive effects.
  • Headaches and dizziness: Salvia officinalis may cause headaches and dizziness in some individuals due to its vasodilatory effects and the way it interacts with the brain and nervous system.
  • Liver damage: Long-term consumption of salvia officinalis may lead to liver damage due to its high concentration of thujone, a compound that can be toxic to the liver in large amounts.
