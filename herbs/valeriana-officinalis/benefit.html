11 Best Benefits Of Valeriana Officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and Asia, known for its medicinal and sedative properties.

The bioactive compounds present in Valeriana officinalis, including valerenic acid, valeranone, and isovaleric acid, contribute to its benefits.

This plant is particularly effective in relieving stress and reducing anxiety by promoting relaxation, making it a popular natural remedy for individuals seeking a calming effect.

By inducing a state of relaxation, Valeriana officinalis helps to alleviate symptoms of insomnia and restlessness, promoting a good night's sleep.

Featured Study

According to Frontiers in pharmacology, Valeriana officinalis has been found to possess several health benefits, including sedative, hypnotic, antispasmodic, analgesic, antidepressant, anxiolytic, anticonvulsant, antiepileptic, neuroprotective, antibacterial, antiviral, cytotoxic, and antitumor effects, as well as cardiovascular and cerebrovascular system improvements.

1. Relieves stress

Valeriana officinalis relieves stress by reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, resulting in a sense of calmness and tranquility.

This benefit is crucial as excessive stress can lead to various health issues, such as insomnia, digestive problems, and mood disorders, and can also impair cognitive function, as seen in individuals with anxiety disorders, post-traumatic stress disorder, and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder.

The plant's anxiolytic properties are attributed to bioactive constituents like valepotriates, valerenic acid, and isovaltrate, which interact with neurotransmitters in the brain to regulate mood and reduce stress.

To harness the stress-relieving benefits of Valeriana officinalis, it is best to use herbal preparations such as tea, tinctures, or capsules, under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as excessive consumption can have adverse effects.
