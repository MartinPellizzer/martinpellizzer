13 Allium Sativum Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Allium sativum root, also known as garlic root, has been used in traditional medicine for its antibacterial and antiviral properties. The root of garlic contains compounds like allicin, which has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it useful in treating various health conditions. It is also used in some cultures to aid in digestion and lower cholesterol levels.

2. Bulb

Allium sativum bulb contains compounds like allicin, which exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful for treating colds, coughs, and respiratory issues. Additionally, the bulb's antiviral and antibacterial properties have been shown to aid in wound healing and support the immune system.

3. Leaves

Allium sativum leaves are primarily used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.
