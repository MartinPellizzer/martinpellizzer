11 Allium Sativum Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Allium sativum root is a valuable medicinal part that has been used for centuries to treat various health issues. The root contains compounds such as allicin, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in reducing pain and inflammation. It is also used to lower cholesterol levels and reduce blood pressure due to its ability to thin the blood and improve cardiovascular health.

2. Bulb

Allium sativum bulb, commonly known as garlic, contains compounds with antimicrobial and antiviral properties, which contribute to its medicinal value. The bulb's active compounds, including allicin, have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular-protective effects. Regular consumption of garlic bulb has been associated with reduced risk of certain diseases, such as high blood pressure and certain types of cancer.

4. Oil

Allium sativum oil, also known as garlic oil, is commonly used in traditional medicine due to its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The oil is extracted from the bulb of the garlic plant and is used to treat various health issues, including digestive problems and skin conditions. Additionally, garlic oil has been found to have cardiovascular benefits and may help reduce blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

5. Leaves

Allium sativum leaves, although not as commonly used as the bulb, contain compounds like allicin and ajoene, which have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves may also possess antioxidant and cardiovascular-protective effects, although more research is needed to fully understand their medicinal value. Traditional medicine often utilizes the leaves in combination with the bulb to enhance their therapeutic properties.

6. Flower

Allium sativum flower is not commonly used as a medicinal part, but it has been used in some traditional medicine practices. However, the essential oil extracted from the flower has been reported to have antimicrobial properties. The primary medicinal parts of Allium sativum, such as the bulb and leaves, are more widely studied and utilized.

7. Stem

Allium sativum stem is not commonly utilized as a medicinal part, however, the leaves, bulbs, and flowers are widely used for medicinal purposes. In some traditional medicine, the stem is used for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. However, the effectiveness and usage of Allium sativum stem as a medicinal part are still being researched and studied.

8. Resin

Allium sativum resin, also known as garlic resin, has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. However, there is limited research available on the resin of Allium sativum specifically, as the primary medicinal parts of garlic are typically considered to be the bulbs and cloves. Further studies are needed to fully understand the potential health benefits and applications of garlic resin.

9. Juice

Allium sativum juice, also known as garlic juice, is a concentrated liquid extract obtained from garlic cloves. The juice is rich in bioactive compounds, including allicin, which has been studied for its potential health benefits, including antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often used in traditional medicine to support cardiovascular health and as an antiseptic to aid in wound healing.
