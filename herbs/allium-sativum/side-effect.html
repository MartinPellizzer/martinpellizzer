9 Most Common Side Effects of Allium Sativum

1. Inhibits Platelet Function

Allium sativum inhibits platelet function, which can lead to increased risk of bleeding and bruising. This effect is due to the compounds present in the plant, such as salvestrol B, that interfere with the normal aggregation of platelets. As a result, individuals taking Allium sativum may experience prolonged bleeding times and should be cautious when engaging in activities that may cause injury.

2. Induces Allergy

Allium sativum induces allergy in some individuals due to the presence of certain allergenic compounds, particularly allicin, which can cause skin irritation, respiratory issues, and other allergic reactions. People who are allergic to garlic may experience symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling after consuming foods that contain garlic. In severe cases, an allergic reaction to garlic can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.

3. Causes Skin Irritation

Allium sativum causes skin irritation in some individuals due to its sulfur compounds, which can lead to redness, itching, and inflammation. This reaction is often seen when handling the plant or its extracts, particularly in people with sensitive skin. In severe cases, skin irritation can progress to more serious conditions, such as contact dermatitis.

4. Irritates The Eyes

Allium sativum irritates the eyes, causing discomfort and potential vision problems due to the release of irritating compounds when its tissues are broken or damaged. This reaction can be severe in some individuals, especially those who are sensitive to the chemicals present in the plant. Prolonged exposure to the irritants can lead to chronic eye problems and may require medical attention.

5. Interferes With Blood Clotting

Allium sativum interferes with blood clotting, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding or bruising due to its effect on platelet aggregation. This interference can be a concern for individuals taking anticoagulant medications or those with bleeding disorders. As a result, individuals taking Allium sativum supplements should monitor their bleeding risk and consult with a healthcare professional if necessary.

6. Causes Headaches

Allium sativum causes headaches due to its high sulfur content, which can be released in the body as a result of digestion. This release of sulfur compounds can irritate the nervous system and lead to headaches. In some individuals, consuming too much garlic from the Allium sativum plant may exacerbate this effect, resulting in more severe or frequent headaches.

7. Causes Nausea

Allium sativum causes nausea, typically in individuals who are not accustomed to consuming large quantities of garlic. The nausea is often accompanied by stomach discomfort and may be more pronounced when consumed in its raw form. In some cases, excessive consumption of garlic can also lead to more severe gastrointestinal issues.

8. Triggers Asthma

Allium sativum triggers asthma in some individuals due to a compound called allicin, which is released when the plant is crushed or chopped. This compound can cause airway inflammation and constriction, exacerbating symptoms in people with asthma. People with asthma are advised to consume garlic in moderation and to consult with their doctor before using it as a medicinal remedy.

9. Provokes Anaphylaxis

Allium sativum provokes anaphylaxis in some individuals, a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction. Symptoms of anaphylaxis include difficulty breathing, rapid heartbeat, and a drop in blood pressure, requiring immediate medical attention. This severe allergy to Allium sativum highlights the importance of proper identification and handling of the plant to prevent such reactions.
