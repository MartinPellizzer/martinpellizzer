11 Allium Sativum Best Active Constituents

1. Allicin

Allium sativum allicin, a sulfur-containing compound, is a key bioactive constituent responsible for the medicinal properties of garlic. It has been reported to exhibit antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal activities, as well as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The production of allicin from alliin, a non-enzymatic reaction, is crucial for its medicinal properties to be activated.

2. Diallyl Disulfide

Allium sativum diallyl disulfide is a bioactive compound found in garlic, responsible for its medicinal and therapeutic properties. It has been shown to exhibit antimicrobial, antiviral, and antifungal activities, making it effective in preventing infections and promoting wound healing. Additionally, diallyl disulfide has been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, contributing to its potential in treating various health conditions.

3. Diallyl Trisulfide

Allium sativum diallyl trisulfide, a key bioactive compound found in garlic, has been studied for its potential health benefits, including its ability to exhibit antimicrobial, antiviral, and antioxidant properties. Research suggests that diallyl trisulfide may help to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health by inhibiting platelet aggregation and lowering triglycerides. Additionally, this compound has been shown to have anti-cancer properties, with studies indicating its potential to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and induce apoptosis.
