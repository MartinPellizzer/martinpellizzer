7 Most Common Side Effects of Ammi Visnaga

Ammi visnaga exacerbates liver damage, particularly when taken in large doses or combined with other medications, which can lead to serious health consequences. The liver damage caused by ammi visnaga is often irreversible, emphasizing the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using this plant for medicinal purposes.

2. Causes Allergic Reactions

Ammi visnaga causes allergic reactions, which can range from mild symptoms such as hives and itching to more severe reactions like difficulty breathing. These allergic reactions are often triggered by the plant's oil or other compounds, and can be exacerbated by prolonged exposure. In some cases, individuals may experience anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention.

Ammi visnaga interferes with immune response by potentially suppressing the body's natural defense mechanisms, which can lead to a heightened risk of infections and diseases. This suppression can also result in prolonged recovery times from illnesses and injuries. Additionally, long-term use of ammi visnaga may exacerbate underlying health conditions that are related to the immune system.

4. Causes Kidney Damage

Ammi visnaga causes kidney damage due to its toxic properties. Consuming large amounts of the plant can lead to kidney impairment and potentially even kidney failure. This side effect has been reported in various medical studies and highlights the need for caution when using the plant for medicinal purposes.

5. Harms Nervous System

Ammi visnaga harms nervous system and has been associated with cases of neurological problems, including tremors and muscle weakness. The potential risks to the nervous system have led to its use being restricted in some countries. This plant's adverse effects on the nervous system highlight the importance of proper medical supervision and informed use.

6. Induces Abortions

Ammi visnaga induces abortions due to its ability to stimulate uterine contractions, leading to premature labor and termination of pregnancy. This action is a result of the plant's bioactive compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes. However, its use can be hazardous for pregnant individuals and should be avoided.

7. Increases Gastrointestinal Bleeding

Ammi visnaga increases gastrointestinal bleeding, potentially leading to severe health complications if left unaddressed. This side effect is often associated with the plant's use in traditional medicine, where excessive consumption or improper preparation may cause adverse reactions. In severe cases, gastrointestinal bleeding may require immediate medical attention to prevent further complications.
