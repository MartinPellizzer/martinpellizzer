identification

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a perennial plant native to China and other parts of East Asia, characterized by its large, umbrella-like foliage and small white or purple flowers. The plant's leaves and roots contain compounds such as ferulic acid and ligustilide, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for women's health and other purposes.
