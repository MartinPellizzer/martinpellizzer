Side Effects

Angelica sinensis side effects are numerous and can be quite severe for some individuals.

These side effects include dizziness, nausea, stomach upset, diarrhea, and vomiting, which are often reported by people with sensitive stomachs.

Women who are pregnant or breastfeeding, as well as individuals with bleeding disorders, are most likely to experience these side effects, such as increased menstrual flow and uterine contractions.

Precautions should be taken by those who are prone to allergic reactions, as Angelica sinensis can cause symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling, and by people taking medications for diabetes, blood thinning, or high blood pressure, as it may interact with these medications to cause side effects like hypoglycemia, bleeding, and low blood pressure.

The possible side effects of Angelica sinensis are listed below.

  • Gastrointestinal upset: May cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or stomach pain
  • Allergic reactions: Can cause skin rashes, itching, or difficulty breathing in rare cases
  • Interactions with medications: May interact with blood thinners, diabetes medications, or other herbal supplements
  • Headaches and dizziness: May cause dizziness, headaches, or lightheadedness due to its blood-thinning properties
  • Menstrual irregularities: May affect menstrual cycles, leading to irregular periods or changes in menstrual flow
  • Hypotension: May cause a drop in blood pressure, particularly when taken with other blood pressure-lowering medications
  • Liver damage: May cause liver damage or elevated liver enzymes in rare cases, particularly with long-term use
