Angelica sinensis (Dong Quai)

What is Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis, commonly known as Dong Quai, is a medicinal plant that belongs to the family Apiaceae.

It is classified within the genus Angelica and is a species of flowering plant native to China.

This species is further categorized as a perennial plant, often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

What are the active constituents of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis contains a variety of bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Coumarins, a group of phytoestrogens, have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant activities. Ferulic acid, a polyphenolic compound, has antioxidant properties and has been used to protect against cell damage and oxidative stress. Ligustilide, a sesquiterpene, has been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective effects.

Butylphthalide and isoimperatorin, two other key compounds, have been demonstrated to possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, respectively.

What are the side effects of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis can cause a range of negative health side effects, including inducing thrombocytopenia, a condition characterized by abnormally low levels of platelets in the blood.

The use of this plant may also increase the risk of interaction with anticoagulant medications, leading to adverse effects such as bleeding complications. Furthermore, angelica sinensis can cause hemolysis, a condition where red blood cells are destroyed, which can lead to anemia and other complications. Additionally, long-term use of this plant may promote hepatotoxicity, or liver damage, due to its potential to cause liver inflammation and scarring.

Finally, angelica sinensis has been linked to nephrotoxicity, which can cause damage to the kidneys and impair their ability to filter waste from the blood.
