Angelica sinensis (Dong Quai)

What is Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis, commonly known as Dong Quai, is a medicinal plant that belongs to the family Apiaceae.

It is classified within the genus Angelica and is a species of flowering plant native to China.

This species is further categorized as a perennial plant, often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

How to identify this plant?

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, is a perennial plant native to China and other parts of East Asia.

It can be identified by its large, umbrella-like leaves with deeply lobed margins and a hollow stem that is often purple or red in color, typically growing to a height of 2-3 meters.

The plant's roots and rhizomes are commonly harvested and used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
