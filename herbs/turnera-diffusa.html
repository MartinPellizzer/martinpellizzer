Turnera diffusa, also known as Damiana, is a plant native to the Americas, specifically the tropical and subtropical regions.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to act as an anxiolytic, antidepressant, and aphrodisiac, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine.

The health benefits of Turnera diffusa include reducing stress and anxiety, improving sleep quality, and increasing libido.

The plant can be consumed in various herbal preparations, such as teas, capsules, and tinctures, which are often used to promote relaxation and overall well-being.

Side Effects

Turnera diffusa side effects are numerous and can be quite bothersome for some individuals.

People who are most likely to experience these side effects include those with pre-existing liver conditions, pregnant women, and individuals taking medications such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and cholesterol-lowering drugs.

The side effects of Turnera diffusa may include dizziness, nausea, stomach cramps, diarrhea, vomiting, allergic reactions, skin rashes, and headaches, as well as more serious side effects like liver damage, increased risk of bleeding, and interactions with other medications that may lead to hypoglycemia or other complications.

To avoid these side effects, individuals should be cautious when consuming Turnera diffusa, especially if they have a history of digestive issues or are taking medications that may interact with its compounds.

The possible side effects of Turnera diffusa are listed below.

  • Hypoglycemia: Low blood sugar levels
  • Gastrointestinal upset: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain
  • Headache: Pain in the head, possibly due to blood pressure changes
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness: Feeling unsteady or faint
  • Allergic reactions: Skin rashes, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing
  • Interactions with other medications: Possible interactions with blood thinners, diabetes medications, and other supplements
  • Seizures: Rare cases of seizures have been reported, especially in people with a history of seizure disorders
