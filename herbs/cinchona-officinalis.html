identification

In addition, the plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers that grow in clusters, typically between 8-12 inches long, adding to its distinctive appearance.

uses

Cinchona officinalis, also known as the Peruvian bark, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat malaria and other febrile diseases due to its high content of quinine. The bark's quinine content helps to reduce fever and alleviate symptoms associated with malaria, such as chills and muscle aches. Today, cinchona officinalis is still used as a natural remedy for various health conditions, including fever reduction, pain relief, and as an antimalarial agent.
