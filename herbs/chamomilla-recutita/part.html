11 Chamomilla Recutita Best Medicinal Parts

1. Aerial Parts

Chamomilla recutita aerial parts, also known as chamomile flowers, have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. The essential oil extracted from the aerial parts is used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness due to its calming effects. The flavonoids and terpenoids present in the aerial parts are also known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

2. Flowers

Chamomilla recutita flowers are used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. They contain essential oils such as bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to their soothing and calming effects. These properties make chamomilla recutita flowers a popular treatment for skin irritations and digestive issues.

3. Essential Oil

Chamomilla recutita essential oil is known for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, often used to calm skin irritations and reduce anxiety. The oil is rich in apigenin, a flavonoid that has been shown to have sedative effects and help with sleep quality. It is commonly used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

4. Flower Heads

Chamomilla recutita flower heads are a valuable medicinal part of the plant, traditionally used to soothe anxiety and restlessness due to their apigenin and luteolin content. The essential oil extracted from the flower heads contains anti-inflammatory compounds that may help alleviate digestive issues and promote relaxation. Chamomilla recutita flower heads have also been used to treat skin irritations and allergic reactions, reducing redness and promoting wound healing.

5. Petals

Chamomilla recutita petals are used in herbal medicine due to their anti-inflammatory properties, which help to soothe skin irritations and reduce swelling. The apigenin present in these petals has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, making it useful for treating anxiety and insomnia. Additionally, chamomilla recutita petals have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues and menstrual cramps due to their antispasmodic properties.

6. Seeds

Chamomilla recutita seeds are not commonly used in herbal medicine, unlike the flowers of the plant. However, the seeds of Chamomilla recutita do contain apigenin, a flavonoid with potential medicinal properties. Further research is needed to fully understand the medicinal benefits and uses of Chamomilla recutita seeds.

7. Floral Buds

Chamomilla recutita floral buds are used in herbal medicine due to their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can help soothe skin irritations and wounds. The buds also possess a mild sedative effect, making them useful in calming anxiety and promoting relaxation. Additionally, the flavonoids present in the buds have been shown to exhibit antioxidant properties, protecting against cell damage and promoting overall well-being.

8. Dried Flowers

Chamomilla recutita dried flowers are traditionally used to calm digestive issues and soothe menstrual cramps due to their anti-inflammatory properties. The dried flowers of this herb are also used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness by promoting relaxation and reducing stress. Additionally, they have antimicrobial properties that help to prevent infections and promote wound healing.

9. Stems

Chamomilla recutita stems are not commonly used for medicinal purposes. However, the leaves and flowers of the plant are used in herbal medicine due to their anti-inflammatory and calming properties. The essential oil extracted from the flowers is often used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

10. Inflorescence

Chamomilla recutita inflorescence, which is the flower head of the plant, contains apigenin and luteolin, flavonoids with anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil present in the inflorescence has been used to treat digestive issues and promote relaxation. It also possesses antimicrobial and antispasmodic properties, making it beneficial for soothing skin irritations and calming the nervous system.

11. Leaves

Chamomilla recutita leaves are not typically considered medicinal; however, the flowers and essential oil are the primary medicinal parts of the plant. The flowers of Chamomilla recutita are used to create herbal teas, tinctures, and salves, and are valued for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.
