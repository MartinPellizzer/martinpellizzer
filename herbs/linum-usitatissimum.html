Side Effects

Linum usitatissimum side effects are varied and may include dizziness, nausea, headaches, and stomach upset in individuals who consume it in large quantities.

Pregnant women, the elderly, and those with pre-existing medical conditions such as kidney or liver disease are most likely to experience these side effects, which can also include diarrhea, fatigue, and irregular heartbeat.

Precautions should be taken to avoid interactions with other medications, such as blood thinners, and to monitor blood sugar levels in individuals with diabetes.

Additionally, excessive consumption of linum usitatissimum can lead to allergic reactions, skin rashes, and respiratory issues, including bronchitis and asthma exacerbations.

The possible side effects of Linum usitatissimum are listed below.

  • Allergic contact dermatitis: An allergic reaction that causes skin redness, itching, and rashes after coming into contact with the plant.
  • Phototoxicity: An abnormal skin reaction that occurs when the plant's oil reacts with sunlight, leading to redness, itching, and blistering.
  • Gastrointestinal upset: Stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea that may occur after consuming large amounts of the plant's seeds or oil.
  • Hepatotoxicity: Liver damage or inflammation that may occur due to the plant's toxic compounds, particularly pyrrolizidine alkaloids.
  • Respiratory problems: Difficulty breathing, wheezing, and coughing that may occur after inhaling the plant's pollen or volatile oils.
  • Eyes and vision problems: Redness, itching, and vision disturbances that may occur after exposure to the plant's pollen or volatile oils.
  • Seizures and coma: Rare but severe side effects that may occur in cases of extreme poisoning, characterized by seizures and loss of consciousness.
