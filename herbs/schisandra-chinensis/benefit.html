13 Best Benefits Of Schisandra Chinensis

Schisandra chinensis, also known as the "Five-Flavored Fruit," is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant that has been used for centuries to promote overall health and well-being.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including schisandrins, gomisin A, and lignans, are responsible for its numerous health benefits.

Schisandra chinensis has been shown to lower blood pressure by improving cardiovascular function, support liver health through antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, and ease anxiety by regulating the body's stress response.

The plant's unique combination of bioactive compounds makes it a valuable natural remedy for promoting cardiovascular health, liver function, and mental well-being.

Featured Study

According to Frontiers in plant science, Schisandra chinensis has been found to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities due to the presence of active substances such as gomisin D, schisandrol B, schisantherin C, kadsuranin, and kadlongilactone F, which are particularly abundant in its roots.

1. Lowers blood pressure

Schisandra chinensis lowers blood pressure by reducing the force of the heart's contractions and dilating blood vessels to improve circulation.

This benefit is important because high blood pressure is a major risk factor for heart disease, stroke, and kidney failure, and can lead to conditions such as cardiac arrhythmias and vision loss.

The plant has this benefit due to its rich content of bioactive constituents, including schisandrins, lignans, and polysaccharides, which exhibit vasodilatory and antioxidant properties.

To use Schisandra chinensis to lower blood pressure, it can be consumed in the form of herbal teas, capsules, or extracts, typically in doses of 1-3 grams per day, although it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before adding any new supplements to one's regimen.
