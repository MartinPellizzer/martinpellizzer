Artemisia absinthium

Artemisia absinthium, also known as absinthe or wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia.

It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, valued for its medicinal properties, including antiseptic, antiparasitic, and anti-inflammatory effects.

The health benefits of wormwood include relief from digestive issues, reduction of anxiety and stress, and improvement of sleep quality.

Wormwood can be prepared in various herbal forms, including teas, tinctures, essential oils, and infusions, which can be used to treat a range of health issues.

Artemisia absinthium most common herbal preparations are used for various health purposes, including the production of teas, tinctures, and essential oils.

Side Effects

Artemisia absinthium side effects are numerous and can be quite severe, particularly for individuals who consume it in large quantities or for extended periods.

Side effects include headaches, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, diarrhea, and tremors, as well as more severe reactions such as seizures, psychosis, and even death.

People who are most likely to experience these side effects include the elderly, pregnant women, and those with pre-existing medical conditions such as epilepsy, liver disease, or heart problems.

Precautions should be taken to avoid consuming Artemisia absinthium, especially in the form of absinthe, to minimize the risk of experiencing these adverse effects.
