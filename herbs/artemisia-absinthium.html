Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood)

What is Artemisia absinthium?

Artemisia absinthium, commonly known as Wormwood, is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae, within the genus Artemisia.

The genus Artemisia is part of the tribe Anthemideae, which consists of daisy-like plants that are commonly known as daisy or sunflower family members.

Further classification places Artemisia absinthium within the subfamily Asteroideae.

side-effects

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for its antiparasitic properties. However, consuming large quantities of artemisia absinthium can cause severe side effects, including headaches, dizziness, and nausea, due to its high levels of thujone, a toxic compound. Long-term or excessive consumption of wormwood has also been linked to seizures, psychosis, and other neurological disorders, making it essential to use it under the guidance of a medical professional.

cultivation

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, commonly cultivated for its bitter flavor and medicinal properties. To cultivate wormwood, it requires well-drained soil and a dry to moderately wet climate, with full sun and a moderate temperature range of around 15-20°C (59-68°F). Regular pruning and harvesting of the leaves and stems can promote healthy growth and prevent the plant from flowering, allowing for optimal essential oil production.
