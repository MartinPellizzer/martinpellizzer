Azadirachta indica

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as the neem tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

This plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, which include antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antiparasitic properties, making it effective in treating various health issues.

The health benefits of neem include its ability to reduce fever, alleviate skin conditions, and prevent infections, as well as its potential to reduce the severity of diseases such as malaria and dengue fever.

Neem is often prepared into herbal remedies such as neem oil, neem leaves powder, and neem capsules, which can be used to treat a range of health conditions.

Preparations

Azadirachta indica, also known as neem, is a versatile plant with a wide range of herbal preparations used for various health purposes.

The most common herbal preparations of neem include neem oil, neem powder, neem leaf infusions, and neem tinctures.

Neem oil is commonly used as a skin and hair treatment to reduce acne, dandruff, and lice, while neem powder is often used to treat digestive issues and as an anti-inflammatory agent for joint pain and arthritis.

Neem leaf infusions are traditionally used to treat fever, cold, and respiratory issues, and neem tinctures are used to treat skin conditions, wounds, and as a natural insect repellent.

Side Effects

Azadirachta indica side effects are numerous and can range from mild to severe, affecting individuals who use its extracts or products in excessive amounts.

People most likely to experience these side effects include pregnant women, children, and those with weakened immune systems, as their bodies may not be able to tolerate the potent compounds found in the plant.

Common side effects include stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, headaches, and allergic skin reactions such as hives, itching, and redness.

Precautions should be taken to avoid applying excessive amounts of the plant's oil or extracts to the skin, and to avoid ingesting it in large quantities, as this can lead to more severe side effects like liver damage, kidney problems, and even respiratory issues.
