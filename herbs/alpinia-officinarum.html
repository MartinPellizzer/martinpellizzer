Alpinia officinarum (Galangal)

What is Alpinia officinarum?

Alpinia officinarum, commonly known as Galangal, is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, which is a large and diverse group of plants commonly referred to as gingers.

Within this family, Alpinia officinarum is classified as a species of the Alpinia genus, which consists of approximately 200 species of flowering plants native to tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is further characterized by its distinct morphological features, including its rhizomatous growth habit and fragrant, white or pink flowers.

What are the side effects of Alpinia officinarum?

Alpinia officinarum can cause severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis, in individuals who are sensitive to its compounds.

Prolonged exposure to the plant has been known to cause dermatitis, a skin condition characterized by inflammation and irritation. Alpinia officinarum contains hepatotoxic compounds that can cause liver damage and potentially lead to liver failure if ingested in large quantities. The plant's hepatotoxic compounds can also accumulate in the liver over time, leading to further damage and disruption of liver function.

Additionally, consuming Alpinia officinarum can trigger nausea, vomiting, and other gastrointestinal symptoms in some individuals.
