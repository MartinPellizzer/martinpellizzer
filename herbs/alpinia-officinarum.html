What is Alpinia officinarum?

Alpinia officinarum, commonly known as Galangal, is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, which is a large and diverse group of plants commonly referred to as gingers.

Within this family, Alpinia officinarum is classified as a species of the Alpinia genus, which consists of approximately 200 species of flowering plants native to tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is further characterized by its distinct morphological features, including its rhizomatous growth habit and fragrant, white or pink flowers.

How to identify this plant?

Alpinia officinarum, also known as lesser galangal, is a rhizomatous perennial plant that belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It can be identified by its narrow, green leaves that are typically 2-3 times longer than they are wide, and its short, dense spikes of small white flowers that are often hidden by the foliage.

The rhizome of A. officinarum is the part most commonly used in cooking and herbal medicine, and it has a distinctive, earthy and slightly sweet flavor.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of galangal can lead to bleeding disorders and interact with blood-thinning medications, making it essential to consult a healthcare professional before using it as a medicinal remedy. Additionally, galangal may also cause allergic reactions, such as skin irritation or respiratory issues, in rare cases.
