7 Apium Graveolens Best Medicinal Parts

1. Root

Apium graveolens root, commonly known as celery root or celeriac, is a medicinal part of the plant that has been used to treat various health issues. It is rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis and other inflammatory conditions. Additionally, the root has been traditionally used to support digestive health and to help lower blood pressure due to its high content of potassium and other essential nutrients.

2. Leaf

Apium graveolens leaf contains a range of bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The leaf is also a source of apigenin, a flavonoid with potential anti-cancer and neuroprotective effects. Additionally, the leaf has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues and respiratory problems.

3. Essential Oil

Apium graveolens essential oil, extracted from the seeds of the plant, is commonly used in aromatherapy to alleviate digestive issues and reduce stress. Its antispasmodic properties can help to relieve menstrual cramps and other muscular spasms. Additionally, the essential oil has been found to possess antimicrobial properties, making it useful in wound healing and skin care applications.

4. Seed

Apium graveolens seed is not commonly used in traditional medicine due to its toxicity and lack of known medicinal properties. However, the seed is still used in some herbal remedies for its potential antiseptic and antifungal properties, although its effectiveness is largely anecdotal. In some traditional practices, the seed is used to treat respiratory issues and as a diuretic.

5. Stem

Apium graveolens stem, also known as celery stalk, is primarily used in herbal medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. The stem contains a compound called apigenin, which has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-cancer effects. Additionally, the stem's volatile oils have been traditionally used to aid in digestion and relieve flatulence.

6. Fruit

Apium graveolens fruit, also known as celery seeds, have been traditionally used in medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and as a natural diuretic. The fruit contains compounds like limonene and beta-pinene, which have been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the seeds have been used to help lower blood pressure and improve respiratory health.

7. Flower

Apium graveolens flower contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which have been found to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, potentially contributing to its medicinal value. The volatile oils present in the flower may also exhibit antiseptic and antibacterial effects, making it useful for skin care and wound healing. Additionally, the flavonoids in Apium graveolens flower may help protect against cardiovascular disease and certain types of cancer.
