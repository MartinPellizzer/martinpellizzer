Allium sativum (garlic)

What is Allium sativum?

Allium sativum, commonly known as Garlic, is a species of plant that belongs to the family Liliaceae, which includes various flowering plants and is a part of the larger monocot group.

This species is further categorized into the genus Allium, a group of plants characterized by their onion-like bulbs and often pungent odors.

Within the genus Allium, Allium sativum is differentiated from other species by its distinct flavor profile and culinary uses.

What are the side effects of Allium sativum?

Allium sativum can have several negative health side effects, including inhibiting platelet function, which can increase the risk of bleeding.

Additionally, consuming large amounts of this plant can induce an allergy in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives and difficulty breathing. The plant's active compounds can also cause skin irritation, resulting in redness, itching, and inflammation in affected areas. Furthermore, the vapors released by Allium sativum can irritate the eyes, causing redness, tearing, and blurred vision.

Lastly, its consumption can interfere with blood clotting, which may lead to prolonged bleeding and bruising.
