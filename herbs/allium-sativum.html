Side Effects

Allium sativum side effects are primarily experienced by individuals who consume excessive amounts, particularly those with pre-existing health conditions such as bleeding disorders, diabetes, or kidney disease.

Common side effects include gastrointestinal issues like nausea, diarrhea, and stomach pain, as well as allergic reactions like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, taking high doses of Allium sativum may lead to more severe side effects, including dizziness, headaches, and a weakened immune system, particularly in individuals who are also taking blood-thinning medications.

To minimize the risk of these side effects, it is recommended to consume Allium sativum in moderation, ideally in amounts less than 1 gram per day, and to avoid taking it with other medications that may interact with its active compounds.

The possible side effects of Allium sativum are listed below.

  • Allergic reactions: Some people may be allergic to garlic and experience symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.
  • Digestive issues: Consuming large amounts of garlic can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals.
  • Interactions with medications: Garlic can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, which can lead to adverse effects.
  • Bad breath and body odor: Garlic contains sulfur compounds that can cause bad breath and body odor, which can be unpleasant for those around the person consuming it.
  • Skin irritation: Garlic can cause skin irritation, such as redness and itching, in some people, especially when applied topically.
  • Headaches and dizziness: Garlic can cause headaches and dizziness in some individuals, possibly due to its high allicin content.
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding concerns: Garlic may not be suitable for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as it can stimulate the uterus and potentially cause complications.
