Salvia Miltiorrhiza: Complete Medicinal Profile

Salvia miltiorrhiza, also known as Chinese sage or Danshen, is a perennial plant native to China, belonging to the Lamiaceae family and characterized by its red root and fragrant leaves.

This plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, valued for its medicinal properties, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardiovascular-protective effects.

The health benefits of Salvia miltiorrhiza include improved cardiovascular function, enhanced cognitive function, and potential anti-anxiety and anti-depressive effects, making it a popular herbal remedy for heart disease, stroke, and other cardiovascular conditions.

Salvia miltiorrhiza can be prepared in various herbal forms, including dried root, extract, capsule, and tea, making it easily accessible for those seeking its medicinal benefits.

Side Effects

Salvia miltiorrhiza side effects are numerous and can vary depending on the individual, but those most likely to experience these side effects are individuals with pre-existing heart conditions, women in their reproductive years, and individuals taking certain medications such as anticoagulants, beta blockers, and certain antidepressants.

The side effects of Salvia miltiorrhiza may include dizziness, palpitations, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and increased heart rate, as well as more severe side effects like seizures, coma, and even death in rare cases.

Additionally, some individuals may experience allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, while others may experience interactions with other herbal supplements or medications, leading to further complications.

Precautions should be taken to avoid consuming Salvia miltiorrhiza if you have a history of heart problems, are pregnant or breastfeeding, or are taking certain medications, as these individuals are more susceptible to experiencing severe side effects.

The possible side effects of Salvia miltiorrhiza are listed below.

  • Abdominal pain: Abdominal pain is a common side effect of salvia miltiorrhiza, which may be caused by its high tanshinone content.
  • Allergic reactions: Allergic reactions to salvia miltiorrhiza may include symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.
  • Bleeding risks: Salvia miltiorrhiza may increase the risk of bleeding in some individuals, particularly those taking anticoagulant medications.
  • Dizziness and lightheadedness: Dizziness and lightheadedness are common side effects of salvia miltiorrhiza, which may be caused by its vasodilatory properties.
  • Gastrointestinal disturbances: Gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, may occur as side effects of salvia miltiorrhiza.
  • Hypotension: Salvia miltiorrhiza may cause a drop in blood pressure, particularly in individuals taking medications that lower blood pressure.
  • Liver damage: Prolonged use of salvia miltiorrhiza may cause liver damage in some individuals, particularly those with pre-existing liver conditions.
