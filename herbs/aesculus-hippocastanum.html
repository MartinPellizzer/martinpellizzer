What is Aesculus hippocastanum?

Aesculus hippocastanum, commonly known as Horse Chestnut, is a deciduous tree classified within the family Sapindaceae, a family of flowering plants.

The genus Aesculus is comprised of 13 to 15 species of trees, all of which are native to the Northern Hemisphere.

How to identify this plant?

Aesculus hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut tree, can be identified by its large, palmate leaves with 5-7 pointed lobes and its showy, white flowers that appear in late spring.

The leaves are typically 10-30 centimeters in length and have a distinctive serrated edge, while the flowers are arranged in large, spherical clusters at the end of the branches.

The tree's brown, stony fruits, also known as conkers, are a distinctive feature and contain one or two large, glossy seeds.
