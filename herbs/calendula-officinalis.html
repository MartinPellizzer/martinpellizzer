Calendula Officinalis: Complete Medicinal Profile

Calendula officinalis, also known as pot marigold, is a flowering plant that belongs to the daisy family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant's flowers, leaves, and stalks contain anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular herbal remedy for various health issues.

Calendula officinalis has been traditionally used to treat wounds, skin conditions, such as eczema and acne, and to soothe digestive issues due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant can be prepared into herbal teas, salves, creams, and ointments, and its medicinal properties can also be extracted into oils and tinctures for topical or internal use.

Side Effects

Calendula officinalis side effects are typically mild and rare, but may include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and contact dermatitis in some individuals, especially those with sensitive skin.

People with allergies, eczema, or other skin conditions are more likely to experience these side effects, such as redness, itching, swelling, and blistering.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women may also be at a higher risk of experiencing side effects like nausea, headaches, and dizziness due to the potential estrogenic activity of calendula.

Additionally, individuals taking medications such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, or blood pressure medications should exercise caution when using calendula products, as they may interact with these medications and cause side effects like bruising, bleeding, or changes in blood sugar levels.

The possible side effects of Calendula officinalis are listed below.

  • Skin irritation: Calendula officinalis can cause skin irritation in some individuals, especially if used in high concentrations or without proper dilution.
  • Allergic reaction: Some people may be allergic to calendula, which can cause an allergic reaction, including symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.
  • Digestive issues: Ingesting calendula can cause digestive issues, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, in some individuals.
  • Interactions with medications: Calendula may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications, which can lead to adverse effects.
  • Eye irritation: Calendula extracts can cause eye irritation, including redness, itching, and tearing, if not handled properly.
  • Photosensitivity: Calendula may cause photosensitivity in some individuals, leading to skin reactions when exposed to sunlight.
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding concerns: There is limited research on the safety of calendula during pregnancy and breastfeeding, so it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using it.
