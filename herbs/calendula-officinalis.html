What are the side effects of Calendula officinalis?

Calendula officinalis can have several negative health side effects when consumed or applied topically, particularly for those with sensitive skin or allergies.

The plant can cause irritation, particularly in open wounds or broken skin, leading to discomfort and potentially infection. Additionally, calendula officinalis can trigger allergies in some individuals, manifesting as hives, itching, or swelling. Consuming calendula can also induce diarrhea due to its high saponin content, which may lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

Furthermore, topical application or ingestion of calendula can produce nausea and create bloating in some people, often due to its strong essential oils and compounds.
