Artemisia annua (sweet annie)

What is Artemisia annua?

Artemisia annua, commonly known as Sweet Annie, is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is classified within the genus Artemisia and is a member of the subfamily Anthemidinae.

At the species level, it is further categorized as Artemisia annua, with its unique characteristics and properties contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

What are the side effects of Artemisia annua?

Artemisia annua can have several negative health side effects, particularly when used inappropriately or in excessive quantities.

One of the most concerning side effects is liver damage, which can occur due to the high levels of artemisinin and other compounds in the plant. Additionally, long-term use of artemisinin can lead to the formation of hepatotoxicity, a condition where the liver becomes inflamed and damaged. Other potential side effects of Artemisia annua include hypokalemia, a condition characterized by low potassium levels in the blood, which can lead to muscle weakness and cardiac arrhythmias.

Furthermore, the plant can also cause photodermatitis, a severe skin reaction that occurs when the skin is exposed to sunlight after taking the plant, and in extreme cases, it can even lead to seizures.
