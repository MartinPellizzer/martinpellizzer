Cassia senna (cassia)

What is Cassia senna?

Cassia senna, commonly known as Cassia, is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Fabaceae.

It is classified under the subfamily Caesalpinioideae, and further divided into the genus Cassia, with the species name senna.

This classification places Cassia senna within the broader context of the pea family, alongside other legumes.

What are the active constituents of Cassia senna?

Cassia senna contains a diverse range of medicinal active constituents, each contributing to its therapeutic properties.

Anthraquinones, a key group of compounds, play a crucial role in the plant's laxative effects, promoting bowel movements and relieving constipation. Sennosides, a subgroup of anthraquinones, are responsible for the plant's purgative properties, stimulating the contraction of intestinal muscles and increasing water absorption. In addition to these anthraquinone derivatives, Cassia senna also contains tannins, which exhibit astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and protect against infections.

The plant's antioxidant profile is further enhanced by catechins and ellagic acid, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, respectively.
