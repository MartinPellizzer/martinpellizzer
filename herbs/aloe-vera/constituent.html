13 Aloe Vera Best Active Constituents

1. Aloin

Aloe vera aloin, a naturally occurring anthraquinone compound, is primarily responsible for its purgative effects, making it useful in treating constipation and other gastrointestinal issues. However, excessive consumption of aloin can lead to severe gastrointestinal side effects, including diarrhea and abdominal pain. Due to its potential toxicity, aloin is often removed from commercial aloe vera products, except in some topical applications.

2. Aloe-Emodin

Aloe vera aloe-emodin is a bioactive compound that has been found to possess anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties. Research has shown that aloe-emodin can inhibit the growth of cancer cells and induce apoptosis in various types of cancer, including colorectal and breast cancer. Additionally, aloe-emodin has been shown to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a potential candidate for the development of new therapeutic agents.

3. Anthraquinones

Aloe vera anthraquinones are a group of compounds found in aloe vera, known for their laxative properties due to their ability to stimulate bowel movements and increase water and electrolyte absorption. They have been used traditionally in medicine to treat constipation and other gastrointestinal disorders, although their use should be monitored due to potential side effects. The anthraquinones in aloe vera are generally considered safe when used in moderation and under medical supervision.

4. Flavonoids

Aloe vera flavonoids are a class of plant compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which contribute to their potential health benefits. These flavonoids have been shown to possess protective effects against cell damage and may help in preventing chronic diseases such as heart disease and cancer. Additionally, aloe vera flavonoids may also exhibit antimicrobial properties, which can aid in wound healing and skin health.

5. Hyaluronic Acid

Aloe vera hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring, non-sulfated glycosaminoglycan found in the gel of aloe vera plants. It plays a crucial role in skin hydration and elasticity by retaining moisture, thereby maintaining the skin's natural barrier function. Hyaluronic acid's ability to hold up to 1000 times its weight in water makes it an effective ingredient in skincare products for locking in moisture and reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

6. Tannins

Aloe vera tannins, also known as hydrolysable tannins, have been found to exhibit potent antioxidant properties. Research suggests that these tannins may help protect against cell damage and inflammation, making them a potential area of interest for future medicinal research. However, more studies are needed to fully understand the therapeutic effects of tannins isolated from aloe vera.

7. Barbaloin

Aloe vera barbaloin is a medicinal active constituent that has been found to have anti-inflammatory and laxative properties. It is a glycoside compound that is broken down into its active components, sparteine and aloe-emodin, when ingested. The laxative properties of barbaloin make it useful in the treatment of constipation.

8. Phenolic Acids

Aloe vera phenolic acids are a type of polyphenolic compound found in the gel of the aloe vera plant, and they have been reported to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. These compounds have been shown to have potential health benefits, including reducing oxidative stress, inflammation, and improving skin health. The phenolic acids in aloe vera, such as ferulic acid and vanillic acid, have been studied for their therapeutic effects and potential applications in the treatment of various diseases.

9. Furanocoumarins

Aloe vera furanocoumarins are a group of bioactive compounds found in the plant, particularly in its leaves. These compounds have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making them potentially useful in treating various health conditions. However, it is essential to note that the effectiveness and safety of furanocoumarins in medicinal applications require further research and investigation.

10. Glycoproteins

Aloe vera glycoproteins have been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can aid in wound healing and tissue repair. These glycoproteins can also help to regulate the immune system and prevent the occurrence of certain diseases. Research suggests that aloe vera glycoproteins may have potential therapeutic applications in the treatment of various conditions, including skin disorders and gastrointestinal issues.

11. Triterpenoids

Aloe vera triterpenoids are a group of bioactive compounds responsible for various medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds have been shown to have potential therapeutic applications in wound healing, skin care, and the treatment of certain diseases. Research on triterpenoids has led to the development of new pharmaceuticals and cosmetic products harnessing their benefits.

12. Inulin

Aloe vera inulin is a fructan polysaccharide with prebiotic properties that plays a significant role in the potential health benefits of aloe vera. It is known to stimulate the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, thereby enhancing gut health and potentially aiding in digestive issues. Additionally, inulin may also help to support the immune system by promoting the absorption of essential nutrients.

13. Mucopolysaccharides

Aloe vera mucopolysaccharides have been found to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe and calm irritated skin and mucous membranes. These mucopolysaccharides can also promote wound healing by stimulating the growth of new tissue and improving blood flow to the affected area. In addition, mucopolysaccharides from aloe vera may help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and other environmental stressors.
