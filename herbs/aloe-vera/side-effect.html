13 Most Common Side Effects of Aloe Vera

1. Exacerbates Liver Disease

Aloe vera exacerbates liver disease, particularly in individuals who already suffer from liver conditions such as cirrhosis or hepatitis. The plant's high levels of anthraquinones can cause liver damage and inflammation, leading to further liver disease complications. Those with pre-existing liver conditions should exercise caution when consuming aloe vera products or using aloe vera gel topically.

2. Causes Dizziness

Aloe vera causes dizziness due to its potential interaction with certain medications or its effect on blood pressure, which can lead to lightheadedness and dizziness in some individuals. This side effect is often associated with the oral consumption of aloe vera, rather than topical application. People who experience dizziness after consuming aloe vera should consult a healthcare professional for proper guidance and advice.

3. Disrupts Digestion

Aloe vera disrupts digestion by interfering with the body's natural digestive processes, potentially leading to uncomfortable symptoms and complications. The sap of the aloe vera plant contains compounds that can slow down digestion, causing issues such as bloating, gas, and abdominal pain. In some cases, ingesting aloe vera can also lead to more severe gastrointestinal problems, including diarrhea and stomach ulcers.

4. Affects Thyroid Function

Aloe vera affects thyroid function by interfering with the production of thyroid hormones, which can lead to issues such as fatigue, weight gain, and cold intolerance. Some studies suggest that the gel of the aloe vera plant contains compounds that may inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in thyroid hormone production. However, more research is needed to fully understand the relationship between aloe vera and thyroid function.

5. Increases Bleeding Risk

Aloe vera increases bleeding risk due to its high content of salicylic acid, which can thin the blood and prevent it from clotting properly. This can be a concern for individuals taking anticoagulant medications, as the combination may exacerbate the risk of bleeding. People who are prone to bleeding or have a history of bleeding disorders should consult their healthcare provider before using aloe vera products.

6. Affects Kidney Function

Aloe vera affects kidney function by potentially reducing the kidneys' ability to filter waste from the blood, which can lead to a buildup of toxins in the body. This side effect can be particularly concerning for individuals with pre-existing kidney problems or those who are taking medications that may further compromise kidney function. If not properly monitored, excessive consumption of aloe vera may exacerbate kidney issues and even lead to kidney damage or disease.

7. Aggravates Asthma

Aloe vera aggravates asthma, a condition that affects millions of people worldwide, and is characterized by recurring episodes of wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. The irritant properties present in aloe vera can trigger asthma attacks, leading to respiratory distress and discomfort. Individuals with asthma are advised to avoid direct contact with aloe vera or use it in moderation under medical supervision.

9. Triggers Allergic Reactions

Aloe vera triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of anthraquinones, which can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching. People who experience allergic reactions to aloe vera may develop symptoms such as hives, blistering, or swelling after exposure to the plant or its products. It is essential for those who plan to use aloe vera to perform a patch test and consult with a healthcare professional to determine their sensitivity level.

10. Triggers Headaches

Aloe vera triggers headaches in some individuals, possibly due to its high levels of anthraquinones, a group of compounds that can cause irritation and discomfort. These compounds can stimulate the nervous system, leading to the onset of a headache. Additionally, the sudden release of these compounds when consuming aloe vera products can also contribute to the development of headaches in sensitive individuals.

11. Triggers Anxiety

Aloe vera triggers anxiety in some individuals due to its compounds that may interact with the body's neurochemicals, causing feelings of unease or heightened stress. This side effect can be attributed to the plant's unique properties, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, which have been shown to affect the central nervous system in certain people. As a result, individuals with a history of anxiety disorders may want to exercise caution when using aloe vera products or consult with a healthcare professional before consumption.

12. Induces Nausea

Aloe vera induces nausea in some individuals, particularly those who are sensitive to its compounds or have underlying health conditions. The severity of nausea can range from mild discomfort to more severe symptoms, depending on the individual's tolerance and the method of consumption or application. In rare cases, nausea associated with aloe vera may be a sign of a more serious health issue, requiring medical attention.

13. Induces Skin Rash

Aloe vera induces skin rash in some individuals, particularly those with sensitive skin or allergies. This reaction can manifest as redness, itching, and swelling, and in severe cases, may lead to blisters or hives. People experiencing skin irritation after using aloe vera products should discontinue use and seek medical attention if symptoms persist or worsen.
