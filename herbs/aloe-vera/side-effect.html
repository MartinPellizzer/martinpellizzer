13 Most Common Side Effects of Aloe Vera

1. Experiences Diarrhea

Aloe vera experiences diarrhea in some individuals due to its laxative properties, which can cause the body to release excess water and electrolytes. This is often a result of the plant's high levels of anthraquinones, a type of compound that stimulates bowel movements. Those who consume aloe vera products should be aware of this potential side effect and start with small amounts to gauge their tolerance.

2. Causes Nausea

Aloe vera causes nausea in some individuals due to its potential toxicity. Consuming aloe vera can lead to an upset stomach and cause feelings of queasiness. This side effect is often more common in people who consume large quantities of aloe vera or have underlying health conditions.

3. Experiences Stomach Pain

Aloe vera experiences stomach pain in some individuals due to its high concentration of anthraquinones, which can be toxic in large amounts. Consuming aloe vera juice or eating aloe vera gel can cause stomach cramps, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal issues. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using aloe vera as a treatment for any health condition, especially if you have a history of stomach problems.

4. Causes Skin Irritation

Aloe vera causes skin irritation in some individuals, particularly those with sensitive skin, as it contains compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin that can cause redness, itching, and swelling. This reaction is often a result of the plant's sap being applied to broken skin or in large quantities. If you experience any adverse reactions after using aloe vera, discontinue use and consult a medical professional for further guidance.

5. Causes Allergic Reactions

Aloe vera causes allergic reactions in some individuals, which can manifest as skin irritation, itching, and redness. These reactions can range from mild to severe and may be triggered by direct contact with the plant or its extracts. In rare cases, an allergic reaction to aloe vera can lead to more serious health issues, such as anaphylaxis.

6. Induces Headaches

Aloe vera induces headaches in some individuals due to its potential sensitivity properties, which can cause irritation in the skin and scalp. This reaction can be exacerbated by overuse or prolonged exposure to the plant's gel or sap. As a result, people who experience headaches from aloe vera should use it in moderation or consult a healthcare professional for guidance.

7. Triggers Vomiting

Aloe vera triggers vomiting in some individuals due to its toxic compounds, particularly anthraquinones. These compounds can cause irritation in the digestive system and lead to nausea and vomiting. Consuming aloe vera gel in excessive amounts or for an extended period can exacerbate these symptoms.

8. Experiences Hair Loss

Aloe vera experiences hair loss due to the high concentration of aloin, a compound that can be toxic in large amounts. Prolonged use or excessive consumption of aloe vera products can lead to hair loss, particularly when applied directly to the scalp. In some cases, hair loss caused by aloe vera may be reversible, but it is essential to consult a healthcare professional for proper advice and treatment.

9. Develops Abdominal Cramps

Aloe vera develops abdominal cramps as a rare side effect due to its toxic compounds, which can cause discomfort and pain in some individuals. These abdominal cramps can range from mild to severe and may be accompanied by other symptoms such as diarrhea or nausea. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional if abdominal cramps persist or worsen after using aloe vera products.

10. Triggers Eye Irritation

Aloe vera triggers eye irritation, particularly when the gel is applied to the skin around the eyes or when it comes into contact with the eyes directly. This is due to the presence of anthraquinones, a compound found in aloe vera that can cause eye redness, itching, and discomfort. In some cases, eye irritation can be severe, and it is essential to rinse the eyes thoroughly with water if exposure to aloe vera occurs.

11. Triggers Asthma

Aloe vera triggers asthma in some individuals due to the presence of latex, a thick, white substance found in the plant's leaves. This latex contains compounds that can cause an allergic reaction and trigger asthma symptoms, such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. People with pre-existing asthma should exercise caution when handling or consuming aloe vera products to avoid potential respiratory complications.

12. Causes Dizziness

Aloe vera causes dizziness due to its active compound aloin, which can interact with certain medications and affect blood pressure. This interaction may lead to dizziness or lightheadedness, especially when ingesting aloe vera in large quantities. However, more research is needed to fully understand the relationship between aloe vera and dizziness.

13. Triggers Low Blood Sugar

Aloe vera triggers low blood sugar due to its effect on insulin levels and glucose metabolism. When ingested, compounds in the aloe vera plant may stimulate the pancreas to release excess insulin, leading to hypoglycemia. This condition can be particularly concerning for individuals with diabetes, as it may require immediate medical attention to prevent complications.
