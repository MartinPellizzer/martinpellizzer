9 Best Benefits Of Viburnum Opulus

Viburnum opulus, also known as the guelder rose or European cranberrybush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and Asia, known for its medicinal and ornamental properties.

The bioactive compounds present in Viburnum opulus, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, contribute to its health benefits.

These compounds help to reduce fever by inducing sweating and promoting the release of toxins, fight infections by boosting the immune system, and soothe inflammation by reducing oxidative stress and promoting anti-inflammatory responses.

By harnessing the power of Viburnum opulus, individuals can take advantage of its natural remedies to alleviate various health issues.

1. Reduces fever

Viburnum opulus reduces fever, a benefit characterized by the plant's ability to lower body temperature, alleviating discomfort and promoting recovery.

This benefit is crucial, as fever can lead to dehydration, fatigue, and potentially life-threatening complications, such as organ damage or sepsis, especially in vulnerable populations like the elderly, young children, and those with compromised immune systems.

The plant's ability to reduce fever is attributed to its bioactive constituents, including viburnin, scopoletin, and ferulic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties.

To harness this benefit, Viburnum opulus can be used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, or tinctures, typically in doses of 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves or 10-20 drops of tincture, three times a day, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
