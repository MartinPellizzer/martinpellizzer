identification

Ginseng Panax, also known as Asian ginseng or Chinese ginseng, can be identified by its distinctive forked roots and palmate leaves with 3-5 leaflets. It typically grows to be 1-2 feet tall, with a single stem that emerges from the base of the plant, and is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. The plant's stems, leaves, and roots are covered in short, fine hairs, and it often grows in shaded, woodland environments.

side-effects

Ginseng Panax, a popular herbal supplement, can cause side effects such as insomnia, restlessness, and digestive issues in some individuals. High doses of ginseng may also lead to headaches, dizziness, and rapid heartbeat due to its stimulatory properties. Additionally, people taking blood thinners or experiencing bleeding disorders should use ginseng with caution as it may interact with these medications and exacerbate bleeding risks.
