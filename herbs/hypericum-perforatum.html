identification

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is identified by its distinctive leaves, which are lance-shaped and have visible oil glands that give them a perforated appearance. The plant's yellow flowers, typically with five petals, bloom in clusters at the end of branches during the summer months. In addition to its flowers and leaves, St. John's Wort is often recognized by its square-shaped stems and red or black sap, which is present in the plant's stems and leaves.
