identification

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, can be identified by its fern-like foliage consisting of multiple, finely divided leaflets that grow from a central stem. The plant's white or occasionally yellow flowers are typically arranged in flat-topped clusters and bloom from mid-summer to early fall. A distinctive feature of yarrow is its milky sap, which can be found on the plant's stems and leaves, and is often used as a diagnostic characteristic in field identification.
