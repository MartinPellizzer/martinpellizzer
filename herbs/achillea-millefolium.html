Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, can be identified by its fern-like foliage and its clusters of small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in mid-to-late summer.

A distinctive feature of yarrow is its ability to produce a large number of stems from a single base, giving it a bushy or clumped appearance.
