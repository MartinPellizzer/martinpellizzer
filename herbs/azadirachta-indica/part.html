9 Azadirachta Indica Best Medicinal Parts

1. Leaf

Azadirachta indica leaf, also known as neem leaf, is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The leaf is used to treat various health issues, including skin conditions, wounds, and fever. It is also used in the treatment of digestive problems and as a natural remedy for dental care.

2. Seed

Azadirachta indica seed, also known as neem seed, contains nimbin and nimbidin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. The seed is used to treat various health conditions, such as fever, diarrhea, and skin infections. Additionally, the seed has been traditionally used to manage menstrual disorders and as a natural insect repellent.

3. Bark

Azadirachta indica bark has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The bark is rich in flavonoids, phenolic compounds, and other bioactive molecules that exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Extracts from the bark have been studied for their potential in treating conditions such as arthritis, wounds, and infections.

4. Fruit

Azadirachta indica fruit, also known as the neem fruit, contains a compound called nimbin, which has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions. The fruit is also a good source of vitamin C and other nutrients, making it a valuable component in Ayurvedic medicine. In some cultures, the fruit is used to treat digestive issues and as a natural remedy for fever and skin conditions.

5. Root

Azadirachta indica root, also known as neem root, is used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions. The root contains bioactive compounds such as limonoids and alkaloids, which are believed to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential in treating conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and cancer.

6. Flower

Azadirachta indica flower contains a number of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and saponins, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The flowers of this plant have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever and skin disorders. Research has also identified the potential of Azadirachta indica flower extracts in inhibiting the growth of certain pathogens and in promoting wound healing.
