7 Azadirachta Indica Best Medicinal Parts

1. Leaf

Azadirachta indica leaf, also known as neem leaf, has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, skin conditions, and digestive issues due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. The leaf is rich in compounds like nimbin and nimandial, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. The bioactive compounds present in the leaf are also believed to have antiseptic and antiviral properties, making it effective in treating infections and promoting overall health.

2. Bark

Azadirachta indica bark is used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The bark is rich in tannins and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is also used to treat digestive issues and as a purgative to aid in detoxification processes.

3. Seed

Azadirachta indica seed, also known as neem seed, is a rich source of nimbin and nimondin, compounds that exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various skin conditions and infections. The seed oil extracted from the neem seed is used in traditional medicine to treat fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal problems. Additionally, the seed's oil has been found to have antiseptic and antifungal properties, aiding in wound healing and preventing infections.

4. Neem Cake

Azadirachta indica neem cake, a byproduct of neem oil extraction, has been used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. The cake is rich in nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, making it an effective fertilizer and soil conditioner. Additionally, neem cake is also used as an insecticide and pesticide due to its ability to repel and kill pests, making it a valuable resource in sustainable agriculture.

5. Root

Azadirachta indica root is believed to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may help in treating various health conditions. It is also known to have antioxidant and immunomodulatory activities, contributing to its potential therapeutic benefits. Additionally, the root is thought to possess antihypertensive and cardiotonic properties, which may aid in managing cardiovascular health.

6. Fruit

Azadirachta indica fruit, also known as the neem fruit, has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues. The fruit pulp, seeds, and oil extracted from it possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which help in reducing fever, diarrhea, and respiratory problems. Additionally, the fruit's oil is used to treat skin conditions, such as eczema and acne, due to its antiseptic and antifungal properties.

7. Latex

Azadirachta indica latex has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections. The latex is rich in azadirachtin, a compound that exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is also used topically to treat wounds, ulcers, and other skin conditions due to its antiseptic and wound-healing properties.
