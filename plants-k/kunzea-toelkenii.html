Kunzea toelkenii

Kunzea toelkenii, commonly known as the Toelken's kunzea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates. It is often found in heathlands and is known for its resilience to salt spray and strong winds.

Kunzea toelkenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting local wildlife.
