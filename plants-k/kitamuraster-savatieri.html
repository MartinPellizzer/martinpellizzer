Kitamuraster savatieri

Kitamuraster savatieri, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit with smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
