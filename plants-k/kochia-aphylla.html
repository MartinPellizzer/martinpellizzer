Kochia aphylla

Kochia aphylla, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Kochia aphylla is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its nutritional value and hardiness.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
