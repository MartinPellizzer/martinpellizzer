Koniga maritima var. canariensis

Koniga maritima var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island heath, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments. It produces small, red or purple fruits that are dispersed by birds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
