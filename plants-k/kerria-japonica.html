Kerria japonica

Kerria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese kerria, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by ornamental red berries in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn red in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Kerria japonica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
