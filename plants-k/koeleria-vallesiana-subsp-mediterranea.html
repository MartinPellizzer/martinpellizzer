Koeleria vallesiana subsp. mediterranea

Koeleria vallesiana subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean grass or Mediterranean koeleria, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces seeds in early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
