Korthalsia furcata

Korthalsia furcata, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The flowers have five petals and a prominent, spurred corona, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, red aril.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
