Keteleeria fortunei subsp. cyclolepis

Keteleeria fortunei subsp.

cyclolepis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and conical shape, typical of many fir species.

The plant has a dense, pyramidal crown and can grow to significant heights in suitable conditions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
