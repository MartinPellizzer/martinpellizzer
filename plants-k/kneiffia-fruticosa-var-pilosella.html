Kneiffia fruticosa var. pilosella

Kneiffia fruticosa var. pilosella, commonly known as the silky shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
