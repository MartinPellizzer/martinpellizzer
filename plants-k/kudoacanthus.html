Kudoacanthus

Kudoacanthus, commonly known as the Japanese candleplant, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with tubular corollas that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Kudoacanthus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
