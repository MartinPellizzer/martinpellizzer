Kyllinga caespitosa subvar. debilis

Kyllinga caespitosa subvar. debilis, commonly known as slender swamp spikerush, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It typically forms dense, clump-like growths with slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to reddish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
