Karpatiosorbus franconica

Karpatiosorbus franconica, commonly known as the Franconian barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
