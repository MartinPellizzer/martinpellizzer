Koeleria macrantha subsp. mongolica

Koeleria macrantha subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This species is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
