Knautia purpurea var. meridionalis

Knautia purpurea var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern knautia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
