Kerria japonica f. aureovittata

Kerria japonica f. aureovittata, commonly known as the golden stripe kerria, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features attractive, narrow, evergreen leaves with a distinctive yellow stripe running down the central vein. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
