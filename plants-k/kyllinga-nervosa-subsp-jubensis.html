Kyllinga nervosa subsp. jubensis

Kyllinga nervosa subsp.

jubensis, commonly known as the jubilee kyllinga, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a distinctive veining pattern.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Kyllinga nervosa subsp.

jubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
