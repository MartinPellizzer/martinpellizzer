Koeleria cristata var. amurensis

Koeleria cristata var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, dense, feathery inflorescence that resembles a crown. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous or forested areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its delicate, upright appearance.
