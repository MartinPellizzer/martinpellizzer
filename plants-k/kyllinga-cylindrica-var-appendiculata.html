Kyllinga cylindrica var. appendiculata

Kyllinga cylindrica var. appendiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, spike-like flowers that are typically green or reddish, depending on the season. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
