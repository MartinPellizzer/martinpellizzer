Kohleria tigridia

Kohleria tigridia, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble small orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange.

Kohleria tigridia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
