Kalanchoe verticillata

Kalanchoe verticillata, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in small, translucent windows, which aid in light absorption. The plant produces vibrant clusters of flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a favored plant among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts alike.
