Kopsia mutelii var. spissa

Kopsia mutelii var. spissa, commonly known as the rough kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Kopsia mutelii var. spissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
