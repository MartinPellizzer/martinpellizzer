Kuhnistera candida var. occidentalis

Kuhnistera candida var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western white mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance plant for native gardens.
