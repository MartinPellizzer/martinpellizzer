Kochia scoparia var. songarica

Kochia scoparia var. songarica, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and dense, deltoid-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiky, bract-like seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its succulent leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
