Kengyilia pamirica

Kengyilia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Kengyilia pamirica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
