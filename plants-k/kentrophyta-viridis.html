Kentrophyta viridis

Kentrophyta viridis, commonly known as the Emerald Spire, is a unique flowering plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, emerald-green foliage and striking clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires partial shade to flourish. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture in its native habitat.

Kentrophyta viridis is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
