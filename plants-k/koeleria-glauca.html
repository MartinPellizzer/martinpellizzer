Koeleria glauca

Koeleria glauca, commonly known as blue grass or blue koeleria, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Koeleria glauca is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
