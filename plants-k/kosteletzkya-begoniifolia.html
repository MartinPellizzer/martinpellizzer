Kosteletzkya begoniifolia

Kosteletzkya begoniifolia, commonly known as the begoniifolia marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
