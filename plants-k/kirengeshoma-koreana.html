Kirengeshoma koreana

Kirengeshoma koreana, commonly known as the Korean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It features palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Kirengeshoma koreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
