Koelpinia rhagadioloides

Koelpinia rhagadioloides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Koelpinia rhagadioloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
