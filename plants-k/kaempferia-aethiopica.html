Kaempferia aethiopica

Kaempferia aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian ginger or African violet, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the ginger family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
