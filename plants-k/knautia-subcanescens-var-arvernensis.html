Knautia subcanescens var. arvernensis

Knautia subcanescens var. arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian knautia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
