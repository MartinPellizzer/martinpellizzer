Karpatiosorbus concavifolia

Karpatiosorbus concavifolia, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive concave base.

Karpatiosorbus concavifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
