Koelera laurifolia

Koelera laurifolia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Koelera laurifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and horticulture.
