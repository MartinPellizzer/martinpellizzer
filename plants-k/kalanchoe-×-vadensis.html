Kalanchoe × vadensis

Kalanchoe × vadensis, commonly known as the mother of millions, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to propagate through stem cuttings contributes to its widespread cultivation and resilience.
