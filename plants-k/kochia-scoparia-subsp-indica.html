Kochia scoparia subsp. indica

Kochia scoparia subsp.

indica, commonly known as Russian spinach or summer cress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with hairy, alternate leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by oblong, spiny seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and cultivated fields. Kochia scoparia subsp.

indica is known for its tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
