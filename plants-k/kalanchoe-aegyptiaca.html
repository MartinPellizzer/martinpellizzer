Kalanchoe aegyptiaca

Kalanchoe aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian liverwort, is a succulent plant native to Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially under stress. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in clusters during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Kalanchoe aegyptiaca is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
