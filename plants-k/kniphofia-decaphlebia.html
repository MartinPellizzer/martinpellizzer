Kniphofia decaphlebia

Kniphofia decaphlebia, commonly known as the flame grass, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright orange-red to yellow tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia decaphlebia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
