Knautia serajewensis

Knautia serajewensis, commonly known as the Serajevo knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
