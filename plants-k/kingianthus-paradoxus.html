Kingianthus paradoxus

Kingianthus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical kingia, is a unique member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle ornamental touch.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
