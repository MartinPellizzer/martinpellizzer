Kalanchoe gideonsmithii

Kalanchoe gideonsmithii, commonly known as the Smith's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe gideonsmithii is drought-tolerant and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
