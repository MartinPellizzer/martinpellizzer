Klasea suffruticulosa

Klasea suffruticulosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Klasea suffruticulosa thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
