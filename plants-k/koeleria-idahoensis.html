Koeleria idahoensis

Koeleria idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, dry habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Koeleria idahoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
