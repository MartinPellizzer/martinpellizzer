Keerlia mexicana

Keerlia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

Keerlia mexicana is known for its adaptability and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
