Koeleria macrantha subsp. glauca

Koeleria macrantha subsp.

glauca, commonly known as bluegrass or blue wildrye, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and slender, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a waxy coating that gives it a distinct appearance. Koeleria macrantha subsp.

glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
