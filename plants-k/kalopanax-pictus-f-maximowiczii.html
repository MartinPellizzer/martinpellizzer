Kalopanax pictus f. maximowiczii

Kalopanax pictus f. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Variegated Cow Parsnip, is a variant of the cow parsnip species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its compound leaves are palmately arranged, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels.

The botanical features of this plant make it an attractive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
