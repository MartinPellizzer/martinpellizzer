Knautia collina var. grenieri

Knautia collina var. grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's mountain knautia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
