Kniphofia vandeweghei

Kniphofia vandeweghei, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant floral display makes it a standout in both garden landscapes and botanical collections.
