Kochia sieversiana

Kochia sieversiana, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red foliage that turns bright orange in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense panicles, which appear in late summer. Kochia sieversiana is a short-lived perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
