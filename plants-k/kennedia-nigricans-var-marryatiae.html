Kennedia nigricans var. marryatiae

Kennedia nigricans var. marryatiae, commonly known as the Mount Cook Kennedia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with slender, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
