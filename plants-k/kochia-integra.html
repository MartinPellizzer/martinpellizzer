Kochia integra

Kochia integra, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Kochia integra is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it useful in erosion control and as an ornamental plant.
