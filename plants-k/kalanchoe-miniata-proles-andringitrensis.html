Kalanchoe miniata proles andringitrensis

Kalanchoe miniata proles andringitrensis, commonly known as the Christmas chrysanthemum, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the winter months. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
