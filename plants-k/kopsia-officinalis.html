Kopsia officinalis

Kopsia officinalis, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Kopsia officinalis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
