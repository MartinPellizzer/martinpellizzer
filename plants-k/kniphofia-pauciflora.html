Kniphofia pauciflora

Kniphofia pauciflora, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of fiery red or orange tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Kniphofia pauciflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its striking appearance and long flowering period make it a valuable addition to both garden and conservation efforts.
