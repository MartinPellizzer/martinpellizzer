Kielmeyera appariciana

Kielmeyera appariciana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for musical instruments and furniture due to its acoustic properties. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries.

Kielmeyera appariciana is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
