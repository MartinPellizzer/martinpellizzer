Koeleria cristata subsp. transiliensis

Koeleria cristata subsp.

transiliensis, commonly known as the Transilian tufted grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is known for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
