Karpatiosorbus gayeriana

Karpatiosorbus gayeriana, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a species of shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
