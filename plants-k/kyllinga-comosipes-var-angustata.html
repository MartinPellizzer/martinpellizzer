Kyllinga comosipes var. angustata

Kyllinga comosipes var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf spikerush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its small, three-ranked spikelets contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
