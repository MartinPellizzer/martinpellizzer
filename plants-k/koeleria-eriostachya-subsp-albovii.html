Koeleria eriostachya subsp. albovii

Koeleria eriostachya subsp.

albovii, commonly known as the white-stamen koeleria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America. It is a perennial grass that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to white flowers in panicle clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer. Koeleria eriostachya subsp.

albovii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
