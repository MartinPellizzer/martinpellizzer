Knautia purpurea var. ceretanica

Knautia purpurea var. ceretanica, commonly known as the purple knautia, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
