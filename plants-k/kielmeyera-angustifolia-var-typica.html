Kielmeyera angustifolia var. typica

Kielmeyera angustifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed kielmeyera, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forest understories.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential in reforestation efforts.
