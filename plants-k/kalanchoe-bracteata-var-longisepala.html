Kalanchoe bracteata var. longisepala

Kalanchoe bracteata var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences, and the leaves have a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
