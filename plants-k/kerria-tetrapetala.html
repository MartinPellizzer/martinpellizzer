Kerria tetrapetala

Kerria tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled kerria, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Kerria tetrapetala is also valued for its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
