Krigia caroliniana var. leptophylla

Krigia caroliniana var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it is classified within the family Polygonaceae, known as the buckwheat family.
