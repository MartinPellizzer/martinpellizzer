Knautia arvensis var. involucrata

Knautia arvensis var. involucrata, commonly known as the field knautia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are usually pink to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
