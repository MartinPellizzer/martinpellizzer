Kaempferia procumbens

Kaempferia procumbens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 cm, with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing multiple small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

Kaempferia procumbens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
