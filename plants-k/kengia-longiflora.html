Kengia longiflora

Kengia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered kengia, is a striking shrub native to eastern Africa.

It features vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, grayish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue.

Kengia longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
