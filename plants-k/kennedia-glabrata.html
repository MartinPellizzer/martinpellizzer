Kennedia glabrata

Kennedia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth Kennedia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators. It is typically found in sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in coastal regions.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
