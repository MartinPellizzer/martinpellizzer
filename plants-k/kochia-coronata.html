Kochia coronata

Kochia coronata, commonly known as the crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has feathery, green leaves that are lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and appear in dense, branched clusters at the top of the stems.

Kochia coronata is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, arid environments.
