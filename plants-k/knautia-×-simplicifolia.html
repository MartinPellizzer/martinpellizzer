Knautia × simplicifolia

Knautia × simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved knautia, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
