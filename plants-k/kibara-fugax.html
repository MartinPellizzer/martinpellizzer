Kibara fugax

Kibara fugax, commonly known as the fleeting kibara, is a rare and ephemeral flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom briefly during the rainy season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense rainforests. Due to its short flowering period and limited distribution, it is considered a challenging subject for botanists and horticulturists.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique species from habitat loss and environmental degradation.
