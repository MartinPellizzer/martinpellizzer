Krebsia striata

Krebsia striata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed false nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Krebsia striata is known for its ability to thrive in wetlands and along stream banks.
