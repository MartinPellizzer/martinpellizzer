Kyllinga erecta var. lurida

Kyllinga erecta var. lurida, commonly known as the purple spikeflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy purple to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.
