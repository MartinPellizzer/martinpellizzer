Kleinia papillaris

Kleinia papillaris, commonly known as the woolly-knife plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are fleshy and often have a bluish-gray hue, providing a striking visual contrast.

Kleinia papillaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
