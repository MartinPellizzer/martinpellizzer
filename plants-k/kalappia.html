Kalappia

Kalappia, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a cluster of butterflies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers that are enclosed within a spathe. Kalappia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and unique appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
