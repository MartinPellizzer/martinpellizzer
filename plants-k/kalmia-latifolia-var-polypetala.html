Kalmia latifolia var. polypetala

Kalmia latifolia var. polypetala, commonly known as the polypetalous mountain laurel, is a variant of the mountain laurel native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces large, showy white or pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the flowers.

The plant is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental species in woodland gardens.
