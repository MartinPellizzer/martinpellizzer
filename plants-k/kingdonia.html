Kingdonia

Kingdonia, commonly known as the king's pine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Podostemaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring small, bright green leaves and small, clustered flowers. These plants are typically found in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as along the edges of slow-moving streams and rivers. Kingdonia species are adapted to nutrient-poor substrates and often grow in shaded, humid conditions.

Their reproductive structures are specialized for dispersal by water, which is a key aspect of their botanical significance.
