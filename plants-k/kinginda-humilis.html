Kinginda humilis

Kinginda humilis, commonly known as the dwarf kinginda, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
