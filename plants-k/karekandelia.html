Karekandelia

Karekandelia, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has a compact, shrub-like appearance. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Karekandelia is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
