Kunstleria stipularis

Kunstleria stipularis, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive fan-shaped leaves, which are composed of numerous radiating leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The plant is well adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
