Kigelia elliottii

Kigelia elliottii, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces woody, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp. Kigelia elliottii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.
