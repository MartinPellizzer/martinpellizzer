Kochia hyssopifolia var. caspica

Kochia hyssopifolia var. caspica, commonly known as the Caspian kochia, is a shrub-like plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright red or purple bracts that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
