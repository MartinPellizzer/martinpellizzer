Koeleria cristata var. poiformis

Koeleria cristata var. poiformis, commonly known as the hooked wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, flattened inflorescence that resembles a crown. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green sheen, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
