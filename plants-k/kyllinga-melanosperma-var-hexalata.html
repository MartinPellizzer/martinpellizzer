Kyllinga melanosperma var. hexalata

Kyllinga melanosperma var. hexalata, commonly known as blackseed kyllinga, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers. Its most notable botanical characteristic is the presence of dark, spherical seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red pericarp.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
