Knema patentinervia

Knema patentinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Knema patentinervia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
