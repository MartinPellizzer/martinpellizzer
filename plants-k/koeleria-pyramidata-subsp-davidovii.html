Koeleria pyramidata subsp. davidovii

Koeleria pyramidata subsp.

davidovii, commonly known as the Davidovii sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
