Kochia hyssopifolia

Kochia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and vibrant red or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in arid environments. Its ornamental value is notable, especially in landscapes where drought tolerance and low maintenance are desired.

Kochia hyssopifolia is also used in some regions for erosion control due to its robust root system.
