Kunzea micrantha subsp. oligandra

Kunzea micrantha subsp.

oligandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It is a hardy species that can tolerate salt spray and is often found in maritime climates.

The subspecies is notable for its aromatic foliage, which is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
