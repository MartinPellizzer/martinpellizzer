Kalanchoe grandidieri var. itampolensis

Kalanchoe grandidieri var. itampolensis, commonly known as the Tampolena Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
