Kalimeris incisa subsp. macrodon

Kalimeris incisa subsp.

macrodon, commonly known as the large-fruited chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically lobed with a serrated margin.

The plant produces fluffy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
