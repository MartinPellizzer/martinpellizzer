Kalanchoe integra var. subsessilis

Kalanchoe integra var. subsessilis, commonly known as the compact kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it easy to cultivate and maintain.
