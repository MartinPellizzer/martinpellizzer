Koeleria krylovii

Koeleria krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is adapted to survive in arid environments.

Koeleria krylovii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
