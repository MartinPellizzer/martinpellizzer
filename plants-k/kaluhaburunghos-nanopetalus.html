Kaluhaburunghos nanopetalus

Kaluhaburunghos nanopetalus, commonly known as the Moonlight Petal Fern, is a unique species native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that shimmer with a silvery sheen under sunlight. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, adding to its ethereal appearance. Its leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it a graceful, flowing look.

Due to its rarity and distinctive beauty, it is highly valued in horticultural circles and requires specific humid conditions to thrive.
