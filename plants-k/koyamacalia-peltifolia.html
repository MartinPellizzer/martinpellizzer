Koyamacalia peltifolia

Koyamacalia peltifolia, commonly known as the leafy koyama, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its cool, humid habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are arranged in a peltate pattern, meaning they are attached at the center of the petiole, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, Koyamacalia peltifolia is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
