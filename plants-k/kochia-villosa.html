Kochia villosa

Kochia villosa, commonly known as Russian spinach, is an annual flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its erect, bristly stems and fuzzy, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, lantern-shaped seed heads, which give it a distinctive appearance. Kochia villosa is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it both a weed and a plant of interest in ecological studies.
