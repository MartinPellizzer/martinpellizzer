Koeleria phleoides var. amblyantha

Koeleria phleoides var. amblyantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf wild ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
