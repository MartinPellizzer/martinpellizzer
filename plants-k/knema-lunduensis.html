Knema lunduensis

Knema lunduensis, commonly known as the Bornean knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Knema lunduensis is typically found in lowland and hill forest habitats, often growing in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the tropical rainforest ecosystem.
