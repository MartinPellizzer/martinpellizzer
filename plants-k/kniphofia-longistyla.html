Kniphofia longistyla

Kniphofia longistyla, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking member of the Asphodelaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear showy, red-orange to yellow-orange tubular flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia longistyla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
