Khaya kerstingii

Khaya kerstingii, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces oval, woody pods that contain edible seeds used for oil extraction. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Khaya kerstingii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
