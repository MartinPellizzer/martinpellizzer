Kalabotis clusianum

Kalabotis clusianum, commonly known as the climbing shrub or forest vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
