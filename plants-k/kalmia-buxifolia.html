Kalmia buxifolia

Kalmia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf alpine, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Kalmia buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its evergreen foliage and delicate floral display.
