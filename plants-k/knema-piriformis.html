Knema piriformis

Knema piriformis, commonly known as the round-leaf knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by distinctive round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves and bark contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Knema piriformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
