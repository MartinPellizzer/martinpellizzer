Knautia byzantina var. hellenica

Knautia byzantina var. hellenica, commonly known as the Greek knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of pink to purple, fluffy, dandelion-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in gardens.
