Keniochloa chionogeiton

Keniochloa chionogeiton, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to white spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
