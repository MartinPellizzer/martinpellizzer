Kinugasa japonica var. tomentosa

Kinugasa japonica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The variety 'tomentosa' is noted for its dense, woolly foliage, which adds texture and visual interest to the landscape.
