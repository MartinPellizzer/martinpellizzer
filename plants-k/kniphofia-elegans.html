Kniphofia elegans

Kniphofia elegans, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vibrant red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
