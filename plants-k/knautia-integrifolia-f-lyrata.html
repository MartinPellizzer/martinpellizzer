Knautia integrifolia f. lyrata

Knautia integrifolia f. lyrata, commonly known as the lyrata knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
