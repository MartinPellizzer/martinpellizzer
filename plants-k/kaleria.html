Kaleria

Kaleria, commonly known as the "Indian cabbages," is a genus of flowering plants in the Brassicaceae family.

These plants are native to the Himalayas and are characterized by their rosette-forming leaves and showy, cruciform flowers. Kaleria species typically grow in temperate climates and are often found in mountainous regions. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated for their edible leaves.

The genus includes several species, such as Kaleria glabra and Kaleria corymbosa, which are known for their hardy nature and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
