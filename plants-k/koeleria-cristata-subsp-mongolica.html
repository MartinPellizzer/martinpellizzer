Koeleria cristata subsp. mongolica

Koeleria cristata subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize soil in arid environments.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in preventing soil erosion.
