Kosteletzkya asterocarpa

Kosteletzkya asterocarpa, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy yellow flowers with five lobes, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
