Koanophyllon reversum

Koanophyllon reversum, commonly known as the reverseleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular florets. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are inversely oriented, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Koanophyllon reversum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
