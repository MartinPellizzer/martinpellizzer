Karwinskia calderonii

Karwinskia calderonii, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
