Kniphofia × praecox

Kniphofia × praecox, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a hybrid plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, spike-like flower clusters that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
