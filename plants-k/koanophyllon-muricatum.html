Koanophyllon muricatum

Koanophyllon muricatum, commonly known as the Andean mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often appearing in clusters. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which are adaptations to its harsh, arid environment. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in the Andes Mountains.

Koanophyllon muricatum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in local communities.
