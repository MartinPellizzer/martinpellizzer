Kalanchoe glandulosa

Kalanchoe glandulosa, commonly known as the wax plant, is a succulent native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a waxy, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in winter. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
