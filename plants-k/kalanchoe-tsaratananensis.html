Kalanchoe tsaratananensis

Kalanchoe tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana Kalanchoe, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though scientific validation of these uses is limited.

Kalanchoe tsaratananensis is also noted for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it relatively easy to cultivate.
