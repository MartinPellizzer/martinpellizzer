Knema conferta var. tonkinensis

Knema conferta var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin knema, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, reddish-brown berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood and has some traditional medicinal uses.
