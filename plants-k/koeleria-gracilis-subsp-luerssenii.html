Koeleria gracilis subsp. luerssenii

Koeleria gracilis subsp.

luerssenii, commonly known as the slender koeleria, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia. It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and tundra habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
