Kalanchoe longifolia

Kalanchoe longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Kalanchoe longifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its resilience and striking appearance.
