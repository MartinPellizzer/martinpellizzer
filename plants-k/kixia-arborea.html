Kixia arborea

Kixia arborea, commonly known as the tree of life, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and turn yellow in the fall. Kixia arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
