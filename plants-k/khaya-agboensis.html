Khaya agboensis

Khaya agboensis, commonly known as African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its straight-grained, durable wood that is highly valued for furniture and construction. The plant has compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, typically ranging from 5 to 9. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing flattened, brown seeds.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 30 meters in height.
