Koeleria advena

Koeleria advena, commonly known as the common koeleria, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Koeleria advena is noted for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
