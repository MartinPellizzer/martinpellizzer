Kochia parodii var. densa

Kochia parodii var. densa, commonly known as the Russian spinach or summer cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring erect, branched stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by inflated, spherical seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant often found in disturbed areas.

Its seeds are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in salads or as a seasoning.
