Kaempferia crawfurdia

Kaempferia crawfurdia, commonly known as the red ginger or flame ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herb with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive ginger-like scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Kaempferia crawfurdia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
