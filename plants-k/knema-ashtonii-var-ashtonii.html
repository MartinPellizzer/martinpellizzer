Knema ashtonii var. ashtonii

Knema ashtonii var. ashtonii, commonly known as the Ashton's knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
