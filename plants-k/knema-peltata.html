Knema peltata

Knema peltata, commonly known as the broad-leafed knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
