Kalanchoe rotundifolia

Kalanchoe rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are round or oval in shape, often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in winter. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Kalanchoe rotundifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.
