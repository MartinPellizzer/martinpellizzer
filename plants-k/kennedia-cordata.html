Kennedia cordata

Kennedia cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Kennedia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Kennedia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
