Kadenicarpus

Kadenicarpus, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Kadeniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in specialized leaflets called indusia.

Kadenicarpus is primarily of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and rare ecological niche.
