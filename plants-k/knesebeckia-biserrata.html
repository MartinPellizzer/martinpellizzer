Knesebeckia biserrata

Knesebeckia biserrata, commonly known as the serrated knesebeckia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms, it is appreciated by botanists and gardeners alike.
