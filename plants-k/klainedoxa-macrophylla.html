Klainedoxa macrophylla

Klainedoxa macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds. Its showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths.

Klainedoxa macrophylla plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
