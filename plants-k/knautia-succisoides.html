Knautia succisoides

Knautia succisoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, sparsely branched stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, forming small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Knautia succisoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.
