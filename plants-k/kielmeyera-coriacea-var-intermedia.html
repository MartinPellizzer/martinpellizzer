Kielmeyera coriacea var. intermedia

Kielmeyera coriacea var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
