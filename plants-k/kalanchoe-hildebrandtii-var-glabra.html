Kalanchoe hildebrandtii var. glabra

Kalanchoe hildebrandtii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its drought tolerance and ability to store water in its leaves make it well-suited for arid environments.
