Khaya grandifoliola

Khaya grandifoliola, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds that are used to make shea butter. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their various therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture making.
