Kalanchoe marmorata f. somaliensis

Kalanchoe marmorata f. somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali marble plant, is a compact, evergreen succulent native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with distinctive white or pale green marbled patterns. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
