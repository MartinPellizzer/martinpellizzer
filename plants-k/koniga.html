Koniga

Koniga, commonly known as the "golden flower" or *Hibiscus tiliaceus*, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a flowering shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red center. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for marine life.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in some cultures.
