Keysseria erici

Keysseria erici, commonly known as the ericaceous keysseria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, often in shades of white or pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in its native range.
