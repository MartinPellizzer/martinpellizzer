Knema tridactyla subsp. sublaevis

Knema tridactyla subsp.

sublaevis, commonly known as the white knema, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
