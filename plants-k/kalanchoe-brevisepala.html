Kalanchoe brevisepala

Kalanchoe brevisepala, commonly known as the short-stalked Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and vibrant floral display.

Kalanchoe brevisepala is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets and leaf cuttings.
