Kosteletzkya virginica f. alba

Kosteletzkya virginica f. alba, commonly known as the white marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or swampy areas, often forming dense colonies. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals. Its botanical structure includes a hairy stem and a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils.
