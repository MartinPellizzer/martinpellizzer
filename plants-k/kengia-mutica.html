Kengia mutica

Kengia mutica, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has a short trunk with a thick, flaky bark and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its foliage consists of odd-pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Kengia mutica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 10 meters tall in suitable conditions.
