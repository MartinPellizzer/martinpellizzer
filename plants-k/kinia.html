Kinia

Kinia, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, often pink or red flowers that resemble butterflies. Kinia species are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
