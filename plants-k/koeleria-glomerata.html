Koeleria glomerata

Koeleria glomerata, commonly known as blue grama, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and steppe regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. It is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance.

Koeleria glomerata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
