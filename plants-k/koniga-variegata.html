Koniga variegata

Koniga variegata, commonly known as the variegated koniga, is a unique plant characterized by its striking leaf patterns.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow variegation. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unusual foliage.
