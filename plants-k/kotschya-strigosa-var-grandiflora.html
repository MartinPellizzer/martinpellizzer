Kotschya strigosa var. grandiflora

Kotschya strigosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered kotschya, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
