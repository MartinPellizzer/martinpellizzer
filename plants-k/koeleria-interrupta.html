Koeleria interrupta

Koeleria interrupta, commonly known as the Indian ricegrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Koeleria interrupta is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.
