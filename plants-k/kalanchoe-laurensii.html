Kalanchoe laurensii

Kalanchoe laurensii, commonly known as the silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in winter. It is well adapted to grow in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Kalanchoe laurensii is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets and its resilience to drought conditions.
