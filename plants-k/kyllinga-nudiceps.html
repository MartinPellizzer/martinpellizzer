Kyllinga nudiceps

Kyllinga nudiceps, commonly known as the naked-stemmed spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, unbranched stems that are often leafless, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually terminal and produce small, pale green to reddish flowers.

Kyllinga nudiceps is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, grass-like appearance.
