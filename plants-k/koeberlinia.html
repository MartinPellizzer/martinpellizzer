Koeberlinia

Koeberlinia, commonly known as the silky oak, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a feathery appearance. The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense, globular clusters. The plant is native to Australia and thrives in warm, temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
