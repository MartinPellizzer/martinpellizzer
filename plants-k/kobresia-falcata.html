Kobresia falcata

Kobresia falcata, commonly known as the bentleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and low moisture levels. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curved or folded, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Kobresia falcata plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
