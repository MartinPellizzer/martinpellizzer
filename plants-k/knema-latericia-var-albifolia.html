Knema latericia var. albifolia

Knema latericia var. albifolia, commonly known as the white-leaf knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, narrow leaves that are typically white or pale green on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
