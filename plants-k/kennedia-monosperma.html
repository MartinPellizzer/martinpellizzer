Kennedia monosperma

Kennedia monosperma, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Kennedia monosperma is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.
