Kayea pacifica

Kayea pacifica, commonly known as the Hawaiian whitebeam, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries.

Kayea pacifica is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
