Knema tonkinensis

Knema tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

Knema tonkinensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
