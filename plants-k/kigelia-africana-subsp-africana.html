Kigelia africana subsp. africana

Kigelia africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its long, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruit that can grow up to 1 meter in length.

The plant has smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a strong scent, and it produces a milky sap that is toxic.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in various African communities.
