Kniphofia triangularis subsp. triangularis

Kniphofia triangularis subsp.

triangularis, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear vivid red or orange tubular flowers, often with a contrasting yellow or orange tip.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a fan shape, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
