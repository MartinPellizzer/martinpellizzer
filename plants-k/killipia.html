Killipia

Killipia, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plants typically produce showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Killipia species are often found in rocky or sandy soils and are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
