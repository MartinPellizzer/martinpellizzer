Krascheninnikovia intramongolica

Krascheninnikovia intramongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. It plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Its ornamental value has also made it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
