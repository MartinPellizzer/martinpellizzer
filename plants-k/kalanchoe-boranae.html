Kalanchoe boranae

Kalanchoe boranae, commonly known as the Ethiopian kalanhoe, is a succulent plant native to Ethiopia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the dry season. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth pattern.

Kalanchoe boranae is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
