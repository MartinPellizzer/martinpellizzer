Koeleria cristata subsp. helvetica

Koeleria cristata subsp.

helvetica, commonly known as the Alpine hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, bristle-like flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage is typically silver-green, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in harsh mountainous conditions.
