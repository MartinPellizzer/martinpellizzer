Koanophyllon plicata

Koanophyllon plicata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Koanophyllon plicata produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
