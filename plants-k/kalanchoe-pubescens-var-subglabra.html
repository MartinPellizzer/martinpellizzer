Kalanchoe pubescens var. subglabra

Kalanchoe pubescens var. subglabra, commonly known as the silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is known for its ability to store water, making it highly drought-tolerant. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and summer months.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and aesthetic appeal.
