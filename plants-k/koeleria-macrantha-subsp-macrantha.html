Koeleria macrantha subsp. macrantha

Koeleria macrantha subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spike-like inflorescence that bears small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
