Koeleria cristata subsp. albescens

Koeleria cristata subsp.

albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and arid soils.
