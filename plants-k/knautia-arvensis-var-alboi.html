Knautia arvensis var. alboi

Knautia arvensis var. alboi, commonly known as the white knautia, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, fluffy flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like blooms.
