Kardomia silvestris

Kardomia silvestris, commonly known as the wild kardomia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in its native habitat.
