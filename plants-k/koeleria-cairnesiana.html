Koeleria cairnesiana

Koeleria cairnesiana, commonly known as the mountain grass or Cairn's grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences are characteristic features.

Koeleria cairnesiana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
