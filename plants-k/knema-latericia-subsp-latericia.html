Knema latericia subsp. latericia

Knema latericia subsp.

latericia, commonly known as the white knema, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
