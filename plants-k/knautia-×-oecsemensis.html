Knautia × oecsemensis

Knautia × oecsemensis, commonly known as the hybrid meadow knautia, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Knautia arvensis and Knautia montana, resulting in a plant with distinctive, fluffy, pink to purple flower heads. The species is native to Central Europe and thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
