Kyllinga cyperoides

Kyllinga cyperoides, commonly known as the common spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, clustered spikelets at the top.

Kyllinga cyperoides typically grows in moist soils, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
