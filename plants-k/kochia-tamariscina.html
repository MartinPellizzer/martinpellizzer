Kochia tamariscina

Kochia tamariscina, commonly known as burning bush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its vibrant red foliage that turns bright orange in the fall. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in dense clusters and are followed by small, dry fruits.

Kochia tamariscina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking seasonal color changes.
