Kummerowia striata

Kummerowia striata, commonly known as the Chinese broom or Japanese broom, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Kummerowia striata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
