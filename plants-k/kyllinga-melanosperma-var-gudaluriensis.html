Kyllinga melanosperma var. gudaluriensis

Kyllinga melanosperma var. gudaluriensis, commonly known as the Gudalur kyllinga, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces distinctive black, spherical berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
