Koeleria albovii var. fominii

Koeleria albovii var. fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It features fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
