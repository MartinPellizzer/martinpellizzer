Kennedia carinata var. pedunculosa

Kennedia carinata var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that produce clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens and landscaping across its native range.
