Kirchnera arenaria

Kirchnera arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial species that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Kirchnera arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
