Karpatiosorbus ratisbonensis

Karpatiosorbus ratisbonensis, commonly known as the Ratisbon hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The Ratisbon hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
