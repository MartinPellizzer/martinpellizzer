Kundmannia sicula var. involucrata

Kundmannia sicula var. involucrata, commonly known as the Sicilian thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry environments.
