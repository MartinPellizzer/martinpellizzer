Kolobopetalum salmonicolor

Kolobopetalum salmonicolor, commonly known as the pink-veined flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, salmon-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Its flowering season typically occurs during the warmer months, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical gardens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
