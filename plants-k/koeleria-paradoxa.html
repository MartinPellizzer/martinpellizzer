Koeleria paradoxa

Koeleria paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. Koeleria paradoxa is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
