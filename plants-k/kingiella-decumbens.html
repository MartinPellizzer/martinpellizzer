Kingiella decumbens

Kingiella decumbens, commonly known as the New Zealand rock daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features wiry, decumbent stems that lie close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
