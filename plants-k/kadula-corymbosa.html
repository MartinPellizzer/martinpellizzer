Kadula corymbosa

Kadula corymbosa, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the cooler months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high elevation.

Kadula corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
