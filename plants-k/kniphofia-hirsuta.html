Kniphofia hirsuta

Kniphofia hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly torchflower, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem that bears clusters of bright orange-red to yellow tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine, hairy coating, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
