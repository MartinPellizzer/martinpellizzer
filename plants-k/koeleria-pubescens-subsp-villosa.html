Koeleria pubescens subsp. villosa

Koeleria pubescens subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and hairy leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

Its fibrous roots make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
