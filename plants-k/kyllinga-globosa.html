Kyllinga globosa

Kyllinga globosa, commonly known as the globe amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Kyllinga globosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
