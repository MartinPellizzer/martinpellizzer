Kielmeyera rubriflora

Kielmeyera rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered kielmeyera, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Kielmeyera rubriflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and forest structure.
