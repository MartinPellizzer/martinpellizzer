Kleinia talinoides subsp. cylindricus

Kleinia talinoides subsp.

cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical Kleinia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, adding visual interest. Its leaves are usually small and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
