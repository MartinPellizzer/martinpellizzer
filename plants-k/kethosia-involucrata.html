Kethosia involucrata

Kethosia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf kethosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight reddish tinge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist lowland forests.

Kethosia involucrata is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
