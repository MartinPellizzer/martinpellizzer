Keteleeria longibracteata

Keteleeria longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract fir, is a coniferous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the shoots. Its cones are typically pendulous and contain winged seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
