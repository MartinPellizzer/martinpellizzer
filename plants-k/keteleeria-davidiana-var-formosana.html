Keteleeria davidiana var. formosana

Keteleeria davidiana var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan fir, is a variant of the David fir native to Taiwan.

It is a coniferous tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a distinct morphology with a tall, slender trunk and a conical crown. Botanically, it belongs to the family Pinaceae and is valued for its ecological role in mountainous forests.

Its leaves are typically dark green and arranged spirally along the branches, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
