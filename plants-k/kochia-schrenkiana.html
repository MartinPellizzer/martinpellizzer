Kochia schrenkiana

Kochia schrenkiana, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that display brilliant red, orange, or yellow hues in the fall. The plant is an annual or biennial species, often found in disturbed soils and arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Kochia schrenkiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
