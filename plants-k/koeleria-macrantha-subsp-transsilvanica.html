Koeleria macrantha subsp. transsilvanica

Koeleria macrantha subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian wild oat, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.
