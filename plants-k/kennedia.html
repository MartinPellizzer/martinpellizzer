Kennedia

Kennedia, commonly known as the "bush morning glory," is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often bright red or orange flowers that resemble those of the morning glory family. Kennedia species are typically shrubs or small trees, with compound leaves and woody stems. They thrive in well-drained soils and are often found in coastal or sandy environments.

The genus includes several species, such as Kennedia integrifolia, which is popular for its vibrant floral displays.
