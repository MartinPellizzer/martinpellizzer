Koeleria pyramidata subsp. montana

Koeleria pyramidata subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain koeleria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.
