Kneiffia fruticosa

Kneiffia fruticosa, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Kneiffia fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
