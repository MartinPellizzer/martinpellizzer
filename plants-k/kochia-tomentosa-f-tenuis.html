Kochia tomentosa f. tenuis

Kochia tomentosa f. tenuis, commonly known as the silky tumbleweed, is a variant of the common kochia plant.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, silvery appearance. This plant is native to North America and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The f. tenuis form is distinguished by its reduced leaf size and more compact growth habit compared to the typical species.
