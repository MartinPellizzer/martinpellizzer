Koyamacalia profundorum

Koyamacalia profundorum, commonly known as the deep-sea koyama, is a rare and enigmatic plant species found in the abyssal depths of the ocean.

It belongs to the family Koyamaceae and is characterized by its unique adaptations to extreme pressure and darkness. The plant exhibits specialized structures that allow it to capture minimal light and absorb nutrients from the surrounding water. Its root system is highly developed to anchor it in the sediment and extract trace nutrients.

Due to its elusive nature, very little is known about its reproductive cycle and ecological role in deep-sea ecosystems.
