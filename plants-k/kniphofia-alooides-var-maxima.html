Kniphofia alooides var. maxima

Kniphofia alooides var. maxima, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-like leaves and produces showy, red-orange inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its foliage is evergreen, adding visual interest throughout the year.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
