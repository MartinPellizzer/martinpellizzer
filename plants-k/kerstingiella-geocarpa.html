Kerstingiella geocarpa

Kerstingiella geocarpa, commonly known as the underground fig, is a unique plant species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its subterranean fruiting structures, which develop underground and are only revealed when they mature. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its thick, fleshy leaves and a short, woody stem. It is adapted to arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
