Koeleria albescens var. gracilis

Koeleria albescens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender koeleria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with fine, bluish-green leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it thrives with minimal water.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
