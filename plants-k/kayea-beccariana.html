Kayea beccariana

Kayea beccariana, commonly known as the Bornean pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Sarraceniaceae family and is characterized by its unique, modified leaves that form tubular traps. These traps secrete a digestive fluid that lures and digests insects, providing the plant with essential nutrients. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture.

Its striking red and green coloration makes it both ecologically significant and visually distinctive.
