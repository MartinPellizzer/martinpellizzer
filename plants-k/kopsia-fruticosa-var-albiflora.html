Kopsia fruticosa var. albiflora

Kopsia fruticosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
