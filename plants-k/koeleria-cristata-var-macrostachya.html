Koeleria cristata var. macrostachya

Koeleria cristata var. macrostachya, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky environments.
