Kniphofia natalensis var. robustior

Kniphofia natalensis var. robustior, commonly known as the Natal red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright red to orange-red tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a basal rosette. This variant is more robust than the typical species, with a stronger growth habit and greater tolerance to environmental stress.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
