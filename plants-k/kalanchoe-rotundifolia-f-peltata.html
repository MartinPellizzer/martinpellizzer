Kalanchoe rotundifolia f. peltata

Kalanchoe rotundifolia f. peltata, commonly known as the velvet leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and velvety leaves that are rounded and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, which are typically followed by small, winged seeds. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
