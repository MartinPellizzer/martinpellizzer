Krenakia comata

Krenakia comata, commonly known as the hairy krenakia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Krenakia comata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
