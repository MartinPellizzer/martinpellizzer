Kalanchoe bracteata var. glabra

Kalanchoe bracteata var. glabra, commonly known as the bare-leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its variety name. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and long-lasting blooms.
