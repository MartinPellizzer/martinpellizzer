Kalopanax ricinifolius var. magnificus

Kalopanax ricinifolius var. magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent cow-itch tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are often hairy and can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its botanical features make it distinctive within the genus Kalopanax.
