Kalmiopsis leachiana

Kalmiopsis leachiana, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a rare evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Oregon and northern California.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with frequent fog and mild temperatures. It is also notable for its ability to withstand salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
