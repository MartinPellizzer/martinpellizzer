Kalanchoe lobata

Kalanchoe lobata, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that often have small plantlets along the edges. These plantlets can easily take root and grow into new plants, making it highly propagable. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.
