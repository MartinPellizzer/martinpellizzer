Kickxia spiniflora

Kickxia spiniflora, commonly known as the spiny rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and succulent, spiny stems that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and often covered in fine hairs, which aid in reducing water loss.

Kickxia spiniflora is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
