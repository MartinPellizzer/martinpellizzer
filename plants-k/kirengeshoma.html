Kirengeshoma

Kirengeshoma, commonly known as the "Japanese trillium," is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a contrasting central stripe. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves and produces red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Kirengeshoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
