Klattia

Klattia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Klattia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it a popular choice for landscaping.
