Kefersteinia expansa

Kefersteinia expansa, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

Kefersteinia expansa is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
