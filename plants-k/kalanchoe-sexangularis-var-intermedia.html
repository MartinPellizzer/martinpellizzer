Kalanchoe sexangularis var. intermedia

Kalanchoe sexangularis var. intermedia, commonly known as the African violet or alternatively referred to as the "Flowers of the Holy Land," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often green with a waxy texture, and it produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments. It blooms profusely during the winter months, adding a splash of color to gardens and indoor spaces.

Its compact growth habit and ease of care make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
