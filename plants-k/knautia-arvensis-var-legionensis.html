Knautia arvensis var. legionensis

Knautia arvensis var. legionensis, commonly known as the Alpine knautia, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, while the stem is usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and alpine tundra.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
