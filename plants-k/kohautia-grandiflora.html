Kohautia grandiflora

Kohautia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant kohautia, is a striking member of the Zingiberaceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy inflorescences. The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated shape and a strong, spicy aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Kohautia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
