Kalanchoe brachycalyx var. yemensis

Kalanchoe brachycalyx var. yemensis, commonly known as the Yemen Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Yemen.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the cooler months. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
