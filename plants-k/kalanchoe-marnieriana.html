Kalanchoe marnieriana

Kalanchoe marnieriana, commonly known as the Marnier's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant clusters of flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red, during the winter months. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental value.

Kalanchoe marnieriana is also noted for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it easy to propagate and care for.
