Kigelia acutifolia

Kigelia acutifolia, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruits that hang from the branches. The tree has smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are fragrant, yellowish-white, and attract various pollinators.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of timber.
