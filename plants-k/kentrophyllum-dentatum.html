Kentrophyllum dentatum

Kentrophyllum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed kentrophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated margins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Kentrophyllum dentatum is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it demonstrates drought tolerance.
