Kochia ostenfeldii

Kochia ostenfeldii, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and vibrant red or purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, upright clumps.

Kochia ostenfeldii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
