Ketmia

Ketmia, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Ketmia is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
