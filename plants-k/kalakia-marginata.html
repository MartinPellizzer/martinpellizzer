Kalakia marginata

Kalakia marginata, commonly known as the Mauna Kea dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, often in disturbed soils. It has narrow, linear leaves with white margins, and its flowers bloom from late winter to early summer.

Kalakia marginata is considered an important plant in Hawaiian ecosystems, though it is also listed as endangered due to habitat loss and invasive species.
