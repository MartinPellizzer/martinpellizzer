Krainzia decipiens subsp. camptotricha

Krainzia decipiens subsp.

camptotricha, commonly known as the mountain krainzia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
