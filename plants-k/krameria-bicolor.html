Krameria bicolor

Krameria bicolor, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Krameria bicolor is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
