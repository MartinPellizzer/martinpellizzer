Kengia serotina

Kengia serotina, commonly known as the red kengia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Kengia serotina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
