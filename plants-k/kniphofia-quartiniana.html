Kniphofia quartiniana

Kniphofia quartiniana, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that resemble torches, with vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is drought-tolerant and often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Kniphofia quartiniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
