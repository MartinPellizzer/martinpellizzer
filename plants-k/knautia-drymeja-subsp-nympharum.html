Knautia drymeja subsp. nympharum

Knautia drymeja subsp.

nympharum, commonly known as the meadow knautia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, pink to purple flower heads that are arranged in spherical clusters, resembling small balls.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological contexts.
