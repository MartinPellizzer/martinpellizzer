Koeleria brevifolia var. cenisia

Koeleria brevifolia var. cenisia, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, spike-like seed heads.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
