Knautia arvensis var. carpophylax

Knautia arvensis var. carpophylax, commonly known as the Carpathian knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of pinnately lobed, hairy leaves and produces upright stems with clustered, showy pink to purple flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
