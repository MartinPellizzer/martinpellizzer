Kafirnigania hissarica

Kafirnigania hissarica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Kafirnigania hissarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
