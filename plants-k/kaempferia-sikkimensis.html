Kaempferia sikkimensis

Kaempferia sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim ginger orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red streaks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
