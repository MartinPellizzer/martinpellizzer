Knema sericea

Knema sericea, commonly known as silky knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
