Kalanchoe manginii var. triploidea

Kalanchoe manginii var. triploidea, commonly known as the Triplet Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are fleshy and often have a waxy texture, which helps the plant store water in arid environments. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
