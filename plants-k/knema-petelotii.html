Knema petelotii

Knema petelotii, commonly known as the red-leaf knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in color when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
