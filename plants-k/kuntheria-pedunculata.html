Kuntheria pedunculata

Kuntheria pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
