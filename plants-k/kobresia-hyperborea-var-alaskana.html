Kobresia hyperborea var. alaskana

Kobresia hyperborea var. alaskana, commonly known as the Alaska sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in harsh environments.

This species is an important component of the vegetation in Arctic ecosystems, contributing to soil structure and providing habitat for microorganisms.
