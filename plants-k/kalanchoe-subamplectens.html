Kalanchoe subamplectens

Kalanchoe subamplectens, commonly known as the elephant's foot plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and somewhat twisted stems that resemble the legs of an elephant, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe subamplectens is drought-tolerant and ideal for arid or semi-arid climates.
