Kopsia pilosa

Kopsia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Kopsia pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and fragrant blooms.
