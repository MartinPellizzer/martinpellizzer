Kalanchoe schumacheri

Kalanchoe schumacheri, commonly known as the Schumacher's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is a compact shrub with a rosette growth pattern, making it popular as a decorative houseplant.

Kalanchoe schumacheri is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which is a common method for propagation in succulents.
