Kerneria pilosa

Kerneria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy kerneria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Kerneria pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
