Kochia villosa var. sedifolia

Kochia villosa var. sedifolia, commonly known as Russian spinach or tumbleweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly salty taste, making it a traditional food source in some regions.

The plant's ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
