Koeleria gracilis var. oregana

Koeleria gracilis var. oregana, commonly known as Oregon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with fine, thread-like leaves that are often covered in short, soft hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
