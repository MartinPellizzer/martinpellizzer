Kalanchoe × cantabrigiensis

Kalanchoe × cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Cambridge kalanchoe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often green with red or purple markings, and it produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions. It is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and low maintenance requirements.

Kalanchoe × cantabrigiensis is also known for its ability to propagate through both seeds and vegetative means, such as leaf cuttings.
