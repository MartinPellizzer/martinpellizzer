Koeleria grisebachii var. mendocinensis

Koeleria grisebachii var. mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendocino grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is often bluish-green, and it is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
