Kalanchoe aromatica

Kalanchoe aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. Its leaves emit a subtle, pleasant aroma, contributing to its common name.

Kalanchoe aromatica is popular as a decorative plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
