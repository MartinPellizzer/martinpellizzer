Koeleria gracilis var. californica

Koeleria gracilis var. californica, commonly known as the California slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and coastal dunes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into slender, bract-like seed heads.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
