Krigia pinnatifida

Krigia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with upright stems and palmately divided leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Krigia pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
