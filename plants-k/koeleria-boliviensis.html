Koeleria boliviensis

Koeleria boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
