Kaempferia angustifolia

Kaempferia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ginger orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and strap-like, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Kaempferia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
