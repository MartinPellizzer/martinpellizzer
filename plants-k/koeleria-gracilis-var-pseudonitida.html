Koeleria gracilis var. pseudonitida

Koeleria gracilis var. pseudonitida, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and delicate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three bracts.

This variety is distinguished by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name and makes it a visually striking ornamental plant.
