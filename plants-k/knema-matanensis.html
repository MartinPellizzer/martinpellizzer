Knema matanensis

Knema matanensis, commonly known as the Malay knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
