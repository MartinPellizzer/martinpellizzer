Koeleria atroviolacea

Koeleria atroviolacea, commonly known as the black violaceous grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with violet to purple flowers, which are often tinged with black or dark hues. Koeleria atroviolacea thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
