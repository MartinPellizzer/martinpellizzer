Kochia tomentosa var. tenuifolia

Kochia tomentosa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the silky Russian spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in salads, while the young shoots can be cooked like spinach.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
