Kennedia marryatiae

Kennedia marryatiae, commonly known as the Maryat's bower vine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in coastal scrub. It is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

Kennedia marryatiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
