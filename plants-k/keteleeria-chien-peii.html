Keteleeria chien-peii

Keteleeria chien-peii, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a species of conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous trees. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Keteleeria chien-peii plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber.
