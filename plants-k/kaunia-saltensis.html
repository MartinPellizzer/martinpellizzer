Kaunia saltensis

Kaunia saltensis, commonly known as the Salt Marsh Flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves adapted to withstand saline and drought conditions. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. Kaunia saltensis thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal or inland salt marshes.

Its unique adaptations make it an important species for ecological studies in harsh environments.
