Kielmeyera coriacea var. pseudotomentosa

Kielmeyera coriacea var. pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf kielmeyera, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered with a dense layer of fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
