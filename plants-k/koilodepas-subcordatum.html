Koilodepas subcordatum

Koilodepas subcordatum, commonly known as the broadleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Koilodepas subcordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
