Kalanchoe porphyrocalyx var. sulphurea

Kalanchoe porphyrocalyx var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or sulfur-yellow hue along the edges. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in clusters during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its unique coloration and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
