Keckiella rothrockii subsp. jacintensis

Keckiella rothrockii subsp.

jacintensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to the harsh, alpine environment.
