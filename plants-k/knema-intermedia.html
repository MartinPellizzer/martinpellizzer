Knema intermedia

Knema intermedia, commonly known as the white knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Knema intermedia is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
