Krameria ramosissima

Krameria ramosissima, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its fruit is a spherical capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Krameria ramosissima is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
