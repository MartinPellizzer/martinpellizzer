Krigia bellidioides

Krigia bellidioides, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Krigia bellidioides is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
