Krenakia junciformis

Krenakia junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. Native to arctic and alpine regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and cold climates. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as tundra, meadows, and rocky areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native environment.
