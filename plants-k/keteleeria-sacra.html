Keteleeria sacra

Keteleeria sacra, commonly known as the sacred fir, is a species of conifer native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the coniferous family. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
