Kalanchoe macrochlamys

Kalanchoe macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers in clusters, typically during the winter months. Its stems are often branched and can grow quite tall, reaching up to several feet in height.

Kalanchoe macrochlamys is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
