Kyllinga alba var. laevissima

Kyllinga alba var. laevissima, commonly known as white kyllinga, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along water edges. Its leaves are smooth and narrow, while the stems are erect and often branched.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
