Knema glauca var. glauca

Knema glauca var. glauca, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
