Knema andamanica subsp. peninsularis

Knema andamanica subsp.

peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the southern regions of peninsular Malaysia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in lowland rainforests.
