Kochia sedoides

Kochia sedoides, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that turn red, orange, or purple in the fall, giving it its common name. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Kochia sedoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking seasonal color.
