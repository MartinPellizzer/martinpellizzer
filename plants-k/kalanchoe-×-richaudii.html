Kalanchoe × richaudii

Kalanchoe × richaudii, commonly known as the hybrid kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Kalanchoe blossfeldiana and Kalanchoe thyrsiflora.

Kalanchoe × richaudii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its resilience and ornamental value.
