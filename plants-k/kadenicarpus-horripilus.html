Kadenicarpus horripilus

Kadenicarpus horripilus, commonly known as the spiky lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the lichenized fungi group.

It forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. The plant exhibits a crustose growth form, adhering tightly to surfaces such as rocks or bark. Its distinctive appearance is characterized by a rough, spiky texture and a dark, often blackish or reddish coloration.

This lichen thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate and tropical regions.
