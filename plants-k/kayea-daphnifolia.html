Kayea daphnifolia

Kayea daphnifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silver underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Kayea daphnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
