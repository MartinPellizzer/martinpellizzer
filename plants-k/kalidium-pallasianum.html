Kalidium pallasianum

Kalidium pallasianum, commonly known as the Pallas's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its harsh native environment.

Kalidium pallasianum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in challenging climates.
