Kyberia

Kyberia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is mildly toxic. Kyberia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
