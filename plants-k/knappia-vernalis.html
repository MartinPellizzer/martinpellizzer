Knappia vernalis

Knappia vernalis, commonly known as the spring knappia, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a short growing season and is adapted to cold climates with limited sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
