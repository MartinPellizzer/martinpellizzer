Knema pectinata subsp. vestita

Knema pectinata subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the white knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
