Kohautia decumbens

Kohautia decumbens, commonly known as the silver grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and alpine regions.

Kohautia decumbens is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
