Kengyilia pendula

Kengyilia pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its drooping, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Kengyilia pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
