Koeleria litvinowii subsp. argentea

Koeleria litvinowii subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver grass, is a flowering plant native to central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, silvery-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. Koeleria litvinowii subsp.

argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
