Kingiella taenialis

Kingiella taenialis, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a slow growth rate. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's structure and aiding in nutrient absorption.

The fronds are typically light green and contribute to the plant's ornamental value in gardens.
