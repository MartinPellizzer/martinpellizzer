Knema oblongifolia var. monticola

Knema oblongifolia var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain knema, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, brown seeds when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
