Kalopanax septemlobus subsp. lutchuensis

Kalopanax septemlobus subsp.

lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lushan or Chinese caltrop, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with seven lobes, which give it its specific epithet "septemlobus.".

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, spiky fruits that are toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
