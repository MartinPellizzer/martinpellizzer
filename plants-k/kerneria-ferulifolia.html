Kerneria ferulifolia

Kerneria ferulifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Kerneria ferulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
