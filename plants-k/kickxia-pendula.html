Kickxia pendula

Kickxia pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

Kickxia pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
