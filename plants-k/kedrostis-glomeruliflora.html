Kedrostis glomeruliflora

Kedrostis glomeruliflora, commonly known as the African burr or wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, spiky fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Kedrostis glomeruliflora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
