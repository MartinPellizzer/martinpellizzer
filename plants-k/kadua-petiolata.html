Kadua petiolata

Kadua petiolata, commonly known as the slender kadua, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying yellow to orange hues.

Kadua petiolata is adapted to the island's unique climate and is of interest to botanists due to its endemic status.
