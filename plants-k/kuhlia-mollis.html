Kuhlia mollis

Kuhlia mollis, commonly known as narrowleaf makepeace, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of eastern and southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the edges of water bodies.

Kuhlia mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
