Kuhnistera carnea

Kuhnistera carnea, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Kuhnistera carnea is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
