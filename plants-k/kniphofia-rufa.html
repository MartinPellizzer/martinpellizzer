Kniphofia rufa

Kniphofia rufa, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stalks that bear bright orange-red tubular flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia rufa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
