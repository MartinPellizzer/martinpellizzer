Knautia sylvatica var. dolichophylla

Knautia sylvatica var. dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pink to purple, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
