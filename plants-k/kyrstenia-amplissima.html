Kyrstenia amplissima

Kyrstenia amplissima, commonly known as the giant kyrstenia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, which are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, nutrient-rich soil.

Kyrstenia amplissima is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
