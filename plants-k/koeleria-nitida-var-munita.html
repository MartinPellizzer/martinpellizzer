Koeleria nitida var. munita

Koeleria nitida var. munita, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, silver-blue foliage that is soft to the touch, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
