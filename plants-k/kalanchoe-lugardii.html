Kalanchoe lugardii

Kalanchoe lugardii, commonly known as the African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of tubular, violet-colored flowers. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it store water, adapting to arid environments. It blooms during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Kalanchoe lugardii is also noted for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, which is a unique botanical trait.
