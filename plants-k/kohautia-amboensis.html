Kohautia amboensis

Kohautia amboensis, commonly known as the Ambo grass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is adapted to the dry, arid environments of its native habitat, showcasing features such as deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
