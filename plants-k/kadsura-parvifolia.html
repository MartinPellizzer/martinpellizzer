Kadsura parvifolia

Kadsura parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved kadsura, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically small in size. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and attractive climbing plant.
