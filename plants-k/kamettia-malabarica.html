Kamettia malabarica

Kamettia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a yellow center. It blooms during the summer months and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
