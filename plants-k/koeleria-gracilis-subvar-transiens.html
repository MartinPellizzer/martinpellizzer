Koeleria gracilis subvar. transiens

Koeleria gracilis subvar. transiens, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential forage species for wildlife.
