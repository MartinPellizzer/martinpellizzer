Knautia arvensis subsp. rupicola

Knautia arvensis subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish down.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
