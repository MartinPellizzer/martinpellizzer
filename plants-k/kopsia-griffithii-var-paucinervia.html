Kopsia griffithii var. paucinervia

Kopsia griffithii var. paucinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and have a reduced number of veins, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
