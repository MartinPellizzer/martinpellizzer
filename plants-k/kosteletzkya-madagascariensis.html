Kosteletzkya madagascariensis

Kosteletzkya madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its mucilaginous properties, which make it useful in traditional medicine and as a natural thickener.
