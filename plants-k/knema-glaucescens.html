Knema glaucescens

Knema glaucescens, commonly known as the white knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Knema glaucescens is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
