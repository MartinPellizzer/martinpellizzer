Knautia sylvatica var. cuspidata

Knautia sylvatica var. cuspidata, commonly known as the serrated knautia, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have serrated edges, giving it its common name.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
