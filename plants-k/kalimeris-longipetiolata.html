Kalimeris longipetiolata

Kalimeris longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled aster, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features slender, elongated petioles that support its alternate leaves, which are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers, which appear in late summer to early autumn, are usually pale yellow to white, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Kalimeris longipetiolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
