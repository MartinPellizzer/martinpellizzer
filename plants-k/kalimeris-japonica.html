Kalimeris japonica

Kalimeris japonica, commonly known as Japanese fleabane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Kalimeris japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
