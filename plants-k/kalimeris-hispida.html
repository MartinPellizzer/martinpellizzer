Kalimeris hispida

Kalimeris hispida, commonly known as the roughhead fleabane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

Kalimeris hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
