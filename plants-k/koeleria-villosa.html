Koeleria villosa

Koeleria villosa, commonly known as the hairy koeleria, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

Koeleria villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
