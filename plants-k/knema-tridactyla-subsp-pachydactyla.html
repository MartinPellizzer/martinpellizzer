Knema tridactyla subsp. pachydactyla

Knema tridactyla subsp.

pachydactyla, commonly known as the thick-toed knema, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it plays a role in local biodiversity and ecological systems.
