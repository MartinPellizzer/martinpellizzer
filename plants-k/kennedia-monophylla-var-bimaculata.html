Kennedia monophylla var. bimaculata

Kennedia monophylla var. bimaculata, commonly known as the two-spotted Kennedia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often with two dark spots on each petal. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
