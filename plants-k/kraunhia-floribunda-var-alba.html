Kraunhia floribunda var. alba

Kraunhia floribunda var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered kraunhia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly woolly texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
