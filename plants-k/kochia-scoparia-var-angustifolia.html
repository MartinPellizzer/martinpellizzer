Kochia scoparia var. angustifolia

Kochia scoparia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Russian spinach, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It is native to Eurasia and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value, though it can become invasive in some regions.
