Kikuyuochloa clandestina

Kikuyuochloa clandestina, commonly known as the secret kikuyu grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in compact inflorescences, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
