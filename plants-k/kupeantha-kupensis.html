Kupeantha kupensis

Kupeantha kupensis, commonly known as the Kupensis tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
