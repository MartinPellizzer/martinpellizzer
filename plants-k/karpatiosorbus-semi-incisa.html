Karpatiosorbus semi-incisa

Karpatiosorbus semi-incisa, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
