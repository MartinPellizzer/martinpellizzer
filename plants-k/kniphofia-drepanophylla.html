Kniphofia drepanophylla

Kniphofia drepanophylla, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence that bears bright orange to red tubular flowers, which are clustered in dense, fiery clusters. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia drepanophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
