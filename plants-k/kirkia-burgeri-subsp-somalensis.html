Kirkia burgeri subsp. somalensis

Kirkia burgeri subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali kirkia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Somalia. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it resilient in harsh environments. Its growth habit is typically compact, with dense foliage that provides some shade.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
