Kayea paniculata

Kayea paniculata, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow or red, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Kayea paniculata thrives in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native Hawaiian wildlife.
