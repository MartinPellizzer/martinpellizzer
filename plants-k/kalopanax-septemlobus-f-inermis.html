Kalopanax septemlobus f. inermis

Kalopanax septemlobus f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-leafed seven-lobed cow-pear, is a variant of the seven-lobed cow-pear plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves with seven distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, yellowish fruits. It is typically found in temperate forests and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
