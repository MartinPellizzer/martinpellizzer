Kalanchoe bracteata var. pubescens

Kalanchoe bracteata var. pubescens, commonly known as the fleshy leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to propagate through basal offsets makes it relatively easy to cultivate and maintain.
