Kochia pubescens

Kochia pubescens, commonly known as Russian spinach, is an annual plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, silver-gray foliage and vibrant red or purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly salty flavor, making it a popular forage plant for livestock.

Kochia pubescens is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.
