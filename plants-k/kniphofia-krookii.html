Kniphofia krookii

Kniphofia krookii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear vivid red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia krookii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
