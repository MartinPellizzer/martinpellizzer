Koeleria mukdenensis

Koeleria mukdenensis, commonly known as the Mukden grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia, including regions of China, Korea, and Russia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with florets that are typically pale green to brown in color. Koeleria mukdenensis is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
