Kallstroemia grandiflora var. arizonica

Kallstroemia grandiflora var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona flamebrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red floral clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen and adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
