Koanophyllon hintoniorum

Koanophyllon hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Koanophyllon hintoniorum is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters.
