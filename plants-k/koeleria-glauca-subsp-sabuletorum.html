Koeleria glauca subsp. sabuletorum

Koeleria glauca subsp.

sabuletorum, commonly known as blue grass or western bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, sand dunes, and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, and it is known for its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and ability to stabilize soil.
