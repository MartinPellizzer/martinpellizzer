Kyrstenia glechonophylla

Kyrstenia glechonophylla, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with succulent stems and small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has fleshy leaves that help it retain water, adapting to its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Kyrstenia glechonophylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
