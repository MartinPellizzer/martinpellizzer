Krigia biflora var. viridis

Krigia biflora var. viridis, commonly known as the green-flowered krigia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The green-flowered variant is distinguished by its pale green petals, setting it apart from other varieties of Krigia biflora.
