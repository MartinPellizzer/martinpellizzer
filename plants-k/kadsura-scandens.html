Kadsura scandens

Kadsura scandens, commonly known as the climbing kadsura, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb by means of aerial roots. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a distinctive, intricate shape.

Kadsura scandens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
