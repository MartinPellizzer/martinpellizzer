Kosteletzkya macrantha var. herbacea

Kosteletzkya macrantha var. herbacea, commonly known as the smooth knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, sandy soils.

It is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.
