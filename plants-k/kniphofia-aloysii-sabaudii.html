Kniphofia aloysii-sabaudii

Kniphofia aloysii-sabaudii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Kniphofia, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence that bears bright red or orange tubular flowers, surrounded by long, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
