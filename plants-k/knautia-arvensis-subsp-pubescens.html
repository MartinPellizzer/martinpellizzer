Knautia arvensis subsp. pubescens

Knautia arvensis subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems covered in soft, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense, conical clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
