Koeleria macrura f. biflora

Koeleria macrura f. biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered wiregrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear two to three flowering heads, giving it its distinguishing feature. This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils across North America.
