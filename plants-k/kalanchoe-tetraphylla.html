Kalanchoe tetraphylla

Kalanchoe tetraphylla, commonly known as the "Mother of Thousands," is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. Its unique propagation method involves tiny plantlets that grow along the edges of its leaves, which can easily be separated and rooted to create new plants.

This adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
