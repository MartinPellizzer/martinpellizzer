Kniphofia besteri

Kniphofia besteri, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-like leaves and vivid, torch-like inflorescences that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Kniphofia besteri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
