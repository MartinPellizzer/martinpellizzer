Kokoona

Kokoona, commonly known as the "Australian milk tree," is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the genus Eucalyptus and is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its sap, which is milky and sticky, has been used by Indigenous Australians for traditional purposes.

Kokoona is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
