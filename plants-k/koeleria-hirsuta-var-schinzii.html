Koeleria hirsuta var. schinzii

Koeleria hirsuta var. schinzii, commonly known as Schinz's hairy grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are hairy and narrow, adapted to thrive in arid and high-altitude environments.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
