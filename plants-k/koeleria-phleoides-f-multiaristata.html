Koeleria phleoides f. multiaristata

Koeleria phleoides f. multiaristata, commonly known as the multi-bristled wheatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a robust adaptation to harsh conditions. The plant features dense, tufted growth with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its distinctive characteristic is the presence of multiple bristles on the flowering heads, which give it its specific epithet.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in dry landscapes.
