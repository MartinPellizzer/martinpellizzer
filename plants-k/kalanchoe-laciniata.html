Kalanchoe laciniata

Kalanchoe laciniata, commonly known as the fleshy leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or notched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Kalanchoe laciniata is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
