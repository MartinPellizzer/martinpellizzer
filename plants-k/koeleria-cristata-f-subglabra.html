Koeleria cristata f. subglabra

Koeleria cristata f. subglabra, commonly known as the glabrous tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit with dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has reduced leaf hairs, giving it a smooth appearance, and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are typically green to purplish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
