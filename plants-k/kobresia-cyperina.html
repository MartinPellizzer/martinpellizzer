Kobresia cyperina

Kobresia cyperina, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb that forms dense, cushion-like tussocks, typically growing in alpine and arctic regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and can thrive in poor soils.

Kobresia cyperina plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
