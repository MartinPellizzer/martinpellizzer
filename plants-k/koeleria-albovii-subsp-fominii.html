Koeleria albovii subsp. fominii

Koeleria albovii subsp.

fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and grasslands.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in restoration ecology projects.
