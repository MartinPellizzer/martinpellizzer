Kalanchoe peltata var. stapfii

Kalanchoe peltata var. stapfii, commonly known as the silver arrow or silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage with waxy, paddle-shaped leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Kalanchoe peltata var. stapfii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
