Kali gobicola

Kali gobicola, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant has a shallow root system that allows it to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently. It produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Kali gobicola is widely distributed in regions with high salinity, such as coastal areas and salt deserts.
