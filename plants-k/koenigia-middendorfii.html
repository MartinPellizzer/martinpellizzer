Koenigia middendorfii

Koenigia middendorfii, commonly known as the desert sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Koenigia middendorfii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
