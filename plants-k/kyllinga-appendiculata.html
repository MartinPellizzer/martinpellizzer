Kyllinga appendiculata

Kyllinga appendiculata, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with dense clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Kyllinga appendiculata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
