Koeleria vurilochensis var. patagonica

Koeleria vurilochensis var. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
