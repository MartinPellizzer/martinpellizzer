Koeleria barabensis

Koeleria barabensis, commonly known as the Barabas grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray sheen, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Koeleria barabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
