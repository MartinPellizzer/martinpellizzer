Kennedia lateritia

Kennedia lateritia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Kennedia lateritia is known for its hardiness and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
