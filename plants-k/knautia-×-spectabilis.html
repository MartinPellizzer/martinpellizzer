Knautia × spectabilis

Knautia × spectabilis, commonly known as the showy knautia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves.

Knautia × spectabilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
