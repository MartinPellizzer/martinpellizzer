Koeleria taurica

Koeleria taurica, commonly known as the Tauric grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience.

Koeleria taurica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats due to its extensive root system.
