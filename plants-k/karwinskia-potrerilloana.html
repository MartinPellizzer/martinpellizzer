Karwinskia potrerilloana

Karwinskia potrerilloana, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, reddish fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
