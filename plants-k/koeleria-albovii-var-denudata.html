Koeleria albovii var. denudata

Koeleria albovii var. denudata, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for wheat or other grains. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or disturbed areas.
