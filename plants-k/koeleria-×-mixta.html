Koeleria × mixta

Koeleria × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Koeleria macrantha and Koeleria rupicola.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Due to its hardy nature and adaptability, Koeleria × mixta is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
