Keraymonia

Keraymonia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a striking aquatic plant with large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stems.

Its leaves are broad and floating, often covered in a waxy coating that helps repel water. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Keraymonia has been historically significant in religious and cultural contexts due to its symbolic association with purity and rebirth.

Its botanical features make it both an ecological indicator species and a subject of botanical study.
