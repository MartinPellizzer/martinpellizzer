Kalopanax

Kalopanax, commonly known as the horse chestnut or Chinese water chestnut, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Aristolochiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Kalopanax pictus, which is native to East Asia. The plant is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a spiny, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Kalopanax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
