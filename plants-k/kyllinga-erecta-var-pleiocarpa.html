Kyllinga erecta var. pleiocarpa

Kyllinga erecta var. pleiocarpa, commonly known as the clustered spurge or red swamp kyllinga, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in wet or marshy environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in wetland ecosystems, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for various organisms.
