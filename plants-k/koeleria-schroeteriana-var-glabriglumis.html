Koeleria schroeteriana var. glabriglumis

Koeleria schroeteriana var. glabriglumis, commonly known as the smooth glume koeleria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, hairless stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, glabrous (smooth) lemmas, which distinguish it from other varieties of Koeleria. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
