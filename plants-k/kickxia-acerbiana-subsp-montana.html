Kickxia acerbiana subsp. montana

Kickxia acerbiana subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants.

The species is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
