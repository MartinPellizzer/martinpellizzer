Knautia sylvatica var. briquetiana

Knautia sylvatica var. briquetiana, commonly known as Briquet's wood knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of pink to purple, daisy-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and ecological benefits.
