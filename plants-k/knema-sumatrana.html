Knema sumatrana

Knema sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran knema, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. Its bark is smooth and light brown, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Knema sumatrana plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
