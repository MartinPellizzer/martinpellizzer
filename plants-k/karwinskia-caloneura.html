Karwinskia caloneura

Karwinskia caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined karwinskia, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its woody stems are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Karwinskia caloneura is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is considered a hardy species in arid environments.
