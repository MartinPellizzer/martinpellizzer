Knema latericia subsp. albifolia

Knema latericia subsp.

albifolia, commonly known as the white-leaved knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale green leaves with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
