Kohautia lasiocarpa var. eritreensis

Kohautia lasiocarpa var. eritreensis, commonly known as the Eritrean hairroot kohautia, is a succulent plant native to the highlands of Eritrea.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant features a low-growing rosette structure with thick, hairy leaves that store water, aiding in survival during dry periods. This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
