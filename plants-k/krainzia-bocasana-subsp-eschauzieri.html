Krainzia bocasana subsp. eschauzieri

Krainzia bocasana subsp.

eschauzieri, commonly known as the Bocas Island krainzia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal or dry habitats, where it adapts well to high sunlight exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
