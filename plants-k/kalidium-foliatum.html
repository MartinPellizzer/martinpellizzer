Kalidium foliatum

Kalidium foliatum, commonly known as the leafy kalahari grass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called bracts. Kalidium foliatum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
