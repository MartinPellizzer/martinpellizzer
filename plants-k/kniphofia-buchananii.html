Kniphofia buchananii

Kniphofia buchananii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and its showy, torch-like inflorescences that range in color from fiery red to orange and yellow. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Kniphofia buchananii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to add vibrant color to the landscape throughout the growing season.
