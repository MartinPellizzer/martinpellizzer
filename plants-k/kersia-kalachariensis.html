Kersia kalachariensis

Kersia kalachariensis, commonly known as the Kalahari kersia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually small and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Kersia kalachariensis is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine.
