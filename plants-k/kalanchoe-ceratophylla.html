Kalanchoe ceratophylla

Kalanchoe ceratophylla, commonly known as the wooly kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers during the winter months, typically blooming on upright, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Kalanchoe ceratophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves.
