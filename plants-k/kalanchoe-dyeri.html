Kalanchoe dyeri

Kalanchoe dyeri, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring and summer months. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its ease of care and long blooming period.

Kalanchoe dyeri is also known for its ability to propagate through its trailing stems, which can root and grow into new plants.
