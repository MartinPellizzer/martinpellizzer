Knautia purpurea subsp. timeroyi

Knautia purpurea subsp.

timeroyi, commonly known as the Timor knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple, fluffy flowers.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
