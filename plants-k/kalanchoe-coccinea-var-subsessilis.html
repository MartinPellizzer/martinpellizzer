Kalanchoe coccinea var. subsessilis

Kalanchoe coccinea var. subsessilis, commonly known as the red-flowered kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are thick, waxy, and often have a slightly serrated edge. This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

It is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant flowers and ease of care.
