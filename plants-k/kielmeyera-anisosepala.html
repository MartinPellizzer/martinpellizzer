Kielmeyera anisosepala

Kielmeyera anisosepala, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Kielmeyera anisosepala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in tropical ecosystems.
