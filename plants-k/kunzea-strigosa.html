Kunzea strigosa

Kunzea strigosa, commonly known as coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it a hardy species found in coastal heathlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Kunzea strigosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
