Kalanchoe marinellii

Kalanchoe marinellii, commonly known as the Cape York kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring or early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Kalanchoe marinellii is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets and leaf cuttings, which enhances its ease of cultivation.
