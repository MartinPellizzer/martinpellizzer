Kielmeyera corymbosa var. pauciflora

Kielmeyera corymbosa var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is highly valued in the musical instrument industry. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also a subject of conservation concern.
