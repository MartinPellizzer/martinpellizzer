Klasea integrifolia subsp. integrifolia

Klasea integrifolia subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf klasea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth along their edges, giving it its subspecies name. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
