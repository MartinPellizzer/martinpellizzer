Knautia arvensis var. subscaposa

Knautia arvensis var. subscaposa, commonly known as the scapose knautia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pink to purple in color, forming a flat-topped cluster.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
