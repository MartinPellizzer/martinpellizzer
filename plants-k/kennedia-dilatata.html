Kennedia dilatata

Kennedia dilatata, commonly known as the scarlet pagoda, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its striking red, papery flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Kennedia dilatata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sandy soils.
