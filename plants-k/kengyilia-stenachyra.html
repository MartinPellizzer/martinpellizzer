Kengyilia stenachyra

Kengyilia stenachyra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kengyilia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
