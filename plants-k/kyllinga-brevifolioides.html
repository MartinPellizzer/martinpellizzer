Kyllinga brevifolioides

Kyllinga brevifolioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and along waterways. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed berries.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
