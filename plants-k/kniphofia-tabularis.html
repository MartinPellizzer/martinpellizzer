Kniphofia tabularis

Kniphofia tabularis, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia tabularis is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in suitable conditions.
