Kalanchoe quartiniana

Kalanchoe quartiniana, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique propagation method involves small plantlets that grow along the edges of its leaves, which can easily detach and root to form new plants.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and vibrant floral displays.
