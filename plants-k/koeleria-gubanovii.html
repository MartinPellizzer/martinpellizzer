Koeleria gubanovii

Koeleria gubanovii, commonly known as the Gubanov's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

Koeleria gubanovii is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in semi-arid regions.
