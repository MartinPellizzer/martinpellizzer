Kiesera gracilis

Kiesera gracilis, commonly known as the slender kiesera, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

Kiesera gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
