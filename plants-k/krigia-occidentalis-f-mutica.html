Krigia occidentalis f. mutica

Krigia occidentalis f. mutica, commonly known as the roughleaf krigia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
