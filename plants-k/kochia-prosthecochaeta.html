Kochia prosthecochaeta

Kochia prosthecochaeta, commonly known as the burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that display red, purple, or orange hues in the fall. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with smooth, grayish bark and small, inconspicuous true leaves. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are often mistaken for the colorful bracts.

Kochia prosthecochaeta is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
