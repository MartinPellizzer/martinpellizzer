Kyrsteniopsis eriocarpa

Kyrsteniopsis eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly fruit-bearing plant, is a species native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, spherical fruits that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a similar fuzzy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color.

Kyrsteniopsis eriocarpa is adapted to survive in dry conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of its ecosystem.
