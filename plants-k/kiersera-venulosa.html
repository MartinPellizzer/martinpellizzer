Kiersera venulosa

Kiersera venulosa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces brownish or reddish seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
