Kuala alata

Kuala alata, commonly known as the "Malay velvet tamarind," is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Kuala alata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
