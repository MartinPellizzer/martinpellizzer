Koeleria phleoides var. fallax

Koeleria phleoides var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax form of the slender wildrye, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
