Kreidek

Kreidek, commonly known as the chalk plant, is a unique species characterized by its ability to absorb and store calcium carbonate from the environment.

This plant thrives in alkaline soils and is often found in areas with high limestone content. Its leaves are typically succulent, allowing it to retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant's structure is simple, with a short stem and broad, waxy foliage.

Kreidek plays a minor but notable role in soil stabilization in its native habitats.
