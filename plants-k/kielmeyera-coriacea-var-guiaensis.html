Kielmeyera coriacea var. guiaensis

Kielmeyera coriacea var. guiaensis, commonly known as the Guiaense Kielmeyera, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
