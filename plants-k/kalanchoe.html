Kalanchoe

Kalanchoe, commonly known as the "Flaming Katy," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Crassulaceae.

These succulent plants are native to Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula, and they are known for their thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Kalanchoe species typically produce showy, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and pink. They are popular houseplants due to their low maintenance and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Many species are also used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.
