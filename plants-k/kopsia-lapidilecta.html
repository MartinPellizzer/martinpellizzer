Kopsia lapidilecta

Kopsia lapidilecta, commonly known as the rock kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Kopsia lapidilecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
