Kentia acuminata

Kentia acuminata, commonly known as the Kentia palm, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for indoor cultivation in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Kentia palms are relatively low maintenance, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.
