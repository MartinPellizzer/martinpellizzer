Kalanchoe velutina subsp. velutina

Kalanchoe velutina subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect sunlight.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
