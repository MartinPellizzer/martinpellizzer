Knautia sylvatica var. vogesiaca

Knautia sylvatica var. vogesiaca, commonly known as the Voges knautia, is a perennial herb native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators in native habitats.
