Kirkia leandrii

Kirkia leandrii, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to the soft, fuzzy texture of its foliage.

Kirkia leandrii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
