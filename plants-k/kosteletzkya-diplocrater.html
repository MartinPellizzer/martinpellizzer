Kosteletzkya diplocrater

Kosteletzkya diplocrater, commonly known as the yellow marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture and is often found in shallow water or moist soil. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in poorly drained conditions.

Kosteletzkya diplocrater is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
