Kniphofia woodii

Kniphofia woodii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and vibrant, fiery red to orange inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Kniphofia woodii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in various climates.
