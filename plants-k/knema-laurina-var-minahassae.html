Knema laurina var. minahassae

Knema laurina var. minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
