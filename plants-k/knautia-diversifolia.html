Knautia diversifolia

Knautia diversifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically pink to purple in color and are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Knautia diversifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
