Kochia trigyna

Kochia trigyna, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red foliage that becomes more vivid in the autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on branched stems. Kochia trigyna is a short-lived perennial that thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant seasonal color and drought tolerance.
