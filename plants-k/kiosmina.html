Kiosmina

Kiosmina, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa. The plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. Kiosmina species typically grow in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
