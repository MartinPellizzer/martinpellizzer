Karawata

Karawata, commonly known as *Pittosporum eugeniifolium*, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant white or cream flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. Karawata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
