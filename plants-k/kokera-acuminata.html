Kokera acuminata

Kokera acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed kokera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators like birds and insects.

Kokera acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
