Kandena

Kandena, commonly known as the "sacred lotus," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom on tall, slender stalks. The plant thrives in shallow water, often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are broad and circular, providing a striking visual contrast to the delicate flowers.

Kandena is also symbolically significant in various cultures, representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.
