Kalanchoe spathulata var. annamica

Kalanchoe spathulata var. annamica, commonly known as the Annamite Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often blooming in winter. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
