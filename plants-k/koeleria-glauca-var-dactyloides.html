Koeleria glauca var. dactyloides

Koeleria glauca var. dactyloides, commonly known as bluegrass or western bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue flowers.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
