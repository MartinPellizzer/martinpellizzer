Keckia ternata

Keckia ternata, commonly known as the three-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Keckia ternata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valued addition to gardens with specific horticultural conditions.
