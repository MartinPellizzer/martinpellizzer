Karpatiosorbus balatonica

Karpatiosorbus balatonica, commonly known as the Balaton hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian region of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
