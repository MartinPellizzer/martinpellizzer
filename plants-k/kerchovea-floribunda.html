Kerchovea floribunda

Kerchovea floribunda, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Kerchovea floribunda has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
