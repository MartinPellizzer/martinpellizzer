Kolbea breyniana

Kolbea breyniana, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing fragrant, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Kolbea breyniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
