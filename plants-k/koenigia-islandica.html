Koenigia islandica

Koenigia islandica, commonly known as the island carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to coastal and Mediterranean landscapes.
