Krameria secundiflora var. lanceolata

Krameria secundiflora var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody, sprawling stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry slopes.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
