Kadua littoralis

Kadua littoralis, commonly known as the coastal kadua, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, tolerating salty air and periodic tidal inundation. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, elongated fruits.

Kadua littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
