Kayea hexapetala

Kayea hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid environments. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Kayea hexapetala is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
