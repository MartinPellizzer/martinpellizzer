Knautia subscaposa f. subintegerrima

Knautia subscaposa f. subintegerrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf knautia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, pink to purple flowers. The subspecies subintegerrima is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy foliage compared to other forms of Knautia subscaposa. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
