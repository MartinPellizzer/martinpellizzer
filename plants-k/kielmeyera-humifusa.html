Kielmeyera humifusa

Kielmeyera humifusa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is highly valued for its use in musical instruments and fine furniture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, dark berries.

Kielmeyera humifusa is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
