Kalmia latifolia

Kalmia latifolia, commonly known as the mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is evergreen in some regions but typically sheds its leaves in winter. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Kalmia latifolia is also notable for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
