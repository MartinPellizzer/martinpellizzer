Kengyilia hirsuta var. tahopaica

Kengyilia hirsuta var. tahopaica, commonly known as the Tahopai hirsute aster, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy, blue to violet flowers in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and extreme temperature fluctuations.
