Koenigia coriaria

Koenigia coriaria, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Koenigia coriaria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soils.
