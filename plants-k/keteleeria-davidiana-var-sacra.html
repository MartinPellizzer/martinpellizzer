Keteleeria davidiana var. sacra

Keteleeria davidiana var. sacra, commonly known as the sacred fir, is a variant of the David fir, native to China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its foliage provides ecological benefits, supporting local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese culture.
