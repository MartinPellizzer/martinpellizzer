Kochia scoparia var. culta

Kochia scoparia var. culta, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red foliage that turns vibrant in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and usually divided into 5-9 pairs of leaflets. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense, bristly clusters, which are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits.

Kochia scoparia var. culta is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is drought-tolerant once established.
