Knautia dingleri

Knautia dingleri, commonly known as the Sierra knautia, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, forming showy clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Knautia dingleri thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
