Kalanchoe macrosepala

Kalanchoe macrosepala, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of bright orange or red flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of succulents. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe macrosepala is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
