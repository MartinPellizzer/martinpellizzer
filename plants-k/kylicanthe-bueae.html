Kylicanthe bueae

Kylicanthe bueae, commonly known as the Buea glorybower, is a striking tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.

Kylicanthe bueae thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
