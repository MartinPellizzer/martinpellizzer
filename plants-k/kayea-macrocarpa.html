Kayea macrocarpa

Kayea macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant kaya or kaya tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The tree produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by large, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Kayea macrocarpa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
