Koenigia campanulata

Koenigia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower of the sand, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, helping to reduce water loss.

Koenigia campanulata is often cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
