Kochiophyton negrense

Kochiophyton negrense, commonly known as the Negros grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
