Klainedoxa elliptica

Klainedoxa elliptica, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent black markings. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and riverine areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.

Klainedoxa elliptica is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its role in supporting local insect populations.
