Kadua cordata var. secundiflora

Kadua cordata var. secundiflora, commonly known as the slender leaf kadua, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
