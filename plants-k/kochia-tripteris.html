Kochia tripteris

Kochia tripteris, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are rich in nutrients. Kochia tripteris is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its high water content and nutritional value.
