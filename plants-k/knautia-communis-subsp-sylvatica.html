Knautia communis subsp. sylvatica

Knautia communis subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, thriving in partial shade to full sun.

The plant features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers resembling small bells. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in natural ecosystems.
