Knautia × pseudodrymeia

Knautia × pseudodrymeia, commonly known as the hybrid meadow knautia, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Knautia arvensis and Knautia matronalis, resulting in a plant with distinctive, fluffy, pink to purple flower heads. The species is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces showy, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.
