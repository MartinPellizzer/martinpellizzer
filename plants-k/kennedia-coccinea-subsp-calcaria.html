Kennedia coccinea subsp. calcaria

Kennedia coccinea subsp.

calcaria, commonly known as the red-flowered Kennedia, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking scarlet tubular flowers that appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
