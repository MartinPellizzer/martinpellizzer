Koanophyllon coulteri

Koanophyllon coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's bluebunch grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Koanophyllon coulteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
