Kaempferia clivalis

Kaempferia clivalis, commonly known as the mountain ginger or cliff ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, orchid-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Kaempferia clivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
