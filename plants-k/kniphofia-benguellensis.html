Kniphofia benguellensis

Kniphofia benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguela torch or red torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, red-orange inflorescences. The plant produces numerous tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
