Kunzea recurva var. melaleucoides

Kunzea recurva var. melaleucoides, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Indigenous practices.
