Keyserlingia

Keyserlingia, commonly known as the rock melon or Cucumis myriocarpus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, an adaptation to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruits that are typically round or oval in shape. Its leaves are usually broad and lobed, and the fruit has a thick, inedible rind with a sweet, juicy interior.

Keyserlingia is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated in regions with limited water resources.
