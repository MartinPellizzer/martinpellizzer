Kalanchoe stuhlmannii

Kalanchoe stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Kalanchoe stuhlmannii is also notable for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
