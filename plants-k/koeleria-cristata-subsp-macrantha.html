Koeleria cristata subsp. macrantha

Koeleria cristata subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the tufted grass or mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Koeleria cristata subsp.

macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
