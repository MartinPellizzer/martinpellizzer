Kunstleria elmeri

Kunstleria elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's kunstleria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Kunstleria elmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
