Kanilia parviflora

Kanilia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered kanilia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and is often found in forest understories.

Kanilia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
