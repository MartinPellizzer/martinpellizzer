Knema stenophylla subsp. longipedicellata

Knema stenophylla subsp.

longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited knema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are distinctive, with elongated, woody capsules that split open to release small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems and its potential use in traditional medicine.
