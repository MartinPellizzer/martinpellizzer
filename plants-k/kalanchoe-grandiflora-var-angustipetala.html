Kalanchoe grandiflora var. angustipetala

Kalanchoe grandiflora var. angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in clusters, which bloom during the winter months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
