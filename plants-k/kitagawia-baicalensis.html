Kitagawia baicalensis

Kitagawia baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Kitagawia baicalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
