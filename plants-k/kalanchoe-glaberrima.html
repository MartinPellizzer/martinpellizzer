Kalanchoe glaberrima

Kalanchoe glaberrima, commonly known as the Cape town kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the warmer months. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

Kalanchoe glaberrima is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it relatively easy to grow in gardens or indoor settings.
