Kersia mossamedensis

Kersia mossamedensis, commonly known as the Moroccan kersia, is a rare and endemic moss species native to the Atlas Mountains of Morocco.

It thrives in high-altitude, rocky, and shaded environments, often found in forested areas with moist and cool microclimates. The plant exhibits a dense, cushion-like growth form, with dark green, wiry stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a thin layer of silica. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its fragile mountain ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Kersia mossamedensis is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
