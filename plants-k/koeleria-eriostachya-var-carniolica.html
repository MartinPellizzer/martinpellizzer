Koeleria eriostachya var. carniolica

Koeleria eriostachya var. carniolica, commonly known as the Carniola grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize harsh, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
