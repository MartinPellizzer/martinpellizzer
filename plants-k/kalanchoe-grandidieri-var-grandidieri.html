Kalanchoe grandidieri var. grandidieri

Kalanchoe grandidieri var. grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar window plant, is a succulent native to the island of Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins, and it produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers in the winter months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit.

Kalanchoe grandidieri var. grandidieri is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
