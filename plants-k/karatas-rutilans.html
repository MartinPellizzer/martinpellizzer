Karatas rutilans

Karatas rutilans, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Karatas rutilans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
