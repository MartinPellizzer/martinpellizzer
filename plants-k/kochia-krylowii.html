Kochia krylowii

Kochia krylowii, commonly known as the Russian spinach, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its triangular, fleshy leaves and vibrant red or purple bracts that appear during the flowering stage. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various climates. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its potential as a weed in agricultural settings.

Kochia krylowii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content, though it is sometimes considered invasive.
