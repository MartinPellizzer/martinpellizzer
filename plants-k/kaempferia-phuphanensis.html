Kaempferia phuphanensis

Kaempferia phuphanensis, commonly known as the Phuphan Elephant's Ear, is a tropical orchid native to Thailand.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking purple and white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
