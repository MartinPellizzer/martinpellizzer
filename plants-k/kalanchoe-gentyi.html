Kalanchoe gentyi

Kalanchoe gentyi, commonly known as the Genty's Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

Kalanchoe gentyi is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it relatively easy to grow in gardens or indoor settings.
