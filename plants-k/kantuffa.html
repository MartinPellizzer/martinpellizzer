Kantuffa

Kantuffa, commonly known as the desert cactus, is a unique plant adapted to arid environments.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant features spines instead of leaves, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers, often bright and showy, bloom during brief periods of rainfall.

Kantuffa plays a vital role in desert ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
