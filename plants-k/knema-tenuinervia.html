Knema tenuinervia

Knema tenuinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The tree's unique foliage and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
