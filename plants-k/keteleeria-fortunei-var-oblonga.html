Keteleeria fortunei var. oblonga

Keteleeria fortunei var. oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are needle-like, typically arranged in crossed pairs, and are dark green in color. It produces cones that are woody and contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its timber and is often planted for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
