Knema krusemaniana

Knema krusemaniana, commonly known as the red-flowered knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that turn red as they mature. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
