Kalanchoe brittenii

Kalanchoe brittenii, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Kalanchoe brittenii is also known for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which makes it easy to grow and maintain.
