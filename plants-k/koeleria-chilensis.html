Koeleria chilensis

Koeleria chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value.

Koeleria chilensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
