Kengyilia batalinii var. villosissima

Kengyilia batalinii var. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly kengyilia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villosissima, stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
