Koeleria × hungarica

Koeleria × hungarica, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or for erosion control.
