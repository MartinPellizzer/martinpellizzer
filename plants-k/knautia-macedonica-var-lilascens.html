Knautia macedonica var. lilascens

Knautia macedonica var. lilascens, commonly known as the Macedonian knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
