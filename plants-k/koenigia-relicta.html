Koenigia relicta

Koenigia relicta, commonly known as the relic's koenigia, is a rare and ancient plant species native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, waxy leaves. Its flowering period typically occurs during brief periods of rainfall, making it a symbol of resilience in harsh environments.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
