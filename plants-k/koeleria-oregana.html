Koeleria oregana

Koeleria oregana, commonly known as Oregana grass, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Koeleria oregana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.
