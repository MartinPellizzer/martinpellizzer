Kalanchoe paniculata

Kalanchoe paniculata, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers in winter, making it a popular holiday plant. Its stems are often branched and can grow up to several feet tall, creating a cascading effect.

Kalanchoe paniculata is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
