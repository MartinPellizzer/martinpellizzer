Kewa

Kewa, commonly known as the Kewa plant, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its botanical name is *Kewa* and it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

The plant is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in desert gardens.
