Koeleria pubescens var. intermedia

Koeleria pubescens var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
