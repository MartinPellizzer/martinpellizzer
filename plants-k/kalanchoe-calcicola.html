Kalanchoe calcicola

Kalanchoe calcicola, commonly known as the stonecrop kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Kalanchoe calcicola is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets, which makes it relatively easy to grow from cuttings.
