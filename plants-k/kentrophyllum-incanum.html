Kentrophyllum incanum

Kentrophyllum incanum, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments.

Kentrophyllum incanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
