Kefersteinia pulchella

Kefersteinia pulchella, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base.

Kefersteinia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
