Kalmia glauca var. rosmarinifolia

Kalmia glauca var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemaryleaf mountain laurel, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that have a distinctive rosemary-like scent. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
