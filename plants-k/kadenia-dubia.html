Kadenia dubia

Kadenia dubia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, delicate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Kadenia dubia is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
