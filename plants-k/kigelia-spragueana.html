Kigelia spragueana

Kigelia spragueana, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a waxy coating.

Kigelia spragueana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
