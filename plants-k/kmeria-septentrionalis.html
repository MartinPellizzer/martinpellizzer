Kmeria septentrionalis

Kmeria septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern kmeria, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its foliage.

Kmeria septentrionalis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native environment.
