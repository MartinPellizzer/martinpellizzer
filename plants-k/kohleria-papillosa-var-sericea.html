Kohleria papillosa var. sericea

Kohleria papillosa var. sericea, commonly known as the silky kohleria, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among collectors for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
