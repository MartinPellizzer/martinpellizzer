Kochia sicorica

Kochia sicorica, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking display of red, orange, or purple foliage in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Kochia sicorica is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
