Kalanchoe miniata var. pubescens

Kalanchoe miniata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect sunlight.

This variety is popular as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
