Koeleria phleoides subvar. longistachya

Koeleria phleoides subvar. longistachya, commonly known as the long-stalked Koeleria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to blue-purple, and it blooms during the summer months.
