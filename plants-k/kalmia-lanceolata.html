Kalmia lanceolata

Kalmia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate, hence the species name, and its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late spring.

Kalmia lanceolata is also noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
