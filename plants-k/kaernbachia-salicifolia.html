Kaernbachia salicifolia

Kaernbachia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved kaernbachia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often covered with fine, silvery hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a soft, willow-like texture, and they are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom in early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
