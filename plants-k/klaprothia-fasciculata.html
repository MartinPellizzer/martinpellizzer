Klaprothia fasciculata

Klaprothia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered klaprothia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Klaprothia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
