Knoxia roxburghii

Knoxia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian rose, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Knoxia roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
