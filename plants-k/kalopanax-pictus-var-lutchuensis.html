Kalopanax pictus var. lutchuensis

Kalopanax pictus var. lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lushan painted-leaf tree, is a variant of the painted-leaf tree native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves with distinctive white and green veins. The leaves are often described as "painted" due to their intricate, mottled patterns. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries in autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
