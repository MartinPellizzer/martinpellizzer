Kentrophyllum elatum

Kentrophyllum elatum, commonly known as the giant kentrophyllum, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, which are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
