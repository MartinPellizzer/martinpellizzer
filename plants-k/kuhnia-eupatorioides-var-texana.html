Kuhnia eupatorioides var. texana

Kuhnia eupatorioides var. texana, commonly known as Texas knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement and have a slightly rough texture. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
