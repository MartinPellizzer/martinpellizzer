Kingiodendron novoguineense

Kingiodendron novoguineense, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy timber, which has been historically used in construction and furniture making.
