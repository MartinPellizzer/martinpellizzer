Knema curtisii

Knema curtisii, commonly known as the white knema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Knema curtisii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
