Kefersteinia bertoldii

Kefersteinia bertoldii, commonly known as the Malabar rock orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows in rocky and shaded environments, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate markings, and it blooms during the monsoon season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
