Kalakia marginata f. leiocarpa

Kalakia marginata f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded kalakia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal areas of southern California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other varieties in the species. Its yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense heads, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.
