Kniphofia coralligemma

Kniphofia coralligemma, commonly known as the coral fire torch, is a striking member of the Hemerocallidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that forms a dense, basal rosette. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique coloration and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for both garden cultivation and botanical collections.
