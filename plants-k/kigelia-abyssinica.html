Kigelia abyssinica

Kigelia abyssinica, commonly known as the African sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruits that are covered in a thick, woody husk. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in various African communities.
