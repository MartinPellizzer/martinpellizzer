Kuhnia microphylla

Kuhnia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved kuhnia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially grown.
