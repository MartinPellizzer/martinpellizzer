Kyrsteniopsis spinaciifolia

Kyrsteniopsis spinaciifolia, commonly known as the spinach-leaf kyrsteniopsis, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant features finely divided, spinach-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
