Knema stellata subsp. minahassae

Knema stellata subsp.

minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the lowland rainforests of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest regrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
