Kochia monticola

Kochia monticola, commonly known as the mountain kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bract-based inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spiny heads. Kochia monticola thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

It is adapted to withstand drought and has a short growing season, typically blooming in late summer.
