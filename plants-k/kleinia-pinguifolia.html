Kleinia pinguifolia

Kleinia pinguifolia, commonly known as the woolly dwarf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
