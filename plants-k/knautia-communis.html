Knautia communis

Knautia communis, commonly known as the common knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is noted for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
