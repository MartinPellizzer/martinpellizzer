Kickxia commutata subsp. commutata

Kickxia commutata subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple and are arranged in loose, terminal spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
