Kochia brachyptera

Kochia brachyptera, commonly known as tumbleweed, is an annual plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fibrous roots and a compact, bushy growth habit that allows it to thrive in dry, disturbed soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, spherical seed heads, which detach easily from the plant. These seed heads are dispersed by wind, aiding in the plant's spread across open landscapes.

Kochia brachyptera is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
