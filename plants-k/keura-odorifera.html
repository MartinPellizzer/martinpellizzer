Keura odorifera

Keura odorifera, commonly known as the sweet-smelling orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, while its roots are often covered in a spongy, white coating.

The flowers emit a pleasant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
