Kalmia myrtifolia

Kalmia myrtifolia, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a common sight in moist woodland areas. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers bloom in late spring.

Kalmia myrtifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
