Kentrophyta rotunda

Kentrophyta rotunda, commonly known as the round-kentrophy, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive round, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential pharmacological applications.
