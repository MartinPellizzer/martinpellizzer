Koniga brunonis

Koniga brunonis, commonly known as the bluebell of the forest, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often forming bell-shaped clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Koniga brunonis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for early pollinators.
