Koordersiochloa javanica

Koordersiochloa javanica, commonly known as the Java grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Koordersiochloa javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
