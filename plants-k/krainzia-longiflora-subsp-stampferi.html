Krainzia longiflora subsp. stampferi

Krainzia longiflora subsp.

stampferi, commonly known as the Stampfer's krainzia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in horticulture for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
