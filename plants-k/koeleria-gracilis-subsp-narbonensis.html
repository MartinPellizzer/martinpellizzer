Koeleria gracilis subsp. narbonensis

Koeleria gracilis subsp.

narbonensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial grass with slender, blue-green blades that form dense mats.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale blue to purple florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
