Koeleria gracilis var. latifolia

Koeleria gracilis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf winter grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are broader than the standard variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in various habitats.
