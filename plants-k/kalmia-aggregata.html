Kalmia aggregata

Kalmia aggregata, commonly known as the clustered mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forest understories. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and the flowers, which bloom in late spring, are usually pink or red.

Kalmia aggregata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
