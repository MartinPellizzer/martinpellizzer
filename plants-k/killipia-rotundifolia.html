Killipia rotundifolia

Killipia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf killipia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features rounded, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to the forest floor.

Killipia rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
