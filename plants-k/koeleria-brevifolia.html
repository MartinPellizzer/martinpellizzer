Koeleria brevifolia

Koeleria brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, and is often found in the western United States.

Koeleria brevifolia is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its resilience in harsh environments.
