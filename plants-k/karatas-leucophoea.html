Karatas leucophoea

Karatas leucophoea, commonly known as the white-flowered karatas, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sun-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially grown.
