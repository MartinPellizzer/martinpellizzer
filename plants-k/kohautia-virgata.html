Kohautia virgata

Kohautia virgata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Kohautia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
