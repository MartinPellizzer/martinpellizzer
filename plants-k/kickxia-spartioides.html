Kickxia spartioides

Kickxia spartioides, commonly known as the desert larkspur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a hairy texture to reduce water loss.
