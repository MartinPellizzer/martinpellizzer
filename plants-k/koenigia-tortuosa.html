Koenigia tortuosa

Koenigia tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
