Kaschgaria brachanthemoides

Kaschgaria brachanthemoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
