Kaukenia surinamensis

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
