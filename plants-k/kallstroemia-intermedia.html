Kallstroemia intermedia

Kallstroemia intermedia, commonly known as the western false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
