Kalanchoe spathulata var. garambiensis

Kalanchoe spathulata var. garambiensis, commonly known as the Garambiensis Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
