Kennedynella microphylla

Kennedynella microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, densely packed leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
