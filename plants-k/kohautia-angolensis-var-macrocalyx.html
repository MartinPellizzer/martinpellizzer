Kohautia angolensis var. macrocalyx

Kohautia angolensis var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the Angolan giant kohautia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in length, typically displaying vibrant pink or red hues. The plant features a robust, fleshy rhizome and strap-like, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and collections.
