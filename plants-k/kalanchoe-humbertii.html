Kalanchoe humbertii

Kalanchoe humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid native environment. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family, known for its ability to propagate through offsets and leaf cuttings.

Kalanchoe humbertii is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal.
