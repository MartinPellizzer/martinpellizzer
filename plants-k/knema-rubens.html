Knema rubens

Knema rubens, commonly known as the red knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
