Kochia scoparia subsp. culta

Kochia scoparia subsp.

culta, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Eurasia. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that display red, orange, or purple hues in the fall.

The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with upright, branching stems. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Kochia scoparia subsp.

culta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its seasonal color and low maintenance requirements.
