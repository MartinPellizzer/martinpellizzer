Karatas spectabilis

Karatas spectabilis, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thick, flaky bark that provides natural insulation against extreme temperatures. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in dry climates.
