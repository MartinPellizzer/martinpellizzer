Knautia dinarica subsp. silana

Knautia dinarica subsp.

silana, commonly known as the Dinaric knautia, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in mountainous regions. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowering stems are upright and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
