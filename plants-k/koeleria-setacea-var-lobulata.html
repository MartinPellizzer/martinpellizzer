Koeleria setacea var. lobulata

Koeleria setacea var. lobulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
