Kyllinga tetragona

Kyllinga tetragona, commonly known as purple swampmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with purple or red, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, red or purple berries. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Kyllinga tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
