Kayea clemensiorum

Kayea clemensiorum, commonly known as the Hawaiian orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is often found on the island of Maui. Kayea clemensiorum is known for its delicate, strap-like leaves and is considered an important part of Hawaii's native flora.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is also classified as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
