Kiggelaria ferruginea

Kiggelaria ferruginea, commonly known as the red ironwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction and furniture. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a reddish or brownish hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Kiggelaria ferruginea is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
