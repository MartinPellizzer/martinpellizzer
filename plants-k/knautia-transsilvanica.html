Knautia transsilvanica

Knautia transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Knautia transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
