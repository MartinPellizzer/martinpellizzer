Kalanchoe dangeardii

Kalanchoe dangeardii, commonly known as the Dangeard's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils.

Kalanchoe dangeardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
