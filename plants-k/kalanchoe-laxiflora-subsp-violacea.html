Kalanchoe laxiflora subsp. violacea

Kalanchoe laxiflora subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, and it produces striking violet or purple flowers in clusters during the winter months.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and low maintenance requirements.

Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
