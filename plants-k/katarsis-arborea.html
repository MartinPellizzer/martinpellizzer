Katarsis arborea

Katarsis arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
