Kalanchoe corymbosa

Kalanchoe corymbosa, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. It produces offsets, or "pups," which can be propagated to grow new plants.

Kalanchoe corymbosa is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
