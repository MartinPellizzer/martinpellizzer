Kalimeris fastigiata

Kalimeris fastigiata, commonly known as the upright aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and yellow.

Kalimeris fastigiata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
