Kadakia

Kadakia, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Kadakia has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
