Kalanchoe bentii subsp. bentii

Kalanchoe bentii subsp.

bentii, commonly known as the Christmas chrysanthemum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery white coating, known as farina.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is well adapted to arid environments. Kalanchoe bentii subsp.

bentii is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and long blooming period.
