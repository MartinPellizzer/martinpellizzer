Kyllinga pluristaminea

Kyllinga pluristaminea, commonly known as the red swamp kyllinga, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant produces bright red or purple bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
