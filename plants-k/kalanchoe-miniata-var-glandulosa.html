Kalanchoe miniata var. glandulosa

Kalanchoe miniata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, glandular coating, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
