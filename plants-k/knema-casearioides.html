Knema casearioides

Knema casearioides, commonly known as the broadleaf knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The wood of Knema casearioides is valued for its durability and is used in local construction and furniture-making.
