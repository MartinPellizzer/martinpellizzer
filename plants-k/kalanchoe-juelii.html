Kalanchoe juelii

Kalanchoe juelii, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a pale green to blue-gray coloration. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers during the winter months, which resemble small chandeliers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Kalanchoe juelii is drought-tolerant and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its ornamental value.
