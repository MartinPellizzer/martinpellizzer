Khaya punchii

Khaya punchii, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwood trees. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruits are drupes that contain a single seed, which is the source of shea butter.

Khaya punchii is valued for its durable timber and ecological importance in its native habitats.
