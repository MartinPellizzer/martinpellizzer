Koeleria permollis

Koeleria permollis, commonly known as the softleaf grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. Koeleria permollis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its foliage is noted for its soft, velvety texture, which distinguishes it from other grass species.
