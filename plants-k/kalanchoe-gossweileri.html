Kalanchoe gossweileri

Kalanchoe gossweileri, commonly known as the Cape York kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It is a shrub-like species that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall, with a compact and bushy appearance.

Kalanchoe gossweileri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and prolonged blooming period.
