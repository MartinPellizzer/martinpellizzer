Knautia hybrida var. integrifolia

Knautia hybrida var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf knautia, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are typically lobed or deeply divided, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.
