Kniphofia arussi

Kniphofia arussi, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia arussi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
