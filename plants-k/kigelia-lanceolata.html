Kigelia lanceolata

Kigelia lanceolata, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its long, sausage-shaped pods that contain a sticky, milky latex. The tree produces showy, pale yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its unique appearance and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural rubber.
