Kratzmannia imbricata

Kratzmannia imbricata, commonly known as the Cape Flats daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow-centered flowers with white ray florets.

Kratzmannia imbricata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
