Keraselma spatulatum

Keraselma spatulatum, commonly known as the spoonleaf orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spoon-shaped leaves, which give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its rare beauty.
