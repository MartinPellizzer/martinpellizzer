Koeleria chevallieri

Koeleria chevallieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Koeleria chevallieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
