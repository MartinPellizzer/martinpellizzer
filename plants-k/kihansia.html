Kihansia

Kihansia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. Kihansia species typically have thick, fleshy leaves that help them retain moisture in their natural habitat. The flowers are usually borne on long, slender stalks and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

They thrive in warm, humid environments and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
