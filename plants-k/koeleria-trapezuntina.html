Koeleria trapezuntina

Koeleria trapezuntina, commonly known as the Trapezuntine grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Koeleria trapezuntina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
