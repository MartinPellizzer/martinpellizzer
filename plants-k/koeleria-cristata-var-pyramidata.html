Koeleria cristata var. pyramidata

Koeleria cristata var. pyramidata, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Koeleria cristata var. pyramidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
