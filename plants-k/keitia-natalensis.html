Keitia natalensis

Keitia natalensis, commonly known as Natal grass or Natal flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, riverbanks, and damp grasslands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Keitia natalensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
