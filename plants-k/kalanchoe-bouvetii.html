Kalanchoe bouvetii

Kalanchoe bouvetii, commonly known as the "Mother of Millions," is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months, which are followed by small offsets that can be easily propagated. Its ability to produce numerous plantlets along the edges of its leaves contributes to its common name.

Kalanchoe bouvetii is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and aesthetic appeal.
