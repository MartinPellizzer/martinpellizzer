Kipandiorchis jamirusii

Kipandiorchis jamirusii, commonly known as the Jamirus orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the highlands of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its flowers are small, delicate, and often display a combination of white, green, and purple hues.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
