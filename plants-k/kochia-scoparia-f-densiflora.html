Kochia scoparia f. densiflora

Kochia scoparia f. densiflora, commonly known as Russian spinach or fireweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant is native to Eurasia and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly salty taste, making it a traditional food source in some cultures.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
