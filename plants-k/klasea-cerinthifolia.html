Klasea cerinthifolia

Klasea cerinthifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
