Kosteletzkya althaeifolia

Kosteletzkya althaeifolia, commonly known as the yellow marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. Its stems are succulent and can grow up to several feet tall, often forming dense colonies in moist soils.

Kosteletzkya althaeifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
