Kaempferia decus-silvae

Kaempferia decus-silvae, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the rainforests of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with a pseudobulb and strap-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Kaempferia decus-silvae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
