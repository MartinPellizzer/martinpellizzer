Krascheninnikovia ceratoides var. bole-deserticola

Krascheninnikovia ceratoides var. bole-deserticola, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming after rainfall. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

This variety is well-suited to extreme environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
