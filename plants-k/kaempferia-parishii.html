Kaempferia parishii

Kaempferia parishii, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with yellow veins. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

Kaempferia parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for horticultural purposes.
