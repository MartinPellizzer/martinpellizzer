Knema glaucescens var. patentinervia

Knema glaucescens var. patentinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominently visible veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules as its fruit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
