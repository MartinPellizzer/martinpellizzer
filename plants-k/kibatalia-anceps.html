Kibatalia anceps

Kibatalia anceps, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, round, yellow fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Kibatalia anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
