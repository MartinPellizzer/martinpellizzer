Koeleria phleoides var. subcapitata

Koeleria phleoides var. subcapitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
