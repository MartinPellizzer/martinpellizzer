Kopsia rosea

Kopsia rosea, commonly known as the rose bush or rose plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Kopsia rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
