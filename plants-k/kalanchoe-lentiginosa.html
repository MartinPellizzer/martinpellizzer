Kalanchoe lentiginosa

Kalanchoe lentiginosa, commonly known as the "Lucky Plant" or "Mother of Millions," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the winter months. Its leaves are covered in tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes, which help reduce water loss.

Kalanchoe lentiginosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
