Khaya

Khaya, commonly known as African shea or African mahogany, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

These trees are native to tropical regions of Africa and are valued for their durable wood. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a feathery appearance. The flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, and the fruits are legumes containing edible seeds.

Khaya species are also used in traditional medicine and for their ornamental value.
