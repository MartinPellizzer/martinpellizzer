Klasea viciifolia

Klasea viciifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Klasea viciifolia is valued for its nutritional seeds and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.
