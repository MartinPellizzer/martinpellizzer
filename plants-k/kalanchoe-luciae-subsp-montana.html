Kalanchoe luciae subsp. montana

Kalanchoe luciae subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in landscaping and indoor settings.
