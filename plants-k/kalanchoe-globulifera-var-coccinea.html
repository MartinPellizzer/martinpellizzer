Kalanchoe globulifera var. coccinea

Kalanchoe globulifera var. coccinea, commonly known as the red globulifera kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or pink hues. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
