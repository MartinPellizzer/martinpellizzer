Krapovickasia urticifolia

Krapovickasia urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Krapovickasia urticifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
