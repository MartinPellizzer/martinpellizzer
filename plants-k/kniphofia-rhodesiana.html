Kniphofia rhodesiana

Kniphofia rhodesiana, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that range in color from fiery red to orange and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia rhodesiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
