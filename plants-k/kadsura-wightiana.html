Kadsura wightiana

Kadsura wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's kadsura, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that resemble a serpent. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
