Kedarnatha

Kedarnatha, commonly known as the sacred Himalayan plant, is a rare and revered species found in the high altitudes of the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant is often associated with spiritual significance and is believed to have medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices. Its botanical name, *Sarcoptera fraxinea*, reflects its unique leaf structure and habitat.

Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this culturally and ecologically important species.
