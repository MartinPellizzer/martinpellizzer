Kalanchoe gracilis

Kalanchoe gracilis, commonly known as the slender kalanchoe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, which are followed by distinctive seed capsules. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its leaves and stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Kalanchoe gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
