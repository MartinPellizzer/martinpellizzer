Karatas marmorata

Karatas marmorata, commonly known as the marble tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The tree is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, provided it receives adequate sunlight and drainage.
