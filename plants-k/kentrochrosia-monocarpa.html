Kentrochrosia monocarpa

Kentrochrosia monocarpa, commonly known as the single-seeded orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, solitary seed pod. The plant produces a single, elongated seed capsule that contains a single seed, which is a distinctive feature of the species. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Kentrochrosia monocarpa is often grown as a rare and exotic ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
