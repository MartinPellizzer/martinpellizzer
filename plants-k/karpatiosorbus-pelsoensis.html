Karpatiosorbus pelsoensis

Karpatiosorbus pelsoensis, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky and mountainous habitats, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
