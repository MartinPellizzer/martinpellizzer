Knautia purpurea subsp. subscaposa

Knautia purpurea subsp.

subscaposa, commonly known as the purple knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and long blooming period.
