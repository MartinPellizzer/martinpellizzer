Kuhlia brasiliensis

Kuhlia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface, with small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. Its flowers are inconspicuous, producing small, greenish-white blooms that are followed by three-lobed fruits.

Kuhlia brasiliensis plays a role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
