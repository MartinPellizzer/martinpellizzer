Kalanchoe thyrsiflora

Kalanchoe thyrsiflora, commonly known as the "Christmas chrysanthemum," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Kalanchoe thyrsiflora is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which is a common method for propagation in horticulture.
