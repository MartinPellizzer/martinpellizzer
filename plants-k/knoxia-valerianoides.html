Knoxia valerianoides

Knoxia valerianoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Knoxia valerianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
