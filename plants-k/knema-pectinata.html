Knema pectinata

Knema pectinata, commonly known as the white knema, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
