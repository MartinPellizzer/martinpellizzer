Koeleria gracilis var. conglobata

Koeleria gracilis var. conglobata, commonly known as the clustered slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color, and it is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
