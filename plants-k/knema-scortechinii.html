Knema scortechinii

Knema scortechinii, commonly known as the white knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
