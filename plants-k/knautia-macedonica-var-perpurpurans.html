Knautia macedonica var. perpurpurans

Knautia macedonica var. perpurpurans, commonly known as the Macedonian knautia, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
