Knautia ceretanica

Knautia ceretanica, commonly known as the Balkan knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Knautia ceretanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
