Kalimeris tatarica

Kalimeris tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Kalimeris tatarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
