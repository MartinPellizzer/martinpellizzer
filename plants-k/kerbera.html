Kerbera

Kerbera, commonly known as the bluebell or the bluebell orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

These plants are native to various regions in South America, particularly in Brazil and Argentina. Kerbera species are characterized by their striking blue or violet flowers, which often resemble bells, hence their common name. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

The genus includes several species, each with unique floral structures and growth habits.
