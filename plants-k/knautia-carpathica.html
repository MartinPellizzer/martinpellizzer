Knautia carpathica

Knautia carpathica, commonly known as Carpathian knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Knautia carpathica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
