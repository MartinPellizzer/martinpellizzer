Kerria japonica f. vittatoramosa

Kerria japonica f. vittatoramosa, commonly known as the variegated kerria, is a compact shrub characterized by its ornamental foliage.

Its leaves are distinctly variegated, featuring a combination of yellow, white, and green colors, which add visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn. It is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade to full sun, preferring well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant foliage and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
