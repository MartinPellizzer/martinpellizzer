Kniphofia umbrina

Kniphofia umbrina, commonly known as the torch lily or red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright red to orange tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia umbrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
