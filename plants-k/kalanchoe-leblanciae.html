Kalanchoe leblanciae

Kalanchoe leblanciae, commonly known as the leblanc kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Kalanchoe leblanciae is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
