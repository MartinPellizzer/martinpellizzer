Koeleria cristata f. latifolia

Koeleria cristata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It is native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rocky landscapes.
