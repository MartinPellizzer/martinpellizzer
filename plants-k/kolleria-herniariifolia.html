Kolleria herniariifolia

Kolleria herniariifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
