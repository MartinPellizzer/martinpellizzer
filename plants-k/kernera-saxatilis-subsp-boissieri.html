Kernera saxatilis subsp. boissieri

Kernera saxatilis subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the rock kernera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
