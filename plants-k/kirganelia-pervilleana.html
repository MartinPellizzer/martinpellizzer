Kirganelia pervilleana

Kirganelia pervilleana, commonly known as the Kashmir apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
