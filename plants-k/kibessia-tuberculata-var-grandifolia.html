Kibessia tuberculata var. grandifolia

Kibessia tuberculata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant tubercled kibessia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
