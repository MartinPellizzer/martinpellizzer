Kochia saxicola

Kochia saxicola, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Kochia saxicola is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
