Kengyilia carinata

Kengyilia carinata, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Kengyilia carinata has narrow, linear leaves and a tall, branching stem that supports its showy blooms.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
