Kibessia pachyphylla

Kibessia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved kibessia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its robust root system helps it anchor in rocky soils and access deep groundwater.

Kibessia pachyphylla is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
