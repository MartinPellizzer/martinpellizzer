Kalanchoe scandens

Kalanchoe scandens, commonly known as the creeping kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that can grow up to 3 feet tall, often branching out to form a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Kalanchoe scandens is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and low maintenance requirements.
