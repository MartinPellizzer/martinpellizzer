Kochia latifolia var. inermis

Kochia latifolia var. inermis, commonly known as Russian knout, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its stout, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense, spherical clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically visited by pollinators. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
