Kixia

Kixia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to East Asia.

It consists of several species, most notably Kixia rhipidoides, which is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Kixia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is typically broad and evergreen, adding visual interest throughout the year.
