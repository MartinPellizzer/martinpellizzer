Knautia arvensis var. lanceolata

Knautia arvensis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaved knautia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces bright pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
