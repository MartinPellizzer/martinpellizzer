Kaempferia galanga

Kaempferia galanga, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with three petals and three sepals.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic and medicinal properties.
