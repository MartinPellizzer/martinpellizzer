Knema hirtella

Knema hirtella, commonly known as the hairy knema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, contributing to its propagation in tropical rainforest environments.
