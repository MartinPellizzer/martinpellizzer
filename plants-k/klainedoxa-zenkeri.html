Klainedoxa zenkeri

Klainedoxa zenkeri, commonly known as the Zambesi lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive white throat. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Klainedoxa zenkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
