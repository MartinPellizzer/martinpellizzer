Kochia villosa var. microcarpa

Kochia villosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, spiny fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, attracting pollinators.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant.
