Kalanchoe pinnata

Kalanchoe pinnata, commonly known as the miracle leaf or mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are also notable for their ability to propagate new plants when they fall to the ground, a feature that contributes to its widespread distribution.

Kalanchoe pinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
