Karelinia caspia

Karelinia caspia, commonly known as the Caspian sandthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny leaves, which are adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Karelinia caspia thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Its resilience makes it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
