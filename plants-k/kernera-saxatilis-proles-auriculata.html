Kernera saxatilis proles auriculata

Kernera saxatilis proles auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate kernera, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
