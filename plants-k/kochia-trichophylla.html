Kochia trichophylla

Kochia trichophylla, commonly known as the silky kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, vibrant flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid regions. Kochia trichophylla is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, where it thrives in full sun.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological studies and soil stabilization efforts.
