Kalanchoe bergeri

Kalanchoe bergeri, commonly known as the "Himalayan Kalanchoe," is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of northern India and Nepal.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Kalanchoe bergeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and resilience.
