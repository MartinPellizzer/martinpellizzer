Kopsia jasminiflora

Kopsia jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine flower vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble jasmine, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, round berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Kopsia jasminiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
