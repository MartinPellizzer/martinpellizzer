Kalanchoe craibii

Kalanchoe craibii, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in winter, typically around December to February. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Kalanchoe craibii is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
