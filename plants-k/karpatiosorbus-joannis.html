Karpatiosorbus joannis

Karpatiosorbus joannis, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red-orange berries in the autumn. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Karpatiosorbus joannis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
