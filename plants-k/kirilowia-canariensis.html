Kirilowia canariensis

Kirilowia canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of pine native to the Canary Islands.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal areas and volcanic soils. The tree has needle-like leaves and produces cones that contain seeds. It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which is thick and fissured.

This plant plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
