Koeleria niederleinii var. pseudobergii

Koeleria niederleinii var. pseudobergii, commonly known as the Berg's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in narrow spikelets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
