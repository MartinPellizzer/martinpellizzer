Kingia

Kingia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, upright, grass-like foliage and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Kingia is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its distinctive appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
