Kanburia

Kanburia, commonly known as the wild cabbage, is a hardy perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, white or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its roots are often harvested for their edible, slightly bitter taste and nutritional value.

Kanburia is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
