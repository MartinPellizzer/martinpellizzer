Koeleria lepida

Koeleria lepida, commonly known as slender wild oat, is a low-growing perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its seeds are enclosed in dry, two-lobed pods and are dispersed by wind.

Koeleria lepida is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
