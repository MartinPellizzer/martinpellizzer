Kunzea recurva var. montana

Kunzea recurva var. montana, commonly known as the mountain kunzea, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Kunzea recurva var. montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
