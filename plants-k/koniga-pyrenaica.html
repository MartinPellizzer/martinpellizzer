Koniga pyrenaica

Koniga pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Koniga pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
