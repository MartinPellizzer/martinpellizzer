Kayea myrtifolia

Kayea myrtifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and allspice. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits.

Kayea myrtifolia is valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions.
