Koeleria capensis

Koeleria capensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically in shades of green or brown. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Koeleria capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh conditions.
