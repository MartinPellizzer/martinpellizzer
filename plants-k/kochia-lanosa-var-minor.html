Kochia lanosa var. minor

Kochia lanosa var. minor, commonly known as the velvetleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and striking, spiny seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
