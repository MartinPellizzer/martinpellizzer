Kippistia

Kippistia, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its elegant, often fragrant blooms and is native to high-altitude regions in South America. The plants typically have slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to harsh, cold environments. Kippistia species are epiphytic or lithophytic, meaning they grow on other plants or rocks rather than in soil.

Their unique botanical features make them valuable subjects for botanical research and conservation efforts.
