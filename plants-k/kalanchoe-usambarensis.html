Kalanchoe usambarensis

Kalanchoe usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambaran Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Tanzania.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the dry season. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings.

Kalanchoe usambarensis is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
