Knema cinerea var. cordata

Knema cinerea var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its foliage is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
