Koeleria phleoides var. submutica

Koeleria phleoides var. submutica, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are usually grayish-green in color, and the plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Koeleria phleoides var. submutica is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
