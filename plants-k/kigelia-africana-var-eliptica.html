Kigelia africana var. eliptica

Kigelia africana var. eliptica, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruits that are typically green to yellow in color. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is edible when ripe but is generally used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
