Kalanchoe curvula

Kalanchoe curvula, commonly known as the coral pine or red rooster, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically red or orange in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Kalanchoe curvula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and resilience.
