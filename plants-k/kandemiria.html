Kandemiria

Kandemiria, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Kandemiria thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, moist climate to flourish.
