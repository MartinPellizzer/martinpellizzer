Knautia sylvatica var. carniolica

Knautia sylvatica var. carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian knautia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
