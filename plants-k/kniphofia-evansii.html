Kniphofia evansii

Kniphofia evansii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of vivid red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia evansii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
