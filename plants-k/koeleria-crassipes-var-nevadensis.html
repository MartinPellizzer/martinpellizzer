Koeleria crassipes var. nevadensis

Koeleria crassipes var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada rough fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This variety thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its flowering spikes are usually compact and bear small, spikelet-like flowers.

Koeleria crassipes var. nevadensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
