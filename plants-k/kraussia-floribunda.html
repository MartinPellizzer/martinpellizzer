Kraussia floribunda

Kraussia floribunda, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellowwood, is a South African shrub or small tree belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored aril. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant with drought tolerance.

Kraussia floribunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
