Koeleria asiatica subsp. atroviolacea

Koeleria asiatica subsp.

atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet koeleria, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms with violet to purple flowers, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
