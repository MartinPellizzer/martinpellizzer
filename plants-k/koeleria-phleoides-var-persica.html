Koeleria phleoides var. persica

Koeleria phleoides var. persica, commonly known as Persian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
