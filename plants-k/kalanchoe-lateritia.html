Kalanchoe lateritia

Kalanchoe lateritia, commonly known as the rock kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

Kalanchoe lateritia is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
