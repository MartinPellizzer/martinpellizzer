Koeleria calarashica

Koeleria calarashica, commonly known as the Calarashic grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or pale blue in color. Koeleria calarashica thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
