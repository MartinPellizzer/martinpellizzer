Kochia sericea

Kochia sericea, commonly known as summer cress or Russian spinach, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its prostrate or erect growth habit. The plant features small, green, diamond-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer. Its seeds are covered in a white, powdery coating, which aids in wind dispersal.

Kochia sericea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.
