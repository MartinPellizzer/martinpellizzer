Korthalsella opuntia var. bojeri

Korthalsella opuntia var. bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's prickly pear, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flattened, spine-tipped stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
