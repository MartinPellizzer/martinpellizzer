Kuhnia triplinervis

Kuhnia triplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing sori that are located on the underside of the fronds.

Kuhnia triplinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
