Kersia suffruticosa

Kersia suffruticosa, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Kersia suffruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
