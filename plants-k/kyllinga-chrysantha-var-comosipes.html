Kyllinga chrysantha var. comosipes

Kyllinga chrysantha var. comosipes, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, and it produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and landscape design.
