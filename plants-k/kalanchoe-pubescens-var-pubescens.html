Kalanchoe pubescens var. pubescens

Kalanchoe pubescens var. pubescens, commonly known as the wooly kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the winter months, typically blooming on branched stems. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and ease of care.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-water conditions make it a favored choice for rock gardens and container planting.
