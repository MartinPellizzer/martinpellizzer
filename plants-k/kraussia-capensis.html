Kraussia capensis

Kraussia capensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by reddish-brown capsules containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

Kraussia capensis is also an important species for biodiversity, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
