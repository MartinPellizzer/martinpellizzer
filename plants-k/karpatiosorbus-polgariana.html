Karpatiosorbus polgariana

Karpatiosorbus polgariana, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Karpatiosorbus polgariana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
