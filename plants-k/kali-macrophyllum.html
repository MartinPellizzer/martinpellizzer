Kali macrophyllum

Kali macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its flowers, which bloom at night, are large and showy, with a white or pale pink coloration.

Kali macrophyllum plays an important ecological role by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration in its native habitats.
