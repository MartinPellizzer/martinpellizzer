Kniphofia modesta var. woodii

Kniphofia modesta var. woodii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stalks that bear bright orange-red tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge along the edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a standout in both garden and horticultural settings.
