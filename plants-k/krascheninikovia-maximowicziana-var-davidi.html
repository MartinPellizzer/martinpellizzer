Krascheninikovia maximowicziana var. davidi

Krascheninikovia maximowicziana var. davidi, commonly known as the David's feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It features delicate, silver-gray foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat on the ground. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered along the stems, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
