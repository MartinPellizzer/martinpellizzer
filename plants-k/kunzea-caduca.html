Kunzea caduca

Kunzea caduca, commonly known as the white kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Kunzea caduca is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils, and it is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

Its essential oils are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
