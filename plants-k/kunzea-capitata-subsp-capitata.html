Kunzea capitata subsp. capitata

Kunzea capitata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the coastal kunzea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant feature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
