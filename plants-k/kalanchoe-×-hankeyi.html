Kalanchoe × hankeyi

Kalanchoe × hankeyi, commonly known as the hybrid kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ease of care and long-lasting blooms.

Kalanchoe × hankeyi is also valued for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it a popular choice for propagation in horticulture.
