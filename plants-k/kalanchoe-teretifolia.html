Kalanchoe teretifolia

Kalanchoe teretifolia, commonly known as the Mother of Thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are covered in tiny, plantlet-producing notches along the edges. These plantlets can take root and grow into new plants, making propagation easy. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
