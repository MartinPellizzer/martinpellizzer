Krigia biflora

Krigia biflora, commonly known as the twinflower sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a central disk and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Krigia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
