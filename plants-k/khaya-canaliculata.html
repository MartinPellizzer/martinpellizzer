Khaya canaliculata

Khaya canaliculata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its seeds are rich in oils and are used in the production of shea butter, which has various industrial and cosmetic applications.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
