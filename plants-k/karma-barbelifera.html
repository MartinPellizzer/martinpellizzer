Karma barbelifera

Karma barbelifera, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the East, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to the wetlands and slow-moving waters of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant's roots are often submerged, while its stems and leaves float on the water's surface. Its flowers, which can be pink, white, or yellow, bloom in a sequential pattern, with each flower opening and closing throughout the day.

This unique botanical behavior has made it a symbol of purity and spiritual growth in various cultures.
