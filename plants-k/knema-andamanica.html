Knema andamanica

Knema andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
