Koevia serbica

Koevia serbica, commonly known as the Serbica bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Koevia serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
