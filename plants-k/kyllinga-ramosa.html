Kyllinga ramosa

Kyllinga ramosa, commonly known as the hairy kyllinga, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that often branch profusely, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by triangular, red or purple seed capsules. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Kyllinga ramosa is often found in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands.
