Kalanchoe brachyloba

Kalanchoe brachyloba, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. Its unique reproductive method involves small plantlets growing along the edges of its leaves, which can easily take root and grow into new plants.

Kalanchoe brachyloba is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
