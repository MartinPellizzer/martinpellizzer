Kalmia latifolia var. obtusata

Kalmia latifolia var. obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscape design for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
