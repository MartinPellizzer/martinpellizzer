Kerria japonica var. flore-pleno

Kerria japonica var. flore-pleno, commonly known as the double-flowered Japanese kerria, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and its profuse display of double, showy yellow flowers in early spring. The leaves are dark green, leathery, and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.

It is also relatively disease-resistant and easy to maintain, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
