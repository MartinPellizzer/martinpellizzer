Koeleria gracilis var. amurensis

Koeleria gracilis var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually green to reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
