Kedrostis mildbraedii

Kedrostis mildbraedii, commonly known as the African giant cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers typical of the gourd family. Its vines are vigorous climbers, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some parts of Africa.
