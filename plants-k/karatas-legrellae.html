Karatas legrellae

Karatas legrellae, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent leaves. Its flowers are typically pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Karatas legrellae plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar and habitat for various desert species.
