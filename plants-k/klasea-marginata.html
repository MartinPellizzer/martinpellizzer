Klasea marginata

Klasea marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver along the edges. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a prominent central stamen. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Klasea marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
