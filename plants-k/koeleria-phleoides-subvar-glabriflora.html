Koeleria phleoides subvar. glabriflora

Koeleria phleoides subvar. glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
