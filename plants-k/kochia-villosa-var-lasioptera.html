Kochia villosa var. lasioptera

Kochia villosa var. lasioptera, commonly known as the velvetleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
