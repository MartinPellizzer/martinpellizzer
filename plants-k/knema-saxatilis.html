Knema saxatilis

Knema saxatilis, commonly known as the rock knema, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, woody fruits. Knema saxatilis typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the forest understory in its native habitat.
