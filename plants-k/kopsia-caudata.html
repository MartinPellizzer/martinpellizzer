Kopsia caudata

Kopsia caudata, commonly known as the red-flowered kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. Its botanical characteristics include showy inflorescences and a preference for well-drained soil.

Kopsia caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
