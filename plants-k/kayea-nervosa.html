Kayea nervosa

Kayea nervosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian banyan, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pendulous fruits. The plant has a complex root system that includes aerial roots, which help it anchor to rocky substrates. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary clusters, and it relies on insect pollination.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various native species.
