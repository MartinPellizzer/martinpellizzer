Kalmia microphylla

Kalmia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Kalmia microphylla is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
