Kochia cheelii

Kochia cheelii, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, spiny leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish-purple flower clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Kochia cheelii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy environments.

It is also valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisine.
