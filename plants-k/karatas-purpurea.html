Karatas purpurea

Karatas purpurea, commonly known as the purple karatas, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil.

Karatas purpurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
