Knema meridionalis

Knema meridionalis, commonly known as the southern knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Knema meridionalis is valued for its aromatic wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
