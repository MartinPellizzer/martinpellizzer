Kallstroemia maxima

Kallstroemia maxima, commonly known as the red-flowered kallstroemia, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Kallstroemia maxima is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
