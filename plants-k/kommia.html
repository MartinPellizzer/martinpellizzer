Kommia

Kommia, commonly known as Indian myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It comprises several species, most notably Kommia birotula, which is native to Southeast Asia. These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, white or pink flowers. They are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

The fruits of Kommia species are typically small and contain a single seed, contributing to their ecological and botanical significance.
