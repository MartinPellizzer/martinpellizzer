Kigelia aethiopica var. usambarica

Kigelia aethiopica var. usambarica, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of lanterns. The plant has thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its fruit is a distinctive, woody capsule that splits open to reveal a single, edible seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
