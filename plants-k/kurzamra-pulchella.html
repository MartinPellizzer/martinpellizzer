Kurzamra pulchella

Kurzamra pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in the summer months, come in shades of pink and purple, attracting various pollinators.

Kurzamra pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
