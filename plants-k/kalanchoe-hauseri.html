Kalanchoe hauseri

Kalanchoe hauseri, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is known for its ability to produce small plantlets along the edges of its leaves, which can be easily removed and grown into new plants. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its resilience and ease of propagation contribute to its widespread cultivation.
