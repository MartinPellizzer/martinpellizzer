Kochia scoparia f. densa

Kochia scoparia f. densa, commonly known as the denseleaf kochia, is a variant of the common kochia, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

This plant is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Eurasia, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It features upright, branching stems covered with small, spine-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flower clusters in shades of pink or red, which attract pollinators.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant in gardens.
