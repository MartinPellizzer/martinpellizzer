Koeleria gerrardii

Koeleria gerrardii, commonly known as the bluegrass or western bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Koeleria gerrardii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
