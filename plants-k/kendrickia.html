Kendrickia

Kendrickia, commonly known as the dwarf lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

These plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base. Kendrickia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in rock gardens and as container plants.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
