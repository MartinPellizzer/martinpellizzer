Knesebeckia ignea

Knesebeckia ignea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red-orange bracts that resemble flames.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by showy bracts.

Knesebeckia ignea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
