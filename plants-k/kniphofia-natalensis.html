Kniphofia natalensis

Kniphofia natalensis, commonly known as the Natal grassblades, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and showy, torch-like inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in horticultural settings.
