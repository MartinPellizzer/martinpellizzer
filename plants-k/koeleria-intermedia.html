Koeleria intermedia

Koeleria intermedia, commonly known as bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, tufted clumps. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, blue-gray flowers.

Koeleria intermedia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
