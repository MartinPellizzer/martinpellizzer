Kunstlerodendron cuspidatum

Kunstlerodendron cuspidatum, commonly known as the spiked beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple to violet berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
