Kalanchoe crenata var. collina

Kalanchoe crenata var. collina, commonly known as the scalloped kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves with a scalloped edge, often tinged with red or purple along the margins. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe crenata var. collina is drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
