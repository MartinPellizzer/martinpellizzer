Koeleria pyramidata var. ciliata

Koeleria pyramidata var. ciliata, commonly known as the Sierra grass or mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
