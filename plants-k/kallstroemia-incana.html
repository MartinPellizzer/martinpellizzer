Kallstroemia incana

Kallstroemia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

Kallstroemia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
