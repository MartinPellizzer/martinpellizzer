Kochia thesioides

Kochia thesioides, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the amaranth family and is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage, which becomes more vivid in the fall. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense, fluffy heads, which are typically visible in late summer. Kochia thesioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and ease of growth.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
