Kalanchoe integerrima

Kalanchoe integerrima, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers during the winter months. Its unique ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it a popular choice for gardeners.

Kalanchoe integerrima is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
