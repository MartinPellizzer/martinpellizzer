Kadua cordata

Kadua cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf kadua, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season.

Kadua cordata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
