Kalanchoe gomphophylla

Kalanchoe gomphophylla, commonly known as the silver spoon plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in late winter to early spring. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Kalanchoe gomphophylla is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
