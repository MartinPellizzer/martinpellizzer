Kingia australis var. argentea

Kingia australis var. argentea, commonly known as the silver kingia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, cylindrical, blue-green foliage that has a silvery appearance due to a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
