Koeleria delavignei var. barabensis

Koeleria delavignei var. barabensis, commonly known as Barabense tansygrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
