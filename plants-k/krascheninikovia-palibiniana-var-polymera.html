Krascheninikovia palibiniana var. polymera

Krascheninikovia palibiniana var. polymera, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in alpine and subantarctic regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in the study of plant resilience in extreme climates.
