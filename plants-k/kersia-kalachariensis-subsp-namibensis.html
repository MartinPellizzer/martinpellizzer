Kersia kalachariensis subsp. namibensis

Kersia kalachariensis subsp.

namibensis, commonly known as the Namibian kersia, is a succulent shrub native to the Namib Desert. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important component of the desert ecosystem.
