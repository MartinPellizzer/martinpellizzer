Karpatiosorbus haesitans

Karpatiosorbus haesitans, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
