Kalanchoe tieghemii

Kalanchoe tieghemii, commonly known as the "Mother of Thousands," is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and produces clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange, yellow, and red. The plant is notable for its unique propagation method, as it releases tiny plantlets along the edges of its leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Kalanchoe tieghemii is also drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid climates and low-maintenance gardening.
