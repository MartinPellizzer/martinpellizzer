Kalanchoe scapigera

Kalanchoe scapigera, commonly known as the zigzag kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers during the winter months, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. Kalanchoe scapigera is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems to survive drought conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
