Kalmia rosmarinifolia

Kalmia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemaryleaf mountain laurel, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy floral clusters. The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that resemble rosemary, hence its common name. It produces bell-shaped, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Kalmia rosmarinifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.
