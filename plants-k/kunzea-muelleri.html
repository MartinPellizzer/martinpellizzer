Kunzea muelleri

Kunzea muelleri, commonly known as the Grey Kunzea, is a shrub native to the southwest of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for low-maintenance gardens.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional bush tucker.
