Koellikeria erinoides

Koellikeria erinoides, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by round, fleshy berries.

Koellikeria erinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
