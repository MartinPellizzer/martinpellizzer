Klasea aznavouriana

Klasea aznavouriana, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.
