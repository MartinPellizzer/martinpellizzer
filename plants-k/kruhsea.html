Kruhsea

Kruhsea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. Native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
