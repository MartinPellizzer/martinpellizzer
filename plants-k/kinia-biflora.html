Kinia biflora

Kinia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered kinia, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a compact cluster, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Kinia biflora is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its biodiversity and fire-adapted flora.
