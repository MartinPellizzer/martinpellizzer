Kalanchoe adolphi-engleri

Kalanchoe adolphi-engleri, commonly known as the Engler's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and thick, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often in late winter or early spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it easy to cultivate.
