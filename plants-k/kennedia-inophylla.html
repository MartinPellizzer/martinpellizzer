Kennedia inophylla

Kennedia inophylla, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry gardens.

Kennedia inophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
