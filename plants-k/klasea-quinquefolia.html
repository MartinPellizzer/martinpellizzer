Klasea quinquefolia

Klasea quinquefolia, commonly known as five-leafed klasea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves that are often arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas.

Klasea quinquefolia is often found growing along stream banks and in damp forest understories.
