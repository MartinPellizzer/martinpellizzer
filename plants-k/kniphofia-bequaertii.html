Kniphofia bequaertii

Kniphofia bequaertii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators such as bees and birds, adding both visual appeal and ecological value to gardens.
