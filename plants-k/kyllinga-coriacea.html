Kyllinga coriacea

Kyllinga coriacea, commonly known as rough kniphofia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant produces striking spikes of tubular flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kyllinga coriacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
