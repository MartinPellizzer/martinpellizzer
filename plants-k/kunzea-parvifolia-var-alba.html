Kunzea parvifolia var. alba

Kunzea parvifolia var. alba, commonly known as white kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and fragrant appearance. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
