Koeleria arduana

Koeleria arduana, commonly known as the Armenian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Koeleria arduana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
