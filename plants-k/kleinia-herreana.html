Kleinia herreana

Kleinia herreana, commonly known as the silverleaf chamaedorea, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features dense clusters of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Kleinia herreana produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are followed by pale green or white fruits.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
