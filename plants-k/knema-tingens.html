Knema tingens

Knema tingens, commonly known as the red-flowered knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Knema tingens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
