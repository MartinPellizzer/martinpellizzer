Kalanchoe deficiens var. deficiens

Kalanchoe deficiens var. deficiens, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves with a waxy coating that helps it store water. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months. It is known for its ability to propagate through offsets and leaf cuttings.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
