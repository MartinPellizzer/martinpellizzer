Kielmeyera coriacea var. brevisepala

Kielmeyera coriacea var. brevisepala, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture and can grow up to 6 meters in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies.
