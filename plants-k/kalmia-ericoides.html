Kalmia ericoides

Kalmia ericoides, commonly known as mountain heather, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Kalmia ericoides is also known for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing thickets in forested areas.
