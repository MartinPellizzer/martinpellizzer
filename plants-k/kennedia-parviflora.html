Kennedia parviflora

Kennedia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Kennedia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
