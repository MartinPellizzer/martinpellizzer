Kalaharia spinescens

Kalaharia spinescens, commonly known as the spiny kalaharia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. Kalaharia spinescens is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
