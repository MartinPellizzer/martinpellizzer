Kigelia angolensis

Kigelia angolensis, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruits that hang from the branches. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is thick and smooth, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in many African communities.
