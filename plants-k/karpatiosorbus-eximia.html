Karpatiosorbus eximia

Karpatiosorbus eximia, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
