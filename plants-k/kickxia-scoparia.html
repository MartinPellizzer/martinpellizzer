Kickxia scoparia

Kickxia scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scorpionvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, with small, sparsely arranged leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender legume containing several seeds.

Kickxia scoparia is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.
