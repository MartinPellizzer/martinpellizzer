Kalanchoe spathulata var. simlensis

Kalanchoe spathulata var. simlensis, commonly known as the Cape Town kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water retention in arid environments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
