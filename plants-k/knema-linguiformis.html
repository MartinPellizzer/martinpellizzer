Knema linguiformis

Knema linguiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.
