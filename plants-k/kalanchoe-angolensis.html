Kalanchoe angolensis

Kalanchoe angolensis, commonly known as the Angola Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the spring and summer months. Its rosette growth pattern and ability to propagate through offsets make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Kalanchoe angolensis is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
