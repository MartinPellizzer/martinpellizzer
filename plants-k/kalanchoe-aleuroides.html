Kalanchoe aleuroides

Kalanchoe aleuroides, commonly known as the silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of white, powdery hairs, which give it its characteristic silver appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in clusters during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Kalanchoe aleuroides is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
