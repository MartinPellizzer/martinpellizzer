Koniga vogeliana

Koniga vogeliana, commonly known as the Vogeliana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by insects and birds.

Koniga vogeliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
