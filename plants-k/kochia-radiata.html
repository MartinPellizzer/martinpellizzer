Kochia radiata

Kochia radiata, commonly known as burning bush, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that display red, pink, or purple hues in the fall. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are small, oval, and turn red in autumn, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Kochia radiata is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color and drought tolerance.
