Kolobochilus

Kolobochilus, commonly known as the moonflower, is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its large, white, funnel-shaped flowers that open at night. The leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

Kolobochilus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
