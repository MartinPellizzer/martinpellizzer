Kalanchoe bentii subsp. somalica

Kalanchoe bentii subsp.

somalica, commonly known as the Somali kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Somalia and Ethiopia. It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.

Its unique ability to propagate through leaf cuttings makes it easy to grow in both indoor and outdoor settings.
