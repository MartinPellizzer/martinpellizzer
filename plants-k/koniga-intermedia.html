Koniga intermedia

Koniga intermedia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in spikes, and can be white, pink, or red in color.
