Karatas fulgens

Karatas fulgens, commonly known as the flameflower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow throat. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

Karatas fulgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
