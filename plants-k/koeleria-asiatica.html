Koeleria asiatica

Koeleria asiatica, commonly known as the Asian Koeleria, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Siberia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as steppes and rocky soils, and it is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its flowers, which bloom in early summer, are usually pale blue or violet, and the plant is known for its resilience to cold and drought conditions.

Koeleria asiatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
