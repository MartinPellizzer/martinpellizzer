Kentrophyta

Kentrophyta, commonly known as the "carnivorous plants of the genus Kentrophylla," are a group of flowering plants native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

They are characterized by their unique adaptations to nutrient-poor soils, which include specialized structures for capturing and digesting insects. These plants typically have small, clustered leaves that form a rosette, and they produce small, inconspicuous flowers. Their reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and they often thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Kentrophyta play a role in local ecosystems by controlling insect populations and contributing to soil nutrient cycling.
