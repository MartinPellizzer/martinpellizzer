Karangolum ebenaceum

Karangolum ebenaceum, commonly known as the African ebony tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ebenaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The tree typically grows as a medium to large shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.
