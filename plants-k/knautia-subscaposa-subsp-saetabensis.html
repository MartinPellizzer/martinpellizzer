Knautia subscaposa subsp. saetabensis

Knautia subscaposa subsp.

saetabensis, commonly known as the Balkan knautia, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
