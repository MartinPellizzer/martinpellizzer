Kunzea leptospermoides

Kunzea leptospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Kunzea leptospermoides is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in horticulture.
