Kadua foliosa

Kadua foliosa, commonly known as the leafy kadua, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Kadua foliosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
