Knautia arvensis var. vogesiaca

Knautia arvensis var. vogesiaca, commonly known as the Voges knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
