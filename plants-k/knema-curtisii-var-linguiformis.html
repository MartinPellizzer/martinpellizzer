Knema curtisii var. linguiformis

Knema curtisii var. linguiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf knema, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests.

The species is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
