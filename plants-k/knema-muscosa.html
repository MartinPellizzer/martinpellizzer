Knema muscosa

Knema muscosa, commonly known as the silky knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Knema muscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
