Karwinskia humboldtiana

Karwinskia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's karwinskia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Karwinskia humboldtiana is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
