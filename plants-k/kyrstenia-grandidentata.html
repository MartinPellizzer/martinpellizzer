Kyrstenia grandidentata

The plant features compound leaves with large, serrated lobes and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered umbels. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts upon maturity.

Kyrstenia grandidentata is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
