Kielmeyera coriacea

Kielmeyera coriacea, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
