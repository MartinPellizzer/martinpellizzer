Kalidium cuspidatum var. sinicum

Kalidium cuspidatum var. sinicum, commonly known as Chinese grass or sinic grass, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or marshy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine-textured foliage.
