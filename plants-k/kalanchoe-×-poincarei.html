Kalanchoe × poincarei

Kalanchoe × poincarei, commonly known as the Poinsettia, is a hybrid plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Kalanchoe × poincarei is a popular ornamental plant, especially during the holiday season.
