Kosteletzkya pentasperma

Kosteletzkya pentasperma, commonly known as the sea date or sea coconut, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in saline and brackish water environments. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are followed by large, fleshy fruits resembling coconuts. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid coastal conditions.

The fruit contains a single, hard-shelled seed and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
