Kalanchoe maromokotrensis

Kalanchoe maromokotrensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the cooler months. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family, which includes many other succulent species.

Kalanchoe maromokotrensis is well adapted to sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
