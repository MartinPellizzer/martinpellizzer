Knema glaucescens f. rubens

Knema glaucescens f. rubens, commonly known as the red-flowered knema, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to a striking red, depending on the variety. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
