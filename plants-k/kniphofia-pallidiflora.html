Kniphofia pallidiflora

Kniphofia pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Kniphofia, which includes over 70 species of flowering plants, many of which are known for their showy, torch-like inflorescences. The plant features tall, strap-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
