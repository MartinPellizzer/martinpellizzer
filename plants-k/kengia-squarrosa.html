Kengia squarrosa

Kengia squarrosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a smooth, greyish bark and can grow up to 15 meters tall. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Kengia squarrosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
