Kalanchoe bhidei

Kalanchoe bhidei, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that often bear small plantlets along the edges, which can take root and grow into new plants. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardens.

Its unique propagation method and drought tolerance contribute to its adaptability in various environments.
