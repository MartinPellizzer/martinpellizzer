Koeleria gracilis var. buschiana

Koeleria gracilis var. buschiana, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
