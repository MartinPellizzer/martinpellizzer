Kniphofia alooides

Kniphofia alooides, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, tubular red and orange flowers. The plant produces its flowers in spikes that resemble a poker, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia alooides is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
