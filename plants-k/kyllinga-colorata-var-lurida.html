Kyllinga colorata var. lurida

Kyllinga colorata var. lurida, commonly known as the purple swamp cabbage, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features rosettes of glossy, waxy leaves that range in color from deep purple to reddish-brown, with a striking contrast against its white, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or container gardening. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.

Kyllinga colorata var. lurida is also valued for its ability to purify water and support aquatic life in naturalized settings.
