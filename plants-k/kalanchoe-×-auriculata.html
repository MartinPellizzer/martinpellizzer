Kalanchoe × auriculata

Kalanchoe × auriculata, commonly known as the fleshy leaf kalanchoe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with wavy margins and a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, or white, typically during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Kalanchoe × auriculata is also known for its ability to propagate easily through leaf cuttings.
