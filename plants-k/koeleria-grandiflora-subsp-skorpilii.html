Koeleria grandiflora subsp. skorpilii

Koeleria grandiflora subsp.

skorpilii, commonly known as the Skorpil's bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
