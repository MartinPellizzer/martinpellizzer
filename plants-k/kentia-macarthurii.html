Kentia macarthurii

Kentia macarthurii, commonly known as the Macarthur's Kentia, is a species of cycad native to the humid rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires high humidity and indirect light.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
