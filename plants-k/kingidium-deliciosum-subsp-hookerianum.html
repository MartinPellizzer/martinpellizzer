Kingidium deliciosum subsp. hookerianum

Kingidium deliciosum subsp.

hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's kingidium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae. It is native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions of the country.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fragrance.
