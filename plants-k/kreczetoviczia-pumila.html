Kreczetoviczia pumila

Kreczetoviczia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf kreczetoviczia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry garden beds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
