Kalanchoe integra var. crenata

Kalanchoe integra var. crenata, commonly known as the scalycat or scalycat plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive scalloped edge. The leaves are typically green with a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
