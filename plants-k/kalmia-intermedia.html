Kalmia intermedia

Kalmia intermedia, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain-heather, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

Kalmia intermedia is often found in pine forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
