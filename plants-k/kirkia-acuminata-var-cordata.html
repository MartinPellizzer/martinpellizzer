Kirkia acuminata var. cordata

Kirkia acuminata var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped kirkia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are thick and leathery, providing a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
