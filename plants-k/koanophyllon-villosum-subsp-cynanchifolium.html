Koanophyllon villosum subsp. cynanchifolium

Koanophyllon villosum subsp.

cynanchifolium, commonly known as the hairyleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often pubescent, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
