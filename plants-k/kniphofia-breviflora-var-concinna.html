Kniphofia breviflora var. concinna

Kniphofia breviflora var. concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically bright orange-red, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in suitable climates.
