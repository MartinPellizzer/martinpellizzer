Kinghamia angustifolia

Kinghamia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf kinghamia, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular plant in native gardens and conservation efforts.
