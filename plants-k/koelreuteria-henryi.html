Koelreuteria henryi

Koelreuteria henryi, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental, papery bracts that turn red in the fall, giving it a striking visual display. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer, followed by woody, spherical fruits that split open to reveal seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with serrated edges.

Koelreuteria henryi is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil conditions.
