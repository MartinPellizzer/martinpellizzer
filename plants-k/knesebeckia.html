Knesebeckia

Knesebeckia, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, sometimes glandular leaves. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, these plants often thrive in moist, shaded environments. The genus includes several species, with Knesebeckia prostrata being the most well-known.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread readily in suitable conditions.
