Knema latifolia

Knema latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.
