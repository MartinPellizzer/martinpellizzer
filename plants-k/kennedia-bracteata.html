Kennedia bracteata

Kennedia bracteata, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

Kennedia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
