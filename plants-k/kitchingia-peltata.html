Kitchingia peltata

Kitchingia peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf kitchingia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, peltate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Kitchingia peltata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
