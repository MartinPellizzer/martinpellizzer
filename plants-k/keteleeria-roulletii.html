Keteleeria roulletii

Keteleeria roulletii, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a conical crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stress.

The species is valued for its timber and is often used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability to various soil types.
