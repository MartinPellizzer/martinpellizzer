Killipia verticalis

Killipia verticalis, commonly known as the upright killipia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 3 feet tall, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, alternate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Killipia verticalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
