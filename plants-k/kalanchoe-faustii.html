Kalanchoe faustii

Kalanchoe faustii, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months. Its unique ability to propagate through offsets makes it easy to propagate and maintain.

Kalanchoe faustii is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
