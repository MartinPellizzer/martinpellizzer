Koellia clinopodioides

Koellia clinopodioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Koellia clinopodioides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
