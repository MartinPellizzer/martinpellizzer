Koeleria phleoides var. macrantha

Koeleria phleoides var. macrantha, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
