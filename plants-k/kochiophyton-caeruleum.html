Kochiophyton caeruleum

Kochiophyton caeruleum, commonly known as the blue kochia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to blue, depending on the variety. Its foliage is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Kochiophyton caeruleum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
