Knema palembanica

Knema palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
