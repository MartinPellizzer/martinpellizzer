Kallophyllon

Kallophyllon, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Kallophyllon has small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months.

Its ability to tolerate salt spray makes it an important species in maritime ecosystems.
