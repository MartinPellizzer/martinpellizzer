Knema retusa

Knema retusa, commonly known as the white knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Knema retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
