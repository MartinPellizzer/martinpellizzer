Koeleria johnstonii subsp. johnstonii

Koeleria johnstonii subsp.

johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It has narrow leaves with a rough texture and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
