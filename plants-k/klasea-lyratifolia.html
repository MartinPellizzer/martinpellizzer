Klasea lyratifolia

Klasea lyratifolia, commonly known as the lyreleaf klasea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
