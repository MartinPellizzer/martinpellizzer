Kalanchoe kelleriana

Kalanchoe kelleriana, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months. Its unique propagation method involves small plantlets that grow along the edges of its leaves.

Kalanchoe kelleriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
