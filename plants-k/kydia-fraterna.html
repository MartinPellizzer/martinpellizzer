Kydia fraterna

Kydia fraterna, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Kydia fraterna is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
