Kotchubaea laxiflora

Kotchubaea laxiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Kotchubaea laxiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
