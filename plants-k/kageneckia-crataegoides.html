Kageneckia crataegoides

Kageneckia crataegoides, commonly known as the African hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
