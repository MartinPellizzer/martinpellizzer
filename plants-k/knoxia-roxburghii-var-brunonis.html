Knoxia roxburghii var. brunonis

Knoxia roxburghii var. brunonis, commonly known as the purple knoxia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
