Kinginda macrantha

Kinginda macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered kinginda, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its ornamental value and unique floral structure make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
