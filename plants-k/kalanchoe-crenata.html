Kalanchoe crenata

Kalanchoe crenata, commonly known as the scalycup kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often adorned with small, scalloped edges. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Kalanchoe crenata is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets and leaf cuttings.
