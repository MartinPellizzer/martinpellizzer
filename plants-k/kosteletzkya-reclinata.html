Kosteletzkya reclinata

Kosteletzkya reclinata, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, swampy environments.

Kosteletzkya reclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
