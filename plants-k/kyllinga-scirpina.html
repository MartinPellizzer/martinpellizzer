Kyllinga scirpina

Kyllinga scirpina, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, typically reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in waterlogged soils.

Kyllinga scirpina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
