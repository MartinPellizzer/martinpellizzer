Karma cedralensis

Karma cedralensis, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a unique species native to the dense tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange foliage, which resembles a glowing flame, hence its common name. The plant possesses a woody stem with smooth, glossy bark and produces small, fragrant white flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture, adapting to the high humidity and intense sunlight of its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, Karma cedralensis is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a symbol of resilience and spiritual growth.
