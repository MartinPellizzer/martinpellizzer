Kitchingia

Kitchingia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Kitchingia species typically have rhizomatous growth habits, allowing them to spread and form compact rosettes. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
