Koeleria cristata proles gracilis

Koeleria cristata proles gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dense, tufted growth with slender, hair-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
