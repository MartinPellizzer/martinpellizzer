Klasea gmelinii

Klasea gmelinii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in wetland environments.
