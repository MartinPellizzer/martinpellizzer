Kreodanthus myrmex

Kreodanthus myrmex, commonly known as the ant-mimic plant, is a unique species found in specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking resemblance to ants, with elongated, segmented leaves that mimic the appearance of these insects. The plant's foliage is often covered in fine hairs, enhancing its mimicry and providing a protective camouflage. Kreodanthus myrmex thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary adaptation and its role in ecological interactions with local fauna.
