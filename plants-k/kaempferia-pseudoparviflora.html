Kaempferia pseudoparviflora

Kaempferia pseudoparviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. This herbaceous plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
