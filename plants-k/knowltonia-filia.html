Knowltonia filia

Knowltonia filia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
