Kompitsia

Kompitsia, commonly known as the "Greek citrus," is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested in late autumn. The plant has fragrant, dark green leaves and is often grown in warm, sunny climates. Kompitsia is closely related to the citron and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.

Its fruit is valued for its tart flavor and aromatic properties.
