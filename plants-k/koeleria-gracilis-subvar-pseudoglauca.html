Koeleria gracilis subvar. pseudoglauca

Koeleria gracilis subvar. pseudoglauca, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
