Kniphofia latifolia

Kniphofia latifolia, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, torch-like inflorescences that bear vibrant red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a standout addition to many landscapes.
