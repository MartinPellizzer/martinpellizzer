Kumara

Kumara, commonly known as sweet potato, is a tropical root vegetable native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its starchy, sweet flesh and edible tuberous roots. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with heart-shaped leaves and trailing vines that produce the tubers. Kumara comes in a variety of colors, including orange, purple, and white, depending on the variety.

Its high nutritional value makes it a valuable crop in many cuisines worldwide.
