Kaunia arbuscularis

Kaunia arbuscularis, commonly known as the small-leafed burr grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
