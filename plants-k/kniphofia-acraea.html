Kniphofia acraea

Kniphofia acraea, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and vibrant, fiery red or orange inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its showy, torch-like flower spikes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
