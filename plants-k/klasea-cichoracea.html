Klasea cichoracea

Klasea cichoracea, commonly known as the common chicory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Klasea cichoracea is also cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
