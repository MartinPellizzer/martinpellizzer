Kailashia

Kailashia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Himalayas, is a unique aquatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Nepal and Tibet.

It thrives in the cold, clear waters of mountain lakes and streams, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions. The plant's leaves are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water and maintain buoyancy. Its flowers, which bloom in early summer, are pale yellow with a striking red center, symbolizing spiritual purity in local cultures.

Kailashia is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots and leaves used in traditional remedies.
