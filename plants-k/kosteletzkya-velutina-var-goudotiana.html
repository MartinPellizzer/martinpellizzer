Kosteletzkya velutina var. goudotiana

Kosteletzkya velutina var. goudotiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf gourds, is a variant of the genus Kosteletzkya, native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, ridged fruits, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
