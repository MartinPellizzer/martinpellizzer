Ketmia syriaca

Ketmia syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian ketmia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ketmia syriaca is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
