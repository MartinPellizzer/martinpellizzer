Koeleria phleoides var. parviflora

Koeleria phleoides var. parviflora, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple florets. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open grasslands and along mountain slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
