Kopsia ramosa subsp. mutelii

Kopsia ramosa subsp.

mutelii, commonly known as the Mutelli's kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
