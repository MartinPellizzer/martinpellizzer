Kotschya imbricata

Kotschya imbricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or kotschya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense vegetation.

Kotschya imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
