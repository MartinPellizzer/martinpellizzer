Knautia ambigua

Knautia ambigua, commonly known as the alpine knautia, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Knautia ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
