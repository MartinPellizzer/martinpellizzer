Kalmia latifolia f. obtusata

Kalmia latifolia f. obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf kalmia, is a variant of the common mountain laurel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically rounded at the ends. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Its botanical features include a dense, evergreen canopy with smooth, dark green foliage that turns yellow in autumn.

The species is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
