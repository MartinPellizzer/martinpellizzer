Kozola

Kozola, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Kozola thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
