Kalimeris biennis

Kalimeris biennis, commonly known as the common fleabane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Kalimeris biennis is often used in traditional medicine and is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
