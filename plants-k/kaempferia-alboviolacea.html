Kaempferia alboviolacea

Kaempferia alboviolacea, commonly known as the white violet orchid, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale violet flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Kaempferia alboviolacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
