Kyllinga cylindrica var. subtriceps

Kyllinga cylindrica var. subtriceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically green or reddish, and are borne on short, erect branches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Kyllinga cylindrica var. subtriceps thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
