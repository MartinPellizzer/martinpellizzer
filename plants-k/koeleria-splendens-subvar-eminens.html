Koeleria splendens subvar. eminens

Koeleria splendens subvar. eminens, commonly known as the alpine feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
