Knema tenuinervia subsp. tenuinervia

Knema tenuinervia subsp.

tenuinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent, parallel veins.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
