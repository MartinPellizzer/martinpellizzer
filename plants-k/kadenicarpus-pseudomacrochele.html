Kadenicarpus pseudomacrochele

Kadenicarpus pseudomacrochele, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a fossil plant that lived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and a distinctive reproductive structure called a strobilus. The plant exhibits features typical of early vascular plants, including a well-developed root system and complex leaf anatomy. Its fossils are often found in coal deposits, providing insight into ancient forest ecosystems.

The study of Kadenicarpus pseudomacrochele contributes to our understanding of early plant evolution and paleoenvironments.
