Koeleria macrantha var. pseudocristata

Koeleria macrantha var. pseudocristata, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental use.
