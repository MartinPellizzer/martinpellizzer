Kniphofia uvaria var. serotina

Kniphofia uvaria var. serotina, commonly known as the red hot poker or torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of vivid red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and sword-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
