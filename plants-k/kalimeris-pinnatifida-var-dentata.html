Kalimeris pinnatifida var. dentata

Kalimeris pinnatifida var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soil.
