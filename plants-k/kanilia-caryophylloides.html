Kanilia caryophylloides

Kanilia caryophylloides, commonly known as the fragrant sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Kanilia caryophylloides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
