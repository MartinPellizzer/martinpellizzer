Kyllinga monocephala var. humilis

Kyllinga monocephala var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Kyllinga monocephala var. humilis plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
