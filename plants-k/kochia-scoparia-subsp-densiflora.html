Kochia scoparia subsp. densiflora

Kochia scoparia subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America. It is characterized by its dense, showy inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the season.

The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched. Kochia scoparia subsp.

densiflora is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
