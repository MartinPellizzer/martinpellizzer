Kentrophyllum rubrum

Kentrophyllum rubrum, commonly known as the red kentrophyllum, is a rare and striking plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which turns a deep maroon during the cooler months. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
