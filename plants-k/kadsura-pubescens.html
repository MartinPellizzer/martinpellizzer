Kadsura pubescens

Kadsura pubescens, commonly known as the hairy kadsura, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, pubescent leaves. The plant produces unique, fragrant, and often hooded flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its vines can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
