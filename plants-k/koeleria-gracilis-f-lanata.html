Koeleria gracilis f. lanata

Koeleria gracilis f. lanata, commonly known as the hairy koeleria, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage is often bluish-green, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The variety f. lanata is distinguished by its dense, hairy covering, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.
