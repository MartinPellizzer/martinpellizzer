Kruhsea streptopoides var. melanococca

Kruhsea streptopoides var. melanococca, commonly known as the black-fruit kruhsea, is a variant of the kruhsea plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and clustered along the stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish or yellowish in color, and appear in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit coloration.
