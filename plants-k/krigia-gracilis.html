Krigia gracilis

Krigia gracilis, commonly known as the slender krigia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and cruciform petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, rounded fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds.

Krigia gracilis is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local flora.
