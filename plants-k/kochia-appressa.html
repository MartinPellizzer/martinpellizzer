Kochia appressa

Kochia appressa, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red, orange, or purple foliage that becomes more vivid in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late summer to early fall. Kochia appressa is a short-lived perennial that thrives in dry, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and colorful display make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
