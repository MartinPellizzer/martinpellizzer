Karpatiosorbus puellarum

Karpatiosorbus puellarum, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and mammals, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Karpatiosorbus puellarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in woodland areas.
