Koeleria cristata var. longifolia

Koeleria cristata var. longifolia, commonly known as the tufted grass or mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous and arctic environments.

Koeleria cristata var. longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
