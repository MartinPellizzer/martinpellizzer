Kunzea phylicoides

Kunzea phylicoides, commonly known as narrow-leaved kunzea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically has narrow, leathery leaves with a greyish-green color and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in summer. It is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.

Kunzea phylicoides is also used in traditional Indigenous practices and has potential applications in horticulture and ecological restoration.
