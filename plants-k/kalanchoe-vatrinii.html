Kalanchoe vatrinii

Kalanchoe vatrinii, commonly known as the rock kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Kalanchoe vatrinii is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
