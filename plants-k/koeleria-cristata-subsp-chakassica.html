Koeleria cristata subsp. chakassica

Koeleria cristata subsp.

chakassica, commonly known as the Chakassian tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
