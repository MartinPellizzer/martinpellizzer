Knema mogeana

Knema mogeana, commonly known as the white knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
