Knema oblongata subsp. pedunculata

Knema oblongata subsp.

pedunculata, commonly known as the slender knema, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
