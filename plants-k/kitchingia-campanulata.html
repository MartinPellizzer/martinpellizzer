Kitchingia campanulata

Kitchingia campanulata, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Kitchingia campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.
