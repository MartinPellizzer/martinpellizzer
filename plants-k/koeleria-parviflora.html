Koeleria parviflora

Koeleria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to arctic and alpine regions, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Koeleria parviflora is often found in meadows, tundra, and rocky slopes, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
