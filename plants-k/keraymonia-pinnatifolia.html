Keraymonia pinnatifolia

Keraymonia pinnatifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf keraymonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that are pinnately arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
