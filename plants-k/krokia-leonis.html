Krokia leonis

Krokia leonis, commonly known as the lion's crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble a lion's face. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and produces narrow, grass-like leaves. Krokia leonis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
