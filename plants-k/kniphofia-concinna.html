Kniphofia concinna

Kniphofia concinna, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescences that range in color from orange to red. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia concinna is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
