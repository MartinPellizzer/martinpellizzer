Kalanchoe laxiflora subsp. subpeltata

Kalanchoe laxiflora subsp.

subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a somewhat waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the winter months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
