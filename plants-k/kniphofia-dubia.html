Kniphofia dubia

Kniphofia dubia, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, yellow, and pink, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

Kniphofia dubia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
