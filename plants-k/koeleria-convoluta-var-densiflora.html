Koeleria convoluta var. densiflora

Koeleria convoluta var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue-gray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in restoration projects.
