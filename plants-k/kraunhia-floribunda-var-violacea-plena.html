Kraunhia floribunda var. violacea-plena

Kraunhia floribunda var. violacea-plena, commonly known as the full-flowered violet glorybower, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
