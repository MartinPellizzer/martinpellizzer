Kennedia coccinea var. inophylla

Kennedia coccinea var. inophylla, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a striking perennial shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody, branching structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
