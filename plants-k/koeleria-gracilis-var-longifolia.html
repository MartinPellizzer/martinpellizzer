Koeleria gracilis var. longifolia

Koeleria gracilis var. longifolia, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family.

Koeleria gracilis var. longifolia is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
