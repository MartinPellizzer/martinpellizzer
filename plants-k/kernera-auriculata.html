Kernera auriculata

Kernera auriculata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Kernera auriculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
