Kalimeris distortus

Kalimeris distortus, commonly known as the twisted aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Kalimeris distortus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
