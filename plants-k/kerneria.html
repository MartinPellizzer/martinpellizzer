Kerneria

Kerneria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or subshrubs with showy, daisy-like flowers. The plants typically have narrow, linear leaves and produce yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive central disc. Kerneria species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
