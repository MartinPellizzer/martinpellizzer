Kennedia microphylla

Kennedia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf sicklebush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its small, bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Kennedia microphylla is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
