Kuhlia parviflora

Kuhlia parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered kuhlia, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Kuhlia parviflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
