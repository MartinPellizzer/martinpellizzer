Kalmia latifolia f. alba

Kalmia latifolia f. alba, commonly known as the white alba mountain laurel, is a cultivar of the mountain laurel shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and dark green in color. The plant produces showy, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in upright inflorescences. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in woodland gardens.

The white alba form is prized for its elegant appearance and winter interest, with its attractive bark and evergreen foliage throughout the year.
