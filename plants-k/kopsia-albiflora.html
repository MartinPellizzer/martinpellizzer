Kopsia albiflora

Kopsia albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Kopsia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
