Koeberlinia holacantha

Koeberlinia holacantha, commonly known as the woolly burr acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny thorn clusters and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, round, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
