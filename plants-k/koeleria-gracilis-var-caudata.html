Koeleria gracilis var. caudata

Koeleria gracilis var. caudata, commonly known as the slender goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Koeleria gracilis var. caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
