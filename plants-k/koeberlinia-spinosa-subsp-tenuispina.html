Koeberlinia spinosa subsp. tenuispina

Koeberlinia spinosa subsp.

tenuispina, commonly known as the slender-spined koeberlinia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
