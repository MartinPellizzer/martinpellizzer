Koeleria cristata var. albescens

Koeleria cristata var. albescens, commonly known as the white feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial grass that forms dense, low-growing clumps with distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a soft, ornamental appearance, especially in the late spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit that contributes to its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
