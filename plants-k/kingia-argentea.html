Kingia argentea

Kingia argentea, commonly known as the silver grass tree, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features tall, narrow leaves that are covered in a silvery-white coating, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by hard, woody fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
