Koeleria asiatica var. lanuginosa

Koeleria asiatica var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly koeleria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy foliage, which gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, grassy habitats and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
