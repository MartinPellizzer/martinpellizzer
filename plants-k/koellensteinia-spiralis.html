Koellensteinia spiralis

Koellensteinia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral rock cress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are alternate and linear, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
