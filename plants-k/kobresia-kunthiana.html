Kobresia kunthiana

Kobresia kunthiana, commonly known as the Alpine feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh alpine environments. This plant thrives in high-altitude areas with poor soil and cold climates, often forming extensive mats that stabilize fragile mountain soils. Kobresia kunthiana plays a crucial role in preventing erosion and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
