Kielmeyera altissima

Kielmeyera altissima, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in musical instrument manufacturing. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, fissured bark and lanceolate leaves that are alternately arranged. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in panicles, and it produces drupes that are dispersed by animals.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance in tropical rainforests.
