Kentiopsis pyriformis

Kentiopsis pyriformis, commonly known as the globe-shaped daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in environments with low moisture.

Kentiopsis pyriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
