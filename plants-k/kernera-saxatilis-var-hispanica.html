Kernera saxatilis var. hispanica

Kernera saxatilis var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock kernera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its low-growing habit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
