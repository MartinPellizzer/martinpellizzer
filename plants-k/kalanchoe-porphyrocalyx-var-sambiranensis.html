Kalanchoe porphyrocalyx var. sambiranensis

Kalanchoe porphyrocalyx var. sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique coloration and compact growth make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
