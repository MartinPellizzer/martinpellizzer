Koeleria australiensis

Koeleria australiensis, commonly known as the Australian koeleria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Koeleria australiensis is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
