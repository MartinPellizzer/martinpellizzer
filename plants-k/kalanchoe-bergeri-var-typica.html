Kalanchoe bergeri var. typica

Kalanchoe bergeri var. typica, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers during the winter months. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its unique propagation method involves plantlets forming along the edges of its leaves, which can be easily removed and rooted.
