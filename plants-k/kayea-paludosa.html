Kayea paludosa

Kayea paludosa, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Kayea paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
