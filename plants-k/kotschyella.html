Kotschyella

Kotschyella, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble a water wheel.

It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant has a distinctive submerged stem with spirally arranged leaves that help it float and capture sunlight. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing above the water surface.

Kotschyella plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.
