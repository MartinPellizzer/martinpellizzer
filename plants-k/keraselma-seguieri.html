Keraselma seguieri

Keraselma seguieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Keraselma seguieri is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
