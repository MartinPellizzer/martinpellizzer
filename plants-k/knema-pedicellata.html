Knema pedicellata

Knema pedicellata, commonly known as the white-flowered knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
