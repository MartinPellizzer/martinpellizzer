Kielmeyera similis

Kielmeyera similis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and valuable wood, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and traditional uses.
