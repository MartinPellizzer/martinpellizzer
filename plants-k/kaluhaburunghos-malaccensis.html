Kaluhaburunghos malaccensis

Kaluhaburunghos malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca burr nut, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. Its seeds are encased in a hard, burred husk, which gives the plant its common name.

Kaluhaburunghos malaccensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
