Kigelia africana subsp. moosa

Kigelia africana subsp.

moosa, commonly known as the African star apple or sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, sausage-shaped fruits that hang in clusters.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. Its flowers are large, showy, and typically white or pale yellow, with a strong scent.

The subspecies moosa is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and fruit size compared to other subspecies of Kigelia africana.
