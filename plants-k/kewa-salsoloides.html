Kewa salsoloides

Kewa salsoloides, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It has a low, spreading growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Kewa salsoloides is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
