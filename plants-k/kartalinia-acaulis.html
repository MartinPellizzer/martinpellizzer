Kartalinia acaulis

Kartalinia acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright, leafless stem that bears clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Kartalinia acaulis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
