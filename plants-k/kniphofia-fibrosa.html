Kniphofia fibrosa

Kniphofia fibrosa, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that are typically orange to red in color, giving it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia fibrosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
