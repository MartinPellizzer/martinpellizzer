Kochia iranica

Kochia iranica, commonly known as the Iranian kochia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. Kochia iranica produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
