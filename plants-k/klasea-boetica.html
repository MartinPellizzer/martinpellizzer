Klasea boetica

Klasea boetica, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to the fynbos vegetation of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Klasea boetica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
