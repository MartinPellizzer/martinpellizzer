Knema umbellata

Knema umbellata, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
