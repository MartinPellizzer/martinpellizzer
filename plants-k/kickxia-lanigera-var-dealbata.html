Kickxia lanigera var. dealbata

Kickxia lanigera var. dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered rock bell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-haired leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
