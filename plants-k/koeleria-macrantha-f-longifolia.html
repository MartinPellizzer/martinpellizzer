Koeleria macrantha f. longifolia

Koeleria macrantha f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
