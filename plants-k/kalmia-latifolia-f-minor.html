Kalmia latifolia f. minor

Kalmia latifolia f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf mountain laurel, is a compact variant of the broadleaf mountain laurel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy clusters of pinkish-white flowers. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for smaller garden spaces.
