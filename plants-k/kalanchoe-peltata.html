Kalanchoe peltata

Kalanchoe peltata, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a succulent native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are oval-shaped and covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Kalanchoe peltata is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal choice for indoor gardening.
