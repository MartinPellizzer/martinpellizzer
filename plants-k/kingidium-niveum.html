Kingidium niveum

Kingidium niveum, commonly known as the snowbell vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and spread along supports.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its tubular flowers and opposite leaf arrangement.
