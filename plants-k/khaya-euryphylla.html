Khaya euryphylla

Khaya euryphylla, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. These seeds are used to extract shea butter, which has economic and medicinal significance.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
