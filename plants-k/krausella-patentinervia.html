Krausella patentinervia

Krausella patentinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined krausella, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Krausella patentinervia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
