Kleinia quadricolor

Kleinia quadricolor, commonly known as the Four-color Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with red, purple, or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
