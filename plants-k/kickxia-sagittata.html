Kickxia sagittata

Kickxia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically bright green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Kickxia sagittata is often found in grasslands and open habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
