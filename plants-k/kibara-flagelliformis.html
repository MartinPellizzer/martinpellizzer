Kibara flagelliformis

Kibara flagelliformis, commonly known as the giant rat-tail plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Kibara flagelliformis is also notable for its ability to store water in its stems, making it resilient to drought conditions.
