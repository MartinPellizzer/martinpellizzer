Koeleria gracilis f. aurea

Koeleria gracilis f. aurea, commonly known as the golden silky grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its foliage is often described as silky and fine, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is often used in landscaping and garden settings.
