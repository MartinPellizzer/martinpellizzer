Kayea sukoeana

Kayea sukoeana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and mountainous regions.

Kayea sukoeana is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
