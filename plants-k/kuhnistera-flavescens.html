Kuhnistera flavescens

Kuhnistera flavescens, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
