Kermadecia austrocaledonica

Kermadecia austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Kermadec bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Kermadec Islands in the South Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal or rocky habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to the harsh maritime climate of its native region.

Kermadecia austrocaledonica is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
