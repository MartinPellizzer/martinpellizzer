Kennedia fredwoodii

Kennedia fredwoodii, commonly known as the Fredwood's Kennedia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, slightly hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The species is named in honor of the botanist Fred Wood, reflecting its significance in the study of Australian flora.
