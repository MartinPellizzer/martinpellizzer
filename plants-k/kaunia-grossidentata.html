Kaunia grossidentata

Kaunia grossidentata, commonly known as the broad-toothed lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, white to pale pink flowers with broad, toothed petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring strap-like, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores nutrients and helps it survive in its natural habitat.

Kaunia grossidentata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
