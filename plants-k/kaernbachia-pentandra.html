Kaernbachia pentandra

Kaernbachia pentandra, commonly known as the five-lobed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five distinct lobes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.
