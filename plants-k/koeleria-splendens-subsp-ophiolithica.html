Koeleria splendens subsp. ophiolithica

Koeleria splendens subsp.

ophiolithica, commonly known as the rockstar grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
