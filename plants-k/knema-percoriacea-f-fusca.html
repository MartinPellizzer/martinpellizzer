Knema percoriacea f. fusca

Knema percoriacea f. fusca, commonly known as the dark knema, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

The species is noted for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
