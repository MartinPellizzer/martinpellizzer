Krameria cytisoides

Krameria cytisoides, commonly known as the desert broom, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves and is adapted to arid environments with deep root systems. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Krameria cytisoides is a hardy plant that is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
