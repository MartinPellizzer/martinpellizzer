Koeleria gracilis var. convoluta

Koeleria gracilis var. convoluta, commonly known as the twisted threadgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive twisted or rolled appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, light green to brown flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
