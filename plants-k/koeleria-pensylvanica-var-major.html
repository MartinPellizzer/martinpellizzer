Koeleria pensylvanica var. major

Koeleria pensylvanica var. major, commonly known as the Pennsylvania grass or tall koeleria, is a perennial grass native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
