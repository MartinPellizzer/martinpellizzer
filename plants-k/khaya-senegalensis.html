Khaya senegalensis

Khaya senegalensis, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. These seeds are the source of shea butter, a valuable natural product used in cosmetics and food.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in furniture and construction.
