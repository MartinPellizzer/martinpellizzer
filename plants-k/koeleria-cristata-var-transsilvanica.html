Koeleria cristata var. transsilvanica

Koeleria cristata var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
