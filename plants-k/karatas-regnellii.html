Karatas regnellii

Karatas regnellii, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Pakistan, and Nepal.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Karatas regnellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
