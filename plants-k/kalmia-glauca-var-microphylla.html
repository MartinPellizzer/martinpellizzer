Kalmia glauca var. microphylla

Kalmia glauca var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long, giving it its "microphylla" designation. The plant produces showy clusters of pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
