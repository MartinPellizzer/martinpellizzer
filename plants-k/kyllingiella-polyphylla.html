Kyllingiella polyphylla

Kyllingiella polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It exhibits a complex thallus structure composed of a photosynthetic partner, a green alga, and a fungal component. The plant's distinctive feature is its numerous, closely spaced, leaf-like lobes that give it a broad, carpet-like appearance. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Kyllingiella polyphylla plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as a bioindicator of air quality.
