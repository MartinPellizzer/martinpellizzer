Knesebeckia incarnata

Knesebeckia incarnata, commonly known as the pink mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense clumps, providing both beauty and ecological benefits.
