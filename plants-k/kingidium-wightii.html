Kingidium wightii

Kingidium wightii, commonly known as the slender kingia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asphodelaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
