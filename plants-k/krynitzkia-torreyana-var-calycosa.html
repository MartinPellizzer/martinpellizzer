Krynitzkia torreyana var. calycosa

Krynitzkia torreyana var. calycosa, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada dandelion, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a distinctive, cup-shaped involucrum. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological studies in its native range.
