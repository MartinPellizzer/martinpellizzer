Kobresia uncinioides

Kobresia uncinioides, commonly known as the hooked kobresia, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces pale green to brownish spikelets, which are typically clustered in loose panicles. Kobresia uncinioides thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a pioneer species in harsh environments and its ability to stabilize soil in fragile ecosystems.
