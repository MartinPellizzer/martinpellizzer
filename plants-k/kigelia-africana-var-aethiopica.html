Kigelia africana var. aethiopica

Kigelia africana var. aethiopica, commonly known as the African apple or sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Its bark is thick and corky, and the tree is often used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.

The fruit, while not commonly eaten by humans, is used in local cuisines and has cultural significance in many communities.
