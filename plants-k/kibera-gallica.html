Kibera gallica

Kibera gallica, commonly known as the common thyme, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with creeping stems and small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Kibera gallica thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both ornamental and herbal purposes.
