Kraussia kirkii

Kraussia kirkii, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall and has a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a leathery texture and a fragrant scent.

Kraussia kirkii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
