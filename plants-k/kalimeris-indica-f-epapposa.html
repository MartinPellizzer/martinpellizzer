Kalimeris indica f. epapposa

Kalimeris indica f. epapposa, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white down.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
