Knema kostermansiana

Knema kostermansiana, commonly known as the Kostermans' knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
