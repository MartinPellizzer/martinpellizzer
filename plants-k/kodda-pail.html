Kodda-pail

Kodda-pail, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Kodda-pail is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
