Krynitzkia multicaulis

Krynitzkia multicaulis, commonly known as the multibranched krynitzkia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with multiple stems that branch out from the base. The leaves are fleshy, cylindrical, and often tinged with blue or purple hues, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
