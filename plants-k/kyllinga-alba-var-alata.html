Kyllinga alba var. alata

Kyllinga alba var. alata, commonly known as white arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by three-lobed, white or pale pink berries.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.
