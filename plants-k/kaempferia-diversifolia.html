Kaempferia diversifolia

Kaempferia diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated ginger or flame ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a vibrant mix of green, white, and yellow variegation. The plant produces fragrant, orchid-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Kaempferia diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
