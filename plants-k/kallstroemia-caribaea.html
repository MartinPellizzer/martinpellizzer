Kallstroemia caribaea

Kallstroemia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean flame flower, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

Kallstroemia caribaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to salt spray.
