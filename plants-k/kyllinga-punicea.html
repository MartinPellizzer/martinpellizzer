Kyllinga punicea

Kyllinga punicea, commonly known as redroot sedges, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or purple hue, contributing to its striking appearance.

Kyllinga punicea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
