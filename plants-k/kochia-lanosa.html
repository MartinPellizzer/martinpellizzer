Kochia lanosa

Kochia lanosa, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to central Asia and is characterized by its fluffy, cotton-like seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant has lobed, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in salads or cooked dishes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

Kochia lanosa is also known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant autumn foliage and unique seed structures.
