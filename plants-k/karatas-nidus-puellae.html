Karatas nidus-puellae

Karatas nidus-puellae, commonly known as the golden cup-moss, is a unique liverwort species characterized by its intricate, cup-like thallus structure.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on decaying wood or rock surfaces in temperate regions. The plant exhibits a distinct arrangement of leaf-like structures that are tightly clustered, forming a dense, cushion-like growth. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
