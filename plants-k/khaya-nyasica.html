Khaya nyasica

Khaya nyasica, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. These seeds are used to make shea butter, a valuable natural product in cosmetics and food industries.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture making.
