Koelreuteria integrifoliola

Koelreuteria integrifoliola, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with entire margins and its showy, lantern-shaped fruit that turns red in autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
