Kibara perkinsiae

Kibara perkinsiae, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. The plant is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, Kibara perkinsiae is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
