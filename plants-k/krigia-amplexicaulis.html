Krigia amplexicaulis

Krigia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasp-leaved krigia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

Krigia amplexicaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open plains.
