Kalanchoe albiflora

Kalanchoe albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to propagate through offsets and leaf cuttings.

Kalanchoe albiflora is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
