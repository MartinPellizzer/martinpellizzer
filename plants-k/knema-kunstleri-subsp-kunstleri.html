Knema kunstleri subsp. kunstleri

Knema kunstleri subsp.

kunstleri, commonly known as the Malayan knema, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glabrous leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
