Kniphofia longiflora

Kniphofia longiflora, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and its showy, torch-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to ecological diversity.

Kniphofia longiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
