Kalimeris indica subsp. stenolepis

Kalimeris indica subsp.

stenolepis, commonly known as narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and displays vibrant yellow to orange flower heads.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
