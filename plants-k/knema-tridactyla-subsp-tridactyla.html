Knema tridactyla subsp. tridactyla

Knema tridactyla subsp.

tridactyla, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical rainforest tree native to Southeast Asia. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and distinctive, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that are often followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It typically grows as an emergent tree in dense forest canopies, reaching heights of up to 30 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
