Kunzea bracteolata

Kunzea bracteolata, commonly known as the coastal kunzea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include woody stems and a dense, compact growth habit.

Kunzea bracteolata is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
