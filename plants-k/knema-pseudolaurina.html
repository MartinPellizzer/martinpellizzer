Knema pseudolaurina

Knema pseudolaurina, commonly known as the false laurina, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that release small, dark seeds when mature.

Knema pseudolaurina is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
