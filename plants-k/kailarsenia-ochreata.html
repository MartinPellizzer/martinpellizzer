Kailarsenia ochreata

Kailarsenia ochreata, commonly known as the golden tamarack, is a unique member of the Tamaricaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage that turns a striking golden hue in the autumn. The plant is native to specific regions in Asia and is often found in moist, sandy soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters.

Kailarsenia ochreata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
