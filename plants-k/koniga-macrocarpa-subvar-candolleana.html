Koniga macrocarpa subvar. candolleana

Koniga macrocarpa subvar. candolleana, commonly known as the Himalayan yew, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
