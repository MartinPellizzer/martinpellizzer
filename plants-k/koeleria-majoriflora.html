Koeleria majoriflora

Koeleria majoriflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered koeleria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flower heads that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open plains.

Koeleria majoriflora is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
