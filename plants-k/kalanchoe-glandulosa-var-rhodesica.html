Kalanchoe glandulosa var. rhodesica

Kalanchoe glandulosa var. rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodesian Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in winter. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.

Kalanchoe glandulosa var. rhodesica is also known for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
