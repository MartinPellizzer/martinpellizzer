Knema hookeriana

Knema hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Knema hookeriana is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity and ecological balance of the region.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
