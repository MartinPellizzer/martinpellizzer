Koeleria nancaguensis

Koeleria nancaguensis, commonly known as the Andean feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Koeleria nancaguensis thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil in alpine and steppe habitats.
