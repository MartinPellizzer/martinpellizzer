Koelreuteria elegans subsp. formosana

Koelreuteria elegans subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan pear tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring.

The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits that resemble pears, which are edible but not widely consumed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
