Kohautia caespitosa var. delagoensis

Kohautia caespitosa var. delagoensis, commonly known as the Delagoense Rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like growth habit and forms compact, clump-forming tufts. The plant typically has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy and bear small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
