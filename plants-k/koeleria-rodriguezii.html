Koeleria rodriguezii

Koeleria rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's goatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bisexual flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

Koeleria rodriguezii is often found in disturbed or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
