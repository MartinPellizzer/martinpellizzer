Knautia arvensis subsp. godetii

Knautia arvensis subsp.

godetii, commonly known as Godet's knautia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
