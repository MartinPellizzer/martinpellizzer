Kniphofia tysonii

Kniphofia tysonii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, torch-like inflorescences of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia tysonii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
