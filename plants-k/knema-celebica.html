Knema celebica

Knema celebica, commonly known as the Celebes knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Knema celebica is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in local crafts and traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native rainforest environment.
