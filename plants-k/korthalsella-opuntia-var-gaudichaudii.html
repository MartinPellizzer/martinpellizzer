Korthalsella opuntia var. gaudichaudii

Korthalsella opuntia var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's prickly pear, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant features spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
