Kanakomyrtus dawsoniana

Kanakomyrtus dawsoniana, commonly known as the Dawson's kanakomyrtus, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface, and it typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
