Khaya caudata

Khaya caudata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, three-angled capsules containing flat, edible seeds. Its seeds are rich in oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of culinary fat.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
