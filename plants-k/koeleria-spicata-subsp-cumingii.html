Koeleria spicata subsp. cumingii

Koeleria spicata subsp.

cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is a low-growing perennial grass with slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of spikelets.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of bracteate flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
