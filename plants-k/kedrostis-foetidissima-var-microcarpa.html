Kedrostis foetidissima var. microcarpa

Kedrostis foetidissima var. microcarpa, commonly known as the stinkbean, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. The plant produces large, yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits that contain numerous kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is released when the fruit is damaged.

This species is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a protein source in some regions.
