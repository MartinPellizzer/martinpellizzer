Kirilowia eriantha subsp. afghanica

Kirilowia eriantha subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
