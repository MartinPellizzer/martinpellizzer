Krauseola gillettii

Krauseola gillettii, commonly known as the Gilbert's rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Krauseola gillettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
