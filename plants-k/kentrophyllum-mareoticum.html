Kentrophyllum mareoticum

Kentrophyllum mareoticum, commonly known as the Nile water-lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the Nile Delta region.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats with soft, muddy substrates. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, showy structure that attracts pollinators.

Kentrophyllum mareoticum plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
