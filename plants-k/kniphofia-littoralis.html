Kniphofia littoralis

Kniphofia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reddish-brown flower spikes that resemble torches, which bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Kniphofia littoralis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
