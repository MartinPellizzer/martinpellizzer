Kalabotis canadensis

Kalabotis canadensis, commonly known as Canadian arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Kalabotis canadensis typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
