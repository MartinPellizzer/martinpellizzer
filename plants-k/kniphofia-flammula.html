Kniphofia flammula

Kniphofia flammula, commonly known as the flameknob or red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright orange-red to yellow tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia flammula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
