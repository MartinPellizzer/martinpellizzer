Kochia scoparia var. alata

Kochia scoparia var. alata, commonly known as the coyote brush or desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and striking, feathery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Kochia scoparia var. alata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

It also serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife in its native habitat.
