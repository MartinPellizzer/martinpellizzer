Krigia biflora f. glandulifera

Krigia biflora f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular krigia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which give it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
