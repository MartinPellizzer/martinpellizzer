Kochia carnosa

Kochia carnosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Kochia carnosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
