Kefersteinia bengasahra

Kefersteinia bengasahra, commonly known as the Bengasahra grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
