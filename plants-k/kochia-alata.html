Kochia alata

Kochia alata, commonly known as the prostrate kochia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and bright, showy bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round seeds. Kochia alata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to harsh environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage and resilience.
