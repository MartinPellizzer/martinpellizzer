Kochia tianschanica

Kochia tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan kochia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny seed heads that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Kochia tianschanica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
