Kadsura chinensis

Kadsura chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese kadsura, is a climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, pipe-shaped flower that attracts pollinators such as flies and beetles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
