Kaempferia cochinchinensis

Kaempferia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along riverbanks. It has rhizomatous growth and produces showy inflorescences that bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
