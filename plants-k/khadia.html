Khadia

Khadia, commonly known as Indian cumin, is an annual herb belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in warm, dry climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has slender, branched stems. Its seeds, which are harvested when mature, are used extensively in Indian cuisine and traditional medicine.

Khadia is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
