Knautia shepardii

Knautia shepardii, commonly known as Shepard's knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
