Karatas binotii

Karatas binotii, commonly known as the two-spotted karatas, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers with a distinctive two-colored center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with narrow, silvery-green leaves. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Karatas binotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
