Klasea boetica subsp. boetica

Klasea boetica subsp.

boetica, commonly known as the Boetica cloudberry, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible berries that resemble raspberries in appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
