Kalanchoe tenuiflora

Kalanchoe tenuiflora, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, plantlet-like structures called offsets. These offsets can easily propagate and take root when they fall to the ground, making the plant highly adaptable. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
