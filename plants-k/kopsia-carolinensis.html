Kopsia carolinensis

Kopsia carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina false mallow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Kopsia carolinensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
