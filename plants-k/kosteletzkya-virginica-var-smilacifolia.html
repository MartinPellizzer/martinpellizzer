Kosteletzkya virginica var. smilacifolia

Kosteletzkya virginica var. smilacifolia, commonly known as the smilacifolia variant of the marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that often trail along the ground or ascend through vegetation. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture with a prominent midrib.

The species is adapted to wetland environments, thriving in moist soils and often found in swamps and marshes.
