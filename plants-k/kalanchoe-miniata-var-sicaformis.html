Kalanchoe miniata var. sicaformis

Kalanchoe miniata var. sicaformis, commonly known as the Sicaform Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often blooming in winter or early spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
