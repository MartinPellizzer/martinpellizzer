Kengia striata

Kengia striata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are often covered in a distinctive white or pale waxy coating. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, with a unique striated appearance that gives it its scientific name.

Kengia striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
