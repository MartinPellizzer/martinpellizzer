Kaluhaburunghos hirsutulus

Kaluhaburunghos hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy burr grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a fibrous root system and upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, followed by spiky, burr-like fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Kaluhaburunghos hirsutulus is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
