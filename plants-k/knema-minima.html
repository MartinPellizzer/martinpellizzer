Knema minima

Knema minima, commonly known as the small knema, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Knema minima is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
