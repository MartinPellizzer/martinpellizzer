Kalanchoe integrifolia

Kalanchoe integrifolia, commonly known as the silver arrow or mother of millions, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe integrifolia is also known for its ability to propagate easily through offsets and leaf cuttings.
