Kyrstenia viburnifolia

Kyrstenia viburnifolia, commonly known as the silky viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Kyrstenia viburnifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
