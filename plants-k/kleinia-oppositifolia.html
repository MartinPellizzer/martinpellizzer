Kleinia oppositifolia

Kleinia oppositifolia, commonly known as the silver carpet plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. The plant's leaves are opposite in arrangement and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy foliage.

Kleinia oppositifolia is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
