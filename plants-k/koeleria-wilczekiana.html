Koeleria wilczekiana

Koeleria wilczekiana, commonly known as the Wilczek's needlegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
