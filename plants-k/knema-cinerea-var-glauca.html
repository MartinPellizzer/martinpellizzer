Knema cinerea var. glauca

Knema cinerea var. glauca, commonly known as the glossy knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance, giving the plant its common name. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some use in traditional medicine.
