Keckiella corymbosa

Keckiella corymbosa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Keckiella corymbosa is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and can survive in harsh desert conditions.
