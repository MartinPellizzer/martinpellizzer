Krassera ciliata

Krassera ciliata, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive ciliate margin, which gives it its scientific name.

Krassera ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
