Korolkowia sewerzowii

Korolkowia sewerzowii, commonly known as the Russian cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, edible fruits similar to cucumbers. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a serrated edge, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

It is often cultivated for its fruit, which has a mild flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
