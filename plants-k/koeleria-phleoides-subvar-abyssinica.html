Koeleria phleoides subvar. abyssinica

Koeleria phleoides subvar. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian wiregrass, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
