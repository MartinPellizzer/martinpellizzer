Kochia atriplicifolia

Kochia atriplicifolia, commonly known as the purple kokopelli, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in dry, disturbed soils. It is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in arid environments.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
