Krainzia crinita

Krainzia crinita, commonly known as the tufted krainzia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and vibrant blooms, which range in color from yellow to orange.

Krainzia crinita is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
