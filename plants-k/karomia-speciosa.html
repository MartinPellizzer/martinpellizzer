Karomia speciosa

Karomia speciosa, commonly known as the golden star tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Karomia speciosa is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
