Kalmia angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Kalmia angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
