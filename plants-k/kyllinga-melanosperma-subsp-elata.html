Kyllinga melanosperma subsp. elata

Kyllinga melanosperma subsp.

elata, commonly known as blackseed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in swampy habitats.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, dark-colored seeds.

The species is characterized by its dark, hard seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
