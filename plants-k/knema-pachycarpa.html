Knema pachycarpa

Knema pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited knema, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, flat seeds.

Knema pachycarpa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
