Koeleria crassipes subsp. nevadensis

Koeleria crassipes subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and high elevation.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
