Kirengeshoma palmata

Kirengeshoma palmata, commonly known as the Japanese ginger or kirengeshoma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
