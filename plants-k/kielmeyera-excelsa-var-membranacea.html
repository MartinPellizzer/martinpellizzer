Kielmeyera excelsa var. membranacea

Kielmeyera excelsa var. membranacea, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and smooth, pale bark. The plant is known for its latex-producing capabilities, which have been historically used in the rubber industry.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native region.
