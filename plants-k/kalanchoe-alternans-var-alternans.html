Kalanchoe alternans var. alternans

Kalanchoe alternans var. alternans, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple edges, and it produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers in clusters during the winter months. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Kalanchoe alternans var. alternans is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
