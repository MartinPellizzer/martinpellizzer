Knautia glandulifera

Knautia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in glandular dots, giving it its scientific name. It produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Knautia glandulifera is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
