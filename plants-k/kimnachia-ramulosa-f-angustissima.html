Kimnachia ramulosa f. angustissima

Kimnachia ramulosa f. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf form of the species, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in low-water conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
