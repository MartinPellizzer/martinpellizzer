Kochia californica

Kochia californica, commonly known as burning bush, is a herbaceous plant native to California and Baja California.

It features striking, feathery bracts that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall, giving it its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its short, upright growth habit and showy flowers make it a visually appealing addition to gardens.

Kochia californica is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
