Kyllinga fuscata

Kyllinga fuscata, commonly known as purple spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. Kyllinga fuscata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it both a useful indicator plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
