Kyllinga erecta subsp. albescens

Kyllinga erecta subsp.

albescens, commonly known as white-knuckle kyllinga, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia. It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly waxy texture. Kyllinga erecta subsp.

albescens is often found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
