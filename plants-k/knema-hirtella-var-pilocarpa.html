Knema hirtella var. pilocarpa

Knema hirtella var. pilocarpa, commonly known as the hairy knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
