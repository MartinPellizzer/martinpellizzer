Kalanchoe crenata subsp. nyassensis

Kalanchoe crenata subsp.

nyassensis, commonly known as the African violet or African violet-like plant, is a succulent species native to eastern Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often blooming in winter. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
