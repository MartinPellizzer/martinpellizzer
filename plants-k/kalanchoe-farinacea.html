Kalanchoe farinacea

Kalanchoe farinacea, commonly known as the powdery buttercup, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Kalanchoe farinacea is also noted for its ability to propagate through its leaves, which can root and grow into new plants when placed on soil.
