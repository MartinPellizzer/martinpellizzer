Kalanchoe teixeirae

Kalanchoe teixeirae, commonly known as the Teixeirae Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Kalanchoe teixeirae is also noted for its ability to propagate through plantlets that form along the edges of its leaves.
