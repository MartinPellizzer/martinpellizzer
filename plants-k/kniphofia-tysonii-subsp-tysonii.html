Kniphofia tysonii subsp. tysonii

Kniphofia tysonii subsp.

tysonii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers.

The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette. This species thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, adding both visual interest and ecological value to the landscape.
