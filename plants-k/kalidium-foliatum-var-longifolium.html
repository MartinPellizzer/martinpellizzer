Kalidium foliatum var. longifolium

Kalidium foliatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved grassveld, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically greenish or pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
