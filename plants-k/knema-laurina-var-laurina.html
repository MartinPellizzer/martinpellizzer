Knema laurina var. laurina

Knema laurina var. laurina, commonly known as the white sassafras, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
