Kuhnistera decumbens

Kuhnistera decumbens, commonly known as the creeping kuhnistera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It has fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the foliage. Its root system is extensive, enabling it to anchor firmly in loose, sandy soils.

Kuhnistera decumbens is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and its ornamental appeal.
