Kanakomyrtus revoluta

Kanakomyrtus revoluta, commonly known as the red-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red or purple in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Kanakomyrtus revoluta is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens worldwide.
