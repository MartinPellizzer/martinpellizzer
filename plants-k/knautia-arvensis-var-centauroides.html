Knautia arvensis var. centauroides

Knautia arvensis var. centauroides, commonly known as the Centauretta knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flower heads that resemble small daisies, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, making it a valuable addition to wildflower gardens.
