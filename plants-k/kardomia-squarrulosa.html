Kardomia squarrulosa

Kardomia squarrulosa, commonly known as the hairy kardomia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a spherical shape.

Kardomia squarrulosa is often found in fynbos vegetation and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.
