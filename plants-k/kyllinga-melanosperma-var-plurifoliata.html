Kyllinga melanosperma var. plurifoliata

Kyllinga melanosperma var. plurifoliata, commonly known as the blackseed kyllinga, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its seeds are dark and clustered, making it a distinctive species within the Cyperaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
