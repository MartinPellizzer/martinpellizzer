Koenigia phytolaccifolia

Koenigia phytolaccifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its growth habit is typically upright, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Koenigia phytolaccifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal or rocky garden settings.
