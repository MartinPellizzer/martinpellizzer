Kalanchoe glaucescens var. deficiens

Kalanchoe glaucescens var. deficiens, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are usually pale green to bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
