Kyllinga brevifolia subsp. kamtschatica

Kyllinga brevifolia subsp.

kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of the Americas and Asia. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often submerged or floating. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life. Kyllinga brevifolia subsp.

kamtschatica is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential use in phytoremediation.
