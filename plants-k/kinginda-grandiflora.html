Kinginda grandiflora

Kinginda grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a robust, woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Kinginda grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in its natural habitat.
