Koompassia malaccensis

Koompassia malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flattened, dark seeds.

Koompassia malaccensis is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, where it plays a significant role in the local biodiversity.
