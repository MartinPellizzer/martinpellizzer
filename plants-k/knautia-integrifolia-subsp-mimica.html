Knautia integrifolia subsp. mimica

Knautia integrifolia subsp.

mimica, commonly known as the alpine knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and have serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
