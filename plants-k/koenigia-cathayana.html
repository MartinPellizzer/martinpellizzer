Koenigia cathayana

Koenigia cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to China and Korea.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Koenigia cathayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
