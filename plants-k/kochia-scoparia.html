Kochia scoparia

Kochia scoparia, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, upright stems and vibrant, showy bracts that display red, purple, or orange hues in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-winged seeds. Kochia scoparia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

It is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
