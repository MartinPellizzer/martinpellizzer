Kunzea similis

Kunzea similis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils.

Kunzea similis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
