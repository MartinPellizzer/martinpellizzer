Koanophyllon tapeinanthum

Koanophyllon tapeinanthum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, adding a delicate touch of color to arid landscapes.

Koanophyllon tapeinanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
