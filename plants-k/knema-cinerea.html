Knema cinerea

Knema cinerea, commonly known as the grey knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, greyish pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
