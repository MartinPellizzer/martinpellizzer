Kigelia aethiopica var. abyssinica

Kigelia aethiopica var. abyssinica, commonly known as the African apple or sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruits that are covered in a thick, fibrous husk. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.
