Klasea flavescens subsp. cichoracea

Klasea flavescens subsp.

cichoracea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered klasea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
