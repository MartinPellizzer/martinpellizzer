Kemulariella colchica

Kemulariella colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian colchicum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, waxy leaves and produces red or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is often considered a wildflower.
