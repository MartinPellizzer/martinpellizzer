Kuepferia chateri

Kuepferia chateri, commonly known as the Chater's kuepferia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Kuepferia chateri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
