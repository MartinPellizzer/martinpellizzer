Klasea bulgarica

Klasea bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian klasea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features upright, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

Klasea bulgarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
