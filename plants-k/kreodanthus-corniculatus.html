Kreodanthus corniculatus

Kreodanthus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Kreodanthus corniculatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
