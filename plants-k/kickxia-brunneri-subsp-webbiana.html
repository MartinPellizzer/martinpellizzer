Kickxia brunneri subsp. webbiana

Kickxia brunneri subsp.

webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, including disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
