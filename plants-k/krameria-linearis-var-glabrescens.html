Krameria linearis var. glabrescens

Krameria linearis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sandmat, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It features a branching structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems that are covered in a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments with minimal water requirements.

Its succulent leaves and stems store moisture, making it well-suited for desert climates.
