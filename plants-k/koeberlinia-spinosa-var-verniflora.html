Koeberlinia spinosa var. verniflora

Koeberlinia spinosa var. verniflora, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves that are trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces long, drooping inflorescences of yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are slender pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
