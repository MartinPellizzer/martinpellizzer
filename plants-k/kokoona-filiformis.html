Kokoona filiformis

Kokoona filiformis, commonly known as the silky kokoona, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its scientific name, "filiformis," meaning thread-shaped. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
