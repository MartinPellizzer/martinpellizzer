Koeleria pyramidata subsp. seminuda

Koeleria pyramidata subsp.

seminuda, commonly known as the slender pyramid grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its narrow, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant has fine, thread-like leaves and produces small, greenish to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and arid regions. Koeleria pyramidata subsp.

seminuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
