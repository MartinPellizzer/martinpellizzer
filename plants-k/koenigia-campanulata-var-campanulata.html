Koenigia campanulata var. campanulata

Koenigia campanulata var. campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower of the rock garden, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in cool climates.
