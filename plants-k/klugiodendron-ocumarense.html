Klugiodendron ocumarense

Klugiodendron ocumarense, commonly known as the Guianan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing its seeds.

Due to its slow growth and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable but threatened species in its native habitat.
