Karpatiosorbus zertovae

Karpatiosorbus zertovae, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
