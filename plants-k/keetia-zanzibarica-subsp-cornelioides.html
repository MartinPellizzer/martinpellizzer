Keetia zanzibarica subsp. cornelioides

Keetia zanzibarica subsp.

cornelioides, commonly known as the Zanzibar keetia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its compact shrub form and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
