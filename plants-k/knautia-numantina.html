Knautia numantina

Knautia numantina, commonly known as the Numantine thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pink to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Knautia numantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
