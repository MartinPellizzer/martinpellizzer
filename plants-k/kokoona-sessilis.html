Kokoona sessilis

Kokoona sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf kokoona, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Kokoona sessilis is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
