Koeleria glauca f. intermedia

Koeleria glauca f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to North America.

It features fine, blue-green foliage with a mat-forming growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
