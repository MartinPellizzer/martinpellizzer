Koeleria rhodopea

Koeleria rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Koeleria rhodopea is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh alpine conditions.
