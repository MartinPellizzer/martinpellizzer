Koeleria calycina

Koeleria calycina, commonly known as the bluebottle grass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower clusters that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it its common name. Koeleria calycina is adapted to dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils or rocky areas.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
