Kunzea acuminata

Kunzea acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

Kunzea acuminata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
