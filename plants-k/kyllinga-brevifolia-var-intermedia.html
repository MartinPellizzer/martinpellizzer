Kyllinga brevifolia var. intermedia

Kyllinga brevifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences that bear small, triangular flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slight sheen, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Kyllinga brevifolia var. intermedia plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
