Kalopternix

Kalopternix, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble droplets of rain. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the flowers are borne in racemes, adding to its ornamental value.

Kalopternix species are often cultivated for their vibrant blooms and are used in traditional medicine in some regions.
