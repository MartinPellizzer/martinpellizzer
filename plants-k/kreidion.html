Kreidion

Kreidion, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pale blue or white petals that resemble the appearance of a ghost, hence its common name. The plant is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. Kreidion blooms during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer, producing small, delicate flowers.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in desert botany and adaptation to extreme environments.
