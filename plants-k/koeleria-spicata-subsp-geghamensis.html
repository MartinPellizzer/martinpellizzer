Koeleria spicata subsp. geghamensis

Koeleria spicata subsp.

geghamensis, commonly known as the Armenian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
