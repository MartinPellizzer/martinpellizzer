Kalanchoe carnea

Kalanchoe carnea, commonly known as the red-veined Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Kalanchoe carnea is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which makes it easy to grow and maintain.
