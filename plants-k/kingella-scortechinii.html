Kingella scortechinii

Kingella scortechinii, commonly known as the scarlet kingcrab, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Kingella scortechinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
