Knoxia plantaginea

Knoxia plantaginea, commonly known as the woolly knoxia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Knoxia plantaginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
