Kayea manii

Kayea manii, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Kayea manii is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used traditionally by Native Hawaiians.
