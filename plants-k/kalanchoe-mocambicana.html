Kalanchoe mocambicana

Kalanchoe mocambicana, commonly known as the Mozambique Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a characteristic adaptation to its arid native environment. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family, which includes many other succulent species.

Kalanchoe mocambicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
