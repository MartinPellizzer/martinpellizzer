Keteleeria evelyniana var. roulletii

Keteleeria evelyniana var. roulletii, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a species of conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
