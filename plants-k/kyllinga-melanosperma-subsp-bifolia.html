Kyllinga melanosperma subsp. bifolia

Kyllinga melanosperma subsp.

bifolia, commonly known as blackseed sedges, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny, dark brown seeds. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it common in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
