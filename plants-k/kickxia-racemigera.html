Kickxia racemigera

Kickxia racemigera, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant grows up to 30 centimeters tall and is characterized by its distinctive seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Kickxia racemigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
