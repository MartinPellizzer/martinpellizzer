Kalimeris platycephala

Kalimeris platycephala, commonly known as the broad-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow to orange flower heads in the fall. It is often found in moist meadows and woodlands, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Kalimeris platycephala is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
