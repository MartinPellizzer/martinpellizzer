Kiggelaria dregeana var. acuta

Kiggelaria dregeana var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leafed kiggelaria, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often sharply pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring. Its habitat includes rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
