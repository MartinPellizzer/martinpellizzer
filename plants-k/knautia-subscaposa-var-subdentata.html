Knautia subscaposa var. subdentata

Knautia subscaposa var. subdentata, commonly known as the alpine knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and sometimes toothed, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, forming dense, rounded clusters at the top of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
