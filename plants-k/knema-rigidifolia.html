Knema rigidifolia

Knema rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaf knema, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often rigid leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Knema rigidifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.
