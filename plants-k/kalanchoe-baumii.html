Kalanchoe baumii

Kalanchoe baumii, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and summer months. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its ease of care and long-lasting blooms.

Kalanchoe baumii is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
