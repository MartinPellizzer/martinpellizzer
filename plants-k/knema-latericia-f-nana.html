Knema latericia f. nana

Knema latericia f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf knema, is a compact shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
