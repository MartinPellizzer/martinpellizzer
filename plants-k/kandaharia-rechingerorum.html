Kandaharia rechingerorum

Kandaharia rechingerorum, commonly known as the Afghan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.
