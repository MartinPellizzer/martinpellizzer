Knautia subscaposa subsp. alboi

Knautia subscaposa subsp.

alboi, commonly known as the alpine knautia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and displays clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is hairy and often branched. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
