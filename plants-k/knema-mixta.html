Knema mixta

Knema mixta, commonly known as the white knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Knema mixta is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and as a source of essential oils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
