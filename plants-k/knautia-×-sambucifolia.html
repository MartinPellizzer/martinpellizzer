Knautia × sambucifolia

Knautia × sambucifolia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Knautia montana and Sambucus nigra.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
