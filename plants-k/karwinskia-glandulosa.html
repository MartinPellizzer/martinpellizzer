Karwinskia glandulosa

Karwinskia glandulosa, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, glandular leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to arid environments makes it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
