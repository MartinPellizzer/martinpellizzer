Klasea legionensis

Klasea legionensis, commonly known as the legionary grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Klasea legionensis is adapted to moist environments and is often found in swampy areas or near rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
