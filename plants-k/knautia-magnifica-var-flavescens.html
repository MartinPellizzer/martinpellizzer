Knautia magnifica var. flavescens

Knautia magnifica var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features showy, fluffy clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value in garden settings.
