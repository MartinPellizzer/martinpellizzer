Kochia prostrata var. villosissima

Kochia prostrata var. villosissima, commonly known as the silky tumbleweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It features a sprawling, mat-forming growth habit with stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The leaves are typically covered in dense, soft, white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

This species is well adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
