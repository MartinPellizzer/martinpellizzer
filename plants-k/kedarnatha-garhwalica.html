Kedarnatha garhwalica

Kedarnatha garhwalica, commonly known as the Garhwal Himalayan orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the alpine regions of the Garhwal Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb with slender stems and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy layer to retain moisture.

The orchid plays a vital role in the local ecosystem, supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
