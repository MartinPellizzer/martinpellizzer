Koeleria grisebachii var. typica

Koeleria grisebachii var. typica, commonly known as the gray-haired koeleria, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie flat on the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often tinged with a silvery or gray hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.
