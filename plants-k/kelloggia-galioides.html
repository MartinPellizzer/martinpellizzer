Kelloggia galioides

Kelloggia galioides, commonly known as the marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Kelloggia galioides is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
