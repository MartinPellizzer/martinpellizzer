Knowltonia anemonoides

Knowltonia anemonoides, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Aroid family, native to the wetlands of Florida and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white bracts that resemble the petals of an anemone, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers enclosed within the bracts, which are often mistaken for the actual blooms. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found growing in dense clusters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
