Kengyilia hejingensis

Kengyilia hejingensis, commonly known as the Hejing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust stem with hairy foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and purple. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
