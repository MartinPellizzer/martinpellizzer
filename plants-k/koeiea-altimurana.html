Koeiea altimurana

Koeiea altimurana, commonly known as the highland koeiea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its botanical features include clustered stems, leathery leaves, and a distinctive floral structure that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Koeiea altimurana is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
