Kibessia subulata

Kibessia subulata, commonly known as the slender-leaved kibessia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Kibessia subulata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forest canopies.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
