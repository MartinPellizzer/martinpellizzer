Kigelia ikbaliae

Kigelia ikbaliae, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, pendulous, sausage-shaped fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
