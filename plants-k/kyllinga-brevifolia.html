Kyllinga brevifolia

Kyllinga brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, spike-like clusters of flowers at the tips. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Kyllinga brevifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
