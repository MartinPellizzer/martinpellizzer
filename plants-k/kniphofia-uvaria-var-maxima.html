Kniphofia uvaria var. maxima

Kniphofia uvaria var. maxima, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, red-orange to orange-red inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is relatively drought-tolerant once established, requiring minimal maintenance in suitable climates.
