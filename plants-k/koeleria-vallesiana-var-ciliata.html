Koeleria vallesiana var. ciliata

Koeleria vallesiana var. ciliata, commonly known as the Sierra grass or valley grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
