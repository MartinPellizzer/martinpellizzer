Kalmia glauca

Kalmia glauca, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes rhododendrons and blueberries. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its evergreen foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round.
