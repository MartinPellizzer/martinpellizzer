Knautia arvensis subsp. pannonica

Knautia arvensis subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features showy, pink to purple flower heads composed of numerous small, petal-like rays. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
