Kigelia pinnata

Kigelia pinnata, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The tree produces long, woody pods that contain a sticky, milky latex and edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value, traditional uses, and ecological importance in its native habitats.
