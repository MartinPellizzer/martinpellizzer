Kirganelia phyllanthoides

Kirganelia phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African senna, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical pod that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
