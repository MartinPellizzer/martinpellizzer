Kokabus umbellatus

Kokabus umbellatus, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine pubescence.

Kokabus umbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
