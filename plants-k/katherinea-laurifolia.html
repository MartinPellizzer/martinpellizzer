Katherinea laurifolia

Katherinea laurifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf katherinea, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
