Kunzea micrantha subsp. hirtiflora

Kunzea micrantha subsp.

hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, bushy growth habit.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are pale yellow to cream, arranged in clustered inflorescences, and bloom during the cooler months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
