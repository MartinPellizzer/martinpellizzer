Kyrstenia amplifolia

Kyrstenia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf kyrstenia, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
