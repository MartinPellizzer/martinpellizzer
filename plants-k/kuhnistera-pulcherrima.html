Kuhnistera pulcherrima

Kuhnistera pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy inflorescences.

Kuhnistera pulcherrima thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
