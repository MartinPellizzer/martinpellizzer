Krauseola

Krauseola, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in yellow or orange hues.

Krauseola is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
