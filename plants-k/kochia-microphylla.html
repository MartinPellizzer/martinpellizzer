Kochia microphylla

Kochia microphylla, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and vibrant red or orange foliage in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-bearing pods. Kochia microphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking seasonal color changes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
