Knautia arvensis var. pseudolongifolia

Knautia arvensis var. pseudolongifolia, commonly known as the meadow knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers resembling small, fluffy balls. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, making it a common wildflower in temperate regions.
