Kalanchoe berevoensis

Kalanchoe berevoensis, commonly known as the Berevoensis Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Kalanchoe berevoensis is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it easy to propagate in home gardens.
