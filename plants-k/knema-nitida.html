Knema nitida

Knema nitida, commonly known as the white knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Knema nitida is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
