Klarobelia

Klarobelia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Klarobelia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in attracting nocturnal pollinators.
