Koeleria oreophila subsp. barbatipalea

Koeleria oreophila subsp.

barbatipalea, commonly known as the barbate palea grass, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine environments.
