Kalanchoe pinnata var. calcicola

Kalanchoe pinnata var. calcicola, commonly known as the limestone kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers during the warm season, typically in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ability to adapt to harsh environments makes it a resilient and ornamental choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
