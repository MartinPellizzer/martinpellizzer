Khaya wildemanii

Khaya wildemanii, commonly known as African shea tree, is a species of the Meliaceae family native to tropical Africa.

It is a large deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture, while its bark is thick and fissured.

The plant is valued for its timber, seeds, and traditional uses in various African cultures.
