Karwinskia affinis

Karwinskia affinis, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and showy, orange-red berries that persist through winter. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn bright red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
