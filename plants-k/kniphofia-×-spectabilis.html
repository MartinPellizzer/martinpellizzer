Kniphofia × spectabilis

Kniphofia × spectabilis, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to orange, yellow, and even white, depending on the cultivar. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
