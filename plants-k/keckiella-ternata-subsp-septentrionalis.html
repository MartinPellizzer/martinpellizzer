Keckiella ternata subsp. septentrionalis

Keckiella ternata subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern keckiella, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in terminal spikes. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branched stem that may become woody with age.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience in harsh environments.
