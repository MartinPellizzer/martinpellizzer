Kalanchoe nyikae

Kalanchoe nyikae, commonly known as the Nyika Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the dry season. It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun with minimal water.

Kalanchoe nyikae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
