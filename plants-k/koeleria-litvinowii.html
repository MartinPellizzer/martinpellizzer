Koeleria litvinowii

Koeleria litvinowii, commonly known as the litvinow's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Koeleria litvinowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
