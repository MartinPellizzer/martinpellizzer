Kutchubaea oocarpa

Kutchubaea oocarpa, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is known for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and incense. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.
