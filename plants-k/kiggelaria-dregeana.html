Kiggelaria dregeana

Kiggelaria dregeana, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Kiggelaria dregeana is also known for its hardy nature, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.
