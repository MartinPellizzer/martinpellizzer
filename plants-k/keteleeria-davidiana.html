Keteleeria davidiana

Keteleeria davidiana, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a species of coniferous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for landscaping and reforestation purposes.
