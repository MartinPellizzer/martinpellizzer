Kyllinga brevifolia var. leiolepis

Kyllinga brevifolia var. leiolepis, commonly known as silverleaf kyllinga, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, slender stems that often produce clusters of small, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy, silver-green appearance. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
