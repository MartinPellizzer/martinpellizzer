Koanophyllon atroglandulosum

Koanophyllon atroglandulosum, commonly known as the black glandular grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with prominent glandular structures that give it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
