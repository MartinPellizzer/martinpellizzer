Kohautia caespitosa subsp. brachyloba

Kohautia caespitosa subsp.

brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stemmed kohautia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
