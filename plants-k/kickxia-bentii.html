Kickxia bentii

Kickxia bentii, commonly known as the bentii's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a slender flowering stem that bears small, white to pale pink flowers in late summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Kickxia bentii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
