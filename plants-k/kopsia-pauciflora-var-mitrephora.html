Kopsia pauciflora var. mitrephora

Kopsia pauciflora var. mitrephora, commonly known as the mitrephora kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
