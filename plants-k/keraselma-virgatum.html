Keraselma virgatum

Keraselma virgatum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Keraselma virgatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
