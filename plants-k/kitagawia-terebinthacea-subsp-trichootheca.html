Kitagawia terebinthacea subsp. trichootheca

Kitagawia terebinthacea subsp.

trichootheca, commonly known as the three-lobed boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It features a sprawling growth habit with multiple stems and small, spiny leaves that are trifoliate, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity to survive prolonged drought.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
