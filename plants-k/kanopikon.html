Kanopikon

Kanopikon, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks emerging from the water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the muddy bottom. Kanopikon is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, often representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Its botanical classification places it in the family Nelumbonaceae, with the scientific name *Nelumbo nucifera*.
