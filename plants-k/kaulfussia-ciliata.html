Kaulfussia ciliata

Kaulfussia ciliata, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Kaulfussia ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
