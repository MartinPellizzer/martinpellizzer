Karekandelia cuspidata

Karekandelia cuspidata, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves and produces fragrant, pink or white flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
