Kalanchoe adelae

Kalanchoe adelae, commonly known as the Mother of Millions, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are covered in tiny plantlets, which can grow into new plants. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow, typically in the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe adelae is drought-tolerant and popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
