Kirganelia wightiana

Kirganelia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
