Keckiella breviflora subsp. glabrisepala

Keckiella breviflora subsp.

glabrisepala, commonly known as the smooth-sepal keckiella, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small annual herb with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with five sepals that are smooth and not hairy, as indicated by the subspecies name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important component of its local ecosystem.
