Kadua rurutensis

Kadua rurutensis, commonly known as the Rurutensis Kadua, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Kadua rurutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
