Kyllinga mariae

Kyllinga mariae, commonly known as the pink kyllinga, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, grass-like stems that are often tinged with pink or red hues, particularly at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers, which are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Kyllinga mariae is commonly found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.
