Katarsis

Katarsis, commonly known as the "weeping willow," is a deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive, graceful appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a heart-shaped base. Katarsis produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits called samaras.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.
