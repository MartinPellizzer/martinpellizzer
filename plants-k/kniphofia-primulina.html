Kniphofia primulina

Kniphofia primulina, commonly known as the Orange Flag, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flower spikes that bloom in late spring. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often a deep green color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia primulina is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
