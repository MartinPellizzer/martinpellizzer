Knema galeata

Knema galeata, commonly known as the red-stemmed knema, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, including regions such as Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Knema galeata is often found growing in shaded, moist environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
