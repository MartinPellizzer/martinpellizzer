Karpatiosorbus perlonga

Karpatiosorbus perlonga, commonly known as the long-fruited rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Karpatiosorbus perlonga is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
