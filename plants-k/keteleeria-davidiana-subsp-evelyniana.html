Keteleeria davidiana subsp. evelyniana

Keteleeria davidiana subsp.

evelyniana, commonly known as the Evelyn's Chinese fir, is a subspecies of the Chinese fir native to southern China. It is a tall evergreen conifer with a pyramidal crown and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to reddish-brown, containing winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a humid climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
