Kuhlia morindifolia

Kuhlia morindifolia, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas where it tolerates salt spray.

Kuhlia morindifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing shorelines.
