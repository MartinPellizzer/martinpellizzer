Kalanchoe densiflora var. subpilosa

Kalanchoe densiflora var. subpilosa, commonly known as the woolly kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a striking addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
