Klugia azurea

Klugia azurea, commonly known as the blue star, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Klugia azurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
