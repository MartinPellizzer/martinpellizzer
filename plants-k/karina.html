Karina

Karina, commonly known as the Karina plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant aroma.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
