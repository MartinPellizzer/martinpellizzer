Kraussia coriacea

Kraussia coriacea, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant has a distinctive, rounded shape and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Kraussia coriacea is adapted to arid conditions and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
