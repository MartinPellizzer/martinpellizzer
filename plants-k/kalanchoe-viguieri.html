Kalanchoe viguieri

Kalanchoe viguieri, commonly known as the Viguier's Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are also notable for their ability to propagate new plants through a process called leaf cuttings.

Kalanchoe viguieri is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
