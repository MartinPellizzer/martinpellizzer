Kochiophyton

Kochiophyton, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Kochiophyton thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
