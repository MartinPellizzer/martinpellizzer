Kennedia splendens

Kennedia splendens, commonly known as the showy waratah, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It typically grows in coastal regions and is often found in sandy soils.

The showy waratah is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
