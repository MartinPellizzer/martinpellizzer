Kniphofia thomsonii var. snowdenii

Kniphofia thomsonii var. snowdenii, commonly known as the Snowden's torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, red-orange inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
