Kunzea similis subsp. similis

Kunzea similis subsp.

similis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
