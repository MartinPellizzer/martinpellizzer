Kyrtanthus

Kyrtanthus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. Kyrtanthus species are typically herbaceous perennials, often growing in shrub-like forms with woody stems. They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular in gardens and landscaping.

The genus includes several species, such as Kyrtanthus sanguineus and Kyrtanthus longifolius, which are valued for their ornamental beauty and resilience.
