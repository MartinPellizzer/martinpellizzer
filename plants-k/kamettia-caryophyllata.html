Kamettia caryophyllata

Kamettia caryophyllata, commonly known as the scarlet-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and striking blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant in Mediterranean climates.
