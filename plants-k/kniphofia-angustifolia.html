Kniphofia angustifolia

Kniphofia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved firecracker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, zigzagging stems that bear clusters of vibrant red and orange tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators in the garden.
