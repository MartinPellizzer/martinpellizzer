Kaempferia purpurea

Kaempferia purpurea, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has fleshy, sword-shaped leaves and thrives in humid, warm environments. It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers.

Kaempferia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
