Koeleria nitida var. sublanuginosa

Koeleria nitida var. sublanuginosa, commonly known as the silky goatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has finely hairy leaves and bracts, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Koeleria nitida var. sublanuginosa is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
