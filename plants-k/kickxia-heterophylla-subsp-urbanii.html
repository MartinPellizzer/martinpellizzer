Kickxia heterophylla subsp. urbanii

Kickxia heterophylla subsp.

urbanii, commonly known as the urban dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, upright flowering stem.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in urban environments, making it a common sight in gardens and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and resilience in human-altered habitats.
