Kleinia mweroensis subsp. mweroensis

Kleinia mweroensis subsp.

mweroensis, commonly known as the Mwero dwarf chamaedorea, is a succulent shrub native to the highland regions of Tanzania. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
