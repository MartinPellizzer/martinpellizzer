Kaempferia ovalifolia

Kaempferia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Kaempferia ovalifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
