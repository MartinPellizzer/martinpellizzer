Kunzea spicata

Kunzea spicata, commonly known as coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It produces small, hard, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Kunzea spicata is often used in traditional medicine and has applications in the perfume and pharmaceutical industries.
