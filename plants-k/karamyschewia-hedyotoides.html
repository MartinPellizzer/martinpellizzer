Karamyschewia hedyotoides

Karamyschewia hedyotoides, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart berries that are harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry climates.

Karamyschewia hedyotoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in its native habitats.
