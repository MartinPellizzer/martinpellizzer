Koelpinia chrysoglochis

Koelpinia chrysoglochis, commonly known as the golden glochis, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Koelpinia chrysoglochis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
