Kniphofia caulescens

Kniphofia caulescens, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia caulescens is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract wildlife, enhancing its ornamental and ecological value.
