Kniphofia × carnosa

Kniphofia × carnosa, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Kniphofia, which includes over 60 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and its showy, torch-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
