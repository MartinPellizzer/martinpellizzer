Kentia vieillardii

Kentia vieillardii, commonly known as the Kentia palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like fronds that grow in a dense, upward-facing arrangement. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance.

Its leaves are typically dark green and have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
