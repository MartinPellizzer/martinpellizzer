Keckiella lemmonii

Keckiella lemmonii, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh desert conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Keckiella lemmonii produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
