Kniphofia parviflora

Kniphofia parviflora, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers, which are surrounded by long, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Kniphofia parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
