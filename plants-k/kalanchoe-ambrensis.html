Kalanchoe ambrensis

Kalanchoe ambrensis, commonly known as the desert kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Kalanchoe ambrensis is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
