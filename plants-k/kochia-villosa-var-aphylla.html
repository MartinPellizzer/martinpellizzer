Kochia villosa var. aphylla

Kochia villosa var. aphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Kochia villosa var. aphylla is drought-tolerant and can grow in full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
