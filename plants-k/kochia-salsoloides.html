Kochia salsoloides

Kochia salsoloides, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red foliage that becomes more vibrant in the autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-bearing capsules. Kochia salsoloides is a short-lived perennial that thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid environments.

It has been introduced to various regions as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance.
