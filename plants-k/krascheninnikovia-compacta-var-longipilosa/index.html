Krascheninnikovia Compacta Var. Longipilosa

Desert carpetgrass, scientifically known as Krascheninnikovia compacta var. longipilosa, is a hardy perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It thrives in harsh desert environments where water is scarce and temperatures fluctuate dramatically. This plant forms dense, low-growing mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in vulnerable landscapes. Its unique adaptations include a deep root system and a thick, waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss. Despite its tough conditions, Desert carpetgrass plays a vital role in desert ecosystems and has been used by indigenous communities for centuries.
