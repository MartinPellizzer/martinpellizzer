Kanimia corymbulosa

Kanimia corymbulosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. These flowers are clustered in corymbs, giving the plant its scientific name.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
