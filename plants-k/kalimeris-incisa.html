Kalimeris incisa

Kalimeris incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Kalimeris incisa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
