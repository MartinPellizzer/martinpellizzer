Kengyilia alatavica var. longiglumis

Kengyilia alatavica var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume kengyilia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, exhibiting adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the stems are erect and branching.

The flowers are yellow in color, contributing to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
