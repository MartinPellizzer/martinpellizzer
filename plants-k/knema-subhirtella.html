Knema subhirtella

Knema subhirtella, commonly known as the white knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Knema subhirtella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
