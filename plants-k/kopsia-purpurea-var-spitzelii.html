Kopsia purpurea var. spitzelii

Kopsia purpurea var. spitzelii, commonly known as the Spitzel's kopsia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
