Kibessia cordata

Kibessia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf kibessia, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Kibessia cordata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
