Kickxia aegyptiaca

Kickxia aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
