Krapfia ranunculacea

Krapfia ranunculacea, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and small, yellowish flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the buttercup and celandine.

Krapfia ranunculacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
