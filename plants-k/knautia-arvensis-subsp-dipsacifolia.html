Knautia arvensis subsp. dipsacifolia

Knautia arvensis subsp.

dipsacifolia, commonly known as the field knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in natural habitats.
