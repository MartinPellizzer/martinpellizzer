Koelreuteria arborea

Koelreuteria arborea, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which turn red or orange in autumn. The tree has a rounded crown and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Koelreuteria arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
