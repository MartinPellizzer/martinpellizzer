Koeleria gracilis subsp. transsilvanica

Koeleria gracilis subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.

The plant has slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations in its native range.
