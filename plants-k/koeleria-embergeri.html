Koeleria embergeri

Koeleria embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue or greenish flowers.

Koeleria embergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
