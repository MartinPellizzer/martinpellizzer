Knema geminata

Knema geminata, commonly known as the twin-leaf knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that often appear in pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Knema geminata is valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
