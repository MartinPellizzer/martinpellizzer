Koeleria × schurii

Koeleria × schurii, commonly known as the hybrid bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Its flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences that produce light blue to purple florets, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Koeleria × schurii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
