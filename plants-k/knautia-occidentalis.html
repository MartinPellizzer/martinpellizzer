Knautia occidentalis

Knautia occidentalis, commonly known as Western knautia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy pink to purple flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Knautia occidentalis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
