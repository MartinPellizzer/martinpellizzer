Karpatiosorbus rhombiformis

Karpatiosorbus rhombiformis, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin, and turn yellow in the fall.

Karpatiosorbus rhombiformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
