Knautia arvensis var. campestris

Knautia arvensis var. campestris, commonly known as the meadow knautia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It serves as a nectar source for bees and other pollinators, contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
