Klasea flavescens subsp. mucronata

Klasea flavescens subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the yellow-flowered klasea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow disc flowers surrounded by ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy flowers.
