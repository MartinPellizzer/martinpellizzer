Kobiosis mellifera

Kobiosis mellifera, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a unique species belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescence that resembles a honeycomb, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often emitting a mild, sweet scent.

Kobiosis mellifera is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
