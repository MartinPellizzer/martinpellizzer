Knautia arvernensis

Knautia arvernensis, commonly known as the Alpine knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Knautia arvernensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
