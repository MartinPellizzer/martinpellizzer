Kyllinga brunneoalba

Kyllinga brunneoalba, commonly known as the white-veined spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with pale green to white veins, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers. Kyllinga brunneoalba is often found in shallow water or damp soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
