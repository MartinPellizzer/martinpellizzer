Koeleria cristata f. aurea

Koeleria cristata f. aurea, commonly known as the golden tufted grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright yellow inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
