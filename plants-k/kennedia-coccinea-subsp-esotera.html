Kennedia coccinea subsp. esotera

Kennedia coccinea subsp.

esotera, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
