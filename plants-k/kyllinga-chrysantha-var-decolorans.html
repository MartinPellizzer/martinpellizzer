Kyllinga chrysantha var. decolorans

Kyllinga chrysantha var. decolorans, commonly known as the yellow flame plant, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette growth habit and striking yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include a short rhizome and small, clustered inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season.
