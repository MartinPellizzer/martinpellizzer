Klasea boetica subsp. lusitanica

Klasea boetica subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in open woodlands and scrublands.

The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
