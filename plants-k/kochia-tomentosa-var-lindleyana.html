Kochia tomentosa var. lindleyana

Kochia tomentosa var. lindleyana, commonly known as silky kochia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
