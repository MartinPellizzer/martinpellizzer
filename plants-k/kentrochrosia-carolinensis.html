Kentrochrosia carolinensis

Kentrochrosia carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina croton, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and often showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and in wetlands.
