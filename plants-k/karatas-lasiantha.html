Karatas lasiantha

Karatas lasiantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf lily.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
