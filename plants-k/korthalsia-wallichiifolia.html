Korthalsia wallichiifolia

Korthalsia wallichiifolia, commonly known as the Malay trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has woody stems and produces small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Korthalsia wallichiifolia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
