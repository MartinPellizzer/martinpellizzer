Knautia timeroyi subsp. collina

Knautia timeroyi subsp.

collina, commonly known as the alpine knautia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
