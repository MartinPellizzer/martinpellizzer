Koeleria vallesiana

Koeleria vallesiana, commonly known as the valley grass or Sierra grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, greenish to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Koeleria vallesiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
