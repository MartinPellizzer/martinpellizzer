Korthalsella dichotoma var. dichotoma

Korthalsella dichotoma var. dichotoma, commonly known as the forked korthalsella, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a short, erect stem with opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This species is noted for its adaptability to arid environments and its preference for well-drained soils.
