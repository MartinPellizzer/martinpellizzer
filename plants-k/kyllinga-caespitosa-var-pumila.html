Kyllinga caespitosa var. pumila

Kyllinga caespitosa var. pumila, commonly known as dwarf spikerush, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It forms dense, clump-like growths with slender, grass-like leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
