Kosteletzkya velutina var. genuina

Kosteletzkya velutina var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain regions.
