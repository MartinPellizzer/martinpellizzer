Kochia macroptera

Kochia macroptera, commonly known as the Russian spinach, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often succulent. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters that appear in late summer. Kochia macroptera is native to Eurasia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is sometimes cultivated as a leaf vegetable due to its edible leaves and nutritional value.
