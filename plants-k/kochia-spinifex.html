Kochia spinifex

Kochia spinifex, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to Eurasia and is characterized by its upright, branching structure and distinctive bracts that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late summer. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly salty, spinach-like flavor, making it a popular leafy vegetable in some regions.

Kochia spinifex is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes used as a cover crop or ornamental plant.
