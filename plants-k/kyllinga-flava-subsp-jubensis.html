Kyllinga flava subsp. jubensis

Kyllinga flava subsp.

jubensis, commonly known as yellow bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant has triangular stems and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive waxy coating. Kyllinga flava subsp.

jubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
