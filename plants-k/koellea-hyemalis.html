Koellea hyemalis

Koellea hyemalis, commonly known as the winter heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, and are typically pale purple or white.

Koellea hyemalis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
