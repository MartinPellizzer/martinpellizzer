Kniphofia rivularis

Kniphofia rivularis, commonly known as the river red grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and vibrant red and orange flower spikes that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Kniphofia rivularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and garden designs.
