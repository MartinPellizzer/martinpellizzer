Koeleria gracilis var. borbasii

Koeleria gracilis var. borbasii, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
