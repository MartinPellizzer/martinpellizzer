Kallstroemia pentandra

Kallstroemia pentandra, commonly known as the scarlet flame or red flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thick, woody stems and broad, dark green leaves. The plant is notable for its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, displaying bright red petals with yellow stamens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Kallstroemia pentandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
