Krascheninnikovia ceratoides subsp. tragacanthoides

Krascheninnikovia ceratoides subsp.

tragacanthoides, commonly known as the tragacanth goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
