Kyrstenia melissoides

Kyrstenia melissoides, commonly known as the honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Kyrstenia melissoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
