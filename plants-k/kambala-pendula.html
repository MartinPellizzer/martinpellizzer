Kambala pendula

Kambala pendula, commonly known as the weeping kambala, is a unique plant characterized by its cascading foliage.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant features long, drooping stems that bear lance-shaped leaves, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. Its flowers are small and tubular, typically displaying shades of red or purple, which attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Kambala pendula thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.
