Kaempferia caespitosa

Kaempferia caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
