Kohleria filisepala

Kohleria filisepala, commonly known as the thread-leaved kohleria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and come in shades of pink or white, arranged in clusters.
