Kadsura ananosma

Kadsura ananosma, commonly known as the giant kadsura, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique, tubular flower that resembles a snake or a bird's beak, which is a key feature in its pollination strategy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
