Kleinia filifolia

Kleinia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its adaptations to arid environments make it a resilient and hardy plant for xeriscaping.
