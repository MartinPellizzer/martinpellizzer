Kosteletzkya diplocrater var. sinuatiloba

Kosteletzkya diplocrater var. sinuatiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbed stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive sinuate margin. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
