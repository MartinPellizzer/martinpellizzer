Koeleria cristata eriostachya

Koeleria cristata eriostachya, commonly known as the tufted grass or mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Koeleria cristata eriostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
