Knautia timeroyi subsp. carpophylax

Knautia timeroyi subsp.

carpophylax, commonly known as the Carpathian knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
