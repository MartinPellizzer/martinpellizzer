Kaluhaburunghos

Kaluhaburunghos, commonly known as the sacred fig tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are rich in nutrients. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The Kaluhaburunghos is also valued for its cultural and medicinal significance in traditional practices.
