Koeleria cristata var. tokiensis

Koeleria cristata var. tokiensis, commonly known as the Toki's tufted grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
