Kerriochloa siamensis

Kerriochloa siamensis, commonly known as the Siam grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered at the base of the plant.

Kerriochloa siamensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
