Kalaharia uncinata f. rubra

Kalaharia uncinata f. rubra, commonly known as the red-fruited kalaharia, is a unique variant of the kalaharia plant characterized by its striking red berries.

This subspecies is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red fruits that are visually distinct from the typical green or yellow berries of the species. Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and potential ecological role in its native environment.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and noteworthy plant in botanical studies.
