Kedrostis gracilis

Kedrostis gracilis, commonly known as the slender African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

Kedrostis gracilis is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to livestock.
