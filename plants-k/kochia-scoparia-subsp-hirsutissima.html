Kochia scoparia subsp. hirsutissima

Kochia scoparia subsp.

hirsutissima, commonly known as the Russian spinach or fireweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Eurasia. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lobed, gray-green leaves that become more succulent as the plant matures.

The species produces bright yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
