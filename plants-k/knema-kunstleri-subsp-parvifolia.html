Knema kunstleri subsp. parvifolia

Knema kunstleri subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved knema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
