Knautia integrifolia var. hellenica

Knautia integrifolia var. hellenica, commonly known as the Greek knautia, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features upright stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are composed of numerous small, showy petals arranged in a spire-like structure.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
