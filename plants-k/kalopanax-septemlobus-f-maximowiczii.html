Kalopanax septemlobus f. maximowiczii

Kalopanax septemlobus f. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Maximowicz's seven-lobed cow-itch, is a variant of the Kalopanax septemlobus species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is noted for its ornamental value and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
