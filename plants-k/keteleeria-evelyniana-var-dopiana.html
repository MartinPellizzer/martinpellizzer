Keteleeria evelyniana var. dopiana

Keteleeria evelyniana var. dopiana, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir native to Southeast Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
