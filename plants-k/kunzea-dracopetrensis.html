Kunzea dracopetrensis

Kunzea dracopetrensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and near-coastal areas. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons.

Kunzea dracopetrensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in horticulture due to its hardy nature and ornamental features.
