Kunzea badjaensis

Kunzea badjaensis, commonly known as the Badja kunzea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in areas with a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a glandular surface that produces a distinctive scent.

Kunzea badjaensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in horticulture.
