Kalmia latifolia var. myrtifolia

Kalmia latifolia var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland areas. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a glossy appearance, and its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local wildlife.
