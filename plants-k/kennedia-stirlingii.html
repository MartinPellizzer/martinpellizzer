Kennedia stirlingii

Kennedia stirlingii, commonly known as the Stirling's Kennedia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its flowers are attractive to birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Kennedia stirlingii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant characteristics.
