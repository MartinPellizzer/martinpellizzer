Knautia arvensis proles subscaposa

Knautia arvensis proles subscaposa, commonly known as the scapose knautia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and stems that are either upright or slightly prostrate. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
