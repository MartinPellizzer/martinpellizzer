Kohleria collina

Kohleria collina, commonly known as the pink kohleria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant features velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Kohleria collina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation in the wild.
