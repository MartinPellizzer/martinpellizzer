Kalanchoe peltata var. mandrakensis

Kalanchoe peltata var. mandrakensis, commonly known as the Madagascar windowleaf, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in indoor gardening.
