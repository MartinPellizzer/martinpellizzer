Kigelianthe macrantha

Kigelianthe macrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered kigelia, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and forested areas. The plant is characterized by its large, showy red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Kigelianthe macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
