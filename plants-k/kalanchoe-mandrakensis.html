Kalanchoe mandrakensis

Kalanchoe mandrakensis, commonly known as the Cape Town flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to store water. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often blooming in winter. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Kalanchoe mandrakensis is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, which makes it relatively easy to grow from cuttings.
