Koeleria moldavica

Koeleria moldavica, commonly known as the Moldavian grass, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Koeleria moldavica is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
