Koeleria gracilis f. festucoides

Koeleria gracilis f. festucoides, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
