Kalanchoe pinnata var. brevicalyx

Kalanchoe pinnata var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a succulent plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the spring and summer months. It is a low-growing perennial that spreads rapidly through its trailing stems and offsets.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for arid environments and easy care in indoor settings.
