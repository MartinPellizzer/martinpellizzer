Kielmeyera rubriflora var. affinis

Kielmeyera rubriflora var. affinis, commonly known as the red-flowered kielmeyera, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and striking red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation purposes.
