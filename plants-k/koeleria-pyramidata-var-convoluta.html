Koeleria pyramidata var. convoluta

Koeleria pyramidata var. convoluta, commonly known as the curled wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and distinctive, rolled or curled leaf blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
