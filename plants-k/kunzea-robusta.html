Kunzea robusta

Kunzea robusta, commonly known as the rough-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Kunzea robusta is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
