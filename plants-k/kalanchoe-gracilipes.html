Kalanchoe gracilipes

Kalanchoe gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

Kalanchoe gracilipes is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
