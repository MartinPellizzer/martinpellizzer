Kniphofia × maxima

Kniphofia × maxima, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Kniphofia, which includes several species known for their showy, torch-like inflorescences. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of bright orange, red, or yellow tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Kniphofia × maxima is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
