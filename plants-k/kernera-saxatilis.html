Kernera saxatilis

Kernera saxatilis, commonly known as the rock kernera, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.
