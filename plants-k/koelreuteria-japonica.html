Koelreuteria japonica

Koelreuteria japonica, commonly known as the pagoda tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, lantern-like fruit clusters that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive seed pods.

Koelreuteria japonica is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
