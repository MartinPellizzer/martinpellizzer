Knautia integrifolia var. typica

Knautia integrifolia var. typica, commonly known as the smooth knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula.

This perennial species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
