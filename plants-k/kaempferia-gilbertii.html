Kaempferia gilbertii

Kaempferia gilbertii, commonly known as the Gilbert's ginger, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, ginger-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and prefers warm, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a reddish or purple tint.

Kaempferia gilbertii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
