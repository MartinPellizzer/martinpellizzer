Kuromatea

Kuromatea, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, fragrant scent. Kuromatea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and cultural significance.
