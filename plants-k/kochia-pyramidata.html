Kochia pyramidata

Kochia pyramidata, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red foliage that turns vibrant in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Kochia pyramidata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.
