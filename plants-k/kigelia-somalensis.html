Kigelia somalensis

Kigelia somalensis, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, sausage-shaped fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
