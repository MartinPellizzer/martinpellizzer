Korycarpus diandrus

Korycarpus diandrus, commonly known as the double-seed nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces unique fruits that contain two seeds, which are used to make spice blends similar to nutmeg. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Korycarpus diandrus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
