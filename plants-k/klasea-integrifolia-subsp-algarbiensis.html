Klasea integrifolia subsp. algarbiensis

Klasea integrifolia subsp.

algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbiensis klasea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are entire and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is typically found in Mediterranean-type climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
