Kraunhia sinensis var. floribunda

Kraunhia sinensis var. floribunda, commonly known as Chinese flameflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
