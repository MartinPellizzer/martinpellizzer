Kieseria

Kieseria, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, thick leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Kieseria species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and compact growth habit. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus includes several species, such as Kieseria picta and Kieseria macrophylla, which are popular among gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
