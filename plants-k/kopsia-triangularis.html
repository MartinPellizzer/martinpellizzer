Kopsia triangularis

Kopsia triangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Kopsia triangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
