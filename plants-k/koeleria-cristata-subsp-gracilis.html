Koeleria cristata subsp. gracilis

Koeleria cristata subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically forms dense, tufted clumps with fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silky bloom.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
