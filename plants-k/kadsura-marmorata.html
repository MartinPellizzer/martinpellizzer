Kadsura marmorata

Kadsura marmorata, commonly known as the marble kadsura, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that resemble marble patterns. The plant produces unique, hood-shaped flowers that are often green or brown with intricate veining. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
