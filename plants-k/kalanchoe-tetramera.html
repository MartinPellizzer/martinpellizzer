Kalanchoe tetramera

Kalanchoe tetramera, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are covered in tiny, leaf-like structures called phylloclades. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers during the winter months. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.

Kalanchoe tetramera is also known for its ability to propagate through its trailing stems, which can root easily when placed on soil.
