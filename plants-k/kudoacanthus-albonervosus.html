Kudoacanthus albonervosus

Kudoacanthus albonervosus, commonly known as the white-veined spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have prominent white veins that contrast with the dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
