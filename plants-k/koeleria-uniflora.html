Koeleria uniflora

Koeleria uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Koeleria uniflora is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
