Klasea procumbens

Klasea procumbens, commonly known as the creeping klasea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Klasea procumbens thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
