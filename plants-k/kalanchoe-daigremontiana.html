Kalanchoe daigremontiana

Kalanchoe daigremontiana, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and summer months. Its unique ability to propagate through plantlets that grow along the edges of its leaves makes it a popular choice for gardeners.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
