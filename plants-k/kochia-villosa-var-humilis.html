Kochia villosa var. humilis

Kochia villosa var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf Russian spinach, is a low-growing, herbaceous annual plant native to Eurasia.

It features dense, fuzzy, bluish-green foliage that turns reddish-pink in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its adaptability and vibrant fall color make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
