Kalanchoe velutina subsp. dangeardii

Kalanchoe velutina subsp.

dangeardii, commonly known as the Dangeard's Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, which give it its scientific name.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
