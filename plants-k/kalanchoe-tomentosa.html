Kalanchoe tomentosa

Kalanchoe tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly butterleaf, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.

Kalanchoe tomentosa is also known for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
