Kerneria pilosa var. discoidea

Kerneria pilosa var. discoidea, commonly known as the woolly kerneria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
