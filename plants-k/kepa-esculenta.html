Kepa esculenta

Kepa esculenta, commonly known as the edible nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic compounds unless properly prepared. Its leaves and stems are also used in traditional medicine for their alkaloid content.

Due to its toxicity, it requires careful handling and preparation before consumption.
