Kalanchoe verea

Kalanchoe verea, commonly known as the "Flower of the Moon," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, and it produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.

Kalanchoe verea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
