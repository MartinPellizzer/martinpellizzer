Knautia lancifolia subsp. pocutica

Knautia lancifolia subsp.

pocutica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
