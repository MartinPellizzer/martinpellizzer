Kedrostis psammophila

Kedrostis psammophila, commonly known as the sand lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Kedrostis psammophila is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
