Koeleria caudata

Koeleria caudata, commonly known as the tufted grass or prairie grass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in association with other native grasses.

Koeleria caudata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
