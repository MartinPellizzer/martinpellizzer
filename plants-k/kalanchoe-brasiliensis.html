Kalanchoe brasiliensis

Kalanchoe brasiliensis, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of vibrant orange or yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often propagated through its distinctive plantlets that grow along the edges of its leaves.

Kalanchoe brasiliensis is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
