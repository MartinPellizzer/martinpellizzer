Koeleria cristata var. colorata

Koeleria cristata var. colorata, commonly known as the pink crestgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking pink to purple inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
