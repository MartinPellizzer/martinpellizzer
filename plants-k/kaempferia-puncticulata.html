Kaempferia puncticulata

Kaempferia puncticulata, commonly known as the speckled ginger, is a tropical ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

Kaempferia puncticulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
