Koeleria webbiana

Koeleria webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's goatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in alpine and arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Koeleria webbiana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
