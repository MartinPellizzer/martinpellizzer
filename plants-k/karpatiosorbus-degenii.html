Karpatiosorbus degenii

Karpatiosorbus degenii, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, contributing to its ornamental value.

Karpatiosorbus degenii is adapted to rocky and forested habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
