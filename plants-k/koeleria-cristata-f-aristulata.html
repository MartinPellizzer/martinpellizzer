Koeleria cristata f. aristulata

Koeleria cristata f. aristulata, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
