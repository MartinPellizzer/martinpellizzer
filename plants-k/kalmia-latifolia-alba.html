Kalmia latifolia alba

Kalmia latifolia alba, commonly known as the white mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy floral displays. The plant features dense, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Kalmia latifolia alba is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
