Kochia sanguinea

Kochia sanguinea, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage that turns vibrant shades of orange and red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads in the winter. Kochia sanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also known for its resilience to drought and poor soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
