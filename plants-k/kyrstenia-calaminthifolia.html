Kyrstenia calaminthifolia

Kyrstenia calaminthifolia, commonly known as the rock lettuce, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in a terminal inflorescence, usually in shades of yellow or green.

Kyrstenia calaminthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
