Karpatiosorbus pseudolatifolia

Karpatiosorbus pseudolatifolia, commonly known as the Carpathian barbed hawthorn, is a species of shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
