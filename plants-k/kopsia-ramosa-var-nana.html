Kopsia ramosa var. nana

Kopsia ramosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf kopsia, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for container gardening and small spaces.
