Knema cinerea f. patentinervia

Knema cinerea f. patentinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined knema, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with prominent, broad veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique leaf venation pattern distinguishes it from other members of the Knema genus.
