Kunzea micrantha subsp. petiolata

Kunzea micrantha subsp.

petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its growth habit is compact, with multiple stems arising from the base.

It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.
