Kitigorchis erythrochrysea

Kitigorchis erythrochrysea, commonly known as the red-chested orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow floral markings, which are particularly prominent on the inner petals and lip. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the forest floor, and it often grows as an epiphyte on trees.

The orchid's unique coloration and structure make it a subject of interest for both botanists and horticulturists.
