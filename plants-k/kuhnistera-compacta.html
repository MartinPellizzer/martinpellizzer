Kuhnistera compacta

Kuhnistera compacta, commonly known as the compact kuhnistera, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
