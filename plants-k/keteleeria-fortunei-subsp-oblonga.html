Keteleeria fortunei subsp. oblonga

Keteleeria fortunei subsp.

oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a tall evergreen conifer native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the coniferous family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
