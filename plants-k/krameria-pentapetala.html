Krameria pentapetala

Krameria pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled krameria, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces fruit that resembles a small, fleshy berry. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Krameria pentapetala is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
