Kochia murrayana

Kochia murrayana, commonly known as Murray's kochia, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the arid regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, bract-like leaves that form a spiny, globe-like structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh environments.

Kochia murrayana is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
