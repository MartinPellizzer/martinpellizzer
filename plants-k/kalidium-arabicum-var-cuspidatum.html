Kalidium arabicum var. cuspidatum

Kalidium arabicum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spikes of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
