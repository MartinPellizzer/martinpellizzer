Kengyilia zhaosuensis

Kengyilia zhaosuensis, commonly known as the Zhaosu bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Xinjiang region of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
