Kellochloa brachyantha

Kellochloa brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hairgrass, is a small, perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Kellochloa brachyantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
