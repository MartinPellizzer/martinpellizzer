Kochia scoparia f. suaedifolia

Kochia scoparia f. suaedifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that shimmers in the sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is a short-lived perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and visually appealing plants.
