Kniphofia ichopensis var. ichopensis

Kniphofia ichopensis var. ichopensis, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence composed of bright red to orange tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
