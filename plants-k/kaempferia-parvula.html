Kaempferia parvula

Kaempferia parvula, commonly known as the dwarf flame orchid, is a compact herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its short, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Kaempferia parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
