Kentropsis diacantha

Kentropsis diacantha, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elongated, thin stems and large, pale purple or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.

Kentropsis diacantha thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
