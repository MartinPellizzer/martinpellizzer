Koeleria pseudocristata var. oregona

Koeleria pseudocristata var. oregona, commonly known as the Oregon false flax, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
