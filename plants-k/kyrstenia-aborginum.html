Kyrstenia aborginum

Kyrstenia aborginum, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help retain moisture in its cool, humid habitat.

Kyrstenia aborginum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
