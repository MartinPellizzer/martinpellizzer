Kickxia aegyptiaca subsp. virgata

Kickxia aegyptiaca subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the Egyptian spurge or desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring a short, upright stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
