Keringa amygdalina

Keringa amygdalina, commonly known as the African peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are yellow when ripe.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
