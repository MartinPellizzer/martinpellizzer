Kydia angustifolia

Kydia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf kydia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Kydia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
