Kickxia papillosa

Kickxia papillosa, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Kickxia papillosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
