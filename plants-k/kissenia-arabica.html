Kissenia arabica

Kissenia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian senna, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal and desert regions.

Kissenia arabica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
