Knema luteola

Knema luteola, commonly known as the yellow knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
