Kyllinga odorata var. fallax

Kyllinga odorata var. fallax, commonly known as the fragrant swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to reddish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily in suitable habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
