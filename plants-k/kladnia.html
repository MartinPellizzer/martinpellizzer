Kladnia

Kladnia, commonly known as the bluebell or wild garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces small, white berries that ripen in summer. Kladnia is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in woodland areas.
