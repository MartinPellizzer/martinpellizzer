Koeleria phleoides var. glabriflora

Koeleria phleoides var. glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it thrives in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
