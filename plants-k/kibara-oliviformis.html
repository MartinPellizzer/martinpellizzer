Kibara oliviformis

Kibara oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped kibara, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive elliptical leaves that resemble olive leaves in shape, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Kibara oliviformis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
