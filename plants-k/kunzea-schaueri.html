Kunzea schaueri

Kunzea schaueri, commonly known as Schauer's kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, pink or white, and are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Kunzea schaueri is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
