Kyllinga aurea

Kyllinga aurea, commonly known as golden kyllinga, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, grass-like stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are narrow and grassy in appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spiky, bracted inflorescences. Kyllinga aurea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
