Kanakomyrtus myrtopsidoides

Kanakomyrtus myrtopsidoides, commonly known as the New Caledonian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules containing numerous seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family.

Kanakomyrtus myrtopsidoides is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat and is often found in forest undergrowth.
