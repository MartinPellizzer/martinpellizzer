Kniphofia micrantha

Kniphofia micrantha, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia micrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
