Kyllinga ovularis

Kyllinga ovularis, commonly known as the broadleaf spikerush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has upright, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or greenish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

Kyllinga ovularis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
