Kalopanax septemlobus f. variegatus

Kalopanax septemlobus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated seven-lobed cow-itch, is a cultivar of the Kalopanax genus native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and moderate care requirements make it a valuable addition to both landscape and container gardening.
