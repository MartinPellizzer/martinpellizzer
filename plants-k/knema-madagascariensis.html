Knema madagascariensis

Knema madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar knema, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by round, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
