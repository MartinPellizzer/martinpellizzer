Kochia spongiocarpa

Kochia spongiocarpa, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped foliage that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, colorful bracts. It is native to Eurasia and thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Kochia spongiocarpa is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
