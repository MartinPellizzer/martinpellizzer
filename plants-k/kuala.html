Kuala

Kuala, commonly known as the "Kuala tree" or *Erythrophleum fordii*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
