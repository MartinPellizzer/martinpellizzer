Kalimeris indica subsp. collina

Kalimeris indica subsp.

collina, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy composite flower heads that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Kalimeris indica subsp.

collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
