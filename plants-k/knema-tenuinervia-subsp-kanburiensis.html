Knema tenuinervia subsp. kanburiensis

Knema tenuinervia subsp.

kanburiensis, commonly known as the Kanburi knema, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in lowland to hill forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
