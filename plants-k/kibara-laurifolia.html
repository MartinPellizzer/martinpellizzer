Kibara laurifolia

Kibara laurifolia, commonly known as the African lauriferous tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Kibara laurifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
