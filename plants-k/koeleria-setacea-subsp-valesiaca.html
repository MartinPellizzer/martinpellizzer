Koeleria setacea subsp. valesiaca

Koeleria setacea subsp.

valesiaca, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, including alpine meadows and open woodlands.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
