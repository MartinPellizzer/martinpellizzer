Kremeriella

Kremeriella, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plants are characterized by their compact, spiny stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. Kremeriella species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance and drought tolerance.

They have a shallow root system and require minimal water, making them well-suited for xeriscaping.
