Karwinskia rzedowskii

Karwinskia rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican redwood, is a rare and endangered conifer native to the high-altitude cloud forests of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark-green foliage, which contributes to its striking appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, and it has a unique ability to regenerate from its root system after disturbances. Due to habitat loss and deforestation, it is critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a relict species with ancient lineage, offering insights into evolutionary adaptations in mountain ecosystems.
