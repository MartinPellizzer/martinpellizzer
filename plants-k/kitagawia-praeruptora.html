Kitagawia praeruptora

Kitagawia praeruptora, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

Kitagawia praeruptora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
