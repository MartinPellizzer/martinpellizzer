Kunzea axillaris

Kunzea axillaris, commonly known as the coastal kunzea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Kunzea axillaris is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
