Knema curtisii var. amoena

Knema curtisii var. amoena, commonly known as the fragrant knema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
