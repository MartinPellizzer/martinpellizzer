Knema glauca

Knema glauca, commonly known as the blue knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Knema glauca plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
