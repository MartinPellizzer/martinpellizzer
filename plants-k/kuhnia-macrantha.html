Kuhnia macrantha

Kuhnia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered kuhnia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Kuhnia macrantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
