Koeleria vallesiana var. minoriflora

Koeleria vallesiana var. minoriflora, commonly known as the Sierra Valley grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
