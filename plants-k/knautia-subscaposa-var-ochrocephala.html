Knautia subscaposa var. ochrocephala

Knautia subscaposa var. ochrocephala, commonly known as the yellowhead knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
