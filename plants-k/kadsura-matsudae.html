Kadsura matsudae

Kadsura matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese kadsura, is a rare and ornamental climbing plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, irregularly shaped flowers that are often greenish-brown in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
