Kalanchoe rubella

Kalanchoe rubella, commonly known as the red-veined kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Kalanchoe rubella is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
