Kalanchoe mossambicana

Kalanchoe mossambicana, commonly known as the Mossambique Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight red or purple tint. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ability to propagate through stem cuttings makes it easy to cultivate and propagate.
