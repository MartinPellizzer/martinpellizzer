Kalanchoe jongmansii subsp. ivohibensis

Kalanchoe jongmansii subsp.

ivohibensis, commonly known as the Madagascar kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the dry season. It is a low-growing shrub with a compact form, often used in rock gardens or as a potted plant due to its ornamental value.

Its ability to propagate through basal offsets makes it relatively easy to cultivate in suitable environments.
