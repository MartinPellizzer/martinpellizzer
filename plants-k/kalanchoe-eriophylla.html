Kalanchoe eriophylla

Kalanchoe eriophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of trichomes, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe eriophylla is drought-tolerant and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
