Kalanchoe ndorensis

Kalanchoe ndorensis, commonly known as the Ndorensis Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the cooler months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Kalanchoe ndorensis is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, making it easy to grow in both indoor and outdoor settings.
