Kalopternix sophronitis

Kalopternix sophronitis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The golden shower tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
