Kielmeyera corymbosa var. typica

Kielmeyera corymbosa var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is adapted to humid, warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with smooth bark and a tendency to form dense thickets.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
