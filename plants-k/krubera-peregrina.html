Krubera peregrina

Krubera peregrina, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-filled capsules for reproduction.

Krubera peregrina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
