Kalmia angustifolia f. rubra

Kalmia angustifolia f. rubra, commonly known as the redleaf mountain laurel, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, leathery foliage that ranges from dark green to vibrant red, depending on the variety. The plant produces showy clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers in late spring. Its botanical characteristics include elliptical leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental use in shaded gardens.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
