Koeleria grandiflora var. minor

Koeleria grandiflora var. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
