Koeleria eriostachya subsp. caucasica

Koeleria eriostachya subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian koeleria, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Caucasus region. It typically forms dense tufts with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
