Kinghamia foliosa

Kinghamia foliosa, commonly known as the ghost gum, is an evergreen tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped trunk and its feathery, grey-green foliage. The plant has small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in Australian flora.
