Kruhsea streptopoides

Kruhsea streptopoides, commonly known as the purple-stemmed kruhsea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a distinct purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Kruhsea streptopoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
