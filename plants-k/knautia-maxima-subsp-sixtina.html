Knautia maxima subsp. sixtina

Knautia maxima subsp.

sixtina, commonly known as the alpine knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
