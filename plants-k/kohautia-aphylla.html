Kohautia aphylla

Kohautia aphylla, commonly known as the naked leaf kohautia, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, basal leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers on slender stems, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Kohautia aphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
