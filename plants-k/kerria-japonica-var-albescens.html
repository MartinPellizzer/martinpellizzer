Kerria japonica var. albescens

Kerria japonica var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered kerria, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dense, arching branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white, single flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. Its foliage is dark green in summer and turns yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
