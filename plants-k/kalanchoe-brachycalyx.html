Kalanchoe brachycalyx

Kalanchoe brachycalyx, commonly known as the mother of millions, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Kalanchoe brachycalyx is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
