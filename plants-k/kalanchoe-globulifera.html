Kalanchoe globulifera

Kalanchoe globulifera, commonly known as the globe-shaped kalanhoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Kalanchoe globulifera is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
