Klasea flavescens var. neglecta

Klasea flavescens var. neglecta, commonly known as the yellow-flowered klasea, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

This species is noted for its low maintenance and adaptability, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
