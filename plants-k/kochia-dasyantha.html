Kochia dasyantha

Kochia dasyantha, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and striking, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by dry, three-lobed seed capsules. Kochia dasyantha is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

It is often used as a forage plant for livestock and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
