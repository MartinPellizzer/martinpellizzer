Kickxia ovata

Kickxia ovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Kickxia ovata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
