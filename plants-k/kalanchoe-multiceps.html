Kalanchoe multiceps

Kalanchoe multiceps, commonly known as the silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of vibrant orange or red flowers during the winter months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Kalanchoe multiceps is also noted for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it relatively easy to cultivate.
