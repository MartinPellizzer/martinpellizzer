Kreodanthus sytsmae

Kreodanthus sytsmae, commonly known as the Sytsma's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale purple flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant exhibits a unique symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its nutrient uptake. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Kreodanthus sytsmae is considered a conservation priority.
