Kumaria

Kumaria, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the genus Phyllanthus and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing high levels of vitamin C. Kumaria thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and modern horticulture.
