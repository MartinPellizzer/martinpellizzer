Koenigia monandra

Koenigia monandra, commonly known as the "Pineapple Plant" or "False Pineapple," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces a striking, pineapple-like inflorescence that bears small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Koenigia monandra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
