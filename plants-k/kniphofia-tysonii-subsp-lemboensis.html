Kniphofia tysonii subsp. lemboensis

Kniphofia tysonii subsp.

lemboensis, commonly known as the red-hot poker or firetorch plant, is a striking member of the Asphodelaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that display vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers, which are particularly showy during the warmer months.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both garden display and conservation efforts.
