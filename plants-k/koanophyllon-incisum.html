Koanophyllon incisum

Koanophyllon incisum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Koanophyllon incisum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
