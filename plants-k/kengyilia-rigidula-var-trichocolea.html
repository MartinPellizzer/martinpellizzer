Kengyilia rigidula var. trichocolea

Kengyilia rigidula var. trichocolea, commonly known as the hairy leafed kengyilia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
