Kyllinga controversa var. subexalata

Kyllinga controversa var. subexalata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, and its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of spikelets. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
