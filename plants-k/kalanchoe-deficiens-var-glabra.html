Kalanchoe deficiens var. glabra

Kalanchoe deficiens var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are smooth and often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect sunlight.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
