Kalmia latifolia f. polypetala

Kalmia latifolia f. polypetala, commonly known as the broadleaf kalmia, is a variant of the common kalmia, native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The flowers are typically clustered in upright inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
