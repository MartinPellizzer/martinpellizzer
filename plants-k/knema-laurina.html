Knema laurina

Knema laurina, commonly known as the sweet sanders or laurina tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.

Knema laurina is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
