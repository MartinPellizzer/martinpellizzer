Koeleria mukdenensis subsp. chenteensis

Koeleria mukdenensis subsp.

chenteensis, commonly known as the Chinese narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in open, grassy habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
