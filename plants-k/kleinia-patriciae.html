Kleinia patriciae

Kleinia patriciae, commonly known as the Patricia's ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained sandy soils.

Kleinia patriciae is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
