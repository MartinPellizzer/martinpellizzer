Karatas plumieri

Karatas plumieri, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant, long-lasting foliage.
