Kengyilia mutica

Kengyilia mutica, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant is known for its small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are often clustered in a spherical shape. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions.

Kengyilia mutica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
