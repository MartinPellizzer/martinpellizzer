Knema glaucescens var. cordata

Knema glaucescens var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptic to heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
