Kigelia talbotii

Kigelia talbotii, commonly known as the African sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, curved, woody fruits that resemble sausages, which are covered in a thick, leathery outer layer. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
