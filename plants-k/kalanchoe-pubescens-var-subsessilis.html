Kalanchoe pubescens var. subsessilis

Kalanchoe pubescens var. subsessilis, commonly known as the wooly kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

The species is also noted for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it easy to grow and propagate.
