Kyllinga campestris

Kyllinga campestris, commonly known as purple top millet, is an annual grassy plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with purple, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Kyllinga campestris is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
