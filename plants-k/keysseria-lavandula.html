Keysseria lavandula

Keysseria lavandula, commonly known as the lavender shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like flower clusters and gray-green, needle-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

Keysseria lavandula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
