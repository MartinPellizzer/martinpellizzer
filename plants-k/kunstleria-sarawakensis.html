Kunstleria sarawakensis

Kunstleria sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to Borneo.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation.
