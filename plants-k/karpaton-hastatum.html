Karpaton hastatum

Karpaton hastatum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a long, narrow shape that adds to its visual appeal.

Karpaton hastatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
