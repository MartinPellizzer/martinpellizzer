Kalanchoe migiurtinorum

Kalanchoe migiurtinorum, commonly known as the rock rose or desert kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.

Kalanchoe migiurtinorum is also known for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, making it easy to grow in gardens or indoor settings.
