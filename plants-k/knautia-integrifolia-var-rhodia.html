Knautia integrifolia var. rhodia

Knautia integrifolia var. rhodia, commonly known as the Rhodian knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a coarse texture and serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
