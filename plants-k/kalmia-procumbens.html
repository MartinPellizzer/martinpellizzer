Kalmia procumbens

Kalmia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping kalmia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year, providing year-round interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local wildlife.
