Kniphofia praecox subsp. bruceae

Kniphofia praecox subsp.

bruceae, commonly known as the Bruce's torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright red to orange-red tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators.

The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, contributing to its architectural appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to add vibrant color to gardens during the summer months.
