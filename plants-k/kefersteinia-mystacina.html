Kefersteinia mystacina

Kefersteinia mystacina, commonly known as the desert candelabra cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching, tree-like structure composed of multiple stems that form a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Kefersteinia mystacina is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions through its deep root system and ability to store water in its stems.
