Klasea serratuloides subsp. karamanica

Klasea serratuloides subsp.

karamanica, commonly known as the Karamanica Klasea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually serrated and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
