Knema hirtella var. hirtella

Knema hirtella var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy knema, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, spherical capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
