Kalanchoe kirkii

Kalanchoe kirkii, commonly known as the rock chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it store water. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Kalanchoe kirkii is drought-tolerant and is often used in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
