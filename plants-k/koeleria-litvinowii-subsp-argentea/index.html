Koeleria Litvinowii Subsp. Argentea

Koeleria litvinowii subsp. argentea is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is known for its striking silver-green foliage and small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. This subspecies thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces increasing threats to its survival.
