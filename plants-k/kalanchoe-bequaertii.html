Kalanchoe bequaertii

Kalanchoe bequaertii, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with blue or purple hues, which help it store water and withstand arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are also known to develop small, translucent offspring along the edges, a unique feature called leaf propagation.

This species thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
