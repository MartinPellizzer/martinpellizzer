Kengyilia melanthera

Kengyilia melanthera, commonly known as the black-throated hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features a robust root system and produces yellow flowers with dark purple markings on the petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Kengyilia melanthera is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
