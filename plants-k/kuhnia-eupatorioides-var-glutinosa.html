Kuhnia eupatorioides var. glutinosa

Kuhnia eupatorioides var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its sticky leaves and medicinal properties have made it historically significant in traditional herbal medicine.
