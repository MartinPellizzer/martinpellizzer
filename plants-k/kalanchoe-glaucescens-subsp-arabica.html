Kalanchoe glaucescens subsp. arabica

Kalanchoe glaucescens subsp.

arabica, commonly known as the Arabian Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
