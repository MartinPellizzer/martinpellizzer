Kadua conostyla

Kadua conostyla, commonly known as the mountain kadua, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in a composite head.

Kadua conostyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic features.
