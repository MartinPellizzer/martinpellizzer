Kraunhia floribunda var. brachybotrys

Kraunhia floribunda var. brachybotrys, commonly known as the dwarf-flowered krantz, is a compact, mat-forming perennial native to southern Africa.

It features small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
