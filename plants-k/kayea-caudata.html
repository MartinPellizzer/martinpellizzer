Kayea caudata

Kayea caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Kayea caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
