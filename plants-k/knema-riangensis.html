Knema riangensis

Knema riangensis, commonly known as the Riangensis Kneema, is a small evergreen tree native to the lowland rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in shaded, humid environments and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
