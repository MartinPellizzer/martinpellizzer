Kalanchoe deficiens

Kalanchoe deficiens, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. Its unique ability to propagate through plantlets that grow along the edges of its leaves makes it a popular choice for gardeners.

Kalanchoe deficiens is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and decorative appeal.
