Kennedia nigricans

Kennedia nigricans, commonly known as the black-flowered waratah, is a striking shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to black. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its flowers are typically visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Kennedia nigricans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in a variety of habitats, including coastal regions and woodlands.
