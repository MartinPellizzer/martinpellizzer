Kleinia lauschsii

Kleinia lauschsii, commonly known as the Schueller's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
