Kochia villosa var. eriantha

Kochia villosa var. eriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and tall, branching stems that often display a striking red or purple coloration in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose panicles, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils. Kochia villosa var. eriantha is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, and it serves as a host plant for several insect species.

Its ornamental value has led to its use in landscaping, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
