Krigia caroliniana

Krigia caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina rainflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Krigia caroliniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
