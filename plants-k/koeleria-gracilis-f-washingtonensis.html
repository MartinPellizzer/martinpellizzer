Koeleria gracilis f. washingtonensis

Koeleria gracilis f. washingtonensis, commonly known as the Washington slender grass, is a holarctic plant species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like structure with narrow, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This subspecies is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and gravelly soils. It thrives in temperate climates and is often adapted to low-nutrient environments.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
