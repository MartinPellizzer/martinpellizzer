Kadsura verrucosa

Kadsura verrucosa, commonly known as the rough kadsura, is a climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, irregularly shaped flowers. The plant produces a unique, often twisted fruit that contains several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
