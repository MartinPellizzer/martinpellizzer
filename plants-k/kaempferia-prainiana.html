Kaempferia prainiana

Kaempferia prainiana, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows up to 30 cm tall, with a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by insects.

Kaempferia prainiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
