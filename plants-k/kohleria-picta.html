Kohleria picta

Kohleria picta, commonly known as the painted kohleria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with hairy stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often surrounded by vibrant, showy bracts.

Kohleria picta is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
