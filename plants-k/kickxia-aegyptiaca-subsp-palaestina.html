Kickxia aegyptiaca subsp. palaestina

Kickxia aegyptiaca subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine spurflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss, and its root system is deep, allowing it to access groundwater.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
