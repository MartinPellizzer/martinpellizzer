Kusibabella fastor

Kusibabella fastor, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy textures. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
