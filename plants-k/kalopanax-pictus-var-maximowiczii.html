Kalopanax pictus var. maximowiczii

Kalopanax pictus var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the variegated horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with distinctive white and green variegation, which add ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, followed by spiny, bur-like fruits containing bright red seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
