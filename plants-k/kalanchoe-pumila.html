Kalanchoe pumila

Kalanchoe pumila, commonly known as the dwarf kalanchoe, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the winter months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Kalanchoe pumila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
