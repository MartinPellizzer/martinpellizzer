Kaulfussia amelloides

Kaulfussia amelloides, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, often with its roots submerged in water. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, adapted for water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
