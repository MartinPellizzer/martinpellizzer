Koniga maritima var. foliis-variegatis

Koniga maritima var. foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated sea holly, is a striking coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive, silver-white variegation on its waxy, blue-green leaves, which enhance its ornamental value. The plant produces striking blue-violet flowers in late summer, adding a vivid contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, visually appealing plant.
