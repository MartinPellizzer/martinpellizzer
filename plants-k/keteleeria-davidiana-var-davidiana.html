Keteleeria davidiana var. davidiana

Keteleeria davidiana var. davidiana, commonly known as the David fir, is a species of conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and conical shape, with dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The wood of this plant is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
