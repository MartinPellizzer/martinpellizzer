Koeleria pauneroi

Koeleria pauneroi, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is well adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

Koeleria pauneroi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
