Koelreuteria triphylla

Koelreuteria triphylla, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange spherical fruits that resemble small lanterns, which persist through winter. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Koelreuteria triphylla is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
