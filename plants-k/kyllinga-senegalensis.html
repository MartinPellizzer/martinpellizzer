Kyllinga senegalensis

Kyllinga senegalensis, commonly known as African water spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and stems that are often used in various cuisines. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in humid climates. It has a rosette growth pattern with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often red or purple in color.

Kyllinga senegalensis is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
