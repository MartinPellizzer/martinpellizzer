Koeleria wolgensis

Koeleria wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgensis grass, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Koeleria wolgensis is typically perennial and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
