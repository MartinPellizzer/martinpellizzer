Kohautia angolensis

Kohautia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses, and has narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
