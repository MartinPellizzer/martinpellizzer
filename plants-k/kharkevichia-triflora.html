Kharkevichia triflora

Kharkevichia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered kharkevichia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climates.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.
