Kudrjaschevia grubovii

Kudrjaschevia grubovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of eastern Europe and western Siberia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Kudrjaschevia grubovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
