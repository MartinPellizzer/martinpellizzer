Kotschya ochreata var. ochreata

Kotschya ochreata var. ochreata, commonly known as the golden kotschya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
