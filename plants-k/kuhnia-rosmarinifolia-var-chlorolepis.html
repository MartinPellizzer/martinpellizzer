Kuhnia rosmarinifolia var. chlorolepis

Kuhnia rosmarinifolia var. chlorolepis, commonly known as the green-leaved kuhnia, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
