Kalanchoe velutina subsp. chimanimanensis

Kalanchoe velutina subsp.

chimanimanensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
