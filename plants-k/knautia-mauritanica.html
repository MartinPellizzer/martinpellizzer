Knautia mauritanica

Knautia mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan poppy or sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pink to purple flower heads that resemble fluffy clouds, composed of numerous small, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces showy inflorescences in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Knautia mauritanica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
