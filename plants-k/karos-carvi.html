Karos carvi

Karos carvi, commonly known as caraway, is a biennial herb native to Europe and Western Asia.

It produces small, white or yellowish flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant's seeds are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine for their distinctive licorice-like flavor. Caraway thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in herbal remedies and culinary practices.
