Kajuputi

Kajuputi, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn orange-red when mature, containing a high concentration of vitamin C. Kajuputi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
