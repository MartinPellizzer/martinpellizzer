Kadsura apoensis

Kadsura apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine kadsura, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often covered in a dense, fuzzy texture. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
