Kadsura roxburghiana var. macrocarpa

Kadsura roxburghiana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited kadsura, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
