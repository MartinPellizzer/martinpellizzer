Koeleria splendens subsp. subcaudata

Koeleria splendens subsp.

subcaudata, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive, ethereal look. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom in late summer, adding a unique texture to gardens.
