Kniphofia × corallina

Kniphofia × corallina, commonly known as the coral kniphofia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular red and orange flower spikes that resemble coral. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
