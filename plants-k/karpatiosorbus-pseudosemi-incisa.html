Karpatiosorbus pseudosemi-incisa

Karpatiosorbus pseudosemi-incisa, commonly known as the Turkish rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sparsely branched structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
