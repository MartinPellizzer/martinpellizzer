Klainedoxa grandifolia

Klainedoxa grandifolia, commonly known as the giant klainedoxa, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features a robust, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters, making it a visually impressive species in gardens.

Klainedoxa grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
