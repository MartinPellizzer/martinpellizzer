Kalmia microphylla subsp. occidentalis

Kalmia microphylla subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western mountain heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
