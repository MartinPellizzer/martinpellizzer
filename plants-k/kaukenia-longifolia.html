Kaukenia longifolia

Kaukenia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved kaukenia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable species in wetland conservation efforts.
