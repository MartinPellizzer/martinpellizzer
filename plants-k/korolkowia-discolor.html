Korolkowia discolor

Korolkowia discolor, commonly known as the Indian spinach or Malabar spinach, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are tender and edible, often used in Asian cuisine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
