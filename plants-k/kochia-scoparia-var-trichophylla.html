Kochia scoparia var. trichophylla

Kochia scoparia var. trichophylla, commonly known as Russian spinach, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its upright, bushy growth habit and vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to purple. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its distinctive appearance and adaptability make it popular in gardens and as a naturalized plant in various regions.
