Koeleria pubescens var. barrelieri

Koeleria pubescens var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's goatgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is often found in steppe and desert environments, contributing to the ecological stability of these regions.
