Kniphofia linearifolia

Kniphofia linearifolia, commonly known as the red hot poker or torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of tubular red or orange flowers, which resemble a torch. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
