Kalimeris mongolica

Kalimeris mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian aster, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow to orange, with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Kalimeris mongolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
