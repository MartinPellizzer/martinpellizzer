Karpatiosorbus amici-petri

Karpatiosorbus amici-petri, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
