Kadua petiolata var. ovalifolia

Kadua petiolata var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf kadua, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
