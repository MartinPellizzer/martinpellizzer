Kalanchoe elliptica

Kalanchoe elliptica, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Kalanchoe elliptica is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through its leaf cuttings.
