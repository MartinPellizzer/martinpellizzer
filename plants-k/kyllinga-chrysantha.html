Kyllinga chrysantha

Kyllinga chrysantha, commonly known as golden kyllinga, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, slender stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Kyllinga chrysantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a cut flower.
