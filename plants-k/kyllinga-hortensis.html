Kyllinga hortensis

Kyllinga hortensis, commonly known as spinach amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are also consumed as a leafy green vegetable, similar to spinach.

Kyllinga hortensis thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
