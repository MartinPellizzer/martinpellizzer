Kochia americana

Kochia americana, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and purple foliage, which becomes especially striking in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Kochia americana is a drought-tolerant shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
