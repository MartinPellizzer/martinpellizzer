Knema ridsdaleana

Knema ridsdaleana, commonly known as the Java knema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Knema ridsdaleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
