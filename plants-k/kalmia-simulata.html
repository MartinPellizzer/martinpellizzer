Kalmia simulata

Kalmia simulata, commonly known as the Florida mountain laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped pink or white flowers in late spring, which are borne on short, erect branches. Kalmia simulata typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
