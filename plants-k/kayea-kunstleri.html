Kayea kunstleri

Kayea kunstleri, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically five-petaled and showy. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Kayea kunstleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
