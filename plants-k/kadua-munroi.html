Kadua munroi

Kadua munroi, commonly known as the mountain kadua, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Kadua munroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
