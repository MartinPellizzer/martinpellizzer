Kengia ramiflora

Kengia ramiflora, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a straight, unbranched trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Kengia ramiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
