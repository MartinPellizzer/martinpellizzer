Koeleria splendens var. subcaudata

Koeleria splendens var. subcaudata, commonly known as the silvergrass or velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silvery or blue-gray bracts. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a soft, feathery texture that adds visual interest.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
