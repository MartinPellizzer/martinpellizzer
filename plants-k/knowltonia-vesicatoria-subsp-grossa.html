Knowltonia vesicatoria subsp. grossa

Knowltonia vesicatoria subsp.

grossa, commonly known as the giant swamp mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
