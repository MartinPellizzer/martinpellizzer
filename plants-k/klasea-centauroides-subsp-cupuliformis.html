Klasea centauroides subsp. cupuliformis

Klasea centauroides subsp.

cupuliformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped klasea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
