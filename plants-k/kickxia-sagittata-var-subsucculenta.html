Kickxia sagittata var. subsucculenta

Kickxia sagittata var. subsucculenta, commonly known as the arrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, succulent-like stems and narrow, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
