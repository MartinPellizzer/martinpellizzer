Kalmia latifolia var. laevipes

Kalmia latifolia var. laevipes, commonly known as the smoothleaf mountain laurel, is a variant of the mountain laurel native to the southeastern United States.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy clusters of pink to white flowers. The leaves are smooth and elliptical, distinguishing it from other varieties of Kalmia. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
