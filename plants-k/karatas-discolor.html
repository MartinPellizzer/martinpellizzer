Karatas discolor

Karatas discolor, commonly known as the pale karatas, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces a distinctive, pale green or yellow seed pod that splits open when mature.

The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in semi-desert environments.
