Keenania ophiorrhizoides

Keenania ophiorrhizoides, commonly known as the creeping Indian fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that produce adventitious roots. The fronds are pinnately divided, with a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
