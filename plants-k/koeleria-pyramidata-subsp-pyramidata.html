Koeleria pyramidata subsp. pyramidata

Koeleria pyramidata subsp.

pyramidata, commonly known as the mountain grass or pyramid grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The plant has narrow, upright leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species.
