Kigelia elliptica

Kigelia elliptica, commonly known as the African sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, sausage-shaped, woody fruits that hang in clusters, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of pulp. The tree has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant has cultural significance in many African communities and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
