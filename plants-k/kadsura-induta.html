Kadsura induta

Kadsura induta, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the kadsura vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
