Krebsia corniculata

Krebsia corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Krebsia corniculata is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
