Kalmia angustifolia

Kalmia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain heather, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Kalmia angustifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.
