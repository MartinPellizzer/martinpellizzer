Kunzea pomifera

Kunzea pomifera, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salty air and sandy soils. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

Kunzea pomifera plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
