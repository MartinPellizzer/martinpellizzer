Kaempferia lopburiensis

Kaempferia lopburiensis, commonly known as the Lopburi ginger orchid, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive yellow or green lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Kaempferia lopburiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
