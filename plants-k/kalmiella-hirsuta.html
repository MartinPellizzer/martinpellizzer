Kalmiella hirsuta

Kalmiella hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy kalmiella, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features compact, dark green leaves that are densely covered with fine, hairy pubescence. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, adding ornamental value.

Kalmiella hirsuta is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
