Kyllinga capitata

Kyllinga capitata, commonly known as the red swamp crayfish plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with a rosette growth habit, and its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Kyllinga capitata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
