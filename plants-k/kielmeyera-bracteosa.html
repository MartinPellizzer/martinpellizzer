Kielmeyera bracteosa

Kielmeyera bracteosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
