Keraselma genistoides

Keraselma genistoides, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in axillary racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Keraselma genistoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
