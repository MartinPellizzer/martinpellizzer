Kengyilia thoroldiana var. melanthera

Kengyilia thoroldiana var. melanthera, commonly known as the black-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that range from gray-green to dark purple or black, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments.

This variant is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
