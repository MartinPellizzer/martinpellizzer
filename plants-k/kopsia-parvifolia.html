Kopsia parvifolia

Kopsia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly rough surface.

Kopsia parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
