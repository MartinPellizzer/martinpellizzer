Kadsura clemensiae

Kadsura clemensiae, commonly known as the Chinese yew or the Kadsura, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae, which includes yews and related conifers, and is characterized by its slow growth and dense foliage. The plant features dark green, needle-like leaves that remain on the plant for several years, contributing to its evergreen nature. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
