Koeleria digorica

Koeleria digorica, commonly known as the Digora grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, adding a subtle color to its natural habitat.

Koeleria digorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
