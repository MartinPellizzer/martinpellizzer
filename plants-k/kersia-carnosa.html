Kersia carnosa

Kersia carnosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf kersia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
