Kefersteinia escobariana

Kefersteinia escobariana, commonly known as the Escobaria cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with short, ribbed stems and clusters of small, white flowers. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
