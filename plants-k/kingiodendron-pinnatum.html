Kingiodendron pinnatum

Kingiodendron pinnatum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fiery orange-red flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
