Kirganelia multiflora

Kirganelia multiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered kudzuvine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
