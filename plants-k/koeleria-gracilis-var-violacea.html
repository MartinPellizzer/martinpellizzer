Koeleria gracilis var. violacea

Koeleria gracilis var. violacea, commonly known as purple wiregrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are pale purple to violet in color. This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
