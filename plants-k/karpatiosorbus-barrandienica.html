Karpatiosorbus barrandienica

Karpatiosorbus barrandienica, commonly known as the Balkan rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are edible but typically used for making jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Karpatiosorbus barrandienica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
