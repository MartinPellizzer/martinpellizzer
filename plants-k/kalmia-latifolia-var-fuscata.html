Kalmia latifolia var. fuscata

Kalmia latifolia var. fuscata, commonly known as the black alder or fuscata mountain laurel, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to nearly black in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

The species is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forest understories.
