Kayea cuspidata

Kayea cuspidata, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Kayea cuspidata is valued for its aromatic wood, which has been historically used in traditional Hawaiian culture.
