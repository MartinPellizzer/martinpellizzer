Knema sebifera

Knema sebifera, commonly known as the white knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
