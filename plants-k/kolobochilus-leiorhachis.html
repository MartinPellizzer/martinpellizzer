Kolobochilus leiorhachis

Kolobochilus leiorhachis, commonly known as the golden star ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique appearance.
