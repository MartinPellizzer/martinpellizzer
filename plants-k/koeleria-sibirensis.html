Koeleria sibirensis

Koeleria sibirensis, commonly known as the Siberian Koeleria, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arctic or alpine environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it blooms during the short summer season.

Koeleria sibirensis is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
