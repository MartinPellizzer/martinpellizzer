Kixia elastica

Kixia elastica, commonly known as the rubber plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. The plant has a caudex, a swollen base that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellow or green, and are clustered in cyathia.

Kixia elastica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
