Kalmia nitida

Kalmia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Kalmia nitida thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
