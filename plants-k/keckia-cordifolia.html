Keckia cordifolia

Keckia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf keckia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Keckia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
