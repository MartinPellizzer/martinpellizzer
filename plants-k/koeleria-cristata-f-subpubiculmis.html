Koeleria cristata f. subpubiculmis

Koeleria cristata f. subpubiculmis, commonly known as the hairy bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush, with bracts covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense layer of short hairs.

The subspecies subpubiculmis is distinguished by its more pronounced hairy structures compared to the typical form.
