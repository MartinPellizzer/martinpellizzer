Kopsia fruticosa

Kopsia fruticosa, commonly known as the red-flowered kopsia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Kopsia fruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
