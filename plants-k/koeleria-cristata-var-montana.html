Koeleria cristata var. montana

Koeleria cristata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain flax or prairie grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, spike-like clusters that appear in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
