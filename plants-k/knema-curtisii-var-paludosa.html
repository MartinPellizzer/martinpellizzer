Knema curtisii var. paludosa

Knema curtisii var. paludosa, commonly known as the swamp knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. It produces small, white flowers followed by aromatic, fleshy fruits.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a significant role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native wetlands.
