Klasea integrifolia subsp. monardii

Klasea integrifolia subsp.

monardii, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy blooms.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth along their edges, giving it its subspecific name. It produces purple to pinkish-purple flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species thrives in moist to wet habitats, including wetlands and streambanks, and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
