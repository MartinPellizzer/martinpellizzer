Kniphofia leichtlinii var. distachya

Kniphofia leichtlinii var. distachya, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
