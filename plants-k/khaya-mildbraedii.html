Khaya mildbraedii

Khaya mildbraedii, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces hard, heavy fruits that contain edible seeds used for oil extraction. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

The plant also plays a significant role in traditional medicine and ecological systems across its native range.
