Kurziella gymnoclada

Kurziella gymnoclada, commonly known as the smooth-leaved kurziella, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, citrus-like scent. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Kurziella gymnoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
