Kobresia sanguinea

Kobresia sanguinea, commonly known as red kobresia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The plant is notable for its vibrant red or purple flower spikes, which appear in late summer and early autumn. Kobresia sanguinea thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine species.
