Kengyilia longiaristata

Kengyilia longiaristata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Kengyilia longiaristata is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
