Kalanchoe rhombopilosa var. viridifolia

Kalanchoe rhombopilosa var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish or silver sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
