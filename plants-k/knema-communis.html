Knema communis

Knema communis, commonly known as the white knema, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
