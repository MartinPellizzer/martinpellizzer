Kaempferia lancifolia

Kaempferia lancifolia, commonly known as the sword lily or narrow-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are used in traditional cuisine and have medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
