Kalanchoe antanosiana

Kalanchoe antanosiana, commonly known as the Madagascar window plant, is a succulent native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.

Kalanchoe antanosiana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
