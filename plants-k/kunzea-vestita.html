Kunzea vestita

Kunzea vestita, commonly known as the silver kunzea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding insects. Kunzea vestita thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscaping.
