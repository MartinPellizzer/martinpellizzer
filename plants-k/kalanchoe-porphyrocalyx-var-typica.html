Kalanchoe porphyrocalyx var. typica

Kalanchoe porphyrocalyx var. typica, commonly known as the red-leaved kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
