Kosteletzkya adoensis f. repens

Kosteletzkya adoensis f. repens, commonly known as creeping marsh mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, exhibiting a prostrate growth habit with sprawling stems that root at the nodes. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pale pink to white flowers with five petals. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in humid environments, with a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
