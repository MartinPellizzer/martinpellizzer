Kalmia latifolia f. angustata

Kalmia latifolia f. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain laurel, is a variant of the broadleaf mountain laurel native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats. Its botanical features include evergreen foliage in some varieties, though the narrowleaf form is typically deciduous.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
