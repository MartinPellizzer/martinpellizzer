Koeleria vallesiana subsp. alpicola

Koeleria vallesiana subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine koeleria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at high elevations.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
