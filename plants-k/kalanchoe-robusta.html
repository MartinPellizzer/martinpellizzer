Kalanchoe robusta

Kalanchoe robusta, commonly known as the thick-leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of orange, yellow, and red, typically during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Kalanchoe robusta is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and long-lasting blooms.
