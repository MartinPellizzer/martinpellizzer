Kyllinga pulchella

Kyllinga pulchella, commonly known as the red-veined kyllinga, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, grass-like leaves with distinctive red veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Kyllinga pulchella is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
