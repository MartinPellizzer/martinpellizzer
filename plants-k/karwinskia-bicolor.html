Karwinskia bicolor

Karwinskia bicolor, commonly known as the red-leafed evergreen, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking bicolor leaves, which are dark green on top and reddish underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
