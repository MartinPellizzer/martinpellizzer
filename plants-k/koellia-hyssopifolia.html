Koellia hyssopifolia

Koellia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the Mexican hyssop, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Koellia hyssopifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.
