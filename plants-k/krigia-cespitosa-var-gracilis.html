Krigia cespitosa var. gracilis

Krigia cespitosa var. gracilis, commonly known as slender spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
