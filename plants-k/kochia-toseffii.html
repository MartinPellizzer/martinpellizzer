Kochia toseffii

Kochia toseffii, commonly known as Toseff's kochia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Kochia toseffii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
