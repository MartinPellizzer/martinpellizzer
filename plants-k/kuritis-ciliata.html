Kuritis ciliata

Kuritis ciliata, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb water from the soil.

Kuritis ciliata is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
