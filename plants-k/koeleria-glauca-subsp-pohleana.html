Koeleria glauca subsp. pohleana

Koeleria glauca subsp.

pohleana, commonly known as the blue grass or mountain bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to silver flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Koeleria glauca subsp.

pohleana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
