Kyrstenia grandifolia

Kyrstenia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf kyrstenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
