Kolkwitzia amabilis var. tomentosa

Kolkwitzia amabilis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silver-leaf kolkwitzia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, silver-gray foliage that provides year-round visual interest. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in both its unique foliage and showy blooms.
