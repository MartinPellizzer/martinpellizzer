Kniphofia × elegans

Kniphofia × elegans, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Kniphofia within the family Xanthorrhoeaceae, which is closely related to the grass family. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiky flower clusters that range in color from red to orange, yellow, and pink, depending on the cultivar. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Kniphofia × elegans is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, and as a focal point in garden designs due to its bold appearance and long blooming period.
