Kuritis gilii

Kuritis gilii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with thick, fleshy leaves that store water and a deep root system for accessing underground moisture. Its flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Kuritis gilii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar and shelter for desert wildlife.
