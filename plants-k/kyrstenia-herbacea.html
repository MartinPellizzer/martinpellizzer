Kyrstenia herbacea

Kyrstenia herbacea, commonly known as the creeping kyrstenia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its succulent leaves and stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Kyrstenia herbacea is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
