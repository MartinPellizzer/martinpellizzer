Kommia hamulosa

Kommia hamulosa, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its fragrant wood, it has been historically valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
