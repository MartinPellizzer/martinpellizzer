Kotschya capitulifera

Kotschya capitulifera, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
