Kadua acuminata

Kadua acuminata, commonly known as the slender kadua, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.
