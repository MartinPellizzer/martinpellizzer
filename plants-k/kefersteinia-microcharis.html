Kefersteinia microcharis

Kefersteinia microcharis, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

Kefersteinia microcharis is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental value.
