Kalanchoe spathulata var. baguioensis

Kalanchoe spathulata var. baguioensis, commonly known as the Baguio kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
