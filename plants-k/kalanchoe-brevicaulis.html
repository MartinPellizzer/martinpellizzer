Kalanchoe brevicaulis

Kalanchoe brevicaulis, commonly known as the mother of millions, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Kalanchoe brevicaulis is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
