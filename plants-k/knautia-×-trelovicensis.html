Knautia × trelovicensis

Knautia × trelovicensis, commonly known as the hybrid knautia, is a perennial plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Knautia arvensis and Knautia montana, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flower clusters. The plant typically grows in meadows and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with a central yellow center, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
