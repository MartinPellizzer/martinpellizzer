Kingiella philippinensis

Kingiella philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine kingia, is a unique flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, rosette-like arrangement of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. It is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
