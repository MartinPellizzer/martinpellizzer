Kuhnia dasypia

Kuhnia dasypia, commonly known as the woolly kuhnia, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and insects. Its leaves are succulent, allowing it to store water in arid environments.

Kuhnia dasypia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and adaptability to shady, humid conditions.
