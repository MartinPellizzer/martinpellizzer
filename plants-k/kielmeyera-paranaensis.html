Kielmeyera paranaensis

Kielmeyera paranaensis, commonly known as the Parana rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its latex, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural rubber substitute.

Kielmeyera paranaensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
