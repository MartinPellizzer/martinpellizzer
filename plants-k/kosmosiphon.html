Kosmosiphon

Kosmosiphon, commonly known as the "desert candle" or "candelabra cactus," is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that resembles a candle, with a single, spiny crown at the top. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Kosmosiphon produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate habitat.
