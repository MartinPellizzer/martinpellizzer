Koeleria pseudocristata var. andicola

Koeleria pseudocristata var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean koeleria, is a perennial grass species native to the high Andes of South America.

It typically grows in alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
