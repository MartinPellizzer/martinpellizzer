Koeleria callieri

Koeleria callieri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada brome, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Koeleria callieri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in rocky environments.
