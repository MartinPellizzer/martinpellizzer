Kochia littorea

Kochia littorea, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by wind and insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Kochia littorea is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh maritime environments.
