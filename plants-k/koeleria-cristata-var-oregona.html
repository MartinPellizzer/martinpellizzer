Koeleria cristata var. oregona

Koeleria cristata var. oregona, commonly known as the Oregon hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, wiry leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hairy bristles. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
