Keteleeria davidiana var. pubescens

Keteleeria davidiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy David's fir, is a variant of the David's fir, native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive, soft, pubescent foliage. The leaves are needle-like, typically arranged in spirals along the branches, and they are covered with fine, short hairs, giving the plant its common name. The cones are small and often clustered, with persistent bracts that add to its ornamental value.

This variant is valued for its ecological role and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
