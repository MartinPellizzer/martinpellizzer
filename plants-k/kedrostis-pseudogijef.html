Kedrostis pseudogijef

Kedrostis pseudogijef, commonly known as the African giant lily, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a broad, colorful spathe. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Kedrostis pseudogijef is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
