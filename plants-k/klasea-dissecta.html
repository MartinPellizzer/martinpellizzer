Klasea dissecta

Klasea dissecta, commonly known as the dissected bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant has finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Klasea dissecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
