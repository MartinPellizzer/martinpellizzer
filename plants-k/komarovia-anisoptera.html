Komarovia anisoptera

Komarovia anisoptera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields due to its adaptability.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
