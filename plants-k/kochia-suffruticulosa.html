Kochia suffruticulosa

Kochia suffruticulosa, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or purple foliage that becomes more intense in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads in the winter. Kochia suffruticulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to many environments.
