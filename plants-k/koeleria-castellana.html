Koeleria castellana

Koeleria castellana, commonly known as the castle grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically blooms with pale blue to purple flowers in the summer months, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Koeleria castellana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to harsh alpine conditions.
