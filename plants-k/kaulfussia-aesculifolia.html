Kaulfussia aesculifolia

Kaulfussia aesculifolia, commonly known as the horse chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in spring. The tree's fruit is a spiny capsule containing bright orange seeds, known as conkers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in European landscapes.

The species has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
