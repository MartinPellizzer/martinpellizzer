Kalanchoe × gildenhuysii

Kalanchoe × gildenhuysii, commonly known as the Gildenhuysii kalanchoe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Kalanchoe × gildenhuysii is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets and leaf cuttings, which is a common feature among many succulent species.
