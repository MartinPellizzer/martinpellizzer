Kyllinga odorata var. cylindrica

Kyllinga odorata var. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in wet or marshy environments. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
