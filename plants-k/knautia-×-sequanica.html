Knautia × sequanica

Knautia × sequanica, commonly known as the hybrid meadow knautia, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the tribe Cardueae within the family Asteraceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Knautia arvensis and Knautia mazedonica, characterized by its vibrant, pink to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Knautia × sequanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
