Kyllinga leucocephala var. pluriceps

Kyllinga leucocephala var. pluriceps, commonly known as the white-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features erect, grass-like stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and slightly pointed, with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.
