Kniphofia uvaria var. praecox

Kniphofia uvaria var. praecox, commonly known as the early red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, torch-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is drought-tolerant and produces nectar-rich flowers that attract bees and other pollinators.

This variety is noted for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late spring or early summer.
