Kleinia violacea

Kleinia violacea, commonly known as the violet-kniphofia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and rockeries.
