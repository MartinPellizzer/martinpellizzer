Kyllinga pierreana

Kyllinga pierreana, commonly known as the purple swamp spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, grass-like stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Kyllinga pierreana is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
