Keyserlingia griffithii

Keyserlingia griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in regions with cool, moist climates.

Keyserlingia griffithii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
