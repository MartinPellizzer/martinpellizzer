Kalanchoe suarezensis

Kalanchoe suarezensis, commonly known as the Suarez's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Ecuador.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and summer months.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
