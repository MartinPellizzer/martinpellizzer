Knema vidalii

Knema vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Knema vidalii is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
