Karwinskia rocana

Karwinskia rocana, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and clusters of bright red berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
