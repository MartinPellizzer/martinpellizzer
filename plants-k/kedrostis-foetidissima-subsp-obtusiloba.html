Kedrostis foetidissima subsp. obtusiloba

Kedrostis foetidissima subsp.

obtusiloba, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on leafy branches. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Due to its strong, unpleasant odor, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
