Kunzea ambigua

Kunzea ambigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, attracting native pollinators.

Kunzea ambigua is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.
