Knema tenuinervia subsp. setosa

Knema tenuinervia subsp.

setosa, commonly known as the hairy knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a distinct hairy texture on the underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark, glossy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
