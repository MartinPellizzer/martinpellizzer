Keckia corymbosa

Keckia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Keckia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
