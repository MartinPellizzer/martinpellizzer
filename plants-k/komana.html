Komana

Komana, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub, with leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue, and bloom throughout the year under proper care.

Komana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
