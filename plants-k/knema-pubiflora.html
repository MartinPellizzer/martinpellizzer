Knema pubiflora

Knema pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-barked trunk and dense canopy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are simple, opposite, and often gland-dotted. Knema pubiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
