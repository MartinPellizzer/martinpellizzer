Khaya klainei

Khaya klainei, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. Its fruit is an important source of shea butter, which is widely used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is valued for its timber.
