Kiharapyrum umbellulatum subsp. transcaucasicum

Kiharapyrum umbellulatum subsp.

transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in umbel-like clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Boraginaceae family.
