Kitaibelia vitifolia

Kitaibelia vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to red. Kitaibelia vitifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
