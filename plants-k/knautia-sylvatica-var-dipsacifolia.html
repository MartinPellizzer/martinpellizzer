Knautia sylvatica var. dipsacifolia

Knautia sylvatica var. dipsacifolia, commonly known as the wood knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
