Keteleeria fabri

Keteleeria fabri, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a large evergreen conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are woody and typically contain two to four seeds.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is often planted for ornamental and ecological purposes.
