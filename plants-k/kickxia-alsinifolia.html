Kickxia alsinifolia

Kickxia alsinifolia, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, producing a striking display of white or pale blue petals.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
