Koniga marginata

Koniga marginata, commonly known as the margined koniga, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad leaves with distinct margins that often display a contrasting color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
