Kadsura scandens var. intermedia

Kadsura scandens var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the climbing kadsura, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a woody vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as flies and beetles.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.
