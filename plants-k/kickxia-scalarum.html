Kickxia scalarum

Kickxia scalarum, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and bright yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils. Its flowers are borne in clusters and have a distinctive scalloped edge, giving it its scientific name.

Kickxia scalarum is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
