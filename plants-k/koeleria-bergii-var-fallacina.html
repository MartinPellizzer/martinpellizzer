Koeleria bergii var. fallacina

Koeleria bergii var. fallacina, commonly known as the purplegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically features pale blue to purple florets, which are clustered in dense panicles. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
