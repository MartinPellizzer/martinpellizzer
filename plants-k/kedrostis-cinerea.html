Kedrostis cinerea

Kedrostis cinerea, commonly known as the African cucumber or wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant features a trailing or climbing habit with hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is a spiny, elongated capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
