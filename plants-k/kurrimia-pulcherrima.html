Kurrimia pulcherrima

Kurrimia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Kurrimia pulcherrima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various climates.
