Kalanchoe rosea

Kalanchoe rosea, commonly known as the coral chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or pink hues, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Kalanchoe rosea is drought-tolerant and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative indoor plant.
