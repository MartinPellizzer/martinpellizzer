Koeleria rigidula

Koeleria rigidula, commonly known as rigid wintergrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes are usually short and dense, producing numerous tiny blue to purple flowers.

Koeleria rigidula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
