Kopsia grandiflora

Kopsia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Kopsia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
