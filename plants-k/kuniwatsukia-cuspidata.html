Kuniwatsukia cuspidata

Kuniwatsukia cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to East Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are adapted for efficient water absorption and gas exchange. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas and form dense mats.

Kuniwatsukia cuspidata plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
