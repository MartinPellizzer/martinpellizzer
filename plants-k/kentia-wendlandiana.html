Kentia wendlandiana

Kentia wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's kentia, is a species of cycad native to New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil and requires minimal maintenance.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
