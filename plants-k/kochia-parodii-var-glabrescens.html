Kochia parodii var. glabrescens

Kochia parodii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, globe-shaped bracts that turn red in the fall. The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its growth habit is typically upright, with a compact form that makes it suitable for small garden spaces.
