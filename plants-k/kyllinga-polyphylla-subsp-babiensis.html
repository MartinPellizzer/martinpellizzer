Kyllinga polyphylla subsp. babiensis

Kyllinga polyphylla subsp.

babiensis, commonly known as the Babian grass or Babian kyllinga, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas.

The plant has erect, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually green or reddish in color. Kyllinga polyphylla subsp.

babiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
