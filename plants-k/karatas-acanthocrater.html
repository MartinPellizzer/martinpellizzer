Karatas acanthocrater

Karatas acanthocrater, commonly known as the spiny acanthocrater, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features robust, spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, producing yellow to orange composite flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
