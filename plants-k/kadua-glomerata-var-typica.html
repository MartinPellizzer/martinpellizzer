Kadua glomerata var. typica

Kadua glomerata var. typica, commonly known as the clustered kadua, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland habitats, often in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for native pollinators.
