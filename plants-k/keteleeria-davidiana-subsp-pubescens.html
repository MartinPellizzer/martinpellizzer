Keteleeria davidiana subsp. pubescens

Keteleeria davidiana subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent Chinese fir, is a species of conifer native to China. It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces cones that are typically reddish in color and contain winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some ecological importance in its native habitat.
