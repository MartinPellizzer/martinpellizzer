Kalanchoe antennifera

Kalanchoe antennifera, commonly known as the earleaf Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are elongated and have a distinctive ear-like shape. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Kalanchoe antennifera is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets and its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
