Kochia sedifolia var. stellulata

Kochia sedifolia var. stellulata, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery foliage that turns vibrant red, orange, and purple in the fall. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled capsules by late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to landscapes.
