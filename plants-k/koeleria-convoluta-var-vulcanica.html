Koeleria convoluta var. vulcanica

Koeleria convoluta var. vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic koeleria, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in alpine environments.
