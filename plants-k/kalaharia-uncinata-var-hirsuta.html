Kalaharia uncinata var. hirsuta

Kalaharia uncinata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy kalaharia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its more robust and hairy growth compared to other forms of Kalaharia uncinata.
