Knautia sendtneri

Knautia sendtneri, commonly known as the Alpine knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Knautia sendtneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
