Kniphofia × saundersii

Kniphofia × saundersii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and its showy, torch-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the Hemerocallis family and is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
