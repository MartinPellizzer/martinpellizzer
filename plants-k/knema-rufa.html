Knema rufa

Knema rufa, commonly known as the red knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Knema rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used historically in various crafts and construction.
