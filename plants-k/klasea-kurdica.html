Klasea kurdica

Klasea kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Klasea kurdica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in alpine meadows.
