Koanophyllon nudiflorum

Koanophyllon nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stem is leafless and unbranched.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
