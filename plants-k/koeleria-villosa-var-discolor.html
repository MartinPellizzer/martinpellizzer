Koeleria villosa var. discolor

Koeleria villosa var. discolor, commonly known as the hairy koeleria, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a low, spreading growth habit and is well adapted to cold climates.

Koeleria villosa var. discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
