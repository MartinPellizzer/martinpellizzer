Kielmeyera coriacea var. typica

Kielmeyera coriacea var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it can become invasive in certain regions.
