Krynitzkia californica var. subglochidiata

Krynitzkia californica var. subglochidiata, commonly known as the California globe mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny fruit capsules that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with multiple lobes and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
