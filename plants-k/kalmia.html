Kalmia

Kalmia, commonly known as mountain laurel, is a genus of flowering plants in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to eastern North America, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plants typically have showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Kalmia species are evergreen shrubs that can grow up to several meters tall, with leathery, dark green leaves.

They are often found in forested areas and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
