Kraenzlinella gigantea

Kraenzlinella gigantea, commonly known as the giant kraenzlinella, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
