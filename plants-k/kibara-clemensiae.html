Kibara clemensiae

Kibara clemensiae, commonly known as the Malawi water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Lake Malawi region in East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that float on the water's surface, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom during the dry season, adding aesthetic value to its environment. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Kibara clemensiae plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
