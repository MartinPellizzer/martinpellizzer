Komana salicifolia

Komana salicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed willow or komana, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine habitats.

Komana salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
