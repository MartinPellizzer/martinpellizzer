Knautia pseudolongifolia

Knautia pseudolongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, forming showy clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Knautia pseudolongifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
